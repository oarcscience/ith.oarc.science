### ĀRYĀ <span id="fr_934"></span>[1](#fn_928.md)

### [FROM](errata.htm#16.md) THE HARIVAMŚĀ

THOU art liberation, <span id="fr_935"></span>[2](#fn_929.md) prosperity,
life, <span id="fr_936"></span>[3](#fn_930.md)  
Fame, modesty, and learning, reverence and intelligence,  
Twilight, <span id="fr_937"></span>[4](#fn_931.md) night, lustrous day,  
Sleep and the night of death, <span id="fr_938"></span>[5](#fn_932.md)  
Āryā, Kātyāyanī, Kauśikī, <span id="fr_939"></span>[6](#fn_933.md)  
Observant of *brahmacarya*, <span id="fr_940"></span>[7](#fn_934.md)  
Mother of the leader of the celestial hosts, <span
id="fr_941"></span>[8](#fn_935.md)  
Formidable one, <span id="fr_942"></span>[9](#fn_936.md)  
She who undergoes great austerities <span
id="fr_943"></span>[10](#fn_937.md)

<span id="page_155">p. 155</span>

Jaya and Vijayā, <span id="fr_944"></span>[1](#fn_938.md)  
Contentment, nourishment, forgiveness, mercy, eldest sister of
Yama, <span id="fr_945"></span>[2](#fn_939.md) clad in blue silken
raiment,  
Of various form, <span id="fr_946"></span>[3](#fn_940.md) without form,
having many forms. <span id="fr_947"></span>[4](#fn_941.md) With red,
half-opened eyes. <span id="fr_948"></span>[5](#fn_942.md)  
Large-eyed protectress of Thy votaries.  
O Goddess! Thou resideth on the peaks of fearful mountains, by rivers,
and in caves, forests, and groves.  
Greatly worshipped by the Śavara, Varvara, and Pulinda tribesmen, <span
id="fr_949"></span>[6](#fn_943.md)  
Thou traverseth in all directions of the world  
With peacock-feathered flags.  
Thou livest on the Vindhya mountain,  
Surrounded by fowls, goats, sheep, lions, and tigers,  
Amidst the constant ringing of bells. <span
id="fr_950"></span>[7](#fn_944.md)  
Thou holdest the trident and spear. <span
id="fr_951"></span>[8](#fn_945.md)

<span id="page_156">p. 156</span>

Sun and moon are Thy banners.  
Thou art the ninth day of the dark half of the month,  
And the eleventh day of the light half thereof. <span
id="fr_952"></span>[1](#fn_946.md)  
Baladeva's <span id="fr_953"></span>[2](#fn_947.md) sister art Thou,
glorious one, <span id="fr_954"></span>[3](#fn_948.md)  
Fond of warring <span id="fr_955"></span>[4](#fn_949) (with demons.md),  
Abode of all creatures.  
Thou art death, <span id="fr_956"></span>[5](#fn_950.md) and the supreme
end <span id="fr_957"></span>[6](#fn_951.md) of men,  
Daughter of the cowherd Nanda, <span id="fr_958"></span>[7](#fn_952.md)  
Unconquered,  
Wearing bark and good cloth;  
Raudrī, <span id="fr_959"></span>[8](#fn_953.md) twilight, <span
id="fr_960"></span>[9](#fn_954.md)  
With dishevelled hair, <span id="fr_961"></span>[10](#fn_955.md)  
And who art death,  
Fond art Thou of offerings of wine and flesh. <span
id="fr_962"></span>[11](#fn_956.md)  
Thou art Lakṣmī, <span id="fr_963"></span>[12](#fn_957.md)  
And assumest the form of Alakṣmī <span id="fr_964"></span>[13](#fn_958.md)
for the destruction of Dānavas, <span id="fr_965"></span>[14](#fn_959.md)

<span id="page_157">p. 157</span>

Thou art Sāvitri <span id="fr_966"></span>[1](#fn_960.md) of the Vedas,  
Mother of Mantras. <span id="fr_967"></span>[2](#fn_961.md)  
Thou art the *Dakṣinā* <span id="fr_968"></span>[3](#fn_962.md) of the
*ṛtvik*, <span id="fr_969"></span>[4](#fn_963.md) and art in the altars of
sacrificial rites,  
And the religious sense <span id="fr_970"></span>[5](#fn_964.md) of
*Ṛṣis*.  
Thou art Aditi of Devas. <span id="fr_971"></span>[6](#fn_965.md)  
Plough of cultivators, earth of all creatures,  
The success of merchants who fare in big ships, <span
id="fr_972"></span>[7](#fn_966.md)  
The coast of ocean,  
And foremost Yakṣi of the Yakṣas, <span
id="fr_973"></span>[8](#fn_967.md)  
Surasā of Nāgās, <span id="fr_974"></span>[9](#fn_968.md)  
Virginity <span id="fr_975"></span>[10](#fn_969.md) of maidens and good
fortune of women,  
Knower of the knowledge of Brahman, <span
id="fr_976"></span>[11](#fn_970.md)  
Initiation and supreme beauty,  
Lustre of light, Rohinī <span id="fr_977"></span>[12](#fn_971.md) of
planets.  
Lakshmī, most successful art Thou in courts and fortresses,  
In the confluence of rivers and in the full moon.  
Thou art called Krittivāsa. <span id="fr_978"></span>[13](#fn_972.md)

<span id="page_158">p. 158</span>

Thou art Sarasvatī in the works of Vālmīki, <span
id="fr_979"></span>[1](#fn_973.md)  
Memory in those of Dvaipāyana, <span id="fr_980"></span>[2](#fn_974.md)  
Religious sense of *Ṛṣis* <span id="fr_981"></span>[3](#fn_975.md) and
(perfect) mind of *Devas*. <span id="fr_982"></span>[4](#fn_976.md)  
Thou art the Goddess of wine, <span id="fr_983"></span>[5](#fn_977.md)  
Adored art Thou by Thy creatures for Thy deeds.  
Thou art the charming look of Indra,  
And art the thousand-eyed <span id="fr_984"></span>[6](#fn_978.md),  
Devī of ascetics,  
*Āranī* <span id="fr_985"></span>[7](#fn_979.md) of Agnihotra
Brāhmaṇas, <span id="fr_986"></span>[8](#fn_980.md)  
Hunger of all creatures,  
Who satisfieth those in heaven.  
Thou art Svāhā, <span id="fr_987"></span>[9](#fn_981.md)  
Contentment, patience,  
Receptacle of the Vasus, <span id="fr_988"></span>[10](#fn_982.md) hope of
men,  
Contentment which comes of work fully done;

<span id="page_159">p. 159</span>

All the quarters and their opposites, <span
id="fr_989"></span>[1](#fn_983.md)  
Flame of fire, lustrous Sakunī, <span id="fr_990"></span>[2](#fn_984.md)  
Pūtanā, <span id="fr_991"></span>[3](#fn_985.md) the terrible Revatī, <span
id="fr_992"></span>[4](#fn_986.md)  
Overpowering sleep of all beings,  
Warrior. <span id="fr_993"></span>[5](#fn_987.md)  
Of learning Thou art, Brahmavidyā, <span
id="fr_994"></span>[6](#fn_988.md)  
*Om* and *Vaṣat*. <span id="fr_995"></span>[7](#fn_989.md)  
The Ṛṣis know Thee as Pārvati amongst women.  
As Prajāpati <span id="fr_996"></span>[8](#fn_990.md) has said, Thou art
Arundhatī <span id="fr_997"></span>[9](#fn_991.md) amongst women, with but
one husband. <span id="fr_998"></span>[10](#fn_992.md)  
The difference of disputants. <span id="fr_999"></span>[11](#fn_993.md)  
Famous also art Thou as Indrāṇī <span
id="fr_1000"></span>[12](#fn_994.md)  
This universe, mobile and immobile, is permeated by Thee.  
Without a doubt Thou art saviour in all battles.  
Amidst fires and on the banks of rivers,  
Amidst robbers, in forests and caverns,  
When in prison or when assailed by enemies,  
And in all times and places where life is in peril.

<span id="page_160">p. 160</span>

My heart, my reason, and mind are devoted to Thee.  
Deliver me from all sins. Be gracious to me.  
Whoever rising at dawn reads <span id="fr_1001"></span>[1](#fn_995.md) for
the space of three months  
This sacred hymn to Devī compiled by Vyāsa,  
Being himself pure and of controlled mind.  
Obtains the desired fruit.  
Whoever reads it for six months, to him also  
The desired fruit is given. Such as read it for nine months obtain
celestial vision, and he who reads it for one year gains all such
success <span id="fr_1002"></span>[2](#fn_996.md) as man may desire.  
O Devī! as was said by Dvaipāyana, Thou art the supreme divine
Brahman.  
Thou destroyest the bonds and the fearful destruction of men,  
The loss of children and wealth, fear of death and disease.  
Thou art in the form of desire, and dost grant the objects thereof.  
Having deluded Kamsa, Thou enjoyest the whole world,  
And I also shall live as a cowherd among kine;  
To accomplish my work I shall become a cowherd of Kamsa. <span
id="fr_1003"></span>[3](#fn_997.md)

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_928"></span>[154:1](htg24.htm#fr_934.md) The sacred hymn as
sung in ancient times by *Ṛṣis*, related in chap. lviii of the
Harivamśa, a sequel of the Mahābhārata.

<span id="fn_929"></span>[154:2](htg24.htm#fr_935) *Siddhi* (success,.md)
which here means the supreme *siddhi* or *mukti* (liberation).

<span id="fn_930"></span>[154:3](htg24.htm#fr_936.md) That is according to
the commentator *jīvanam*.

<span id="fn_931"></span>[154:4](htg24.htm#fr_937.md) *Sandhyā* or
junction-time, morning and evening.

<span id="fn_932"></span>[154:5](htg24.htm#fr_938.md) *Kālarātri* which is
*pralayarātri*, or the night of the dissolution of all things.

<span id="fn_933"></span>[154:6](htg24.htm#fr_939.md) Born in the race of
the sage Kuśika.

<span id="fn_934"></span>[154:7](htg24.htm#fr_940.md) The virgin state, or
the first of the *āśramas*.

<span id="fn_935"></span>[154:8](htg24.htm#fr_941.md) Skanda, or Kārtikeya,
son of Śiva and the Devī.

<span id="fn_936"></span>[154:9](htg24.htm#fr_942.md) *Ugrachārī*, which
should be ugrachārinī, but this is *Arsha* (composed by *Ṛṣis*), to whom
the rules of grammar do not apply.

<span id="fn_937"></span>[154:10](htg24.htm#fr_943.md) The Devī practised
great austerities to gain Śiva as Her husband.

<span id="fn_938"></span>[155:1](htg24.htm#fr_944.md) Companion Devatās of
Durgā.

<span id="fn_939"></span>[155:2](htg24.htm#fr_945.md) Mrityu, the God of
Death.

<span id="fn_940"></span>[155:3](htg24.htm#fr_946.md) *Bahurūpā* (see the
Lalitā, verse 155). The Devī Bhāg. Pr. says, "She is formless because
She is supreme, She has many forms because of Her activity" (see also
Devī and Vāmana Purāṇas): "She is also the *Śakti* of the countless
Rudras." The term is much commented upon in the Purāṇas, Upapurāṇas, and
Tantras.

<span id="fn_941"></span>[155:4](htg24.htm#fr_947.md) *Anekavidharūpinī*.

<span id="fn_942"></span>[155:5](htg24.htm#fr_948.md) *Virūpākṣī*. Śiva is
also called *Virūpākṣa*. His eyes are, either owing to his state of
*samādhi*, or consumption of *bhang*, pictured as in a vague, dreamy,
half-open state.

<span id="fn_943"></span>[155:6](htg24.htm#fr_949.md) *Śavarair
varvaraischaiva pulindaischa supūjitā*--a line worthy of remark, for
these were savage and non-aryan tribes.

<span id="fn_944"></span>[155:7](htg24.htm#fr_950.md) During worship of the
image the worshipper rings a bell. There was a constant ringing of
bells.

<span id="fn_945"></span>[155:8](htg24.htm#fr_951.md) *Pattisha*, a kind of
spear with a sharp edge.

<span id="fn_946"></span>[156:1](htg24.htm#fr_952.md) Auspicious days. On
the ninth (*Navamī*) there is *Caṇḍīpātha* (reading of Caṇḍī), and on
the eleventh (*Ekādaśī*) fasting.

<span id="fn_947"></span>[156:2](htg24.htm#fr_953.md) Kṛṣṇa's eldest
brother.

<span id="fn_948"></span>[156:3](htg24.htm#fr_954.md) *Rajanīya* in text is
said to be a wrong reading for *mahanīya*.

<span id="fn_949"></span>[156:4](htg24.htm#fr_955.md) *Kalahapriyā*.
Literally, quarrelsome.

<span id="fn_950"></span>[156:5](htg24.htm#fr_956.md) *Niṣṭha*, which
according to the commentator, here means *maraṇam*.

<span id="fn_951"></span>[156:6](htg24.htm#fr_957.md) That is, *mukti*
(Liberation).

<span id="fn_952"></span>[156:7](htg24.htm#fr_958.md) Foster-father of
Kṛṣṇa.

<span id="fn_953"></span>[156:8](htg24.htm#fr_959) The dark (*tāmasika*.md)
energy, called Raudrī, is said to be Cāmuṇḍā. There are said to be nine
crores of different Cāmuṇḍās. (see Bhāskararāya Comm., Lalitā, verse
155).

<span id="fn_954"></span>[156:9](htg24.htm#fr_960.md) *Sandhyā*.

<span id="fn_955"></span>[156:10](htg24.htm#fr_961.md) Kālī and Tārā are
always so represented (see Karpūrādistotra).

<span id="fn_956"></span>[156:11](htg24.htm#fr_962.md)
*Surāmāmsabalipriyā*.

<span id="fn_957"></span>[156:12](htg24.htm#fr_963.md) Devī of wealth and
prosperity.

<span id="fn_958"></span>[156:13](htg24.htm#fr_964.md) Devī of misfortune
and poverty.

<span id="fn_959"></span>[156:14](htg24.htm#fr_965.md) Sons of Danu,
enemies of the Devas.

<span id="fn_960"></span>[157:1](htg24.htm#fr_966.md) The Gāyatrī *mantra*.

<span id="fn_961"></span>[157:2](htg24.htm#fr_967.md) *Mantraganasya*, or,
according to another reading, *bhūtaganasya*.

<span id="fn_962"></span>[157:3](htg24.htm#fr_968.md) The present offered
to the officiating Brāhmaṇa.

<span id="fn_963"></span>[157:4](htg24.htm#fr_969.md) Priest.

<span id="fn_964"></span>[157:5](htg24.htm#fr_970.md) *Dharmabuddhi*, a
term difficult to translate. A man is said to have *dharmabuddhi* who
has great respect for religion and duty.

<span id="fn_965"></span>[157:6](htg24.htm#fr_971.md) The Devas were
children of Aditi, as the Daityas were of Diti.

<span id="fn_966"></span>[157:7](htg24.htm#fr_972.md) *Sāngyātrikānām* =
*potavanijām*.

<span id="fn_967"></span>[157:8](htg24.htm#fr_973.md) That is, She is the
Mother of Kubera, the King of the Yakṣas, a class of *Devayoni*.

<span id="fn_968"></span>[157:9](htg24.htm#fr_974.md) Mother of the serpent
divinities (Nāgās).

<span id="fn_969"></span>[157:10](htg24.htm#fr_975.md) *Brahmacarya*.

<span id="fn_970"></span>[157:11](htg24.htm#fr_976.md) *Brahmavādinī*.

<span id="fn_971"></span>[157:12](htg24.htm#fr_977.md) The name of an
asterism.

<span id="fn_972"></span>[157:13](htg24.htm#fr_978.md) The name of Śiva as
clad in tiger-skin.

<span id="fn_973"></span>[158:1](htg24.htm#fr_979.md) Author of the
Rāmāyana. Tradition says that he obtained a boon from the Goddess of
learning and composed that work.

<span id="fn_974"></span>[158:2](htg24.htm#fr_980.md) Kṛṣṇa Dvaipāyana, one
of the Vyasas, arranger of the Purāṇas, etc., who is said to have had
all such *śāstra* by heart.

<span id="fn_975"></span>[158:3](htg24.htm#fr_981.md) *Vide ante* (see [p.
157](#page_157), note 5.md).

<span id="fn_976"></span>[158:4](htg24.htm#fr_982.md) Mānasī, which the
Commentator says = "*Satyasangkalpātmikā chetovritti*" that is, whose
will and thought fully realizes itself.

<span id="fn_977"></span>[158:5](htg24.htm#fr_983.md) *Surādevi*.

<span id="fn_978"></span>[158:6](htg24.htm#fr_984.md) Indra.

<span id="fn_979"></span>[158:7](htg24.htm#fr_985.md) *Ārani* are the two
sticks of *samid* wood used to kindle sacrificial fire.

<span id="fn_980"></span>[158:8](htg24.htm#fr_986.md) Brāhmaṇas who cherish
fire in the house and perform *homa* thrice daily.

<span id="fn_981"></span>[158:9](htg24.htm#fr_987.md) Wife of Agni, the
*mantra* used when making *homa*.

<span id="fn_982"></span>[158:10](htg24.htm#fr_988.md) Of whom there are
eight: Apa, Dḥruva, Soma, Dhara, Anila, Anala, Pratyusha, Prabhāsa (see
Vishnupurāṇa, Book I., chap. xv.)

<span id="fn_983"></span>[159:1](htg24.htm#fr_989.md) *e.g.*, north and
south, east and west, etc.

<span id="fn_984"></span>[159:2](htg24.htm#fr_990.md) A terrible spirit of
that name.

<span id="fn_985"></span>[159:3](htg24.htm#fr_991.md) A female demon who
attempted to destroy, but who was destroyed by the infant Kṛṣṇa.

<span id="fn_986"></span>[159:4](htg24.htm#fr_992.md) Name of the
twenty-seventh constellation, containing thirty-two stars.

<span id="fn_987"></span>[159:5](htg24.htm#fr_993.md) *Kṣatriyā*.

<span id="fn_988"></span>[159:6](htg24.htm#fr_994.md) The science of
Brahman.

<span id="fn_989"></span>[159:7](htg24.htm#fr_995.md) The *Mahāmantra*
"*om*". *Vaṣat* is a *mantra*. As *Svāhā* is used with *homa*, so
*srauṣat*, *vauṣat*, *vaṣat*, and *svadhā* are used in *pitṛkriyā*.

<span id="fn_990"></span>[159:8](htg24.htm#fr_996.md) Brahmā.

<span id="fn_991"></span>[159:9](htg24.htm#fr_997.md) Wife of the sage
Vaśiṣṭha, famous for her constancy and devotion.

<span id="fn_992"></span>[159:10](htg24.htm#fr_998.md) *Ekabhartrinām*.

<span id="fn_993"></span>[159:11](htg24.htm#fr_999.md)
*Bhedovivādashīlānām*.

<span id="fn_994"></span>[159:12](htg24.htm#fr_1000.md) Spouse of Indra.

<span id="fn_995"></span>[160:1](htg24.htm#fr_1001.md) Here follows the
*phala* portion. (see [p. 73](htg13.htm#page_73), note 1.md).

<span id="fn_996"></span>[160:2](htg24.htm#fr_1002.md) *Siddhi*.

<span id="fn_997"></span>[160:3](htg24.htm#fr_1003.md) The tyrant who
sought to slay Kṛṣṇa. The Chapter concludes: "Having thus addressed the
Devī, the Lord disappeared, and She, too, saluting Him, expressed Her
consent by saying, 'So be it'."
