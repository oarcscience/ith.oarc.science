The Cattle-Raid of Cooley  
(Táin Bó Cúalnge)  

from "The Ancient Irish Epic Tale Táin Bó Cúalnge" (1914) by Joseph
Dunn, London: David Nutt.

The Cattle-Raid of Cooley (Táin Bó Cúalnge) is the central epic of the
Ulster cycle. Queen Medb of Connaught gathers an army in order to gain
possession of the most famous bull in Ireland, which is the property of
Daire, a chieftain of Ulster. Because the men of Ulster are afflicted by
a debilitating curse, the seventeen-year-old Cuchulain must defend
Ulster single-handedly.
