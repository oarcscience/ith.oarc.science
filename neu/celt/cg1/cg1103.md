<td data-valign="top"><p>SIAN a chuir Moire nam buadh,<br />
Moch is anamoch dol dachaidh is uath,<br />
Buachaille Padruig, is banachaig Bride,<br />
D’ ur sion, d’ ur dion, ’s d’ ur comhnadh.<br />
     Ho hi holigan, ho m’ aighean,<br />
     Ho hi holigan, ho m’ aighean,<br />
     Ho hi holigan, ho m’ aighean,<br />
         Mo chrodh-laoigh air gach taobh an abhuinn.</p>
<p>Bith buarach chioba air m’ aighean siocha,<br />
Bith buarach shioda air m’ aighean laoigh,<br />
Bith buarach shugain air crodh na duthcha,<br />
Ach buarach ur air m’ aighean gaoil.<br />
     Ho hi holigan, ho m’ aighean.</p>
<p>Fhaic thu bho ud air an lianu,<br />
’S a laogh mear aic air a bialu,<br />
Dean, a chaomhag, mar a rinn i chianu,<br />
Thoir am bainne, a laoigh na Fiannaich.<br />
     Ho hi holigan, ho m’ aighean.</p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p> </p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p>THE charm placed of Mary of light,<br />
Early and late going to and from home,<br />
The herdsman Patrick and the milkmaid Bride,<br />
Be saining you and saving you and shielding you.<br />
     Ho hi holigan, ho my heifer,<br />
     Ho hi holigan, ho my heifer,<br />
     Ho hi holigan, ho my heifer,<br />
         My calving kine on each side of the river.</p>
<p>A shackle of lint on my elfish heifer,<br />
A shackle of silk on my heifer of calves,<br />
A shackle of straw on the cows of the townland,<br />
But a brand new shackle on my heifer beloved.<br />
     Ho hi holigan, ho my heifer.</p>
<p>Seest thou that cow on the plain,<br />
With her frisky calf before her,<br />
Do, thou lovable one, as she did erstwhile,<br />
Give thou thy milk, O calf of 'Fiannach.'<br />
     Ho hi holigan, ho my heifer.</p></td>
