<td data-valign="top"><p>BHEIR Calum-cille dhi-se piseach,<br />
Bheir Coibhi cinneil dhi-se fiar,<br />
Bheir m’ aghan ballaidh dhomh-s’ am bainne<br />
’S a laogh bainionn air a bial.<br />
      Ho! m’ aghan, m’ aghan, m’ aghan,<br />
      Ho! m’ aghan, caon, ciuin,<br />
      M’ aghan caomh, caomh, gradhaidh,<br />
           Gur e gaol do mhathar thu.</p>
<p>Seall thu ’n druis ud thall a froineadh,<br />
’S an druis eil air loin nan smiar,<br />
Is ionann sin is m’ aghan goirridh,<br />
’S a laogh boirionn air a bial.<br />
     Ho! m’ aghan,--</p>
<p>Bheir Bride bhith nan cire geala,<br />
Li na h-eal am aghan gaoil,<br />
’S bheir Muire mhin nam mire meala,<br />
Dhi-se ceal nan cearca-fraoich,<br />
     Ho! m’ aghan,--</p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p> </p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p>COLUMBA will give to her progeny,<br />
Coivi the propitious, will give to her grass,<br />
My speckled heifer will give me her milk,<br />
And her female calf before her.<br />
      Ho my heifer! heifer! heifer!<br />
      Ho my heifer! kindly, calm,<br />
      My heifer gentle, gentle, beloved,<br />
           Thou art the love of thy mother.</p>
<p>Seest yonder thriving bramble bush<br />
And the other bush glossy with brambles,<br />
Such like is my fox-coloured heifer,<br />
And her female calf before her.<br />
              Ho my heifer!--</p>
<p>The calm Bride of the white combs<br />
Will give to my loved heifer the lustre of the swan,<br />
While the loving Mary, of the combs of honey.<br />
Will give to her the mottle of the heather hen.<br />
              Ho my heifer!--</p></td>
