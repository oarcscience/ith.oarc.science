OLD people in the Isles sing this or some other short hymn before
prayer. Sometimes the hymn and the prayer are intoned in low tremulous
unmeasured cadences like the moving and moaning, the soughing and the
sighing, of the ever-murmuring sea on their own wild shores.

They generally retire to a closet, to an outhouse, <span id="page_3">p.
3</span> to the lee of a knoll, or to the shelter of a dell, that they
may not be seen nor heard of men. I have known men and women of eighty,
ninety, and a hundred years of age continue the practice of their lives
in going from one to two miles to the seashore to join their voices with
the voicing, of the waves and their praises with the praises of the
ceaseless sea.
