long cycle of times, and vast ages. The constellations of the stars are
the following--

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_368"></span>[403:3](bim1149.htm#fr_370.md) <span
id="page_402">p. 402</span> The daughter of Don, and styled in the
Triads (Myv. Arch. ii. 73) one of the three beautiful ladies of the Isle
of Britain." This constellation is the <span id="page_403">p. 403</span>
same with the Corona Borealis. Mentioned in " Hanes Taliesin," Ap. Myv.
Arch. i. 19.

<span id="fn_369"></span>[403:4](bim1149.htm#fr_371.md) The Lyre.

<span id="fn_370"></span>[403:5](bim1149.htm#fr_372.md) The son of Don; one
of "the three sublime astronomers of the Isle of Britain." Tr. 89, third
Series. The Galaxy.

<span id="fn_371"></span>[403:6](bim1149.htm#fr_373.md) The Great Bear.

<span id="fn_372"></span>[403:7](bim1149.htm#fr_374.md) The Orion.

<span id="fn_373"></span>[403:8](bim1149.htm#fr_375.md) The Pleiades.
Mentioned in "Hanes Taliesin," Ap. Myv. Arch. i. 19.

<span id="fn_374"></span>[403:9](bim1149.htm#fr_376.md) Cassiopeia.

<span id="fn_375"></span>[405:1](bim1149.htm#fr_377.md) <span
id="page_404">p. 404</span> The title of one of Taliesin's poems, Myv.
Arch. i. 65.

<span id="fn_376"></span>[405:2](bim1149.htm#fr_378.md) The zodiac, or
ecliptic. Mentioned in "Hanes Taliesin."

<span id="fn_377"></span>[405:3](bim1149.htm#fr_379.md) Styled in the
Triads as one of "the three monarch bulls." Tr. 73; third Series.

<span id="fn_378"></span>[405:4](bim1149.htm#fr_380.md) There was a king of
Britain of this name, who flourished from B.C. 859 to 839. He founded
Bath.

<span id="fn_379"></span>[405:5](bim1149.htm#fr_381.md) Mentioned in "Hanes
Taliesin."

<span id="fn_380"></span>[405:6](bim1149.htm#fr_382.md) The Twins.

<span id="fn_381"></span>[405:7](bim1149.htm#fr_383.md) The son of
Bleiddyd--the celebrated king Lear of Shakespeare.

<span id="fn_382"></span>[405:8](bim1149.htm#fr_384.md) Elfin is said to
have first discovered Taliesin, in a leathern bag, fastened to one of
the poles of a weir. He is frequently mentioned by the Bard.

<span id="fn_383"></span>[405:9](bim1149.htm#fr_385.md) <span
id="page_405">p. 405</span> A distinguished character in Welsh Romance.
