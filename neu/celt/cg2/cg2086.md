<span class="small">THE two following poems were got in Kintail. They
are obscure in themselves, and the dialect of Kintail in which they were
recited <span id="page_173">p. 173</span> increases their obscurity. The
reciters repeated them as one poem, but were uncertain whether they were
one or two poems.</span>

<td data-valign="top"><p>A compact there was between Joseph and Virgin,<br />
     In order well-becoming and just,<br />
That the compact might be confirmed<br />
     By the seal of the Great King of virtues.</p>
<p>They went with him to the Temple of God,<br />
     Where the clerics sat within;<br />
As ordained of the Great High King,<br />
     They married ere they came out.</p>
<p>An angel came afterwards:<br />
     'Joseph, why excited thou?'<br />
'I got a woman from the clerics,<br />
     It is not natural for me to be calm.'</p>
<p>'Joseph, abide thou by thy reason,<br />
     Not enlightened of thee to find fault,<br />
What thou hast gotten is a virgin pure,<br />
     On whom man never put hand.'</p>
<p>'How can I believe that from thee?<br />
