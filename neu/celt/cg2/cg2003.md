<span class="small">WHEN this charm is applied, the point of a knife or
a needle, or the tongue of a brooch or of some other sharp instrument,
is pointed threateningly at the part affected. The part is then spat
upon and crossed three times in the names <span id="page_3">p. 3</span>
of the three Persons of the Trinity, whether it be the breast of a woman
or the udder of a cow. The legend says that Mary and Jesus were walking
together when Mary took rose (erysipelas) in her breast, and she said to
Jesus:--</span>

<td data-valign="top"><p>AIC, a Mhic ’s a Chriosda,<br />
Cioch do Mhathar air at;<br />
Thoir-sa fois dh’ an chich,<br />
Cuir-s’ an crion an t-at;<br />
     Thoir-sa fois dh’ an chich,<br />
     Cuir-s’ an crion an t-at.</p>
<p>Faic fein i, Righinn,<br />
’S tu a rug am Mac,<br />
Cuir-sa casgadh air a chich,<br />
Cuir-sa crionadh air an at;<br />
     Cuir-sa casgadh air a chich,<br />
     Cuir-sa crionadh air an at.</p>
<p>Faic thus i, Iosda,<br />
Is tu Righ nan dul;<br />
Cuir-sa casgadh air a chich,<br />
Cuir-sa crionadh air an uth;<br />
     Cuir-sa casgadh air a chich,<br />
     Cuir-sa crionadh air an uth.</p>
<p>Chithim, thubhairt Criosda,<br />
Is nithim mar is fiu,<br />
Bheirim fois dh’ an chich,<br />
’S bheirim sith dh’ an uth;<br />
     Bheirim fois dh’ an chich,<br />
     ’S bheirim sith dh’ an uth.</p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p> </p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p>BEHOLD, Son and Christ,<br />
The breast of Thy Mother swollen;<br />
Give Thou peace to the breast,<br />
Subdue Thou the swelling;<br />
     Give Thou peace to the breast,<br />
     Subdue Thou the swelling.</p>
<p>Behold it thyself, Queen,<br />
Since of thee the Son was born,<br />
Appease thou the breast,<br />
Subdue thou the swelling;<br />
     Appease thou the breast,<br />
     Subdue thou the swelling.</p>
<p>See Thou it, Jesu,<br />
Since Thou art King of life;<br />
Appease Thou the breast,<br />
Subdue Thou the udder;<br />
     Appease Thou the breast,<br />
     Subdue Thou the udder.</p>
<p>I behold, said Christ,<br />
And I do as is meet,<br />
I give ease to the breast,<br />
And rest to the udder;<br />
     I give ease to the breast,<br />
     And rest to the udder.</p></td>
