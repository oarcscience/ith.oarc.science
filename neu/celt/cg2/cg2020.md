THE people quote many proverbs relating to love and to love charms. 'Is
leth-aoin an caothach agus an gaol,'--Twins are lunacy and love. 'Is
ionann an galar gaoil agus an galar caothaich,'--Alike the complaint of
love and the complaint of madness. 'Duinidh gaol mile suil ach duisgidh
<span id="page_39">p. 39</span> cuig mile farmaid,'--Love will close a
thousand eyes but waken five thousand jealousies.

The lucky bones are the joint of the big toe of the right foot and the
nail-joints of the left foot of an old man. These are said to be the
first part of the human body to decay.

<td data-valign="top"><p>CHAN eolas gradhach duit<br />
Uisge thraghadh tromh shop,<br />
Ach gradh an fhir [te] thig riut,<br />
Le bhlaths a tharsainn ort.</p>
<p>Eirich moth ’s an Domhnach,<br />
Gu leac comhnard pleatach<br />
Beir leat currachd sagart,<br />
Agus puball beannach.</p>
<p>Tog sid air do ghualainn<br />
Ann an sluasaid mhaide,<br />
Faigh naoi gasa roinnich<br />
Air an gearradh le tuaigh,</p>
<p>Tri cnamhan seann-duine,<br />
Air an tarruinn a uaigh,<br />
Loisg iad air teine crionaich,<br />
Is dean gu leir ’n an luath.</p>
<p>Crath an dearbh bhrollach do leannain,<br />
An aghaidh gath gaoth tuath,<br />
’S theid mis an rath, ’s am baran duit,<br />
Nach falbh am fear [bean] sin uat.</p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p> </p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p>IT is not love knowledge to thee<br />
To draw water through a reed,<br />
But the love of him [her] thou choosest,<br />
With his warmth to draw to thee.</p>
<p>Arise thou early on the day of the Lord,<br />
To the broad flat flag<br />
Take with thee the biretta of a priest,   [fox-glove (?)<br />
And the pinnacled canopy.              [butter-bur (?)</p>
<p>Lift them on thy shoulder<br />
In a wooden shovel,<br />
Get thee nine stems of ferns<br />
Cut with an axe,</p>
<p>The three bones of an old man,<br />
That have been drawn from the grave,<br />
Burn them on a fire of faggots,<br />
And make them all into ashes.</p>
<p>Shake it in the very breast of thy lover,<br />
Against the sting of the north wind,<br />
And I will pledge, and warrant thee,<br />
That man [woman] will never leave thee.</p></td>
