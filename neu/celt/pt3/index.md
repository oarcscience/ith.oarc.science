## Popular Tales

# of the West Highlands

### by J. F. Campbell

#### Volume III

#### \[1890\]

------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Contents](#contents)    [Start Reading](pt300)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the third of four volumes of Campbell's collection of Scottish
folklore. This volume is less 'fairy tale' oriented than the previous
two volumes, and includes several significant pieces of poetry,
including the [The Lay of the Diarmaid](pt309), [The Yellow
Muilearteach](pt323), [The Lay of the Great Fool](pt325), and the [The
Lay of Osgar](pt335). These have been transcribed in both English and
Gaelic, and the Gaelic text of these lays is included in the etext. The
longest tale so far, the [Story of Conall Gulban](pt327), is found in
this volume. Also of interest are the (fairly transparent) pagan
mythological themes in many of the pieces in this volume.

--John Bruno Hare, April 21st, 2004
