### Gypsy variants.

<span class="contnote">\[paragraph continues\]</span> We have five Gypsy
versions of 'The Master Thief' (Nos. [11](gft071.htm#an_11.md),
[12](gft072.htm#an_12).md), one from Roumania, two from Hungary, and two
from Wales; and two of the cognate story, 'Tropsyn' (Nos.
[27](gft087.htm#an_27), [28](gft088.htm#an_28).md), from the Bukowina and
Wales. We have two of 'The Vampire' ([No. 5](gft065.htm#an_05).md),
Roumanian and Hungarian; three of 'The Bad Mother' (Nos.
[8](gft068.htm#an_08), [9](gft069.htm#an_09).md), Roumanian, Bukowinian,
and Hungarian; two of 'Mare's Son' (Nos. 20, 58), Bukowinian and Welsh;
three of 'It all comes to Light' (Nos. [17](gft077.htm#an_17.md),
[18](gft078.htm#an_18), [19](gft079.htm#an_19).md), Bukowinian, Roumanian,
and Slovak; two of 'The Rich and the Poor Brother' (Nos.
[30](gft090.htm#an_30), [31](gft091.htm#an_31).md), Bukowinian and
Hungarian; three of 'The Robber Bridegroom' ([No.
47](gft107.htm#an_47).md), Polish, Hungarian, and Welsh; three of 'The
Master Smith' (Nos. [59](gft119.htm#an_59), [60](gft120.htm#an_60).md),
Welsh, Catalonian, and Slovak; two of 'The Golden Bush and the Good
Hare' (Nos. [49](gft109.htm#an_49), [75](gft135.htm#an_75).md), Polish and
Scotch; and four of 'The Deluded Dragon' (Nos. [21](gft081.htm#an_21.md),
[22](gft082.htm#an_22).md), Bukowinian, Slovak, Transylvanian, and Turkish.
It is something to have established this much; and it will be seen how

<span id="page_lxv">p. lxv</span>

enormously Mr. Sampson has extended the area of Gypsy folk-tales since
1896. But it still needs much greater extension.
