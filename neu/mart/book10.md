[BOOK XI CHAPTER I](mart326) <span class="small">How Sir Launcelot rode
on his adventure, and how he holp a dolorous lady from her pain, and how
that he fought with a dragon.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER II](mart327) <span class="small">How Sir Launcelot came
to Pelles, and of the Sangreal, and of Elaine, King Pelles'
daughter.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER III](mart328) <span class="small">How Sir Launcelot was
displeased when he knew that he had lain by Dame Elaine, and how she was
delivered of Galahad.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER IV](mart329) <span class="small">How Sir Bors came to
Dame Elaine and saw Galahad, and how he was fed with the
Sangreal.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER V](mart330) <span class="small">How Sir Bors made Sir
Pedivere to yield him, and of marvellous adventures that he had, and how
he achieved them.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER VI](mart331) <span class="small">How Sir Bors departed;
and how Sir Launcelot was rebuked of Queen Guenever, and of his
excuse.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER VII](mart332) <span class="small">How Dame Elaine,
Galahad's mother, came in great estate unto Camelot, and how Sir
Launcelot behaved him there.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER VIII](mart333) <span class="small">How Dame Brisen by
enchantment brought Sir Launcelot to Dame Elaine's bed, and how Queen
Guenever rebuked him.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER IX](mart334) <span class="small">How Dame Elaine was
commanded by Queen Guenever to avoid the court, and how Sir Launcelot
became mad.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER X](mart335) <span class="small">What sorrow Queen
Guenever made for Sir Launcelot, and how he was sought by knights of his
kin.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER XI](mart336) <span class="small">How a servant of Sir
Aglovale's was slain, and what vengeance Sir Aglovale and Sir Percivale
did therefore.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER XII](mart337) <span class="small">How Sir Pervivale
departed secretly from his brother, and how he loosed a knight bound
with a chain, and of other doings.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER XIII](mart338) <span class="small">How Sir Percivale
met with Sir Ector, and how they fought long, and each had almost slain
other.</span>  
[BOOK XI CHAPTER XIV](mart339) <span class="small">How by miracle they
were both made whole by the coming of the holy vessel of
Sangreal.</span>
