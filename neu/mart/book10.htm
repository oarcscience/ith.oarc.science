Le Morte d'Arthur BOOK XI

Intangible Textual Heritage  Legends and Sagas  Index  Previous  Next 
BOOK XI CHAPTER I How Sir Launcelot rode on his adventure, and how he holp a dolorous lady from her pain, and how that he fought with a dragon.
BOOK XI CHAPTER II How Sir Launcelot came to Pelles, and of the Sangreal, and of Elaine, King Pelles' daughter.
BOOK XI CHAPTER III How Sir Launcelot was displeased when he knew that he had lain by Dame Elaine, and how she was delivered of Galahad.
BOOK XI CHAPTER IV How Sir Bors came to Dame Elaine and saw Galahad, and how he was fed with the Sangreal.
BOOK XI CHAPTER V How Sir Bors made Sir Pedivere to yield him, and of marvellous adventures that he had, and how he achieved them.
BOOK XI CHAPTER VI How Sir Bors departed; and how Sir Launcelot was rebuked of Queen Guenever, and of his excuse.
BOOK XI CHAPTER VII How Dame Elaine, Galahad's mother, came in great estate unto Camelot, and how Sir Launcelot behaved him there.
BOOK XI CHAPTER VIII How Dame Brisen by enchantment brought Sir Launcelot to Dame Elaine's bed, and how Queen Guenever rebuked him.
BOOK XI CHAPTER IX How Dame Elaine was commanded by Queen Guenever to avoid the court, and how Sir Launcelot became mad.
BOOK XI CHAPTER X What sorrow Queen Guenever made for Sir Launcelot, and how he was sought by knights of his kin.
BOOK XI CHAPTER XI How a servant of Sir Aglovale's was slain, and what vengeance Sir Aglovale and Sir Percivale did therefore.
BOOK XI CHAPTER XII How Sir Pervivale departed secretly from his brother, and how he loosed a knight bound with a chain, and of other doings.
BOOK XI CHAPTER XIII How Sir Percivale met with Sir Ector, and how they fought long, and each had almost slain other.
BOOK XI CHAPTER XIV How by miracle they were both made whole by the coming of the holy vessel of Sangreal.
