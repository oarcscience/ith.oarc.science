### THE FLY.

Little Fly  
Thy summers play,  
My thoughtless hand  
Has brush’d away.

Am not I  
A fly like thee?  
Or art not thou  
A man like me?

For I dance  
And drink & sing:  
Till some blind hand  
Shall brush my wing.

If thought is life  
And strength & breath  
And the want  
Of thought is death;

Then am I  
A happy fly,  
If I live,  
Or if I die.
