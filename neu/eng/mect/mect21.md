#### The Canterbury Tales

### The Merchant's Epilogue

<span id="an_2419"></span> "Ey! Goddes mercy!" seyde oure Hooste tho,  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2420">2420</span></span> "Now swich
a wyf I pray God kepe me fro!  
<span id="an_2421"></span> Lo, whiche sleightes and subtilitees  
<span id="an_2422"></span> In wommen been! For ay as bisy as bees  
<span id="an_2423"></span> Been they, us sely men for to deceyve,  
<span id="an_2424"></span> And from the soothe evere wol they weyve;  
<span id="an_2425"></span> By this Marchauntes tale it preveth weel.  
<span id="an_2426"></span> But doutelees, as trewe as any steel  
<span id="an_2427"></span> I have a wyf, though that she povre be,  
<span id="an_2428"></span> But of hir tonge, a labbyng shrewe is she,  
<span id="an_2429"></span> And yet she hath an heep of vices mo;  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2430">2430</span></span> Therof no
fors! Lat alle swiche thynges go.  
<span id="an_2431"></span> But wyte ye what? In conseil be it seyd,  
<span id="an_2432"></span> Me reweth soore I am unto hire teyd.  
<span id="an_2433"></span> For and I sholde rekenen every vice  
<span id="an_2434"></span> Which that she hath, ywis I were to nyce.  
<span id="an_2435"></span> And cause why? It sholde reported be  
<span id="an_2436"></span> And toold to hire of somme of this meynee
--  
<span id="an_2437"></span> Of whom, it nedeth nat for to declare,  
<span id="an_2438"></span> Syn wommen konnen outen swich chaffare;  
<span id="an_2439"></span> And eek my wit suffiseth nat therto  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2440">2440</span></span> To tellen
al; wherfore my tale is do."
