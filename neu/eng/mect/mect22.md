#### The Canterbury Tales

### The Squire's Introduction

<span id="an_1"></span> "Squier, com neer, if it youre wille be,  
<span id="an_2"></span> And sey somwhat of love, for certes ye  
<span id="an_3"></span> Konnen theron as muche as any man."  
<span id="an_4"></span> "Nay, sire," quod he, "but I wol seye as I kan  
<span id="an_5"></span> With hertly wyl, for I wol nat rebelle  
<span id="an_6"></span> Agayn youre lust; a tale wol I telle.  
<span id="an_7"></span> Have me excused if I speke amys;  
<span id="an_8"></span> My wyl is good, and lo, my tale is this."
