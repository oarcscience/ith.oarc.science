#### The Shorter Poems

### Against Women Unconstant

<span id="an_1"></span> Madame, for your newefangelnesse  
<span id="an_2"></span> Many a servaunt have ye put out of grace.  
<span id="an_3"></span> I take my leve of your unstedfastnesse,  
<span id="an_4"></span> For wel I wot, whyl ye have lyves space,  
<span id="an_5"></span> Ye can not love ful half yeer in a place,  
<span id="an_6"></span> To newe thing your lust is ay so kene.  
<span id="an_7"></span> In stede of blew, thus may ye were al grene.  
<span id="an_8"></span> Right as a mirour nothing may impresse,  
<span id="an_9"></span> But, lightly as it cometh, so mot it pace,  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_10">10</span></span> So fareth your
love, your werkes beren witnesse.  
<span id="an_11"></span> Ther is no feith that may your herte enbrace,  
<span id="an_12"></span> But as a wedercok, that turneth his face  
<span id="an_13"></span> With every wind, ye fare, and that is sene;  
<span id="an_14"></span> In stede of blew, thus may ye were al grene.  
<span id="an_15"></span> Ye might be shryned for your brotelnesse  
<span id="an_16"></span> Bet than Dalyda, Creseyde or Candace,  
<span id="an_17"></span> For ever in chaunging stant your sikernesse;  
<span id="an_18"></span> That tache may no wight fro your herte arace.  
<span id="an_19"></span> If ye lese oon, ye can wel tweyn purchace;  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_20">20</span></span> Al light for
somer (ye woot wel what I mene),  
<span id="an_21"></span> In stede of blew, thus may ye were al grene.
