A NATIVE PIGSEY STORY.

  
" 'YE see that 'ere hoss there?" said a Liskeard farmer to a
West-Country miner.

"What ov it?" asked the miner.

"Well, that 'ere hoss he 'n been ridden to death a'most by the pigsies
again."

"Pigsies!" said the miner; "thee don't b'leve in they, do 'ee?"

"Ees I do; but I specks you 're a West-Country bucca, ain't 'ee? It you
'd a had yourn hosses wrode to death every riite, you 'd tell another
tayl, I reckon. But as sure as I 'se living the pigsies do ride on 'em
whenever they 'ye a mind to."
