### GREEK

The myths given are mainly from Hesiod's Theogony and the Homeric Hymns,
works composed between the eighth and the sixth century B.C. Little
remains to be said on the subject of Greek mythology. But it is worth
while repeating some sentences written by Miss Jane Harrison:

All men, in virtue of their humanity, are image-makers, but in some the
image is clear and vivid, in others dull, lifeless, wavering. The Greeks
were the

<span id="page_xv">p. xv</span>

supreme *ikonists*, the greatest image-makers that the world has ever
seen, and, therefore, their mythology lives on today. <span
id="fr_11"></span>[12](#fn_11)
