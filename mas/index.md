The Internet has long been home to all kinds of sites about Freemasonry,
*pro* and *con*. However, it has been hard to find reliable, vetted
information on the web on this topic. This page has electronic editions
of core texts on Freemasonry in the public domain, presented complete
and unaltered.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[The Dionysian
Artificers](../cla/dart/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">Hippolyto
Joseph da Costa</span> \[<span class="c_d">1820</span>\]   <span
class="c_b">An essay by one of the pioneers of Freemasonry on an
important ancient mystery religion.</span> </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Illustrations of
Masonry](morgan/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">William
Morgan</span> \[<span class="c_d">1827</span>\]   <span class="c_b">An
early 19th century account of Freemasonry which inspired a great deal of
controversy.</span> </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Duncan's Masonic Ritual and
Monitor](dun/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">Malcolm C.
Duncan</span> \[<span class="c_d">1866</span>\]   <span class="c_b">A
comprehensive guide to York rite Freemasonry</span>, including details
of ceremonies, grips and passwords, extensively illustrated. </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[General Ahiman
Rezon](gar/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">Daniel Sickels</span>
\[<span class="c_d">1868</span>\]   <span class="c_b">One of the most
sought-after Masonic collections</span>, with descriptions of
ceremonies, hymns, building dedications, funerals, and more. </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Morals and Dogma](md/index.md)</span>
  by <span class="c_a">Albert Pike</span> \[<span
class="c_d">1871</span>\]   <span class="c_b">Albert Pikes' magnum opus;
a advanced textbook of the esoteric basis of Freemasonry.</span> </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Devil Worship in
France](../evil/dwf/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">A.E.
Waite</span> \[<span class="c_d">1882</span>\]   <span class="c_b">Waite
debunks the notorious Taxil hoax which falsely accused Freemasons of
worshipping the Devil.</span> </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[The Symbolism of
Freemasonry](sof/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">Albert G.
Mackey</span> \[<span class="c_d">1882</span>\] </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Shibboleth: A Templar
Monitor](shib/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">George Cooper
Connor</span> \[<span class="c_d">1894</span>\]   <span class="c_b">A
Masonic organization modeled after the Templars of old</span> </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[The Builders](bui/index.md)</span>  
by <span class="c_a">Joseph Fort Newton</span> \[<span
class="c_d">1914</span>\]   <span class="c_b">One of the most readable
introductions to Masonic history and philosophy</span>, including (for
once) a factual account of how Freemasonry emerged in the 18th century.
</span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[The Meaning of
Masonry](mom/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">W. L.
Wilmshurst</span> \[<span class="c_d">1922</span>\]   <span
class="c_b">A look inside the symbolism of Masonry.</span> </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Symbolical
Masonry](syma/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">H. L. Haywood</span>
\[<span class="c_d">1923</span>\]   <span class="c_b">A study guide to
the history and symbolism of Freemasonry.</span> </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[The Official Monitor of the Grand
Lodge of Ancient Free and Accepted Masons, State of
Texas](omtx/index.md)</span>  
\[<span class="c_d">1922</span>\]   <span class="c_b">Details of
important Masonic rituals</span>, including initiations for the first
three degrees, installation of a lodge, and a Masonic burial service.
</span>
