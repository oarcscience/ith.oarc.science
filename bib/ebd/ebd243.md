<span id="000">**Maschil**</span> Instructing, occurs in the title of
thirteen Psalms ([Psa 32:1](../kjv/psa032.htm#001), [Psa
42:1](../kjv/psa042.htm#001), 44, etc.). It denotes a song enforcing
some lesson of wisdom or piety, a didactic song. In [Psa
47:7](../kjv/psa047.htm#007) it is rendered, Authorized Version, "with
understanding;" Revised Version, marg., "in a skillful psalm."

<span id="001">**Mash**</span> =Meshech ([Ch1
1:17](../kjv/ch1001.htm#017)), one of the four sons of Aram, and the
name of a tribe descended from him ([Gen 10:23](../kjv/gen010.htm#023))
inhabiting some part probably of Mesopotamia. Some have supposed that
they were the inhabitants of Mount Masius, the present Karja Baghlar,
which forms part of the chain of Taurus.

<span id="002">**Mashal**</span> Entreaty, a levitical town in the tribe
of Asher ([Ch1 6:74](../kjv/ch1006.htm#074)); called Mishal ([Jos
21:30](../kjv/jos021.htm#030)).

<span id="003">**Mason**</span> An artificer in stone. The Tyrians seem
to have been specially skilled in architecture ([Kg1
5:17](../kjv/kg1005.htm#017), [Kg1 5:18](../kjv/kg1005.htm#018); [Sa2
5:11](../kjv/sa2005.htm#011)). This art the Hebrews no doubt learned in
Egypt ([Exo 1:11](../kjv/exo001.htm#011), [Exo
1:14](../kjv/exo001.htm#014)), where ruins of temples and palaces fill
the traveler with wonder at the present day.

<span id="004">**Masrekah**</span> Vineyard of noble vines, a place in
Idumea, the native place of Samlah, one of the Edomitish kings ([Gen
36:36](../kjv/gen036.htm#036); [Ch1 1:47](../kjv/ch1001.htm#047)).

<span id="005">**Massa**</span> A lifting up, gift, one of the sons of
Ishmael, the founder of an Arabian tribe ([Gen
25:14](../kjv/gen025.htm#014)); a nomad tribe inhabiting the Arabian
desert toward Babylonia.

<span id="006">**Massah**</span> Trial, temptation, a name given to the
place where the Israelites, by their murmuring for want of water,
provoked Jehovah to anger against them. It is also called Meribah ([Exo
17:7](../kjv/exo017.htm#007); [Deu 6:16](../kjv/deu006.htm#016); [Psa
95:8](../kjv/psa095.htm#008), [Psa 95:9](../kjv/psa095.htm#009); [Heb
3:8](../kjv/heb003.htm#008)).

<span id="007">**Mattan**</span> Gift. (1.) A priest of Baal, slain
before his altar during the reformation under Jehoiada ([Kg2
11:18](../kjv/kg2011.htm#018)). (2.) The son of Eleazar, and father of
Jacob, who was the father of Joseph, the husband of the Virgin Mary
([Mat 1:15](../kjv/mat001.htm#015)). (3.) The father of Shephatiah ([Jer
38:1](../kjv/jer038.htm#001)).

<span id="008">**Mattanah**</span> A gift, a station of the Israelites
([Num 21:18](../kjv/num021.htm#018), [Num 21:19](../kjv/num021.htm#019))
between the desert and the borders of Moab, in the Wady Waleh.

<span id="009">**Mattaniah**</span> Gift of Jehovah. (1.) A Levite, son
of Heman, the chief of the ninth class of temple singers ([Ch1
25:4](../kjv/ch1025.htm#004), [Ch1 25:16](../kjv/ch1025.htm#016)). (2.)
A Levite who assisted in purifying the temple at the reformation under
Hezekiah ([Ch2 29:13](../kjv/ch2029.htm#013)). (3.) The original name of
Zedekiah (q.v.), the last of the kings of Judah ([Kg2
24:17](../kjv/kg2024.htm#017)). He was the third son of Josiah, who fell
at Megiddo. He succeeded his nephew Jehoiakin.
