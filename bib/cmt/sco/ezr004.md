### [Ezra Chapter 4](../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#001.md)

[Ezra 4:2](../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">ezr 4:2</span></span>

for we seek

The people of the land sought to hinder the work in three ways:

\(1\) by seeking to draw the Jews into an unreal union, ([Ezr
4:3](../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#003)). (cf.md) ([Kg2
17:32](../../../bib/kjv/kg2017.htm#032).md).

\(2\) by "weakening the hands of the people of Judah," ([Ezr
4:4](../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#004).md) i.e, by withholding supplies,
etc.; and

\(3\) by accusations lodged with Ahasuerus and Darius. The first was by
far the most subtle and dangerous. The lives of Ezra and Nehemiah afford
many illustrations of true separation. ([Co2
6:14-18](../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#014).md); ([Ti2
2:19-21](../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#019).md).

[Ezra 4:6](../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">ezr 4:6</span></span>

Ahasuerus

The Cambyses of secular history (529-521 BC); not the Ahasuerus of
Esther, who is the Xerxes of secular history (485 BC).

(See Scofield) - ([Dan 5:31](../../../bib/kjv/dan005.htm#031).md).

[Ezra 4:7](../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">ezr 4:7</span></span>

Artaxerxes

The Artaxerxes of ([Ezr 4:7](../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#007).md) is
identical with Ahasuerus of ([Ezr
4:6](../../../bib/kjv/ezr004.htm#006).md); that is, the Cambyses of profane
history. The Artaxerxes of ([Ezr 7:1](../../../bib/kjv/ezr007.htm#001).md)
is the Longimanus of secular history, BC 418. But

(See Scofield) - ([Dan 5:31](../../../bib/kjv/dan005.htm#031).md).
