[Hebrews 11:39](../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#039.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_039">heb 11:39</span></span>

faith

The essence of faith consists in receiving what God has revealed, and
may be defined as that trust in the God of the Scriptures and in Jesus
Christ whom He hath sent, which receives Him as Saviour and Lord, and
impels to loving obedience and good works ([Joh
1:12](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012).md); ([Jam
2:14-26](../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#014).md). The particular uses of faith
give rise to its secondary definitions:

\(1\) For salvation, faith is personal trust, apart from meritorious
works, in the Lord Jesus Christ, as delivered for our offences, and
raised again for our justification. ([Rom
4:5](../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#005).md); ([Rom
4:23-25](../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#023).md).

\(2\) As used in prayer, faith is the "confidence that we have in him,
that if we ask anything according to his will, he heareth us". ([Jo1
5:14](../../../bib/kjv/jo1005.htm#014).md); ([Jo1
5:15](../../../bib/kjv/jo1005.htm#015).md).

\(3\) As used in reference to unseen things of which Scripture speaks,
faith "gives substance" to them, so that we act upon the conviction of
their reality. ([Heb 11:1-3](../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#001).md).

\(4\) As a working principle in life, the uses of faith are illustrated
in ([Heb 11:1-39](../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#001).md).
