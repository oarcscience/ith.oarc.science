Book Introduction - Philemon

Writer: The Apostle Paul ([Plm 1:1](../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#001).md).

Date: Probably A.D. 64. It is one of the Prison Epistles. See
Introductions to Ephesians and Colossians.

Theme: Onesimus ("profitable"), a slave of Philemon, a Christian of
Colosse, had robbed his master and fled to Rome. There he became a
convert through Paul, who sent him back to Philemon with this letter. It
is of priceless value as a teaching

1\. in practical righteousness;

2\. in Christian brotherhood;

3\. in Christian courtesy;

4\. in the law of love.

The divisions are four

1\. Greeting (Philemon 1:1-3).

2\. The character of Philemon (Philemon 1:4-7).

3\. Intercession for Onesimus (Philemon 1:8-21).

4\. Salutations and conclusion (Philemon 1:22-25).
