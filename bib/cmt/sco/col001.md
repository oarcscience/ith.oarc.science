[Colossians 1:21](../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#021.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">col 1:21</span></span>

reconciled

Reconciliation. The Greek word signifies "to change thoroughly from,"
and occurs in ([Rom 5:10](../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#010).md); ([Rom
11:15](../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#015).md); ([Co1
7:11](../../../bib/kjv/co1007.htm#011).md); ([Co2
5:18](../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#018).md); ([Co2
5:19](../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#019).md); ([Co2
5:20](../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#020).md). Reconciliation looks toward the
effect of the death of christ upon man, as propitiation,

(See Scofield) - ([Rom 3:25](../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#025).md),

is the Godward aspect, and is that effect of the death of Christ upon
the believing sinner which, through divine power, works in him a
"thorough change" toward God from enmity and aversion to love and trust.
It is never said that God is reconciled. God is propitiated, the sinner
reconciled.

Compare ([Co2 5:18-21](../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#018).md).
