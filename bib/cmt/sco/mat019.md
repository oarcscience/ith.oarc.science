[Matthew 19:28](../../../bib/kjv/mat019.htm#028)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">mat 19:28</span></span>

regeneration

(Greek, "palingensia", means "re-creation," "making new)." The word
occurs once again in ([Tit 3:5](../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#005)). There
it refers to the new birth or a believing person; here to the
re-creation of the social order, and renewal of the earth; ([Isa
11:6-9](../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#006)); ([Rom
8:19-23](../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#019)) when the kingdom shall come.

(See "Kingdom (Old Testament),"

(See Scofield) - ([Zac 12:8](../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#008)).

(See Scofield) - ([Co1 15:24](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#024)).

judging

disclosing how the promise ([Isa 1:26](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#026))
will be fulfilled when the kingdom is set up. The kingdom will be
administered over Israel through the apostles, according to the ancient
theocratic judgeship. ([Jdg 2:18](../../../bib/kjv/jdg002.htm#018)).
