Book Introduction - Jude

Writer: Jude, the brother of James ([Jde
1:1](../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#001)).

Date: Probably A.D. 66

Theme: It is not so much Jude who speaks, as the constraining Spirit
([Jde 1:3](../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#003)) and the theme is,
"Contending for the faith" ([Luk
18:8](../../../bib/kjv/luk018.htm#008)),

See Scofield - [Luk 18:8](../../../bib/kjv/luk018.htm#008)) . In this
brief letter the apostasy

See Scofield - [Th2 2:3](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#003)) of the
professing church is predicted, and the cause and course described. As
in Second Timothy and Second Peter the apostasy is treated as having
already set in.

The Epistle is in five divisions:

1\. Introduction (Jude 1:1-2).

2\. Occasion of the Epistle (Jude 1:3-4).

3\. Apostasy is possible (Jude 1:5-7).

4\. Apostate teachers described (Jude 1:8-19).

5\. The saints assured and comforted (Jude 1:20-25).
