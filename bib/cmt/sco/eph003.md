[Ephesians 3:6](../../../bib/kjv/eph003.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">eph 3:6</span></span>

That the Gentiles

That the Gentiles were to be saved was no mystery ([Rom
9:24-33](../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#024)); ([Rom
10:19-21](../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#019)). The mystery, "hid in God",
was the divine purpose to make of Jew and Gentile a wholly new thing --
"the church, which is his Christ's body," formed by the baptism with the
Holy Spirit, ([Co1 12:12](../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#012)); ([Co1
12:13](../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#013)), and in which the earthly
distinction of Jew and Gentile disappears; ([Eph
2:14](../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#014)); ([Eph
2:15](../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#015)); ([Col
3:10](../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#010)); ([Col
3:11](../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#011)). The revelation of this mystery,
which was foretold, but not explained, by Christ ([Mat
16:18](../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#018)) was committed to Paul. In his
writings alone, we find the doctrine, position, walk, and destiny of the
church.
