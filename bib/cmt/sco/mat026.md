### [Matthew Chapter 26](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#001.md)

[Matthew 26:7](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">mat 26:7</span></span>

head

No contradiction of ([Joh 12:3](../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#003).md) is
implied. The ordinary anointing of hospitality and honour was of the
feet ([Luk 7:38](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#038).md) and head ([Luk
7:46](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#046).md). But Mary of Bethany, who alone
of our Lord's disciples had comprehended His thrice repeated
announcement of His coming death and resurrection, invested the
anointing with the deeper meaning of the preparation of His body for
burying. Mary of Bethany was not among the women who went to the
sepulchre with intent to embalm the body of Jesus.

woman

That is, Mary of Bethany.

[Matthew 26:20](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">mat 26:20</span></span>

sat down with the twelve

The order of events on the night of the Passover supper appears to have
been:

\(1\) The taking by our Lord and the disciples of their places at the
table;

\(2\) the contention who should be greatest;

\(3\) the feet washing;

\(4\) the identification Judas as the traitor;

\(5\) the withdrawal of Judas;

\(6\) the institution of the supper;

\(7\) the words of Jesus while still in the room ([Mat
26:26-29](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#026).md); ([Luk
22:35-38](../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#035).md); ([Joh
13:3-35](../../../bib/kjv/joh013.htm#003).md); ([Mat
14:1-31](../../../bib/kjv/mat014.htm#001).md).

\(8\) the words of Jesus between the room and the garden ([Mat
26:31-35](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#031).md); ([Mar
14:26-31](../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#026).md); ([Joh
15:16](../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#016).md); ([Joh
15:17](../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#017).md) it seems probable that the
high-priestly prayer ([Joh 17:1-26](../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#001).md)
was uttered after they reached the garden;

\(9\) the agony in the garden;

\(10\) the betrayal and arrest;

\(11\) Jesus before Caiaphas; Peter's denial.

[Matthew 26:28](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#028.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">mat 26:28</span></span>

remission

Forgiveness. Summary:

The Greek word translated "remission" in ([Mat
26:28](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#028).md); ([Act
10:43](../../../bib/kjv/act010.htm#043).md); ([Heb
9:22](../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#022).md) is elsewhere rendered
"forgiveness." It means, to send off, or away. And this, throughout
Scripture, is the one fundamental meaning of forgiveness -- to separate
the sin from the sinner. Distinction must be made between divine and
human forgiveness:

\(1\) Human forgiveness means the remission of penalty. In the Old
Testament and the New, in type and fulfilment, the divine forgiveness
follows the execution of the penalty. "The priest shall make an
atonement for his sin that he had committed, and it shall be forgiven
him" ([Lev 4:35](../../../bib/kjv/lev004.htm#035).md).

"This is my blood of the new covenant, which is shed for many for the
remission sending away, forgiveness of sins" ([Mat
25:28](../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#028).md). "Without shedding of blood
there is no remission" ([Heb 9:22](../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#022).md).

See "Sacrifice"

(See Scofield) - ([Gen 4:4](../../../bib/kjv/gen004.htm#004).md); ([Gen
4:4](../../../bib/kjv/gen004.htm#004).md).

(See Scofield) - ([Heb 10:18](../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#018).md).

The sin of the justified believer interrupts his fellowship, and is
forgiven upon confession, but always on the ground of Christ's
propitiating sacrifice; ([Jo1 1:6-9](../../../bib/kjv/jo1001.htm#006).md);
([Jo1 2:2](../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#002).md).

\(2\) Human forgiveness rests upon and results from the divine
forgiveness. In many passages this is assumed rather than stated, but
the principle is declared in ([Eph
4:32](../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#032).md); ([Mat
18:32](../../../bib/kjv/mat018.htm#032).md); ([Mat
18:33](../../../bib/kjv/mat018.htm#033).md).

Sin

(See Scofield) - ([Rom 3:23](../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#023).md).

[Matthew 26:39](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#039.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_039">mat 26:39</span></span>

cup

The "cup" must be interpreted by our Lord's own use of that symbol in
speaking of His approaching sacrificial death ([Mat
20:22](../../../bib/kjv/mat020.htm#022).md); ([Joh
18:11](../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#011).md). In view of ([Joh
10:17](../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#017).md); ([Joh
10:18](../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#018).md); He could have been in no fear
of an unwilling death. The value of the account of the agony in the
Garden is in the evidence it affords that He knew fully what the agony
of the cross would mean when His soul was made an offering for sin,
([Isa 53:10](../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#010).md); in the hiding of the
Father's face. Knowing the cost to the utmost, he voluntarily paid it.

A comparison of the narratives gives the following order of events in
the crucifixion day:

26:57-68](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#057).md); ([Mar
14:55-65](../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#055).md); ([Luk
22:63-71](../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#063).md); ([Joh
18:19-24](../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#019).md).

\(2\) The Sanhedrin lead Jesus to Pilate, ([Mat
27:1](../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#001).md); ([Mat
27:2](../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#002).md); ([Mat
27:11-14](../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#011).md); ([Mar
15:1-5](../../../bib/kjv/mar015.htm#001).md); ([Luk
23:1-5](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#001).md); ([Joh
18:28-38](../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#028).md).

\(3\) Pilate sends Jesus to Herod ([Luk
23:6-12](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#006).md); ([Joh

\(5\) Jesus is crowned with thorns and mocked ([Mat

\(6\) Suicide of Judas ([Mat 27:3-10](../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#003).md).

[Matthew 26:71](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#071.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_071">mat 26:71</span></span>

maid

Peter's denial.

Compare ([Mat 26:69](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#069).md); ([Mar
14:69](../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#069).md); ([Luk
22:58](../../../bib/kjv/luk022.htm#058).md); ([Joh
18:25](../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#025).md).

A discrepancy has been imagined in these accounts. Let it be remembered
that an excited crowd had gathered, and that Peter was interrogated in
two places: "With the servants" ([Mat
26:58](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#058).md) where the first charge was made
([Mat 26:69](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#069).md); "the porch" where a
great number of people would be gathered, and where the second and third
interrogations were made by "another maid" and by the crowd, that is,
"they"; ([Mat 26:71](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#071).md); ([Mat
26:73](../../../bib/kjv/mat026.htm#073).md); ([Joh
18:25](../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#025).md).
