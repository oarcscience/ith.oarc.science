### [1 Corinthians Chapter 7](../../../bib/kjv/co1007.htm#001)

[1 Corinthians 7:12](../../../bib/kjv/co1007.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">co1 7:12</span></span>

speak

So far from disclaiming inspiration, the apostle associates his teaching
with the Lord's. Cases had arisen (for example) ([Co1
7:12-16](../../../bib/kjv/co1007.htm#012)) as the Gospel overflowed
Jewish limitations, not comprehended in the words of Jesus; ([Mat
5:31-32](../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#031)); ([Mat
19:5-9](../../../bib/kjv/mat019.htm#005)) which were an instruction,
primarily, to Israel. These new conditions demanded authoritative
settlement, and only the inspired words of an apostle could give that.
([Co1 7:40](../../../bib/kjv/co1007.htm#040)).
