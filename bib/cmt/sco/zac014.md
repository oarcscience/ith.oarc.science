### [Zechariah Chapter 14](../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#001.md)

[Zechariah 14:4](../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">zac 14:4</span></span>

and the mount of Olives

([Zac 14:5](../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#005).md) implies that the cleavage
of the Mount of Olives is due to an earthquake, and this is confirmed
by; ([Isa 29:6](../../../bib/kjv/isa029.htm#006).md); ([Rev
16:19](../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#019).md). In both passages the context,
as in ([Zac 14:1-3](../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#001).md) associates the
earthquake with the Gentile invasion under the Beast; ([Dan
7:8](../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#008).md); ([Rev
19:20](../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020).md). Surely, in a land seamed by
seismic disturbances it should not be difficult to believe that another
earthquake might cleave the little hill called the Mount of Olives. Not
one of the associated events of Zechariah 14 occurred at the first
coming of Christ, closely associated though He then was with the Mount
of Olives.
