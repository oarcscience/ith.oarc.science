[2 Corinthians 6:17](../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">co2 6:17</span></span>

come out from among them

Separation, Summary:

\(1\) Separation in Scripture is twofold:

"from" whatever is contrary to the mind of God; and

"unto" God Himself.

The underlying principle is that in a moral universe it is impossible
for God to fully bless and use His children who are in compromise or
complicity with evil. The unequal yoke is anything which unites a child
of God and an unbeliever in a common purpose. ([Deu
22:10](../../../bib/kjv/deu022.htm#010).md).

\(2\) Separation from evil implies

\(a\) separation in desire, motive, and act, from the world, in the
ethically bad sense of this present world-system.

(See Scofield) - ([Rev 13:8](../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#008).md); and

\(b\) separation from believers, especially false teachers, who are
"vessels unto dishonour". ([Ti2 2:20](../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#020).md);
([Ti2 2:21](../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#021).md); ([Jo2
1:9-11](../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#009).md).

\(3\) Separation is not from contact with evil in the world or the
church, but from complicity with and conformity to. ([Joh
17:15](../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#015).md); ([Co2
6:14-18](../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#014).md); ([Gal
6:1](../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#001).md).

\(4\) The reward of separation is the full manifestation of the divine
fatherhood, ([Co2 6:17](../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#017).md); ([Co2
6:18](../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#018).md), unhindered communion and
worship, ([Heb 13:13-15](../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#013).md), and fruitful
service, ([Ti2 2:21](../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#021).md), as
world-conformity involves the loss of these, though not of salvation.
Here, as in all else, Christ is the model. He was "holy, harmless,
undefiled, and separate from sinners", ([Heb
7:26](../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#026).md), and yet in such contact with
them for their salvation that the Pharisees, who illustrate the
mechanical and ascetic conception of separation,

(See Scofield) - ([Mat 3:7](../../../bib/kjv/mat003.htm#007).md),

judged Him as having lost His Nazarite character. ([Luk
7:39](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#039).md) Cf; ([Co1
9:19-23](../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#019).md); ([Co1
10:27](../../../bib/kjv/co1010.htm#027).md).
