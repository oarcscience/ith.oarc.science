[Matthew 24:3](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">mat 24:3</span></span>

Tell us

The beginning of the Olivet Discourse.

Comparing Matthew 24 with ([Luk
21:20-24](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#020)) answers the threefold
question. The order is as follows: "when shall these things be?" -- that
is, destruction of the temple and city. Answer: ([Luk
21:20-24](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#020)).

Second and third questions: "And what shall be the sign of thy coming,
and of the end of the age?" Answer: ([Mat
24:4-33](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#004)).

Verses 4 to 14 ([Mat 24:4-14](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#004)) have a
double interpretation: They give

\(1\) the character of the age -- wars, international conflicts,
famines, pestilences, persecutions, and false Christs. Compare ([Dan
9:26](../../../bib/kjv/dan009.htm#026)).

This is not the description of a converted world.

\(2\) But the same answer ([Mat
24:4-14](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#004)) applies in a specific way to
the end of the age, namely, Daniel's seventieth week. ([Dan
9:24-27](../../../bib/kjv/dan009.htm#024)).

(See Scofield) - ([Dan 9:24](../../../bib/kjv/dan009.htm#024)).

All that has characterized the age gathers into awful intensity at the
end. Verse 14 ([Mat 24:14](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#014)) has
specific reference to the proclamation of the good news that the kingdom
is again "at hand" by the Jewish remnant ([Isa
1:9](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#009)); ([Rev
14:6](../../../bib/kjv/rev014.htm#006)); ([Rev
14:7](../../../bib/kjv/rev014.htm#007)).

(See Scofield) - ([Rom 11:5](../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#005)).

Verse 15 ([Mat 24:15](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#015)) gives the sign
of the abomination,

(See Scofield) - ([Dan 9:27](../../../bib/kjv/dan009.htm#027)),

the "man of sin," or "Beast". ([Th2
2:3-8](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#003)); ([Dan
9:27](../../../bib/kjv/dan009.htm#027)); ([Dan
12:11](../../../bib/kjv/dan012.htm#011)); ([Rev
13:4-7](../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#004)).

This introduces the great tribulation ([Psa
2:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#005)).

(See Scofield) - ([Rev 7:14](../../../bib/kjv/rev007.htm#014)),

which runs its awful course of three and a half years, culminating in
the battle of

(See Scofield) - ([Rev 19:19](../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#019)),

at which time Christ becomes the smiting Stone of ([Dan
2:34](../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#034)).

The detail of this period ([Mat
24:15-28](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#015)) is:

\(1\) The abomination in the holy place ([Mat
24:15](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#015));

\(2\) the warning ([Mat 24:16-20](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#016)) to
believing Jews who will then be in Jerusalem;

\(3\) the great tribulation, with renewed warning as to false Christs
([Mat 24:21-26](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#021));

\(4\) the sudden smiting of the Gentile world-power ([Mat
24:27](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#027)); ([Mat
24:28](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#028));

\(5\) the glorious appearing of the Lord, visible to all nations, and
the regathering of Israel ([Mat
24:29-31](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#029));

\(6\) the sign of the fig-tree ([Mat
24:32](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#032)); ([Mat
24:33](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#033));

\(7\) warnings, applicable to this present age over which these events
are ever impending ([Mat 24:34-51](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#034)).
([Plm 4:5](../../../bib/kjv/plm004.htm#005)).

Careful study of Daniel 2, 7, 9 and Revelation 13 will make the
interpretation clear.

See, also, "Remnant" ([Isa 1:9](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#009)); ([Rom
11:5](../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#005)).

[Matthew 24:16](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">mat 24:16</span></span>

Then let them which

Compare ([Luk 21:20-24](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#020)).

The passage in Luke refers in express terms to a destruction of
Jerusalem which was fulfilled by Titus, A.D. 70; the passage in Matthew
to a future crisis in Jerusalem after the manifestation of the
abomination.

See "Beast"; ([Dan 7:8](../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#008)); ([Rev
19:20](../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020)); and

"Armageddon"; ([Rev 16:14](../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#014)); ([Rev
19:17](../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#017)).

As the circumstances in both cases will be similar, so are the warnings.
In the former case, Jerusalem was destroyed; in the latter, it will be
delivered by divine interposition.

[Matthew 24:34](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#034)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_034">mat 24:34</span></span>

This generation

(Greek, "genea", the primary definition of which is, "race, kind,
family, stock, breed"). (So all lexicons.) That the word is used in this
sense because none of "these things," that is, the world-wide preaching
of the kingdom, the great tribulation, the return of the Lord in visible
glory, and the regathering of the elect, occurred at the destruction of
Jerusalem by Titus, A.D. 70. The promise is, therefore, that the
generation -- nation, or family of Israel -- will be preserved unto
"these things"; a promise wonderfully fulfilled to this day.
