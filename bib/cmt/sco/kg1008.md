### [3 Kings (1 Kings) Chapter 8](../../../bib/kjv/kg1008.htm#001.md)

[3 Kings (1 Kings) 8:1](../../../bib/kjv/kg1008.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">kg1 8:1</span></span>

Then Solomon

The consecration of the temple illustrates all consecration. The temple,
like the believer ([Th1 5:23](../../../bib/kjv/th1005.htm#023).md) was
threefold: the court, that which was outward, visible, answered to the
body; the holy place, where everything appealed to the sacred emotions,
answered to the soul; the holy of holies, the place of communion with
God ([Exo 25:22](../../../bib/kjv/exo025.htm#022).md) answered to the
spirit of man. The ark was the most all-inclusive type of Christ of any
one of the vessels of the tabernacle.

(See Scofield) - ([Exo 25:9](../../../bib/kjv/exo025.htm#009).md).

When, therefore, the priests brought the ark into the court, the holy
place, and the holy of holies, they were, in type, enthroning Christ
over the body, with its powers and appetites; the soul, seat of the
emotions and desires; and the mind, seat of the capacity to know and
commune with God.

(See Scofield) - ([Gen 1:26](../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#026).md) note 3.

In Christian experience this answers to; ([Rom
12:1-3](../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#001).md); ([Eph
5:18](../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#018).md).
