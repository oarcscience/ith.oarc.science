### [1 Corinthians Chapter 1](../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#001)

[1 Corinthians 1:2](../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">co1 1:2</span></span>

them

([Co1 1:2-9](../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#002)); in contrast with 1
Corinthians 10-13, illustrate a distinction constantly made in the
Epistles between the believer's position in Christ Jesus, in the family
of God, and his walk, or actual state. Christian position in grace is
the result of the work of Christ, and is fully entered the moment that
Christ is received by faith; ([Joh
1:12](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012)); ([Joh
1:13](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#013)); ([Rom
8:1](../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#001)); ([Rom
8:15-17](../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#015)); ([Co1
1:2](../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#002)); ([Co1
1:30](../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#030)); ([Co1
12:12](../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#012)); ([Co1
12:13](../../../bib/kjv/co1012.htm#013)); ([Gal
3:26](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#026)); ([Eph
1:3-14](../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#003)); ([Eph
2:4-9](../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#004)); ([Pe1
2:9](../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#009)); ([Rev
1:6](../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#006)); ([Rev
5:9](../../../bib/kjv/rev005.htm#009)); ([Rev
5:10](../../../bib/kjv/rev005.htm#010)).

The weakest, most ignorant, and fallible believer has precisely the same
relationships in grace as the most illustrious saint. All the after work
of God in his behalf, the application of the word to walk and
conscience; ([Joh 17:17](../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#017)); ([Eph
5:26](../../../bib/kjv/eph005.htm#026)) the divine chastenings; ([Co1
11:32](../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#032)); ([Heb
12:10](../../../bib/kjv/heb012.htm#010)); the ministry of the Spirit
([Eph 4:11](../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#011)); ([Eph
4:12](../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#012)) the difficulties and trials of
the path ([Pe1 4:12](../../../bib/kjv/pe1004.htm#012)); ([Pe1
4:13](../../../bib/kjv/pe1004.htm#013)) and the final transformation at
the appearing of Christ ([Jo1 3:2](../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#002))
have for their object to make the believer's character conform to his
exalted position in Christ. He grows in grace, not into grace.

[1 Corinthians 1:7](../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">co1 1:7</span></span>

coming

Three words are used in connection with the return of the Lord:

\(1\) Greek, "Parousia", meaning "personal presence," also used by Paul
of the "coming" of Stephanas ([Co1
16:17](../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#017)) of Titus ([Co2
7:6](../../../bib/kjv/co2007.htm#006)); ([Co2
7:7](../../../bib/kjv/co2007.htm#007)) and of his own "coming" to
Philippi ([Phi 1:26](../../../bib/kjv/phi001.htm#026)). The word means
simply personal presence, and is used of the return of the Lord as that
event relates to the blessing of the saints; ([Co1
15:23](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#023)); ([Th1
4:14](../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#014)); ([Th1
4:17](../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#017)) and to the destruction of the
man of sin ([Th2 2:8](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#008)).

\(2\) Greek, "Apokalupsis", meaning "unveiling," "revelation." The use
of this word emphasizes the visibility of the Lord's return. It is used
of the Lord ([Th2 1:7](../../../bib/kjv/th2001.htm#007)); ([Pe1
1:7](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#007)); ([Pe1
1:13](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#013)); ([Pe1
4:13](../../../bib/kjv/pe1004.htm#013)) of the sons of God in connection
with the Lord's return ([Rom 8:19](../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#019)) and
of the man of sin ([Th2 2:3](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#003)); ([Th2
2:6](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#006)); ([Th2
2:8](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#008)) and always implies visibility.

\(3\) Greek, "Epiphaneia", meaning "appearing," translated, "brightness"
([Th2 2:8](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#008)) A.V.; "manifestation,"
R.V.), and means simply an appearing. It is used of both advents. ([Ti2
1:10](../../../bib/kjv/ti2001.htm#010)); ([Th2
2:8](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#008)); ([Ti1
6:14](../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#014)); ([Ti2
4:1](../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#001)); ([Ti2
4:8](../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#008)); ([Tit
2:13](../../../bib/kjv/tit002.htm#013)).

[1 Corinthians 1:8](../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">co1 1:8</span></span>

day of our Lord Jesus

The expression, "day of Christ," occurs in the following passages: ([Co1
1:8](../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#008)); ([Co1
5:5](../../../bib/kjv/co1005.htm#005)); ([Co2
1:14](../../../bib/kjv/co2001.htm#014)); ([Phi
1:6](../../../bib/kjv/phi001.htm#006)); ([Phi
1:10](../../../bib/kjv/phi001.htm#010)); ([Phi
2:16](../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#016)). A.V. Has "day of Christ," ([Th2
2:2](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#002)) incorrectly, for "day of the
Lord"; ([Isa 2:12](../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#012)); ([Rev
19:11-21](../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011)). The "day of Christ" relates
wholly to the reward and blessing of saints at his coming, as "day of
the Lord" is connected with judgment.
