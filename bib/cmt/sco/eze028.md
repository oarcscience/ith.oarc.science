### [Ezekiel Chapter 28](../../../bib/kjv/eze028.htm#001.md)

Thou

Here ([Eze 28:12-15](../../../bib/kjv/eze028.htm#012).md), as in ([Isa
14:12](../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#012).md) the language goes beyond the
king of Tyre to Satan, inspirer and unseen ruler of all such pomp and
pride as that of Tyre. Instances of thus indirectly addressing Satan
are: ([Gen 3:14](../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#014).md); ([Gen
3:15](../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#015).md); ([Mat
16:23](../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#023).md). The unfallen state of Satan is
here described; his fall in ([Isa
14:12-14](../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#012).md).

(See Scofield) - ([Rev 20:10](../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#010).md).

But there is more. The vision is not of Satan in his own person, but of
Satan fulfilling himself in and through an earthly king who arrogates to
himself divine honours, so that the prince of Tyrus foreshadows the
Beast. ([Dan 7:8](../../../bib/kjv/dan007.htm#008).md); ([Rev
19:20](../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#020).md).
