### [2 Timothy Chapter 3](../../../bib/kjv/ti2003.htm#001)

[2 Timothy 3:1](../../../bib/kjv/ti2003.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">ti2 3:1</span></span>

know

Apostasy, Summary:

Apostasy, "falling away," is the act of professed Christians who
deliberately reject revealed truth

\(1\) as to the deity of Jesus Christ, and

\(2\) redemption through His atoning and redeeming sacrifice ([Jo1
4:1-3](../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#001)); ([Phi
3:18](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#018)); ([Pe2
2:1](../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#001)). Apostasy differs from error
concerning truth, which may be the result of ignorance ([Act
19:1-6](../../../bib/kjv/act019.htm#001)) or heresy, which may be due to
the sphere of Satan ([Ti2 2:25](../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#025)); ([Ti2
2:26](../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#026)) both of which may consist with
true faith.

The apostate is perfectly described in ([Ti2
4:3](../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#003)); ([Ti2
4:4](../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#004)). Apostates depart from the faith,
but not from the outward profession of Christianity ([Ti2
3:5](../../../bib/kjv/ti2003.htm#005)). Apostate teachers are described
in; ([Ti2 4:3](../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#003)); ([Pe2
2:1-19](../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#001)); ([Jde
1:4](../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#004)); ([Jde
1:8](../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#008)); ([Jde
1:11-13](../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#011)); ([Jde
1:16](../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#016)).

Apostasy in the church, as in Israel ([Isa
1:5](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#005)); ([Isa
1:6](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#006)); ([Isa
5:5-7](../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#005)) is irremediable, and awaits
judgment; ([Th2 2:10-12](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#010)); ([Pe2
2:17](../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#017)); ([Pe2
2:21](../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#021)); ([Jde
1:11-15](../../../bib/kjv/jde001.htm#011)); ([Rev
3:14-16](../../../bib/kjv/rev003.htm#014)).
