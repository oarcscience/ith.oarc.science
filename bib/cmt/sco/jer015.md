[Jeremiah 15:11](../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">jer 15:11</span></span>

the Lord said

The Remnant, of whom Jeremiah was the representative, are carefully
distinguished from the unbelieving mass of the people. The coming
captivity, which they must share, for they too have sinned ([Jer
15:13](../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#013)) though Jehovah's judgment upon
the nation, will be but a purifying chastisement to them, and they
receive a special promise ([Jer
15:11](../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#011)). Verses ([Jer
15:15-18](../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#015)) give the answer of the
Remnant to verses ([Jer 15:11-14](../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#011)). Two
things characterize the believing Remnant always-loyal to the word of
God, and separation from those who mock at that word ([Jer
15:16](../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#016)); ([Jer
15:17](../../../bib/kjv/jer015.htm#017)).

Compare ([Rev 3:8-10](../../../bib/kjv/rev003.htm#008)).
