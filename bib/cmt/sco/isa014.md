son of the morning

Verses 12-14 ([Isa 14:12-14](../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#012)) evidently
refer to Satan, who, as prince of this world-system (see "World," ([Joh
7:7](../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#007)).

(See Scofield) - ([Rev 13:8](../../../bib/kjv/rev013.htm#008))

is the real unseen ruler of the successive world-powers. Tyre, Babylon,
Medo-Persia, Greece, Rome, etc. (see ([Eze
28:12-14](../../../bib/kjv/eze028.htm#012)) Lucifer, "day-star," can be
none other that Satan. This tremendous passage marks the beginning of
sin in the universe. When Lucifer said, "I will," sin began. See

(See Scofield) - ([Rev 20:10](../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#010)).

3:15](../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#015)); ([Mat
16:22](../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#022)); ([Mat
16:23](../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#023)).

[Isaiah 14:26](../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#026)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">isa 14:26</span></span>

whole earth

This universality is significant and marks the whole passage as
referring, not merely to a near judgment upon Assyria, but in a yet
larger sense to the final crash of the present world-system at the end
of the age. (See "Times of the Gentiles," ([Luk
21:24](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#024)); ([Rev
16:14](../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#014)); ([Dan
2:44](../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#044)); ([Dan
2:45](../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#045)) "Armageddon,"; ([Rev
16:14](../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#014)); ([Rev
19:17](../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#017)). No other such universal
catastrophe on the nations is known to Scripture.
