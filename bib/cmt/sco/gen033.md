### [Genesis Chapter 33](../../../bib/kjv/gen033.htm#001)

[Genesis 33:13](../../../bib/kjv/gen033.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">gen 33:13</span></span>

My lord

Not all at once does "Jacob" cease to dominate the walk of "Israel."
Compare ([Gen 35:1-10](../../../bib/kjv/gen035.htm#001)) where the walk
becomes according to the new name.

[Genesis 33:19](../../../bib/kjv/gen033.htm#019)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">gen 33:19</span></span>

Shechem's

Called "Sychem," ([Act 7:16](../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#016)).

[Genesis 33:20](../../../bib/kjv/gen033.htm#020)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">gen 33:20</span></span>

El-elohe-Israel

That is, God, the God of Israel. Jacob's act of faith, appropriating his
new name, but also claiming Elohim in this new sense as the God through
whom alone he could walk according to his new name. See ([Gen
14:18-23](../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#018)).

See note 2 of (See Scofield) - ([Gen
14:18](../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#018)) for a similar appropriation by
Abraham.
