### [Amos Chapter 1](../../../bib/kjv/amo001.htm#001.md)

[Amos 1:2](../../../bib/kjv/amo001.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">amo 1:2</span></span>

roar

"Roar," etc.

Compare ([Isa 42:13](../../../bib/kjv/isa042.htm#013).md); ([Jer
25:30-33](../../../bib/kjv/jer025.htm#030).md); ([Hos
11:10](../../../bib/kjv/hos011.htm#010).md); ([Hos
11:11](../../../bib/kjv/hos011.htm#011).md); ([Joe
3:16](../../../bib/kjv/joe003.htm#016).md).

It will be found that wherever the phrase occurs it is connected with
the destruction of Gentile dominion

(see "Times of the Gentiles," ([Luk
21:24](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#024).md).

(See Scofield) - ([Rev 16:19](../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#019).md)

and the blessing of Israel in the kingdom. Without a doubt a near
fulfilment upon Syria occurred ([Kg2
14:28](../../../bib/kjv/kg2014.htm#028).md) but the expression, "the Lord
will roar," looks forward to a vaster fulfilment.

(See Scofield) - ([Joe 1:4](../../../bib/kjv/joe001.htm#004).md).
