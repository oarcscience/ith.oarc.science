### [Romans Chapter 4](../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#001)

[Romans 4:2](../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">rom 4:2</span></span>

works

Compare ([Jam 2:24](../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#024)).

These are two aspects of one truth. Paul speaks of that which justifies
man before God, namely: faith alone, wholly apart from works; James of
the proof before men, that he who professes to have justifying faith
really has it. Paul speaks of what God sees -- faith; James of what men
see -- works, as the visible evidence of faith. Paul draws his
illustration from ([Gen 15:6](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#006)) James
from ([Gen 22:1-19](../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#001)). James' key phrase
is "ye see" ([Jam 2:24](../../../bib/kjv/jam002.htm#024)) for men cannot
see faith except as manifested through works.

[Romans 4:25](../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#025)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">rom 4:25</span></span>

raised

Christ died under our sins ([Pe1
2:24](../../../bib/kjv/pe1002.htm#024)); ([Co2
5:21](../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#021)) that He was raised and exalted
to God's right hand, "now to appear in the presence of God for us" ([Heb
9:24](../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#024)) is the token that our sins are
gone, that His work for us has the divine approbation and that we, for
whom He suffered, are completely justified.
