### [Jeremiah Chapter 3](../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#001)

[Jeremiah 3:6](../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">jer 3:6</span></span>

The Lord said

The general character of the second message to Judah is:

\(1\) of reproach that the example of Jehovah's chastening of the
northern kingdom ([Kg2 17:1-18](../../../bib/kjv/kg2017.htm#001)) had
produced no effect upon Judah, for example ([Jer
3:6-10](../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#006)).

\(2\) of warning of a like chastisement impending over Judah, for
example ([Jer 3:15-17](../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#015)).

\(3\) of touching appeals to return to Jehovah, for example ([Jer
3:12-14](../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#012)).

\(4\) of promises of final national restoration and blessing, for
example ([Jer 3:16-18](../../../bib/kjv/jer003.htm#016)).

Israel

"Israel" and "Ephraim": names by which the northern kingdom (the ten
tribes) is usually called in the prophets. When by "Israel" the whole
nation is meant, it will appear from the context.
