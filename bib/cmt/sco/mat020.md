[Matthew 20:30](../../../bib/kjv/mat020.htm#030)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_030">mat 20:30</span></span>

two blind men

A discrepancy has been imagined between this account and those in ([Mar
10:46](../../../bib/kjv/mar010.htm#046)); ([Luk
18:35](../../../bib/kjv/luk018.htm#035)). Matthew and Mark obviously
refer to a work of healing as Jesus departed from Jericho. Bartimaeus,
the active one of the two, the one who cried, "Jesus, thou Son of
David," is specifically mentioned by Mark. Of the other one of the
"two," we know nothing. The healing described by ([Luk
18:35](../../../bib/kjv/luk018.htm#035)) occurred before Jesus entered
Jericho. As to the form of appeal, "Son of David",

(compare ([Mat 9:27](../../../bib/kjv/mat009.htm#027)); ([Mat
15:22](../../../bib/kjv/mat015.htm#022)); ([Mat
21:9](../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#009)),

Jesus must have been so addressed constantly. The narratives therefore
supplement, but in no way contradict each other.
