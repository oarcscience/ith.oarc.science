### [1 Kings (1 Samuel) Chapter 16](../../../bib/kjv/sa1016.htm#001)

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 16:21](../../../bib/kjv/sa1016.htm#021)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">sa1 16:21</span></span>

And David

Compare ([Sa1 17:55-56](../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#055)).

The order of events is:

\(1\) David, whose skill on the harp, and valour in the combat with the
lion and bear ([Sa1 17:34](../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#034)); ([Sa1
17:36](../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#036)) were known to "one of the
servants" of Saul, was brought to play before the king ([Sa1
16:17](../../../bib/kjv/sa1016.htm#017)); ([Sa1
16:18](../../../bib/kjv/sa1016.htm#018)).

\(2\) David returns to Bethlehem ([Sa1
17:15](../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#015)).

\(3\) David is sent to Saul's camp ([Sa1
17:17](../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#017)); ([Sa1
17:18](../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#018)) and performs his great exploit.

\(4\) Saul's question ([Sa1 17:55](../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#055));
([Sa1 17:56](../../../bib/kjv/sa1017.htm#056)) implies only that he had
forgotten the name of David's father -- not remarkable certainly in an
oriental king.
