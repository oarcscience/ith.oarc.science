### [Ecclesiastes Chapter 9](../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#001.md)

[Ecclesiastes 9:10](../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">ecc 9:10</span></span>

there is no work

Verse 10 ([Ecc 9:10](../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#010).md) is no more a
divine revelation concerning the state of the dead than any other
conclusion of "the Preacher" ([Ecc
1:1](../../../bib/kjv/ecc001.htm#001).md) is such a revelation. Reasoning
from the standpoint of man "under the sun" the natural man can see no
difference between a dead man and a dead lion. ([Ecc
9:4](../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#004).md). A living dog is better than
either. No one would quote verse 2 ([Ecc
9:2](../../../bib/kjv/ecc009.htm#002).md) as a divine revelation. These
reasonings of man apart from divine revelation are set down by
inspiration just as the words of Satan; ([Gen
3:4](../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#004).md); ([Job
2:4](../../../bib/kjv/job002.htm#004).md); ([Job
2:5](../../../bib/kjv/job002.htm#005).md) are so set down. But that life
and consciousness continue between death and resurrection is directly
affirmed in Scripture. ([Isa 14:9-11](../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#009).md);
([Mat 22:32](../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#032).md); ([Mar
9:43-48](../../../bib/kjv/mar009.htm#043).md); ([Luk
16:19-31](../../../bib/kjv/luk016.htm#019).md); ([Joh
11:26](../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#026).md); ([Co2
5:6-8](../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#006).md); ([Phi
1:21-23](../../../bib/kjv/phi001.htm#021).md); ([Rev
6:9-11](../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#009).md).

grave

Hebrew, "Sheol,"

(See Scofield) - ([Hab 2:5](../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#005).md).
