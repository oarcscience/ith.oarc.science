### [2 Peter Introduction](../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#001.md)

[2 Peter](../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">pe2 0:0</span></span>

Book Introduction - 2 Peter

Writer: The Apostle Peter ([Pe2 1:1](../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#001).md).

Date: Probably A.D. 66

Theme: Second Peter and Second Timothy have much in common. In both, the
Writers are aware that martyrdom is near ([Ti2
4:6](../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#006.md); [Pe2
1:14](../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#014.md) with; [Joh
21:18-19](../../../bib/kjv/joh021.htm#018).md); both are singularly
sustained and joyful; both foresee the apostasy in which the history of
the professing church will end. Paul finds that apostasy in its last
stage when the so-called laity

See Scofield - [Rev 2:6](../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#006).md) , have become
infected ([Ti2 3:1-5](../../../bib/kjv/ti2003.htm#001.md); [Ti2
4:3-4](../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#003).md); Peter traces the origin of the
apostasy to false teachers ([Pe2
2:1-3](../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#001.md); [Pe2
2:15-19](../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#015).md). In Peter the false teachers
deny redemption truth ([Pe2 2:1](../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#001).md); we
shall find in First John a deeper depth--denial of the truth concerning
Christ's person ([Jo1 4:1-5](../../../bib/kjv/jo1004.htm#001).md). In Jude
all phases of the apostasy are seen. But in none of these Epistles is
the tone one of dejection or pessimism. God and His promises are still
the resource of the believer.

The Epistle is in four divisions:

1\. The great Christian virtues (2 Peter 1:1-14).

2\. The Scriptures exalted (2 Peter 1:15-21).

3\. Warnings concerning apostate teachers (2 Peter 2:1-22).

4\. The second coming of Christ and the day of Jehovah (2 Peter 3:1-18).
