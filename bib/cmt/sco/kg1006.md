### [3 Kings (1 Kings) Chapter 6](../../../bib/kjv/kg1006.htm#001.md)

[3 Kings (1 Kings) 6:1](../../../bib/kjv/kg1006.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">kg1 6:1</span></span>

the house of the Lord

The typology of the temple, if indeed it has any typical significance,
is most obscure and difficult. The New Testament invariably expounds the
typology of the tabernacle, not of the temple. The symbolism of the
latter may be revealed in the kingdom-age (see "Kingdom" Old Testament,

(See Scofield) - ([Gen 1:26](../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#026).md).

(See Scofield) - ([Zac 12:8](../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#008).md)

New Testament, ([Luk 1:32](../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#032).md); ([Co1
15:28](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#028).md). In the New Testament the usual
Gk. word for sanctuary (naos) is used

\(1\) of the temple in Jerusalem ([Mat
23:16](../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#016).md).

\(2\) of the believer's body ([Co1
3:16](../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#016).md); ([Co1
3:17](../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#017).md); ([Co1
6:19](../../../bib/kjv/co1006.htm#019).md).

\(3\) of the local church ([Co2 6:16](../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#016).md)
and

\(4\) of the true church ([Eph 2:21](../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#021).md)
But in all these instances the thought is simply of a habitation of God.
No reference to the structure of the temple, as in the case of the
tabernacle ([Heb 9:1-10](../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#001).md); ([Heb
9:39](../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#039).md).

Zif

Second month, that is, May.

[3 Kings (1 Kings) 6:4](../../../bib/kjv/kg1006.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">kg1 6:4</span></span>

windows of narrow lights

Compare ([Ch2 4:20](../../../bib/kjv/ch2004.htm#020).md).

In the holy of holies in the tabernacle no light but the shekinah glory
was provided. In many ways Solomon's temple manifests the spiritual
deterioration of the people, and Jehovah's condescension to it in grace.
