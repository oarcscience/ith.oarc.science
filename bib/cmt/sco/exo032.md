### [Exodus Chapter 32](../../../bib/kjv/exo032.htm#001.md)

[Exodus 32:10](../../../bib/kjv/exo032.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">exo 32:10</span></span>

let me alone

This whole scene affords a striking contrast between law and grace. (Cf)
Moses' intercession with Christ's ([Joh
17:1-26](../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#001).md) Israel was a nation, under
probation ([Exo 19:5](../../../bib/kjv/exo019.htm#005).md); ([Exo
19:6](../../../bib/kjv/exo019.htm#006).md) believers under grace are a
family, awaiting glory; ([Joh 20:17](../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#017).md);
([Rom 5:1](../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#001).md); ([Rom
5:2](../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#002).md). For them there is "an advocate
with the Father, whose propitiatory sacrifice never loses efficacy ([Jo1
2:1](../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#001).md); ([Jo1
2:2](../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#002).md). Moses pleads a covenant ([Exo
32:13](../../../bib/kjv/exo032.htm#013).md). Christ points to a sacrifice
([Joh 17:4](../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#004).md).
