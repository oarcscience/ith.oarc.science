### [Exodus Chapter 8](../../../bib/kjv/exo008.htm#001.md)

[Exodus 8:23](../../../bib/kjv/exo008.htm#023.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">exo 8:23</span></span>

division

Hebrew, peduth, translated, "reemption.";

([Psa 111:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#009).md); ([Psa
130:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#007).md). It is, in type, ([Gal
6:14](../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#014).md).

[Exodus 8:25](../../../bib/kjv/exo008.htm#025.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">exo 8:25</span></span>

in the land

The compromises proposed by Pharaoh are those urged upon Christians
today. The first says in effect: "Be a Christian if you will, but not a
'narrow' one -- stay in Egypt." Invariably it ends in world-conformity,
world-pleasing, and seeking the world's money for God ([Psa
50:9-17](../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#009)) (Cf.md); ([Co2
6:14-18](../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#014).md); ([Gal
1:4](../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#004).md).

[Exodus 8:28](../../../bib/kjv/exo008.htm#028.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">exo 8:28</span></span>

very far

The second compromise is a modification merely of the first. "Do not be
too unwordly." Compare ([Sa1 15:3](../../../bib/kjv/sa1015.htm#003).md);
([Sa1 15:9](../../../bib/kjv/sa1015.htm#009).md); ([Sa1
15:13-15](../../../bib/kjv/sa1015.htm#013).md); ([Sa1
15:19-23](../../../bib/kjv/sa1015.htm#019).md).
