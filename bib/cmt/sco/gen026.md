[Genesis 26:20](../../../bib/kjv/gen026.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">gen 26:20</span></span>

name of the well

The wells of Genesis have significant names, and are associated with
significant events:

\(1\) Beer-lahai-roi, the well of him that liveth and seeth me ([Gen
16:14](../../../bib/kjv/gen016.htm#014).md); ([Gen
24:62](../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#062).md); ([Gen
25:11](../../../bib/kjv/gen025.htm#011).md).

\(2\) Beer-sheba, the well of the oath or covenant ([Gen
21:15-33](../../../bib/kjv/gen021.htm#015).md); ([Gen
22:19](../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#019).md); ([Gen
26:23-25](../../../bib/kjv/gen026.htm#023).md); ([Gen
46:1-5](../../../bib/kjv/gen046.htm#001).md).

\(3\) Esek, contention ([Gen 26:20](../../../bib/kjv/gen026.htm#020).md).

\(4\) Sitnah, hatred ([Gen 26:21](../../../bib/kjv/gen026.htm#021).md).
Esek and Sitnah were Isaac's own attempts at well-digging. Afterward, he
dwelt by the old wells of his father.

\(5\) Rehoboth, enlargement ([Gen
26:22](../../../bib/kjv/gen026.htm#022).md).
