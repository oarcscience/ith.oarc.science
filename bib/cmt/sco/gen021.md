### [Genesis Chapter 21](../../../bib/kjv/gen021.htm#001.md)

[Genesis 21:3](../../../bib/kjv/gen021.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">gen 21:3</span></span>

Sarah

Sarah, type of grace, "the freewoman," and of the "Jerusalem which is
above." See ([Gen 17:15-19](../../../bib/kjv/gen017.htm#015).md); ([Gal
4:22-31](../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#022).md).

2 Isaac is typical in fourfold way:

\(1\) of the Church as composed of the spiritual children of Abraham
([Gal 4:28](../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#028).md).

\(2\) of Christ as the Son "obedient unto death" ([Gen
22:1-10](../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#001).md); ([Phi
2:5-8](../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#005).md).

\(3\) of Christ as the Bridegroom of a called-out bride see Gen 24;
also, "Church," ([Mat 16:18](../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#018).md);

(See Scofield) - ([Mat 16:18](../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#018).md).

\(4\) of the new nature of the believer as "born after the Spirit" ([Gal
4:29](../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#029).md).

[Genesis 21:33](../../../bib/kjv/gen021.htm#033.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_033">gen 21:33</span></span>

everlasting God

\(1\) The Hebrew "Olam" is used in Scripture:

\(a\) of secret or hidden things (for example ([Lev
5:2](../../../bib/kjv/lev005.htm#002).md) "hidden"; ([Kg2
4:27](../../../bib/kjv/kg2004.htm#027).md); "hid"; ([Psa
10:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#001)); "hidest".md);

\(b\) an indefinite time or age ([Lev
25:32](../../../bib/kjv/lev025.htm#032).md); "at any time"; ([Jos
24:2](../../../bib/kjv/jos024.htm#002)) "in old time".md). Hence the word
is used to express the eternal duration of the being of God, ([Psa
90:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa090.htm#002).md). "From everlasting to
everlasting"), and is the Hebrew synonym of the Greek "aion," meaning
"age" or "dispensation".

(See Scofield) - ([Gen 1:26](../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#026).md), note
(4).

\(2\) The ideas therefore of things kept secret and of indefinite
duration combine in this word. Both ideas inhere in the doctrine of the
dispensations or ages. They are among the "mysteries" of God ([Eph
1:9](../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#009).md); ([Eph
1:10](../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#010).md); ([Eph
3:2-6](../../../bib/kjv/eph003.htm#002).md); ([Mat
13:11](../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#011).md). The "everlasting" God (El
Olam) is therefore that name of Deity in virtue of which He is the God
whose wisdom has divided all time and eternity into the mystery of
successive ages or dispensations. It is not merely that He is
everlasting, but that He is God over everlasting things. See, for other
names of Deity:
