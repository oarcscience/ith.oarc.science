[Acts 15:13](../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">act 15:13</span></span>

Men and brethren

dispensationally, this is the most important passage in the New
Testament It gives the divine purpose for this age, and for the
beginning of the next.

\(1\) The taking out from among the Gentiles of a people for His name,
the distinctive work of the present, or church-age. The church is the
ecclesia -- the "called-out assembly." Precisely this has been in
progress since Pentecost. The Gospel has never anywhere converted all,
but everywhere has called out some.

\(2\) "After this namely, the out-calling I will return." James quotes
from ([Amo 9:11](../../../bib/kjv/amo009.htm#011).md); ([Amo
9:12](../../../bib/kjv/amo009.htm#012).md). The verses which follow in Amos
describe the final regathering of Israel, which the other prophets
invariably connect with the fulfilment of the Davidic Covenant (for
example); ([Isa 11:1](../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#001).md); ([Isa
11:10-12](../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#010).md); ([Jer
23:5-8](../../../bib/kjv/jer023.htm#005).md).

\(3\) "And will build again the tabernacle of David," that is,
re-establish the Davidic rule over Israel ([Sa2
7:8-17](../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#008).md); ([Luk
1:31-33](../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031).md).

\(4\) "That the residue of men Israelites may seek after the Lord"
Compare ([Zac 12:7](../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#007).md); ([Zac
12:8](../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#008).md); ([Zac
13:1](../../../bib/kjv/zac013.htm#001).md); ([Zac
13:2](../../../bib/kjv/zac013.htm#002).md).

\(5\) "And all the Gentiles," etc.

Compare ([Mic 4:2](../../../bib/kjv/mic004.htm#002).md); ([Zac
8:21](../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#021).md); ([Zac
8:22](../../../bib/kjv/zac008.htm#022).md).

This is also the order of ([Rom
11:24-27](../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#024).md).

[Acts 15:19](../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#019.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">act 15:19</span></span>

Wherefore

The scope of the decision goes far beyond the mere question of
circumcision. The whole question of the relation of the law to Gentile
believers had been put in issue ([Act
15:5](../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#005).md), and their exemption is declared
in the decision ([Act 15:19](../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#019).md); ([Act
15:24](../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#024).md). The decision might be
otherwise stated in the terms of ([Rom
6:14](../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#014).md). "Ye are not under the law, but
under grace." Gentile believers were to show grace by abstaining from
the practices offensive to godly Jews;

([Act 15:20](../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#020).md); ([Act
15:21](../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#021).md); ([Act
15:28](../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#028).md); ([Act
15:29](../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#029).md).

Compare ([Rom 14:12-17](../../../bib/kjv/rom014.htm#012).md); ([Co1
8:1-13](../../../bib/kjv/co1008.htm#001).md).
