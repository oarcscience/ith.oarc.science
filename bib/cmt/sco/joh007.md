[John 7:53](../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#053)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_053">joh 7:53</span></span>

and every man

([Joh 7:53-8](../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#053)); ([Joh
7:11](../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#011)); is not found in some of the
most ancient manuscripts. Augustine declares that it was stricken from
many copies of the sacred story because of a prudish fear that it might
teach immorality! But the immediate context ([Joh
7:12-46](../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#012)) beginning with Christ's
declaration, "I am the light of the world." seems clearly to have its
occasion in the conviction wrought in the hearts of the Pharisees, as
recorded in; ([Joh 7:9](../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#009)); as also, it
explains the peculiar virulence of the Pharisee's words ([Joh
7:41](../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#041)).
