<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">joh 5:18</span></span>

his Father

(Greek, "patēr", "his own Father"). The Jews understood perfectly that
Jesus was claiming to be God.

Compare ([Joh 10:33](../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#033)).

[John 5:31](../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#031)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_031">joh 5:31</span></span>

witness

Compare ([Joh 8:14](../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#014)). In ([Joh
5:31](../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#031)).

Our Lord, defending His Messianic claims before Jews who denied those
claims, accepts the biblical rule of evidence, which required "two
witnesses"; ([Joh 8:17](../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#017)); ([Num
35:30](../../../bib/kjv/num035.htm#030)); ([Deu
17:6](../../../bib/kjv/deu017.htm#006)). A paraphrase of verse ([Joh
5:31](../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#031)) would be: "If I bear witness of
myself ye will say my witness is not true."

Compare ([Joh 8:14](../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#014)).
