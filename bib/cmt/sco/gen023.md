### [Genesis Chapter 23](../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#001.md)

[Genesis 23:4](../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">gen 23:4</span></span>

buryingplace

Compare ([Gen 33:19](../../../bib/kjv/gen033.htm#019).md); ([Gen
50:13](../../../bib/kjv/gen050.htm#013).md); ([Jos
24:32](../../../bib/kjv/jos024.htm#032).md); ([Act
7:15](../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#015).md); ([Act
7:16](../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#016).md).

A discrepancy in these statements has been fancied. It disappears
entirely before the natural supposition that in the interval of about
eighty years between the purchase by Abraham of the family sepulchre
([Gen 23:4-20](../../../bib/kjv/gen023.htm#004).md) and Jacob's purchase
([Gen 33:19](../../../bib/kjv/gen033.htm#019).md); the descendants of Hamor
(or "Emmor," ([Act 7:15](../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#015).md); ([Act
7:16](../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#016).md) had resumed possession of the
field in which the burial cave was situated. Instead of asserting an
ancient title by inheritance, Jacob repurchased the field. Heth was the
common ancestor.
