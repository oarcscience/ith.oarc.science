[Micah 7:7](../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">mic 7:7</span></span>

Therefore

([Mic 7:7-20](../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#007).md) is, primarily, the
confession and intercession of the prophet, who identifies himself with
Israel.

Compare ([Dan 9:3-19](../../../bib/kjv/dan009.htm#003).md).

Intercession was a test of the prophetic office; ([Jer
27:18](../../../bib/kjv/jer027.htm#018).md); ([Gen
20:7](../../../bib/kjv/gen020.htm#007).md) But Micah's prayer voices also
the heart exercise of the remnant in the last days. Such is prophecy, an
intermingling of the near and the far.

Compare ([Psa 22:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001).md); ([Mat
27:46](../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#046).md).
