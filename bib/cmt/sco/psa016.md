[Psalms 16:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">psa 16:9</span></span>

thy flesh

The 16th Psalm is a prediction of the resurrection of the King. As a
prophet David understood that, not at His first advent, but at some time
subsequent to His death and resurrection Messiah would assume the
Davidic throne. See ([Act 2:25-31](../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#025).md);
([Luk 1:32](../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#032).md); ([Luk
1:33](../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#033).md); ([Act
15:13-17](../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#013).md). See "Davidic Covenant,"

(See Scofield) - ([Sa2 7:14](../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#014).md);

"Kingdom (Old Testament),"

(See Scofield) - ([Zac 12:8](../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#008).md).

See Psalm 22, next in order of the Messianic Psalms.
