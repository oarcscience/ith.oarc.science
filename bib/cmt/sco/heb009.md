[Hebrews 9:27](../../../bib/kjv/heb009.htm#027)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">heb 9:27</span></span>

die

Death, physical, Summary:

\(1\) Physical death is a consequence of sin ([Gen
3:19](../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#019)) and the universality of death
proves the universality of sin. ([Rom
5:12-14](../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#012)).

\(2\) Physical death affects the body only, and is neither cessation of
life nor of consciousness.

(See Scofield) - ([Hab 2:5](../../../bib/kjv/hab002.htm#005)).

(See Scofield) - ([Luk 16:23](../../../bib/kjv/luk016.htm#023)).

([Rev 6:9](../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#009)); ([Rev
6:10](../../../bib/kjv/rev006.htm#010)).

\(3\) All physical death ends in the resurrection of the body.

See "Resurrection" ([Job 19:25](../../../bib/kjv/job019.htm#025)).

(See Scofield) - ([Co1 15:52](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#052)).

\(4\) Because physical death is a consequence of sin, it is not
inevitable to the redeemed. ([Gen
5:24](../../../bib/kjv/gen005.htm#024)); ([Co1
15:51](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#051)); ([Co1
15:52](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#052)); ([Th1
4:15-17](../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#015)).

\(5\) Physical death has for the believer a peculiar qualification. It
is called "sleep," because his body may be "awakened" at any moment.
([Phi 3:20](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#020)); ([Phi
3:21](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#021)); ([Th1
4:14-18](../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#014)).

\(6\) The soul and spirit live, independently of the death of the body,
which is described as a "tabernacle" (tent), in which the "I" dwells,
and which may be put off. ([Co2
5:1-8](../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#001)); ([Co1
15:42-44](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#042)); ([Pe2
1:13-15](../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#013)).

\(7\) At the believer's death, he is "clothed upon" with a "house from
heaven" pending the resurrection of the "earthly house," and is at once
"with the Lord". ([Co2 5:1-8](../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#001)); ([Phi
1:23](../../../bib/kjv/phi001.htm#023)); ([Luk
23:43](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#043)).
