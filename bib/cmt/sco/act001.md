[Acts 1:6](../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">act 1:6</span></span>

again the kingdom to Israel

For forty days, the risen Lord had been instructing the apostles "of the
things pertaining to the kingdom of God," doubtless, according to His
custom ([Luk 24:27](../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#027).md); ([Luk
24:32](../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#032).md); ([Luk
24:44](../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#044).md); ([Luk
24:45](../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#045).md) teaching them out of the
Scriptures. One point was left untouched, namely, the time when He would
restore the kingdom to Israel; hence the apostles' question. The answer
was according to His repeated teaching; the time was God's secret. ([Mat
24:36](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#036).md); ([Mat
24:42](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#042).md); ([Mat
24:44](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#044).md); ([Mat
25:13](../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#013).md); ([Th1
5:1](../../../bib/kjv/th1005.htm#001).md).

[Acts 1:11](../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">act 1:11</span></span>

come

The two Advents -- Summary:

\(1\) The Old Testament foreview of the coming Messiah is in two aspects
-- that of rejection and suffering (as, for example, in Isaiah 53), and
that of earthly glory and power (as, for example, in Isaiah 11; Jeremiah
23, and Ezekiel 37). Often these two aspects blend in one passage (for
example Psalm 2). The prophets themselves were perplexed by this seeming
contradiction ([Pe1 1:10](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#010).md); ([Pe1
1:11](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#011).md). It was solved by partial
fulfilment. In due time the Messiah, born of a virgin according to
Isaiah, appeared among men and began His ministry by announcing the
predicted kingdom as "at hand."

(See Scofield) - ([Mat 4:17](../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#017).md).

The rejection of King and kingdom followed.

\(2\) Thereupon the rejected King announced His approaching crucifixion,
resurrection, departure, and return (Matthew 24, 25). ([Mat
12:38-40](../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#038).md); ([Mat
16:1-4](../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#001).md); ([Mat
16:21](../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#021).md); ([Mat
16:27](../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#027).md); ([Luk
12:35-46](../../../bib/kjv/luk012.htm#035).md); ([Luk
17:20-36](../../../bib/kjv/luk017.htm#020).md); ([Luk
18:31-34](../../../bib/kjv/luk018.htm#031).md); ([Luk
19:12-27](../../../bib/kjv/luk019.htm#012).md).

\(3\) He uttered predictions concerning the course of events between His
departure and return ([Mat 13:1-50](../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#001).md);
([Mat 16:18](../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#018).md); ([Mat
24:4-26](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#004).md).

\(4\) This promised return of Christ becomes a prominent theme in the
Acts, Epistles, and Revelation.

Taken together, the New Testament teachings concerning the return of
Jesus Christ may be summarized as follows:

\(1\) That return is an event, not a process, and is personal and
corporeal ([Mat 23:39](../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#039).md); ([Mat
24:30](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#030).md); ([Mat
25:31](../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#031).md); ([Mar
14:62](../../../bib/kjv/mar014.htm#062).md); ([Luk
17:24](../../../bib/kjv/luk017.htm#024).md); ([Joh
14:3](../../../bib/kjv/joh014.htm#003).md); ([Act
1:11](../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#011).md); ([Phi
3:20](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#020).md); ([Phi
3:21](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#021).md); ([Th1
4:14-17](../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#014).md).

\(2\) His coming has a threefold relation: to the church, to Israel, to
the nations.

\(a\) To the church the descent of the Lord into the air to raise the
sleeping and change the living saints is set forth as a constant
expectation and hope ([Mat 24:36](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#036).md);
([Mat 24:44](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#044).md); ([Mat
24:48-51](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#048).md); ([Mat
25:13](../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#013).md); ([Co1
15:51](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#051).md); ([Co1
15:52](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#052).md); ([Phi
3:20](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#020).md); ([Th1
1:10](../../../bib/kjv/th1001.htm#010).md); ([Th1
4:14-17](../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#014).md); ([Ti1
6:14](../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#014).md); ([Tit
2:13](../../../bib/kjv/tit002.htm#013).md); ([Rev
22:20](../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#020).md).

\(b\) To Israel, the return of the Lord is predicted to accomplish the
yet unfulfilled prophecies of her national regathering, conversion, and
establishment in peace and power under the Davidic Covenant ([Act
15:14-17](../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#014).md) with ([Zac
14:1-9](../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#001).md).

See "Kingdom (Old Testament)" ([Sa2
7:8-17](../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#008).md).

(See Scofield) - ([Zac 13:8](../../../bib/kjv/zac013.htm#008).md); ([Luk
1:31-33](../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#031).md).

(See Scofield) - ([Co1 15:24](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#024).md).

\(c\) To the Gentile nations, the return of Christ is predicted to bring
the destruction of the present political world-system ([Dan
2:34](../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#034).md); ([Dan
2:35](../../../bib/kjv/dan002.htm#035).md),

(See Scofield) - ([Rev 19:11](../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011).md),

the judgment of ([Mat 25:31-46](../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#031).md)
followed by world-wide Gentile conversion and participation in the
blessings of the kingdom; ([Isa
2:2-4](../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#002).md); ([Isa
11:10](../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#010).md); ([Isa
60:3](../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#003).md); ([Zac
