### [1 Corinthians Chapter 9](../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#001)

[1 Corinthians 9:5](../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">co1 9:5</span></span>

A wife who is a sister.

[1 Corinthians 9:11](../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">co1 9:11</span></span>

carnel

That is, things for the body.

[1 Corinthians 9:21](../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#021)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">co1 9:21</span></span>

without

The expression is peculiar and might be literally rendered, "not lawless
toward God, but inlawed to Christ."

See "Law (of Christ)," ([Gal 6:2](../../../bib/kjv/gal006.htm#002));
([Jo2 1:5](../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#005)).

It is another way of saying, "not under the law, but under the rule of
grace." ([Rom 6:14](../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#014)).

[1 Corinthians 9:24](../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#024)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">co1 9:24</span></span>

race

Race-course.

[1 Corinthians 9:27](../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#027)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">co1 9:27</span></span>

castaway

(Greek, "adokimos", "disapproved"). Greek, "dokimos", without the
private, "a", is translated "approved" in ([Rom
14:18](../../../bib/kjv/rom014.htm#018)); ([Rom
16:10](../../../bib/kjv/rom016.htm#010)); ([Co1
11:19](../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#019)); ([Co2
10:18](../../../bib/kjv/co2010.htm#018)); ([Co2
13:7](../../../bib/kjv/co2013.htm#007)); ([Ti2
2:15](../../../bib/kjv/ti2002.htm#015)); and in ([Jam
1:12](../../../bib/kjv/jam001.htm#012)); by the word "tried." The prefix
simply changes the word to a negative, that is, not approved, or,
disapproved. The apostle is writing of service, not of salvation. He is
not expressing fear that he may fail of salvation but of his crown.

See "Rewards"; ([Dan 12:3](../../../bib/kjv/dan012.htm#003)); ([Co1
3:14](../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#014)).
