### [Galatians Chapter 4](../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#001.md)

[Galatians 4:3](../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">gal 4:3</span></span>

world

Greek, "kosmos", means "mankind".

(See Scofield) - ([Mat 4:8](../../../bib/kjv/mat004.htm#008).md).

[Galatians 4:19](../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#019.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">gal 4:19</span></span>

little children

The allegory ([Gal 4:22-31](../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#022).md) is
addressed to justified but immature believers (compare ([Co1
3:1](../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#001).md); ([Co1
3:2](../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#002).md) who, under the influence of
legalistic teachers, "desire to be under the law," and has, therefore,
no application to a sinner seeking justification. It raises and answers,
for the fifth time in this Epistle, the question, Is the believer under
the law? ([Gal 2:19-21](../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#019).md); ([Gal
3:1-33](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#001).md); ([Gal
3:25](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#025).md); ([Gal
3:26](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#026).md); ([Gal
4:4-6](../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#004).md); ([Gal
4:4](../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#004).md); ([Gal
4:9-31](../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#009).md).
