[Matthew 17:2](../../../bib/kjv/mat017.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">mat 17:2</span></span>

was transfigured

The transfiguration scene contains, in miniature, all the elements of
the future kingdom in manifestation:

\(1\) the Lord, not in humiliation, but in glory. ([Mat
17:2](../../../bib/kjv/mat017.htm#002)).

\(2\) Moses, glorified, representative of the redeemed who have passed
through death into the kingdom. ([Mat
13:43](../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#043)); ([Luk
9:30-31](../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#030)).

\(3\) Elijah, glorified, representative of the redeemed who have entered
the kingdom by translation. ([Co1
15:50-53](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#050)); ([Th1
4:14-17](../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#014)).

\(4\) Peter, James, and John, not glorified, representatives (for the
moment) of Israel in the flesh in the future kingdom ([Eze
37:21-27](../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#021)).

\(5\) The multitude at the foot of the mountain ([Mat
17:2](../../../bib/kjv/mat017.htm#002)); representative of the nations
who are to be brought into the kingdom after it is established over
Israel ([Isa 11:10-12](../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#010)); etc.

[Matthew 17:10](../../../bib/kjv/mat017.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">mat 17:10</span></span>

Why then say the scribes that Elias must first come

Compare ([Mat 11:14](../../../bib/kjv/mat011.htm#014)); ([Mar
9:11-13](../../../bib/kjv/mar009.htm#011)); ([Luk
1:17](../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#017)); ([Mal
3:1](../../../bib/kjv/mal003.htm#001)); ([Mal
4:5-6](../../../bib/kjv/mal004.htm#005)).

All the passages must be construed together.

\(1\) Christ confirms the specific and still unfulfilled prophecy of
([Mal 4:5-6](../../../bib/kjv/mal004.htm#005)): "Elias shall truly first
come and restore all things." Here, as in Malachi, the prediction
fulfilled in John the Baptist, and that yet to be fulfilled in Elijah,
are kept distinct.

\(2\) But John the Baptist had come already, and with a ministry so
completely in the spirit and power of Elijah's future ministry ([Luk
1:17](../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#017)) that in an adumbrative and
typical sense it could be said: "Elias is come already."

Compare ([Mat 10:40](../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#040)); ([Plm
1:12](../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#012)); ([Plm
1:17](../../../bib/kjv/plm001.htm#017)) where the same thought of
identification, while yet preserving personal distinction, occurs. ([Joh
1:27](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#027)).
