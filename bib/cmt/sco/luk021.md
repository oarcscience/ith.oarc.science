[Luke 21:20](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">luk 21:20</span></span>

when

Verses 20, 24 ([Luk 21:20](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#020).md); ([Luk
21:24](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#024).md) are not included in the report
of the Olivet discourse as given by Matthew and Mark. Two sieges of
Jerusalem are in view in that discourse. ([Luk
21:20-24](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#020).md) refers to the siege by
Titus, A.D. 70, when the city was taken, and verse 24 ([Luk
21:24](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#024).md) literally fulfilled. But that
siege and its horrors but adumbrate the final siege at the end of this
age, in which the "great tribulation" culminates. At that time the city
will be taken, but delivered by the glorious appearing of the Lord ([Rev
19:11-21](../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011).md). The references in; ([Mat
24:15-28](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#015).md); ([Mar
13:14-26](../../../bib/kjv/mar013.htm#014).md) are to the final tribulation
siege; ([Luk 21:20-24](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#020).md) to the
destruction of Jerusalem by Titus. In Luke the sign is the compassing of
Jerusalem by armies ([Luk 21:20](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#020).md) in;
([Mat 24:15](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#015).md); ([Mar
13:14](../../../bib/kjv/mar013.htm#014).md) the sign is the abomination in
the holy place. ([Th2 2:4](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#004).md).

[Luke 21:24](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#024.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">luk 21:24</span></span>

trodden down of the Gentiles

The "times of the Gentiles" began with the captivity of Judah under
Nebuchadnezzar ([Ch2 36:1-21](../../../bib/kjv/ch2036.htm#001).md); since
which time Jerusalem has been under Gentile overlordship.
