[Psalms 119:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 119:1</span></span>

am 3560, bc 444 (Title), This Psalm, which was probably composed by
Ezra, is another of the alphabetical Psalms. it consists of twenty-two
parts, answering to the number of the Hebrew letters; every part being
divided into eight verses, and each verse beginning with that letter
which forms the title of the part; that is, the first part of eight
verses with א, the second with ב, etc. It is an elegant, important, and
useful composition; the chief subjects of which are the excellence of
God's laws, and the happiness of those who observe them.
