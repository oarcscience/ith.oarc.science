Not for: Though the Canaanites were expelled for their wickedness, it
does not follow, that the Israelites were established in their room on
account of any distinguished virtue, or because they deserved it. On
many occasions, it may be seen in the history of the world, that God
punishes the wicked by the instrumentality of other men, who are as
wicked as themselves. Not the Israelites' righteousness, but the
wickedness of the inhabitants, and the promise of God to their fathers,
was the cause of their obtaining Canaan. [Tit
3:5](../../../bib/kjv/tit003.htm#005)

cast them: Moses might have done this through distress and anguish of

probably he did it emblematically, and perhaps by the direction of God;
intimating thereby, that as by this act of his the tables were broken in
pieces, on which the Law of God was written, so they, by their present
conduct, had made a breach in the covenant, and broken the laws of their
Maker and Sovereign. [Deu 9:17](../../../bib/kjv/deu009.htm#017)
