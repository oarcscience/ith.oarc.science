in their due season: The stated sacrifices and service of the tabernacle
having, probably, been greatly interrupted for several years, and a new
generation having arisen, who were children or minors when the law was
given respecting these ordinances; and as they were now about to enter
into the promised land, where they must be established and constantly
observed; God commands Moses to repeat them to the people in the
following order:

28:19](../../../bib/kjv/num028.htm#019); [Lev
23:18](../../../bib/kjv/lev023.htm#018), [Lev
23:19](../../../bib/kjv/lev023.htm#019); Bishop Patrick observes that no
peace offerings are appointed in this chapter, which were chiefly for
the benefit of the offerers, and therefore in them they were left more
to themselves; but burnt offerings, which were purely for the honour of
God, and confessions of his dominion, and which figured evangelical
piety and devotion, by which the soul is wholly offered up to God, in
the flames of holy love; and sin offerings, which were typical of
Christ's sacrifice of himself, by which we and our services are
perfected and sanctified.
