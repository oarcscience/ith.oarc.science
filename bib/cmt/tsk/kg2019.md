[4 Kings (2 Kings)](../../../bib/kjv/kg2001.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">kg2 19:0</span></span>

Overview

[Kg2 19:1](../../../bib/kjv/kg2019.htm#001.md), Hezekiah mourning, sends to
Isaiah to pray for them; [Kg2 19:6](../../../bib/kjv/kg2019.htm#006.md),
Isaiah comforts them; [Kg2 19:8](../../../bib/kjv/kg2019.htm#008.md),
Sennacherib, going to encounter Tirhakah, sends a blasphemous letter to
Hezekiah; [Kg2 19:14](../../../bib/kjv/kg2019.htm#014.md), Hezekiah's
prayer; [Kg2 19:20](../../../bib/kjv/kg2019.htm#020.md), Isaiah's prophecy
of the pride and destruction of Sennacherib, and the good of Zion; [Kg2
19:35](../../../bib/kjv/kg2019.htm#035.md), An angel slays the Assyrians;
[Kg2 19:36](../../../bib/kjv/kg2019.htm#036.md), Sennacherib is slain by
his own sons.

when he heard: When Sennacherib had levied contributions on Hezekiah, he
marched his army into Egypt; where, after several successes, he laid
siege to Pelusium, and spent much time in it; but hearing that Tirhakah,
king of Ethiopia, whom Strabo calls Therchon, was marching against him
with a great army, to assist his kinsman Sevechus, or Sethon, the king
of Egypt, he durst not abide his coming, but raised the siege; and
returning to Judea, he encamped against Lachish, and afterwards against
Libnah. But finding that Tirhakah pursued him as a fugitive, he marched
back to encounter him; and having totally routed his army, he returned
to wreak his vengeance on Hezekiah. [Sa1
23:27](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#027.md); [Isa
37:9](../../../bib/kjv/isa037.htm#009.md)
