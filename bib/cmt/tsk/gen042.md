he spake unto them by an interpreter: Heb. an interpreter was between
them, The mailitz does not seem to have been an interpreter in our sense

(particularly [Gen 42:24](../../../bib/kjv/gen042.htm#024)), that Joseph
and his brethren understood each others' language, as his brethren and

who is called in Abyssinia, according to Mr. Bruce, Kal Hatze, "the
voice or word of the king," who always stands at the side of a lattice
window of a balcony, within which the king sits; who is never seen, but
who speaks through a hole in the side of it, covered in the inside with
a curtain, to this officer, by whom he speaks to the persons present.
[Joh 16:13](../../../bib/kjv/joh016.htm#013), [Joh

[Genesis 42:27](../../../bib/kjv/gen042.htm#027)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">gen 42:27</span></span>

the inn: [Gen 43:21](../../../bib/kjv/gen043.htm#021), [Gen
44:11](../../../bib/kjv/gen044.htm#011); [Exo
4:24](../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#024); [Luk
2:7](../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#007), [Luk
10:34](../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#034)

inn: Malon, from loon, to stay, abide, lodge, denotes any place to stay
and lodge in, particularly a place where travellers usually stop to
lodge, which is generally near a well, where they fill their girbehs, or
leathern bottles, with fresh water, and having unladen and clogged their
camels, asses, etc., permit them to crop any little verdure there may be
in the place, keeping watch over them by turns. Our word inn here gives
us a false idea, there were no such places of entertainment in the
desert which Joseph's brethren had to pass; nor are there any at the
present day. The only accommodation such a place affords is either a
well, or a khan, or caravanserai, which is generally no more than four
bare walls, perfectly exposed, the place being open at the top, and
furnishing a wretched lodging, and even these, it is probable, were not
in use at this early period.
