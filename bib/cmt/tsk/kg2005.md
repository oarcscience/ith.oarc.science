Overview

[Kg2 5:1](../../../bib/kjv/kg2005.htm#001), Naaman, by the report of a
captive maid, is sent to Samaria to be cured of his leprosy; [Kg2
5:8](../../../bib/kjv/kg2005.htm#008), Elisha, sending him to Jordan
cures him; [Kg2 5:15](../../../bib/kjv/kg2005.htm#015), He refusing
Naaman's gifts grants him some of the earth; [Kg2
5:20](../../../bib/kjv/kg2005.htm#020), Gehazi, abusing his master's
name unto Naaman, is smitten with leprosy.

Abana and Pharpar: or, Amana, This river is evidently the Barrada, or
Barda, as the Arabic renders, the Chrysorrhoas of the Greeks, which
taking its rise in Antilibanus, runs eastward towards Damascus, where it
is divided into three streams, one of which passes through the city, and
the other two through the gardens; which reuniting at the east of the
city, forms a lake about five or six leagues to the south-east, called
Behairat el Marj, or, Lake of the Meadow. Pharpar was probably one of
the branches.

and he leaneth: This verse should probably, as many learned men have

house of Rimmon, the Lord pardon thy servant in this thing." Rimmon is
supposed by Selden to the same with Elion, a god of the Phoenicians,
borrowed undoubtedly from the Elyon of the Hebrews, one of the names of
