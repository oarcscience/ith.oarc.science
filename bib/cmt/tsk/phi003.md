Overview

[Phi 3:1](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#001.md), He warns them to beware of
the false teachers of the circumcision; [Phi
3:4](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#004.md), shewing that himself has greater
cause than they to trust in the righteousness of the law; [Phi
3:7](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#007.md), which notwithstanding he counts
as dung and loss, to gain Christ and his righteousness; [Phi
3:12](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#012.md), therein acknowledging his own
imperfection; [Phi 3:15](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#015.md), He exhorts
them to be thus minded; [Phi 3:17](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#017.md), and
to imitate him, [Phi 3:18](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#018.md). and to
decline the ways of carnal Christians.
