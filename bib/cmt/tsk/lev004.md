and shall pour out: The reason for pouring out the blood, which is so
constantly and strictly required by the law, was in opposition to an
idolatrous custom of the ancient Zabii, who "were accustomed to eat of
the blood of their sacrifices, because they imagined this to be the food
of their gods, with whom they thought they had such communion, by eating
their meat, that they revealed to them future things." - Maimonides

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">lev 4:22</span></span>

a ruler hath sinned: Under the term nasi, that is, one preferred or
elevated above others, from nasa, to exalt, it is probable that any
person is meant who held any kind of political dignity among the people;
and it evidently means the head of a tribe as in [Num
1:4](../../../bib/kjv/num001.htm#004), [Num
1:16](../../../bib/kjv/num001.htm#016); [Num
7:2](../../../bib/kjv/num007.htm#002). But the Rabbins generally
understand it of the prince of the great sanhedrin, who, when they were
under the regal form of government, was the king. A kid of the goats was
the sacrifice in this case; and the rites were nearly the same as in the
preceding, only the fat was burnt as that of the peace offering ([Lev
4:26](../../../bib/kjv/lev004.htm#026)). [Exo
18:21](../../../bib/kjv/exo018.htm#021); [Num
16:2](../../../bib/kjv/num016.htm#002); [Sa2
21:1-3](../../../bib/kjv/sa2021.htm#001), [Sa2
