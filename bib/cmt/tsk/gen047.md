### [Genesis Chapter 47](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#001.md)

[Genesis](../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">gen 47:0</span></span>

Overview

[Gen 47:1](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#001.md), Joseph presents his father,
and five of his brethren before Pharaoh; [Gen
47:11](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#011.md), He gives them habitation and
maintenance; [Gen 47:13](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#013.md), He gets the
Egyptians' money; [Gen 47:16](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#016.md), their
cattle; [Gen 47:18](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#018.md), and their lands,
except the priests', to Pharaoh; [Gen
47:23](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#023.md), He restores the land for a
fifth; [Gen 47:28](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#028.md), Jacob's age; [Gen
47:29](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#029.md), He swears Joseph to bury him
with his fathers.

Give your cattle: This was the wisest measure that could be adopted,
both for the preservation of the people and the cattle. As the people
had not grain for their own sustenance, consequently they could have
none for their cattle. hence they were in the most imminent danger of
starving; and the people also were in equal danger; as they must have
divided a portion of that bought for themselves with the cattle, which,
for the sake of tillage, etc., they wished of course to preserve till
the seven years' famine should end. [Dan
6:5-7](../../../bib/kjv/dan006.htm#005.md); [Pro

made it a law: From this history, and from Diodorus Siculus (lib. i.),
we learn that the land of Egypt was divided into three parts: one
belonged to the Priests ([Gen 47:22](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#022.md)
and [Gen 47:26](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#026).md); a second was the
king's (which appears to have been the land of Rameses, or Goshen, [Gen
47:11](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#011).md); the remainder was the
subjects'. Now Joseph, having purchased the land of the people ([Gen
47:19](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#019.md) and [Gen
47:20](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#020).md), restored it, on the condition
of their paying a fifth part of the produce to the king, beyond which he
appears to have made no demand.

And Israel bowed: As shachah, which primarily signifies to bow, denotes
also to worship; and as mittah, a bed, pronounced matteh, is a staff;

worshipped upon the top of his staff," which is literally copied by the
apostle Paul, in his Epistle to the Hebrews. The present reading is,
however, supported by the Masoretic pointing and the Targums; and is
most probably correct, as it would appear that Jacob was confined to his
bed. [Gen 47:29](../../../bib/kjv/gen047.htm#029.md), [Gen
