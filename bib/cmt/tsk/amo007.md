### [Amos Chapter 7](../../../bib/kjv/amo007.htm#001)

[Amos](../../../bib/kjv/amo001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">amo 7:0</span></span>

Overview

[Amo 7:1](../../../bib/kjv/amo007.htm#001), The judgments of the
grasshoppers, [Amo 7:4](../../../bib/kjv/amo007.htm#004), and of the
fire are diverted by the prayer of Amos; [Amo
7:7](../../../bib/kjv/amo007.htm#007), By the wall of a plumbline is
signified the rejection of Israel; [Amo
7:10](../../../bib/kjv/amo007.htm#010), Amaziah complains of Amos; [Amo
7:14](../../../bib/kjv/amo007.htm#014), Amos shews his calling; [Amo
7:16](../../../bib/kjv/amo007.htm#016), and Amaziah's judgment.

grasshoppers: or, green worms, Govai in Arabic gabee "locusts," probably
in their caterpillar state, in which they are most destructive. This is
supposed to have been an emblem of the first invasion of the Assyrians.

mowings: Or rather, feedings or grazings, as the people of the East make
no hay. This was probably in the month of March, which is the only time
of the year that the Arabs to this day feed their horses with grass.
