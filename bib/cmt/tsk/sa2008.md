### [2 Kings (2 Samuel) Chapter 8](../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#001)

[2 Kings (2 Samuel)](../../../bib/kjv/sa2001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">sa2 8:0</span></span>

Overview

[Sa2 8:1](../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#001), David subdues the
Philistines and the Moabites; [Sa2
8:3](../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#003), He smites Hadadezer, and the
Syrians; [Sa2 8:9](../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#009), Toi sends Joram
with presents to bless him; [Sa2 8:11](../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#011),
The presents and the spoil David dedicates to God; [Sa2
8:14](../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#014), He puts garrisons in Edom; [Sa2
8:16](../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#016), David's officers.

Methegammah: or, the bridle of Ammah, [Sa2
2:24](../../../bib/kjv/sa2002.htm#024); 1Chr. 18:1-17, Gath, In the
parallel passage of Chronicles, we read, "David took Gath and her
towns;" and it is probable, that Gath and its districts were called
Metheg-ammah in David's time; which, being unusual or becoming obsolete,
in the time of the author of the Chronicles, led him thus to explain it.

from him: or, of his

chariots: As [Ch1 18:4](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#004) seven hundred.
In the parallel place in Chronicles it is "seven thousand horsemen, a
far more probable number. The letter ז, zayin, with a dot upon it stands
for seven thousand, and the final letter ן, noon, for seven hundred. The
great similarity of these letters might easily cause the one to be
mistaken for the other, and so produce an error in this place. David
houghed, [Deu 17:16](../../../bib/kjv/deu017.htm#016); [Jos
11:6](../../../bib/kjv/jos011.htm#006), [Jos

Zobah: From [Ch2 8:3](../../../bib/kjv/ch2008.htm#003), we learn that
Zobah was the district in which Tadmor or Palymyra was situated; and
consequently lay between the land of Israel and the Euphrates. The
capital was probably the same as the Sabe mentioned by Ptolemy as a city
of Arabia Deserta.
