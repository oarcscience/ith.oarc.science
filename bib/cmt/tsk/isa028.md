Ephraim for their pride and drunkenness; [Isa

am 3279, bc 725

the crown: This chapter begins a new subject, chiefly relating to the
devastations of Israel and Judah by the Assyrian kings. The ancient
Samaria being beautifully situated on the top of a round hill, and
surrounded immediately with a rich valley, and then a circle of other
hills around it, suggested the idea of a chaplet, or wreath of flowers.
