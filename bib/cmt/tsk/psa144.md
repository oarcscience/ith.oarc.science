### [Psalms Chapter 144](../../../bib/kjv/psa144.htm#001.md)

[Psalms](../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">psa 144:0</span></span>

Overview

[Psa 144:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa144.htm#001.md), David blesses God for his
mercy both to him and to man; [Psa
144:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa144.htm#005.md), He prays that God would
powerfully deliver him from his enemies; [Psa
144:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa144.htm#009.md), He promises to praise God; [Psa
144:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa144.htm#011.md), He prays for the happy state
of the kingdom.

Calmet and others think that this Psalm was composed by David after the
death of Absalom; and from a collation of it with Psa. 18, in which the
same ideas and form of expression occur, there can be no doubt of both
having proceeded from the same pen, and that David was the author.
