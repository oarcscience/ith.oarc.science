[Psalms](../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#001)

Overview

[Psa 75:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#001), The prophet praises God;
[Psa 75:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#002), He promises to judge
uprightly; [Psa 75:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#004), He rebukes the
proud by consideration of God's providence; [Psa
75:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#009), He praises God, and promises to
execute justice.

not. [Psa 57:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#001), [Psa
58:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa058.htm#001) \*titles

[Psalms 75:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 75:1</span></span>

A Psalm: Some consider this Psalm to have been written by David on his
accession to the throne over all Israel; others refer it to the time of
the captivity, considering it as a continuation of the subject in the
preceding; but Bp. Patrick and others are of opinion that it was
composed by Asaph to commemorate the overthrow of Sennacherib's army,
[Kg2 19:1](../../../bib/kjv/kg2019.htm#001).
