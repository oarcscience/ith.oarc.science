### [Isaiah Chapter 50](../../../bib/kjv/isa050.htm#001)

[Isaiah](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">isa 50:0</span></span>

Overview

[Isa 50:1](../../../bib/kjv/isa050.htm#001), Christ shews that the
dereliction of the Jews is not to be imputed to him, by his ability to
save; [Isa 50:5](../../../bib/kjv/isa050.htm#005), by his obedience in
that work; [Isa 50:7](../../../bib/kjv/isa050.htm#007), and by his
confidence in that assistance; [Isa
50:10](../../../bib/kjv/isa050.htm#010), An exhortation to trust in God,
and not in ourselves.

Behold: Husbands often sent bills of divorcement to their wives on
slight occasions; and fathers, oppressed with debt, sold their children
till the year of release. But this, saith God, cannot be my case: I am
not governed by any such motives, nor am I urged by any such necessity.
Your captivity and afflictions are the fruits of your own folly and
wickedness.

my cheeks: The eastern people always held the beard in great veneration;
and to pluck a man's beard is one of the grossest indignities that can
be offered. D'Arvieux gives a remarkable instance of an Arab, who,
having received a wound in his jaw, chose to hazard his life rather than
suffer the surgeon to cut off his beard. See note on [Sa2
10:4](../../../bib/kjv/sa2010.htm#004).

that plucked: [Neh 13:25](../../../bib/kjv/neh013.htm#025)

I hid: Another instance of the utmost contempt and detestation.
Throughout the East it is highly offensive to spit in any one's
presence; and if this is such an indignity, how much more spitting in
the face?
