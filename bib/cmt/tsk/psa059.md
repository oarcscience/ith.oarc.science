<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 59:1</span></span>

Michtam: The seven poems of the celebrated Arabian poets who flourished
before the time of Mohammed, called Moallakat, from being suspended on
the walls of the temple of Mecca, were also called Modhabat, "golden,"
because they were written in letters of gold on the papyrus; and
probably this is another reason why the six poems of David were called
golden.
