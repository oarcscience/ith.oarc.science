<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">mar 16:2</span></span>

Mr. West supposes that the women made two different visits to the
sepulchre, and, in consequence of that, two distinct reports to the
disciples; that Mary Magdalene, with the other Mary and Salome, set out
not only early, but very early in the morning, λιαν \[Strong's G3029\],
πρωι \[Strong's G4404\], i.e., before the time appointed to meet Joanna
and the other women there. ([Luk
24:10](../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#010)). This interpretation, which is
adopted by several eminent writers, is very probable, and reconciles the
apparent discrepancy in the evangelists.
