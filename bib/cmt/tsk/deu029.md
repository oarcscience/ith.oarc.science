enter: Heb. pass, This is an allusion to the solemn ceremony used by
several ancient nations, when they entered into a covenant with each
other. The victims, slain as a sacrifice on this occasion, were divided,
and and parts laid asunder. the contracting parties then passed between
them, imprecating, as a curse on those who violated the sacred compact,
that they might in like manner be cut asunder ([Gen
15:10](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#010).md). St. Cyril, in his work against
Julian, shows that passing between the divided parts of a victim was
used also among the Chaldeans and other people.
