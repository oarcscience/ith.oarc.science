[Ezekiel 46:1](../../../bib/kjv/eze046.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">eze 46:1</span></span>

Thus saith: Whether the rules for public worship here laid down were
designed to be observed in those things wherein they differed from the
law of Moses, in the ministrations of the second temple, is not certain.
In the latter history of the Jewish church, the law of Moses only was
followed, except in the corruption of following the traditions of the
fathers.

The gate: The prophet had before observed that the east gate of the
outer court was shut, and was told that it must only be opened for the
prince; and now he is informed that the gate of the inner court on the
east was also shut, and is to be opened only on the sabbath and new
moons, till the evening.

is, by touching them incapacitate them from discharging their ordinary
occupations.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">eze 46:22</span></span>

court: These court in the corners of the outer court, or court of the
people, appear to have been a kind of uncovered apartments, surrounded
with little chambers for the cooks, and used for dressing the peace
offerings of the people. On these their families and friends feasted;
and portions were sent to the poor, the widow, and the orphan; and thus
the spirit of devotion preserved the spirit of mercy, charity, and
benevolence, in the land.

joined: or, made with chimneys
