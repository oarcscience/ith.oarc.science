<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">sa2 9:10</span></span>

shall eat bread: The eating at courts was of two kinds; the one public
and ceremonious, the other private. Sir John Chardin understands those
passages which speak of a right to eat at the royal table, as pointing
out a right to a seat there, when the repast was public and solemn. So
in a manuscript Note on [Kg1 2:7](../../../bib/kjv/kg1002.htm#007.md), he
tells us that it was to be understood of the majilis, (the term for an
assembly of lords, or a public feast), and not of the daily and ordinary
repast. Hence, though Mephibosheth was to eat at all public times at the
king's table, yet he would want the produce of his lands for food at
other times, which it was necessary for Ziba to understand. [Sa2
9:7](../../../bib/kjv/sa2009.htm#007.md), [Sa2
9:11-13](../../../bib/kjv/sa2009.htm#011.md), [Sa2
19:28](../../../bib/kjv/sa2019.htm#028.md); [Kg2
25:29](../../../bib/kjv/kg2025.htm#029.md); [Luk
14:15](../../../bib/kjv/luk014.htm#015.md)
