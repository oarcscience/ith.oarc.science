Overview

[Deu 27:1](../../../bib/kjv/deu027.htm#001.md), The people are commanded to
write the law upon stones, [Deu 27:5](../../../bib/kjv/deu027.htm#005.md),
and to build an altar of whole stones; [Deu
27:11](../../../bib/kjv/deu027.htm#011.md), The tribes to be divided on
Gerizim and Ebal; [Deu 27:14](../../../bib/kjv/deu027.htm#014.md), The
curses to be pronounced on mount Ebal.

to be filled up by the mortar or cement; as is frequently the case with
eastern inscriptions.

Amen: To each of the curses the people were to say Amen, as well as to
the blessings; to denote a profession of their faith in the truth of
them, that they were the real declarations of the wrath of God; and an
acknowledgment of the equity of these curses. It was such an imprecation
upon themselves, as strongly obliged them to have nothing to do with
those evil practices on which the curse is entailed. We read of those
who entered a curse to walk in God's law. [Neh
10:29](../../../bib/kjv/neh010.htm#029.md). All the people, by saying this
Amen, became bound one for another, that they would observe God's laws,
by which every man was obliged, as far as he could, to prevent his
neighbour from breaking these laws, and to reprove those that had
offended, lest they should bear sin and the curse for them.
