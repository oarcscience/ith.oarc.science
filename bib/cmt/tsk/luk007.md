### [Luke Chapter 7](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#001.md)

[Luke](../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">luk 7:0</span></span>

Overview

[Luk 7:1](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#001.md), Christ finds a greater faith
in the centurion, a Gentile than in any of the Jews; [Luk
7:10](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#010.md), heals his servant, being absent;
[Luk 7:11](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#011.md), raises from death the
widow's son at Nain; [Luk 7:18](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#018.md),
answers John's messengers with the declaration of his miracles; [Luk
7:24](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#024.md), testifies to the people what
opinion he held of John; [Luk 7:31](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#031.md),
inveighs against the Jews, who with neither the manners of John nor of
Jesus could be won; [Luk 7:36](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#036.md), and
suffering his feet to be washed and anointed by a woman who was a
sinner, he shows how he is a friend to sinners, to forgive them their
sins, upon their repentance.

the sister of Lazarus. But there is no indication in the gospel history,
that Mary Magdalene was the sister of Lazarus; but on the contrary, it
would appear that they were perfectly distinct persons, the sister of
Lazarus residing at Bethany, while Mary Magdalene appears to have
resided at Magdala, east of Jordan, a distance of nearly ninety miles.
Add to this, that our Saviour seems to have been now in or near Nain,
not at Bethany; and the woman appears from the recital to have been
previously unknown to him.
