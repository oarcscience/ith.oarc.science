### [Psalms Chapter 79](../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#001)

[Psalms](../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">psa 79:0</span></span>

Overview

[Psa 79:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#001), The psalmist complains of
the desolation of Jerusalem; [Psa
79:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#008), He prays for deliverance; [Psa
79:13](../../../bib/kjv/psa079.htm#013), and promises thankfulness.

This Psalm is supposed, with much probability, to have been written on
the destruction of the city and temple of Jerusalem by Nebuchadnezzar.

to bury the body of a malefactor without an order from the emperor; and
Windus, speaking of a man who was to have been sawn in two, informs us,
