Overview

[Psa 132:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#001.md), David in his prayer
commends unto God the religious care he had for the ark; [Psa
132:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#008.md), His prayer at the removing of
the ark; [Psa 132:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#011.md), with a
repetition of God's promises.

Some attribute this Psalm to Solomon; and others refer it to the
building of the second temple; but it seems more probable that it was
sung at the solemn induction of the ark into the tabernacle of Mount
Zion, expressing the holy joy and triumph of that event. [Psa
120:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa120.htm#001.md), [Psa
