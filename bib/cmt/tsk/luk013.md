### [Luke Chapter 13](../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#001)

[Luke](../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">luk 13:0</span></span>

Overview

[Luk 13:1](../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#001), Christ preaches repentance
upon the punishment of the Galilaeans and others; [Luk
13:6](../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#006), The fruitless fig-tree may not
stand; [Luk 13:10](../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#010), He heals the
crooked woman; [Luk 13:18](../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#018), shows the
powerful working of the word in the hearts of his chosen, by the parable
of the grain of mustard seed, and of leaven; [Luk
13:22](../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#022), exhorts to enter in at the
strait gate; [Luk 13:31](../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#031), and reproves
Herod and Jerusalem.

[Luke 13:1](../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">luk 13:1</span></span>

the Galilaeans: The Galilaeans are frequently mentioned by Josephus as
the most turbulent and seditious people, being upon all occasions ready
to disturb the Roman authority. It is uncertain to what event our Lord
refers; but is probable that they were the followers of Judas
Gaulonitis, who opposed paying tribute to Caesar and submitting to the
Roman government. A party of them coming to Jerusalem during one of the
great festivals, and presenting their oblations in the court of the
temple, Pilate treacherously sent a company of soldiers, who slew them,
and "mingled their blood with their sacrifices." [Act
5:37](../../../bib/kjv/act005.htm#037)
