<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">ch2 30:0</span></span>

Overview

[Ch2 30:1](../../../bib/kjv/ch2030.htm#001.md), Hezekiah proclaims a solemn
passover on the second month for Judah and Israel; [Ch2
30:13](../../../bib/kjv/ch2030.htm#013.md), The assembly, having destroyed
the altars of idolatry, keep the feast fourteen days; [Ch2
30:27](../../../bib/kjv/ch2030.htm#027.md), The priests and Levites bless
the people.

in the second month: In Ijar, as they could not celebrate in Nisan, the
14th of which month was the proper time. But Hezekiah and his
counsellors justly concluded, that the regulation of the 14th day of the
second month, which had been made for individuals who were hindered from
eating the passover at the appointed season, might in their present
circumstances be extended to the people at large. [Num
9:10](../../../bib/kjv/num009.htm#010.md), [Num

divers of Asher: It has been said, that Hezekiah had no right to invite
Hoshea's subjects to repair to Jerusalem to his passover; but it may be
presumed, that he was encouraged to do this by Hoshea himself, who was
one of their best kings; besides which, both the golden calves having
been taken away by the Assyrians, the apostate Israelites, being thus
deprived of idols, had begun to return to the Lord, and to go up to
Jerusalem to worship, some time before Hezekiah gave them this
invitation. [Ch2 30:18](../../../bib/kjv/ch2030.htm#018.md), [Ch2
