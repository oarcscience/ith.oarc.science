### [Leviticus Chapter 10](../../../bib/kjv/lev010.htm#001)

[Leviticus](../../../bib/kjv/lev001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">lev 10:0</span></span>

Overview

[Lev 10:1](../../../bib/kjv/lev010.htm#001), Nadab and Abihu, for
offering strange fire, are burnt by fire; [Lev
10:6](../../../bib/kjv/lev010.htm#006), Aaron and his sons are forbidden
to mourn for them; [Lev 10:8](../../../bib/kjv/lev010.htm#008), The
priests are forbidden wine when they are to go into the tabernacle; [Lev
10:12](../../../bib/kjv/lev010.htm#012), The law of eating the holy
things; [Lev 10:16](../../../bib/kjv/lev010.htm#016), Aaron's excuse for
transgressing thereof.

They died.

The wages of sin is death.

They died suddenly.

They died before the Lord; that is, before the vail that covered the
mercy-seat.

They died by fire, as by fire they sinned.

The fire did not burn them to ashes, as it had done the sacrifices, nor
so much as singe their coats ([Lev
10:5](../../../bib/kjv/lev010.htm#005)) but struck them dead in an
instant. By these different effects of the same fire, we learn that it
was no common fire, but kindled by the breath of the Almighty. [Isa
30:33](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#033)

strong drink: The Hebrew shecher, Arabic sakar, or sukr, Greek σικερα,
from shachar, to inebriate, signifies any kind of fermented and
inebriating liquor beside wine. So St. Jerome informs us, that sicera in
Hebrew denotes any inebriating liquor, whether made of corn, the juice
of apples, honey, dates, or any other fruit. These different kinds of
liquors are described by Pliny, who calls then vina factitia. One of the
four prohibited drinks among the Mohammedans in India is called sakar,
which denotes inebriating liquor in general, but especially date wine.
