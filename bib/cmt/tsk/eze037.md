Overview

[Eze 37:1](../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#001), By the resurrection of dry
bones, [Eze 37:11](../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#011). the dead hope of
Israel is revived; [Eze 37:15](../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#015), By the
uniting of two sticks, [Eze 37:18](../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#018). is
shewn the incorporation of Israel into Judah; [Eze
37:21](../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#021), The promises of Christ's
kingdom.

[Ezekiel 37:1](../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">eze 37:1</span></span>

hand: In this vision, the dry bones aptly represent the ruined and
desperate state of both Israel and Judah; and the revivification of
these bones signifies their restoration to their own land after their
captivity, and also their recovery from their present long dispersion.
Although this is the primary and genuine scope of the vision, yet the
doctrine of a general resurrection of the dead may justly be inferred
from it; for "a simile of the resurrection," says Jerome, after
Tertullian and others, "would never have been used to signify the
restoration of the people of Israel, unless such a future resurrection
had been believed and known; because no one attempts to confirm
uncertain things by things which have no existence." [Eze

I will open: This is a pointed allusion to the resurrection; under which
figure Isaiah ([Isa 26:9](../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#009)) also
describes the restoration of the house of Israel, when he says, "thy
dead men shall live;" at which time their bones are said to flourish
([Isa 66:14](../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#014)), or to be restored to
their former strength and vigour; and, in like manner, St. Paul ([Rom
11:15](../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#015)), expresses their conversion by
"life from the dead." In the land of their captivity, they seemed as
absolutely deprived of their country as persons committed to the grave
are cut off from the land of the living; but when Cyrus issued his
proclamation, Jehovah, as it were, opened their graves, and when he
stirred up their spirits to embrace the proffered liberty, he put his
Spirit within them, that they might live; and their re-establishment in
their own land evinced the truth of God in the prediction, and his power
in its accomplishment. [Eze 37:21](../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#021);
[Isa 26:19](../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#019), [Isa
