Media: Media, which comprehended the modern Azerbijan and part of Irak,
was a celebrated country of Asia, bounded on the north by the Caspian
Sea and Armenia, west by Assyria, south by Susiana and Persia, and east
by Hyrcania and Parthia, extending from 30 degrees to 37 degrees n lat.
and 45 degrees to 53 degrees e long.

Persia: Persia Proper, now Fars, was but a small province, being bounded
on the north by Media, west by Susiana, south by the Persian Gulf, and
east by Caramania, extending from 27 degrees to 33 degrees n lat. and 50
degrees to 55 degrees e long. But the Persian empire in its ancient
state extended from the Hellespont to the Indus, above 2,800 miles, and
from Pontus to the shores of Arabia, above 2,000 miles; comprehending a
multitude of various nations.
