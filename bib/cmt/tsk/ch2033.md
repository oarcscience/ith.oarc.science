Overview

[Ch2 33:1](../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#001), Manasseh's wicked reign;
[Ch2 33:3](../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#003), He sets up idolatry, and
will not be admonished; [Ch2 33:11](../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#011), He
is carried into Babylon; [Ch2 33:12](../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#012),
Upon his prayer to God he is released and puts down idolatry; [Ch2
33:18](../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#018), His acts; [Ch2
33:20](../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#020), He dying, Amon succeeds him;
[Ch2 33:21](../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#021), who is slain by his
servants; [Ch2 33:25](../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#025), The murderers
being slain, Josiah succeeds him.

he set a carved image: The Targumist says, "He set up an image, the
likeness of himself, in the house of the sanctuary." In the parallel
passage it is, "a graven image of the grove," or rather, Asherah or
Astarte. Manasseh, as Bp. Patrick observes, seems to have studied to
find out what God had forbidden in his law, that he might practice it. a
most prodigious change from the height of piety in his father's time,
into the sink of impiety in this!

for Amon sacrificed: Amon's conduct is recorded as like his father
Manasseh, in sacrificing to graven images; by which some think it is an
evidence that Manasseh did not truly repent, but they forget how many
good kings had wicked sons. In one point of view Manasseh was defective,
although it cannot be supposed that it affected his eternal state; for
when he cast out the images, he did not utterly deface and destroy them,
according to the law in [Deu 7:5](../../../bib/kjv/deu007.htm#005),
which required, moreover, that the graven images should be burnt with
fire. How necessary that law was, this instance shows; for the carved
images being only thrown aside, and not burnt, Amon knew where to find
them, soon set them up, and sacrificed to them. [Isa
