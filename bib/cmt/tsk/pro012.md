[Proverbs 12:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">pro 12:9</span></span>

He that is: etc. Or, rather, as in the old translation "He that is
despised, and is his own servant, is better than he that boasteth
himself and wanteth bread;" with which the versions generally agree.
That is, it is better to be in lowness and obscurity, and to support
oneself by manual labour, than to want the necessaries of life, through
a foolish vanity, or the pride of birth, which refuses to labour.

despised: [Pro 13:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#007); [Luk
14:11](../../../bib/kjv/luk014.htm#011)
