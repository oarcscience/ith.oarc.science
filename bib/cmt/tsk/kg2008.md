Overview

[Kg2 8:1](../../../bib/kjv/kg2008.htm#001), The Shunammite, having left
her country seven years, to avoid the forewarned famine, for Elisha's
miracle's sake has her land restored by the king; [Kg2
8:7](../../../bib/kjv/kg2008.htm#007), Hazael, being sent with a present
by Ben-hadad to Elisha at Damascus, after he had heard the prophecy,
kills his master, and succeeds him; [Kg2
8:16](../../../bib/kjv/kg2008.htm#016), Jehoram's wicked reign in Judah;
[Kg2 8:20](../../../bib/kjv/kg2008.htm#020), Edom and Libnah revolt;
[Kg2 8:23](../../../bib/kjv/kg2008.htm#023), Ahaziah succeeds Jehoram;
[Kg2 8:25](../../../bib/kjv/kg2008.htm#025), Ahaziah's wicked reign;
[Kg2 8:28](../../../bib/kjv/kg2008.htm#028), He visits Joram, being
wounded, at Jezreel.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">kg2 8:4</span></span>

the king: As it appears not likely that the king would hold conversation
with a leprous man; or, that, knowing Gehazi had been dismissed with the
highest disgrace from the prophet's service, he would talk with him
concerning his late master; some have supposed that this happened before
the cleansing of Naaman. But it agrees better with the chronology to
consider it as having taken place after that event; the king, probably,
having an insatiable curiosity to know the private history of a man who
had done such astonishing things. As to the circumstances of Gehazi's
disease, he might overlook that, and converse with him, keeping at a
reasonable distance, as nothing but actual contact could defile.

understand by it Ben-hadad; who, encouraged by the favourable answer of

heat of his fever, and put over his face the keever, or fly-net. (See
note on [Sa1 19:13](../../../bib/kjv/sa1019.htm#013)), dipped in water,
