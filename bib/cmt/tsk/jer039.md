Overview

[Jer 39:1](../../../bib/kjv/jer039.htm#001), Jerusalem is taken; [Jer
39:4](../../../bib/kjv/jer039.htm#004), Zedekiah is made blind and sent
to Babylon; [Jer 39:8](../../../bib/kjv/jer039.htm#008), The city laid
in ruins, [Jer 39:9](../../../bib/kjv/jer039.htm#009), and the people
captivated; [Jer 39:11](../../../bib/kjv/jer039.htm#011),
Nebuchadrezzar's charge for the good usage of Jeremiah; [Jer
39:15](../../../bib/kjv/jer039.htm#015), God's promise to Ebed-melech.

Sarsechim: These were the principal commanders; but Dr. Blayney thinks
that, instead of six persons, we have in reality but three, as the name
that follows each is the title of the office. Thus Nergal-Sharezer, who
was Samgar-nebo, or keeper, i.e., priest of Nebo; Sarsechim, who was
Rab-saris, or chief eunuch; and Nergal-sharezer, who was Rab-mag, or
chief magi; as the words mog in Persian, magoos in Arabic, magooshai in
Syriac, and μαγος \[Strong's G3097\], in Greek, signify; and we learn
from Justin and Curtius that the magi attended the king in war.
