For I was: The office of cup-bearer was one of great trust, honour, and
emolument, in the Persian court. To be in such a place of trust he must
have been in the king's confidence; for no eastern potentate would have
a cup-bearer to whom he could not trust his life, poison being often
administered in that way. It was an office much desired, because it gave
access to the king in those seasons of hilarity when men are most
disposed to grant favours. [Neh 2:1](../../../bib/kjv/neh002.htm#001);
