### [Psalms Chapter 108](../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#001.md)

[Psalms](../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">psa 108:0</span></span>

INTRODUCTION TO PSALM 108

A Song or Psalm of David. This psalm consists of several passages out of
the fifty seventh and sixtieth psalms, with very little variation.
Jarchi and Kimchi refer it to the times of the Messiah. The title in the
Syriac version is,

"concerning the calling of the Gentiles,''

to which, no doubt, it has respect.

[Psalms 108:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 108:1</span></span>

O God, my heart is fixed; I will sing and give praise,.... From hence to
[Psa 108:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#006.md) the words are taken out of
[Psa 57:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#007.md), which see.

Even with my glory; my tongue; in [Psa
57:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#008.md), it is read, "awake up my glory".
See Gill on [Psa 57:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#007.md),

[Psalms 108:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 108:4</span></span>

For thy mercy is great above the heavens,..... It is in [Psa
57:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#010.md), "thy mercy is great unto the
heavens". See Gill on [Psa 57:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa057.htm#010.md).

[Psalms 108:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">psa 108:6</span></span>

That thy beloved may be delivered,.... From hence to the end of the
psalm the words are taken out of [Psa
60:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#005.md). See Gill on [Psa
60:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#005.md).

[Psalms 108:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">psa 108:9</span></span>

Over Philistia will I triumph,.... In [Psa
60:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#008.md), it is, "Philistia, triumph thou
because of me"; See Gill on [Psa 60:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#008.md).

[Psalms 108:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa108.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">psa 108:11</span></span>

And wilt not thou, O God, go forth with our hosts?.... In [Psa
60:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#010.md), it is, "and thou, O God, which
didst not go out with our armies". See Gill on [Psa
60:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#010.md).
