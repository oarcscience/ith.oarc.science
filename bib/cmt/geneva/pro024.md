### [Proverbs Chapter 24](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#001.md)

[Proverbs 24:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">pro 24:7</span></span>

Wisdom \[is\] too high for a fool: he openeth not his mouth in the (a)
gate.

\(a\) In the place where wisdom should be shown.

[Proverbs 24:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">pro 24:10</span></span>

\[If\] thou (b) faintest in the day of adversity, thy strength \[is\]
small.

\(b\) Man has no trial of his strength till he is in trouble.

[Proverbs 24:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 24:11</span></span>

If thou refraineth to deliver \[them that are\] drawn to (c) death, and
\[those that are\] ready to be slain;

\(c\) No one can be excused, if he does not help the innocent when he is
in danger.

[Proverbs 24:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">pro 24:13</span></span>

My son, eat thou (d) honey, because \[it is\] good; and the honeycomb,
\[which is\] sweet to thy taste:

\(d\) As honey is sweet and pleasant to the taste, so wisdom is to the
soul.

[Proverbs 24:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 24:16</span></span>

For a just \[man\] (e) falleth seven times, and riseth again: but the
wicked shall fall into mischief.

\(e\) He is subject to many perils, but God delivers him.

[Proverbs 24:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#018.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">pro 24:18</span></span>

Lest the LORD see \[it\], and it displease him, and he turn away his
wrath (f) from him.

\(f\) To be avenged on you.

[Proverbs 24:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#022.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">pro 24:22</span></span>

For their calamity shall rise suddenly; and who knoweth the ruin of them
(g) both?

\(g\) Meaning, either of the wicked and seditious, as in ([Pro
24:19](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#019.md), [Pro
24:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#021).md) or of them who do not fear God
or obey their king.

[Proverbs 24:27](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#027.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">pro 24:27</span></span>

Prepare thy work outside, and make it fit for thyself in the field; (h)
and afterwards build thy house.

\(h\) Be sure of the means how to compass it, before you take any
enterprise in hand.

[Proverbs 24:29](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#029.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_029">pro 24:29</span></span>

Say not, I will do so to him as he hath done to me: I (i) will render to
the man according to his work.

\(i\) He shows what is the nature of the wicked, to revenge wrong for
wrong.

[Proverbs 24:32](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#032.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_032">pro 24:32</span></span>

Then I saw, \[and\] considered \[it\] well: I looked upon \[it, and\]
received (k) instruction.

\(k\) That I might learn by another man's fault.

[Proverbs 24:33](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#033.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_033">pro 24:33</span></span>

\[Yet\] a little sleep, (l) a little slumber, a little folding of the
hands to sleep:

\(l\) See [Pro 6:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#010.md)
