*The Geneva Bible Translation Notes*, \[1599\], at Intangible Textual

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### [Proverbs Chapter 2](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#001.md)

[Proverbs 2:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">pro 2:1</span></span>

My son, if thou wilt receive my words, and (a) hide my commandments with
thee;

\(a\) That is, keep them in your heart.

[Proverbs 2:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pro 2:2</span></span>

So that thou incline thine ear unto wisdom, \[and\] apply (b) thine
heart to understanding;

\(b\) If you give yourself to the true knowledge of God without
hypocrisy.

[Proverbs 2:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">pro 2:3</span></span>

Yea, if thou criest after knowledge, \[and\] (c) liftest up thy voice
for understanding;

\(c\) Meaning that we must seek the knowledge of God with care and
diligence.

[Proverbs 2:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">pro 2:4</span></span>

If thou seekest her as silver, and searchest for her as \[for\] hid (d)
treasures;

\(d\) Showing that no labour must be spared.

[Proverbs 2:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">pro 2:5</span></span>

Then shalt thou understand the fear of the LORD, and find the (e)
knowledge of God.

\(e\) This (he says) is the true wisdom to know and fear God.

[Proverbs 2:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 2:11</span></span>

\(f\) Discretion shall preserve thee, understanding shall keep thee:

\(f\) The word of God will guide you, and teach you how to govern
yourself.

[Proverbs 2:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">pro 2:13</span></span>

Who leave the (g) paths of uprightness, to walk in the ways of darkness;

\(g\) That is, the word of God, which is the only light, to follow their
own fantasies which are darkness.

[Proverbs 2:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">pro 2:14</span></span>

Who rejoice to do evil, \[and\] delight (h) in the frowardness of the
wicked;

\(h\) When they see any given to evil as they are.

[Proverbs 2:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 2:16</span></span>

To deliver thee from the strange (i) woman, \[even\] from the stranger
\[which\] flattereth with her words;

\(i\) Meaning, the wisdom which is the word of God, will preserve us
from all vices: naming this vice of whoredom to which man is most prone.

[Proverbs 2:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">pro 2:17</span></span>

Which forsaketh the (k) guide of her youth, and forgetteth the covenant
of her God.

\(k\) That is, her husband, who is her head and guide to govern her,
from whom she ought not to depart, but remain in his subjection.

[Proverbs 2:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#018.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">pro 2:18</span></span>

For her (m) house inclineth unto death, and her paths unto (n) the dead.

\(m\) Her acquaintance with her spirits and they that haunt her.

\(n\) To them who are dead in body and soul.

[Proverbs 2:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#021.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">pro 2:21</span></span>

For the upright shall dwell in the (o) land, and the perfect shall
remain in it.

\(o\) They will enjoy the temporal and spiritual promises of God, as the
wicked will be void of them.
