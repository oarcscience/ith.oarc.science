### [Proverbs Chapter 4](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#001)

[Proverbs 4:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">pro 4:1</span></span>

Hear, ye children, the instruction of a (a) father, and attend to know
understanding.

\(a\) He speaks this as a preacher and minister which is as a father to
the people, ([Pro 2:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#008)).

[Proverbs 4:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">pro 4:4</span></span>

He (b) taught me also, and said unto me, Let thine heart retain my
words: keep my commandments, and live.

\(b\) Meaning, David his father.

[Proverbs 4:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">pro 4:7</span></span>

\(c\) Wisdom \[is\] the principal thing; \[therefore\] get wisdom: and
with all thy getting get understanding.

\(c\) He shows that we must first begin with God's word, if we will that
other things prosper with us, contrary to the judgment of the world,
which make it their last study, or else care not for it at all.

[Proverbs 4:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 4:11</span></span>

I have (d) taught thee in the way of wisdom; I have led thee in right
paths.

\(d\) Solomon declares what care his father had to bring him up in the
true fear of God: for this was David's protest.

[Proverbs 4:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">pro 4:12</span></span>

When thou goest, thy steps shall not be (e) straitened; and when thou
runnest, thou shalt not stumble.

\(e\) You will walk at liberty without offence.

[Proverbs 4:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 4:16</span></span>

For they (f) sleep not, except they have done mischief; and their sleep
is taken away, unless they cause \[some\] to fall.

\(f\) Meaning that to do evil is more proper and natural to the wicked
than to sleep, eat or drink.

[Proverbs 4:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">pro 4:17</span></span>

For they eat the bread of (g) wickedness, and drink the wine of
violence.

\(g\) Gotten my wicked means and cruel oppression.

[Proverbs 4:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#018)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">pro 4:18</span></span>

But the path of the just \[is\] as the shining light, that (h) shineth
more and more unto the perfect day.

\(h\) Signifying that the godly increase daily in knowledge and
perfection, till they come to full persecution, which is when the are
joined to their head in the heavens.

[Proverbs 4:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">pro 4:22</span></span>

For they \[are\] life unto those that find them, and health to all their
(i) flesh.

\(i\) That is, they will have health of body: under which all other
blessings promised in the law are contained.

[Proverbs 4:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#023)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">pro 4:23</span></span>

Keep thy heart with all diligence; for out of it \[are\] the issues of
(k) life.

\(k\) For as the heart is either pure or corrupt, so is the whole course
of man's life.

[Proverbs 4:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#026)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">pro 4:26</span></span>

\(l\) Ponder the path of thy feet, and let all thy ways be established.

\(l\) Keep a measure in all your doings.
