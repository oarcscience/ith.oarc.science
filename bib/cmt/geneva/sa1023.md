### [1 Kings (1 Samuel) Chapter 23](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#001.md)

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 23:1](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">sa1 23:1</span></span>

Then they told David, saying, Behold, the Philistines fight against (a)
Keilah, and they rob the threshingfloors.

\(a\) Which was a city in the tribe of Judah, ([Jos
15:44](../../../bib/kjv/jos015.htm#044).md).

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 23:3](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">sa1 23:3</span></span>

And David's men said unto him, Behold, we be afraid here in (b) Judah:
how much more then if we come to Keilah against the armies of the
Philistines?

\(b\) That is, in the midst of Judah, much more when we come to the
borders against our enemies.

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 23:6](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">sa1 23:6</span></span>

And it came to pass, when Abiathar the son of Ahimelech fled to David to
Keilah, \[that\] he came down \[with\] an (c) ephod in his hand.

\(c\) By God's providence the ephod was preserved and kept with David
the true king.

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 23:9](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">sa1 23:9</span></span>

And David knew that Saul secretly practised mischief against him; and he
said to Abiathar the priest, (d) Bring hither the ephod.

\(d\) To consult with the Lord by Urim and Thummim.

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 23:14](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">sa1 23:14</span></span>

And David abode in the wilderness in strong holds, and remained in a
mountain in the wilderness of Ziph. And Saul sought him every day, but
God (e) delivered him not into his hand.

\(e\) No power nor policy can prevail against God's children, but when
he appoints the time.

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 23:17](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">sa1 23:17</span></span>

And he said unto him, Fear not: for the hand of Saul my father shall not
find thee; and thou shalt be (f) king over Israel, and I shall be next
unto thee; and that also Saul my father knoweth.

\(f\) Jonathan assured David, that God would accomplish his promise, and
that his father warred against his own conscience.

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 23:21](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#021.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">sa1 23:21</span></span>

And Saul said, (g) Blessed \[be\] ye of the LORD; for ye have compassion
on me.

\(g\) the Lord recompense this friendship.

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 23:23](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#023.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">sa1 23:23</span></span>

See therefore, and take knowledge of all the lurking places where he
hideth himself, and come ye again to me with the certainty, and I will
go with you: and it shall come to pass, if he be in the (h) land, that I
will search him out throughout all the thousands of Judah.

\(h\) In your country of Ziph, which is in Judah.

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 23:25](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#025.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">sa1 23:25</span></span>

Saul also and his men went to seek \[him\]. And they told David:
wherefore he came down into a rock, and abode in the wilderness of (i)
Maon. And when Saul heard \[that\], he pursued after David in the
wilderness of Maon.

\(i\) Which was also in the tribe of Judah, ([Jos
15:55](../../../bib/kjv/jos015.htm#055).md).

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 23:27](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#027.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">sa1 23:27</span></span>

But there came a (k) messenger unto Saul, saying, Haste thee, and come;
for the Philistines have invaded the land.

\(k\) Thus the Lord can pull back the bridle of the tyrants and deliver
his out of the lion's mouth.

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 23:28](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#028.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">sa1 23:28</span></span>

Wherefore Saul returned from pursuing after David, and went against the
Philistines: therefore they called that place (l) Selahammahlekoth.

\(l\) That is, stone of division, because there they divided themselves
one from another.

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 23:29](../../../bib/kjv/sa1023.htm#029.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_029">sa1 23:29</span></span>

And David went up from thence, and dwelt in (a) strong holds at Engedi.

\(a\) That is, in strong places, which were defended by nature.
