*The Geneva Bible Translation Notes*, \[1599\], at Intangible Textual

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### [Psalms Chapter 25](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#001.md)

[Psalms 25:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 25:1</span></span>

"\[A Psalm\] of David." Unto thee, (a) O LORD, do I lift up my soul.

\(a\) I did not put my trust in any worldly thing.

[Psalms 25:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">psa 25:2</span></span>

O my God, I (b) trust in thee: let me not be ashamed, let not mine
enemies triumph over me.

\(b\) That you will take away my enemies, which are your rods.

[Psalms 25:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 25:4</span></span>

\(c\) Shew me thy ways, O LORD; teach me thy paths.

\(c\) Retain me in the faith of your promise that I swore not on any
side.

[Psalms 25:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">psa 25:5</span></span>

Lead me in thy truth, and teach me: for thou \[art\] the God of my
salvation; on thee do I wait (d) all the day.

\(d\) Constantly and against all temptations.

[Psalms 25:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">psa 25:7</span></span>

Remember not the (e) sins of my youth, nor my transgressions: according
to thy mercy remember thou me for thy goodness' sake, O LORD.

\(e\) He confesses that his many sins were the reason that his enemies
persecuted him, desiring that the cause of the evil may be taken away,
so that the effect may cease.

[Psalms 25:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#008.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">psa 25:8</span></span>

Good and upright \[is\] the LORD: therefore will he (f) teach sinners in
the way.

\(f\) That is, call them to repentance.

[Psalms 25:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">psa 25:9</span></span>

The meek will he (g) guide in judgment: and the meek will he teach his
way.

\(g\) He will govern and comfort them that are truly humbled for their
sins.

[Psalms 25:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">psa 25:11</span></span>

For thy (h) name's sake, O LORD, pardon mine iniquity; for it \[is\]
great.

\(h\) For no other respect.

[Psalms 25:12](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#012.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">psa 25:12</span></span>

What (i) man \[is\] he that feareth the LORD? him shall he teach in the
way \[that\] he shall (k) choose.

\(i\) Meaning, the number is very small.

\(k\) He will direct such with his spirit to follow the right way.

[Psalms 25:13](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">psa 25:13</span></span>

His soul shall dwell at (l) ease; and his seed shall inherit the earth.

\(l\) He will prosper both in spiritual and corporal things.

[Psalms 25:14](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">psa 25:14</span></span>

The (m) secret of the LORD \[is\] with them that fear him; and he will
shew them his covenant.

\(m\) His counsel contained in his word, by which he declares that he is
the protector of the faithful.

[Psalms 25:17](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">psa 25:17</span></span>

The troubles of my heart (n) are enlarged: \[O\] bring thou me out of my
distresses.

\(n\) My grief is increased because of my enemies cruelty.

[Psalms 25:19](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#019.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">psa 25:19</span></span>

Consider mine (o) enemies; for they are many; and they hate me with
cruel hatred.

\(o\) The greater that his afflictions were and the more that his
enemies increased, the more near he felt God's help.

[Psalms 25:21](../../../bib/kjv/psa025.htm#021.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">psa 25:21</span></span>

Let (p) integrity and uprightness preserve me; for I wait on thee.

\(p\) As I have behaved myself uprightly toward my enemies, let them
know that you are the defender of my just cause.
