John Nelson Darby (b. 18 Nov 1800, d. 29 Apr 1882) was an Anglo-Irish
evangelist and author. He was a founder and prominent member of the
Evangelical Christian Plymouth Brethren. He also published [The Darby
Bible](../../dar/index).

Darby is considered the father of Dispensationalism, an evangelical
theology which divides history into periods with evolving divine
covenants. Many of Darby's successors also believe that the final
dispensation has yet to occur (and may shortly), which will involve the
rapture into heaven of believers prior to the 'tribulations.' Along with
[The Scofield Bible Commentary](../sco/index), Darby's Bible commentary
had much influence on the evangelical movement during the 19th and 20th
century.
