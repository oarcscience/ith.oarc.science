### [Proverbs Chapter 7](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#001)

[Proverbs 7:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">pro 7:1</span></span>

The subject continued, by a delineation of the arts of strange women, as
a caution to the unwary. (Pro. 7:1-27)

Similar calls ([Pro 3:1-3](../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#001); [Pro
4:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#010), &c.).

[Proverbs 7:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pro 7:2</span></span>

apple . . . eye--pupil of eye, a custody ([Pro
4:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#023)) of special value.

[Proverbs 7:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">pro 7:3</span></span>

Bind . . . fingers--as inscriptions on rings.

[Proverbs 7:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">pro 7:5</span></span>

The design of the teaching (compare [Pro
2:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#016); [Pro
6:24](../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#024)).

[Proverbs 7:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">pro 7:6</span></span>

For--or, "Since," introducing an example to illustrate the warning,
which, whether a narrative or a parable, is equally pertinent.

window--or, "opening"

looked--literally, "watched earnestly" ([Jdg
5:28](../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#028)).

casement--or, "lattice."

[Proverbs 7:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">pro 7:7</span></span>

simple--as in [Pro 1:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#004).

void of, &c.--(Compare [Pro 6:32](../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#032)).

[Proverbs 7:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">pro 7:8</span></span>

her corner--where she was usually found.

went . . . house--implying, perhaps, confidence in himself by his
manner, as denoted in the word

went--literally, "tread pompously."

[Proverbs 7:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">pro 7:9</span></span>

The time, twilight, ending in darkness.

black . . . night--literally, "pupil," or, "eye," that is, middle of
night.

[Proverbs 7:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">pro 7:10</span></span>

attire--that of harlots was sometimes peculiar.

subtile--or, "wary," "cunning."

[Proverbs 7:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 7:11</span></span>

loud--or, "noisy," "bustling."

stubborn--not submissive.

without . . . streets, . . . corner--(Compare [Ti1
5:13](../../../bib/kjv/ti1005.htm#013); [Tit
2:5](../../../bib/kjv/tit002.htm#005)).

[Proverbs 7:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">pro 7:13</span></span>

The preparations for a feast do not necessarily imply peculiar religious
professions. The offerer retained part of the victim for a feast ([Lev
3:9](../../../bib/kjv/lev003.htm#009), &c.). This feast she professes
was prepared for him whom she boldly addresses as one sought specially
to partake of it.

[Proverbs 7:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 7:16</span></span>

my bed--or, "couch," adorned in the costliest manner.

[Proverbs 7:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">pro 7:17</span></span>

bed--a place for sleeping.

[Proverbs 7:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#018)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">pro 7:18</span></span>

There is no fear of discovery.

[Proverbs 7:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#020)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">pro 7:20</span></span>

the day appointed--perhaps, literally, "a full moon," that is, a
fortnight's time (compare [Pro 7:19](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#019)).

[Proverbs 7:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#021)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">pro 7:21</span></span>

caused . . . yield--or, "inclines."

flattering--(Compare [Pro 5:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#003)).

forced him--by persuasion overcoming his scruples.

[Proverbs 7:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">pro 7:22</span></span>

straightway--quickly, either as ignorant of danger, or incapable of
resistance.

[Proverbs 7:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#023)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">pro 7:23</span></span>

Till--He is now caught ([Pro 6:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#026)).

[Proverbs 7:24](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#024)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">pro 7:24</span></span>

The inferential admonition is followed ([Pro
7:26-27](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#026)), by a more general allegation
of the evils of this vice.

[Proverbs 7:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro007.htm#026)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">pro 7:26</span></span>

Even the mightiest fail to resist her deathly allurements.
