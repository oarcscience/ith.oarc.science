### [Psalms Chapter 88](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#001)

[Psalms 88:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 88:1</span></span>

Upon Mahalath--either an instrument, as a lute, to be used as an
accompaniment (Leannoth, "for singing") or, as others think, an
enigmatic title (see on [Psa 5:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#001), [Psa
22:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001), and [Psa
45:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa045.htm#001), titles), denoting the
subject--that is, "sickness or disease, for humbling," the idea of
spiritual maladies being often represented by disease (compare [Psa
6:5-6](../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#005); [Psa
22:14-15](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#014), &c.). On the other terms,
see on [Psa 42:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#001) and [Psa
32:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#001). Heman and Ethan (see on [Psa
89:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#001), title) were David's singers
([Ch1 6:18](../../../bib/kjv/ch1006.htm#018), [Ch1
6:33](../../../bib/kjv/ch1006.htm#033); [Ch1
15:17](../../../bib/kjv/ch1015.htm#017)), of the family of Kohath. If
the persons alluded to ([Kg1 4:31](../../../bib/kjv/kg1004.htm#031);
[Ch1 2:6](../../../bib/kjv/ch1002.htm#006)), they were probably adopted
into the tribe of Judah. Though called a song, which usually implies joy
([Psa 83:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa083.htm#001)), both the style and matter
of the Psalm are very despondent; yet the appeals to God evince faith,
and we may suppose that the word "song" might be extended to such
compositions. (Psa. 88:1-18)

Compare on the terms used, [Psa 22:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#002);
[Psa 31:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#002).

[Psalms 88:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">psa 88:3</span></span>

grave--literally, "hell" ([Psa 16:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#010)),
death in wide sense.

[Psalms 88:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 88:4</span></span>

go . . . pit--of destruction ([Psa
28:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#001)).

as a man--literally, "a stout man," whose strength is utterly gone.

[Psalms 88:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">psa 88:5</span></span>

Free . . . dead--Cut off from God's care, as are the slain, who, falling
under His wrath, are left, no longer sustained by His hand.

[Psalms 88:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">psa 88:6</span></span>

Similar figures for distress in [Psa
63:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#009); [Psa
69:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#003).

[Psalms 88:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">psa 88:7</span></span>

Compare [Psa 38:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#002), on first, and [Psa
42:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa042.htm#007), on last clause.

[Psalms 88:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">psa 88:8</span></span>

Both cut off from sympathy and made hateful to friends ([Psa
31:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#011)).

[Psalms 88:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">psa 88:9</span></span>

Mine eye mourneth--literally, "decays," or fails, denoting exhaustion
([Psa 6:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#007); [Psa
31:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#009)).

I . . . called-- ([Psa 86:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#005), [Psa
86:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa086.htm#007)).

stretched out--for help ([Psa 44:20](../../../bib/kjv/psa044.htm#020)).

[Psalms 88:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">psa 88:10</span></span>

shall the dead--the remains of ghosts.

arise--literally, "rise up," that is, as dead persons.

[Psalms 88:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">psa 88:11</span></span>

amplify the foregoing, the whole purport (as [Psa
6:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#005)) being to contrast death and life
as seasons for praising God.

[Psalms 88:13](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">psa 88:13</span></span>

prevent--meet--that is, he will diligently come before God for help
([Psa 18:41](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#041)).

[Psalms 88:14](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">psa 88:14</span></span>

On the terms ([Psa 27:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#009); [Psa
74:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa074.htm#001); [Psa
77:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa077.htm#007)).

[Psalms 88:15](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">psa 88:15</span></span>

from . . . youth up--all my life.

[Psalms 88:16](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">psa 88:16</span></span>

the extremes of anguish and despair are depicted.

[Psalms 88:18](../../../bib/kjv/psa088.htm#018)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">psa 88:18</span></span>

into darkness--Better omit "into"--"mine acquaintances (are) darkness,"
the gloom of death, &c. ([Job
17:13-14](../../../bib/kjv/job017.htm#013)).
