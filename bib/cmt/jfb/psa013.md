### [Psalms Chapter 13](../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#001)

[Psalms 13:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 13:1</span></span>

On title, see Introduction. The Psalmist, mourning God's absence and the
triumph of his enemies, prays for relief before he is totally destroyed,
and is encouraged to hope his trust will not be in vain. ([Psa
13:1-6](../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#001))

The forms of expression and figure here used are frequent (compare [Psa
9:12](../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#012), [Psa
9:18](../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#018); [Psa
10:11-12](../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#011)).

How long . . . for ever--Shall it be for ever?

[Psalms 13:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">psa 13:2</span></span>

The counsels or devices of his heart afford no relief.

[Psalms 13:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">psa 13:3</span></span>

lighten mine eyes--dim with weakness, denoting approaching death
(compare [Sa1 14:27-29](../../../bib/kjv/sa1014.htm#027); [Psa
6:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa006.htm#007); [Psa
38:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa038.htm#010)).

[Psalms 13:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 13:4</span></span>

rejoice--literally, "shout as in triumph."

I am moved--cast down from a firm position ([Psa
10:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#006)).

[Psalms 13:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">psa 13:5</span></span>

Trust is followed by rejoicing in the deliverance which God effects,
and, instead of his enemy, he can lift the song of triumph.
