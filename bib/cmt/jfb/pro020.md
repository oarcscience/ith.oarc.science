### [Proverbs Chapter 20](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#001.md)

[Proverbs 20:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">pro 20:1</span></span>

(Pro. 20:1-30)

mocker--scorner. Such men are made by wine.

strong drink--made by spicing wine (compare [Isa
5:11](../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#011.md), [Isa
5:22](../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#022).md); and it may include wine.

raging--or boisterous as a drunkard.

deceived--literally, "erring," or reeling.

[Proverbs 20:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pro 20:2</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 19:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#012).md). Men who resist
authority injure themselves ([Rom
13:2](../../../bib/kjv/rom013.htm#002).md).

[Proverbs 20:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">pro 20:3</span></span>

to cease from strife--or, better, "to dwell from or without strife,"
denoting the habit of life.

fool . . . meddling-- ([Pro 17:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#014).md).

[Proverbs 20:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">pro 20:4</span></span>

shall . . . beg--literally, "ask" (in this sense, [Psa
109:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa109.htm#010).md).

[Proverbs 20:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">pro 20:5</span></span>

Counsel . . . water--that is, deeply hidden ([Pro
18:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#004.md); [Psa
13:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#002).md). The wise can discern well.

[Proverbs 20:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">pro 20:6</span></span>

Boasters are unreliable.

goodness--or, "kind disposition."

[Proverbs 20:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">pro 20:7</span></span>

The conduct of good men proclaims their sound principles. God's covenant
and their good example secure blessing to their children ([Pro
4:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#026.md); [Psa
112:1-2](../../../bib/kjv/psa112.htm#001).md).

[Proverbs 20:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#008.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">pro 20:8</span></span>

As in [Pro 14:35](../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#035.md); [Pro
16:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#010.md), [Pro
16:15](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#015.md), this is the character of a good
king, not of all kings.

[Proverbs 20:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">pro 20:9</span></span>

The interrogation in the affirmative strengthens the implied negation
(compare [Job 15:14](../../../bib/kjv/job015.htm#014.md); [Ecc
7:20](../../../bib/kjv/ecc007.htm#020).md).

[Proverbs 20:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">pro 20:10</span></span>

Various measures, implying that some are wrong (compare [Pro
11:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#001.md); [Pro
16:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#011).md).

[Proverbs 20:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 20:11</span></span>

The conduct of children even is the best test of principle (compare [Mat
7:16](../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#016).md).

[Proverbs 20:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#012.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">pro 20:12</span></span>

Hence, of course, God will know all you do ([Psa
94:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa094.htm#009).md).

[Proverbs 20:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">pro 20:13</span></span>

Activity and diligence contrasted with sloth ([Pro
6:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#009.md); [Pro
10:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#011).md).

lest . . . poverty--literally, "be deprived of inheritance."

[Proverbs 20:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">pro 20:14</span></span>

when . . . his way--implying that he goes about boasting of his
bargains.

[Proverbs 20:15](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#015.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">pro 20:15</span></span>

The contrast denotes the greater value of knowledge (compare [Pro
3:14-16](../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#014).md).

[Proverbs 20:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 20:16</span></span>

Take his garment--implies severe exaction, justified by the surety's
rashness.

a strange woman--by some readings "strangers," but the former here, and
in [Pro 27:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#013.md), is allowable, and
strengthens the sense. The debauchee is less reliable than the merely
careless.

[Proverbs 20:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">pro 20:17</span></span>

Bread . . . sweet--either as unlawfully ([Pro
9:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#017).md) or easily obtained.

mouth . . . gravel--well expresses the pain and grief given at last.

[Proverbs 20:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#018.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">pro 20:18</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 15:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#022).md). Be careful and
considerate in important plans.

[Proverbs 20:19](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#019.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">pro 20:19</span></span>

Those who love to tell news will hardly keep secrets.

flattereth . . . lips--(compare Margin; [Pro
1:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#010).md).

meddle . . . him--literally, "join," or "associate with."

[Proverbs 20:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">pro 20:20</span></span>

his lamp--(Compare [Pro 13:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#009.md); [Pro
24:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#020).md).

[Proverbs 20:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#021.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">pro 20:21</span></span>

gotten hastily--contrary to God's providence ([Pro
28:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#020).md), implying its unjust or easy
attainment; hence the man is punished, or spends freely what he got
easily (compare [Pro 20:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#017).md).

[Proverbs 20:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#022.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">pro 20:22</span></span>

(Compare [Psa 27:14](../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#014.md); [Rom
12:17-19](../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#017).md).

[Proverbs 20:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#023.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">pro 20:23</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 20:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#010.md); [Pro
11:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#001).md).

[Proverbs 20:24](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#024.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">pro 20:24</span></span>

Man's goings--literally, "Stately steppings of a strong man."

a man--any common man.

understand--or, "perceive."

[Proverbs 20:25](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#025.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">pro 20:25</span></span>

devoureth . . . holy--or, better, "who rashly speaks promises," or
"devotes what is holy," consecrating any thing. This suits better the
last clause, which expresses a similar view of the results of rashly
vowing.

[Proverbs 20:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#026.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">pro 20:26</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 20:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#008).md).

bringeth . . . over them--The wheel was used for threshing grain. The
figure denotes severity (compare [Amo
1:3](../../../bib/kjv/amo001.htm#003).md).

[Proverbs 20:27](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#027.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">pro 20:27</span></span>

The spirit . . . Lord--Men's minds are God's gifts, and thus able to
search one another (compare [Pro 20:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#005.md);
[Pro 18:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#008.md), [Pro
18:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#017.md); [Co1
2:11](../../../bib/kjv/co1002.htm#011).md).

[Proverbs 20:28](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#028.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">pro 20:28</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 3:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#003.md); [Pro
16:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#006.md), [Pro
16:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#012).md).

[Proverbs 20:29](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#029.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_029">pro 20:29</span></span>

The glory of young men . . . the beauty of old men--Each age has its
peculiar excellence ([Pro 16:31](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#031).md).

[Proverbs 20:30](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#030.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_030">pro 20:30</span></span>

blueness--literally, "joining," the process of uniting the edges of a
wound throws off purulent matter.

stripes . . . belly--So punishment provides healing of soul ([Pro
18:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#008).md), by deterring from evil courses.
