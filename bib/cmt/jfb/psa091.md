### [Psalms Chapter 91](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#001)

[Psalms 91:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 91:1</span></span>

David is the most probable author; and the pestilence, mentioned in [Sa2
24:13-15](../../../bib/kjv/sa2024.htm#013), the most probable of any
special occasion to which the Psalm may refer. The changes of person
allowable in poetry are here frequently made. (Psa. 91:1-16)

dwelleth in the secret place-- ([Psa
27:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#005); [Psa
31:20](../../../bib/kjv/psa031.htm#020)) denotes nearness to God. Such
as do so abide or lodge secure from assaults, and can well use the terms
of trust in [Psa 91:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#002).

[Psalms 91:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">psa 91:3</span></span>

snares . . . \[and\] . . . noisome pestilence--literally, "plagues of
mischiefs" ([Psa 5:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#009); [Psa
52:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa052.htm#007)), are expressive figures for
various evils.

[Psalms 91:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 91:4</span></span>

For the first figure compare [Deu
32:11](../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#011); [Mat
23:37](../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#037).

buckler--literally, "surrounding"--that is, a kind of shield covering
all over.

[Psalms 91:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">psa 91:5</span></span>

terror--or, what causes it ([Pro
20:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#002)).

by night--then aggravated.

arrow--that is, of enemies.

[Psalms 91:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">psa 91:7</span></span>

The security is more valuable, as being special, and, therefore,
evidently of God; and while ten thousands of the wicked fall, the
righteous are in such safety that they only see the calamity.

[Psalms 91:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">psa 91:9</span></span>

This exemption from evil is the result of trust in God, who employs
angels as ministering spirits ([Heb
1:14](../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#014)).

[Psalms 91:13](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">psa 91:13</span></span>

Even the fiercest, strongest, and most insidious animals may be trampled
on with impunity.

[Psalms 91:14](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">psa 91:14</span></span>

God Himself speaks (compare [Psa
46:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#010); [Psa
75:2-3](../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#002)). All the terms to express
safety and peace indicate the most undoubting confidence (compare [Psa
18:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#002); [Psa
20:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#001); [Psa
22:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#005)).

set his love--that of the most ardent kind.

[Psalms 91:16](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">psa 91:16</span></span>

show him--literally, "make him see" ([Psa
50:23](../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#023); [Luk
2:30](../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#030)).
