### [Leviticus Introduction](../../../bib/kjv/lev001.htm#001)

[Leviticus](../../../bib/kjv/lev001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">lev 0:0</span></span>

LEVITICUS. So called from its treating of the laws relating to the
ritual, the services, and sacrifices of the Jewish religion, the
superintendence of which was entrusted to the Levitical priesthood. It
is chiefly, however, the duties of the priests, "the sons of Aaron,"
which this book describes; and its claim to be the work of Moses is
established by the following passages:-- [Ch2
30:16](../../../bib/kjv/ch2030.htm#016); [Neh
8:14](../../../bib/kjv/neh008.htm#014); [Jer
7:22-23](../../../bib/kjv/jer007.htm#022); [Eze
20:11](../../../bib/kjv/eze020.htm#011) [Mat
8:4](../../../bib/kjv/mat008.htm#004); [Luk
2:22](../../../bib/kjv/luk002.htm#022); [Joh
8:5](../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#005); [Rom
10:4](../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#004); [Rom
13:9](../../../bib/kjv/rom013.htm#009); [Co2
6:16](../../../bib/kjv/co2006.htm#016); [Gal
3:12](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#012); [Pe1
1:16](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#016).
