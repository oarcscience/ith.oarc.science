### [Psalms Chapter 63](../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#001.md)

[Psalms 63:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 63:1</span></span>

The historical occasion referred to by the title was probably during
Absalom's rebellion (compare [Sa2
15:23](../../../bib/kjv/sa2015.htm#023.md), [Sa2
15:28](../../../bib/kjv/sa2015.htm#028.md); [Sa2
16:2](../../../bib/kjv/sa2016.htm#002).md). David expresses an earnest
desire for God's favor, and a confident expectation of realizing it in
his deliverance and the ruin of his enemies. ([Psa
63:1-11](../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#001).md)

early . . . seek thee--earnestly ([Isa
26:9](../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#009).md). The figurative terms--

dry and thirsty--literally, "weary," denoting moral destitution, suited
his outward circumstances.

soul--and--flesh--the whole man ([Psa
16:9-10](../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#009).md).

[Psalms 63:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">psa 63:2</span></span>

The special object of desire was God's perfections as displayed in his
worship ([Psa 27:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#004).md).

[Psalms 63:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">psa 63:3</span></span>

Experiencing God's mercy, which exceeds all the blessings of life, his
lips will be opened for his praise ([Psa
51:15](../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#015).md).

[Psalms 63:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 63:4</span></span>

Thus--literally, "Truly."

will I bless--praise Thee ([Psa 34:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa034.htm#001).md).

lift up my hands--in worship (compare [Psa
28:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa028.htm#002).md).

in thy name--in praise of Thy perfections.

[Psalms 63:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">psa 63:5</span></span>

Full spiritual blessings satisfy his desires, and acts of praise fill
his thoughts and time.

[Psalms 63:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">psa 63:6</span></span>

night--as well as day. Past favors assure him of future, and hence he
presses earnestly near to God, whose power sustains him ([Psa
17:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#008.md); [Psa
60:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#005).md).

[Psalms 63:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">psa 63:9</span></span>

those . . . to destroy it--or literally, "to ruin," or, "for ruin"; that
is, such as seek to injure me (are) for ruin, appointed to it (compare
[Psa 35:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#008).md).

shall go . . . earth--into the grave, or, to death; as their bodies are
represented as a portion for--

[Psalms 63:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">psa 63:10</span></span>

foxes--literally, "jackals."

[Psalms 63:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa063.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">psa 63:11</span></span>

the king--that is, David himself, and all who reverence God, "shall
share a glorious part," while treacherous foes shall be for ever
silenced ([Psa 62:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa062.htm#004).md).
