### [Proverbs Chapter 28](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#001)

[Proverbs 28:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">pro 28:1</span></span>

(Pro. 28:1-28)

A bad conscience makes men timid; the righteous are alone truly bold
([Pro 14:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#026); [Psa
27:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#001)).

[Proverbs 28:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pro 28:2</span></span>

Anarchy producing contending rulers shortens the reign of each.

but by a man . . . prolonged--or, "by a man of understanding--that is, a
good ruler--he who knows or regards the right, that is, a good citizen,
shall prolong (his days)." Good rulers are a blessing to the people. Bad
government as a punishment for evil is contrasted with good as blessing
to the good.

[Proverbs 28:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">pro 28:3</span></span>

A poor man, &c.--Such, in power, exact more severely, and so leave
subjects bare.

[Proverbs 28:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">pro 28:4</span></span>

They that forsake . . . wicked--Wrongdoers encourage one another.

[Proverbs 28:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">pro 28:5</span></span>

(Compare [Joh 7:17](../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#017)). Ignorance of
moral truth is due to unwillingness to know it.

[Proverbs 28:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">pro 28:6</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 10:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#006)). Riches cannot
compensate for sin, nor the want of them affect integrity.

[Proverbs 28:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">pro 28:7</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 17:25](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#025)).

riotous men--or, "gluttons" ([Pro
23:20-21](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#020)).

[Proverbs 28:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">pro 28:8</span></span>

usury . . . unjust gain--(Compare Margin). The two terms, meaning nearly
the same, may denote excessive interest. God's providence directs the
proper use of wealth.

[Proverbs 28:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">pro 28:9</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 15:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#008); [Pro
21:27](../../../bib/kjv/pro021.htm#027)).

hearing--that is, obeying. God requires sincere worshippers ([Psa
66:18](../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#018); [Joh
4:24](../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#024)).

[Proverbs 28:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">pro 28:10</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 26:27](../../../bib/kjv/pro026.htm#027)).

[Proverbs 28:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 28:11</span></span>

A poor but wise man can discover (and expose) the rich and
self-conceited.

[Proverbs 28:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">pro 28:12</span></span>

great glory--or, cause for it to a people, for the righteous rejoice in
good, and righteousness exalts a nation ([Pro
14:34](../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#034)).

a man . . . hidden--that is, the good retire, or all kinds try to escape
a wicked rule.

[Proverbs 28:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">pro 28:13</span></span>

(Compare [Psa 32:3-5](../../../bib/kjv/psa032.htm#003)). Concealment of
sin delivers none from God's wrath, but He shows mercy to the humble
penitent ([Psa 51:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#004)).

[Proverbs 28:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">pro 28:14</span></span>

feareth--that is, God, and so repents.

hardeneth his heart--makes himself insensible to sin, and so will not
repent ([Pro 14:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#016); [Pro
29:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro029.htm#001)).

[Proverbs 28:15](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">pro 28:15</span></span>

The rapacity and cruelty of such beasts well represent some wicked men
(compare [Psa 7:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#002); [Psa
17:12](../../../bib/kjv/psa017.htm#012)).

[Proverbs 28:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 28:16</span></span>

The prince . . . understanding--that is, He does not perceive that
oppression jeopards his success. Covetousness often produces oppression,
hence the contrast.

[Proverbs 28:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">pro 28:17</span></span>

doeth violence . . . blood, &c.--or, that is oppressed by the blood of
life ([Gen 9:6](../../../bib/kjv/gen009.htm#006)), which he has taken.

to the pit--the grave or destruction ([Pro
1:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#012); [Job
33:18-24](../../../bib/kjv/job033.htm#018); [Psa
143:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa143.htm#007)).

stay him--sustain or deliver him.

[Proverbs 28:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#018)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">pro 28:18</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 10:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#009); [Pro
17:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#020)). Double dealing is eventually
fatal.

[Proverbs 28:19](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#019)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">pro 28:19</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 10:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#004); [Pro
20:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#004)).

vain persons--idle, useless drones, implying that they are also wicked
([Pro 12:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#011); [Psa
26:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa026.htm#004)).

[Proverbs 28:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#020)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">pro 28:20</span></span>

maketh haste . . . rich--implying deceit or fraud ([Pro
20:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#021)), and so opposed to "faithful"
or reliable.

[Proverbs 28:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#021)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">pro 28:21</span></span>

respect of persons-- ([Pro 24:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro024.htm#023)).
Such are led to evil by the slightest motive.

[Proverbs 28:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">pro 28:22</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 28:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#020)).

evil eye--in the general sense of [Pro
23:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#006), here more specific for
covetousness (compare [Pro 22:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#009); [Mat
20:15](../../../bib/kjv/mat020.htm#015)).

poverty . . . him--by God's providence.

[Proverbs 28:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#023)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">pro 28:23</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 9:8-9](../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#008); [Pro
27:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro027.htm#005)). Those benefited by reproof will
love their monitors.

[Proverbs 28:24](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#024)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">pro 28:24</span></span>

(Compare [Mat 15:4-6](../../../bib/kjv/mat015.htm#004)). Such, though
heirs, are virtually thieves, to be ranked with highwaymen.

[Proverbs 28:25](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#025)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">pro 28:25</span></span>

of a proud heart--literally, "puffed up of soul"--that is,
self-confident, and hence overbearing and litigious.

made fat--or, "prosperous" ([Pro
11:25](../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#025); [Pro
16:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#020)).

[Proverbs 28:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#026)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">pro 28:26</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 3:6-8](../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#006)).

walketh wisely--that is, trusting in God ([Pro
22:17-19](../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#017)).

[Proverbs 28:27](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#027)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">pro 28:27</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 11:24-26](../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#024)).

hideth his eyes--as the face ([Psa
27:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa027.htm#009); [Psa
69:17](../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#017)), denotes inattention.

[Proverbs 28:28](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#028)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">pro 28:28</span></span>

The elevation of the wicked to power drives men to seek refuge from
tyranny (compare [Pro 28:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#012); [Pro
11:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#010); [Psa
12:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa012.htm#008)).
