### [Proverbs Chapter 23](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#001.md)

[Proverbs 23:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">pro 23:1</span></span>

(Pro. 23:1-35)

Avoid the dangers of gluttony.

[Proverbs 23:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pro 23:2</span></span>

put a knife--an Eastern figure for putting restraint on the appetite.

[Proverbs 23:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">pro 23:3</span></span>

are deceitful meat--though well tasted, injurious.

[Proverbs 23:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">pro 23:4</span></span>

(Compare [Ti1 6:9-10](../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#009).md).

thine own wisdom--which regards riches intrinsically as a blessing.

[Proverbs 23:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">pro 23:5</span></span>

Wilt . . . eyes--As the eyes fly after or seek riches, they are not,
that is, either become transitory or unsatisfying; fully expressed by
their flying away.

[Proverbs 23:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">pro 23:6</span></span>

Beware of deceitful men, whose courtesies even you will repent of having
accepted.

evil eye--or purpose ([Pro 22:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#009.md); [Deu
15:9](../../../bib/kjv/deu015.htm#009.md); [Mat
6:23](../../../bib/kjv/mat006.htm#023).md).

[Proverbs 23:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#008.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">pro 23:8</span></span>

The morsel . . . words--that is, disgusted with his true character, all
pleasant intercourse will be destroyed.

[Proverbs 23:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">pro 23:9</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 9:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#008).md). "Cast not your
pearls before swine" ([Mat 7:6](../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#006).md).

[Proverbs 23:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">pro 23:10</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 22:22-23](../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#022).md).

[Proverbs 23:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 23:11</span></span>

redeemer--or avenger ([Lev 25:25-26](../../../bib/kjv/lev025.htm#025.md);
[Num 35:12](../../../bib/kjv/num035.htm#012).md), hence advocate ([Job
19:25](../../../bib/kjv/job019.htm#025).md).

plead . . . thee--(Compare [Job 31:21](../../../bib/kjv/job031.htm#021.md);
[Psa 35:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa035.htm#001.md); [Psa
68:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#005).md).

[Proverbs 23:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#012.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">pro 23:12</span></span>

Here begins another series of precepts.

[Proverbs 23:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">pro 23:13</span></span>

While there is little danger that the use of the "divine ordinance of
the rod" will produce bodily harm, there is great hope of spiritual
good.

[Proverbs 23:15](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#015.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">pro 23:15</span></span>

The pleasure afforded the teacher by the pupil's progress is a motive to
diligence.

[Proverbs 23:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 23:16</span></span>

my reins--(Compare [Psa 7:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa007.htm#009).md).

[Proverbs 23:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">pro 23:17</span></span>

(Compare Margin). The prosperity of the wicked is short.

[Proverbs 23:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#018.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">pro 23:18</span></span>

an end--or, "hereafter," another time, when apparent inequalities shall
be adjusted (compare [Psa 37:28-38](../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#028).md).

[Proverbs 23:19](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#019.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">pro 23:19</span></span>

guide . . . way--or direct thy thoughts to a right course of conduct
(compare [Pro 4:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#004.md); [Pro
9:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#006).md).

[Proverbs 23:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">pro 23:20</span></span>

riotous . . . flesh--prodigal, or eating more than necessary. Instead of
"their flesh" (compare Margin), better, "flesh to them," that is, used
for pleasure.

[Proverbs 23:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#021.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">pro 23:21</span></span>

drowsiness--the dreamy sleep of the slothful.

[Proverbs 23:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#022.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">pro 23:22</span></span>

Hearken--that is, obey ([Pro 1:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#008.md); [Eph
6:1](../../../bib/kjv/eph006.htm#001).md).

despise . . . old--Adults revere the parents whom, as children, they
once obeyed.

[Proverbs 23:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#023.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">pro 23:23</span></span>

Buy--literally, "get" ([Pro 4:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#005).md).

truth--generally and specially as opposed to errors of all kinds.

[Proverbs 23:24](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#024.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">pro 23:24</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 10:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#001.md); [Pro
17:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#021.md), [Pro
17:25](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#025).md).

[Proverbs 23:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#026.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">pro 23:26</span></span>

A solemn warning against whoredom and drunkenness ([Hos
4:11](../../../bib/kjv/hos004.htm#011).md).

give me--This is the address of that divine wisdom so often presented
([Pro 8:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#001.md); [Pro
9:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#003), &c..md).

heart--confidence.

observe--keep.

my ways--such as I teach you ([Pro
3:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#017.md); [Pro
9:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#006).md).

[Proverbs 23:27](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#027.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">pro 23:27</span></span>

deep ditch--a narrow pit, out of which it is hard to climb.

lieth in wait--to ensnare men into the pit, as hunters entrap game
(compare [Pro 22:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro022.htm#014).md).

[Proverbs 23:28](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#028.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">pro 23:28</span></span>

increaseth . . . transgressors-- ([Pro
5:8-10](../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#008).md). The vice alluded to is
peculiarly hardening to the heart.

[Proverbs 23:29](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#029.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_029">pro 23:29</span></span>

This picture is often sadly realized now.

mixed wine--(Compare [Pro 9:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#002.md); [Isa
5:11](../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#011).md).

[Proverbs 23:31](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#031.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_031">pro 23:31</span></span>

when . . . red--the color denoting greater strength (compare [Gen
49:11](../../../bib/kjv/gen049.htm#011.md); [Deu
32:14](../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#014).md).

giveth . . . cup--literally, "gives its eye," that is, sparkles.

moveth . . . aright--Perhaps its foaming is meant.

[Proverbs 23:32](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#032.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_032">pro 23:32</span></span>

The acute miseries resulting from drunkenness contrasted with the
temptations.

[Proverbs 23:33](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#033.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_033">pro 23:33</span></span>

The moral effects: it inflames passion ([Gen
19:31](../../../bib/kjv/gen019.htm#031.md), [Gen
19:35](../../../bib/kjv/gen019.htm#035).md), lays open the heart, produces
insensibility to the greatest dangers, and debars from reformation,
under the severest sufferings.

[Proverbs 23:35](../../../bib/kjv/pro023.htm#035.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_035">pro 23:35</span></span>

awake--that is, from drunkenness ([Gen
9:24](../../../bib/kjv/gen009.htm#024).md). This is the language rather of
acts than of the tongue.
