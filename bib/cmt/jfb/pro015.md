### [Proverbs Chapter 15](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#001)

[Proverbs 15:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">pro 15:1</span></span>

(Pro. 15:1-33)

soft--tender or gentle.

turneth . . . wrath--from any one.

stir up--as a smouldering fire is excited.

[Proverbs 15:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pro 15:2</span></span>

useth . . . aright--commends knowledge by its proper use.

poureth out--utters abundantly ([Pro
12:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#023)), and so disgusts others.

[Proverbs 15:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">pro 15:3</span></span>

beholding--watching (compare [Pro
5:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro005.htm#021); [Psa
66:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa066.htm#007)).

[Proverbs 15:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">pro 15:4</span></span>

A wholesome tongue--(Compare Margin), pacifying and soothing language.

tree of life-- ([Pro 3:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#018); [Pro
11:30](../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#030)).

perverseness therein--cross, ill-natured language.

breach . . . spirit--(compare [Isa
65:14](../../../bib/kjv/isa065.htm#014), Hebrew), grieves, instead of
appeasing.

[Proverbs 15:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">pro 15:5</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 4:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#001); [Pro
10:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017); Pro. 13:1-18).

is prudent--acts discreetly.

[Proverbs 15:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">pro 15:6</span></span>

treasure--implying utility.

trouble--vexation and affliction.

[Proverbs 15:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">pro 15:7</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 10:20-21](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#020)).

heart . . . not so--not right, or vain.

[Proverbs 15:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">pro 15:8</span></span>

The sacrifice \[and\] prayer--are acts of worship.

way . . . followeth . . . righteousness--denote conduct. God's regard
for the worship and deeds of the righteous and wicked respectively, so
stated in [Psa 50:17](../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#017); [Isa
1:11](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#011).

[Proverbs 15:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">pro 15:10</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 10:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017)).

the way--that in which God would have him to go ([Pro
2:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#013); [Psa
119:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#001)).

[Proverbs 15:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 15:11</span></span>

Hell-- ([Psa 16:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa016.htm#010)).

destruction--or, "Abaddon," the place of the destroyer. All the unseen
world is open to God, much more men's hearts.

[Proverbs 15:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">pro 15:12</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 9:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#008)).

go unto the wise--to be instructed.

[Proverbs 15:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">pro 15:13</span></span>

maketh . . . countenance--or, "benefits the countenance."

spirit is broken--and so the countenance is sad.

[Proverbs 15:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">pro 15:14</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 10:21-22](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#021)). The wise grow
wiser, the fools more foolish ([Pro
9:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#009)).

[Proverbs 15:15](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">pro 15:15</span></span>

The state of the heart governs the outward condition.

evil--sad, contrasted with the cheerfulness of a feast.

[Proverbs 15:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 15:16</span></span>

trouble--agitation, implying the anxieties and perplexities attending
wealth held by worldlings ([Pro 16:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#018);
[Ti1 6:6](../../../bib/kjv/ti1006.htm#006)).

[Proverbs 15:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">pro 15:17</span></span>

dinner--or, "allowance" ([Kg2 25:30](../../../bib/kjv/kg2025.htm#030))
--

of herbs--and that the plainest.

and hatred--(compare [Pro 10:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#012), [Pro
10:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#018)).

[Proverbs 15:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#018)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">pro 15:18</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 14:29](../../../bib/kjv/pro014.htm#029); [Pro
16:32](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#032)).

[Proverbs 15:19](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#019)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">pro 15:19</span></span>

The difficulties of the slothful result from want of energy; the
righteous find a

plain \[and open\] way--literally, "a highway," by diligence ([Sa1
10:7](../../../bib/kjv/sa1010.htm#007); [Psa
1:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa001.htm#003)).

[Proverbs 15:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#020)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">pro 15:20</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 10:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#001)).

[Proverbs 15:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#021)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">pro 15:21</span></span>

walketh uprightly--and so finds his joy ([Pro
3:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#006); [Pro
10:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#023)).

[Proverbs 15:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">pro 15:22</span></span>

Without counsel--or, "deliberation," implying a wise deference to the
opinions of the wise and good, contrasted with rashness.

[Proverbs 15:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#023)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">pro 15:23</span></span>

Good advice blesses the giver and receiver.

[Proverbs 15:24](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#024)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">pro 15:24</span></span>

(Compare [Col 3:2](../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#002)). Holy purposes
prevent sinning, and so its evils.

[Proverbs 15:25](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#025)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">pro 15:25</span></span>

The most desolate who have God's aid have more permanent good than the
self-reliant sinner ([Pro 2:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#022); [Pro
12:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#007)).

border--or, "boundary for possessions" ([Psa
78:54](../../../bib/kjv/psa078.htm#054)).

[Proverbs 15:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#026)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">pro 15:26</span></span>

are pleasant words--that is, pleasing to God ([Pro
8:8-9](../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#008)).

[Proverbs 15:27](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#027)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">pro 15:27</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 11:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#017)). Avarice brings
trouble to him and his.

hateth gifts--or, "bribes" ([Exo 23:8](../../../bib/kjv/exo023.htm#008);
[Psa 15:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa015.htm#005)), and is not avaricious.

[Proverbs 15:28](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#028)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">pro 15:28</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 15:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#014); [Pro
10:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#011)). Caution is the fruit of
wisdom; rashness of folly.

[Proverbs 15:29](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#029)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_029">pro 15:29</span></span>

far . . . wicked--in His love and favor ([Psa
22:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#011); [Psa
119:155](../../../bib/kjv/psa119.htm#155)).

[Proverbs 15:30](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#030)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_030">pro 15:30</span></span>

light of the eyes-- ([Pro 13:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#009)). What
gives light rejoiceth the heart, by relieving from anxiety as to our
course; so

good report--or, "doctrine" ([Isa
28:9](../../../bib/kjv/isa028.htm#009); [Isa
53:1](../../../bib/kjv/isa053.htm#001)),

maketh . . . fat--or, "gives prosperity" ([Pro
3:13-17](../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#013); [Pro
9:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro009.htm#011)). The last clause is illustrated
by the first.

[Proverbs 15:31](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#031)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_031">pro 15:31</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 10:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017)).

reproof of life--which leads to life.

abideth . . . wise--is numbered among them.

[Proverbs 15:32](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#032)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_032">pro 15:32</span></span>

refuseth--or, "neglects," "passes by" ([Pro
1:25](../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#025); [Pro
4:15](../../../bib/kjv/pro004.htm#015)).

despiseth . . . soul--so acts as if esteeming its interests of no value.

[Proverbs 15:33](../../../bib/kjv/pro015.htm#033)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_033">pro 15:33</span></span>

The fear . . . wisdom--Wisdom instructs in true piety.

before . . . humility--(compare [Luk
24:26](../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#026); [Pe1
1:11](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#011)); opposite (compare [Pro
16:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#018)).
