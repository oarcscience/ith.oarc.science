### [Proverbs Chapter 13](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#001.md)

[Proverbs 13:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">pro 13:1</span></span>

(Pro. 13:1-25)

(Compare [Pro 6:1-5](../../../bib/kjv/pro006.htm#001.md); [Pro
10:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#001.md), [Pro
10:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017).md).

[Proverbs 13:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pro 13:2</span></span>

shall eat--that is, obtain ([Pro
12:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#014).md).

transgressors--as in [Pro 2:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro002.htm#022.md).

violence--or, "mischief" to themselves.

[Proverbs 13:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">pro 13:3</span></span>

He . . . mouth . . . life--because evil speeches may provoke violence
from others.

he that openeth wide his lips shall have destruction--On last clause,
compare [Pro 10:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#014.md).

[Proverbs 13:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">pro 13:4</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 12:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#011.md), [Pro
12:27](../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#027).md).

[Proverbs 13:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">pro 13:5</span></span>

loathsome . . . shame--better, causeth shame and reproach (compare [Pro
19:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro019.htm#026).md), by slander, &c., which the
righteous hates.

[Proverbs 13:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">pro 13:6</span></span>

A sentiment of frequent recurrence, that piety benefits and sin injures.

[Proverbs 13:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">pro 13:7</span></span>

In opposite ways men act hypocritically for gain of honor or wealth.

[Proverbs 13:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#008.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">pro 13:8</span></span>

Riches save some from punishment, while others suffer because they will
not heed the rebuke of sloth, which makes and keeps them poor.

[Proverbs 13:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">pro 13:9</span></span>

light . . . lamp--prosperity; the first, the greater, and it

rejoiceth--burns brightly, or continues, while the other, at best small,
soon fails.

[Proverbs 13:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">pro 13:10</span></span>

The obstinacy which attends self-conceit, produces contention, which the
well-advised, thus evincing modesty, avoid.

[Proverbs 13:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 13:11</span></span>

by vanity--or, "nothingness," that is, which is vain or useless to the
public (as card playing or similar vices).

gathereth . . . labour--(Compare Margin), little by little, laboriously.

[Proverbs 13:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#012.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">pro 13:12</span></span>

desire cometh--is realized.

a tree of life--or, "cause of happiness."

[Proverbs 13:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">pro 13:13</span></span>

the word--that is, of advice, or, instruction (compare [Pro
10:27](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#027.md); [Pro
11:31](../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#031).md).

[Proverbs 13:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">pro 13:14</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 10:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#011).md).

fountain--or, "source of life."

to depart--(compare [Pro 1:2-4](../../../bib/kjv/pro001.htm#002).md), or,
"for departing," &c., and so gives life.

[Proverbs 13:15](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#015.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">pro 13:15</span></span>

Right perception and action secure good will, while evil ways are
difficult as a stony road. The wicked left of God find punishment of sin
in sinning.

hard--or, "harsh" (compare Hebrew: [Deu
21:4](../../../bib/kjv/deu021.htm#004.md); [Jer
5:15](../../../bib/kjv/jer005.htm#015).md).

[Proverbs 13:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 13:16</span></span>

dealeth--acts with foresight.

a fool . . . folly--for want of caution.

[Proverbs 13:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">pro 13:17</span></span>

A wicked--or, "unfaithful"

messenger falleth into mischief--or, "by mischief," or "evil," and so
his errand fails. Contrasted is the character of the faithful, whose
faithfulness benefits others.

[Proverbs 13:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#018.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">pro 13:18</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 10:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro010.htm#017.md); [Pro
12:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro012.htm#001).md).

[Proverbs 13:19](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#019.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">pro 13:19</span></span>

Self-denial, which fools will not endure, is essential to success.

[Proverbs 13:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">pro 13:20</span></span>

The benefits of good and evil of bad society are contrasted.

[Proverbs 13:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#021.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">pro 13:21</span></span>

(Compare [Pro 11:31](../../../bib/kjv/pro011.htm#031).md).

good . . . repaid--or, "He (God) will repay good."

[Proverbs 13:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#022.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">pro 13:22</span></span>

wealth . . . just--While good men's estates remain in their families,
God so orders that the gains of sinners enure to the just (compare [Pro
28:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro028.htm#008.md); [Psa
37:18](../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#018.md), [Psa
37:22](../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#022.md), [Psa
37:26](../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#026), &c..md).

[Proverbs 13:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#023.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">pro 13:23</span></span>

The laboring poor prosper more than those who injudiciously or wickedly
strive, by fraud and violence, to supersede the necessity of lawful
labor.

[Proverbs 13:24](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#024.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">pro 13:24</span></span>

spareth--or, "withholds."

rod--of correction.

hateth--or, acts as if he hated him (compare [Pro
3:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro003.htm#012.md); [Pro
8:36](../../../bib/kjv/pro008.htm#036).md).

chasteneth . . . betimes--or, "diligently seeks for him all useful
discipline."

[Proverbs 13:25](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#025.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">pro 13:25</span></span>

The comparative temporal prosperity of the righteous and wicked, rather
than contentment and discontent, is noted.
