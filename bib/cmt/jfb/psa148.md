### [Psalms Chapter 148](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#001.md)

[Psalms 148:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 148:1</span></span>

The scope of this Psalm is the same as that of the preceding. ([Psa
148:1-14](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#001).md)

heavens \[and\] heights--are synonymous.

[Psalms 148:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">psa 148:2</span></span>

hosts--(compare [Psa 103:21](../../../bib/kjv/psa103.htm#021).md).

[Psalms 148:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 148:4</span></span>

heavens of heavens--the very highest.

waters--clouds, resting above the visible heavens (compare [Gen
1:7](../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#007).md).

[Psalms 148:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">psa 148:5</span></span>

praise the name--as representing His perfections.

he commanded--"He" is emphatic, ascribing creation to God alone.

[Psalms 148:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">psa 148:6</span></span>

The perpetuity of the frame of nature is, of course, subject to Him who
formed it.

a decree . . . pass--His ordinances respecting them shall not change
([Jer 36:31](../../../bib/kjv/jer036.htm#031).md), or perish ([Job
34:20](../../../bib/kjv/job034.htm#020.md); [Psa
37:36](../../../bib/kjv/psa037.htm#036).md).

[Psalms 148:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">psa 148:7</span></span>

The call on the earth, as opposed to heaven, includes seas or depths,
whose inhabitants the dragon, as one of the largest (on leviathan, see
on [Psa 104:26](../../../bib/kjv/psa104.htm#026).md), is selected to
represent. The most destructive and ungovernable agents of inanimate
nature are introduced.

[Psalms 148:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#008.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">psa 148:8</span></span>

fulfilling his word--or, law, may be understood of each. Next the most
distinguished productions of the vegetable world.

[Psalms 148:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">psa 148:9</span></span>

fruitful trees--or, "trees of fruit," as opposed to forest trees. Wild
and domestic, large and small animals are comprehended.

[Psalms 148:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">psa 148:11</span></span>

Next all rational beings, from the highest in rank to little children.

princes--or, military leaders.

[Psalms 148:13](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">psa 148:13</span></span>

Let them--all mentioned.

excellent--or, exalted ([Isa 12:4](../../../bib/kjv/isa012.htm#004).md).

his glory--majesty ([Psa 45:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa045.htm#003).md).

above the earth and heaven--Their united splendors fail to match His.

[Psalms 148:14](../../../bib/kjv/psa148.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">psa 148:14</span></span>

exalteth the horn--established power ([Psa
75:5-6](../../../bib/kjv/psa075.htm#005).md).

praise of--or literally, "for"

his saints--that is, occasions for them to praise Him. They are further
described as "His people," and "near unto Him," sustaining by covenanted
care a peculiarly intimate relation.
