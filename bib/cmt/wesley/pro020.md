### [Proverbs Chapter 20](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#001.md)

[Proverbs 20:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">pro 20:1</span></span>

A mocker - Wine immoderately drunk makes men mockers. Raging - Makes men
full of rage.

[Proverbs 20:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pro 20:2</span></span>

The fear - The terror which the wrath of a king causes.

[Proverbs 20:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">pro 20:3</span></span>

Meddling - Is always ready to begin strife, and obstinate in the
continuance of it.

[Proverbs 20:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">pro 20:5</span></span>

Counsel - Designs of doing something of moment. Deep water - Is secret
and hard to be discovered.

[Proverbs 20:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#008.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">pro 20:8</span></span>

His eyes - With his very looks, or by his diligent inspection into
affairs.

[Proverbs 20:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">pro 20:10</span></span>

Divers - One greater for shew and one lesser for use.

[Proverbs 20:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 20:11</span></span>

Is known - The future disposition of a man may be probably conjectured
from his childish manners.

[Proverbs 20:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">pro 20:13</span></span>

Open - Shake off sloth and betake thyself to thy employment with
diligence and vigour.

[Proverbs 20:15](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#015.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">pro 20:15</span></span>

The lips - But wise speeches are of far greater worth.

[Proverbs 20:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 20:16</span></span>

Take - As a pledge, without which he ought not to be trusted. Of him -
That is surety.

[Proverbs 20:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#018.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">pro 20:18</span></span>

Established - The way to bring our purposes to good effect is to manage
them with serious consideration.

[Proverbs 20:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">pro 20:20</span></span>

Lamp - His name and memory shall utterly perish.

[Proverbs 20:24](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#024.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">pro 20:24</span></span>

His way - What the issue of his designs will be.

[Proverbs 20:25](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#025.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">pro 20:25</span></span>

A snare - It brings guilt upon him. After - After a man has made vows to
enquire for ways to break them.

[Proverbs 20:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#026.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">pro 20:26</span></span>

The wheel - As the cart - wheel was anciently turned over the sheaves to
beat the corn out of them. He punishes them as their offences deserve.

[Proverbs 20:27](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#027.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">pro 20:27</span></span>

The spirit - The reasonable soul. The candle - Is a clear and glorious
light set up in man for his information and direction. Of the Lord - So
called because it comes from God in a more immediate manner than the
body, [Ecc 12:7](../../../bib/kjv/ecc012.htm#007.md), and because it is in
God's stead, to observe and judge all our actions. Searching -
Discerning not only his outward actions, which are visible to others,
but his most inward thoughts and affections. The belly is here put for
the heart, as it is frequently.

[Proverbs 20:30](../../../bib/kjv/pro020.htm#030.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_030">pro 20:30</span></span>

The blueness - Grievous wounds, which make men black and blue; or severe
punishments. Cleanseth - Are the means to reclaim a wicked man, and to
purge out his corruption. The belly - Of the heart. Grievous wounds or
stripes cleanse not only the outward man by keeping it from evil
actions, but even the inward man, by expelling or subduing vile
affections: which is a great and blessed benefit of afflictions.
