*Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible*, by John Wesley, \[1754-65\], at
Intangible Textual Heritage

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### [Lamentations Chapter 1](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#001.md)

[Lamentations 1:1](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">lam 1:1</span></span>

A widow - She that had a king, or rather a God, that was an husband to
her, now was forsaken of God, and her king taken from her.

[Lamentations 1:3](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">lam 1:3</span></span>

Because - Because of the servitude and oppression exercised among them:
oppression by their rulers, and servitude more generally; keeping their
servants beyond the year of jubilee, when they ought to be set at
liberty. The straits - Those that pursued them overtook them in places
where they could not escape.

[Lamentations 1:4](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">lam 1:4</span></span>

She - Persons of all ages and ranks are in bitterness.

[Lamentations 1:10](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">lam 1:10</span></span>

Pleasant things - Has laid violent hands on them. The things of the
sanctuary were always pleasant things to those that feared God.

[Lamentations 1:11](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">lam 1:11</span></span>

Bread - Even in a land that ordinarily flowed with milk and honey, they
were at a loss for bread to eat. Given - And gave any thing for
something to satisfy their hunger. Vile - Miserable or contemptible.

[Lamentations 1:12](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#012.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">lam 1:12</span></span>

Is it nothing - The prophet speaks in the name of the Jewish church.

[Lamentations 1:13](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">lam 1:13</span></span>

Fire - A judgment as consuming, and afflictive as fire.

[Lamentations 1:14](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">lam 1:14</span></span>

Is bound - Put upon my neck on account of my transgressions. Wreathed -
My punishments are twisted as cords; I have a complication of judgments
upon me, sword, famine, pestilence, captivity.

[Lamentations 1:15](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#015.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">lam 1:15</span></span>

An assembly - God had called an assembly of Chaldeans against the city,
to crush the inhabitants of it. Trodden - God had trodden upon the Jews
as men use to stamp grapes in a wine - press.

[Lamentations 1:16](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">lam 1:16</span></span>

The comforter - God.

[Lamentations 1:17](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">lam 1:17</span></span>

Jerusalem - Is become loathsome and filthy.

[Lamentations 1:19](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#019.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">lam 1:19</span></span>

Deceived - They did not answer my expectation.

[Lamentations 1:20](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">lam 1:20</span></span>

Death - By famine and pestilence.

[Lamentations 1:21](../../../bib/kjv/lam001.htm#021.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">lam 1:21</span></span>

They - The neighbouring nations. Like me - But thou hast foretold their
destruction also, and hast by me proclaimed it: and thou shalt in that
day bring them into as sad a condition as I am in now.
