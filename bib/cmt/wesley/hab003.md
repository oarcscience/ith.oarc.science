### [Habakkuk Chapter 3](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#001.md)

[Habakkuk 3:1](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">hab 3:1</span></span>

Upon Sigionoth - A musical instrument.

[Habakkuk 3:2](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">hab 3:2</span></span>

Thy speech - In answer to the inquiry made [Hab
1:13-14](../../../bib/kjv/hab001.htm#013.md). Was afraid - Trembled at what
thou speakest. In the midst of the years - Even before the seventy years
are expired. Make known - Thy truth, wisdom, power, and compassion.

[Habakkuk 3:3](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">hab 3:3</span></span>

God - The God of our fathers, discovered himself from Teman, a mountain
not far from mount Sinai, where the law was given. Paran - Near Sinai.
His glory - This the prophet mentions as a support of his faith, that
God so gloriously appeared among their fathers. Full of his praise - Of
works which were worthy of all praise.

[Habakkuk 3:4](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">hab 3:4</span></span>

As the light - Pure, clear as the sun, but much more dazzling. His hand
- The face of Moses shined; the face, yea, hands of our God, shine with
glorious light. There - In that light wherewith he appeared. The hiding
- Which discovered much of it, but hid much more; it was light
inaccessible.

[Habakkuk 3:5](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">hab 3:5</span></span>

Before him - When God was leading the Israelites out of Egypt, he made
the pestilence go before him, so preparing room for his people.

[Habakkuk 3:6](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">hab 3:6</span></span>

He stood - Gave his presence with Joshua, as one that stood by while the
work was done. The land - The promised land. He beheld - Looked with a
frowning countenance. Drove asunder - Cast them out, his eye did this,
for he looked on them, and did this. His ways - The wisdom, goodness,
justice, holiness, and power of God, which he shews in governing his
people.

[Habakkuk 3:7](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">hab 3:7</span></span>

The tents - The people that dwelt in them. Arabia - Near whose borders
Israel marched. In affliction - In fear and pain, lest that mighty
people should fall on them. The curtains - Those that dwelt within them;
these people dwelt in tents, which were made up on the sides with
curtains.

[Habakkuk 3:8](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#008.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">hab 3:8</span></span>

The sea - The Red Sea. Ride - As a general in the head of his army. Upon
thine horses - Alluding to the manner of men. Salvation - No; but he
came to save his people.

[Habakkuk 3:9](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">hab 3:9</span></span>

Thy bow - One part of armour is put for the whole. The Lord is
represented as armed, in readiness to smite through all his enemies.
According to the oaths - In pursuance of his oath made to our fathers,
and their posterity. Cleave the earth - When they were to march through
a dry and thirsty land.

[Habakkuk 3:10](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">hab 3:10</span></span>

Overflowing - The inundation which at that season was wont to be very
great in and round Jordan, passed away at the word of God; the waters
below flowed, and ran from those above, which stood on a heap to make a
path for Israel. The deep - Either the deep channel in which Jordan
flowed, or the Red Sea with dreadful roaring parted its waters. Lift up
his hands - Its waves which stood on an heap.

[Habakkuk 3:11](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">hab 3:11</span></span>

Stood still - At the prayer of Joshua. In the light - Which was most
miraculously continued.

[Habakkuk 3:13](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">hab 3:13</span></span>

With thine anointed - Under the conduct of thine anointed, Joshua, the
type of the Messiah. Thou woundest - Gavest a deadly wound to the kings
of Canaan. The house of the wicked - The courts of these kings were
houses of the vilest wickedness. By discovering - Destroying all from
head to foot.

[Habakkuk 3:14](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">hab 3:14</span></span>

Villages - All the cities and all the unwalled towns. They - The
inhabitants of Canaan. As a whirlwind - With violence invading me on
every side. To scatter - To disperse and drive away the Israelites.
Their rejoicing - They rejoiced in full confidence of swallowing up
Israel unawares.

[Habakkuk 3:15](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#015.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">hab 3:15</span></span>

Didst walk - Thou heldest on thy way, from thy entering in on the east
of the land, to the west thereof.

[Habakkuk 3:16](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">hab 3:16</span></span>

When I heard - What dreadful desolations God threatened against Israel.
My heart trembled - Another effect of surprising fears and astonishment.
Rottenness - A decay of all my strength. That I might rest - These fears
made me betake myself to God, that I might rest in him. He - The king of
Babylon. The people - The Jews.

[Habakkuk 3:17](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">hab 3:17</span></span>

The labour - The labour bestowed upon the olive. Yield no meat - Corn.
Flock - Of sheep.

[Habakkuk 3:19](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#019.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">hab 3:19</span></span>

Like hinds feet - That I may escape to God my refuge. He will make me -
To conquer and triumph.
