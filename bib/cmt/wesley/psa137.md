### [Psalms Chapter 137](../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#001)

[Psalms 137:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 137:1</span></span>

Sat - The usual posture of mourners.

[Psalms 137:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">psa 137:2</span></span>

Harps - Harps are here put for all instruments of musick.

[Psalms 137:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">psa 137:3</span></span>

A song - Such songs as you used to sing in the temple of Zion.

[Psalms 137:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 137:4</span></span>

The Lord's - Those songs which were appointed by God to be sung only in
his service.

[Psalms 137:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">psa 137:6</span></span>

If - If I do not value Jerusalem's prosperity more than all other
delights.

[Psalms 137:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">psa 137:7</span></span>

The day - In the time of its destruction.

[Psalms 137:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa137.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">psa 137:8</span></span>

Happy - As being God's instrument to vindicate his honour, and execute
his just judgments.
