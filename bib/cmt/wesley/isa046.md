### [Isaiah Chapter 46](../../../bib/kjv/isa046.htm#001)

[Isaiah 46:1](../../../bib/kjv/isa046.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">isa 46:1</span></span>

Bel - The chief idol of the Babylonians, called by profane historians
Jupiter Belus. Boweth - As the Babylonians used to bow down to him to
worship, so now he bows down to the victorious Persians. Nebo - Another
of the famous idols, which used to deliver oracles. Their idols - Were
taken and broken, and the materials of them, gold, silver, and brass,
were carried upon beasts into Persia. Your carriages - O ye Persians, to
whom he turns his speech.

[Isaiah 46:2](../../../bib/kjv/isa046.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">isa 46:2</span></span>

They - The Babylonians. Together - The Babylonians and their idols
together, neither could help the other. Deliver - The Babylonians could
not deliver their idols.

[Isaiah 46:3](../../../bib/kjv/isa046.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">isa 46:3</span></span>

Carried - Whom I have nourished, ever since you were a people, and came
out of Egypt; and that as tenderly, as parents bring up their own
children.

[Isaiah 46:7](../../../bib/kjv/isa046.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">isa 46:7</span></span>

Remove - He can stir neither hand nor foot to help his people.

[Isaiah 46:8](../../../bib/kjv/isa046.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">isa 46:8</span></span>

Bring it - Think of this again and again, ye who have been guilty of
this foolish sin.

[Isaiah 46:10](../../../bib/kjv/isa046.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">isa 46:10</span></span>

Declaring - Foretelling from the beginning of the world, future events
which should happen in succeeding ages, even to the end of the world.

[Isaiah 46:11](../../../bib/kjv/isa046.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">isa 46:11</span></span>

A bird - Cyrus, called a bird for his swiftness, and ravenous for his
fierceness, and victoriousness over his enemies.

[Isaiah 46:13](../../../bib/kjv/isa046.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">isa 46:13</span></span>

I bring - Tho' you are unrighteous, I will shew myself a righteous and
faithful God, making good my promise of delivering you out of Babylon
after seventy years. It - My work of saving you from the Babylonish
captivity. Will place - I will bring my people to Zion, and save them
from all their enemies. My glory - In whom I will once again glory as my
people.
