### [Amos Chapter 6](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#001.md)

[Amos 6:1](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">amo 6:1</span></span>

At ease - That neither fear nor believe the threatened judgments of God.
In Zion - That is put for the kingdom of the two tribes, and principally
the inhabitants of Jerusalem. Samaria - Woe to them also who rely upon
the strength, wealth, and policy of the kingdom of Samaria or Israel.
Which - Which two cities, Zion and Samaria. Named chief - Accounted the
chief cities of that part of the world. To whom - To which place all
Israel had recourse, the two tribes to Zion, the ten tribes to Samaria.

[Amos 6:2](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">amo 6:2</span></span>

Pass ye - Run over the history of that great and ancient city. Hemath -
Head of the Syrian kingdom, lately overthrown by Tiglath - Pilneser, and
a fresh instance of God's just indignation against secure sinners. Gath
- The chief city of the Philistines, a few years before wasted by the
arms of Hazael; by these examples learn to amend your ways, or expect to
perish in them. Greater - That is, greater than these kingdoms of Israel
and Judah, and their borders, or bounds, greater than these of Israel
and Judah.

[Amos 6:3](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">amo 6:3</span></span>

Ye - That flatter yourselves the day of darkness foretold, is far off.
The seat - The judgment seat which should relieve the oppressed, is made
a seat of violence.

[Amos 6:4](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">amo 6:4</span></span>

That lie - That out of laziness or luxury, lay themselves to rest. And
eat - The very best in all their flock.

[Amos 6:5](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">amo 6:5</span></span>

That chant - That in a time of deep mourning entertain themselves with
songs, and musical instruments.

[Amos 6:6](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">amo 6:6</span></span>

In bowls - Not in little vessels, but probably bowls: they drank these
filled as full as they could hold too, and in design to drink each other
down. And anoint - In those hot countries this anointing was much used.
Not grieved - Nothing affected with the calamities of their country.

[Amos 6:7](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">amo 6:7</span></span>

The banquet - The feastings of voluptuous ones shall cease.

[Amos 6:8](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#008.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">amo 6:8</span></span>

The excellency - All that the seed of Jacob accounts a glory and
excellency to them, all their external privileges and worship.

[Amos 6:9](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">amo 6:9</span></span>

Remain - Escaping the enemies sword. Ten men - Many men, a certain
number for an uncertain. They shall die - Tho' they escape a while, they
shall not finally escape.

[Amos 6:10](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">amo 6:10</span></span>

Uncle - Or near kinsman, instead of those who were wont to do this, and
were paid for it; but now none of these remaining, the next to the dead
must, as well as he is able, take him up on his shoulders, and carry
him. That burneth - Though the Jews mostly buried, yet in some cases
they burned the dead bodies, as in this of pestilence. The bones - The
flesh being consumed, the bones are reserved to be buried. Unto him -
Any one he sees near the house out of which the bones are carried. Is
there yet any - Is any one living in your house. Hold thy tongue - Do
not complain, lest thou thyself be killed, lest all be rifled. For - It
is too late to seek God, who is executing his immutable decree.

[Amos 6:11](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">amo 6:11</span></span>

For behold - It seems to be the continued speech of him who took care of
the dead, [Amo 6:10](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#010.md), God hath sent out
war, famine, and pestilence. The great house - The palaces of great men
shall have great breaches made in them, and the cottages of poor men
shall, by lesser strokes, be ruined.

[Amos 6:12](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#012.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">amo 6:12</span></span>

Shall horses - If prophets exhort or advise, it does no more good than
if you would run your horses upon the precipices of rocks.

[Amos 6:13](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">amo 6:13</span></span>

Who rejoice - In your victories, alliances, and idols. Have we not - We
have raised ourselves to greatness by our wisdom and courage.

[Amos 6:14](../../../bib/kjv/amo006.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">amo 6:14</span></span>

Hemath - A city of Syria, bordering on Israel, north - east. The
wilderness - Which is the south - west parts of Canaan. So all your
country shall be destroyed.
