*Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible*, by John Wesley, \[1754-65\], at

### [Ezekiel Chapter 6](../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#001)

[Ezekiel 6:2](../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">eze 6:2</span></span>

The mountains - The inhabitants of the mountains, who were secure in
their fastnesses.

[Ezekiel 6:3](../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">eze 6:3</span></span>

Rivers - To those who dwell by river sides, or in the valleys. High
places - The places of your idolatrous worship.

[Ezekiel 6:4](../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">eze 6:4</span></span>

Cast down - Before the altars of your idols, which you fly to for
refuge.

[Ezekiel 6:5](../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">eze 6:5</span></span>

And - Thus the idols were upbraided with their inability to help their
worshippers, and the idolaters, with the folly of trusting in them.

[Ezekiel 6:6](../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">eze 6:6</span></span>

Your works - All your costly work for your idols.

[Ezekiel 6:8](../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">eze 6:8</span></span>

Remnant - It is the Lord that preserves a remnant, the enemies rage
would destroy all.

[Ezekiel 6:9](../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">eze 6:9</span></span>

Shall remember - So as to turn unto me. Broken - I am much grieved.
Whorish heart - Idolatrous hearts depart from God, as an adulterous wife
departs from her husband. Loath - With a mixture of grief towards God,
of indignation against themselves, and abhorrence of the offence.

[Ezekiel 6:10](../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">eze 6:10</span></span>

In vain - Either without cause, the sufferers gave him just cause to
pronounce that evil; or without effect. Their sins where the cause, and
their destruction is the effect of their sufferings.

[Ezekiel 6:11](../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">eze 6:11</span></span>

Smite - To shew thy wonder, indignation, sorrow, and pity, for their
sins and sufferings.

[Ezekiel 6:12](../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">eze 6:12</span></span>

Far off - Either by flight, or captivity. Shall fall - Who dwell near to
Jerusalem, or would retire to it, when the Babylonians approach.

[Ezekiel 6:14](../../../bib/kjv/eze006.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">eze 6:14</span></span>

Wilderness - The horrid wilderness of Moab. Therein the fiery serpents
so much annoyed Israel. Accordingly the land of Canaan is at this day
one of the most desolate countries in the world.
