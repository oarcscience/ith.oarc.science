### [Ezekiel Chapter 23](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#001.md)

[Ezekiel 23:2](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">eze 23:2</span></span>

Two women - Judah, and Israel, two kingdoms.

[Ezekiel 23:3](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">eze 23:3</span></span>

Whoredoms - Idolatry.

[Ezekiel 23:4](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">eze 23:4</span></span>

Aholah - That is, his own tabernacle; for Israel falling off from the
house of David, fell off from the tabernacle, or temple of God; so that
all the temple they had was of their own making. The elder - Greater for
number of tribes, and for power, wealth, and for multitudes of people.
Aholibah - That is, my tabernacle in her: the two tribes had the temple
of God with them. Mine - By solemn marriage - covenant. Bare sons - Were
fruitful and brought forth children to me; they increased in numbers of
people; and among these, some there were that were children of God by
faith, love, and obedience.

[Ezekiel 23:5](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">eze 23:5</span></span>

Played the harlot - United in idolatry, with the Assyrians. Mine - When
under my government, and protection.

[Ezekiel 23:6](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">eze 23:6</span></span>

Horsemen - Skillful in riding, and well furnished with choice horses.

[Ezekiel 23:7](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">eze 23:7</span></span>

With all - Other nations, with whom she had commerce.

[Ezekiel 23:10](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">eze 23:10</span></span>

Discovered - Stript her naked, and exposed her to shame. Took her sons -
Captives. Slew her - The kingdom of Israel, under Hoshea, was by
Salmanesar utterly destroyed. They - The Assyrians, had executed God's
just displeasure upon her.

[Ezekiel 23:15](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#015.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">eze 23:15</span></span>

Girded - With soldiers belts, which includes the rest of the habit of
soldiers. In dyed attire - Both rich, comely, large, and of divers
colours. Princes - Of princely aspect and majesty.

[Ezekiel 23:17](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">eze 23:17</span></span>

Alienated - She grew weary of the Chaldeans.

[Ezekiel 23:18](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#018.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">eze 23:18</span></span>

Discovered - Made it appear to all, far and near.

[Ezekiel 23:19](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#019.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">eze 23:19</span></span>

By - Remembering her idolatries in Egypt, her alliance with it in days
past, which she now resolved to act over again.

[Ezekiel 23:20](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">eze 23:20</span></span>

Paramours - The nations, that were confederate with the Egyptians.

[Ezekiel 23:23](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#023.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">eze 23:23</span></span>

Pekod - Pekod is the province between Tigris, and Lycus; in this was old
Nineveh. Shoa - Either Sia in Armenia, or the Sohia, among which were
the Adiabeni, and this contained the middle part of the kingdom of
Babylon. Koa - This bordered upon Media, the inhabitants were called
Kohai, and dwelt about Arbela. And all - All subjects of the Assyrian
monarchy.

[Ezekiel 23:24](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#024.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">eze 23:24</span></span>

And wheels - Lest in their march the carriage wheels should break, a
store of these were provided. An assembly - A mighty confluence of
people. I will set - Give them a power in right of conquest over their
rebels, as well as mine, and I will give them a spirit of judgment to
discern the greatness of this people's sins. Judge - Condemn, and
execute sentence upon thee. According - To their will, power, wrath, and
custom, against rebels; for these are their rules of judgment.

[Ezekiel 23:25](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#025.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">eze 23:25</span></span>

I will set my jealousy - As a jealous provoked husband, I will be as
much against thee as they are. Thy residue - Either the people, who hid
themselves in vaults and cellars, or what the Chaldeans cannot carry
away, all this shall be devoured by fire.

[Ezekiel 23:29](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#029.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_029">eze 23:29</span></span>

Take away - Deprive thee of the comfortable use of all thy labour, which
they will exact of thee in captivity.

[Ezekiel 23:32](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#032.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_032">eze 23:32</span></span>

It - Is large, and contains what will last many years, even 'till the
seventy years be expired.

[Ezekiel 23:34](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#034.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_034">eze 23:34</span></span>

Thou - Shalt stagger with sorrows, that shall intoxicate, and astonish.
Suck it out - The dregs shalt thou drink, and multiply thine own
sorrows. Break the sheards - To suck out what remains. And pluck -
Revenging thyself upon thyself.

[Ezekiel 23:35](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#035.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_035">eze 23:35</span></span>

Bear thou - The guilt, I will impute it, the punishment, I will not
pardon it.

[Ezekiel 23:38](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#038.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_038">eze 23:38</span></span>

In the same day - When they had newly polluted themselves with idolatry
and murder, they thrust into the temple.

[Ezekiel 23:39](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#039.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_039">eze 23:39</span></span>

My house - Nay, these things have been in my house.

[Ezekiel 23:40](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#040.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_040">eze 23:40</span></span>

Wash thyself - After the manner of harlots.

[Ezekiel 23:41](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#041.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_041">eze 23:41</span></span>

Sattest - Prepared to feast them. A stately bed - A magnificent bed, on
which women sat to feast, when men leaned on their sides. Incense -
Offered to their idols.

[Ezekiel 23:42](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#042.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_042">eze 23:42</span></span>

A voice - A shout for joy, that there was a treaty of peace between the
Jews, and the Chaldeans.

[Ezekiel 23:45](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#045.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_045">eze 23:45</span></span>

Righteous men - Men that keep the law of their God.

[Ezekiel 23:46](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#046.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_046">eze 23:46</span></span>

Upon them - Against the Jews, the children of this Aholibah.

[Ezekiel 23:47](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#047.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_047">eze 23:47</span></span>

The company - The Babylonian army.

[Ezekiel 23:48](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#048.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_048">eze 23:48</span></span>

Lewdness - Idolatry. And indeed we do not read of any after their return
out of this captivity.

[Ezekiel 23:49](../../../bib/kjv/eze023.htm#049.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_049">eze 23:49</span></span>

They - The Babylonians. The sins - The guilt of worshipping idols; and
you shall bear the punishment of idolaters.
