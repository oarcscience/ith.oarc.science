*Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible*, by John Wesley, \[1754-65\], at
Intangible Textual Heritage

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### [Isaiah Chapter 3](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#001.md)

[Isaiah 3:2](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">isa 3:2</span></span>

The judge - The civil magistrates. The ancient - Whose wisdom was
increased by long experience.

[Isaiah 3:5](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">isa 3:5</span></span>

Oppressed - By thy command or permission of such childish rulers.

[Isaiah 3:6](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">isa 3:6</span></span>

Thou hast - We are utterly undone, and have neither food nor raiment;
but thou hast something left to support the dignity which we offer to
thee. Under thine hand - To heal it.

[Isaiah 3:7](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">isa 3:7</span></span>

An healer - A repairer of the ruins of the state.

[Isaiah 3:9](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">isa 3:9</span></span>

The shew - Their pride, and wantonness, and impiety m manifestly shews
itself in their very looks. They declare - They act it publickly,
casting off all fear of God and reverence to men. Rewarded - Procured a
fit recompense for their wickedness, even utter ruin.

[Isaiah 3:10](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">isa 3:10</span></span>

Say ye - O ye priests and Levites, that God will be their safeguard and
portion.

[Isaiah 3:12](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#012.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">isa 3:12</span></span>

Women - Weak and effeminate rulers. They - Thy rulers civil and
ecclesiastical.

[Isaiah 3:13](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">isa 3:13</span></span>

Standeth - He will shortly and certainly stand up as a judge, to enquire
into the cause, and to give sentence. To judge - To defend and deliver
them.

[Isaiah 3:14](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">isa 3:14</span></span>

Ancients - The princes or rulers; such were commonly chosen out of those
who were in ripe years. Eaten - Destroyed instead of preserving the
church and commonwealth of Israel. Spoil - The goods which you have
violently taken away from the poor.

[Isaiah 3:16](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">isa 3:16</span></span>

The daughters - The women; (hitherto he reproved the men). A tinkling -
By some ornaments which they wore upon their shoes.

[Isaiah 3:17](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">isa 3:17</span></span>

Secret paths - By giving her into the power of those enemies that shall
strip her of all her raiment.

[Isaiah 3:18](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#018.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">isa 3:18</span></span>

Cauls - It is agreed by all, that this and several words that follow,
were ornaments used in those times. And it is of no concern, exactly to
understand the nature and differences of them. The moon - There were in
ancient times, and at this day there are some ornaments worn, which
carry a manifest resemblance to the moon or half moon.

[Isaiah 3:20](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">isa 3:20</span></span>

Tablets - He seems to mean boxes of perfumes.

[Isaiah 3:21](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#021.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">isa 3:21</span></span>

Nose - jewels - Which were fastened to the head, and hung down upon the
forehead to the beginning of the nose.

[Isaiah 3:22](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#022.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">isa 3:22</span></span>

Pins - Of silver or gold, either used to curl the hair, or fastened and
worn in the hair.

[Isaiah 3:23](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#023.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">isa 3:23</span></span>

Glasses - The looking - glasses, as we call them, tho' in truth they
were not made of glass, but of bright and burnished brass.

[Isaiah 3:24](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#024.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">isa 3:24</span></span>

Girdle - Which were fine and costly, and useful to gird their garments
about them. A rent - Torn and tattered garments. Burning - By the heat
of the sun, to which they are now commonly exposed, from which they used
formerly to guard themselves with the utmost care.

[Isaiah 3:26](../../../bib/kjv/isa003.htm#026.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">isa 3:26</span></span>

Gates - The gates of Zion or Jerusalem, which, by a figure, are said to
lament, to imply the great desolation of the place; that there would be
no people to go out and come in by the gates, as they used to do. Shall
sit - Like a mournful woman bewailing the loss of her husband and
children.
