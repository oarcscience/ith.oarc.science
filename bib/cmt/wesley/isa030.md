### [Isaiah Chapter 30](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#001)

[Isaiah 30:1](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">isa 30:1</span></span>

The rebellious - The Jews. Take counsel - That consult together. Cover -
That seek protection. But not - Not such as by my spirit, speaking in my
word, I have required them to do. That they may add - That unto all
their sins, they may add distrust of my power and mercy, and put
confidence in an arm of flesh.

[Isaiah 30:2](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">isa 30:2</span></span>

Asked - Either by the priests or prophets.

[Isaiah 30:4](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">isa 30:4</span></span>

His princes - The princes of Judah. Hanes - An eminent city of Egypt.

[Isaiah 30:5](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">isa 30:5</span></span>

They - Both the messengers, and they who sent them.

[Isaiah 30:6](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">isa 30:6</span></span>

The burden - The treasures, which were carried upon asses or camels,
into Egypt, which lay southward from Judea. The land of trouble - Egypt,
so called prophetically. From whence - This may be understood properly,
but withal, seems to design the craft and cruelty of that people. They -
The Jews. Their riches - To procure their assistance. Bunches - Upon the
backs.

[Isaiah 30:7](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">isa 30:7</span></span>

To her - To Jerusalem or Judah. Sit still - It is safer and better for
them to sit quietly at home, seeking to me for help.

[Isaiah 30:8](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">isa 30:8</span></span>

Write - This warning. Before - In their presence. Note it - So this was
to be written twice over, once in a table, to be hanged up in some
public place, that all present might read it; and again, in a book, that
it might be kept for the use of posterity. The time to come - As a
witness for me and against them.

[Isaiah 30:11](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">isa 30:11</span></span>

Cause, &c. - Do not trouble us with harsh messages from God.

[Isaiah 30:12](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">isa 30:12</span></span>

And trust - In the wealth which you have gotten by oppression, and in
your perverse course of sending to Egypt for help.

[Isaiah 30:13](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">isa 30:13</span></span>

This iniquity - Of trusting to Egypt, shall be like a wall which is
high, but swelling forth in some parts, which, upon the least accident,
falls down suddenly.

[Isaiah 30:14](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">isa 30:14</span></span>

He - God.

[Isaiah 30:15](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">isa 30:15</span></span>

In returning - To God. Quietness - In sitting still, and quieting your
minds. Confidence - Placed upon me, and my promises.

[Isaiah 30:17](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">isa 30:17</span></span>

'Till - 'Till you be destroyed, and but a few of you left.

[Isaiah 30:18](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#018)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">isa 30:18</span></span>

Wait - Patiently expect your repentance. Exalted - He will work
gloriously. Judgment - Or mercy. That wait - In his way, with faith and
patience.

[Isaiah 30:19](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#019)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">isa 30:19</span></span>

Shall dwell - After a set time, they shall return to Jerusalem, and have
a fixed abode. This was in part accomplished upon their return from
Babylon; but more fully in the times of the gospel, when many of them
were, and the whole body of them shall be brought into Christ's church.

[Isaiah 30:21](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#021)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">isa 30:21</span></span>

Shall hear - Thou shalt hear the voice of God's word and spirit. Behind
thee - A metaphor borrowed from shepherds, who use to follow their
sheep, and recall them when they go out of the way.

[Isaiah 30:22](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">isa 30:22</span></span>

Defile - To shew your contempt of it. Covering - The leaves or plates
wherewith their images were frequently covered. Ornament - It was a
costly and glorious robe.

[Isaiah 30:23](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#023)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">isa 30:23</span></span>

Bread - Which shall be the fruit of thy own land and labour: and
excellent for quality, which is called, fat, [Deu
32:14](../../../bib/kjv/deu032.htm#014), and abundant for quantity.

[Isaiah 30:24](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#024)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">isa 30:24</span></span>

Clean provender - There should be such plenty of corn, that the very
beasts, instead of straw, should eat corn; and that not in the ear, or
with the straw, but the pure grain.

[Isaiah 30:25](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#025)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">isa 30:25</span></span>

Hill - Which is commonly dry and barren. In the day - When God shall
destroy the enemies of his people. The towers - The mighty potentates,
who fought against God's people.

[Isaiah 30:26](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#026)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">isa 30:26</span></span>

Sevenfold - As if the light of seven days were combined together in one.
Healeth - When God shall effectually cure the wounds of his people,
making Israel and Judah to be one, and making Jew and Gentile to be one
fold under one shepherd.

[Isaiah 30:27](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#027)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">isa 30:27</span></span>

Behold - Here he gives them an earnest of those greater mercies in times
to come, by assuring them of the approaching destruction of the Assyrian
forces. The name - The Lord himself. From far - From a remote place:
even from heaven. Heavy - He will inflict heavy judgments upon them.
Indignation - He hath pronounced a severe sentence against them, and
will give command for the execution of it.

[Isaiah 30:28](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#028)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">isa 30:28</span></span>

His breath - God's anger. A stream - Coming from him as vehemently, as a
mighty torrent of waters. To sift - To shake and scatter, as it were
with a sieve. The nations - The Assyrian army, which was made up of
several nations. With - Not with an ordinary sieve, which casteth away
the chaff only, but with a sieve, which should shake them so long and so
vehemently, as to cast away altogether. A bridle - God will over - rule
them by his powerful providence. To err - Whereas other bridles guide
into the right way, this shall turn them out of the way, by giving them
up to their own foolish counsels, which shall bring them to certain
ruin.

[Isaiah 30:29](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#029)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_029">isa 30:29</span></span>

A song - You shall have songs of praise. The night - He seems to have a
particular respect to the solemnity of the passover, in which they spent
some considerable part of the night in rejoicing, and singing psalms
before the Lord. As when - Like the joy of one that is going up to the
solemn feasts with musick.

[Isaiah 30:30](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#030)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_030">isa 30:30</span></span>

His voice - His thunder, metaphorically taken for some terrible
judgment. The lightning - Upon the Assyrian. With - With great wrath;
which is signified by heaping so many words of the same signification
together.

[Isaiah 30:32](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#032)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_032">isa 30:32</span></span>

The rod - Heb. the founded rod, the judgment of God, called a founded
rod, because it was firmly established, by God's immutable purpose. Him
- Upon the Assyrian. With harps - Their destruction shall be celebrated
by God's people, with joy and musick, and songs of praise. Of shaking -
Or, shaking of the hand, of which kind of shaking this Hebrew word is
constantly used. God will fight against them, and destroy them by his
own hand. With it - With the army of the Assyrians.

[Isaiah 30:33](../../../bib/kjv/isa030.htm#033)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_033">isa 30:33</span></span>

Tophet - This was a place near Jerusalem, in which the idolatrous
Israelites used to offer up their children to Moloch. It may be put, for
any place of torment; and particularly it is put for hell. For the king
- For the king of Assyria. Fire - He alludes to the ancient custom, of
burning sacrifices, and particularly of burning children to Moloch. The
breath - The immediate hand of God, or his word of anger. Brimstone - He
seems to allude to that shower of fire and brimstone, [Gen
19:24](../../../bib/kjv/gen019.htm#024).
