*Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible*, by John Wesley, \[1754-65\], at
Intangible Textual Heritage

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### [Psalms Chapter 91](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#001.md)

[Psalms 91:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 91:1</span></span>

He - He that makes God his habitation and refuge.

[Psalms 91:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">psa 91:3</span></span>

Pestilence - From the pestilence, which like a fowler's snare takes men
suddenly and unexpectedly.

[Psalms 91:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">psa 91:5</span></span>

By night - When evil accidents are most terrible and least avoidable.
Arrow - The pestilence, or any such destructive calamity; such are
frequently called God's arrows. By day - Thou shalt be kept from secret
and open mischiefs.

[Psalms 91:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">psa 91:6</span></span>

Darkness - Invisibly, so that we can neither foresee nor prevent it.

[Psalms 91:12](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#012.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">psa 91:12</span></span>

Bear thee - Sustain or uphold thee in thy goings, as we do a child.

[Psalms 91:13](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">psa 91:13</span></span>

The lion - Shall lie prostrate at thy feet, and thou shalt securely put
thy feet upon his neck. Dragon - By which he understands all pernicious
creatures, though never so strong, and all sorts of enemies.

[Psalms 91:14](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">psa 91:14</span></span>

Because - This and the two following verses are the words of God.
