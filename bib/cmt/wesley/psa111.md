*Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible*, by John Wesley, \[1754-65\], at
Intangible Textual Heritage

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### [Psalms Chapter 111](../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#001.md)

[Psalms 111:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">psa 111:2</span></span>

Sought - Diligently meditated upon.

[Psalms 111:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">psa 111:3</span></span>

Work - Either all his works, or that eminent branch of those works, his
providence towards his people. Righteousness - His justice or
faithfulness in performing his word.

[Psalms 111:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 111:4</span></span>

Remembered - By their own nature, and the lasting benefits flowing from
them, which are such as cannot easily be forgotten.

[Psalms 111:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">psa 111:5</span></span>

Meat - All necessary provisions for their being and well - being.

[Psalms 111:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">psa 111:7</span></span>

The works - All that he doth on the behalf of his people, or against
their enemies. Truth - Are exactly agreeable to his promises, and to
justice. Commandments - His laws given to the Israelites, especially the
moral law. Sure - Constant and unchangeable.

[Psalms 111:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#008.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">psa 111:8</span></span>

Done - Constituted or ordered.

[Psalms 111:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">psa 111:9</span></span>

Redemption - The deliverance out of Egypt, which was a type of that
higher redemption by Christ. Commanded - Appointed, or established
firmly by his power and authority. For ever - Through all successive
generations of his people to the end of the world. Reverend - Terrible
to his enemies, venerable in his peoples eyes, and holy in all his
dealings with all men.

[Psalms 111:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa111.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">psa 111:10</span></span>

The fear - True religion. Beginning - Is the only foundation of, and
introduction to, true wisdom.
