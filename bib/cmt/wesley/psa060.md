*Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible*, by John Wesley, \[1754-65\], at

### [Psalms Chapter 60](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#001)

[Psalms 60:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">psa 60:2</span></span>

Tremble - A poetical expression, signifying great changes among the
people.

[Psalms 60:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">psa 60:3</span></span>

To drink - Thou hast filled us with no less honor, than men intoxicated
with strong drink.

[Psalms 60:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 60:4</span></span>

A banner - Which is a sign and instrument, Of union. This people who
were lately divided, thou hast united under one banner, under my
government: Of battle. Thou hast given us an army, and power to oppose
our enemies; which blessing God gave to Israel, for the sake of those
few sincere Israelites who were among them. The truth - Not for any
merit of ours, but to shew thy faithfulness in making good thy promises.

[Psalms 60:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">psa 60:5</span></span>

Beloved - Thy beloved people.

[Psalms 60:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">psa 60:6</span></span>

Rejoice - Therefore I will turn my prayers into praises, for what God
has already done. Divide - Which supposeth possession and dominion.
Shechem - A place within Jordan, in mount Ephraim. Succoth - A place
without Jordan. He mentions Shechem, and Succoth; for all the land of
Canaan, within and without Jordan.

[Psalms 60:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">psa 60:7</span></span>

Gilead - All the land beyond Jordan, which was possessed by Reuben and
Gad, and half of the tribe of Manasseh. Manasseh - The other half of
that tribe within Jordan. The strength - A chief part of my strength,
either to offend mine enemies, or to defend myself. For this tribe was
very numerous, and valiant and rich. Law - giver - The chief seat of my
throne and kingdom, and of the inferior throne of judgment, [Psa
122:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa122.htm#005).

[Psalms 60:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">psa 60:8</span></span>

Wash - pot - In which I shall wash my feet. I shall bring them into the
lowest degree of servitude. Shoe - I will use them like slaves; a
proverbial expression. Triumph - It is an ironical expression,
signifying that her triumphs were come to an end.

[Psalms 60:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">psa 60:9</span></span>

Who - None can do it but God. City - The cities; the singular number for
the plural. Having beaten his enemies out of the field, he desires God's
assistance to take their strong - holds, and so secure himself from
farther attempts. Edom - Which was an high and rocky country, [Oba
1:1-3](../../../bib/kjv/oba001.htm#001), fortified by nature, as well as
by art, and therefore not to be subdued without a Divine hand.

[Psalms 60:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa060.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">psa 60:10</span></span>

Hadst cut off - But now hast graciously returned to us.
