*Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible*, by John Wesley, \[1754-65\], at
Intangible Textual Heritage

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### [Ecclesiastes Chapter 10](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#001.md)

[Ecclesiastes 10:2](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">ecc 10:2</span></span>

Heart - His understanding is always present with him and ready to direct
him. He mentions the right hand, because that is the common instrument
of action. A fool's - His understanding is not effectual to govern his
affections and actions.

[Ecclesiastes 10:3](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">ecc 10:3</span></span>

Walketh - In his daily conversation. He saith - He discovers his folly
to all that meet him.

[Ecclesiastes 10:4](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">ecc 10:4</span></span>

The spirit - The passion. Leave not - In anger or discontent. Continue
in a diligent and faithful discharge of thy duty, and modestly and
humbly submit to him. Yielding - A gentle and submissive carriage.

[Ecclesiastes 10:6](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">ecc 10:6</span></span>

The rich - Wise and worthy men, rich in endowments of mind.

[Ecclesiastes 10:8](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#008.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">ecc 10:8</span></span>

An hedge - Whereby another man's fields or vineyards are distinguished,
that he may either take away their fruits, or enlarge his own fields.

[Ecclesiastes 10:9](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">ecc 10:9</span></span>

Whoso removeth - Stones too heavy for them: who rashly attempts things
too high and hard for them.

[Ecclesiastes 10:10](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">ecc 10:10</span></span>

Wisdom - As wisdom instructs a man in the smallest matters, so it is
useful for a man's direction in all weighty affairs.

[Ecclesiastes 10:11](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">ecc 10:11</span></span>

Without - If not prevented by the art and care of the charmer; which
practice he does not justify, but only mentions by way of resemblance.

[Ecclesiastes 10:12](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#012.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">ecc 10:12</span></span>

Gracious - Procure him favour with those who hear him.

[Ecclesiastes 10:14](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">ecc 10:14</span></span>

Full of words - Forward to promise and boast what he will do, whereas
none can be sure of future events, even during his own life, much more
after his death.

[Ecclesiastes 10:15](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#015.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">ecc 10:15</span></span>

Wearieth - Fools discover their folly by their wearisome and fruitless
endeavours after things which are too high for them. Because - He is
ignorant of those things which are most easy, as of the way to the great
city whither he is going.

[Ecclesiastes 10:16](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">ecc 10:16</span></span>

A child - Either in age, or childish qualities. Eat - Give up themselves
to eating and drinking. Morning - The fittest time for God's service,
for the dispatch of weighty affairs, and for sitting in judgment.

[Ecclesiastes 10:17](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">ecc 10:17</span></span>

Nobles - Not so much by birth, as by their noble dispositions.

[Ecclesiastes 10:20](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">ecc 10:20</span></span>

Thy thought - In the most secret manner. The rich - Princes or
governors. A bird - The king will hear of it by unknown and unsuspected
hands, as if a bird had heard and carried the report of it.
