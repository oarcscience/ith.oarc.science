### [Ezekiel Chapter 29](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#001.md)

[Ezekiel 29:1](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">eze 29:1</span></span>

The tenth year - Of Jeconiah's captivity.

[Ezekiel 29:3](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">eze 29:3</span></span>

The great dragon - The crocodile; our prophet, as well as Isaiah,
compares the Egyptian king to that devouring serpent, or dragon. That
lieth - Not only at rest, but waiting for prey. My river - My kingdom,
power, riches, and forces, all the strength and glory of Egypt.

[Ezekiel 29:4](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">eze 29:4</span></span>

Put hooks - The Allegory is continued. The fish - The people of Egypt.
To stick - To adhere to their king.

[Ezekiel 29:5](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">eze 29:5</span></span>

Leave thee - When thus brought out, I will leave thee. The wilderness -
The deserts of Libya and Syene. All the fish - The whole army of the
Egyptians. The open fields - There was this king and his army ruined.
Gathered - These were not buried, but left in the wilderness, a prey to
wild beasts, and birds.

[Ezekiel 29:7](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">eze 29:7</span></span>

Rent - Didst them much mischief instead of benefiting them, as thou hast
promised, [Jer 37:7](../../../bib/kjv/jer037.htm#007.md).

[Ezekiel 29:10](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">eze 29:10</span></span>

Syene - Boundary between Ethiopia and Egypt; that is, all Egypt from
north - east to south - west.

[Ezekiel 29:11](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">eze 29:11</span></span>

Forty years - These forty years began about the thirtieth year of
Jeconiah's captivity, and end with the seventieth year of the captivity,
which was the first of Cyrus.

[Ezekiel 29:14](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">eze 29:14</span></span>

Pathros - The southern part of Egypt, in which was the famous city
Thebae, known for its hundred gates. Their habitation - The ancient
habitation of their fathers. A base - A low, tributary, dependent
kingdom.

[Ezekiel 29:15](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#015.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">eze 29:15</span></span>

No more rule - Though in the times of the Ptolemeys, it was
considerable, yet then, even then it did not rule the nations about her.

[Ezekiel 29:16](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">eze 29:16</span></span>

Which - Which sinful reliance on the arm of flesh provoked God to call
to mind their other iniquities. When - When they forgot God, and
respected Egypt. They - The house of Israel.

[Ezekiel 29:17](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">eze 29:17</span></span>

In the seven and twentieth year - Of Jeconiah's captivity, the year
after the conquest of Tyre.

[Ezekiel 29:18](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#018.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">eze 29:18</span></span>

Caused - The army, and commanders were weary of the siege, but the
immovable resolution of the king kept them on. A great service - It was
service to the justice of God. It was great service both for hardness of
work, heaviness of burdens, and length of the siege, thirteen years
together. Made bald - Through age, or sicknesses, or continued wearing
of helmets. Peeled - Galled with carrying burdens. No wages - For though
Tyre was very rich, when first besieged, much wealth was carried away
during the siege, much spent and wasted in the siege, and what was left,
preserved by articles of surrender.

[Ezekiel 29:19](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#019.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">eze 29:19</span></span>

Her multitude - Common people, who shall be made captives, and servants
or slaves. Her prey - What she had before taken from others. The wages -
God will be behind - hand with none, who do any service for him; one way
or other he will recompence them. None shall kindle a fire at his altar
for nought.

[Ezekiel 29:20](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">eze 29:20</span></span>

They - The Babylonians. For me - God's work was doing by them, tho' they
thought nothing less.

[Ezekiel 29:21](../../../bib/kjv/eze029.htm#021.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">eze 29:21</span></span>

The horn - Jehoiakim, who was then advanced by Evil - Merodach. The
opening of the mouth - Thou shalt have liberty, to open thy mouth in
comforting the good among them, and to give praise to God.
