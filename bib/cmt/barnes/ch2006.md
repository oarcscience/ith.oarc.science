### [2 Chronicles Chapter 6](../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#001)

[2 Chronicles 6:1](../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">ch2 6:1</span></span>

Compare Kings (marginal references).

Compare Kings (marginal references).

[2 Chronicles 6:40](../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#040)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_040">ch2 6:40</span></span>

In Kings, a different conclusion takes the place of these verses. The
document from which both writers copied contained the full prayer of
dedication, which each givcs in a somewhat abbreviated form.

[Ch2 6:41](../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#041)

Thy resting place - i. e., the holy of holies. Solomon follows closely
the words of David his father, spoken probably when he brought the ark
into Jerusalem. See the marginal references.

[Ch2 6:42](../../../bib/kjv/ch2006.htm#042)

Turn not away the face of thine anointed - i. e., make him not to hide
his face through shame at having his prayers rejected (compare the [Kg1
2:16](../../../bib/kjv/kg1002.htm#016) note).

The mercies of David - i. e., "God's mercies toward David."
