### [1 Chronicles Chapter 17](../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#001)

[1 Chronicles 17:1](../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">ch1 17:1</span></span>

Compare throughout 2 Sam. 7 and the notes found there.

[1 Chronicles 17:13](../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">ch1 17:13</span></span>

My son - The minatory clause which occurs after this in Samuel is here
omitted, because the writer is not about to record the sins of Solomon,
or the sufferings 1 Kings 11:9-40 which he thereby brought upon himself.

[1 Chronicles 17:17](../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">ch1 17:17</span></span>

Hast regarded me ... - i. e., "Thou hast elevated me above other men, by
making my kingdom perpetual, regarding me as if I were a man of high
degree." Compare the [Sa2 7:19](../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#019) note.

[1 Chronicles 17:18](../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#018)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">ch1 17:18</span></span>

For the honor of thy servant - i. e., "for the honor which Thou hast
done for Thy servant." The Septuagint omits "Thy servant," and renders
it: "What can David say more to Thee to glorify Thee? For Thou knowest,"
etc.

[1 Chronicles 17:24](../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#024)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">ch1 17:24</span></span>

Some prefer, "And let Thy name be established and magnified forever:" i.
e., "Let not only Thy promise stand firm, but let Thy Name also stand
firm (continue to be held in honor) and be magnified," etc.

[1 Chronicles 17:27](../../../bib/kjv/ch1017.htm#027)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">ch1 17:27</span></span>

The marginal rendering is preferable.
