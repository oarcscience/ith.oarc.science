### [1 Chronicles Chapter 14](../../../bib/kjv/ch1014.htm#001.md)

[1 Chronicles 14:1](../../../bib/kjv/ch1014.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">ch1 14:1</span></span>

Compare [Sa2 5:11-25](../../../bib/kjv/sa2005.htm#011.md), the only
important variations from which are in [Ch1
14:4-7](../../../bib/kjv/ch1014.htm#004.md), the list of the sons of David
(see [Ch1 3:1](../../../bib/kjv/ch1003.htm#001) note.md), and in [Ch1
14:12](../../../bib/kjv/ch1014.htm#012.md), where the fact is added that
the idols taken from the Philistines were burned.

[1 Chronicles 14:12](../../../bib/kjv/ch1014.htm#012.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">ch1 14:12</span></span>

When they had left their gods there - The practice of carrying images of
the gods to battle was common among the nations of antiquity, and arose
from the belief that there was virtue in the images themselves, and that
military success would be obtained by means of them.
