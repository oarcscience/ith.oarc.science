Albert Barnes (b. 1798, d. 24 Dec. 1870) was an American theologian who
graduated Hamilton College, Clinton, New York in 1820, and Princeton
Theological Seminary in 1823. He was ordained in 1825 as a Presbyterian
minister. Born in Rome, New York, Barnes was pastor of the Presbyterian
Church in Morristown, New Jersey from 1825-30, and the First
Presbyterian Church of Philadelphia from 1830-67.

On one hand Barnes became known for his role in the Old School-New
School controversy, during which he was accused, but not convicted of
heresy. On the other, he was known for his very popular Bible
commentaries, which are presented here on the web. This massive text is
nearly five times the size of KJV, with which it is linked.
