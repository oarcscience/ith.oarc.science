### [Luke Chapter 8](../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#001.md)

[Luke](../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">luk 8:0</span></span>

SUMMARY.--Women Ministering to Christ. The Parable of the Sower. That of
a Candle Under a Bed. His Mother and Brethren. The Storm on the Sea. In
the Country of the Gadarenes. The Devils and the Swine. The Daughter of
Jairus Raised. The Woman with the Bloody Issue Healed.

[Luke 8:1](../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">luk 8:1</span></span>

He went throughout every city and village. A thorough missionary tour of
Galilee.

[Luke 8:2](../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">luk 8:2</span></span>

And certain women. That these women should attend the footsteps of
Christ was opposed to the custom of Palestine. The admixture of the
sexes was not common. The rabbis held that the law should not be taught
to women.

Mary that was called Magdalene. That is, of Magdala, a village near the
Sea of Galilee. This is the first mention of her and we know nothing
more of her preceding history. We know that after this she was one of
the most devoted friends of Christ.

Seven devils. For notes on demoniac possession see [Mat
8:29](../../../bib/kjv/mat008.htm#029.md).

[Luke 8:3](../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">luk 8:3</span></span>

Joanna the wife of Chuza. Nothing more is known of her. As her husband
held a very responsible position, she must have been a woman of wealth
and influence.

Herod. Herod Antipas. See notes on [Mat
2:1](../../../bib/kjv/mat002.htm#001.md).

Susanna. Not named elsewhere.

Ministered unto him. Contributed to his support. They used their means
to support Jesus and the apostles while preaching.

[Luke 8:4](../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">luk 8:4</span></span>

A sower went forth to sow. See notes on Mat. 13:3-23; [Mar
4:1](../../../bib/kjv/mar004.htm#001.md).

[Luke 8:16](../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">luk 8:16</span></span>

When he hath lighted a candle. See notes on [Mat
5:15](../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#015.md) and [Mar
4:21-25](../../../bib/kjv/mar004.htm#021.md). Mark says, "Take heed what
you hear;" Luke, "how you hear." What we hear depends on how we hear; i.
e., our attention and honesty.

[Luke 8:19](../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#019.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">luk 8:19</span></span>

Then came to him his mother. See notes on [Mat
12:46-50](../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#046.md); [Mar
3:21](../../../bib/kjv/mar003.htm#021.md).

[Luke 8:22](../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#022.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">luk 8:22</span></span>

For notes on Stilling the Tempest, see [Mat
8:23-27](../../../bib/kjv/mat008.htm#023.md) and [Mar
4:35-41](../../../bib/kjv/mar004.htm#035.md).

[Luke 8:26](../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#026.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">luk 8:26</span></span>

A certain man who had devils. For notes on the cure of the Gadarene
demoniac, see [Mat 8:28-34](../../../bib/kjv/mat008.htm#028.md); Mark
5:1-21.

[Luke 8:40](../../../bib/kjv/luk008.htm#040.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_040">luk 8:40</span></span>

A man named Jairus. For the miracles of raising from the dead the
daughter of Jairus, and the healing of the woman with the bloody issue,
see [Mat 9:18-26](../../../bib/kjv/mat009.htm#018.md) and the notes; also
Mark 5:22-42. This was the second instance of the Lord raising the dead.
