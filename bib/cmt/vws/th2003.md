### [2 Thessalonians Chapter 3](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#001)

[2 Thessalonians 3:1](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">th2 3:1</span></span>

Finally (τὸ λοιπὸν)

See on [Th1 4:1](../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#001).

May have free course (τρέχῃ)

More literally, simply, and better, may run. Have swift progress through
the world. An O.T. idea. See [Psa
147:15](../../../bib/kjv/psa147.htm#015), and comp. [Isa
55:11](../../../bib/kjv/isa055.htm#011) and [Act
12:24](../../../bib/kjv/act012.htm#024).

Be glorified (δοξάζηται)

Acknowledged in its true power and glory. Comp. [Joh
12:28](../../../bib/kjv/joh012.htm#028). The phrase the word of the Lord
- be glorified, only here.

[2 Thessalonians 3:2](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">th2 3:2</span></span>

Unreasonable (ἀτόπων)

See on [Luk 23:41](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#041), and comp. [Act
25:5](../../../bib/kjv/act025.htm#005); [Act
28:6](../../../bib/kjv/act028.htm#006). In lxx in a moral sense,
iniquitous, [Job 4:8](../../../bib/kjv/job004.htm#008); [Job
11:11](../../../bib/kjv/job011.htm#011); [Job
34:12](../../../bib/kjv/job034.htm#012). The word originally means out
of place.

All men have not faith

See on [Act 6:7](../../../bib/kjv/act006.htm#007); see on [Gal
1:23](../../../bib/kjv/gal001.htm#023).

[2 Thessalonians 3:3](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">th2 3:3</span></span>

From evil (ἀπὸ τοῦ πονηροῦ)

Possibly, from the evil one. Τὸ πονηρόν evil is found [Rom
12:9](../../../bib/kjv/rom012.htm#009); [Mat
5:39](../../../bib/kjv/mat005.htm#039); but general N.T. usage favors
the masculine, personal sense. See [Mat
13:19](../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#019), [Mat
13:38](../../../bib/kjv/mat013.htm#038); [Eph
6:16](../../../bib/kjv/eph006.htm#016); [Jo1
2:13](../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#013), [Jo1
2:14](../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#014); [Jo1
3:12](../../../bib/kjv/jo1003.htm#012); [Jo1
5:18](../../../bib/kjv/jo1005.htm#018). In lxx, τὸ πονηρόν evil is very
common: ὁ πονηρὸς a few times, but always of men. See [Deu
24:7](../../../bib/kjv/deu024.htm#007); [Est
7:6](../../../bib/kjv/est007.htm#006); [Job
21:30](../../../bib/kjv/job021.htm#030). In Tob 3:8, 17, τὸ πονηρόν
δαιμόνιον the wicked demon. The masculine is favored by the Jewish
formularies, of which traces appear in the Lord's prayer; by the
unanimous tradition of Greek interpreters; by the interpretations of
Tertullian and Cyprian, and by the evidence of the Syriac and Sahidic
Versions.

[2 Thessalonians 3:5](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">th2 3:5</span></span>

Hearts (καρδίας)

See on [Rom 1:21](../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#021); see on [Rom
10:10](../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#010); see on [Eph
1:18](../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#018).

Patient waiting for Christ (ὑπομονὴν τοῦ χριστοῦ)

Rather patience of Christ. The prayer is that their hearts may be
directed to love God and to exhibit the patience of Christ.

[2 Thessalonians 3:6](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">th2 3:6</span></span>

Withdraw yourselves from (στέλλεσθαι ὑμᾶς ἀπὸ)

Στέλλεσθαι, Po. In the active voice, to place, arrange, equip: in the
middle voice, to provide for, take care. See [Co2
8:20](../../../bib/kjv/co2008.htm#020). Here with ἀπὸ from, to place
one's self away from.

Disorderly (ἀτάκτως)

This adverb, the verb ἀτακτέω, and the adjective ἄτακτος are found only
in Paul, and only in the Thessalonian Epistles. See on [Th1
5:14](../../../bib/kjv/th1005.htm#014).

[2 Thessalonians 3:7](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">th2 3:7</span></span>

Follow (μιμεῖσθαι)

Better, imitate. Comp. [Co1 4:16](../../../bib/kjv/co1004.htm#016); [Co1
11:1](../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#001); [Phi
3:17](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#017); [Th1
1:6](../../../bib/kjv/th1001.htm#006).

[2 Thessalonians 3:8](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">th2 3:8</span></span>

Any man's bread (ἄρτον παρά τινος)

Lit. bread from any one, or at any man's hand.

For nought (δωρεὰν)

The word is a noun, meaning a gift. See [Joh
4:10](../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#010); [Act
2:38](../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#038); [Rom
5:15](../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#015). The accusative often adverbially
as here; as a gift, gratis. Comp. [Mat
10:8](../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#008); [Rom
3:24](../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#024); [Rev
21:6](../../../bib/kjv/rev021.htm#006).

Labor and travail

See on [Th1 1:3](../../../bib/kjv/th1001.htm#003).

Be chargeable (ἐπιβαρῆσαι)

Po. Better, burden. By depending upon them for pecuniary support. Comp.
1 Corinthians 9:3-18, and see on [Th1
2:6](../../../bib/kjv/th1002.htm#006).

[2 Thessalonians 3:9](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">th2 3:9</span></span>

Power (ἐξουσίαν)

Better, right. See on [Mar 2:10](../../../bib/kjv/mar002.htm#010); see
on [Joh 1:12](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012).

[2 Thessalonians 3:10](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">th2 3:10</span></span>

If any would not work, etc.

A Jewish proverb.

[2 Thessalonians 3:11](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">th2 3:11</span></span>

Working not at all - busybodies (μηδὲν ἐργαζομένους - περιεργαζομένους)

One of Paul's frequent wordplays. See on reprobate mind, [Rom
1:28](../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#028). Not busy, but busybodies.
Περιεργάζεσθαι (N.T.o.) is to bustle about a thing: here, to be
officious in others' affairs. See on τὰ περίεργα curious arts, [Act
19:19](../../../bib/kjv/act019.htm#019), and see on [Ti1
5:13](../../../bib/kjv/ti1005.htm#013).

[2 Thessalonians 3:12](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">th2 3:12</span></span>

With quietness - work

See on study to be quiet, [Th1 4:11](../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#011).

[2 Thessalonians 3:13](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">th2 3:13</span></span>

Be not weary (ἐντραπῇ)

With one exception, [Luk 13:1](../../../bib/kjv/luk013.htm#001), only in
Paul. To faint or lose heart.

Well doing (καλοποιοῦντες)

N.T.o. According to the Greek idiom, doing well, be not weary. Not
limited to works of charity, but including Christian conduct generally,
as, for instance, steadily attending to their own business, [Th2
3:12](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#012).

[2 Thessalonians 3:14](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">th2 3:14</span></span>

By this epistle

Connect with our word. The message we send in this letter. Not, as some,
with the following words, note that man in your epistle.

Note (σημειοῦσθε)

N.T.o. Lit. set a mark on. The nature of the mark is indicated in the
next clause.

Have no company with (μὴ συναναμίγνυσθαι)

Po. See on [Co1 5:9](../../../bib/kjv/co1005.htm#009).

Be ashamed (ἐντραπῇ)

See on [Mat 21:37](../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#037), and see on [Co1
4:14](../../../bib/kjv/co1004.htm#014).

[2 Thessalonians 3:15](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">th2 3:15</span></span>

Admonish (νουθετεῖτε)

See on [Act 20:31](../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#031), and see on [Eph
6:4](../../../bib/kjv/eph006.htm#004).

[2 Thessalonians 3:16](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">th2 3:16</span></span>

The Lord of peace (ὁ κύριος τῆς εἰρήνης)

The only instance of the formula.

By all means (ἐν παντὶ τρόπῳ)

Or in every way. The alternative reading τόπῳ place is rejected by the
principal texts.

[2 Thessalonians 3:17](../../../bib/kjv/th2003.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">th2 3:17</span></span>

The salutation of Paul with mine own hand (ἀσπασμὸς τῇ ἐμῇ χειρὶ Παύλου)

Rev. properly, "the salutation of me Paul." The genitive of me is
contained, according to a familiar Greek idiom, in the possessive
pronoun my. Paul had apparently been employing an amanuensis.

In every epistle

Comp. [Co1 16:21](../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#021); [Col
4:18](../../../bib/kjv/col004.htm#018).
