### [Luke Chapter 20](../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#001.md)

[Luke 20:5](../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">luk 20:5</span></span>

They reasoned (συνελογίσαντο)

Only here in New Testament. The preposition, σύν, together, and the
additional with themselves, denote a very close conference.

[Luke 20:6](../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">luk 20:6</span></span>

Will stone (καταλιθάσει)

Only here in New Testament. "Stone us down" (κατά); i.e., stone us to
death.

They be persuaded (πεπεισμένος ἐστιν)

Lit., It (the people collectively) is hawing been persuaded. Denoting a
long-standing and settled persuasion.

[Luke 20:13](../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">luk 20:13</span></span>

It may be (ἴσως)

Only here in New Testament. The adverb of ἴσος, equal. It expresses more
than perhaps, implying rather a strong probability. Compare the phrase,
it is an even chance that.

[Luke 20:20](../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">luk 20:20</span></span>

Watched

See on [Mar 3:2](../../../bib/kjv/mar003.htm#002.md).

Spies (ἐγκαθέτους)

Only here in New Testament. From ἐγκαθίμηι, to send in, as a garrison
into a city. Hence of persons sent in for the purpose of espionage.

Which should feign (ὑποκρινομένους)

Lit., feigning. Rev., which feigned. Only here in New Testament. See on
hypocrites, [Mat 23:13](../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#013.md).

The power and authority (τῇ ἀρχῇ καὶ τῇ ἐξουσίᾳ)

The former, the Roman power in general; the latter, the specific
authority of the official.

[Luke 20:22](../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#022.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">luk 20:22</span></span>

Tribute (φόρον)

From φέρω, to bring. Something, therefore, which is brought in by way of
payment. Luke uses the Greek word instead of the Latin κῆνσον, census,
in Matthew and Mark.

[Luke 20:23](../../../bib/kjv/luk020.htm#023.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">luk 20:23</span></span>

Perceived

See on considerest, [Mat 7:3](../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#003.md).

Craftiness (πανουργίαν)

From πᾶν, every, and ἔργον, deed. Readiness for every and any deed.
Hence unscrupulousness, and so, generally, knavery.
