### [3 John Introduction](../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#001)

[3 John](../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">jo3 0:0</span></span>

This epistle is addressed to a converted Gentile. The scope is to
commend his stedfastness in the faith, and his hospitality, especially
to the ministers of Christ.

The apostle commends Gaius for piety and hospitality. ([Jo2
1:1-8](../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#001)) Cautions him against siding
with Diotrephes, who was a turbulent spirit; but recommends Demetrius as
a man of excellent character. ([Jo2
1:9-12](../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#009)) He hopes soon to see Gaius.
([Jo2 1:13](../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#013), [Jo2
1:1:14](../../../bib/kjv/jo2001.htm#001))
