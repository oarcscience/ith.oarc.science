### [Proverbs Chapter 17](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#001.md)

[Proverbs 17:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#001.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">pro 17:1</span></span>

These words recommend family love and peace, as needful for the comfort
of human life.

[Proverbs 17:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#002.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pro 17:2</span></span>

The wise servant is more deserving, and more likely to appear one of the
family, than a profligate son.

[Proverbs 17:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#003.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">pro 17:3</span></span>

God tries the heart by affliction. He thus has often shown the sin
remaining in the heart of the believer.

[Proverbs 17:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#004.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">pro 17:4</span></span>

Flatterers, especially false teachers, are welcome to those that live in
sin.

[Proverbs 17:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#005.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">pro 17:5</span></span>

Those that laugh at poverty, treat God's providence and precepts with
contempt.

[Proverbs 17:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#006.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">pro 17:6</span></span>

It is an honour to children to have wise and godly parents continued to
them, even after they are grown up and settled in the world.

[Proverbs 17:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#007.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">pro 17:7</span></span>

A fool, in Solomon's Proverbs, signifies a wicked man, whom excellent
speech does not become, because his conversation contradicts it.

[Proverbs 17:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#008.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">pro 17:8</span></span>

Those who set their hearts upon money, will do any thing for it. What
influence should the gifts of God have on our hearts!

[Proverbs 17:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#009.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">pro 17:9</span></span>

The way to preserve peace is to make the best of every thing; not to
notice what has been said or done against ourselves.

[Proverbs 17:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#010.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">pro 17:10</span></span>

A gentle reproof will enter, not only into the head, but into the heart
of a wise man.

[Proverbs 17:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#011.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 17:11</span></span>

Satan, and the messengers of Satan, shall be let loose upon an evil man.

[Proverbs 17:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#012.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">pro 17:12</span></span>

Let us watch over our own passions, and avoid the company of furious
men.

[Proverbs 17:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#013.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">pro 17:13</span></span>

To render evil for good is devilish. He that does so, brings a curse
upon his family.

[Proverbs 17:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#014.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">pro 17:14</span></span>

What danger there is in the beginning of strife! Resist its earliest
display; and leave it off, if it were possible, before you begin.

[Proverbs 17:15](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#015.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">pro 17:15</span></span>

It is an offence to God to acquit the guilty, or to condemn those who
are not guilty.

[Proverbs 17:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#016.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 17:16</span></span>

Man's neglect of God's favour and his own interest is very absurd.

[Proverbs 17:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#017.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">pro 17:17</span></span>

No change of outward circumstances should abate our affection for our
friends or relatives. But no friend, except Christ, deserves unlimited
confidence. In Him this text did receive, and still receives its most
glorious fulfilment.

[Proverbs 17:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#018.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">pro 17:18</span></span>

Let not any wrong their families. Yet Christ's becoming Surety for men,
was a glorious display of Divine wisdom; for he was able to discharge
the bond.

[Proverbs 17:19](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#019.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">pro 17:19</span></span>

If we would keep a clear conscience and a quiet mind, we must shun all
excitements to anger. And a man who affects a style of living above his
means, goes the way to ruin.

[Proverbs 17:20](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#020.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">pro 17:20</span></span>

There is nothing got by ill designs. And many have paid dear for an
unbridled tongue.

[Proverbs 17:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#021.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">pro 17:21</span></span>

This speaks very plainly what many wise and good men feel very strongly,
how grievous it is to have a foolish, wicked child.

[Proverbs 17:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#022.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">pro 17:22</span></span>

It is great mercy that God gives us leave to be cheerful, and cause to
be cheerful, if by his grace he gives us hearts to be cheerful.

[Proverbs 17:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#023.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">pro 17:23</span></span>

The wicked are ready to part with their money, though loved, that they
may not suffer for their crimes.

[Proverbs 17:24](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#024.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">pro 17:24</span></span>

The prudent man keeps the word of God continually in view. But the
foolish man cannot fix his thoughts, nor pursue any purpose with
steadiness.

[Proverbs 17:25](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#025.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">pro 17:25</span></span>

Wicked children despise the authority of their father, and the
tenderness of their mother.

[Proverbs 17:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#026.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">pro 17:26</span></span>

It is very wrong to find fault for doing what is duty.

[Proverbs 17:27](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#027.md)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">pro 17:27</span></span>

A man may show himself to be a wise man, by the good temper of his mind,
and by the good government of his tongue. He is careful when he does
speak, to speak to the purpose. God knows his heart, and the folly that
is bound there; therefore he cannot be deceived in his judgment as men
may be.
