### Psalms 60

[KJV](../kjv/psa060)

1  To the chief Musician<sup>[5329](h5329)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> Shushan-eduth,<sup>[7802](h7802)</sup>
Michtam<sup>[4387](h4387)</sup> of David,<sup>[1732](h1732)</sup> to
teach;<sup>[3925](h3925)</sup> when he strove
with<sup>[5327](h5327)</sup> <sup>[853](h0853)</sup>
Aram-naharaim<sup>[763](h0763)</sup> and with
Aram-zobah,<sup>[760](h0760)</sup> when Joab<sup>[3097](h3097)</sup>
returned,<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> and smote<sup>[5221](h5221)</sup> of
<sup>[853](h0853)</sup> Edom<sup>[123](h0123)</sup> in the
valley<sup>[1516](h1516)</sup> of salt<sup>[4417](h4417)</sup>
twelve<sup>[8147](h8147)</sup> <sup>[6240](h6240)</sup>
thousand.<sup>[505](h0505)</sup> O God,<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> thou hast
cast us off,<sup>[2186](h2186)</sup> thou hast
scattered<sup>[6555](h6555)</sup> us, thou hast been
displeased;<sup>[599](h0599)</sup> O turn<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup>
thyself to us again.

2  Thou hast made the earth<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> to
tremble;<sup>[7493](h7493)</sup> thou hast
broken<sup>[6480](h6480)</sup> it: heal<sup>[7495](h7495)</sup> the
breaches<sup>[7667](h7667)</sup> thereof; for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> it
shaketh.<sup>[4131](h4131)</sup>

3  Thou hast showed<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> thy
people<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup> hard<sup>[7186](h7186)</sup> things: thou
hast made us to drink<sup>[8248](h8248)</sup> the
wine<sup>[3196](h3196)</sup> of astonishment.<sup>[8653](h8653)</sup>

4  Thou hast given<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> a
banner<sup>[5251](h5251)</sup> to them that fear<sup>[3373](h3373)</sup>
thee, that it may be displayed<sup>[5127](h5127)</sup>
because<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> of the
truth.<sup>[7189](h7189)</sup> Selah.<sup>[5542](h5542)</sup>

5  That<sup>[4616](h4616)</sup> thy beloved<sup>[3039](h3039)</sup> may
be delivered;<sup>[2502](h2502)</sup> save<sup>[3467](h3467)</sup> with
thy right hand,<sup>[3225](h3225)</sup> and hear<sup>[6030](h6030)</sup>
me.

6  God<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> hath spoken<sup>[1696](h1696)</sup> in his
holiness;<sup>[6944](h6944)</sup> I will
rejoice,<sup>[5937](h5937)</sup> I will divide<sup>[2505](h2505)</sup>
Shechem,<sup>[7927](h7927)</sup> and mete out<sup>[4058](h4058)</sup>
the valley<sup>[6010](h6010)</sup> of Succoth.<sup>[5523](h5523)</sup>

7  Gilead<sup>[1568](h1568)</sup> is mine, and
Manasseh<sup>[4519](h4519)</sup> is mine; Ephraim<sup>[669](h0669)</sup>
also is the strength<sup>[4581](h4581)</sup> of mine
head;<sup>[7218](h7218)</sup> Judah<sup>[3063](h3063)</sup> is my
lawgiver;<sup>[2710](h2710)</sup>

8  Moab<sup>[4124](h4124)</sup> is my washpot;<sup>[5518](h5518)</sup>
<sup>[7366](h7366)</sup> over<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup>
Edom<sup>[123](h0123)</sup> will I cast out<sup>[7993](h7993)</sup> my
shoe:<sup>[5275](h5275)</sup> Philistia,<sup>[6429](h6429)</sup>
triumph<sup>[7321](h7321)</sup> thou because<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> of
me.

9  Who<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup> will bring<sup>[2986](h2986)</sup> me
into the strong<sup>[4692](h4692)</sup> city?<sup>[5892](h5892)</sup>
who<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup> will lead<sup>[5148](h5148)</sup> me
into<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> Edom?<sup>[123](h0123)</sup>

10  Wilt not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> thou,<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> O
God,<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> which hadst cast us
off?<sup>[2186](h2186)</sup> and thou, O God,<sup>[430](h0430)</sup>
which didst not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> go out<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup>
with our armies?<sup>[6635](h6635)</sup>

11  Give<sup>[3051](h3051)</sup> us help<sup>[5833](h5833)</sup> from
trouble:<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6862](h6862)</sup> for
vain<sup>[7723](h7723)</sup> is the help<sup>[8668](h8668)</sup> of
man.<sup>[120](h0120)</sup>

12  Through God<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> we shall
do<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> valiantly:<sup>[2428](h2428)</sup> for
he<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> it is that shall tread
down<sup>[947](h0947)</sup> our enemies.<sup>[6862](h6862)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/psa060)

1 εἰς τὸ τέλος ἐν ὕμνοις τῷ Δαυιδ

2 εἰσάκουσον ὁ θεός τῆς δεήσεώς μου πρόσχες τῇ προσευχῇ μου

3 ἀπὸ τῶν περάτων τῆς γῆς πρὸς σὲ ἐκέκραξα ἐν τῷ ἀκηδιάσαι τὴν καρδίαν
μου ἐν πέτρᾳ ὕψωσάς με

4 ὡδήγησάς με ὅτι ἐγενήθης ἐλπίς μου πύργος ἰσχύος ἀπὸ προσώπου ἐχθροῦ

5 παροικήσω ἐν τῷ σκηνώματί σου εἰς τοὺς αἰῶνας σκεπασθήσομαι ἐν σκέπῃ
τῶν πτερύγων σου διάψαλμα

6 ὅτι σύ ὁ θεός εἰσήκουσας τῶν εὐχῶν μου ἔδωκας κληρονομίαν τοῖς
φοβουμένοις τὸ ὄνομά σου

7 ἡμέρας ἐφ᾽ ἡμέρας βασιλέως προσθήσεις ἔτη αὐτοῦ ἕως ἡμέρας γενεᾶς καὶ
γενεᾶς

8 διαμενεῖ εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα ἐνώπιον τοῦ θεοῦ ἔλεος καὶ ἀλήθειαν αὐτοῦ τίς
ἐκζητήσει

9 οὕτως ψαλῶ τῷ ὀνόματί σου εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα τοῦ αἰῶνος τοῦ ἀποδοῦναί με
τὰς εὐχάς μου ἡμέραν ἐξ ἡμέρας

[Vul](../vul/psa060)

1 In finem. In hymnis David.

2 \[Exaudi, Deus, deprecationem meam;  
intende orationi meæ.  

3 A finibus terræ ad te clamavi, dum anxiaretur cor meum;  
in petra exaltasti me.  
Deduxisti me,

4 quia factus es spes mea:  
turris fortitudinis a facie inimici.  

5 Inhabitabo in tabernaculo tuo in sæcula;  
protegar in velamento alarum tuarum.  

6 Quoniam tu, Deus meus, exaudisti orationem meam;  
dedisti hæreditatem timentibus nomen tuum.  

7 Dies super dies regis adjicies;  
annos ejus usque in diem generationis et generationis.  

8 Permanet in æternum in conspectu Dei:  
misericordiam et veritatem ejus quis requiret?  

9 Sic psalmum dicam nomini tuo in sæculum sæculi,  
ut reddam vota mea de die in diem.\]
