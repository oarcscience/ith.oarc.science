### Proverbs 13

[KJV](../kjv/pro013.md)

1  A wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> son<sup>[1121](h1121.md)</sup> heareth
his father's<sup>[1](h0001)</sup> instruction:<sup>[4148](h4148.md)</sup>
but a scorner<sup>[3887](h3887)</sup> heareth<sup>[8085](h8085.md)</sup>
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> rebuke.<sup>[1606](h1606.md)</sup>

2  A man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> shall eat<sup>[398](h0398.md)</sup>
good<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> by the fruit<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[6529](h6529)</sup> of his mouth:<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> but the
soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> of the transgressors<sup>[898](h0898.md)</sup>
shall eat violence.<sup>[2555](h2555.md)</sup>

3  He that keepeth<sup>[5341](h5341.md)</sup> his
mouth<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> keepeth<sup>[8104](h8104.md)</sup> his
life:<sup>[5315](h5315.md)</sup> but he that openeth
wide<sup>[6589](h6589)</sup> his lips<sup>[8193](h8193.md)</sup> shall have
destruction.<sup>[4288](h4288.md)</sup>

4  The soul<sup>[5315](h5315.md)</sup> of the
sluggard<sup>[6102](h6102)</sup> desireth,<sup>[183](h0183.md)</sup> and
hath nothing:<sup>[369](h0369.md)</sup> but the
soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> of the diligent<sup>[2742](h2742.md)</sup>
shall be made fat.<sup>[1878](h1878.md)</sup>

5  A righteous<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> man
hateth<sup>[8130](h8130)</sup> lying:<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup>
<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> but a wicked<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup> man is
loathsome,<sup>[887](h0887.md)</sup> and cometh to
shame.<sup>[2659](h2659.md)</sup>

6  Righteousness<sup>[6666](h6666)</sup> keepeth<sup>[5341](h5341.md)</sup>
him that is upright<sup>[8537](h8537.md)</sup> in the
way:<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> but wickedness<sup>[7564](h7564.md)</sup>
overthroweth<sup>[5557](h5557)</sup> the sinner.<sup>[2403](h2403.md)</sup>

7  There is<sup>[3426](h3426.md)</sup> that maketh himself
rich,<sup>[6238](h6238)</sup> yet hath nothing:<sup>[369](h0369.md)</sup>
<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup> there is that maketh himself
poor,<sup>[7326](h7326)</sup> yet hath great<sup>[7227](h7227.md)</sup>
riches.<sup>[1952](h1952.md)</sup>

8  The ransom<sup>[3724](h3724)</sup> of a man's<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup>
life<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> are his riches:<sup>[6239](h6239.md)</sup> but
the poor<sup>[7326](h7326)</sup> heareth<sup>[8085](h8085.md)</sup>
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> rebuke.<sup>[1606](h1606.md)</sup>

9  The light<sup>[216](h0216.md)</sup> of the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> rejoiceth:<sup>[8055](h8055.md)</sup> but
the lamp<sup>[5216](h5216)</sup> of the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup>
shall be put out.<sup>[1846](h1846.md)</sup>

10  Only<sup>[7535](h7535)</sup> by pride<sup>[2087](h2087.md)</sup>
cometh<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> contention:<sup>[4683](h4683.md)</sup> but
with<sup>[854](h0854)</sup> the well advised<sup>[3289](h3289.md)</sup> is
wisdom.<sup>[2451](h2451.md)</sup>

11  Wealth<sup>[1952](h1952.md)</sup> gotten by
vanity<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[1892](h1892.md)</sup> shall be
diminished:<sup>[4591](h4591.md)</sup> but he that
gathereth<sup>[6908](h6908)</sup> by<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
labor<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> shall increase.<sup>[7235](h7235.md)</sup>

12  Hope<sup>[8431](h8431)</sup> deferred<sup>[4900](h4900.md)</sup> maketh
the heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> sick:<sup>[2470](h2470.md)</sup> but when
the desire<sup>[8378](h8378)</sup> cometh,<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup> it is
a tree<sup>[6086](h6086)</sup> of life.<sup>[2416](h2416.md)</sup>

13  Whoso despiseth<sup>[936](h0936.md)</sup> the
word<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup> shall be destroyed:<sup>[2254](h2254.md)</sup>
but he<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> that feareth<sup>[3373](h3373.md)</sup> the
commandment<sup>[4687](h4687.md)</sup> shall be
rewarded.<sup>[7999](h7999.md)</sup>

14  The law<sup>[8451](h8451)</sup> of the wise<sup>[2450](h2450.md)</sup>
is a fountain<sup>[4726](h4726)</sup> of life,<sup>[2416](h2416.md)</sup>
to depart<sup>[5493](h5493.md)</sup> from the
snares<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[4170](h4170.md)</sup> of
death.<sup>[4194](h4194.md)</sup>

15  Good<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> understanding<sup>[7922](h7922.md)</sup>
giveth<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> favor:<sup>[2580](h2580.md)</sup> but the
way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of transgressors<sup>[898](h0898.md)</sup> is
hard.<sup>[386](h0386.md)</sup>

16  Every<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> prudent<sup>[6175](h6175.md)</sup> man
dealeth<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> with knowledge:<sup>[1847](h1847.md)</sup>
but a fool<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> layeth open<sup>[6566](h6566.md)</sup>
his folly.<sup>[200](h0200.md)</sup>

17  A wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> messenger<sup>[4397](h4397.md)</sup>
falleth<sup>[5307](h5307)</sup> into mischief:<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup>
but a faithful<sup>[529](h0529)</sup> ambassador<sup>[6735](h6735.md)</sup>
is health.<sup>[4832](h4832.md)</sup>

18  Poverty<sup>[7389](h7389)</sup> and shame<sup>[7036](h7036.md)</sup>
shall be to him that refuseth<sup>[6544](h6544.md)</sup>
instruction:<sup>[4148](h4148.md)</sup> but he that
regardeth<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> reproof<sup>[8433](h8433.md)</sup> shall
be honored.<sup>[3513](h3513.md)</sup>

19  The desire<sup>[8378](h8378.md)</sup>
accomplished<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> is sweet<sup>[6149](h6149.md)</sup> to
the soul:<sup>[5315](h5315.md)</sup> but it is
abomination<sup>[8441](h8441)</sup> to fools<sup>[3684](h3684.md)</sup> to
depart<sup>[5493](h5493)</sup> from evil.<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup>

20  He that walketh<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> with<sup>[854](h0854.md)</sup>
wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> men shall be wise:<sup>[2449](h2449.md)</sup>
but a companion<sup>[7462](h7462)</sup> of fools<sup>[3684](h3684.md)</sup>
shall be destroyed.<sup>[7321](h7321.md)</sup>

21  Evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> pursueth<sup>[7291](h7291.md)</sup>
sinners:<sup>[2400](h2400.md)</sup> but to the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> good<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup> shall be
repaid.<sup>[7999](h7999.md)</sup>

22  A good<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup> man leaveth an
inheritance<sup>[5157](h5157.md)</sup> to his
children's<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> children:<sup>[1121](h1121.md)</sup> and
the wealth<sup>[2428](h2428)</sup> of the sinner<sup>[2398](h2398.md)</sup>
is laid up<sup>[6845](h6845)</sup> for the just.<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup>

23  Much<sup>[7230](h7230)</sup> food<sup>[400](h0400.md)</sup> is in the
tillage<sup>[5215](h5215)</sup> of the poor:<sup>[7326](h7326.md)</sup> but
there is<sup>[3426](h3426.md)</sup> that is
destroyed<sup>[5595](h5595)</sup> for want<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> of
judgment.<sup>[4941](h4941.md)</sup>

24  He that spareth<sup>[2820](h2820.md)</sup> his
rod<sup>[7626](h7626)</sup> hateth<sup>[8130](h8130.md)</sup> his
son:<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> but he that loveth<sup>[157](h0157.md)</sup>
him chasteneth<sup>[4148](h4148.md)</sup> him
quickly.<sup>[7836](h7836.md)</sup>

25  The righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> eateth<sup>[398](h0398.md)</sup>
to the satisfying<sup>[7648](h7648.md)</sup> of his
soul:<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> but the belly<sup>[990](h0990.md)</sup> of
the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> shall want.<sup>[2637](h2637.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/pro013.md)

1 υἱὸς πανοῦργος ὑπήκοος πατρί υἱὸς δὲ ἀνήκοος ἐν ἀπωλείᾳ

2 ἀπὸ καρπῶν δικαιοσύνης φάγεται ἀγαθός ψυχαὶ δὲ παρανόμων ὀλοῦνται
ἄωροι

3 ὃς φυλάσσει τὸ ἑαυτοῦ στόμα τηρεῖ τὴν ἑαυτοῦ ψυχήν ὁ δὲ προπετὴς
χείλεσιν πτοήσει ἑαυτόν

4 ἐν ἐπιθυμίαις ἐστὶν πᾶς ἀεργός χεῖρες δὲ ἀνδρείων ἐν ἐπιμελείᾳ

5 λόγον ἄδικον μισεῖ δίκαιος ἀσεβὴς δὲ αἰσχύνεται καὶ οὐχ ἕξει παρρησίαν

6 δικαιοσύνη φυλάσσει ἀκάκους τοὺς δὲ ἀσεβεῖς φαύλους ποιεῖ ἁμαρτία

7 εἰσὶν οἱ πλουτίζοντες ἑαυτοὺς μηδὲν ἔχοντες καὶ εἰσὶν οἱ ταπεινοῦντες
ἑαυτοὺς ἐν πολλῷ πλούτῳ

8 λύτρον ἀνδρὸς ψυχῆς ὁ ἴδιος πλοῦτος πτωχὸς δὲ οὐχ ὑφίσταται ἀπειλήν

9 φῶς δικαίοις διὰ παντός φῶς δὲ ἀσεβῶν σβέννυται  
ψυχαὶ δόλιαι πλανῶνται ἐν ἁμαρτίαις δίκαιοι δὲ οἰκτίρουσιν καὶ ἐλεῶσιν

10 κακὸς μεθ᾽ ὕβρεως πράσσει κακά οἱ δὲ ἑαυτῶν ἐπιγνώμονες σοφοί

11 ὕπαρξις ἐπισπουδαζομένη μετὰ ἀνομίας ἐλάσσων γίνεται ὁ δὲ συνάγων
ἑαυτῷ μετ᾽ εὐσεβείας πληθυνθήσεται δίκαιος οἰκτίρει καὶ κιχρᾷ

12 κρείσσων ἐναρχόμενος βοηθῶν καρδίᾳ τοῦ ἐπαγγελλομένου καὶ εἰς ἐλπίδα
ἄγοντος δένδρον γὰρ ζωῆς ἐπιθυμία ἀγαθή

13 ὃς καταφρονεῖ πράγματος καταφρονηθήσεται ὑπ᾽ αὐτοῦ ὁ δὲ φοβούμενος
ἐντολήν οὗτος ὑγιαίνει  
υἱῷ δολίῳ οὐδὲν ἔσται ἀγαθόν οἰκέτῃ δὲ σοφῷ εὔοδοι ἔσονται πράξεις καὶ
κατευθυνθήσεται ἡ ὁδὸς αὐτοῦ

14 νόμος σοφοῦ πηγὴ ζωῆς ὁ δὲ ἄνους ὑπὸ παγίδος θανεῖται

15 σύνεσις ἀγαθὴ δίδωσιν χάριν τὸ δὲ γνῶναι νόμον διανοίας ἐστὶν ἀγαθῆς
ὁδοὶ δὲ καταφρονούντων ἐν ἀπωλείᾳ

16 πᾶς πανοῦργος πράσσει μετὰ γνώσεως ὁ δὲ ἄφρων ἐξεπέτασεν ἑαυτοῦ
κακίαν

17 βασιλεὺς θρασὺς ἐμπεσεῖται εἰς κακά ἄγγελος δὲ πιστὸς ῥύσεται αὐτόν

18 πενίαν καὶ ἀτιμίαν ἀφαιρεῖται παιδεία ὁ δὲ φυλάσσων ἐλέγχους
δοξασθήσεται

19 ἐπιθυμίαι εὐσεβῶν ἡδύνουσιν ψυχήν ἔργα δὲ ἀσεβῶν μακρὰν ἀπὸ γνώσεως

20 ὁ συμπορευόμενος σοφοῖς σοφὸς ἔσται ὁ δὲ συμπορευόμενος ἄφροσι
γνωσθήσεται

21 ἁμαρτάνοντας καταδιώξεται κακά τοὺς δὲ δικαίους καταλήμψεται ἀγαθά

22 ἀγαθὸς ἀνὴρ κληρονομήσει υἱοὺς υἱῶν θησαυρίζεται δὲ δικαίοις πλοῦτος
ἀσεβὼν

23 δίκαιοι ποιήσουσιν ἐν πλούτῳ ἔτη πολλά ἄδικοι δὲ ἀπολοῦνται συντόμως

24 ὃς φείδεται τῆς βακτηρίας μισεῖ τὸν υἱὸν αὐτοῦ ὁ δὲ ἀγαπῶν ἐπιμελῶς
παιδεύει

25 δίκαιος ἔσθων ἐμπιπλᾷ τὴν ψυχὴν αὐτοῦ ψυχαὶ δὲ ἀσεβῶν ἐνδεεῖς

[Vul](../vul/pro013.md)

1 \[Filius sapiens doctrina patris;  
qui autem illusor est non audit cum arguitur.  

2 De fructu oris sui homo satiabitur bonis:  
anima autem prævaricatorum iniqua.  

3 Qui custodit os suum custodit animam suam;  
qui autem inconsideratus est ad loquendum, sentiet mala.  

4 Vult et non vult piger;  
anima autem operantium impinguabitur.  

5 Verbum mendax justus detestabitur;  
impius autem confundit, et confundetur.  

6 Justitia custodit innocentis viam,  
impietas autem peccatorem supplantat.\]  

7 \[Est quasi dives, cum nihil habeat,  
et est quasi pauper, cum in multis divitiis sit.  

8 Redemptio animæ viri divitiæ suæ;  
qui autem pauper est, increpationem non sustinet.  

9 Lux justorum lætificat:  
lucerna autem impiorum extinguetur.  

10 Inter superbos semper jurgia sunt;  
qui autem agunt omnia cum consilio, reguntur sapientia.  

11 Substantia festinata minuetur;  
quæ autem paulatim colligitur manu, multiplicabitur.  

12 Spes quæ differtur affligit animam;  
lignum vitæ desiderium veniens.\]  

13 \[Qui detrahit alicui rei, ipse se in futurum obligat;  
qui autem timet præceptum, in pace versabitur.  
Animæ dolosæ errant in peccatis:  
justi autem misericordes sunt, et miserantur.  

14 Lex sapientis fons vitæ,  
ut declinet a ruina mortis.  

15 Doctrina bona dabit gratiam;  
in itinere contemptorum vorago.  

16 Astutus omnia agit cum consilio;  
qui autem fatuus est aperit stultitiam.  

17 Nuntius impii cadet in malum;  
legatus autem fidelis, sanitas.  

18 Egestas et ignominia ei qui deserit disciplinam;  
qui autem acquiescit arguenti glorificabitur.  

19 Desiderium si compleatur delectat animam;  
detestantur stulti eos qui fugiunt mala.  

20 Qui cum sapientibus graditur sapiens erit;  
amicus stultorum similis efficietur.\]  

21 \[Peccatores persequitur malum,  
et justis retribuentur bona.  

22 Bonus reliquit hæredes filios et nepotes,  
et custoditur justo substantia peccatoris.  

23 Multi cibi in novalibus patrum,  
et aliis congregantur absque judicio.  

24 Qui parcit virgæ odit filium suum;  
qui autem diligit illum instanter erudit.  

25 Justus comedit et replet animam suam;  
venter autem impiorum insaturabilis.\]
