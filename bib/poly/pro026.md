### Proverbs 26

[KJV](../kjv/pro026.md)

1  As snow<sup>[7950](h7950)</sup> in summer,<sup>[7019](h7019.md)</sup>
and as rain<sup>[4306](h4306)</sup> in harvest,<sup>[7105](h7105.md)</sup>
so<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> honor<sup>[3519](h3519.md)</sup> is
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> seemly<sup>[5000](h5000.md)</sup> for a
fool.<sup>[3684](h3684.md)</sup>

2  As the bird<sup>[6833](h6833.md)</sup> by
wandering,<sup>[5110](h5110.md)</sup> as the
swallow<sup>[1866](h1866)</sup> by flying,<sup>[5774](h5774.md)</sup>
so<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> the curse<sup>[7045](h7045.md)</sup>
causeless<sup>[2600](h2600)</sup> shall not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
come.<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup>

3  A whip<sup>[7752](h7752)</sup> for the horse,<sup>[5483](h5483.md)</sup>
a bridle<sup>[4964](h4964)</sup> for the ass,<sup>[2543](h2543.md)</sup>
and a rod<sup>[7626](h7626)</sup> for the fool's<sup>[3684](h3684.md)</sup>
back.<sup>[1460](h1460.md)</sup>

4  Answer<sup>[6030](h6030)</sup> not<sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup> a
fool<sup>[3684](h3684.md)</sup> according to his
folly,<sup>[200](h0200)</sup> lest<sup>[6435](h6435.md)</sup>
thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> also<sup>[1571](h1571.md)</sup> be
like<sup>[7737](h7737.md)</sup> unto him.

5  Answer<sup>[6030](h6030)</sup> a fool<sup>[3684](h3684.md)</sup>
according to his folly,<sup>[200](h0200.md)</sup>
lest<sup>[6435](h6435)</sup> he be<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup>
wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> in his own conceit.<sup>[5869](h5869.md)</sup>

6  He that sendeth<sup>[7971](h7971.md)</sup> a
message<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup> by the hand<sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup> of a
fool<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> cutteth off<sup>[7096](h7096.md)</sup> the
feet,<sup>[7272](h7272)</sup> and drinketh<sup>[8354](h8354.md)</sup>
damage.<sup>[2555](h2555.md)</sup>

7  The legs<sup>[7785](h7785)</sup> of the lame<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[6455](h6455)</sup> are not equal:<sup>[1809](h1809.md)</sup> so is a
parable<sup>[4912](h4912)</sup> in the mouth<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> of
fools.<sup>[3684](h3684.md)</sup>

8  As he that bindeth<sup>[6887](h6887.md)</sup> a
stone<sup>[68](h0068)</sup> in a sling,<sup>[4773](h4773.md)</sup>
so<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> is he that giveth<sup>[5414](h5414.md)</sup>
honor<sup>[3519](h3519)</sup> to a fool.<sup>[3684](h3684.md)</sup>

9  As a thorn<sup>[2336](h2336)</sup> goeth up<sup>[5927](h5927.md)</sup>
into the hand<sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup> of a
drunkard,<sup>[7910](h7910.md)</sup> so is a
parable<sup>[4912](h4912)</sup> in the mouth<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> of
fools.<sup>[3684](h3684.md)</sup>

10  The great<sup>[7227](h7227.md)</sup> God that
formed<sup>[2342](h2342)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup> things both
rewardeth<sup>[7936](h7936)</sup> the fool,<sup>[3684](h3684.md)</sup> and
rewardeth<sup>[7936](h7936)</sup> transgressors.<sup>[5674](h5674.md)</sup>

11  As a dog<sup>[3611](h3611)</sup> returneth<sup>[7725](h7725.md)</sup>
to<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> his vomit,<sup>[6892](h6892.md)</sup> so a
fool<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> returneth<sup>[8138](h8138.md)</sup> to his
folly.<sup>[200](h0200.md)</sup>

12  Seest<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> thou a man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup>
wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> in his own conceit?<sup>[5869](h5869.md)</sup>
there is more hope<sup>[8615](h8615.md)</sup> of a
fool<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> than of<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup> him.

13  The slothful<sup>[6102](h6102.md)</sup> man
saith,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> There is a lion<sup>[7826](h7826.md)</sup> in
the way;<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> a lion<sup>[738](h0738.md)</sup> is
in<sup>[996](h0996)</sup> the streets.<sup>[7339](h7339.md)</sup>

14  As the door<sup>[1817](h1817)</sup> turneth<sup>[5437](h5437.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> his hinges,<sup>[6735](h6735.md)</sup> so doth
the slothful<sup>[6102](h6102)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> his
bed.<sup>[4296](h4296.md)</sup>

15  The slothful<sup>[6102](h6102)</sup> hideth<sup>[2934](h2934.md)</sup>
his hand<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> in his bosom;<sup>[6747](h6747.md)</sup>
it grieveth<sup>[3811](h3811.md)</sup> him to bring it
again<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> to<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup> his
mouth.<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup>

16  The sluggard<sup>[6102](h6102.md)</sup> is
wiser<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> in his own conceit<sup>[5869](h5869.md)</sup>
than seven<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7651](h7651.md)</sup> men that can
render<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> a reason.<sup>[2940](h2940.md)</sup>

17  He that passeth by,<sup>[5674](h5674.md)</sup> and
meddleth<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup> with<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
strife<sup>[7379](h7379)</sup> belonging not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> to
him, is like one that taketh<sup>[2388](h2388.md)</sup> a
dog<sup>[3611](h3611)</sup> by the ears.<sup>[241](h0241.md)</sup>

18  As a mad<sup>[3856](h3856.md)</sup> man who
casteth<sup>[3384](h3384)</sup> firebrands,<sup>[2131](h2131.md)</sup>
arrows,<sup>[2671](h2671)</sup> and death,<sup>[4194](h4194.md)</sup>

19  So<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> is the man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> that
deceiveth<sup>[7411](h7411)</sup> <sup>[853](h0853.md)</sup> his
neighbor,<sup>[7453](h7453)</sup> and saith,<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup> Am
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> I<sup>[589](h0589.md)</sup> in
sport?<sup>[7832](h7832.md)</sup>

20  Where no<sup>[657](h0657)</sup> wood<sup>[6086](h6086.md)</sup> is,
there the fire<sup>[784](h0784)</sup> goeth out:<sup>[3518](h3518.md)</sup>
so where there is no<sup>[369](h0369.md)</sup>
talebearer,<sup>[5372](h5372)</sup> the strife<sup>[4066](h4066.md)</sup>
ceaseth.<sup>[8367](h8367.md)</sup>

21  As coals<sup>[6352](h6352.md)</sup> are to burning
coals,<sup>[1513](h1513)</sup> and wood<sup>[6086](h6086.md)</sup> to
fire;<sup>[784](h0784)</sup> so is a contentious<sup>[4079](h4079.md)</sup>
man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> to kindle<sup>[2787](h2787.md)</sup>
strife.<sup>[7379](h7379.md)</sup>

22  The words<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup> of a
talebearer<sup>[5372](h5372.md)</sup> are as
wounds,<sup>[3859](h3859)</sup> and they<sup>[1992](h1992.md)</sup> go
down<sup>[3381](h3381.md)</sup> into the innermost
parts<sup>[2315](h2315)</sup> of the belly.<sup>[990](h0990.md)</sup>

23  Burning<sup>[1814](h1814)</sup> lips<sup>[8193](h8193.md)</sup> and a
wicked<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> heart<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup> are like a
potsherd<sup>[2789](h2789)</sup> covered<sup>[6823](h6823.md)</sup> with
silver<sup>[3701](h3701)</sup> dross.<sup>[5509](h5509.md)</sup>

24  He that hateth<sup>[8130](h8130.md)</sup>
dissembleth<sup>[5234](h5234.md)</sup> with his
lips,<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> and layeth up<sup>[7896](h7896.md)</sup>
deceit<sup>[4820](h4820)</sup> within<sup>[7130](h7130.md)</sup> him;

25  When<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> he speaketh<sup>[6963](h6963.md)</sup>
fair,<sup>[2603](h2603)</sup> believe<sup>[539](h0539.md)</sup> him
not:<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup> there are
seven<sup>[7651](h7651)</sup> abominations<sup>[8441](h8441.md)</sup> in
his heart.<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup>

26  Whose hatred<sup>[8135](h8135.md)</sup> is
covered<sup>[3680](h3680)</sup> by deceit,<sup>[4860](h4860.md)</sup> his
wickedness<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup> shall be
showed<sup>[1540](h1540.md)</sup> before the whole
congregation.<sup>[6951](h6951.md)</sup>

27  Whoso diggeth<sup>[3738](h3738)</sup> a pit<sup>[7845](h7845.md)</sup>
shall fall<sup>[5307](h5307.md)</sup> therein: and he that
rolleth<sup>[1556](h1556)</sup> a stone,<sup>[68](h0068.md)</sup> it will
return<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> upon<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup> him.

28  A lying<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> tongue<sup>[3956](h3956.md)</sup>
hateth<sup>[8130](h8130.md)</sup> those that are
afflicted<sup>[1790](h1790.md)</sup> by it; and a
flattering<sup>[2509](h2509)</sup> mouth<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup>
worketh<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> ruin.<sup>[4072](h4072.md)</sup>

[Vul](../vul/pro026.md)

1 \[Quomodo nix in æstate, et pluviæ in messe,  
sic indecens est stulto gloria.  

2 Sicut avis ad alia transvolans, et passer quolibet vadens,  
sic maledictum frustra prolatum in quempiam superveniet.  

3 Flagellum equo, et camus asino,  
et virga in dorso imprudentium.  

4 Ne respondeas stulto juxta stultitiam suam,  
ne efficiaris ei similis.  

5 Responde stulto juxta stultitiam suam,  
ne sibi sapiens esse videatur.  

6 Claudus pedibus, et iniquitatem bibens,  
qui mittit verba per nuntium stultum.  

7 Quomodo pulchras frustra habet claudus tibias,  
sic indecens est in ore stultorum parabola.  

8 Sicut qui mittit lapidem in acervum Mercurii,  
ita qui tribuit insipienti honorem.  

9 Quomodo si spina nascatur in manu temulenti,  
sic parabola in ore stultorum.  

10 Judicium determinat causas,  
et qui imponit stulto silentium iras mitigat.  

11 Sicut canis qui revertitur ad vomitum suum,  
sic imprudens qui iterat stultitiam suam.  

12 Vidisti hominem sapientem sibi videri?  
magis illo spem habebit insipiens.\]  

13 \[Dicit piger: Leo est in via,  
et leæna in itineribus.  

14 Sicut ostium vertitur in cardine suo,  
ita piger in lectulo suo.  

15 Abscondit piger manum sub ascella sua,  
et laborat si ad os suum eam converterit.  

16 Sapientior sibi piger videtur  
septem viris loquentibus sententias.\]  

17 \[Sicut qui apprehendit auribus canem,  
sic qui transit impatiens et commiscetur rixæ alterius.  

18 Sicut noxius est qui mittit sagittas et lanceas in mortem,  

19 ita vir fraudulenter nocet amico suo,  
et cum fuerit deprehensus dicit: Ludens feci.  

20 Cum defecerint ligna extinguetur ignis,  
et susurrone subtracto, jurgia conquiescent.  

21 Sicut carbones ad prunas, et ligna ad ignem,  
sic homo iracundus suscitat rixas.  

22 Verba susurronis quasi simplicia,  
et ipsa perveniunt ad intima ventris.  

23 Quomodo si argento sordido ornare velis vas fictile,  
sic labia tumentia cum pessimo corde sociata.  

24 Labiis suis intelligitur inimicus,  
cum in corde tractaverit dolos.  

25 Quando submiserit vocem suam, ne credideris ei,  
quoniam septem nequitiæ sunt in corde illius.  

26 Qui operit odium fraudulenter,  
revelabitur malitia ejus in consilio.  

27 Qui fodit foveam incidet in eam,  
et qui volvit lapidem revertetur ad eum.  

28 Lingua fallax non amat veritatem,  
et os lubricum operatur ruinas.\]
