### Proverbs 18

[KJV](../kjv/pro018.md)

1  Through desire<sup>[8378](h8378.md)</sup> a man, having separated
himself,<sup>[6504](h6504)</sup> seeketh<sup>[1245](h1245.md)</sup> and
intermeddleth<sup>[1566](h1566)</sup> with all<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup>
wisdom.<sup>[8454](h8454.md)</sup>

2  A fool<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> hath no<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
delight<sup>[2654](h2654.md)</sup> in
understanding,<sup>[8394](h8394)</sup> but<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup>
<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> that his heart<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup> may
discover itself.<sup>[1540](h1540.md)</sup>

3  When the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup>
cometh,<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> then cometh<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup>
also<sup>[1571](h1571)</sup> contempt,<sup>[937](h0937.md)</sup> and
with<sup>[5973](h5973)</sup> ignominy<sup>[7036](h7036.md)</sup>
reproach.<sup>[2781](h2781.md)</sup>

4  The words<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup> of a man's<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup>
mouth<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> are as deep<sup>[6013](h6013.md)</sup>
waters,<sup>[4325](h4325.md)</sup> and the
wellspring<sup>[4726](h4726)</sup> of wisdom<sup>[2451](h2451.md)</sup> as
a flowing<sup>[5042](h5042)</sup> brook.<sup>[5158](h5158.md)</sup>

5  It is not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> good<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup> to
accept<sup>[5375](h5375)</sup> the person<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> of the
wicked,<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> to overthrow<sup>[5186](h5186.md)</sup> the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> in judgment.<sup>[4941](h4941.md)</sup>

6  A fool's<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> lips<sup>[8193](h8193.md)</sup>
enter<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> into contention,<sup>[7379](h7379.md)</sup>
and his mouth<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> calleth<sup>[7121](h7121.md)</sup>
for strokes.<sup>[4112](h4112.md)</sup>

7  A fool's<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> mouth<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> is his
destruction,<sup>[4288](h4288.md)</sup> and his
lips<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> are the snare<sup>[4170](h4170.md)</sup> of
his soul.<sup>[5315](h5315.md)</sup>

8  The words<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup> of a
talebearer<sup>[5372](h5372.md)</sup> are as
wounds,<sup>[3859](h3859)</sup> and they<sup>[1992](h1992.md)</sup> go
down<sup>[3381](h3381.md)</sup> into the innermost
parts<sup>[2315](h2315)</sup> of the belly.<sup>[990](h0990.md)</sup>

9  He<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> also<sup>[1571](h1571.md)</sup> that is
slothful<sup>[7503](h7503)</sup> in his work<sup>[4399](h4399.md)</sup> is
brother<sup>[251](h0251.md)</sup> to him that is a
great<sup>[1167](h1167)</sup> waster.<sup>[4889](h4889.md)</sup>

10  The name<sup>[8034](h8034)</sup> of the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup>
is a strong<sup>[5797](h5797)</sup> tower:<sup>[4026](h4026.md)</sup> the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> runneth<sup>[7323](h7323.md)</sup> into
it, and is safe.<sup>[7682](h7682.md)</sup>

11  The rich man's<sup>[6223](h6223.md)</sup>
wealth<sup>[1952](h1952)</sup> is his strong<sup>[5797](h5797.md)</sup>
city,<sup>[7151](h7151)</sup> and as a high<sup>[7682](h7682.md)</sup>
wall<sup>[2346](h2346)</sup> in his own conceit.<sup>[4906](h4906.md)</sup>

12  Before<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> destruction<sup>[7667](h7667.md)</sup>
the heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> of man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> is
haughty,<sup>[1361](h1361)</sup> and before<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup>
honor<sup>[3519](h3519)</sup> is humility.<sup>[6038](h6038.md)</sup>

13  He that answereth<sup>[7725](h7725.md)</sup> a
matter<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup> before<sup>[2962](h2962.md)</sup> he
heareth<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> it, it<sup>[1931](h1931.md)</sup> is
folly<sup>[200](h0200)</sup> and shame<sup>[3639](h3639.md)</sup> unto him.

14  The spirit<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> of a man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup>
will sustain<sup>[3557](h3557.md)</sup> his
infirmity;<sup>[4245](h4245)</sup> but a wounded<sup>[5218](h5218.md)</sup>
spirit<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> who<sup>[4310](h4310.md)</sup> can
bear?<sup>[5375](h5375.md)</sup>

15  The heart<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup> of the
prudent<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> getteth<sup>[7069](h7069.md)</sup>
knowledge;<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup> and the ear<sup>[241](h0241.md)</sup> of
the wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> seeketh<sup>[1245](h1245.md)</sup>
knowledge.<sup>[1847](h1847.md)</sup>

16  A man's<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> gift<sup>[4976](h4976.md)</sup> maketh
room<sup>[7337](h7337.md)</sup> for him, and
bringeth<sup>[5148](h5148)</sup> him before<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup>
great<sup>[1419](h1419.md)</sup> men.

17  He that is first<sup>[7223](h7223.md)</sup> in his own
cause<sup>[7379](h7379)</sup> seemeth just;<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> but
his neighbor<sup>[7453](h7453)</sup> cometh<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup> and
searcheth<sup>[2713](h2713.md)</sup> him.

18  The lot<sup>[1486](h1486.md)</sup> causeth
contentions<sup>[4079](h4079)</sup> to cease,<sup>[7673](h7673.md)</sup>
and parteth<sup>[6504](h6504)</sup> between<sup>[996](h0996.md)</sup> the
mighty.<sup>[6099](h6099.md)</sup>

19  A brother<sup>[251](h0251)</sup> offended<sup>[6586](h6586.md)</sup> is
harder to be won than a strong<sup>[5797](h5797.md)</sup>
city:<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7151](h7151.md)</sup> and their
contentions<sup>[4079](h4079.md)</sup> are like the
bars<sup>[1280](h1280)</sup> of a castle.<sup>[759](h0759.md)</sup>

20  A man's<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> belly<sup>[990](h0990.md)</sup> shall be
satisfied<sup>[7646](h7646)</sup> with the fruit<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[6529](h6529)</sup> of his mouth;<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> and with
the increase<sup>[8393](h8393)</sup> of his lips<sup>[8193](h8193.md)</sup>
shall he be filled.<sup>[7646](h7646.md)</sup>

21  Death<sup>[4194](h4194)</sup> and life<sup>[2416](h2416.md)</sup> are
in the power<sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup> of the
tongue:<sup>[3956](h3956.md)</sup> and they that
love<sup>[157](h0157)</sup> it shall eat<sup>[398](h0398.md)</sup> the
fruit<sup>[6529](h6529.md)</sup> thereof.

22  Whoso findeth<sup>[4672](h4672)</sup> a wife<sup>[802](h0802.md)</sup>
findeth<sup>[4672](h4672)</sup> a good<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup> thing,
and obtaineth<sup>[6329](h6329)</sup> favor<sup>[7522](h7522.md)</sup> of
the LORD.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup>

23  The poor<sup>[7326](h7326)</sup> useth<sup>[1696](h1696.md)</sup>
entreaties;<sup>[8469](h8469)</sup> but the rich<sup>[6223](h6223.md)</sup>
answereth<sup>[6030](h6030)</sup> roughly.<sup>[5794](h5794.md)</sup>

24  A man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> that hath
friends<sup>[7453](h7453.md)</sup> must show himself
friendly:<sup>[7489](h7489)</sup> and there is<sup>[3426](h3426.md)</sup> a
friend<sup>[157](h0157.md)</sup> that sticketh
closer<sup>[1695](h1695)</sup> than a brother.<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[251](h0251.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/pro018.md)

1 προφάσεις ζητεῖ ἀνὴρ βουλόμενος χωρίζεσθαι ἀπὸ φίλων ἐν παντὶ δὲ καιρῷ
ἐπονείδιστος ἔσται

2 οὐ χρείαν ἔχει σοφίας ἐνδεὴς φρενῶν μᾶλλον γὰρ ἄγεται ἀφροσύνῃ

3 ὅταν ἔλθῃ ἀσεβὴς εἰς βάθος κακῶν καταφρονεῖ ἐπέρχεται δὲ αὐτῷ ἀτιμία
καὶ ὄνειδος

4 ὕδωρ βαθὺ λόγος ἐν καρδίᾳ ἀνδρός ποταμὸς δὲ ἀναπηδύει καὶ πηγὴ ζωῆς

5 θαυμάσαι πρόσωπον ἀσεβοῦς οὐ καλόν οὐδὲ ὅσιον ἐκκλίνειν τὸ δίκαιον ἐν
κρίσει

6 χείλη ἄφρονος ἄγουσιν αὐτὸν εἰς κακά τὸ δὲ στόμα αὐτοῦ τὸ θρασὺ
θάνατον ἐπικαλεῖται

7 στόμα ἄφρονος συντριβὴ αὐτῷ τὰ δὲ χείλη αὐτοῦ παγὶς τῇ ψυχῇ αὐτοῦ

8 ὀκνηροὺς καταβάλλει φόβος ψυχαὶ δὲ ἀνδρογύνων πεινάσουσιν

9 ὁ μὴ ἰώμενος ἑαυτὸν ἐν τοῖς ἔργοις αὐτοῦ ἀδελφός ἐστιν τοῦ
λυμαινομένου ἑαυτόν

10 ἐκ μεγαλωσύνης ἰσχύος ὄνομα κυρίου αὐτῷ δὲ προσδραμόντες δίκαιοι
ὑψοῦνται

11 ὕπαρξις πλουσίου ἀνδρὸς πόλις ὀχυρά ἡ δὲ δόξα αὐτῆς μέγα ἐπισκιάζει

12 πρὸ συντριβῆς ὑψοῦται καρδία ἀνδρός καὶ πρὸ δόξης ταπεινοῦται

13 ὃς ἀποκρίνεται λόγον πρὶν ἀκοῦσαι ἀφροσύνη αὐτῷ ἐστιν καὶ ὄνειδος

14 θυμὸν ἀνδρὸς πραΰνει θεράπων φρόνιμος ὀλιγόψυχον δὲ ἄνδρα τίς ὑποίσει

15 καρδία φρονίμου κτᾶται αἴσθησιν ὦτα δὲ σοφῶν ζητεῖ ἔννοιαν

16 δόμα ἀνθρώπου ἐμπλατύνει αὐτὸν καὶ παρὰ δυνάσταις καθιζάνει αὐτόν

17 δίκαιος ἑαυτοῦ κατήγορος ἐν πρωτολογίᾳ ὡς δ᾽ ἂν ἐπιβάλῃ ὁ ἀντίδικος
ἐλέγχεται

18 ἀντιλογίας παύει κλῆρος ἐν δὲ δυνάσταις ὁρίζει

19 ἀδελφὸς ὑπὸ ἀδελφοῦ βοηθούμενος ὡς πόλις ὀχυρὰ καὶ ὑψηλή ἰσχύει δὲ
ὥσπερ τεθεμελιωμένον βασίλειον

20 ἀπὸ καρπῶν στόματος ἀνὴρ πίμπλησιν κοιλίαν αὐτοῦ ἀπὸ δὲ καρπῶν
χειλέων αὐτοῦ ἐμπλησθήσεται

21 θάνατος καὶ ζωὴ ἐν χειρὶ γλώσσης οἱ δὲ κρατοῦντες αὐτῆς ἔδονται τοὺς
καρποὺς αὐτῆς

22 ὃς εὗρεν γυναῖκα ἀγαθήν εὗρεν χάριτας ἔλαβεν δὲ παρὰ θεοῦ ἱλαρότητα  
ὃς ἐκβάλλει γυναῖκα ἀγαθήν ἐκβάλλει τὰ ἀγαθά ὁ δὲ κατέχων μοιχαλίδα
ἄφρων καὶ ἀσεβής

[Vul](../vul/pro018.md)

1 \[Occasiones quærit qui vult recedere ab amico:  
omni tempore erit exprobrabilis.  

2 Non recipit stultus verba prudentiæ,  
nisi ea dixeris quæ versantur in corde ejus.  

3 Impius, cum in profundum venerit peccatorum, contemnit;  
sed sequitur eum ignominia et opprobrium.  

4 Aqua profunda verba ex ore viri,  
et torrens redundans fons sapientiæ.  

5 Accipere personam impii non est bonum,  
ut declines a veritate judicii.\]  

6 \[Labia stulti miscent se rixis,  
et os ejus jurgia provocat.  

7 Os stulti contritio ejus,  
et labia ipsius ruina animæ ejus.  

8 Verba bilinguis quasi simplicia,  
et ipsa perveniunt usque ad interiora ventris.  
Pigrum dejicit timor;  
animæ autem effeminatorum esurient.  

9 Qui mollis et dissolutus est in opere suo  
frater est sua opera dissipantis.  

10 Turris fortissima nomen Domini;  
ad ipsum currit justus, et exaltabitur.  

11 Substantia divitis urbs roboris ejus,  
et quasi murus validus circumdans eum.  

12 Antequam conteratur, exaltatur cor hominis,  
et antequam glorificetur, humiliatur.  

13 Qui prius respondet quam audiat,  
stultum se esse demonstrat, et confusione dignum.  

14 Spiritus viri sustentat imbecillitatem suam;  
spiritum vero ad irascendum facilem quis poterit sustinere?  

15 Cor prudens possidebit scientiam,  
et auris sapientium quærit doctrinam.\]  

16 \[Donum hominis dilatat viam ejus,  
et ante principes spatium ei facit.  

17 Justus prior est accusator sui:  
venit amicus ejus, et investigabit eum.  

18 Contradictiones comprimit sors,  
et inter potentes quoque dijudicat.  

19 Frater qui adjuvatur a fratre quasi civitas firma,  
et judicia quasi vectes urbium.  

20 De fructu oris viri replebitur venter ejus,  
et genimina labiorum ipsius saturabunt eum.  

21 Mors et vita in manu linguæ;  
qui diligunt eam comedent fructus ejus.  

22 Qui invenit mulierem bonam invenit bonum,  
et hauriet jucunditatem a Domino.  
Qui expellit mulierem bonam expellit bonum;  
qui autem tenet adulteram stultus est et impius.  

23 Cum obsecrationibus loquetur pauper,  
et dives effabitur rigide.  

24 Vir amabilis ad societatem  
magis amicus erit quam frater.\]
