### Job 8

[KJV](../kjv/job008.md)

1  Then answered<sup>[6030](h6030)</sup> Bildad<sup>[1085](h1085.md)</sup>
the Shuhite,<sup>[7747](h7747)</sup> and said,<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup>

2  How long<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> <sup>[575](h0575.md)</sup> wilt thou
speak<sup>[4448](h4448)</sup> these<sup>[428](h0428.md)</sup> things? and
how long shall the words<sup>[561](h0561.md)</sup> of thy
mouth<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> be like a strong<sup>[3524](h3524.md)</sup>
wind?<sup>[7307](h7307.md)</sup>

3  Doth God<sup>[410](h0410)</sup> pervert<sup>[5791](h5791.md)</sup>
judgment?<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup> or<sup>[518](h0518.md)</sup> doth the
Almighty<sup>[7706](h7706)</sup> pervert<sup>[5791](h5791.md)</sup>
justice?<sup>[6664](h6664.md)</sup>

4  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> thy children<sup>[1121](h1121.md)</sup> have
sinned<sup>[2398](h2398.md)</sup> against him, and he have cast them
away<sup>[7971](h7971)</sup> for<sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup> their
transgression;<sup>[6588](h6588.md)</sup>

5  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> thou<sup>[859](h0859.md)</sup> wouldest seek
unto God quickly,<sup>[7836](h7836)</sup> <sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup>
<sup>[410](h0410.md)</sup> and make thy
supplication<sup>[2603](h2603)</sup> to<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup> the
Almighty;<sup>[7706](h7706.md)</sup>

6  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> thou<sup>[859](h0859.md)</sup> wast
pure<sup>[2134](h2134)</sup> and upright;<sup>[3477](h3477.md)</sup>
surely<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> now<sup>[6258](h6258.md)</sup> he would
awake<sup>[5782](h5782)</sup> for<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> thee, and make
the habitation<sup>[5116](h5116.md)</sup> of thy
righteousness<sup>[6664](h6664.md)</sup>
prosperous.<sup>[7999](h7999.md)</sup>

7  Though thy beginning<sup>[7225](h7225.md)</sup>
was<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> small,<sup>[4705](h4705.md)</sup> yet thy
latter end<sup>[319](h0319)</sup> should greatly<sup>[3966](h3966.md)</sup>
increase.<sup>[7685](h7685.md)</sup>

8  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> inquire,<sup>[7592](h7592.md)</sup> I pray
thee,<sup>[4994](h4994)</sup> of the former<sup>[7223](h7223.md)</sup>
age,<sup>[1755](h1755)</sup> and prepare<sup>[3559](h3559.md)</sup> thyself
to the search<sup>[2714](h2714.md)</sup> of their
fathers:<sup>[1](h0001.md)</sup>

9  (For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> we<sup>[587](h0587.md)</sup> are but of
yesterday,<sup>[8543](h8543)</sup> and know<sup>[3045](h3045.md)</sup>
nothing,<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> because<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup> our
days<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
earth<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> are a shadow:)<sup>[6738](h6738.md)</sup>

10  Shall not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> they<sup>[1992](h1992.md)</sup>
teach<sup>[3384](h3384)</sup> thee, and tell<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup>
thee, and utter<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup> words<sup>[4405](h4405.md)</sup>
out of their heart?<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup>

11  Can the rush<sup>[1573](h1573)</sup> grow up<sup>[1342](h1342.md)</sup>
without<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> mire?<sup>[1207](h1207.md)</sup> can the
flag<sup>[260](h0260)</sup> grow<sup>[7685](h7685.md)</sup>
without<sup>[1097](h1097)</sup> water?<sup>[4325](h4325.md)</sup>

12  Whilst it is yet<sup>[5750](h5750.md)</sup> in his
greenness,<sup>[3](h0003)</sup> and not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> cut
down,<sup>[6998](h6998)</sup> it withereth<sup>[3001](h3001.md)</sup>
before<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> any<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup> other
herb.<sup>[2682](h2682.md)</sup>

13  So<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> are the paths<sup>[734](h0734.md)</sup> of
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> that forget<sup>[7911](h7911.md)</sup>
God;<sup>[410](h0410)</sup> and the hypocrite's<sup>[2611](h2611.md)</sup>
hope<sup>[8615](h8615)</sup> shall perish:<sup>[6](h0006.md)</sup>

14  Whose<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> hope<sup>[3689](h3689.md)</sup> shall be
cut off,<sup>[6990](h6990)</sup> and whose trust<sup>[4009](h4009.md)</sup>
shall be a spider's<sup>[5908](h5908)</sup> web.<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup>

15  He shall lean<sup>[8172](h8172)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
his house,<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup> but it shall
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> stand:<sup>[5975](h5975.md)</sup> he shall hold
it fast,<sup>[2388](h2388.md)</sup> but it shall
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> endure.<sup>[6965](h6965.md)</sup>

16  He<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> is green<sup>[7373](h7373.md)</sup>
before<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> the sun,<sup>[8121](h8121.md)</sup> and his
branch<sup>[3127](h3127)</sup> shooteth forth<sup>[3318](h3318.md)</sup>
in<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> his garden.<sup>[1593](h1593.md)</sup>

17  His roots<sup>[8328](h8328.md)</sup> are
wrapped<sup>[5440](h5440)</sup> about<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> the
heap,<sup>[1530](h1530)</sup> and seeth<sup>[2372](h2372.md)</sup> the
place<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> of stones.<sup>[68](h0068.md)</sup>

18  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> he destroy<sup>[1104](h1104.md)</sup> him
from his place,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[4725](h4725.md)</sup> then it
shall deny<sup>[3584](h3584.md)</sup> him, saying, I have
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> seen<sup>[7200](h7200.md)</sup> thee.

19  Behold,<sup>[2005](h2005)</sup> this<sup>[1931](h1931.md)</sup> is the
joy<sup>[4885](h4885)</sup> of his way,<sup>[1870](h1870.md)</sup> and out
of the earth<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6083](h6083.md)</sup> shall
others<sup>[312](h0312)</sup> grow.<sup>[6779](h6779.md)</sup>

20  Behold,<sup>[2005](h2005)</sup> God<sup>[410](h0410.md)</sup> will
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> cast away<sup>[3988](h3988.md)</sup> a
perfect<sup>[8535](h8535)</sup> man, neither<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
will he help<sup>[2388](h2388)</sup> <sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup> the
evildoers:<sup>[7489](h7489.md)</sup>

21  Till<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> he fill<sup>[4390](h4390.md)</sup> thy
mouth<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> with laughing,<sup>[7814](h7814.md)</sup> and
thy lips<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> with rejoicing.<sup>[8643](h8643.md)</sup>

22  They that hate<sup>[8130](h8130.md)</sup> thee shall be
clothed<sup>[3847](h3847)</sup> with shame;<sup>[1322](h1322.md)</sup> and
the dwelling place<sup>[168](h0168.md)</sup> of the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup> shall come to
naught.<sup>[369](h0369.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/job008.md)

1 ὑπολαβὼν δὲ Βαλδαδ ὁ Σαυχίτης λέγει

2 μέχρι τίνος λαλήσεις ταῦτα πνεῦμα πολυρῆμον τοῦ στόματός σου

3 μὴ ὁ κύριος ἀδικήσει κρίνων ἢ ὁ τὰ πάντα ποιήσας ταράξει τὸ δίκαιον

4 εἰ οἱ υἱοί σου ἥμαρτον ἐναντίον αὐτοῦ ἀπέστειλεν ἐν χειρὶ ἀνομίας
αὐτῶν

5 σὺ δὲ ὄρθριζε πρὸς κύριον παντοκράτορα δεόμενος

6 εἰ καθαρὸς εἶ καὶ ἀληθινός δεήσεως ἐπακούσεταί σου ἀποκαταστήσει δέ
σοι δίαιταν δικαιοσύνης

7 ἔσται οὖν τὰ μὲν πρῶτά σου ὀλίγα τὰ δὲ ἔσχατά σου ἀμύθητα

8 ἐπερώτησον γὰρ γενεὰν πρώτην ἐξιχνίασον δὲ κατὰ γένος πατέρων

9 χθιζοὶ γάρ ἐσμεν καὶ οὐκ οἴδαμεν σκιὰ γάρ ἐστιν ἡμῶν ἐπὶ τῆς γῆς ὁ
βίος

10 ἦ οὐχ οὗτοί σε διδάξουσιν καὶ ἀναγγελοῦσιν καὶ ἐκ καρδίας ἐξάξουσιν
ῥήματα

11 μὴ θάλλει πάπυρος ἄνευ ὕδατος ἢ ὑψωθήσεται βούτομον ἄνευ πότου

12 ἔτι ὂν ἐπὶ ῥίζης καὶ οὐ μὴ θερισθῇ πρὸ τοῦ πιεῖν πᾶσα βοτάνη οὐχὶ
ξηραίνεται

13 οὕτως τοίνυν ἔσται τὰ ἔσχατα πάντων τῶν ἐπιλανθανομένων τοῦ κυρίου
ἐλπὶς γὰρ ἀσεβοῦς ἀπολεῖται

14 ἀοίκητος γὰρ αὐτοῦ ἔσται ὁ οἶκος ἀράχνη δὲ αὐτοῦ ἀποβήσεται ἡ σκηνή

15 ἐὰν ὑπερείσῃ τὴν οἰκίαν αὐτοῦ οὐ μὴ στῇ ἐπιλαβομένου δὲ αὐτοῦ οὐ μὴ
ὑπομείνῃ

16 ὑγρὸς γάρ ἐστιν ὑπὸ ἡλίου καὶ ἐκ σαπρίας αὐτοῦ ὁ ῥάδαμνος αὐτοῦ
ἐξελεύσεται

17 ἐπὶ συναγωγὴν λίθων κοιμᾶται ἐν δὲ μέσῳ χαλίκων ζήσεται

18 ἐὰν καταπίῃ ὁ τόπος ψεύσεται αὐτόν οὐχ ἑόρακας τοιαῦτα

19 ὅτι καταστροφὴ ἀσεβοῦς τοιαύτη ἐκ δὲ γῆς ἄλλον ἀναβλαστήσει

20 ὁ γὰρ κύριος οὐ μὴ ἀποποιήσηται τὸν ἄκακον πᾶν δὲ δῶρον ἀσεβοῦς οὐ
δέξεται

21 ἀληθινῶν δὲ στόμα ἐμπλήσει γέλωτος τὰ δὲ χείλη αὐτῶν ἐξομολογήσεως

22 οἱ δὲ ἐχθροὶ αὐτῶν ἐνδύσονται αἰσχύνην δίαιτα δὲ ἀσεβοῦς οὐκ ἔσται

[Vul](../vul/job008.md)

1 Respondens autem Baldad Suhites, dixit:

2 \[Usquequo loqueris talia,  
et spiritus multiplex sermones oris tui?  

3 Numquid Deus supplantat judicium?  
aut Omnipotens subvertit quod justum est?  

4 Etiam si filii tui peccaverunt ei,  
et dimisit eos in manu iniquitatis suæ:  

5 tu tamen si diluculo consurrexeris ad Deum,  
et Omnipotentem fueris deprecatus;  

6 si mundus et rectus incesseris:  
statim evigilabit ad te,  
et pacatum reddet habitaculum justitiæ tuæ,  

7 in tantum ut si priora tua fuerint parva,  
et novissima tua multiplicentur nimis.  

8 Interroga enim generationem pristinam,  
et diligenter investiga patrum memoriam  

9 (hesterni quippe sumus, et ignoramus,  
quoniam sicut umbra dies nostri sunt super terram),  

10 et ipsi docebunt te, loquentur tibi,  
et de corde suo proferent eloquia.  

11 Numquid vivere potest scirpus absque humore?  
aut crescere carectum sine aqua?  

12 Cum adhuc sit in flore, nec carpatur manu,  
ante omnes herbas arescit.  

13 Sic viæ omnium qui obliviscuntur Deum,  
et spes hypocritæ peribit.  

14 Non ei placebit vecordia sua,  
et sicut tela aranearum fiducia ejus.  

15 Innitetur super domum suam, et non stabit;  
fulciet eam, et non consurget.  

16 Humectus videtur antequam veniat sol,  
et in ortu suo germen ejus egredietur.  

17 Super acervum petrarum radices ejus densabuntur,  
et inter lapides commorabitur.  

18 Si absorbuerit eum de loco suo,  
negabit eum, et dicet: Non novi te.  

19 Hæc est enim lætitia viæ ejus,  
ut rursum de terra alii germinentur.  

20 Deus non projiciet simplicem,  
nec porriget manum malignis,  

21 donec impleatur risu os tuum,  
et labia tua jubilo.  

22 Qui oderunt te induentur confusione,  
et tabernaculum impiorum non subsistet.\]
