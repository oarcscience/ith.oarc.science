### Isaiah 32

[KJV](../kjv/isa032.md)

1  Behold,<sup>[2005](h2005)</sup> a king<sup>[4428](h4428.md)</sup> shall
reign<sup>[4427](h4427)</sup> in righteousness,<sup>[6664](h6664.md)</sup>
and princes<sup>[8269](h8269)</sup> shall rule<sup>[8323](h8323.md)</sup>
in judgment.<sup>[4941](h4941.md)</sup>

2  And a man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> shall be<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup> as
a hiding place<sup>[4224](h4224.md)</sup> from the
wind,<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> and a covert<sup>[5643](h5643.md)</sup> from
the tempest;<sup>[2230](h2230)</sup> as rivers<sup>[6388](h6388.md)</sup>
of water<sup>[4325](h4325)</sup> in a dry place,<sup>[6724](h6724.md)</sup>
as the shadow<sup>[6738](h6738)</sup> of a great<sup>[3515](h3515.md)</sup>
rock<sup>[5553](h5553)</sup> in a weary<sup>[5889](h5889.md)</sup>
land.<sup>[776](h0776.md)</sup>

3  And the eyes<sup>[5869](h5869.md)</sup> of them that
see<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> shall not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> be
dim,<sup>[8159](h8159)</sup> and the ears<sup>[241](h0241.md)</sup> of them
that hear<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> shall hearken.<sup>[7181](h7181.md)</sup>

4  The heart<sup>[3824](h3824.md)</sup> also of the
rash<sup>[4116](h4116)</sup> shall understand<sup>[995](h0995.md)</sup>
knowledge,<sup>[3045](h3045.md)</sup> and the
tongue<sup>[3956](h3956)</sup> of the stammerers<sup>[5926](h5926.md)</sup>
shall be ready<sup>[4116](h4116)</sup> to speak<sup>[1696](h1696.md)</sup>
plainly.<sup>[6703](h6703.md)</sup>

5  The vile person<sup>[5036](h5036.md)</sup> shall be
no<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> more<sup>[5750](h5750.md)</sup>
called<sup>[7121](h7121)</sup> liberal,<sup>[5081](h5081.md)</sup>
nor<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> the churl<sup>[3596](h3596.md)</sup>
said<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> to be bountiful.<sup>[7771](h7771.md)</sup>

6  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> the vile person<sup>[5036](h5036.md)</sup>
will speak<sup>[1696](h1696)</sup> villainy,<sup>[5039](h5039.md)</sup> and
his heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> will work<sup>[6213](h6213.md)</sup>
iniquity,<sup>[205](h0205)</sup> to practice<sup>[6213](h6213.md)</sup>
hypocrisy,<sup>[2612](h2612)</sup> and to utter<sup>[1696](h1696.md)</sup>
error<sup>[8442](h8442)</sup> against<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup> the
LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> to make empty<sup>[7324](h7324.md)</sup> the
soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> of the hungry,<sup>[7457](h7457.md)</sup> and
he will cause the drink<sup>[4945](h4945.md)</sup> of the
thirsty<sup>[6771](h6771)</sup> to fail.<sup>[2637](h2637.md)</sup>

7  The instruments<sup>[3627](h3627.md)</sup> also of the
churl<sup>[3596](h3596)</sup> are evil:<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup>
he<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> deviseth<sup>[3289](h3289.md)</sup> wicked
devices<sup>[2154](h2154)</sup> to destroy<sup>[2254](h2254.md)</sup> the
poor<sup>[6041](h6041)</sup> with lying<sup>[8267](h8267.md)</sup>
words,<sup>[561](h0561)</sup> even when the needy<sup>[34](h0034.md)</sup>
speaketh<sup>[1696](h1696)</sup> right.<sup>[4941](h4941.md)</sup>

8  But the liberal<sup>[5081](h5081.md)</sup>
deviseth<sup>[3289](h3289)</sup> liberal things;<sup>[5081](h5081.md)</sup>
and by<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> liberal things<sup>[5081](h5081.md)</sup>
shall he stand.<sup>[6965](h6965.md)</sup>

9  Rise up,<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> ye women<sup>[802](h0802.md)</sup> that
are at ease;<sup>[7600](h7600)</sup> hear<sup>[8085](h8085.md)</sup> my
voice,<sup>[6963](h6963)</sup> ye careless<sup>[982](h0982.md)</sup>
daughters;<sup>[1323](h1323)</sup> give ear<sup>[238](h0238.md)</sup> unto
my speech.<sup>[565](h0565.md)</sup>

10  Many days<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> and years<sup>[8141](h8141.md)</sup>
shall ye be troubled,<sup>[7264](h7264.md)</sup> ye careless
women:<sup>[982](h0982)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup> the
vintage<sup>[1210](h1210)</sup> shall fail,<sup>[3615](h3615.md)</sup> the
gathering<sup>[625](h0625)</sup> shall not<sup>[1097](h1097.md)</sup>
come.<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup>

11  Tremble,<sup>[2729](h2729.md)</sup> ye women that are at
ease;<sup>[7600](h7600)</sup> be troubled,<sup>[7264](h7264.md)</sup> ye
careless ones:<sup>[982](h0982)</sup> strip<sup>[6584](h6584.md)</sup> you,
and make you bare,<sup>[6209](h6209.md)</sup> and
gird<sup>[2290](h2290)</sup> sackcloth upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> your
loins.<sup>[2504](h2504.md)</sup>

12  They shall lament<sup>[5594](h5594.md)</sup>
for<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the teats,<sup>[7699](h7699.md)</sup>
for<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the pleasant<sup>[2531](h2531.md)</sup>
fields,<sup>[7704](h7704)</sup> for<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> the
fruitful<sup>[6509](h6509)</sup> vine.<sup>[1612](h1612.md)</sup>

13  Upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the land<sup>[127](h0127.md)</sup> of my
people<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup> shall come up<sup>[5927](h5927.md)</sup>
thorns<sup>[6975](h6975)</sup> and briers;<sup>[8068](h8068.md)</sup>
yea,<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> the houses<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup> of
joy<sup>[4885](h4885)</sup> in the joyous<sup>[5947](h5947.md)</sup>
city:<sup>[7151](h7151.md)</sup>

14  Because<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> the palaces<sup>[759](h0759.md)</sup>
shall be forsaken;<sup>[5203](h5203.md)</sup> the
multitude<sup>[1995](h1995)</sup> of the city<sup>[5892](h5892.md)</sup>
shall be left;<sup>[5800](h5800)</sup> the forts<sup>[6076](h6076.md)</sup>
and towers<sup>[975](h0975)</sup> shall be<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup>
for<sup>[1157](h1157)</sup> dens<sup>[4631](h4631.md)</sup>
forever,<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> <sup>[5769](h5769.md)</sup> a
joy<sup>[4885](h4885)</sup> of wild asses,<sup>[6501](h6501.md)</sup> a
pasture<sup>[4829](h4829)</sup> of flocks;<sup>[5739](h5739.md)</sup>

15  Until<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> the spirit<sup>[7307](h7307.md)</sup> be
poured<sup>[6168](h6168)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> us from on
high,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[4791](h4791.md)</sup> and the
wilderness<sup>[4057](h4057)</sup> be<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup> a fruitful
field,<sup>[3759](h3759.md)</sup> and the fruitful
field<sup>[3759](h3759)</sup> be counted<sup>[2803](h2803.md)</sup> for a
forest.<sup>[3293](h3293.md)</sup>

16  Then judgment<sup>[4941](h4941.md)</sup> shall
dwell<sup>[7931](h7931)</sup> in the wilderness,<sup>[4057](h4057.md)</sup>
and righteousness<sup>[6666](h6666)</sup> remain<sup>[3427](h3427.md)</sup>
in the fruitful field.<sup>[3759](h3759.md)</sup>

17  And the work<sup>[4639](h4639.md)</sup> of
righteousness<sup>[6666](h6666)</sup> shall be<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup>
peace;<sup>[7965](h7965)</sup> and the effect<sup>[5656](h5656.md)</sup> of
righteousness<sup>[6666](h6666)</sup> quietness<sup>[8252](h8252.md)</sup>
and assurance<sup>[983](h0983)</sup> forever.<sup>[5704](h5704.md)</sup>
<sup>[5769](h5769.md)</sup>

18  And my people<sup>[5971](h5971.md)</sup> shall
dwell<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup> in a peaceable<sup>[7965](h7965.md)</sup>
habitation,<sup>[5116](h5116)</sup> and in sure<sup>[4009](h4009.md)</sup>
dwellings,<sup>[4908](h4908)</sup> and in quiet<sup>[7600](h7600.md)</sup>
resting places;<sup>[4496](h4496.md)</sup>

19  When it shall hail,<sup>[1258](h1258.md)</sup> coming
down<sup>[3381](h3381)</sup> on the forest;<sup>[3293](h3293.md)</sup> and
the city<sup>[5892](h5892)</sup> shall be low<sup>[8213](h8213.md)</sup> in
a low place.<sup>[8218](h8218.md)</sup>

20  Blessed<sup>[835](h0835.md)</sup> are ye that
sow<sup>[2232](h2232)</sup> beside<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> waters,<sup>[4325](h4325.md)</sup> that send
forth<sup>[7971](h7971)</sup> thither the feet<sup>[7272](h7272.md)</sup>
of the ox<sup>[7794](h7794)</sup> and the ass.<sup>[2543](h2543.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/isa032.md)

1 ἰδοὺ γὰρ βασιλεὺς δίκαιος βασιλεύσει καὶ ἄρχοντες μετὰ κρίσεως
ἄρξουσιν

2 καὶ ἔσται ὁ ἄνθρωπος κρύπτων τοὺς λόγους αὐτοῦ καὶ κρυβήσεται ὡς ἀφ᾽
ὕδατος φερομένου καὶ φανήσεται ἐν Σιων ὡς ποταμὸς φερόμενος ἔνδοξος ἐν
γῇ διψώσῃ

3 καὶ οὐκέτι ἔσονται πεποιθότες ἐπ᾽ ἀνθρώποις ἀλλὰ τὰ ὦτα δώσουσιν
ἀκούειν

4 καὶ ἡ καρδία τῶν ἀσθενούντων προσέξει τοῦ ἀκούειν καὶ αἱ γλῶσσαι αἱ
ψελλίζουσαι ταχὺ μαθήσονται λαλεῖν εἰρήνην

5 καὶ οὐκέτι μὴ εἴπωσιν τῷ μωρῷ ἄρχειν καὶ οὐκέτι μὴ εἴπωσιν οἱ ὑπηρέται
σου σίγα

6 ὁ γὰρ μωρὸς μωρὰ λαλήσει καὶ ἡ καρδία αὐτοῦ μάταια νοήσει τοῦ
συντελεῖν ἄνομα καὶ λαλεῖν πρὸς κύριον πλάνησιν τοῦ διασπεῖραι ψυχὰς
πεινώσας καὶ τὰς ψυχὰς τὰς διψώσας κενὰς ποιῆσαι

7 ἡ γὰρ βουλὴ τῶν πονηρῶν ἄνομα βουλεύσεται καταφθεῖραι ταπεινοὺς ἐν
λόγοις ἀδίκοις καὶ διασκεδάσαι λόγους ταπεινῶν ἐν κρίσει

8 οἱ δὲ εὐσεβεῖς συνετὰ ἐβουλεύσαντο καὶ αὕτη ἡ βουλὴ μενεῖ

9 γυναῖκες πλούσιαι ἀνάστητε καὶ ἀκούσατε τῆς φωνῆς μου θυγατέρες ἐν
ἐλπίδι ἀκούσατε τοὺς λόγους μου

10 ἡμέρας ἐνιαυτοῦ μνείαν ποιήσασθε ἐν ὀδύνῃ μετ᾽ ἐλπίδος ἀνήλωται ὁ
τρύγητος πέπαυται ὁ σπόρος καὶ οὐκέτι μὴ ἔλθῃ

11 ἔκστητε λυπήθητε αἱ πεποιθυῖαι ἐκδύσασθε γυμναὶ γένεσθε περιζώσασθε
σάκκους τὰς ὀσφύας

12 καὶ ἐπὶ τῶν μαστῶν κόπτεσθε ἀπὸ ἀγροῦ ἐπιθυμήματος καὶ ἀμπέλου
γενήματος

13 ἡ γῆ τοῦ λαοῦ μου ἄκανθα καὶ χόρτος ἀναβήσεται καὶ ἐκ πάσης οἰκίας
εὐφροσύνη ἀρθήσεται πόλις πλουσία

14 οἶκοι ἐγκαταλελειμμένοι πλοῦτον πόλεως καὶ οἴκους ἐπιθυμητοὺς
ἀφήσουσιν καὶ ἔσονται αἱ κῶμαι σπήλαια ἕως τοῦ αἰῶνος εὐφροσύνη ὄνων
ἀγρίων βοσκήματα ποιμένων

15 ἕως ἂν ἐπέλθῃ ἐφ᾽ ὑμᾶς πνεῦμα ἀφ᾽ ὑψηλοῦ καὶ ἔσται ἔρημος ὁ Χερμελ
καὶ ὁ Χερμελ εἰς δρυμὸν λογισθήσεται

16 καὶ ἀναπαύσεται ἐν τῇ ἐρήμῳ κρίμα καὶ δικαιοσύνη ἐν τῷ Καρμήλῳ
κατοικήσει

17 καὶ ἔσται τὰ ἔργα τῆς δικαιοσύνης εἰρήνη καὶ κρατήσει ἡ δικαιοσύνη
ἀνάπαυσιν καὶ πεποιθότες ἕως τοῦ αἰῶνος

18 καὶ κατοικήσει ὁ λαὸς αὐτοῦ ἐν πόλει εἰρήνης καὶ ἐνοικήσει πεποιθώς
καὶ ἀναπαύσονται μετὰ πλούτου

19 ἡ δὲ χάλαζα ἐὰν καταβῇ οὐκ ἐφ᾽ ὑμᾶς ἥξει καὶ ἔσονται οἱ ἐνοικοῦντες
ἐν τοῖς δρυμοῖς πεποιθότες ὡς οἱ ἐν τῇ πεδινῇ

20 μακάριοι οἱ σπείροντες ἐπὶ πᾶν ὕδωρ οὗ βοῦς καὶ ὄνος πατεῖ

[Vul](../vul/isa032.md)

1 \[Ecce in justitia regnabit rex,  
et principes in judicio præerunt.  

2 Et erit vir sicut qui absconditur a vento,  
et celat se a tempestate;  
sicut rivi aquarum in siti,  
et umbra petræ prominentis in terra deserta.  

3 Non caligabunt oculi videntium,  
et aures audientium diligenter auscultabunt.  

4 Et cor stultorum intelliget scientiam,  
et lingua balborum velociter loquetur et plane.  

5 Non vocabitur ultra is qui insipiens est, princeps,  
neque fraudulentus appellabitur major;  

6 stultus enim fatua loquetur,  
et cor ejus faciet iniquitatem,  
ut perficiat simulationem,  
et loquatur ad Dominum fraudulenter,  
et vacuam faciat animam esurientis,  
et potum sitienti auferat.  

7 Fraudulenti vasa pessima sunt;  
ipse enim cogitationes concinnavit  
ad perdendos mites in sermone mendacii,  
cum loqueretur pauper judicium.  

8 Princeps vero ea quæ digna sunt principe cogitabit,  
et ipse super duces stabit.\]  

9 \[Mulieres opulentæ, surgite,  
et audite vocem meam;  
filiæ confidentes,  
percipite auribus eloquium meum.  

10 Post dies enim et annum,  
vos conturbabimini confidentes;  
consummata est enim vindemia,  
collectio ultra non veniet.  

11 Obstupescite, opulentæ;  
conturbamini, confidentes:  
exuite vos et confundimini;  
accingite lumbos vestros.  

12 Super ubera plangite,  
super regione desiderabili,  
super vinea fertili.  

13 Super humum populi mei  
spinæ et vepres ascendent:  
quanto magis super omnes domos gaudii  
civitatis exultantis!  

14 Domus enim dimissa est,  
multitudo urbis relicta est,  
tenebræ et palpatio  
factæ sunt super speluncas usque in æternum;  
gaudium onagrorum,  
pascua gregum.  

15 Donec effundatur super nos  
spiritus de excelso,  
et erit desertum in carmel,  
et carmel in saltum reputabitur.  

16 Et habitabit in solitudine judicium,  
et justitia in carmel sedebit.  

17 Et erit opus justitiæ pax,  
et cultus justitiæ silentium,  
et securitas usque in sempiternum.  

18 Et sedebit populus meus in pulchritudine pacis,  
et in tabernaculis fiduciæ,  
et in requie opulenta.  

19 Grando autem in descensione saltus,  
et humilitate humiliabitur civitas.  

20 Beati qui seminatis super omnes aquas,  
immittentes pedem bovis et asini.\]
