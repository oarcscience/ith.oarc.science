### Song of Solomon 8

[KJV](../kjv/sol008.md)

1  O that<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup> <sup>[5414](h5414.md)</sup> thou wast as
my brother,<sup>[251](h0251)</sup> that sucked<sup>[3243](h3243.md)</sup>
the breasts<sup>[7699](h7699)</sup> of my mother!<sup>[517](h0517.md)</sup>
when I should find<sup>[4672](h4672.md)</sup> thee
without,<sup>[2351](h2351)</sup> I would kiss<sup>[5401](h5401.md)</sup>
thee; yea,<sup>[1571](h1571)</sup> I should not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
be despised.<sup>[936](h0936.md)</sup>

2  I would lead<sup>[5090](h5090.md)</sup> thee, and
bring<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> thee into<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup> my
mother's<sup>[517](h0517)</sup> house,<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup> who would
instruct<sup>[3925](h3925.md)</sup> me: I would cause thee to
drink<sup>[8248](h8248)</sup> of spiced<sup>[7544](h7544.md)</sup>
wine<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3196](h3196.md)</sup> of the
juice<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6071](h6071.md)</sup> of my
pomegranate.<sup>[7416](h7416.md)</sup>

3  His left hand<sup>[8040](h8040.md)</sup> should be
under<sup>[8478](h8478)</sup> my head,<sup>[7218](h7218.md)</sup> and his
right hand<sup>[3225](h3225.md)</sup> should
embrace<sup>[2263](h2263.md)</sup> me.

4  I charge<sup>[7650](h7650.md)</sup> you, O
daughters<sup>[1323](h1323)</sup> of Jerusalem,<sup>[3389](h3389.md)</sup>
that<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> ye stir not up,<sup>[5782](h5782.md)</sup>
nor<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> awake<sup>[5782](h5782.md)</sup>
<sup>[853](h0853)</sup> my love,<sup>[160](h0160.md)</sup>
until<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> he please.<sup>[7945](h7945.md)</sup>
<sup>[2654](h2654.md)</sup>

5  Who<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup> is this<sup>[2063](h2063.md)</sup> that
cometh up<sup>[5927](h5927)</sup> from<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup> the
wilderness,<sup>[4057](h4057)</sup> leaning<sup>[7514](h7514.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> her beloved?<sup>[1730](h1730.md)</sup> I
raised thee up<sup>[5782](h5782)</sup> under<sup>[8478](h8478.md)</sup> the
apple tree:<sup>[8598](h8598)</sup> there<sup>[8033](h8033.md)</sup> thy
mother<sup>[517](h0517.md)</sup> brought thee
forth:<sup>[2254](h2254)</sup> there<sup>[8033](h8033.md)</sup> she brought
thee forth<sup>[2254](h2254)</sup> that bore<sup>[3205](h3205.md)</sup>
thee.

6  Set<sup>[7760](h7760)</sup> me as a seal<sup>[2368](h2368.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> thine heart,<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup> as a
seal<sup>[2368](h2368)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> thine
arm:<sup>[2220](h2220)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup>
love<sup>[160](h0160)</sup> is strong<sup>[5794](h5794.md)</sup> as
death;<sup>[4194](h4194)</sup> jealousy<sup>[7068](h7068.md)</sup> is
cruel<sup>[7186](h7186)</sup> as the grave:<sup>[7585](h7585.md)</sup> the
coals<sup>[7565](h7565)</sup> thereof are coals<sup>[7565](h7565.md)</sup>
of fire,<sup>[784](h0784.md)</sup> which hath a most vehement
flame.<sup>[7957](h7957.md)</sup>

7  Many<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup> waters<sup>[4325](h4325.md)</sup>
cannot<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> <sup>[3201](h3201.md)</sup>
<sup>[853](h0853)</sup> quench<sup>[3518](h3518.md)</sup>
love,<sup>[160](h0160)</sup> neither<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> can the
floods<sup>[5104](h5104)</sup> drown<sup>[7857](h7857.md)</sup> it:
if<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> a man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> would
give<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> <sup>[853](h0853.md)</sup>
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> the substance<sup>[1952](h1952.md)</sup> of his
house<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> for love,<sup>[160](h0160.md)</sup> it would
utterly be contemned.<sup>[936](h0936)</sup> <sup>[936](h0936.md)</sup>

8  We have a little<sup>[6996](h6996.md)</sup>
sister,<sup>[269](h0269)</sup> and she hath no<sup>[369](h0369.md)</sup>
breasts:<sup>[7699](h7699)</sup> what<sup>[4100](h4100.md)</sup> shall we
do<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> for our sister<sup>[269](h0269.md)</sup> in the
day<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup> when she shall be spoken
for?<sup>[7945](h7945)</sup> <sup>[1696](h1696.md)</sup>

9  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> she<sup>[1931](h1931.md)</sup> be a
wall,<sup>[2346](h2346)</sup> we will build<sup>[1129](h1129.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> her a palace<sup>[2918](h2918.md)</sup> of
silver:<sup>[3701](h3701)</sup> and if<sup>[518](h0518.md)</sup>
she<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> be a door,<sup>[1817](h1817.md)</sup> we will
enclose<sup>[6696](h6696)</sup> <sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> her with
boards<sup>[3871](h3871)</sup> of cedar.<sup>[730](h0730.md)</sup>

10  I<sup>[589](h0589)</sup> am a wall,<sup>[2346](h2346.md)</sup> and my
breasts<sup>[7699](h7699)</sup> like towers:<sup>[4026](h4026.md)</sup>
then<sup>[227](h0227)</sup> was<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup> I in his
eyes<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> as one that found<sup>[4672](h4672.md)</sup>
favor.<sup>[7965](h7965.md)</sup>

11  Solomon<sup>[8010](h8010)</sup> had<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup> a
vineyard<sup>[3754](h3754)</sup> at Baal-hamon;<sup>[1174](h1174.md)</sup>
he let out<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> <sup>[853](h0853.md)</sup> the
vineyard<sup>[3754](h3754)</sup> unto keepers;<sup>[5201](h5201.md)</sup>
every one<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> for the fruit<sup>[6529](h6529.md)</sup>
thereof was to bring<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup> a
thousand<sup>[505](h0505.md)</sup> pieces of
silver.<sup>[3701](h3701.md)</sup>

12  My vineyard,<sup>[3754](h3754.md)</sup> which is
mine,<sup>[7945](h7945)</sup> is before<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> me:
thou, O Solomon,<sup>[8010](h8010.md)</sup> must have a
thousand,<sup>[505](h0505.md)</sup> and those that
keep<sup>[5201](h5201)</sup> <sup>[853](h0853.md)</sup> the
fruit<sup>[6529](h6529.md)</sup> thereof two
hundred.<sup>[3967](h3967.md)</sup>

13  Thou that dwellest<sup>[3427](h3427.md)</sup> in the
gardens,<sup>[1588](h1588)</sup> the companions<sup>[2270](h2270.md)</sup>
hearken<sup>[7181](h7181)</sup> to thy voice:<sup>[6963](h6963.md)</sup>
cause me to hear<sup>[8085](h8085.md)</sup> it.

14  Make haste,<sup>[1272](h1272.md)</sup> my
beloved,<sup>[1730](h1730.md)</sup> and be thou
like<sup>[1819](h1819)</sup> to a roe<sup>[6643](h6643.md)</sup>
or<sup>[176](h0176)</sup> to a young<sup>[6082](h6082.md)</sup>
hart<sup>[354](h0354)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> the
mountains<sup>[2022](h2022)</sup> of spices.<sup>[1314](h1314.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/sol008.md)

1 τίς δῴη σε ἀδελφιδόν μου θηλάζοντα μαστοὺς μητρός μου εὑροῦσά σε ἔξω
φιλήσω σε καί γε οὐκ ἐξουδενώσουσίν μοι

2 παραλήμψομαί σε εἰσάξω σε εἰς οἶκον μητρός μου καὶ εἰς ταμίειον τῆς
συλλαβούσης με ποτιῶ σε ἀπὸ οἴνου τοῦ μυρεψικοῦ ἀπὸ νάματος ῥοῶν μου

3 εὐώνυμος αὐτοῦ ὑπὸ τὴν κεφαλήν μου καὶ ἡ δεξιὰ αὐτοῦ περιλήμψεταί με

4 ὥρκισα ὑμᾶς θυγατέρες Ιερουσαλημ ἐν ταῖς δυνάμεσιν καὶ ἐν ταῖς
ἰσχύσεσιν τοῦ ἀγροῦ τί ἐγείρητε καὶ τί ἐξεγείρητε τὴν ἀγάπην ἕως ἂν
θελήσῃ

5 τίς αὕτη ἡ ἀναβαίνουσα λελευκανθισμένη ἐπιστηριζομένη ἐπὶ τὸν
ἀδελφιδὸν αὐτῆς ὑπὸ μῆλον ἐξήγειρά σε ἐκεῖ ὠδίνησέν σε ἡ μήτηρ σου ἐκεῖ
ὠδίνησέν σε ἡ τεκοῦσά σου

6 θές με ὡς σφραγῖδα ἐπὶ τὴν καρδίαν σου ὡς σφραγῖδα ἐπὶ τὸν βραχίονά
σου ὅτι κραταιὰ ὡς θάνατος ἀγάπη σκληρὸς ὡς ᾅδης ζῆλος περίπτερα αὐτῆς
περίπτερα πυρός φλόγες αὐτῆς

7 ὕδωρ πολὺ οὐ δυνήσεται σβέσαι τὴν ἀγάπην καὶ ποταμοὶ οὐ συγκλύσουσιν
αὐτήν ἐὰν δῷ ἀνὴρ τὸν πάντα βίον αὐτοῦ ἐν τῇ ἀγάπῃ ἐξουδενώσει
ἐξουδενώσουσιν αὐτόν

8 ἀδελφὴ ἡμῖν μικρὰ καὶ μαστοὺς οὐκ ἔχει τί ποιήσωμεν τῇ ἀδελφῇ ἡμῶν ἐν
ἡμέρᾳ ᾗ ἐὰν λαληθῇ ἐν αὐτῇ

9 εἰ τεῖχός ἐστιν οἰκοδομήσωμεν ἐπ᾽ αὐτὴν ἐπάλξεις ἀργυρᾶς καὶ εἰ θύρα
ἐστίν διαγράψωμεν ἐπ᾽ αὐτὴν σανίδα κεδρίνην

10 ἐγὼ τεῖχος καὶ μαστοί μου ὡς πύργοι ἐγὼ ἤμην ἐν ὀφθαλμοῖς αὐτοῦ ὡς
εὑρίσκουσα εἰρήνην

11 ἀμπελὼν ἐγενήθη τῷ Σαλωμων ἐν Βεελαμων ἔδωκεν τὸν ἀμπελῶνα αὐτοῦ τοῖς
τηροῦσιν ἀνὴρ οἴσει ἐν καρπῷ αὐτοῦ χιλίους ἀργυρίου

12 ἀμπελών μου ἐμὸς ἐνώπιόν μου οἱ χίλιοι σοί Σαλωμων καὶ οἱ διακόσιοι
τοῖς τηροῦσι τὸν καρπὸν αὐτοῦ

13 ὁ καθήμενος ἐν κήποις ἑταῖροι προσέχοντες τῇ φωνῇ σου ἀκούτισόν με

14 φύγε ἀδελφιδέ μου καὶ ὁμοιώθητι τῇ δορκάδι ἢ τῷ νεβρῷ τῶν ἐλάφων ἐπὶ
ὄρη ἀρωμάτων

[Vul](../vul/sol008.md)

1 \[Quis mihi det te fratrem meum,  
sugentem ubera matris meæ,  
ut inveniam te foris, et deosculer te,  
et jam me nemo despiciat?  

2 Apprehendam te, et ducam in domum matris meæ:  
ibi me docebis,  
et dabo tibi poculum ex vino condito,  
et mustum malorum granatorum meorum.  

3 Læva ejus sub capite meo,  
et dextera illius amplexabitur me.  

4 Adjuro vos, filiæ Jerusalem,  
ne suscitetis, neque evigilare faciatis dilectam,  
donec ipsa velit.  

5 Quæ est ista quæ ascendit de deserto, deliciis affluens,  
innixa super dilectum suum?  
Sub arbore malo suscitavi te;  
ibi corrupta est mater tua,  
ibi violata est genitrix tua.  

6 Pone me ut signaculum super cor tuum,  
ut signaculum super brachium tuum,  
quia fortis est ut mors dilectio,  
dura sicut infernus æmulatio:  
lampades ejus lampades ignis atque flammarum.  

7 Aquæ multæ non potuerunt extinguere caritatem,  
nec flumina obruent illam.  
Si dederit homo omnem substantiam domus suæ pro dilectione,  
quasi nihil despiciet eam.  

8 Soror nostra parva,  
et ubera non habet;  
quid faciemus sorori nostræ  
in die quando alloquenda est?  

9 Si murus est,  
ædificemus super eum propugnacula argentea;  
si ostium est, compingamus illud tabulis cedrinis.  

10 Ego murus, et ubera mea sicut turris,  
ex quo facta sum coram eo, quasi pacem reperiens.  

11 Vinea fuit pacifico in ea quæ habet populos:  
tradidit eam custodibus;  
vir affert pro fructu ejus mille argenteos.  

12 Vinea mea coram me est.  
Mille tui pacifici,  
et ducenti his qui custodiunt fructus ejus.  

13 Quæ habitas in hortis, amici auscultant;  
fac me audire vocem tuam.  

14 Fuge, dilecte mi, et assimilare capreæ,  
hinnuloque cervorum super montes aromatum.\]
