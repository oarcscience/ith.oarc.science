H582  
אנושׁ  
אֱנוֹשׁ ‎ ‘ĕnôsh  
*en-oshe‘*  
From [605](h0605.md) properly a *mortal* (and thus differeing from the more
dignified [120](h0120.md) ; hence a *man* in general (singly or
collectively). It is often unexpressed in the English Version,
especially when used in apposition with another word: - another, X
\[blood-\] thirsty, certain, chap \[-man\], divers, fellow, X in the
flower of their age, husband, (certain, mortal) man, people, person,
servant, some (X of them), + stranger, those, + their trade. It is often
unexpressed in the Engl. version, especially when used in apposition
with another word. Compare [376](h0376.md)
