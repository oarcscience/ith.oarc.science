### Proverbs 11

[KJV](../kjv/pro011.md)

1  A false<sup>[4820](h4820)</sup> balance<sup>[3976](h3976.md)</sup> is
abomination<sup>[8441](h8441)</sup> to the LORD:<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup>
but a just<sup>[8003](h8003)</sup> weight<sup>[68](h0068.md)</sup> is his
delight.<sup>[7522](h7522.md)</sup>

2  When pride<sup>[2087](h2087)</sup> cometh,<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup>
then cometh<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> shame:<sup>[7036](h7036.md)</sup> but
with<sup>[854](h0854)</sup> the lowly<sup>[6800](h6800.md)</sup> is
wisdom.<sup>[2451](h2451.md)</sup>

3  The integrity<sup>[8538](h8538.md)</sup> of the
upright<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup> shall guide<sup>[5148](h5148.md)</sup>
them: but the perverseness<sup>[5558](h5558.md)</sup> of
transgressors<sup>[898](h0898.md)</sup> shall
destroy<sup>[7703](h7703.md)</sup> them.

4  Riches<sup>[1952](h1952)</sup> profit<sup>[3276](h3276.md)</sup>
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> in the day<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup> of
wrath:<sup>[5678](h5678)</sup> but righteousness<sup>[6666](h6666.md)</sup>
delivereth<sup>[5337](h5337)</sup> from death.<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[4194](h4194.md)</sup>

5  The righteousness<sup>[6666](h6666.md)</sup> of the
perfect<sup>[8549](h8549)</sup> shall direct<sup>[3474](h3474.md)</sup> his
way:<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> but the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup>
shall fall<sup>[5307](h5307.md)</sup> by his own
wickedness.<sup>[7564](h7564.md)</sup>

6  The righteousness<sup>[6666](h6666.md)</sup> of the
upright<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup> shall deliver<sup>[5337](h5337.md)</sup>
them: but transgressors<sup>[898](h0898.md)</sup> shall be
taken<sup>[3920](h3920.md)</sup> in their own
naughtiness.<sup>[1942](h1942.md)</sup>

7  When a wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> man<sup>[120](h0120.md)</sup>
dieth,<sup>[4194](h4194)</sup> his expectation<sup>[8615](h8615.md)</sup>
shall perish:<sup>[6](h0006)</sup> and the hope<sup>[8431](h8431.md)</sup>
of unjust<sup>[205](h0205)</sup> men perisheth.<sup>[6](h0006.md)</sup>

8  The righteous<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> is
delivered<sup>[2502](h2502.md)</sup> out of
trouble,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6869](h6869.md)</sup> and the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> cometh<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup> in his
stead.<sup>[8478](h8478.md)</sup>

9  A hypocrite<sup>[2611](h2611.md)</sup> with his
mouth<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> destroyeth<sup>[7843](h7843.md)</sup> his
neighbor:<sup>[7453](h7453.md)</sup> but through
knowledge<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup> shall the just<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup>
be delivered.<sup>[2502](h2502.md)</sup>

10  When it goeth well<sup>[2898](h2898.md)</sup> with the
righteous,<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> the city<sup>[7151](h7151.md)</sup>
rejoiceth:<sup>[5970](h5970.md)</sup> and when the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> perish,<sup>[6](h0006.md)</sup> there is
shouting.<sup>[7440](h7440.md)</sup>

11  By the blessing<sup>[1293](h1293.md)</sup> of the
upright<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup> the city<sup>[7176](h7176.md)</sup> is
exalted:<sup>[7311](h7311.md)</sup> but it is
overthrown<sup>[2040](h2040)</sup> by the mouth<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup>
of the wicked.<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup>

12  He that is void<sup>[2638](h2638.md)</sup> of
wisdom<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> despiseth<sup>[936](h0936.md)</sup> his
neighbor:<sup>[7453](h7453)</sup> but a man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> of
understanding<sup>[8394](h8394.md)</sup> holdeth his
peace.<sup>[2790](h2790.md)</sup>

13  A talebearer<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> <sup>[7400](h7400.md)</sup>
revealeth<sup>[1540](h1540)</sup> secrets:<sup>[5475](h5475.md)</sup> but
he that is of a faithful<sup>[539](h0539.md)</sup>
spirit<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> concealeth<sup>[3680](h3680.md)</sup> the
matter.<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup>

14  Where no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> counsel<sup>[8458](h8458.md)</sup> is,
the people<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup> fall:<sup>[5307](h5307.md)</sup> but in
the multitude<sup>[7230](h7230.md)</sup> of
counselors<sup>[3289](h3289.md)</sup> there is
safety.<sup>[8668](h8668.md)</sup>

15  He that is surety<sup>[6148](h6148.md)</sup> for a
stranger<sup>[2114](h2114)</sup> shall smart<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup>
<sup>[7489](h7489.md)</sup> for it: and he that
hateth<sup>[8130](h8130)</sup> suretiship<sup>[8628](h8628.md)</sup> is
sure.<sup>[982](h0982.md)</sup>

16  A gracious<sup>[2580](h2580)</sup> woman<sup>[802](h0802.md)</sup>
retaineth<sup>[8551](h8551)</sup> honor:<sup>[3519](h3519.md)</sup> and
strong<sup>[6184](h6184)</sup> men retain<sup>[8551](h8551.md)</sup>
riches.<sup>[6239](h6239.md)</sup>

17  The merciful<sup>[2617](h2617)</sup> man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup>
doeth good<sup>[1580](h1580.md)</sup> to his own
soul:<sup>[5315](h5315.md)</sup> but he that is
cruel<sup>[394](h0394)</sup> troubleth<sup>[5916](h5916.md)</sup> his own
flesh.<sup>[7607](h7607.md)</sup>

18  The wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> worketh<sup>[6213](h6213.md)</sup> a
deceitful<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> work:<sup>[6468](h6468.md)</sup> but to
him that soweth<sup>[2232](h2232.md)</sup>
righteousness<sup>[6666](h6666.md)</sup> shall be a
sure<sup>[571](h0571)</sup> reward.<sup>[7938](h7938.md)</sup>

19  As<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> righteousness<sup>[6666](h6666.md)</sup>
tendeth to life:<sup>[2416](h2416.md)</sup> so he that
pursueth<sup>[7291](h7291)</sup> evil<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup> pursueth
it to his own death.<sup>[4194](h4194.md)</sup>

20  They that are of a froward<sup>[6141](h6141.md)</sup>
heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> are abomination<sup>[8441](h8441.md)</sup> to
the LORD:<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup> but such as are
upright<sup>[8549](h8549)</sup> in their way<sup>[1870](h1870.md)</sup> are
his delight.<sup>[7522](h7522.md)</sup>

21  Though hand<sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup> join in
hand,<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> the wicked<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup> shall
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> be unpunished:<sup>[5352](h5352.md)</sup> but
the seed<sup>[2233](h2233.md)</sup> of the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> shall be
delivered.<sup>[4422](h4422.md)</sup>

22  As a jewel<sup>[5141](h5141)</sup> of gold<sup>[2091](h2091.md)</sup>
in a swine's<sup>[2386](h2386)</sup> snout,<sup>[639](h0639.md)</sup> so is
a fair<sup>[3303](h3303)</sup> woman<sup>[802](h0802.md)</sup> which is
without<sup>[5493](h5493)</sup> discretion.<sup>[2940](h2940.md)</sup>

23  The desire<sup>[8378](h8378.md)</sup> of the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> is only<sup>[389](h0389.md)</sup>
good:<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup> but the
expectation<sup>[8615](h8615.md)</sup> of the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> is wrath.<sup>[5678](h5678.md)</sup>

24  There is<sup>[3426](h3426.md)</sup> that
scattereth,<sup>[6340](h6340)</sup> and yet<sup>[5750](h5750.md)</sup>
increaseth;<sup>[3254](h3254.md)</sup> and there is that
withholdeth<sup>[2820](h2820.md)</sup> more than is
meet,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3476](h3476.md)</sup> but it tendeth to
poverty.<sup>[4270](h4270.md)</sup>

25  The liberal<sup>[1293](h1293)</sup> soul<sup>[5315](h5315.md)</sup>
shall be made fat:<sup>[1878](h1878.md)</sup> and he that
watereth<sup>[7301](h7301.md)</sup> shall be
watered<sup>[3384](h3384)</sup> also<sup>[1571](h1571.md)</sup>
himself.<sup>[1931](h1931.md)</sup>

26  He that withholdeth<sup>[4513](h4513.md)</sup>
corn,<sup>[1250](h1250)</sup> the people<sup>[3816](h3816.md)</sup> shall
curse<sup>[6895](h6895)</sup> him: but blessing<sup>[1293](h1293.md)</sup>
shall be upon the head<sup>[7218](h7218.md)</sup> of him that
selleth<sup>[7666](h7666.md)</sup> it.

27  He that diligently seeketh<sup>[7836](h7836.md)</sup>
good<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> procureth<sup>[1245](h1245.md)</sup>
favor:<sup>[7522](h7522.md)</sup> but he that
seeketh<sup>[1875](h1875)</sup> mischief,<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup> it
shall come<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup> unto him.

28  He<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> that trusteth<sup>[982](h0982.md)</sup> in
his riches<sup>[6239](h6239)</sup> shall fall:<sup>[5307](h5307.md)</sup>
but the righteous<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> shall
flourish<sup>[6524](h6524)</sup> as a branch.<sup>[5929](h5929.md)</sup>

29  He that troubleth<sup>[5916](h5916.md)</sup> his own
house<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> shall inherit<sup>[5157](h5157.md)</sup> the
wind:<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> and the fool<sup>[191](h0191.md)</sup> shall
be servant<sup>[5650](h5650)</sup> to the wise<sup>[2450](h2450.md)</sup>
of heart.<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup>

30  The fruit<sup>[6529](h6529.md)</sup> of the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> is a tree<sup>[6086](h6086.md)</sup> of
life;<sup>[2416](h2416.md)</sup> and he that
winneth<sup>[3947](h3947)</sup> souls<sup>[5315](h5315.md)</sup> is
wise.<sup>[2450](h2450.md)</sup>

31  Behold,<sup>[2005](h2005.md)</sup> the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> shall be
recompensed<sup>[7999](h7999)</sup> in the earth:<sup>[776](h0776.md)</sup>
much more<sup>[637](h0637)</sup> <sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup> the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> and the sinner.<sup>[2398](h2398.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/pro011.md)

1 ζυγοὶ δόλιοι βδέλυγμα ἐνώπιον κυρίου στάθμιον δὲ δίκαιον δεκτὸν αὐτῷ

2 οὗ ἐὰν εἰσέλθῃ ὕβρις ἐκεῖ καὶ ἀτιμία στόμα δὲ ταπεινῶν μελετᾷ σοφίαν

3 ἀποθανὼν δίκαιος ἔλιπεν μετάμελον πρόχειρος δὲ γίνεται καὶ ἐπίχαρτος
ἀσεβῶν ἀπώλεια

5 δικαιοσύνη ἀμώμους ὀρθοτομεῖ ὁδούς ἀσέβεια δὲ περιπίπτει ἀδικίᾳ

6 δικαιοσύνη ἀνδρῶν ὀρθῶν ῥύεται αὐτούς τῇ δὲ ἀπωλείᾳ αὐτῶν ἁλίσκονται
παράνομοι

7 τελευτήσαντος ἀνδρὸς δικαίου οὐκ ὄλλυται ἐλπίς τὸ δὲ καύχημα τῶν
ἀσεβῶν ὄλλυται

8 δίκαιος ἐκ θήρας ἐκδύνει ἀντ᾽ αὐτοῦ δὲ παραδίδοται ὁ ἀσεβής

9 ἐν στόματι ἀσεβῶν παγὶς πολίταις αἴσθησις δὲ δικαίων εὔοδος

10 ἐν ἀγαθοῖς δικαίων κατώρθωσεν πόλις

11 στόμασιν δὲ ἀσεβῶν κατεσκάφη

12 μυκτηρίζει πολίτας ἐνδεὴς φρενῶν ἀνὴρ δὲ φρόνιμος ἡσυχίαν ἄγει

13 ἀνὴρ δίγλωσσος ἀποκαλύπτει βουλὰς ἐν συνεδρίῳ πιστὸς δὲ πνοῇ κρύπτει
πράγματα

14 οἷς μὴ ὑπάρχει κυβέρνησις πίπτουσιν ὥσπερ φύλλα σωτηρία δὲ ὑπάρχει ἐν
πολλῇ βουλῇ

15 πονηρὸς κακοποιεῖ ὅταν συμμείξῃ δικαίῳ μισεῖ δὲ ἦχον ἀσφαλείας

16 γυνὴ εὐχάριστος ἐγείρει ἀνδρὶ δόξαν θρόνος δὲ ἀτιμίας γυνὴ μισοῦσα
δίκαια πλούτου ὀκνηροὶ ἐνδεεῖς γίνονται οἱ δὲ ἀνδρεῖοι ἐρείδονται πλούτῳ

17 τῇ ψυχῇ αὐτοῦ ἀγαθὸν ποιεῖ ἀνὴρ ἐλεήμων ἐξολλύει δὲ αὐτοῦ σῶμα ὁ
ἀνελεήμων

18 ἀσεβὴς ποιεῖ ἔργα ἄδικα σπέρμα δὲ δικαίων μισθὸς ἀληθείας

19 υἱὸς δίκαιος γεννᾶται εἰς ζωήν διωγμὸς δὲ ἀσεβοῦς εἰς θάνατον

20 βδέλυγμα κυρίῳ διεστραμμέναι ὁδοί προσδεκτοὶ δὲ αὐτῷ πάντες ἄμωμοι ἐν
ταῖς ὁδοῖς αὐτῶν

21 χειρὶ χεῖρας ἐμβαλὼν ἀδίκως οὐκ ἀτιμώρητος ἔσται ὁ δὲ σπείρων
δικαιοσύνην λήμψεται μισθὸν πιστόν

22 ὥσπερ ἐνώτιον ἐν ῥινὶ ὑός οὕτως γυναικὶ κακόφρονι κάλλος

23 ἐπιθυμία δικαίων πᾶσα ἀγαθή ἐλπὶς δὲ ἀσεβῶν ἀπολεῖται

24 εἰσὶν οἳ τὰ ἴδια σπείροντες πλείονα ποιοῦσιν εἰσὶν καὶ οἳ συνάγοντες
ἐλαττονοῦνται

25 ψυχὴ εὐλογουμένη πᾶσα ἁπλῆ ἀνὴρ δὲ θυμώδης οὐκ εὐσχήμων

26 ὁ συνέχων σῖτον ὑπολίποιτο αὐτὸν τοῖς ἔθνεσιν εὐλογία δὲ εἰς κεφαλὴν
τοῦ μεταδιδόντος

27 τεκταινόμενος ἀγαθὰ ζητεῖ χάριν ἀγαθήν ἐκζητοῦντα δὲ κακά
καταλήμψεται αὐτόν

28 ὁ πεποιθὼς ἐπὶ πλούτῳ οὗτος πεσεῖται ὁ δὲ ἀντιλαμβανόμενος δικαίων
οὗτος ἀνατελεῖ

29 ὁ μὴ συμπεριφερόμενος τῷ ἑαυτοῦ οἴκῳ κληρονομήσει ἄνεμον δουλεύσει δὲ
ἄφρων φρονίμῳ

30 ἐκ καρποῦ δικαιοσύνης φύεται δένδρον ζωῆς ἀφαιροῦνται δὲ ἄωροι ψυχαὶ
παρανόμων

31 εἰ ὁ μὲν δίκαιος μόλις σῴζεται ὁ ἀσεβὴς καὶ ἁμαρτωλὸς ποῦ φανεῖται

‎23 ‏תַּאֲוַ֣ת צַדִּיקִ֣ים אַךְ־ט֑וֹב תִּקְוַ֖ת רְשָׁעִ֣ים עֶבְרָֽה׃

[Vul](../vul/pro011.md)

1 \[Statera dolosa abominatio est apud Dominum,  
et pondus æquum voluntas ejus.  

2 Ubi fuerit superbia, ibi erit et contumelia;  
ubi autem est humilitas, ibi et sapientia.  

3 Simplicitas justorum diriget eos,  
et supplantatio perversorum vastabit illos.  

4 Non proderunt divitiæ in die ultionis;  
justitia autem liberabit a morte.  

5 Justitia simplicis diriget viam ejus,  
et in impietate sua corruet impius.  

6 Justitia rectorum liberabit eos,  
et in insidiis suis capientur iniqui.  

7 Mortuo homine impio, nulla erit ultra spes,  
et exspectatio sollicitorum peribit.  

8 Justus de angustia liberatus est,  
et tradetur impius pro eo.\]  

9 \[Simulator ore decipit amicum suum;  
justi autem liberabuntur scientia.  

10 In bonis justorum exsultabit civitas,  
et in perditione impiorum erit laudatio.  

11 Benedictione justorum exaltabitur civitas,  
et ore impiorum subvertetur.  

12 Qui despicit amicum suum indigens corde est;  
vir autem prudens tacebit.  

13 Qui ambulat fraudulenter, revelat arcana;  
qui autem fidelis est animi, celat amici commissum.  

14 Ubi non est gubernator, populus corruet;  
salus autem, ubi multa consilia.  

15 Affligetur malo qui fidem facit pro extraneo;  
qui autem cavet laqueos securus erit.  

16 Mulier gratiosa inveniet gloriam,  
et robusti habebunt divitias.\]  

17 \[Benefacit animæ suæ vir misericors;  
qui autem crudelis est, etiam propinquos abjicit.  

18 Impius facit opus instabile,  
seminanti autem justitiam merces fidelis.  

19 Clementia præparat vitam,  
et sectatio malorum mortem.  

20 Abominabile Domino cor pravum,  
et voluntas ejus in iis qui simpliciter ambulant.  

21 Manus in manu non erit innocens malus;  
semen autem justorum salvabitur.  

22 Circulus aureus in naribus suis,  
mulier pulchra et fatua.  

23 Desiderium justorum omne bonum est;  
præstolatio impiorum furor.  

24 Alii dividunt propria, et ditiores fiunt;  
alii rapiunt non sua, et semper in egestate sunt.  

25 Anima quæ benedicit impinguabitur,  
et qui inebriat, ipse quoque inebriabitur.  

26 Qui abscondit frumenta maledicetur in populis;  
benedictio autem super caput vendentium.  

27 Bene consurgit diluculo qui quærit bona;  
qui autem investigator malorum est, opprimetur ab eis.  

28 Qui confidit in divitiis suis corruet:  
justi autem quasi virens folium germinabunt.  

29 Qui conturbat domum suam possidebit ventos,  
et qui stultus est serviet sapienti.  

30 Fructus justi lignum vitæ,  
et qui suscipit animas sapiens est.  

31 Si justus in terra recipit,  
quanto magis impius et peccator!\]
