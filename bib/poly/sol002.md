### Song of Solomon 2

[KJV](../kjv/sol002)

1  I<sup>[589](h0589)</sup> am the rose<sup>[2261](h2261)</sup> of
Sharon,<sup>[8289](h8289)</sup> and the lily<sup>[7799](h7799)</sup> of
the valleys.<sup>[6010](h6010)</sup>

2  As the lily<sup>[7799](h7799)</sup> among<sup>[996](h0996)</sup>
thorns,<sup>[2336](h2336)</sup> so<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> is my
love<sup>[7474](h7474)</sup> among<sup>[996](h0996)</sup> the
daughters.<sup>[1323](h1323)</sup>

3  As the apple tree<sup>[8598](h8598)</sup> among the
trees<sup>[6086](h6086)</sup> of the wood,<sup>[3293](h3293)</sup>
so<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> is my beloved<sup>[1730](h1730)</sup>
among<sup>[996](h0996)</sup> the sons.<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> I sat
down<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup> under his shadow<sup>[6738](h6738)</sup>
with great delight,<sup>[2530](h2530)</sup> and his
fruit<sup>[6529](h6529)</sup> was sweet<sup>[4966](h4966)</sup> to my
taste.<sup>[2441](h2441)</sup>

4  He brought<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> me to<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> the
banqueting<sup>[3196](h3196)</sup> house,<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> and
his banner<sup>[1714](h1714)</sup> over<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> me was
love.<sup>[160](h0160)</sup>

5  Stay<sup>[5564](h5564)</sup> me with flagons,<sup>[809](h0809)</sup>
comfort<sup>[7502](h7502)</sup> me with apples:<sup>[8598](h8598)</sup>
for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> I<sup>[589](h0589)</sup> am
sick<sup>[2470](h2470)</sup> of love.<sup>[160](h0160)</sup>

6  His left hand<sup>[8040](h8040)</sup> is
under<sup>[8478](h8478)</sup> my head,<sup>[7218](h7218)</sup> and his
right hand<sup>[3225](h3225)</sup> doth embrace<sup>[2263](h2263)</sup>
me.

7  I charge<sup>[7650](h7650)</sup> you, O ye
daughters<sup>[1323](h1323)</sup> of Jerusalem,<sup>[3389](h3389)</sup>
by the roes,<sup>[6643](h6643)</sup> and<sup>[176](h0176)</sup> by the
hinds<sup>[355](h0355)</sup> of the field,<sup>[7704](h7704)</sup> that
ye stir not up,<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> <sup>[5782](h5782)</sup>
nor<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> awake<sup>[5782](h5782)</sup>
<sup>[853](h0853)</sup> my love,<sup>[160](h0160)</sup> till he
please.<sup>[7945](h7945)</sup> <sup>[2654](h2654)</sup>

8  The voice<sup>[6963](h6963)</sup> of my
beloved!<sup>[1730](h1730)</sup> behold,<sup>[2009](h2009)</sup>
he<sup>[2088](h2088)</sup> cometh<sup>[935](h0935)</sup>
leaping<sup>[1801](h1801)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the
mountains,<sup>[2022](h2022)</sup> skipping<sup>[7092](h7092)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the hills.<sup>[1389](h1389)</sup>

9  My beloved<sup>[1730](h1730)</sup> is like<sup>[1819](h1819)</sup> a
roe<sup>[6643](h6643)</sup> or<sup>[176](h0176)</sup> a
young<sup>[6082](h6082)</sup> hart:<sup>[354](h0354)</sup>
behold,<sup>[2009](h2009)</sup> he<sup>[2088](h2088)</sup>
standeth<sup>[5975](h5975)</sup> behind<sup>[310](h0310)</sup> our
wall,<sup>[3796](h3796)</sup> he looketh forth<sup>[7688](h7688)</sup>
at<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> the windows,<sup>[2474](h2474)</sup> showing
himself<sup>[6692](h6692)</sup> through<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> the
lattice.<sup>[2762](h2762)</sup>

10  My beloved<sup>[1730](h1730)</sup> spoke,<sup>[6030](h6030)</sup>
and said<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> unto me, Rise
up,<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> my love,<sup>[7474](h7474)</sup> my fair
one,<sup>[3303](h3303)</sup> and come away.<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup>

11  For,<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> lo,<sup>[2009](h2009)</sup> the
winter<sup>[5638](h5638)</sup> is past,<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup> the
rain<sup>[1653](h1653)</sup> is over<sup>[2498](h2498)</sup> and
gone;<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup>

12  The flowers<sup>[5339](h5339)</sup> appear<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup>
on the earth;<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> the time<sup>[6256](h6256)</sup> of
the singing<sup>[2158](h2158)</sup> of birds is
come,<sup>[5060](h5060)</sup> and the voice<sup>[6963](h6963)</sup> of
the turtle<sup>[8449](h8449)</sup> is heard<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> in
our land;<sup>[776](h0776)</sup>

13  The fig tree<sup>[8384](h8384)</sup> putteth
forth<sup>[2590](h2590)</sup> her green figs,<sup>[6291](h6291)</sup>
and the vines<sup>[1612](h1612)</sup> with the tender
grape<sup>[5563](h5563)</sup> give<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> a good
smell.<sup>[7381](h7381)</sup> Arise,<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> my
love,<sup>[7474](h7474)</sup> my fair one,<sup>[3303](h3303)</sup> and
come away.<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup>

14  O my dove,<sup>[3123](h3123)</sup> that art in the
clefts<sup>[2288](h2288)</sup> of the rock,<sup>[5553](h5553)</sup> in
the secret<sup>[5643](h5643)</sup> places of the
stairs,<sup>[4095](h4095)</sup> let me see<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup>
<sup>[853](h0853)</sup> thy countenance,<sup>[4758](h4758)</sup> let me
hear<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> <sup>[853](h0853)</sup> thy
voice;<sup>[6963](h6963)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup>
sweet<sup>[6156](h6156)</sup> is thy voice,<sup>[6963](h6963)</sup> and
thy countenance<sup>[4758](h4758)</sup> is
comely.<sup>[5000](h5000)</sup>

15  Take<sup>[270](h0270)</sup> us the foxes,<sup>[7776](h7776)</sup>
the little<sup>[6996](h6996)</sup> foxes,<sup>[7776](h7776)</sup> that
spoil<sup>[2254](h2254)</sup> the vines:<sup>[3754](h3754)</sup> for our
vines<sup>[3754](h3754)</sup> have tender
grapes.<sup>[5563](h5563)</sup>

16  My beloved<sup>[1730](h1730)</sup> is mine, and
I<sup>[589](h0589)</sup> am his: he feedeth<sup>[7462](h7462)</sup>
among the lilies.<sup>[7799](h7799)</sup>

17  Until<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> the day<sup>[7945](h7945)</sup>
<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> break,<sup>[6315](h6315)</sup> and the
shadows<sup>[6752](h6752)</sup> flee away,<sup>[5127](h5127)</sup>
turn,<sup>[5437](h5437)</sup> my beloved,<sup>[1730](h1730)</sup> and be
thou like<sup>[1819](h1819)</sup> a roe<sup>[6643](h6643)</sup>
or<sup>[176](h0176)</sup> a young<sup>[6082](h6082)</sup>
hart<sup>[354](h0354)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the
mountains<sup>[2022](h2022)</sup> of Bether.<sup>[1336](h1336)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/sol002)

1 ἐγὼ ἄνθος τοῦ πεδίου κρίνον τῶν κοιλάδων

2 ὡς κρίνον ἐν μέσῳ ἀκανθῶν οὕτως ἡ πλησίον μου ἀνὰ μέσον τῶν θυγατέρων

3 ὡς μῆλον ἐν τοῖς ξύλοις τοῦ δρυμοῦ οὕτως ἀδελφιδός μου ἀνὰ μέσον τῶν
υἱῶν ἐν τῇ σκιᾷ αὐτοῦ ἐπεθύμησα καὶ ἐκάθισα καὶ καρπὸς αὐτοῦ γλυκὺς ἐν
λάρυγγί μου

4 εἰσαγάγετέ με εἰς οἶκον τοῦ οἴνου τάξατε ἐπ᾽ ἐμὲ ἀγάπην

5 στηρίσατέ με ἐν ἀμόραις στοιβάσατέ με ἐν μήλοις ὅτι τετρωμένη ἀγάπης
ἐγώ

6 εὐώνυμος αὐτοῦ ὑπὸ τὴν κεφαλήν μου καὶ ἡ δεξιὰ αὐτοῦ περιλήμψεταί με

7 ὥρκισα ὑμᾶς θυγατέρες Ιερουσαλημ ἐν ταῖς δυνάμεσιν καὶ ἐν ταῖς
ἰσχύσεσιν τοῦ ἀγροῦ ἐὰν ἐγείρητε καὶ ἐξεγείρητε τὴν ἀγάπην ἕως οὗ θελήσῃ

8 φωνὴ ἀδελφιδοῦ μου ἰδοὺ οὗτος ἥκει πηδῶν ἐπὶ τὰ ὄρη διαλλόμενος ἐπὶ
τοὺς βουνούς

9 ὅμοιός ἐστιν ἀδελφιδός μου τῇ δορκάδι ἢ νεβρῷ ἐλάφων ἐπὶ τὰ ὄρη Βαιθηλ
ἰδοὺ οὗτος ἕστηκεν ὀπίσω τοῦ τοίχου ἡμῶν παρακύπτων διὰ τῶν θυρίδων
ἐκκύπτων διὰ τῶν δικτύων

10 ἀποκρίνεται ἀδελφιδός μου καὶ λέγει μοι ἀνάστα ἐλθέ ἡ πλησίον μου
καλή μου περιστερά μου

11 ὅτι ἰδοὺ ὁ χειμὼν παρῆλθεν ὁ ὑετὸς ἀπῆλθεν ἐπορεύθη ἑαυτῷ

12 τὰ ἄνθη ὤφθη ἐν τῇ γῇ καιρὸς τῆς τομῆς ἔφθακεν φωνὴ τοῦ τρυγόνος
ἠκούσθη ἐν τῇ γῇ ἡμῶν

13 ἡ συκῆ ἐξήνεγκεν ὀλύνθους αὐτῆς αἱ ἄμπελοι κυπρίζουσιν ἔδωκαν ὀσμήν
ἀνάστα ἐλθέ ἡ πλησίον μου καλή μου περιστερά μου

14 καὶ ἐλθὲ σύ περιστερά μου ἐν σκέπῃ τῆς πέτρας ἐχόμενα τοῦ
προτειχίσματος δεῖξόν μοι τὴν ὄψιν σου καὶ ἀκούτισόν με τὴν φωνήν σου
ὅτι ἡ φωνή σου ἡδεῖα καὶ ἡ ὄψις σου ὡραία

15 πιάσατε ἡμῖν ἀλώπεκας μικροὺς ἀφανίζοντας ἀμπελῶνας καὶ αἱ ἄμπελοι
ἡμῶν κυπρίζουσιν

16 ἀδελφιδός μου ἐμοί κἀγὼ αὐτῷ ὁ ποιμαίνων ἐν τοῖς κρίνοις

17 ἕως οὗ διαπνεύσῃ ἡ ἡμέρα καὶ κινηθῶσιν αἱ σκιαί ἀπόστρεψον ὁμοιώθητι
σύ ἀδελφιδέ μου τῷ δόρκωνι ἢ νεβρῷ ἐλάφων ἐπὶ ὄρη κοιλωμάτων

1 \[Ego flos campi,  
et lilium convallium.  

2 Sicut lilium inter spinas,  
sic amica mea inter filias.  

3 Sicut malus inter ligna silvarum,  
sic dilectus meus inter filios.  
Sub umbra illius quem desideraveram sedi,  
et fructus ejus dulcis gutturi meo.  

4 Introduxit me in cellam vinariam;  
ordinavit in me caritatem.  

5 Fulcite me floribus,  
stipate me malis,  
quia amore langueo.  

6 Læva ejus sub capite meo,  
et dextera illius amplexabitur me.  

7 Adjuro vos, filiæ Jerusalem,  
per capreas cervosque camporum,  
ne suscitetis, neque evigilare faciatis dilectam,  
quoadusque ipsa velit.  

8 Vox dilecti mei; ecce iste venit,  
saliens in montibus, transiliens colles.  

9 Similis est dilectus meus capreæ,  
hinnuloque cervorum.  
En ipse stat post parietem nostrum,  
respiciens per fenestras,  
prospiciens per cancellos.  

10 En dilectus meus loquitur mihi.  
Surge, propera, amica mea,  
columba mea, formosa mea, et veni:  

11 jam enim hiems transiit;  
imber abiit, et recessit.  

12 Flores apparuerunt in terra nostra;  
tempus putationis advenit:  
vox turturis audita est in terra nostra;  

13 ficus protulit grossos suos;  
vineæ florentes dederunt odorem suum.  
Surge, amica mea, speciosa mea, et veni:  

14 columba mea, in foraminibus petræ, in caverna maceriæ,  
ostende mihi faciem tuam,  
sonet vox tua in auribus meis:  
vox enim tua dulcis, et facies tua decora.  

15 Capite nobis vulpes parvulas  
quæ demoliuntur vineas:  
nam vinea nostra floruit.  

16 Dilectus meus mihi, et ego illi,  
qui pascitur inter lilia,  

17 donec aspiret dies, et inclinentur umbræ.  
Revertere; similis esto, dilecte mi, capreæ,  
hinnuloque cervorum super montes Bether.\]
