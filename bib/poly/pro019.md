### Proverbs 19

[KJV](../kjv/pro019)

1  Better<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> is the poor<sup>[7326](h7326)</sup>
that walketh<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> in his
integrity,<sup>[8537](h8537)</sup> than he that is
perverse<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6141](h6141)</sup> in his
lips,<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> and is a fool.<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup>

2  Also,<sup>[1571](h1571)</sup> that the soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup>
be without<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> knowledge,<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup> it
is not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> good;<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> and he that
hasteth<sup>[213](h0213)</sup> with his feet<sup>[7272](h7272)</sup>
sinneth.<sup>[2398](h2398)</sup>

3  The foolishness<sup>[200](h0200)</sup> of man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup>
perverteth<sup>[5557](h5557)</sup> his way:<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> and
his heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> fretteth<sup>[2196](h2196)</sup>
against<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the LORD.<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>

4  Wealth<sup>[1952](h1952)</sup> maketh<sup>[3254](h3254)</sup>
many<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup> friends;<sup>[7453](h7453)</sup> but the
poor<sup>[1800](h1800)</sup> is separated<sup>[6504](h6504)</sup> from
his neighbor.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7453](h7453)</sup>

5  A false<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> witness<sup>[5707](h5707)</sup> shall
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> be unpunished,<sup>[5352](h5352)</sup> and
he that speaketh<sup>[6315](h6315)</sup> lies<sup>[3577](h3577)</sup>
shall not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> escape.<sup>[4422](h4422)</sup>

6  Many<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup> will entreat<sup>[2470](h2470)</sup> the
favor<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> of the prince:<sup>[5081](h5081)</sup> and
every man<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> is a friend<sup>[7453](h7453)</sup> to
him that giveth gifts.<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> <sup>[4976](h4976)</sup>

7  All<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> the brethren<sup>[251](h0251)</sup> of
the poor<sup>[7326](h7326)</sup> do hate<sup>[8130](h8130)</sup> him:
how much more<sup>[637](h0637)</sup> <sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> do his
friends<sup>[4828](h4828)</sup> go far<sup>[7368](h7368)</sup>
from<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> him? he pursueth<sup>[7291](h7291)</sup>
them with words,<sup>[561](h0561)</sup> yet they<sup>[1992](h1992)</sup>
are wanting<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> to him.

8  He that getteth<sup>[7069](h7069)</sup>
wisdom<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> loveth<sup>[157](h0157)</sup> his own
soul:<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> he that keepeth<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup>
understanding<sup>[8394](h8394)</sup> shall find<sup>[4672](h4672)</sup>
good.<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup>

9  A false<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> witness<sup>[5707](h5707)</sup> shall
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> be unpunished,<sup>[5352](h5352)</sup> and
he that speaketh<sup>[6315](h6315)</sup> lies<sup>[3577](h3577)</sup>
shall perish.<sup>[6](h0006)</sup>

10  Delight<sup>[8588](h8588)</sup> is not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
seemly<sup>[5000](h5000)</sup> for a fool;<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> much
less<sup>[637](h0637)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> a
servant<sup>[5650](h5650)</sup> to have rule<sup>[4910](h4910)</sup>
over princes.<sup>[8269](h8269)</sup>

11  The discretion<sup>[7922](h7922)</sup> of a
man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> deferreth<sup>[748](h0748)</sup> his
anger;<sup>[639](h0639)</sup> and it is his
glory<sup>[8597](h8597)</sup> to pass<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup>
over<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> a transgression.<sup>[6588](h6588)</sup>

12  The king's<sup>[4428](h4428)</sup> wrath<sup>[2197](h2197)</sup> is
as the roaring<sup>[5099](h5099)</sup> of a
lion;<sup>[3715](h3715)</sup> but his favor<sup>[7522](h7522)</sup> is
as dew<sup>[2919](h2919)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the
grass.<sup>[6212](h6212)</sup>

13  A foolish<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> son<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> is the
calamity<sup>[1942](h1942)</sup> of his father:<sup>[1](h0001)</sup> and
the contentions<sup>[4079](h4079)</sup> of a wife<sup>[802](h0802)</sup>
are a continual<sup>[2956](h2956)</sup>
dropping.<sup>[1812](h1812)</sup>

14  House<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> and riches<sup>[1952](h1952)</sup> are
the inheritance<sup>[5159](h5159)</sup> of fathers:<sup>[1](h0001)</sup>
and a prudent<sup>[7919](h7919)</sup> wife<sup>[802](h0802)</sup> is
from the LORD.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>

15  Slothfulness<sup>[6103](h6103)</sup> casteth<sup>[5307](h5307)</sup>
into a deep sleep;<sup>[8639](h8639)</sup> and an
idle<sup>[7423](h7423)</sup> soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> shall suffer
hunger.<sup>[7456](h7456)</sup>

16  He that keepeth<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> the
commandment<sup>[4687](h4687)</sup> keepeth<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> his
own soul;<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> but he that
despiseth<sup>[959](h0959)</sup> his ways<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> shall
die.<sup>[4191](h4191)</sup>

17  He that hath pity<sup>[2603](h2603)</sup> upon the
poor<sup>[1800](h1800)</sup> lendeth<sup>[3867](h3867)</sup> unto the
LORD;<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> and that which he hath
given<sup>[1576](h1576)</sup> will he pay him
again.<sup>[7999](h7999)</sup>

18  Chasten<sup>[3256](h3256)</sup> thy son<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup>
while<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> there is<sup>[3426](h3426)</sup>
hope,<sup>[8615](h8615)</sup> and let not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> thy
soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> spare<sup>[5375](h5375)</sup>
for<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> his crying.<sup>[4191](h4191)</sup>

19  A man of great<sup>[1419](h1419)</sup> wrath<sup>[2534](h2534)</sup>
shall suffer<sup>[5375](h5375)</sup> punishment:<sup>[6066](h6066)</sup>
for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> if<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> thou
deliver<sup>[5337](h5337)</sup> him, yet<sup>[5750](h5750)</sup> thou
must do it again.<sup>[3254](h3254)</sup>

20  Hear<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> counsel,<sup>[6098](h6098)</sup> and
receive<sup>[6901](h6901)</sup> instruction,<sup>[4148](h4148)</sup>
that<sup>[4616](h4616)</sup> thou mayest be wise<sup>[2449](h2449)</sup>
in thy latter<sup>[319](h0319)</sup> end.

21  There are many<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup>
devices<sup>[4284](h4284)</sup> in a man's<sup>[376](h0376)</sup>
heart;<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> nevertheless the
counsel<sup>[6098](h6098)</sup> of the LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>
that<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> shall stand.<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup>

22  The desire<sup>[8378](h8378)</sup> of a man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup>
is his kindness:<sup>[2617](h2617)</sup> and a
poor<sup>[7326](h7326)</sup> man<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> is better<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> than a
liar.<sup>[3577](h3577)</sup>

23  The fear<sup>[3374](h3374)</sup> of the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>
tendeth to life:<sup>[2416](h2416)</sup> and he that hath it shall
abide<sup>[3885](h3885)</sup> satisfied;<sup>[7649](h7649)</sup> he
shall not<sup>[1077](h1077)</sup> be visited<sup>[6485](h6485)</sup>
with evil.<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup>

24  A slothful<sup>[6102](h6102)</sup> man
hideth<sup>[2934](h2934)</sup> his hand<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> in his
bosom,<sup>[6747](h6747)</sup> and will not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> so
much<sup>[1571](h1571)</sup> as bring it to his mouth
again.<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> <sup>[6310](h6310)</sup>
<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup>

25  Smite<sup>[5221](h5221)</sup> a scorner,<sup>[3887](h3887)</sup> and
the simple<sup>[6612](h6612)</sup> will beware:<sup>[6191](h6191)</sup>
and reprove<sup>[3198](h3198)</sup> one that hath
understanding,<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> and he will
understand<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> knowledge.<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup>

26  He that wasteth<sup>[7703](h7703)</sup> his
father,<sup>[1](h0001)</sup> and chaseth away<sup>[1272](h1272)</sup>
his mother,<sup>[517](h0517)</sup> is a son<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> that
causeth shame,<sup>[954](h0954)</sup> and bringeth
reproach.<sup>[2659](h2659)</sup>

27  Cease,<sup>[2308](h2308)</sup> my son,<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> to
hear<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> the instruction<sup>[4148](h4148)</sup>
that causeth to err<sup>[7686](h7686)</sup> from the
words<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[561](h0561)</sup> of
knowledge.<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup>

28  An ungodly<sup>[1100](h1100)</sup> witness<sup>[5707](h5707)</sup>
scorneth<sup>[3887](h3887)</sup> judgment:<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup> and
the mouth<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> of the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup>
devoureth<sup>[1104](h1104)</sup> iniquity.<sup>[205](h0205)</sup>

29  Judgments<sup>[8201](h8201)</sup> are
prepared<sup>[3559](h3559)</sup> for scorners,<sup>[3887](h3887)</sup>
and stripes<sup>[4112](h4112)</sup> for the back<sup>[1460](h1460)</sup>
of fools.<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup>

3 ἀφροσύνη ἀνδρὸς λυμαίνεται τὰς ὁδοὺς αὐτοῦ τὸν δὲ θεὸν αἰτιᾶται τῇ
καρδίᾳ αὐτοῦ

4 πλοῦτος προστίθησιν φίλους πολλούς ὁ δὲ πτωχὸς καὶ ἀπὸ τοῦ ὑπάρχοντος
φίλου λείπεται

5 μάρτυς ψευδὴς οὐκ ἀτιμώρητος ἔσται ὁ δὲ ἐγκαλῶν ἀδίκως οὐ διαφεύξεται

6 πολλοὶ θεραπεύουσιν πρόσωπα βασιλέων πᾶς δὲ ὁ κακὸς γίνεται ὄνειδος
ἀνδρί

7 πᾶς ὃς ἀδελφὸν πτωχὸν μισεῖ καὶ φιλίας μακρὰν ἔσται ἔννοια ἀγαθὴ τοῖς
εἰδόσιν αὐτὴν ἐγγιεῖ ἀνὴρ δὲ φρόνιμος εὑρήσει αὐτήν ὁ πολλὰ κακοποιῶν
τελεσιουργεῖ κακίαν ὃς δὲ ἐρεθίζει λόγους οὐ σωθήσεται

8 ὁ κτώμενος φρόνησιν ἀγαπᾷ ἑαυτόν ὃς δὲ φυλάσσει φρόνησιν εὑρήσει ἀγαθά

9 μάρτυς ψευδὴς οὐκ ἀτιμώρητος ἔσται ὃς δ᾽ ἂν ἐκκαύσῃ κακίαν ἀπολεῖται
ὑπ᾽ αὐτῆς

10 οὐ συμφέρει ἄφρονι τρυφή καὶ ἐὰν οἰκέτης ἄρξηται μεθ᾽ ὕβρεως
δυναστεύειν

11 ἐλεήμων ἀνὴρ μακροθυμεῖ τὸ δὲ καύχημα αὐτοῦ ἐπέρχεται παρανόμοις

12 βασιλέως ἀπειλὴ ὁμοία βρυγμῷ λέοντος ὥσπερ δὲ δρόσος ἐπὶ χόρτῳ οὕτως
τὸ ἱλαρὸν αὐτοῦ

13 αἰσχύνη πατρὶ υἱὸς ἄφρων καὶ οὐχ ἁγναὶ εὐχαὶ ἀπὸ μισθώματος ἑταίρας

14 οἶκον καὶ ὕπαρξιν μερίζουσιν πατέρες παισίν παρὰ δὲ θεοῦ ἁρμόζεται
γυνὴ ἀνδρί

15 δειλία κατέχει ἀνδρογύναιον ψυχὴ δὲ ἀεργοῦ πεινάσει

16 ὃς φυλάσσει ἐντολήν τηρεῖ τὴν ἑαυτοῦ ψυχήν ὁ δὲ καταφρονῶν τῶν ἑαυτοῦ
ὁδῶν ἀπολεῖται

17 δανίζει θεῷ ὁ ἐλεῶν πτωχόν κατὰ δὲ τὸ δόμα αὐτοῦ ἀνταποδώσει αὐτῷ

18 παίδευε υἱόν σου οὕτως γὰρ ἔσται εὔελπις εἰς δὲ ὕβριν μὴ ἐπαίρου τῇ
ψυχῇ σου

19 κακόφρων ἀνὴρ πολλὰ ζημιωθήσεται ἐὰν δὲ λοιμεύηται καὶ τὴν ψυχὴν
αὐτοῦ προσθήσει

20 ἄκουε υἱέ παιδείαν πατρός σου ἵνα σοφὸς γένῃ ἐπ᾽ ἐσχάτων σου

21 πολλοὶ λογισμοὶ ἐν καρδίᾳ ἀνδρός ἡ δὲ βουλὴ τοῦ κυρίου εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα
μένει

22 καρπὸς ἀνδρὶ ἐλεημοσύνη κρείσσων δὲ πτωχὸς δίκαιος ἢ πλούσιος ψεύστης

23 φόβος κυρίου εἰς ζωὴν ἀνδρί ὁ δὲ ἄφοβος αὐλισθήσεται ἐν τόποις οὗ οὐκ
ἐπισκοπεῖται γνῶσις

24 ὁ ἐγκρύπτων εἰς τὸν κόλπον αὐτοῦ χεῖρας ἀδίκως οὐδὲ τῷ στόματι οὐ μὴ
προσαγάγῃ αὐτάς

25 λοιμοῦ μαστιγουμένου ἄφρων πανουργότερος γίνεται ἐὰν δὲ ἐλέγχῃς ἄνδρα
φρόνιμον νοήσει αἴσθησιν

26 ὁ ἀτιμάζων πατέρα καὶ ἀπωθούμενος μητέρα αὐτοῦ καταισχυνθήσεται καὶ
ἐπονείδιστος ἔσται

27 υἱὸς ἀπολειπόμενος φυλάξαι παιδείαν πατρὸς μελετήσει ῥήσεις κακάς

28 ὁ ἐγγυώμενος παῖδα ἄφρονα καθυβρίζει δικαίωμα στόμα δὲ ἀσεβῶν
καταπίεται κρίσεις

29 ἑτοιμάζονται ἀκολάστοις μάστιγες καὶ τιμωρίαι ὤμοις ἀφρόνων

[Vul](../vul/pro019)

1 \[Melior est pauper qui ambulat in simplicitate sua  
quam dives torquens labia sua, et insipiens.  

2 Ubi non est scientia animæ, non est bonum,  
et qui festinus est pedibus offendet.  

3 Stultitia hominis supplantat gressus ejus,  
et contra Deum fervet animo suo.  

4 Divitiæ addunt amicos plurimos;  
a paupere autem et hi quos habuit separantur.  

5 Testis falsus non erit impunitus,  
et qui mendacia loquitur non effugiet.  

6 Multi colunt personam potentis,  
et amici sunt dona tribuentis.  

7 Fratres hominis pauperis oderunt eum;  
insuper et amici procul recesserunt ab eo.\]  
\[Qui tantum verba sectatur nihil habebit;  

8 qui autem possessor est mentis diligit animam suam,  
et custos prudentiæ inveniet bona.  

9 Falsus testis non erit impunitus,  
et qui loquitur mendacia peribit.  

10 Non decent stultum deliciæ,  
nec servum dominari principibus.  

11 Doctrina viri per patientiam noscitur,  
et gloria ejus est iniqua prætergredi.  

12 Sicut fremitus leonis, ita et regis ira,  
et sicut ros super herbam, ita et hilaritas ejus.  

13 Dolor patris filius stultus,  
et tecta jugiter perstillantia litigiosa mulier.  

14 Domus et divitiæ dantur a parentibus;  
a Domino autem proprie uxor prudens.  

15 Pigredo immittit soporem,  
et anima dissoluta esuriet.  

16 Qui custodit mandatum custodit animam suam;  
qui autem negligit viam suam mortificabitur.  

17 Fœneratur Domino qui miseretur pauperis,  
et vicissitudinem suam reddet ei.  

18 Erudi filium tuum; ne desperes:  
ad interfectionem autem ejus ne ponas animam tuam.  

19 Qui impatiens est sustinebit damnum,  
et cum rapuerit, aliud apponet.  

20 Audi consilium, et suscipe disciplinam,  
ut sis sapiens in novissimis tuis.  

21 Multæ cogitationes in corde viri;  
voluntas autem Domini permanebit.  

22 Homo indigens misericors est,  
et melior est pauper quam vir mendax.  

23 Timor Domini ad vitam,  
et in plenitudine commorabitur absque visitatione pessima.\]  

24 \[Abscondit piger manum suam sub ascella,  
nec ad os suum applicat eam.  

25 Pestilente flagellato stultus sapientior erit;  
si autem corripueris sapientem, intelliget disciplinam.  

26 Qui affligit patrem, et fugat matrem,  
ignominiosus est et infelix.  

27 Non cesses, fili, audire doctrinam,  
nec ignores sermones scientiæ.  

28 Testis iniquus deridet judicium,  
et os impiorum devorat iniquitatem.  

29 Parata sunt derisoribus judicia,  
et mallei percutientes stultorum corporibus.\]
