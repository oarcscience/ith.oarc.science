### Psalms 110

[KJV](../kjv/psa110)

1  A Psalm<sup>[4210](h4210)</sup> of David.<sup>[1732](h1732)</sup> The
LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> said<sup>[5002](h5002)</sup> unto my
Lord,<sup>[113](h0113)</sup> Sit<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup> thou at my
right hand,<sup>[3225](h3225)</sup> until<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> I
make<sup>[7896](h7896)</sup> thine enemies<sup>[341](h0341)</sup> thy
footstool.<sup>[1916](h1916)</sup> <sup>[7272](h7272)</sup>

2  The LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> shall send<sup>[7971](h7971)</sup>
the rod<sup>[4294](h4294)</sup> of thy strength<sup>[5797](h5797)</sup>
out of Zion:<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6726](h6726)</sup>
rule<sup>[7287](h7287)</sup> thou in the midst<sup>[7130](h7130)</sup>
of thine enemies.<sup>[341](h0341)</sup>

3  Thy people<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup> shall be
willing<sup>[5071](h5071)</sup> in the day<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> of
thy power,<sup>[2428](h2428)</sup> in the
beauties<sup>[1926](h1926)</sup> of holiness<sup>[6944](h6944)</sup>
from the womb<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7358](h7358)</sup> of the
morning:<sup>[4891](h4891)</sup> thou hast the
dew<sup>[2919](h2919)</sup> of thy youth.<sup>[3208](h3208)</sup>

4  The LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> hath sworn,<sup>[7650](h7650)</sup>
and will not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> repent,<sup>[5162](h5162)</sup>
Thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> art a priest<sup>[3548](h3548)</sup>
forever<sup>[5769](h5769)</sup> after<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the
order<sup>[1700](h1700)</sup> of Melchizedek.<sup>[4442](h4442)</sup>

5  The Lord<sup>[136](h0136)</sup> at<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> thy right
hand<sup>[3225](h3225)</sup> shall strike
through<sup>[4272](h4272)</sup> kings<sup>[4428](h4428)</sup> in the
day<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> of his wrath.<sup>[639](h0639)</sup>

6  He shall judge<sup>[1777](h1777)</sup> among the
heathen,<sup>[1471](h1471)</sup> he shall fill<sup>[4390](h4390)</sup>
the places with the dead bodies;<sup>[1472](h1472)</sup> he shall
wound<sup>[4272](h4272)</sup> the heads<sup>[7218](h7218)</sup>
over<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> many<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup>
countries.<sup>[776](h0776)</sup>

7  He shall drink<sup>[8354](h8354)</sup> of the
brook<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[5158](h5158)</sup> in the
way:<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> therefore<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup>
<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> shall he lift up<sup>[7311](h7311)</sup> the
head.<sup>[7218](h7218)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/psa110)

1 αλληλουια ἐξομολογήσομαί σοι κύριε ἐν ὅλῃ καρδίᾳ μου ἐν βουλῇ εὐθείων
καὶ συναγωγῇ

2 μεγάλα τὰ ἔργα κυρίου ἐξεζητημένα εἰς πάντα τὰ θελήματα αὐτοῦ

3 ἐξομολόγησις καὶ μεγαλοπρέπεια τὸ ἔργον αὐτοῦ καὶ ἡ δικαιοσύνη αὐτοῦ
μένει εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα τοῦ αἰῶνος

4 μνείαν ἐποιήσατο τῶν θαυμασίων αὐτοῦ ἐλεήμων καὶ οἰκτίρμων ὁ κύριος

5 τροφὴν ἔδωκεν τοῖς φοβουμένοις αὐτόν μνησθήσεται εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα
διαθήκης αὐτοῦ

6 ἰσχὺν ἔργων αὐτοῦ ἀνήγγειλεν τῷ λαῷ αὐτοῦ τοῦ δοῦναι αὐτοῖς
κληρονομίαν ἐθνῶν

7 ἔργα χειρῶν αὐτοῦ ἀλήθεια καὶ κρίσις πισταὶ πᾶσαι αἱ ἐντολαὶ αὐτοῦ

8 ἐστηριγμέναι εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα τοῦ αἰῶνος πεποιημέναι ἐν ἀληθείᾳ καὶ
εὐθύτητι

9 λύτρωσιν ἀπέστειλεν τῷ λαῷ αὐτοῦ ἐνετείλατο εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα διαθήκην
αὐτοῦ ἅγιον καὶ φοβερὸν τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ

10 ἀρχὴ σοφίας φόβος κυρίου σύνεσις ἀγαθὴ πᾶσι τοῖς ποιοῦσιν αὐτήν ἡ
αἴνεσις αὐτοῦ μένει εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα τοῦ αἰῶνος

[Vul](../vul/psa110)

1 Alleluja. \[Confitebor tibi, Domine, in toto corde meo,  
in consilio justorum, et congregatione.  

2 Magna opera Domini:  
exquisita in omnes voluntates ejus.  

3 Confessio et magnificentia opus ejus,  
et justitia ejus manet in sæculum sæculi.  

4 Memoriam fecit mirabilium suorum,  
misericors et miserator Dominus.  

5 Escam dedit timentibus se;  
memor erit in sæculum testamenti sui.  

6 Virtutem operum suorum annuntiabit populo suo,  

7 ut det illis hæreditatem gentium.  
Opera manuum ejus veritas et judicium.  

8 Fidelia omnia mandata ejus,  
confirmata in sæculum sæculi,  
facta in veritate et æquitate.  

9 Redemptionem misit populo suo;  
mandavit in æternum testamentum suum.  
Sanctum et terribile nomen ejus.  

10 Initium sapientiæ timor Domini;  
intellectus bonus omnibus facientibus eum:  
laudatio ejus manet in sæculum sæculi.\]
