### Job 11

[KJV](../kjv/job011)

1  Then answered<sup>[6030](h6030)</sup> Zophar<sup>[6691](h6691)</sup>
the Naamathite,<sup>[5284](h5284)</sup> and said,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup>

2  Should not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> the
multitude<sup>[7230](h7230)</sup> of words<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup> be
answered?<sup>[6030](h6030)</sup> and should<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> a
man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> full of talk<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> be
justified?<sup>[6663](h6663)</sup>

3  Should thy lies<sup>[907](h0907)</sup> make
men<sup>[4962](h4962)</sup> hold their peace?<sup>[2790](h2790)</sup>
and when thou mockest,<sup>[3932](h3932)</sup> shall
no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> man make thee ashamed?<sup>[3637](h3637)</sup>

4  For thou hast said,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> My
doctrine<sup>[3948](h3948)</sup> is pure,<sup>[2134](h2134)</sup> and I
am<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> clean<sup>[1249](h1249)</sup> in thine
eyes.<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup>

5  But<sup>[199](h0199)</sup> oh that<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup>
God<sup>[433](h0433)</sup> would<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup>
speak,<sup>[1696](h1696)</sup> and open<sup>[6605](h6605)</sup> his
lips<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> against<sup>[5973](h5973)</sup> thee;

6  And that he would show<sup>[5046](h5046)</sup> thee the
secrets<sup>[8587](h8587)</sup> of wisdom,<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup>
that<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> they are double<sup>[3718](h3718)</sup> to
that which is!<sup>[8454](h8454)</sup> Know<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup>
therefore that<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> God<sup>[433](h0433)</sup>
exacteth<sup>[5382](h5382)</sup> of thee less than thine
iniquity<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[5771](h5771)</sup> deserveth.

7  Canst thou by searching<sup>[2714](h2714)</sup> find
out<sup>[4672](h4672)</sup> God?<sup>[433](h0433)</sup> canst thou find
out<sup>[4672](h4672)</sup> the Almighty<sup>[7706](h7706)</sup>
unto<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> perfection?<sup>[8503](h8503)</sup>

8  It is as high<sup>[1363](h1363)</sup> as
heaven;<sup>[8064](h8064)</sup> what<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> canst thou
do?<sup>[6466](h6466)</sup> deeper<sup>[6013](h6013)</sup> than
hell;<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7585](h7585)</sup>
what<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> canst thou know?<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup>

9  The measure<sup>[4055](h4055)</sup> thereof is
longer<sup>[752](h0752)</sup> than the earth,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> and broader<sup>[7342](h7342)</sup>
than<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> the sea.<sup>[3220](h3220)</sup>

10  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> he cut off,<sup>[2498](h2498)</sup> and
shut up,<sup>[5462](h5462)</sup> or gather
together,<sup>[6950](h6950)</sup> then who<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup> can
hinder<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> him?

11  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> he<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup>
knoweth<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> vain<sup>[7723](h7723)</sup>
men:<sup>[4962](h4962)</sup> he seeth<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup>
wickedness<sup>[205](h0205)</sup> also; will he
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> then consider<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> it?

12  For vain<sup>[5014](h5014)</sup> man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> would be
wise,<sup>[3823](h3823)</sup> though man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> be
born<sup>[3205](h3205)</sup> like a wild ass's<sup>[6501](h6501)</sup>
colt.<sup>[5895](h5895)</sup>

13  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup>
prepare<sup>[3559](h3559)</sup> thine heart,<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> and
stretch out<sup>[6566](h6566)</sup> thine hands<sup>[3709](h3709)</sup>
toward<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> him;

14  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> iniquity<sup>[205](h0205)</sup> be in
thine hand,<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> put it far
away,<sup>[7368](h7368)</sup> and let not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup>
wickedness<sup>[5766](h5766)</sup> dwell<sup>[7931](h7931)</sup> in thy
tabernacles.<sup>[168](h0168)</sup>

15  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> then<sup>[227](h0227)</sup> shalt thou
lift up<sup>[5375](h5375)</sup> thy face<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> without
spot;<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3971](h3971)</sup> yea, thou shalt
be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> steadfast,<sup>[3332](h3332)</sup> and shalt
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> fear:<sup>[3372](h3372)</sup>

16  Because<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> shalt
forget<sup>[7911](h7911)</sup> thy misery,<sup>[5999](h5999)</sup> and
remember<sup>[2142](h2142)</sup> it as waters<sup>[4325](h4325)</sup>
that pass away:<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup>

17  And thine age<sup>[2465](h2465)</sup> shall be
clearer<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> than the
noonday;<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6672](h6672)</sup> thou shalt
shine forth,<sup>[5774](h5774)</sup> thou shalt
be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> as the morning.<sup>[1242](h1242)</sup>

18  And thou shalt be secure,<sup>[982](h0982)</sup>
because<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> there<sup>[3426](h3426)</sup> is
hope;<sup>[8615](h8615)</sup> yea, thou shalt
dig<sup>[2658](h2658)</sup> about thee, and thou shalt take thy
rest<sup>[7901](h7901)</sup> in safety.<sup>[983](h0983)</sup>

19  Also thou shalt lie down,<sup>[7257](h7257)</sup> and
none<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> shall make thee
afraid;<sup>[2729](h2729)</sup> yea, many<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup> shall
make suit<sup>[2470](h2470)</sup> unto<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> thee.

20  But the eyes<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> of the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> shall fail,<sup>[3615](h3615)</sup> and
they shall not escape,<sup>[4498](h4498)</sup> <sup>[6](h0006)</sup>
<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> and their hope<sup>[8615](h8615)</sup> shall be
as the giving up<sup>[4646](h4646)</sup> of the
ghost.<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/job011)

1 ὑπολαβὼν δὲ Σωφαρ ὁ Μιναῖος λέγει

2 ὁ τὰ πολλὰ λέγων καὶ ἀντακούσεται ἢ καὶ ὁ εὔλαλος οἴεται εἶναι δίκαιος
εὐλογημένος γεννητὸς γυναικὸς ὀλιγόβιος

3 μὴ πολὺς ἐν ῥήμασιν γίνου οὐ γάρ ἐστιν ὁ ἀντικρινόμενός σοι

4 μὴ γὰρ λέγε ὅτι καθαρός εἰμι τοῖς ἔργοις καὶ ἄμεμπτος ἐναντίον αὐτοῦ

5 ἀλλὰ πῶς ἂν ὁ κύριος λαλήσαι πρὸς σέ καὶ ἀνοίξει χείλη αὐτοῦ μετὰ σοῦ

6 εἶτα ἀναγγελεῖ σοι δύναμιν σοφίας ὅτι διπλοῦς ἔσται τῶν κατὰ σέ καὶ
τότε γνώσῃ ὅτι ἄξιά σοι ἀπέβη ἀπὸ κυρίου ὧν ἡμάρτηκας

7 ἦ ἴχνος κυρίου εὑρήσεις ἢ εἰς τὰ ἔσχατα ἀφίκου ἃ ἐποίησεν ὁ
παντοκράτωρ

8 ὑψηλὸς ὁ οὐρανός καὶ τί ποιήσεις βαθύτερα δὲ τῶν ἐν ᾅδου τί οἶδας

9 ἢ μακρότερα μέτρου γῆς ἢ εὔρους θαλάσσης

10 ἐὰν δὲ καταστρέψῃ τὰ πάντα τίς ἐρεῖ αὐτῷ τί ἐποίησας

11 αὐτὸς γὰρ οἶδεν ἔργα ἀνόμων ἰδὼν δὲ ἄτοπα οὐ παρόψεται

12 ἄνθρωπος δὲ ἄλλως νήχεται λόγοις βροτὸς δὲ γεννητὸς γυναικὸς ἴσα ὄνῳ
ἐρημίτῃ

13 εἰ γὰρ σὺ καθαρὰν ἔθου τὴν καρδίαν σου ὑπτιάζεις δὲ χεῖρας πρὸς αὐτόν

14 εἰ ἄνομόν τί ἐστιν ἐν χερσίν σου πόρρω ποίησον αὐτὸ ἀπὸ σοῦ ἀδικία δὲ
ἐν διαίτῃ σου μὴ αὐλισθήτω

15 οὕτως γὰρ ἀναλάμψει σου τὸ πρόσωπον ὥσπερ ὕδωρ καθαρόν ἐκδύσῃ δὲ
ῥύπον καὶ οὐ μὴ φοβηθῇς

16 καὶ τὸν κόπον ἐπιλήσῃ ὥσπερ κῦμα παρελθὸν καὶ οὐ πτοηθήσῃ

17 ἡ δὲ εὐχή σου ὥσπερ ἑωσφόρος ἐκ δὲ μεσημβρίας ἀνατελεῖ σοι ζωή

18 πεποιθώς τε ἔσῃ ὅτι ἔστιν σοι ἐλπίς ἐκ δὲ μερίμνης καὶ φροντίδος
ἀναφανεῖταί σοι εἰρήνη

19 ἡσυχάσεις γάρ καὶ οὐκ ἔσται ὁ πολεμῶν σε μεταβαλόμενοι δὲ πολλοί σου
δεηθήσονται

20 σωτηρία δὲ αὐτοὺς ἀπολείψει ἡ γὰρ ἐλπὶς αὐτῶν ἀπώλεια ὀφθαλμοὶ δὲ
ἀσεβῶν τακήσονται

[Vul](../vul/job011)

1 Respondens autem Sophar Naamathites, dixit:

2 \[Numquid qui multa loquitur, non et audiet?  
aut vir verbosus justificabitur?  

3 Tibi soli tacebunt homines?  
et cum ceteros irriseris, a nullo confutaberis?  

4 Dixisti enim: Purus est sermo meus,  
et mundus sum in conspectu tuo.  

5 Atque utinam Deus loqueretur tecum,  
et aperiret labia sua tibi,  

6 ut ostenderet tibi secreta sapientiæ,  
et quod multiplex esset lex ejus:  
et intelligeres quod multo minora exigaris ab eo  
quam meretur iniquitas tua!  

7 Forsitan vestigia Dei comprehendes,  
et usque ad perfectum Omnipotentem reperies?  

8 Excelsior cælo est, et quid facies?  
profundior inferno, et unde cognosces?  

9 Longior terra mensura ejus,  
et latior mari.  

10 Si subverterit omnia, vel in unum coarctaverit,  
quis contradicet ei?  

11 Ipse enim novit hominum vanitatem;  
et videns iniquitatem, nonne considerat?  

12 Vir vanus in superbiam erigitur,  
et tamquam pullum onagri se liberum natum putat.  

13 Tu autem firmasti cor tuum,  
et expandisti ad eum manus tuas.  

14 Si iniquitatem quæ est in manu tua abstuleris a te,  
et non manserit in tabernaculo tuo injustitia,  

15 tunc levare poteris faciem tuam absque macula;  
et eris stabilis, et non timebis.  

16 Miseriæ quoque oblivisceris,  
et quasi aquarum quæ præterierunt recordaberis.  

17 Et quasi meridianus fulgor consurget tibi ad vesperam;  
et cum te consumptum putaveris, orieris ut lucifer.  

18 Et habebis fiduciam, proposita tibi spe:  
et defossus securus dormies.  

19 Requiesces, et non erit qui te exterreat;  
et deprecabuntur faciem tuam plurimi.  

20 Oculi autem impiorum deficient,  
et effugium peribit ab eis:  
et spes illorum abominatio animæ.\]
