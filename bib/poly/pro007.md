### Proverbs 7

[KJV](../kjv/pro007)

1  My son,<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> keep<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> my
words,<sup>[561](h0561)</sup> and lay up<sup>[6845](h6845)</sup> my
commandments<sup>[4687](h4687)</sup> with<sup>[854](h0854)</sup> thee.

2  Keep<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> my commandments,<sup>[4687](h4687)</sup>
and live;<sup>[2421](h2421)</sup> and my law<sup>[8451](h8451)</sup> as
the apple<sup>[380](h0380)</sup> of thine eye.<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup>

3  Bind<sup>[7194](h7194)</sup> them upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> thy
fingers,<sup>[676](h0676)</sup> write<sup>[3789](h3789)</sup> them
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the table<sup>[3871](h3871)</sup> of thine
heart.<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup>

4  Say<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> unto wisdom,<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup>
Thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> art my sister;<sup>[269](h0269)</sup> and
call<sup>[7121](h7121)</sup> understanding<sup>[998](h0998)</sup> thy
kinswoman:<sup>[4129](h4129)</sup>

5  That they may keep<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> thee from the
strange<sup>[2114](h2114)</sup> woman,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[802](h0802)</sup> from the stranger<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[5237](h5237)</sup> which flattereth<sup>[2505](h2505)</sup> with
her words.<sup>[561](h0561)</sup>

6  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> at the window<sup>[2474](h2474)</sup> of
my house<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> I looked<sup>[8259](h8259)</sup>
through<sup>[1157](h1157)</sup> my casement,<sup>[822](h0822)</sup>

7  And beheld<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> among the simple
ones,<sup>[6612](h6612)</sup> I discerned<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> among
the youths,<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> a young man<sup>[5288](h5288)</sup>
void<sup>[2638](h2638)</sup> of understanding,<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup>

8  Passing<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup> through the
street<sup>[7784](h7784)</sup> near<sup>[681](h0681)</sup> her
corner;<sup>[6438](h6438)</sup> and he went<sup>[6805](h6805)</sup> the
way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> to her house,<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup>

9  In the twilight,<sup>[5399](h5399)</sup> in the
evening,<sup>[6153](h6153)</sup> <sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> in the
black<sup>[380](h0380)</sup> and dark<sup>[653](h0653)</sup>
night:<sup>[3915](h3915)</sup>

10  And, behold,<sup>[2009](h2009)</sup> there
met<sup>[7125](h7125)</sup> him a woman<sup>[802](h0802)</sup> with the
attire<sup>[7897](h7897)</sup> of a harlot,<sup>[2181](h2181)</sup> and
subtle<sup>[5341](h5341)</sup> of heart.<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup>

11  (She<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> is loud<sup>[1993](h1993)</sup> and
stubborn;<sup>[5637](h5637)</sup> her feet<sup>[7272](h7272)</sup>
abide<sup>[7931](h7931)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> in her
house:<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup>

12  Now<sup>[6471](h6471)</sup> is she without,<sup>[2351](h2351)</sup>
now<sup>[6471](h6471)</sup> in the streets,<sup>[7339](h7339)</sup> and
lieth in wait<sup>[693](h0693)</sup> at<sup>[681](h0681)</sup>
every<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> corner.)<sup>[6438](h6438)</sup>

13  So she caught<sup>[2388](h2388)</sup> him, and
kissed<sup>[5401](h5401)</sup> him, and with an
impudent<sup>[5810](h5810)</sup> face<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup>
said<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> unto him,

14  I have peace offerings<sup>[2077](h2077)</sup>
<sup>[8002](h8002)</sup> with<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> me; this
day<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> have I paid<sup>[7999](h7999)</sup> my
vows.<sup>[5088](h5088)</sup>

15  Therefore<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> <sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> came I
forth<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup> to meet<sup>[7125](h7125)</sup> thee,
diligently to seek<sup>[7836](h7836)</sup> thy
face,<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> and I have found<sup>[4672](h4672)</sup>
thee.

16  I have decked<sup>[7234](h7234)</sup> my bed<sup>[6210](h6210)</sup>
with coverings of tapestry,<sup>[4765](h4765)</sup> with
carved<sup>[2405](h2405)</sup> works, with fine
linen<sup>[330](h0330)</sup> of Egypt.<sup>[4714](h4714)</sup>

17  I have perfumed<sup>[5130](h5130)</sup> my
bed<sup>[4904](h4904)</sup> with myrrh,<sup>[4753](h4753)</sup>
aloes,<sup>[174](h0174)</sup> and cinnamon.<sup>[7076](h7076)</sup>

18  Come,<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> let us take our
fill<sup>[7301](h7301)</sup> of love<sup>[1730](h1730)</sup>
until<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> the morning:<sup>[1242](h1242)</sup> let
us solace ourselves<sup>[5965](h5965)</sup> with
loves.<sup>[159](h0159)</sup>

19  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> the goodman<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> is
not<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> at home,<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> he is
gone<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> a long<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[7350](h7350)</sup> journey:<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup>

20  He hath taken<sup>[3947](h3947)</sup> a bag<sup>[6872](h6872)</sup>
of money<sup>[3701](h3701)</sup> with<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> him, and
will come<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> home<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> at the
day<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> appointed.<sup>[3677](h3677)</sup>

21  With her much<sup>[7230](h7230)</sup> fair
speech<sup>[3948](h3948)</sup> she caused him to
yield,<sup>[5186](h5186)</sup> with the
flattering<sup>[2506](h2506)</sup> of her lips<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup>
she forced<sup>[5080](h5080)</sup> him.

22  He goeth<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> after<sup>[310](h0310)</sup> her
straightway,<sup>[6597](h6597)</sup> as an ox<sup>[7794](h7794)</sup>
goeth<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> to<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> the
slaughter,<sup>[2874](h2874)</sup> or as a fool<sup>[191](h0191)</sup>
to<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> the correction<sup>[4148](h4148)</sup> of the
stocks;<sup>[5914](h5914)</sup>

23  Till<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> a dart<sup>[2671](h2671)</sup> strike
through<sup>[6398](h6398)</sup> his liver;<sup>[3516](h3516)</sup> as a
bird<sup>[6833](h6833)</sup> hasteth<sup>[4116](h4116)</sup>
to<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> the snare,<sup>[6341](h6341)</sup> and
knoweth<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
that<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> it<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> is for his
life.<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup>

24  Hearken<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> unto me now<sup>[6258](h6258)</sup>
therefore, O ye children,<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> and
attend<sup>[7181](h7181)</sup> to the words<sup>[561](h0561)</sup> of my
mouth.<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup>

25  Let not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> thine heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup>
decline<sup>[7847](h7847)</sup> to<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> her
ways,<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> go not astray<sup>[8582](h8582)</sup>
<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> in her paths.<sup>[5410](h5410)</sup>

26  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> she hath cast
down<sup>[5307](h5307)</sup> many<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup>
wounded:<sup>[2491](h2491)</sup> yea, many<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup>
strong<sup>[6099](h6099)</sup> men have been
slain<sup>[2026](h2026)</sup> by her.

27  Her house<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> is the way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup>
to hell,<sup>[7585](h7585)</sup> going down<sup>[3381](h3381)</sup>
to<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> the chambers<sup>[2315](h2315)</sup> of
death.<sup>[4194](h4194)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/pro007)

1 υἱέ φύλασσε ἐμοὺς λόγους τὰς δὲ ἐμὰς ἐντολὰς κρύψον παρὰ σεαυτῷ  
υἱέ τίμα τὸν κύριον καὶ ἰσχύσεις πλὴν δὲ αὐτοῦ μὴ φοβοῦ ἄλλον

2 φύλαξον ἐμὰς ἐντολάς καὶ βιώσεις τοὺς δὲ ἐμοὺς λόγους ὥσπερ κόρας
ὀμμάτων

3 περίθου δὲ αὐτοὺς σοῖς δακτύλοις ἐπίγραψον δὲ ἐπὶ τὸ πλάτος τῆς
καρδίας σου

4 εἶπον τὴν σοφίαν σὴν ἀδελφὴν εἶναι τὴν δὲ φρόνησιν γνώριμον
περιποίησαι σεαυτῷ

5 ἵνα σε τηρήσῃ ἀπὸ γυναικὸς ἀλλοτρίας καὶ πονηρᾶς ἐάν σε λόγοις τοῖς
πρὸς χάριν ἐμβάληται

6 ἀπὸ γὰρ θυρίδος ἐκ τοῦ οἴκου αὐτῆς εἰς τὰς πλατείας παρακύπτουσα

7 ὃν ἂν ἴδῃ τῶν ἀφρόνων τέκνων νεανίαν ἐνδεῆ φρενῶν

8 παραπορευόμενον παρὰ γωνίαν ἐν διόδοις οἴκων αὐτῆς

9 καὶ λαλοῦντα ἐν σκότει ἑσπερινῷ ἡνίκα ἂν ἡσυχία νυκτερινὴ ᾖ καὶ
γνοφώδης

10 ἡ δὲ γυνὴ συναντᾷ αὐτῷ εἶδος ἔχουσα πορνικόν ἣ ποιεῖ νέων ἐξίπτασθαι
καρδίας

11 ἀνεπτερωμένη δέ ἐστιν καὶ ἄσωτος ἐν οἴκῳ δὲ οὐχ ἡσυχάζουσιν οἱ πόδες
αὐτῆς

12 χρόνον γάρ τινα ἔξω ῥέμβεται χρόνον δὲ ἐν πλατείαις παρὰ πᾶσαν γωνίαν
ἐνεδρεύει

13 εἶτα ἐπιλαβομένη ἐφίλησεν αὐτόν ἀναιδεῖ δὲ προσώπῳ προσεῖπεν αὐτῷ

14 θυσία εἰρηνική μοί ἐστιν σήμερον ἀποδίδωμι τὰς εὐχάς μου

15 ἕνεκα τούτου ἐξῆλθον εἰς συνάντησίν σοι ποθοῦσα τὸ σὸν πρόσωπον
εὕρηκά σε

16 κειρίαις τέτακα τὴν κλίνην μου ἀμφιτάποις δὲ ἔστρωκα τοῖς ἀπ᾽
Αἰγύπτου

17 διέρραγκα τὴν κοίτην μου κρόκῳ τὸν δὲ οἶκόν μου κινναμώμῳ

18 ἐλθὲ καὶ ἀπολαύσωμεν φιλίας ἕως ὄρθρου δεῦρο καὶ ἐγκυλισθῶμεν ἔρωτι

19 οὐ γὰρ πάρεστιν ὁ ἀνήρ μου ἐν οἴκῳ πεπόρευται δὲ ὁδὸν μακρὰν

20 ἔνδεσμον ἀργυρίου λαβὼν ἐν χειρὶ αὐτοῦ δι᾽ ἡμερῶν πολλῶν ἐπανήξει εἰς
τὸν οἶκον αὐτοῦ

21 ἀπεπλάνησεν δὲ αὐτὸν πολλῇ ὁμιλίᾳ βρόχοις τε τοῖς ἀπὸ χειλέων
ἐξώκειλεν αὐτόν

22 ὁ δὲ ἐπηκολούθησεν αὐτῇ κεπφωθείς ὥσπερ δὲ βοῦς ἐπὶ σφαγὴν ἄγεται καὶ
ὥσπερ κύων ἐπὶ δεσμοὺς

23 ἢ ὡς ἔλαφος τοξεύματι πεπληγὼς εἰς τὸ ἧπαρ σπεύδει δὲ ὥσπερ ὄρνεον
εἰς παγίδα οὐκ εἰδὼς ὅτι περὶ ψυχῆς τρέχει

24 νῦν οὖν υἱέ ἄκουέ μου καὶ πρόσεχε ῥήμασιν στόματός μου

25 μὴ ἐκκλινάτω εἰς τὰς ὁδοὺς αὐτῆς ἡ καρδία σου

26 πολλοὺς γὰρ τρώσασα καταβέβληκεν καὶ ἀναρίθμητοί εἰσιν οὓς πεφόνευκεν

27 ὁδοὶ ᾅδου ὁ οἶκος αὐτῆς κατάγουσαι εἰς τὰ ταμίεια τοῦ θανάτου

[Vul](../vul/pro007)

1 \[Fili mi, custodi sermones meos,  
et præcepta mea reconde tibi.  
Fili,

2 serva mandata mea, et vives;  
et legem meam quasi pupillam oculi tui:  

3 liga eam in digitis tuis,  
scribe illam in tabulis cordis tui.  

4 Dic sapientiæ: Soror mea es,  
et prudentiam voca amicam tuam:  

5 ut custodiant te a muliere extranea,  
et ab aliena quæ verba sua dulcia facit.  

6 De fenestra enim domus meæ  
per cancellos prospexi,  

7 et video parvulos;  
considero vecordem juvenem,  

8 qui transit per plateam juxta angulum  
et prope viam domus illius graditur:  

9 in obscuro, advesperascente die,  
in noctis tenebris et caligine.  

10 Et ecce occurrit illi mulier ornatu meretricio,  
præparata ad capiendas animas:  
garrula et vaga,  

11 quietis impatiens,  
nec valens in domo consistere pedibus suis;  

12 nunc foris, nunc in plateis,  
nunc juxta angulos insidians.  

13 Apprehensumque deosculatur juvenem,  
et procaci vultu blanditur, dicens:  

14 Victimas pro salute vovi;  
hodie reddidi vota mea:  

15 idcirco egressa sum in occursum tuum,  
desiderans te videre, et reperi.  

16 Intexui funibus lectulum meum;  
stravi tapetibus pictis ex Ægypto:  

17 aspersi cubile meum myrrha,  
et aloë, et cinnamomo.  

18 Veni, inebriemur uberibus,  
et fruamur cupitis amplexibus donec illucescat dies.  

19 Non est enim vir in domo sua:  
abiit via longissima:  

20 sacculum pecuniæ secum tulit;  
in die plenæ lunæ reversurus est in domum suam.  

21 Irretivit eum multis sermonibus,  
et blanditiis labiorum protraxit illum.  

22 Statim eam sequitur quasi bos ductus ad victimam,  
et quasi agnus lasciviens,  
et ignorans quod ad vincula stultus trahatur:  

23 donec transfigat sagitta jecur ejus,  
velut si avis festinet ad laqueum,  
et nescit quod de periculo animæ illius agitur.  

24 Nunc ergo, fili mi, audi me,  
et attende verbis oris mei.  

25 Ne abstrahatur in viis illius mens tua,  
neque decipiaris semitis ejus;  

26 multos enim vulneratos dejecit,  
et fortissimi quique interfecti sunt ab ea.  

27 Viæ inferi domus ejus,  
penetrantes in interiora mortis.\]
