### Proverbs 12

[KJV](../kjv/pro012.md)

1  Whoso loveth<sup>[157](h0157.md)</sup>
instruction<sup>[4148](h4148)</sup> loveth<sup>[157](h0157.md)</sup>
knowledge:<sup>[1847](h1847.md)</sup> but he that
hateth<sup>[8130](h8130)</sup> reproof<sup>[8433](h8433.md)</sup> is
brutish.<sup>[1198](h1198.md)</sup>

2  A good<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> man obtaineth<sup>[6329](h6329.md)</sup>
favor<sup>[7522](h7522)</sup> of the LORD:<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> but a man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> of wicked
devices<sup>[4209](h4209)</sup> will he condemn.<sup>[7561](h7561.md)</sup>

3  A man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> shall not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> be
established<sup>[3559](h3559.md)</sup> by
wickedness:<sup>[7562](h7562)</sup> but the root<sup>[8328](h8328.md)</sup>
of the righteous<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> shall
not<sup>[1077](h1077)</sup> be moved.<sup>[4131](h4131.md)</sup>

4  A virtuous<sup>[2428](h2428)</sup> woman<sup>[802](h0802.md)</sup> is a
crown<sup>[5850](h5850)</sup> to her husband:<sup>[1167](h1167.md)</sup>
but she that maketh ashamed<sup>[954](h0954.md)</sup> is as
rottenness<sup>[7538](h7538)</sup> in his bones.<sup>[6106](h6106.md)</sup>

5  The thoughts<sup>[4284](h4284.md)</sup> of the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> are right:<sup>[4941](h4941.md)</sup> but
the counsels<sup>[8458](h8458.md)</sup> of the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> are deceit.<sup>[4820](h4820.md)</sup>

6  The words<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup> of the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> are to lie in wait<sup>[693](h0693.md)</sup>
for blood:<sup>[1818](h1818)</sup> but the mouth<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup>
of the upright<sup>[3477](h3477.md)</sup> shall
deliver<sup>[5337](h5337.md)</sup> them.

7  The wicked<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup> are
overthrown,<sup>[2015](h2015)</sup> and are not:<sup>[369](h0369.md)</sup>
but the house<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup> of the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> shall stand.<sup>[5975](h5975.md)</sup>

8  A man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> shall be
commended<sup>[1984](h1984)</sup> according<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> to
his wisdom:<sup>[7922](h7922.md)</sup> but he that is of a
perverse<sup>[5753](h5753)</sup> heart<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup> shall
be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> despised.<sup>[937](h0937.md)</sup>

9  He that is despised,<sup>[7034](h7034.md)</sup> and hath a
servant,<sup>[5650](h5650)</sup> is better<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup> than
he that honoreth himself,<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[3513](h3513)</sup> and lacketh<sup>[2638](h2638.md)</sup>
bread.<sup>[3899](h3899.md)</sup>

10  A righteous<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> man
regardeth<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> the life<sup>[5315](h5315.md)</sup> of
his beast:<sup>[929](h0929.md)</sup> but the tender
mercies<sup>[7356](h7356)</sup> of the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup>
are cruel.<sup>[394](h0394.md)</sup>

11  He that tilleth<sup>[5647](h5647.md)</sup> his
land<sup>[127](h0127)</sup> shall be satisfied<sup>[7646](h7646.md)</sup>
with bread:<sup>[3899](h3899.md)</sup> but he that
followeth<sup>[7291](h7291)</sup> vain<sup>[7386](h7386.md)</sup> persons
is void<sup>[2638](h2638.md)</sup> of
understanding.<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup>

12  The wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> desireth<sup>[2530](h2530.md)</sup>
the net<sup>[4685](h4685)</sup> of evil<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup> men: but
the root<sup>[8328](h8328.md)</sup> of the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> yieldeth<sup>[5414](h5414.md)</sup>
fruit.

13  The wicked<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> is snared<sup>[4170](h4170.md)</sup>
by the transgression<sup>[6588](h6588.md)</sup> of his
lips:<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> but the just<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> shall
come out<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup> of trouble.<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[6869](h6869.md)</sup>

14  A man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> shall be
satisfied<sup>[7646](h7646)</sup> with good<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup> by
the fruit<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6529](h6529.md)</sup> of his
mouth:<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> and the
recompense<sup>[1576](h1576)</sup> of a man's<sup>[120](h0120.md)</sup>
hands<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> shall be rendered<sup>[7725](h7725.md)</sup>
unto him.

15  The way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of a fool<sup>[191](h0191.md)</sup> is
right<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup> in his own eyes:<sup>[5869](h5869.md)</sup>
but he that hearkeneth<sup>[8085](h8085.md)</sup> unto
counsel<sup>[6098](h6098)</sup> is wise.<sup>[2450](h2450.md)</sup>

16  A fool's<sup>[191](h0191)</sup> wrath<sup>[3708](h3708.md)</sup> is
presently<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> known:<sup>[3045](h3045.md)</sup> but a
prudent<sup>[6175](h6175)</sup> man covereth<sup>[3680](h3680.md)</sup>
shame.<sup>[7036](h7036.md)</sup>

17  He that speaketh<sup>[6315](h6315.md)</sup>
truth<sup>[530](h0530)</sup> showeth forth<sup>[5046](h5046.md)</sup>
righteousness:<sup>[6664](h6664.md)</sup> but a
false<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> witness<sup>[5707](h5707.md)</sup>
deceit.<sup>[4820](h4820.md)</sup>

18  There is<sup>[3426](h3426.md)</sup> that
speaketh<sup>[981](h0981.md)</sup> like the
piercings<sup>[4094](h4094)</sup> of a sword:<sup>[2719](h2719.md)</sup>
but the tongue<sup>[3956](h3956.md)</sup> of the
wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> is health.<sup>[4832](h4832.md)</sup>

19  The lip<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> of truth<sup>[571](h0571.md)</sup>
shall be established<sup>[3559](h3559.md)</sup>
forever:<sup>[5703](h5703)</sup> but<sup>[5704](h5704.md)</sup> a
lying<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> tongue<sup>[3956](h3956.md)</sup> is but for
a moment.<sup>[7280](h7280.md)</sup>

20  Deceit<sup>[4820](h4820.md)</sup> is in the
heart<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup> of them that
imagine<sup>[2790](h2790)</sup> evil:<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup> but to the
counselors<sup>[3289](h3289)</sup> of peace<sup>[7965](h7965.md)</sup> is
joy.<sup>[8057](h8057.md)</sup>

21  There shall no<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> <sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup>
evil<sup>[205](h0205)</sup> happen<sup>[579](h0579.md)</sup> to the
just:<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> but the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup>
shall be filled<sup>[4390](h4390.md)</sup> with
mischief.<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup>

22  Lying<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> lips<sup>[8193](h8193.md)</sup> are
abomination<sup>[8441](h8441)</sup> to the LORD:<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup>
but they that deal<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> truly<sup>[530](h0530.md)</sup>
are his delight.<sup>[7522](h7522.md)</sup>

23  A prudent<sup>[6175](h6175)</sup> man<sup>[120](h0120.md)</sup>
concealeth<sup>[3680](h3680)</sup> knowledge:<sup>[1847](h1847.md)</sup>
but the heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> of fools<sup>[3684](h3684.md)</sup>
proclaimeth<sup>[7121](h7121)</sup> foolishness.<sup>[200](h0200.md)</sup>

24  The hand<sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup> of the
diligent<sup>[2742](h2742.md)</sup> shall bear
rule:<sup>[4910](h4910)</sup> but the slothful<sup>[7423](h7423.md)</sup>
shall be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> under tribute.<sup>[4522](h4522.md)</sup>

25  Heaviness<sup>[1674](h1674.md)</sup> in the
heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> of man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> maketh it
stoop:<sup>[7812](h7812)</sup> but a good<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup>
word<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup> maketh it glad.<sup>[8055](h8055.md)</sup>

26  The righteous<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> is more
excellent<sup>[8446](h8446.md)</sup> than his
neighbor:<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7453](h7453.md)</sup> but the
way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup>
seduceth<sup>[8582](h8582.md)</sup> them.

27  The slothful<sup>[7423](h7423.md)</sup> man
roasteth<sup>[2760](h2760)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> that which
he took in hunting:<sup>[6718](h6718.md)</sup> but the
substance<sup>[1952](h1952)</sup> of a diligent<sup>[2742](h2742.md)</sup>
man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> is precious.<sup>[3368](h3368.md)</sup>

28  In the way<sup>[734](h0734.md)</sup> of
righteousness<sup>[6666](h6666)</sup> is life;<sup>[2416](h2416.md)</sup>
and in the pathway<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> <sup>[5410](h5410.md)</sup>
thereof there is no<sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup>
death.<sup>[4194](h4194.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/pro012.md)

1 ὁ ἀγαπῶν παιδείαν ἀγαπᾷ αἴσθησιν ὁ δὲ μισῶν ἐλέγχους ἄφρων

2 κρείσσων ὁ εὑρὼν χάριν παρὰ κυρίῳ ἀνὴρ δὲ παράνομος παρασιωπηθήσεται

3 οὐ κατορθώσει ἄνθρωπος ἐξ ἀνόμου αἱ δὲ ῥίζαι τῶν δικαίων οὐκ
ἐξαρθήσονται

4 γυνὴ ἀνδρεία στέφανος τῷ ἀνδρὶ αὐτῆς ὥσπερ δὲ ἐν ξύλῳ σκώληξ οὕτως
ἄνδρα ἀπόλλυσιν γυνὴ κακοποιός

5 λογισμοὶ δικαίων κρίματα κυβερνῶσιν δὲ ἀσεβεῖς δόλους

6 λόγοι ἀσεβῶν δόλιοι στόμα δὲ ὀρθῶν ῥύσεται αὐτούς

7 οὗ ἐὰν στραφῇ ἀσεβὴς ἀφανίζεται οἶκοι δὲ δικαίων παραμένουσιν

8 στόμα συνετοῦ ἐγκωμιάζεται ὑπὸ ἀνδρός νωθροκάρδιος δὲ μυκτηρίζεται

9 κρείσσων ἀνὴρ ἐν ἀτιμίᾳ δουλεύων ἑαυτῷ ἢ τιμὴν ἑαυτῷ περιτιθεὶς καὶ
προσδεόμενος ἄρτου

10 δίκαιος οἰκτίρει ψυχὰς κτηνῶν αὐτοῦ τὰ δὲ σπλάγχνα τῶν ἀσεβῶν
ἀνελεήμονα

11 ὁ ἐργαζόμενος τὴν ἑαυτοῦ γῆν ἐμπλησθήσεται ἄρτων οἱ δὲ διώκοντες
μάταια ἐνδεεῖς φρενῶν  
ὅς ἐστιν ἡδὺς ἐν οἴνων διατριβαῖς ἐν τοῖς ἑαυτοῦ ὀχυρώμασιν καταλείψει
ἀτιμίαν

12 ἐπιθυμίαι ἀσεβῶν κακαί αἱ δὲ ῥίζαι τῶν εὐσεβῶν ἐν ὀχυρώμασιν

13 δι᾽ ἁμαρτίαν χειλέων ἐμπίπτει εἰς παγίδας ἁμαρτωλός ἐκφεύγει δὲ ἐξ
αὐτῶν δίκαιος  
ὁ βλέπων λεῖα ἐλεηθήσεται ὁ δὲ συναντῶν ἐν πύλαις ἐκθλίψει ψυχάς

14 ἀπὸ καρπῶν στόματος ψυχὴ ἀνδρὸς πλησθήσεται ἀγαθῶν ἀνταπόδομα δὲ
χειλέων αὐτοῦ δοθήσεται αὐτῷ

15 ὁδοὶ ἀφρόνων ὀρθαὶ ἐνώπιον αὐτῶν εἰσακούει δὲ συμβουλίας σοφός

16 ἄφρων αὐθημερὸν ἐξαγγέλλει ὀργὴν αὐτοῦ κρύπτει δὲ τὴν ἑαυτοῦ ἀτιμίαν
πανοῦργος

17 ἐπιδεικνυμένην πίστιν ἀπαγγέλλει δίκαιος ὁ δὲ μάρτυς τῶν ἀδίκων
δόλιος

18 εἰσὶν οἳ λέγοντες τιτρώσκουσιν μαχαίρᾳ γλῶσσαι δὲ σοφῶν ἰῶνται

19 χείλη ἀληθινὰ κατορθοῖ μαρτυρίαν μάρτυς δὲ ταχὺς γλῶσσαν ἔχει ἄδικον

20 δόλος ἐν καρδίᾳ τεκταινομένου κακά οἱ δὲ βουλόμενοι εἰρήνην
εὐφρανθήσονται

21 οὐκ ἀρέσει τῷ δικαίῳ οὐδὲν ἄδικον οἱ δὲ ἀσεβεῖς πλησθήσονται κακῶν

22 βδέλυγμα κυρίῳ χείλη ψευδῆ ὁ δὲ ποιῶν πίστεις δεκτὸς παρ᾽ αὐτῷ

23 ἀνὴρ συνετὸς θρόνος αἰσθήσεως καρδία δὲ ἀφρόνων συναντήσεται ἀραῖς

24 χεὶρ ἐκλεκτῶν κρατήσει εὐχερῶς δόλιοι δὲ ἔσονται εἰς προνομήν

25 φοβερὸς λόγος καρδίαν ταράσσει ἀνδρὸς δικαίου ἀγγελία δὲ ἀγαθὴ
εὐφραίνει αὐτόν

26 ἐπιγνώμων δίκαιος ἑαυτοῦ φίλος ἔσται αἱ δὲ γνῶμαι τῶν ἀσεβῶν
ἀνεπιεικεῖς ἁμαρτάνοντας καταδιώξεται κακά ἡ δὲ ὁδὸς τῶν ἀσεβῶν πλανήσει
αὐτούς

27 οὐκ ἐπιτεύξεται δόλιος θήρας κτῆμα δὲ τίμιον ἀνὴρ καθαρός

28 ἐν ὁδοῖς δικαιοσύνης ζωή ὁδοὶ δὲ μνησικάκων εἰς θάνατον

‎26 ‏יָתֵ֣ר מֵרֵעֵ֣הוּ צַדִּ֑יק וְדֶ֖רֶךְ רְשָׁעִ֣ים תַּתְעֵֽם׃

‎27 ‏לֹא־יַחֲרֹ֣ךְ רְמִיָּ֣ה צֵיד֑וֹ וְהוֹן־אָדָ֖ם יָקָ֣ר חָרֽוּץ׃

‎28 ‏בְּאֹֽרַח־צְדָקָ֥ה חַיִּ֑ים וְדֶ֖רֶךְ נְתִיבָ֣ה אַל־מָֽוֶת׃

[Vul](../vul/pro012.md)

1 \[Qui diligit disciplinam diligit scientiam;  
qui autem odit increpationes insipiens est.  

2 Qui bonus est hauriet gratiam a Domino;  
qui autem confidit in cogitationibus suis impie agit.  

3 Non roborabitur homo ex impietate,  
et radix justorum non commovebitur.  

4 Mulier diligens corona est viro suo;  
et putredo in ossibus ejus, quæ confusione res dignas gerit.  

5 Cogitationes justorum judicia,  
et consilia impiorum fraudulenta.  

6 Verba impiorum insidiantur sanguini;  
os justorum liberabit eos.  

7 Verte impios, et non erunt;  
domus autem justorum permanebit.  

8 Doctrina sua noscetur vir;  
qui autem vanus et excors est patebit contemptui.  

9 Melior est pauper et sufficiens sibi  
quam gloriosus et indigens pane.  

10 Novit justus jumentorum suorum animas;  
viscera autem impiorum crudelia.  

11 Qui operatur terram suam satiabitur panibus;  
qui autem sectatur otium stultissimus est.  
Qui suavis est in vini demorationibus,  
in suis munitionibus relinquit contumeliam.  

12 Desiderium impii munimentum est pessimorum;  
radix autem justorum proficiet.\]  

13 \[Propter peccata labiorum ruina proximat malo;  
effugiet autem justus de angustia.  

14 De fructu oris sui unusquisque replebitur bonis,  
et juxta opera manuum suarum retribuetur ei.  

15 Via stulti recta in oculis ejus;  
qui autem sapiens est audit consilia.  

16 Fatuus statim indicat iram suam;  
qui autem dissimulat injuriam callidus est.  

17 Qui quod novit loquitur, index justitiæ est;  
qui autem mentitur, testis est fraudulentus.  

18 Est qui promittit, et quasi gladio pungitur conscientiæ:  
lingua autem sapientium sanitas est.  

19 Labium veritatis firmum erit in perpetuum;  
qui autem testis est repentinus, concinnat linguam mendacii.  

20 Dolus in corde cogitantium mala;  
qui autem pacis ineunt consilia, sequitur eos gaudium.  

21 Non contristabit justum quidquid ei acciderit:  
impii autem replebuntur malo.  

22 Abominatio est Domino labia mendacia;  
qui autem fideliter agunt placent ei.  

23 Homo versatus celat scientiam,  
et cor insipientium provocat stultitiam.\]  

24 \[Manus fortium dominabitur;  
quæ autem remissa est, tributis serviet.  

25 Mœror in corde viri humiliabit illum,  
et sermone bono lætificabitur.  

26 Qui negligit damnum propter amicum, justus est;  
iter autem impiorum decipiet eos.  

27 Non inveniet fraudulentus lucrum,  
et substantia hominis erit auri pretium.  

28 In semita justitiæ vita;  
iter autem devium ducit ad mortem.\]
