### Job 26

[KJV](../kjv/job026.md)

1  But Job<sup>[347](h0347)</sup> answered<sup>[6030](h6030.md)</sup> and
said,<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup>

2  How<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> hast thou helped<sup>[5826](h5826.md)</sup>
him that is without<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
power?<sup>[3581](h3581)</sup> how savest<sup>[3467](h3467.md)</sup> thou
the arm<sup>[2220](h2220)</sup> that hath no<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
strength?<sup>[5797](h5797.md)</sup>

3  How<sup>[4100](h4100.md)</sup> hast thou
counseled<sup>[3289](h3289.md)</sup> him that hath
no<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> wisdom?<sup>[2451](h2451.md)</sup> and how hast
thou plentifully<sup>[7230](h7230.md)</sup>
declared<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> the thing<sup>[8454](h8454.md)</sup> as it
is?

4  To<sup>[854](h0854)</sup> whom<sup>[4310](h4310.md)</sup> hast thou
uttered<sup>[5046](h5046)</sup> words?<sup>[4405](h4405.md)</sup> and
whose<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup> spirit<sup>[5397](h5397.md)</sup>
came<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup> from<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup> thee?

5  Dead<sup>[7496](h7496.md)</sup> things are
formed<sup>[2342](h2342)</sup> from under<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[8478](h8478)</sup> the waters,<sup>[4325](h4325.md)</sup> and the
inhabitants<sup>[7931](h7931.md)</sup> thereof.

6  Hell<sup>[7585](h7585)</sup> is naked<sup>[6174](h6174.md)</sup>
before<sup>[5048](h5048.md)</sup> him, and
destruction<sup>[11](h0011)</sup> hath no<sup>[369](h0369.md)</sup>
covering.<sup>[3682](h3682.md)</sup>

7  He stretcheth out<sup>[5186](h5186.md)</sup> the
north<sup>[6828](h6828)</sup> over<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> the empty
place,<sup>[8414](h8414)</sup> and hangeth<sup>[8518](h8518.md)</sup> the
earth<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
nothing.<sup>[1099](h1099.md)</sup>

8  He bindeth up<sup>[6887](h6887.md)</sup> the
waters<sup>[4325](h4325.md)</sup> in his thick
clouds;<sup>[5645](h5645)</sup> and the cloud<sup>[6051](h6051.md)</sup> is
not rent<sup>[1234](h1234)</sup> <sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
under<sup>[8478](h8478.md)</sup> them.

9  He holdeth back<sup>[270](h0270.md)</sup> the
face<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> of his throne,<sup>[3678](h3678.md)</sup> and
spreadeth<sup>[6576](h6576)</sup> his cloud<sup>[6051](h6051.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> it.

10  He hath compassed<sup>[2328](h2328)</sup> <sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
the waters<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> <sup>[4325](h4325.md)</sup> with
bounds,<sup>[2706](h2706)</sup> until<sup>[5704](h5704.md)</sup> the
day<sup>[216](h0216)</sup> and night<sup>[2822](h2822.md)</sup> come to an
end.<sup>[8503](h8503.md)</sup>

11  The pillars<sup>[5982](h5982.md)</sup> of
heaven<sup>[8064](h8064)</sup> tremble<sup>[7322](h7322.md)</sup> and are
astonished<sup>[8539](h8539.md)</sup> at his
reproof.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[1606](h1606.md)</sup>

12  He divideth<sup>[7280](h7280)</sup> the sea<sup>[3220](h3220.md)</sup>
with his power,<sup>[3581](h3581.md)</sup> and by his
understanding<sup>[8394](h8394.md)</sup> he smiteth
through<sup>[4272](h4272)</sup> the proud.<sup>[7293](h7293.md)</sup>

13  By his spirit<sup>[7307](h7307.md)</sup> he hath
garnished<sup>[8235](h8235)</sup> the heavens;<sup>[8064](h8064.md)</sup>
his hand<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> hath formed<sup>[2490](h2490.md)</sup> the
crooked<sup>[1281](h1281)</sup> serpent.<sup>[5175](h5175.md)</sup>

14  Lo,<sup>[2005](h2005)</sup> these<sup>[428](h0428.md)</sup> are
parts<sup>[7098](h7098)</sup> of his ways:<sup>[1870](h1870.md)</sup> but
how<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> little<sup>[8102](h8102.md)</sup> a
portion<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup> is heard<sup>[8085](h8085.md)</sup> of him?
but the thunder<sup>[7482](h7482.md)</sup> of his
power<sup>[1369](h1369)</sup> who<sup>[4310](h4310.md)</sup> can
understand?<sup>[995](h0995.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/job026.md)

1 ὑπολαβὼν δὲ Ιωβ λέγει

2 τίνι πρόσκεισαι ἢ τίνι μέλλεις βοηθεῖν πότερον οὐχ ᾧ πολλὴ ἰσχὺς καὶ ᾧ
βραχίων κραταιός ἐστιν

3 τίνι συμβεβούλευσαι οὐχ ᾧ πᾶσα σοφία ἢ τίνι ἐπακολουθήσεις οὐχ ᾧ
μεγίστη δύναμις

4 τίνι ἀνήγγειλας ῥήματα πνοὴ δὲ τίνος ἐστὶν ἡ ἐξελθοῦσα ἐκ σοῦ

5 μὴ γίγαντες μαιωθήσονται ὑποκάτωθεν ὕδατος καὶ τῶν γειτόνων αὐτοῦ

6 γυμνὸς ὁ ᾅδης ἐπώπιον αὐτοῦ καὶ οὐκ ἔστιν περιβόλαιον τῇ ἀπωλείᾳ

7 ἐκτείνων βορέαν ἐπ᾽ οὐδέν κρεμάζων γῆν ἐπὶ οὐδενός

8 δεσμεύων ὕδωρ ἐν νεφέλαις αὐτοῦ καὶ οὐκ ἐρράγη νέφος ὑποκάτω αὐτοῦ

9 ὁ κρατῶν πρόσωπον θρόνου ἐκπετάζων ἐπ᾽ αὐτὸν νέφος αὐτοῦ

10 πρόσταγμα ἐγύρωσεν ἐπὶ πρόσωπον ὕδατος μέχρι συντελείας φωτὸς μετὰ
σκότους

11 στῦλοι οὐρανοῦ ἐπετάσθησαν καὶ ἐξέστησαν ἀπὸ τῆς ἐπιτιμήσεως αὐτοῦ

12 ἰσχύι κατέπαυσεν τὴν θάλασσαν ἐπιστήμῃ δὲ ἔτρωσε τὸ κῆτος

13 κλεῖθρα δὲ οὐρανοῦ δεδοίκασιν αὐτόν προστάγματι δὲ ἐθανάτωσεν
δράκοντα ἀποστάτην

14 ἰδοὺ ταῦτα μέρη ὁδοῦ αὐτοῦ καὶ ἐπὶ ἰκμάδα λόγου ἀκουσόμεθα ἐν αὐτῷ
σθένος δὲ βροντῆς αὐτοῦ τίς οἶδεν ὁπότε ποιήσει

[Vul](../vul/job026.md)

1 Respondens autem Job dixit:

2 \[Cujus adjutor es? numquid imbecillis?  
et sustentas brachium ejus qui non est fortis?  

3 Cui dedisti consilium?  
forsitan illi qui non habet sapientiam:  
et prudentiam tuam ostendisti plurimam.  

4 Quem docere voluisti?  
nonne eum qui fecit spiramentum?  

5 Ecce gigantes gemunt sub aquis,  
et qui habitant cum eis.  

6 Nudus est infernus coram illo,  
et nullum est operimentum perditioni.  

7 Qui extendit aquilonem super vacuum,  
et appendit terram super nihilum.  

8 Qui ligat aquas in nubibus suis,  
ut non erumpant pariter deorsum.  

9 Qui tenet vultum solii sui,  
et expandit super illud nebulam suam.  

10 Terminum circumdedit aquis,  
usque dum finiantur lux et tenebræ.  

11 Columnæ cæli contremiscunt,  
et pavent ad nutum ejus.  

12 In fortitudine illius repente maria congregata sunt,  
et prudentia ejus percussit superbum.  

13 Spiritus ejus ornavit cælos,  
et obstetricante manu ejus, eductus est coluber tortuosus.  

14 Ecce hæc ex parte dicta sunt viarum ejus:  
et cum vix parvam stillam sermonis ejus audierimus,  
quis poterit tonitruum magnitudinis illius intueri?\]
