### Psalms 55

[KJV](../kjv/psa055.md)

1  To the chief Musician<sup>[5329](h5329.md)</sup> on
Neginoth,<sup>[5058](h5058)</sup> Maschil,<sup>[4905](h4905.md)</sup> A
Psalm of David.<sup>[1732](h1732)</sup> Give ear<sup>[238](h0238.md)</sup>
to my prayer,<sup>[8605](h8605)</sup> O God;<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup> and
hide not thyself<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> <sup>[5956](h5956.md)</sup> from my
supplication.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[8467](h8467.md)</sup>

2  Attend<sup>[7181](h7181.md)</sup> unto me, and
hear<sup>[6030](h6030)</sup> me: I mourn<sup>[7300](h7300.md)</sup> in my
complaint,<sup>[7879](h7879.md)</sup> and make a
noise;<sup>[1949](h1949.md)</sup>

3  Because of the voice<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6963](h6963.md)</sup>
of the enemy,<sup>[341](h0341)</sup> because<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> of the oppression<sup>[6125](h6125.md)</sup> of
the wicked:<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup> they
cast<sup>[4131](h4131)</sup> iniquity<sup>[205](h0205.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> me, and in wrath<sup>[639](h0639.md)</sup>
they hate<sup>[7852](h7852.md)</sup> me.

4  My heart<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup> is sore
pained<sup>[2342](h2342)</sup> within<sup>[7130](h7130.md)</sup> me: and
the terrors<sup>[367](h0367)</sup> of death<sup>[4194](h4194.md)</sup> are
fallen<sup>[5307](h5307)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> me.

5  Fearfulness<sup>[3374](h3374.md)</sup> and
trembling<sup>[7461](h7461)</sup> are come<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup> upon
me, and horror<sup>[6427](h6427.md)</sup> hath
overwhelmed<sup>[3680](h3680.md)</sup> me.

6  And I said,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> Oh that<sup>[4310](h4310.md)</sup> I
had<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> wings<sup>[83](h0083.md)</sup> like a
dove!<sup>[3123](h3123.md)</sup> for then would I fly
away,<sup>[5774](h5774)</sup> and be at rest.<sup>[7931](h7931.md)</sup>

7  Lo,<sup>[2009](h2009.md)</sup> then would I
wander<sup>[5074](h5074)</sup> far off,<sup>[7368](h7368.md)</sup> and
remain<sup>[3885](h3885.md)</sup> in the
wilderness.<sup>[4057](h4057)</sup> Selah.<sup>[5542](h5542.md)</sup>

8  I would hasten<sup>[2363](h2363.md)</sup> my
escape<sup>[4655](h4655)</sup> from the windy<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> storm<sup>[5584](h5584.md)</sup> and
tempest.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[5591](h5591.md)</sup>

9  Destroy,<sup>[1104](h1104)</sup> O Lord,<sup>[136](h0136.md)</sup> and
divide<sup>[6385](h6385)</sup> their tongues:<sup>[3956](h3956.md)</sup>
for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> I have seen<sup>[7200](h7200.md)</sup>
violence<sup>[2555](h2555)</sup> and strife<sup>[7379](h7379.md)</sup> in
the city.<sup>[5892](h5892.md)</sup>

10  Day<sup>[3119](h3119)</sup> and night<sup>[3915](h3915.md)</sup> they
go about<sup>[5437](h5437)</sup> it upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> the
walls<sup>[2346](h2346)</sup> thereof: mischief<sup>[205](h0205.md)</sup>
also and sorrow<sup>[5999](h5999.md)</sup> are in the
midst<sup>[7130](h7130.md)</sup> of it.

11  Wickedness<sup>[1942](h1942.md)</sup> is in the
midst<sup>[7130](h7130)</sup> thereof: deceit<sup>[8496](h8496.md)</sup>
and guile<sup>[4820](h4820)</sup> depart<sup>[4185](h4185.md)</sup>
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> from<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup> her
streets.<sup>[7339](h7339.md)</sup>

12  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> it was not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> an
enemy<sup>[341](h0341)</sup> that reproached<sup>[2778](h2778.md)</sup> me;
then I could have borne<sup>[5375](h5375.md)</sup> it:
neither<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> was it he that
hated<sup>[8130](h8130.md)</sup> me that did
magnify<sup>[1431](h1431)</sup> himself against<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
me; then I would have hid myself<sup>[5641](h5641.md)</sup>
from<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup> him:

13  But it was thou,<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> a man<sup>[582](h0582.md)</sup>
mine equal,<sup>[6187](h6187)</sup> my guide,<sup>[441](h0441.md)</sup> and
mine acquaintance.<sup>[3045](h3045.md)</sup>

14  We took sweet<sup>[4985](h4985.md)</sup>
counsel<sup>[5475](h5475)</sup> together,<sup>[3162](h3162.md)</sup> and
walked<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> unto the house<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup> of
God<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> in company.<sup>[7285](h7285.md)</sup>

15  Let death<sup>[4194](h4194)</sup> seize<sup>[5377](h5377.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> them, and let them go
down<sup>[3381](h3381)</sup> quick<sup>[2416](h2416.md)</sup> into
hell:<sup>[7585](h7585)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup>
wickedness<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup> is in their
dwellings,<sup>[4033](h4033)</sup> and among<sup>[7130](h7130.md)</sup>
them.

16  As for me, I<sup>[589](h0589.md)</sup> will
call<sup>[7121](h7121)</sup> upon<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup>
God;<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> and the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup> shall
save<sup>[3467](h3467.md)</sup> me.

17  Evening,<sup>[6153](h6153.md)</sup> and
morning,<sup>[1242](h1242)</sup> and at noon,<sup>[6672](h6672.md)</sup>
will I pray,<sup>[7878](h7878.md)</sup> and cry
aloud:<sup>[1993](h1993)</sup> and he shall hear<sup>[8085](h8085.md)</sup>
my voice.<sup>[6963](h6963.md)</sup>

18  He hath delivered<sup>[6299](h6299.md)</sup> my
soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> in peace<sup>[7965](h7965.md)</sup> from the
battle<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7128](h7128.md)</sup> that was against
me: for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> there were<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup>
many<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup> with<sup>[5978](h5978.md)</sup> me.

19  God<sup>[410](h0410)</sup> shall hear,<sup>[8085](h8085.md)</sup> and
afflict<sup>[6031](h6031.md)</sup> them, even he that
abideth<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup> of old.<sup>[6924](h6924.md)</sup>
Selah.<sup>[5542](h5542)</sup> Because<sup>[834](h0834.md)</sup> they have
no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> changes,<sup>[2487](h2487.md)</sup> therefore
they fear<sup>[3372](h3372)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
God.<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup>

20  He hath put forth<sup>[7971](h7971.md)</sup> his
hands<sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup> against such as be at
peace<sup>[7965](h7965.md)</sup> with him: he hath
broken<sup>[2490](h2490)</sup> his covenant.<sup>[1285](h1285.md)</sup>

21  The words of his mouth<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> were
smoother<sup>[2505](h2505)</sup> than butter,<sup>[4260](h4260.md)</sup>
but war<sup>[7128](h7128.md)</sup> was in his
heart:<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> his words<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup> were
softer<sup>[7401](h7401)</sup> than oil,<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[8081](h8081)</sup> yet were they<sup>[1992](h1992.md)</sup> drawn
swords.<sup>[6609](h6609.md)</sup>

22  Cast<sup>[7993](h7993)</sup> thy burden<sup>[3053](h3053.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup> and
he<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> shall sustain<sup>[3557](h3557.md)</sup> thee:
he shall never<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> <sup>[5769](h5769.md)</sup>
suffer<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> the righteous<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> to
be moved.<sup>[4131](h4131.md)</sup>

23  But thou,<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> O God,<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup> shalt
bring them down<sup>[3381](h3381.md)</sup> into the
pit<sup>[875](h0875)</sup> of destruction:<sup>[7845](h7845.md)</sup>
bloody<sup>[1818](h1818)</sup> and deceitful<sup>[4820](h4820.md)</sup>
men<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> shall not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> live out
half<sup>[2673](h2673)</sup> their days;<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup> but
I<sup>[589](h0589)</sup> will trust<sup>[982](h0982.md)</sup> in thee.

[Sep](../sep/psa055.md)

1 εἰς τὸ τέλος ὑπὲρ τοῦ λαοῦ τοῦ ἀπὸ τῶν ἁγίων μεμακρυμμένου τῷ Δαυιδ
εἰς στηλογραφίαν ὁπότε ἐκράτησαν αὐτὸν οἱ ἀλλόφυλοι ἐν Γεθ

2 ἐλέησόν με κύριε ὅτι κατεπάτησέν με ἄνθρωπος ὅλην τὴν ἡμέραν πολεμῶν
ἔθλιψέν με

3 κατεπάτησάν με οἱ ἐχθροί μου ὅλην τὴν ἡμέραν ὅτι πολλοὶ οἱ πολεμοῦντές
με ἀπὸ ὕψους

4 ἡμέρας φοβηθήσομαι ἐγὼ δὲ ἐπὶ σοὶ ἐλπιῶ

5 ἐν τῷ θεῷ ἐπαινέσω τοὺς λόγους μου ὅλην τὴν ἡμέραν ἐπὶ τῷ θεῷ ἤλπισα
οὐ φοβηθήσομαι τί ποιήσει μοι σάρξ

6 ὅλην τὴν ἡμέραν τοὺς λόγους μου ἐβδελύσσοντο κατ᾽ ἐμοῦ πάντες οἱ
διαλογισμοὶ αὐτῶν εἰς κακόν

7 παροικήσουσιν καὶ κατακρύψουσιν αὐτοὶ τὴν πτέρναν μου φυλάξουσιν
καθάπερ ὑπέμειναν τὴν ψυχήν μου

8 ὑπὲρ τοῦ μηθενὸς σώσεις αὐτούς ἐν ὀργῇ λαοὺς κατάξεις ὁ θεός

9 τὴν ζωήν μου ἐξήγγειλά σοι ἔθου τὰ δάκρυά μου ἐνώπιόν σου ὡς καὶ ἐν τῇ
ἐπαγγελίᾳ σου

10 ἐπιστρέψουσιν οἱ ἐχθροί μου εἰς τὰ ὀπίσω ἐν ᾗ ἂν ἡμέρᾳ ἐπικαλέσωμαί
σε ἰδοὺ ἔγνων ὅτι θεός μου εἶ σύ

11 ἐπὶ τῷ θεῷ αἰνέσω ῥῆμα ἐπὶ τῷ κυρίῳ αἰνέσω λόγον

12 ἐπὶ τῷ θεῷ ἤλπισα οὐ φοβηθήσομαι τί ποιήσει μοι ἄνθρωπος

13 ἐν ἐμοί ὁ θεός αἱ εὐχαὶ ἃς ἀποδώσω αἰνέσεώς σοι

14 ὅτι ἐρρύσω τὴν ψυχήν μου ἐκ θανάτου καὶ τοὺς πόδας μου ἐξ ὀλισθήματος
τοῦ εὐαρεστῆσαι ἐνώπιον τοῦ θεοῦ ἐν φωτὶ ζώντων

[Vul](../vul/psa055.md)

1 In finem, pro populo qui a sanctis longe factus est. David in tituli
inscriptionem, cum tenuerunt eum Allophyli in Geth.

2 \[Miserere mei, Deus, quoniam conculcavit me homo;  
tota die impugnans, tribulavit me.  

3 Conculcaverunt me inimici mei tota die,  
quoniam multi bellantes adversum me.  

4 Ab altitudine diei timebo:  
ego vero in te sperabo.  

5 In Deo laudabo sermones meos;  
in Deo speravi:  
non timebo quid faciat mihi caro.  

6 Tota die verba mea execrabantur;  
adversum me omnes cogitationes eorum in malum.  

7 Inhabitabunt, et abscondent;  
ipsi calcaneum meum observabunt.  
Sicut sustinuerunt animam meam,  

8 pro nihilo salvos facies illos;  
in ira populos confringes.  

9 Deus, vitam meam annuntiavi tibi;  
posuisti lacrimas meas in conspectu tuo,  
sicut et in promissione tua:  

10 tunc convertentur inimici mei retrorsum.  
In quacumque die invocavero te,  
ecce cognovi quoniam Deus meus es.  

11 In Deo laudabo verbum;  
in Domino laudabo sermonem.  
In Deo speravi:  
non timebo quid faciat mihi homo.  

12 In me sunt, Deus, vota tua,  
quæ reddam, laudationes tibi:  

13 quoniam eripuisti animam meam de morte,  
et pedes meos de lapsu,  
ut placeam coram Deo in lumine viventium.\]
