### Job 24

[KJV](../kjv/job024.md)

1  Why,<sup>[4069](h4069)</sup> seeing times<sup>[6256](h6256.md)</sup> are
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> hidden<sup>[6845](h6845.md)</sup> from the
Almighty,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7706](h7706.md)</sup> do they that
know<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> him not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
see<sup>[2372](h2372)</sup> his days?<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup>

2  Some remove<sup>[5381](h5381.md)</sup> the
landmarks;<sup>[1367](h1367.md)</sup> they violently take
away<sup>[1497](h1497)</sup> flocks,<sup>[5739](h5739.md)</sup> and
feed<sup>[7462](h7462.md)</sup> thereof.

3  They drive away<sup>[5090](h5090.md)</sup> the
ass<sup>[2543](h2543)</sup> of the fatherless,<sup>[3490](h3490.md)</sup>
they take the widow's ox for a pledge.<sup>[2254](h2254.md)</sup>
<sup>[7794](h7794)</sup> <sup>[490](h0490.md)</sup>

4  They turn<sup>[5186](h5186)</sup> the needy<sup>[34](h0034.md)</sup> out
of the way:<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[1870](h1870.md)</sup> the
poor<sup>[6041](h6041)</sup> of the earth<sup>[776](h0776.md)</sup>
hide<sup>[2244](h2244.md)</sup> themselves
together.<sup>[3162](h3162.md)</sup>

5  Behold,<sup>[2005](h2005)</sup> as wild asses<sup>[6501](h6501.md)</sup>
in the desert,<sup>[4057](h4057.md)</sup> go they
forth<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup> to their work;<sup>[6467](h6467.md)</sup>
rising quickly<sup>[7836](h7836.md)</sup> for a
prey:<sup>[2964](h2964)</sup> the wilderness<sup>[6160](h6160.md)</sup>
yieldeth food<sup>[3899](h3899.md)</sup> for them and for their
children.<sup>[5288](h5288.md)</sup>

6  They reap<sup>[7114](h7114.md)</sup> every one his
corn<sup>[1098](h1098)</sup> in the field:<sup>[7704](h7704.md)</sup> and
they gather<sup>[3953](h3953)</sup> the vintage<sup>[3754](h3754.md)</sup>

7  They cause the naked<sup>[6174](h6174.md)</sup> to
lodge<sup>[3885](h3885)</sup> without<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[1097](h1097)</sup> clothing,<sup>[3830](h3830.md)</sup> that they
have no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> covering<sup>[3682](h3682.md)</sup> in the
cold.<sup>[7135](h7135.md)</sup>

8  They are wet<sup>[7372](h7372.md)</sup> with the
showers<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[2230](h2230.md)</sup> of the

<sup>[1097](h1097)</sup> of a shelter.<sup>[4268](h4268.md)</sup>

9  They pluck<sup>[1497](h1497.md)</sup> the
fatherless<sup>[3490](h3490.md)</sup> from the
breast,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7699](h7699.md)</sup> and take a
pledge<sup>[2254](h2254)</sup> of<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> the
poor.<sup>[6041](h6041.md)</sup>

10  They cause him to go<sup>[1980](h1980.md)</sup>
naked<sup>[6174](h6174)</sup> without<sup>[1097](h1097.md)</sup>
clothing,<sup>[3830](h3830.md)</sup> and they take
away<sup>[5375](h5375)</sup> the sheaf<sup>[6016](h6016.md)</sup> from the
hungry;<sup>[7457](h7457.md)</sup>

11  Which make oil<sup>[6671](h6671)</sup> within<sup>[996](h0996.md)</sup>
their walls,<sup>[7791](h7791)</sup> and tread<sup>[1869](h1869.md)</sup>
their winepresses,<sup>[3342](h3342.md)</sup> and suffer
thirst.<sup>[6770](h6770.md)</sup>

12  Men<sup>[4962](h4962)</sup> groan<sup>[5008](h5008.md)</sup> from out
of the city,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[5892](h5892.md)</sup> and the
soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> of the wounded<sup>[2491](h2491.md)</sup>
crieth out:<sup>[7768](h7768)</sup> yet God<sup>[433](h0433.md)</sup>
layeth<sup>[7760](h7760)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
folly<sup>[8604](h8604.md)</sup> to them.

13  They<sup>[1992](h1992)</sup> are<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup> of those
that rebel<sup>[4775](h4775.md)</sup> against the
light;<sup>[216](h0216)</sup> they know<sup>[5234](h5234.md)</sup>
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> the ways<sup>[1870](h1870.md)</sup> thereof,
nor<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> abide<sup>[3427](h3427.md)</sup> in the
paths<sup>[5410](h5410.md)</sup> thereof.

14  The murderer<sup>[7523](h7523)</sup> rising<sup>[6965](h6965.md)</sup>
with the light<sup>[216](h0216)</sup> killeth<sup>[6991](h6991.md)</sup>
the poor<sup>[6041](h6041)</sup> and needy,<sup>[34](h0034.md)</sup> and in
the night<sup>[3915](h3915)</sup> is<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup> as a
thief.<sup>[1590](h1590.md)</sup>

15  The eye<sup>[5869](h5869.md)</sup> also of the
adulterer<sup>[5003](h5003)</sup> waiteth<sup>[8104](h8104.md)</sup> for
the twilight,<sup>[5399](h5399)</sup> saying,<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup>
No<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> eye<sup>[5869](h5869.md)</sup> shall
see<sup>[7789](h7789)</sup> me: and disguiseth<sup>[5643](h5643.md)</sup>
<sup>[7760](h7760)</sup> his face.<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup>

16  In the dark<sup>[2822](h2822.md)</sup> they dig
through<sup>[2864](h2864)</sup> houses,<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup> which
they had marked<sup>[2856](h2856.md)</sup> for themselves in the
daytime:<sup>[3119](h3119)</sup> they know<sup>[3045](h3045.md)</sup>
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> the light.<sup>[216](h0216.md)</sup>

17  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> the morning<sup>[1242](h1242.md)</sup> is
to them even as<sup>[3162](h3162.md)</sup> the shadow of
death:<sup>[6757](h6757)</sup> if<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup> one
know<sup>[5234](h5234.md)</sup> them, they are in the
terrors<sup>[1091](h1091.md)</sup> of the shadow of
death.<sup>[6757](h6757.md)</sup>

18  He<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> is swift<sup>[7031](h7031.md)</sup>
as<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> <sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> the
waters;<sup>[4325](h4325)</sup> their portion<sup>[2513](h2513.md)</sup> is
cursed<sup>[7043](h7043)</sup> in the earth:<sup>[776](h0776.md)</sup> he
beholdeth<sup>[6437](h6437)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> the
way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of the vineyards.<sup>[3754](h3754.md)</sup>

19  Drought<sup>[6723](h6723)</sup> and<sup>[1571](h1571.md)</sup>
heat<sup>[2527](h2527)</sup> consume<sup>[1497](h1497.md)</sup> the
snow<sup>[7950](h7950)</sup> waters:<sup>[4325](h4325.md)</sup> so doth the
grave<sup>[7585](h7585.md)</sup> those which have
sinned.<sup>[2398](h2398.md)</sup>

20  The womb<sup>[7358](h7358.md)</sup> shall
forget<sup>[7911](h7911)</sup> him; the worm<sup>[7415](h7415.md)</sup>
shall feed sweetly<sup>[4988](h4988.md)</sup> on him; he shall be
no<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> more<sup>[5750](h5750.md)</sup>
remembered;<sup>[2142](h2142.md)</sup> and
wickedness<sup>[5766](h5766.md)</sup> shall be
broken<sup>[7665](h7665)</sup> as a tree.<sup>[6086](h6086.md)</sup>

21  He evil entreateth<sup>[7462](h7462.md)</sup> the
barren<sup>[6135](h6135)</sup> that beareth<sup>[3205](h3205.md)</sup>
not:<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> and doeth not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
good<sup>[3190](h3190)</sup> to the widow.<sup>[490](h0490.md)</sup>

22  He draweth<sup>[4900](h4900.md)</sup> also the
mighty<sup>[47](h0047)</sup> with his power:<sup>[3581](h3581.md)</sup> he
riseth up,<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> and no<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> man is
sure<sup>[539](h0539)</sup> of life.<sup>[2416](h2416.md)</sup>

23  Though it be given<sup>[5414](h5414.md)</sup> him to be in
safety,<sup>[983](h0983.md)</sup> whereon he
resteth;<sup>[8172](h8172)</sup> yet his eyes<sup>[5869](h5869.md)</sup>
are upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> their ways.<sup>[1870](h1870.md)</sup>

24  They are exalted<sup>[7426](h7426.md)</sup> for a little
while,<sup>[4592](h4592)</sup> but are gone<sup>[369](h0369.md)</sup> and
brought low;<sup>[4355](h4355.md)</sup> they are taken out of the
way<sup>[7092](h7092)</sup> as all<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup> other, and
cut off<sup>[5243](h5243)</sup> as the tops<sup>[7218](h7218.md)</sup> of
the ears of corn.<sup>[7641](h7641.md)</sup>

25  And if<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> it be not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> so
now,<sup>[645](h0645)</sup> who<sup>[4310](h4310.md)</sup> will make me a

speech<sup>[4405](h4405)</sup> nothing<sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup> worth?

[Sep](../sep/job024.md)

1 διὰ τί δὲ κύριον ἔλαθον ὧραι

2 ἀσεβεῖς δὲ ὅριον ὑπερέβησαν ποίμνιον σὺν ποιμένι ἁρπάσαντες

3 ὑποζύγιον ὀρφανῶν ἀπήγαγον καὶ βοῦν χήρας ἠνεχύρασαν

4 ἐξέκλιναν ἀδυνάτους ἐξ ὁδοῦ δικαίας ὁμοθυμαδὸν ἐκρύβησαν πραεῖς γῆς

5 ἀπέβησαν δὲ ὥσπερ ὄνοι ἐν ἀγρῷ ὑπὲρ ἐμοῦ ἐξελθόντες τὴν ἑαυτῶν πρᾶξιν
ἡδύνθη αὐτῷ ἄρτος εἰς νεωτέρους

6 ἀγρὸν πρὸ ὥρας οὐκ αὐτῶν ὄντα ἐθέρισαν ἀδύνατοι δὲ ἀμπελῶνας ἀσεβῶν
ἀμισθὶ καὶ ἀσιτὶ ἠργάσαντο

7 γυμνοὺς πολλοὺς ἐκοίμισαν ἄνευ ἱματίων ἀμφίασιν δὲ ψυχῆς αὐτῶν
ἀφείλαντο

8 ἀπὸ ψεκάδων ὀρέων ὑγραίνονται παρὰ τὸ μὴ ἔχειν αὐτοὺς σκέπην πέτραν
περιεβάλοντο

9 ἥρπασαν ὀρφανὸν ἀπὸ μαστοῦ ἐκπεπτωκότα δὲ ἐταπείνωσαν

10 γυμνοὺς δὲ ἐκοίμισαν ἀδίκως πεινώντων δὲ τὸν ψωμὸν ἀφείλαντο

11 ἐν στενοῖς ἀδίκως ἐνήδρευσαν ὁδὸν δὲ δικαίαν οὐκ ᾔδεισαν

12 οἳ ἐκ πόλεως καὶ οἴκων ἰδίων ἐξεβάλλοντο ψυχὴ δὲ νηπίων ἐστέναξεν
μέγα αὐτὸς δὲ διὰ τί τούτων ἐπισκοπὴν οὐ πεποίηται

13 ἐπὶ γῆς ὄντων αὐτῶν καὶ οὐκ ἐπέγνωσαν ὁδὸν δὲ δικαιοσύνης οὐκ ᾔδεισαν
οὐδὲ ἀτραποὺς αὐτῆς ἐπορεύθησαν

14 γνοὺς δὲ αὐτῶν τὰ ἔργα παρέδωκεν αὐτοὺς εἰς σκότος καὶ νυκτὸς ἔσται
ὡς κλέπτης

15 καὶ ὀφθαλμὸς μοιχοῦ ἐφύλαξεν σκότος λέγων οὐ προσνοήσει με ὀφθαλμός
καὶ ἀποκρυβὴν προσώπου ἔθετο

16 διώρυξεν ἐν σκότει οἰκίας ἡμέρας ἐσφράγισαν ἑαυτούς οὐκ ἐπέγνωσαν φῶς

17 ὅτι ὁμοθυμαδὸν τὸ πρωὶ αὐτοῖς σκιὰ θανάτου ὅτι ἐπιγνώσεται ταραχὰς
σκιᾶς θανάτου

18 ἐλαφρός ἐστιν ἐπὶ πρόσωπον ὕδατος καταραθείη ἡ μερὶς αὐτῶν ἐπὶ γῆς

19 ἀναφανείη δὲ τὰ φυτὰ αὐτῶν ἐπὶ γῆς ξηρά ἀγκαλίδα γὰρ ὀρφανῶν ἥρπασαν

20 εἶτ᾽ ἀνεμνήσθη αὐτοῦ ἡ ἁμαρτία ὥσπερ δὲ ὁμίχλη δρόσου ἀφανὴς ἐγένετο
ἀποδοθείη δὲ αὐτῷ ἃ ἔπραξεν συντριβείη δὲ πᾶς ἄδικος ἴσα ξύλῳ ἀνιάτῳ

21 στεῖραν γὰρ οὐκ εὖ ἐποίησεν καὶ γύναιον οὐκ ἠλέησεν

22 θυμῷ δὲ κατέστρεψεν ἀδυνάτους ἀναστὰς τοιγαροῦν οὐ μὴ πιστεύσῃ κατὰ
τῆς ἑαυτοῦ ζωῆς

23 μαλακισθεὶς μὴ ἐλπιζέτω ὑγιασθῆναι ἀλλὰ πεσεῖται νόσῳ

24 πολλοὺς γὰρ ἐκάκωσεν τὸ ὕψωμα αὐτοῦ ἐμαράνθη δὲ ὥσπερ μολόχη ἐν
καύματι ἢ ὥσπερ στάχυς ἀπὸ καλάμης αὐτόματος ἀποπεσών

25 εἰ δὲ μή τίς ἐστιν ὁ φάμενος ψευδῆ με λέγειν καὶ θήσει εἰς οὐδὲν τὰ
ῥήματά μου

‎25 ‏וְאִם־לֹ֣א אֵ֭פוֹ מִ֣י יַכְזִיבֵ֑נִי וְיָשֵׂ֥ם לְ֝אַ֗ל מִלָּתִֽי׃ ס

[Vul](../vul/job024.md)

1 \[Ab Omnipotente non sunt abscondita tempora:  
qui autem noverunt eum,  
ignorant dies illius.  

2 Alii terminos transtulerunt;  
diripuerunt greges, et paverunt eos.  

3 Asinum pupillorum abegerunt,  
et abstulerunt pro pignore bovem viduæ.  

4 Subverterunt pauperum viam,  
et oppresserunt pariter mansuetos terræ.  

5 Alii quasi onagri in deserto egrediuntur ad opus suum:  
vigilantes ad prædam, præparant panem liberis.  

6 Agrum non suum demetunt,  
et vineam ejus, quem vi oppresserint, vindemiant.  

7 Nudos dimittunt homines, indumenta tollentes,  
quibus non est operimentum in frigore:  

8 quos imbres montium rigant,  
et non habentes velamen, amplexantur lapides.  

9 Vim fecerunt deprædantes pupillos,  
et vulgum pauperem spoliaverunt.  

10 Nudis et incedentibus absque vestitu,  
et esurientibus tulerunt spicas.  

11 Inter acervos eorum meridiati sunt,  
qui calcatis torcularibus sitiunt.  

12 De civitatibus fecerunt viros gemere,  
et anima vulneratorum clamavit:  
et Deus inultum abire non patitur.  

13 Ipsi fuerunt rebelles lumini:  
nescierunt vias ejus,  
nec reversi sunt per semitas ejus.  

14 Mane primo consurgit homicida;  
interficit egenum et pauperem:  
per noctem vero erit quasi fur.  

15 Oculus adulteri observat caliginem,  
dicens: Non me videbit oculus:  
et operiet vultum suum.  

16 Perfodit in tenebris domos,  
sicut in die condixerant sibi,  
et ignoraverunt lucem.  

17 Si subito apparuerit aurora, arbitrantur umbram mortis:  
et sic in tenebris quasi in luce ambulant.  

18 Levis est super faciem aquæ:  
maledicta sit pars ejus in terra,  
nec ambulet per viam vinearum.  

19 Ad nimium calorem transeat ab aquis nivium,  
et usque ad inferos peccatum illius.  

20 Obliviscatur ejus misericordia; dulcedo illius vermes:  
non sit in recordatione,  
sed conteratur quasi lignum infructuosum.  

21 Pavit enim sterilem quæ non parit,  
et viduæ bene non fecit.  

22 Detraxit fortes in fortitudine sua,  
et cum steterit, non credet vitæ suæ.  

23 Dedit ei Deus locum pœnitentiæ,  
et ille abutitur eo in superbiam:  
oculi autem ejus sunt in viis illius.  

24 Elevati sunt ad modicum, et non subsistent:  
et humiliabuntur sicut omnia, et auferentur,  
et sicut summitates spicarum conterentur.  

25 Quod si non est ita, quis me potest arguere esse mentitum,  
et ponere ante Deum verba mea?\]
