### Job 16

[KJV](../kjv/job016.md)

1  Then Job<sup>[347](h0347)</sup> answered<sup>[6030](h6030.md)</sup> and
said,<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup>

2  I have heard<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> many<sup>[7227](h7227.md)</sup>
such things:<sup>[428](h0428)</sup> miserable<sup>[5999](h5999.md)</sup>
comforters<sup>[5162](h5162)</sup> are ye all.<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup>

3  Shall vain<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> words<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup> have
an end?<sup>[7093](h7093)</sup> or<sup>[176](h0176.md)</sup>
what<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> emboldeneth<sup>[4834](h4834.md)</sup> thee
that<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thou answerest?<sup>[6030](h6030.md)</sup>

4  I<sup>[595](h0595)</sup> also<sup>[1571](h1571.md)</sup> could
speak<sup>[1696](h1696)</sup> as ye do: if<sup>[3863](h3863.md)</sup> your
soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> were<sup>[3426](h3426.md)</sup> in my soul's
stead,<sup>[8478](h8478)</sup> <sup>[5315](h5315.md)</sup> I could heap
up<sup>[2266](h2266)</sup> words<sup>[4405](h4405.md)</sup>
against<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> you, and shake<sup>[5128](h5128.md)</sup>
mine<sup>[1119](h1119)</sup> head<sup>[7218](h7218.md)</sup>
at<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> you.

5  But I would strengthen<sup>[553](h0553.md)</sup> you
with<sup>[1119](h1119)</sup> my mouth,<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> and the
moving<sup>[5205](h5205)</sup> of my lips<sup>[8193](h8193.md)</sup> should
assuage<sup>[2820](h2820.md)</sup> your grief.

6  Though<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> I speak,<sup>[1696](h1696.md)</sup> my
grief<sup>[3511](h3511)</sup> is not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
assuaged:<sup>[2820](h2820.md)</sup> and though I
forbear,<sup>[2308](h2308)</sup> what<sup>[4100](h4100.md)</sup>
<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> am I eased?<sup>[1980](h1980.md)</sup>

7  But<sup>[389](h0389)</sup> now<sup>[6258](h6258.md)</sup> he hath made
me weary:<sup>[3811](h3811.md)</sup> thou hast made
desolate<sup>[8074](h8074)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup> my
company.<sup>[5712](h5712.md)</sup>

8  And thou hast filled me with wrath,<sup>[7059](h7059.md)</sup> which
is<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> a witness<sup>[5707](h5707.md)</sup> against me:
and my leanness<sup>[3585](h3585.md)</sup> rising
up<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> in me beareth witness<sup>[6030](h6030.md)</sup>
to my face.<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup>

9  He teareth<sup>[2963](h2963.md)</sup> me in his
wrath,<sup>[639](h0639)</sup> who hateth<sup>[7852](h7852.md)</sup> me: he
gnasheth<sup>[2786](h2786)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> me with
his teeth;<sup>[8127](h8127)</sup> mine enemy<sup>[6862](h6862.md)</sup>
sharpeneth<sup>[3913](h3913)</sup> his eyes<sup>[5869](h5869.md)</sup> upon
me.

10  They have gaped<sup>[6473](h6473)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
me with their mouth;<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> they have
smitten<sup>[5221](h5221.md)</sup> me upon the
cheek<sup>[3895](h3895)</sup> reproachfully;<sup>[2781](h2781.md)</sup>
they have gathered themselves<sup>[4390](h4390.md)</sup>
together<sup>[3162](h3162)</sup> against<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> me.

11  God<sup>[410](h0410)</sup> hath delivered<sup>[5462](h5462.md)</sup> me
to<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> the ungodly,<sup>[5760](h5760.md)</sup> and
turned me over<sup>[3399](h3399)</sup> into<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> the
hands<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> of the wicked.<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup>

12  I was<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> at ease,<sup>[7961](h7961.md)</sup> but
he hath broken me asunder:<sup>[6565](h6565.md)</sup> he hath also
taken<sup>[270](h0270)</sup> me by my neck,<sup>[6203](h6203.md)</sup> and
shaken me to pieces,<sup>[6327](h6327.md)</sup> and set me
up<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> for his mark.<sup>[4307](h4307.md)</sup>

13  His archers<sup>[7228](h7228.md)</sup> compass me round
about,<sup>[5437](h5437)</sup> <sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> he
cleaveth<sup>[6398](h6398)</sup> my reins<sup>[3629](h3629.md)</sup>
asunder, and doth not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
spare;<sup>[2550](h2550)</sup> he poureth out<sup>[8210](h8210.md)</sup> my
gall<sup>[4845](h4845)</sup> upon the ground.<sup>[776](h0776.md)</sup>

14  He breaketh<sup>[6555](h6555.md)</sup> me with
breach<sup>[6556](h6556)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> breach,<sup>[6556](h6556.md)</sup> he
runneth<sup>[7323](h7323)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> me like a
giant.<sup>[1368](h1368.md)</sup>

15  I have sewed<sup>[8609](h8609.md)</sup>
sackcloth<sup>[8242](h8242)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> my
skin,<sup>[1539](h1539)</sup> and defiled<sup>[5953](h5953.md)</sup> my
horn<sup>[7161](h7161)</sup> in the dust.<sup>[6083](h6083.md)</sup>

16  My face<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> is foul<sup>[2560](h2560.md)</sup>
with<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> weeping,<sup>[1065](h1065.md)</sup> and
on<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> my eyelids<sup>[6079](h6079.md)</sup> is the
shadow of death;<sup>[6757](h6757.md)</sup>

17  Not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> for<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> any
injustice<sup>[2555](h2555)</sup> in mine hands:<sup>[3709](h3709.md)</sup>
also my prayer<sup>[8605](h8605)</sup> is pure.<sup>[2134](h2134.md)</sup>

18  O earth,<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> cover<sup>[3680](h3680.md)</sup>
not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> thou my blood,<sup>[1818](h1818.md)</sup> and
let my cry<sup>[2201](h2201)</sup> have<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup>
no<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> place.<sup>[4725](h4725.md)</sup>

19  Also<sup>[1571](h1571)</sup> now,<sup>[6258](h6258.md)</sup>
behold,<sup>[2009](h2009)</sup> my witness<sup>[5707](h5707.md)</sup> is in
heaven,<sup>[8064](h8064)</sup> and my record<sup>[7717](h7717.md)</sup> is
on high.<sup>[4791](h4791.md)</sup>

20  My friends<sup>[7453](h7453)</sup> scorn<sup>[3887](h3887.md)</sup> me:
but mine eye<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> poureth out<sup>[1811](h1811.md)</sup>
tears unto<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> God.<sup>[433](h0433.md)</sup>

21  O that one might plead<sup>[3198](h3198.md)</sup> for a
man<sup>[1397](h1397)</sup> with<sup>[5973](h5973.md)</sup>
God,<sup>[433](h0433)</sup> as a man<sup>[1121](h1121.md)</sup>
<sup>[120](h0120.md)</sup> pleadeth for his
neighbor!<sup>[7453](h7453.md)</sup>

22  When<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> a few<sup>[4557](h4557.md)</sup>
years<sup>[8141](h8141)</sup> are come,<sup>[857](h0857.md)</sup> then I
shall go<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> the way<sup>[734](h0734.md)</sup> whence I
shall not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> return.<sup>[7725](h7725.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/job016.md)

1 ὑπολαβὼν δὲ Ιωβ λέγει

2 ἀκήκοα τοιαῦτα πολλά παρακλήτορες κακῶν πάντες

3 τί γάρ μὴ τάξις ἐστὶν ῥήμασιν πνεύματος ἢ τί παρενοχλήσει σοι ὅτι
ἀποκρίνῃ

4 κἀγὼ καθ᾽ ὑμᾶς λαλήσω εἰ ὑπέκειτό γε ἡ ψυχὴ ὑμῶν ἀντὶ τῆς ἐμῆς εἶτ᾽
ἐναλοῦμαι ὑμῖν ῥήμασιν κινήσω δὲ καθ᾽ ὑμῶν κεφαλήν

5 εἴη δὲ ἰσχὺς ἐν τῷ στόματί μου κίνησιν δὲ χειλέων οὐ φείσομαι

6 ἐὰν γὰρ λαλήσω οὐκ ἀλγήσω τὸ τραῦμα ἐὰν δὲ καὶ σιωπήσω τί ἔλαττον
τρωθήσομαι

7 νῦν δὲ κατάκοπόν με πεποίηκεν μωρόν σεσηπότα

8 καὶ ἐπελάβου μου εἰς μαρτύριον ἐγενήθη καὶ ἀνέστη ἐν ἐμοὶ τὸ ψεῦδός
μου κατὰ πρόσωπόν μου ἀνταπεκρίθη

9 ὀργῇ χρησάμενος κατέβαλέν με ἔβρυξεν ἐπ᾽ ἐμὲ τοὺς ὀδόντας βέλη
πειρατῶν αὐτοῦ ἐπ᾽ ἐμοὶ ἔπεσεν

10 ἀκίσιν ὀφθαλμῶν ἐνήλατο ὀξεῖ ἔπαισέν με εἰς σιαγόνα ὁμοθυμαδὸν δὲ
κατέδραμον ἐπ᾽ ἐμοί

11 παρέδωκεν γάρ με ὁ κύριος εἰς χεῖρας ἀδίκου ἐπὶ δὲ ἀσεβέσιν ἔρριψέν
με

12 εἰρηνεύοντα διεσκέδασέν με λαβών με τῆς κόμης διέτιλεν κατέστησέν με
ὥσπερ σκοπόν

13 ἐκύκλωσάν με λόγχαις βάλλοντες εἰς νεφρούς μου οὐ φειδόμενοι ἐξέχεαν
εἰς τὴν γῆν τὴν χολήν μου

14 κατέβαλόν με πτῶμα ἐπὶ πτώματι ἔδραμον πρός με δυνάμενοι

15 σάκκον ἔρραψα ἐπὶ βύρσης μου τὸ δὲ σθένος μου ἐν γῇ ἐσβέσθη

16 ἡ γαστήρ μου συγκέκαυται ἀπὸ κλαυθμοῦ ἐπὶ δὲ βλεφάροις μου σκιά

17 ἄδικον δὲ οὐδὲν ἦν ἐν χερσίν μου εὐχὴ δέ μου καθαρά

18 γῆ μὴ ἐπικαλύψῃς ἐφ᾽ αἵματι τῆς σαρκός μου μηδὲ εἴη τόπος τῇ κραυγῇ
μου

19 καὶ νῦν ἰδοὺ ἐν οὐρανοῖς ὁ μάρτυς μου ὁ δὲ συνίστωρ μου ἐν ὑψίστοις

20 ἀφίκοιτό μου ἡ δέησις πρὸς κύριον ἔναντι δὲ αὐτοῦ στάζοι μου ὁ
ὀφθαλμός

21 εἴη δὲ ἔλεγχος ἀνδρὶ ἔναντι κυρίου καὶ υἱὸς ἀνθρώπου τῷ πλησίον αὐτοῦ

22 ἔτη δὲ ἀριθμητὰ ἥκασιν ὁδῷ δέ ᾗ οὐκ ἐπαναστραφήσομαι πορεύσομαι

1 Respondens autem Job, dixit:

2 \[Audivi frequenter talia:  
consolatores onerosi omnes vos estis.  

3 Numquid habebunt finem verba ventosa?  
aut aliquid tibi molestum est, si loquaris?  

4 Poteram et ego similia vestri loqui,  
atque utinam esset anima vestra pro anima mea:  

5 consolarer et ego vos sermonibus,  
et moverem caput meum super vos;  

6 roborarem vos ore meo,  
et moverem labia mea, quasi parcens vobis.  

7 Sed quid agam? Si locutus fuero, non quiescet dolor meus,  
et si tacuero, non recedet a me.  

8 Nunc autem oppressit me dolor meus,  
et in nihilum redacti sunt omnes artus mei.  

9 Rugæ meæ testimonium dicunt contra me,  
et suscitatur falsiloquus adversus faciem meam, contradicens mihi.  

10 Collegit furorem suum in me,  
et comminans mihi, infremuit contra me dentibus suis:  
hostis meus terribilibus oculis me intuitus est.  

11 Aperuerunt super me ora sua,  
et exprobrantes percusserunt maxillam meam:  
satiati sunt pœnis meis.  

12 Conclusit me Deus apud iniquum,  
et manibus impiorum me tradidit.  

13 Ego ille quondam opulentus, repente contritus sum:  
tenuit cervicem meam, confregit me,  
et posuit me sibi quasi in signum.  

14 Circumdedit me lanceis suis;  
convulneravit lumbos meos:  
non pepercit, et effudit in terra viscera mea.  

15 Concidit me vulnere super vulnus:  
irruit in me quasi gigas.  

16 Saccum consui super cutem meam,  
et operui cinere carnem meam.  

17 Facies mea intumuit a fletu,  
et palpebræ meæ caligaverunt.  

18 Hæc passus sum absque iniquitate manus meæ,  
cum haberem mundas ad Deum preces.  

19 Terra, ne operias sanguinem meum,  
neque inveniat in te locum latendi clamor meus:  

20 ecce enim in cælo testis meus,  
et conscius meus in excelsis.  

21 Verbosi amici mei:  
ad Deum stillat oculus meus:  

22 atque utinam sic judicaretur vir cum Deo,  
quomodo judicatur filius hominis cum collega suo.  

23 Ecce enim breves anni transeunt,  
et semitam per quam non revertar ambulo.\]
