### Proverbs 24

[KJV](../kjv/pro024.md)

1  Be not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> thou envious<sup>[7065](h7065.md)</sup>
against evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> men,<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup>
neither<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> desire<sup>[183](h0183.md)</sup> to
be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> with<sup>[854](h0854.md)</sup> them.

2  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> their heart<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup>
studieth<sup>[1897](h1897)</sup> destruction,<sup>[7701](h7701.md)</sup>
and their lips<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> talk<sup>[1696](h1696.md)</sup> of
mischief.<sup>[5999](h5999.md)</sup>

3  Through wisdom<sup>[2451](h2451.md)</sup> is a
house<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> built;<sup>[1129](h1129.md)</sup> and by
understanding<sup>[8394](h8394.md)</sup> it is
established:<sup>[3559](h3559.md)</sup>

4  And by knowledge<sup>[1847](h1847.md)</sup> shall the
chambers<sup>[2315](h2315)</sup> be filled<sup>[4390](h4390.md)</sup> with
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> precious<sup>[3368](h3368.md)</sup> and
pleasant<sup>[5273](h5273)</sup> riches.<sup>[1952](h1952.md)</sup>

5  A wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> man<sup>[1397](h1397.md)</sup> is
strong;<sup>[5797](h5797)</sup> yea, a man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> of
knowledge<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup> increaseth<sup>[553](h0553.md)</sup>
strength.<sup>[3581](h3581.md)</sup>

6  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> by wise counsel<sup>[8458](h8458.md)</sup>
thou shalt make<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> thy war:<sup>[4421](h4421.md)</sup>
and in multitude<sup>[7230](h7230.md)</sup> of
counselors<sup>[3289](h3289.md)</sup> there is
safety.<sup>[8668](h8668.md)</sup>

7  Wisdom<sup>[2454](h2454)</sup> is too high<sup>[7311](h7311.md)</sup>
for a fool:<sup>[191](h0191)</sup> he openeth<sup>[6605](h6605.md)</sup>
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> his mouth<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> in the
gate.<sup>[8179](h8179.md)</sup>

8  He that deviseth<sup>[2803](h2803.md)</sup> to do
evil<sup>[7489](h7489)</sup> shall be called<sup>[7121](h7121.md)</sup> a
mischievous<sup>[4209](h4209)</sup> person.<sup>[1167](h1167.md)</sup>

9  The thought<sup>[2154](h2154.md)</sup> of
foolishness<sup>[200](h0200)</sup> is sin:<sup>[2403](h2403.md)</sup> and
the scorner<sup>[3887](h3887.md)</sup> is an
abomination<sup>[8441](h8441)</sup> to men.<sup>[120](h0120.md)</sup>

10  If thou faint<sup>[7503](h7503.md)</sup> in the
day<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> of adversity,<sup>[6869](h6869.md)</sup> thy
strength<sup>[3581](h3581)</sup> is small.<sup>[6862](h6862.md)</sup>

11  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> thou forbear<sup>[2820](h2820.md)</sup> to
deliver<sup>[5337](h5337.md)</sup> them that are
drawn<sup>[3947](h3947)</sup> unto death,<sup>[4194](h4194.md)</sup> and
those that are ready<sup>[4131](h4131.md)</sup> to be
slain;<sup>[2027](h2027.md)</sup>

12  If<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thou sayest,<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup>
Behold,<sup>[2005](h2005)</sup> we knew<sup>[3045](h3045.md)</sup>
it<sup>[2088](h2088)</sup> not;<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> doth
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> he that pondereth<sup>[8505](h8505.md)</sup>
the heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> consider<sup>[995](h0995.md)</sup> it?
and he that keepeth<sup>[5341](h5341.md)</sup> thy
soul,<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> doth not he<sup>[1931](h1931.md)</sup>
know<sup>[3045](h3045.md)</sup> it? and shall not he
render<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> to every man<sup>[120](h0120.md)</sup>
according to his works?<sup>[6467](h6467.md)</sup>

13  My son,<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> eat<sup>[398](h0398.md)</sup> thou
honey,<sup>[1706](h1706)</sup> because<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup> it is
good;<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> and the honeycomb,<sup>[5317](h5317.md)</sup>
which is sweet<sup>[4966](h4966)</sup> to<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> thy
taste:<sup>[2441](h2441.md)</sup>

14  So<sup>[3651](h3651.md)</sup> shall the
knowledge<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> of wisdom<sup>[2451](h2451.md)</sup> be
unto thy soul:<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> when<sup>[518](h0518.md)</sup> thou
hast found<sup>[4672](h4672.md)</sup> it, then there shall
be<sup>[3426](h3426)</sup> a reward,<sup>[319](h0319.md)</sup> and thy
expectation<sup>[8615](h8615)</sup> shall not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> be
cut off.<sup>[3772](h3772.md)</sup>

15  Lay not wait,<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> <sup>[693](h0693.md)</sup> O
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup> man, against the
dwelling<sup>[5116](h5116.md)</sup> of the
righteous;<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> spoil<sup>[7703](h7703.md)</sup>
not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> his resting place:<sup>[7258](h7258.md)</sup>

16  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> a just<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> man
falleth<sup>[5307](h5307)</sup> seven<sup>[7651](h7651.md)</sup> times, and
riseth up again:<sup>[6965](h6965.md)</sup> but the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> shall fall<sup>[3782](h3782.md)</sup> into
mischief.<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup>

17  Rejoice<sup>[8055](h8055)</sup> not<sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup> when
thine enemy<sup>[341](h0341)</sup> falleth,<sup>[5307](h5307.md)</sup> and
let not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> thine heart<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup> be
glad<sup>[1523](h1523)</sup> when he stumbleth:<sup>[3782](h3782.md)</sup>

18  Lest<sup>[6435](h6435)</sup> the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup>
see<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> it, and it displease<sup>[7489](h7489.md)</sup>
<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> him, and he turn away<sup>[7725](h7725.md)</sup>
his wrath<sup>[639](h0639)</sup> from<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> him.

19  Fret not thyself<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> <sup>[2734](h2734.md)</sup>
because of evil<sup>[7489](h7489.md)</sup> men,
neither<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> be thou envious<sup>[7065](h7065.md)</sup>
at the wicked;<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup>

20  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> there shall be<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup>
no<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> reward<sup>[319](h0319.md)</sup> to the
evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> man; the candle<sup>[5216](h5216.md)</sup> of
the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup> shall be put
out.<sup>[1846](h1846.md)</sup>

21  My son,<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> fear<sup>[3372](h3372.md)</sup> thou
<sup>[853](h0853)</sup> the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup> and the
king:<sup>[4428](h4428)</sup> and meddle<sup>[6148](h6148.md)</sup>
not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> with<sup>[5973](h5973.md)</sup> them that are
given to change:<sup>[8138](h8138.md)</sup>

22  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> their calamity<sup>[343](h0343.md)</sup>
shall rise<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> suddenly;<sup>[6597](h6597.md)</sup> and
who<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup> knoweth<sup>[3045](h3045.md)</sup> the
ruin<sup>[6365](h6365)</sup> of them both?<sup>[8147](h8147.md)</sup>

23  These<sup>[428](h0428)</sup> things also<sup>[1571](h1571.md)</sup>
belong to the wise.<sup>[2450](h2450.md)</sup> It is
not<sup>[1077](h1077)</sup> good<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup> to have
respect<sup>[5234](h5234)</sup> of persons<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> in
judgment.<sup>[4941](h4941.md)</sup>

24  He that saith<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup> unto the
wicked,<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> Thou<sup>[859](h0859.md)</sup> art
righteous;<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> him shall the
people<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup> curse,<sup>[5344](h5344.md)</sup>
nations<sup>[3816](h3816)</sup> shall abhor<sup>[2194](h2194.md)</sup> him:

25  But to them that rebuke<sup>[3198](h3198.md)</sup> him shall be
delight,<sup>[5276](h5276)</sup> and a good<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup>
blessing<sup>[1293](h1293)</sup> shall come<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> them.

26  Every man shall kiss<sup>[5401](h5401.md)</sup> his
lips<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> that giveth<sup>[7725](h7725.md)</sup> a
right<sup>[5228](h5228)</sup> answer.<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup>

27  Prepare<sup>[3559](h3559)</sup> thy work<sup>[4399](h4399.md)</sup>
without,<sup>[2351](h2351)</sup> and make it fit<sup>[6257](h6257.md)</sup>
for thyself in the field;<sup>[7704](h7704.md)</sup> and
afterwards<sup>[310](h0310)</sup> build<sup>[1129](h1129.md)</sup> thine
house.<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup>

28  Be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> not<sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup> a
witness<sup>[5707](h5707.md)</sup> against thy
neighbor<sup>[7453](h7453)</sup> without cause;<sup>[2600](h2600.md)</sup>
and deceive<sup>[6601](h6601.md)</sup> not with thy
lips.<sup>[8193](h8193.md)</sup>

29  Say<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> not,<sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup> I will
do<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> so<sup>[3651](h3651.md)</sup> to him
as<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> he hath done<sup>[6213](h6213.md)</sup> to me: I
will render<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> to the man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup>
according to his work.<sup>[6467](h6467.md)</sup>

30  I went<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup> by<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> the
field<sup>[7704](h7704)</sup> of the slothful,<sup>[6102](h6102.md)</sup>
<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> and by<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> the
vineyard<sup>[3754](h3754)</sup> of the man<sup>[120](h0120.md)</sup>
void<sup>[2638](h2638)</sup> of understanding;<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup>

31  And, lo,<sup>[2009](h2009)</sup> it was all<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup>
grown over<sup>[5927](h5927)</sup> with thorns,<sup>[7063](h7063.md)</sup>
and nettles<sup>[2738](h2738)</sup> had covered<sup>[3680](h3680.md)</sup>
the face<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> thereof, and the
stone<sup>[68](h0068)</sup> wall<sup>[1444](h1444.md)</sup> thereof was
broken down.<sup>[2040](h2040.md)</sup>

32  Then I<sup>[595](h0595)</sup> saw,<sup>[2372](h2372.md)</sup> and
considered it well:<sup>[7896](h7896)</sup> <sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup> I
looked<sup>[7200](h7200.md)</sup> upon it, and
received<sup>[3947](h3947)</sup> instruction.<sup>[4148](h4148.md)</sup>

33  Yet a little<sup>[4592](h4592)</sup> sleep,<sup>[8142](h8142.md)</sup>
a little<sup>[4592](h4592)</sup> slumber,<sup>[8572](h8572.md)</sup> a
little<sup>[4592](h4592)</sup> folding<sup>[2264](h2264.md)</sup> of the
hands<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> to sleep:<sup>[7901](h7901.md)</sup>

34  So shall thy poverty<sup>[7389](h7389.md)</sup>
come<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup> as one that
traveleth;<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> and thy want<sup>[4270](h4270.md)</sup>
as an armed<sup>[4043](h4043)</sup> man.<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/pro024.md)

1 υἱέ μὴ ζηλώσῃς κακοὺς ἄνδρας μηδὲ ἐπιθυμήσῃς εἶναι μετ᾽ αὐτῶν

2 ψεύδη γὰρ μελετᾷ ἡ καρδία αὐτῶν καὶ πόνους τὰ χείλη αὐτῶν λαλεῖ

3 μετὰ σοφίας οἰκοδομεῖται οἶκος καὶ μετὰ συνέσεως ἀνορθοῦται

4 μετὰ αἰσθήσεως ἐμπίμπλαται ταμίεια ἐκ παντὸς πλούτου τιμίου καὶ καλοῦ

5 κρείσσων σοφὸς ἰσχυροῦ καὶ ἀνὴρ φρόνησιν ἔχων γεωργίου μεγάλου

6 μετὰ κυβερνήσεως γίνεται πόλεμος βοήθεια δὲ μετὰ καρδίας βουλευτικῆς

7 σοφία καὶ ἔννοια ἀγαθὴ ἐν πύλαις σοφῶν σοφοὶ οὐκ ἐκκλίνουσιν ἐκ
στόματος κυρίου

8 ἀλλὰ λογίζονται ἐν συνεδρίοις ἀπαιδεύτοις συναντᾷ θάνατος

9 ἀποθνῄσκει δὲ ἄφρων ἐν ἁμαρτίαις ἀκαθαρσία δὲ ἀνδρὶ λοιμῷ
ἐμμολυνθήσεται

10 ἐν ἡμέρᾳ κακῇ καὶ ἐν ἡμέρᾳ θλίψεως ἕως ἂν ἐκλίπῃ

11 ῥῦσαι ἀγομένους εἰς θάνατον καὶ ἐκπρίου κτεινομένους μὴ φείσῃ

12 ἐὰν δὲ εἴπῃς οὐκ οἶδα τοῦτον γίνωσκε ὅτι κύριος καρδίας πάντων
γινώσκει καὶ ὁ πλάσας πνοὴν πᾶσιν αὐτὸς οἶδεν πάντα ὃς ἀποδίδωσιν ἑκάστῳ
κατὰ τὰ ἔργα αὐτοῦ

13 φάγε μέλι υἱέ ἀγαθὸν γὰρ κηρίον ἵνα γλυκανθῇ σου ὁ φάρυγξ

14 οὕτως αἰσθήσῃ σοφίαν τῇ σῇ ψυχῇ ἐὰν γὰρ εὕρῃς ἔσται καλὴ ἡ τελευτή
σου καὶ ἐλπίς σε οὐκ ἐγκαταλείψει

15 μὴ προσαγάγῃς ἀσεβῆ νομῇ δικαίων μηδὲ ἀπατηθῇς χορτασίᾳ κοιλίας

16 ἑπτάκι γὰρ πεσεῖται ὁ δίκαιος καὶ ἀναστήσεται οἱ δὲ ἀσεβεῖς
ἀσθενήσουσιν ἐν κακοῖς

17 ἐὰν πέσῃ ὁ ἐχθρός σου μὴ ἐπιχαρῇς αὐτῷ ἐν δὲ τῷ ὑποσκελίσματι αὐτοῦ
μὴ ἐπαίρου

18 ὅτι ὄψεται κύριος καὶ οὐκ ἀρέσει αὐτῷ καὶ ἀποστρέψει τὸν θυμὸν αὐτοῦ
ἀπ᾽ αὐτοῦ

19 μὴ χαῖρε ἐπὶ κακοποιοῖς μηδὲ ζήλου ἁμαρτωλούς

20 οὐ γὰρ μὴ γένηται ἔκγονα πονηρῶν λαμπτὴρ δὲ ἀσεβῶν σβεσθήσεται

21 φοβοῦ τὸν θεόν υἱέ καὶ βασιλέα καὶ μηθετέρῳ αὐτῶν ἀπειθήσῃς

22 ἐξαίφνης γὰρ τείσονται τοὺς ἀσεβεῖς τὰς δὲ τιμωρίας ἀμφοτέρων τίς
γνώσεται  
λόγον φυλασσόμενος υἱὸς ἀπωλείας ἐκτὸς ἔσται δεχόμενος δὲ ἐδέξατο
αὐτόν  
μηδὲν ψεῦδος ἀπὸ γλώσσης βασιλεῖ λεγέσθω καὶ οὐδὲν ψεῦδος ἀπὸ γλώσσης
αὐτοῦ οὐ μὴ ἐξέλθῃ  
μάχαιρα γλῶσσα βασιλέως καὶ οὐ σαρκίνη ὃς δ᾽ ἂν παραδοθῇ συντριβήσεται  
ἐὰν γὰρ ὀξυνθῇ ὁ θυμὸς αὐτοῦ σὺν νεύροις ἀνθρώπους ἀναλίσκει  
καὶ ὀστᾶ ἀνθρώπων κατατρώγει καὶ συγκαίει ὥσπερ φλὸξ ὥστε ἄβρωτα εἶναι
νεοσσοῖς ἀετῶν

23 ταῦτα δὲ λέγω ὑμῖν τοῖς σοφοῖς ἐπιγινώσκειν αἰδεῖσθαι πρόσωπον ἐν
κρίσει οὐ καλόν

24 ὁ εἰπὼν τὸν ἀσεβῆ δίκαιός ἐστιν ἐπικατάρατος λαοῖς ἔσται καὶ μισητὸς
εἰς ἔθνη

25 οἱ δὲ ἐλέγχοντες βελτίους φανοῦνται ἐπ᾽ αὐτοὺς δὲ ἥξει εὐλογία ἀγαθή

26 χείλη δὲ φιλήσουσιν ἀποκρινόμενα λόγους ἀγαθούς

27 ἑτοίμαζε εἰς τὴν ἔξοδον τὰ ἔργα σου καὶ παρασκευάζου εἰς τὸν ἀγρὸν
καὶ πορεύου κατόπισθέν μου καὶ ἀνοικοδομήσεις τὸν οἶκόν σου

28 μὴ ἴσθι ψευδὴς μάρτυς ἐπὶ σὸν πολίτην μηδὲ πλατύνου σοῖς χείλεσιν

29 μὴ εἴπῃς ὃν τρόπον ἐχρήσατό μοι χρήσομαι αὐτῷ τείσομαι δὲ αὐτὸν ἅ με
ἠδίκησεν

30 ὥσπερ γεώργιον ἀνὴρ ἄφρων καὶ ὥσπερ ἀμπελὼν ἄνθρωπος ἐνδεὴς φρενῶν

31 ἐὰν ἀφῇς αὐτόν χερσωθήσεται καὶ χορτομανήσει ὅλος καὶ γίνεται
ἐκλελειμμένος οἱ δὲ φραγμοὶ τῶν λίθων αὐτοῦ κατασκάπτονται

32 ὕστερον ἐγὼ μετενόησα ἐπέβλεψα τοῦ ἐκλέξασθαι παιδείαν

33 ὀλίγον νυστάζω ὀλίγον δὲ καθυπνῶ ὀλίγον δὲ ἐναγκαλίζομαι χερσὶν στήθη

34 ἐὰν δὲ τοῦτο ποιῇς ἥξει προπορευομένη ἡ πενία σου καὶ ἡ ἔνδειά σου
ὥσπερ ἀγαθὸς δρομεύς

הֽוּא־יָבִ֗ין וְנֹצֵ֣ר נַ֭פְשְׁךָ ה֣וּא יֵדָ֑ע וְהֵשִׁ֖יב לְאָדָ֣ם
כְּפָעֳלֽוֹ׃

‎13 ‏אֱכָל־בְּנִ֣י דְבַ֣שׁ כִּי־ט֑וֹב וְנֹ֥פֶת מָ֝ת֗וֹק עַל־חִכֶּֽךָ׃

‎14 ‏כֵּ֤ן׀ דְּעֶ֥ה חָכְמָ֗ה לְנַ֫פְשֶׁ֥ךָ אִם־מָ֭צָאתָ וְיֵ֣שׁ
אַחֲרִ֑ית וְ֝תִקְוָתְךָ֗ לֹ֣א תִכָּרֵֽת׃ פ

‎15 ‏אַל־תֶּאֱרֹ֣ב רָ֭שָׁע לִנְוֵ֣ה צַדִּ֑יק אַֽל־תְּשַׁדֵּ֥ד רִבְצֽוֹ׃

‎16 ‏כִּ֤י שֶׁ֨בַע׀ יִפּ֣וֹל צַדִּ֣יק וָקָ֑ם וּ֝רְשָׁעִ֗ים יִכָּשְׁל֥וּ
בְרָעָֽה׃

‎17 ‏בִּנְפֹ֣ל אויביך אֽ֭וֹיִבְךָ<sup>y</sup> אַל־תִּשְׂמָ֑ח
וּ֝בִכָּשְׁל֗וֹ אַל־יָגֵ֥ל לִבֶּֽךָ׃

‎18 ‏פֶּן־יִרְאֶ֣ה יְ֭הוָה וְרַ֣ע בְּעֵינָ֑יו וְהֵשִׁ֖יב מֵעָלָ֣יו
אַפּֽוֹ׃

‎19 ‏אַל־תִּתְחַ֥ר בַּמְּרֵעִ֑ים אַל־תְּ֝קַנֵּ֗א בָּרְשָׁעִֽים׃

‎20 ‏כִּ֤י׀ לֹֽא־תִהְיֶ֣ה אַחֲרִ֣ית לָרָ֑ע נֵ֖ר רְשָׁעִ֣ים יִדְעָֽךְ׃

‎21 ‏יְרָֽא־אֶת־יְהוָ֣ה בְּנִ֣י וָמֶ֑לֶךְ עִם־שׁ֝וֹנִ֗ים
אַל־תִּתְעָרָֽב׃

‎22 ‏כִּֽי־פִ֭תְאֹם יָק֣וּם אֵידָ֑ם וּפִ֥יד שְׁ֝נֵיהֶ֗ם מִ֣י יוֹדֵֽעַ׃ ס

‎23 ‏גַּם־אֵ֥לֶּה לַֽחֲכָמִ֑ים הַֽכֵּר־פָּנִ֖ים בְּמִשְׁפָּ֣ט
בַּל־טֽוֹב׃

‎24 ‏אֹ֤מֵ֨ר׀ לְרָשָׁע֮ צַדִּ֪יק אָ֥תָּה יִקְּבֻ֥הוּ עַמִּ֑ים
יִזְעָמ֥וּהוּ לְאֻמִּֽים׃

‎25 ‏וְלַמּוֹכִיחִ֥ים יִנְעָ֑ם וַֽ֝עֲלֵיהֶ֗ם תָּב֥וֹא בִרְכַּת־טֽוֹב׃

‎26 ‏שְׂפָתַ֥יִם יִשָּׁ֑ק מֵ֝שִׁ֗יב דְּבָרִ֥ים נְכֹחִֽים׃

‎27 ‏הָ֘כֵ֤ן בַּח֨וּץ׀ מְלַאכְתֶּ֗ךָ וְעַתְּדָ֣הּ בַּשָּׂדֶ֣ה לָ֑ךְ
אַ֝חַ֗ר וּבָנִ֥יתָ בֵיתֶֽךָ׃ פ

‎28 ‏אַל־תְּהִ֣י עֵד־חִנָּ֣ם בְּרֵעֶ֑ךָ וַ֝הֲפִתִּ֗יתָ בִּשְׂפָתֶֽיךָ׃

‎29 ‏אַל־תֹּאמַ֗ר כַּאֲשֶׁ֣ר עָֽשָׂה־לִ֭י כֵּ֤ן אֶֽעֱשֶׂה־לּ֑וֹ אָשִׁ֖יב
לָאִ֣ישׁ כְּפָעֳלֽוֹ׃

‎30 ‏עַל־שְׂדֵ֣ה אִישׁ־עָצֵ֣ל עָבַ֑רְתִּי וְעַל־כֶּ֝֗רֶם אָדָ֥ם
חֲסַר־לֵֽב׃

‎31 ‏וְהִנֵּ֨ה עָ֘לָ֤ה כֻלּ֨וֹ׀ קִמְּשֹׂנִ֗ים כָּסּ֣וּ פָנָ֣יו
חֲרֻלִּ֑ים וְגֶ֖דֶר אֲבָנָ֣יו נֶהֱרָֽסָה׃

‎32 ‏וָֽאֶחֱזֶ֣ה אָ֭נֹכִֽי אָשִׁ֣ית לִבִּ֑י רָ֝אִ֗יתִי לָקַ֥חְתִּי
מוּסָֽר׃

‎33 ‏מְעַ֣ט שֵׁ֭נוֹת מְעַ֣ט תְּנוּמ֑וֹת מְעַ֓ט׀ חִבֻּ֖ק יָדַ֣יִם
לִשְׁכָּֽב׃

‎34 ‏וּבָֽא־מִתְהַלֵּ֥ךְ רֵישֶׁ֑ךָ וּ֝מַחְסֹרֶ֗יךָ כְּאִ֣ישׁ מָגֵֽן׃ פ

[Vul](../vul/pro024.md)

1 \[Ne æmuleris viros malos,  
nec desideres esse cum eis:  

2 quia rapinas meditatur mens eorum,  
et fraudes labia eorum loquuntur.  

3 Sapientia ædificabitur domus,  
et prudentia roborabitur.  

4 In doctrina replebuntur cellaria,  
universa substantia pretiosa et pulcherrima.  

5 Vir sapiens fortis est,  
et vir doctus robustus et validus:  

6 quia cum dispositione initur bellum,  
et erit salus ubi multa consilia sunt.  

7 Excelsa stulto sapientia;  
in porta non aperiet os suum.  

8 Qui cogitat mala facere stultus vocabitur:  

9 cogitatio stulti peccatum est,  
et abominatio hominum detractor.  

10 Si desperaveris lassus in die angustiæ,  
imminuetur fortitudo tua.\]  

11 \[Erue eos qui ducuntur ad mortem,  
et qui trahuntur ad interitum, liberare ne cesses.  

12 Si dixeris: Vires non suppetunt;  
qui inspector est cordis ipse intelligit:  
et servatorem animæ tuæ nihil fallit,  
reddetque homini juxta opera sua.  

13 Comede, fili mi, mel, quia bonum est,  
et favum dulcissimum gutturi tuo.  

14 Sic et doctrina sapientiæ animæ tuæ:  
quam cum inveneris, habebis in novissimis spem,  
et spes tua non peribit.  

15 Ne insidieris, et quæras impietatem in domo justi,  
neque vastes requiem ejus.  

16 Septies enim cadet justus, et resurget:  
impii autem corruent in malum.  

17 Cum ceciderit inimicus tuus ne gaudeas,  
et in ruina ejus ne exsultet cor tuum:  

18 ne forte videat Dominus, et displiceat ei,  
et auferat ab eo iram suam.  

19 Ne contendas cum pessimis,  
nec æmuleris impios:  

20 quoniam non habent futurorum spem mali,  
et lucerna impiorum extinguetur.  

21 Time Dominum, fili mi, et regem,  
et cum detractoribus non commiscearis:  

22 quoniam repente consurget perditio eorum,  
et ruinam utriusque quis novit?\]  

23 Hæc quoque sapientibus. \[Cognoscere personam in judicio non est
bonum.  

24 Qui dicunt impio: Justus es: maledicent eis populi,  
et detestabuntur eos tribus.  

25 Qui arguunt eum laudabuntur,  
et super ipsos veniet benedictio.  

26 Labia deosculabitur  
qui recta verba respondet.  

27 Præpara foris opus tuum,  
et diligenter exerce agrum tuum,  
ut postea ædifices domum tuam.  

28 Ne sis testis frustra contra proximum tuum,  
nec lactes quemquam labiis tuis.  

29 Ne dicas: Quomodo fecit mihi, sic faciam ei;  
reddam unicuique secundum opus suum.\]  

30 \[Per agrum hominis pigri transivi,  
et per vineam viri stulti:  

31 et ecce totum repleverant urticæ,  
et operuerant superficiem ejus spinæ,  
et maceria lapidum destructa erat.  

32 Quod cum vidissem, posui in corde meo,  
et exemplo didici disciplinam.  

33 Parum, inquam, dormies, modicum dormitabis;  
pauxillum manus conseres ut quiescas:  

34 et veniet tibi quasi cursor egestas,  
et mendicitas quasi vir armatus.\]
