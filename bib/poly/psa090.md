### Psalms 90

[KJV](../kjv/psa090)

1  A Prayer<sup>[8605](h8605)</sup> of Moses<sup>[4872](h4872)</sup> the
man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> of God.<sup>[430](h0430)</sup>
Lord,<sup>[136](h0136)</sup> thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> hast
been<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> our dwelling place<sup>[4583](h4583)</sup>
in all generations.<sup>[1755](h1755)</sup> <sup>[1755](h1755)</sup>

2  Before<sup>[2962](h2962)</sup> the mountains<sup>[2022](h2022)</sup>
were brought forth,<sup>[3205](h3205)</sup> or ever thou hadst
formed<sup>[2342](h2342)</sup> the earth<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> and the
world,<sup>[8398](h8398)</sup> even from
everlasting<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[5769](h5769)</sup>
to<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> everlasting,<sup>[5769](h5769)</sup>
thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> art God.<sup>[410](h0410)</sup>

3  Thou turnest<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> man<sup>[582](h0582)</sup>
to<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> destruction;<sup>[1793](h1793)</sup> and
sayest,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> Return,<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> ye
children<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> of men.<sup>[120](h0120)</sup>

4  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> a thousand<sup>[505](h0505)</sup>
years<sup>[8141](h8141)</sup> in thy sight<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> are
but as yesterday<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> <sup>[865](h0865)</sup>
when<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> it is past,<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup> and as
a watch<sup>[821](h0821)</sup> in the night.<sup>[3915](h3915)</sup>

5  Thou carriest them away as with a flood;<sup>[2229](h2229)</sup> they
are<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> as a sleep:<sup>[8142](h8142)</sup> in the
morning<sup>[1242](h1242)</sup> they are like
grass<sup>[2682](h2682)</sup> which groweth up.<sup>[2498](h2498)</sup>

6  In the morning<sup>[1242](h1242)</sup> it
flourisheth,<sup>[6692](h6692)</sup> and groweth
up;<sup>[2498](h2498)</sup> in the evening<sup>[6153](h6153)</sup> it is
cut down,<sup>[4135](h4135)</sup> and withereth.<sup>[3001](h3001)</sup>

7  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> we are consumed<sup>[3615](h3615)</sup>
by thine anger,<sup>[639](h0639)</sup> and by thy
wrath<sup>[2534](h2534)</sup> are we troubled.<sup>[926](h0926)</sup>

8  Thou hast set<sup>[7896](h7896)</sup> our
iniquities<sup>[5771](h5771)</sup> before<sup>[5048](h5048)</sup> thee,
our secret<sup>[5956](h5956)</sup> sins in the
light<sup>[3974](h3974)</sup> of thy
countenance.<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup>

9  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> our
days<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> are passed away<sup>[6437](h6437)</sup> in
thy wrath:<sup>[5678](h5678)</sup> we spend<sup>[3615](h3615)</sup> our
years<sup>[8141](h8141)</sup> as<sup>[3644](h3644)</sup> a
tale<sup>[1899](h1899)</sup> that is told.

10  The days<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> of our
years<sup>[8141](h8141)</sup> are threescore years and
ten;<sup>[7657](h7657)</sup> <sup>[8141](h8141)</sup> and
if<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> by reason of strength<sup>[1369](h1369)</sup>
they be fourscore<sup>[8084](h8084)</sup> years,<sup>[8141](h8141)</sup>
yet is their strength<sup>[7296](h7296)</sup>
labor<sup>[5999](h5999)</sup> and sorrow;<sup>[205](h0205)</sup>
for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> it is soon<sup>[2440](h2440)</sup> cut
off,<sup>[1468](h1468)</sup> and we fly away.<sup>[5774](h5774)</sup>

11  Who<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup> knoweth<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> the
power<sup>[5797](h5797)</sup> of thine anger?<sup>[639](h0639)</sup>
even according to thy fear,<sup>[3374](h3374)</sup> so is thy
wrath.<sup>[5678](h5678)</sup>

12  So<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> teach<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> us to
number<sup>[4487](h4487)</sup> our days,<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> that we
may apply<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> our hearts<sup>[3824](h3824)</sup> unto
wisdom.<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup>

13  Return,<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> O LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> how
long?<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> <sup>[4970](h4970)</sup> and let it
repent<sup>[5162](h5162)</sup> thee concerning<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup>
thy servants.<sup>[5650](h5650)</sup>

14  O satisfy<sup>[7646](h7646)</sup> us early<sup>[1242](h1242)</sup>
with thy mercy;<sup>[2617](h2617)</sup> that we may
rejoice<sup>[7442](h7442)</sup> and be glad<sup>[8055](h8055)</sup>
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> our days.<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup>

15  Make us glad<sup>[8055](h8055)</sup> according to the
days<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> wherein thou hast
afflicted<sup>[6031](h6031)</sup> us, and the
years<sup>[8141](h8141)</sup> wherein we have
seen<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> evil.<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup>

16  Let thy work<sup>[6467](h6467)</sup> appear<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup>
unto<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> thy servants,<sup>[5650](h5650)</sup> and
thy glory<sup>[1926](h1926)</sup> unto<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> their
children.<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup>

17  And let the beauty<sup>[5278](h5278)</sup> of the
LORD<sup>[136](h0136)</sup> our God<sup>[430](h0430)</sup>
be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> us: and
establish<sup>[3559](h3559)</sup> thou the work<sup>[4639](h4639)</sup>
of our hands<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> us;
yea, the work<sup>[4639](h4639)</sup> of our
hands<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> establish<sup>[3559](h3559)</sup> thou it.

[Sep](../sep/psa090)

1 αἶνος ᾠδῆς τῷ Δαυιδ ὁ κατοικῶν ἐν βοηθείᾳ τοῦ ὑψίστου ἐν σκέπῃ τοῦ
θεοῦ τοῦ οὐρανοῦ αὐλισθήσεται

2 ἐρεῖ τῷ κυρίῳ ἀντιλήμπτωρ μου εἶ καὶ καταφυγή μου ὁ θεός μου ἐλπιῶ ἐπ᾽
αὐτόν

3 ὅτι αὐτὸς ῥύσεταί με ἐκ παγίδος θηρευτῶν καὶ ἀπὸ λόγου ταραχώδους

4 ἐν τοῖς μεταφρένοις αὐτοῦ ἐπισκιάσει σοι καὶ ὑπὸ τὰς πτέρυγας αὐτοῦ
ἐλπιεῖς ὅπλῳ κυκλώσει σε ἡ ἀλήθεια αὐτοῦ

5 οὐ φοβηθήσῃ ἀπὸ φόβου νυκτερινοῦ ἀπὸ βέλους πετομένου ἡμέρας

6 ἀπὸ πράγματος διαπορευομένου ἐν σκότει ἀπὸ συμπτώματος καὶ δαιμονίου
μεσημβρινοῦ

7 πεσεῖται ἐκ τοῦ κλίτους σου χιλιὰς καὶ μυριὰς ἐκ δεξιῶν σου πρὸς σὲ δὲ
οὐκ ἐγγιεῖ

8 πλὴν τοῖς ὀφθαλμοῖς σου κατανοήσεις καὶ ἀνταπόδοσιν ἁμαρτωλῶν ὄψῃ

9 ὅτι σύ κύριε ἡ ἐλπίς μου τὸν ὕψιστον ἔθου καταφυγήν σου

10 οὐ προσελεύσεται πρὸς σὲ κακά καὶ μάστιξ οὐκ ἐγγιεῖ τῷ σκηνώματί σου

11 ὅτι τοῖς ἀγγέλοις αὐτοῦ ἐντελεῖται περὶ σοῦ τοῦ διαφυλάξαι σε ἐν
πάσαις ταῖς ὁδοῖς σου

12 ἐπὶ χειρῶν ἀροῦσίν σε μήποτε προσκόψῃς πρὸς λίθον τὸν πόδα σου

13 ἐπ᾽ ἀσπίδα καὶ βασιλίσκον ἐπιβήσῃ καὶ καταπατήσεις λέοντα καὶ
δράκοντα

14 ὅτι ἐπ᾽ ἐμὲ ἤλπισεν καὶ ῥύσομαι αὐτόν σκεπάσω αὐτόν ὅτι ἔγνω τὸ ὄνομά
μου

15 ἐπικαλέσεταί με καὶ εἰσακούσομαι αὐτοῦ μετ᾽ αὐτοῦ εἰμι ἐν θλίψει καὶ
ἐξελοῦμαι καὶ δοξάσω αὐτόν

16 μακρότητα ἡμερῶν ἐμπλήσω αὐτὸν καὶ δείξω αὐτῷ τὸ σωτήριόν μου

[Vul](../vul/psa090)

1 Laus cantici David. \[Qui habitat in adjutorio Altissimi,  
in protectione Dei cæli commorabitur.  

2 Dicet Domino: Susceptor meus es tu, et refugium meum;  
Deus meus, sperabo in eum.  

3 Quoniam ipse liberavit me de laqueo venantium,  
et a verbo aspero.  

4 Scapulis suis obumbrabit tibi,  
et sub pennis ejus sperabis.  

5 Scuto circumdabit te veritas ejus:  
non timebis a timore nocturno;  

6 a sagitta volante in die,  
a negotio perambulante in tenebris,  
ab incursu, et dæmonio meridiano.  

7 Cadent a latere tuo mille, et decem millia a dextris tuis;  
ad te autem non appropinquabit.  

8 Verumtamen oculis tuis considerabis,  
et retributionem peccatorum videbis.  

9 Quoniam tu es, Domine, spes mea;  
Altissimum posuisti refugium tuum.  

10 Non accedet ad te malum,  
et flagellum non appropinquabit tabernaculo tuo.  

11 Quoniam angelis suis mandavit de te,  
ut custodiant te in omnibus viis tuis.  

12 In manibus portabunt te,  
ne forte offendas ad lapidem pedem tuum.  

13 Super aspidem et basiliscum ambulabis,  
et conculcabis leonem et draconem.  

14 Quoniam in me speravit, liberabo eum;  
protegam eum, quoniam cognovit nomen meum.  

15 Clamabit ad me, et ego exaudiam eum;  
cum ipso sum in tribulatione:  
eripiam eum, et glorificabo eum.  

16 Longitudine dierum replebo eum,  
et ostendam illi salutare meum.\]
