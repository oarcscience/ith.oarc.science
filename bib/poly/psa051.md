### Psalms 51

[KJV](../kjv/psa051.md)

1  To the chief Musician,<sup>[5329](h5329.md)</sup> A
Psalm<sup>[4210](h4210)</sup> of David,<sup>[1732](h1732.md)</sup> when
Nathan<sup>[5416](h5416)</sup> the prophet<sup>[5030](h5030.md)</sup>
came<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> unto<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup> him,
after<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> he had gone in<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup>
to<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> Bathsheba.<sup>[1339](h1339.md)</sup> Have
mercy<sup>[2603](h2603)</sup> upon me, O God,<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup>
according to thy lovingkindness:<sup>[2617](h2617.md)</sup> according unto
the multitude<sup>[7230](h7230.md)</sup> of thy tender
mercies<sup>[7356](h7356)</sup> blot out<sup>[4229](h4229.md)</sup> my
transgressions.<sup>[6588](h6588.md)</sup>

2  Wash<sup>[3526](h3526)</sup> me throughly<sup>[7235](h7235.md)</sup>
from mine iniquity,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[5771](h5771.md)</sup> and
cleanse<sup>[2891](h2891)</sup> me from my sin.<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[2403](h2403.md)</sup>

3  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> I<sup>[589](h0589.md)</sup>
acknowledge<sup>[3045](h3045.md)</sup> my
transgressions:<sup>[6588](h6588.md)</sup> and my
sin<sup>[2403](h2403)</sup> is ever<sup>[8548](h8548.md)</sup>
before<sup>[5048](h5048.md)</sup> me.

4  Against thee, thee only,<sup>[905](h0905.md)</sup> have I
sinned,<sup>[2398](h2398)</sup> and done<sup>[6213](h6213.md)</sup> this
evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> in thy sight:<sup>[5869](h5869.md)</sup>
that<sup>[4616](h4616.md)</sup> thou mightest be
justified<sup>[6663](h6663.md)</sup> when thou
speakest,<sup>[1696](h1696)</sup> and be clear<sup>[2135](h2135.md)</sup>
when thou judgest.<sup>[8199](h8199.md)</sup>

5  Behold,<sup>[2005](h2005)</sup> I was shapen<sup>[2342](h2342.md)</sup>
in iniquity;<sup>[5771](h5771)</sup> and in sin<sup>[2399](h2399.md)</sup>
did my mother<sup>[517](h0517)</sup> conceive<sup>[3179](h3179.md)</sup>
me.

6  Behold,<sup>[2005](h2005)</sup> thou desirest<sup>[2654](h2654.md)</sup>
truth<sup>[571](h0571.md)</sup> in the inward
parts:<sup>[2910](h2910)</sup> and in the hidden<sup>[5640](h5640.md)</sup>
part thou shalt make me to know<sup>[3045](h3045.md)</sup>
wisdom.<sup>[2451](h2451.md)</sup>

7  Purge<sup>[2398](h2398)</sup> me with hyssop,<sup>[231](h0231.md)</sup>
and I shall be clean:<sup>[2891](h2891.md)</sup>
wash<sup>[3526](h3526.md)</sup> me, and I shall be
whiter<sup>[3835](h3835)</sup> than snow.<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[7950](h7950.md)</sup>

8  Make me to hear<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> joy<sup>[8342](h8342.md)</sup>
and gladness;<sup>[8057](h8057.md)</sup> that the
bones<sup>[6106](h6106.md)</sup> which thou hast
broken<sup>[1794](h1794)</sup> may rejoice.<sup>[1523](h1523.md)</sup>

9  Hide<sup>[5641](h5641)</sup> thy face<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> from my
sins,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[2399](h2399.md)</sup> and blot
out<sup>[4229](h4229)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup> mine
iniquities.<sup>[5771](h5771.md)</sup>

10  Create<sup>[1254](h1254)</sup> in me a clean<sup>[2889](h2889.md)</sup>
heart,<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> O God;<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup> and
renew<sup>[2318](h2318)</sup> a right<sup>[3559](h3559.md)</sup>
spirit<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> within<sup>[7130](h7130.md)</sup> me.

11  Cast<sup>[7993](h7993)</sup> me not<sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup> away from
thy presence;<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> and
take<sup>[3947](h3947)</sup> not<sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup> thy
holy<sup>[6944](h6944)</sup> spirit<sup>[7307](h7307.md)</sup>
from<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup> me.

12  Restore<sup>[7725](h7725.md)</sup> unto me the
joy<sup>[8342](h8342)</sup> of thy salvation;<sup>[3468](h3468.md)</sup>
and uphold<sup>[5564](h5564.md)</sup> me with thy
free<sup>[5082](h5082)</sup> spirit.<sup>[7307](h7307.md)</sup>

13  Then will I teach<sup>[3925](h3925.md)</sup>
transgressors<sup>[6586](h6586)</sup> thy ways;<sup>[1870](h1870.md)</sup>
and sinners<sup>[2400](h2400.md)</sup> shall be
converted<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> unto<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup> thee.

14  Deliver<sup>[5337](h5337.md)</sup> me from
bloodguiltiness,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[1818](h1818.md)</sup> O
God,<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> thou God<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup> of my
salvation:<sup>[8668](h8668)</sup> and my tongue<sup>[3956](h3956.md)</sup>
shall sing aloud<sup>[7442](h7442.md)</sup> of thy
righteousness.<sup>[6666](h6666.md)</sup>

15  O Lord,<sup>[136](h0136)</sup> open<sup>[6605](h6605.md)</sup> thou my
lips;<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> and my mouth<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> shall
show forth<sup>[5046](h5046)</sup> thy praise.<sup>[8416](h8416.md)</sup>

16  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thou desirest<sup>[2654](h2654.md)</sup>
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> sacrifice;<sup>[2077](h2077.md)</sup> else
would I give<sup>[5414](h5414.md)</sup> it: thou
delightest<sup>[7521](h7521)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> in burnt
offering.<sup>[5930](h5930.md)</sup>

17  The sacrifices<sup>[2077](h2077)</sup> of God<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup>
are a broken<sup>[7665](h7665)</sup> spirit:<sup>[7307](h7307.md)</sup> a
broken<sup>[7665](h7665)</sup> and a contrite<sup>[1794](h1794.md)</sup>
heart,<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> O God,<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup> thou wilt
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> despise.<sup>[959](h0959.md)</sup>

18  Do good<sup>[3190](h3190.md)</sup> in thy good
pleasure<sup>[7522](h7522)</sup> unto <sup>[853](h0853.md)</sup>
Zion:<sup>[6726](h6726)</sup> build<sup>[1129](h1129.md)</sup> thou the
walls<sup>[2346](h2346)</sup> of Jerusalem.<sup>[3389](h3389.md)</sup>

19  Then<sup>[227](h0227.md)</sup> shalt thou be
pleased<sup>[2654](h2654.md)</sup> with the
sacrifices<sup>[2077](h2077.md)</sup> of
righteousness,<sup>[6664](h6664.md)</sup> with burnt
offering<sup>[5930](h5930.md)</sup> and whole burnt
offering:<sup>[3632](h3632)</sup> then<sup>[227](h0227.md)</sup> shall they
offer<sup>[5927](h5927)</sup> bullocks<sup>[6499](h6499.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> thine altar.<sup>[4196](h4196.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/psa051.md)

1 εἰς τὸ τέλος συνέσεως τῷ Δαυιδ

2 ἐν τῷ ἐλθεῖν Δωηκ τὸν Ιδουμαῖον καὶ ἀναγγεῖλαι τῷ Σαουλ καὶ εἰπεῖν
αὐτῷ ἦλθεν Δαυιδ εἰς τὸν οἶκον Αβιμελεχ

3 τί ἐγκαυχᾷ ἐν κακίᾳ ὁ δυνατός ἀνομίαν ὅλην τὴν ἡμέραν

4 ἀδικίαν ἐλογίσατο ἡ γλῶσσά σου ὡσεὶ ξυρὸν ἠκονημένον ἐποίησας δόλον

5 ἠγάπησας κακίαν ὑπὲρ ἀγαθωσύνην ἀδικίαν ὑπὲρ τὸ λαλῆσαι δικαιοσύνην
διάψαλμα

6 ἠγάπησας πάντα τὰ ῥήματα καταποντισμοῦ γλῶσσαν δολίαν

7 διὰ τοῦτο ὁ θεὸς καθελεῖ σε εἰς τέλος ἐκτίλαι σε καὶ μεταναστεύσαι σε
ἀπὸ σκηνώματος καὶ τὸ ῥίζωμά σου ἐκ γῆς ζώντων διάψαλμα

8 καὶ ὄψονται δίκαιοι καὶ φοβηθήσονται καὶ ἐπ᾽ αὐτὸν γελάσονται καὶ
ἐροῦσιν

9 ἰδοὺ ἄνθρωπος ὃς οὐκ ἔθετο τὸν θεὸν βοηθὸν αὐτοῦ ἀλλ᾽ ἐπήλπισεν ἐπὶ τὸ
πλῆθος τοῦ πλούτου αὐτοῦ καὶ ἐδυναμώθη ἐπὶ τῇ ματαιότητι αὐτοῦ

10 ἐγὼ δὲ ὡσεὶ ἐλαία κατάκαρπος ἐν τῷ οἴκῳ τοῦ θεοῦ ἤλπισα ἐπὶ τὸ ἔλεος
τοῦ θεοῦ εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα καὶ εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα τοῦ αἰῶνος

11 ἐξομολογήσομαί σοι εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα ὅτι ἐποίησας καὶ ὑπομενῶ τὸ ὄνομά
σου ὅτι χρηστὸν ἐναντίον τῶν ὁσίων σου

[Vul](../vul/psa051.md)

1 In finem. Intellectus David,

2 cum venit Doëg Idumæus, et nuntiavit Sauli: Venit David in domum
Achimelech.

3 \[Quid gloriaris in malitia,  
qui potens es in iniquitate?  

4 Tota die injustitiam cogitavit lingua tua;  
sicut novacula acuta fecisti dolum.  

5 Dilexisti malitiam super benignitatem;  
iniquitatem magis quam loqui æquitatem.  

6 Dilexisti omnia verba præcipitationis;  
lingua dolosa.  

7 Propterea Deus destruet te in finem;  
evellet te, et emigrabit te de tabernaculo tuo,  
et radicem tuam de terra viventium.  

8 Videbunt justi, et timebunt;  
et super eum ridebunt, et dicent:  

9 Ecce homo qui non posuit Deum adjutorem suum;  
sed speravit in multitudine divitiarum suarum,  
et prævaluit in vanitate sua.  

10 Ego autem, sicut oliva fructifera in domo Dei;  
speravi in misericordia Dei, in æternum et in sæculum sæculi.  

11 Confitebor tibi in sæculum, quia fecisti;  
et exspectabo nomen tuum,  
quoniam bonum est in conspectu sanctorum tuorum.\]
