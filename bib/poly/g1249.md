G1249  
διάκονος  
diakonos  
*dee-ak‘-on-os*  
Probably from διάκω diakō (obsolete, to *run* on errands; compare
[1377](g1377) ; an *attendant*, that is, (generally) a *waiter* (at
table or in other menial duties); specifically a Christian *teacher* and
*pastor* (technically a *deacon* or *deaconess*): - deacon, minister,
servant.
