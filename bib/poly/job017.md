### Job 17

[KJV](../kjv/job017)

1  My breath<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> is corrupt,<sup>[2254](h2254)</sup>
my days<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> are extinct,<sup>[2193](h2193)</sup> the
graves<sup>[6913](h6913)</sup> are ready for me.

2  Are there not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> mockers<sup>[2049](h2049)</sup>
with<sup>[5978](h5978)</sup> me? and doth not mine
eye<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> continue<sup>[3885](h3885)</sup> in their
provocation?<sup>[4784](h4784)</sup>

3  Lay down<sup>[7760](h7760)</sup> now,<sup>[4994](h4994)</sup> put me
in a surety<sup>[6148](h6148)</sup> with<sup>[5973](h5973)</sup> thee;
who<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup> is he<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> that will
strike<sup>[8628](h8628)</sup> hands<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> with me?

4  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thou hast hid<sup>[6845](h6845)</sup>
their heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> from
understanding:<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7922](h7922)</sup>
therefore<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> <sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> shalt thou
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> exalt<sup>[7311](h7311)</sup> them.

5  He that speaketh<sup>[5046](h5046)</sup>
flattery<sup>[2506](h2506)</sup> to his friends,<sup>[7453](h7453)</sup>
even the eyes<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> of his
children<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> shall fail.<sup>[3615](h3615)</sup>

6  He hath made<sup>[3322](h3322)</sup> me also a
byword<sup>[4914](h4914)</sup> of the people;<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup>
and formerly<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> I was<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> as a
tabret.<sup>[8611](h8611)</sup>

7  Mine eye<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> also is dim<sup>[3543](h3543)</sup>
by reason of sorrow,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3708](h3708)</sup>
and all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> my members<sup>[3338](h3338)</sup> are
as a shadow.<sup>[6738](h6738)</sup>

8  Upright<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup> men shall be
astonished<sup>[8074](h8074)</sup> at<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup>
this,<sup>[2063](h2063)</sup> and the innocent<sup>[5355](h5355)</sup>
shall stir up himself<sup>[5782](h5782)</sup>
against<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the hypocrite.<sup>[2611](h2611)</sup>

9  The righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> also shall
hold<sup>[270](h0270)</sup> on his way,<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> and he
that hath clean<sup>[2889](h2889)</sup> hands<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup>
shall be stronger and stronger.<sup>[3254](h3254)</sup>
<sup>[555](h0555)</sup>

10  But<sup>[199](h0199)</sup> as for you all,<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup>
do ye return,<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> and come<sup>[935](h0935)</sup>
now:<sup>[4994](h4994)</sup> for I cannot<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
find<sup>[4672](h4672)</sup> one wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> man among
you.

11  My days<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> are past,<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup> my
purposes<sup>[2154](h2154)</sup> are broken off,<sup>[5423](h5423)</sup>
even the thoughts<sup>[4180](h4180)</sup> of my
heart.<sup>[3824](h3824)</sup>

12  They change<sup>[7760](h7760)</sup> the
night<sup>[3915](h3915)</sup> into day:<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> the
light<sup>[216](h0216)</sup> is short<sup>[7138](h7138)</sup>
because<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> of
darkness.<sup>[2822](h2822)</sup>

13  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> I wait,<sup>[6960](h6960)</sup> the
grave<sup>[7585](h7585)</sup> is mine house:<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> I
have made<sup>[7502](h7502)</sup> my bed<sup>[3326](h3326)</sup> in the
darkness.<sup>[2822](h2822)</sup>

14  I have said<sup>[7121](h7121)</sup> to
corruption,<sup>[7845](h7845)</sup> Thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> art my
father:<sup>[1](h0001)</sup> to the worm,<sup>[7415](h7415)</sup> Thou
art my mother,<sup>[517](h0517)</sup> and my
sister.<sup>[269](h0269)</sup>

15  And where<sup>[346](h0346)</sup> is now<sup>[645](h0645)</sup> my
hope?<sup>[8615](h8615)</sup> as for my hope,<sup>[8615](h8615)</sup>
who<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup> shall see<sup>[7789](h7789)</sup> it?

16  They shall go down<sup>[3381](h3381)</sup> to the
bars<sup>[905](h0905)</sup> of the pit,<sup>[7585](h7585)</sup>
when<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> our rest<sup>[5183](h5183)</sup>
together<sup>[3162](h3162)</sup> is in<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the
dust.<sup>[6083](h6083)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/job017)

1 ὀλέκομαι πνεύματι φερόμενος δέομαι δὲ ταφῆς καὶ οὐ τυγχάνω

2 λίσσομαι κάμνων καὶ τί ποιήσας

3 ἔκλεψαν δέ μου τὰ ὑπάρχοντα ἀλλότριοι τίς ἐστιν οὗτος τῇ χειρί μου
συνδεθήτω

4 ὅτι καρδίαν αὐτῶν ἔκρυψας ἀπὸ φρονήσεως διὰ τοῦτο οὐ μὴ ὑψώσῃς αὐτούς

5 τῇ μερίδι ἀναγγελεῖ κακίας ὀφθαλμοὶ δέ μου ἐφ᾽ υἱοῖς ἐτάκησαν

6 ἔθου δέ με θρύλημα ἐν ἔθνεσιν γέλως δὲ αὐτοῖς ἀπέβην

7 πεπώρωνται γὰρ ἀπὸ ὀργῆς οἱ ὀφθαλμοί μου πεπολιόρκημαι μεγάλως ὑπὸ
πάντων

8 θαῦμα ἔσχεν ἀληθινοὺς ἐπὶ τούτῳ δίκαιος δὲ ἐπὶ παρανόμῳ ἐπανασταίη

9 σχοίη δὲ πιστὸς τὴν ἑαυτοῦ ὁδόν καθαρὸς δὲ χεῖρας ἀναλάβοι θάρσος

10 οὐ μὴν δὲ ἀλλὰ πάντες ἐρείδετε καὶ δεῦτε δή οὐ γὰρ εὑρίσκω ἐν ὑμῖν
ἀληθές

11 αἱ ἡμέραι μου παρῆλθον ἐν βρόμῳ ἐρράγη δὲ τὰ ἄρθρα τῆς καρδίας μου

12 νύκτα εἰς ἡμέραν ἔθηκαν φῶς ἐγγὺς ἀπὸ προσώπου σκότους

13 ἐὰν γὰρ ὑπομείνω ᾅδης μου ὁ οἶκος ἐν δὲ γνόφῳ ἔστρωταί μου ἡ στρωμνή

14 θάνατον ἐπεκαλεσάμην πατέρα μου εἶναι μητέρα δέ μου καὶ ἀδελφὴν
σαπρίαν

15 ποῦ οὖν μου ἔτι ἐστὶν ἡ ἐλπίς ἦ τὰ ἀγαθά μου ὄψομαι

16 ἦ μετ᾽ ἐμοῦ εἰς ᾅδην καταβήσονται ἢ ὁμοθυμαδὸν ἐπὶ χώματος
καταβησόμεθα

[Vul](../vul/job017)

1 \[Spiritus meus attenuabitur;  
dies mei breviabuntur:  
et solum mihi superest sepulchrum.  

2 Non peccavi,  
et in amaritudinibus moratur oculus meus.  

3 Libera me, Domine, et pone me juxta te,  
et cujusvis manus pugnet contra me.  

4 Cor eorum longe fecisti a disciplina:  
propterea non exaltabuntur.  

5 Prædam pollicetur sociis,  
et oculi filiorum ejus deficient.  

6 Posuit me quasi in proverbium vulgi,  
et exemplum sum coram eis.  

7 Caligavit ab indignatione oculus meus,  
et membra mea quasi in nihilum redacta sunt.  

8 Stupebunt justi super hoc,  
et innocens contra hypocritam suscitabitur.  

9 Et tenebit justus viam suam,  
et mundis manibus addet fortitudinem.  

10 Igitur omnes vos convertimini, et venite,  
et non inveniam in vobis ullum sapientem.  

11 Dies mei transierunt;  
cogitationes meæ dissipatæ sunt,  
torquentes cor meum.  

12 Noctem verterunt in diem,  
et rursum post tenebras spero lucem.  

13 Si sustinuero, infernus domus mea est,  
et in tenebris stravi lectulum meum.  

14 Putredini dixi: Pater meus es;  
Mater mea, et soror mea, vermibus.  

15 Ubi est ergo nunc præstolatio mea?  
et patientiam meam quis considerat?  

16 In profundissimum infernum descendent omnia mea:  
putasne saltem ibi erit requies mihi?\]
