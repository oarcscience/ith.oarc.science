### Amos 8

[KJV](../kjv/amo008.md)

1  Thus<sup>[3541](h3541)</sup> hath the Lord<sup>[136](h0136.md)</sup>
GOD<sup>[3069](h3069)</sup> showed<sup>[7200](h7200.md)</sup> unto me: and
behold<sup>[2009](h2009)</sup> a basket<sup>[3619](h3619.md)</sup> of
summer fruit.<sup>[7019](h7019.md)</sup>

2  And he said,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> Amos,<sup>[5986](h5986.md)</sup>
what<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> seest<sup>[7200](h7200.md)</sup>
thou?<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> And I said,<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup> A
basket<sup>[3619](h3619)</sup> of summer fruit.<sup>[7019](h7019.md)</sup>
Then said<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup>
unto<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> me, The end<sup>[7093](h7093.md)</sup> is
come<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> upon<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup> my
people<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup> of Israel;<sup>[3478](h3478.md)</sup> I will
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> again<sup>[3254](h3254.md)</sup> pass
by<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup> them any more.<sup>[5750](h5750.md)</sup>

3  And the songs<sup>[7892](h7892.md)</sup> of the
temple<sup>[1964](h1964)</sup> shall be howlings<sup>[3213](h3213.md)</sup>
in that<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> day,<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup>
saith<sup>[5002](h5002)</sup> the Lord<sup>[136](h0136.md)</sup>
GOD:<sup>[3069](h3069)</sup> there shall be many<sup>[7227](h7227.md)</sup>
dead bodies<sup>[6297](h6297)</sup> in every<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup>
place;<sup>[4725](h4725.md)</sup> they shall cast them
forth<sup>[7993](h7993)</sup> with silence.<sup>[2013](h2013.md)</sup>

4  Hear<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> this,<sup>[2063](h2063.md)</sup> O ye that
swallow up<sup>[7602](h7602)</sup> the needy,<sup>[34](h0034.md)</sup> even
to make the poor<sup>[6041](h6041.md)</sup> of the
land<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> to fail,<sup>[7673](h7673.md)</sup>

5  Saying,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> When<sup>[4970](h4970.md)</sup> will the
new moon<sup>[2320](h2320)</sup> be gone,<sup>[5674](h5674.md)</sup> that
we may sell<sup>[7666](h7666)</sup> corn?<sup>[7668](h7668.md)</sup> and
the sabbath,<sup>[7676](h7676.md)</sup> that we may set
forth<sup>[6605](h6605)</sup> wheat,<sup>[1250](h1250.md)</sup> making the
ephah small,<sup>[6994](h6994)</sup> <sup>[374](h0374.md)</sup> and the
shekel<sup>[8255](h8255)</sup> great,<sup>[1431](h1431.md)</sup> and
falsifying<sup>[5791](h5791)</sup> the balances<sup>[3976](h3976.md)</sup>
by deceit?<sup>[4820](h4820.md)</sup>

6  That we may buy<sup>[7069](h7069.md)</sup> the
poor<sup>[1800](h1800)</sup> for silver,<sup>[3701](h3701.md)</sup> and the
needy<sup>[34](h0034)</sup> for<sup>[5668](h5668.md)</sup> a pair of
shoes;<sup>[5275](h5275)</sup> yea, and sell<sup>[7666](h7666.md)</sup> the
refuse<sup>[4651](h4651)</sup> of the wheat?<sup>[1250](h1250.md)</sup>

7  The LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> hath sworn<sup>[7650](h7650.md)</sup>
by the excellency<sup>[1347](h1347.md)</sup> of
Jacob,<sup>[3290](h3290)</sup> Surely<sup>[518](h0518.md)</sup> I will
never<sup>[5331](h5331)</sup> forget<sup>[7911](h7911.md)</sup>
any<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> of their works.<sup>[4639](h4639.md)</sup>

8  Shall not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> the land<sup>[776](h0776.md)</sup>
tremble<sup>[7264](h7264)</sup> for<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
this,<sup>[2063](h2063)</sup> and every one<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup>
mourn<sup>[56](h0056)</sup> that dwelleth<sup>[3427](h3427.md)</sup>
therein? and it shall rise up<sup>[5927](h5927.md)</sup>
wholly<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> as a flood;<sup>[2975](h2975.md)</sup> and
it shall be cast out<sup>[1644](h1644.md)</sup> and
drowned,<sup>[8257](h8257)</sup> as by the flood<sup>[2975](h2975.md)</sup>
of Egypt.<sup>[4714](h4714.md)</sup>

9  And it shall come to pass<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup> in
that<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> day,<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup>
saith<sup>[5002](h5002)</sup> the Lord<sup>[136](h0136.md)</sup>
GOD,<sup>[3069](h3069.md)</sup> that I will cause the
sun<sup>[8121](h8121)</sup> to go down<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup> at
noon,<sup>[6672](h6672)</sup> and I will darken<sup>[2821](h2821.md)</sup>
the earth<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> in the clear<sup>[216](h0216.md)</sup>
day:<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup>

10  And I will turn<sup>[2015](h2015.md)</sup> your
feasts<sup>[2282](h2282)</sup> into mourning,<sup>[60](h0060.md)</sup> and
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> your songs<sup>[7892](h7892.md)</sup> into
lamentation;<sup>[7015](h7015.md)</sup> and I will bring
up<sup>[5927](h5927)</sup> sackcloth<sup>[8242](h8242.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup>
loins,<sup>[4975](h4975)</sup> and baldness<sup>[7144](h7144.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> every<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup>
head;<sup>[7218](h7218)</sup> and I will make<sup>[7760](h7760.md)</sup> it
as the mourning<sup>[60](h0060)</sup> of an only<sup>[3173](h3173.md)</sup>
son, and the end<sup>[319](h0319.md)</sup> thereof as a
bitter<sup>[4751](h4751)</sup> day.<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup>

11  Behold,<sup>[2009](h2009)</sup> the days<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup>
come,<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> saith<sup>[5002](h5002.md)</sup> the
Lord<sup>[136](h0136)</sup> GOD,<sup>[3069](h3069.md)</sup> that I will
send<sup>[7971](h7971)</sup> a famine<sup>[7458](h7458.md)</sup> in the
land,<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> a
famine<sup>[7458](h7458)</sup> of bread,<sup>[3899](h3899.md)</sup>
nor<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> a thirst<sup>[6772](h6772.md)</sup> for
water,<sup>[4325](h4325)</sup> but<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup>
<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> of hearing<sup>[8085](h8085.md)</sup>
<sup>[853](h0853)</sup> the words<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup> of the
LORD:<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup>

12  And they shall wander<sup>[5128](h5128.md)</sup> from
sea<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3220](h3220.md)</sup>
to<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> sea,<sup>[3220](h3220.md)</sup> and from the
north<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6828](h6828.md)</sup> even
to<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> the east,<sup>[4217](h4217.md)</sup> they shall
run to and fro<sup>[7751](h7751)</sup> to seek<sup>[1245](h1245.md)</sup>
<sup>[853](h0853)</sup> the word<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup> of the
LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> and shall not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
find<sup>[4672](h4672.md)</sup> it.

13  In that<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> day<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup> shall
the fair<sup>[3303](h3303)</sup> virgins<sup>[1330](h1330.md)</sup> and
young men<sup>[970](h0970)</sup> faint<sup>[5968](h5968.md)</sup> for
thirst.<sup>[6772](h6772.md)</sup>

14  They that swear<sup>[7650](h7650.md)</sup> by the
sin<sup>[819](h0819)</sup> of Samaria,<sup>[8111](h8111.md)</sup> and
say,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> Thy god,<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup> O
Dan,<sup>[1835](h1835)</sup> liveth;<sup>[2416](h2416.md)</sup> and, The
manner<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of Beer-sheba<sup>[884](h0884.md)</sup>
liveth;<sup>[2416](h2416.md)</sup> even they shall
fall,<sup>[5307](h5307)</sup> and never<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> rise
up<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> again.<sup>[5750](h5750.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/amo008.md)

1 οὕτως ἔδειξέν μοι κύριος καὶ ἰδοὺ ἄγγος ἰξευτοῦ

2 καὶ εἶπεν τί σὺ βλέπεις Αμως καὶ εἶπα ἄγγος ἰξευτοῦ καὶ εἶπεν κύριος
πρός με ἥκει τὸ πέρας ἐπὶ τὸν λαόν μου Ισραηλ οὐκέτι μὴ προσθῶ τοῦ
παρελθεῖν αὐτόν

3 καὶ ὀλολύξει τὰ φατνώματα τοῦ ναοῦ ἐν ἐκείνῃ τῇ ἡμέρᾳ λέγει κύριος
πολὺς ὁ πεπτωκὼς ἐν παντὶ τόπῳ ἐπιρρίψω σιωπήν

4 ἀκούσατε δὴ ταῦτα οἱ ἐκτρίβοντες εἰς τὸ πρωὶ πένητα καὶ
καταδυναστεύοντες πτωχοὺς ἀπὸ τῆς γῆς

5 οἱ λέγοντες πότε διελεύσεται ὁ μὴν καὶ ἐμπολήσομεν καὶ τὰ σάββατα καὶ
ἀνοίξομεν θησαυροὺς τοῦ ποιῆσαι μικρὸν μέτρον καὶ τοῦ μεγαλῦναι στάθμια
καὶ ποιῆσαι ζυγὸν ἄδικον

6 τοῦ κτᾶσθαι ἐν ἀργυρίῳ πτωχοὺς καὶ ταπεινὸν ἀντὶ ὑποδημάτων καὶ ἀπὸ
παντὸς γενήματος ἐμπορευσόμεθα

7 ὀμνύει κύριος καθ᾽ ὑπερηφανίας Ιακωβ εἰ ἐπιλησθήσεται εἰς νεῖκος πάντα
τὰ ἔργα ὑμῶν

8 καὶ ἐπὶ τούτοις οὐ ταραχθήσεται ἡ γῆ καὶ πενθήσει πᾶς ὁ κατοικῶν ἐν
αὐτῇ καὶ ἀναβήσεται ὡς ποταμὸς συντέλεια καὶ καταβήσεται ὡς ποταμὸς
Αἰγύπτου

9 καὶ ἔσται ἐν ἐκείνῃ τῇ ἡμέρᾳ λέγει κύριος ὁ θεός καὶ δύσεται ὁ ἥλιος
μεσημβρίας καὶ συσκοτάσει ἐπὶ τῆς γῆς ἐν ἡμέρᾳ τὸ φῶς

10 καὶ μεταστρέψω τὰς ἑορτὰς ὑμῶν εἰς πένθος καὶ πάσας τὰς ᾠδὰς ὑμῶν εἰς
θρῆνον καὶ ἀναβιβῶ ἐπὶ πᾶσαν ὀσφὺν σάκκον καὶ ἐπὶ πᾶσαν κεφαλὴν
φαλάκρωμα καὶ θήσομαι αὐτὸν ὡς πένθος ἀγαπητοῦ καὶ τοὺς μετ᾽ αὐτοῦ ὡς
ἡμέραν ὀδύνης

11 ἰδοὺ ἡμέραι ἔρχονται λέγει κύριος καὶ ἐξαποστελῶ λιμὸν ἐπὶ τὴν γῆν οὐ
λιμὸν ἄρτου οὐδὲ δίψαν ὕδατος ἀλλὰ λιμὸν τοῦ ἀκοῦσαι λόγον κυρίου

12 καὶ σαλευθήσονται ὕδατα ἕως θαλάσσης καὶ ἀπὸ βορρᾶ ἕως ἀνατολῶν
περιδραμοῦνται ζητοῦντες τὸν λόγον κυρίου καὶ οὐ μὴ εὕρωσιν

13 ἐν τῇ ἡμέρᾳ ἐκείνῃ ἐκλείψουσιν αἱ παρθένοι αἱ καλαὶ καὶ οἱ νεανίσκοι
ἐν δίψει

14 οἱ ὀμνύοντες κατὰ τοῦ ἱλασμοῦ Σαμαρείας καὶ οἱ λέγοντες ζῇ ὁ θεός σου
Δαν καὶ ζῇ ὁ θεός σου Βηρσαβεε καὶ πεσοῦνται καὶ οὐ μὴ ἀναστῶσιν ἔτι

[Vul](../vul/amo008.md)

1 Hæc ostendit mihi Dominus Deus: et ecce uncinus pomorum.

2 Et dixit: Quid tu vides, Amos? Et dixi: Uncinum pomorum. Et dixit
Dominus ad me: \[Venit finis super populum meum Israël;  
non adjiciam ultra ut pertranseam eum.  

3 Et stridebunt cardines templi in die illa,  
dicit Dominus Deus:  
multi morientur;  
in omni loco projicietur silentium.\]  

4 \[Audite hoc, qui conteritis pauperem,  
et deficere facitis egenos terræ,  

5 dicentes: Quando transibit mensis,  
et venundabimus merces?  
et sabbatum, et aperiemus frumentum,  
ut imminuamus mensuram, et augeamus siclum,  
et supponamus stateras dolosas,  

6 ut possideamus in argento egenos et pauperes pro calceamentis,  
et quisquilias frumenti vendamus?  

7 Juravit Dominus in superbiam Jacob:  
Si oblitus fuero usque ad finem omnia opera eorum.  

8 Numquid super isto non commovebitur terra,  
et lugebit omnis habitator ejus,  
et ascendet quasi fluvius universus,  
et ejicicetur, et defluet, quasi rivus Ægypti?  

9 Et erit in die illa, dicit Dominus Deus:  
occidet sol in meridie,  
et tenebrescere faciam terram in die luminis:  

10 et convertam festivitates vestras in luctum,  
et omnia cantica vestra in planctum,  
et inducam super omne dorsum vestrum saccum,  
et super omne caput calvitium:  
et ponam eam quasi luctum unigeniti,  
et novissima ejus quasi diem amarum.  

11 Ecce dies veniunt, dicet Dominus,  
et mittam famem in terram:  
non famem panis, neque sitim aquæ,  
sed audiendi verbum Domini.  

12 Et commovebuntur a mari usque ad mare,  
et ab aquilone usque ad orientem:  
circuibunt quærentes verbum Domini,  
et non invenient.  

13 In die illa deficient virgines pulchræ et adolescentes in siti,  

14 qui jurant in delicto Samariæ,  
et dicunt: Vivit Deus tuus, Dan,  
et vivit via Bersabee;  
et cadent, et non resurgent ultra.\]
