### Job 4

[KJV](../kjv/job004.md)

1  Then Eliphaz<sup>[464](h0464.md)</sup> the
Temanite<sup>[8489](h8489)</sup> answered<sup>[6030](h6030.md)</sup> and
said,<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup>

2  If we attempt to commune<sup>[5254](h5254.md)</sup>
<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup> with<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup> thee, wilt thou be
grieved?<sup>[3811](h3811)</sup> but who<sup>[4310](h4310.md)</sup>
can<sup>[3201](h3201)</sup> withhold<sup>[6113](h6113.md)</sup> himself
from speaking?<sup>[4405](h4405.md)</sup>

3  Behold,<sup>[2009](h2009.md)</sup> thou hast
instructed<sup>[3256](h3256)</sup> many,<sup>[7227](h7227.md)</sup> and
thou hast strengthened<sup>[2388](h2388.md)</sup> the
weak<sup>[7504](h7504)</sup> hands.<sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup>

4  Thy words<sup>[4405](h4405)</sup> have upheld<sup>[6965](h6965.md)</sup>
him that was falling,<sup>[3782](h3782.md)</sup> and thou hast
strengthened<sup>[553](h0553)</sup> the feeble<sup>[3766](h3766.md)</sup>
knees.<sup>[1290](h1290.md)</sup>

5  But<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> now<sup>[6258](h6258.md)</sup> it is
come<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> upon<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup> thee, and thou
faintest;<sup>[3811](h3811)</sup> it toucheth<sup>[5060](h5060.md)</sup>
<sup>[5704](h5704.md)</sup> thee, and thou art
troubled.<sup>[926](h0926.md)</sup>

6  Is not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> this thy fear,<sup>[3374](h3374.md)</sup>
thy confidence,<sup>[3690](h3690.md)</sup> thy
hope,<sup>[8615](h8615.md)</sup> and the
uprightness<sup>[8537](h8537)</sup> of thy ways?<sup>[1870](h1870.md)</sup>

7  Remember,<sup>[2142](h2142.md)</sup> I pray
thee,<sup>[4994](h4994)</sup> who<sup>[4310](h4310.md)</sup> ever
perished,<sup>[6](h0006)</sup> being innocent?<sup>[5355](h5355.md)</sup>
or where<sup>[375](h0375.md)</sup> were the
righteous<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup> cut off?<sup>[3582](h3582.md)</sup>

8  Even as<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> I have seen,<sup>[7200](h7200.md)</sup>
they that plow<sup>[2790](h2790)</sup> iniquity,<sup>[205](h0205.md)</sup>
and sow<sup>[2232](h2232)</sup> wickedness,<sup>[5999](h5999.md)</sup>
reap<sup>[7114](h7114.md)</sup> the same.

9  By the blast<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[5397](h5397.md)</sup> of
God<sup>[433](h0433)</sup> they perish,<sup>[6](h0006.md)</sup> and by the
breath<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7307](h7307.md)</sup> of his
nostrils<sup>[639](h0639.md)</sup> are they
consumed.<sup>[3615](h3615.md)</sup>

10  The roaring<sup>[7581](h7581.md)</sup> of the
lion,<sup>[738](h0738)</sup> and the voice<sup>[6963](h6963.md)</sup> of
the fierce lion,<sup>[7826](h7826.md)</sup> and the
teeth<sup>[8127](h8127.md)</sup> of the young
lions,<sup>[3715](h3715)</sup> are broken.<sup>[5421](h5421.md)</sup>

11  The old lion<sup>[3918](h3918)</sup> perisheth<sup>[6](h0006.md)</sup>
for lack<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[1097](h1097.md)</sup> of
prey,<sup>[2964](h2964.md)</sup> and the stout
lion's<sup>[3833](h3833)</sup> whelps<sup>[1121](h1121.md)</sup> are
scattered abroad.<sup>[6504](h6504.md)</sup>

12  Now a thing<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup> was secretly
brought<sup>[1589](h1589)</sup> to<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup> me, and mine
ear<sup>[241](h0241)</sup> received<sup>[3947](h3947.md)</sup> a
little<sup>[8102](h8102)</sup> thereof.<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>

13  In thoughts<sup>[5587](h5587.md)</sup> from the
visions<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[2384](h2384.md)</sup> of the
night,<sup>[3915](h3915)</sup> when deep sleep<sup>[8639](h8639.md)</sup>
falleth<sup>[5307](h5307)</sup> on<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
men,<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup>

14  Fear<sup>[6343](h6343)</sup> came upon<sup>[7122](h7122.md)</sup> me,
and trembling,<sup>[7460](h7460.md)</sup> which made
all<sup>[7230](h7230)</sup> my bones<sup>[6106](h6106.md)</sup> to
shake.<sup>[6342](h6342.md)</sup>

15  Then a spirit<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> passed<sup>[2498](h2498.md)</sup>
before<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> my face;<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> the
hair<sup>[8185](h8185)</sup> of my flesh<sup>[1320](h1320.md)</sup> stood
up:<sup>[5568](h5568.md)</sup>

16  It stood still,<sup>[5975](h5975.md)</sup> but I could
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> discern<sup>[5234](h5234.md)</sup> the
form<sup>[4758](h4758)</sup> thereof: an image<sup>[8544](h8544.md)</sup>
was before<sup>[5048](h5048)</sup> mine eyes,<sup>[5869](h5869.md)</sup>
there was silence,<sup>[1827](h1827.md)</sup> and I
heard<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> a voice,<sup>[6963](h6963.md)</sup> saying,

17  Shall mortal man<sup>[582](h0582.md)</sup> be more just than
God?<sup>[6663](h6663)</sup> <sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[433](h0433)</sup> shall a man<sup>[1397](h1397.md)</sup> be more
pure<sup>[2891](h2891)</sup> than his maker?<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[6213](h6213.md)</sup>

18  Behold,<sup>[2005](h2005.md)</sup> he put no
trust<sup>[539](h0539)</sup> <sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> in his
servants;<sup>[5650](h5650)</sup> and his angels<sup>[4397](h4397.md)</sup>
he charged<sup>[7760](h7760)</sup> with folly:<sup>[8417](h8417.md)</sup>

19  How much less<sup>[637](h0637.md)</sup> in them that dwell
in<sup>[7931](h7931)</sup> houses<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup> of
clay,<sup>[2563](h2563)</sup> whose<sup>[834](h0834.md)</sup>
foundation<sup>[3247](h3247.md)</sup> is in the
dust,<sup>[6083](h6083)</sup> which are crushed<sup>[1792](h1792.md)</sup>
before<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> the moth?<sup>[6211](h6211.md)</sup>

20  They are destroyed<sup>[3807](h3807.md)</sup> from
morning<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[1242](h1242.md)</sup> to
evening:<sup>[6153](h6153)</sup> they perish<sup>[6](h0006.md)</sup>
forever<sup>[5331](h5331)</sup> without any<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[1097](h1097)</sup> regarding<sup>[7760](h7760.md)</sup> it.

21  Doth not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> their
excellency<sup>[3499](h3499.md)</sup> which is in them go
away?<sup>[5265](h5265)</sup> they die,<sup>[4191](h4191.md)</sup> even
without<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> wisdom.<sup>[2451](h2451.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/job004.md)

1 ὑπολαβὼν δὲ Ελιφας ὁ Θαιμανίτης λέγει

2 μὴ πολλάκις σοι λελάληται ἐν κόπῳ ἰσχὺν δὲ ῥημάτων σου τίς ὑποίσει

3 εἰ γὰρ σὺ ἐνουθέτησας πολλοὺς καὶ χεῖρας ἀσθενοῦς παρεκάλεσας

4 ἀσθενοῦντάς τε ἐξανέστησας ῥήμασιν γόνασίν τε ἀδυνατοῦσιν θάρσος
περιέθηκας

5 νῦν δὲ ἥκει ἐπὶ σὲ πόνος καὶ ἥψατό σου σὺ δὲ ἐσπούδασας

6 πότερον οὐχ ὁ φόβος σού ἐστιν ἐν ἀφροσύνῃ καὶ ἡ ἐλπίς σου καὶ ἡ ἀκακία
τῆς ὁδοῦ σου

7 μνήσθητι οὖν τίς καθαρὸς ὢν ἀπώλετο ἢ πότε ἀληθινοὶ ὁλόρριζοι ἀπώλοντο

8 καθ᾽ ὃν τρόπον εἶδον τοὺς ἀροτριῶντας τὰ ἄτοπα οἱ δὲ σπείροντες αὐτὰ
ὀδύνας θεριοῦσιν ἑαυτοῖς

9 ἀπὸ προστάγματος κυρίου ἀπολοῦνται ἀπὸ δὲ πνεύματος ὀργῆς αὐτοῦ
ἀφανισθήσονται

10 σθένος λέοντος φωνὴ δὲ λεαίνης γαυρίαμα δὲ δρακόντων ἐσβέσθη

11 μυρμηκολέων ὤλετο παρὰ τὸ μὴ ἔχειν βοράν σκύμνοι δὲ λεόντων ἔλιπον
ἀλλήλους

12 εἰ δέ τι ῥῆμα ἀληθινὸν ἐγεγόνει ἐν λόγοις σου οὐθὲν ἄν σοι τούτων
κακὸν ἀπήντησεν πότερον οὐ δέξεταί μου τὸ οὖς ἐξαίσια παρ᾽ αὐτοῦ

13 φόβοι δὲ καὶ ἠχὼ νυκτερινή ἐπιπίπτων φόβος ἐπ᾽ ἀνθρώπους

14 φρίκη δέ μοι συνήντησεν καὶ τρόμος καὶ μεγάλως μου τὰ ὀστᾶ συνέσεισεν

15 καὶ πνεῦμα ἐπὶ πρόσωπόν μου ἐπῆλθεν ἔφριξαν δέ μου τρίχες καὶ σάρκες

16 ἀνέστην καὶ οὐκ ἐπέγνων εἶδον καὶ οὐκ ἦν μορφὴ πρὸ ὀφθαλμῶν μου ἀλλ᾽
ἢ αὔραν καὶ φωνὴν ἤκουον

17 τί γάρ μὴ καθαρὸς ἔσται βροτὸς ἐναντίον κυρίου ἢ ἀπὸ τῶν ἔργων αὐτοῦ
ἄμεμπτος ἀνήρ

18 εἰ κατὰ παίδων αὐτοῦ οὐ πιστεύει κατὰ δὲ ἀγγέλων αὐτοῦ σκολιόν τι
ἐπενόησεν

19 τοὺς δὲ κατοικοῦντας οἰκίας πηλίνας ἐξ ὧν καὶ αὐτοὶ ἐκ τοῦ αὐτοῦ
πηλοῦ ἐσμεν ἔπαισεν αὐτοὺς σητὸς τρόπον

20 καὶ ἀπὸ πρωίθεν ἕως ἑσπέρας οὐκέτι εἰσίν παρὰ τὸ μὴ δύνασθαι αὐτοὺς
ἑαυτοῖς βοηθῆσαι ἀπώλοντο

21 ἐνεφύσησεν γὰρ αὐτοῖς καὶ ἐξηράνθησαν ἀπώλοντο παρὰ τὸ μὴ ἔχειν
αὐτοὺς σοφίαν

1 Respondens autem Eliphaz Themanites, dixit:

2 \[Si cœperimus loqui tibi, forsitan moleste accipies;  
sed conceptum sermonem tenere quis poterit?  

3 Ecce docuisti multos,  
et manus lassas roborasti;  

4 vacillantes confirmaverunt sermones tui,  
et genua trementia confortasti.  

5 Nunc autem venit super te plaga, et defecisti;  
tetigit te, et conturbatus es.  

6 Ubi est timor tuus, fortitudo tua,  
patientia tua, et perfectio viarum tuarum?  

7 Recordare, obsecro te, quis umquam innocens periit?  
aut quando recti deleti sunt?  

8 Quin potius vidi eos qui operantur iniquitatem,  
et seminant dolores, et metunt eos,  

9 flante Deo perisse,  
et spiritu iræ ejus esse consumptos.  

10 Rugitus leonis, et vox leænæ,  
et dentes catulorum leonum contriti sunt.  

11 Tigris periit, eo quod non haberet prædam,  
et catuli leonis dissipati sunt.  

12 Porro ad me dictum est verbum absconditum,  
et quasi furtive suscepit auris mea venas susurri ejus.  

13 In horrore visionis nocturnæ,  
quando solet sopor occupare homines,  

14 pavor tenuit me, et tremor,  
et omnia ossa mea perterrita sunt;  

15 et cum spiritus, me præsente, transiret,  
inhorruerunt pili carnis meæ.  

16 Stetit quidam, cujus non agnoscebam vultum,  
imago coram oculis meis,  
et vocem quasi auræ lenis audivi.  

17 Numquid homo, Dei comparatione, justificabitur?  
aut factore suo purior erit vir?  

18 Ecce qui serviunt ei, non sunt stabiles,  
et in angelis suis reperit pravitatem;  

19 quanto magis hi qui habitant domos luteas,  
qui terrenum habent fundamentum,  
consumentur velut a tinea?  

20 De mane usque ad vesperam succidentur;  
et quia nullus intelligit, in æternum peribunt.  

21 Qui autem reliqui fuerint, auferentur ex eis;  
morientur, et non in sapientia.\]
