### Hosea 13

[KJV](../kjv/hos013.md)

1  When Ephraim<sup>[669](h0669)</sup> spoke<sup>[1696](h1696.md)</sup>
trembling,<sup>[7578](h7578)</sup> he<sup>[1931](h1931.md)</sup>
exalted<sup>[5375](h5375.md)</sup> himself in
Israel;<sup>[3478](h3478.md)</sup> but when he
offended<sup>[816](h0816)</sup> in Baal,<sup>[1168](h1168.md)</sup> he
died.<sup>[4191](h4191.md)</sup>

2  And now<sup>[6258](h6258)</sup> they sin<sup>[2398](h2398.md)</sup> more
and more,<sup>[3254](h3254)</sup> and have made<sup>[6213](h6213.md)</sup>
them molten images<sup>[4541](h4541.md)</sup> of their
silver,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3701](h3701.md)</sup> and
idols<sup>[6091](h6091.md)</sup> according to their own
understanding,<sup>[8394](h8394)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup> of it
the work<sup>[4639](h4639.md)</sup> of the
craftsmen:<sup>[2796](h2796)</sup> they<sup>[1992](h1992.md)</sup>
say<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> of them, Let the men<sup>[120](h0120.md)</sup>
that sacrifice<sup>[2076](h2076)</sup> kiss<sup>[5401](h5401.md)</sup> the
calves.<sup>[5695](h5695.md)</sup>

3  Therefore<sup>[3651](h3651.md)</sup> they shall
be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> as the morning<sup>[1242](h1242.md)</sup>
cloud,<sup>[6051](h6051)</sup> and as the early<sup>[7925](h7925.md)</sup>
dew<sup>[2919](h2919)</sup> that passeth away,<sup>[1980](h1980.md)</sup>
as the chaff<sup>[4671](h4671.md)</sup> that is driven with the
whirlwind<sup>[5590](h5590.md)</sup> out of the
floor,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[1637](h1637.md)</sup> and as the
smoke<sup>[6227](h6227.md)</sup> out of the
chimney.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[699](h0699.md)</sup>

4  Yet I<sup>[595](h0595)</sup> am the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup> thy
God<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> from the land<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> of Egypt,<sup>[4714](h4714.md)</sup> and thou shalt
know<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> no<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
god<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> but<sup>[2108](h2108.md)</sup> me: for there is
no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> savior<sup>[3467](h3467.md)</sup>
beside<sup>[1115](h1115.md)</sup> me.

5  I<sup>[589](h0589)</sup> did know<sup>[3045](h3045.md)</sup> thee in the
wilderness,<sup>[4057](h4057)</sup> in the land<sup>[776](h0776.md)</sup>
of great drought.<sup>[8514](h8514.md)</sup>

6  According to their pasture,<sup>[4830](h4830.md)</sup> so were they
filled;<sup>[7646](h7646.md)</sup> they were
filled,<sup>[7646](h7646)</sup> and their heart<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup>
was exalted;<sup>[7311](h7311)</sup> therefore<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> have they forgotten<sup>[7911](h7911.md)</sup> me.

7  Therefore I will be<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup> unto them
as<sup>[3644](h3644)</sup> a lion:<sup>[7826](h7826.md)</sup> as a
leopard<sup>[5246](h5246)</sup> by<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> the
way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> will I observe<sup>[7789](h7789.md)</sup> them:

8  I will meet<sup>[6298](h6298.md)</sup> them as a
bear<sup>[1677](h1677)</sup> that is bereaved<sup>[7909](h7909.md)</sup> of
her whelps, and will rend<sup>[7167](h7167.md)</sup> the
caul<sup>[5458](h5458)</sup> of their heart,<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup> and
there<sup>[8033](h8033)</sup> will I devour<sup>[398](h0398.md)</sup> them
like a lion:<sup>[3833](h3833)</sup> the wild<sup>[7704](h7704.md)</sup>
beast<sup>[2416](h2416)</sup> shall tear<sup>[1234](h1234.md)</sup> them.

9  O Israel,<sup>[3478](h3478.md)</sup> thou hast
destroyed<sup>[7843](h7843)</sup> thyself; but<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup>
in me is thine help.<sup>[5828](h5828.md)</sup>

10  I will be<sup>[165](h0165)</sup> thy king:<sup>[4428](h4428.md)</sup>
where<sup>[645](h0645.md)</sup> is any other that may
save<sup>[3467](h3467)</sup> thee in all<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup> thy
cities?<sup>[5892](h5892)</sup> and thy judges<sup>[8199](h8199.md)</sup>
of whom<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> thou saidst,<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup>
Give<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> me a king<sup>[4428](h4428.md)</sup> and
princes?<sup>[8269](h8269.md)</sup>

11  I gave<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> thee a king<sup>[4428](h4428.md)</sup>
in mine anger,<sup>[639](h0639.md)</sup> and took him
away<sup>[3947](h3947)</sup> in my wrath.<sup>[5678](h5678.md)</sup>

12  The iniquity<sup>[5771](h5771.md)</sup> of
Ephraim<sup>[669](h0669)</sup> is bound up;<sup>[6887](h6887.md)</sup> his
sin<sup>[2403](h2403)</sup> is hid.<sup>[6845](h6845.md)</sup>

13  The sorrows<sup>[2256](h2256.md)</sup> of a travailing
woman<sup>[3205](h3205)</sup> shall come<sup>[935](h0935.md)</sup> upon
him: he<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> is an unwise<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> son;<sup>[1121](h1121.md)</sup>
for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> he should not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
stay<sup>[5975](h5975)</sup> long<sup>[6256](h6256.md)</sup> in the place
of the breaking forth<sup>[4866](h4866.md)</sup> of
children.<sup>[1121](h1121.md)</sup>

14  I will ransom<sup>[6299](h6299.md)</sup> them from the
power<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup> of the
grave;<sup>[7585](h7585)</sup> I will redeem<sup>[1350](h1350.md)</sup>
them from death:<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[4194](h4194.md)</sup> O
death,<sup>[4194](h4194)</sup> I will be<sup>[165](h0165.md)</sup> thy
plagues;<sup>[1698](h1698)</sup> O grave,<sup>[7585](h7585.md)</sup> I will
be<sup>[165](h0165)</sup> thy destruction:<sup>[6987](h6987.md)</sup>
repentance<sup>[5164](h5164)</sup> shall be hid<sup>[5641](h5641.md)</sup>
from mine eyes.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[5869](h5869.md)</sup>

15  Though<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> he<sup>[1931](h1931.md)</sup> be
fruitful<sup>[6500](h6500)</sup> among<sup>[996](h0996.md)</sup> his
brethren,<sup>[251](h0251)</sup> an east wind<sup>[6921](h6921.md)</sup>
shall come,<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> the wind<sup>[7307](h7307.md)</sup> of
the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> shall come up<sup>[5927](h5927.md)</sup>
from the wilderness,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[4057](h4057.md)</sup>
and his spring<sup>[4726](h4726.md)</sup> shall become
dry,<sup>[954](h0954)</sup> and his fountain<sup>[4599](h4599.md)</sup>
shall be dried up:<sup>[2717](h2717)</sup> he<sup>[1931](h1931.md)</sup>
shall spoil<sup>[8154](h8154)</sup> the treasure<sup>[214](h0214.md)</sup>
of all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> pleasant<sup>[2532](h2532.md)</sup>
vessels.<sup>[3627](h3627.md)</sup>

16  Samaria<sup>[8111](h8111.md)</sup> shall become
desolate;<sup>[816](h0816)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup> she hath
rebelled<sup>[4784](h4784)</sup> against her God:<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup>
they shall fall<sup>[5307](h5307.md)</sup> by the
sword:<sup>[2719](h2719)</sup> their infants<sup>[5768](h5768.md)</sup>
shall be dashed in pieces,<sup>[7376](h7376.md)</sup> and their women with
child<sup>[2030](h2030.md)</sup> shall be ripped
up.<sup>[1234](h1234.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/hos013.md)

1 κατὰ τὸν λόγον Εφραιμ δικαιώματα αὐτὸς ἔλαβεν ἐν τῷ Ισραηλ καὶ ἔθετο
αὐτὰ τῇ Βααλ καὶ ἀπέθανεν

2 καὶ προσέθετο τοῦ ἁμαρτάνειν ἔτι καὶ ἐποίησαν ἑαυτοῖς χώνευμα ἐκ τοῦ
ἀργυρίου αὐτῶν κατ᾽ εἰκόνα εἰδώλων ἔργα τεκτόνων συντετελεσμένα αὐτοῖς
αὐτοὶ λέγουσιν θύσατε ἀνθρώπους μόσχοι γὰρ ἐκλελοίπασιν

3 διὰ τοῦτο ἔσονται ὡς νεφέλη πρωινὴ καὶ ὡς δρόσος ὀρθρινὴ πορευομένη
ὥσπερ χνοῦς ἀποφυσώμενος ἀφ᾽ ἅλωνος καὶ ὡς ἀτμὶς ἀπὸ ἀκρίδων

4 ἐγὼ δὲ κύριος ὁ θεός σου στερεῶν οὐρανὸν καὶ κτίζων γῆν οὗ αἱ χεῖρες
ἔκτισαν πᾶσαν τὴν στρατιὰν τοῦ οὐρανοῦ καὶ οὐ παρέδειξά σοι αὐτὰ τοῦ
πορεύεσθαι ὀπίσω αὐτῶν καὶ ἐγὼ ἀνήγαγόν σε ἐκ γῆς Αἰγύπτου καὶ θεὸν πλὴν
ἐμοῦ οὐ γνώσῃ καὶ σῴζων οὐκ ἔστιν πάρεξ ἐμοῦ

5 ἐγὼ ἐποίμαινόν σε ἐν τῇ ἐρήμῳ ἐν γῇ ἀοικήτῳ

6 κατὰ τὰς νομὰς αὐτῶν καὶ ἐνεπλήσθησαν εἰς πλησμονήν καὶ ὑψώθησαν αἱ
καρδίαι αὐτῶν ἕνεκα τούτου ἐπελάθοντό μου

7 καὶ ἔσομαι αὐτοῖς ὡς πανθὴρ καὶ ὡς πάρδαλις κατὰ τὴν ὁδὸν Ἀσσυρίων

8 ἀπαντήσομαι αὐτοῖς ὡς ἄρκος ἀπορουμένη καὶ διαρρήξω συγκλεισμὸν
καρδίας αὐτῶν καὶ καταφάγονται αὐτοὺς ἐκεῖ σκύμνοι δρυμοῦ θηρία ἀγροῦ
διασπάσει αὐτούς

9 τῇ διαφθορᾷ σου Ισραηλ τίς βοηθήσει

10 ποῦ ὁ βασιλεύς σου οὗτος καὶ διασωσάτω σε ἐν πάσαις ταῖς πόλεσίν σου
κρινάτω σε ὃν εἶπας δός μοι βασιλέα καὶ ἄρχοντα

11 καὶ ἔδωκά σοι βασιλέα ἐν ὀργῇ μου καὶ ἔσχον ἐν τῷ θυμῷ μου

12 συστροφὴν ἀδικίας Εφραιμ ἐγκεκρυμμένη ἡ ἁμαρτία αὐτοῦ

13 ὠδῖνες ὡς τικτούσης ἥξουσιν αὐτῷ οὗτος ὁ υἱός σου οὐ φρόνιμος διότι
οὐ μὴ ὑποστῇ ἐν συντριβῇ τέκνων

14 ἐκ χειρὸς ᾅδου ῥύσομαι αὐτοὺς καὶ ἐκ θανάτου λυτρώσομαι αὐτούς ποῦ ἡ
δίκη σου θάνατε ποῦ τὸ κέντρον σου ᾅδη παράκλησις κέκρυπται ἀπὸ ὀφθαλμῶν
μου

15 διότι οὗτος ἀνὰ μέσον ἀδελφῶν διαστελεῖ ἐπάξει ἄνεμον καύσωνα κύριος
ἐκ τῆς ἐρήμου ἐπ᾽ αὐτόν καὶ ἀναξηρανεῖ τὰς φλέβας αὐτοῦ ἐξερημώσει τὰς
πηγὰς αὐτοῦ αὐτὸς καταξηρανεῖ τὴν γῆν αὐτοῦ καὶ πάντα τὰ σκεύη τὰ
ἐπιθυμητὰ αὐτοῦ

[Vul](../vul/hos013.md)

1 \[Loquente Ephraim, horror invasit Israël;  
et deliquit in Baal, et mortuus est.  

2 Et nunc addiderunt ad peccandum;  
feceruntque sibi conflatile de argento suo  
quasi similitudinem idolorum:  
factura artificum totum est:  
his ipsi dicunt: Immolate homines, vitulos adorantes.  

3 Idcirco erunt quasi nubes matutina,  
et sicut ros matutinus præteriens;  
sicut pulvis turbine raptus ex area,  
et sicut fumus de fumario.  

4 Ego autem Dominus Deus tuus, ex terra Ægypti;  
et Deum absque me nescies,  
et salvator non est præter me.  

5 Ego cognovi te in deserto,  
in terra solitudinis.  

6 Juxta pascua sua adimpleti sunt et saturati sunt;  
et levaverunt cor suum, et obliti sunt mei.  

7 Et ego ero eis quasi leæna,  
sicut pardus in via Assyriorum.  

8 Occurram eis quasi ursa raptis catulis,  
et dirumpam interiora jecoris eorum,  
et consumam eos ibi quasi leo:  
bestia agri scindet eos.  

9 Perditio tua, Israël: tantummodo in me auxilium tuum.  

10 Ubi est rex tuus?  
maxime nunc salvet te in omnibus urbibus tuis;  
et judices tui, de quibus dixisti:  
Da mihi regem et principes.  

11 Dabo tibi regem in furore meo,  
et auferam in indignatione mea.  

12 Colligata est iniquitas Ephraim;  
absconditum peccatum ejus.  

13 Dolores parturientis venient ei:  
ipse filius non sapiens:  
nunc enim non stabit in contritione filiorum.  

14 De manu mortis liberabo eos;  
de morte redimam eos.  
Ero mors tua, o mors!  
morsus tuus ero, inferne!  
consolatio abscondita est ab oculis meis.  

15 Quia ipse inter fratres dividet:  
adducet urentem ventum Dominus de deserto ascendentem,  
et siccabit venas ejus,  
et desolabit fontem ejus:  
et ipse diripiet thesaurum omnis vasis desiderabilis.\]
