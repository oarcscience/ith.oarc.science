### Proverbs 29

[KJV](../kjv/pro029)

1  He,<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> that being often
reproved<sup>[8433](h8433)</sup> hardeneth<sup>[7185](h7185)</sup> his
neck,<sup>[6203](h6203)</sup> shall suddenly<sup>[6621](h6621)</sup> be
destroyed,<sup>[7665](h7665)</sup> and that
without<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> remedy.<sup>[4832](h4832)</sup>

2  When the righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> are in
authority,<sup>[7235](h7235)</sup> the people<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup>
rejoice:<sup>[8055](h8055)</sup> but when the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> beareth rule,<sup>[4910](h4910)</sup> the
people<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup> mourn.<sup>[584](h0584)</sup>

3  Whoso<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> loveth<sup>[157](h0157)</sup>
wisdom<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup> rejoiceth<sup>[8055](h8055)</sup> his
father:<sup>[1](h0001)</sup> but he that keepeth
company<sup>[7462](h7462)</sup> with harlots<sup>[2181](h2181)</sup>
spendeth<sup>[6](h0006)</sup> his substance.<sup>[1952](h1952)</sup>

4  The king<sup>[4428](h4428)</sup> by judgment<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup>
establisheth<sup>[5975](h5975)</sup> the land:<sup>[776](h0776)</sup>
but he<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> that receiveth
gifts<sup>[8641](h8641)</sup> overthroweth<sup>[2040](h2040)</sup> it.

5  A man<sup>[1397](h1397)</sup> that flattereth<sup>[2505](h2505)</sup>
<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> his neighbor<sup>[7453](h7453)</sup>
spreadeth<sup>[6566](h6566)</sup> a net<sup>[7568](h7568)</sup>
for<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> his feet.<sup>[6471](h6471)</sup>

6  In the transgression<sup>[6588](h6588)</sup> of an
evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> there is a
snare:<sup>[4170](h4170)</sup> but the righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup>
doth sing<sup>[7442](h7442)</sup> and rejoice.<sup>[8055](h8055)</sup>

7  The righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup>
considereth<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> the cause<sup>[1779](h1779)</sup> of
the poor:<sup>[1800](h1800)</sup> but the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup>
regardeth<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> to
know<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup> it.

8  Scornful<sup>[3944](h3944)</sup> men<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> bring a
city into a snare:<sup>[6315](h6315)</sup> <sup>[7151](h7151)</sup> but
wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> men turn away<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup>
wrath.<sup>[639](h0639)</sup>

9  If a wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup>
contendeth<sup>[8199](h8199)</sup> with<sup>[854](h0854)</sup> a
foolish<sup>[191](h0191)</sup> man,<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> whether he
rage<sup>[7264](h7264)</sup> or laugh,<sup>[7832](h7832)</sup> there is
no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> rest.<sup>[5183](h5183)</sup>

10  The bloodthirsty<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> <sup>[1818](h1818)</sup>
hate<sup>[8130](h8130)</sup> the upright:<sup>[8535](h8535)</sup> but
the just<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup> seek<sup>[1245](h1245)</sup> his
soul.<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup>

11  A fool<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> uttereth<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup>
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> his mind:<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> but a
wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> man keepeth<sup>[7623](h7623)</sup> it in
till afterwards.<sup>[268](h0268)</sup>

12  If a ruler<sup>[4910](h4910)</sup> hearken<sup>[7181](h7181)</sup>
to<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> lies,<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup>
<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> his
servants<sup>[8334](h8334)</sup> are wicked.<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup>

13  The poor<sup>[7326](h7326)</sup> and the
deceitful<sup>[8501](h8501)</sup> man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> meet
together:<sup>[6298](h6298)</sup> the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>
lighteneth<sup>[215](h0215)</sup> both<sup>[8147](h8147)</sup> their
eyes.<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup>

14  The king<sup>[4428](h4428)</sup> that
faithfully<sup>[571](h0571)</sup> judgeth<sup>[8199](h8199)</sup> the
poor,<sup>[1800](h1800)</sup> his throne<sup>[3678](h3678)</sup> shall
be established<sup>[3559](h3559)</sup> forever.<sup>[5703](h5703)</sup>

15  The rod<sup>[7626](h7626)</sup> and reproof<sup>[8433](h8433)</sup>
give<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> wisdom:<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup> but a
child<sup>[5288](h5288)</sup> left<sup>[7971](h7971)</sup> to himself
bringeth his mother<sup>[517](h0517)</sup> to
shame.<sup>[954](h0954)</sup>

16  When the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> are
multiplied,<sup>[7235](h7235)</sup>
transgression<sup>[6588](h6588)</sup>
increaseth:<sup>[7235](h7235)</sup> but the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> shall see<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup>
their fall.<sup>[4658](h4658)</sup>

17  Correct<sup>[3256](h3256)</sup> thy son,<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> and
he shall give thee rest;<sup>[5117](h5117)</sup> yea, he shall
give<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> delight<sup>[4574](h4574)</sup> unto thy
soul.<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup>

18  Where there is no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup>
vision,<sup>[2377](h2377)</sup> the people<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup>
perish:<sup>[6544](h6544)</sup> but he that
keepeth<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> the law,<sup>[8451](h8451)</sup>
happy<sup>[835](h0835)</sup> is he.

19  A servant<sup>[5650](h5650)</sup> will not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
be corrected<sup>[3256](h3256)</sup> by words:<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup>
for though<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> he understand<sup>[995](h0995)</sup>
he will not<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> answer.<sup>[4617](h4617)</sup>

20  Seest<sup>[2372](h2372)</sup> thou a man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> that
is hasty<sup>[213](h0213)</sup> in his words?<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup>
there is more hope<sup>[8615](h8615)</sup> of a
fool<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> than of<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> him.

21  He that delicately bringeth up<sup>[6445](h6445)</sup> his
servant<sup>[5650](h5650)</sup> from a child<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[5290](h5290)</sup> shall have him become<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup>
his son<sup>[4497](h4497)</sup> at the length.<sup>[319](h0319)</sup>

22  An angry<sup>[639](h0639)</sup> man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> stirreth
up<sup>[1624](h1624)</sup> strife,<sup>[4066](h4066)</sup> and a
furious<sup>[2534](h2534)</sup> man<sup>[1167](h1167)</sup>
aboundeth<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup> in
transgression.<sup>[6588](h6588)</sup>

23  A man's<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> pride<sup>[1346](h1346)</sup> shall
bring him low:<sup>[8213](h8213)</sup> but honor<sup>[3519](h3519)</sup>
shall uphold<sup>[8551](h8551)</sup> the humble<sup>[8217](h8217)</sup>
in spirit.<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup>

24  Whoso is partner<sup>[2505](h2505)</sup>
with<sup>[5973](h5973)</sup> a thief<sup>[1590](h1590)</sup>
hateth<sup>[8130](h8130)</sup> his own soul:<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> he
heareth<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> cursing,<sup>[423](h0423)</sup> and
betrayeth<sup>[5046](h5046)</sup> it not.<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>

25  The fear<sup>[2731](h2731)</sup> of man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup>
bringeth<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> a snare:<sup>[4170](h4170)</sup> but
whoso putteth his trust<sup>[982](h0982)</sup> in the
LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> shall be safe.<sup>[7682](h7682)</sup>

26  Many<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup> seek<sup>[1245](h1245)</sup> the
ruler's<sup>[4910](h4910)</sup> favor;<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> but every
man's<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> judgment<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup> cometh
from the LORD.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>

27  An unjust<sup>[5766](h5766)</sup> man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> is an
abomination<sup>[8441](h8441)</sup> to the just:<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup>
and he that is upright<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup> in the
way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> is abomination<sup>[8441](h8441)</sup> to
the wicked.<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup>

[Vul](../vul/pro029)

1 \[Viro qui corripientem dura cervice contemnit,  
repentinus ei superveniet interitus,  
et eum sanitas non sequetur.  

2 In multiplicatione justorum lætabitur vulgus;  
cum impii sumpserint principatum, gemet populus.  

3 Vir qui amat sapientiam lætificat patrem suum;  
qui autem nutrit scorta perdet substantiam.  

4 Rex justus erigit terram;  
vir avarus destruet eam.  

5 Homo qui blandis fictisque sermonibus loquitur amico suo  
rete expandit gressibus ejus.  

6 Peccantem virum iniquum involvet laqueus,  
et justus laudabit atque gaudebit.  

7 Novit justus causam pauperum;  
impius ignorat scientiam.  

8 Homines pestilentes dissipant civitatem;  
sapientes vero avertunt furorem.  

9 Vir sapiens si cum stulto contenderit,  
sive irascatur, sive rideat, non inveniet requiem.  

10 Viri sanguinum oderunt simplicem;  
justi autem quærunt animam ejus.  

11 Totum spiritum suum profert stultus;  
sapiens differt, et reservat in posterum.  

12 Princeps qui libenter audit verba mendacii,  
omnes ministros habet impios.  

13 Pauper et creditor obviaverunt sibi:  
utriusque illuminator est Dominus.  

14 Rex qui judicat in veritate pauperes,  
thronus ejus in æternum firmabitur.\]  

15 \[Virga atque correptio tribuit sapientiam;  
puer autem qui dimittitur voluntati suæ confundit matrem suam.  

16 In multiplicatione impiorum multiplicabuntur scelera,  
et justi ruinas eorum videbunt.  

17 Erudi filium tuum, et refrigerabit te,  
et dabit delicias animæ tuæ.  

18 Cum prophetia defecerit, dissipabitur populus;  
qui vero custodit legem beatus est.  

19 Servus verbis non potest erudiri,  
quia quod dicis intelligit, et respondere contemnit.  

20 Vidisti hominem velocem ad loquendum?  
stultitia magis speranda est quam illius correptio.  

21 Qui delicate a pueritia nutrit servum suum  
postea sentiet eum contumacem.\]  

22 \[Vir iracundus provocat rixas,  
et qui ad indignandum facilis est erit ad peccandum proclivior.  

23 Superbum sequitur humilitas,  
et humilem spiritu suscipiet gloria.  

24 Qui cum fure participat odit animam suam;  
adjurantem audit, et non indicat.  

25 Qui timet hominem cito corruet;  
qui sperat in Domino sublevabitur.  

26 Multi requirunt faciem principis,  
et judicium a Domino egreditur singulorum.  

27 Abominantur justi virum impium,  
et abominantur impii eos qui in recta sunt via.  
Verbum custodiens filius  
extra perditionem erit.\]
