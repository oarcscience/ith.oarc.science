H3117  
יום  
יוֹם ‎ yôm  
*yome*  
From an unused root meaning to *be* *hot*; a *day* (as the *warm*
hours), whether literally (from sunrise to sunset, or from one sunset to
the next), or figuratively (a space of time defined by an associated
term), (often used adverbially): - age, + always, + chronicles,
continually (-ance), daily, (\[birth-\], each, to) day, (now a, two)
days (agone), + elder, X end, + evening, + (for) ever (-lasting, -more),
X full, life, as (so) long as (. . . live), (even) now, + old, +
outlived, + perpetually, presently, + remaineth, X required, season, X
since, space, then, (process of) time, + as at other times, + in
trouble, weather, (as) when, (a, the, within a) while (that), X whole (+
age), (full) year (-ly), + younger.
