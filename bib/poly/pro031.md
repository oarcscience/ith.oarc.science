### Proverbs 31

[KJV](../kjv/pro031.md)

1  The words<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup> of king<sup>[4428](h4428.md)</sup>
Lemuel,<sup>[3927](h3927)</sup> the prophecy<sup>[4853](h4853.md)</sup>
that<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> his mother<sup>[517](h0517.md)</sup>
taught<sup>[3256](h3256.md)</sup> him.

2  What,<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> my son?<sup>[1248](h1248.md)</sup> and
what,<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> the son<sup>[1248](h1248.md)</sup> of my
womb?<sup>[990](h0990)</sup> and what,<sup>[4100](h4100.md)</sup> the
son<sup>[1248](h1248)</sup> of my vows?<sup>[5088](h5088.md)</sup>

3  Give<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> not<sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup> thy
strength<sup>[2428](h2428)</sup> unto women,<sup>[802](h0802.md)</sup> nor
thy ways<sup>[1870](h1870.md)</sup> to that which
destroyeth<sup>[4229](h4229)</sup> kings.<sup>[4428](h4428.md)</sup>

4  It is not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> for kings,<sup>[4428](h4428.md)</sup> O
Lemuel,<sup>[3927](h3927)</sup> it is not<sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup> for
kings<sup>[4428](h4428)</sup> to drink<sup>[8354](h8354.md)</sup>
wine;<sup>[3196](h3196)</sup> nor<sup>[176](h0176.md)</sup> for
princes<sup>[7336](h7336)</sup> strong drink:<sup>[7941](h7941.md)</sup>

5  Lest<sup>[6435](h6435)</sup> they drink,<sup>[8354](h8354.md)</sup> and
forget<sup>[7911](h7911)</sup> the law,<sup>[2710](h2710.md)</sup> and
pervert<sup>[8138](h8138)</sup> the judgment<sup>[1779](h1779.md)</sup> of

<sup>[6040](h6040.md)</sup>

6  Give<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> strong drink<sup>[7941](h7941.md)</sup>
unto him that is ready to perish,<sup>[6](h0006.md)</sup> and
wine<sup>[3196](h3196.md)</sup> unto those that be of
heavy<sup>[4751](h4751)</sup> hearts.<sup>[5315](h5315.md)</sup>

7  Let him drink,<sup>[8354](h8354.md)</sup> and
forget<sup>[7911](h7911)</sup> his poverty,<sup>[7389](h7389.md)</sup> and
remember<sup>[2142](h2142)</sup> his misery<sup>[5999](h5999.md)</sup>
no<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> more.<sup>[5750](h5750.md)</sup>

8  Open<sup>[6605](h6605)</sup> thy mouth<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup> for
the dumb<sup>[483](h0483)</sup> in<sup>[413](h0413.md)</sup> the
cause<sup>[1779](h1779)</sup> of all<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup> such as are
appointed<sup>[1121](h1121.md)</sup> to
destruction.<sup>[2475](h2475.md)</sup>

9  Open<sup>[6605](h6605)</sup> thy mouth,<sup>[6310](h6310.md)</sup>
judge<sup>[8199](h8199)</sup> righteously,<sup>[6664](h6664.md)</sup> and
plead the cause<sup>[1777](h1777.md)</sup> of the
poor<sup>[6041](h6041)</sup> and needy.<sup>[34](h0034.md)</sup>

10  Who<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup> can find<sup>[4672](h4672.md)</sup> a
virtuous<sup>[2428](h2428)</sup> woman?<sup>[802](h0802.md)</sup> for her
price<sup>[4377](h4377)</sup> is far<sup>[7350](h7350.md)</sup> above
rubies.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6443](h6443.md)</sup>

11  The heart<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup> of her
husband<sup>[1167](h1167)</sup> doth safely trust<sup>[982](h0982.md)</sup>
in her, so that he shall have no<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>

12  She will do<sup>[1580](h1580)</sup> him good<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup>
and not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> evil<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup>
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> the days<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup> of her
life.<sup>[2416](h2416.md)</sup>

13  She seeketh<sup>[1875](h1875)</sup> wool,<sup>[6785](h6785.md)</sup>
and flax,<sup>[6593](h6593)</sup> and worketh<sup>[6213](h6213.md)</sup>
willingly<sup>[2656](h2656.md)</sup> with her
hands.<sup>[3709](h3709.md)</sup>

14  She is<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup> like the
merchants'<sup>[5503](h5503)</sup> ships;<sup>[591](h0591.md)</sup> she
bringeth<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> her food<sup>[3899](h3899.md)</sup> from
afar.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[4801](h4801.md)</sup>

15  She riseth<sup>[6965](h6965.md)</sup> also while it is
yet<sup>[5750](h5750)</sup> night,<sup>[3915](h3915.md)</sup> and
giveth<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> meat<sup>[2964](h2964.md)</sup> to her
household,<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> and a portion<sup>[2706](h2706.md)</sup>
to her maidens.<sup>[5291](h5291.md)</sup>

16  She considereth<sup>[2161](h2161.md)</sup> a
field,<sup>[7704](h7704)</sup> and buyeth<sup>[3947](h3947.md)</sup> it:
with the fruit<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6529](h6529.md)</sup> of her
hands<sup>[3709](h3709)</sup> she planteth<sup>[5193](h5193.md)</sup> a
vineyard.<sup>[3754](h3754.md)</sup>

17  She girdeth<sup>[2296](h2296.md)</sup> her
loins<sup>[4975](h4975)</sup> with strength,<sup>[5797](h5797.md)</sup> and
strengtheneth<sup>[553](h0553)</sup> her arms.<sup>[2220](h2220.md)</sup>

18  She perceiveth<sup>[2938](h2938)</sup> that<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup>
her merchandise<sup>[5504](h5504)</sup> is good:<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup>
her candle<sup>[5216](h5216)</sup> goeth not out<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
<sup>[3518](h3518)</sup> by night.<sup>[3915](h3915.md)</sup>

19  She layeth<sup>[7971](h7971)</sup> her hands<sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup>
to the spindle,<sup>[3601](h3601.md)</sup> and her
hands<sup>[3709](h3709)</sup> hold<sup>[8551](h8551.md)</sup> the
distaff.<sup>[6418](h6418.md)</sup>

20  She stretcheth out<sup>[6566](h6566.md)</sup> her
hand<sup>[3709](h3709)</sup> to the poor;<sup>[6041](h6041.md)</sup> yea,
she reacheth forth<sup>[7971](h7971.md)</sup> her
hands<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> to the needy.<sup>[34](h0034.md)</sup>

21  She is not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> afraid<sup>[3372](h3372.md)</sup> of
the snow<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7950](h7950.md)</sup> for her
household:<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup>
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> her household<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup> are
clothed<sup>[3847](h3847)</sup> with scarlet.<sup>[8144](h8144.md)</sup>

22  She maketh<sup>[6213](h6213.md)</sup> herself coverings of
tapestry;<sup>[4765](h4765)</sup> her clothing<sup>[3830](h3830.md)</sup>
is silk<sup>[8336](h8336)</sup> and purple.<sup>[713](h0713.md)</sup>

23  Her husband<sup>[1167](h1167)</sup> is known<sup>[3045](h3045.md)</sup>
in the gates,<sup>[8179](h8179.md)</sup> when he
sitteth<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup> among<sup>[5973](h5973.md)</sup> the
elders<sup>[2205](h2205)</sup> of the land.<sup>[776](h0776.md)</sup>

24  She maketh<sup>[6213](h6213.md)</sup> fine
linen,<sup>[5466](h5466)</sup> and selleth<sup>[4376](h4376.md)</sup> it;
and delivereth<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> girdles<sup>[2289](h2289.md)</sup>
unto the merchant.<sup>[3669](h3669.md)</sup>

25  Strength<sup>[5797](h5797)</sup> and honor<sup>[1926](h1926.md)</sup>
are her clothing;<sup>[3830](h3830.md)</sup> and she shall
rejoice<sup>[7832](h7832)</sup> in time<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup> to
come.<sup>[314](h0314.md)</sup>

26  She openeth<sup>[6605](h6605.md)</sup> her
mouth<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> with wisdom;<sup>[2451](h2451.md)</sup> and
in<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> her tongue<sup>[3956](h3956.md)</sup> is the
law<sup>[8451](h8451)</sup> of kindness.<sup>[2617](h2617.md)</sup>

27  She looketh well<sup>[6822](h6822.md)</sup> to the
ways<sup>[1979](h1979)</sup> of her household,<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup>
and eateth<sup>[398](h0398)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> the
bread<sup>[3899](h3899)</sup> of idleness.<sup>[6104](h6104.md)</sup>

28  Her children<sup>[1121](h1121.md)</sup> arise
up,<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> and call her blessed;<sup>[833](h0833.md)</sup>
her husband<sup>[1167](h1167.md)</sup> also, and he
praiseth<sup>[1984](h1984.md)</sup> her.

29  Many<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup> daughters<sup>[1323](h1323.md)</sup> have
done<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> virtuously,<sup>[2428](h2428.md)</sup> but
thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> excellest<sup>[5927](h5927.md)</sup>
<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> them all.<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup>

30  Favor<sup>[2580](h2580)</sup> is deceitful,<sup>[8267](h8267.md)</sup>
and beauty<sup>[3308](h3308)</sup> is vain:<sup>[1892](h1892.md)</sup> but
a woman<sup>[802](h0802)</sup> that feareth<sup>[3373](h3373.md)</sup> the
LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> she<sup>[1931](h1931.md)</sup> shall be
praised.<sup>[1984](h1984.md)</sup>

31  Give<sup>[5414](h5414.md)</sup> her of the
fruit<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6529](h6529.md)</sup> of her
hands;<sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup> and let her own
works<sup>[4639](h4639)</sup> praise<sup>[1984](h1984.md)</sup> her in the
gates.<sup>[8179](h8179.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/pro031.md)

1 οἱ ἐμοὶ λόγοι εἴρηνται ὑπὸ θεοῦ βασιλέως χρηματισμός ὃν ἐπαίδευσεν ἡ
μήτηρ αὐτοῦ

2 τί τέκνον τηρήσεις τί ῥήσεις θεοῦ πρωτογενές σοὶ λέγω υἱέ τί τέκνον
ἐμῆς κοιλίας τί τέκνον ἐμῶν εὐχῶν

3 μὴ δῷς γυναιξὶ σὸν πλοῦτον καὶ τὸν σὸν νοῦν καὶ βίον εἰς ὑστεροβουλίαν

4 μετὰ βουλῆς πάντα ποίει μετὰ βουλῆς οἰνοπότει οἱ δυνάσται θυμώδεις
εἰσίν οἶνον δὲ μὴ πινέτωσαν

5 ἵνα μὴ πιόντες ἐπιλάθωνται τῆς σοφίας καὶ ὀρθὰ κρῖναι οὐ μὴ δύνωνται
τοὺς ἀσθενεῖς

6 δίδοτε μέθην τοῖς ἐν λύπαις καὶ οἶνον πίνειν τοῖς ἐν ὀδύναις

7 ἵνα ἐπιλάθωνται τῆς πενίας καὶ τῶν πόνων μὴ μνησθῶσιν ἔτι

8 ἄνοιγε σὸν στόμα λόγῳ θεοῦ καὶ κρῖνε πάντας ὑγιῶς

9 ἄνοιγε σὸν στόμα καὶ κρῖνε δικαίως διάκρινε δὲ πένητα καὶ ἀσθενῆ

10 γυναῖκα ἀνδρείαν τίς εὑρήσει τιμιωτέρα δέ ἐστιν λίθων πολυτελῶν ἡ
τοιαύτη

11 θαρσεῖ ἐπ᾽ αὐτῇ ἡ καρδία τοῦ ἀνδρὸς αὐτῆς ἡ τοιαύτη καλῶν σκύλων οὐκ
ἀπορήσει

12 ἐνεργεῖ γὰρ τῷ ἀνδρὶ ἀγαθὰ πάντα τὸν βίον

13 μηρυομένη ἔρια καὶ λίνον ἐποίησεν εὔχρηστον ταῖς χερσὶν αὐτῆς

14 ἐγένετο ὡσεὶ ναῦς ἐμπορευομένη μακρόθεν συνάγει δὲ αὕτη τὸν βίον

15 καὶ ἀνίσταται ἐκ νυκτῶν καὶ ἔδωκεν βρώματα τῷ οἴκῳ καὶ ἔργα ταῖς
θεραπαίναις

16 θεωρήσασα γεώργιον ἐπρίατο ἀπὸ δὲ καρπῶν χειρῶν αὐτῆς κατεφύτευσεν
κτῆμα

17 ἀναζωσαμένη ἰσχυρῶς τὴν ὀσφὺν αὐτῆς ἤρεισεν τοὺς βραχίονας αὐτῆς εἰς
ἔργον

18 ἐγεύσατο ὅτι καλόν ἐστιν τὸ ἐργάζεσθαι καὶ οὐκ ἀποσβέννυται ὅλην τὴν
νύκτα ὁ λύχνος αὐτῆς

19 τοὺς πήχεις αὐτῆς ἐκτείνει ἐπὶ τὰ συμφέροντα τὰς δὲ χεῖρας αὐτῆς
ἐρείδει εἰς ἄτρακτον

20 χεῖρας δὲ αὐτῆς διήνοιξεν πένητι καρπὸν δὲ ἐξέτεινεν πτωχῷ

21 οὐ φροντίζει τῶν ἐν οἴκῳ ὁ ἀνὴρ αὐτῆς ὅταν που χρονίζῃ πάντες γὰρ οἱ
παρ᾽ αὐτῆς ἐνδιδύσκονται

22 δισσὰς χλαίνας ἐποίησεν τῷ ἀνδρὶ αὐτῆς ἐκ δὲ βύσσου καὶ πορφύρας
ἑαυτῇ ἐνδύματα

23 περίβλεπτος δὲ γίνεται ἐν πύλαις ὁ ἀνὴρ αὐτῆς ἡνίκα ἂν καθίσῃ ἐν
συνεδρίῳ μετὰ τῶν γερόντων κατοίκων τῆς γῆς

24 σινδόνας ἐποίησεν καὶ ἀπέδοτο περιζώματα δὲ τοῖς Χαναναίοις

25 στόμα αὐτῆς διήνοιξεν προσεχόντως καὶ ἐννόμως καὶ τάξιν ἐστείλατο τῇ
γλώσσῃ αὐτῆς

26 ἰσχὺν καὶ εὐπρέπειαν ἐνεδύσατο καὶ εὐφράνθη ἐν ἡμέραις ἐσχάταις

27 στεγναὶ διατριβαὶ οἴκων αὐτῆς σῖτα δὲ ὀκνηρὰ οὐκ ἔφαγεν

28 τὸ στόμα δὲ ἀνοίγει σοφῶς καὶ νομοθέσμως ἡ δὲ ἐλεημοσύνη αὐτῆς
ἀνέστησεν τὰ τέκνα αὐτῆς καὶ ἐπλούτησαν καὶ ὁ ἀνὴρ αὐτῆς ᾔνεσεν αὐτήν

29 πολλαὶ θυγατέρες ἐκτήσαντο πλοῦτον πολλαὶ ἐποίησαν δυνατά σὺ δὲ
ὑπέρκεισαι καὶ ὑπερῆρας πάσας

30 ψευδεῖς ἀρέσκειαι καὶ μάταιον κάλλος γυναικός γυνὴ γὰρ συνετὴ
εὐλογεῖται φόβον δὲ κυρίου αὕτη αἰνείτω

31 δότε αὐτῇ ἀπὸ καρπῶν χειρῶν αὐτῆς καὶ αἰνείσθω ἐν πύλαις ὁ ἀνὴρ αὐτῆς

מַעֲשֶֽׂיהָ׃

[Vul](../vul/pro031.md)

1 Verba Lamuelis regis. Visio qua erudivit eum mater sua.

2 \[Quid, dilecte mi? quid, dilecte uteri mei?  
quid, dilecte votorum meorum?  

3 Ne dederis mulieribus substantiam tuam,  
et divitias tuas ad delendos reges.  

4 Noli regibus, o Lamuel, noli regibus dare vinum,  
quia nullum secretum est ubi regnat ebrietas;  

5 et ne forte bibant, et obliviscantur judiciorum,  
et mutent causam filiorum pauperis.  

6 Date siceram mœrentibus,  
et vinum his qui amaro sunt animo.  

7 Bibant, et obliviscantur egestatis suæ,  
et doloris sui non recordentur amplius.  

8 Aperi os tuum muto,  
et causis omnium filiorum qui pertranseunt.  

9 Aperi os tuum, decerne quod justum est,  
et judica inopem et pauperem.\]  

10 \[Mulierem fortem quis inveniet?  
procul et de ultimis finibus pretium ejus.  

11 Confidit in ea cor viri sui,  
et spoliis non indigebit.  

12 Reddet ei bonum, et non malum,  
omnibus diebus vitæ suæ.  

13 Quæsivit lanam et linum,  
et operata est consilia manuum suarum.  

14 Facta est quasi navis institoris,  
de longe portans panem suum.  

15 Et de nocte surrexit,  
deditque prædam domesticis suis,  
et cibaria ancillis suis.  

16 Consideravit agrum, et emit eum;  
de fructu manuum suarum plantavit vineam.  

17 Accinxit fortitudine lumbos suos,  
et roboravit brachium suum.  

18 Gustavit, et vidit quia bona est negotiatio ejus;  
non extinguetur in nocte lucerna ejus.  

19 Manum suam misit ad fortia,  
et digiti ejus apprehenderunt fusum.  

20 Manum suam aperuit inopi,  
et palmas suas extendit ad pauperem.  

21 Non timebit domui suæ a frigoribus nivis;  
omnes enim domestici ejus vestiti sunt duplicibus.  

22 Stragulatam vestem fecit sibi;  
byssus et purpura indumentum ejus.  

23 Nobilis in portis vir ejus,  
quando sederit cum senatoribus terræ.  

24 Sindonem fecit, et vendidit,  
et cingulum tradidit Chananæo.  

25 Fortitudo et decor indumentum ejus,  
et ridebit in die novissimo.  

26 Os suum aperuit sapientiæ,  
et lex clementiæ in lingua ejus.  

27 Consideravit semitas domus suæ,  
et panem otiosa non comedit.  

28 Surrexerunt filii ejus, et beatissimam prædicaverunt;  
vir ejus, et laudavit eam.  

29 Multæ filiæ congregaverunt divitias;  
tu supergressa es universas.  

30 Fallax gratia, et vana est pulchritudo:  
mulier timens Dominum, ipsa laudabitur.  

31 Date ei de fructu manuum suarum,  
et laudent eam in portis opera ejus.\]
