### Proverbs 25

[KJV](../kjv/pro025.md)

1  These<sup>[428](h0428)</sup> are also<sup>[1571](h1571.md)</sup>
proverbs<sup>[4912](h4912)</sup> of Solomon,<sup>[8010](h8010.md)</sup>
which<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> the men<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> of
Hezekiah<sup>[2396](h2396)</sup> king<sup>[4428](h4428.md)</sup> of
Judah<sup>[3063](h3063)</sup> copied out.<sup>[6275](h6275.md)</sup>

2  It is the glory<sup>[3519](h3519)</sup> of God<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup>
to conceal<sup>[5641](h5641)</sup> a thing:<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup> but
the honor<sup>[3519](h3519)</sup> of kings<sup>[4428](h4428.md)</sup> is to
search out<sup>[2713](h2713)</sup> a matter.<sup>[1697](h1697.md)</sup>

3  The heaven<sup>[8064](h8064.md)</sup> for
height,<sup>[7312](h7312)</sup> and the earth<sup>[776](h0776.md)</sup> for
depth,<sup>[6011](h6011)</sup> and the heart<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup> of
kings<sup>[4428](h4428)</sup> is unsearchable.<sup>[369](h0369.md)</sup>
<sup>[2714](h2714.md)</sup>

4  Take away<sup>[1898](h1898)</sup> the dross<sup>[5509](h5509.md)</sup>
from the silver,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3701](h3701.md)</sup> and
there shall come forth<sup>[3318](h3318.md)</sup> a
vessel<sup>[3627](h3627)</sup> for the refiner.<sup>[6884](h6884.md)</sup>

5  Take away<sup>[1898](h1898)</sup> the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563.md)</sup>
from before<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> the king,<sup>[4428](h4428.md)</sup>
and his throne<sup>[3678](h3678.md)</sup> shall be
established<sup>[3559](h3559.md)</sup> in
righteousness.<sup>[6664](h6664.md)</sup>

6  Put not forth thyself<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> <sup>[1921](h1921.md)</sup>
in the presence<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> of the
king,<sup>[4428](h4428)</sup> and stand<sup>[5975](h5975.md)</sup>
not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> in the place<sup>[4725](h4725.md)</sup> of
great<sup>[1419](h1419.md)</sup> men:

7  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> better<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup> it is that
it be said<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup> unto thee, Come
up<sup>[5927](h5927)</sup> hither;<sup>[2008](h2008.md)</sup> than that
thou shouldest be put lower<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[8213](h8213)</sup> in the presence<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> of the
prince<sup>[5081](h5081)</sup> whom<sup>[834](h0834.md)</sup> thine
eyes<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> have seen.<sup>[7200](h7200.md)</sup>

8  Go not forth<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup> <sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup>
hastily<sup>[4118](h4118)</sup> to strive,<sup>[7378](h7378.md)</sup>
lest<sup>[6435](h6435)</sup> thou know not what<sup>[4100](h4100.md)</sup>
to do<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> in the end<sup>[319](h0319.md)</sup> thereof,
when thy neighbor<sup>[7453](h7453.md)</sup> hath put thee to
shame.<sup>[3637](h3637)</sup> <sup>[853](h0853.md)</sup>

9  Debate<sup>[7378](h7378)</sup> thy cause<sup>[7379](h7379.md)</sup>
with<sup>[854](h0854)</sup> thy neighbor<sup>[7453](h7453.md)</sup>
himself; and discover<sup>[1540](h1540)</sup> not<sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup>
a secret<sup>[5475](h5475)</sup> to another:<sup>[312](h0312.md)</sup>

10  Lest<sup>[6435](h6435)</sup> he that heareth<sup>[8085](h8085.md)</sup>
it put thee to shame,<sup>[2616](h2616.md)</sup> and thine
infamy<sup>[1681](h1681)</sup> turn not away.<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
<sup>[7725](h7725.md)</sup>

11  A word<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup> fitly<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
<sup>[655](h0655)</sup> spoken<sup>[1696](h1696.md)</sup> is like
apples<sup>[8598](h8598)</sup> of gold<sup>[2091](h2091.md)</sup> in
pictures<sup>[4906](h4906)</sup> of silver.<sup>[3701](h3701.md)</sup>

12  As an earring<sup>[5141](h5141.md)</sup> of
gold,<sup>[2091](h2091)</sup> and an ornament<sup>[2481](h2481.md)</sup> of
fine gold,<sup>[3800](h3800)</sup> so is a wise<sup>[2450](h2450.md)</sup>
reprover<sup>[3198](h3198)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> an
obedient<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> ear.<sup>[241](h0241.md)</sup>

13  As the cold<sup>[6793](h6793)</sup> of snow<sup>[7950](h7950.md)</sup>
in the time<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> of harvest,<sup>[7105](h7105.md)</sup>
so is a faithful<sup>[539](h0539.md)</sup>
messenger<sup>[6735](h6735.md)</sup> to them that
send<sup>[7971](h7971.md)</sup> him: for he
refresheth<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> the soul<sup>[5315](h5315.md)</sup> of
his masters.<sup>[113](h0113.md)</sup>

14  Whoso<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> boasteth
himself<sup>[1984](h1984)</sup> of a false<sup>[8267](h8267.md)</sup>
gift<sup>[4991](h4991)</sup> is like clouds<sup>[5387](h5387.md)</sup> and
wind<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> without<sup>[369](h0369.md)</sup>
rain.<sup>[1653](h1653.md)</sup>

15  By long<sup>[753](h0753)</sup> forbearing<sup>[639](h0639.md)</sup> is
a prince<sup>[7101](h7101)</sup> persuaded,<sup>[6601](h6601.md)</sup> and
a soft<sup>[7390](h7390)</sup> tongue<sup>[3956](h3956.md)</sup>
breaketh<sup>[7665](h7665)</sup> the bone.<sup>[1634](h1634.md)</sup>

16  Hast thou found<sup>[4672](h4672.md)</sup>
honey?<sup>[1706](h1706)</sup> eat<sup>[398](h0398.md)</sup> so much as is
sufficient<sup>[1767](h1767.md)</sup> for thee,
lest<sup>[6435](h6435)</sup> thou be filled<sup>[7646](h7646.md)</sup>
therewith, and vomit<sup>[6958](h6958.md)</sup> it.

17  Withdraw<sup>[3365](h3365)</sup> thy foot<sup>[7272](h7272.md)</sup>
from thy neighbor's<sup>[7453](h7453.md)</sup>
house;<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup>
lest<sup>[6435](h6435)</sup> he be weary<sup>[7646](h7646.md)</sup> of
thee, and so hate<sup>[8130](h8130.md)</sup> thee.

18  A man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> that beareth<sup>[6030](h6030.md)</sup>
false<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> witness<sup>[5707](h5707.md)</sup> against
his neighbor<sup>[7453](h7453)</sup> is a maul,<sup>[4650](h4650.md)</sup>
and a sword,<sup>[2719](h2719)</sup> and a sharp<sup>[8150](h8150.md)</sup>
arrow.<sup>[2671](h2671.md)</sup>

19  Confidence<sup>[4009](h4009.md)</sup> in an unfaithful
man<sup>[898](h0898)</sup> in time<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup> of
trouble<sup>[6869](h6869)</sup> is like a broken<sup>[7465](h7465.md)</sup>
tooth,<sup>[8127](h8127)</sup> and a foot<sup>[7272](h7272.md)</sup> out of
joint.<sup>[4154](h4154.md)</sup>

20  As he that taketh away<sup>[5710](h5710.md)</sup> a
garment<sup>[899](h0899)</sup> in cold<sup>[7135](h7135.md)</sup>
weather,<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> and as vinegar<sup>[2558](h2558.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> niter,<sup>[5427](h5427.md)</sup> so is he
that singeth<sup>[7891](h7891)</sup> songs<sup>[7892](h7892.md)</sup>
to<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> a heavy<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup>
heart.<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup>

21  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> thine enemy<sup>[8130](h8130.md)</sup> be
hungry,<sup>[7457](h7457)</sup> give him bread<sup>[3899](h3899.md)</sup>
to eat;<sup>[398](h0398)</sup> and if<sup>[518](h0518.md)</sup> he be
thirsty,<sup>[6771](h6771)</sup> give him water<sup>[4325](h4325.md)</sup>
to drink:<sup>[8248](h8248.md)</sup>

22  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thou<sup>[859](h0859.md)</sup> shalt
heap<sup>[2846](h2846)</sup> coals of fire<sup>[1513](h1513.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> his head,<sup>[7218](h7218.md)</sup> and the
LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> shall reward<sup>[7999](h7999.md)</sup> thee.

23  The north<sup>[6828](h6828)</sup> wind<sup>[7307](h7307.md)</sup>
driveth away<sup>[2342](h2342)</sup> rain:<sup>[1653](h1653.md)</sup> so
doth an angry<sup>[2194](h2194.md)</sup>
countenance<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> a backbiting<sup>[5643](h5643.md)</sup>
tongue.<sup>[3956](h3956.md)</sup>

24  It is better<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup> to
dwell<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup> in<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> the
corner<sup>[6438](h6438)</sup> of the housetop,<sup>[1406](h1406.md)</sup>
than with a brawling woman<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[802](h0802)</sup> <sup>[4079](h4079.md)</sup> and in a
wide<sup>[2267](h2267)</sup> house.<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup>

25  As cold<sup>[7119](h7119)</sup> waters<sup>[4325](h4325.md)</sup>
to<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> a thirsty<sup>[5889](h5889.md)</sup>
soul,<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> so is good<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup>
news<sup>[8052](h8052)</sup> from a far country.<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> <sup>[4801](h4801.md)</sup>

26  A righteous<sup>[6662](h6662.md)</sup> man falling
down<sup>[4131](h4131)</sup> before<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> is as a troubled<sup>[7515](h7515.md)</sup>
fountain,<sup>[4599](h4599)</sup> and a corrupt<sup>[7843](h7843.md)</sup>
spring.<sup>[4726](h4726.md)</sup>

27  It is not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> good<sup>[2896](h2896.md)</sup> to
eat<sup>[398](h0398)</sup> much<sup>[7235](h7235.md)</sup>
honey:<sup>[1706](h1706.md)</sup> so for men to
search<sup>[2714](h2714)</sup> their own glory<sup>[3519](h3519.md)</sup>
is not glory.<sup>[3519](h3519.md)</sup>

28  He<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> that<sup>[834](h0834.md)</sup> hath
no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> rule over<sup>[4623](h4623.md)</sup> his own
spirit<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> is like a city<sup>[5892](h5892.md)</sup>
that is broken down,<sup>[6555](h6555.md)</sup> and
without<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> walls.<sup>[2346](h2346.md)</sup>

[Vul](../vul/pro025.md)

1 Hæ quoque parabolæ Salomonis, quas transtulerunt viri Ezechiæ regis
Juda.

2 \[Gloria Dei est celare verbum,  
et gloria regum investigare sermonem.  

3 Cælum sursum, et terra deorsum,  
et cor regum inscrutabile.  

4 Aufer rubiginem de argento,  
et egredietur vas purissimum.  

5 Aufer impietatem de vultu regis,  
et firmabitur justitia thronus ejus.  

6 Ne gloriosus appareas coram rege,  
et in loco magnorum ne steteris.  

7 Melius est enim ut dicatur tibi: Ascende huc,  
quam ut humilieris coram principe.\]  

8 \[Quæ viderunt oculi tui ne proferas in jurgio cito,  
ne postea emendare non possis,  
cum dehonestaveris amicum tuum.  

9 Causam tuam tracta cum amico tuo,  
et secretum extraneo ne reveles:  

10 ne forte insultet tibi cum audierit,  
et exprobrare non cesset.  
Gratia et amicitia liberant:  
quas tibi serva, ne exprobrabilis fias.  

11 Mala aurea in lectis argenteis,  
qui loquitur verbum in tempore suo.  

12 Inauris aurea, et margaritum fulgens,  
qui arguit sapientem et aurem obedientem.  

13 Sicut frigus nivis in die messis,  
ita legatus fidelis ei qui misit eum:  
animam ipsius requiescere facit.  

14 Nubes, et ventus, et pluviæ non sequentes,  
vir gloriosus et promissa non complens.  

15 Patientia lenietur princeps,  
et lingua mollis confringet duritiam.\]  

16 \[Mel invenisti: comede quod sufficit tibi,  
ne forte satiatus evomas illud.  

17 Subtrahe pedem tuum de domo proximi tui,  
nequando satiatus oderit te.  

18 Jaculum, et gladius, et sagitta acuta,  
homo qui loquitur contra proximum suum falsum testimonium.  

19 Dens putridus, et pes lassus,  
qui sperat super infideli in die angustiæ,  

20 et amittit pallium in die frigoris.  
Acetum in nitro,  
qui cantat carmina cordi pessimo.  
Sicut tinea vestimento, et vermis ligno,  
ita tristitia viri nocet cordi.  

21 Si esurierit inimicus tuus, ciba illum;  
si sitierit, da ei aquam bibere:  

22 prunas enim congregabis super caput ejus,  
et Dominus reddet tibi.  

23 Ventus aquilo dissipat pluvias,  
et facies tristis linguam detrahentem.  

24 Melius est sedere in angulo domatis  
quam cum muliere litigiosa et in domo communi.  

25 Aqua frigida animæ sitienti,  
et nuntius bonus de terra longinqua.  

26 Fons turbatus pede et vena corrupta,  
justus cadens coram impio.  

27 Sicut qui mel multum comedit non est ei bonum,  
sic qui scrutator est majestatis opprimetur a gloria.  

28 Sicut urbs patens et absque murorum ambitu,  
ita vir qui non potest in loquendo cohibere spiritum suum.\]
