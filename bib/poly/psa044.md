### Psalms 44

[KJV](../kjv/psa044.md)

1  To the chief Musician<sup>[5329](h5329.md)</sup> for the
sons<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> of Korah,<sup>[7141](h7141.md)</sup>
Maschil.<sup>[4905](h4905)</sup> We have heard<sup>[8085](h8085.md)</sup>
with our ears,<sup>[241](h0241)</sup> O God,<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup> our
fathers<sup>[1](h0001)</sup> have told<sup>[5608](h5608.md)</sup> us, what
work<sup>[6467](h6467)</sup> thou didst<sup>[6466](h6466.md)</sup> in their
days,<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> in the times<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup> of
old.<sup>[6924](h6924.md)</sup>

2  How thou<sup>[859](h0859.md)</sup> didst drive
out<sup>[3423](h3423)</sup> the heathen<sup>[1471](h1471.md)</sup> with thy
hand,<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> and plantedst<sup>[5193](h5193.md)</sup>
them; how thou didst afflict<sup>[7489](h7489.md)</sup> the
people,<sup>[3816](h3816.md)</sup> and cast them
out.<sup>[7971](h7971.md)</sup>

3  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> they got not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> the
land<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> in possession<sup>[3423](h3423.md)</sup> by
their own sword,<sup>[2719](h2719)</sup> neither<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
did their own arm<sup>[2220](h2220)</sup> save<sup>[3467](h3467.md)</sup>
them: but<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup> thy right
hand,<sup>[3225](h3225)</sup> and thine arm,<sup>[2220](h2220.md)</sup> and
the light<sup>[216](h0216.md)</sup> of thy
countenance,<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> because<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup>
thou hadst a favor<sup>[7521](h7521.md)</sup> unto them.

4  Thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> art my King,<sup>[4428](h4428.md)</sup> O
God:<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> command<sup>[6680](h6680.md)</sup>
deliverances<sup>[3444](h3444)</sup> for Jacob.<sup>[3290](h3290.md)</sup>

5  Through thee will we push down<sup>[5055](h5055.md)</sup> our
enemies:<sup>[6862](h6862.md)</sup> through thy
name<sup>[8034](h8034.md)</sup> will we tread them
under<sup>[947](h0947.md)</sup> that rise up
against<sup>[6965](h6965.md)</sup> us.

6  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> I will not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
trust<sup>[982](h0982)</sup> in my bow,<sup>[7198](h7198.md)</sup>
neither<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> shall my sword<sup>[2719](h2719.md)</sup>
save<sup>[3467](h3467.md)</sup> me.

7  But<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thou hast saved<sup>[3467](h3467.md)</sup>
us from our enemies,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6862](h6862.md)</sup>
and hast put them to shame<sup>[954](h0954.md)</sup> that
hated<sup>[8130](h8130.md)</sup> us.

8  In God<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> we boast<sup>[1984](h1984.md)</sup>
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> the day<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup> long, and
praise<sup>[3034](h3034)</sup> thy name<sup>[8034](h8034.md)</sup>
forever.<sup>[5769](h5769)</sup> Selah.<sup>[5542](h5542.md)</sup>

9  But<sup>[637](h0637.md)</sup> thou hast cast
off,<sup>[2186](h2186.md)</sup> and put us to
shame;<sup>[3637](h3637.md)</sup> and goest not
forth<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup> <sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> with our
armies.<sup>[6635](h6635.md)</sup>

10  Thou makest us to turn<sup>[7725](h7725.md)</sup>
back<sup>[268](h0268)</sup> from<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup> the
enemy:<sup>[6862](h6862.md)</sup> and they which
hate<sup>[8130](h8130)</sup> us spoil<sup>[8154](h8154.md)</sup> for
themselves.

11  Thou hast given<sup>[5414](h5414.md)</sup> us like
sheep<sup>[6629](h6629.md)</sup> appointed for
meat;<sup>[3978](h3978)</sup> and hast scattered<sup>[2219](h2219.md)</sup>
us among the heathen.<sup>[1471](h1471.md)</sup>

12  Thou sellest<sup>[4376](h4376.md)</sup> thy
people<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup> for naught,<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
<sup>[1952](h1952)</sup> and dost not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
increase<sup>[7235](h7235.md)</sup> thy wealth by their
price.<sup>[4242](h4242.md)</sup>

13  Thou makest<sup>[7760](h7760.md)</sup> us a
reproach<sup>[2781](h2781.md)</sup> to our
neighbors,<sup>[7934](h7934)</sup> a scorn<sup>[3933](h3933.md)</sup> and a
derision<sup>[7047](h7047.md)</sup> to them that are round
about<sup>[5439](h5439.md)</sup> us.

14  Thou makest<sup>[7760](h7760.md)</sup> us a
byword<sup>[4912](h4912.md)</sup> among the
heathen,<sup>[1471](h1471)</sup> a shaking<sup>[4493](h4493.md)</sup> of
the head<sup>[7218](h7218.md)</sup> among the
people.<sup>[3816](h3816.md)</sup>

15  My confusion<sup>[3639](h3639.md)</sup> is
continually<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> <sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup>
before<sup>[5048](h5048)</sup> me, and the shame<sup>[1322](h1322.md)</sup>
of my face<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> hath covered<sup>[3680](h3680.md)</sup>
me,

16  For the voice<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6963](h6963.md)</sup> of
him that reproacheth<sup>[2778](h2778.md)</sup> and
blasphemeth;<sup>[1442](h1442)</sup> by reason<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> of the enemy<sup>[341](h0341.md)</sup> and
avenger.<sup>[5358](h5358.md)</sup>

17  All<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> this<sup>[2063](h2063.md)</sup> is come
upon<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> us; yet have we not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
forgotten<sup>[7911](h7911)</sup> thee, neither<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
have we dealt falsely<sup>[8266](h8266.md)</sup> in thy
covenant.<sup>[1285](h1285.md)</sup>

18  Our heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> is not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
turned<sup>[5472](h5472)</sup> back,<sup>[268](h0268.md)</sup> neither have
our steps<sup>[838](h0838)</sup> declined<sup>[5186](h5186.md)</sup>
from<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> thy way;<sup>[734](h0734.md)</sup>

19  Though<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup> thou hast sore
broken<sup>[1794](h1794)</sup> us in the place<sup>[4725](h4725.md)</sup>
of dragons,<sup>[8577](h8577)</sup> and covered<sup>[3680](h3680.md)</sup>
<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> us with the shadow of
death.<sup>[6757](h6757.md)</sup>

20  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> we have forgotten<sup>[7911](h7911.md)</sup>
the name<sup>[8034](h8034)</sup> of our God,<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup> or
stretched out<sup>[6566](h6566)</sup> our hands<sup>[3709](h3709.md)</sup>
to a strange<sup>[2114](h2114)</sup> god;<sup>[410](h0410.md)</sup>

21  Shall not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> God<sup>[430](h0430.md)</sup>
search<sup>[2713](h2713)</sup> this<sup>[2063](h2063.md)</sup> out?
for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> he<sup>[1931](h1931.md)</sup>
knoweth<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> the secrets<sup>[8587](h8587.md)</sup> of
the heart.<sup>[3820](h3820.md)</sup>

22  Yea,<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> for<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup> thy sake
are we killed<sup>[2026](h2026)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup> the
day<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> long; we are counted<sup>[2803](h2803.md)</sup>
as sheep<sup>[6629](h6629.md)</sup> for the
slaughter.<sup>[2878](h2878.md)</sup>

23  Awake,<sup>[5782](h5782)</sup> why<sup>[4100](h4100.md)</sup>
sleepest<sup>[3462](h3462)</sup> thou, O Lord?<sup>[136](h0136.md)</sup>
arise,<sup>[6974](h6974)</sup> cast<sup>[2186](h2186.md)</sup> us
not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> off forever.<sup>[5331](h5331.md)</sup>

24  Wherefore<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> hidest<sup>[5641](h5641.md)</sup>
thou thy face,<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> and
forgettest<sup>[7911](h7911.md)</sup> our
affliction<sup>[6040](h6040.md)</sup> and our
oppression?<sup>[3906](h3906.md)</sup>

25  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> our soul<sup>[5315](h5315.md)</sup> is
bowed down<sup>[7743](h7743)</sup> to the dust:<sup>[6083](h6083.md)</sup>
our belly<sup>[990](h0990)</sup> cleaveth<sup>[1692](h1692.md)</sup> unto
the earth.<sup>[776](h0776.md)</sup>

26  Arise<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> for our help,<sup>[5833](h5833.md)</sup>
and redeem<sup>[6299](h6299.md)</sup> us for thy mercies'
sake.<sup>[4616](h4616)</sup> <sup>[2617](h2617.md)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/psa044.md)

1 εἰς τὸ τέλος ὑπὲρ τῶν ἀλλοιωθησομένων τοῖς υἱοῖς Κορε εἰς σύνεσιν ᾠδὴ
ὑπὲρ τοῦ ἀγαπητοῦ

2 ἐξηρεύξατο ἡ καρδία μου λόγον ἀγαθόν λέγω ἐγὼ τὰ ἔργα μου τῷ βασιλεῖ ἡ
γλῶσσά μου κάλαμος γραμματέως ὀξυγράφου

3 ὡραῖος κάλλει παρὰ τοὺς υἱοὺς τῶν ἀνθρώπων ἐξεχύθη χάρις ἐν χείλεσίν
σου διὰ τοῦτο εὐλόγησέν σε ὁ θεὸς εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα

4 περίζωσαι τὴν ῥομφαίαν σου ἐπὶ τὸν μηρόν σου δυνατέ τῇ ὡραιότητί σου
καὶ τῷ κάλλει σου

5 καὶ ἔντεινον καὶ κατευοδοῦ καὶ βασίλευε ἕνεκεν ἀληθείας καὶ πραΰτητος
καὶ δικαιοσύνης καὶ ὁδηγήσει σε θαυμαστῶς ἡ δεξιά σου

6 τὰ βέλη σου ἠκονημένα δυνατέ λαοὶ ὑποκάτω σου πεσοῦνται ἐν καρδίᾳ τῶν
ἐχθρῶν τοῦ βασιλέως

7 ὁ θρόνος σου ὁ θεός εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα τοῦ αἰῶνος ῥάβδος εὐθύτητος ἡ ῥάβδος
τῆς βασιλείας σου

8 ἠγάπησας δικαιοσύνην καὶ ἐμίσησας ἀνομίαν διὰ τοῦτο ἔχρισέν σε ὁ θεὸς
ὁ θεός σου ἔλαιον ἀγαλλιάσεως παρὰ τοὺς μετόχους σου

9 σμύρνα καὶ στακτὴ καὶ κασία ἀπὸ τῶν ἱματίων σου ἀπὸ βάρεων ἐλεφαντίνων
ἐξ ὧν ηὔφρανάν σε

10 θυγατέρες βασιλέων ἐν τῇ τιμῇ σου παρέστη ἡ βασίλισσα ἐκ δεξιῶν σου
ἐν ἱματισμῷ διαχρύσῳ περιβεβλημένη πεποικιλμένη

11 ἄκουσον θύγατερ καὶ ἰδὲ καὶ κλῖνον τὸ οὖς σου καὶ ἐπιλάθου τοῦ λαοῦ
σου καὶ τοῦ οἴκου τοῦ πατρός σου

12 ὅτι ἐπεθύμησεν ὁ βασιλεὺς τοῦ κάλλους σου ὅτι αὐτός ἐστιν ὁ κύριός
σου

13 καὶ προσκυνήσουσιν αὐτῷ θυγατέρες Τύρου ἐν δώροις τὸ πρόσωπόν σου
λιτανεύσουσιν οἱ πλούσιοι τοῦ λαοῦ

14 πᾶσα ἡ δόξα αὐτῆς θυγατρὸς βασιλέως ἔσωθεν ἐν κροσσωτοῖς χρυσοῖς
περιβεβλημένη πεποικιλμένη

15 ἀπενεχθήσονται τῷ βασιλεῖ παρθένοι ὀπίσω αὐτῆς αἱ πλησίον αὐτῆς
ἀπενεχθήσονταί σοι

16 ἀπενεχθήσονται ἐν εὐφροσύνῃ καὶ ἀγαλλιάσει ἀχθήσονται εἰς ναὸν
βασιλέως

17 ἀντὶ τῶν πατέρων σου ἐγενήθησάν σοι υἱοί καταστήσεις αὐτοὺς ἄρχοντας
ἐπὶ πᾶσαν τὴν γῆν

18 μνησθήσονται τοῦ ὀνόματός σου ἐν πάσῃ γενεᾷ καὶ γενεᾷ διὰ τοῦτο λαοὶ
ἐξομολογήσονταί σοι εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα καὶ εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα τοῦ αἰῶνος

1 In finem, pro iis qui commutabuntur. Filiis Core, ad intellectum.
Canticum pro dilecto.

2 \[Eructavit cor meum verbum bonum:  
dico ego opera mea regi.  
Lingua mea calamus scribæ  
velociter scribentis.  

3 Speciosus forma præ filiis hominum,  
diffusa est gratia in labiis tuis:  
propterea benedixit te Deus in æternum.  

4 Accingere gladio tuo super femur tuum, potentissime.  

5 Specie tua et pulchritudine tua  
intende, prospere procede, et regna,  
propter veritatem, et mansuetudinem, et justitiam;  
et deducet te mirabiliter dextera tua.  

6 Sagittæ tuæ acutæ:  
populi sub te cadent,  
in corda inimicorum regis.  

7 Sedes tua, Deus, in sæculum sæculi;  
virga directionis virga regni tui.  

8 Dilexisti justitiam, et odisti iniquitatem;  
propterea unxit te Deus, Deus tuus,  
oleo lætitiæ, præ consortibus tuis.  

9 Myrrha, et gutta, et casia a vestimentis tuis,  
a domibus eburneis; ex quibus delectaverunt te  

10 filiæ regum in honore tuo.  
Astitit regina a dextris tuis  
in vestitu deaurato, circumdata varietate.  

11 Audi, filia, et vide, et inclina aurem tuam;  
et obliviscere populum tuum, et domum patris tui.  

12 Et concupiscet rex decorem tuum,  
quoniam ipse est Dominus Deus tuus, et adorabunt eum.  

13 Et filiæ Tyri in muneribus vultum tuum deprecabuntur;  
omnes divites plebis.  

14 Omnis gloria ejus filiæ regis ab intus,  
in fimbriis aureis,  

15 circumamicta varietatibus.  
Adducentur regi virgines post eam;  
proximæ ejus afferentur tibi.  

16 Afferentur in lætitia et exsultatione;  
adducentur in templum regis.  

17 Pro patribus tuis nati sunt tibi filii;  
constitues eos principes super omnem terram.  

18 Memores erunt nominis tui in omni generatione et generationem:  
propterea populi confitebuntur tibi in æternum, et in sæculum sæculi.\]
