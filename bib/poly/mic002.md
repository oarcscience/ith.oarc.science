### Micah 2

[KJV](../kjv/mic002.md)

1  Woe<sup>[1945](h1945.md)</sup> to them that
devise<sup>[2803](h2803)</sup> iniquity,<sup>[205](h0205.md)</sup> and
work<sup>[6466](h6466)</sup> evil<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> their beds!<sup>[4904](h4904.md)</sup> when
the morning<sup>[1242](h1242)</sup> is light,<sup>[216](h0216.md)</sup>
they practice<sup>[6213](h6213.md)</sup> it,
because<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> it is<sup>[3426](h3426.md)</sup> in the
power<sup>[410](h0410)</sup> of their hand.<sup>[3027](h3027.md)</sup>

2  And they covet<sup>[2530](h2530.md)</sup>
fields,<sup>[7704](h7704.md)</sup> and take them by
violence;<sup>[1497](h1497)</sup> and houses,<sup>[1004](h1004.md)</sup>
and take them away:<sup>[5375](h5375.md)</sup> so they
oppress<sup>[6231](h6231)</sup> a man<sup>[1397](h1397.md)</sup> and his
house<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> even a man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup> and his
heritage.<sup>[5159](h5159.md)</sup>

3  Therefore<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> thus<sup>[3541](h3541.md)</sup>
saith<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> the LORD;<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup>
Behold,<sup>[2009](h2009)</sup> against<sup>[5921](h5921.md)</sup>
this<sup>[2063](h2063)</sup> family<sup>[4940](h4940.md)</sup> do I
devise<sup>[2803](h2803)</sup> an evil,<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup>
from<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[8033](h8033.md)</sup>
which<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> ye shall not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
remove<sup>[4185](h4185)</sup> your necks;<sup>[6677](h6677.md)</sup>
neither<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> shall ye go<sup>[1980](h1980.md)</sup>
haughtily:<sup>[7317](h7317)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup>
this<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> time<sup>[6256](h6256.md)</sup> is
evil.<sup>[7451](h7451.md)</sup>

4  In that<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> day<sup>[3117](h3117.md)</sup> shall one
take up<sup>[5375](h5375)</sup> a parable<sup>[4912](h4912.md)</sup>
against<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> you, and lament<sup>[5091](h5091.md)</sup>
with a doleful<sup>[5093](h5093.md)</sup>
lamentation,<sup>[5092](h5092)</sup> and say,<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup> We
be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> utterly spoiled:<sup>[7703](h7703.md)</sup>
<sup>[7703](h7703)</sup> he hath changed<sup>[4171](h4171.md)</sup> the
portion<sup>[2506](h2506)</sup> of my people:<sup>[5971](h5971.md)</sup>
how<sup>[349](h0349)</sup> hath he removed<sup>[4185](h4185.md)</sup> it
from me! turning away<sup>[7728](h7728.md)</sup> he hath
divided<sup>[2505](h2505)</sup> our fields.<sup>[7704](h7704.md)</sup>

5  Therefore<sup>[3651](h3651.md)</sup> thou shalt
have<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> none<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> that shall
cast<sup>[7993](h7993)</sup> a cord<sup>[2256](h2256.md)</sup> by
lot<sup>[1486](h1486)</sup> in the congregation<sup>[6951](h6951.md)</sup>
of the LORD.<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup>

6  Prophesy<sup>[5197](h5197)</sup> ye not,<sup>[408](h0408.md)</sup> say
they to them that prophesy:<sup>[5197](h5197.md)</sup> they shall
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> prophesy<sup>[5197](h5197.md)</sup> to
them,<sup>[428](h0428)</sup> that they shall not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup>
take<sup>[5253](h5253)</sup> shame.<sup>[3639](h3639.md)</sup>

7  O thou that art named<sup>[559](h0559.md)</sup> the
house<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> of Jacob,<sup>[3290](h3290.md)</sup> is the
spirit<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> of the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068.md)</sup>
straitened?<sup>[7114](h7114)</sup> are these<sup>[428](h0428.md)</sup> his
doings?<sup>[4611](h4611)</sup> do not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> my
words<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup> do good<sup>[3190](h3190.md)</sup>
to<sup>[5973](h5973)</sup> him that walketh<sup>[1980](h1980.md)</sup>
uprightly?<sup>[3477](h3477.md)</sup>

8  Even of late<sup>[865](h0865)</sup> my people<sup>[5971](h5971.md)</sup>
is risen up<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> as an enemy:<sup>[341](h0341.md)</sup>
ye pull off<sup>[6584](h6584)</sup> the robe<sup>[145](h0145.md)</sup>
with<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[4136](h4136.md)</sup> the
garment<sup>[8008](h8008.md)</sup> from them that pass
by<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[5674](h5674.md)</sup>
securely<sup>[983](h0983)</sup> as men averse<sup>[7725](h7725.md)</sup>
from war.<sup>[4421](h4421.md)</sup>

9  The women<sup>[802](h0802)</sup> of my people<sup>[5971](h5971.md)</sup>
have ye cast out<sup>[1644](h1644.md)</sup> from their
pleasant<sup>[8588](h8588)</sup> houses;<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> from<sup>[4480](h4480.md)</sup>
<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> their children<sup>[5768](h5768.md)</sup> have ye
taken away<sup>[3947](h3947)</sup> my glory<sup>[1926](h1926.md)</sup>
forever.<sup>[5769](h5769.md)</sup>

10  Arise<sup>[6965](h6965.md)</sup> ye, and
depart;<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588.md)</sup>
this<sup>[2063](h2063)</sup> is not<sup>[3808](h3808.md)</sup> your
rest:<sup>[4496](h4496)</sup> because<sup>[5668](h5668.md)</sup> it is
polluted,<sup>[2930](h2930.md)</sup> it shall
destroy<sup>[2254](h2254.md)</sup> you, even with a
sore<sup>[4834](h4834)</sup> destruction.<sup>[2256](h2256.md)</sup>

11  If<sup>[3863](h3863)</sup> a man<sup>[376](h0376.md)</sup>
walking<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> in the spirit<sup>[7307](h7307.md)</sup>
and falsehood<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> do lie,<sup>[3576](h3576.md)</sup>
saying, I will prophesy<sup>[5197](h5197.md)</sup> unto thee of
wine<sup>[3196](h3196.md)</sup> and of strong
drink;<sup>[7941](h7941)</sup> he shall even be<sup>[1961](h1961.md)</sup>
the prophet<sup>[5197](h5197)</sup> of this<sup>[2088](h2088.md)</sup>
people.<sup>[5971](h5971.md)</sup>

12  I will surely assemble,<sup>[622](h0622.md)</sup>
<sup>[622](h0622)</sup> O Jacob,<sup>[3290](h3290.md)</sup>
all<sup>[3605](h3605.md)</sup> of thee; I will surely
gather<sup>[6908](h6908)</sup> <sup>[6908](h6908.md)</sup> the
remnant<sup>[7611](h7611)</sup> of Israel;<sup>[3478](h3478.md)</sup> I
will put<sup>[7760](h7760)</sup> them together<sup>[3162](h3162.md)</sup>
as the sheep<sup>[6629](h6629)</sup> of Bozrah,<sup>[1223](h1223.md)</sup>
as the flock<sup>[5739](h5739.md)</sup> in the
midst<sup>[8432](h8432)</sup> of their fold:<sup>[1699](h1699.md)</sup>
they shall make great noise<sup>[1949](h1949.md)</sup> by reason of the
multitude of men.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[120](h0120.md)</sup>

13  The breaker<sup>[6555](h6555.md)</sup> is come
up<sup>[5927](h5927)</sup> before<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> them: they
have broken up,<sup>[6555](h6555.md)</sup> and have passed
through<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup> the gate,<sup>[8179](h8179.md)</sup> and
are gone out<sup>[3318](h3318.md)</sup> by it: and their
king<sup>[4428](h4428)</sup> shall pass<sup>[5674](h5674.md)</sup>
before<sup>[6440](h6440.md)</sup> them, and the
LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> on the head<sup>[7218](h7218.md)</sup> of

[Sep](../sep/mic002.md)

1 ἐγένοντο λογιζόμενοι κόπους καὶ ἐργαζόμενοι κακὰ ἐν ταῖς κοίταις αὐτῶν
καὶ ἅμα τῇ ἡμέρᾳ συνετέλουν αὐτά διότι οὐκ ἦραν πρὸς τὸν θεὸν τὰς χεῖρας
αὐτῶν

2 καὶ ἐπεθύμουν ἀγροὺς καὶ διήρπαζον ὀρφανοὺς καὶ οἴκους κατεδυνάστευον
καὶ διήρπαζον ἄνδρα καὶ τὸν οἶκον αὐτοῦ ἄνδρα καὶ τὴν κληρονομίαν αὐτοῦ

3 διὰ τοῦτο τάδε λέγει κύριος ἰδοὺ ἐγὼ λογίζομαι ἐπὶ τὴν φυλὴν ταύτην
κακά ἐξ ὧν οὐ μὴ ἄρητε τοὺς τραχήλους ὑμῶν καὶ οὐ μὴ πορευθῆτε ὀρθοὶ
ἐξαίφνης ὅτι καιρὸς πονηρός ἐστιν

4 ἐν τῇ ἡμέρᾳ ἐκείνῃ λημφθήσεται ἐφ᾽ ὑμᾶς παραβολή καὶ θρηνηθήσεται
θρῆνος ἐν μέλει λέγων ταλαιπωρίᾳ ἐταλαιπωρήσαμεν μερὶς λαοῦ μου
κατεμετρήθη ἐν σχοινίῳ καὶ οὐκ ἦν ὁ κωλύσων αὐτὸν τοῦ ἀποστρέψαι οἱ
ἀγροὶ ἡμῶν διεμερίσθησαν

5 διὰ τοῦτο οὐκ ἔσται σοι βάλλων σχοινίον ἐν κλήρῳ ἐν ἐκκλησίᾳ κυρίου

6 μὴ κλαίετε δάκρυσιν μηδὲ δακρυέτωσαν ἐπὶ τούτοις οὐ γὰρ ἀπώσεται
ὀνείδη

7 ὁ λέγων οἶκος Ιακωβ παρώργισεν πνεῦμα κυρίου εἰ ταῦτα τὰ ἐπιτηδεύματα
αὐτοῦ ἐστιν οὐχ οἱ λόγοι αὐτοῦ εἰσιν καλοὶ μετ᾽ αὐτοῦ καὶ ὀρθοὶ
πεπόρευνται

8 καὶ ἔμπροσθεν ὁ λαός μου εἰς ἔχθραν ἀντέστη κατέναντι τῆς εἰρήνης
αὐτοῦ τὴν δορὰν αὐτοῦ ἐξέδειραν τοῦ ἀφελέσθαι ἐλπίδα συντριμμὸν πολέμου

9 διὰ τοῦτο ἡγούμενοι λαοῦ μου ἀπορριφήσονται ἐκ τῶν οἰκιῶν τρυφῆς αὐτῶν
διὰ τὰ πονηρὰ ἐπιτηδεύματα αὐτῶν ἐξώσθησαν ἐγγίσατε ὄρεσιν αἰωνίοις

10 ἀνάστηθι καὶ πορεύου ὅτι οὐκ ἔστιν σοι αὕτη ἡ ἀνάπαυσις ἕνεκεν
ἀκαθαρσίας διεφθάρητε φθορᾷ

11 κατεδιώχθητε οὐδενὸς διώκοντος πνεῦμα ἔστησεν ψεῦδος ἐστάλαξέν σοι
εἰς οἶνον καὶ μέθυσμα καὶ ἔσται ἐκ τῆς σταγόνος τοῦ λαοῦ τούτου

12 συναγόμενος συναχθήσεται Ιακωβ σὺν πᾶσιν ἐκδεχόμενος ἐκδέξομαι τοὺς
καταλοίπους τοῦ Ισραηλ ἐπὶ τὸ αὐτὸ θήσομαι τὴν ἀποστροφὴν αὐτῶν ὡς
πρόβατα ἐν θλίψει ὡς ποίμνιον ἐν μέσῳ κοίτης αὐτῶν ἐξαλοῦνται ἐξ
ἀνθρώπων

13 διὰ τῆς διακοπῆς πρὸ προσώπου αὐτῶν διέκοψαν καὶ διῆλθον πύλην καὶ
ἐξῆλθον δι᾽ αὐτῆς καὶ ἐξῆλθεν ὁ βασιλεὺς αὐτῶν πρὸ προσώπου αὐτῶν ὁ δὲ
κύριος ἡγήσεται αὐτῶν

[Vul](../vul/mic002.md)

1 \[Væ qui cogitatis inutile,  
et operamini malum in cubilibus vestris!  
In luce matutina faciunt illud,  
quoniam contra Deum est manus eorum.  

2 Et concupierunt agros, et violenter tulerunt:  
et rapuerunt domos,  
et calumniabantur virum, et domum ejus:  
virum, et hæreditatem ejus.  

3 Idcirco hæc dicit Dominus:  
Ecce ego cogito super familiam istam malum,  
unde non auferetis colla vestra,  
et non ambulabitis superbi:  
quoniam tempus pessimum est.  

4 In die illa sumetur super vos parabola,  
et cantabitur canticum cum suavitate, dicentium:  
Depopulatione vastati sumus;  
pars populi mei commutata est:  
quomodo recedet a me, cum revertatur,  
qui regiones nostras dividat?  

5 Propter hoc non erit tibi mittens funiculum sortis in cœtu Domini.  

6 Ne loquamini loquentes;  
non stillabit super istos, non comprehendet confusio.  

7 Dicit domus Jacob:  
Numquid abbreviatus est spiritus Domini,  
aut tales sunt cogitationes ejus?  
nonne verba mea bona sunt cum eo qui recte graditur?  

8 et e contrario populus meus in adversarium consurrexit.  
Desuper tunica pallium sustulistis:  
et eos qui transibant simpliciter convertistis in bellum.  

9 Mulieres populi mei ejecistis de domo deliciarum suarum;  
a parvulis earum tulistis laudem meam in perpetuum.  

10 Surgite, et ite, quia non habetis hic requiem:  
propter immunditiam ejus corrumpetur putredine pessima.  

11 Utinam non essem vir habens spiritum,  
et mendacium potius loquerer!  
Stillabo tibi in vinum et in ebrietatem;  
et erit super quem stillatur populus iste.  

12 Congregatione congregabo, Jacob, totum te;  
in unum conducam reliquias Israël:  
pariter ponam illum quasi gregem in ovili,  
quasi pecus in medio caularum:  
tumultuabuntur a multitudine hominum.  

13 Ascendet enim pandens iter ante eos:  
divident, et transibunt portam,  
et ingredientur per eam:  
et transibit rex eorum coram eis,  
et Dominus in capite eorum.\]
