### JOSHUA CHAPTER 12

<span id="an_jos:12:1">1</span> Now these are the kings of the land,
whom the children of Israel smote, and possessed their land beyond the
Jordan toward the sunrising, from the valley of Arnon unto mount Hermon,
and all the Arabah eastward: <span id="an_jos:12:2">2</span> Sihon king
of the Amorites, who dwelt in Heshbon, and ruled from Aroer, which is on
the edge of the valley of Arnon, and the middle of the valley, and half
Gilead, even unto the river Jabbok, the border of the children of Ammon;
<span id="an_jos:12:3">3</span> and the Arabah unto the sea of
Chinneroth, eastward, and unto the sea of the Arabah, even the Salt Sea,
eastward, the way to Beth-jeshimoth; and on the south, under the slopes
of Pisgah; <span id="an_jos:12:4">4</span> and the border of Og king of
Bashan, of the remnant of the Rephaim, who dwelt at Ashtaroth and at
Edrei, <span id="an_jos:12:5">5</span> and ruled in mount Hermon, and in
Salcah, and in all Bashan, unto the border of the Geshurites and the
Maacathites, and half Gilead, even unto the border of Sihon king of
Heshbon. <span id="an_jos:12:6">6</span> Moses the servant of the

<span id="page_276">p. 276</span>

<span class="contnote">\[paragraph continues\]</span> LORD and the
children of Israel smote them; and Moses the servant of the LORD gave it
for a possession unto the Reubenites, and the Gadites, and the
half-tribe of Manasseh.

<span id="an_jos:12:7">7</span> And these are the kings of the land whom
Joshua and the children of Israel smote beyond the Jordan westward, from
Baal-gad in the valley of Lebanon even unto the bare mountain, that
goeth up to Seir; and Joshua gave it unto the tribes of Israel for a
possession according to their divisions; <span
id="an_jos:12:8">8</span> in the hill-country, and in the Lowland, and
in the Arabah, and in the slopes, and in the wilderness, and in the
South; the Hittite, the Amorite, and the Canaanite, the Perizzite, the
Hivite, and the Jebusite:

<span id="an_jos:12:9">9</span> the king of Jericho, one;  
the king of Ai, which is beside Beth-el, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:10">10</span> the king of Jerusalem, one;  
the king of Hebron, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:11">11</span> the king of Jarmuth, one;  
the king of Lachish, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:12">12</span> the king of Eglon, one;  
the king of Gezer, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:13">13</span> the king of Debir, one;  
the king of Geder, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:14">14</span> the king of Hormah, one;  
the king of Arad, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:15">15</span> the king of Libnah, one;  
the king of Adullam, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:16">16</span> the king of Makkedah, one;  
the king of Beth-el, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:17">17</span> the king of Tappuah, one;  
the king of Hepher, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:18">18</span> the king of Aphek, one;  
the king of the Sharon, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:19">19</span> the king of Madon, one;  
the king of Hazor, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:20">20</span> the king of Shimron-meron, one;  
the king of Achshaph, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:21">21</span> the king of Taanach, one;  
the king of Megiddo, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:22">22</span> the king of Kedesh, one;  
the king of Jokneam in Carmel, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:23">23</span> the king of Dor in the region of Dor,
one;  
the king of Goiim in the Gilgal, one;  
<span id="an_jos:12:24">24</span> the king of Tirzah, one.  
All the kings thirty and one.
