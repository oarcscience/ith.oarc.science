### King James Version: Psalms Chapter 137

<span id="001">1</span> By the rivers of
[Babylon](../ebd/ebd041.htm#001.md), there we sat down, yea, we wept, when
we remembered [Zion](../ebd/ebd394.htm#002.md).  

<span id="002">2</span> We hanged our harps upon the
[willows](../ebd/ebd381.htm#006.md) in the midst thereof.  

<span id="003">3</span> For there they that carried us away
[captive](../ebd/ebd072.htm#001.md) required of us a song; and they that
wasted us required of us mirth, saying, Sing us one of the
[songs](../ebd/ebd348.htm#002) of [Zion](../ebd/ebd394.htm#002.md).  

<span id="004">4</span> How shall we sing the LORD's song in a strange
land?  

<span id="005">5</span> If I forget thee, O
[Jerusalem](../ebd/ebd204.htm#005.md), let my right hand forget her
cunning.  

<span id="006">6</span> If I do not remember thee, let my tongue cleave
to the roof of my mouth; if I prefer not
[Jerusalem](../ebd/ebd204.htm#005.md) above my chief joy.  

<span id="007">7</span> Remember, O LORD, the children of
[Edom](../ebd/ebd113.htm#001.md) in the day of
[Jerusalem](../ebd/ebd204.htm#005.md); who said, Rase it, rase it, even to
the foundation thereof.  

<span id="008">8</span> O daughter of [Babylon](../ebd/ebd041.htm#001.md),
who art to be destroyed; happy shall he be, that rewardeth thee as thou
hast served us.  

<span id="009">9</span> Happy shall he be, that taketh and dasheth thy
little ones against the stones.
