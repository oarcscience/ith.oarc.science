# The Sikh Religion, Volume 1

### by Max Arthur MacAuliffe \[1842-1913\]

#### Oxford University Press \[1909\]

This is one of the first (and still one of the few) comprehensive books
about the Sikh religion in the English language. MacAuliffe had
extensive access to manuscripts of the Sikh sacred writings (the
Granth), as well as support from Sikh scholars and leaders of the time.
This volume covers Guru Nanak, the founder of Sikhism. This is volume
one of six; future volumes are forthcoming.
