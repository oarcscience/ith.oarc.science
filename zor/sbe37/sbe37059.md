### CHAPTER X.

*Sû*d*kar Nask*.

1\. The ninth fargar*d*, Yathâi*s* <span
id="fr_545"></span>[1](#fn_541.md), is about the devilry, the blighted
destiny, the complete pollution, the grievous stench, the heinous
sinfulness, and the annoyance *to* all spiritual and worldly virtue of
the sodomite. 2. The atonement for grievous sinfulness and the
appropriation of great good works by him who is a molester, and the
awful sinfulness of him who is a propitiator, of that sinner. 3. Of the
seven one mentions as evil, who are accounted equal to the evil spirit
in vileness—*such* as *Az*-î Dahâk <span id="fr_546"></span>[2](#fn_542.md)
in witchcraft, the serpent Srôbar in violence, Va*d*ak <span
id="fr_547"></span>[3](#fn_543.md) in producing evil progeny <span
id="fr_548"></span>[4](#fn_544.md), Tûr-î Brâ*d*ar-vakhsh in destroying a
righteous *man*, and an apostate <span id="fr_549"></span>[5](#fn_545.md)
in grievous sinfulness—the permitter and performer of

<span id="page_186">p. 186</span>

unnatural intercourse are unique in heinous sinfulness.

4\. Perfect is the excellence of righteousness.

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_541"></span>[185:1](sbe37059.htm#fr_545.md) The first word of
the sixth hâ of the first Gâtha (Yas. XXXIII, 1), here written yâsâî*s*
in Pahlavi in both MSS.

<span id="fn_542"></span>[185:2](sbe37059.htm#fr_546.md) See Bk. VIII,
Chaps. XIII, 8, XXXV, 13, and Dd. LXXII, 2-9, which last chapter
contains further details regarding these seven heinous sinners, probably
derived from the actual text of this ninth fargar*d* of the Sû*d*kar
Nask.

<span id="fn_543"></span>[185:3](sbe37059.htm#fr_547.md) The mother of
Dahâk (Dd. LXXII, 5), the same as Udaî in Bd. XXXI, 6; for her
viciousness see Dd. LXXVIII, 2.

<span id="fn_544"></span>[185:4](sbe37059.htm#fr_548.md) Pahl. saryâ
hûnŏ-dahakîh, which last word indicates an original Av. hunu*s*ta (see
Pahl. Yas. L, 10 b).

<span id="fn_545"></span>[185:5](sbe37059.htm#fr_549.md) Both MSS. have
Aharmanŏ, but this differs only in its last letter from aharmôk, 'an
apostate,' which is the reading of Dd. LXXII, 9 and more suitable to the
context.
