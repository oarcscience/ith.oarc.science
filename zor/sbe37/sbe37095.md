### CHAPTER XLVI.

*Var*s*tmânsar Nask*.

1\. The twenty-third fargar*d*, Airyaman <span
id="fr_1198"></span>[3](#fn_1186), is the Airman supplication <span
id="fr_1199"></span>[4](#fn_1187):—'That is the greatest, I tell thee, O
Spîtâmân! of the pure sayings of every kind, in so much Avesta lore,
this is the best, because it is given forth by him who is a very eminent
producer (madam-kârtar) of sayings of every kind. 2. Which *A*îrmân
supplication they should recite who are beneficial <span
id="fr_1200"></span>[5](#fn_1188), *and* the benefiter <span
id="fr_1201"></span>[5](#fn_1188), through the recital of it aloud, O
Spîtâmân! becomes predominant. 3. The evil spirit, who is heretical
(dû*s*dênŏ), O Zaratû*s*t! with his own creatures, O Spîtâmân! becomes
buried *in* the earth; the evil spirit is among *those* buried *in* the
earth—who are the demons—where their bodily form (kâlpu*d*ŏ) is
completely

<span id="page_303">p. 303</span>

shattered. 4. And up the dead are arrayed by it; through its assistance
they give life back unto the body, and the embodied life they *then*
possess is *such* that they do not die.'

5\. It is perfect *is* the excellence of righteousness; it is perfect
excellence *that is* righteousness.
