### CHAPTER XI.

1\. The tenth question is that which you ask thus: Does the growth which
increases become as commendable in the fourth night <span
id="fr_83"></span>[2](#fn_82.md) as the original good work <span
id="fr_84"></span>[3](#fn_83.md) in *his* possession, or does it become
otherwise?

2\. The reply is this, that *it* is otherwise; for the original good
work stands up opposing sin, and the growing good work <span
id="fr_85"></span>[4](#fn_84.md) stands up opposed to the growth of sin.

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_82"></span>[30:2](sbe18019.htm#fr_83.md) After death, when all
the immediate ceremonies for the dead have been completed (see Sls.

<span id="fn_83"></span>[30:3](sbe18019.htm#fr_84.md) Literally 'the good

XII, 1, where it is written ki*r*fakŏ-î bûn.

<span id="fn_84"></span>[30:4](sbe18019.htm#fr_85.md) Literally 'the good
work of growth.'
