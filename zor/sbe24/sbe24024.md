### CHAPTER XVIII.

1\. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Wherefore do people
consider these very little,

<span id="page_50">p. 50</span>

these four things which it is necessary for them to consider more, as
warnings (dakhshak), (3) the changeableness of the things of the worldly
*existence*, the death of the body, the account of the soul <span
id="fr_271"></span>[1](#fn_267), *and* the fear of hell?'

4\. The spirit of wisdom answered (5) thus: 'On account of the
delusiveness (nîyâ*z*ânîh) of the demon of greediness <span
id="fr_272"></span>[2](#fn_268), and of discontent.'
