### CHAPTER XXIX.

1\. The twenty-ninth subject is this, that, when they provide any
munificence (sa‘*h*âvat) *or* liberality, it is necessary that they
provide *it for* the worthy; and *one is* to consider thus: 'Is this
person, to whom I am giving this thing, worthy or not?'

2\. Therefore it is necessary to make an effort, so that they *may* not
give to the unworthy. 3. For in revelation, as regards <span
id="fr_1255"></span>[2](#fn_1245.md) that person who provides any
munificence for the unworthy, they call *it* a vain work *and* a gift
without advantage; and day by day *it* is <span
id="fr_1256"></span>[3](#fn_1246.md)

<span id="page_292">p. 292</span>

the punishment *and* torment of that person. 4. And, whatever they give
to the unworthy, they have made that thing extinct.
