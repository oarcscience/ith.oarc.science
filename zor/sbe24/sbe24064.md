### CHAPTER LVIII.

1\. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: Wherefore *is it* when
*one* turns the ignorance and foolishness of an ignorant king back to
knowledge and cleverness, on account of the sovereignty which is his;
(3) and, *as to* a poor man, who is wise <span
id="fr_570"></span>[1](#fn_562.md), *one* turns the knowledge and sagacity,
which are his, back to foolishness and uselessness, on account of the
poverty?'

4\. The spirit of wisdom answered (5) thus: 'On account of the deceit
and violence of the fiend <span id="fr_571"></span>[2](#fn_563.md) of
greediness (6) men utter more words as to the manliness of every one
whose wealth and power are more, and recount *his* deeds and actions
more fully; (7) but, in the eyes of the angels and archangels, a poor
*man* who is innocent and wise is better and more precious than a king
*or* opulent *man* <span id="fr_572"></span>[3](#fn_564.md) who is
ignorant.'
