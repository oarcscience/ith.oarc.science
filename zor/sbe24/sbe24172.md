### CHAPTER LXXXIV.

1\. The eighty-fourth subject is this, when they wish to sleep, it is
requisite to utter one Yathâ-ahû-vairyô *and* one Ashem-vohû <span
id="fr_1526"></span>[1](#fn_1515), and to accomplish repentance *one is*
to speak thus: 'I am sorrowing for, and repentant and in renunciation of
all that sin which I have spoken and *was* imagined by

<span id="page_349">p. 349</span>

me, and *has* assailed me; of these actions I am in renunciation <span
id="fr_1527"></span>[1](#fn_1516).'

2\. Afterwards *one is* to lie down; and every time that *one* acts *in*
the manner that I have mentioned, and wears the *sacred thread-*girdle
on the waist—while *he is* equally sharing the whole of the good works
which they are performing in all the world during that night, *and he
is* of similar merit <span id="fr_1528"></span>[2](#fn_1517)—every
single breath that he inhales and exhales is a good work of a weight of
three dirhams. 3. *And* when he turns from side to side he *should, in*
like manner, recite one Ashem-vohû <span
id="fr_1529"></span>[3](#fn_1518).
