### CHAPTER LXXXVI.

1\. The eighty-sixth subject is this, that it is not proper to kill a
beaver <span id="fr_1533"></span>[4](#fn_1522.md); *but*, if they see *it
in* any place, it is necessary to take *it* up and carry *it* to running
water. 2. For, in the commentary of the Vendîdâ*d* it is ranked <span
id="fr_1534"></span>[5](#fn_1523.md) *as* a great sin for the
*killer* <span id="fr_1535"></span>[6](#fn_1524.md); and, *as to* every one
who kills a beaver, the source of his seed becomes exhausted.
