### CHAPTER XXVIII.

1\. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Who is the more
forgiving (vakhshayânîktar)? 3. What is the more in strength? 4. What is
the swifter <span id="fr_354"></span>[1](#fn_349.md)? 5. What is the
happier? 6. What is the more miserable <span
id="fr_355"></span>[2](#fn_350.md)?'

7\. The spirit of wisdom answered (8) thus: 'Aûha*r*ma*zd*, the lord, is
the more forgiving. 9. He saw <span id="fr_356"></span>[3](#fn_351.md) the
nine thousand years’ mischief <span id="fr_357"></span>[4](#fn_352.md)
among his own creatures, owing to Aharman, yet afterwards, through
justice and forgiveness, he does not then smite him *for* it <span
id="fr_358"></span>[5](#fn_353.md).

10\. 'And the *celestial* sphere is the more in strength. 11. The
intellect <span id="fr_359"></span>[6](#fn_354.md) of mankind is the
swifter. 12. The souls of the righteous are the happier. 13. *And* those
of the wicked are the more penitent <span
id="fr_360"></span>[7](#fn_355.md).'
