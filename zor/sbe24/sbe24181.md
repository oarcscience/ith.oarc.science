### CHAPTER XCIII.

1\. The ninety-third subject is this, that it is necessary for all those
of the good religion to practise abstinence from uttering slander
(*gh*aîbat) behind *one's* back <span id="fr_1564"></span>[2](#fn_1553).

2\. In the commentary of the Vendîdâ*d* it states, that <span
id="fr_1565"></span>[3](#fn_1554) slander is the greatest of all sins.
3. Every one who perpetrates slander *about* any one is like him who has
eaten dead matter—and the eating of dead matter is a sin <span
id="fr_1566"></span>[4](#fn_1555) *that* has likewise been mentioned,
before this, in this book <span id="fr_1567"></span>[5](#fn_1556)—but
the statement is like this, while they do not indicate *any* punishment
for it in this world <span id="fr_1568"></span>[6](#fn_1557), it does
not go *without it* in that *other* world. 4. Therefore it is necessary
for those of the good religion to make an effort, so that they *may*
guard themselves from this slander.

5\. In order that they *may* show thy soul, *when* <span
id="fr_1569"></span>[7](#fn_1558) resigning life, the satisfaction
(i*g*zâ) for the sin, it

<span id="page_357">p. 357</span>

states, in the commentary of the Vendîdâ*d*, *as to* any one by whom
slander is perpetrated, if *the injured person* goes before him *and*
begs a righteous gift from him, and he provides a righteous gift for
that *person*, the sin departs from him.
