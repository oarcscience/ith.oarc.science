### CHAPTER LXIX.

1\. The sixty-ninth subject is this, that it is not proper that sunshine
*should* fall on a fire, for, every time that sunshine falls upon a
fire <span id="fr_1458"></span>[6](#fn_1447), it is a <span
id="fr_1459"></span>[7](#fn_1448) sin. 2. If thou expose a fire to the
sun it is a sin of three

<span id="page_335">p. 335</span>

stîrs <span id="fr_1460"></span>[1](#fn_1449); and, if thou set down
anything on the top of the fire, it is necessary that it *should* not
have any hole—so that the light (nûr) and strength of the fire *might*
become less—so far as thou knowest.
