### CHAPTER XIV.

1\. The fourteenth subject is this, that, when the nails *are pared*
according to custom (ba-‘*h*ilâl) <span
id="fr_1180"></span>[1](#fn_1171), it is necessary that they put *the
parings* into a paper. 2. And it is further necessary to take the
Srôsh-bâ*z* <span id="fr_1181"></span>[2](#fn_1172) *inwardly*, and *to
utter* three Yathâ-ahû-vairyôs <span id="fr_1182"></span>[3](#fn_1173).
3. And for the speaking *of this*—to say with each
Yathâ-ahû-vairyô <span id="fr_1183"></span>[4](#fn_1174)—the Avesta is
this:—Paiti tê, meregha Ashô-zu*s*ta! im*a*u srv*a*u vaêdhayêmi, im*a*u
srv*a*u âwaêdhayêmi; im*a*use tê srv*a*u, meregha Ashô-zu*s*ta! hyâre
ar*s*tayas*k*a, karetayas*k*a, thanvare*k*a, ishavas*k*a
erezifyô-parena, asna*k*a fradakhshanya paiti daêvô-Mâzainyãn <span
id="fr_1184"></span>[5](#fn_1175); ashâ vohû mananghâ yâ sruyê par*e*
magaonô <span id="fr_1185"></span>[6](#fn_1176). 4. Afterwards, *one*
completes the Bâ*z* in the manner that *it was* taken *inwardly*.

5\. At those two Yathâ-ahû-vairyôs, *with* which *one* completes the
Bâ*z*, at each one, he makes lines (‘*h*a*tt*hâ) *in* a little dust in
the midst of the nail*-parings*.

<span id="page_276">p. 276</span>

<span class="contnote">\[paragraph continues\]</span> 6. And, if he does
not know this Bâ*z* <span id="fr_1186"></span>[1](#fn_1177), on uttering
the Srôsh-bâ*z* and those three Yathâ-ahûvairyôs *he is* to furrow three
lines, with the nail-cutter <span id="fr_1187"></span>[2](#fn_1178),
around the nail*-parings*, *and* then *he is* to complete the Bâ*z* with
those Yathâ-ahû-vairyôs, *and* to put the dust, with the end of the
nail-cutter, into the midst of the nail-*parings*, and carry *them* to a
desert *spot*. 7. It is necessary that he *should* carry *a hole* down
*through* four finger*-breadths* of earth, and, *having* placed *the
nail-parings* in that spot, he puts the soil overhead.

8\. For Hôrmazd, the good *and* propitious, has created a bird which
they call Ashô-zu*s*t <span id="fr_1188"></span>[3](#fn_1179), and they
call *it* the bird of Bahman <span id="fr_1189"></span>[4](#fn_1180). 9.
They also call it the owl, *and* it eats nails.

10\. It is altogether necessary that they do not leave *them* unbroken,
for they *would* come into use as weapons (silâ‘h) of wizards <span
id="fr_1190"></span>[5](#fn_1181). 11. And they have also said that, if
they fall in the midst of food, *there* is danger of pulmonary
consumption.
