### CHAPTER LIII.

1\. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'How are the homage
and glorifying of the sacred beings to be performed?'

3\. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus:

<span id="page_96">p. 96</span>

<span class="contnote">\[paragraph continues\]</span> 'Every day three
times, standing opposite the sun and Mit*r*ô <span
id="fr_508"></span>[1](#fn_501.md), as they proceed together <span
id="fr_509"></span>[2](#fn_502), (5.md) and <span
id="fr_510"></span>[3](#fn_503.md) the moon and fire of Vâhrâm <span
id="fr_511"></span>[4](#fn_504.md), or the fire of fires <span
id="fr_512"></span>[5](#fn_505.md), *in* like manner, morning, noon, and
evening, homage and glorifying are performed, (6) and *one has* become
grateful <span id="fr_513"></span>[6](#fn_506.md). 7. And if a sin, or a
deficiency (frô*d*mând-1) <span id="fr_514"></span>[7](#fn_507.md), *has*
occurred, especially <span id="fr_515"></span>[8](#fn_508.md) as regards
the angels of the spiritual and worldly *existences*, then and beasts
*of burden* <span id="fr_516"></span>[9](#fn_509.md), oxen and sheep, dogs
and the dog species, and other creatures and creations of Aûha*r*ma*zd*
the lord, (8) *one* is to become sorrowful, penitent, and in
renunciation *of sin* before the sun and Mit*r*ô, the moon and the fire
of Aûha*r*ma*zd* <span id="fr_517"></span>[10](#fn_510); (9.md) and, *for*
the sake of atonement for the sin, good works are to be practised as
much as is well possible.'

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_501"></span>[96:1](sbe24059.htm#fr_508.md) The angel of the
sun's light (see Chap. II; 118 n).

<span id="fn_502"></span>[96:2](sbe24059.htm#fr_509.md) L19 adds 'homage
and glorifying are to be performed;' but this is unnecessary.

<span id="fn_503"></span>[96:3](sbe24059.htm#fr_510.md) L19 inserts
'opposite.'

<span id="fn_504"></span>[96:4](sbe24059.htm#fr_511.md) The sacred fire
(see Chap. XXXVI, 9 n).

<span id="fn_505"></span>[96:5](sbe24059.htm#fr_512.md) A fire in which the
remnants of all other fires are deposited from time to time.

<span id="fn_506"></span>[96:6](sbe24059.htm#fr_513.md) We ought probably
to read '*one* is to perform homage and glorifying, and to be grateful.'

<span id="fn_507"></span>[96:7](sbe24059.htm#fr_514.md) L19 omits 'or a
deficiency.'

<span id="fn_508"></span>[96:8](sbe24059.htm#fr_515.md) Reading fraêstô;
Nêr. reads pargast and translates 'somewhat.'

<span id="fn_509"></span>[96:9](sbe24059.htm#fr_516.md) L19 omits the
'beasts *of burden*.'

<span id="fn_510"></span>[96:10](sbe24059.htm#fr_517.md) These are four out
of the five existences to which the daily Nyâyi*s*es or supplications
are addressed.
