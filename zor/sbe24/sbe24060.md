### CHAPTER LIV.

1\. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: 'Wherefore *is it*
when an ignorant man—when they bring advancement to him—considers the
learning

<span id="page_97">p. 97</span>

and advancement of the wise and good mostly so <span
id="fr_518"></span>[1](#fn_511), through greediness, that to teach *it*
to him is difficult?'

3\. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus: 'For this reason, because
the ignorant man considers, in thought, his own ignorance as good as the
sage *does*, in thought, his own knowledge.'
