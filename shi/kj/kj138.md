## \[SECT. CXXXI.—EMPEROR RI-CHIU (PART I.—GENEALOGIES).\]

His Augustness Iza-ho-wake dwelt in the palace of Wakasakura at
Ihare, <span id="fr_2139"></span>[1](#fn_2134) and ruled the Empire.
This Heavenly Sovereign wedded Her Augustness Princess Kuro, <span
id="fr_2140"></span>[2](#fn_2135) daughter of the Noble of Ashida, <span
id="fr_2141"></span>[3](#fn_2136) child of So-tsu-biko \[287\] of
Kadzuraki, <span id="fr_2142"></span>[4](#fn_2137) and begot august
children: King Oshiha of Ichinobe; <span
id="fr_2143"></span>[5](#fn_2138) next King Mima: <span
id="fr_2144"></span>[6](#fn_2139) next his younger sister
Awomi-no-iratsume, <span id="fr_2145"></span>[7](#fn_2140) another name
for whom was Ichi-toyo-no-iratsume. <span
id="fr_2146"></span>[8](#fn_2141)

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_2134"></span>[357:1](kj138.htm#fr_2139) <span
id="page_357">p. 357</span> For *Ihare* see Sect. XLIII, Note 26.
*Waka-sakura* signifies "young cherry-tree." The origin of the name is
traced, rightly or wrongly, to an incident mentioned in the "Chronicles"
under the reign of this Emperor, 3rd year.

<span id="fn_2135"></span>[357:2](kj138.htm#fr_2140) *Kuro-hime* *i.e.*,
"black princess." The same name occurs several times, and has reference
to the black hair of the person so designated.

<span id="fn_2136"></span>[357:3](kj138.htm#fr_2141) *Ashida no sukune*.
*Ashida* signifies "reed-moor." It is the name of a place in Yamato.

<span id="fn_2137"></span>[357:4](kj138.htm#fr_2142) *Kadzuraki no
So-tsu-biko*. For this name, which is here abbreviated, see Sect. LXI,
Note 55.

<span id="fn_2138"></span>[357:5](kj138.htm#fr_2143) *Ichinobe no Oshiha
no miko*. Ichinobe is in the province of Yamashiro, and the name seems
to mean "near the market." The name of Oshiha refers to the "uneven
teeth" of this personage which are mentioned in Sect. CLXVII (near Note
5).

<span id="fn_2139"></span>[357:6](kj138.htm#fr_2144) *Mima no miko*. The
signification of this name is quite obscure.

<span id="fn_2140"></span>[357:7](kj138.htm#fr_2145) *Awomi no
iratsume*. *Awomi* is supposed by Motowori to be the name of a place.

<span id="fn_2141"></span>[357:8](kj138.htm#fr_2146) *Ihitoyo no
iratsume*. *Ihitoyo* is supposed by Motowori to be the name of a bird,
perhaps a kind of owl.
