### 64

### THE ASSISTANT IMPERIAL ADVISER SADA-YORI

### GON CHŪ-NAGON SADA-YORI

<div style="margin-left: 32px">

  Asaborake  
Uji no kawagiri  
  Tae-dae ni  
Araware wataru  
Seze no ajiro-gi.

SO thickly lies the morning mist,  
  That I can scarcely see  
The fish-nets on the river bank,  
  The River of Uji,  
  Past daybreak though it be.

</div>

<span class="small">The writer was the son of the author of verse No.
[55](hvj056.htm#page_55.md); he died in the year 1004. The River Uji is in
the Province of Omi, and drains into Lake Biwa. Seze is a village on the
lake-side, and a suburb of the larger town of Otsu. The poet, looking
across the river, can hardly make out the fish-nets on the shore at
Seze, because of the rising morning mist.</span>
