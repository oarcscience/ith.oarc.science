This novel, in the form of fictional journals and letters, was not the
first or even the best 19th Century vampire tale, but is today the best
known, due to the endless number of movies based on it. Today we know
that Dracula had a medieval counterpart, Vlad the Impaler, who lived in
Wallachia (which is to the south of Transylvania), today part of
Romania. Although not an actual vampire, Vlad was said to have spilled
quite a bit of blood in his day.--J.B. Hare
