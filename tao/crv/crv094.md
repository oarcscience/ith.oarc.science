### CHAPTER 9.

A German proverb says: "*Allzu scharf macht schartig*." This is a truth
which few learn, and so it is daily verified again and again in
business, in politics and in private life.

The word *rh* is a copula often translated "and" or "but." The character
depicts the side portions of the face, the whiskers, or the bristles of
an animal, thus denoting something added or an extension. The sense of
the chapter depends on the grammatical significance of this word, and we
can scarcely be mistaken when we translate "Grasp to

<span id="page_142">p. 142</span>

the full, is it not likely stopped? Scheme to being sharp, will you be
able long to guard \[your position\]?" The verb *jui* = scheme, means
"to scrutinize, to examine," and *pao* = "to guard, to maintain, to
protect, to defend."
