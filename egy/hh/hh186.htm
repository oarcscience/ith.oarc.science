CXIII. HOW A MAN THAT EATS UNSPARINGLY OF ANOTHER'S SUBSTANCE.

When they would symbolise a man that eats unsparingly 

p. 155

of another's substance, and afterwards consumes his own, they depict a POLYPUS; for, if he be in want of food from other things, he eats his own feelers.
