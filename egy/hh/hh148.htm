LXXV. HOW A MAN CALMED BY FIRE DURING ANGER.

 1

When they would denote a man calmed by fire even during 

p. 133

anger, they portray LIONS AND TORCHES; for the lion dreads nothing so much as lighted torches, and is tamed by nothing so readily as by them.

Footnotes

132:1 Treb. igne castigatum "chastised by fire."
