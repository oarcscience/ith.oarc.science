### VERSO COL. XIX.

\(1\) A spell for bringing a woman out of her house.

<span id="page_192">p. 192</span> <span id="page_193">p. 193</span>

\[paragraph continues\] You take a ....(2) of a wild she-cat; you dry
it; you take a heel-tendon (?) \[of a (?).... which has been (?)\] (3)
drowned; you fashion a ring the body (? bezel) of which is variegated
(?) with gold \[in the form of two (?)\] (4) lions, their mouths being
open, the face of each being turned to the other; you put some .... its
face (?). (5) If you wish to bring a woman to you at any time, you place
the ring on the upper part of a lamp, (6) which is lighted, you say,
'Bring N. daughter of N. to this place (7) in which I am, quickly in
these moments of to-day.'

Then she comes at once.
