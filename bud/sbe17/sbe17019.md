### 5.

1\. Now at that time the Bhikkhus who were sick had need of leaves as
medicine.

They told this thing to the Blessed One.

'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of leaves as

<span id="page_46">p. 46</span>

medicine--the leaves of the nimba <span id="fr_93"></span>[1](#fn_92.md),
of the ku*t*aga <span id="fr_94"></span>[1](#fn_92.md), of the
pa*t*ola <span id="fr_95"></span>[2](#fn_93.md), of the tulasi <span
id="fr_96"></span>[3](#fn_94.md), of the kappâsika <span
id="fr_97"></span>[4](#fn_95.md), and whatsoever other leaves are used for
medicine, and impart an appetising flavour to foods, either hard or
soft, which the sick Bhikkhus could not otherwise eat. They may be
received, and stored up (&c., as in last chapter, down to:) guilty of a
dukka*t*a offence.'
