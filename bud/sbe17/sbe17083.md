### 16.

1, 2. Now at that time Bhikkhus who had eaten sweet foods went to sleep
unmindful and unthoughtful. And they who had thus gone to sleep,
dreamed <span id="fr_417"></span>[1](#fn_409.md) . . . . . . . . . . . . .
. . . . . . . . .

3 'I allow, O Bhikkhus, for the protection of the body, and of the robe,
and of the sleeping-place, the use of a mat.'

4\. Now at that time the mat, being too short <span
id="fr_418"></span>[2](#fn_410.md), did not protect the whole of the
sleeping-place.

'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to have a covering made as large as you like.'

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_409"></span>[226:1](sbe17083.htm#fr_417.md) The remainder of
this introductory story scarcely bears translation. The first sentences
recur in the Sutta-vibhaṅga, Sa*m*ghâdisesa I, 2, I, and Pâ*k*ittiya V,
1, I.

<span id="fn_410"></span>[226:2](sbe17083.htm#fr_418.md) The length of a
mat (nisîdana*m*) was limited by the 89th Pâ*k*ittiya to two spans by
one.
