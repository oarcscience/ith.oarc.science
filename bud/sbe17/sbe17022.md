### 8.

1\. Now at that time the Bhikkhus who were sick had need of various
kinds of salt <span id="fr_108"></span>[5](#fn_106) as medicine. They
told this thing to the Blessed One.

'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of salts as

<span id="page_48">p. 48</span>

medicine--sea-salt <span id="fr_109"></span>[1](#fn_107), black
salt <span id="fr_110"></span>[2](#fn_108), rock salt <span
id="fr_111"></span>[3](#fn_109), kitchen salt <span
id="fr_112"></span>[4](#fn_110), red salt <span
id="fr_113"></span>[5](#fn_111), and whatsoever other salts are used in
medicine (&c., as in chap. 4, down to:) is guilty of a dukka*t*a
offence.'
