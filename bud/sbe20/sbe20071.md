### 19.

1\. Now at that time there was a very valuable rug, and a very valuable
piece of cloth, among the bedding furniture belonging to the Sa*m*gha.

They told this matter to the Blessed One <span
id="fr_619"></span>[3](#fn_609.md).

'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to barter either of these things in order to
increase the stock of legally permissible furniture <span
id="fr_620"></span>[4](#fn_610.md).'

Now at that time the Sa*m*gha had received a

<span id="page_218">p. 218</span>

bear-skin, and a *k*akkali <span id="fr_621"></span>[1](#fn_611.md) rug,
and a *k*olaka cloth <span id="fr_622"></span>[2](#fn_612.md).

They told this matter to the Blessed One.

'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to use them as mats to wipe your feet on <span
id="fr_623"></span>[3](#fn_613.md)'

<span id="fn_609"></span>[217:3](sbe20071.htm#fr_619.md) Because such
things were forbidden by Mahâvagga V, 10, 4, though kambala is not there
specially mentioned.

<span id="fn_610"></span>[217:4](sbe20071.htm#fr_620.md) Phâtikammatthâyâ
ti va*ddh*ikammatthâyâ ti. Vaddhikammatthâya phâtikamma*m* k’ ettha
samaka*m* vâ atireka*m* va agghanaka*m* ma*ñk*a-pî*th*âdi-senâsanam eva
va*tt*ati (B.).

<span id="fn_611"></span>[218:1](sbe20071.htm#fr_621.md) According to VI,
2, 2, and VI, 3, 5, this could also be used as a screen or curtain over
the space in a wall left for a window.

<span id="fn_612"></span>[218:2](sbe20071.htm#fr_622.md) According to
Mahâvagga VIII, 18, this might be used to wipe faces with; and according
to *K*ullavagga V, 9, 4, VI, 19, to place crockery or furniture on. The
word *k*ola means simply cotton cloth, but it is clear from these
passages that *k*olaka has some special connotation.

<span id="fn_613"></span>[218:3](sbe20071.htm#fr_623.md) Not to sit upon.
