### 18.

1\. Now at that time the Bhikkhus made use elsewhere of beds which were
appurtenances <span id="fr_616"></span>[2](#fn_606) to the Vihâra of a
certain lay-disciple (upâsaka).

Then that upâsaka murmured, &c.

They told the matter to the Blessed One.

'Things appurtenant to one place are not, O Bhikkhus, to be used in
another. Whosoever does so, shall be guilty of a dukka*t*a.'

Now at that time the Bhikkhus, fearing to offend if they took (things to
sit upon) even into the

<span id="page_217">p. 217</span>

<span class="contnote">\[paragraph continues\]</span> Uposatha Hall, or
the meeting-place, sat on the ground; and their legs and robes got
soiled.

They told the matter to the Blessed One.

'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to take things away for a certain time
only <span id="fr_617"></span>[1](#fn_607).'

Now at that time a large Vihâra belonging to the Sa*m*gha went to
ruin <span id="fr_618"></span>[2](#fn_608). The Bhikkhus, fearing to
offend, did not take the bedding in it away.

They told this matter to the Blessed One.

'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to take away things in order to save them from
destruction.'
