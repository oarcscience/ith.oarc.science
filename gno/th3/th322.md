#### EXCERPT XXI.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### OF ISIS TO HORUS

<span class="small">(Title in Patrizzi (p. 45) is “From Isis.”</span>

<span class="small">Text: Stob., *Flor*., xiii. 50, under the heading:
“Of Hermes from the \[Sermon\] of Isis to Horus”; G. i. 328; M. i. 265;
H. iii. 467.</span>

<span class="small">Schow gives another heading, which Gaisford (in a
note) thinks is from the Vienna codex, namely: “Of Hermes from the
Intercession (or Supplication,—Πρεσβείας) of Isis.” <span
id="fr_285"></span>[1](#fn_285.md)</span>

<span class="small">Ménard, Livre IV., No. ii. of “Fragments Divers,” p.
272.)</span>

A <span class="smallcaps">refutation</span>, when it is recognized, O
greatest King, carries the man who is refuted towards the desire of
things he did not know before.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### COMMENT

This fragment is clearly not in the style of the excerpt from the
“Sermon of Isis to Hermes” (Ex. xxvii.); it is far more closely
reminiscent of *C. H.*, xvi. or xvii., and is, therefore, probably from
the Sermon of Asclepius to the King.
