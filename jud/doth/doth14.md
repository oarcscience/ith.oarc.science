### <span class="smallcaps">Faith without Knowledge</span>

<span class="smallcaps">The</span> faith of the believer is not complete
unless he knows the meaning and the reasons of his belief. And this
knowledge that enables one to fulfil the duties of the heart, is the
hidden wisdom that is the light of hearts and the bright effulgence of
souls; and concerning it Scripture says:—(Ps. li. 6) "Behold Thou
desirest truth in the inward parts and in the hidden, Thou wilt make me
to know wisdom." It is only those of weak intellect who are not culpable
if they take on trust what it is man's duty to search out. But whoever
has the strength of

<span id="page_23">p. 23</span>

intellect, and the power to sift and prove, is sinful if he neglects to
do so, and also even his lack of knowledge is a sin.
