#### Prayer on a Ship or on a Raft.

<span class="margnote">**M**.</span>IV. 6. Should one be sitting in a
ship, or on a raft, <span id="fr_200"></span>[3](#fn_199.md) let him direct
his intention towards the most Holy Sanctuary.

<span id="page_33">p. 33</span>

<span class="margnote">B.</span>

**T**. III. 19. If a man wakes up early to go on a journey, behold, he
takes a trumpet <span id="fr_201"></span>[1](tbr32.htm#fn_200.md) and blows
it, a Lulab <span id="fr_202"></span>[2](tbr32.htm#fn_201.md) and shakes
it, a roll of the Book of Esther <span
id="fr_203"></span>[3](tbr32.htm#fn_202.md) and reads in it, and prays (the
Eighteen). Then when the time of reciting the Shma‘ arrives, behold, he
recites it. <span id="fr_204"></span>[4](tbr32.htm#fn_203.md) If he wakes
up early to sit in a waggon or in a ship, behold, he prays (the
Eighteen). Then when the time of reciting the Shma‘ arrives, behold, he
recites it.
