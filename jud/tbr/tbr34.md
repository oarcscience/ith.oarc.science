#### The Mention of, and the Petition for, Rain, also the Habdalah, in the Prayer.

<span class="margnote">**M**.</span>V. 2. They make mention of <span
id="fr_250"></span>[3](#fn_249) the powers of the M. rains <span
id="fr_251"></span>[4](#fn_250) in the \[Benediction of\] the
resurrection of the dead, <span id="fr_252"></span>[5](#fn_251) and they
ask for rains in the Benediction of the years <span
id="fr_253"></span>[6](#fn_252) and \[they add\] the *Habdalah* <span
id="fr_254"></span>[7](#fn_253) in "Thou that graciously givest
knowledge." <span id="fr_255"></span>[8](#fn_254) R. Aqiba used to say:
One says it (the Habdalah) as a fourth Benediction by itself. R. Eliezer
used to say: With the *Hoda’ah*. <span id="fr_256"></span>[9](#fn_255)

**T**. III. 9. If he has not made mention of the power of the rains in
\[the Benediction of\] the Resurrection of the dead, and has not asked
for the rains in the Benediction of the years, he must be turned back.

If he has not said the Habdalah at "Thou that graciously givest
knowledge," he says it over the.

<span id="page_40">p. 40</span>

<span class="margnote">**T**.</span>

cup. <span id="fr_257"></span>[1](tbr35.htm#fn_256) If he has not said
it \[there\] he must be turned back.

R. Jose said: Also he who has not made mention of \[the covenant\] in
the thanksgiving for the land <span
id="fr_258"></span>[2](tbr35.htm#fn_257) must be turned back.

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_249"></span>[39:3](tbr34.htm#fr_250) *make mention of*. In
contrast to direct petition, see note 5.

<span id="fn_250"></span>[39:4](tbr34.htm#fr_251) *the powers of the
rains*. So literally, but the phrase connotes the manifestations of the
Divine power in sending rain.

<span id="fn_251"></span>[39:5](tbr34.htm#fr_252) *the resurrection of
the dead*. The words "Thou causest the wind to blow and the rain to
fall" are inserted in the second Benediction of the Eighteen during the
winter months (SA, P. 44).

<span id="fn_252"></span>[39:6](tbr34.htm#fr_253) *the years*. The
petition for rain is used in the ninth Benediction from Dec. 4 to the
first day of Passover (SA, p. 47).

<span id="fn_253"></span>[39:7](tbr34.htm#fr_254) *the Habdalah*. A
short Benediction at the conclusion of sabbath. See Staerk, p. 26; *cf*.
SA, pp. 216 *sq*., *Jewish Encyclopedia*, vi. pp. 118-121.

<span id="fn_254"></span>[39:8](tbr34.htm#fr_255) *knowledge*. The
fourth Benediction (SA, p. 46).

<span id="fn_255"></span>[39:9](tbr34.htm#fr_256) *the Hoda’ah*, [p.
34](tbr32.htm#page_34) note.
