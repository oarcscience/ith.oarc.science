#### Gamaliel: Other Incidents of his Infraction of Traditional Teaching.

<span class="margnote">**M**.</span>II. 7 (6). He bathed the first night
after the death of his wife. They said to him: Didst thou not teach us,
that a mourner is forbidden to bathe?

<span id="page_21">p. 21</span>

<span class="contnote">\[paragraph continues\]</span> <span
class="margnote">**M**.</span>He said to them: I am not like all other
men; <span id="fr_136"></span>[1](#fn_136.md) I am weakly. <span
id="fr_137"></span>[2](#fn_137.md)

II\. 8 (7 *cont*.). And when his slave Tabi died he accepted
condolence <span id="fr_138"></span>[3](#fn_138.md) for him. They said to
him: Didst thou not teach us, that men do not accept condolence for
slaves? He said to them: My slave Tabi was not like all other slaves; he
was a worthy man.

<span id="fn_138"></span>[21:3](tbr20.htm#fr_138.md) *condolence*. In the
formal utterance of prayers for the mourners by those who visited them
during the week of mourning. For examples of such prayers used to-day
see SA, p. 324. R. Eliezer refused to accept such condolence from his
disciples, and said that only the same phrases should be used as at the
death of an ox, "God make up to thee thy loss," but other Rabbis did
permit more to be said when the male or female slave was pious (Gemara
T.B. 18<sup>b</sup>). Cf. *infra*. [p. 42](tbr36.htm#page_42.md).
