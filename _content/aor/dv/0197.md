### 197.

A luminous body which is long and narrow in shape gives more confused
outlines to the derived shadow than a spherical light, and this
contradicts the proposition next following: A shadow will have its
outlines more clearly defined in proportion as it is nearer to the
primary shadow or, I should say, the body casting the shadow;  <span
id="fr_98"></span>[96](#fn_98) the cause of this is the elongated form
of the luminous body *a c*, &c  <span id="fr_99"></span>[97](#fn_99) .
