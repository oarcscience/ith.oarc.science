### 1448.

An algebra, which the Marliani have, written by their father,  <span
id="fr_457"></span>[763](#fn_457) --

On the bone, by the Marliani,--

On the bone which penetrates, Gian Giacomo of Bellinzona, to draw out
the nail with facility,--

The measurement of Boccalino,--

The measurement of Milan and the suburbs,  <span
id="fr_458"></span>[764](#fn_458) --

A book, treating of Milan and its churches which is to be had at the
last stationer's on the way to Corduso  <span
id="fr_459"></span>[765](#fn_459) ,--

The measurement of the Corte Vecchia,--

The measurement of the Castle,--

Get the master of arithmetic to show you how to square a....,--

Get Messer Fazio to show you \[the book\] on proportion,--

<span id="page_435">p. 435</span>

Get the Friar di Brera to show you \[the book\] '*de Ponderibus*',--
 <span id="fr_460"></span>[766](#fn_460)

Of the measurement of San Lorenzo,-- <span
id="fr_461"></span>[767](#fn_461)

I lent certain groups to Fra Filippo de Brera,--  <span
id="fr_462"></span>[768](#fn_462)

Memorandum: to ask Maestro Giovannino as to the mode in which the tower
of Ferrara is walled without loopholes,--

Ask Maestro Antonio how mortars are placed on bastions by day or by
night,--

Ask Benedetto Portinari how the people go on the ice in Flanders,--
 <span id="fr_463"></span>[769](#fn_463)

On proportions by Alchino, with notes by Marliano, from Messer Fazio,--

The measurement of the sun, promised me by Maestro Giovanni, the
Frenchman,--

The cross bow of Maestro Gianetto,--

The book by Giovanni Taverna that Messer Fazio,--

You will draw Milan \[21\],--

The measurement of the canal, locks and supports, and large boats; and
the expense,--

Plan of Milan  <span id="fr_464"></span>[770](#fn_464) ,--

Groups by Bramante  <span id="fr_465"></span>[771](#fn_465) ,--

The book on celestial phenomena by Aristoteles, in Italian  <span
id="fr_466"></span>[772](#fn_466) ,--

Try to get Vitolone, which is in the library at Pavia  <span
id="fr_467"></span>[773](#fn_467) and which treats of Mathematics,--He
had a master \[learned\] in waterworks and get him to explain the
repairs and the costs, and a lock and a canal and a mill in the Lombard
fashion.

A grandson of Gian Angelo's, the painter has a book on water which was
his fathers.

Paolino Scarpellino, called Assiolo has great knowledge of water works.

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_457"></span>[434:763](1448.htm#fr_457) 1: *Marliani*, an
old Milanese family, now extinct.

<span id="fn_458"></span>[434:764](1448.htm#fr_458) 5: 21. See [Pl.
CIX](pl109.htm#img_pl109) and No. 1016.

<span id="fn_459"></span>[434:765](1448.htm#fr_459) 6: *Corduso*, see
No. 1413, note.

<span id="fn_460"></span>[435:766](1448.htm#fr_460) : 11. 13. *Brera*,
now *Palazzo delle Scienze ed Arti*. Until 1571 it was the monastery of
the order of the Umiliati and afterwards of the Jesuits.

*De ponderibus*, compare No. 1436, 3.

<span id="fn_461"></span>[435:767](1448.htm#fr_461) : 12. *Sco Lorenzo*.
A church at Milan, see pp. 39, 40 and 50.

<span id="fn_462"></span>[435:768](1448.htm#fr_462) : 13. 24. *Gruppi*.
See Vol. I p. 355, No. 600, note 9.

<span id="fn_463"></span>[435:769](1448.htm#fr_463) : 16. The
*Portinari* were one of the great merchant- families of Florence.

<span id="fn_464"></span>[435:770](1448.htm#fr_464) 23: *Fondamento* is
commonly used by Leonardo to mean ground-plan. See for instance p. 53.

<span id="fn_465"></span>[435:771](1448.htm#fr_465) 24: *Gruppi*. See
Vol. I p. 355, No. 600, note 9.

<span id="fn_466"></span>[435:772](1448.htm#fr_466) 25: *Meteora*. By
this Leonardo means no doubt the four books τὰ μετεωρολογιχά. He must
refer here to a MS. translation, as no Italian translation is known to
have been published (see No. 1477 note).

<span id="fn_467"></span>[435:773](1448.htm#fr_467) 26: *Vitolone* see
No. 1506, note.

*Libreria di Pavia*. One of the most famous of Italian libraries. After
the victory of Novara in April 1500, Louis XII had it conveyed to
France, '*come trofeo di vittoria*'!
