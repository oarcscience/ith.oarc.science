### VI.

### THE STARS.

<span class="margnote"> On the light of the stars (911-913).</span>The
stars are visible by night and not by day, because we are beneath the
dense atmosphere, which is full of innumerable particles of moisture,
each of which independently, when the rays of the sun fall upon it,
reflects a radiance, and so these numberless bright particles conceal
the stars; and if it were not for this atmosphere the sky would always
display the stars against its darkness.  <span
id="fr_153"></span>[487](#fn_153)
