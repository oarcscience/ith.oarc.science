### 163.

<span id="page_94">p. 94</span>

<span class="margnote"> On the relation of derived and primary shadow
(163-165).</span>The derived shadow can never resemble the body from
which it proceeds unless the light is of the same form and size as the
body causing the shadow.

The derived shadow cannot be of the same form as the primary shadow
unless it is intercepted by a plane parallel to it.
