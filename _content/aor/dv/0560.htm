560.

ON PAINTING.

The juxtaposition of light and shade (560, 561).The comparison of the various qualities of shadows and lights not infrequently seems ambiguous and confused to the painter who desires to imitate and copy the objects he sees. The reason is this: If you see a white drapery side by side with a black one, that part of the white drapery which lies against the black one will certainly look much whiter than the part which lies against something whiter than itself.  263 And the reason of this is shown in my [book on] perspective.
