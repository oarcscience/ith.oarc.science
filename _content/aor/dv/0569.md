*The Da Vinci Notebooks at Intangible Textual Heritage*

### 569.

Whenever a figure is placed at a considerable distance you lose first
the distinctness of the smallest parts; while the larger parts are left
to the last, losing all distinctness of detail and outline; and what
remains is an oval or spherical figure with confused edges.
