[BOOK IV CHAPTER I](mart061) <span class="small">How Merlin was assotted
and doted on one of the ladies of the lake, and how he was shut in a
rock under a stone and there died.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER II](mart062) <span class="small">How five kings came
into this land to war against King Arthur, and what counsel Arthur had
against them.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER III](mart063) <span class="small">How King Arthur had
ado with them and overthrew them, and slew the five kings and made the
remnant to flee.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER IV](mart064) <span class="small">How the battle was
finished or he came, and how King Arthur founded an abbey where the
battle was.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER V](mart065) <span class="small">How Sir Tor was made
knight of the Round Table, and how Bagdemagus was displeased.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER VI](mart066) <span class="small">How King Arthur, King
Uriens, and Sir Accolon of Gaul, chased an hart, and of their marvellous
adventures.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER VII](mart067) <span class="small">How Arthur took upon
him to fight to be delivered out of prison, and also for to deliver
twenty knights that were in prison.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER VIII](mart068) <span class="small">How Accolon found
himself by a well, and he took upon him to do battle against
Arthur.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER IX](mart069) <span class="small">Of the battle between
King Arthur and Accolon.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER X](mart070) <span class="small">How King Arthur's sword
that he fought with brake, and how he recovered of Accolon his own sword
Excalibur, and overcame his enemy.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XI](mart071) <span class="small">How Accolon confessed
the treason of Morgan le Fay, King Arthur's sister, and how she would
have done slay him.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XII](mart072) <span class="small">How Arthur accorded
the two brethren, and delivered the twenty knights, and how Sir Accolon
died.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XIII](mart073) <span class="small">How Morgan would
have slain Sir Uriens her husband, and how Sir Uwaine her son saved
him.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XIV](mart074) <span class="small">How Queen Morgan le
Fay made great sorrow for the death of Accolon, and how she stole away
the scabbard from Arthur.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XV](mart075) <span class="small">How Morgan le Fay
saved a knight that should have been drowned, and how King Arthur
returned home again.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XVI](mart076) <span class="small">How the Damosel of
the Lake saved King Arthur from mantle that should have burnt
him.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XVII](mart077) <span class="small">How Sir Gawaine and
Sir Uwaine met with twelve fair damosels, and how they complained on Sir
Marhaus.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XVIII](mart078) <span class="small">How Sir Marhaus
jousted with Sir Gawaine and Sir Uwaine, and overthrew them
both.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XIX](mart079) <span class="small">How Sir Marhaus, Sir
Gawaine, and Sir Uwaine met three damosels, and each of them took
one.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XX](mart080) <span class="small">How a knight and a
dwarf strove for a lady.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XXI](mart081) <span class="small">How King Pelleas
suffered himself to be taken prisoner because he would have a sight of
his lady, and how Sir Gawaine promised him to get to him the love of his
lady.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XXII](mart082) <span class="small">How Sir Gawaine came
to the Lady Ettard, and how Sir Pelleas found them sleeping.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XXIII](mart083) <span class="small">How Sir Pelleas
loved no more Ettard by means of the Damosel of the Lake, whom he loved
ever after.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XXIV](mart084) <span class="small">How Sir Marhaus rode
with the damosel, and how he came to the Duke of the South
Marches.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XXV](mart085) <span class="small">How Sir Marhaus
fought with the duke and his four sons and made them to yield
them.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XXVI](mart086) <span class="small">How Sir Uwaine rode
with the damosel of sixty year of age, and how he gat the prize at
tourneying.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XXVII](mart087) <span class="small">How Sir Uwaine
fought with two knights and overcame them.</span>  
[BOOK IV CHAPTER XXVIII](mart088) <span class="small">How at the year's
end all three knights with their three damosels met at the
fountain.</span>
