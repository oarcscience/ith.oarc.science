<td data-valign="top"><p>IONNLAIDH mi m’ aodann<br />
’S na naoi gatha greine,<br />
Mar a dh’ ionnlaid Moir a Mac,<br />
     Am bainne bragh na breine.</p>
<p>Mil a bhi ’na m’ bheul,<br />
Seirc a bhi ’na m’ aodann;<br />
An gaol thug Moire dha Mac<br />
     Bhi an cridhe gach cairc domhsa.</p>
<p>Gum bu suileach, cluasach, briathrach Dia,<br />
Da m’ riarachadh, is da m’ neartachadh;<br />
Gum bu dall, bodhar, balbh, sion sior,<br />
     Mo luchd tair is mo luchd taimhlis.</p>
<p>Teanga Chalum-chille ’na mo cheann,<br />
Agall Chalum-chille ’na mo chainn;<br />
Foisneachd Mhic bhuadhaich nan gras<br />
     Dhol thugam-sa an lathair sluaigh.</p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p> </p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p>I BATHE my face<br />
In the nine rays of the sun,<br />
As Mary bathed her Son<br />
     In the rich fermented milk.</p>
<p>Honey be in my mouth,<br />
Affection be in my face;<br />
The love that Mary gave her Son<br />
     Be in the heart of all flesh for me.</p>
<p>All-seeing, all-hearing, all-inspiring may God be,<br />
To satisfy and to strengthen me;<br />
Blind, deaf, and dumb, ever, ever be<br />
     My contemners and my mockers,</p>
<p>The tongue of Columba in my head,<br />
The eloquence of Columba in my speech;<br />
The composure of the Victorious Son of grace<br />
     Be mine in presence of the multitude.</p></td>
