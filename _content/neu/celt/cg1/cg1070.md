<td data-valign="top"><p>THAINE sinne chon an doruis,<br />
Feuch am feairrde sinn an turas,<br />
Dh’ innis a mhnathan coir a bhaile,<br />
Gur e maireach La Cullaig.</p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p> </p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p>WE are come to the door,<br />
To see if we be the better of our visit,<br />
To tell the generous women of the townland<br />
That to-morrow is Calendae Day.</p></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td colspan="3" data-valign="top"><p><span class="small">After being entertained the guisers go sunwise <span id="page_157">p. 157</span> round the fire singing--</span></p></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td data-valign="top"><p>Gum beannaicheadh Dia an t-ardrach,<br />
Eadar chlach, is chuaille, is chrann,<br />
Eadar bhithe, bhliochd, is aodach,<br />
Slainte dhaoin bhi daonnan ann,</p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p> </p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p>May God bless the dwelling,<br />
Each stone, and beam, and stave,<br />
All food, and drink, and clothing,<br />
May health of men he always there.</p></td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td colspan="3" data-valign="top">Should the guisers be inhospitably treated, they file round the fire withershins and walk out, and raise a cairn in or near the door, called 'carnan mollachd,' cairn of malison, 'carnan cronachd,' scaith cairn. <span id="page_157">p. 157</span>
<p>They tramp loudly, shaking the dust of the place off their feet, and intoning with a deep voice the following and other maledictions:--</p></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td data-valign="top"><p>Mallachd Dhe is Challaig oirbh,<br />
’S cronachd chlaimhein chiuchaich,<br />
Fioinn, fithich agus fiolair,<br />
’S cronachd sionnaich liugaich.</p>
<p>Cronachd chon is chat oirbh,<br />
Thorc is bhroc is bhrugha,<br />
Mhaghain mais ’s mhadaidh-alla,<br />
’S cronachd thaghain tutaidh.</p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p> </p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p>The malison of God and of Hogmanay be on you,<br />
And the scath of the plaintive buzzard,<br />
Of the hen-harrier, of the raven, of the eagle,<br />
And the scath of the sneaking fox.</p>
<p>The scath of the dog and of the cat be on you,<br />
Of the boar, of the badger, and of the 'brugha,'<br />
Of the hipped bear and of the wild wolf,<br />
And the scath of the foul foumart.</p></td>
</tr>
