<span class="small">THE Soul Shrine is sung by the people as they retire
to rest. They say that the angels of heaven guard them in sleep and
shield them from harm. Should any untoward event <span id="page_91">p.
91</span> occur to themselves or to their flocks, they avow that the
cause was the deadness of their hearts, the coldness of their faith, and
the fewness of their prayers.</span>

<td data-valign="top"><p> </p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p>GOD, give charge to Thy blessed angels,<br />
     To keep guard around this stead to-night,<br />
A band sacred, strong, and steadfast,<br />
     That will shield this soul-shrine from harm.</p>
<p>Safeguard Thou, God, this household to-night,<br />
     Themselves and their means and their fame,<br />
Deliver them from death, from distress, from harm,<br />
     From the fruits of envy and of enmity.</p>
<p>Give Thou to us, O God of peace,<br />
     Thankfulness despite our loss,<br />
To obey Thy statutes here below,<br />
     And to enjoy Thyself above.</p></td>
