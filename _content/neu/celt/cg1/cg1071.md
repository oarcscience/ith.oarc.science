<span class="small">THIS poem was repeated the first thing on the first
day of the year. It was <span id="page_159">p. 159</span> common
throughout the Highlands and Islands. The writer has heard versions of
it in many places.</span>

<td data-valign="top"><p>DHE, beannaich dhomh an la ur,<br />
Nach do thuradh dhomh roimhe riamh;<br />
Is ann gu beannachadh do ghnuis,<br />
Thug thu ’n uine seo dhomh, a Dhia.</p>
<p>Beannaich thusa dhomh mo shuil,<br />
Beannaicheadh mo shuil na chi;<br />
Beannaichidh mise mo nabaidh,<br />
Beannaicheadh mo nabaidh mi.</p>
<p>Dhe tabhair dhomh-sa cridhe glan,<br />
Na leig a seall do shula mi;<br />
Beannaich dhomh mo ghin ’s mo bhean,<br />
’S beannaich domh mo nearc ’s mo ni.</p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p> </p></td>
<td data-valign="top"><p>GOD, bless to me the new day,<br />
Never vouchsafed to me before;<br />
It is to bless Thine own presence<br />
Thou hast given me this time, O God.</p>
<p>Bless Thou to me mine eye,<br />
May mine eye bless all it sees;<br />
I will bless my neighbour,<br />
May my neighbour bless me.</p>
<p>God, give me a clean heart,<br />
Let me not from sight of Thine eye;<br />
Bless to me my children and my wife,<br />
And bless to me my means and my cattle.</p></td>
