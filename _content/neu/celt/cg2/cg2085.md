<span class="small">THE following lines are whispered by mothers into
the ears of sons and daughters when leaving their homes in the Outer
Isles for the towns of the south and for foreign lands. <span
id="page_171">p. 171</span> Probably they are the last accents of the
mother's voice--heard in the far-away home among the hills clothed with
mist or on the machair washed by the sea--that linger on the Gaelic ear
as it sinks in the sleep that knows no waking.</span>
