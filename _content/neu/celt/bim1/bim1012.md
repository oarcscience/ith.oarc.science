### AMMIANUS MARCELLINUS, A.D. 380.

Ammianus Marcellinus says that "the Bards record the exploits of heroes,
in poems, which they sing to the soft sound of the lyre," <span
id="fr_32"></span>[1](#fn_32) which is quite in accordance with the
practice of the Cymric Bards. He also observes that the Druids were
similar to the Pythagoreans, as indeed they were with reference to the
doctrine of the metempsychosis.
