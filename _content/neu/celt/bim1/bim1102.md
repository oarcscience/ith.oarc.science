### ABRED.

To consociate with evil will make one the lowest and meanest of all
animated beings; therefore a wicked man,

<span id="page_244">p. 244</span> <span id="page_245">p. 245</span>

when he dies, and his soul enters the meanest worm <span
id="fr_256"></span>[1](#fn_254) in existence, becomes better, and
ascends in the migration of Abred. From this has arisen the
saying,--"Trample not on thy better," addressed to one who tramples on a
worm voluntarily, and without a cause.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_254"></span>[245:1](bim1102.htm#fr_256) <span
id="page_244">p. 244</span> "Pryf," a worm, originally written *prif*,
probably obtained its name from its being the first *prif* =
*primus*--link in the chain of Abred.

There seems to be some allusion to the doctrine enunciated here in those
lines by Casnodyn, A.D. 1290-1340;

<div style="margin-left: 32px">

Trefnaist wern uffern affaith sathan;  
Trefred i *bryfed*, lle yd ymbrofan’.

Thou didst prepare the slough of hell suitable for Satan;  
The habitation for worms, where they will be in mutual strife.

</div>
