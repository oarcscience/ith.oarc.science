#### The Canterbury Tales

### The Franklin's Prologue

<span id="an_709"></span> Thise olde gentil Britouns in hir dayes  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_710">710</span></span> Of diverse
aventures maden layes,  
<span id="an_711"></span> Rymeyed in hir firste Briton tonge,  
<span id="an_712"></span> Whiche layes with hir instrumentz they songe  
<span id="an_713"></span> Or elles redden hem for hir plesaunce;  
<span id="an_714"></span> And oon of hem have I in remembraunce,  
<span id="an_715"></span> Which I shal seyn with good wyl as I kan.  
<span id="an_716"></span> But, sires, by cause I am a burel man,  
<span id="an_717"></span> At my bigynnyng first I yow biseche,  
<span id="an_718"></span> Have me excused of my rude speche.  
<span id="an_719"></span> I lerned nevere rethorik, certeyn;  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_720">720</span></span> Thyng that I
speke, it moot be bare and pleyn.  
<span id="an_721"></span> I sleep nevere on the Mount of Pernaso,  
<span id="an_722"></span> Ne lerned Marcus Tullius Scithero.  
<span id="an_723"></span> Colours ne knowe I none, withouten drede,  
<span id="an_724"></span> But swiche colours as growen in the mede,  
<span id="an_725"></span> Or elles swiche as men dye or peynte.  
<span id="an_726"></span> Colours of rethoryk been to me queynte;  
<span id="an_727"></span> My spirit feeleth noght of swich mateere.  
<span id="an_728"></span> But if yow list, my tale shul ye heere.
