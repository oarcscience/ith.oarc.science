#### A Treatise on the Astrolabe

### Part 2

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_Rub">Rub</span></span> To fynde the
degre in which the sonne is  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubA">RubA</span></span> day by day,
after his cours aboute.  
<span id="an_1"></span> Rekne and knowe which is the day of thy  
<span id="an_2"></span> month, and ley thy rewle upon that same day,  
<span id="an_3"></span> and than wol the verrey poynt of thy rewle  
<span id="an_4"></span> sitten in the bordure upon the degre of thy  
<span id="an_5"></span> sonne.  
<span id="an_6"></span> Ensample as thus: The yeer of oure Lord  
<span id="an_7"></span> 1391, the 12 day of March at midday, I wolde  
<span id="an_8"></span> knowe the degre of the sonne. I soughte in  
<span id="an_9"></span> the bakhalf of myn Astrelabie and fond the  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_10">10</span></span> cercle of the
daies, the whiche I knowe by  
<span id="an_11"></span> the names of the monthes writen under the  
<span id="an_12"></span> same cercle. Tho leyde I my reule over this  
<span id="an_13"></span> foreseide day, and fond the point of my reule  
<span id="an_14"></span> in the bordure upon the firste degre of
Aries,  
<span id="an_15"></span> a litel within the degre. And thus knowe I
this  
<span id="an_16"></span> conclusioun.  
<span id="an_17"></span> Anothir day I wolde knowen the degre of  
<span id="an_18"></span> my sonne, and this was at midday in the 13  
<span id="an_19"></span> day of Decembre. I fond the day of the  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_20">20</span></span> month in manere
as I seide; tho leide I my  
<span id="an_21"></span> rewle upon this forseide 13 day, and fond  
<span id="an_22"></span> the point of my rewle in the bordure upon  
<span id="an_23"></span> the firste degre of Capricorne a lite within
the  
<span id="an_24"></span> degre. And than had I of this conclusioun the  
<span id="an_25"></span> ful experience.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_Rub">Rub</span></span> To knowe the
altitude of the sonne or of  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubA">RubA</span></span> othre
celestial bodies.  
<span id="an_1"></span> Put the ryng of thyn Astrelabie upon thy  
<span id="an_2"></span> right thombe, and turne thi lift syde ageyn  
<span id="an_3"></span> the light of the sonne; and remewe thy rewle  
<span id="an_4"></span> up and doun til that the stremes of the sonne  
<span id="an_5"></span> shine thorugh bothe holes of thi rewle. Loke  
<span id="an_6"></span> than how many degrees thy rule is areised fro  
<span id="an_7"></span> the litel cros upon thin est lyne, and tak
there  
<span id="an_8"></span> the altitude of thi sonne. And in this same  
<span id="an_9"></span> wise maist thow knowe by night the altitude  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_10">10</span></span> of the mone or
of brighte sterres.  
<span id="an_11"></span> This chapitre is so generall evere in oon  
<span id="an_12"></span> that there nedith no more declaracioun; but  
<span id="an_13"></span> forget it not.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_Rub">Rub</span></span> To knowe
every tyme of the day by light  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubA">RubA</span></span> of the
sonne; and every tyme of the nyght by  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubB">RubB</span></span> the sterres
fixe; and eke to knowe by nyght or  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubC">RubC</span></span> by day the
degre of eny signe that ascendith on  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubD">RubD</span></span> the est
orisonte, which that is clepid comounly  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubE">RubE</span></span> the
ascendent, or ellis horoscopum.  
<span id="an_1"></span> Tak the altitude of the sonne whan the list,  
<span id="an_2"></span> as I have seid, and set the degre of the
sonne,  
<span id="an_3"></span> in caas that it be beforn the myddel of the
day,  
<span id="an_4"></span> among thyn almykanteras on the est syde of  
<span id="an_5"></span> thin Astrelabie; and if it be after the myddel  
<span id="an_6"></span> of the day, set the degre of thy sonne upon
the  
<span id="an_7"></span> west syde. Tak this manere of settyng for a  
<span id="an_8"></span> general rule, ones for evere. And whan thou  
<span id="an_9"></span> hast set the degre of thy sonne upon as  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_10">10</span></span> many
almykanteras of height as was the altitude  
<span id="an_11"></span> of the sonne taken by thy rule, ley  
<span id="an_12"></span> over thi label upon the degre of the sonne;
and  
<span id="an_13"></span> than wol the point of thi labell sitte in the  
<span id="an_14"></span> bordure upon the verrey tyde of the day.  
<span id="an_15"></span> Ensample as thus: The yeer of oure lord  
<span id="an_16"></span> 1391, the 12 day of March, I wolde knowe the  
<span id="an_17"></span> tyde of the day. I tok the altitude of my
sonne,  
<span id="an_18"></span> and fond that it was 25 degrees and 30
minutes  
<span id="an_19"></span> of height in the bordure on the bak  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_20">20</span></span> side. Tho
turned I myn Astrelabye, and by  
<span id="an_21"></span> cause that it was before mydday, I turned  
<span id="an_22"></span> my riet and sette the degre of the sonne,
that  
<span id="an_23"></span> is to seyn the firste degre of Aries, on the
right  
<span id="an_24"></span> side of myn Astrelabye upon 25 degrees and  
<span id="an_25"></span> 30 mynutes of height among myn almykanteras.  
<span id="an_26"></span> Tho leide I my label upon the degre of my  
<span id="an_27"></span> sonne, and fond the point of my label in the  
<span id="an_28"></span> bordure upon a capital lettre that is clepid  
<span id="an_29"></span> an X. Tho rekned I alle the capitale lettres  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_30">30</span></span> fro the lyne of
mydnight unto this forseide  
<span id="an_31"></span> lettre X, and fond that it was 9 of the  
<span id="an_32"></span> clokke of the day. Tho loked I doun upon myn  
<span id="an_33"></span> est orizonte, and fond there the 20 degre of  
<span id="an_34"></span> Geminis ascendyng, which that I tok for myn  
<span id="an_35"></span> ascendent. And in this wise had I the
experience  
<span id="an_36"></span> for evermo in which manere I shulde  
<span id="an_37"></span> knowe the tyde of the day and eke myn
ascendent.  
<span id="an_39"></span> Tho wolde I wite the same nyght folewyng  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_40">40</span></span> the houre of
the nyght, and wroughte  
<span id="an_41"></span> in this wise: Among an heep of sterres  
<span id="an_42"></span> fixe it liked me for to take the altitude of
the  
<span id="an_43"></span> faire white sterre that is clepid Alhabor,
and  
<span id="an_44"></span> fond hir sittyng on the west side of the lyne  
<span id="an_45"></span> of midday, 12 degrees of heighte taken by my  
<span id="an_46"></span> rewle on the bak side. Tho sette I the centre  
<span id="an_47"></span> of this Alhabor upon 12 degrees among myn  
<span id="an_48"></span> almykanteras upon the west side, by cause
that  
<span id="an_49"></span> she was founde on the west side. Tho  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_50">50</span></span> leyde I my
label over the degre of the  
<span id="an_51"></span> sonne, that was discendid under the west  
<span id="an_52"></span> orisounte, and rekned all the lettres
capitals  
<span id="an_53"></span> fro the lyne of midday unto the point of my  
<span id="an_54"></span> label in the bordure, and fond that it was  
<span id="an_55"></span> passed 9 of the clokke the space of 10
degrees.  
<span id="an_56"></span> Tho lokid I doun upon myn est orisounte, and  
<span id="an_57"></span> fond there 10 degrees of Scorpius ascendyng,  
<span id="an_58"></span> whom I tok for myn ascendent. And thus  
<span id="an_59"></span> lerned I to knowe onys for ever in which  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_60">60</span></span> manere I shuld
come to the houre of the  
<span id="an_61"></span> nyght, and to myn ascendent, as verrely as  
<span id="an_62"></span> may be taken by so smal an instrument.  
<span id="an_63"></span> But natheles this rule in generall wol I
warne  
<span id="an_64"></span> the for evere: Ne make the nevere bold to  
<span id="an_65"></span> have take a just ascendent by thin
Astrelabie,  
<span id="an_66"></span> or elles to have set justly a clokke, whan
eny  
<span id="an_67"></span> celestial body by which that thou wenyst
governe  
<span id="an_68"></span> thilke thinges be nigh the south lyne.  
<span id="an_69"></span> For trust wel, whan the sonne is nygh the  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_70">70</span></span> meridional
lyne, the degre of the sonne  
<span id="an_71"></span> renneth so longe consentrik upon the
almykanteras  
<span id="an_72"></span> that sothly thou shalt erre fro the  
<span id="an_73"></span> just ascendent. The same conclusioun sey I by  
<span id="an_74"></span> the centre of eny sterre fix by nyght. And  
<span id="an_75"></span> more over, by experience I wot wel that in  
<span id="an_76"></span> oure orisounte, from xi of the clokke unto
oon  
<span id="an_77"></span> of the clokke, in taking of a just ascendent
in  
<span id="an_78"></span> a portatif Astrelabie it is to hard to knowe
--  
<span id="an_79"></span> I mene from xi of the clokke before the  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_80">80</span></span> houre of noon
til oon of the clokke next  
<span id="an_81"></span> folewyng.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_Rub">Rub</span></span> A special
declaracioun of the ascendent.  
<span id="an_1"></span> The ascendent sothly, as wel in alle nativites  
<span id="an_2"></span> as in questions and eleccions of tymes, is a  
<span id="an_3"></span> thing which that these astrologiens gretly
observen.  
<span id="an_4"></span> Wherfore me semeth convenyent, syth  
<span id="an_5"></span> that I speke of the ascendent, to make of it  
<span id="an_6"></span> speciall declaracioun.  
<span id="an_7"></span> The ascendent sothly, to take it at the
largest,  
<span id="an_8"></span> is thilke degre that ascendith at eny of  
<span id="an_9"></span> these forseide tymes upon the est orisounte.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_10">10</span></span> And therfore,
yf that eny planete ascende  
<span id="an_11"></span> at thatt same tyme in thilke forseide degre,  
<span id="an_12"></span> than hath he no latitude fro the ecliptik
lyne,  
<span id="an_13"></span> but he is than in the degre of the ecliptik  
<span id="an_14"></span> which that is the degre of his longitude. Men  
<span id="an_15"></span> sayn that thilke planete is in horoscopo.  
<span id="an_16"></span> But sothly the hous of the ascendent, that  
<span id="an_17"></span> is to seyn, the first hous or the est angle, is
a  
<span id="an_18"></span> thing more brod and large. For, after the
statutes  
<span id="an_19"></span> of astrologiens, what celestial body  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_20">20</span></span> that is 5
degrees above thilke degre that  
<span id="an_21"></span> ascendith, or withinne that nombre, that is  
<span id="an_22"></span> to seyn neer the degree that ascendith, yit  
<span id="an_23"></span> rekne they thilke planete in the ascendent.  
<span id="an_24"></span> And what planete that is under thilke degre  
<span id="an_25"></span> that ascendith the space of 25 degres, yit
seyn  
<span id="an_26"></span> thei that thilke planete is "like to him that
is  
<span id="an_27"></span> the hous of the ascendent." But sothly, if he  
<span id="an_28"></span> passe the boundes of these forseide spaces,  
<span id="an_29"></span> above or bynethe, thei seyn that the planete  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_30">30</span></span> is "fallyng fro
the ascendent." Yit saien  
<span id="an_31"></span> these astrologiens that the ascendent and  
<span id="an_32"></span> eke the lord of the ascendent may be shapen  
<span id="an_33"></span> for to be fortunat or infortunat, as thus: A  
<span id="an_34"></span> "fortunat ascendent" clepen they whan that no  
<span id="an_35"></span> wicked planete, as Saturne or Mars or elles  
<span id="an_36"></span> the Tayl of the Dragoun, is in the hous of
the  
<span id="an_37"></span> ascendent, ne that no wicked planete have  
<span id="an_38"></span> noon aspect of enemyte upon the ascendent.  
<span id="an_39"></span> But thei wol caste that thei have a fortunat  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_40">40</span></span> planete in hir
ascendent, and yit in his felicite;  
<span id="an_41"></span> and than sey thei that it is wel.  
<span id="an_42"></span> Further over thei seyn that the infortunyng
of  
<span id="an_43"></span> an ascendent is the contrarie of these
forseide  
<span id="an_44"></span> thinges. The lord of the ascendent, sey thei  
<span id="an_45"></span> that he is fortunat whan he is in god place  
<span id="an_46"></span> fro the ascendent, as in an angle, or in a
succident  
<span id="an_47"></span> where as he is in hys dignite and comfortid  
<span id="an_48"></span> with frendly aspectes of planetes and  
<span id="an_49"></span> wel resceyved; and eke that he may seen  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_50">50</span></span> the ascendent;
and that he be not retrograd,  
<span id="an_51"></span> ne combust, ne joyned with no  
<span id="an_52"></span> shrewe in the same signe; ne that he be not  
<span id="an_53"></span> in his discencioun, ne joyned with no planete  
<span id="an_54"></span> in his descencioun, ne have upon him noon  
<span id="an_55"></span> aspect infortunat; and than sey thei that he
is  
<span id="an_56"></span> well.  
<span id="an_57"></span> Natheles these ben observaunces of judicial  
<span id="an_58"></span> matere and rytes of payens, in whiche my  
<span id="an_59"></span> spirit hath no feith, ne knowing of her  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_60">60</span></span> horoscopum. For
they seyn that every  
<span id="an_61"></span> signe is departid in thre evene parties by  
<span id="an_62"></span> 10 degrees, and thilke porcioun they clepe a  
<span id="an_63"></span> face. And although that a planete have a
latitude  
<span id="an_64"></span> fro the ecliptik, yit sey somme folk, so  
<span id="an_65"></span> that the planete arise in that same signe
with  
<span id="an_66"></span> eny degre of the forseide face in which his  
<span id="an_67"></span> longitude is rekned, that yit is the planete  
<span id="an_68"></span> in horoscopo, be it in nativyte or in
eleccion,  
<span id="an_69"></span> etc.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_Rub">Rub</span></span> To knowe the
verrey equacioun of the  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubA">RubA</span></span> degre of
the sonne yf so be that it falle bitwene  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubC">RubC</span></span> thyn
almykanteras.  
<span id="an_1"></span> For as muche as the almykanteras in thin  
<span id="an_2"></span> Astrelabie ben compowned by two and two,  
<span id="an_3"></span> where as somme almykanteras in sondry
astrelabies  
<span id="an_4"></span> be compowned by 1 and 1, or elles by 2  
<span id="an_5"></span> and 2, it is necessarie to thy lernyng to
teche  
<span id="an_6"></span> the first to knowe and worke with thin oune  
<span id="an_7"></span> instrument. Wherfore whan that the degre of  
<span id="an_8"></span> thi sonne fallith bytwixe 2 almykanteras, or  
<span id="an_9"></span> ellis yf thin almykanteras ben graven with  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_10">10</span></span> over-gret a
poynt of a compas (for bothe  
<span id="an_11"></span> these thinges may causen errour as wel in  
<span id="an_12"></span> knowing of the tide of the day, as of the
verrey  
<span id="an_13"></span> ascendent), thou must worken in this  
<span id="an_14"></span> wise:  
<span id="an_15"></span> Set the degre of thy sonne upon the hyer  
<span id="an_16"></span> almykanteras of bothe, and wayte wel where  
<span id="an_17"></span> as thin almury touchith the bordure and set  
<span id="an_18"></span> there a prikke of ynke. Sett doun agayn the  
<span id="an_19"></span> degre of the sunne upon the nether
almykanteras  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_20">20</span></span> of bothe, and
sett there another  
<span id="an_21"></span> pricke. Remeve than thin almury in  
<span id="an_22"></span> the bordure evene amiddes bothe prickes, and  
<span id="an_23"></span> this wol lede justly the degre of thi sonne
to  
<span id="an_24"></span> sitte bitwene bothe almykanteras in his right  
<span id="an_25"></span> place. Ley than thy label over the degre of  
<span id="an_26"></span> thi sonne, and fynd in the bordure the verrey  
<span id="an_27"></span> tyde of the day, or of the night. And as
verraily  
<span id="an_28"></span> shalt thou fynde upon thin est orisonte  
<span id="an_29"></span> thin ascendent.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_Rub">Rub</span></span> To knowe the
spryng of the dawenyng  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubA">RubA</span></span> and the
ende of the evenyng, the whiche ben  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubB">RubB</span></span> called the
two crepuscules.  
<span id="an_1"></span> Set the nadir of thy sonne upon 18 degrees  
<span id="an_2"></span> of height among thyn almykanteras on the west  
<span id="an_3"></span> syde; and ley thy label on the degre of thy  
<span id="an_4"></span> sonne, and than shal the point of thy label  
<span id="an_5"></span> shewen the spryng of the day. Also set the  
<span id="an_6"></span> nader of thy sonne upon 18 degrees of height  
<span id="an_7"></span> among thin almykanteras on the est side, and  
<span id="an_8"></span> ley over thy label upon the degre of the
sonne,  
<span id="an_9"></span> and with the point of thy label fynd in the  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_10">10</span></span> bordure the
ende of the evenyng, that is  
<span id="an_11"></span> verrey nyght.  
<span id="an_12"></span> The nader of the sonne is thilke degre that  
<span id="an_13"></span> is opposyt to the degre of the sonne, in the  
<span id="an_14"></span> 7 signe, as thus: every degre of Aries by  
<span id="an_15"></span> ordir is nadir to every degre of Libra by
ordre,  
<span id="an_16"></span> and Taurus to Scorpioun, Gemini to
Sagittarie,  
<span id="an_17"></span> Cancer to Capricorne, Leo to Aquarie, Virgo  
<span id="an_18"></span> to Piscis. And if eny degre in thy zodiak be  
<span id="an_19"></span> derk, his nadir shal declare hym.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_Rub">Rub</span></span> To knowe the
arch of the day, that some  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubA">RubA</span></span> folk callen
the day artificiall, fro sonne arisyng  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RubB">RubB</span></span> tyl it go
to reste.  
<span id="an_1"></span> Set the degre of thi sonne upon thin est  
<span id="an_2"></span> orisonte, and ley thy label on the degre of  
<span id="an_3"></span> the sonne, and at the point of thy label in
the  
<span id="an_4"></span> bordure set a pricke. Turne than thy riet  
<span id="an_5"></span> aboute tyl the degre of the sonne sitte upon  
<span id="an_6"></span> the west orisonte, and ley thy label upon the  
<span id="an_7"></span> same degre of the sonne, and at the poynt of  
<span id="an_8"></span> thy label set another pricke. Rekne than  
<span id="an_9"></span> the quantite of tyme in the bordure bitwixe  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_10">10</span></span> bothe prickes,
and tak there thyn arch of  
<span id="an_11"></span> the day. The remenaunt of the bordure  
<span id="an_12"></span> under the orisonte is the arch of the nyght.  
<span id="an_13"></span> Thus maist thou rekne bothe arches, or every  
<span id="an_14"></span> porcioun, of whether that the liketh. And by  
<span id="an_15"></span> this manere of worching maist thou se how  
<span id="an_16"></span> longe that eny sterre fix dwelleth above the  
<span id="an_17"></span> erthe, fro tyme that he riseth til he go to
reste.  
<span id="an_18"></span> But the day naturall, that is to seyn 24
