#### The Canterbury Tales

### The Nun's Priest's Epilogue

<span id="an_3447"></span> \["Sire Nonnes Preest," oure Hooste seide
anoon,  
<span id="an_3448"></span> "I-blessed be thy breche, and every stoon!  
<span id="an_3449"></span> This was a murie tale of Chauntecleer.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3450">3450</span></span> But by my
trouthe, if thou were seculer,  
<span id="an_3451"></span> Thou woldest ben a trede-foul aright.  
<span id="an_3452"></span> For if thou have corage as thou hast myght,  
<span id="an_3453"></span> Thee were nede of hennes, as I wene,  
<span id="an_3454"></span> Ya, moo than seven tymes seventene.  
<span id="an_3455"></span> See, whiche braunes hath this gentil
preest,  
<span id="an_3456"></span> So gret a nekke, and swich a large breest!  
<span id="an_3457"></span> He loketh as a sperhauk with his yen;  
<span id="an_3458"></span> Him nedeth nat his colour for to dyen  
<span id="an_3459"></span> With brasile ne with greyn of Portyngale.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3460">3460</span></span> Now, sire,
faire falle yow for youre tale!"  
<span id="an_3461"></span> And after that he, with ful merie chere,  
<span id="an_3462"></span> Seide unto another, as ye shuln heere.\]
