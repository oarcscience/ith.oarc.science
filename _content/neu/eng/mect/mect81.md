#### The Shorter Poems

### The Complaint of Chaucer to His Purse

<span id="an_1"></span> To yow, my purse, and to noon other wight  
<span id="an_2"></span> Complayne I, for ye be my lady dere.  
<span id="an_3"></span> I am so sory, now that ye been lyght;  
<span id="an_4"></span> For certes but yf ye make me hevy chere,  
<span id="an_5"></span> Me were as leef be layd upon my bere;  
<span id="an_6"></span> For which unto your mercy thus I crye,  
<span id="an_7"></span> Beth hevy ageyn, or elles mot I dye.  
<span id="an_8"></span> Now voucheth sauf this day or hyt be nyght  
<span id="an_9"></span> That I of yow the blisful soun may here  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_10">10</span></span> Or see your
colour lyk the sonne bryght  
<span id="an_11"></span> That of yelownesse hadde never pere.  
<span id="an_12"></span> Ye be my lyf, ye be myn hertes stere.  
<span id="an_13"></span> Quene of comfort and of good companye,  
<span id="an_14"></span> Beth hevy ageyn, or elles moot I dye.  
<span id="an_15"></span> Now purse that ben to me my lyves lyght  
<span id="an_16"></span> And saveour as doun in this world here,  
<span id="an_17"></span> Out of this toune helpe me thurgh your myght,  
<span id="an_18"></span> Syn that ye wole nat ben my tresorere;  
<span id="an_19"></span> For I am shave as nye as any frere.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_20">20</span></span> But yet I pray
unto your curtesye,  
<span id="an_21"></span> Beth hevy agen, or elles moot I dye.  
<span id="an_22"></span> O conquerour of Brutes Albyon,  
<span id="an_23"></span> Which that by lyne and free eleccion  
<span id="an_24"></span> Been verray kyng, this song to yow I sende,  
<span id="an_25"></span> And ye, that mowen alle oure harmes amende,  
<span id="an_26"></span> Have mynde upon my supplicacion.
