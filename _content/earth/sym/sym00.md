E IT REMEMBERED, that on the second day of November, in the forty-fifth year of the Independence of the United States of America, <span class="smallcaps">Jonathan Seymour</span>, of the said District, hath deposited in this office the title of a book, the right whereof he claims as proprietor, in the words following, to wit:
<p>"Symzonia; a Voyage of Discovery. By Captain Adam Seaborn."</p>
<p>in conformity to the Act of the Congress of the United States, entitled "An Act for the encouragement of Learning, by securing the Copies of Maps, Charts, and Books, to the authors and proprietors of such copies, during the time therein mentioned" And also to an Act, entitled "an Act, supplementary to an Act, entitled an Act for the encouragement of Learning, by securing the copies of Maps, Charts, and Books, to the authors and proprietors of such copies, during the times therein mentioned, and extending the benefits thereof to the arts of designing, engraving, and etching historical and other prints."</p>
