### TRIPUṬĀ <span id="fr_564"></span>[1](#fn_563)

### (TRIPUṬĀSTOTRAM)

### FROM THE TANTRASĀRA <span id="fr_565"></span>[2](#fn_564)

1

I CONTEMPLATE the good Guru who is Light itself, <span
id="fr_566"></span>[3](#fn_565)  
Sitting with his Śakti <span id="fr_567"></span>[4](#fn_566)  
In the lotus of the head, <span id="fr_568"></span>[5](#fn_567)  
Two-armed, gracious, very gracious,  
Whose moon-like face is full of grace,  
Making with his hands the gestures which grant boons and dispel
fear. <span id="fr_569"></span>[6](#fn_568)

2

Such as recite <span id="fr_570"></span>[7](#fn_569) thy primordial
golden *bīja* 

<span id="page_92">p. 92</span>

ŚRĪM, <span id="fr_571"></span>[1](#fn_570)  
Attain all prosperity and fortune.

3

O Mother!  
He who contemplates Thy second *bīja*,  
Adorned by numbers of Devas,  
"HRĪM," <span id="fr_572"></span>[2](#fn_571)  
Gains all prosperity.

4

The chiefs of men who meditate upon Thy *bīja*,  
Lustrous as the sun,

<span id="page_93">p. 93</span>

"*KLĪM*," <span id="fr_573"></span>[1](#fn_572)  
Charm the three worlds,  
And by recitation thereof become like unto Īśvara.

5

O beloved of the enemy of Smara! <span id="fr_574"></span>[2](#fn_573)  
Those who contemplate Thy body <span id="fr_575"></span>[3](#fn_574)
thrice <span id="fr_576"></span>[4](#fn_575) and recite these three
*bījas* <span id="fr_577"></span>[5](#fn_576)  
Render their enemies speechless,  
Lakṣmī shines in their house,  
And they become the God of Love <span id="fr_578"></span>[6](#fn_577) to
women.

6

The presiding Devatā of Speech  
Blesses their mouth with poetry and prose.  
Harmful animals cause them no harm,  
Even the Suras <span id="fr_579"></span>[7](#fn_578) salute them.

<span id="page_94">p. 94</span>

Their feet are the head ornaments of kings, <span
id="fr_580"></span>[1](#fn_579)  
The *siddhis* <span id="fr_581"></span>[2](#fn_580) are in their
hands,  
Malignant stars relinquish them.

7

Let the *Sādhaka* meditate upon an eight-petalled lotus <span
id="fr_582"></span>[3](#fn_581)  
Set upon a throne studded with various gems,  
Placed upon an altar  
Standing on the floor of a jewelled house  
Amidst a forest of *Pārijāta* trees. <span
id="fr_583"></span>[4](#fn_582)

8

Let him then meditate upon two angles <span
id="fr_584"></span>[5](#fn_583) in the lotus,  
And the Devī Herself in the lotus as follows:  
Her lustre is that of molten gold,  
With earrings <span id="fr_585"></span>[6](#fn_584) on her ears,  
Three-eyed, of beauteous throat,  
Her face like the moon,  
And bending from the weight of Her breasts. <span
id="fr_586"></span>[7](#fn_585)

9

She holds in many arms, decked with diamonds and other gems,

<span id="page_95">p. 95</span>

Two lotuses, a noose, <span id="fr_587"></span>[1](#fn_586) bow, golden
goad, <span id="fr_588"></span>[2](#fn_587) and flowery arrows. <span
id="fr_589"></span>[3](#fn_588)  
Her body is adorned with great jewels,  
Slender is She of waist <span id="fr_590"></span>[4](#fn_589) and
beautifully girdled. <span id="fr_591"></span>[5](#fn_590)

10

Her lotus feet glitter with beautiful anklets, <span
id="fr_592"></span>[6](#fn_591)  
Crowned, adorned, and gracious,  
Holding two white fly-whisks, <span id="fr_593"></span>[7](#fn_592) a
mirror, jewel-case, <span id="fr_594"></span>[8](#fn_593) and a box
filled with camphor. <span id="fr_595"></span>[9](#fn_594)

11

Creatrix of the three worlds,  
Destructress of the pain of the world,  
Destructress and ruler of the world,  
Ever full of Bliss,

<span id="page_96">p. 96</span>

Half of the letter *Hā*; <span id="fr_596"></span>[1](#fn_595) of the
nature of the three-fold *Bindu*, <span
id="fr_597"></span>[2](#fn_596)  
The threefold *Śakti*, <span id="fr_598"></span>[3](#fn_597)  
It is Her I worship.

12

The *Sadhaka* who, having thus for a long time contemplated Her  
On a *yantra* <span id="fr_599"></span>[4](#fn_598) set before him,  
And welcomed <span id="fr_600"></span>[5](#fn_599) her with great
[devotion](errata.htm#11),  
Worshipping Her with *Svayambhu* flower <span
id="fr_601"></span>[6](#fn_600)  
Attains, even though he be of the lowest <span
id="fr_602"></span>[7](#fn_601) *siddhi* <span
id="fr_603"></span>[8](#fn_602) in the *caturvarga*. <span
id="fr_604"></span>[9](#fn_603)

<span id="page_97">p. 97</span>

13

Whoever after having done worship <span
id="fr_605"></span>[1](#fn_604)  
Of Śrī, <span id="fr_606"></span>[2](#fn_605) Śrīpatī, <span
id="fr_607"></span>[3](#fn_606) Pārvatī, <span
id="fr_608"></span>[4](#fn_607) Īśvara, <span
id="fr_609"></span>[5](#fn_608) Ratī, <span
id="fr_610"></span>[6](#fn_609) and Kāmadeva, <span
id="fr_611"></span>[7](#fn_610)  
Together with the *Ṣādang*a Devatā <span id="fr_612"></span>[8](#fn_611)
of the Devī,  
Recites <span id="fr_613"></span>[9](#fn_612) the *mantra* on Thy
*yantra*, <span id="fr_614"></span>[10](#fn_613)  
Becomes a King among men.

14

Having worshipped the two nidhis, <span id="fr_615"></span>[11](#fn_614)
Śankha and Padma,  
On the two sides of the lotus,

<span id="page_98">p. 98</span>

And the Mahīṣīs, <span id="fr_616"></span>[1](#fn_615) regents of the
quarters, <span id="fr_617"></span>[2](#fn_616) and their weapons,  
Attains, even though he be of the most vile, <span
id="fr_618"></span>[3](#fn_617) the eight *siddhis* <span
id="fr_619"></span>[4](#fn_618) of Śiva.

15

Thou art the earth, *Vidhātrī*, <span id="fr_620"></span>[5](#fn_619)
creatrix of the world; <span id="fr_621"></span>[6](#fn_620)  
Thou art water, and in the form of Viṣṇu preserveth the world;  
Thou art fire, and in the form of Rudra destroyeth the world;  
Thou existeth in the form of *Aiśvarya*; <span
id="fr_622"></span>[7](#fn_621)  
Thou art the air of the world.

16

Thou art the primeval <span id="fr_623"></span>[5](#fn_619) and
auspicious one, <span id="fr_624"></span>[8](#fn_622)  
Spouse of Śambhu, <span id="fr_625"></span>[9](#fn_623) refuge (of Thy
worshippers).

<span id="page_99">p. 99</span>

Who ever moves in the *Brahmarandhra* <span
id="fr_626"></span>[1](#fn_624) of the world  
The supporter of all, yet Thyself without support.  
The only pure One in the form of ether. <span
id="fr_627"></span>[2](#fn_625)  
O Bhavānī! be gracious to me.

17

Thou hast humbled the pride even of the *Rṣis*  
By plunging them into the ocean of the world.  
Thou art intelligence and bliss and light itself. <span
id="fr_628"></span>[3](#fn_626)  
How, then, can I know thee?  
O Bhavānī! be gracious to me.

18

O Bhavānī! even an ignorant man <span id="fr_629"></span>[4](#fn_627)  
Who, meditating on Thy form, recites <span
id="fr_630"></span>[5](#fn_628) Thy *mantra* a *lakh* of times  
Acquires all poetic power,  
And those things in the three worlds which are most difficult of
attainment.  
O Bhavānī! be gracious to me.

19

Thou art that which supports <span id="fr_631"></span>[6](#fn_629) and
that which is supported. <span id="fr_632"></span>[7](#fn_630)  
Thou pervadeth the world,

<span id="page_100">p. 100</span>

And art in the form of the world which is pervaded by Thee. <span
id="fr_633"></span>[1](#fn_631)  
Thou art both negation <span id="fr_634"></span>[2](#fn_632) and
existence. <span id="fr_635"></span>[3](#fn_633)  
O Bhavānī! be gracious to me.

20

Thou art the atom <span id="fr_636"></span>[4](#fn_634) and
ever-pervading. <span id="fr_637"></span>[5](#fn_635)  
Thou art the whole universe.  
No praise of Thee is sufficient.  
Yet Thy qualities prompt me to sing Thy praise.  
O Bhavānī! be gracious to me.

21

To him who reads and recites <span id="fr_638"></span>[6](#fn_636) at
morn, noon, and evening  
This most secret hymn,  
There is nothing impossible in the three worlds, Such an one attains Thy
nature. <span id="fr_639"></span>[7](#fn_637)  
O Bhavānī! be gracious to me.

<span id="fn_570"></span>[92:1](htg16.htm#fr_571) As is frequently the
case in Tāntrik works, the *mantra* is not given in the text, but must
be spelt out. Thus the Sanskrit is *vakamvahnisamstham trimurtyā
prajuṣtam saśānkenayuktam*--that is, as "*vakam* with *vahni* attended
by *trimurti*, combined with *śaśāngka*." *Vakam* = "Ś" (*tālavya*).
*Vahni* is the "fire." = "r," *trimūrti* = the long vowel ī, and
*śaśāngka*, the moon in "whose lap is the hare," or "man in the moon" =
"m" (*anusvāra*). Ś + r + i + m = *śrīm* the *Lakshmībīja* (see the
Ādyakālīstotra of Mahānirvāṇa Tantra).

<span id="fn_571"></span>[92:2](htg16.htm#fr_572) Literally,
*Nabhovahni* (not *vāyu*, as the text has it, for the *vāyu bīja* is
*yam*); *miśram* (not *mitram* as Prasanna Kumāra Shāstrī’s edition has
it); *tatovāmanetram sudhā dhāmavimbam niyojyaikāvaktram*--that is,
*nabhas* combined with *vahni*, and then *vāmanetra* and the receptacle
of nectar (the moon) applicable in the case of Ekavaktra. *Nabhas* =
"*ha*" or "*bha*" (here the former), *vahni* = "*ra*," *vāmanetra* =
long *ī*, and the moon is *anusvāra* ("m"), H + r + ī + m = *Hrīm*,
known as the *māyā bīja*. Then the *śloka* more clearly points to the
*bīja* meant by saying it is that applicable to Ekavaktra. The latter is
the Bhairava of Bagalāmukhī, whose *bīja* is also *Hrīm*.

<span id="fn_572"></span>[93:1](htg16.htm#fr_573) Literally, *Virinchim
kṣitistham tatovāmanetram vidhum nādayuktam*--that is, *virinchi*,
*kṣiti* and *vāmanetra*, together with moon combined with *nāda*.
*Virinchi* = "*ka*", *kṣiti* = "La," *vāmanetra* =long *ī*, the moon =
"m" (*anusvāra*). K + l + ī + m = *Klīm*. The three elements of the
mantra are given--viz., *Śrīm*, *Hrīm*, *Klīm*--but the actual *bīja* of
Tripuṭā is *Śrīm*, *Hrīm*, *Klīm*, *Hrīm*, *Śrīm*, *Klīm*, *Klīm*,
