### LAKṢMĪ <span id="fr_308"></span>[1](#fn_308)

### (LAKSMĪSTOTRAM)

### FROM THE TANTRASĀRA <span id="fr_309"></span>[2](#fn_309)

O DEVĪ KAMALĀ, <span id="fr_310"></span>[3](#fn_310) beloved of Viṣṇu,  
Adored by the three worlds,  
As Thou art constant to Viṣṇu, be Thou constant to me.  
Whoever worshipping Lakṣmī, reads these twelve names of Her--  
Iśvarī, Kamala, <span id="fr_311"></span>[4](#fn_311) Lakṣmī,
Calā, <span id="fr_312"></span>[5](#fn_312)  
Bhūti, <span id="fr_313"></span>[6](#fn_313) Haripriyā, <span
id="fr_314"></span>[7](#fn_314) Padmā, <span
id="fr_315"></span>[8](#fn_315) Padmālayā, <span
id="fr_316"></span>[9](#fn_316) Sampat, <span
id="fr_317"></span>[10](#fn_317)

<span id="page_55">p. 55</span>

Uchaih, <span id="fr_318"></span>[1](#fn_318) Śrī <span
id="fr_319"></span>[2](#fn_319) Padmadhārinī, <span
id="fr_320"></span>[3](#fn_320)  
With such an one, his wife and children,  
Lakṣmī ever abides.

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_308"></span>[54:1](htg10.htm#fr_308) Lakṣmī, commonly
called Śrī, Devī of prosperity and beauty: the Śakti, or Spouse of
Viṣṇu, who rose resplendent from the sea at the churning of the ocean by
the Devas and Asuras, and then reclining on the breast of Hari, gazed
upon the enraptured Devas. As her Lord assumes various forms, so does
She.

<span id="fn_309"></span>[54:2](htg10.htm#fr_309) P. 577.

<span id="fn_310"></span>[54:3](htg10.htm#fr_310) Because She appeared
from the lotus.

<span id="fn_311"></span>[54:4](htg10.htm#fr_311) Feminine of Īśvara, or
Lord, or Ruler.

<span id="fn_312"></span>[54:5](htg10.htm#fr_312) Or Cancalā, the fickle
one, for nothing is so fickle as wealth and prosperity--"here to-day and
gone to-morrow."

<span id="fn_313"></span>[54:6](htg10.htm#fr_313) Prosperity.

<span id="fn_314"></span>[54:7](htg10.htm#fr_314) Beloved of Hari
(Viṣṇu).

<span id="fn_315"></span>[54:8](htg10.htm#fr_315) Lotus.

<span id="fn_316"></span>[54:9](htg10.htm#fr_316) Who dwells in the
lotus.

<span id="fn_317"></span>[54:10](htg10.htm#fr_317) Wealth.

<span id="fn_318"></span>[55:1](htg10.htm#fr_318) The exalted One, for
prosperity exalts.

<span id="fn_319"></span>[55:2](htg10.htm#fr_319) Beauty and prosperity.

<span id="fn_320"></span>[55:3](htg10.htm#fr_320) Holding the lotus in
her hands.
