#### DURGĀ <span id="fr_1004"></span>[1](#fn_998)

#### FROM THE MAHĀBHĀRATA <span id="fr_1005"></span>[2](#fn_999)

1

I SALUTE Thee, leader of Yogis, <span id="fr_1006"></span>[3](#fn_1000)
one with the Brahman, <span id="fr_1007"></span>[4](#fn_1001)  
Dweller in the Mandāra forest.  
Virgin, <span id="fr_1008"></span>[5](#fn_1002) Kālī, <span
id="fr_1009"></span>[6](#fn_1003) Spouse of Kāpāla, <span
id="fr_1010"></span>[7](#fn_1004) of tawny hue. <span
id="fr_1011"></span>[8](#fn_1005)  
Salutation to Thee, Bhadrakālī. <span
id="fr_1012"></span>[9](#fn_1006)  
Reverence to Thee, Mahākāli, <span id="fr_1013"></span>[10](#fn_1007)

<span id="page_162">p. 162</span>

Caṇḍī, <span id="fr_1014"></span>[1](#fn_1008) Fearless one. <span
id="fr_1015"></span>[2](#fn_1009) Salutation to Thee, Saviour <span
id="fr_1016"></span>[3](#fn_1010) imbued with all good fortune. <span
id="fr_1017"></span>[4](#fn_1011)

2

Of the race of Kata <span id="fr_1018"></span>[5](#fn_1012) greatly
worshipful,  
Dreadful one, <span id="fr_1019"></span>[6](#fn_1013) Giver of
victory, <span id="fr_1020"></span>[7](#fn_1014) Victrix, <span
id="fr_1021"></span>[8](#fn_1015)  
Who holdeth a peacock's tail for Thy banner,  
And art adorned with various jewels,  
Bearing formidable spear, sword, and shield (made of skin).  
Younger Sister of the chief of cowherds, <span
id="fr_1022"></span>[9](#fn_1016)  
Eldest one, <span id="fr_1023"></span>[10](#fn_1017) born in the family
of the cowherd Nanda, <span id="fr_1024"></span>[11](#fn_1018)  
Delighting in the blood of Mahiṣa, <span
id="fr_1025"></span>[12](#fn_1019)

<span id="page_163">p. 163</span>

Kauśikī, <span id="fr_1026"></span>[1](#fn_1020) wearing yellow
garments.

3

With auspicious smile,  
Whose mouth devoured all demons, <span
id="fr_1027"></span>[2](#fn_1021)  
Salutation to Thee, delighter in battle.  
Umā, <span id="fr_1028"></span>[3](#fn_1022) giver of *shāka*, <span
id="fr_1029"></span>[4](#fn_1023)  
In the form of Maheśvara, <span id="fr_1030"></span>[5](#fn_1024) and in
that of Vāsudeva, <span id="fr_1031"></span>[6](#fn_1025)  
Destructress of Kaitabha, <span id="fr_1032"></span>[7](#fn_1026)  
Golden-eyed, with half-opened eyes <span
id="fr_1033"></span>[8](#fn_1027), grey-eyed, <span
id="fr_1034"></span>[9](#fn_1028)  
Veda and *Śruti*, <span id="fr_1035"></span>[10](#fn_1029) and most
sacred.  
Propitious to Brāhmanas engaged in the sacrificial rites,  
Thou art Jātaveda, <span id="fr_1036"></span>[11](#fn_1030)  
And art ever present in the sacred shrines <span
id="fr_1037"></span>[12](#fn_1031) in the chief cities of
Jambudvīpa. <span id="fr_1038"></span>[13](#fn_1032)

<span id="page_164">p. 164</span>

4

Of Sciences Thou art the knowledge of Brahman,  
Thou art the liberation of embodied beings, <span
id="fr_1039"></span>[1](#fn_1033)  
Mother of Skanda. <span id="fr_1040"></span>[2](#fn_1034)  
O Bhagavatī <span id="fr_1041"></span>[3](#fn_1035) Durgā! <span
id="fr_1042"></span>[4](#fn_1036) Thou liveth in inaccessible
regions--  
*Svāhā*, <span id="fr_1043"></span>[5](#fn_1037) *Svadhā*, <span
id="fr_1044"></span>[6](#fn_1038) *Kalā*, and *Kāṣṭḥā*, <span
id="fr_1045"></span>[7](#fn_1039)  
Sarasvatī, <span id="fr_1046"></span>[8](#fn_1040) Savitrī. <span
id="fr_1047"></span>[9](#fn_1041)  
Mother of Vedas and Vedānta <span id="fr_1048"></span>[10](#fn_1042) art
Thou called.  
I praise Thee from the pure depth of my heart.  
By Thy favour let us be victorious in battle.  
Ever dost Thou abide in inaccessible regions,  
In places full of fear and difficulty;  
In the houses of Thy devotees, and in Pātāla. <span
id="fr_1049"></span>[11](#fn_1043)  
In battle Thou conquereth the Dānavas. <span
id="fr_1050"></span>[12](#fn_1044)

<span id="page_165">p. 165</span>

Thou art drowsiness <span id="fr_1051"></span>[1](#fn_1045) and
slumber. <span id="fr_1052"></span>[2](#fn_1046)

5

Thou hast power to show wonderfully the world, <span
id="fr_1053"></span>[3](#fn_1047)  
Modesty, <span id="fr_1054"></span>[4](#fn_1048) and beauty. <span
id="fr_1055"></span>[5](#fn_1049)  
Cause of creation and destruction, <span
id="fr_1056"></span>[6](#fn_1050)  
Creatrix, <span id="fr_1057"></span>[7](#fn_1051) Mother, <span
id="fr_1058"></span>[8](#fn_1052) contentment, nourishment, constancy,  
Light, Supportress of the sun and moon,  
Power <span id="fr_1059"></span>[9](#fn_1053) of Him who possesses
power, <span id="fr_1060"></span>[10](#fn_1054)  
In ecstasy <span id="fr_1061"></span>[11](#fn_1055) Thou art perceived
by Siddhas and Cāranas. <span id="fr_1062"></span>[12](#fn_1056)

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_998"></span>[161:1](htg25.htm#fr_1004) See [p.
120](htg20.htm#page_120), note 9; , note 1: [p. 81](htg15.htm#page_81),
note 1.

<span id="fn_999"></span>[161:2](htg25.htm#fr_1005) Bhīshma Parvan, s.
43 (see Muir, O. S. T. iv, 432).

<span id="fn_1000"></span>[161:3](htg25.htm#fr_1006) *Siddhasenānī*. The
*siddhas* are here *yogis* and sages. Nīlakanṭha (cited *post* as N.),
in his Commentary, says the term means: She who, as leader (literally,
commander of an army), gives success in *yoga* and attainment of the
supreme abode.

<span id="fn_1001"></span>[161:4](htg25.htm#fr_1007) *Āryye*. Literally,
noble, but here means, as Nīlakantha says, *prapya-brahmasvarūpa*--the
own form of the accessible Brahman, as distinguished from the *nirguṇa
Brahman* beyond thought and speech.

<span id="fn_1002"></span>[161:5](htg25.htm#fr_1008) Kumārī. It also
means (N.) that She is very young.

<span id="fn_1003"></span>[161:6](htg25.htm#fr_1009) See [p.
152](htg23.htm#page_152), note 3.

<span id="fn_1004"></span>[161:7](htg25.htm#fr_1010) Kāpālī, one of Her
forms. Kāpāla is Rudrā, as leader of Kāpālas (Kāpālikas).

<span id="fn_1005"></span>[161:8](htg25.htm#fr_1011)
*Kapilākṛṣṇapingalā*.

<span id="fn_1006"></span>[161:9](htg25.htm#fr_1012) Auspicious Kālī,
who gives prosperity to Her devotees.

<span id="fn_1007"></span>[161:10](htg25.htm#fr_1013) See [p.
152](htg23.htm#page_152), note 3. The great Kālī, Destructress in the
form of death.

<span id="fn_1008"></span>[162:1](htg25.htm#fr_1014) Spouse of Caṇḍa, or
Kālāntaka, or Yama.

<span id="fn_1009"></span>[162:2](htg25.htm#fr_1015) *Candā*--bold,
daring, brave, courageous.

<span id="fn_1010"></span>[162:3](htg25.htm#fr_1016) *Tārinī*, for She
delivers from calamity.

<span id="fn_1011"></span>[162:4](htg25.htm#fr_1017) *Varavarninī* (N.),
not "beautiful coloured," as it has been translated.

<span id="fn_1012"></span>[162:5](htg25.htm#fr_1018) *Kātyāyanī*.

<span id="fn_1013"></span>[162:6](htg25.htm#fr_1019) *Karālī* = *krure*
or cruel (to demons and other ill-doers). *Karālavadanā* (wide-opened
mouth) is an epithet of Kālī. "Gaping-mouthed, terrible, four-armed,
with dishevelled hair"--*Karālavadanām ghorām, chatur-bhujām*, as the
Kālī *dhyāna* runs.

<span id="fn_1014"></span>[162:7](htg25.htm#fr_1020) *Vijaya*--that is,
particular (*viśisṭ.a*) victory (N.).

<span id="fn_1015"></span>[162:8](htg25.htm#fr_1021) *Jayā*. *Jayā* and
*Vijayā* are also the names of two female attendants (*Sakhī*) of Durgā.

<span id="fn_1016"></span>[162:9](htg25.htm#fr_1022) Gopendra or Kṛṣṇa.
In the Harivamśa and Caṇḍī it is said that with the view of defeating
the designs of Kamsa in regard to the destruction of Devakī's offspring,
Devī will be born as the ninth child of Yaśodā in the same night as
Kṛṣṇa was born as the eight child of Devakī, when Kṛṣṇa would be carried
to Yaśodā and She to Devakī.

<span id="fn_1017"></span>[162:10](htg25.htm#fr_1023)
*Jyeṣtha*--*śreṣthā*--superior to or best of all.

<span id="fn_1018"></span>[162:11](htg25.htm#fr_1024) In whose house
Kṛṣṇa was reared.

<span id="fn_1019"></span>[162:12](htg25.htm#fr_1025) The Asura of that
name (see Caṇḍī).

<span id="fn_1020"></span>[163:1](htg25.htm#fr_1026) A name of the Devī,
as born in the race of the sage Kuśika.

<span id="fn_1021"></span>[163:2](htg25.htm#fr_1027) *Kokamukhe*--when
in battle with Raktabīja.

<span id="fn_1022"></span>[163:3](htg25.htm#fr_1028) See Hymn to
Annapūrṇa, *post*.

<span id="fn_1023"></span>[163:4](htg25.htm#fr_1029) *Shākambarī*.
*Shōka* is a vegetable food given by Devī at the time of famine (Caṇḍī).

<span id="fn_1024"></span>[163:5](htg25.htm#fr_1030) *Svetā* (N.), not
white, as it has been translated.

<span id="fn_1025"></span>[163:6](htg25.htm#fr_1031) *Kṛṣṇā* (N.), not
black, as it has been translated.

<span id="fn_1026"></span>[163:7](htg25.htm#fr_1032) A Daitya brother of
Madhu (Caṇḍī).

<span id="fn_1027"></span>[163:8](htg25.htm#fr_1033) *Virūpākṣī* (see
[p. 155](htg24.htm#page_155), note 5).

<span id="fn_1028"></span>[163:9](htg25.htm#fr_1034) *Dhūmrākṣī* (N.)
says grey and green, like those of a cat.

<span id="fn_1029"></span>[163:10](htg25.htm#fr_1035) Here Upaniṣads.

<span id="fn_1030"></span>[163:11](htg25.htm#fr_1036) *Jātavedasī*.
Jataveda is a name of Agni (Fire).

<span id="fn_1031"></span>[163:12](htg25.htm#fr_1037) *Chaityeshu*.
Ordinarily this term is applied to the Buddhist shrine, of which it is
commonly said: "One should not enter a Jaina's temple or Buddhist
*chaitya*, even if pursued by an elephant" (*Hastinā tādyamānopi na
gachchet jaina-mandiram* also *dhaitya mandiram*.) Here the term means
*devatālaya*.

<span id="fn_1032"></span>[163:13](htg25.htm#fr_1038) A Purāṇic island
by that name, not as it has been translated; "Who dwellest continually
near to mountain precipices and sepulchres."

<span id="fn_1033"></span>[164:1](htg25.htm#fr_1039) "The great sleep of
embodied beings," according to the last translator: But *Mahānidrā*
(great sleep) is here *mukti* (liberation), which is the result of the
*Brahmavidyā*, spoken of in the preceding line.

<span id="fn_1034"></span>[164:2](htg25.htm#fr_1040) Kārtikeya. By this
it is meant that She is *sarvadevatārūpā*, in the form of all Devas, of
whom Skanda is selected as a type (N.).

<span id="fn_1035"></span>[164:3](htg25.htm#fr_1041) See [p.
120](htg20.htm#page_120), note 5; [p. 98](htg16.htm#page_98), note 5.

<span id="fn_1036"></span>[164:4](htg25.htm#fr_1042) *Kāntāravāsinī*
(N.).

<span id="fn_1037"></span>[164:5](htg25.htm#fr_1043) Mantra used with
*homa*, but here it means that all ritual acts are her embodiment
(*Sarvakarmarūpā*).

<span id="fn_1038"></span>[164:6](htg25.htm#fr_1044) *Mantra* used in
*pitṛkriyā* (see last note).

<span id="fn_1039"></span>[164:7](htg25.htm#fr_1045) *Kalā* is a
division of time--one minute forty-eight seconds, and *kāṣṭḥa* is
one-thirtieth of that.

<span id="fn_1040"></span>[164:8](htg25.htm#fr_1046) Devī of speech and
learning.

<span id="fn_1041"></span>[164:9](htg25.htm#fr_1047) She is
*sarvavāng-māyā-rūpa* (N.).

<span id="fn_1042"></span>[164:10](htg25.htm#fr_1048) End of the Vedas
or Upaniṣad.

<span id="fn_1043"></span>[164:11](htg25.htm#fr_1049) The nether world.

<span id="fn_1044"></span>[164:12](htg25.htm#fr_1050) See [p.
156](htg24.htm#page_156), note 14.

<span id="fn_1045"></span>[165:1](htg25.htm#fr_1051) *Jambhane* =
*tandrā* (N.), not "destroyer" as it has been translated.

<span id="fn_1046"></span>[165:2](htg25.htm#fr_1052) *Mohinī* = *Nidrā*
(N.).

<span id="fn_1047"></span>[165:3](htg25.htm#fr_1053) *Māyā* =
*adbhutapradarśanām* (N.).

<span id="fn_1048"></span>[165:4](htg25.htm#fr_1054) *Hrī* = *lajjā*
representative of, and including all other actions of mind (N.).

<span id="fn_1049"></span>[165:5](htg25.htm#fr_1055) *Śrī*, or
prosperity, and other attributes of Lakṣmī.

<span id="fn_1050"></span>[165:6](htg25.htm#fr_1056) *Sandhyā*. the
intervening period when night is going and morn coming. and vice versa,
applied here to similar junction times in the creation and dissolution
of the world (N.).

<span id="fn_1051"></span>[165:7](htg25.htm#fr_1057) *Sāvitri*. She who,
by the lustre of Sūrya, reveals (N.).

<span id="fn_1052"></span>[165:8](htg25.htm#fr_1058) Because, as a
mother, She supports the world and all beings therein.

<span id="fn_1053"></span>[165:9](htg25.htm#fr_1059) *Aiśvary*a. The
supreme faculties of omnipresence, omnipotence, etc.

<span id="fn_1054"></span>[165:10](htg25.htm#fr_1060) *Maheśvar*a. She
is the greatest wealth of Brahman.

<span id="fn_1055"></span>[165:11](htg25.htm#fr_1061) *Sankya* or
*Samādhi*, where light appears and the *ātman* is known (N).

<span id="fn_1056"></span>[165:12](htg25.htm#fr_1062) Siddhas here mean
those who are liberated whilst yet living (*jīvanmukta*), and Cāranas
those who are *siddhas* from their birth.
