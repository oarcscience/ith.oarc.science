#### VIII

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Yüeh Ch‘u;* allusive. A gentleman tells all the excitement of his
desire for the possession of a beautiful lady.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>The moon comes
forth, bright in the sky;  
A lovelier sight to draw my eye  
  Is she, that lady fair.  
She round my heart has fixed love's chain,  
But all my longings are in vain.  
  ’Tis hard the grief to bear.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>The moon comes
forth, a splendid sight;  
More winning far that lady bright,  
  Object of my desire!  
Deep-seated is my anxious grief;  
In vain I seek to find relief,  
  While glows the secret fire. <span id="page_157">p. 157</span>

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>The rising moon
shines mild and fair;  
More bright is she, whose beauty rare  
  My heart with longing fills.  
With eager wish I pine in vain;  
O for relief from constant pain,  
  Which through my bosom thrills!

</div>
