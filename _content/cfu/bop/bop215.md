#### II

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Wu Chiang Ta Ch‘ê*; narrative. Some officer, overloaded in the
king's service, thinks it better to try and dismiss his troubles from
his mind.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>Push not the cart
you stand behind;—  
  You'll only raise the dust. Nor dwell  
On your anxieties of mind;—  
  You'll only make yourself unwell.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>Push not the cart
you stand behind;—  
  The dust will only blind your view.  
Dwell not on things that vex your mind;—  
  You never thus can see them true.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>Push not the cart
you stand behind;—  
  The dust will but becloud your eyes.  
Heed not the troubles of your mind;—  
  ’Twill weight you as you seek to rise.

</div>
