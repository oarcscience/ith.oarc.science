#### XVII

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Tzŭ Chin;* narrative. A lady mourns the indifference and absence of
her student lover.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>You student, with
the collar blue,  
  Long pines my heart with anxious pain.  
Although I do not go to you,  
  Why from all word do you refrain?

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>O you, with girdle
strings of blue,  
  My thoughts to you forever roam!  
Although I do not go to you,  
  Yet why to me should you not come?

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>How reckless you,
how light and wild,  
  There by the tower upon the wall!  
One day, from sight of you exiled,  
  As long as three long months I call.

</div>
