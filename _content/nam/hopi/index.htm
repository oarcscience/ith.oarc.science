The Hopi, who occupy an area in the high desert plateaus of the Southwest, are one of the best known Native American tribes. They have a rich mythological cycle about their ascent from a world deep inside the earth.

Traditions of the Hopi   by H.R. Voth, Field Columbian Museum Anthropogical Publication Vol. VIII. [1905].   A treasure trove of Hopi myth and legend.

Truth of a Hopi   by Edmund Nequatewa, [1936]   Hopi myth, legend, and history, written by a Hopi, from a Hopi viewpoint.

If you are interested in the Hopi and their spiritual traditions, you may also be interested in this book: Book of the Hopi by Frank Waters
