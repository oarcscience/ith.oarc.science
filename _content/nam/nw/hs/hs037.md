### 35.

Lû'gua nAñ na'n<u>g</u>a sL!a-i dja'kia q<u>î</u>ndjâ'waxañ: wagi  
At that time \| someone's \| grandmother \| hand \| wooden tray with
square sides \| has been hurt, I hear: \| for it  
gag<u>î</u>ña'ñ, wagi <u>g</u>â'~~l~~<u>g</u>a~~l~~wa'ñ q!oldjida,
q!oldjida, gadja'o  
(you) cry, \| for it \| (you) sit and move around (i. e., the body), \|
chief-woman \| chief-woman, \| sit and move <span
id="fr_45"></span>[1](#fn_41)

Perhaps you are crying and are moving around for your grandmother's
hand, which was hurt on a wooden tray, chief-woman, chief-woman.

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_41"></span>[27:1](hs037.htm#fr_45) A woman of this family
