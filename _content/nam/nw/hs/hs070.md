### 68.

Gut sî'+~~l~~<u>g</u>adaga'ñ xA'nhao kî'<u>g</u>a kuqêda's at (a) lA  
Each other \| right \| after \| even \| names \| aristocratic \| with \|
he (baby)  
    kia'gañao gûdâ'gû.  
    call it sitting \| thought he would.  
KîlsLa'-i kugwê'dalañ <span id="fr_76"></span>[3](#fn_70) sûwa'ñ.  
Chief \| while walking hither \| he said.  
Â'yaña kî'n<u>g</u>et gô'ñ<u>g</u>a ~~l~~iñga'n.  
Be careful \| chief's \| father \| is going to be!  
Dî'nAñ ayâ'ña q!o'ldjat xâ't<u>g</u>a l<u>î</u>ñgA'n dîna'ñ.  
My child \| be careful, \| chief-woman \| father \| is going to be my
child.

<span id="page_46">p. 46</span>

He thinks he would use high-sounding names, one after the other, (to the
others of his own family.) <span
id="fr_77"></span>[1](hs071.htm#fn_71)  
He says the chief comes walking.  
Be careful of the future chief's father!  
Be careful of my child, the future chief-woman's father!

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_70"></span>[45:3](hs070.htm#fr_76) Kugwê'dalañ ("while
walking hither") is a low-class word referring to the chief himself.
