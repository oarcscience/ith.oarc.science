### 58.

Îhî îhî hî, etc.  
Gû'gus t!a'<u>g</u>ahas dînA'ñ kûñg<u>î</u>ñâ'ñodi<u>g</u>añ?  
What \| for \| my child \| sits crying,  
Gu'lxas t!a'<u>g</u>a gwA dînA'ñ kuñg<u>î</u>ña'ño?  
Abalone-shells for my child cries?  
DAñ gaña'ñ <u>g</u>wA dî k<u>î</u>lsLia'wus qâñ ~~l~~ qê'gAn.  
You \| like \| ? \| I \| sit a chief, \| my uncle \| I \| bore.

<span id="page_41">p. 41</span>

Îhî îhî hî, etc.  
For what does my child cry?  
Does my child cry for abalone-shells?  
Like you I sit as a chief, uncle <span id="fr_65"></span>[1](#fn_60)
that I bore?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_60"></span>[41:1](hs060.htm#fr_65) One of the parents'
ancestors is reborn: therefore the child is called "uncle."
