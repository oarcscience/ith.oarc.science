#### EXCERPT XIII.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### OF THE WHOLE ECONOMY

<span class="small">(Patrizzi (p. 38) gives no title, but only the
heading: “To the Same Ammon (Αμμωνα).”</span>

<span class="small">Text: Stob., *Phys*., v. 16, under sub-heading: “Of
the Whole Economy,” followed by: “Of Hermes from the \[Sermons\] to
Ammon (Ἀμοῦν <span id="fr_201"></span>[1](#fn_201))”; G. p. 68; M. i.
47; W. i. 79, 80.</span>

<span class="small">Ménard, Livre IV., No. i. of “Fragments of the Books
of Hermes to Ammon”).</span>

<span class="smallcaps">Now</span> what supporteth the whole
World, <span id="fr_202"></span>[2](#fn_202) is Providence; what holdeth
it together and encircleth it about, is \[called\] Necessity; what
drives all on and drives them round, <span
id="fr_203"></span>[3](#fn_203) is Fate, bringing Necessity to bear on
them (for that its nature is the bringing into play of \[this\]
Necessity); \[it <span id="fr_204"></span>[4](#fn_204) is\] the cause of
birth and death <span id="fr_205"></span>[5](#fn_205) of life.

So, then, the Cosmos is beneath the sway of

<span id="page_62">p. 62</span>

<span class="contnote">\[paragraph continues\]</span> Providence <span
id="fr_206"></span>[1](#fn_206) (for ’tis the first to meet with it);
but Providence \[itself\] <span id="fr_207"></span>[2](#fn_207) extends
itself to Heaven.

For which cause, <span id="fr_208"></span>[3](#fn_208) too, the Gods
revolve, and speed round \[Heaven\], <span
id="fr_209"></span>[4](#fn_209) possessed of tireless, never-ceasing
motion.

But Fate \[extends itself in Cosmos\]; for which cause, too, Necessity
\[encompasses the Cosmos\]. <span id="fr_210"></span>[5](#fn_210)

And Providence foreknows; but Fate’s the reason of the disposition of
the Stars. <span id="fr_211"></span>[6](#fn_211)

Such is the law that no one can escape, by which all things are
ordered. <span id="fr_212"></span>[7](#fn_212)
