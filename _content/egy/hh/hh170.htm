XCVII. HOW A MAN LIVING PERPETUALLY IN MOTION, AND AGITATION OF MIND.

 1

When they would symbolise a man living perpetually 

p. 145

in motion, and agitation of mind, and not even remaining quiet during meals, they depict THE YOUNG ONES OF A CROW 1 [a crow with its young]; for whilst she flies she feeds her young.

Footnotes

144:1 Al. om.

145:1 De Pauw sug. and Treb. reads κορώνην νεοσσοῖς, a crow with its young.
