revenges: The word parôth, rendered revenges, a sense in which it never
seems to be used, has rendered this passage very obscure. As the word
paira signifies the hair of the head, both in Hebrew and Arabic, Mr.
Parkhurst and others render mairosh parôth, "from the hairy head;" but
to have this sense, the words should rather have been mipparôth rosh,
according the Hebrew idiom. The word farôu, in Arabic, however, also
denotes a prince or chief; and the words may be literally rendered, with
the LXX, απο κεφαλης αρχοντων εχθρων, "from the head of the chiefs of
the enemies." The hyperbaton, or transposition of words from their
grammatical order, is very observable in this verse; the third member
forming a continuation of the first, and the fourth of the second. [Job
13:24](../../../bib/kjv/job013.htm#024); [Jer
30:14](../../../bib/kjv/jer030.htm#014); [Lam
2:5](../../../bib/kjv/lam002.htm#005)
