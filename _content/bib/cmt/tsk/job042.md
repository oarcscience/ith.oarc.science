Overview

[Job 42:1](../../../bib/kjv/job042.htm#001), Job submits himself unto
God; [Job 42:7](../../../bib/kjv/job042.htm#007), God, preferring Job's
cause, makes his friends submit themselves, and accepts him; [Job
42:10](../../../bib/kjv/job042.htm#010), He magnifies and blesses Job;
[Job 42:16](../../../bib/kjv/job042.htm#016), Job's age and death.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">job 42:8</span></span>

Therefore: From this it appears that Job was considered as a priest, not
only to his own family, but also to others. For his children he offered
burnt offerings ([Job 1:5](../../../bib/kjv/job001.htm#005)), and now he
is to make the same kind of offerings, accompanied with intercession, in
behalf of his three friends. This is a full proof of the innocence and
integrity of Job.
