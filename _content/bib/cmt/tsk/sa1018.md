Overview

[Sa1 18:1](../../../bib/kjv/sa1018.htm#001), Jonathan loves David; [Sa1
18:5](../../../bib/kjv/sa1018.htm#005), Saul envies his praise; [Sa1
18:10](../../../bib/kjv/sa1018.htm#010), seeks to kill him in his fury;
[Sa1 18:12](../../../bib/kjv/sa1018.htm#012), fears him for his good
success; [Sa1 18:17](../../../bib/kjv/sa1018.htm#017), offers him his
daughters for a snare; [Sa1 18:23](../../../bib/kjv/sa1018.htm#023),
David persuaded to be the king's son-in-law, gives two hundred foreskins
of the Philistines for Michal's dowry; [Sa1
18:28](../../../bib/kjv/sa1018.htm#028), Saul's hatred and David's glory
increase.

[1 Kings (1 Samuel) 18:1](../../../bib/kjv/sa1018.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">sa1 18:1</span></span>

the soul of Jonathan: The modesty, piety, and courage of David were so
congenial to the character of the amiable Jonathan, that they attracted
his most cordial esteem and affection; so that the most intimate
friendship subsisted between them from that time, and they loved each
other with pure hearts fervently. Their friendship could not be affected
by the common vicissitudes of life; and it exemplifies by fact what the
ancients have written on the subject.

represented on ancient monuments. In ancient times, says Justin, kings
used a spear instead of a sceptre.
