<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">eze 43:14</span></span>

the lower settle: These settles were ledges by which the altar was
narrowed towards the top; and the whole of it may thus be computed:

Temple Dimensions Height (in Cubits) Base [Eze
43:13](../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#013) 1 To first ledge [Eze
43:14](../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#014) 1 To upper ledge [Eze
43:14](../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#014) 4 To hearth [Eze
43:15](../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#015) 4 Total Height 10 Breadth (in
Cubits) Upper ledge [Eze 43:17](../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#017) 14 For
higher ledge [Eze 43:14](../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#014) 2 For lower
ledge [Eze 43:14](../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#014) 2 For base [Eze
43:13](../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#013) 2 Total Breadth 20 Hence the
upper part of the altar was only twelve cubits square ([Eze
43:16](../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#016)), the upper settle, or ledge,
being in all fourteen cubits ([Eze
43:17](../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#017)), deducting two cubits from its
dimensions. Though this altar was the same in height, and breadth with
that of Solomon, yet it differed materially from it in having settles or
ledges; on which the priests walked round the altar, to officiate in
offering sacrifices.
