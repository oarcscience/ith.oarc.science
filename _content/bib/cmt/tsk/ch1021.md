### [1 Chronicles Chapter 21](../../../bib/kjv/ch1021.htm#001)

[1 Chronicles](../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">ch1 21:0</span></span>

Overview

[Ch1 21:1](../../../bib/kjv/ch1021.htm#001), David, tempted by Satan,
forces Joab to number the people; [Ch1
21:5](../../../bib/kjv/ch1021.htm#005), The number of the people being
brought, David repents of it; [Ch1
21:9](../../../bib/kjv/ch1021.htm#009), David having three plagues
propounded by God, chooses the pestilence; [Ch1
21:14](../../../bib/kjv/ch1021.htm#014), After the death of seventy
thousand, David by repentance prevents the destruction of Jerusalem;
[Ch1 21:18](../../../bib/kjv/ch1021.htm#018), David, by Gad's direction,
purchases Ornan's threshing- floor; where having built an altar, God
gives a sign of his favour by fire, and stays the plague; [Ch1
21:28](../../../bib/kjv/ch1021.htm#028), David sacrifices there, being
restrained from Gibeon by fear of the angel.

for I will not: It is a maxim from heaven, "Honour the Lord with thy
substance." He who has a religion that costs him nothing, has a religion
that is worth nothing; nor will any man esteem the ordinances of God, if
those ordinances cost him nothing. Had Araunah's noble offer been
accepted, it would have been Araunah's sacrifice, not David's; nor would
it have answered the end of turning away the displeasure of the Most
High. It was David that sinned, not Araunah; therefore David must offer
sacrifice.
