### [Judges Chapter 12](../../../bib/kjv/jdg012.htm#001)

[Judges](../../../bib/kjv/jdg001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">jdg 12:0</span></span>

Overview

[Jdg 12:1](../../../bib/kjv/jdg012.htm#001), The Ephraimites,
quarrelling with Jephthah, and discerned by Shibboleth, are slain by the
Gileadites; [Jdg 12:7](../../../bib/kjv/jdg012.htm#007), Jephthah dies;
[Jdg 12:8](../../../bib/kjv/jdg012.htm#008), Ibzan, who had thirty sons,
and thirty daughters; [Jdg 12:11](../../../bib/kjv/jdg012.htm#011), and
Elon; [Jdg 12:13](../../../bib/kjv/jdg012.htm#013), and Abdon, who had
forty sons, and thirty nephews, judge Israel.

Shibboleth: which signifieth a stream, or flood, [Psa
69:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#002), [Psa
69:15](../../../bib/kjv/psa069.htm#015); [Isa
27:12](../../../bib/kjv/isa027.htm#012) \*Heb: Shibboleth also means an
ear of corn ([Job 24:24](../../../bib/kjv/job024.htm#024)), and
sibboleth signifies a burden ([Exo
6:6](../../../bib/kjv/exo006.htm#006)); and a heavy burden they were
obliged to bear who could not pronounce this test letter. It is well
known that several nations cannot pronounce certain letters. The sound
of th cannot be pronounced by the Persians, no more than by some of our
Continental neighbours; though it is a common sound among the Arabians.
To this day, many of the German Jews cannot articulate ת th, for which
they substitute ss; thus for baith, a house, they say baiss.

there fell: [Pro 17:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro017.htm#014), [Pro
18:19](../../../bib/kjv/pro018.htm#019); [Ecc
10:12](../../../bib/kjv/ecc010.htm#012); [Mat
12:25](../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#025); [Gal
5:15](../../../bib/kjv/gal005.htm#015)

forty: Arbaim ooshenayim aleph, "forty and two thousand." Here the ו,
and, may mean simple addition; and this number may denote 2,040 and not
42,000. At the last census of the Israelites ([Num
26:37](../../../bib/kjv/num026.htm#037)) the whole tribe of Ephraim only
amounted to 32,500, compared with which this last number appears far too
great.
