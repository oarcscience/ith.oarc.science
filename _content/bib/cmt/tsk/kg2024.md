### [4 Kings (2 Kings) Chapter 24](../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#001)

[4 Kings (2 Kings)](../../../bib/kjv/kg2001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">kg2 24:0</span></span>

Overview

[Kg2 24:1](../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#001), Jehoiakim, first subdued by
Nebuchadnezzar, then rebelling against him, procures his own ruin; [Kg2
24:5](../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#005), Jehoiachin succeeds him; [Kg2
24:7](../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#007), The king of Egypt is vanquished
by the king of Babylon; [Kg2 24:8](../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#008),
Jehoiachin's evil reign; [Kg2 24:10](../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#010),
Jerusalem is taken and carried captive into Babylon; [Kg2
24:17](../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#017), Zedekiah is made king, and
reigns ill, unto the utter destruction of Judah.

[4 Kings (2 Kings) 24:6](../../../bib/kjv/kg2024.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">kg2 24:6</span></span>

am 3405. bc 599.

slept: As Jehoiakim was "buried with the burial of an ass," by being
"drawn and cast forth beyond the gates of Jerusalem," without
internment, the expression "slept with his fathers," can only mean that
the died, or slept the sleep of death. In the East, a body exposed
during the night would be a prey to wild animals; if any of it were left
till the morning, the carnivorous birds would devour it. [Ch2
36:6](../../../bib/kjv/ch2036.htm#006), [Ch2
36:8](../../../bib/kjv/ch2036.htm#008); [Jer
22:18](../../../bib/kjv/jer022.htm#018), [Jer
22:19](../../../bib/kjv/jer022.htm#019), [Jer
36:30](../../../bib/kjv/jer036.htm#030)

Jehoiachin: As this man reigned only three months, and was a mere vassal
of the king of Babylon, his reign is scarcely reckoned; and therefore
Jeremiah ([Jer 36:30](../../../bib/kjv/jer036.htm#030)) says of
Jehoiakim, "he shall have none to sit upon the throne of David.
