### [Joshua Chapter 1](../../../bib/kjv/jos001.htm#001)

Overview

[Jos 1:1](../../../bib/kjv/jos001.htm#001), The Lord appoints Joshua to
succeed Moses; [Jos 1:3](../../../bib/kjv/jos001.htm#003), The borders
of the promised land; [Jos 1:5](../../../bib/kjv/jos001.htm#005), God
promises to assist Joshua; [Jos 1:8](../../../bib/kjv/jos001.htm#008),
He gives him instructions; [Jos 1:10](../../../bib/kjv/jos001.htm#010),
Joshua prepares the people to pass over Jordan; [Jos
1:12](../../../bib/kjv/jos001.htm#012), He puts the two tribes and half
in mind of their promise to Moses; [Jos
1:16](../../../bib/kjv/jos001.htm#016), They promise him fealty.

Desert of Arabia Petrea on the south, to Lebanon on the north; and from

till the time of David. [Gen 15:18-21](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#018);

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">jos 1:10</span></span>

the officers of the people: The shoterim, or officers, were different
from the shophetim, who were the judges among the people. The shoterim
have been supposed to be subordinate officers, whose business it was to
see the decisions of the shophetim carried into effect. Calmet
conjectures, that the shoterim here may have been the heralds of the
army. [Jos 1:10](../../../bib/kjv/jos001.htm#010)
