[Job 39:26](../../../bib/kjv/job039.htm#026)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">job 39:26</span></span>

the hawk: Netz, Arabic naz, Latin nisus, the hawk, so called from
natzah, to shoot away, fly, because of the rapidity of its flight. It
probably comprehends various species of the falcon family, as the
ger-falcon, goshawk, and sparrowhawk. [Lev
16:11](../../../bib/kjv/lev016.htm#011); [Deu
14:15](../../../bib/kjv/deu014.htm#015)

stretch: Is it through thy teaching that the falcon, or any other bird
of passage, knows the precise time for taking flight, and the direction
in which she is to go to arrive at a warmer climate? [Sol
2:12](../../../bib/kjv/sol002.htm#012); [Jer
8:7](../../../bib/kjv/jer008.htm#007)
