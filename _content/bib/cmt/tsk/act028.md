Overview

[Act 28:1](../../../bib/kjv/act028.htm#001), Paul, after his shipwreck,
is kindly entertained of the barbarians; [Act
28:5](../../../bib/kjv/act028.htm#005), The viper on his hand hurts him

in the island; [Act 28:11](../../../bib/kjv/act028.htm#011), They depart
towards Rome; [Act 28:17](../../../bib/kjv/act028.htm#017), He declares
to the Jews the cause of his coming; [Act
28:24](../../../bib/kjv/act028.htm#024), After his preaching some were
persuaded, and some believed not; [Act

years.

Syracuse: Syracuse was the capital of Sicily, situated on the eastern
side of the island, 72 miles s by e of Messina, and about 112 of
Palermo. In its ancient state of splendour it was 22+ in extent,
according to Strabo; and such was its opulence, that when the Romans
took it, they found more riches than they did at Carthage. [Act
28:12](../../../bib/kjv/act028.htm#012)
