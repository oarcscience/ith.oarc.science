### [Jeremiah Chapter 29](../../../bib/kjv/jer029.htm#001)

[Jeremiah](../../../bib/kjv/jer001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">jer 29:0</span></span>

Overview

[Jer 29:1](../../../bib/kjv/jer029.htm#001), Jeremiah sends a letter to
the captives in Babylon to be quiet there, [Jer
29:8](../../../bib/kjv/jer029.htm#008), and not to believe the dreams of
their prophets; [Jer 29:10](../../../bib/kjv/jer029.htm#010), and that
they shall return with grace after seventy years; [Jer
29:15](../../../bib/kjv/jer029.htm#015), He foretells the destruction of
the rest for their disobedience; [Jer
29:20](../../../bib/kjv/jer029.htm#020), He shews the fearful end of
Ahab and Zedekiah, two lying prophets; [Jer
29:24](../../../bib/kjv/jer029.htm#024), Shemaiah writes a letter
against Jeremiah; [Jer 29:30](../../../bib/kjv/jer029.htm#030), Jeremiah
foretells his doom.

[Jeremiah 29:20](../../../bib/kjv/jer029.htm#020)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">jer 29:20</span></span>

hear: Dr. Blayney thinks there were two letters written by the prophet
to the captives in Babylon, and the first ends with this verse. That
having heard, on the return of the embassy, that the captives had
received his advice favourably, and because they were deceived by false
prophets, who promised them a speedier deliverance, he therefore wrote a
second letter, beginning with the [Jer
