### [2 Corinthians Chapter 11](../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#001)

[2 Corinthians](../../../bib/kjv/co2001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">co2 11:0</span></span>

Overview

[Co2 11:1](../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#001), Out of his jealousy over
the Corinthians, who seemed to make more account of the false apostles
than of him, he enters into a forced commendation of himself, [Co2
11:5](../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#005), of his equality with the chief
apostles, [Co2 11:7](../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#007), of his preaching
the gospel to them freely, and without any charge to them; [Co2
11:13](../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#013), shewing that he was not
inferior to those deceitful workers in any legal prerogative; [Co2
11:23](../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#023), and in the service of Christ,
and in all kinds of sufferings for his ministry, far superior.

Aretas: This Aretas was an Arabian king, and the father-in-law of Herod
Antipas, upon whom he made war in consequence of his having divorced his
daughter. Herod applied to Tiberius for help, who sent Vitellius to
reduce Aretas, and to bring him alive or dead to Rome. By some means or
other Vitellius delayed his operations, and in the mean time Tiberius
died; and it is probable that Aretas, who was thus snatched from ruin,
availed himself of the favourable state of things, and seized on
Damascus, which had belonged to his ancestors.
