Overview

[Luk 23:1](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#001), Jesus is accused before
Pilate, and sent to Herod; [Luk 23:8](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#008),
Herod mocks him; [Luk 23:12](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#012), Herod and
Pilate are made friends; [Luk 23:13](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#013),
Barabbas is desired of the people, and is loosed by Pilate, and Jesus is
given to be crucified; [Luk 23:26](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#026), He
tells the women, that lament him, the destruction of Jerusalem; [Luk
23:34](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#034), prays for his enemies; [Luk
23:39](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#039), Two evildoers are crucified
with him; [Luk 23:46](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#046), His death; [Luk
23:50](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#050), His burial.

[Luke 23:29](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#029)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_029">luk 23:29</span></span>

the days: Our Lord here refers to the destruction of Jerusalem, and the
final desolation of the Jewish state; an evil associated with so many
miseries, that sterility, which had otherwise been considered an
opprobrium, was accounted a circumstance most felicitous. No history can
furnish us with a parallel to the calamities and miseries of the Jews;
rapine and murder, famine and pestilence, within; fire and sword, and
all the terrors of war, without. Our Saviour himself wept at the
foresight of these calamities; and it is almost impossible for persons
of any humanity to read the relation of them in Josephus without weeping
also. He might justly affirm, "if the misfortunes of all, from the
beginning of the world, were compared with those of the Jews, they would
appear much inferior in the comparison." [Luk
21:23](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#023), [Luk
21:24](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#024); [Mat
24:19](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#019); [Mar
13:17-19](../../../bib/kjv/mar013.htm#017)
