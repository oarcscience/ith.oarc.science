Kenath: Kenath was situated in the tribe of Manasseh, east of Jordan.
Eusebius says it was called in his time Καναθα, Kanatha; and was a town
in the Trachonitis, near Bozra. Josephus places it in Coelo-syria; and
Pliny reckons it among the cities of the Decapolis. It was also called
Nobah, after Nobah an Israelite, who conquered it ([Num
32:42](../../../bib/kjv/num032.htm#042)); which is placed by Eusebius,
eight miles south of Heshbon. see note on [Jdg
8:11](../../../bib/kjv/jdg008.htm#011).
