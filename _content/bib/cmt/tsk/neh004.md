and the other half: This is no unusual thing in Palestine, even at the
present day; people sowing their seed are often attended by armed men,
to prevent the Arabs from robbing them of it.

habergeons: Habergeon, from the Teutonic hals, the neck, and bergen, to
cover, defend, may be considered as signifying a breast-plate, though
the Franco-Gallic hautbergon signifies a coat of mail; the original
shiryon, we have already seen, denotes a corslet.

much a soldier as a builder.
