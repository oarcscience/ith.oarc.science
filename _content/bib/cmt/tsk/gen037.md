Overview

[Gen 37:1](../../../bib/kjv/gen037.htm#001), Joseph is loved by Jacob,
but hated by his brethren; [Gen 37:5](../../../bib/kjv/gen037.htm#005),
His dreams and the interpretation; [Gen
37:12](../../../bib/kjv/gen037.htm#012), Jacob sends him to his
brethren, who counsel to slay him; [Gen
37:21](../../../bib/kjv/gen037.htm#021), At Reuben's desire they cast
him into a pit; [Gen 37:25](../../../bib/kjv/gen037.htm#025), and
afterwards sell him to the Ishmaelites; while Ruben grieves at not
finding him; [Gen 37:31](../../../bib/kjv/gen037.htm#031), His coat,
covered with blood, is sent to Jacob, who mourns him inordinately; [Gen
37:36](../../../bib/kjv/gen037.htm#036), Joseph is brought to Egypt and
sold to Potiphar.

and could not speak peaceably unto him: Or, rather, "and they could not
speak peace to him," i.e., they would not accost him in a friendly
manner. they would not even wish him well, in the eastern mode of
salutation of, Peace be to thee! It is not an unusual thing for an Arab
or Turk to hesitate to return the salâm, if given by a Christian, or by
one of whom he has not a favourable opinion; and this may always be
considered as an act of hostility.

spicery: Nechoth, is rendered by the LXX "incense;" Syriac, "resin;"
Samaritan, "balsam;" Acquila, "storax;" which is followed by Bochart.
This drug is abundant in Syria, and here Moses joins with it resin,
honey, and myrrh; which agrees with the nature of the storax, which is
the resin of a tree of the same name, of a reddish colour, and
peculiarly pleasant fragrance.

balm: Tzeri, which in Arabic, as a verb, is to flow, seems to be a
common name, as balm or balsam with us, for many of those oily, resinous
substances, which flow spontaneously, or by incision, from various trees
or plants; accordingly the ancients have generally interpreted it resin.

myrrh: Lot, is probably, as Junius, Deut. Dieu, Celsius, and Ursinus
