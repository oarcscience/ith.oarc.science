Overview

[Gen 38:1](../../../bib/kjv/gen038.htm#001), Judah begets Er, Onan, and
Shelah; [Gen 38:6](../../../bib/kjv/gen038.htm#006), Er's marriage with
Tamar, and death; [Gen 38:8](../../../bib/kjv/gen038.htm#008), The
trespass of Onan; [Gen 38:11](../../../bib/kjv/gen038.htm#011), Tamar is
reserved for Shelah; [Gen 38:12](../../../bib/kjv/gen038.htm#012),
Judah's wife dies; [Gen 38:13](../../../bib/kjv/gen038.htm#013), Tamar
deceives Judah; [Gen 38:27](../../../bib/kjv/gen038.htm#027), She bears
twins, Pharez and Zarah.

[Genesis 38:1](../../../bib/kjv/gen038.htm#001)

am 2265, bc 1739

it came: As there cannot be above 23 years from the selling of Joseph,
unto Israel's going down into Egypt; and as it is impossible that Judah
should take a wife, and by her have three sons successively, and Shelah,
the youngest, marriageable when Judah begat Pharez of Tamar, and Pharez
be grown up, married, and have two sons, all within so short a period;
Mr. Ainsworth conceives that the time here spoken of is soon after
Jacob's coming to Shechem ([Gen 33:1](../../../bib/kjv/gen033.htm#001)).
We have accordingly adapted the chronology to correspond with that time.

Thy signet: Chothem, or chothemeth, as in [Gen
38:25](../../../bib/kjv/gen038.htm#025), is properly a ring-seal, with
which impressions were made to ascertain property, etc. From [Jer
22:24](../../../bib/kjv/jer022.htm#024), we find that it was worn on the
hand; though it might also have been suspended from the neck by a
ribband, as the Arabs still wear it. [Gen
38:25](../../../bib/kjv/gen038.htm#025), [Gen
38:26](../../../bib/kjv/gen038.htm#026); [Jer
22:24](../../../bib/kjv/jer022.htm#024); [Luk
15:22](../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#022)

bracelets: Pathil, from pathal, to twist, wreathe, may denote either a
wreath for the arm or neck, a twisted collar, or bracelet. In the former
sense the LXX render it by ορμισκον, and Aquila and Symmachus by
στρεπτον; and in the latter sense, the Vulgate renders it by armillam.
It may have been a collar by which the signet was suspended; though its
being used in the plural seems to favour the opinion of its being a
bracelet.
