apart for the priest's office; [Exo
28:2](../../../bib/kjv/exo028.htm#002), Holy garments are appointed;
[Exo 28:6](../../../bib/kjv/exo028.htm#006), The ephod and girdle; [Exo
28:15](../../../bib/kjv/exo028.htm#015), The breast-plate with twelve
precious stones; [Exo 28:30](../../../bib/kjv/exo028.htm#030), The Urim
and Thummim; [Exo 28:31](../../../bib/kjv/exo028.htm#031), The robe of
the ephod, with pomegranates and bells; [Exo
28:36](../../../bib/kjv/exo028.htm#036), The plate of the mitre; [Exo
28:39](../../../bib/kjv/exo028.htm#039), The embroidered coat; [Exo
28:40](../../../bib/kjv/exo028.htm#040), The garments for Aaron's sons.

Among the various and contradictory opinions respecting the form and
substance of these mysterious appendages, the most probable seems to be
that of Josephus, Philo, Bp. Patrick, Parkhurst, and the Jewish writers
generally; who state, that they were no other than the twelve precious

[Lev 8:8](../../../bib/kjv/lev008.htm#008), the Urim and Thummim are
expressly mentioned, but not a word is said of the four rows of stones.

2\. As Moses has given such a particular description of every thing
relative to the high priest's dress, these would certainly have been
described had they been different from what was previously mentioned.
