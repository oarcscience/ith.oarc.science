Overview

[Exo 4:1](../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#001), Moses's rod is turned into a
serpent; [Exo 4:6](../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#006), His hand is
leprous; [Exo 4:10](../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#010), He is loath to be
sent; [Exo 4:13](../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#013), Aaron is appointed to
assist him; [Exo 4:18](../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#018), Moses departs
from Jethro; [Exo 4:21](../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#021), God's message
to Pharaoh; [Exo 4:24](../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#024), Zipporah
circumcises her son; [Exo 4:27](../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#027), Aaron
is sent to meet Moses; [Exo 4:29](../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#029), The
people believe them.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">exo 4:19</span></span>

Midian: A country in Arabia Petrea, on the eastern coast of the Red Sea,
near Mount Sinai. This place is still called by the Arabs the Land of
Midian, or of Jethro. Abulfeda, speaking of Midian, says, "Madyan is a
city, in ruins, on the shore of the Red Sea, on the opposite side to
Tabuc, from which it is distant about six days' journey. At Midian may
be seen the famous well at which Moses watered the flocks of Shoaib
(Jethro). This city was the capital of the tribe of Midian in the days
of the Israelites."
