[Judges 19:9](../../../bib/kjv/jdg019.htm#009)

draweth: etc. Heb. is weak

the day groweth to an end: Heb. it is the pitching time of the day, [Jer
6:4](../../../bib/kjv/jer006.htm#004). That is, it was near the time in
which travellers ordinarily pitched their tents, to take up their
lodging for the night. In the latter part of the afternoon, eastern
travellers begin to look out for a place for this purpose. So Dr. Shaw
observes, "Our constant practice was to rise at break of day, set
forward with the sun, and travel to the middle of the afternoon; at
which time we began to look out for encampments of Arabs; who, to
prevent such parties as ours from living at free charges upon them, take
care to pitch in woods, valleys, or places the least conspicuous."

[Judges 19:29](../../../bib/kjv/jdg019.htm#029)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_029">jdg 19:29</span></span>

divided her: It is probable, that with the pieces he sent to each tribe
a circumstantial account of the barbarity of the men of Gibeah; and that
they considered each of the pieces as expressing an execration. That a
similar custom prevailed in ancient times is evident from [Sa1
11:7](../../../bib/kjv/sa1011.htm#007). It had an inhuman appearance,
thus to mangle the corpse of this unhappy woman; but it was intended to
excite a keener resentment against so horrible a crime, which called for
a punishment proportionally severe. [Jdg
20:6](../../../bib/kjv/jdg020.htm#006), [Jdg
20:7](../../../bib/kjv/jdg020.htm#007); [Rom
10:2](../../../bib/kjv/rom010.htm#002)
