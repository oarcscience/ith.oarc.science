<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">mic 7:0</span></span>

Overview

[Mic 7:1](../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#001), The church, complaining of
her small number, [Mic 7:3](../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#003), and the
general corruption, [Mic 7:5](../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#005), puts her
confidence not in man, but in God; [Mic
7:8](../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#008), She triumphs over her enemies;
[Mic 7:14](../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#014), She prays to God; [Mic
7:15](../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#015), God comforts her by promises of
confusion to her enemies; [Mic 7:18](../../../bib/kjv/mic007.htm#018),
and by his mercies.

and from: or, even to, This verse may be rendered, "In that day they
(people) shall come to thee from Assyria and the fenced cities; and from
the fortress (probably Pelusium at the entrance of Egypt), even to the
river (Euphrates)," etc. The expressions employed in this prophecy
appear to be too strong for the events which transpired after the
Babylonian captivity; and seem to refer to the future restoration of
Israel, after their land has lain desolate for ages.
