Overview

[Num 12:1](../../../bib/kjv/num012.htm#001), God rebukes the sedition of
Miriam and Aaron; [Num 12:11](../../../bib/kjv/num012.htm#011), Miriam's
leprosy is healed at the prayer of Moses; [Num
12:14](../../../bib/kjv/num012.htm#014), God commands her to be shut out
of the host; [Num 12:16](../../../bib/kjv/num012.htm#016), The people
encamp in the wilderness of Paran.

afterward: [Num 11:35](../../../bib/kjv/num011.htm#035), [Num
33:18](../../../bib/kjv/num033.htm#018)

Hazeroth: The exact situation of this place is unknown. Dr. Shaw
computes it to be three days' journey, i.e., thirty miles from Sinai.
From this passage, it appears that the wilderness of Paran commenced
immediately upon their leaving this station. Calmet observes, that there
is a town called Hazor in Arabia Petrea, in all probability the same as
Hazerim, the ancient habitation of the Hivites ([Deu
2:23](../../../bib/kjv/deu002.htm#023)); and likewise, according to all
appearances, the Hazeroth, where the Hebrews encamped.
