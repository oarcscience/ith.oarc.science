<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">mat 21:0</span></span>

Overview

[Mat 21:1](../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#001), Christ rides into Jerusalem
upon an ass; [Mat 21:12](../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#012), drives the
buyers and sellers out of the temple; [Mat
21:17](../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#017), curses the fig-tree; [Mat
21:23](../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#023), puts to silence the priests and
elders, [Mat 21:28](../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#028). and rebukes them
by the similitude of the two sons, [Mat
21:33](../../../bib/kjv/mat021.htm#033). and the husbandmen who slew
such as were sent to them.

Bethany: Bethany was a village to the east of the mount of Olives, on
the road to Jericho; fifteen stadia ([Joh
11:18](../../../bib/kjv/joh011.htm#018)) or nearly two miles, as Jerome
states, from Jerusalem. This village is now small and poor, and the
cultivation of the soil around it is much neglected; but it is a
pleasant, romantic spot, shaded by the mount of Olives, and abounding in
vines and long grass. It consists of from thirty to forty dwellings
inhabited by about 600; Mohammedans, for whose use there is a neat
little mosque standing on an eminence. Here they shew the ruins of a
sort of castle as the house of Lazarus, and a grotto as his tomb; and
the house of Simon the leper, of Mary Magdalene and of Martha, and the
identical tree which our Lord cursed, are among the monkish curiosities
of the place. [Mar 11:11](../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#011), [Mar
11:19](../../../bib/kjv/mar011.htm#019); [Luk
10:38](../../../bib/kjv/luk010.htm#038); [Joh
