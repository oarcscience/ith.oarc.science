Overview

[Joh 6:1](../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#001), Christ feeds five thousand
men with five loaves and two fishes; [Joh
6:15](../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#015), Thereupon the people would have
made him king; [Joh 6:16](../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#016), but
withdrawing himself, he walks on the sea to his disciples; [Joh
6:26](../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#026), reproves the people flocking
after him, and all the fleshly hearers of his word; [Joh
6:32](../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#032), declares himself to be the bread
of life to believers; [Joh 6:66](../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#066), Many
disciples depart from him; [Joh 6:68](../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#068),
Peter confesses him; [Joh 6:70](../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#070), Judas
is a devil.

Tiberias: Tiberias was a celebrated city of Galilee, on the western
shore of the lake to which it gave name, so called because built by
Herod Agrippa in honour of the emperor Tiberius; distant 30 furlongs
from Hippos, 60 from Gadara, 120 from Scythopolis, and 30 from Tarichea.
It is still called Tabaria, or Tabbareeah, by the natives, is situated
close to the edge of the lake, has tolerably high but ill-built walls on
three of its sides, flanked with circular towers, and is of nearly a
quadrangular form, according to Pococke, containing a population
estimated at from 2,000 to 4,000 souls. [Joh
