God Almighty: El shadday, God Almighty; for shadday is evidently of
affinity with the Arabic shadid, strong, mighty, and shiddat, strength,
might; so the LXX in Job render it παντοκρατωρ, Vulgate, in Pentateuch,
Omnipotens, and Syriac, in Job, chasino. [Gen
17:1](../../../bib/kjv/gen017.htm#001), [Gen
28:3](../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#003), [Gen
35:11](../../../bib/kjv/gen035.htm#011), [Gen
48:3](../../../bib/kjv/gen048.htm#003)

but by my name: If Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, did not know the name
Jehovah, then Moses must have used it in Genesis by prolepsis, or
anticipation. Mr. Locke and others read it interrogatively, for the
negative particle, lo, not, has frequently this power in Hebrew: "I
appeared unto Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, by the name of God Almighty,
and by my name Jehovah was I not also made known unto them?"

am: The italics shew that this word has been supplied by our
translators; hence it may be inferred by some that Moses was
uncircumcised. The difficulty is in some measure cleared away, by using
the word uncircumcised in the sense of unsuitable or incapacitated (see
note on [Exo 6:30](../../../bib/kjv/exo006.htm#030)), which agrees with
[Exo 4:10](../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#010) where Moses complains of
want of eloquence; but by substituting is for am in this place, the
connection with the former part of the verse is preserved; "For if the
chosen seed, the circumcised sons of Jacob, refuse to hear me," as
though Moses had said, "how can I expect to make uncircumcised Pharaoh
pay attention to me?" [Exo 6:30](../../../bib/kjv/exo006.htm#030), [Exo
