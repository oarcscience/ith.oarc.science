<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">exo 21:15</span></span>

To smite either father or mother, in a manner which indicated either
contempt or malice, or left marks of violence, was deemed a proof of so
ungrateful and unnatural a disposition, that no provocation was admitted
as an excuse, but the offence was made capital. nay, he who cursed his
father or mother, who uttered imprecations, ill wishes, or revilings,
against a parent, was included in the same sense; though few crimes were
made capital by the law of Moses. The law of God, as delegated to
parents is honoured when they are honoured, and despised when they are
despised, and to rebel against the lawful exercise of this authority is
rebellion against God. - Rev. T. Scott
