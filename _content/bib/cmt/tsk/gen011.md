Babel: that is, Confusion, The tower of Babel, Herodotus informs us, was
a furlong or 660 feet, in length and breadth; and, according to Strabo,
it rose to the same altitude. It was of a pyramidical form, consisting
of eight square towers, gradually decreasing in breadth, with a winding
ascent on the outside, so very broad as to allow horses and carriages to
pass each other, and even to turn. This magnificent structure is so
completely destroyed that its very site is doubtful; and when supposed
to be discovered, in all cases exhibiting a heap of rubbish. [Gen
10:5](../../../bib/kjv/gen010.htm#005), [Gen
10:10](../../../bib/kjv/gen010.htm#010), [Gen
10:20](../../../bib/kjv/gen010.htm#020), [Gen
10:31](../../../bib/kjv/gen010.htm#031); Isa. 13:1-14:32; Jer.
50:1-51:64; [Co1 14:23](../../../bib/kjv/co1014.htm#023)

the face: [Gen 10:25](../../../bib/kjv/gen010.htm#025), [Gen

Iscah: Iscah is called the daughter-in-law of Terah ([Gen
11:31](../../../bib/kjv/gen011.htm#031)), as being Abram's wife; yet
Abram afterwards said, "she is the daughter of my father, but not the
daughter of my mother" ([Gen 20:12](../../../bib/kjv/gen020.htm#012)).
Probably Haran was the eldest son of Terah, and Abram his youngest by
another wife; and thus Sarai was the daughter, or grand-daughter of
Terah, Abram's father, but not of his mother.
