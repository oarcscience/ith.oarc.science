thence will the: As this promise refers to a return from a captivity
among all nations, consequently it cannot be exclusively the Babylonish
captivity which is intended; and the repossession of their land must be
different from that which was consequent on their return from Babylon.
Nor at that period could it be said that they were multiplied more than
their fathers, or, as the Hebrew imports, made greater than their
fathers, when after their return they were tributary to the Persians,
and afterwards fell under the power of the Greeks, under whom they
suffered much; nor have their hearts, as a nation, yet been circumcised.
