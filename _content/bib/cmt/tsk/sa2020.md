[Sa2 20:1](../../../bib/kjv/sa2020.htm#001), By occasion of the quarrel,
Sheba makes a party in Israel; [Sa2
20:3](../../../bib/kjv/sa2020.htm#003), David's ten concubines are put
in confinement for life; [Sa2 20:4](../../../bib/kjv/sa2020.htm#004),
Amasa, made captain over Judah, is slain by Joab; [Sa2
20:14](../../../bib/kjv/sa2020.htm#014), Joab pursues Sheba unto Abel;
[Sa2 20:16](../../../bib/kjv/sa2020.htm#016), A wise woman saves the
city by Sheba's head; [Sa2 20:23](../../../bib/kjv/sa2020.htm#023),

took Amasa: Thevenot says, that among the Turks it is a great affront to
take one by the beard, unless it be to kiss him, in which case they
often do it. D'Arvieux, describing an assembly of Arab emirs at an
entertainment, says, "After the usual civilities, caresses, kissings of
the beard, and of the hand, which every one gave and received according
to his rank and dignity, they sat down upon mats." The doing this by the
Arab emirs corresponds with the conduct of Joab, and illustrates this
horrid assassination.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">sa2 20:14</span></span>

Abel: Or rather, probably, Abel of Beth-Maachah, as in the next verse.
It appears, from Joab having marched "through all the tribes of Israel,"
to have been situated in the northern confines of the land of Israel,
and in the half tribe of Manasseh, east of Jordan, as that was the
situation of Maachah, to which it belonged. This agrees with the
situation of the Abila which Eusebius and Jerome place between Paneas,
or Cesarea Philippi, and Damascus. Josephus says it was a fortified
city, and a metropolis of the Israelites; and also that it belonged to
the ten tribes, having been taken from the king of Damascus. [Kg1
15:20](../../../bib/kjv/kg1015.htm#020); [Kg2
15:29](../../../bib/kjv/kg2015.htm#029); [Ch2
16:4](../../../bib/kjv/ch2016.htm#004)
