### [Ezekiel Chapter 33](../../../bib/kjv/eze033.htm#001)

[Ezekiel](../../../bib/kjv/eze001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">eze 33:0</span></span>

Overview

[Eze 33:1](../../../bib/kjv/eze033.htm#001), According to the duty of a
watchman in warning the people, [Eze
33:7](../../../bib/kjv/eze033.htm#007), Ezekiel is admonished of his
duty; [Eze 33:10](../../../bib/kjv/eze033.htm#010), God shews the
justice of his ways towards the penitent and towards revolters; [Eze
33:17](../../../bib/kjv/eze033.htm#017), He maintains his justice; [Eze
33:21](../../../bib/kjv/eze033.htm#021), Upon the news of the taking of
Jerusalem he prophecies the desolation of the land; [Eze
33:30](../../../bib/kjv/eze033.htm#030), God's judgment upon the mockers
of the prophets.

If our: The impenitent Jews seem to have charged the prophet's messages
with inconsistency. for whilst he warned them to repent, and assured the
penitent of forgiveness, he also predicted that the people "would pine
away in their transgressions." The prediction, however, merely implied
that God foresaw that the people in general would be impenitent, though
some individuals would repent and be pardoned. [Eze
24:23](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#023); [Lev
26:39](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#039)
