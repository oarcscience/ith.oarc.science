human courses are vain; [Ecc 1:4](../../../bib/kjv/ecc001.htm#004),

1:9](../../../bib/kjv/ecc001.htm#009), they bring forth nothing new, and
all old things are forgotten; [Ecc

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">ecc 1:6</span></span>

The wind: This verse should be connected with the preceding, and
rendered, "The sun also riseth, and the sun goeth down, and hasteth to
his place where he arose; going toward the south, and turning about unto
the north. The wind whirleth about continually," etc. Alluding, in the
former part, to the apparent daily motion of the sun from east to west,
and to his annual course through the signs of the zodiac. [Job
37:9](../../../bib/kjv/job037.htm#009), [Job
37:17](../../../bib/kjv/job037.htm#017); [Psa
107:25](../../../bib/kjv/psa107.htm#025), [Psa
107:29](../../../bib/kjv/psa107.htm#029); [Jon
1:4](../../../bib/kjv/jon001.htm#004); [Mat
7:24](../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#024), [Mat
7:27](../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#027); [Joh
