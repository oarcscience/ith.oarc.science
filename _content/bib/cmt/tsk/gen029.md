a great stone: In Arabia, and other places in the East, they cover up
their wells of water, lest the sand, which is put in motion by the
winds, should fill and quite stop them up. So great was their care not
to leave the well open any length of time, that they waited till the
flocks were all gathered together before they began to draw water; and
when they had finished, the well was immediately closed again.

[Genesis 29:3](../../../bib/kjv/gen029.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">gen 29:3</span></span>

the flocks: Instead of haadarim, "the flocks," the Samaritan reads
haroim, "the shepherds," as does also the Arabic in Walton's Polyglott.
This verse describes what was usually done by some mutual compact among
the shepherds, and shows the purpose for which the flocks lay by the
well; for the stone was not removed till all the flocks had been
collected. Scott. [Gen 29:3](../../../bib/kjv/gen029.htm#003)
