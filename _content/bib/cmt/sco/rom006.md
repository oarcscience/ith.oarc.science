[Romans 6:6](../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">rom 6:6</span></span>

old self

The expression occurs elsewhere, in ([Eph
4:22](../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#022)); ([Col
3:9](../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#009)), and always means the man of old,
corrupt human nature, the inborn tendency to evil in all men. In ([Rom
6:6](../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#006)), it is the natural man himself;
in ([Eph 4:22](../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#022)); ([Col
3:9](../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#009)), his ways. Positionally, in the
reckoning of God, the old man is crucified, and the believer is exhorted
to make this good in experience, reckoning it to be so by definitely
"putting off" the old man and "putting on" the new; ([Col
3:8-14](../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#008)); ([Col
3:4](../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#004)); ([Col
3:24](../../../bib/kjv/col003.htm#024));

(See Scofield) - ([Eph 4:24](../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#024)), note 3.

[Romans 6:15](../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">rom 6:15</span></span>

What then

The old relation to the law and sin, and the new relation to Christ and
life are illustrated by the effect of death upon servitude ([Rom
6:16-23](../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#016)) and marriage ([Rom
7:1-6](../../../bib/kjv/rom007.htm#001)).

\(1\) The old servitude was nominally to the law, but, since the law had
no delivering power, the real master continued to be sin in the nature.
The end was death. The law could not give life, and "sin" (here
personified as the old self) is in itself deathful. But death in another
form, ie, crucifixion with Christ, has intervened to free the servant
from his double bondage to sin ([Rom
6:6](../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#006)); ([Rom
6:7](../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#007)), and to the law ([Rom
7:4](../../../bib/kjv/rom007.htm#004)); ([Rom
7:6](../../../bib/kjv/rom007.htm#006)).

\(2\) This effect of death is further illustrated by widowhood. Death
dissolves the marriage relation ([Rom
7:1-3](../../../bib/kjv/rom007.htm#001)). As natural death frees a wife
from the law of her husband, so crucifixion with Christ sets the
believer free from the law.

(See Scofield) - ([Gal 3:24](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#024)).
