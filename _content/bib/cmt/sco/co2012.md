[2 Corinthians 12:7](../../../bib/kjv/co2012.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">co2 12:7</span></span>

thorn

It has been conjectured that Paul's "thorn in the flesh" was chronic
ophthalmia, inducing bodily weakness, and a repulsive appearance ([Gal
4:15](../../../bib/kjv/gal004.htm#015)); ([Co1
2:3](../../../bib/kjv/co1002.htm#003)); ([Co1
2:4](../../../bib/kjv/co1002.htm#004)); ([Co2
10:10](../../../bib/kjv/co2010.htm#010)). This cannot be positively
known, and the reserve of Scripture is as sure a mark of inspiration as
its revelations. Paul's particular "thorn" is not described that his
consolations may avail for all to who any thorn is given.
