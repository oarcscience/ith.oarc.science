### [Psalms Chapter 40](../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#001)

I waited

The 40th Psalm speaks of Messiah, Jehovah's Servant, obedient unto
death. The Psalm begins with the joy of Christ in resurrection ([Psa
40:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#001)); ([Psa
40:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#002)). He has been in the horrible pit
of the grave, but has been brought up. Verses 3-5 ([Psa
40:3-5](../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#003)) are His resurrection
testimony, His "new song." Verses 6 and 7 ([Psa
40:6-7](../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#006)) are retrospective. When
sacrifice and offering had become abominable because of the wickedness
of the people ([Isa 1:10-15](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#010)) then the
obedient Servant came to make the pure offering; ([Psa
40:7-17](../../../bib/kjv/psa040.htm#007)); ([Heb
10:5-17](../../../bib/kjv/heb010.htm#005)). See Psalm 41, next in order
of the Messianic Psalms.
