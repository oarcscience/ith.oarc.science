### [Matthew Chapter 28](../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#001)

[Matthew 28:1](../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">mat 28:1</span></span>

In the end of the Sabbath

The order of events, combining the four narratives, is as follows:

Three women, Mary Magdalene, and Mary the mother of Jesus, and Salome,
start for the sepulchre, followed by other women bearing spices. The
three find the stone rolled away, and Mary Magdalene goes to tell the
disciples. ([Luk 23:55-24](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#055)); ([Luk
23:9](../../../bib/kjv/luk023.htm#009)); ([Joh
20:1](../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#001)); ([Joh
20:2](../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#002)). Mary, the mother of James and
Joses, draws nearer the tomb and sees the angel of the Lord ([Mat
28:2](../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#002)). She goes back to meet the other
women following with the spices. Meanwhile Peter and John, warned by
Mary Magdalene, arrive, look in, and go away ([Joh
20:3-10](../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#003)). Mary Magdalene returns
weeping, sees the two angels and then Jesus ([Joh
20:11-18](../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#011)) and goes as He bade her to
tell the disciples. Mary (mother of James and Joses), meanwhile, has met
the women with the spices and, returning with them, they see the two
angels. ([Luk 24:4](../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#004)); ([Luk
24:5](../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#005)); ([Mar
16:5](../../../bib/kjv/mar016.htm#005)). They also receive the angelic
message, and, going to seek the disciples, are met by Jesus. ([Mat
28:8-10](../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#008)).

end of

Literally, end of the sabbaths. The sabbaths end, the first day comes.

1:10](../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#010)).

[Matthew 28:9](../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">mat 28:9</span></span>

Jesus

The order of our Lord's appearances would seem to be:

On the day of his resurrection:

\(1\) To Mary Magdalene ([Joh
10:14-18](../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#014)).

\(2\) To the women returning from the tomb with angelic message ([Mat
28:8-10](../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#008)).

\(3\) To Peter, probably in the afternoon ([Luk
24:34](../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#034)); ([Co1
15:5](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#005)).

\(4\) To the Emmaus disciples toward evening ([Luk
24:13-31](../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#013)).

\(5\) To the apostles, except Thomas ([Luk
24:36-43](../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#036)); ([Joh
20:19-24](../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#019)).

Eight days afterward:

\(1\) to the apostles, Thomas being present ([Joh
20:24-29](../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#024)).

In Galilee:

(1a) To the seven by the Lake of Tiberias ([Joh
21:1-23](../../../bib/kjv/joh021.htm#001)).

\(2\) On a mountain, to the apostles and five hundred brethren ([Co1
15:6](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#006)).

At Jerusalem and Bethany again:

\(1\) To James ([Co1 15:7](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#007)).

\(2\) To the eleven ([Mat 28:16-20](../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#016));
([Mar 16:14-20](../../../bib/kjv/mar016.htm#014)); ([Luk
24:33-53](../../../bib/kjv/luk024.htm#033)); ([Act
1:3-12](../../../bib/kjv/act001.htm#003)).

To Paul:

\(1\) Near Damascus ([Act 9:3-6](../../../bib/kjv/act009.htm#003));
([Co1 15:8](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#008)).

\(2\) In the temple ([Act 22:17-21](../../../bib/kjv/act022.htm#017));
([Act 23:11](../../../bib/kjv/act023.htm#011)).

To Stephen: outside Jerusalem ([Act
7:55](../../../bib/kjv/act007.htm#055)).

To John: on Patmos ([Rev 1:10-19](../../../bib/kjv/rev001.htm#010)).

[Matthew 28:19](../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#019)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">mat 28:19</span></span>

Go

With the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ begins the "dispensation
of the grace of God" ([Eph 3:2](../../../bib/kjv/eph003.htm#002)); which
is defined as "his kindness toward us through Christ Jesus"; and, "the
gift of God: not of works, lest any man should boast." ([Eph
2:7-9](../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#007)); under grace God freely gives
to the believing sinner eternal life ([Rom
6:23](../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#023)) accounts to him a perfect
righteousness; ([Rom 3:21](../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#021)); ([Rom
3:22](../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#022)); ([Rom
4:4](../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#004)); ([Rom
4:5](../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#005)) and accords to him a perfect
position ([Eph 1:6](../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#006)).

The predicted results of this sixth testing of man are

\(1\) the salvation of all who believe ([Act
16:31](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#031)).

\(2\) judgment upon an unbelieving world and an apostate church. ([Mat
25:31-46](../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#031)); ([Th2
1:7-10](../../../bib/kjv/th2001.htm#007)); ([Pe1
4:17](../../../bib/kjv/pe1004.htm#017)); ([Pe1
4:18](../../../bib/kjv/pe1004.htm#018)); ([Rev
3:15](../../../bib/kjv/rev003.htm#015)); ([Rev
3:16](../../../bib/kjv/rev003.htm#016)).

\(1\) Man's state at the beginning of the dispensation of Grace ([Rom
3:19](../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#019)); ([Gal
3:22](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#022)); ([Eph
2:11](../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#011)); ([Eph
2:12](../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#012)).

\(2\) Man's responsibility under grace ([Joh
1:11](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#011)); ([Joh
1:12](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012)); ([Joh
3:36](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#036)); ([Joh
6:28](../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#028)); ([Joh
6:29](../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#029)).

\(3\) His predicted failure ([Mat
24:37-39](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#037)); ([Luk
18:8](../../../bib/kjv/luk018.htm#008)); ([Luk
19:12-14](../../../bib/kjv/luk019.htm#012)).

\(4\) The judgment ([Th2 2:7-12](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#007)).

Name

The word is in the singular, the "name," not names. Father, Son, and
Holy Spirit is the final name of the one true God. It affirms:

\(1\) That God is one.

\(2\) That He subsists in a personality which is threefold, indicated by
relationship as Father and Son; by a mode of being as Spirit; and by the
different parts taken by the Godhead in manifestation and in the work of
redemption, for example ([Joh 3:5](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#005));
([Joh 3:6](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#006)); (Spirit), ([Joh
3:16](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#016)); ([Joh
3:17](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#017)) (Father and Son). In; ([Mat
3:16](../../../bib/kjv/mat003.htm#016)); ([Mat
3:17](../../../bib/kjv/mat003.htm#017)); ([Mar
1:10](../../../bib/kjv/mar001.htm#010)); ([Mar
1:11](../../../bib/kjv/mar001.htm#011)); ([Luk
3:21](../../../bib/kjv/luk003.htm#021)); ([Luk
3:22](../../../bib/kjv/luk003.htm#022)); the three persons are in
manifestation together.

\(3\) The conjunction in one name of the Three affirms equality and
oneness of substance.
