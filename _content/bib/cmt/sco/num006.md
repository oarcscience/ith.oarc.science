There is a beautiful moral order in chapters 6-7.

separation, ([Num 6:1-12](../../../bib/kjv/num006.htm#001));

worship, ([Num 6:13-21](../../../bib/kjv/num006.htm#013));

blessing, ([Num 6:22-27](../../../bib/kjv/num006.htm#022))

[Numbers 6:2](../../../bib/kjv/num006.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">num 6:2</span></span>

Nazarite

The Nazarite (more accurately Nazirite, one separated) was a person of
either sex separated wholly unto the Lord. Abstention from wine, the
symbol of mere natural joy ([Psa
104:15](../../../bib/kjv/psa104.htm#015)) was the expression of a
devotedness which found all its joy in the Lord (cf); ([Psa
87:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa087.htm#007)); ([Psa
97:12](../../../bib/kjv/psa097.htm#012)); ([Hab
3:18](../../../bib/kjv/hab003.htm#018)); ([Plm
3:1](../../../bib/kjv/plm003.htm#001)); ([Plm
3:3](../../../bib/kjv/plm003.htm#003)); ([Plm
4:4](../../../bib/kjv/plm004.htm#004)); ([Plm
4:10](../../../bib/kjv/plm004.htm#010)). The long hair, naturally a
reproach to man ([Co1 11:14](../../../bib/kjv/co1011.htm#014)) was at
once the visible sign of the Nazarite's separation, and of his
willingness to bear reproach for Jehovah's sake. The type found its
perfect fulfilment in Jesus, who was "holy, harmless, undefiled and
separate from sinners" ([Heb 7:26](../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#026)) who
was utterly separated unto the Father; ([Joh
1:18](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#018)); ([Joh
6:38](../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#038)) who allowed no mere natural
claim to hinder or divert Him. ([Mat
12:46-50](../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#046)).
