### [Isaiah Chapter 61](../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#001)

[Isaiah 61:2](../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">isa 61:2</span></span>

acceptable year of the Lord

Observe that Jesus suspended the reading of this passage in the
synagogue at Nazareth ([Luk 4:16-21](../../../bib/kjv/luk004.htm#016))
at the comma in the middle of ([Isa
61:2](../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#002)). The first advent, therefore,
opened the day of grace, "the acceptable year of Jehovah," but does not
fulfil the day of vengeance. That will be taken up when Messiah returns
([Th2 1:7-10](../../../bib/kjv/th2001.htm#007)).

Compare ([Isa 34:8](../../../bib/kjv/isa034.htm#008)); ([Isa
35:4-10](../../../bib/kjv/isa035.htm#004)).

The last verse ([Isa 61:11](../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#011)), taken
with the 4th ([Isa 61:4](../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#004)), gives the
historic connection: the vengeance precedes the regathering of Israel,
and synchronizes with the day of the Lord. ([Isa
2:10-22](../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#010)); ([Rev
19:11-21](../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011)); ([Isa
63:1-6](../../../bib/kjv/isa063.htm#001)).
