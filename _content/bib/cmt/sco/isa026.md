### [Isaiah Chapter 26](../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#001)

[Isaiah 26:3](../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">isa 26:3</span></span>

trusteth

(See Scofield) - ([Psa 2:12](../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#012)).

[Isaiah 26:19](../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#019)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">isa 26:19</span></span>

thy dead men

Eliminate the supplied words, men, and, together with. "Body" is in the
plural, "bodies." ([Isa 26:19-21](../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#019)) with
chapter 27, constitute Jehovah's answer to the plaint of Israel, ([Isa
26:11-18](../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#011)). Verse 19 ([Isa
26:19](../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#019)) should read: "Thy dead shall
live: my dead bodies shall rise" (that is, the dead bodies of Jehovah's
people). The restoration and re-establishment of Israel as a nation is
also spoken of as a resurrection ([Eze
37:1-11](../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#001)) and many hold that no more
than this is meant in ([Isa 26:19](../../../bib/kjv/isa026.htm#019)).
But since the first resurrection is unto participation in the kingdom
([Rev 20:4-6](../../../bib/kjv/rev020.htm#004)) it seems the better view
that both meanings are here.
