[John 20:17](../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">joh 20:17</span></span>

Touch me not

Compare ([Mat 28:9](../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#009)); "and they came
and held him by the feet." A contradiction has been supposed.

Three views are held:

\(1\) That Jesus speaks to Mary as the High Priest fulfilling the day of
atonement (Leviticus 16). Having accomplished the sacrifice, He was on
His way to present the sacred blood in heaven, and that, between the
meeting with Mary in the garden and the meeting of ([Mat
28:9](../../../bib/kjv/mat028.htm#009)). He had so ascended and
returned: a view in harmony with types.

\(2\) That Mary Magdalene, knowing as yet only Christ after the flesh
([Co2 5:15-17](../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#015)) and having found her
Beloved, sought only to hold Him so; while He, about to assume a new
relation to His disciples in ascension, gently teaches Mary that now she
must not seek to hold Him to the earth, but rather become His messenger
of the new joy.

\(3\) That He merely meant: "Do not detain me now; I am not yet
ascended; you will see me again; run rather to my brethren," etc.

Touch me not

Or, do not detain me.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">joh 20:28</span></span>

My Lord and My God

The deity of Jesus Christ is declared in Scripture:

\(1\) In the intimations and explicit predictions of the Old Testament.

\(a\) The theophanies intimate the appearance of God in human form, and
His ministry thus to man ([Gen
16:7-13](../../../bib/kjv/gen016.htm#007)); ([Gen
18:2-23](../../../bib/kjv/gen018.htm#002)) especially; ([Gen
18:17](../../../bib/kjv/gen018.htm#017)); ([Gen
32:28](../../../bib/kjv/gen032.htm#028)) with; ([Hos
12:3-5](../../../bib/kjv/hos012.htm#003)); ([Exo
3:2-14](../../../bib/kjv/exo003.htm#002)).

\(b\) The Messiah is expressly declared to be the Son of God ([Psa
2:2-9](../../../bib/kjv/psa002.htm#002)) and God; ([Psa
45:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa045.htm#006)); ([Psa
45:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa045.htm#007)); ([Heb
1:8](../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#008)); ([Heb
1:9](../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#009)); ([Psa
110:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#001)); ([Mat
22:44](../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#044)); ([Act
2:34](../../../bib/kjv/act002.htm#034)); ([Heb
1:13](../../../bib/kjv/heb001.htm#013)); ([Psa
110:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa110.htm#004)); ([Heb
5:6](../../../bib/kjv/heb005.htm#006)); ([Heb
6:20](../../../bib/kjv/heb006.htm#020)); ([Heb
7:17-21](../../../bib/kjv/heb007.htm#017)); ([Zac
6:13](../../../bib/kjv/zac006.htm#013)).

\(c\) His virgin birth was foretold as the means through which God could
be "Immanuel," God with us; ([Isa
7:13](../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#013)); ([Isa
7:14](../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#014)); ([Mat
1:22](../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#022)); ([Mat
1:23](../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#023)).

\(d\) The Messiah is expressly invested with the divine names ([Isa
9:6](../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#006)); ([Isa

\(e\) In a prophecy of His death He is called Jehovah's "fellow"; ([Zac

2:6](../../../bib/kjv/mat002.htm#006)); ([Joh
7:42](../../../bib/kjv/joh007.htm#042)).

\(2\) Christ Himself affirmed His deity.

\(a\) He applied to Himself the Jehovistic I AM. (The pronoun "he" is
not in the Greek;

Compare ([Joh 8:24](../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#024)); ([Joh
8:56-58](../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#056)). The Jews correctly
understood this to be our Lord's claim to full deity. ([Joh
8:59](../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#059)).

See also, ([Joh 10:33](../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#033)); ([Joh
18:4-6](../../../bib/kjv/joh018.htm#004)) where, also, "he" is not in
the original.)

\(b\) He claimed to be the Adonai of the Old Testament ([Mat
22:42-45](../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#042)).

(See Scofield) - ([Gen 15:2](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#002)).
