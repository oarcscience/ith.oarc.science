### [Acts Chapter 20](../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#001)

[Acts 20:6](../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">act 20:6</span></span>

we

From the use of the pronoun, Luke here rejoins the apostle.

[Acts 20:7](../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">act 20:7</span></span>

first day

It was the breaking of bread for which the disciples were assembled. The
passage indicates the use by the apostolic churches of the first day,
not the seventh.

Compare ([Co1 16:2](../../../bib/kjv/co1016.htm#002)).

[Acts 20:22](../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">act 20:22</span></span>

spirit

Compare ([Act 21:4](../../../bib/kjv/act021.htm#004)).

In ([Act 20:22](../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#022)) Paul's own spirit

(See Scofield) - ([Th1 5:23](../../../bib/kjv/th1005.htm#023))

is meant; in ([Act 21:4](../../../bib/kjv/act021.htm#004)), the Holy
Spirit is meant. Paul's motive in going to Jerusalem seems to have been
his great affection for the Jews ([Rom
9:1-5](../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#001)) and his hope that gifts of the
Gentile churches, sent by him to the poor saints at Jerusalem ([Rom
15:25-28](../../../bib/kjv/rom015.htm#025)) would open the hearts of the
law-bound Jewish believers to the "gospel of the grace of God" ([Act
20:24](../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#024)).

[Acts 20:24](../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#024)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">act 20:24</span></span>

neither

Or, I hold not my life of any account, as unto myself, in comparison
with accomplishing my course.

See ([Co1 9:26](../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#026)); ([Phi
3:13](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#013)); ([Phi
3:14](../../../bib/kjv/phi003.htm#014)). ([Ti2
4:7](../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#007)); ([Ti2
4:8](../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#008)).

[Acts 20:29](../../../bib/kjv/act020.htm#029)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_029">act 20:29</span></span>

wolves

The two sources of the apostasy:

false teachers from without, ([Co2
11:13-15](../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#013)); ([Pe2
2:1-3](../../../bib/kjv/pe2002.htm#001));

ambitious leaders from within, ([Jo3
1:9](../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#009)); ([Jo3
1:10](../../../bib/kjv/jo3001.htm#010)); ([Rev
2:6](../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#006)); ([Rev
2:15](../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#015)).

Also, ([Ti1 1:20](../../../bib/kjv/ti1001.htm#020)); ([Jo1
2:19](../../../bib/kjv/jo1002.htm#019)).

It is more
