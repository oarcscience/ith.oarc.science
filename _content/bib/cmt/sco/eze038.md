### [Ezekiel Chapter 38](../../../bib/kjv/eze038.htm#001)

[Ezekiel 38:2](../../../bib/kjv/eze038.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">eze 38:2</span></span>

Gog

That the primary reference is to the northern (European) powers, headed
up by Russia, all agree. The whole passage should be read in connection
with ([Zac 12:1-4](../../../bib/kjv/zac012.htm#001)); ([Zac
14:1-9](../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#001)); ([Mat
24:14-30](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#014)); ([Rev
14:14-20](../../../bib/kjv/rev014.htm#014)); ([Rev
19:17-21](../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#017)); "gog" is the prince,
"Magog," his land. The reference to Meshech and Tubal (Moscow and
Tobolsk) is a clear mark of identification. Russia and the northern
powers have been the latest persecutors of dispersed Israel, and it is
congruous both with divine justice and with the covenants (for example

(See Scofield) - ([Gen 15:18](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#018)).

(See Scofield) - ([Deu 30:3](../../../bib/kjv/deu030.htm#003)).

that destruction should fall at the climax of the last mad attempt to
exterminate the remnant of Israel in Jerusalem. The whole prophecy
belongs to the yet future "day of Jehovah"; ([Isa
2:10-22](../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#010)); ([Rev
19:11-21](../../../bib/kjv/rev019.htm#011)) and to the battle of
Armageddon ([Rev 16:14](../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#014)).
