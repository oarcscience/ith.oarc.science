### [Isaiah Chapter 1](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#001)

[Isaiah 1:2](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">isa 1:2</span></span>

Hear, O heavens

The chapter, down to verse 23, states the case of Jehovah against Judah.
Chastening, according to Deuteronomy 28, 29, had been visited upon
Israel in the land (vs. 5-8 ([Isa
1:5-8](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#005)), and now the time of expulsion
from the land is near. But just here Jehovah renews the promise of the
Palestinian Covenant of future restoration and exaltation ([Isa
1:26](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#026)); ([Isa
1:27](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#027)); ([Isa
2:1-4](../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#001)).

[Isaiah 1:9](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">isa 1:9</span></span>

remnant

11:5](../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#005)).

(See Scofield) - ([Rom 11:5](../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#005)).

[Isaiah 1:26](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#026)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">isa 1:26</span></span>

thy judges

Under the kingdom the ancient method of administering the theocratic
government over Israel is to be restored.

Compare ([Jdg 2:18](../../../bib/kjv/jdg002.htm#018)); ([Mat
19:28](../../../bib/kjv/mat019.htm#028)).
