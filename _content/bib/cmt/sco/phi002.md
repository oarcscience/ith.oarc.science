### [Philippians Chapter 2](../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#001)

[Philippians 2:6](../../../bib/kjv/phi002.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">phi 2:6</span></span>

form of God

"Form," etc. (Greek, "morphē", "the form by which a person or thing
strikes the vision", the "external appearance"). -- Thayer.

Compare ([Joh 17:15](../../../bib/kjv/joh017.htm#015)).

"The glory which I had with Thee before the world was." Nothing in this
passage teaches that the Eternal Word ([Joh
1:1](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#001)) emptied Himself of either His
divine nature, or His attributes, but only of the outward and visible
manifestation of the Godhead. "He emptied, stripped Himself of the
insignia of Majesty." -- Lightfoot. "When occasion demanded He exercised
His divine attributes." -- Moorehead.

Compare (See Scofield) - ([Joh 1:1](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#001)).
