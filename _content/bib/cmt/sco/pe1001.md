### [1 Peter Chapter 1](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#001)

[1 Peter 1:2](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pe1 1:2</span></span>

Elect

Election, Summary:

In both Testaments, the Hebrew and Greek words are rendered "elect,"
"election," "choose," "chosen." In all cases, they mean, simply,
"chosen," or "to choose"; and are used of both human and divine choices.

\(1\) In the latter use, election is:

\(a\) corporate, as of the nation of Israel, or the church, ([Isa
45:4](../../../bib/kjv/isa045.htm#004)); ([Eph
1:4](../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#004)) and

\(b\) individual. ([Pe1 1:2](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#002)).

\(2\) Election is according to the foreknowledge of God ([Pe1
1:2](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#002)) and wholly of grace, apart from
human merit. ([Rom 9:11](../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#011)); ([Rom
11:5](../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#005)); ([Rom
11:6](../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#006)).

\(3\) Election proceeds from the divine volition. ([Joh
15:16](../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#016)).

Election is, therefore:

\(1\) The sovereign act of God in grace whereby certain are chosen from
among mankind for Himself. ([Joh
15:19](../../../bib/kjv/joh015.htm#019)).

\(2\) The sovereign act of God whereby certain elect persons are chosen
for distinctive service for Him. ([Luk
6:13](../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#013)); ([Act
9:15](../../../bib/kjv/act009.htm#015)); ([Co1
1:27](../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#027)); ([Co1
1:28](../../../bib/kjv/co1001.htm#028)).

[1 Peter 1:20](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#020)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">pe1 1:20</span></span>

foreordained

The divine order is foreknowledge, election, predestination. That
foreknowledge determines the election or choice is clear from ([Pe1
1:2](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#002)) and predestination is the
bringing to pass of the election. "election looks back to foreknowledge;
predestination forward to the destiny." But Scripture nowhere declares
what it is in the divine foreknowledge which determines the divine
election and predestination. The foreknown are elected, and the elect
are predestinated, and this election is certain to every believer by the
mere fact that he believes ([Th1 1:4](../../../bib/kjv/th1001.htm#004));
([Th1 1:5](../../../bib/kjv/th1001.htm#005)).

See Predestination. (See Scofield) - ([Eph
1:5](../../../bib/kjv/eph001.htm#005)).
