### [Psalms Chapter 22](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001)

[Psalms 22:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 22:1</span></span>

Aijeleth Shahar

Or, Ay-ys-leth Shachar, "hind of the morning," a title, not a musical
instrument.

My God, My God

Psalms 22, 23, and 24 form a trilogy. In Psalm 22, the good Shepherd
gives His life for the sheep ([Joh
10:11](../../../bib/kjv/joh010.htm#011)); in Psalm 23 ([Psa
23:1-6](../../../bib/kjv/psa023.htm#001)), the great Shepherd, "brought
again from the dead through the blood of the everlasting covenant."
([Heb 13:20](../../../bib/kjv/heb013.htm#020)) tenderly cares for the
sheep; in Psalm 24 ([Psa 24:1-10](../../../bib/kjv/psa024.htm#001)), the
chief Shepherd appears as King of glory to own and reward the sheep
([Pe1 5:4](../../../bib/kjv/pe1005.htm#004)).

[Psalms 22:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">psa 22:7</span></span>

All they that see me

Psalm 22 is a graphic picture of death by crucifixion. The bones (of the
hands, arms, shoulders, and pelvis) out of joint ([Psa
22:14](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#014)) the profuse perspiration caused
by intense suffering ([Psa 22:14](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#014)); the
action of the heart affected ([Psa
22:14](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#014)); strength exhausted, and
extreme thirst ([Psa 22:15](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#015)); the hands
and feet pierced ([Psa 22:16](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#016)) partial
nudity with hurt to modesty ([Psa
22:17](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#017)), are all incidental to that
mode of death. The accompanying circumstances are precisely those
fulfilled in the crucifixion of Christ. ([Psa
22:14-17](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#014)). The desolate cry of; ([Psa
22:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#001)); ([Mat
27:46](../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#046)); the periods of light and
darkness of; ([Psa 22:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#002)); ([Mat
27:45](../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#045)) the contumely of; ([Psa
22:6-8](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#006)); ([Psa
22:12](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#012)); ([Psa
22:13](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#013)); ([Psa
22:18](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#018)); ([Mat
27:29-43](../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#029)) the casting lots of verse
([Psa 22:18](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#018)); ([Mat
27:35](../../../bib/kjv/mat027.htm#035)), all were literally fulfilled.
When it is remembered that crucifixion was a Roman, not Jewish form of
execution, the proof of inspiration is irresistible.

[Psalms 22:22](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">psa 22:22</span></span>

I will declare

At verse 22 ([Psa 22:22](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#022)), the Psalm
breaks from crucifixion to resurrection; fulfilled in the "Go to my
brethren," etc, of ([Joh 20:17](../../../bib/kjv/joh020.htm#017)). The
risen Christ declares to His brethren the name, "Father."

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">psa 22:28</span></span>

Compare ([Psa 22:30](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#030)).

The kingdom is Jehovah's. In verse 30 ([Psa
22:30](../../../bib/kjv/psa022.htm#030)), Adonai is in view as ruling on

22:42-45](../../../bib/kjv/mat022.htm#042)). The great end and object of
the rule of Adonai (Lord) is the restoration of the kingdom to Jehovah
(Lord).
