### [Genesis Chapter 11](../../../bib/kjv/gen011.htm#001)

[Genesis 11:1](../../../bib/kjv/gen011.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">gen 11:1</span></span>

one language

The history of Babel ("confusion") strikingly parallels that of the
professing Church.

\(1\) Unity ([Gen 11:1](../../../bib/kjv/gen011.htm#001)) -- the
Apostolic Church ([Act 4:32](../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#032)); ([Act
4:33](../../../bib/kjv/act004.htm#033)).

\(2\) Ambition ([Gen 11:4](../../../bib/kjv/gen011.htm#004)) using
worldly, not spiritual means ([Gen
11:3](../../../bib/kjv/gen011.htm#003)) ending in a man-made unity --
the papacy;

\(3\) the confusion of tongues ([Gen
11:7](../../../bib/kjv/gen011.htm#007)) -- Protestantism, with its
innumerable sects.

(See Scofield) - ([Isa 13:1](../../../bib/kjv/isa013.htm#001)).

[Genesis 11:10](../../../bib/kjv/gen011.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">gen 11:10</span></span>

generations of Shem

Genesis 11. and 12. mark an important turning point in the divine
dealing. Heretofore the history has been that of the whole Adamic race.
There has been neither Jew nor Gentile; all have been one in "the first
man Adam." Henceforth, in the Scripture record, humanity must be thought
of as a vast stream from which God, in the call of Abram and the
creation of the nation of Israel, has but drawn off a slender rill,
through which He may at last purify the great river itself. Israel was
called to be a witness to the unity of God in the midst of universal
idolatry ([Deu 6:4](../../../bib/kjv/deu006.htm#004)); ([Isa
43:10-12](../../../bib/kjv/isa043.htm#010)) to illustrate the
blessedness of serving the true God ([Deu
33:26-29](../../../bib/kjv/deu033.htm#026)) to receive and preserve the
divine revelations; ([Rom 3:1](../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#001)); ([Rom
3:2](../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#002)); ([Deu
4:5-8](../../../bib/kjv/deu004.htm#005)) and to produce the messiah;
([Gen 3:15](../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#015)); ([Gen
21:12](../../../bib/kjv/gen021.htm#012)); ([Gen
28:10](../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#010)); ([Gen
28:14](../../../bib/kjv/gen028.htm#014)); ([Gen
49:10](../../../bib/kjv/gen049.htm#010)); ([Sa2
7:16](../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#016)); ([Sa2
7:17](../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#017)); ([Isa
4:3](../../../bib/kjv/isa004.htm#003)); ([Isa
4:4](../../../bib/kjv/isa004.htm#004)); ([Mat
1:1](../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#001)).

The reader of scripture should hold firmly in mind:

\(1\) that from Gen 12. to ([Mat
12:45](../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#045)) the Scriptures have primarily
in view Israel, the little rill, not the great Gentile river; though
again and again the universality of the ultimate divine intent breaks
into view (for example; ([Gen 12:3](../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#003));
([Isa 2:2](../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#002)); ([Isa
2:4](../../../bib/kjv/isa002.htm#004)); ([Isa
5:26](../../../bib/kjv/isa005.htm#026)); ([Isa
9:1-2](../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#001)); ([Isa
11:10-12](../../../bib/kjv/isa011.htm#010)); ([Isa
42:1-6](../../../bib/kjv/isa042.htm#001)); ([Isa
49:6](../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#006)); ([Isa
49:12](../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#012)); ([Isa
52:15](../../../bib/kjv/isa052.htm#015)); ([Isa
54:3](../../../bib/kjv/isa054.htm#003)); ([Isa
55:5](../../../bib/kjv/isa055.htm#005)); ([Isa
60:3](../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#003)); ([Isa
60:5](../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#005)); ([Isa
60:11-16](../../../bib/kjv/isa060.htm#011)); ([Isa
61:6](../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#006)); ([Isa
61:9](../../../bib/kjv/isa061.htm#009)); ([Isa
62:2](../../../bib/kjv/isa062.htm#002)); ([Isa
66:12](../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#012)); ([Isa
66:18](../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#018)); ([Isa
66:19](../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#019)); ([Jer
16:19](../../../bib/kjv/jer016.htm#019)); ([Joe
3:9](../../../bib/kjv/joe003.htm#009)); ([Joe
3:10](../../../bib/kjv/joe003.htm#010)); ([Mal
1:11](../../../bib/kjv/mal001.htm#011)) Ro 9. 10. 11. ([Gal

\(2\) that the human race, henceforth called Gentile in distinction from
Israel, goes on under the Adamic and Noahic covenants; and that for the
race (outside Israel) the dispensations of Conscience and of Human
government continue. The moral history of the great Gentile world is
told in ([Rom 1:21-32](../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#021)) and its moral
accountability in ([Rom 2:1-16](../../../bib/kjv/rom002.htm#001)).
Conscience never acquits: it either "accuses" or "excuses." Where the
law is known to the Gentiles it is to them, as to Israel, "a
ministration of death," a "curse"; ([Rom
3:19](../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#019)); ([Rom
3:20](../../../bib/kjv/rom003.htm#020)); ([Rom
7:9-10](../../../bib/kjv/rom007.htm#009)); ([Co2
3:7](../../../bib/kjv/co2003.htm#007)); ([Gal
3:10](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#010)). A wholly new responsibility
arises when either Jew or Gentile knows the Gospel; ([Joh
3:18](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#018)); ([Joh
3:19](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#019)); ([Joh
