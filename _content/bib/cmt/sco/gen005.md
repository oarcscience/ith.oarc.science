### [Genesis Chapter 5](../../../bib/kjv/gen005.htm#001)

[Genesis 5:1](../../../bib/kjv/gen005.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">gen 5:1</span></span>

Adam

Adam, as the natural head of the race ([Luk
3:38](../../../bib/kjv/luk003.htm#038)) is a contrasting type of Christ,
the Head of the new creation. See ([Rom
5:14](../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#014)); ([Co1
15:21](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#021)); ([Co1
15:22](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#022)); ([Co1
15:45-47](../../../bib/kjv/co1015.htm#045)).

[Genesis 5:22](../../../bib/kjv/gen005.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">gen 5:22</span></span>

Enoch

Enoch, "translated that he should not see death" ([Heb
11:5](../../../bib/kjv/heb011.htm#005)) before the judgment of the
Flood, is a type of those saints who are to be translated before the
apocalyptic judgments ([Th1 4:14-17](../../../bib/kjv/th1004.htm#014)).
Noah, left on the earth, but preserved through the judgment of the
Flood, is a type of the Jewish people, who will be kept through the
apocalyptic judgments; ([Jer 30:5-9](../../../bib/kjv/jer030.htm#005));
([Rev 12:13-16](../../../bib/kjv/rev012.htm#013)) and brought as an
earthly people to the new heaven and new earth; ([Isa
65:17-19](../../../bib/kjv/isa065.htm#017)); ([Isa
66:20-22](../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#020)); ([Rev
21:1](../../../bib/kjv/rev021.htm#001)).
