### [Acts Chapter 16](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#001)

[Acts 16:7](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">act 16:7</span></span>

suffered

R.V. adds "of Jesus", as in the best authorities.

[Acts 16:9](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">act 16:9</span></span>

prayed him

beseeching him. Here the Gospel turns toward Europe.

[Acts 16:10](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">act 16:10</span></span>

we

The change here from "they," as in the preceding verses, to "we"
indicates that at Troas, Luke, the narrator, joined Paul's company.

[Acts 16:12](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">act 16:12</span></span>

colony

That is, a Roman colony.

[Acts 16:13](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">act 16:13</span></span>

was wont

might legally, that is, a legal meeting-place for Jews where there was
no synagogue.

[Acts 16:16](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">act 16:16</span></span>

spirit

(Greek, "pneuma", "a Python").

[Acts 16:17](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">act 16:17</span></span>

most high God

(See Scofield) - ([Mat 7:22](../../../bib/kjv/mat007.htm#022)).

This marks the "spirit", ([Act 16:18](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#018));
as being a demon.

salvation

See note, (See Scofield) - ([Rom
1:16](../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#016)).

[Acts 16:19](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#019)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">act 16:19</span></span>

drew

(Greek, "helkuō" / "helkō", means, probably "by the feet").

Compare ([Act 14:19](../../../bib/kjv/act014.htm#019)).

[Acts 16:20](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#020)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">act 16:20</span></span>

magistrates

(Greek, "stratēgos", "Roman magistrates").

[Acts 16:25](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#025)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">act 16:25</span></span>

prayed

Literally, were praying and singing hymns.

[Acts 16:30](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#030)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_030">act 16:30</span></span>

saved

(See Scofield) - ([Rom 1:16](../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#016)).

[Acts 16:31](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#031)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_031">act 16:31</span></span>

saved

(See Scofield) - ([Rom 1:16](../../../bib/kjv/rom001.htm#016)).

[Acts 16:35](../../../bib/kjv/act016.htm#035)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_035">act 16:35</span></span>

serjeants

(Greek, "rhabdouchos").
