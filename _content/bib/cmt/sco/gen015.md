### [Genesis Chapter 15](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#001)

[Genesis 15:2](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">gen 15:2</span></span>

1\) "Lord" (Hebrew, Adon, Adonai)

\(1\) The primary meaning of Adon, Adonai, is Master, and it is applied
in the Old Testament Scriptures both to Deity and to man. The latter
instances are distinguished in the English version by the omission of
the capital. As applied to man, the word is used of two relationships;
master and husband ([Gen 24:9](../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#009)); ([Gen
24:10](../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#010)); ([Gen
24:12](../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#012)) "master" may illustrate the
former; ([Gen 18:12](../../../bib/kjv/gen018.htm#012)) "lord," the
latter). Both these relationships exist between Christ and the believer
([Joh 13:13](../../../bib/kjv/joh013.htm#013)) "master"; ([Co2
11:2](../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#002)); ([Co2
11:3](../../../bib/kjv/co2011.htm#003)) "husband").

\(2\) Two principles inhere in the relation of master and servant:

\(a\) the Master's right to implicit obedience ([Joh
13:13](../../../bib/kjv/joh013.htm#013)); ([Mat
23:10](../../../bib/kjv/mat023.htm#010)); ([Luk
6:46](../../../bib/kjv/luk006.htm#046)).

\(b\) the servant's right to direction in service ([Isa
6:8-11](../../../bib/kjv/isa006.htm#008)).

Clear distinction in the use of the divine names is illustrated in ([Exo
4:10-12](../../../bib/kjv/exo004.htm#010)). Moses feels his weakness and
incompetency, and "Moses said unto the Lord Jehovah, O my Lord Adonai, I
am not eloquent," etc. Since service is in question, Moses appropriately
addresses Jehovah as Lord. But now power is in question, and it not the
Lord (Adonai) but Jehovah (Lord) who answers (referring to creation
power) -- "and Jehovah said unto him, Who hath made man's mouth? . . Now
therefore go, and I will be with thy mouth." The same distinction in
([Jos 7:8-11](../../../bib/kjv/jos007.htm#008)). See, for other names of
Deity:

[Genesis 15:18](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#018)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">gen 15:18</span></span>

covenant

The Abrahamic Covenant as formed ([Gen
12:1-4](../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#001)) and confirmed; ([Gen
13:14-17](../../../bib/kjv/gen013.htm#014)); ([Gen
15:1-7](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#001)); ([Gen
17:1-8](../../../bib/kjv/gen017.htm#001)) is in seven distinct parts:

\(1\) "I will make of thee a great nation." Fulfilled in a threefold
way:

\(a\) In a natural posterity -- "as the dust of the earth ([Gen
13:16](../../../bib/kjv/gen013.htm#016)); ([Joh
8:37](../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#037)); namely, the Hebrew people.

\(b\) In a spiritual posterity -- "look now toward heaven . . . so shall
thy seed be" ([Joh 8:39](../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#039)); ([Rom
4:16-17](../../../bib/kjv/rom004.htm#016)); ([Rom
9:7-8](../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#007)); ([Gal
3:6](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#006)); ([Gal
3:7](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#007)); ([Gal
3:29](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#029)) namely, all men of faith,
whether Jew or Gentile.

\(c\) fulfilled also through Ishmael ([Gen
17:18-20](../../../bib/kjv/gen017.htm#018)).

\(2\) "I will bless thee." Fulfilled in two ways:

\(a\) temporally ([Gen 13:14](../../../bib/kjv/gen013.htm#014)); ([Gen
13:15](../../../bib/kjv/gen013.htm#015)); ([Gen
13:17](../../../bib/kjv/gen013.htm#017)); ([Gen
15:18](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#018)); ([Gen
24:34-35](../../../bib/kjv/gen024.htm#034))

\(b\) spiritually; ([Gen 15:6](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#006)); ([Joh
8:56](../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#056)).

\(3\) "And make thy name great." Abraham's is one of the universal
names.

\(4\) "And thou shalt be a blessing" ([Gal
3:13](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#013)); ([Gal
3:14](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#014)).

\(5\) "I will bless them that bless thee." In fulfilment closely related
to the next clause.

\(6\) "And curse him that curseth thee." Wonderfully fulfilled in the
history of the dispersion. It has invariably fared ill with the people
who have persecuted the Jew -- well with those who have protected him.
The future will still more remarkably prove this principle ([Deu
30:7](../../../bib/kjv/deu030.htm#007)); ([Isa
14:1](../../../bib/kjv/isa014.htm#001)); ([Joe
3:1-8](../../../bib/kjv/joe003.htm#001)); ([Mic
5:7-9](../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#007)); ([Hag
2:22](../../../bib/kjv/hag002.htm#022)); ([Zac
14:1-3](../../../bib/kjv/zac014.htm#001)); ([Mat
25:40](../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#040)); ([Mat
25:45](../../../bib/kjv/mat025.htm#045)).

\(7\) "In thee shall all the families of the earth be blessed." This is
the great evangelic promise fulfilled in Abraham's Seed, Christ ([Gal
3:16](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#016)); ([Joh
8:56-58](../../../bib/kjv/joh008.htm#056)).

It brings into greater definiteness the promise of the Adamic Covenant
concerning the Seed of the woman ([Gen
3:15](../../../bib/kjv/gen003.htm#015)).

NOTE. -- The gift of the land is modified by prophecies of three
dispossessions and restorations ([Gen
15:13](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#013)); ([Gen
15:14](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#014)); ([Gen
15:16](../../../bib/kjv/gen015.htm#016)); ([Jer
25:11](../../../bib/kjv/jer025.htm#011)); ([Jer
25:12](../../../bib/kjv/jer025.htm#012)); ([Deu
28:62-65](../../../bib/kjv/deu028.htm#062)); ([Deu
30:1-3](../../../bib/kjv/deu030.htm#001)). Two dispossessions and
restorations have been accomplished. Israel is now in the third
dispersion, from which she will be restored at the return of the Lord as
King under the Davidic Covenant; ([Deu
30:3](../../../bib/kjv/deu030.htm#003)); ([Jer
23:5-8](../../../bib/kjv/jer023.htm#005)); ([Eze
37:21-25](../../../bib/kjv/eze037.htm#021)); ([Luk
1:30-33](../../../bib/kjv/luk001.htm#030)); ([Act
15:14-17](../../../bib/kjv/act015.htm#014)).
