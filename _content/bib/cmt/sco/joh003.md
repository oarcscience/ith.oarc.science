### [John Chapter 3](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#001)

[John 3:3](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">joh 3:3</span></span>

born again

Regeneration:

\(1\) The necessity of the new birth grows out of the incapacity of the
natural man to "see" or "enter into" the kingdom of God. However gifted,
moral, or refined, the natural man is absolutely blind to spiritual
truth, and impotent to enter the kingdom; for he can neither obey,
understand, nor please God ([Joh 3:3](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#003));
([Joh 3:5](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#005)); ([Joh
3:6](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#006)); ([Psa
51:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa051.htm#005)); ([Jer
17:9](../../../bib/kjv/jer017.htm#009)); ([Mar
7:21-23](../../../bib/kjv/mar007.htm#021)); ([Co1
2:14](../../../bib/kjv/co1002.htm#014)); ([Rom
8:7](../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#007)); ([Rom
8:8](../../../bib/kjv/rom008.htm#008)); ([Eph
2:3](../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#003)).

(See Scofield) - ([Mat 6:33](../../../bib/kjv/mat006.htm#033)).

\(2\) The new birth is not a reformation of the old nature

(See Scofield) - ([Rom 6:6](../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#006))

but a creative act of the holy Spirit ([Joh
3:5](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#005)); ([Joh
1:12](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012)); ([Joh
1:13](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#013)); ([Co2
5:17](../../../bib/kjv/co2005.htm#017)); ([Eph
2:10](../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#010)); ([Eph
4:24](../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#024)).

\(3\) The condition of the new birth is faith in Christ crucified ([Joh
3:14](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#014)); ([Joh
3:15](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#015)); ([Joh
1:12](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#012)); ([Joh
1:13](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#013)); ([Gal
3:24](../../../bib/kjv/gal003.htm#024)).

\(4\) Through the new birth the believer becomes a partaker of the
divine nature and of the life of Christ Himself ([Gal
2:20](../../../bib/kjv/gal002.htm#020)); ([Eph
2:10](../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#010)); ([Eph
4:24](../../../bib/kjv/eph004.htm#024)); ([Col
1:27](../../../bib/kjv/col001.htm#027)); ([Pe1
1:23-25](../../../bib/kjv/pe1001.htm#023)); ([Pe2
1:4](../../../bib/kjv/pe2001.htm#004)); ([Jo1
5:10-12](../../../bib/kjv/jo1005.htm#010)).

[John 3:16](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">joh 3:16</span></span>

perish

(Greek, "apollumi") translated "marred," ([Mar
2:22](../../../bib/kjv/mar002.htm#022)); "lost,"; ([Mat
10:6](../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#006)); ([Mat
15:24](../../../bib/kjv/mat015.htm#024)); ([Mat
18:11](../../../bib/kjv/mat018.htm#011)); ([Luk
15:4](../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#004)); ([Luk
15:6](../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#006)); ([Luk
15:32](../../../bib/kjv/luk015.htm#032)). In no New Testament instance
does it signify cessation of existence or of consciousness. It is the
condition of every non-believer.
