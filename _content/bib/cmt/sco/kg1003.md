### [3 Kings (1 Kings) Chapter 3](../../../bib/kjv/kg1003.htm#001)

[3 Kings (1 Kings) 3:2](../../../bib/kjv/kg1003.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">kg1 3:2</span></span>

high places

Compare ([Lev 26:30](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#030)); ([Deu
12:11-14](../../../bib/kjv/deu012.htm#011)).

The use of commanding elevations for altars seems to have been
immemorial and universal. In itself the practice was not evil; ([Gen
12:7](../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#007)); ([Gen
12:8](../../../bib/kjv/gen012.htm#008)); ([Gen
22:2-4](../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#002)); ([Gen
31:54](../../../bib/kjv/gen031.htm#054)); ([Jdg
6:25](../../../bib/kjv/jdg006.htm#025)); ([Jdg
6:26](../../../bib/kjv/jdg006.htm#026)); ([Jdg
13:16-23](../../../bib/kjv/jdg013.htm#016)). After the establishment of
Mount Moriah and the temple as the centre of divine worship; ([Deu
12:5](../../../bib/kjv/deu012.htm#005)); ([Ch2
7:12](../../../bib/kjv/ch2007.htm#012)) the pentateuchal prohibition of
the use of high places ([Deu 12:11-14](../../../bib/kjv/deu012.htm#011))
which had looked forward to the setting up of such a centre, came into
effect, and high places became identified with idolatrous practices. The
constant recurrence to the use of high places, even for Jehovistic
worship

(See Scofield) - ([Kg1 15:14](../../../bib/kjv/kg1015.htm#014))

and after the building of the temple, proves how deeply rooted the
custom was.

See ([Kg2 18:4-22](../../../bib/kjv/kg2018.htm#004)); ([Kg2
23:1-37](../../../bib/kjv/kg2023.htm#001)); ([Ch2
33:3](../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#003)); ([Ch2
33:17](../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#017)); ([Ch2
33:19](../../../bib/kjv/ch2033.htm#019)). Note below refers to "Groves."

(See Scofield) - ([Jdg 3:7](../../../bib/kjv/jdg003.htm#007)).
