Maschil, instruction.

[Psalms 89:27](../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#027)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">psa 89:27</span></span>

higher than the kings of the earth

The eighty-ninth Psalm is at once the confirmation and exposition of the
Davidic Covenant ([Sa2 7:9-14](../../../bib/kjv/sa2007.htm#009)). That
the covenant itself looks far beyond David and Solomon is sure from
([Psa 89:27](../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#027)). "Higher than the kings
of the earth" can only refer to Immanuel. ([Isa
7:13-15](../../../bib/kjv/isa007.htm#013)); ([Isa
9:6](../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#006)); ([Isa
9:7](../../../bib/kjv/isa009.htm#007)); ([Mic
5:2](../../../bib/kjv/mic005.htm#002)).

The Psalm is in four parts:

\(1\) The covenant, though springing from the lovingkindness of Jehovah,
yet rests upon His oath (vs. 1-4).

\(2\) Jehovah is glorified for His power and goodness in connection with
the covenant (vs 5-18).

\(3\) The response of Jehovah (vs. 19-37). This is in two parts:

\(a\) it confirms the covenant ([Psa
89:19-29](../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#019)), but

\(b\) warns that disobedience in the royal posterity of David will be
punished with chastening ([Psa
89:30-32](../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#030)). Historically this
chastening began in the division of the Davidic kingdom; ([Kg1
11:26-36](../../../bib/kjv/kg1011.htm#026)); ([Kg1
12:16-20](../../../bib/kjv/kg1012.htm#016)) and culminated in the
captivities and that subordination of Israel to the Gentiles which still
continues. See "Gentiles, times of"; ([Luk
21:24](../../../bib/kjv/luk021.htm#024)); ([Rev
16:14](../../../bib/kjv/rev016.htm#014)).

\(4\) The plea of the Remnant ([Isa
1:9](../../../bib/kjv/isa001.htm#009)); ([Rom
11:5](../../../bib/kjv/rom011.htm#005)) who urge the severity and long
continuance of the chastening ([Psa
89:38-52](../../../bib/kjv/psa089.htm#038)).

See Psalm 102, next in order of the Messianic Psalms.
