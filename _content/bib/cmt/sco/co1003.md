[1 Corinthians 3:14](../../../bib/kjv/co1003.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">co1 3:14</span></span>

reward

God, in the New Testament Scriptures, offers to the lost, salvation,
and, for the faithful service of the saved, rewards. The passages are
easily distinguished by remembering that salvation is invariably spoken
of as a free gift, (for example), ([Joh
4:10](../../../bib/kjv/joh004.htm#010)); ([Rom
6:23](../../../bib/kjv/rom006.htm#023)); ([Eph
2:8](../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#008)); ([Eph
2:9](../../../bib/kjv/eph002.htm#009)), while rewards are earned by
works; ([Mat 10:42](../../../bib/kjv/mat010.htm#042)); ([Luk
19:17](../../../bib/kjv/luk019.htm#017)); ([Co1
9:24](../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#024)); ([Co1
9:25](../../../bib/kjv/co1009.htm#025)); ([Ti2
4:7](../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#007)); ([Ti2
4:8](../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#008)); ([Rev
2:10](../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#010)); ([Rev
22:12](../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#012)).

A further distinction is that salvation is a present possession ([Luk
7:50](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#050)); ([Joh
3:36](../../../bib/kjv/joh003.htm#036)); ([Joh
5:24](../../../bib/kjv/joh005.htm#024)); ([Joh
6:47](../../../bib/kjv/joh006.htm#047)); while rewards are a future
attainment, to be given at the coming of the Lord; ([Mat
16:27](../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#027)); ([Ti2
4:8](../../../bib/kjv/ti2004.htm#008)); ([Rev
22:12](../../../bib/kjv/rev022.htm#012)).
