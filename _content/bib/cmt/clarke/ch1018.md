### [1 Chronicles Chapter 18](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#001)

[1 Chronicles](../../../bib/kjv/ch1001.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_000">ch1 18:0</span></span>

David smites the Philistines, and takes Gath, [Ch1
18:1](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#001). Reduces the Moabites, [Ch1
18:2](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#002). Vanquishes Hadarezer, king of
Zobah, [Ch1 18:3](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#003), [Ch1
18:4](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#004). Overcomes the Syrians of
Damascus, and takes several of their cities, [Ch1
18:5-8](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#005). Tou, king of Hamath,
congratulates him on his victory, and sends him vessels of silver, gold,
and brass, [Ch1 18:9](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#009), [Ch1
18:10](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#010). Those and the different spoils
he had taken from the conquered nations, he dedicates to God, [Ch1
18:11](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#011). Abishai defeats the Edomites,
[Ch1 18:12](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#012), [Ch1
18:13](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#013). David reigns over all Israel,
[Ch1 18:14](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#014). His officers, [Ch1
18:15-17](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#015).

[1 Chronicles 18:1](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">ch1 18:1</span></span>

David - took Gath and her towns - See the comparison between this
chapter and [Sa2 8:1](../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#001) (note), etc., in
the notes on the latter.

[1 Chronicles 18:2](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">ch1 18:2</span></span>

Brought gifts - Were laid under tribute.

[1 Chronicles 18:9](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">ch1 18:9</span></span>

Tou king of Hamath - Called Toi in [Sa2
8:9](../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#009).

[1 Chronicles 18:12](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">ch1 18:12</span></span>

Abishai - slew of the Edomites - This victory is attributed to David,
[Sa2 8:13](../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#013). He sent Abishai against
them, and he defeated them: this is with great propriety attributed to
David as commander-in-chief; qui facit per alterum, facit per se.

[1 Chronicles 18:15](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">ch1 18:15</span></span>

Joab - was over the host - General-in-chief.

Jehoshaphat - recorder - The king's remembrancer, or historiographer
royal.

[1 Chronicles 18:16](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">ch1 18:16</span></span>

Zadok - and Abimelech - priests - Both high priests; one at Gibeon, and
the other at Jerusalem, as we have seen [Ch1
16:39](../../../bib/kjv/ch1016.htm#039).

Shavsha was scribe - Called Seraiah, [Sa2
8:17](../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#017).

[1 Chronicles 18:17](../../../bib/kjv/ch1018.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">ch1 18:17</span></span>

Cherethites and the Pelethites - See the note on [Sa2
8:18](../../../bib/kjv/sa2008.htm#018).

The Targum says, "Benaiah was over the great Sanhedrin and the small
Sanhedrin, and consulted Urim and Thummim. And at his command the
archers and slingers went to battle."

The sons of David - These were the highest in authority.
