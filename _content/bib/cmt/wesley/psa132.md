*Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible*, by John Wesley, \[1754-65\], at
Intangible Textual Heritage

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### [Psalms Chapter 132](../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#001)

[Psalms 132:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 132:1</span></span>

Afflictions - All his sufferings for thy sake.

[Psalms 132:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">psa 132:5</span></span>

Until - Until I have raised an house in which the ark may be put.

[Psalms 132:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">psa 132:6</span></span>

It - Of the ark. Ephratah - In the tribe of Ephraim, which was called
also Ephratah. Found it - Afterwards we found it in Kirjath - jearim,
which signifies a city of woods, in the territory whereof the ark was
seated for twenty years.

[Psalms 132:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">psa 132:7</span></span>

Tabernacles - Into his temple. Footstool - The ark, is often said to sit
between the cherubim, which were above the ark.

[Psalms 132:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">psa 132:8</span></span>

Rest - Into thy resting place, the temple so called, [Isa
66:1](../../../bib/kjv/isa066.htm#001), where thou hast now a fixed
habitation. The ark - The seat of thy powerful and glorious presence.

[Psalms 132:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">psa 132:10</span></span>

David's sake - In regard of thy promises vouchsafed to David. Turn not -
Cast me not out of thy presence. Of - Of me whom thou hast anointed to
be king over thy people.

[Psalms 132:16](../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">psa 132:16</span></span>

Salvation - With thy saving graces and blessings.

[Psalms 132:17](../../../bib/kjv/psa132.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">psa 132:17</span></span>

There - In Jerusalem. To bud - His power and glory to flourish. A lamp -
A successor to continue for ever in his family, as this phrase is
expounded [Kg1 11:36](../../../bib/kjv/kg1011.htm#036), [Kg1
15:4](../../../bib/kjv/kg1015.htm#004), and particularly one eminent and
glorious light, namely, the Messiah.
