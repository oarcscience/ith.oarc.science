### [Jeremiah Chapter 50](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#001)

[Jeremiah 50:2](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">jer 50:2</span></span>

Bel - Bel and Merodach were the two principal idols of the Babylonians.

[Jeremiah 50:3](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">jer 50:3</span></span>

The north - From Media which lay northward to Babylon and Assyria.

[Jeremiah 50:4](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">jer 50:4</span></span>

In those days - In the days wherein God shall begin to execute judgment
upon Babylon, (which was in the time of Cyrus) the children of Judah
shall come out of captivity, and some of the children of Israel hearing
that their brethren were gone out of Babylon, shall go up also from the
several places into which they were disposed by the Assyrians: weeping
for their sins, or for joy that God should shew them such mercy.

[Jeremiah 50:6](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">jer 50:6</span></span>

Their shepherds - Their civil and ecclesiastical governors have been a
cause of it. The former by their wicked commands and example; the latter
by example as well as doctrine. Turned them - To offer sacrifices unto
idols. From mountain - From one idolatry to another. Forgotten - They
have forgotten me.

[Jeremiah 50:7](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">jer 50:7</span></span>

Habitation - Some think this is a name here given to God, who indeed is
the habitation of justice, but whether the Chaldeans would call him so,
may be a question. Others therefore think the preposition in is
understood, making this the aggravation of the Jews sins, that they were
committed in a land which ought to have been an habitation of justice.

[Jeremiah 50:8](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">jer 50:8</span></span>

Remove - God commands his people to remove out of Babylon, and to go
forth chearfully like the he - goats of a flock leading the way.

[Jeremiah 50:10](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">jer 50:10</span></span>

Satisfied - Satisfied with spoil and plunder.

[Jeremiah 50:11](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">jer 50:11</span></span>

Because - They rejoiced at the ruin of the Jews. Fat - The cause for
which Babylon is threatened, was doubtless their luxury of all sorts
commonly attending great wealth.

[Jeremiah 50:12](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">jer 50:12</span></span>

Mother - Your country, shall be ashamed of you, who are not able to
defend her.

[Jeremiah 50:15](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">jer 50:15</span></span>

Given her hand - Acknowledging themselves overcome, and yielding. As she
hath done - Unmerciful men find no mercy.

[Jeremiah 50:16](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">jer 50:16</span></span>

Every one - Either such strangers as for commerce had their abodes in
Babylon, or such assistance as the Babylonians had gotten against their
enemies.

[Jeremiah 50:17](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">jer 50:17</span></span>

Israel - The whole twelve tribes. Lions - Enemies cruel as lions had
carried them into captivity.

[Jeremiah 50:20](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#020)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">jer 50:20</span></span>

Not found - God will no longer punish the sins of the Jews, they should
be sought for as to punishment and not found. Reserve - Whom I save from
the captivity of Babylon.

[Jeremiah 50:21](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#021)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">jer 50:21</span></span>

Merathaim - The names of some places which Cyrus took in his way to
Babylon.

[Jeremiah 50:22](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">jer 50:22</span></span>

The land - Of Chaldea.

[Jeremiah 50:26](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#026)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">jer 50:26</span></span>

Open her store - houses - The granaries, or treasures of the
Babylonians.

[Jeremiah 50:27](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#027)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">jer 50:27</span></span>

Bullocks - The great and rich men of Babylon.

[Jeremiah 50:28](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#028)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">jer 50:28</span></span>

The vengeance - The revenge which God had taken for his holy temple,
which the Chaldeans had destroyed.

[Jeremiah 50:33](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#033)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_033">jer 50:33</span></span>

Together - Together in this place signifies no more than that they were
both oppressed, or alike oppressed.

[Jeremiah 50:34](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#034)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_034">jer 50:34</span></span>

Plead - He will actually and readily effect it.

[Jeremiah 50:36](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#036)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_036">jer 50:36</span></span>

Dote - Their soothsayers and wizards shall dote, not foreseeing what
will be. Dismayed - Their hearts shall fail them when this day comes.

[Jeremiah 50:37](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#037)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_037">jer 50:37</span></span>

Horses - Through they be full of chariots and horses, the enemy shall
destroy them. Mingled people - People that were not native Chaldeans,
but under their dominion.

[Jeremiah 50:38](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#038)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_038">jer 50:38</span></span>

Dried - This phrase has a plain reference to Cyrus's stratagem used in
the surprize of Babylon; one part of it was fortified by the great river
Euphrates, which Cyrus diverted by cutting several channels, 'till he
had drained it so low, that it became passable for his army; others
think that a want of rain is here threatened.

[Jeremiah 50:40](../../../bib/kjv/jer050.htm#040)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_040">jer 50:40</span></span>

No man - Cyrus only made them tributaries, and took away their
government. But Seleucus Nicanor, a Grecian prince, utterly destroyed
Babylon, so that in the time of Adrian the Roman emperor, there was
nothing left standing of that great city.
