### [Proverbs Chapter 13](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#001)

[Proverbs 13:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pro 13:2</span></span>

Shall eat - Shall receive comfort, and benefit by his wise and
profitable discourses.

[Proverbs 13:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">pro 13:6</span></span>

Keepeth him - From that over - throw which befal sinners.

[Proverbs 13:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">pro 13:7</span></span>

Maketh rich - Some men who have little or nothing, pretend to have great
riches. Maketh rich - Some rich men profess to be poor.

[Proverbs 13:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">pro 13:8</span></span>

Riches - Riches enable a man to redeem his life when it is in greatest
danger, and poverty preserves a man from many injuries. Because they are
cautious, that they may not provoke others; and because they are
esteemed objects of pity.

[Proverbs 13:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">pro 13:9</span></span>

The light - The prosperity. Rejoiceth - Shines with a pleasant and
constant brightness. Rejoicing is here ascribed to the light, as it is
to the sun, [Psa 19:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa019.htm#005), metaphorically.
Put out - Their felicity shall have a hidden end.

[Proverbs 13:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">pro 13:10</span></span>

Pride - It is chiefly, pride which blows up the coals of contention.
Well - advised - Who are not governed by their passions, but by prudent
considerations. Wisdom - Which teaches them to avoid contention.

[Proverbs 13:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 13:11</span></span>

Vanity - By wicked practices.

[Proverbs 13:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">pro 13:12</span></span>

Hope deferred - Delays in obtaining what a man passionately desires. The
desire - The good desired. A tree of life - It is satisfactory, and
reviving.

[Proverbs 13:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">pro 13:13</span></span>

The word - The word of God.

[Proverbs 13:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">pro 13:14</span></span>

The law - The instruction, or counsel; as the word law, is frequently
understood in scripture.

[Proverbs 13:15](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">pro 13:15</span></span>

Giveth - Makes a man acceptable to God. Rough - Offensive and hateful to
God and men, as rough ways are to a traveller.

[Proverbs 13:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 13:16</span></span>

Knowledge - Considerately and discreetly. Layeth open - By his foolish
actions.

[Proverbs 13:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">pro 13:17</span></span>

A messenger - Who is unfaithful in the execution of that which is
committed to his charge. Is health - Procures benefit, as to his master,
so to himself.

[Proverbs 13:19](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#019)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">pro 13:19</span></span>

Sweet - Whatsoever men earnestly desire, the enjoyment of it is sweet to
them; therefore sinners rejoice in the satisfaction of their sinful
lusts, and abhor all restraint of them.

[Proverbs 13:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#021)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">pro 13:21</span></span>

Evil - Punishment proportionable to their sin.

[Proverbs 13:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#023)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">pro 13:23</span></span>

The poor - Poor persons by their diligent labours, and God's blessing
often grow rich. Destroyed - Or, consumed, brought to poverty, for want
of discretion.

[Proverbs 13:24](../../../bib/kjv/pro013.htm#024)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">pro 13:24</span></span>

Hateth his son - His fond affection, is as pernicious to him as hatred.
