### [Proverbs Chapter 16](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#001)

[Proverbs 16:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">pro 16:1</span></span>

The renewing grace of God alone prepares the heart for every good work.
This teaches us that we are not sufficient of ourselves to think or
speak any thing wise and good.

[Proverbs 16:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pro 16:2</span></span>

Ignorance, pride, and self-flattery render us partial judges respecting
our own conduct.

[Proverbs 16:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">pro 16:3</span></span>

Roll the burden of thy care upon God, and leave it with him, by faith
and dependence on him.

[Proverbs 16:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">pro 16:4</span></span>

God makes use of the wicked to execute righteous vengeance on each
other; and he will be glorified by their destruction at last.

[Proverbs 16:5](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">pro 16:5</span></span>

Though sinners strengthen themselves and one another, they shall not
escape God's judgments.

[Proverbs 16:6](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">pro 16:6</span></span>

By the mercy and truth of God in Christ Jesus, the sins of believers are
taken away, and the power of sin is broken.

[Proverbs 16:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">pro 16:7</span></span>

He that has all hearts in his hand, can make a man's enemies to be at
peace with him.

[Proverbs 16:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">pro 16:8</span></span>

A small estate, honestly come by, will turn to better account than a
great estate ill-gotten.

[Proverbs 16:9](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">pro 16:9</span></span>

If men make God's glory their end, and his will their rule, he will
direct their steps by his Spirit and grace.

[Proverbs 16:10](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">pro 16:10</span></span>

Let kings and judges of the earth be just, and rule in the fear of God.

[Proverbs 16:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 16:11</span></span>

To observe justice in dealings between man and man is God's appointment.

[Proverbs 16:12](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">pro 16:12</span></span>

The ruler that uses his power aright, will find that to be his best
security.

[Proverbs 16:13](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">pro 16:13</span></span>

Put those in power who know how to speak to the purpose.

[Proverbs 16:14](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">pro 16:14</span></span>

Those are fools, who, to obtain the favour of an earthly prince, throw
themselves out of God's favour.

[Proverbs 16:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 16:16</span></span>

There is joy and satisfaction of spirit, only in getting wisdom.

[Proverbs 16:17](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">pro 16:17</span></span>

A sincerely religious man keeps at a distance from every appearance of
evil. Happy is the man that walks in Christ, and is led by the Spirit of
Christ.

[Proverbs 16:18](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#018)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">pro 16:18</span></span>

When men defy God's judgments, and think themselves far from them, it is
a sign they are at the door. Let us not fear the pride of others, but
fear pride in ourselves.

[Proverbs 16:19](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#019)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">pro 16:19</span></span>

Humility, though it exposes to contempt in the world, is much better
than high-spiritedness, which makes God an enemy. He that understands
God's word shall find good.

[Proverbs 16:21](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#021)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">pro 16:21</span></span>

The man whose wisdom dwells in his heart, will be found more truly
prudent than many who possess shining talents.

[Proverbs 16:22](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">pro 16:22</span></span>

As waters to a thirsty land, so is a wise man to his friends and
neighbours.

[Proverbs 16:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#023)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">pro 16:23</span></span>

The wise man's self-knowledge, always suggests something proper to be
spoken to others.

[Proverbs 16:24](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#024)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_024">pro 16:24</span></span>

The word of God cures the diseases that weaken our souls.

[Proverbs 16:25](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#025)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">pro 16:25</span></span>

This is caution to all, to take heed of deceiving themselves as to their
souls.

[Proverbs 16:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#026)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">pro 16:26</span></span>

We must labour for the meat which endureth to everlasting life, or we
must perish.

[Proverbs 16:27](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#027)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">pro 16:27</span></span>

Ungodly men bestow more pains to do mischief than would be needful to do
good. The whisperer separates friends: what a hateful, but how common a
character!

[Proverbs 16:29](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#029)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_029">pro 16:29</span></span>

Some do all the mischief they can by force and violence, and are blind
to the result.

[Proverbs 16:31](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#031)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_031">pro 16:31</span></span>

Old people especially should be found in the way of religion and
godliness.

[Proverbs 16:32](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#032)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_032">pro 16:32</span></span>

To overcome our own passions, requires more steady management, than
obtaining victory over an enemy.

[Proverbs 16:33](../../../bib/kjv/pro016.htm#033)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_033">pro 16:33</span></span>

All the disposal of Providence concerning our affairs, we must look upon
to be the determining what we referred to God; and we must be reconciled
to them accordingly. Blessed are those that give themselves up to the
will of God; for he knows what is good for them.
