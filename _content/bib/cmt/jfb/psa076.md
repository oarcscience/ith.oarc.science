### [Psalms Chapter 76](../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#001)

[Psalms 76:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 76:1</span></span>

On Neginoth--(See on [Psa 4:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa004.htm#001), title).
This Psalm commemorates what the preceding anticipates: God's
deliverance of His people by a signal interposition of power against
their enemies. The occasion was probably the events narrated in [Kg2
19:35](../../../bib/kjv/kg2019.htm#035); Isa. 37:1-28. (Compare [Psa
46:1-11](../../../bib/kjv/psa046.htm#001)). ([Psa
76:1-12](../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#001))

These well-known terms denote God's people and Church and His intimate
and glorious relations to them.

[Psalms 76:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">psa 76:2</span></span>

Salem-- ([Gen 14:18](../../../bib/kjv/gen014.htm#018)) is Jerusalem.

[Psalms 76:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">psa 76:3</span></span>

brake . . . the arrows--literally, "thunderbolts" ([Psa
78:48](../../../bib/kjv/psa078.htm#048)), from their rapid flight or
ignition (compare [Psa 18:14](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#014); [Eph
6:16](../../../bib/kjv/eph006.htm#016)).

the battle--for arms ([Hos 2:18](../../../bib/kjv/hos002.htm#018)).

[Psalms 76:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 76:4</span></span>

Thou--God.

mountains of prey--great victorious nations, as Assyria ([Isa
41:15](../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#015); [Eze
38:11-12](../../../bib/kjv/eze038.htm#011); [Zac
4:7](../../../bib/kjv/zac004.htm#007)).

[Psalms 76:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">psa 76:5</span></span>

slept their sleep--died ([Psa 13:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa013.htm#003)).

none . . . found . . . hands--are powerless.

[Psalms 76:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">psa 76:6</span></span>

chariot and horse--for those fighting on them (compare [Psa
68:17](../../../bib/kjv/psa068.htm#017)).

[Psalms 76:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">psa 76:7</span></span>

may . . . sight--contend with Thee ([Deu
9:4](../../../bib/kjv/deu009.htm#004); [Jos
7:12](../../../bib/kjv/jos007.htm#012)).

[Psalms 76:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">psa 76:8</span></span>

God's judgment on the wicked is His people's deliverance ([Psa
9:12](../../../bib/kjv/psa009.htm#012); [Psa
10:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa010.htm#007)).

[Psalms 76:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">psa 76:10</span></span>

Man's wrath praises God by its futility before His power.

restrain--or, "gird"; that is, Thyself, as with a sword, with which to
destroy, or as an ornament to Thy praise.

[Psalms 76:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa076.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">psa 76:11</span></span>

Invite homage to such a God ([Ch2
32:23](../../../bib/kjv/ch2032.htm#023)), who can stop the breath of
kings and princes when He wills ([Dan
5:23](../../../bib/kjv/dan005.htm#023)).
