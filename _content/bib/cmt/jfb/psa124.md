### [Psalms Chapter 124](../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#001)

[Psalms 124:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 124:1</span></span>

The writer, for the Church, praises God for past, and expresses trust
for future, deliverance from foes. ([Psa
124:1-8](../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#001))

on our side--for us ([Psa 56:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa056.htm#009)).

now--or, "oh! let Israel"

[Psalms 124:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">psa 124:2</span></span>

rose . . . against, &c.-- ([Psa 3:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#001);
[Psa 56:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa056.htm#011)).

[Psalms 124:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">psa 124:3</span></span>

Then--that is, the time of our danger.

quick--literally, "living" ([Num
16:32-33](../../../bib/kjv/num016.htm#032)), description of ferocity.

[Psalms 124:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 124:4</span></span>

(Compare [Psa 18:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#004), [Psa
18:16](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#016)).

[Psalms 124:5](../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">psa 124:5</span></span>

The epithet proud added to waters denotes insolent enemies.

[Psalms 124:6](../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">psa 124:6</span></span>

The figure is changed to that of a rapacious wild beast ([Psa
3:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa003.htm#007)), and then of a fowler ([Psa
91:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa091.htm#003)), and complete escape is denoted
by breaking the net.

[Psalms 124:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa124.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">psa 124:8</span></span>

(Compare [Psa 121:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa121.htm#002)).

name--in the usual sense ([Psa 5:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa005.htm#011);
[Psa 20:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa020.htm#001)). He thus places over
against the great danger the omnipotent God, and drowns, as it were in
an anthem, the wickedness of the whole world and of hell, just as a
great fire consumes a little drop of water \[LUTHER\].
