### [2 Kings (2 Samuel) Chapter 23](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#001)

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:1](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">sa2 23:1</span></span>

Now these \[be\] the (a) last words of David. David the son of Jesse
said, and the man \[who was\] raised up on high, the anointed of the God
of Jacob, and the sweet psalmist of Israel, said,

\(a\) Which he spoke after he had written the psalms.

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:2](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">sa2 23:2</span></span>

The Spirit of the LORD spake by me, and his word \[was\] in my (b)
tongue.

\(b\) Meaning, he spoke nothing but by the motion of God's Spirit.

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:4](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">sa2 23:4</span></span>

And \[he shall be\] as the light of the morning, \[when\] the sun
riseth, \[even\] a morning without clouds; \[as\] the tender (c) grass
\[springing\] out of the earth by clear shining after rain.

\(c\) Which grows quickly, and fades soon.

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:5](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">sa2 23:5</span></span>

Although my house \[be\] not so with God; yet he hath made with me an
everlasting covenant, ordered in all \[things\], and sure: for \[this
is\] all my salvation, and all \[my\] desire, although he make \[it\]
not to (d) grow.

\(d\) But that my kingdom may continue for ever according to his
promise.

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:8](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">sa2 23:8</span></span>

These \[be\] the names of the mighty men whom David had: The Tachmonite
that sat in the (e) seat, chief among the captains; the same \[was\]
Adino the Eznite: \[he lift up his spear\] against eight hundred, whom
he slew at one time.

\(e\) As one of the king's counsel.

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:9](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">sa2 23:9</span></span>

And after him \[was\] Eleazar the son of Dodo the Ahohite, \[one\] of
the three mighty men with David, when they defied the Philistines
\[that\] were there gathered together to battle, and the men of Israel
were (f) gone away:

\(f\) Meaning, fled from the battle.

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:10](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">sa2 23:10</span></span>

He arose, and smote the Philistines until his hand was weary, and his
(g) hand clave unto the sword: and the LORD wrought a great victory that
day; and the people returned after him only to spoil.

\(g\) By a cramp which came from weariness and fighting.

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:12](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">sa2 23:12</span></span>

But he stood in the midst of the ground, and defended it, and slew the
Philistines: and the LORD wrought a (h) great victory.

\(h\) Which has no respect for many or few, when he will show his power.

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:15](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">sa2 23:15</span></span>

And David (i) longed, and said, Oh that one would give me drink of the
water of the well of Bethlehem, which \[is\] by the gate!

\(i\) Being overcome with weariness and thirst.

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:16](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">sa2 23:16</span></span>

And the three mighty men brake through the host of the Philistines, and
drew water out of the well of Bethlehem, that \[was\] by the gate, and
took \[it\], and brought \[it\] to David: nevertheless he would not
drink thereof, but (k) poured it out unto the LORD.

\(k\) Bridling his affection, and also desiring God not to be offended
for that rash enterprise.

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:21](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#021)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">sa2 23:21</span></span>

And he slew an Egyptian, a goodly man: and the Egyptian had a (l) spear
in his hand; but he went down to him with a staff, and plucked the spear
out of the Egyptian's hand, and slew him with his own spear.

\(l\) Which was as big as a weavers beam, ([Ch1
11:23](../../../bib/kjv/ch1011.htm#023)).

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:23](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#023)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">sa2 23:23</span></span>

He was more honourable than the (m) thirty, but he attained not to the
\[first\] three. And David set him over his guard.

\(m\) He was more valiant than the thirty that follow and not so valiant
as the six before.

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:27](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#027)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">sa2 23:27</span></span>

Abiezer the Anethothite, (n) Mebunnai the Hushathite,

\(n\) Some of these had two names, ([Ch1
11:29](../../../bib/kjv/ch1011.htm#029)) and also many more are
mentioned there.

[2 Kings (2 Samuel) 23:39](../../../bib/kjv/sa2023.htm#039)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_039">sa2 23:39</span></span>

Uriah the Hittite: (o) thirty and seven in all.

\(o\) These came to David, and helped restore him to his kingdom.
