*The Geneva Bible Translation Notes*, \[1599\], at Intangible Textual
Heritage

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### [Psalms Chapter 130](../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#001)

[Psalms 130:1](../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">psa 130:1</span></span>

"A Song of degrees." Out of the (a) depths have I cried unto thee, O
LORD.

\(a\) Being in great distress and sorrow.

[Psalms 130:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">psa 130:3</span></span>

If thou, LORD, shouldest mark iniquities, O Lord, (b) who shall stand?

\(b\) He declares that we cannot be just before God but by forgiveness
of sins.

[Psalms 130:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 130:4</span></span>

But \[there is\] forgiveness with thee, that thou (c) mayest be feared.

\(c\) Because by nature you are merciful therefore the faithful revere
you.

[Psalms 130:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa130.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">psa 130:7</span></span>

Let Israel hope in the LORD: for with the LORD \[there is\] (d) mercy,
and with him \[is\] plenteous redemption.

\(d\) He shows to whom the mercy of God belongs, that is, Israel, to the
Church and not the reprobate.
