### [Proverbs Chapter 31](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#001)

[Proverbs 31:1](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">pro 31:1</span></span>

The words of king (a) Lemuel, the (b) prophecy that his mother taught
him.

\(a\) That is, of Solomon who was called Lemuel, that is, of God,
because God had ordained him to be king over Israel.

\(b\) The doctrine which his mother Bathsheba taught him.

[Proverbs 31:2](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">pro 31:2</span></span>

What, my son? and what, the son of (c) my womb? and what, the son of my
vows?

\(c\) By this often repetition of one thing, she declares her motherly
affection.

[Proverbs 31:3](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">pro 31:3</span></span>

Give not thy strength to women, (d) nor thy ways to that which
destroyeth kings.

\(d\) Meaning, that women are the destruction of kings, if they hunt
them.

[Proverbs 31:4](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">pro 31:4</span></span>

\[It is\] not for kings, O Lemuel, \[it is\] not for kings to drink
wine; nor for princes (e) strong drink:

\(e\) That is, the king must not give himself to wantonness, and neglect
of his office, which is to execute judgment.

[Proverbs 31:7](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">pro 31:7</span></span>

Let him drink, and forget (f) his poverty, and remember his misery no
more.

\(f\) For wine comforts the heart as in ([Psa
104:15](../../../bib/kjv/psa104.htm#015)).

[Proverbs 31:8](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">pro 31:8</span></span>

Open thy mouth for the (g) dumb in the cause of all such as are
appointed to destruction.

\(g\) Defend their cause that are not able to help themselves.

[Proverbs 31:11](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">pro 31:11</span></span>

The heart of her husband doth safely trust in her, so that he shall have
no need of (h) spoil.

\(h\) He will not need to use any unlawful means to gain his living.

[Proverbs 31:15](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">pro 31:15</span></span>

She riseth also while it is yet night, and giveth food to her household,
and a (i) portion to her maidens.

\(i\) She prepares their food early.

[Proverbs 31:16](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">pro 31:16</span></span>

She considereth a field, and (k) buyeth it: with the fruit of her hands
she planteth a vineyard.

\(k\) She purchases it with the gains of her travail.

[Proverbs 31:23](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#023)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">pro 31:23</span></span>

Her husband is known in the (l) gates, when he sitteth among the elders
of the land.

\(l\) In the assemblies and places of judgment.

[Proverbs 31:25](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#025)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">pro 31:25</span></span>

\(m\) Strength and honour \[are\] her clothing; and she shall rejoice in
time to come.

\(m\) After he had spoken of the apparel of the body, he now declares
the apparel of the spirit.

[Proverbs 31:26](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#026)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">pro 31:26</span></span>

She openeth her mouth with wisdom; and on her tongue \[is\] the (n) law
of kindness.

\(n\) Her tongue is a book by which one might learn many good things:
for she delights to talk of the word of God.

[Proverbs 31:28](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#028)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_028">pro 31:28</span></span>

Her children rise up, and (o) call her blessed; her husband \[also\],
and he praiseth her.

\(o\) That is, do her reverence.

[Proverbs 31:31](../../../bib/kjv/pro031.htm#031)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_031">pro 31:31</span></span>

Give (p) her of the fruit of her hands; and let her own works praise her
in the (q) gates.

\(p\) Confess her diligent labours, and commend her therefore.

\(q\) Forasmuch as the most honourable are clad in the apparel that she
made.
