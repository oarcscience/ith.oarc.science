### [Job Chapter 41](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#001)

[Job 41:1](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">job 41:1</span></span>

Canst thou draw out (l) leviathan with an hook? or his tongue with a
cord \[which\] thou lettest down?

\(l\) Meaning the whale.

[Job 41:2](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">job 41:2</span></span>

Canst thou put an hook into his nose? or (m) bore his jaw through with a
thorn?

\(m\) Because he fears lest you should take him.

[Job 41:4](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">job 41:4</span></span>

Will he make a covenant with thee? wilt thou take (n) him for a servant
for ever?

\(n\) To do your business, and be at your command?

[Job 41:8](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">job 41:8</span></span>

Lay thine hand upon him, remember (o) the battle, do no more.

\(o\) If you once consider the danger, you will not meddle with him.

[Job 41:9](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">job 41:9</span></span>

Behold, (p) the hope of him is in vain: shall not \[one\] be cast down
even at the sight of him?

\(p\) That is, that trusts to take him.

[Job 41:10](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">job 41:10</span></span>

None \[is so\] fierce that dare stir him up: who then is able to (a)
stand before me?

\(a\) If no one dare stand against a whale, which is but a creature, who
is able to compare with God the creator?

[Job 41:11](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">job 41:11</span></span>

Who hath prevented me, that I should (b) repay \[him? whatsoever is\]
under the whole heaven is mine.

\(b\) Who has taught me to accomplish my work?

[Job 41:12](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">job 41:12</span></span>

I will not conceal (c) his parts, nor his power, nor his comely
proportion.

\(c\) The parts and members of the whale?

[Job 41:13](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">job 41:13</span></span>

Who can discover the face (d) of his garment? \[or\] who can come \[to
him\] with his double (e) bridle?

\(d\) That is, who dare pull off his skin?

\(e\) Who dare put a bridle in his mouth?

[Job 41:14](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">job 41:14</span></span>

Who can (f) open the doors of his face? his teeth \[are\] terrible round
about.

\(f\) Who dare look in his mouth?

[Job 41:18](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#018)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">job 41:18</span></span>

By his neesings (g) a light doth shine, and his eyes \[are\] like the
eyelids of the morning.

\(g\) That is, casts out flames of fire.

[Job 41:22](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">job 41:22</span></span>

In his neck remaineth strength, and (h) sorrow is turned into joy before
him.

\(h\) Nothing is painful or hard for him.

[Job 41:30](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#030)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_030">job 41:30</span></span>

Sharp stones (i) \[are\] under him: he spreadeth sharp pointed things
upon the mire.

\(i\) His skin is so hard that he lies with a great ease on the stones
as in the mud.

[Job 41:31](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#031)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_031">job 41:31</span></span>

He maketh the deep to (k) boil like a pot: he maketh the sea like a pot
of ointment.

\(k\) Either he makes the sea to seem like it is boiling by his
wallowing, or else he spouts water in such abundance as it would seem
that the sea boiled.

[Job 41:32](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#032)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_032">job 41:32</span></span>

He maketh a path to (l) shine after him; \[one\] would think the deep
\[to be\] hoary.

\(l\) That is, a white froth and shining stream before him.

[Job 41:34](../../../bib/kjv/job041.htm#034)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_034">job 41:34</span></span>

He beholdeth (m) all high \[things\]: he \[is\] a king over all the
children of pride.

\(m\) He despises all other beasts and monsters, and is the proudest of
all others.
