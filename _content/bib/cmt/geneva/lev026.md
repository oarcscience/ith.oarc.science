### [Leviticus Chapter 26](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#001)

[Leviticus 26:4](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">lev 26:4</span></span>

Then I will give you (a) rain in due season, and the land shall yield
her increase, and the trees of the field shall yield their fruit.

\(a\) By promising abundance of earthly things, he stirs the mind to
consider the rich treasures of the spiritual blessings.

[Leviticus 26:6](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">lev 26:6</span></span>

And I will give peace in the land, and ye shall lie down, and none shall
make \[you\] afraid: and I will rid evil beasts out of the land, neither
shall the (b) sword go through your land.

\(b\) You shall have no war.

[Leviticus 26:9](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">lev 26:9</span></span>

For I will have respect unto you, and make you fruitful, and multiply
you, and (c) establish my covenant with you.

\(c\) Perform that which I have promised.

[Leviticus 26:11](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">lev 26:11</span></span>

And I will set my (d) tabernacle among you: and my soul shall not abhor
you.

\(d\) I will be daily present with you.

[Leviticus 26:13](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">lev 26:13</span></span>

I \[am\] the LORD your God, which brought you forth out of the land of
Egypt, that ye should not be their bondmen; and I have broken the (e)
bands of your yoke, and made you go upright.

\(e\) I have set you at full liberty, while before you were as beasts
tied in bands.

[Leviticus 26:15](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">lev 26:15</span></span>

And if ye shall despise my statutes, or if your soul abhor my judgments,
so that ye will not do all my commandments, \[but\] that ye break my (f)
covenant:

\(f\) Which I made with you in choosing you to be my people.

[Leviticus 26:17](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">lev 26:17</span></span>

And I will set (g) my face against you, and ye shall be slain before
your enemies: they that hate you shall reign over you; and ye shall flee
when none pursueth you.

\(g\) Read ([Lev 17:10](../../../bib/kjv/lev017.htm#010)).

[Leviticus 26:18](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#018)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">lev 26:18</span></span>

And if ye will not yet for all this hearken unto me, then I will punish
you (h) seven times more for your sins.

\(h\) That is, more extremely.

[Leviticus 26:19](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#019)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">lev 26:19</span></span>

And I will break the pride of your power; and I will make your heaven as
(i) iron, and your earth as brass:

\(i\) You shall have drought and barrenness.

[Leviticus 26:21](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#021)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">lev 26:21</span></span>

And if ye walk (k) contrary unto me, and will not hearken unto me; I
will bring seven times more plagues upon you according to your sins.

\(k\) Or as some read, by fortune, imputing my plagues to chance and
fortune.

[Leviticus 26:22](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">lev 26:22</span></span>

I will also send wild beasts among you, which shall (l) rob you of your
children, and destroy your cattle, and make you few in number; and your
(m) \[high\] ways shall be desolate.

\(l\) Read ([Kg2 17:25](../../../bib/kjv/kg2017.htm#025)).

\(m\) Because no one dares to pass by it for fear of the beasts.

[Leviticus 26:26](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#026)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_026">lev 26:26</span></span>

\[And\] when I have broken the (n) staff of your bread, ten women shall
bake your bread in one (o) oven, and they shall deliver \[you\] your
bread again by weight: and ye shall eat, and not be satisfied.

\(n\) That is, the strength by which life is sustained, ([Eze
4:16](../../../bib/kjv/eze004.htm#016), [Eze
5:16](../../../bib/kjv/eze005.htm#016)).

\(o\) One oven will be sufficient for ten families.

[Leviticus 26:31](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#031)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_031">lev 26:31</span></span>

And I will make your cities waste, and bring your sanctuaries unto
desolation, and I (p) will not smell the savour of your sweet odours.

\(p\) I will not accept your sacrifices.

[Leviticus 26:35](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#035)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_035">lev 26:35</span></span>

As long as it lieth desolate it shall rest; because it did not rest in
your (r) sabbaths, when ye dwelt upon it.

\(r\) Which I commanded you to keep.

[Leviticus 26:36](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#036)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_036">lev 26:36</span></span>

And upon them that are left \[alive\] of you I will send a faintness
into their hearts in the lands of their enemies; and the sound of a
shaken leaf shall chase them; and they shall (s) flee, as fleeing from a
sword; and they shall fall when none pursueth.

\(s\) As if their enemies chased them.

[Leviticus 26:39](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#039)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_039">lev 26:39</span></span>

And they that are left of you shall pine away in their iniquity in your
enemies' lands; and also in the iniquities of their fathers shall they
pine away with (t) them.

\(t\) In that, as they are blameworthy of their fathers faults, they
shall be punished as well as their fathers.

[Leviticus 26:43](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#043)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_043">lev 26:43</span></span>

\(u\) The land also shall be left of them, and shall enjoy her sabbaths,
while she lieth desolate without them: and they shall accept of the
punishment of their iniquity: because, even because they despised my
judgments, and because their soul abhorred my statutes.

\(u\) While they are captives, and without repentance.

[Leviticus 26:45](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#045)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_045">lev 26:45</span></span>

But I will for their sakes remember the (x) covenant of their ancestors,
whom I brought forth out of the land of Egypt in the sight of the
heathen, that I might be their God: I \[am\] the LORD.

\(x\) Made to their forefathers.

[Leviticus 26:46](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#046)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_046">lev 26:46</span></span>

These \[are\] the statutes and judgments and laws, which the LORD made
between him and the children of Israel in mount (y) Sinai by the hand of
Moses.

\(y\) Fifty days after they came out of Egypt.
