### [Ezekiel Chapter 24](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#001)

[Ezekiel 24:1](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#001)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_001">eze 24:1</span></span>

Again in the (a) ninth year, in the tenth month, in the tenth \[day\] of
the (b) month, the word of the LORD came to me, saying,

\(a\) Of Jeconiah's captivity and of the reign of Zedekiah, ([Kg2
25:1](../../../bib/kjv/kg2025.htm#001)).

\(b\) Called Tebeth, which contains part of December and part of
January: in which month and day Nebuchadnezzar besieged Jerusalem.

[Ezekiel 24:3](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">eze 24:3</span></span>

And utter a parable to the rebellious house, and say to them, Thus saith
the Lord GOD; Set on a (c) pot, set \[it\] on, and also pour water into
it:

\(c\) By which was meant Jerusalem.

[Ezekiel 24:4](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">eze 24:4</span></span>

Gather its (d) pieces into it, \[even\] every good piece, the thigh, and
the shoulder; fill \[it\] with the choice bones.

\(d\) That is the citizens and the chief men of it.

[Ezekiel 24:5](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#005)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_005">eze 24:5</span></span>

Take the choice of the flock, and burn also the (e) bones under it,
\[and\] make it boil well, and let them boil its bones in it.

\(e\) Meaning, of the innocents whom they had slain, who were the cause
of the kindling of God's wrath against them.

[Ezekiel 24:6](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#006)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_006">eze 24:6</span></span>

Wherefore thus saith the Lord GOD; Woe to the bloody city, to the pot
(f) whose scum \[is\] in it, and whose scum is not gone out of it! bring
it out (g) piece by piece; let no (h) lot fall upon it.

\(f\) Whose iniquities and wicked citizens there yet remain.

\(g\) Signifying that they should not be destroyed all at once, but
little by little.

\(h\) Spare no estate or condition.

[Ezekiel 24:7](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">eze 24:7</span></span>

For her blood is in the midst of her; she set it upon the top of a (i)
rock; she poured it not upon the ground, to cover it with dust;

\(i\) The city showed her cruelty to all the world, and was not ashamed
of it, neither yet hid it.

[Ezekiel 24:10](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">eze 24:10</span></span>

Heap on wood, (k) kindle the fire, consume the flesh, and spice it well,
and let the bones be burned.

\(k\) Meaning that the city would be utterly destroyed and that he would
give the enemies an appetite for it.

[Ezekiel 24:12](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#012)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_012">eze 24:12</span></span>

\(l\) She hath wearied \[herself\] with lies, and her great scum went
not forth out of her: her scum \[shall be\] in the fire.

\(l\) The city has flattered herself in vain.

[Ezekiel 24:13](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">eze 24:13</span></span>

In thy filthiness \[is\] lewdness: because I (m) have purged thee, and
thou wast not purged, thou shalt not be purged from thy filthiness any
more, till I have caused my fury to rest upon thee.

\(m\) I laboured by sending my prophets to call you to repentance but
you would not.

[Ezekiel 24:14](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">eze 24:14</span></span>

I the LORD have spoken \[it\]: it shall come to pass, and I will do
\[it\]; I will not go back, neither will I spare, neither will I repent;
according to thy ways, and according to thy doings, shall (n) they judge
thee, saith the Lord GOD.

\(n\) That is, the Babylonians.

[Ezekiel 24:16](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">eze 24:16</span></span>

Son of man, behold, I take away from thee the (o) desire of thy eyes
with a stroke: yet neither shalt thou mourn nor weep, neither shall thy
tears run down.

\(o\) Meaning his wife in whom he delighted, as in ([Eze
24:18](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#018)).

[Ezekiel 24:17](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">eze 24:17</span></span>

Forbear to cry, make no mourning for the dead, bind thy turban upon
thee, (p) and put on thy shoes upon thy feet, and cover not \[thy\]
lips, and eat (q) not the bread of men.

\(p\) For in mourning they went bare headed and barefooted and also
covered their lips.

\(q\) That is, which the neighbours sent to them that mourned.

[Ezekiel 24:18](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#018)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_018">eze 24:18</span></span>

So I spoke to the people in the morning: and at evening my wife died;
and I did (r) in the morning as I was commanded.

\(r\) Meaning the morning following.

[Ezekiel 24:21](../../../bib/kjv/eze024.htm#021)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_021">eze 24:21</span></span>

Speak to the house of Israel, Thus saith the Lord GOD; Behold, I will
(s) profane my sanctuary, the (t) excellence of your strength, the
desire of your eyes, and that which your soul pitieth; and your sons and
your daughters whom ye have left shall fall by the sword.

\(s\) By sending the Chaldeans to destroy it, as in See [Eze
7:22](../../../bib/kjv/eze007.htm#022).

\(t\) In which you boast and delight.
