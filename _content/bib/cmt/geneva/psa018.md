### [Psalms Chapter 18](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#001)

[Psalms 18:2](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#002)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_002">psa 18:2</span></span>

\(a\) The LORD \[is\] my rock, and my fortress, and my deliverer; my
God, my strength, in whom I will trust; my buckler, and the horn of my
salvation, \[and\] my high tower.

\(a\) He uses this diversity of names to show that as the wicked have
many means to hurt, so God has many ways to help.

[Psalms 18:3](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#003)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_003">psa 18:3</span></span>

I will call upon the LORD, \[who is worthy\] to be (b) praised: so shall
I be saved from mine enemies.

\(b\) For no one can obtain their request from God if they do not join
his glory with their petition.

[Psalms 18:4](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#004)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_004">psa 18:4</span></span>

\(c\) The sorrows of death compassed me, and the floods of ungodly men
made me afraid.

\(c\) He speaks of the dangers and malice of his enemies from which God
had delivered him.

[Psalms 18:7](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#007)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_007">psa 18:7</span></span>

\(d\) Then the earth shook and trembled; the foundations also of the
hills moved and were shaken, because he was wroth.

\(d\) A description of the wrath of God against his enemies, after he
had heard his prayer.

[Psalms 18:8](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#008)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_008">psa 18:8</span></span>

There went up a smoke out of his nostrils, and (e) fire out of his mouth
devoured: coals were kindled by it.

\(e\) He shows how horrible God's judgments will be to the wicked.

[Psalms 18:9](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#009)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_009">psa 18:9</span></span>

He bowed the heavens also, and came down: and (f) darkness \[was\] under
his feet.

\(f\) Darkness signifies the wrath of God as the clear light signifies
God's favour.

[Psalms 18:10](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#010)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_010">psa 18:10</span></span>

And he rode upon a (g) cherub, and did fly: yea, he did fly upon the
wings of the wind.

\(g\) This is described at large in (Psa. 104:1-35).

[Psalms 18:11](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#011)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_011">psa 18:11</span></span>

He made darkness his (h) secret place; his pavilion round about him
\[were\] dark waters \[and\] thick clouds of the skies.

\(h\) As a king angry with the people, will not show himself to them.

[Psalms 18:13](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#013)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_013">psa 18:13</span></span>

The LORD also thundered in the heavens, and the Highest gave (i) his
voice; hail \[stones\] and coals of fire.

\(i\) Thunders, lightnings and hail.

[Psalms 18:14](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#014)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_014">psa 18:14</span></span>

Yea, he sent out (k) his arrows, and scattered them; and he shot out
lightnings, and discomfited them.

\(k\) His lightening.

[Psalms 18:15](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#015)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_015">psa 18:15</span></span>

Then the channels of waters were seen, and the (l) foundations of the
world were discovered at thy rebuke, O LORD, at the blast of the breath
of thy nostrils.

\(l\) That is, the deep bottoms were seen when the Red sea was divided.

[Psalms 18:16](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#016)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_016">psa 18:16</span></span>

He sent from above, he took me, he drew me out of many (m) waters.

\(m\) Out of many great dangers.

[Psalms 18:17](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#017)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_017">psa 18:17</span></span>

He delivered me from my (n) strong enemy, and from them which hated me:
for they were (o) too strong for me.

\(n\) That is, Saul.

\(o\) Therefore God sent me help.

[Psalms 18:19](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#019)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_019">psa 18:19</span></span>

He brought me forth also into a large place; (p) he delivered me,
because he delighted in me.

\(p\) The cause of God's deliverance is his favour and love for us.

[Psalms 18:20](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#020)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_020">psa 18:20</span></span>

The LORD rewarded me according to my (q) righteousness; according to the
cleanness of my hands hath he recompensed me.

\(q\) David was sure of his righteous cause and good behaviour toward
Saul and his enemies and therefore was assured of God's favour and
deliverance.

[Psalms 18:22](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#022)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_022">psa 18:22</span></span>

For all his judgments \[were\] before me, and I did not put away his (r)
statutes from me.

\(r\) For all his dangers he exercised himself in the law of God.

[Psalms 18:23](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#023)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_023">psa 18:23</span></span>

I was also upright before him, and I kept myself from mine (f) iniquity.

\(f\) I neither gave place to their wicked temptations nor to my own
desires.

[Psalms 18:25](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#025)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_025">psa 18:25</span></span>

With the (t) merciful thou wilt shew thyself merciful; with an upright
man thou wilt shew thyself upright;

\(t\) Here he speaks of God according to our capacity, who shows mercy
to his and punishes the wicked, as is also said in ([Lev
26:21](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#021), [Lev
26:24](../../../bib/kjv/lev026.htm#024)).

[Psalms 18:27](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#027)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_027">psa 18:27</span></span>

For thou wilt save the afflicted people; but wilt (u) bring down high
looks.

\(u\) When their sin is come to the full measure.

[Psalms 18:29](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#029)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_029">psa 18:29</span></span>

For by thee I have (x) run through a troop; and by my God have I leaped
over a wall.

\(x\) He attributes it to God that he both got the victory in the field
and also destroyed the cities of his enemies.

[Psalms 18:30](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#030)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_030">psa 18:30</span></span>

\[As for\] God, his way \[is\] perfect: the (y) word of the LORD is
tried: he \[is\] a buckler to all those that trust in him.

\(y\) No matter how great or many the dangers may be, yet God's promise
must take effect.

[Psalms 18:32](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#032)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_032">psa 18:32</span></span>

\[It is\] God that girdeth me with strength, and maketh my (z) way
perfect.

\(z\) He gives good success to all my enterprises.

[Psalms 18:33](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#033)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_033">psa 18:33</span></span>

He maketh my feet like hinds' \[feet\], and setteth me upon my (a) high
places.

\(a\) As towers and forts, which he took out of the hands of God's
enemies.

[Psalms 18:35](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#035)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_035">psa 18:35</span></span>

Thou hast also given me the (b) shield of thy salvation: and thy right
hand hath holden me up, and thy (c) gentleness hath made me great.

\(b\) To defend me from dangers.

\(c\) He attributed the beginning, continuance and increase in well
doing only to God's favour.

[Psalms 18:37](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#037)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_037">psa 18:37</span></span>

\(d\) I have pursued mine enemies, and overtaken them: neither did I
turn again till they were consumed.

\(d\) David declares that he did nothing besides his calling, but was
stirred up by God's Spirit to execute his judgments.

[Psalms 18:40](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#040)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_040">psa 18:40</span></span>

Thou hast also (e) given me the necks of mine enemies; that I might
destroy them that hate me.

\(e\) You have given them to my hands to be slain.

[Psalms 18:41](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#041)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_041">psa 18:41</span></span>

They (f) cried, but \[there was\] none to save \[them: even\] unto the
LORD, but he answered them not.

\(f\) They who reject the cry of the afflicted, God will also reject
them when they cry for help, for either pain or fear causes those
hypocrites to cry.

[Psalms 18:43](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#043)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_043">psa 18:43</span></span>

Thou hast delivered me from the strivings of the people; \[and\] thou
hast made me the head of the (g) heathen: a people \[whom\] I have not
(h) known shall serve me.

\(g\) Who dwell round about me.

\(h\) The kingdom of Christ is prefigured in David's kingdom, who by the
preaching of his word brings all to his subjection.

[Psalms 18:44](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#044)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_044">psa 18:44</span></span>

As soon as they hear of me, they shall obey me: the strangers shall (i)
submit themselves unto me.

\(i\) Or lie: signifying a subjection constrained and not voluntary.

[Psalms 18:45](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#045)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_045">psa 18:45</span></span>

The strangers shall (k) fade away, and be afraid out of their close
places.

\(k\) Fear will cause them to be afraid and come forth from their secret
holes and holds to seek pardon.

[Psalms 18:48](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#048)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_048">psa 18:48</span></span>

He delivereth me from mine enemies: yea, thou liftest me up above those
that rise up against me: thou hast delivered me from the (l) violent
man.

\(l\) That is, Saul who out of malice persecuted him.

[Psalms 18:49](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#049)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_049">psa 18:49</span></span>

Therefore will (m) I give thanks unto thee, O LORD, among the heathen,
and sing praises unto thy name.

\(m\) This prophecy belongs to the kingdom of Christ and calling of the
Gentiles, as in ([Rom 15:9](../../../bib/kjv/rom015.htm#009)).

[Psalms 18:50](../../../bib/kjv/psa018.htm#050)

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_050">psa 18:50</span></span>

Great deliverance giveth he to his king; and sheweth mercy to his
anointed, to David, and to his (n) seed for evermore.

\(n\) This did not properly belong to Solomon, but to Jesus Christ.
