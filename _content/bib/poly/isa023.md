### Isaiah 23

[KJV](../kjv/isa023)

1  The burden<sup>[4853](h4853)</sup> of Tyre.<sup>[6865](h6865)</sup>
Howl,<sup>[3213](h3213)</sup> ye ships<sup>[591](h0591)</sup> of
Tarshish;<sup>[8659](h8659)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> it is laid
waste,<sup>[7703](h7703)</sup> so that there is no
house,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> no entering

land<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[776](h0776)</sup> of
Chittim<sup>[3794](h3794)</sup> it is revealed<sup>[1540](h1540)</sup>
to them.

2  Be still,<sup>[1826](h1826)</sup> ye
inhabitants<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup> of the isle;<sup>[339](h0339)</sup>
thou whom the merchants<sup>[5503](h5503)</sup> of
Zidon,<sup>[6721](h6721)</sup> that pass over<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup>
the sea,<sup>[3220](h3220)</sup> have
replenished.<sup>[4390](h4390)</sup>

3  And by great<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup> waters<sup>[4325](h4325)</sup>
the seed<sup>[2233](h2233)</sup> of Sihor,<sup>[7883](h7883)</sup> the
harvest<sup>[7105](h7105)</sup> of the river,<sup>[2975](h2975)</sup> is
her revenue;<sup>[8393](h8393)</sup> and she is<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup>
a mart<sup>[5505](h5505)</sup> of nations.<sup>[1471](h1471)</sup>

4  Be thou ashamed,<sup>[954](h0954)</sup> O
Zidon:<sup>[6721](h6721)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> the
sea<sup>[3220](h3220)</sup> hath spoken,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> even the
strength<sup>[4581](h4581)</sup> of the sea,<sup>[3220](h3220)</sup>
saying,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> I travail<sup>[2342](h2342)</sup>
not,<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> nor<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> bring forth
children,<sup>[3205](h3205)</sup> neither<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> do I
nourish up<sup>[1431](h1431)</sup> young men,<sup>[970](h0970)</sup> nor
bring up<sup>[7311](h7311)</sup> virgins.<sup>[1330](h1330)</sup>

5  As<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> at the report<sup>[8088](h8088)</sup>
concerning Egypt,<sup>[4714](h4714)</sup> so shall they be sorely
pained<sup>[2342](h2342)</sup> at the report<sup>[8088](h8088)</sup> of
Tyre.<sup>[6865](h6865)</sup>

6  Pass ye over<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup> to
Tarshish;<sup>[8659](h8659)</sup> howl,<sup>[3213](h3213)</sup> ye
inhabitants<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup> of the isle.<sup>[339](h0339)</sup>

7  Is this<sup>[2063](h2063)</sup> your joyous<sup>[5947](h5947)</sup>
city, whose antiquity<sup>[6927](h6927)</sup> is of
ancient<sup>[6924](h6924)</sup> days?<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> her own feet<sup>[7272](h7272)</sup> shall
carry<sup>[2986](h2986)</sup> her afar off<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[7350](h7350)</sup> to sojourn.<sup>[1481](h1481)</sup>

8  Who<sup>[4310](h4310)</sup> hath taken this
counsel<sup>[3289](h3289)</sup> <sup>[2063](h2063)</sup>
against<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> Tyre,<sup>[6865](h6865)</sup> the
crowning<sup>[5849](h5849)</sup> city, whose<sup>[834](h0834)</sup>
merchants<sup>[5503](h5503)</sup> are princes,<sup>[8269](h8269)</sup>
whose traders<sup>[3669](h3669)</sup> are the
honorable<sup>[3513](h3513)</sup> of the earth?<sup>[776](h0776)</sup>

9  The LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> of hosts<sup>[6635](h6635)</sup>
hath purposed<sup>[3289](h3289)</sup> it, to
stain<sup>[2490](h2490)</sup> the pride<sup>[1347](h1347)</sup> of
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> glory,<sup>[6643](h6643)</sup> and to bring
into contempt<sup>[7043](h7043)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> the
honorable<sup>[3513](h3513)</sup> of the earth.<sup>[776](h0776)</sup>

10  Pass through<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup> thy land<sup>[776](h0776)</sup>
as a river,<sup>[2975](h2975)</sup> O daughter<sup>[1323](h1323)</sup>

11  He stretched out<sup>[5186](h5186)</sup> his
hand<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> over<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the
sea,<sup>[3220](h3220)</sup> he shook<sup>[7264](h7264)</sup> the

given a commandment<sup>[6680](h6680)</sup>
against<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> the merchant<sup>[3667](h3667)</sup>
city, to destroy<sup>[8045](h8045)</sup> the
strongholds<sup>[4581](h4581)</sup> thereof.

12  And he said,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> Thou shalt
no<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> more<sup>[5750](h5750)</sup>
rejoice,<sup>[5937](h5937)</sup> O thou
oppressed<sup>[6231](h6231)</sup> virgin,<sup>[1330](h1330)</sup>
daughter<sup>[1323](h1323)</sup> of Zidon:<sup>[6721](h6721)</sup>
arise,<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> pass over<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup> to
Chittim;<sup>[3794](h3794)</sup> there<sup>[8033](h8033)</sup>
also<sup>[1571](h1571)</sup> shalt thou have no
rest.<sup>[5117](h5117)</sup> <sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>

13  Behold<sup>[2005](h2005)</sup> the land<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> of
the Chaldeans;<sup>[3778](h3778)</sup> this<sup>[2088](h2088)</sup>

not,<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> till the Assyrian<sup>[804](h0804)</sup>
founded<sup>[3245](h3245)</sup> it for them that dwell in the
wilderness:<sup>[6728](h6728)</sup> they set up<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup>
the towers<sup>[971](h0971)</sup> thereof, they raised
up<sup>[6209](h6209)</sup> the palaces<sup>[759](h0759)</sup> thereof;
and he brought<sup>[7760](h7760)</sup> it to
ruin.<sup>[4654](h4654)</sup>

14  Howl,<sup>[3213](h3213)</sup> ye ships<sup>[591](h0591)</sup> of
Tarshish:<sup>[8659](h8659)</sup> for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> your
strength<sup>[4581](h4581)</sup> is laid waste.<sup>[7703](h7703)</sup>

15  And it shall come to pass<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> in
that<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> day,<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> that
Tyre<sup>[6865](h6865)</sup> shall be forgotten<sup>[7911](h7911)</sup>
seventy<sup>[7657](h7657)</sup> years,<sup>[8141](h8141)</sup> according

king:<sup>[4428](h4428)</sup> after the end<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[7093](h7093)</sup> of seventy<sup>[7657](h7657)</sup>
years<sup>[8141](h8141)</sup> shall Tyre<sup>[6865](h6865)</sup>

16  Take<sup>[3947](h3947)</sup> a harp,<sup>[3658](h3658)</sup> go
about<sup>[5437](h5437)</sup> the city,<sup>[5892](h5892)</sup> thou
harlot<sup>[2181](h2181)</sup> that hast been
forgotten;<sup>[7911](h7911)</sup> make sweet<sup>[3190](h3190)</sup>
melody,<sup>[5059](h5059)</sup> sing many<sup>[7235](h7235)</sup>
songs,<sup>[7892](h7892)</sup> that<sup>[4616](h4616)</sup> thou mayest

17  And it shall come to pass<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> after the
end<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7093](h7093)</sup> of
seventy<sup>[7657](h7657)</sup> years,<sup>[8141](h8141)</sup> that the
LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> will visit<sup>[6485](h6485)</sup>
<sup>[853](h0853)</sup> Tyre,<sup>[6865](h6865)</sup> and she shall
turn<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> to her hire,<sup>[868](h0868)</sup> and

with<sup>[854](h0854)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> the
kingdoms<sup>[4467](h4467)</sup> of the world<sup>[776](h0776)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the face<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> of the
earth.<sup>[127](h0127)</sup>

18  And her merchandise<sup>[5504](h5504)</sup> and her
hire<sup>[868](h0868)</sup> shall be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup>
holiness<sup>[6944](h6944)</sup> to the LORD:<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> it
shall not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> be treasured<sup>[686](h0686)</sup>
nor<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> laid up;<sup>[2630](h2630)</sup>
for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> her merchandise<sup>[5504](h5504)</sup>
shall be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> for them that
dwell<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup> before<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> the

[Sep](../sep/isa023)

1 τὸ ὅραμα Τύρου ὀλολύζετε πλοῖα Καρχηδόνος ὅτι ἀπώλετο καὶ οὐκέτι
ἔρχονται ἐκ γῆς Κιτιαίων ἦκται αἰχμάλωτος

2 τίνι ὅμοιοι γεγόνασιν οἱ ἐνοικοῦντες ἐν τῇ νήσῳ μεταβόλοι Φοινίκης
διαπερῶντες τὴν θάλασσαν

3 ἐν ὕδατι πολλῷ σπέρμα μεταβόλων ὡς ἀμητοῦ εἰσφερομένου οἱ μεταβόλοι
τῶν ἐθνῶν

4 αἰσχύνθητι Σιδών εἶπεν ἡ θάλασσα ἡ δὲ ἰσχὺς τῆς θαλάσσης εἶπεν οὐκ
ὤδινον οὐδὲ ἔτεκον οὐδὲ ἐξέθρεψα νεανίσκους οὐδὲ ὕψωσα παρθένους

5 ὅταν δὲ ἀκουστὸν γένηται Αἰγύπτῳ λήμψεται αὐτοὺς ὀδύνη περὶ Τύρου

6 ἀπέλθατε εἰς Καρχηδόνα ὀλολύξατε οἱ ἐνοικοῦντες ἐν τῇ νήσῳ ταύτῃ

7 οὐχ αὕτη ἦν ὑμῶν ἡ ὕβρις ἡ ἀπ᾽ ἀρχῆς πρὶν ἢ παραδοθῆναι αὐτήν

8 τίς ταῦτα ἐβούλευσεν ἐπὶ Τύρον μὴ ἥσσων ἐστὶν ἢ οὐκ ἰσχύει οἱ ἔμποροι
αὐτῆς ἔνδοξοι ἄρχοντες τῆς γῆς

9 κύριος σαβαωθ ἐβουλεύσατο παραλῦσαι πᾶσαν τὴν ὕβριν τῶν ἐνδόξων καὶ
ἀτιμάσαι πᾶν ἔνδοξον ἐπὶ τῆς γῆς

10 ἐργάζου τὴν γῆν σου καὶ γὰρ πλοῖα οὐκέτι ἔρχεται ἐκ Καρχηδόνος

11 ἡ δὲ χείρ σου οὐκέτι ἰσχύει κατὰ θάλασσαν ἡ παροξύνουσα βασιλεῖς
κύριος σαβαωθ ἐνετείλατο περὶ Χανααν ἀπολέσαι αὐτῆς τὴν ἰσχύν

12 καὶ ἐροῦσιν οὐκέτι μὴ προσθῆτε τοῦ ὑβρίζειν καὶ ἀδικεῖν τὴν θυγατέρα
Σιδῶνος καὶ ἐὰν ἀπέλθῃς εἰς Κιτιεῖς οὐδὲ ἐκεῖ σοι ἀνάπαυσις ἔσται

13 καὶ εἰς γῆν Χαλδαίων καὶ αὕτη ἠρήμωται ἀπὸ τῶν Ἀσσυρίων οὐδὲ ἐκεῖ σοι
ἀνάπαυσις ἔσται ὅτι ὁ τοῖχος αὐτῆς πέπτωκεν

14 ὀλολύζετε πλοῖα Καρχηδόνος ὅτι ἀπώλετο τὸ ὀχύρωμα ὑμῶν

15 καὶ ἔσται ἐν τῇ ἡμέρᾳ ἐκείνῃ καταλειφθήσεται Τύρος ἔτη ἑβδομήκοντα ὡς
χρόνος βασιλέως ὡς χρόνος ἀνθρώπου καὶ ἔσται μετὰ ἑβδομήκοντα ἔτη ἔσται
Τύρος ὡς ᾆσμα πόρνης

16 λαβὲ κιθάραν ῥέμβευσον πόλεις πόρνη ἐπιλελησμένη καλῶς κιθάρισον
πολλὰ ᾆσον ἵνα σου μνεία γένηται

17 καὶ ἔσται μετὰ ἑβδομήκοντα ἔτη ἐπισκοπὴν ποιήσει ὁ θεὸς Τύρου καὶ
πάλιν ἀποκατασταθήσεται εἰς τὸ ἀρχαῖον καὶ ἔσται ἐμπόριον πάσαις ταῖς
βασιλείαις τῆς οἰκουμένης

18 καὶ ἔσται αὐτῆς ἡ ἐμπορία καὶ ὁ μισθὸς ἅγιον τῷ κυρίῳ οὐκ αὐτοῖς
συναχθήσεται ἀλλὰ τοῖς κατοικοῦσιν ἔναντι κυρίου πᾶσα ἡ ἐμπορία αὐτῆς
φαγεῖν καὶ πιεῖν καὶ ἐμπλησθῆναι εἰς συμβολὴν μνημόσυνον ἔναντι κυρίου

1 Onus Tyri. \[Ululate, naves maris,  
quia vastata est domus  
unde venire consueverant:  
de terra Cethim  
revelatum est eis.  

2 Tacete, qui habitatis in insula;  
negotiatores Sidonis, transfretantes mare, repleverunt te.  

3 In aquis multis semen Nili;  
messis fluminis fruges ejus:  
et facta est negotiatio gentium.  

4 Erubesce, Sidon; ait enim mare,  
fortitudo maris, dicens:  
Non parturivi, et non peperi,  
et non enutrivi juvenes,  
nec ad incrementum perduxi virgines.  

5 Cum auditum fuerit in Ægypto,  
dolebunt cum audierint de Tiro.  

6 Transite maria, ululate,  
qui habitatis in insula!  

7 Numquid non vestra hæc est, quæ gloriabatur  
a diebus pristinis in antiquitate sua?  
Ducent eam pedes sui  
longe ad peregrinandum.  

8 Quis cogitavit hoc  
super Tyrum quondam coronatam,  
cujus negotiatores principes,  
institores ejus inclyti terræ?  

9 Dominus exercituum cogitavit hoc,  
ut detraheret superbiam omnis gloriæ,  
et ad ignominiam deduceret universos inclytos terræ.  

10 Transi terram tuam quasi flumen, filia maris!  
non est cingulum ultra tibi.  

11 Manum suam extendit super mare;  
conturbavit regna.  
Dominus mandavit adversus Chanaan,  
ut contereret fortes ejus;  

12 et dixit: Non adjicies ultra ut glorieris,  
calumniam sustinens virgo filia Sidonis:  
in Cethim consurgens transfreta:  
ibi quoque non erit requies tibi.  

13 Ecce terra Chaldæorum, talis populus non fuit:  
Assur fundavit eam;  
in captivitatem traduxerunt robustos ejus,  
suffoderunt domos ejus,  
posuerunt eam in ruinam.  

14 Ululate, naves maris,  
quia devastata est fortitudo vestra.\]

15 Et erit in die illa: in oblivione eris, o Tyre! septuaginta annis,
sicut dies regis unius; post septuaginta autem annos erit Tyro quasi
canticum meretricis:

16 \[Sume citharam, circui civitatem,  
meretrix oblivioni tradita:  
bene cane, frequenta canticum,  
ut memoria tui sit.  

17 Et erit post septuaginta annos:  
visitabit Dominus Tyrum,  
et reducet eam ad mercedes suas,  
et rursum fornicabitur cum universis regnis terræ  
super faciem terræ;  

18 et erunt negotiationes ejus et mercedes ejus sanctificatæ Domino:  
non condentur neque reponentur,  
quia his qui habitaverint coram Domino erit negotiatio ejus,  
ut manducent in saturitatem, et vestiantur usque ad vetustatem.\]
