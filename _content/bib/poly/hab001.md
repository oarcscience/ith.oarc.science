### Habakkuk 1

[KJV](../kjv/hab001)

1  The burden<sup>[4853](h4853)</sup> which<sup>[834](h0834)</sup>
Habakkuk<sup>[2265](h2265)</sup> the prophet<sup>[5030](h5030)</sup> did
see.<sup>[2372](h2372)</sup>

2  O LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> how long<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup>
<sup>[575](h0575)</sup> shall I cry,<sup>[7768](h7768)</sup> and thou
wilt not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> hear!<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> even cry
out<sup>[2199](h2199)</sup> unto<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> thee of
violence,<sup>[2555](h2555)</sup> and thou wilt
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> save!<sup>[3467](h3467)</sup>

3  Why<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> dost thou show<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> me
iniquity,<sup>[205](h0205)</sup> and cause me to
behold<sup>[5027](h5027)</sup> grievance?<sup>[5999](h5999)</sup> for
spoiling<sup>[7701](h7701)</sup> and violence<sup>[2555](h2555)</sup>
are before<sup>[5048](h5048)</sup> me: and there
are<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> that raise up<sup>[5375](h5375)</sup>
strife<sup>[7379](h7379)</sup> and contention.<sup>[4066](h4066)</sup>

4  Therefore<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> <sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> the
law<sup>[8451](h8451)</sup> is slacked,<sup>[6313](h6313)</sup> and
judgment<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup> doth never<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
<sup>[5331](h5331)</sup> go forth:<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup>
for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> doth
compass about<sup>[3803](h3803)</sup> <sup>[853](h0853)</sup> the
righteous;<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> therefore<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup>
<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> wrong<sup>[6127](h6127)</sup>
judgment<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup> proceedeth.<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup>

5  Behold<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> ye among the
heathen,<sup>[1471](h1471)</sup> and regard,<sup>[5027](h5027)</sup> and
wonder marvelously:<sup>[8539](h8539)</sup> <sup>[8539](h8539)</sup>
for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> I will work<sup>[6466](h6466)</sup> a
work<sup>[6467](h6467)</sup> in your days,<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> which
ye will not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> believe,<sup>[539](h0539)</sup>
though<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> it be told<sup>[5608](h5608)</sup> you.

6  For,<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> lo,<sup>[2009](h2009)</sup> I raise
up<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> <sup>[853](h0853)</sup> the
Chaldeans,<sup>[3778](h3778)</sup> that bitter<sup>[4751](h4751)</sup>
and hasty<sup>[4116](h4116)</sup> nation,<sup>[1471](h1471)</sup> which
shall march<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> through the
breadth<sup>[4800](h4800)</sup> of the land,<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> to
possess<sup>[3423](h3423)</sup> the dwelling
places<sup>[4908](h4908)</sup> that are not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
theirs.

7  They<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> are terrible<sup>[366](h0366)</sup> and
dreadful:<sup>[3372](h3372)</sup> their judgment<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup>
and their dignity<sup>[7613](h7613)</sup> shall
proceed<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup> of<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> themselves.

8  Their horses<sup>[5483](h5483)</sup> also are
swifter<sup>[7043](h7043)</sup> than the
leopards,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[5246](h5246)</sup> and are more
fierce<sup>[2300](h2300)</sup> than the evening<sup>[6153](h6153)</sup>
wolves:<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[2061](h2061)</sup> and their
horsemen<sup>[6571](h6571)</sup> shall spread<sup>[6335](h6335)</sup>
themselves, and their horsemen<sup>[6571](h6571)</sup> shall
come<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> from far;<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[7350](h7350)</sup> they shall fly<sup>[5774](h5774)</sup> as the
eagle<sup>[5404](h5404)</sup> that hasteth<sup>[2363](h2363)</sup> to
eat.<sup>[398](h0398)</sup>

9  They shall come<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup>
for violence:<sup>[2555](h2555)</sup> their
faces<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> shall sup up<sup>[4041](h4041)</sup> as
the east wind,<sup>[6921](h6921)</sup> and they shall
gather<sup>[622](h0622)</sup> the captivity<sup>[7628](h7628)</sup> as
the sand.<sup>[2344](h2344)</sup>

10  And they<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> shall scoff<sup>[7046](h7046)</sup>
at the kings,<sup>[4428](h4428)</sup> and the
princes<sup>[7336](h7336)</sup> shall be a scorn<sup>[4890](h4890)</sup>
unto them: they<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> shall
deride<sup>[7832](h7832)</sup> every<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup>
stronghold;<sup>[4013](h4013)</sup> for they shall
heap<sup>[6651](h6651)</sup> dust,<sup>[6083](h6083)</sup> and
take<sup>[3920](h3920)</sup> it.

11  Then<sup>[227](h0227)</sup> shall his mind<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup>
change,<sup>[2498](h2498)</sup> and he shall pass
over,<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup> and offend,<sup>[816](h0816)</sup>
imputing this<sup>[2098](h2098)</sup> his power<sup>[3581](h3581)</sup>
unto his god.<sup>[433](h0433)</sup>

12  Art thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> from
everlasting,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6924](h6924)</sup> O
LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> my God,<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> mine Holy
One?<sup>[6918](h6918)</sup> we shall not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
die.<sup>[4191](h4191)</sup> O LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> thou hast
ordained<sup>[7760](h7760)</sup> them for
judgment;<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup> and, O mighty
God,<sup>[6697](h6697)</sup> thou hast
established<sup>[3245](h3245)</sup> them for
correction.<sup>[3198](h3198)</sup>

13  Thou art of purer<sup>[2889](h2889)</sup>
eyes<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> than to behold<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> evil,<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> and
canst<sup>[3201](h3201)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
look<sup>[5027](h5027)</sup> on<sup>[413](h0413)</sup>
iniquity:<sup>[5999](h5999)</sup> wherefore<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup>
lookest<sup>[5027](h5027)</sup> thou upon them that deal
treacherously,<sup>[898](h0898)</sup> and holdest thy
tongue<sup>[2790](h2790)</sup> when the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup>
devoureth<sup>[1104](h1104)</sup> the man that is more
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> than<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> he?

14  And makest<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> men<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> as the
fishes<sup>[1709](h1709)</sup> of the sea,<sup>[3220](h3220)</sup> as
the creeping things,<sup>[7431](h7431)</sup> that have
no<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> ruler<sup>[4910](h4910)</sup> over them?

15  They take up<sup>[5927](h5927)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> of
them with the angle,<sup>[2443](h2443)</sup> they
catch<sup>[1641](h1641)</sup> them in their net,<sup>[2764](h2764)</sup>
and gather<sup>[622](h0622)</sup> them in their
drag:<sup>[4365](h4365)</sup> therefore<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup>
<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> they rejoice<sup>[8055](h8055)</sup> and are
glad.<sup>[1523](h1523)</sup>

16  Therefore<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> <sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> they
sacrifice<sup>[2076](h2076)</sup> unto their
net,<sup>[2764](h2764)</sup> and burn incense<sup>[6999](h6999)</sup>
unto their drag;<sup>[4365](h4365)</sup> because<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup>
by them<sup>[1992](h1992)</sup> their portion<sup>[2506](h2506)</sup> is
fat,<sup>[8082](h8082)</sup> and their meat<sup>[3978](h3978)</sup>
plenteous.<sup>[1277](h1277)</sup>

17  Shall they therefore<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup>
<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup> empty<sup>[7324](h7324)</sup> their
net,<sup>[2764](h2764)</sup> and not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
spare<sup>[2550](h2550)</sup> continually<sup>[8548](h8548)</sup> to
slay<sup>[2026](h2026)</sup> the nations?<sup>[1471](h1471)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/hab001)

1 τὸ λῆμμα ὃ εἶδεν Αμβακουμ ὁ προφήτης

2 ἕως τίνος κύριε κεκράξομαι καὶ οὐ μὴ εἰσακούσῃς βοήσομαι πρὸς σὲ
ἀδικούμενος καὶ οὐ σώσεις

3 ἵνα τί μοι ἔδειξας κόπους καὶ πόνους ἐπιβλέπειν ταλαιπωρίαν καὶ
ἀσέβειαν ἐξ ἐναντίας μου γέγονεν κρίσις καὶ ὁ κριτὴς λαμβάνει

4 διὰ τοῦτο διεσκέδασται νόμος καὶ οὐ διεξάγεται εἰς τέλος κρίμα ὅτι ὁ
ἀσεβὴς καταδυναστεύει τὸν δίκαιον ἕνεκεν τούτου ἐξελεύσεται τὸ κρίμα
διεστραμμένον

5 ἴδετε οἱ καταφρονηταί καὶ ἐπιβλέψατε καὶ θαυμάσατε θαυμάσια καὶ
ἀφανίσθητε διότι ἔργον ἐγὼ ἐργάζομαι ἐν ταῖς ἡμέραις ὑμῶν ὃ οὐ μὴ
πιστεύσητε ἐάν τις ἐκδιηγῆται

6 διότι ἰδοὺ ἐγὼ ἐξεγείρω ἐφ᾽ ὑμᾶς τοὺς Χαλδαίους τοὺς μαχητάς τὸ ἔθνος
τὸ πικρὸν καὶ τὸ ταχινὸν τὸ πορευόμενον ἐπὶ τὰ πλάτη τῆς γῆς τοῦ
κατακληρονομῆσαι σκηνώματα οὐκ αὐτοῦ

7 φοβερὸς καὶ ἐπιφανής ἐστιν ἐξ αὐτοῦ τὸ κρίμα αὐτοῦ ἔσται καὶ τὸ λῆμμα
αὐτοῦ ἐξ αὐτοῦ ἐξελεύσεται

8 καὶ ἐξαλοῦνται ὑπὲρ παρδάλεις οἱ ἵπποι αὐτοῦ καὶ ὀξύτεροι ὑπὲρ τοὺς
λύκους τῆς Ἀραβίας καὶ ἐξιππάσονται οἱ ἱππεῖς αὐτοῦ καὶ ὁρμήσουσιν
μακρόθεν καὶ πετασθήσονται ὡς ἀετὸς πρόθυμος εἰς τὸ φαγεῖν

9 συντέλεια εἰς ἀσεβεῖς ἥξει ἀνθεστηκότας προσώποις αὐτῶν ἐξ ἐναντίας
καὶ συνάξει ὡς ἄμμον αἰχμαλωσίαν

10 καὶ αὐτὸς ἐν βασιλεῦσιν ἐντρυφήσει καὶ τύραννοι παίγνια αὐτοῦ καὶ
αὐτὸς εἰς πᾶν ὀχύρωμα ἐμπαίξεται καὶ βαλεῖ χῶμα καὶ κρατήσει αὐτοῦ

11 τότε μεταβαλεῖ τὸ πνεῦμα καὶ διελεύσεται καὶ ἐξιλάσεται αὕτη ἡ ἰσχὺς
τῷ θεῷ μου

12 οὐχὶ σὺ ἀπ᾽ ἀρχῆς κύριε ὁ θεὸς ὁ ἅγιός μου καὶ οὐ μὴ ἀποθάνωμεν κύριε
εἰς κρίμα τέταχας αὐτόν καὶ ἔπλασέν με τοῦ ἐλέγχειν παιδείαν αὐτοῦ

13 καθαρὸς ὀφθαλμὸς τοῦ μὴ ὁρᾶν πονηρά καὶ ἐπιβλέπειν ἐπὶ πόνους οὐ
δυνήσῃ ἵνα τί ἐπιβλέπεις ἐπὶ καταφρονοῦντας παρασιωπήσῃ ἐν τῷ καταπίνειν
ἀσεβῆ τὸν δίκαιον

14 καὶ ποιήσεις τοὺς ἀνθρώπους ὡς τοὺς ἰχθύας τῆς θαλάσσης καὶ ὡς τὰ
ἑρπετὰ τὰ οὐκ ἔχοντα ἡγούμενον

15 συντέλειαν ἐν ἀγκίστρῳ ἀνέσπασεν καὶ εἵλκυσεν αὐτὸν ἐν ἀμφιβλήστρῳ
καὶ συνήγαγεν αὐτὸν ἐν ταῖς σαγήναις αὐτοῦ ἕνεκεν τούτου εὐφρανθήσεται
καὶ χαρήσεται ἡ καρδία αὐτοῦ

16 ἕνεκεν τούτου θύσει τῇ σαγήνῃ αὐτοῦ καὶ θυμιάσει τῷ ἀμφιβλήστρῳ αὐτοῦ
ὅτι ἐν αὐτοῖς ἐλίπανεν μερίδα αὐτοῦ καὶ τὰ βρώματα αὐτοῦ ἐκλεκτά

17 διὰ τοῦτο ἀμφιβαλεῖ τὸ ἀμφίβληστρον αὐτοῦ καὶ διὰ παντὸς ἀποκτέννειν
ἔθνη οὐ φείσεται

[Vul](../vul/hab001)

1 Onus quod vidit Habacuc propheta.  

2 \[Usquequo, Domine, clamabo,  
et non exaudies?  
vociferabor ad te, vim patiens,  
et non salvabis?  

3 Quare ostendisti mihi iniquitatem et laborem,  
videre prædam et injustitiam contra me?  
Et factum est judicium, et contradictio potentior.  

4 Propter hoc lacerata est lex,  
et non pervenit usque ad finem judicium;  
quia impius prævalet adversus justum,  
propterea egreditur judicium perversum.  

5 Aspicite in gentibus, et videte;  
admiramini, et obstupescite:  
quia opus factum est in diebus vestris,  
quod nemo credet cum narrabitur.  

6 Quia ecce ego suscitabo Chaldæos,  
gentem amaram et velocem,  
ambulantem super latitudinem terræ,  
ut possideat tabernacula non sua.  

7 Horribilis et terribilis est:  
ex semetipsa judicium et onus ejus egredietur.  

8 Leviores pardis equi ejus,  
et velociores lupis vespertinis:  
et diffundentur equites ejus:  
equites namque ejus de longe venient;  
volabunt quasi aquila festinans ad comedendum.  

9 Omnes ad prædam venient,  
facies eorum ventus urens;  
et congregabit quasi arenam captivitatem.  

10 Et ipse de regibus triumphabit,  
et tyranni ridiculi ejus erunt;  
ipse super omnem munitionem ridebit,  
et comportabit aggerem, et capiet eam.  

11 Tunc mutabitur spiritus, et pertransibit, et corruet:  
hæc est fortitudo ejus dei sui.\]  

12 \[Numquid non tu a principio,  
Domine, Deus meus, sancte meus,  
et non moriemur?  
Domine, in judicium posuisti eum,  
et fortem, ut corriperes, fundasti eum.  

13 Mundi sunt oculi tui, ne videas malum,  
et respicere ad iniquitatem non poteris.  
Quare respicis super iniqua agentes,  
et taces devorante impio justiorem se?  

14 Et facies homines quasi pisces maris,  
et quasi reptile non habens principem.  

15 Totum in hamo sublevavit,  
traxit illud in sagena sua,  
et congregavit in rete suum.  
Super hoc lætabitur, et exsultabit.  

16 Propterea immolabit sagenæ suæ,  
et sacrificabit reti suo,  
quia in ipsis incrassata est pars ejus,  
et cibus ejus electus.  

17 Propter hoc ergo expandit sagenam suam,  
et semper interficere gentes non parcet.\]
