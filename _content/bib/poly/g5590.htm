ψυχή
psuchē
psoo-khay‘
From 5594 breath, that is, (by implication) spirit, abstractly or concretely (the animal sentient principle only; thus distinguished on the one hand from 4151 which is the rational and immortal soul; and on the other from 2222 which is mere vitality, even of plants: these terms thus exactly correspond respectively to the Hebrew [5315 , [7307 and [2416 : - heart (+ -ily), life, mind, soul, + us, + you.
