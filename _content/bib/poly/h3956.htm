H3956
לשׁנה לשׁן לשׁון
לָשׁוֹן לָשׁוֹן לְשׁוֹנָה ‎ lâshôn lâshôn leshônâh
law-shone‘, law-shone‘, lesh-o-naw‘
From 3960 the tongue (of man or animals), used literally (as the instrument of licking, eating, or speech), and figuratively (speech, an ingot, a fork of flame, a cove of water): - + babbler, bay, + evil speaker, language, talker, tongue, wedge.
