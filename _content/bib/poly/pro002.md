### Proverbs 2

[KJV](../kjv/pro002)

1  My son,<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> if<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> thou wilt
receive<sup>[3947](h3947)</sup> my words,<sup>[561](h0561)</sup> and
hide<sup>[6845](h6845)</sup> my commandments<sup>[4687](h4687)</sup>
with<sup>[854](h0854)</sup> thee;

2  So that thou incline<sup>[7181](h7181)</sup> thine
ear<sup>[241](h0241)</sup> unto wisdom,<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup> and
apply<sup>[5186](h5186)</sup> thine heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> to
understanding;<sup>[8394](h8394)</sup>

3  Yea,<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> if<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> thou
criest<sup>[7121](h7121)</sup> after knowledge,<sup>[998](h0998)</sup>
and liftest up<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> thy voice<sup>[6963](h6963)</sup>
for understanding;<sup>[8394](h8394)</sup>

4  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> thou seekest<sup>[1245](h1245)</sup> her as
silver,<sup>[3701](h3701)</sup> and searchest<sup>[2664](h2664)</sup>
for her as for hid treasures;<sup>[4301](h4301)</sup>

5  Then<sup>[227](h0227)</sup> shalt thou
understand<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> the fear<sup>[3374](h3374)</sup> of
the LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> and find<sup>[4672](h4672)</sup> the
knowledge<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup> of God.<sup>[430](h0430)</sup>

6  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>
giveth<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> wisdom:<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup> out of
his mouth<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> cometh
knowledge<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup> and
understanding.<sup>[8394](h8394)</sup>

7  He layeth up<sup>[6845](h6845)</sup> sound
wisdom<sup>[8454](h8454)</sup> for the
righteous:<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup> he is a
buckler<sup>[4043](h4043)</sup> to them that
walk<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> uprightly.<sup>[8537](h8537)</sup>

8  He keepeth<sup>[5341](h5341)</sup> the paths<sup>[734](h0734)</sup>
of judgment,<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup> and
preserveth<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> the way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of
his saints.<sup>[2623](h2623)</sup>

9  Then<sup>[227](h0227)</sup> shalt thou
understand<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> righteousness,<sup>[6664](h6664)</sup>
and judgment,<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup> and
equity;<sup>[4339](h4339)</sup> yea, every<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup>
good<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> path.<sup>[4570](h4570)</sup>

10  When<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> wisdom<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup>
entereth<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> into thine
heart,<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> and knowledge<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup> is
pleasant<sup>[5276](h5276)</sup> unto thy soul;<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup>

11  Discretion<sup>[4209](h4209)</sup> shall
preserve<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> <sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> thee,
understanding<sup>[8394](h8394)</sup> shall keep<sup>[5341](h5341)</sup>
thee:

12  To deliver<sup>[5337](h5337)</sup> thee from the
way<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of the
evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> man, from the man<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> that speaketh<sup>[1696](h1696)</sup> froward
things;<sup>[8419](h8419)</sup>

13  Who leave<sup>[5800](h5800)</sup> the paths<sup>[734](h0734)</sup>
of uprightness,<sup>[3476](h3476)</sup> to walk<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup>
in the ways<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of darkness;<sup>[2822](h2822)</sup>

14  Who rejoice<sup>[8056](h8056)</sup> to do<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup>
evil,<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> and delight in<sup>[1523](h1523)</sup> the
frowardness<sup>[8419](h8419)</sup> of the
wicked;<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup>

15  Whose<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> ways<sup>[734](h0734)</sup> are
crooked,<sup>[6141](h6141)</sup> and they
froward<sup>[3868](h3868)</sup> in their paths:<sup>[4570](h4570)</sup>

16  To deliver<sup>[5337](h5337)</sup> thee from the strange
woman,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[802](h0802)</sup>
<sup>[2114](h2114)</sup> even from the stranger<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[5237](h5237)</sup> which flattereth<sup>[2505](h2505)</sup> with
her words;<sup>[561](h0561)</sup>

17  Which forsaketh<sup>[5800](h5800)</sup> the
guide<sup>[441](h0441)</sup> of her youth,<sup>[5271](h5271)</sup> and
forgetteth<sup>[7911](h7911)</sup> the covenant<sup>[1285](h1285)</sup>
of her God.<sup>[430](h0430)</sup>

18  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> her house<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup>
inclineth<sup>[7743](h7743)</sup> unto<sup>[413](h0413)</sup>
death,<sup>[4194](h4194)</sup> and her paths<sup>[4570](h4570)</sup>
unto<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> the dead.<sup>[7496](h7496)</sup>

19  None<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> <sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> that go
unto<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> her return again,<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup>
neither<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> take they hold<sup>[5381](h5381)</sup>
of the paths<sup>[734](h0734)</sup> of life.<sup>[2416](h2416)</sup>

20  That<sup>[4616](h4616)</sup> thou mayest
walk<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> in the way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of
good<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> men, and keep<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> the
paths<sup>[734](h0734)</sup> of the righteous.<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup>

21  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> the upright<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup>
shall dwell in<sup>[7931](h7931)</sup> the land,<sup>[776](h0776)</sup>
and the perfect<sup>[8549](h8549)</sup> shall
remain<sup>[3498](h3498)</sup> in it.

22  But the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> shall be cut
off<sup>[3772](h3772)</sup> from the earth,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> and the transgressors<sup>[898](h0898)</sup>
shall be rooted<sup>[5255](h5255)</sup> out of<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
it.

[Sep](../sep/pro002)

1 υἱέ ἐὰν δεξάμενος ῥῆσιν ἐμῆς ἐντολῆς κρύψῃς παρὰ σεαυτῷ

2 ὑπακούσεται σοφίας τὸ οὖς σου καὶ παραβαλεῖς καρδίαν σου εἰς σύνεσιν
παραβαλεῖς δὲ αὐτὴν ἐπὶ νουθέτησιν τῷ υἱῷ σου

3 ἐὰν γὰρ τὴν σοφίαν ἐπικαλέσῃ καὶ τῇ συνέσει δῷς φωνήν σου τὴν δὲ
αἴσθησιν ζητήσῃς μεγάλῃ τῇ φωνῇ

4 καὶ ἐὰν ζητήσῃς αὐτὴν ὡς ἀργύριον καὶ ὡς θησαυροὺς ἐξερευνήσῃς αὐτήν

5 τότε συνήσεις φόβον κυρίου καὶ ἐπίγνωσιν θεοῦ εὑρήσεις

6 ὅτι κύριος δίδωσιν σοφίαν καὶ ἀπὸ προσώπου αὐτοῦ γνῶσις καὶ σύνεσις

7 καὶ θησαυρίζει τοῖς κατορθοῦσι σωτηρίαν ὑπερασπιεῖ τὴν πορείαν αὐτῶν

8 τοῦ φυλάξαι ὁδοὺς δικαιωμάτων καὶ ὁδὸν εὐλαβουμένων αὐτὸν διαφυλάξει

9 τότε συνήσεις δικαιοσύνην καὶ κρίμα καὶ κατορθώσεις πάντας ἄξονας
ἀγαθούς

10 ἐὰν γὰρ ἔλθῃ ἡ σοφία εἰς σὴν διάνοιαν ἡ δὲ αἴσθησις τῇ σῇ ψυχῇ καλὴ
εἶναι δόξῃ

11 βουλὴ καλὴ φυλάξει σε ἔννοια δὲ ὁσία τηρήσει σε

12 ἵνα ῥύσηταί σε ἀπὸ ὁδοῦ κακῆς καὶ ἀπὸ ἀνδρὸς λαλοῦντος μηδὲν πιστόν

13 ὦ οἱ ἐγκαταλείποντες ὁδοὺς εὐθείας τοῦ πορεύεσθαι ἐν ὁδοῖς σκότους

14 οἱ εὐφραινόμενοι ἐπὶ κακοῖς καὶ χαίροντες ἐπὶ διαστροφῇ κακῇ

15 ὧν αἱ τρίβοι σκολιαὶ καὶ καμπύλαι αἱ τροχιαὶ αὐτῶν

16 τοῦ μακράν σε ποιῆσαι ἀπὸ ὁδοῦ εὐθείας καὶ ἀλλότριον τῆς δικαίας
γνώμης

17 υἱέ μή σε καταλάβῃ κακὴ βουλὴ ἡ ἀπολείπουσα διδασκαλίαν νεότητος καὶ
διαθήκην θείαν ἐπιλελησμένη

18 ἔθετο γὰρ παρὰ τῷ θανάτῳ τὸν οἶκον αὐτῆς καὶ παρὰ τῷ ᾅδῃ μετὰ τῶν
γηγενῶν τοὺς ἄξονας αὐτῆς

19 πάντες οἱ πορευόμενοι ἐν αὐτῇ οὐκ ἀναστρέψουσιν οὐδὲ μὴ καταλάβωσιν
τρίβους εὐθείας οὐ γὰρ καταλαμβάνονται ὑπὸ ἐνιαυτῶν ζωῆς

20 εἰ γὰρ ἐπορεύοντο τρίβους ἀγαθάς εὕροσαν ἂν τρίβους δικαιοσύνης
λείους

21 χρηστοὶ ἔσονται οἰκήτορες γῆς ἄκακοι δὲ ὑπολειφθήσονται ἐν αὐτῇ ὅτι
εὐθεῖς κατασκηνώσουσι γῆν καὶ ὅσιοι ὑπολειφθήσονται ἐν αὐτῇ

22 ὁδοὶ ἀσεβῶν ἐκ γῆς ὀλοῦνται οἱ δὲ παράνομοι ἐξωσθήσονται ἀπ᾽ αὐτῆς

[Vul](../vul/pro002)

1 \[Fili mi, si susceperis sermones meos,  
et mandata mea absconderis penes te:  

2 ut audiat sapientiam auris tua,  
inclina cor tuum ad cognoscendam prudentiam.  

3 Si enim sapientiam invocaveris,  
et inclinaveris cor tuum prudentiæ;  

4 si quæsieris eam quasi pecuniam,  
et sicut thesauros effoderis illam:  

5 tunc intelliges timorem Domini,  
et scientiam Dei invenies,  

6 quia Dominus dat sapientiam,  
et ex ore ejus prudentia et scientia.  

7 Custodiet rectorum salutem,  
et proteget gradientes simpliciter,  

8 servans semitas justitiæ,  
et vias sanctorum custodiens.  

9 Tunc intelliges justitiam, et judicium,  
et æquitatem, et omnem semitam bonam.\]  

10 \[Si intraverit sapientia cor tuum,  
et scientia animæ tuæ placuerit,  

11 consilium custodiet te,  
et prudentia servabit te:  

12 ut eruaris a via mala,  
et ab homine qui perversa loquitur;  

13 qui relinquunt iter rectum,  
et ambulant per vias tenebrosas;  

14 qui lætantur cum malefecerint,  
et exsultant in rebus pessimis;  

15 quorum viæ perversæ sunt,  
et infames gressus eorum.  

16 Ut eruaris a muliere aliena,  
et ab extranea quæ mollit sermones suos,  

17 et relinquit ducem pubertatis suæ,  

18 et pacti Dei sui oblita est.  
Inclinata est enim ad mortem domus ejus,  
et ad inferos semitæ ipsius.  

19 Omnes qui ingrediuntur ad eam non revertentur,  
nec apprehendent semitas vitæ.  

20 Ut ambules in via bona,  
et calles justorum custodias:  

21 qui enim recti sunt habitabunt in terra,  
et simplices permanebunt in ea;  

22 impii vero de terra perdentur,  
et qui inique agunt auferentur ex ea.\]
