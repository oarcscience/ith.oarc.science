### Proverbs 28

[KJV](../kjv/pro028)

1  The wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> flee<sup>[5127](h5127)</sup> when
no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> man pursueth:<sup>[7291](h7291)</sup> but the
righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> are bold<sup>[982](h0982)</sup> as a
lion.<sup>[3715](h3715)</sup>

2  For the transgression<sup>[6588](h6588)</sup> of a
land<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> many<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup> are the
princes<sup>[8269](h8269)</sup> thereof: but by a
man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> of understanding<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> and
knowledge<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> the state<sup>[3651](h3651)</sup>
thereof shall be prolonged.<sup>[748](h0748)</sup>

3  A poor<sup>[7326](h7326)</sup> man<sup>[1397](h1397)</sup> that
oppresseth<sup>[6231](h6231)</sup> the poor<sup>[1800](h1800)</sup> is
like a sweeping<sup>[5502](h5502)</sup> rain<sup>[4306](h4306)</sup>
which leaveth no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> food.<sup>[3899](h3899)</sup>

4  They that forsake<sup>[5800](h5800)</sup> the
law<sup>[8451](h8451)</sup> praise<sup>[1984](h1984)</sup> the
wicked:<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> but such as keep<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup>
the law<sup>[8451](h8451)</sup> contend<sup>[1624](h1624)</sup> with
them.

5  Evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> men<sup>[376](h0376)</sup>
understand<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
judgment:<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup> but they that
seek<sup>[1245](h1245)</sup> the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>
understand<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> things.

6  Better<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> is the poor<sup>[7326](h7326)</sup>
that walketh<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> in his
uprightness,<sup>[8537](h8537)</sup> than he that is
perverse<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6141](h6141)</sup> in his
ways,<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> though he<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> be
rich.<sup>[6223](h6223)</sup>

7  Whoso keepeth<sup>[5341](h5341)</sup> the law<sup>[8451](h8451)</sup>
is a wise<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> son:<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> but he
that is a companion<sup>[7462](h7462)</sup> of
riotous<sup>[2151](h2151)</sup> men shameth<sup>[3637](h3637)</sup> his
father.<sup>[1](h0001)</sup>

8  He that by usury<sup>[5392](h5392)</sup> and unjust
gain<sup>[8636](h8636)</sup> increaseth<sup>[7235](h7235)</sup> his
substance,<sup>[1952](h1952)</sup> he shall
gather<sup>[6908](h6908)</sup> it for him that will
pity<sup>[2603](h2603)</sup> the poor.<sup>[1800](h1800)</sup>

9  He that turneth away<sup>[5493](h5493)</sup> his
ear<sup>[241](h0241)</sup> from hearing<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> the law,<sup>[8451](h8451)</sup>
even<sup>[1571](h1571)</sup> his prayer<sup>[8605](h8605)</sup> shall be
abomination.<sup>[8441](h8441)</sup>

10  Whoso causeth the righteous<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup> to go
astray<sup>[7686](h7686)</sup> in an evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup>
way,<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> he shall fall<sup>[5307](h5307)</sup>
himself<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> into his own
pit:<sup>[7816](h7816)</sup> but the upright<sup>[8549](h8549)</sup>
shall have good<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> things in
possession.<sup>[5157](h5157)</sup>

11  The rich<sup>[6223](h6223)</sup> man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> is
wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> in his own conceit;<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup>
but the poor<sup>[1800](h1800)</sup> that hath
understanding<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> searcheth him
out.<sup>[2713](h2713)</sup>

12  When righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> men do
rejoice,<sup>[5970](h5970)</sup> there is great<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup>
glory:<sup>[8597](h8597)</sup> but when the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> rise,<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> a
man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> is hidden.<sup>[2664](h2664)</sup>

13  He that covereth<sup>[3680](h3680)</sup> his
sins<sup>[6588](h6588)</sup> shall not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
prosper:<sup>[6743](h6743)</sup> but whoso
confesseth<sup>[3034](h3034)</sup> and forsaketh<sup>[5800](h5800)</sup>
them shall have mercy.<sup>[7355](h7355)</sup>

14  Happy<sup>[835](h0835)</sup> is the man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> that
feareth<sup>[6342](h6342)</sup> always:<sup>[8548](h8548)</sup> but he
that hardeneth<sup>[7185](h7185)</sup> his heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup>
shall fall<sup>[5307](h5307)</sup> into
mischief.<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup>

15  As a roaring<sup>[5098](h5098)</sup> lion,<sup>[738](h0738)</sup>
and a ranging<sup>[8264](h8264)</sup> bear;<sup>[1677](h1677)</sup> so
is a wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> ruler<sup>[4910](h4910)</sup>
over<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the poor<sup>[1800](h1800)</sup>
people.<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup>

16  The prince<sup>[5057](h5057)</sup> that
wanteth<sup>[2638](h2638)</sup> understanding<sup>[8394](h8394)</sup> is
also a great<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup> oppressor:<sup>[4642](h4642)</sup>
but he that hateth<sup>[8130](h8130)</sup>
covetousness<sup>[1215](h1215)</sup> shall
prolong<sup>[748](h0748)</sup> his days.<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup>

17  A man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> that doeth
violence<sup>[6231](h6231)</sup> to the blood<sup>[1818](h1818)</sup> of
any person<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> shall flee<sup>[5127](h5127)</sup>
to<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> the pit;<sup>[953](h0953)</sup> let
no<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> man stay<sup>[8551](h8551)</sup> him.

18  Whoso walketh<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup>
uprightly<sup>[8549](h8549)</sup> shall be
saved:<sup>[3467](h3467)</sup> but he that is
perverse<sup>[6140](h6140)</sup> in his ways<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup>
shall fall<sup>[5307](h5307)</sup> at once.<sup>[259](h0259)</sup>

19  He that tilleth<sup>[5647](h5647)</sup> his
land<sup>[127](h0127)</sup> shall have plenty<sup>[7646](h7646)</sup> of
bread:<sup>[3899](h3899)</sup> but he that
followeth<sup>[7291](h7291)</sup> after vain<sup>[7386](h7386)</sup>
persons shall have poverty<sup>[7389](h7389)</sup>
enough.<sup>[7646](h7646)</sup>

20  A faithful<sup>[530](h0530)</sup> man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> shall
abound<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup> with blessings:<sup>[1293](h1293)</sup>
but he that maketh haste<sup>[213](h0213)</sup> to be
rich<sup>[6238](h6238)</sup> shall not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> be
innocent.<sup>[5352](h5352)</sup>

21  To have respect<sup>[5234](h5234)</sup> of
persons<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> is not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
good:<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> for for<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> a
piece<sup>[6595](h6595)</sup> of bread<sup>[3899](h3899)</sup> that
man<sup>[1397](h1397)</sup> will transgress.<sup>[6586](h6586)</sup>

22  He<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> that hasteth<sup>[926](h0926)</sup> to be
rich<sup>[1952](h1952)</sup> hath an evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup>
eye,<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> and considereth<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup>
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> that<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup>
poverty<sup>[2639](h2639)</sup> shall come upon<sup>[935](h0935)</sup>
him.

23  He that rebuketh<sup>[3198](h3198)</sup> a
man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> afterwards<sup>[310](h0310)</sup> shall
find<sup>[4672](h4672)</sup> more favor<sup>[2580](h2580)</sup> than he
that flattereth<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[2505](h2505)</sup> with
the tongue.<sup>[3956](h3956)</sup>

24  Whoso robbeth<sup>[1497](h1497)</sup> his
father<sup>[1](h0001)</sup> or his mother,<sup>[517](h0517)</sup> and
saith,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> It is no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup>
transgression;<sup>[6588](h6588)</sup> the same<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup>
is the companion<sup>[2270](h2270)</sup> of a
destroyer.<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> <sup>[7843](h7843)</sup>

25  He that is of a proud<sup>[7342](h7342)</sup>
heart<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> stirreth up<sup>[1624](h1624)</sup>
strife:<sup>[4066](h4066)</sup> but he that putteth his
trust<sup>[982](h0982)</sup> in<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the
LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> shall be made fat.<sup>[1878](h1878)</sup>

26  He<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> that trusteth<sup>[982](h0982)</sup> in
his own heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> is a fool:<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup>
but whoso walketh<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup>
wisely,<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup> he<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> shall be
delivered.<sup>[4422](h4422)</sup>

27  He that giveth<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> unto the
poor<sup>[7326](h7326)</sup> shall not<sup>[369](h0369)</sup>
lack:<sup>[4270](h4270)</sup> but he that hideth<sup>[5956](h5956)</sup>
his eyes<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> shall have many<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup>
a curse.<sup>[3994](h3994)</sup>

28  When the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup>
rise,<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> men<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> hide
themselves:<sup>[5641](h5641)</sup> but when they
perish,<sup>[6](h0006)</sup> the righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup>
increase.<sup>[7235](h7235)</sup>

[Vul](../vul/pro028)

1 \[Fugit impius nemine persequente;  
justus autem, quasi leo confidens, absque terrore erit.  

2 Propter peccata terræ multi principes ejus;  
et propter hominis sapientiam, et horum scientiam quæ dicuntur,  
vita ducis longior erit.  

3 Vir pauper calumnians pauperes  
similis est imbri vehementi in quo paratur fames.\]  

4 \[Qui derelinquunt legem laudant impium;  
qui custodiunt, succenduntur contra eum.  

5 Viri mali non cogitant judicium;  
qui autem inquirunt Dominum animadvertunt omnia.  

6 Melior est pauper ambulans in simplicitate sua  
quam dives in pravis itineribus.  

7 Qui custodit legem filius sapiens est;  
qui autem comessatores pascit confundit patrem suum.  

8 Qui coacervat divitias usuris et fœnore,  
liberali in pauperes congregat eas.  

9 Qui declinat aures suas ne audiat legem,  
oratio ejus erit execrabilis.  

10 Qui decipit justos in via mala, in interitu suo corruet,  
et simplices possidebunt bona ejus.  

11 Sapiens sibi videtur vir dives;  
pauper autem prudens scrutabitur eum.  

12 In exsultatione justorum multa gloria est;  
regnantibus impiis, ruinæ hominum.  

13 Qui abscondit scelera sua non dirigetur;  
qui autem confessus fuerit et reliquerit ea, misericordiam
consequetur.  

14 Beatus homo qui semper est pavidus;  
qui vero mentis est duræ corruet in malum.  

15 Leo rugiens et ursus esuriens,  
princeps impius super populum pauperem.  

16 Dux indigens prudentia multos opprimet per calumniam;  
qui autem odit avaritiam, longi fient dies ejus.  

17 Hominem qui calumniatur animæ sanguinem,  
si usque ad lacum fugerit, nemo sustinet.  

18 Qui ambulat simpliciter salvus erit;  
qui perversis graditur viis concidet semel.  

19 Qui operatur terram suam satiabitur panibus;  
qui autem sectatur otium replebitur egestate.\]  

20 \[Vir fidelis multum laudabitur;  
qui autem festinat ditari non erit innocens.  

21 Qui cognoscit in judicio faciem non bene facit;  
iste et pro buccella panis deserit veritatem.  

22 Vir qui festinat ditari, et aliis invidet,  
ignorat quod egestas superveniet ei.  

23 Qui corripit hominem gratiam postea inveniet apud eum,  
magis quam ille qui per linguæ blandimenta decipit.  

24 Qui subtrahit aliquid a patre suo et a matre,  
et dicit hoc non esse peccatum,  
particeps homicidæ est.  

25 Qui se jactat et dilatat, jurgia concitat;  
qui vero sperat in Domino sanabitur.  

26 Qui confidit in corde suo stultus est;  
qui autem graditur sapienter, ipse salvabitur.  

27 Qui dat pauperi non indigebit;  
qui despicit deprecantem sustinebit penuriam.  

28 Cum surrexerint impii, abscondentur homines;  
cum illi perierint, multiplicabuntur justi.\]
