### Job 10

[KJV](../kjv/job010)

1  My soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> is weary<sup>[5354](h5354)</sup> of
my life;<sup>[2416](h2416)</sup> I will leave<sup>[5800](h5800)</sup> my
complaint<sup>[7879](h7879)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> myself; I
will speak<sup>[1696](h1696)</sup> in the
bitterness<sup>[4751](h4751)</sup> of my soul.<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup>

2  I will say<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> unto<sup>[413](h0413)</sup>
God,<sup>[433](h0433)</sup> Do not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup>
condemn<sup>[7561](h7561)</sup> me; show<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> me
wherefore<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> <sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> thou
contendest<sup>[7378](h7378)</sup> with me.

3  Is it good<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> unto thee
that<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thou shouldest
oppress,<sup>[6231](h6231)</sup> that<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thou
shouldest despise<sup>[3988](h3988)</sup> the
work<sup>[3018](h3018)</sup> of thine hands,<sup>[3709](h3709)</sup> and
shine<sup>[3313](h3313)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the
counsel<sup>[6098](h6098)</sup> of the wicked?<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup>

4  Hast thou eyes<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> of
flesh?<sup>[1320](h1320)</sup> or<sup>[518](h0518)</sup>
seest<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> thou as man<sup>[582](h0582)</sup>
seeth?<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup>

5  Are thy days<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> as the
days<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> of man?<sup>[582](h0582)</sup> are thy
years<sup>[8141](h8141)</sup> as man's<sup>[1397](h1397)</sup>
days,<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup>

6  That<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thou inquirest<sup>[1245](h1245)</sup>
after mine iniquity,<sup>[5771](h5771)</sup> and
searchest<sup>[1875](h1875)</sup> after my sin?<sup>[2403](h2403)</sup>

7  Thou knowest<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup> that<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> I
am not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> wicked;<sup>[7561](h7561)</sup> and there
is none<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> that can deliver<sup>[5337](h5337)</sup>
out of thine hand.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3027](h3027)</sup>

8  Thine hands<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> have made<sup>[6087](h6087)</sup>
me and fashioned<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> me
together<sup>[3162](h3162)</sup> round about;<sup>[5439](h5439)</sup>
yet thou dost destroy<sup>[1104](h1104)</sup> me.

9  Remember,<sup>[2142](h2142)</sup> I beseech
thee,<sup>[4994](h4994)</sup> that<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thou hast
made<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> me as the clay;<sup>[2563](h2563)</sup> and
wilt thou bring me into dust again?<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup>
<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> <sup>[6083](h6083)</sup>

10  Hast thou not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> poured me
out<sup>[5413](h5413)</sup> as milk,<sup>[2461](h2461)</sup> and
curdled<sup>[7087](h7087)</sup> me like cheese?<sup>[1385](h1385)</sup>

11  Thou hast clothed<sup>[3847](h3847)</sup> me with
skin<sup>[5785](h5785)</sup> and flesh,<sup>[1320](h1320)</sup> and hast
fenced<sup>[7753](h7753)</sup> me with bones<sup>[6106](h6106)</sup> and
sinews.<sup>[1517](h1517)</sup>

12  Thou hast granted<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> <sup>[5978](h5978)</sup>
me life<sup>[2416](h2416)</sup> and favor,<sup>[2617](h2617)</sup> and
thy visitation<sup>[6486](h6486)</sup> hath
preserved<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> my spirit.<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup>

13  And these<sup>[428](h0428)</sup> things hast thou
hid<sup>[6845](h6845)</sup> in thine heart:<sup>[3824](h3824)</sup> I
know<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> that<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup>
this<sup>[2063](h2063)</sup> is with<sup>[5973](h5973)</sup> thee.

14  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> I sin,<sup>[2398](h2398)</sup> then thou
markest<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> me, and thou wilt
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> acquit<sup>[5352](h5352)</sup> me from mine
iniquity.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[5771](h5771)</sup>

15  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> I be wicked,<sup>[7561](h7561)</sup>
woe<sup>[480](h0480)</sup> unto me; and if I be
righteous,<sup>[6663](h6663)</sup> yet will I
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> lift up<sup>[5375](h5375)</sup> my
head.<sup>[7218](h7218)</sup> I am full<sup>[7649](h7649)</sup> of
confusion;<sup>[7036](h7036)</sup> therefore see<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup>
thou mine affliction;<sup>[6040](h6040)</sup>

16  For it increaseth.<sup>[1342](h1342)</sup> Thou
huntest<sup>[6679](h6679)</sup> me as a fierce
lion:<sup>[7826](h7826)</sup> and again<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> thou
showest thyself marvelous<sup>[6381](h6381)</sup> upon me.

17  Thou renewest<sup>[2318](h2318)</sup> thy
witnesses<sup>[5707](h5707)</sup> against<sup>[5048](h5048)</sup> me,
and increasest<sup>[7235](h7235)</sup> thine
indignation<sup>[3708](h3708)</sup> upon<sup>[5973](h5973)</sup> me;
changes<sup>[2487](h2487)</sup> and war<sup>[6635](h6635)</sup> are
against<sup>[5973](h5973)</sup> me.

18  Wherefore<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> then hast thou brought me
forth<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup> out of the womb?<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[7358](h7358)</sup> Oh that I had given up the
ghost,<sup>[1478](h1478)</sup> and no<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
eye<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> had seen<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> me!

19  I should have been<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> as
though<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> I had not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
been;<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> I should have been
carried<sup>[2986](h2986)</sup> from the womb<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[990](h0990)</sup> to the grave.<sup>[6913](h6913)</sup>

20  Are not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> my days<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup>
few?<sup>[4592](h4592)</sup> cease<sup>[2308](h2308)</sup> then, and let
me alone,<sup>[7896](h7896)</sup> <sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> that I may
take comfort<sup>[1082](h1082)</sup> a little,<sup>[4592](h4592)</sup>

21  Before<sup>[2962](h2962)</sup> I go<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> whence I
shall not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> return,<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> even
to<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> the land<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> of
darkness<sup>[2822](h2822)</sup> and the shadow of
death;<sup>[6757](h6757)</sup>

22  A land<sup>[776](h0776)</sup> of darkness,<sup>[5890](h5890)</sup>
as<sup>[3644](h3644)</sup> darkness<sup>[652](h0652)</sup> itself; and
of the shadow of death,<sup>[6757](h6757)</sup>
without<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> any order,<sup>[5468](h5468)</sup> and
where the light<sup>[3313](h3313)</sup> is as<sup>[3644](h3644)</sup>
darkness.<sup>[652](h0652)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/job010)

1 κάμνων τῇ ψυχῇ μου στένων ἐπαφήσω ἐπ᾽ αὐτὸν τὰ ῥήματά μου λαλήσω
πικρίᾳ ψυχῆς μου συνεχόμενος

2 καὶ ἐρῶ πρὸς κύριον μή με ἀσεβεῖν δίδασκε καὶ διὰ τί με οὕτως ἔκρινας

3 ἦ καλόν σοι ἐὰν ἀδικήσω ὅτι ἀπείπω ἔργα χειρῶν σου βουλῇ δὲ ἀσεβῶν
προσέσχες

4 ἦ ὥσπερ βροτὸς ὁρᾷ καθορᾷς ἢ καθὼς ὁρᾷ ἄνθρωπος βλέψῃ

5 ἦ ὁ βίος σου ἀνθρώπινός ἐστιν ἢ τὰ ἔτη σου ἀνδρός

6 ὅτι ἀνεζήτησας τὴν ἀνομίαν μου καὶ τὰς ἁμαρτίας μου ἐξιχνίασας

7 οἶδας γὰρ ὅτι οὐκ ἠσέβησα ἀλλὰ τίς ἐστιν ὁ ἐκ τῶν χειρῶν σου
ἐξαιρούμενος

8 αἱ χεῖρές σου ἔπλασάν με καὶ ἐποίησάν με μετὰ ταῦτα μεταβαλών με
ἔπαισας

9 μνήσθητι ὅτι πηλόν με ἔπλασας εἰς δὲ γῆν με πάλιν ἀποστρέφεις

10 ἦ οὐχ ὥσπερ γάλα με ἤμελξας ἐτύρωσας δέ με ἴσα τυρῷ

11 δέρμα καὶ κρέας με ἐνέδυσας ὀστέοις δὲ καὶ νεύροις με ἐνεῖρας

12 ζωὴν δὲ καὶ ἔλεος ἔθου παρ᾽ ἐμοί ἡ δὲ ἐπισκοπή σου ἐφύλαξέν μου τὸ
πνεῦμα

13 ταῦτα ἔχων ἐν σεαυτῷ οἶδα ὅτι πάντα δύνασαι ἀδυνατεῖ δέ σοι οὐθέν

14 ἐάν τε γὰρ ἁμάρτω φυλάσσεις με ἀπὸ δὲ ἀνομίας οὐκ ἀθῷόν με πεποίηκας

15 ἐάν τε γὰρ ἀσεβὴς ὦ οἴμμοι ἐάν τε ὦ δίκαιος οὐ δύναμαι ἀνακύψαι
πλήρης γὰρ ἀτιμίας εἰμί

16 ἀγρεύομαι γὰρ ὥσπερ λέων εἰς σφαγήν πάλιν δὲ μεταβαλὼν δεινῶς με
ὀλέκεις

17 ἐπανακαινίζων ἐπ᾽ ἐμὲ τὴν ἔτασίν μου ὀργῇ δὲ μεγάλῃ μοι ἐχρήσω
ἐπήγαγες δὲ ἐπ᾽ ἐμὲ πειρατήρια

18 ἵνα τί οὖν ἐκ κοιλίας με ἐξήγαγες καὶ οὐκ ἀπέθανον ὀφθαλμὸς δέ με οὐκ
εἶδεν

19 καὶ ὥσπερ οὐκ ὢν ἐγενόμην διὰ τί γὰρ ἐκ γαστρὸς εἰς μνῆμα οὐκ
ἀπηλλάγην

20 ἦ οὐκ ὀλίγος ἐστὶν ὁ χρόνος τοῦ βίου μου ἔασόν με ἀναπαύσασθαι μικρὸν

21 πρὸ τοῦ με πορευθῆναι ὅθεν οὐκ ἀναστρέψω εἰς γῆν σκοτεινὴν καὶ
γνοφεράν

22 εἰς γῆν σκότους αἰωνίου οὗ οὐκ ἔστιν φέγγος οὐδὲ ὁρᾶν ζωὴν βροτῶν

[Vul](../vul/job010)

1 \[Tædet animam meam vitæ meæ;  
dimittam adversum me eloquium meum:  
loquar in amaritudine animæ meæ.  

2 Dicam Deo: Noli me condemnare;  
indica mihi cur me ita judices.  

3 Numquid bonum tibi videtur, si calumnieris me,  
et opprimas me opus manuum tuarum,  
et consilium impiorum adjuves?  

4 Numquid oculi carnei tibi sunt?  
aut sicut videt homo, et tu videbis?  

5 Numquid sicut dies hominis dies tui,  
et anni tui sicut humana sunt tempora,  

6 ut quæras iniquitatem meam,  
et peccatum meum scruteris,  

7 et scias quia nihil impium fecerim,  
cum sit nemo qui de manu tua possit eruere?  

8 Manus tuæ fecerunt me,  
et plasmaverunt me totum in circuitu:  
et sic repente præcipitas me?  

9 Memento, quæso, quod sicut lutum feceris me,  
et in pulverem reduces me.  

10 Nonne sicut lac mulsisti me,  
et sicut caseum me coagulasti?  

11 Pelle et carnibus vestisti me;  
ossibus et nervis compegisti me.  

12 Vitam et misericordiam tribuisti mihi,  
et visitatio tua custodivit spiritum meum.  

13 Licet hæc celes in corde tuo,  
tamen scio quia universorum memineris.  

14 Si peccavi, et ad horam pepercisti mihi,  
cur ab iniquitate mea mundum me esse non pateris?  

15 Et si impius fuero, væ mihi est;  
et si justus, non levabo caput,  
saturatus afflictione et miseria.  

16 Et propter superbiam quasi leænam capies me,  
reversusque mirabiliter me crucias.  

17 Instauras testes tuos contra me,  
et multiplicas iram tuam adversum me,  
et pœnæ militant in me.  

18 Quare de vulva eduxisti me?  
qui utinam consumptus essem, ne oculus me videret.  

19 Fuissem quasi non essem,  
de utero translatus ad tumulum.  

20 Numquid non paucitas dierum meorum finietur brevi?  
dimitte ergo me, ut plangam paululum dolorem meum,  

21 antequam vadam, et non revertar,  
ad terram tenebrosam, et opertam mortis caligine:  

22 terram miseriæ et tenebrarum,  
ubi umbra mortis et nullus ordo,  
sed sempiternus horror inhabitat.\]
