### Proverbs 17

[KJV](../kjv/pro017)

1  Better<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> is a dry<sup>[2720](h2720)</sup>
morsel,<sup>[6595](h6595)</sup> and quietness<sup>[7962](h7962)</sup>
therewith, than a house<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[1004](h1004)</sup>
full<sup>[4392](h4392)</sup> of sacrifices<sup>[2077](h2077)</sup> with
strife.<sup>[7379](h7379)</sup>

2  A wise<sup>[7919](h7919)</sup> servant<sup>[5650](h5650)</sup> shall
have rule<sup>[4910](h4910)</sup> over a son<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup>
that causeth shame,<sup>[954](h0954)</sup> and shall have
part<sup>[2505](h2505)</sup> of the inheritance<sup>[5159](h5159)</sup>
among<sup>[8432](h8432)</sup> the brethren.<sup>[251](h0251)</sup>

3  The refining pot<sup>[4715](h4715)</sup> is for
silver,<sup>[3701](h3701)</sup> and the furnace<sup>[3564](h3564)</sup>
for gold:<sup>[2091](h2091)</sup> but the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>
trieth<sup>[974](h0974)</sup> the hearts.<sup>[3826](h3826)</sup>

4  A wicked doer<sup>[7489](h7489)</sup> giveth
heed<sup>[7181](h7181)</sup> to<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup>
false<sup>[205](h0205)</sup> lips;<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> and a
liar<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> giveth ear<sup>[238](h0238)</sup>
to<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> a naughty<sup>[1942](h1942)</sup>
tongue.<sup>[3956](h3956)</sup>

5  Whoso mocketh<sup>[3932](h3932)</sup> the

Maker:<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> and he that is

not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> be unpunished.<sup>[5352](h5352)</sup>

6  Children's<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> children<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup>
are the crown<sup>[5850](h5850)</sup> of old
men;<sup>[2205](h2205)</sup> and the glory<sup>[8597](h8597)</sup> of

7  Excellent<sup>[3499](h3499)</sup> speech<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup>
becometh<sup>[5000](h5000)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> a
fool:<sup>[5036](h5036)</sup> much less<sup>[637](h0637)</sup>
<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> do lying<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup>

8  A gift<sup>[7810](h7810)</sup> is as a

eyes<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> of him that hath<sup>[1167](h1167)</sup>
it: whithersoever<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> <sup>[3605](h3605)</sup>
<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> it turneth,<sup>[6437](h6437)</sup> it
prospereth.<sup>[7919](h7919)</sup>

9  He that covereth<sup>[3680](h3680)</sup> a
transgression<sup>[6588](h6588)</sup> seeketh<sup>[1245](h1245)</sup>
love;<sup>[160](h0160)</sup> but he that
repeateth<sup>[8138](h8138)</sup> a matter<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup>
separateth<sup>[6504](h6504)</sup> very friends.<sup>[441](h0441)</sup>

10  A reproof<sup>[1606](h1606)</sup> entereth
more<sup>[5181](h5181)</sup> into a wise<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> man than
a hundred<sup>[3967](h3967)</sup> stripes<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[5221](h5221)</sup> into a fool.<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup>

11  An evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> man seeketh<sup>[1245](h1245)</sup>
only<sup>[389](h0389)</sup> rebellion:<sup>[4805](h4805)</sup> therefore
a cruel<sup>[394](h0394)</sup> messenger<sup>[4397](h4397)</sup> shall
be sent<sup>[7971](h7971)</sup> against him.

12  Let a bear<sup>[1677](h1677)</sup> robbed<sup>[7909](h7909)</sup> of
her whelps meet<sup>[6298](h6298)</sup> a man,<sup>[376](h0376)</sup>
rather<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> than a fool<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> in his
folly.<sup>[200](h0200)</sup>

13  Whoso rewardeth<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup>
for<sup>[8478](h8478)</sup> good,<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup>
evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> shall not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
depart<sup>[4185](h4185)</sup> from his house.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup>

14  The beginning<sup>[7225](h7225)</sup> of
strife<sup>[4066](h4066)</sup> is as when one letteth
out<sup>[6362](h6362)</sup> water:<sup>[4325](h4325)</sup> therefore
leave off<sup>[5203](h5203)</sup> contention,<sup>[7379](h7379)</sup>
before<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> it be meddled
with.<sup>[1566](h1566)</sup>

15  He that justifieth<sup>[6663](h6663)</sup> the
wicked,<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> and he that
condemneth<sup>[7561](h7561)</sup> the just,<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup>
even<sup>[1571](h1571)</sup> they both<sup>[8147](h8147)</sup> are
abomination<sup>[8441](h8441)</sup> to the LORD.<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>

16  Wherefore<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> <sup>[2088](h2088)</sup> is there
a price<sup>[4242](h4242)</sup> in the hand<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> of a
fool<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> to get<sup>[7069](h7069)</sup>
wisdom,<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup> seeing he hath no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup>
heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> to it?

17  A friend<sup>[7453](h7453)</sup> loveth<sup>[157](h0157)</sup> at
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> times,<sup>[6256](h6256)</sup> and a
brother<sup>[251](h0251)</sup> is born<sup>[3205](h3205)</sup> for
adversity.<sup>[6869](h6869)</sup>

18  A man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> void<sup>[2638](h2638)</sup> of
understanding<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> striketh<sup>[8628](h8628)</sup>
hands,<sup>[3709](h3709)</sup> and becometh<sup>[6148](h6148)</sup>
surety<sup>[6161](h6161)</sup> in the presence<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup>
of his friend.<sup>[7453](h7453)</sup>

19  He loveth<sup>[157](h0157)</sup>
transgression<sup>[6588](h6588)</sup> that loveth<sup>[157](h0157)</sup>
strife:<sup>[4683](h4683)</sup> and he that
exalteth<sup>[1361](h1361)</sup> his gate<sup>[6607](h6607)</sup>
seeketh<sup>[1245](h1245)</sup> destruction.<sup>[7667](h7667)</sup>

20  He that hath a froward<sup>[6141](h6141)</sup>
heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> findeth<sup>[4672](h4672)</sup>
no<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> good:<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> and he that
hath a perverse<sup>[2015](h2015)</sup> tongue<sup>[3956](h3956)</sup>
falleth<sup>[5307](h5307)</sup> into mischief.<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup>

21  He that begetteth<sup>[3205](h3205)</sup> a
fool<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> doeth it to his
sorrow:<sup>[8424](h8424)</sup> and the father<sup>[1](h0001)</sup> of a
fool<sup>[5036](h5036)</sup> hath no<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
joy.<sup>[8055](h8055)</sup>

22  A merry<sup>[8056](h8056)</sup> heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> doeth
good<sup>[3190](h3190)</sup> like a medicine:<sup>[1456](h1456)</sup>
but a broken<sup>[5218](h5218)</sup> spirit<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup>
drieth<sup>[3001](h3001)</sup> the bones.<sup>[1634](h1634)</sup>

23  A wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> man taketh<sup>[3947](h3947)</sup>
a gift<sup>[7810](h7810)</sup> out of the bosom<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[2436](h2436)</sup> to pervert<sup>[5186](h5186)</sup> the
ways<sup>[734](h0734)</sup> of judgment.<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup>

24  Wisdom<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup> is before<sup>[854](h0854)</sup>
<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> him that hath
understanding;<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> but the
eyes<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> of a fool<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> are in
the ends<sup>[7097](h7097)</sup> of the earth.<sup>[776](h0776)</sup>

25  A foolish<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> son<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> is a
grief<sup>[3708](h3708)</sup> to his father,<sup>[1](h0001)</sup> and
bitterness<sup>[4470](h4470)</sup> to her that
bore<sup>[3205](h3205)</sup> him.

26  Also<sup>[1571](h1571)</sup> to punish<sup>[6064](h6064)</sup> the
just<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> is not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
good,<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> nor to strike<sup>[5221](h5221)</sup>
princes<sup>[5081](h5081)</sup> for<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup>
equity.<sup>[3476](h3476)</sup>

27  He that hath knowledge<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup>
<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup> spareth<sup>[2820](h2820)</sup> his
words:<sup>[561](h0561)</sup> and a man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> of
understanding<sup>[8394](h8394)</sup> is of an
excellent<sup>[3368](h3368)</sup> spirit.<sup>[7307](h7307)</sup>

28  Even<sup>[1571](h1571)</sup> a fool,<sup>[191](h0191)</sup> when he
holdeth his peace,<sup>[2790](h2790)</sup> is
counted<sup>[2803](h2803)</sup> wise:<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> and he
that shutteth<sup>[331](h0331)</sup> his lips<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> is
esteemed a man of understanding.<sup>[995](h0995)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/pro017)

1 κρείσσων ψωμὸς μεθ᾽ ἡδονῆς ἐν εἰρήνῃ ἢ οἶκος πλήρης πολλῶν ἀγαθῶν καὶ
ἀδίκων θυμάτων μετὰ μάχης

2 οἰκέτης νοήμων κρατήσει δεσποτῶν ἀφρόνων ἐν δὲ ἀδελφοῖς διελεῖται μέρη

3 ὥσπερ δοκιμάζεται ἐν καμίνῳ ἄργυρος καὶ χρυσός οὕτως ἐκλεκταὶ καρδίαι
παρὰ κυρίῳ

4 κακὸς ὑπακούει γλώσσης παρανόμων δίκαιος δὲ οὐ προσέχει χείλεσιν
ψευδέσιν

5 ὁ καταγελῶν πτωχοῦ παροξύνει τὸν ποιήσαντα αὐτόν ὁ δὲ ἐπιχαίρων
ἀπολλυμένῳ οὐκ ἀθῳωθήσεται ὁ δὲ ἐπισπλαγχνιζόμενος ἐλεηθήσεται

6 στέφανος γερόντων τέκνα τέκνων καύχημα δὲ τέκνων πατέρες αὐτῶν  
τοῦ πιστοῦ ὅλος ὁ κόσμος τῶν χρημάτων τοῦ δὲ ἀπίστου οὐδὲ ὀβολός

7 οὐχ ἁρμόσει ἄφρονι χείλη πιστὰ οὐδὲ δικαίῳ χείλη ψευδῆ

8 μισθὸς χαρίτων ἡ παιδεία τοῖς χρωμένοις οὗ δ᾽ ἂν ἐπιστρέψῃ
εὐοδωθήσεται

9 ὃς κρύπτει ἀδικήματα ζητεῖ φιλίαν ὃς δὲ μισεῖ κρύπτειν διίστησιν
φίλους καὶ οἰκείους

10 συντρίβει ἀπειλὴ καρδίαν φρονίμου ἄφρων δὲ μαστιγωθεὶς οὐκ αἰσθάνεται

11 ἀντιλογίας ἐγείρει πᾶς κακός ὁ δὲ κύριος ἄγγελον ἀνελεήμονα ἐκπέμψει
αὐτῷ

12 ἐμπεσεῖται μέριμνα ἀνδρὶ νοήμονι οἱ δὲ ἄφρονες διαλογιοῦνται κακά

13 ὃς ἀποδίδωσιν κακὰ ἀντὶ ἀγαθῶν οὐ κινηθήσεται κακὰ ἐκ τοῦ οἴκου αὐτοῦ

14 ἐξουσίαν δίδωσιν λόγοις ἀρχὴ δικαιοσύνης προηγεῖται δὲ τῆς ἐνδείας
στάσις καὶ μάχη

15 ὃς δίκαιον κρίνει τὸν ἄδικον ἄδικον δὲ τὸν δίκαιον ἀκάθαρτος καὶ
βδελυκτὸς παρὰ θεῷ

16 ἵνα τί ὑπῆρξεν χρήματα ἄφρονι κτήσασθαι γὰρ σοφίαν ἀκάρδιος οὐ
δυνήσεται  
ὃς ὑψηλὸν ποιεῖ τὸν ἑαυτοῦ οἶκον ζητεῖ συντριβήν ὁ δὲ σκολιάζων τοῦ
μαθεῖν ἐμπεσεῖται εἰς κακά

17 εἰς πάντα καιρὸν φίλος ὑπαρχέτω σοι ἀδελφοὶ δὲ ἐν ἀνάγκαις χρήσιμοι
ἔστωσαν τούτου γὰρ χάριν γεννῶνται

18 ἀνὴρ ἄφρων ἐπικροτεῖ καὶ ἐπιχαίρει ἑαυτῷ ὡς καὶ ὁ ἐγγυώμενος ἐγγύῃ
τὸν ἑαυτοῦ φίλον

19 φιλαμαρτήμων χαίρει μάχαις

20 ὁ δὲ σκληροκάρδιος οὐ συναντᾷ ἀγαθοῖς ἀνὴρ εὐμετάβολος γλώσσῃ
ἐμπεσεῖται εἰς κακά

21 καρδία δὲ ἄφρονος ὀδύνη τῷ κεκτημένῳ αὐτήν οὐκ εὐφραίνεται πατὴρ ἐπὶ
υἱῷ ἀπαιδεύτῳ υἱὸς δὲ φρόνιμος εὐφραίνει μητέρα αὐτοῦ

22 καρδία εὐφραινομένη εὐεκτεῖν ποιεῖ ἀνδρὸς δὲ λυπηροῦ ξηραίνεται τὰ
ὀστᾶ

23 λαμβάνοντος δῶρα ἐν κόλπῳ ἀδίκως οὐ κατευοδοῦνται ὁδοί ἀσεβὴς δὲ
ἐκκλίνει ὁδοὺς δικαιοσύνης

24 πρόσωπον συνετὸν ἀνδρὸς σοφοῦ οἱ δὲ ὀφθαλμοὶ τοῦ ἄφρονος ἐπ᾽ ἄκρα γῆς

25 ὀργὴ πατρὶ υἱὸς ἄφρων καὶ ὀδύνη τῇ τεκούσῃ αὐτοῦ

26 ζημιοῦν ἄνδρα δίκαιον οὐ καλόν οὐδὲ ὅσιον ἐπιβουλεύειν δυνάσταις
δικαίοις

27 ὃς φείδεται ῥῆμα προέσθαι σκληρόν ἐπιγνώμων μακρόθυμος δὲ ἀνὴρ
φρόνιμος

28 ἀνοήτῳ ἐπερωτήσαντι σοφίαν σοφία λογισθήσεται ἐνεὸν δέ τις ἑαυτὸν
ποιήσας δόξει φρόνιμος εἶναι

[Vul](../vul/pro017)

1 \[Melior est buccella sicca cum gaudio  
quam domus plena victimis cum jurgio.  

2 Servus sapiens dominabitur filiis stultis,  
et inter fratres hæreditatem dividet.  

3 Sicut igne probatur argentum et aurum camino,  
ita corda probat Dominus.  

4 Malus obedit linguæ iniquæ,  
et fallax obtemperat labiis mendacibus.  

5 Qui despicit pauperem exprobrat factori ejus,  
et qui ruina lætatur alterius non erit impunitus.  

6 Corona senum filii filiorum,  
et gloria filiorum patres eorum.  

7 Non decent stultum verba composita,  
nec principem labium mentiens.  

8 Gemma gratissima exspectatio præstolantis;  
quocumque se vertit, prudenter intelligit.  

9 Qui celat delictum quærit amicitias;  
qui altero sermone repetit, separat fœderatos.  

10 Plus proficit correptio apud prudentem,  
quam centum plagæ apud stultum.  

11 Semper jurgia quærit malus:  
angelus autem crudelis mittetur contra eum.  

12 Expedit magis ursæ occurrere raptis fœtibus,  
quam fatuo confidenti in stultitia sua.  

13 Qui reddit mala pro bonis,  
non recedet malum de domo ejus.  

14 Qui dimittit aquam caput est jurgiorum,  
et antequam patiatur contumeliam judicium deserit.\]  

15 \[Qui justificat impium, et qui condemnat justum,  
abominabilis est uterque apud Deum.  

16 Quid prodest stulto habere divitias,  
cum sapientiam emere non possit?  
Qui altum facit domum suam quærit ruinam,  
et qui evitat discere incidet in mala.  

17 Omni tempore diligit qui amicus est,  
et frater in angustiis comprobatur.  

18 Stultus homo plaudet manibus,  
cum spoponderit pro amico suo.  

19 Qui meditatur discordias diligit rixas,  
et qui exaltat ostium quærit ruinam.  

20 Qui perversi cordis est non inveniet bonum,  
et qui vertit linguam incidet in malum.  

21 Natus est stultus in ignominiam suam;  
sed nec pater in fatuo lætabitur.  

22 Animus gaudens ætatem floridam facit;  
spiritus tristis exsiccat ossa.  

23 Munera de sinu impius accipit,  
ut pervertat semitas judicii.  

24 In facie prudentis lucet sapientia;  
oculi stultorum in finibus terræ.  

25 Ira patris filius stultus,  
et dolor matris quæ genuit eum.  

26 Non est bonum damnum inferre justo,  
nec percutere principem qui recta judicat.  

27 Qui moderatur sermones suos doctus et prudens est,  
et pretiosi spiritus vir eruditus.  

28 Stultus quoque, si tacuerit, sapiens reputabitur,  
et si compresserit labia sua, intelligens.\]
