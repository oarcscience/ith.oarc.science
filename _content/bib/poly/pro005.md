### Proverbs 5

[KJV](../kjv/pro005)

1  My son,<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> attend<sup>[7181](h7181)</sup> unto
my wisdom,<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup> and bow<sup>[5186](h5186)</sup> thine
ear<sup>[241](h0241)</sup> to my understanding:<sup>[8394](h8394)</sup>

2  That thou mayest regard<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup>
discretion,<sup>[4209](h4209)</sup> and that thy
lips<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> may keep<sup>[5341](h5341)</sup>
knowledge.<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup>

3  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> the lips<sup>[8193](h8193)</sup> of a
strange woman<sup>[2114](h2114)</sup> drop<sup>[5197](h5197)</sup> as a
honeycomb,<sup>[5317](h5317)</sup> and her mouth<sup>[2441](h2441)</sup>
is smoother<sup>[2509](h2509)</sup> than oil:<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[8081](h8081)</sup>

4  But her end<sup>[319](h0319)</sup> is bitter<sup>[4751](h4751)</sup>
as wormwood,<sup>[3939](h3939)</sup> sharp<sup>[2299](h2299)</sup> as a
twoedged<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> sword.<sup>[2719](h2719)</sup>

5  Her feet<sup>[7272](h7272)</sup> go down<sup>[3381](h3381)</sup> to
death;<sup>[4194](h4194)</sup> her steps<sup>[6806](h6806)</sup> take
hold<sup>[8551](h8551)</sup> on hell.<sup>[7585](h7585)</sup>

6  Lest<sup>[6435](h6435)</sup> thou shouldest
ponder<sup>[6424](h6424)</sup> the path<sup>[734](h0734)</sup> of
life,<sup>[2416](h2416)</sup> her ways<sup>[4570](h4570)</sup> are
movable,<sup>[5128](h5128)</sup> that thou canst
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> know<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> them.

7  Hear<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> me now<sup>[6258](h6258)</sup>
therefore, O ye children,<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> and
depart<sup>[5493](h5493)</sup> not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> from the
words<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[561](h0561)</sup> of my
mouth.<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup>

8  Remove thy way far<sup>[7368](h7368)</sup> <sup>[1870](h1870)</sup>
from<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> her, and come not
nigh<sup>[7126](h7126)</sup> <sup>[408](h0408)</sup>
<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> the door<sup>[6607](h6607)</sup> of her
house:<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup>

9  Lest<sup>[6435](h6435)</sup> thou give<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> thine
honor<sup>[1935](h1935)</sup> unto others,<sup>[312](h0312)</sup> and
thy years<sup>[8141](h8141)</sup> unto the cruel:<sup>[394](h0394)</sup>

10  Lest<sup>[6435](h6435)</sup> strangers<sup>[2114](h2114)</sup> be
filled<sup>[7646](h7646)</sup> with thy wealth;<sup>[3581](h3581)</sup>
and thy labors<sup>[6089](h6089)</sup> be in the
house<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> of a stranger;<sup>[5237](h5237)</sup>

11  And thou mourn<sup>[5098](h5098)</sup> at the
last,<sup>[319](h0319)</sup> when thy flesh<sup>[1320](h1320)</sup> and
thy body<sup>[7607](h7607)</sup> are consumed,<sup>[3615](h3615)</sup>

12  And say,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> How<sup>[349](h0349)</sup> have I
hated<sup>[8130](h8130)</sup> instruction,<sup>[4148](h4148)</sup> and
my heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> despised<sup>[5006](h5006)</sup>
reproof;<sup>[8433](h8433)</sup>

13  And have not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> obeyed<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup>
the voice<sup>[6963](h6963)</sup> of my
teachers,<sup>[3384](h3384)</sup> nor<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
inclined<sup>[5186](h5186)</sup> mine ear<sup>[241](h0241)</sup> to them
that instructed<sup>[3925](h3925)</sup> me!

14  I was<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> almost<sup>[4592](h4592)</sup> in
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup> in the
midst<sup>[8432](h8432)</sup> of the
congregation<sup>[6951](h6951)</sup> and
assembly.<sup>[5712](h5712)</sup>

15  Drink<sup>[8354](h8354)</sup> waters<sup>[4325](h4325)</sup> out of
thine own cistern,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[953](h0953)</sup> and
running waters<sup>[5140](h5140)</sup> out of<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[8432](h8432)</sup> thine own well.<sup>[875](h0875)</sup>

16  Let thy fountains<sup>[4599](h4599)</sup> be
dispersed<sup>[6327](h6327)</sup> abroad,<sup>[2351](h2351)</sup> and
rivers<sup>[6388](h6388)</sup> of waters<sup>[4325](h4325)</sup> in the
streets.<sup>[7339](h7339)</sup>

17  Let them be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> only<sup>[905](h0905)</sup>
thine own, and not<sup>[369](h0369)</sup>
strangers'<sup>[2114](h2114)</sup> with<sup>[854](h0854)</sup> thee.

18  Let thy fountain<sup>[4726](h4726)</sup> be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup>
blessed:<sup>[1288](h1288)</sup> and rejoice<sup>[8055](h8055)</sup>
with the wife<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[802](h0802)</sup> of thy
youth.<sup>[5271](h5271)</sup>

19  Let her be as the loving<sup>[158](h0158)</sup>
hind<sup>[365](h0365)</sup> and pleasant<sup>[2580](h2580)</sup>
roe;<sup>[3280](h3280)</sup> let her breasts<sup>[1717](h1717)</sup>
satisfy<sup>[7301](h7301)</sup> thee at all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup>
times;<sup>[6256](h6256)</sup> and be thou
ravished<sup>[7686](h7686)</sup> always<sup>[8548](h8548)</sup> with her
love.<sup>[160](h0160)</sup>

20  And why<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> wilt thou, my
son,<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> be ravished<sup>[7686](h7686)</sup> with a
strange woman,<sup>[2114](h2114)</sup> and
embrace<sup>[2263](h2263)</sup> the bosom<sup>[2436](h2436)</sup> of a
stranger?<sup>[5237](h5237)</sup>

21  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> the ways<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of
man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> are before<sup>[5227](h5227)</sup> the
eyes<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> of the LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> and he
pondereth<sup>[6424](h6424)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> his
goings.<sup>[4570](h4570)</sup>

22  His own iniquities<sup>[5771](h5771)</sup> shall
take<sup>[3920](h3920)</sup> <sup>[853](h0853)</sup> the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> himself, and he shall be
holden<sup>[8551](h8551)</sup> with the cords<sup>[2256](h2256)</sup> of
his sins.<sup>[2403](h2403)</sup>

23  He<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> shall die<sup>[4191](h4191)</sup>
without<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> instruction;<sup>[4148](h4148)</sup> and
in the greatness<sup>[7230](h7230)</sup> of his
folly<sup>[200](h0200)</sup> he shall go astray.<sup>[7686](h7686)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/pro005)

1 υἱέ ἐμῇ σοφίᾳ πρόσεχε ἐμοῖς δὲ λόγοις παράβαλλε σὸν οὖς

2 ἵνα φυλάξῃς ἔννοιαν ἀγαθήν αἴσθησιν δὲ ἐμῶν χειλέων ἐντέλλομαί σοι

3 μὴ πρόσεχε φαύλῃ γυναικί μέλι γὰρ ἀποστάζει ἀπὸ χειλέων γυναικὸς
πόρνης ἣ πρὸς καιρὸν λιπαίνει σὸν φάρυγγα

4 ὕστερον μέντοι πικρότερον χολῆς εὑρήσεις καὶ ἠκονημένον μᾶλλον
μαχαίρας διστόμου

5 τῆς γὰρ ἀφροσύνης οἱ πόδες κατάγουσιν τοὺς χρωμένους αὐτῇ μετὰ θανάτου
εἰς τὸν ᾅδην τὰ δὲ ἴχνη αὐτῆς οὐκ ἐρείδεται

6 ὁδοὺς γὰρ ζωῆς οὐκ ἐπέρχεται σφαλεραὶ δὲ αἱ τροχιαὶ αὐτῆς καὶ οὐκ
εὔγνωστοι

7 νῦν οὖν υἱέ ἄκουέ μου καὶ μὴ ἀκύρους ποιήσῃς ἐμοὺς λόγους

8 μακρὰν ποίησον ἀπ᾽ αὐτῆς σὴν ὁδόν μὴ ἐγγίσῃς πρὸς θύραις οἴκων αὐτῆς

9 ἵνα μὴ πρόῃ ἄλλοις ζωήν σου καὶ σὸν βίον ἀνελεήμοσιν

10 ἵνα μὴ πλησθῶσιν ἀλλότριοι σῆς ἰσχύος οἱ δὲ σοὶ πόνοι εἰς οἴκους
ἀλλοτρίων εἰσέλθωσιν

11 καὶ μεταμεληθήσῃ ἐπ᾽ ἐσχάτων ἡνίκα ἂν κατατριβῶσιν σάρκες σώματός σου

12 καὶ ἐρεῖς πῶς ἐμίσησα παιδείαν καὶ ἐλέγχους ἐξέκλινεν ἡ καρδία μου

13 οὐκ ἤκουον φωνὴν παιδεύοντός με καὶ διδάσκοντός με οὐδὲ παρέβαλλον τὸ
οὖς μου

14 παρ᾽ ὀλίγον ἐγενόμην ἐν παντὶ κακῷ ἐν μέσῳ ἐκκλησίας καὶ συναγωγῆς

15 πῖνε ὕδατα ἀπὸ σῶν ἀγγείων καὶ ἀπὸ σῶν φρεάτων πηγῆς

16 μὴ ὑπερεκχείσθω σοι τὰ ὕδατα ἐκ τῆς σῆς πηγῆς εἰς δὲ σὰς πλατείας
διαπορευέσθω τὰ σὰ ὕδατα

17 ἔστω σοι μόνῳ ὑπάρχοντα καὶ μηδεὶς ἀλλότριος μετασχέτω σοι

18 ἡ πηγή σου τοῦ ὕδατος ἔστω σοι ἰδία καὶ συνευφραίνου μετὰ γυναικὸς
τῆς ἐκ νεότητός σου

19 ἔλαφος φιλίας καὶ πῶλος σῶν χαρίτων ὁμιλείτω σοι ἡ δὲ ἰδία ἡγείσθω
σου καὶ συνέστω σοι ἐν παντὶ καιρῷ ἐν γὰρ τῇ ταύτης φιλίᾳ
συμπεριφερόμενος πολλοστὸς ἔσῃ

20 μὴ πολὺς ἴσθι πρὸς ἀλλοτρίαν μηδὲ συνέχου ἀγκάλαις τῆς μὴ ἰδίας

21 ἐνώπιον γάρ εἰσιν τῶν τοῦ θεοῦ ὀφθαλμῶν ὁδοὶ ἀνδρός εἰς δὲ πάσας τὰς
τροχιὰς αὐτοῦ σκοπεύει

22 παρανομίαι ἄνδρα ἀγρεύουσιν σειραῖς δὲ τῶν ἑαυτοῦ ἁμαρτιῶν ἕκαστος
σφίγγεται

23 οὗτος τελευτᾷ μετὰ ἀπαιδεύτων ἐκ δὲ πλήθους τῆς ἑαυτοῦ βιότητος
ἐξερρίφη καὶ ἀπώλετο δι᾽ ἀφροσύνην

[Vul](../vul/pro005)

1 \[Fili mi, attende ad sapientiam meam,  
et prudentiæ meæ inclina aurem tuam:  

2 ut custodias cogitationes, et disciplinam labia tua conservent.  
Ne attendas fallaciæ mulieris;  

3 favus enim distillans labia meretricis,  
et nitidius oleo guttur ejus:  

4 novissima autem illius amara quasi absinthium,  
et acuta quasi gladius biceps.  

5 Pedes ejus descendunt in mortem,  
et ad inferos gressus illius penetrant.  

6 Per semitam vitæ non ambulant;  
vagi sunt gressus ejus et investigabiles.  

7 Nunc ergo fili mi, audi me,  
et ne recedas a verbis oris mei.  

8 Longe fac ab ea viam tuam,  
et ne appropinques foribus domus ejus.  

9 Ne des alienis honorem tuum,  
et annos tuos crudeli:  

10 ne forte implentur extranei viribus tuis,  
et labores tui sint in domo aliena,  

11 et gemas in novissimis,  
quando consumpseris carnes tuas et corpus tuum,  
et dicas:

12 Cur detestatus sum disciplinam,  
et increpationibus non acquievit cor meum,  

13 nec audivi vocem docentium me,  
et magistris non inclinavi aurem meam?  

14 pene fui in omni malo,  
in medio ecclesiæ et synagogæ.  

15 Bibe aquam de cisterna tua,  
et fluenta putei tui;  

16 deriventur fontes tui foras,  
et in plateis aquas tuas divide.  

17 Habeto eas solus,  
nec sint alieni participes tui.  

18 Sit vena tua benedicta,  
et lætare cum muliere adolescentiæ tuæ.  

19 Cerva carissima, et gratissimus hinnulus:  
ubera ejus inebrient te in omni tempore;  
in amore ejus delectare jugiter.  

20 Quare seduceris, fili mi, ab aliena,  
et foveris in sinu alterius?  

21 Respicit Dominus vias hominis,  
et omnes gressus ejus considerat.  

22 Iniquitates suas capiunt impium,  
et funibus peccatorum suorum constringitur.  

23 Ipse morietur, quia non habuit disciplinam,  
et in multitudine stultitiæ suæ decipietur.\]
