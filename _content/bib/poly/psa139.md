### Psalms 139

[KJV](../kjv/psa139)

1  To the chief Musician,<sup>[5329](h5329)</sup> A
Psalm<sup>[4210](h4210)</sup> of David.<sup>[1732](h1732)</sup> O
LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> thou hast searched<sup>[2713](h2713)</sup>
me, and known<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> me.

2  Thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> knowest<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> my
downsitting<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup> and mine
uprising,<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> thou
understandest<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> my thought<sup>[7454](h7454)</sup>
afar off.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7350](h7350)</sup>

3  Thou compassest<sup>[2219](h2219)</sup> my
path<sup>[734](h0734)</sup> and my lying down,<sup>[7252](h7252)</sup>
and art acquainted<sup>[5532](h5532)</sup> with
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> my ways.<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup>

4  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> there is not<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> a
word<sup>[4405](h4405)</sup> in my tongue,<sup>[3956](h3956)</sup> but,
lo,<sup>[2005](h2005)</sup> O LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> thou
knowest<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> it altogether.<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup>

5  Thou hast beset<sup>[6696](h6696)</sup> me
behind<sup>[268](h0268)</sup> and before,<sup>[6924](h6924)</sup> and
laid<sup>[7896](h7896)</sup> thine hand<sup>[3709](h3709)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> me.

6  Such knowledge<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup> is too
wonderful<sup>[6383](h6383)</sup> for<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> me; it is
high,<sup>[7682](h7682)</sup> I cannot<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
<sup>[3201](h3201)</sup> attain unto it.

7  Whither<sup>[575](h0575)</sup> shall I go<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup>
from thy spirit?<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[7307](h7307)</sup> or
whither<sup>[575](h0575)</sup> shall I flee<sup>[1272](h1272)</sup> from
thy presence?<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6440](h6440)</sup>

8  If<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> I ascend up<sup>[5266](h5266)</sup> into
heaven,<sup>[8064](h8064)</sup> thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> art
there:<sup>[8033](h8033)</sup> if I make my bed<sup>[3331](h3331)</sup>
in hell,<sup>[7585](h7585)</sup> behold,<sup>[2009](h2009)</sup> thou
art there.

9  If I take<sup>[5375](h5375)</sup> the wings<sup>[3671](h3671)</sup>
of the morning,<sup>[7837](h7837)</sup> and
dwell<sup>[7931](h7931)</sup> in the uttermost
parts<sup>[319](h0319)</sup> of the sea;<sup>[3220](h3220)</sup>

10  Even<sup>[1571](h1571)</sup> there<sup>[8033](h8033)</sup> shall thy
hand<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> lead<sup>[5148](h5148)</sup> me, and thy
right hand<sup>[3225](h3225)</sup> shall hold<sup>[270](h0270)</sup> me.

11  If I say,<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> Surely<sup>[389](h0389)</sup> the
darkness<sup>[2822](h2822)</sup> shall cover<sup>[7779](h7779)</sup> me;
even the night<sup>[3915](h3915)</sup> shall be
light<sup>[216](h0216)</sup> about<sup>[1157](h1157)</sup> me.

12  Yea,<sup>[1571](h1571)</sup> the darkness<sup>[2822](h2822)</sup>
hideth<sup>[2821](h2821)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
from<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> thee; but the night<sup>[3915](h3915)</sup>
shineth<sup>[215](h0215)</sup> as the day:<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> the
darkness<sup>[2825](h2825)</sup> and the light<sup>[219](h0219)</sup>
are both alike to thee.

13  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> hast
possessed<sup>[7069](h7069)</sup> my reins:<sup>[3629](h3629)</sup> thou
hast covered<sup>[5526](h5526)</sup> me in my
mother's<sup>[517](h0517)</sup> womb.<sup>[990](h0990)</sup>

14  I will praise<sup>[3034](h3034)</sup> thee;
for<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> <sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> I am
fearfully<sup>[3372](h3372)</sup> and
wonderfully<sup>[6395](h6395)</sup> made:
marvelous<sup>[6381](h6381)</sup> are thy works;<sup>[4639](h4639)</sup>
and that my soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> knoweth<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup>
right well.<sup>[3966](h3966)</sup>

15  My substance<sup>[6108](h6108)</sup> was not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>
hid<sup>[3582](h3582)</sup> from<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> thee,
when<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> I was made<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> in
secret,<sup>[5643](h5643)</sup> and curiously
wrought<sup>[7551](h7551)</sup> in the lowest
parts<sup>[8482](h8482)</sup> of the earth.<sup>[776](h0776)</sup>

16  Thine eyes<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> did see<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup>
my substance, yet being unperfect;<sup>[1564](h1564)</sup> and
in<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> thy book<sup>[5612](h5612)</sup>
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> my members were
written,<sup>[3789](h3789)</sup> which in
continuance<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> were
fashioned,<sup>[3335](h3335)</sup> when as yet there was
none<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> <sup>[259](h0259)</sup> of them.

17  How<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> precious<sup>[3365](h3365)</sup> also
are thy thoughts<sup>[7454](h7454)</sup> unto me, O
God!<sup>[410](h0410)</sup> how<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup>
great<sup>[6105](h6105)</sup> is the sum<sup>[7218](h7218)</sup> of
them!

18  If I should count<sup>[5608](h5608)</sup> them, they are more in
number<sup>[7235](h7235)</sup> than the sand:<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[2344](h2344)</sup> when I awake,<sup>[6974](h6974)</sup> I am
still<sup>[5750](h5750)</sup> with thee.<sup>[5973](h5973)</sup>

19  Surely<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> thou wilt slay<sup>[6991](h6991)</sup>
the wicked,<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> O God:<sup>[433](h0433)</sup>
depart<sup>[5493](h5493)</sup> from<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> me
therefore, ye bloody<sup>[1818](h1818)</sup> men.<sup>[376](h0376)</sup>

20  For<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> they speak<sup>[559](h0559)</sup> against
thee wickedly,<sup>[4209](h4209)</sup> and thine
enemies<sup>[6145](h6145)</sup> take<sup>[5375](h5375)</sup> thy name in
vain.<sup>[7723](h7723)</sup>

21  Do not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> I hate<sup>[8130](h8130)</sup> them,
O LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> that hate<sup>[8130](h8130)</sup> thee?
and am not I grieved<sup>[6962](h6962)</sup> with those that rise up
against<sup>[8618](h8618)</sup> thee?

22  I hate<sup>[8130](h8130)</sup> them with
perfect<sup>[8503](h8503)</sup> hatred:<sup>[8135](h8135)</sup> I
count<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> them mine enemies.<sup>[341](h0341)</sup>

23  Search<sup>[2713](h2713)</sup> me, O God,<sup>[410](h0410)</sup> and
know<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> my heart:<sup>[3824](h3824)</sup>
try<sup>[974](h0974)</sup> me, and know<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup> my
thoughts:<sup>[8312](h8312)</sup>

24  And see<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> if<sup>[518](h0518)</sup> there be
any wicked<sup>[6090](h6090)</sup> way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> in me,
and lead<sup>[5148](h5148)</sup> me in the way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup>
everlasting.<sup>[5769](h5769)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/psa139)

1 εἰς τὸ τέλος ψαλμὸς τῷ Δαυιδ

2 ἐξελοῦ με κύριε ἐξ ἀνθρώπου πονηροῦ ἀπὸ ἀνδρὸς ἀδίκου ῥῦσαί με

3 οἵτινες ἐλογίσαντο ἀδικίας ἐν καρδίᾳ ὅλην τὴν ἡμέραν παρετάσσοντο
πολέμους

4 ἠκόνησαν γλῶσσαν αὐτῶν ὡσεὶ ὄφεως ἰὸς ἀσπίδων ὑπὸ τὰ χείλη αὐτῶν
διάψαλμα

5 φύλαξόν με κύριε ἐκ χειρὸς ἁμαρτωλοῦ ἀπὸ ἀνθρώπων ἀδίκων ἐξελοῦ με
οἵτινες ἐλογίσαντο ὑποσκελίσαι τὰ διαβήματά μου

6 ἔκρυψαν ὑπερήφανοι παγίδα μοι καὶ σχοινία διέτειναν παγίδας τοῖς ποσίν
μου ἐχόμενα τρίβου σκάνδαλον ἔθεντό μοι διάψαλμα

7 εἶπα τῷ κυρίῳ θεός μου εἶ σύ ἐνώτισαι κύριε τὴν φωνὴν τῆς δεήσεώς μου

8 κύριε κύριε δύναμις τῆς σωτηρίας μου ἐπεσκίασας ἐπὶ τὴν κεφαλήν μου ἐν
ἡμέρᾳ πολέμου

9 μὴ παραδῷς με κύριε ἀπὸ τῆς ἐπιθυμίας μου ἁμαρτωλῷ διελογίσαντο κατ᾽
ἐμοῦ μὴ ἐγκαταλίπῃς με μήποτε ὑψωθῶσιν διάψαλμα

10 ἡ κεφαλὴ τοῦ κυκλώματος αὐτῶν κόπος τῶν χειλέων αὐτῶν καλύψει αὐτούς

11 πεσοῦνται ἐπ᾽ αὐτοὺς ἄνθρακες ἐν πυρὶ καταβαλεῖς αὐτούς ἐν
ταλαιπωρίαις οὐ μὴ ὑποστῶσιν

12 ἀνὴρ γλωσσώδης οὐ κατευθυνθήσεται ἐπὶ τῆς γῆς ἄνδρα ἄδικον κακὰ
θηρεύσει εἰς διαφθοράν

13 ἔγνων ὅτι ποιήσει κύριος τὴν κρίσιν τοῦ πτωχοῦ καὶ τὴν δίκην τῶν
πενήτων

14 πλὴν δίκαιοι ἐξομολογήσονται τῷ ὀνόματί σου καὶ κατοικήσουσιν εὐθεῖς
σὺν τῷ προσώπῳ σου

[Vul](../vul/psa139)

1 In finem. Psalmus David.

2 \[Eripe me, Domine, ab homine malo;  
a viro iniquo eripe me.  

3 Qui cogitaverunt iniquitates in corde,  
tota die constituebant prælia.  

4 Acuerunt linguas suas sicut serpentis;  
venenum aspidum sub labiis eorum.  

5 Custodi me, Domine, de manu peccatoris,  
et ab hominibus iniquis eripe me.  
Qui cogitaverunt supplantare gressus meos:  

6 absconderunt superbi laqueum mihi.  
Et funes extenderunt in laqueum;  
juxta iter, scandalum posuerunt mihi.  

7 Dixi Domino: Deus meus es tu;  
exaudi, Domine, vocem deprecationis meæ.  

8 Domine, Domine, virtus salutis meæ,  
obumbrasti super caput meum in die belli.  

9 Ne tradas me, Domine, a desiderio meo peccatori:  
cogitaverunt contra me;  
ne derelinquas me, ne forte exaltentur.  

10 Caput circuitus eorum:  
labor labiorum ipsorum operiet eos.  

11 Cadent super eos carbones;  
in ignem dejicies eos:  
in miseriis non subsistent.  

12 Vir linguosus non dirigetur in terra;  
virum injustum mala capient in interitu.  

13 Cognovi quia faciet Dominus judicium inopis,  
et vindictam pauperum.  

14 Verumtamen justi confitebuntur nomini tuo,  
et habitabunt recti cum vultu tuo.\]
