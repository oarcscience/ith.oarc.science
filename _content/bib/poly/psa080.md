### Psalms 80

[KJV](../kjv/psa080)

1  To the chief Musician<sup>[5329](h5329)</sup>
upon<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> Shoshannim-eduth,<sup>[7802](h7802)</sup> A
Psalm<sup>[4210](h4210)</sup> of Asaph.<sup>[623](h0623)</sup> Give
ear,<sup>[238](h0238)</sup> O Shepherd<sup>[7462](h7462)</sup> of
Israel,<sup>[3478](h3478)</sup> thou that
leadest<sup>[5090](h5090)</sup> Joseph<sup>[3130](h3130)</sup> like a
flock;<sup>[6629](h6629)</sup> thou that
dwellest<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup> between the
cherubims,<sup>[3742](h3742)</sup> shine forth.<sup>[3313](h3313)</sup>

2  Before<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> Ephraim<sup>[669](h0669)</sup> and
Benjamin<sup>[1144](h1144)</sup> and Manasseh<sup>[4519](h4519)</sup>
stir up<sup>[5782](h5782)</sup> <sup>[853](h0853)</sup> thy
strength,<sup>[1369](h1369)</sup> and come<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> and
save<sup>[3444](h3444)</sup> us.

3  Turn us again,<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> O God,<sup>[430](h0430)</sup>
and cause thy face<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> to
shine;<sup>[215](h0215)</sup> and we shall be
saved.<sup>[3467](h3467)</sup>

4  O LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> God<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> of
hosts,<sup>[6635](h6635)</sup> how long<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup>
<sup>[4970](h4970)</sup> wilt thou be angry<sup>[6225](h6225)</sup>
against the prayer<sup>[8605](h8605)</sup> of thy
people?<sup>[5971](h5971)</sup>

5  Thou feedest<sup>[398](h0398)</sup> them with the
bread<sup>[3899](h3899)</sup> of tears;<sup>[1832](h1832)</sup> and
givest them tears<sup>[1832](h1832)</sup> to
drink<sup>[8248](h8248)</sup> in great measure.<sup>[7991](h7991)</sup>

6  Thou makest<sup>[7760](h7760)</sup> us a
strife<sup>[4066](h4066)</sup> unto our
neighbors:<sup>[7934](h7934)</sup> and our
enemies<sup>[341](h0341)</sup> laugh<sup>[3932](h3932)</sup> among
themselves.

7  Turn us again,<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> O God<sup>[430](h0430)</sup>
of hosts,<sup>[6635](h6635)</sup> and cause thy
face<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> to shine;<sup>[215](h0215)</sup> and we
shall be saved.<sup>[3467](h3467)</sup>

8  Thou hast brought<sup>[5265](h5265)</sup> a
vine<sup>[1612](h1612)</sup> out of Egypt:<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[4714](h4714)</sup> thou hast cast out<sup>[1644](h1644)</sup> the
heathen,<sup>[1471](h1471)</sup> and planted<sup>[5193](h5193)</sup> it.

9  Thou preparedst<sup>[6437](h6437)</sup> room
before<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> it, and didst cause it to take deep
root,<sup>[8327](h8327)</sup> <sup>[8328](h8328)</sup> and it
filled<sup>[4390](h4390)</sup> the land.<sup>[776](h0776)</sup>

10  The hills<sup>[2022](h2022)</sup> were
covered<sup>[3680](h3680)</sup> with the shadow<sup>[6738](h6738)</sup>
of it, and the boughs<sup>[6057](h6057)</sup> thereof were like the
goodly<sup>[410](h0410)</sup> cedars.<sup>[730](h0730)</sup>

11  She sent out<sup>[7971](h7971)</sup> her
boughs<sup>[7105](h7105)</sup> unto<sup>[5704](h5704)</sup> the
sea,<sup>[3220](h3220)</sup> and her branches<sup>[3127](h3127)</sup>
unto<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> the river.<sup>[5104](h5104)</sup>

12  Why<sup>[4100](h4100)</sup> hast thou then broken
down<sup>[6555](h6555)</sup> her hedges,<sup>[1447](h1447)</sup> so that
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> they which pass by<sup>[5674](h5674)</sup>
the way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> do pluck<sup>[717](h0717)</sup> her?

13  The boar<sup>[2386](h2386)</sup> out of the
wood<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[3293](h3293)</sup> doth
waste<sup>[3765](h3765)</sup> it, and the wild
beast<sup>[2123](h2123)</sup> of the field<sup>[7704](h7704)</sup> doth
devour<sup>[7462](h7462)</sup> it.

14  Return,<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> we beseech<sup>[4994](h4994)</sup>
thee, O God<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> of hosts:<sup>[6635](h6635)</sup>
look down<sup>[5027](h5027)</sup> from heaven,<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[8064](h8064)</sup> and behold,<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> and
visit<sup>[6485](h6485)</sup> this<sup>[2063](h2063)</sup>
vine;<sup>[1612](h1612)</sup>

15  And the vineyard<sup>[3657](h3657)</sup>
which<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> thy right hand<sup>[3225](h3225)</sup> hath
planted,<sup>[5193](h5193)</sup> and the branch<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup>
that thou madest strong<sup>[553](h0553)</sup> for thyself.

16  It is burned<sup>[8313](h8313)</sup> with
fire,<sup>[784](h0784)</sup> it is cut down:<sup>[3683](h3683)</sup>
they perish<sup>[6](h0006)</sup> at the rebuke<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[1606](h1606)</sup> of thy countenance.<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup>

17  Let thy hand<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup>
upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> of thy right
hand,<sup>[3225](h3225)</sup> upon<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> the
son<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> of man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> whom thou
madest strong<sup>[553](h0553)</sup> for thyself.

18  So will not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> we go
back<sup>[5472](h5472)</sup> from<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> thee:
quicken<sup>[2421](h2421)</sup> us, and we will
call<sup>[7121](h7121)</sup> upon thy name.<sup>[8034](h8034)</sup>

19  Turn us again,<sup>[7725](h7725)</sup> O
LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> God<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> of
hosts,<sup>[6635](h6635)</sup> cause thy face<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> to
shine;<sup>[215](h0215)</sup> and we shall be
saved.<sup>[3467](h3467)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/psa080)

1 εἰς τὸ τέλος ὑπὲρ τῶν ληνῶν τῷ Ασαφ ψαλμός

2 ἀγαλλιᾶσθε τῷ θεῷ τῷ βοηθῷ ἡμῶν ἀλαλάξατε τῷ θεῷ Ιακωβ

3 λάβετε ψαλμὸν καὶ δότε τύμπανον ψαλτήριον τερπνὸν μετὰ κιθάρας

4 σαλπίσατε ἐν νεομηνίᾳ σάλπιγγι ἐν εὐσήμῳ ἡμέρᾳ ἑορτῆς ἡμῶν

5 ὅτι πρόσταγμα τῷ Ισραηλ ἐστὶν καὶ κρίμα τῷ θεῷ Ιακωβ

6 μαρτύριον ἐν τῷ Ιωσηφ ἔθετο αὐτὸν ἐν τῷ ἐξελθεῖν αὐτὸν ἐκ γῆς Αἰγύπτου
γλῶσσαν ἣν οὐκ ἔγνω ἤκουσεν

7 ἀπέστησεν ἀπὸ ἄρσεων τὸν νῶτον αὐτοῦ αἱ χεῖρες αὐτοῦ ἐν τῷ κοφίνῳ
ἐδούλευσαν

8 ἐν θλίψει ἐπεκαλέσω με καὶ ἐρρυσάμην σε ἐπήκουσά σου ἐν ἀποκρύφῳ
καταιγίδος ἐδοκίμασά σε ἐπὶ ὕδατος ἀντιλογίας διάψαλμα

9 ἄκουσον λαός μου καὶ διαμαρτύρομαί σοι Ισραηλ ἐὰν ἀκούσῃς μου

10 οὐκ ἔσται ἐν σοὶ θεὸς πρόσφατος οὐδὲ προσκυνήσεις θεῷ ἀλλοτρίῳ

11 ἐγὼ γάρ εἰμι κύριος ὁ θεός σου ὁ ἀναγαγών σε ἐκ γῆς Αἰγύπτου πλάτυνον
τὸ στόμα σου καὶ πληρώσω αὐτό

12 καὶ οὐκ ἤκουσεν ὁ λαός μου τῆς φωνῆς μου καὶ Ισραηλ οὐ προσέσχεν μοι

13 καὶ ἐξαπέστειλα αὐτοὺς κατὰ τὰ ἐπιτηδεύματα τῶν καρδιῶν αὐτῶν
πορεύσονται ἐν τοῖς ἐπιτηδεύμασιν αὐτῶν

14 εἰ ὁ λαός μου ἤκουσέν μου Ισραηλ ταῖς ὁδοῖς μου εἰ ἐπορεύθη

15 ἐν τῷ μηδενὶ ἂν τοὺς ἐχθροὺς αὐτῶν ἐταπείνωσα καὶ ἐπὶ τοὺς θλίβοντας
αὐτοὺς ἐπέβαλον τὴν χεῖρά μου

16 οἱ ἐχθροὶ κυρίου ἐψεύσαντο αὐτῷ καὶ ἔσται ὁ καιρὸς αὐτῶν εἰς τὸν
αἰῶνα

17 καὶ ἐψώμισεν αὐτοὺς ἐκ στέατος πυροῦ καὶ ἐκ πέτρας μέλι ἐχόρτασεν
αὐτούς

[Vul](../vul/psa080)

1 In finem, pro torcularibus. Psalmus ipsi Asaph.

2 \[Exsultate Deo adjutori nostro;  
jubilate Deo Jacob.  

3 Sumite psalmum, et date tympanum;  
psalterium jucundum cum cithara.  

4 Buccinate in neomenia tuba,  
in insigni die solemnitatis vestræ:  

5 quia præceptum in Israël est,  
et judicium Deo Jacob.  

6 Testimonium in Joseph posuit illud,  
cum exiret de terra Ægypti;  
linguam quam non noverat, audivit.  

7 Divertit ab oneribus dorsum ejus;  
manus ejus in cophino servierunt.  

8 In tribulatione invocasti me, et liberavi te.  
Exaudivi te in abscondito tempestatis;  
probavi te apud aquam contradictionis.  

9 Audi, populus meus, et contestabor te.  
Israël, si audieris me,  

10 non erit in te deus recens,  
neque adorabis deum alienum.  

11 Ego enim sum Dominus Deus tuus,  
qui eduxi te de terra Ægypti.  
Dilata os tuum, et implebo illud.  

12 Et non audivit populus meus vocem meam,  
et Israël non intendit mihi.  

13 Et dimisi eos secundum desideria cordis eorum;  
ibunt in adinventionibus suis.  

14 Si populus meus audisset me,  
Israël si in viis meis ambulasset,  

15 pro nihilo forsitan inimicos eorum humiliassem,  
et super tribulantes eos misissem manum meam.  

16 Inimici Domini mentiti sunt ei,  
et erit tempus eorum in sæcula.  

17 Et cibavit eos ex adipe frumenti,  
et de petra melle saturavit eos.\]
