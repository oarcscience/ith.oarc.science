G4623
σιωπάω
siōpaō
see-o-pah‘-o
From σιωπη siōpē (silence, that is, a hush; properly muteness, that is, involuntary stillness, or inability ot speak; and thus differing from 4602 which is rather a voluntary refusal or indisposition to speak, although the terms are often used synonymously); to be dumb (but not deaf also, like 2974 properly); figuratively to be calm (as quiet water): - dumb, (hold) peace.
