### Proverbs 21

[KJV](../kjv/pro021)

1  The king's<sup>[4428](h4428)</sup> heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> is
in the hand<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup> of the LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>
as the rivers<sup>[6388](h6388)</sup> of water:<sup>[4325](h4325)</sup>

whithersoever<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> <sup>[3605](h3605)</sup>
<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> he will.<sup>[2654](h2654)</sup>

2  Every<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of a

eyes:<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> but the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>
pondereth<sup>[8505](h8505)</sup> the hearts.<sup>[3826](h3826)</sup>

3  To do<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> justice<sup>[6666](h6666)</sup> and
judgment<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup> is more
acceptable<sup>[977](h0977)</sup> to the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>
than sacrifice.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[2077](h2077)</sup>

4  A high<sup>[7312](h7312)</sup> look,<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup> and a

plowing<sup>[5215](h5215)</sup> of the wicked,<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup>
is sin.<sup>[2403](h2403)</sup>

5  The thoughts<sup>[4284](h4284)</sup> of the
diligent<sup>[2742](h2742)</sup> tend only<sup>[389](h0389)</sup> to

7  The robbery<sup>[7701](h7701)</sup> of the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> shall destroy<sup>[1641](h1641)</sup>
them; because<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> they
refuse<sup>[3985](h3985)</sup> to do<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup>
judgment.<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup>

8  The way<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> is

as for the pure,<sup>[2134](h2134)</sup> his
work<sup>[6467](h6467)</sup> is right.<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup>

9  It is better<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> to dwell<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup>
in<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> a corner<sup>[6438](h6438)</sup> of the

brawling<sup>[4079](h4079)</sup> woman<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[802](h0802)</sup> in a wide<sup>[2267](h2267)</sup>
house.<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup>

10  The soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> of the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> desireth<sup>[183](h0183)</sup>

findeth no<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> favor<sup>[2603](h2603)</sup> in his
eyes.<sup>[5869](h5869)</sup>

11  When the scorner<sup>[3887](h3887)</sup> is
punished,<sup>[6064](h6064)</sup> the simple<sup>[6612](h6612)</sup> is

receiveth<sup>[3947](h3947)</sup> knowledge.<sup>[1847](h1847)</sup>

12  The righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> man wisely
considereth<sup>[7919](h7919)</sup> the house<sup>[1004](h1004)</sup> of
the wicked:<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> but God
overthroweth<sup>[5557](h5557)</sup> the wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup>
for their wickedness.<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup>

13  Whoso stoppeth<sup>[331](h0331)</sup> his
ears<sup>[241](h0241)</sup> at the cry<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[2201](h2201)</sup> of the poor,<sup>[1800](h1800)</sup> he

himself,<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup> but shall not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>

14  A gift<sup>[4976](h4976)</sup> in secret<sup>[5643](h5643)</sup>
pacifieth<sup>[3711](h3711)</sup> anger:<sup>[639](h0639)</sup> and a
reward<sup>[7810](h7810)</sup> in the bosom<sup>[2436](h2436)</sup>
strong<sup>[5794](h5794)</sup> wrath.<sup>[2534](h2534)</sup>

15  It is joy<sup>[8057](h8057)</sup> to the
just<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> to do<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup>
judgment:<sup>[4941](h4941)</sup> but
destruction<sup>[4288](h4288)</sup> shall be to the
workers<sup>[6466](h6466)</sup> of iniquity.<sup>[205](h0205)</sup>

16  The man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup> that
wandereth<sup>[8582](h8582)</sup> out of the way<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup> of understanding<sup>[7919](h7919)</sup> shall
remain<sup>[5117](h5117)</sup> in the
congregation<sup>[6951](h6951)</sup> of the
dead.<sup>[7496](h7496)</sup>

17  He that loveth<sup>[157](h0157)</sup>
pleasure<sup>[8057](h8057)</sup> shall be a poor<sup>[4270](h4270)</sup>
man:<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> he that loveth<sup>[157](h0157)</sup>
wine<sup>[3196](h3196)</sup> and oil<sup>[8081](h8081)</sup> shall
not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> be rich.<sup>[6238](h6238)</sup>

18  The wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> shall be a
ransom<sup>[3724](h3724)</sup> for the
righteous,<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> and the
transgressor<sup>[898](h0898)</sup> for<sup>[8478](h8478)</sup> the
upright.<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup>

19  It is better<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> to
dwell<sup>[3427](h3427)</sup> in the wilderness,<sup>[776](h0776)</sup>
<sup>[4057](h4057)</sup> than with a contentious<sup>[4079](h4079)</sup>
and an angry<sup>[3708](h3708)</sup> woman.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[802](h0802)</sup>

20  There is treasure<sup>[214](h0214)</sup> to be
desired<sup>[2530](h2530)</sup> and oil<sup>[8081](h8081)</sup> in the
dwelling<sup>[5116](h5116)</sup> of the wise;<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup>
but a foolish<sup>[3684](h3684)</sup> man<sup>[120](h0120)</sup>
spendeth it up.<sup>[1104](h1104)</sup>

21  He that followeth<sup>[7291](h7291)</sup> after
righteousness<sup>[6666](h6666)</sup> and mercy<sup>[2617](h2617)</sup>
findeth<sup>[4672](h4672)</sup> life,<sup>[2416](h2416)</sup>
righteousness,<sup>[6666](h6666)</sup> and
honor.<sup>[3519](h3519)</sup>

22  A wise<sup>[2450](h2450)</sup> man scaleth<sup>[5927](h5927)</sup>
the city<sup>[5892](h5892)</sup> of the mighty,<sup>[1368](h1368)</sup>
and casteth down<sup>[3381](h3381)</sup> the
strength<sup>[5797](h5797)</sup> of the
confidence<sup>[4009](h4009)</sup> thereof.

23  Whoso keepeth<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> his
mouth<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> and his tongue<sup>[3956](h3956)</sup>
keepeth<sup>[8104](h8104)</sup> his soul<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup> from
troubles.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6869](h6869)</sup>

24  Proud<sup>[2086](h2086)</sup> and haughty<sup>[3093](h3093)</sup>
scorner<sup>[3887](h3887)</sup> is his name,<sup>[8034](h8034)</sup> who
dealeth<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> in proud<sup>[2087](h2087)</sup>
wrath.<sup>[5678](h5678)</sup>

25  The desire<sup>[8378](h8378)</sup> of the
slothful<sup>[6102](h6102)</sup> killeth<sup>[4191](h4191)</sup> him;
for<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> his hands<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup>
refuse<sup>[3985](h3985)</sup> to labor.<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup>

26  He coveteth<sup>[183](h0183)</sup> greedily<sup>[8378](h8378)</sup>
all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup> the day<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> long: but
the righteous<sup>[6662](h6662)</sup> giveth<sup>[5414](h5414)</sup> and
spareth<sup>[2820](h2820)</sup> not.<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup>

27  The sacrifice<sup>[2077](h2077)</sup> of the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> is abomination:<sup>[8441](h8441)</sup>
how much more,<sup>[637](h0637)</sup> <sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> when he
bringeth<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> it with a wicked
mind?<sup>[2154](h2154)</sup>

28  A false<sup>[3577](h3577)</sup> witness<sup>[5707](h5707)</sup>
shall perish:<sup>[6](h0006)</sup> but the man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup>
that heareth<sup>[8085](h8085)</sup> speaketh<sup>[1696](h1696)</sup>
constantly.<sup>[5331](h5331)</sup>

29  A wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> man<sup>[376](h0376)</sup>
hardeneth<sup>[5810](h5810)</sup> his face:<sup>[6440](h6440)</sup> but
as for the upright,<sup>[3477](h3477)</sup> he<sup>[1931](h1931)</sup>
directeth<sup>[995](h0995)</sup> his way.<sup>[1870](h1870)</sup>

30  There is no<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> wisdom<sup>[2451](h2451)</sup>
nor<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> understanding<sup>[8394](h8394)</sup>
nor<sup>[369](h0369)</sup> counsel<sup>[6098](h6098)</sup>
against<sup>[5048](h5048)</sup> the LORD.<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>

31  The horse<sup>[5483](h5483)</sup> is
prepared<sup>[3559](h3559)</sup> against the day<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup>
of battle:<sup>[4421](h4421)</sup> but safety<sup>[8668](h8668)</sup> is
of the LORD.<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup>

[Sep](../sep/pro021)

1 ὥσπερ ὁρμὴ ὕδατος οὕτως καρδία βασιλέως ἐν χειρὶ θεοῦ οὗ ἐὰν θέλων
νεύσῃ ἐκεῖ ἔκλινεν αὐτήν

2 πᾶς ἀνὴρ φαίνεται ἑαυτῷ δίκαιος κατευθύνει δὲ καρδίας κύριος

3 ποιεῖν δίκαια καὶ ἀληθεύειν ἀρεστὰ παρὰ θεῷ μᾶλλον ἢ θυσιῶν αἷμα

4 μεγαλόφρων ἐφ᾽ ὕβρει θρασυκάρδιος λαμπτὴρ δὲ ἀσεβῶν ἁμαρτία

6 ὁ ἐνεργῶν θησαυρίσματα γλώσσῃ ψευδεῖ μάταια διώκει ἐπὶ παγίδας θανάτου

7 ὄλεθρος ἀσεβέσιν ἐπιξενωθήσεται οὐ γὰρ βούλονται πράσσειν τὰ δίκαια

8 πρὸς τοὺς σκολιοὺς σκολιὰς ὁδοὺς ἀποστέλλει ὁ θεός ἁγνὰ γὰρ καὶ ὀρθὰ
τὰ ἔργα αὐτοῦ

9 κρεῖσσον οἰκεῖν ἐπὶ γωνίας ὑπαίθρου ἢ ἐν κεκονιαμένοις μετὰ ἀδικίας
καὶ ἐν οἴκῳ κοινῷ

10 ψυχὴ ἀσεβοῦς οὐκ ἐλεηθήσεται ὑπ᾽ οὐδενὸς τῶν ἀνθρώπων

11 ζημιουμένου ἀκολάστου πανουργότερος γίνεται ὁ ἄκακος συνίων δὲ σοφὸς
δέξεται γνῶσιν

12 συνίει δίκαιος καρδίας ἀσεβῶν καὶ φαυλίζει ἀσεβεῖς ἐν κακοῖς

13 ὃς φράσσει τὰ ὦτα τοῦ μὴ ἐπακοῦσαι ἀσθενοῦς καὶ αὐτὸς ἐπικαλέσεται
καὶ οὐκ ἔσται ὁ εἰσακούων

14 δόσις λάθριος ἀνατρέπει ὀργάς δώρων δὲ ὁ φειδόμενος θυμὸν ἐγείρει
ἰσχυρόν

15 εὐφροσύνη δικαίων ποιεῖν κρίμα ὅσιος δὲ ἀκάθαρτος παρὰ κακούργοις

16 ἀνὴρ πλανώμενος ἐξ ὁδοῦ δικαιοσύνης ἐν συναγωγῇ γιγάντων ἀναπαύσεται

17 ἀνὴρ ἐνδεὴς ἀγαπᾷ εὐφροσύνην φιλῶν οἶνον καὶ ἔλαιον εἰς πλοῦτον

18 περικάθαρμα δὲ δικαίου ἄνομος

19 κρεῖσσον οἰκεῖν ἐν γῇ ἐρήμῳ ἢ μετὰ γυναικὸς μαχίμου καὶ γλωσσώδους
καὶ ὀργίλου

20 θησαυρὸς ἐπιθυμητὸς ἀναπαύσεται ἐπὶ στόματος σοφοῦ ἄφρονες δὲ ἄνδρες
καταπίονται αὐτόν

21 ὁδὸς δικαιοσύνης καὶ ἐλεημοσύνης εὑρήσει ζωὴν καὶ δόξαν

22 πόλεις ὀχυρὰς ἐπέβη σοφὸς καὶ καθεῖλεν τὸ ὀχύρωμα ἐφ᾽ ᾧ ἐπεποίθεισαν
οἱ ἀσεβεῖς

23 ὃς φυλάσσει τὸ στόμα αὐτοῦ καὶ τὴν γλῶσσαν διατηρεῖ ἐκ θλίψεως τὴν
ψυχὴν αὐτοῦ

24 θρασὺς καὶ αὐθάδης καὶ ἀλαζὼν λοιμὸς καλεῖται ὃς δὲ μνησικακεῖ
παράνομος

25 ἐπιθυμίαι ὀκνηρὸν ἀποκτείνουσιν οὐ γὰρ προαιροῦνται αἱ χεῖρες αὐτοῦ
ποιεῖν τι

26 ἀσεβὴς ἐπιθυμεῖ ὅλην τὴν ἡμέραν ἐπιθυμίας κακάς ὁ δὲ δίκαιος ἐλεᾷ καὶ
οἰκτίρει ἀφειδῶς

27 θυσίαι ἀσεβῶν βδέλυγμα κυρίῳ καὶ γὰρ παρανόμως προσφέρουσιν αὐτάς

28 μάρτυς ψευδὴς ἀπολεῖται ἀνὴρ δὲ ὑπήκοος φυλασσόμενος λαλήσει

29 ἀσεβὴς ἀνὴρ ἀναιδῶς ὑφίσταται προσώπῳ ὁ δὲ εὐθὴς αὐτὸς συνίει τὰς
ὁδοὺς αὐτοῦ

30 οὐκ ἔστιν σοφία οὐκ ἔστιν ἀνδρεία οὐκ ἔστιν βουλὴ πρὸς τὸν ἀσεβῆ

31 ἵππος ἑτοιμάζεται εἰς ἡμέραν πολέμου παρὰ δὲ κυρίου ἡ βοήθεια

[Vul](../vul/pro021)

1 \[Sicut divisiones aquarum, ita cor regis in manu Domini:  
quocumque voluerit, inclinabit illud.  

2 Omnis via viri recta sibi videtur:  
appendit autem corda Dominus.  

3 Facere misericordiam et judicium  
magis placet Domino quam victimæ.  

4 Exaltatio oculorum est dilatatio cordis;  
lucerna impiorum peccatum.  

5 Cogitationes robusti semper in abundantia;  
omnis autem piger semper in egestate est.\]  

6 \[Qui congregat thesauros lingua mendacii vanus et excors est,  
et impingetur ad laqueos mortis.  

7 Rapinæ impiorum detrahent eos,  
quia noluerunt facere judicium.  

8 Perversa via viri aliena est;  
qui autem mundus est, rectum opus ejus.  

9 Melius est sedere in angulo domatis,  
quam cum muliere litigiosa, et in domo communi.  

10 Anima impii desiderat malum:  
non miserebitur proximo suo.  

11 Mulctato pestilente, sapientior erit parvulus,  
et si sectetur sapientem, sumet scientiam.  

12 Excogitat justus de domo impii,  
ut detrahat impios a malo.\]  

13 \[Qui obturat aurem suam ad clamorem pauperis,  
et ipse clamabit, et non exaudietur.  

14 Munus absconditum extinguit iras,  
et donum in sinu indignationem maximam.  

15 Gaudium justo est facere judicium,  
et pavor operantibus iniquitatem.  

16 Vir qui erraverit a via doctrinæ  
in cœtu gigantum commorabitur.  

17 Qui diligit epulas in egestate erit;  
qui amat vinum et pinguia non ditabitur.  

18 Pro justo datur impius,  
et pro rectis iniquus.  

19 Melius est habitare in terra deserta  
quam cum muliere rixosa et iracunda.  

20 Thesaurus desiderabilis, et oleum in habitaculo justi:  
et imprudens homo dissipabit illud.  

21 Qui sequitur justitiam et misericordiam  
inveniet vitam, justitiam, et gloriam.  

22 Civitatem fortium ascendit sapiens,  
et destruxit robur fiduciæ ejus.  

23 Qui custodit os suum et linguam suam  
custodit ab angustiis animam suam.  

24 Superbus et arrogans vocatur indoctus,  
qui in ira operatur superbiam.  

25 Desideria occidunt pigrum:  
noluerunt enim quidquam manus ejus operari.  

26 Tota die concupiscit et desiderat;  
qui autem justus est, tribuet, et non cessabit.  

27 Hostiæ impiorum abominabiles,  
quia offeruntur ex scelere.  

28 Testis mendax peribit;  
vir obediens loquetur victoriam.  

29 Vir impius procaciter obfirmat vultum suum;  
qui autem rectus est corrigit viam suam.  

30 Non est sapientia, non est prudentia,  
non est consilium contra Dominum.  

31 Equus paratur ad diem belli;  
Dominus autem salutem tribuit.\]
