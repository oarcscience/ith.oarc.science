### Psalms 109

[KJV](../kjv/psa109)

1  To the chief Musician,<sup>[5329](h5329)</sup> A
Psalm<sup>[4210](h4210)</sup> of David.<sup>[1732](h1732)</sup>
Hold<sup>[2790](h2790)</sup> not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> thy peace, O
God<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> of my praise;<sup>[8416](h8416)</sup>

2  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> the mouth<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> of the
wicked<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> and the mouth<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> of

against<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> me: they have
spoken<sup>[1696](h1696)</sup> against<sup>[854](h0854)</sup> me with a
lying<sup>[8267](h8267)</sup> tongue.<sup>[3956](h3956)</sup>

3  They compassed me about<sup>[5437](h5437)</sup> also with
words<sup>[1697](h1697)</sup> of hatred;<sup>[8135](h8135)</sup> and
fought against<sup>[3898](h3898)</sup> me without a
cause.<sup>[2600](h2600)</sup>

4  For<sup>[8478](h8478)</sup> my love<sup>[160](h0160)</sup> they are
my adversaries:<sup>[7853](h7853)</sup> but I<sup>[589](h0589)</sup>
give myself unto prayer.<sup>[8605](h8605)</sup>

5  And they have rewarded<sup>[7760](h7760)</sup>
<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> me evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup>
for<sup>[8478](h8478)</sup> good,<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> and
hatred<sup>[8135](h8135)</sup> for<sup>[8478](h8478)</sup> my
love.<sup>[160](h0160)</sup>

6  Set<sup>[6485](h6485)</sup> thou a wicked man<sup>[7563](h7563)</sup>

stand<sup>[5975](h5975)</sup> at<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> his right

7  When he shall be judged,<sup>[8199](h8199)</sup> let him be
condemned:<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup> <sup>[7563](h7563)</sup> and
let<sup>[3318](h3318)</sup> his prayer<sup>[8605](h8605)</sup>
become<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> sin.<sup>[2401](h2401)</sup>

8  Let his days<sup>[3117](h3117)</sup> be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup>
few;<sup>[4592](h4592)</sup> and let another<sup>[312](h0312)</sup>
take<sup>[3947](h3947)</sup> his office.<sup>[6486](h6486)</sup>

9  Let his children<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup>
fatherless,<sup>[3490](h3490)</sup> and his wife<sup>[802](h0802)</sup>
a widow.<sup>[490](h0490)</sup>

10  Let his children<sup>[1121](h1121)</sup> be continually
vagabonds,<sup>[5128](h5128)</sup> <sup>[5128](h5128)</sup> and
beg:<sup>[7592](h7592)</sup> let them seek<sup>[1875](h1875)</sup> their
bread also out of their desolate places.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[2723](h2723)</sup>

11  Let the extortioner<sup>[5383](h5383)</sup>
catch<sup>[5367](h5367)</sup> all<sup>[3605](h3605)</sup>
that<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> he hath; and let the
strangers<sup>[2114](h2114)</sup> spoil<sup>[962](h0962)</sup> his
labor.<sup>[3018](h3018)</sup>

12  Let there be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> none<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> to
extend<sup>[4900](h4900)</sup> mercy<sup>[2617](h2617)</sup> unto him:
neither<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> let there be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> any
to favor<sup>[2603](h2603)</sup> his fatherless
children.<sup>[3490](h3490)</sup>

13  Let his posterity<sup>[319](h0319)</sup> be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup>
cut off;<sup>[3772](h3772)</sup> and in the
generation<sup>[1755](h1755)</sup> following<sup>[312](h0312)</sup> let
their name<sup>[8034](h8034)</sup> be blotted
out.<sup>[4229](h4229)</sup>

14  Let the iniquity<sup>[5771](h5771)</sup> of his
fathers<sup>[1](h0001)</sup> be remembered<sup>[2142](h2142)</sup>
with<sup>[413](h0413)</sup> the LORD;<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> and let
not<sup>[408](h0408)</sup> the sin<sup>[2403](h2403)</sup> of his
mother<sup>[517](h0517)</sup> be blotted out.<sup>[4229](h4229)</sup>

15  Let them be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> before<sup>[5048](h5048)</sup>
the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> continually,<sup>[8548](h8548)</sup>
that he may cut off<sup>[3772](h3772)</sup> the
memory<sup>[2143](h2143)</sup> of them from the
earth.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[776](h0776)</sup>

16  Because<sup>[3282](h3282)</sup> that<sup>[834](h0834)</sup> he
remembered<sup>[2142](h2142)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> to
show<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> mercy,<sup>[2617](h2617)</sup> but
persecuted<sup>[7291](h7291)</sup> the poor<sup>[6041](h6041)</sup> and
needy<sup>[34](h0034)</sup> man,<sup>[376](h0376)</sup> that he might
even slay<sup>[4191](h4191)</sup> the broken<sup>[3512](h3512)</sup> in
heart.<sup>[3824](h3824)</sup>

17  As he loved<sup>[157](h0157)</sup> cursing,<sup>[7045](h7045)</sup>
so let it come<sup>[935](h0935)</sup> unto him: as he
delighted<sup>[2654](h2654)</sup> not<sup>[3808](h3808)</sup> in
blessing,<sup>[1293](h1293)</sup> so let it be
far<sup>[7368](h7368)</sup> from<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> him.

18  As he clothed<sup>[3847](h3847)</sup> himself with
cursing<sup>[7045](h7045)</sup> like as with his
garment,<sup>[4055](h4055)</sup> so let it come<sup>[935](h0935)</sup>
into his bowels<sup>[7130](h7130)</sup> like
water,<sup>[4325](h4325)</sup> and like oil<sup>[8081](h8081)</sup> into
his bones.<sup>[6106](h6106)</sup>

19  Let it be<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> unto him as the
garment<sup>[899](h0899)</sup> which covereth<sup>[5844](h5844)</sup>
him, and for a girdle<sup>[4206](h4206)</sup> wherewith he is
girded<sup>[2296](h2296)</sup> continually.<sup>[8548](h8548)</sup>

20  Let this<sup>[2063](h2063)</sup> be the
reward<sup>[6468](h6468)</sup> of mine
adversaries<sup>[7853](h7853)</sup> from<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup>
<sup>[854](h0854)</sup> the LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> and of them
that speak<sup>[1696](h1696)</sup> evil<sup>[7451](h7451)</sup>
against<sup>[5921](h5921)</sup> my soul.<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup>

21  But do<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> thou<sup>[859](h0859)</sup>
for<sup>[854](h0854)</sup> me, O GOD<sup>[3069](h3069)</sup> the
Lord,<sup>[136](h0136)</sup> for thy name's
sake:<sup>[4616](h4616)</sup> <sup>[8034](h8034)</sup>
because<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> thy mercy<sup>[2617](h2617)</sup> is
good,<sup>[2896](h2896)</sup> deliver<sup>[5337](h5337)</sup> thou me.

22  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> I<sup>[595](h0595)</sup> am
poor<sup>[6041](h6041)</sup> and needy,<sup>[34](h0034)</sup> and my
heart<sup>[3820](h3820)</sup> is wounded<sup>[2490](h2490)</sup>
within<sup>[7130](h7130)</sup> me.

23  I am gone<sup>[1980](h1980)</sup> like the
shadow<sup>[6738](h6738)</sup> when it
declineth:<sup>[5186](h5186)</sup> I am tossed up and
down<sup>[5287](h5287)</sup> as the locust.<sup>[697](h0697)</sup>

24  My knees<sup>[1290](h1290)</sup> are weak<sup>[3782](h3782)</sup>
through fasting;<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[6685](h6685)</sup> and my
flesh<sup>[1320](h1320)</sup> faileth<sup>[3584](h3584)</sup> of
fatness.<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[8081](h8081)</sup>

25  I<sup>[589](h0589)</sup> became<sup>[1961](h1961)</sup> also a
reproach<sup>[2781](h2781)</sup> unto them: when they looked
upon<sup>[7200](h7200)</sup> me they shaked<sup>[5128](h5128)</sup>
their heads.<sup>[7218](h7218)</sup>

26  Help<sup>[5826](h5826)</sup> me, O LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> my
God:<sup>[430](h0430)</sup> O save<sup>[3467](h3467)</sup> me according
to thy mercy:<sup>[2617](h2617)</sup>

27  That<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> they may know<sup>[3045](h3045)</sup>
that this<sup>[2063](h2063)</sup> is thy hand;<sup>[3027](h3027)</sup>
that thou,<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> LORD,<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> hast
done<sup>[6213](h6213)</sup> it.

28  Let them<sup>[1992](h1992)</sup> curse,<sup>[7043](h7043)</sup> but
bless<sup>[1288](h1288)</sup> thou:<sup>[859](h0859)</sup> when they
arise,<sup>[6965](h6965)</sup> let them be
ashamed;<sup>[954](h0954)</sup> but let thy
servant<sup>[5650](h5650)</sup> rejoice.<sup>[8055](h8055)</sup>

29  Let mine adversaries<sup>[7853](h7853)</sup> be
clothed<sup>[3847](h3847)</sup> with shame,<sup>[3639](h3639)</sup> and
let them cover<sup>[5844](h5844)</sup> themselves with their own
confusion,<sup>[1322](h1322)</sup> as with a
mantle.<sup>[4598](h4598)</sup>

30  I will greatly<sup>[3966](h3966)</sup>
praise<sup>[3034](h3034)</sup> the LORD<sup>[3068](h3068)</sup> with my
mouth;<sup>[6310](h6310)</sup> yea, I will
praise<sup>[1984](h1984)</sup> him among<sup>[8432](h8432)</sup> the
multitude.<sup>[7227](h7227)</sup>

31  For<sup>[3588](h3588)</sup> he shall stand<sup>[5975](h5975)</sup>
at the right hand<sup>[3225](h3225)</sup> of the
poor,<sup>[34](h0034)</sup> to save<sup>[3467](h3467)</sup> him from
those that condemn<sup>[4480](h4480)</sup> <sup>[8199](h8199)</sup> his
soul.<sup>[5315](h5315)</sup>

1 τῷ Δαυιδ ψαλμός εἶπεν ὁ κύριος τῷ κυρίῳ μου κάθου ἐκ δεξιῶν μου ἕως ἂν
θῶ τοὺς ἐχθρούς σου ὑποπόδιον τῶν ποδῶν σου

2 ῥάβδον δυνάμεώς σου ἐξαποστελεῖ κύριος ἐκ Σιων καὶ κατακυρίευε ἐν μέσῳ
τῶν ἐχθρῶν σου

3 μετὰ σοῦ ἡ ἀρχὴ ἐν ἡμέρᾳ τῆς δυνάμεώς σου ἐν ταῖς λαμπρότησιν τῶν
ἁγίων ἐκ γαστρὸς πρὸ ἑωσφόρου ἐξεγέννησά σε

4 ὤμοσεν κύριος καὶ οὐ μεταμεληθήσεται σὺ εἶ ἱερεὺς εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα κατὰ
τὴν τάξιν Μελχισεδεκ

5 κύριος ἐκ δεξιῶν σου συνέθλασεν ἐν ἡμέρᾳ ὀργῆς αὐτοῦ βασιλεῖς

6 κρινεῖ ἐν τοῖς ἔθνεσιν πληρώσει πτώματα συνθλάσει κεφαλὰς ἐπὶ γῆς
πολλῶν

7 ἐκ χειμάρρου ἐν ὁδῷ πίεται διὰ τοῦτο ὑψώσει κεφαλήν
