### King James Version: Jeremiah Chapter 45

<span id="001">1</span> The word that [Jeremiah](../ebd/ebd203.htm#006)
the [prophet](../ebd/ebd300.htm#008) spake unto
[Baruch](../ebd/ebd046.htm#001) the son of Neriah, when he had written
these words in a book at the mouth of [Jeremiah](../ebd/ebd203.htm#006),
in the fourth year of [Jehoiakim](../ebd/ebd201.htm#001) the son of
[Josiah](../ebd/ebd211.htm#008) [king](../ebd/ebd219.htm#001) of
[Judah](../ebd/ebd212.htm#007), saying,  

<span id="002">2</span> Thus saith the LORD, the God of
[Israel](../ebd/ebd191.htm#000), unto thee, O
[Baruch](../ebd/ebd046.htm#001):  

<span id="003">3</span> Thou didst say, Woe is me now! for the LORD hath
added grief to my sorrow; I fainted in my sighing, and I find no rest.  

<span id="004">4</span> Thus shalt thou say unto him, The LORD saith
thus; Behold, that which I have built will I break down, and that which
I have planted I will pluck up, even this whole land.  

<span id="005">5</span> And seekest thou great things for thyself? seek
them not: for, behold, I will bring evil upon all
[flesh](../ebd/ebd135.htm#007), saith the LORD: but thy life will I give
unto thee for a prey in all places whither thou goest.
