# THE LAW AND THE WORD

### by Thomas Troward

#### \[1917\]

------------------------------------------------------------------------

|                                                                                                                           |
|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| <img src="https://rcm-images.amazon.com/images/G/01/rcm/120x240.gif" width="120" height="240" alt="Shop at Amazon.com" /> |

Thomas Troward was a leading proponent of the 'New Thought' movement, a
forerunner of what is now known as 'New Age' thinking. In this book he
attempts to elucidate what he calls the 'Promise' of the Bible: Eternal
Life, and of the Word made Real. He dresses up the text with analogies
and references to contemporary science and technology, such as the
telegraph, radioactivity, and 'flying machines.' This usage will be
familiar to anyone who has sat through the recent movie [What the Bleep
do We
Know?](https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B0006UEVQ8/internetsacredte),
which uses special-effects film vocabulary, quantum physics, and string
theory to similar effect.
