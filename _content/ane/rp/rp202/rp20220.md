### THE INSCRIPTIONS OF TELLOH (*Continued*)

<span class="smallcaps">Inscriptions of Ur-Bau</span>

No. 2.—<span class="smallcaps">On the Stone of a Threshold</span> <span
id="fr_201"></span>[1](#fn_201)

1\. <span class="smallcaps">For</span> the god <span
class="smallcaps">En-ki</span>,  
2. his king,  
3. Ur-Bau,  
4. the patesi  
5. Of <span class="smallcaps">Shirpurla</span>,  
6. the offspring begotten  
7. by the god Nin-âgal,  
8. his temple  
9. has constructed.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

No 3.—<span class="smallcaps">On large Bricks</span> <span
id="fr_202"></span>[2](#fn_202)

1\. For the god <span class="smallcaps">Nin-girsu</span>,  
2. the powerful warrior  
3. of the god <span class="smallcaps">Ellilla</span>,  
4. Ur-bau  
5. the patesi  
6. Of <span class="smallcaps">Shirpurla</span>  
7. his temple  
8. has constructed.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

No. 4.—<span class="smallcaps">On a Small Round Object of White
Stone</span>

1\. For the goddess <span class="smallcaps">Bau</span>  
2. the daughter of <span class="smallcaps">Anna</span>,

<span id="page_74">p. 74</span>

3\. for the life  
4. of Ur-bau  
5. the patesi  
6. Of <span class="smallcaps">Shirpurla</span>,  
7. Ur-Ellilla has brought this *da;*  
8. and for the life of the wife of his son  
9. he has consecrated it.
