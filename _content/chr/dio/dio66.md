<span id="iv.iv.i-p45"></span>10. Guitelin.

<span id="iv.iv.i-p46"></span>11. Tremorinus, died about 490, and was
succeeded by Dubritius of Llandaff, after which the Primacy seems to
have wavered between Llandaff and Menevia. Geoff. Hist. VIII. 10.

<span id="iv.iv.i-p47"></span>Dubritius consecrated in 449 (Benedict of
Gloster); in 490 (Geoffrey), Bishop of Llandaff, and became Metropolitan
on the death of Tremorinus, as stated' above, but his seat remained at
Llandaff. <span id="page_166">p. 166</span> <span
id="iv.iv.i-Page_166"></span>

<span id="iv.iv.i-p48"></span>St. David, 1st Bishop of Menevia, was
consecrated at Jerusalem, with two companions <span
id="fr_283"></span>[284](#fn_283) , a.d. 519, and succeeded as
Metropolitan on the death of Dubritius, but his seat remained at St.
David's.

<span id="iv.iv.i-p49"></span>After him came Teilo, consecrated at the
same time as St. David, at Jerusalem, A.D. 519, to Llandaff. He
succeeded to the Metropolitan's office on St. David's death, retaining
Llandaff, and consecrating Ismael to St. David's as a Suffragan Bishop.

<span id="iv.iv.i-p50"></span>Simon the Cananite, afterwards Bishop of
Jerusalem, having preached the Gospel in Britain ("Apostolic
Constitutions," Lagarde, p. 284); as also Aristobulus, ordained by St.
Paul "Bishop for Britain" (Migne, ser. Graeca, tome III.); there must
have been many Bishops in Britain before King Lucius was able to
supersede the Druid by the Christian organisation. "Within ten years
after the arrival of Joseph of Arimathea, the first-fruit of Britain was
sent to Rome, for instruction and consecration. He founded a Church in
Beatenberg, Switzerland.

<span id="iv.iv.i-p51"></span>For Bishops in France, see *Gallia
Christiana.*

<span id="iv.iv.i-p52"></span>For Bishops in Britain, see Archbishop
Parker, Alford, "St. Paul in Britain," Wakeman.
