#### <span id="iv.iv.iv.xxi-p0.1"></span>Chapter XXI.

<span id="iv.iv.iv.xxi-p1"></span>An objection on the power of free
will.

<span id="iv.iv.iv.xxi-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iv.iv.xxi-p2.1"></span>Germanus</span>: This passage very clearly
shows the freedom of the will, where it is said “If My people would have
hearkened unto Me,” and elsewhere “But My people would not hear My
voice.” <span id="fr_1282"></span>[1283](#fn_1282) For when He says “If
they would have heard” He shows that the decision to yield or not to
yield lay in their own power. How then is it true that our salvation
does not depend upon ourselves, if God Himself has given us the power
either to hearken or not to hearken?
