#### <span id="iv.iii.xi.xv-p0.1"></span>Chapter XV.

<span id="iv.iii.xi.xv-p1"></span>How vainglory intoxicates the mind.

<span id="iv.iii.xi.xv-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iii.xi.xv-p2.1"></span>And</span> so the miserable soul is
affected by such vanity—as if it were deluded by a profound slumber—that
it is often led away by the pleasure of such thoughts, and filled with
such imaginations, so that it cannot even look at things present, or the
brethren, while it enjoys dwelling upon these things, of which with its
wandering thoughts it has waking dreams, as if they were true.
