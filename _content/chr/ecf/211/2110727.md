#### <span id="iv.iv.viii.xviii-p0.1"></span>Chapter XVIII.

<span id="iv.iv.viii.xviii-p1"></span>A question whether among the
devils there is any order observed in the attack, or system in its
changes.

<span id="iv.iv.viii.xviii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iv.viii.xviii-p2.1"></span>Germanus</span>: Must we then believe
that wickedness is arranged and so to speak systematized among them in
such a way that there is some order in the changes observed by them, and
a regular plan of attack carried out, though it is clear that method and
system can only exist among good and upright men, as Scripture says:
“Thou shalt seek wisdom among the ungodly and shalt not find it; and:
“our enemies are senseless;” and this: “There is neither wisdom, nor
courage, nor counsel among the ungodly.” <span
id="fr_1476"></span>[1477](#fn_1476)
