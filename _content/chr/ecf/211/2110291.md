#### <span id="iv.iii.i.vi-p0.1"></span>Chapter VI.

<span id="iv.iii.i.vi-p1"></span>Of their Capes. <span
id="fr_655"></span>[656](#fn_655)

<span id="iv.iii.i.vi-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iii.i.vi-p3.1"></span>Next</span> they cover their necks and
shoulders with a narrow cape, aiming at modesty of dress as well as
cheapness and economy; and this is called in our language as well as
theirs *mafors*; and so they avoid both the expense and the display of
cloaks and great coats.
