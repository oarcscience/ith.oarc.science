#### <span id="iv.iii.xii.xii-p0.1"></span>Chapter XII.

<span id="iv.iii.xii.xii-p1"></span>That no toil is worthy to be
compared with the promised bliss.

<span id="iv.iii.xii.xii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iii.xii.xii-p2.1"></span>For</span> all the long years of this
present life disappear when you have regard to the eternity of the
future glory: and all our sorrows vanish away in the contemplation of
that vast bliss, and like smoke melt away, and come to nothing, and like
ashes are no more seen.
