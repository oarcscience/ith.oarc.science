#### <span id="iv.iv.ii.xix-p0.1"></span>Chapter XIX.

<span id="iv.iv.ii.xix-p1"></span>Of the three origins of our thoughts.

<span id="iv.iv.ii.xix-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iv.ii.xix-p2.1"></span>Above</span> all we ought at least to know
that there are three origins of our thoughts, i.e., from God, from the
devil, and from ourselves. They come from God when He vouchsafes to
visit us with the illumination of the Holy Ghost, lifting us up to a
higher state of progress, and where we have made but little progress, or
through acting slothfully have been overcome, He chastens us with most
salutary compunction, or when He discloses to us heavenly mysteries, or
turns our purpose and will to better actions, as in the case where the
king Ahasuerus, being chastened by the Lord, was prompted to ask for the
books of the annals, by which he was reminded of the good deeds of
Mordecai, and promoted him to a position of the highest honour and at
once recalled his most cruel sentence concerning the slaughter of the
Jews. <span id="fr_1131"></span>[1132](#fn_1131) Or when the prophet
says: “I will hearken what the Lord God will say in me.” <span
id="fr_1132"></span>[1133](#fn_1132) Another too tells us “And an angel
spoke, and said in me,” <span id="fr_1133"></span>[1134](#fn_1133) or
when the Son of God promised that He would come with His Father, and
make His abode in us, <span id="fr_1134"></span>[1135](#fn_1134) and “It
is not ye that speak, but the Spirit of your Father which speaketh in
you.” <span id="fr_1135"></span>[1136](#fn_1135) And the chosen vessel:
“Ye seek a proof of Christ that speaketh in me.” <span
id="fr_1136"></span>[1137](#fn_1136) But a whole range of thoughts
springs from the devil, when he endeavours to destroy us either by the
pleasures of sin or by secret attacks, in his crafty wiles deceitfully
showing us evil as good, and transforming himself into an angel of light
to us: <span id="fr_1137"></span>[1138](#fn_1137) as when the evangelist
tells us: “And when supper was ended, when the devil had already put it
into the heart of Judas Iscariot, Simon’s son, to betray” <span
id="fr_1138"></span>[1139](#fn_1138) the Lord: and again also “after the
sop,” he says, “Satan entered into him.” <span
id="fr_1139"></span>[1140](#fn_1139) Peter also says to Ananias: “Why
hath Satan tempted thine heart, to lie to the Holy Ghost?” <span
id="fr_1140"></span>[1141](#fn_1140) And that which we read in the
gospel much earlier as predicted by Ecclesiastes: “If the spirit of the
ruler rise up against thee, leave not thy place.” <span
id="fr_1141"></span>[1142](#fn_1141) That too which is said to God
against Ahab in the third book of Kings, in the character of an unclean
spirit: “I will go forth and will be a lying spirit in the mouth of all
his prophets.” <span id="fr_1142"></span>[1143](#fn_1142) But they arise
from ourselves, when in the course of nature we recollect what we are
doing or have done or have heard. Of which the blessed David speaks: “I
thought upon the ancient days, and had in mind the years from of old,
and I meditated, by night I exercised myself with my heart, and searched
out my spirit.” <span id="fr_1143"></span>[1144](#fn_1143) And again:
“the Lord knoweth the thoughts of man, that they are vain:” <span
id="fr_1144"></span>[1145](#fn_1144) and “the thoughts of the righteous
are judgments.” <span id="fr_1145"></span>[1146](#fn_1145) In the gospel
too the Lord says to the Pharisees: “why do ye think evil in your
hearts?” <span id="fr_1146"></span>[1147](#fn_1146)
