##### <span id="iv.ii.iii.xiv-p0.1"></span>Chapter XIV.—Of Loving Our Enemies.

<span id="iv.ii.iii.xiv-p1"></span>And that we should be kindly
disposed, not only towards those of our own stock, as some suppose,
Isaiah the prophet said: “Say to those that hate you, and that cast you
out, Ye are our brethren, that the name of the <span class="sc"><span
id="iv.ii.iii.xiv-p1.1"></span>Lord</span> may be glorified, and be
apparent in their joy.” <span id="fr_664"></span>[665](#fn_664) And the
Gospel says: “Love your enemies, and pray for them that despitefully use
you. For if ye love them who love you, what reward have ye? This do also
the robbers and the publicans.” <span id="fr_665"></span>[666](#fn_665)
And those that do good it teaches not to boast, lest they become
men-pleasers. For it says: “Let not your left hand know what your right
hand doeth.” <span id="fr_666"></span>[667](#fn_666) Moreover,
concerning subjection to authorities and powers, and prayer for them,
the divine word gives us instructions, in order that “we may lead a
quiet and peaceable life.” <span id="fr_667"></span>[668](#fn_667) And
it teaches us to render all things to all, <span
id="fr_668"></span>[669](#fn_668) “honour to whom honour, fear to whom
fear, tribute to whom tribute; to owe no man anything, but to love all.”
