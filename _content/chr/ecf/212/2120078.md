### Footnotes

<span id="fn_409"></span>[64:410](2120078.htm#fr_409) <span
id="ii.iv.lxviii-p3.1"></span>

<span id="ii.iv.lxviii-p4"></span> Valentinian III. had been nominally
Emperor of the West since 425, but his mother’s (Galla Placidia) death
this year compelled him to rule as well as have the name of ruler: 
almost simultaneously in the East the death of Theodisius II. brought to
the front his sister Pulcheria and her soldier husband Marcian.
