<span id="v.iii.cvi-p2"></span><span class="c12"><span
id="v.iii.cvi-p2.1"></span>Apollinarus</span>, <span
id="fr_2530"></span>[2531](#fn_2530) bishop of Laodicea, in Syria, the
son of a presbyter, applied himself in his youth to the diligent study
of grammar, and afterwards, writing innumerable volumes on the Holy
Scriptures, died in the reign of the Emperor Theodosius. There are
extant thirty books by him *Against Porphyry,* which are generally
considered as among the best of his works. <span
id="fr_2531"></span>[2532](#fn_2531)
