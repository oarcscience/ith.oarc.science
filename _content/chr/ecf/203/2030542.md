id="v.iv.xxiii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXII.</span>

<span id="v.iv.xxiii-p2"></span><span class="c12"><span
id="v.iv.xxiii-p2.1"></span>Niceas</span>, <span
id="fr_2609"></span>[2610](#fn_2609)  <span
id="fr_2610"></span>[2611](#fn_2610) bishop of the city of Romatia,
composed, in simple and clear language, six books of *Instruction for
neophites.* The first of these contains, How candidates who seek to
obtain grace of baptism ought to act, the second, On the errors of
relationship, in which he relates that not far from his own time a
certain Melodius, father of a family, on account of his liberality and
Garadius <span id="fr_2611"></span>[2612](#fn_2611) a peasant, on
account of his bravery, were placed, by the heathen, among the gods. A
third book *On faith in one sovereign,* a fourth *Against
genealogy*, <span id="fr_2612"></span>[2613](#fn_2612) a fifth *On the
creed,* a sixth *On the sacrifice of the paschal lamb.* He addressed a
work also *To the fallen virgin,* an incentive to amendment for all who
have fallen.
