<span id="v.iii.lxi-p1"></span> <span class="c12"><span
id="v.iii.lxi-p1.1"></span>Chapter LIX.</span>

<span id="v.iii.lxi-p2"></span><span class="c12"><span
id="v.iii.lxi-p2.1"></span>Gaius</span>, <span
id="fr_2471"></span>[2472](#fn_2471) bishop of Rome, in the time of
Zephyrinus, that is, in the reign of Antoninus, the son of Severus,
delivered a very notable disputation *Against Proculus,* the follower of
Montanus, convicting him of <span id="page_375">p. 375</span> <span
id="v.iii.lxi-Page_375"></span>temerity in his defence of the new
prophecy, and in the same volume also enumerating only thirteen epistles
of Paul, says that the fourteenth, which is now called, *To the
Hebrews,* is not by him, and is not considered among the Romans to the
present day as being by the apostle Paul.
