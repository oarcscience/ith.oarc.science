id="v.iii.cxv-p1.1"></span>Chapter CXIII.</span>

<span id="v.iii.cxv-p2"></span><span class="c12"><span
id="v.iii.cxv-p2.1"></span>Euzoius</span>, <span
id="fr_2545"></span>[2546](#fn_2545) as a young man, together with
Gregory, bishop of Nazianzan, was educated by Thespesius the rhetorician
at Cæsarea, and afterwards when bishop of the same city, with great
pains attempted to restore the library, collected by Origen and
Pamphilus, which had already suffered injury. At last, in the reign of
the Emperor Theodosian, he was expelled from the church. Many and
various treatises of his are in circulation, and one may easily become
acquainted with them.
