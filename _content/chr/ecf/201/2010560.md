<span id="iv.vi.iii.xli-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.iii.xli-p1.1"></span>Chapter XLI</span>.—*Of the Erection of
Churches in Bethlehem, and on the Mount of Olives.*

<span id="iv.vi.iii.xli-p2"></span><span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.iii.xli-p2.1"></span>In</span> the same country he discovered
other places, venerable as being the localities of two sacred caves: and
these also he adorned with lavish magnificence. In the one case, he
rendered due honor to that which had been the scene of the first
manifestation of our Saviour’s divine presence, when he submitted to be
born in mortal flesh; while in the case of the second cavern he hallowed
the remembrance of his ascension to heaven from the mountain top. And
while he thus nobly testified his reverence for these places, he at the
same time eternized the memory of his mother, <span
id="fr_3272"></span>[3273](#fn_3272) who had been the instrument of
conferring so valuable a benefit on mankind.
