<span id="iv.vi.ii.lxii-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.ii.lxii-p1.1"></span>Chapter LXII</span>.—*Concerning the Same
Arius, and the Melitians.* <span id="fr_3209"></span>[3210](#fn_3209)

<span id="iv.vi.ii.lxii-p3"></span><span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.ii.lxii-p3.1"></span>Some</span> thus at Alexandria maintained
an obstinate conflict on the highest questions. Others throughout Egypt
and the Upper Thebaid, were at variance on account of an earlier
controversy: so that the churches were everywhere distracted by
divisions. The body therefore being thus diseased, the whole of Libya
caught the contagion; and the rest of the remoter provinces became
affected with the same disorder. For the disputants at Alexandria sent
emissaries to the bishops of the several provinces, who accordingly
ranged themselves as partisans on either side, and shared in the same
spirit of discord.
