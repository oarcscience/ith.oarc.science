<span id="iv.vi.ii.vii-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.ii.vii-p1.1"></span>Chapter VII</span>.—*That Victory
everywhere followed the Presence of the Standard of the Cross in
Battle.*

<span id="iv.vi.ii.vii-p2"></span><span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.ii.vii-p2.1"></span>Indeed</span>, wherever this appeared, the
enemy soon fled before his victorious troops. And the emperor perceiving
this, whenever he saw any part of his forces hard pressed, gave orders
that the salutary trophy should be moved in that direction, like some
triumphant charm <span id="fr_3167"></span>[3168](#fn_3167) against
disasters: at which the combatants were divinely inspired, as it were,
with fresh strength and courage, and immediate victory was the result.
