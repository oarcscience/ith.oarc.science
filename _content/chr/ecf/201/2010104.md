<span id="iii.viii.xxxv-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="iii.viii.xxxv-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXXV</span>.—*Justus, the Third
Bishop of Jerusalem.*

<span id="iii.viii.xxxv-p2"></span>1. <span class="c13"><span
id="iii.viii.xxxv-p2.1"></span>But</span> when Symeon also had died in
the manner described, <span id="fr_903"></span>[904](#fn_903) a certain
Jew by the name of Justus <span id="fr_904"></span>[905](#fn_904)
succeeded to the episcopal throne in Jerusalem. He was one of the many
thousands of the circumcision who at that time believed in Christ.
