<span id="v.vi.xxii-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="v.vi.xxii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXII.—Origin of the Devil, in the
Criminal Excess of the Sorrow of Achamoth. The Devil, Called Also
Munditenens, Actually Wiser Than the Demiurge, Although His Work.</span>

<span id="v.vi.xxii-p2"></span>The odium felt amongst them <span
id="fr_6829"></span>[6830](#fn_6829) against the devil is the more
excusable, <span id="fr_6830"></span>[6831](#fn_6830) even because the
peculiarly sordid character of his origin justifies it. <span
id="fr_6831"></span>[6832](#fn_6831) For he is supposed by them to have
had his origin in that criminal excess <span
id="fr_6832"></span>[6833](#fn_6832) of her <span
id="fr_6833"></span>[6834](#fn_6833) sorrow, from which they also derive
the birth of the angels, and demons, and all the wicked spirits. Yet
they affirm that the devil is the work of the Demiurge, and they call
him Munditenens <span id="fr_6834"></span>[6835](#fn_6834) (*Ruler of
the World*), and maintain that, as he is of a spiritual nature, he has a
better knowledge of the things above than the Demiurge, an animal being.
He deserves from them the pre-eminence which all heresies provide him
with.
