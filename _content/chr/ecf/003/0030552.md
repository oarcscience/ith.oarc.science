<span id="v.vi.xxxvii-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="v.vi.xxxvii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXXVII.—Other Turgid and Ridiculous
Theories About the Origin of the Æons and Creation, Stated and
Condemned.</span>

<span id="v.vi.xxxvii-p2"></span>Now listen to some other
buffooneries <span id="fr_6923"></span>[6924](#fn_6923) of a master who
is a great swell among them, <span id="fr_6924"></span>[6925](#fn_6924)
and who has pronounced his *dicta* with an even priestly authority. They
run thus: There comes, says he, before all things Pro-arche, the
inconceivable, and indescribable, and nameless, which I for my own part
call Monotes (*Solitude*). With this was associated another power, to
which also I give the name of Henotes (*Unity*). Now, inasmuch as
Monotes and Henotes—that is to say, Solitude and Union—were only one
being, they produced, and yet not in the way of production, <span
id="fr_6925"></span>[6926](#fn_6925) the intellectual, innascible,
invisible beginning of all things, which human language <span
id="fr_6926"></span>[6927](#fn_6926) has called Monad
(*Solitude*). <span id="fr_6927"></span>[6928](#fn_6927) This has
inherent in itself a consubstantial force, which it calls Unity <span
id="fr_6928"></span>[6929](#fn_6928) These powers, accordingly, Solitude
or Solitariness, *and* Unity, or Union, propagated all the other
emanations of Æons. <span id="fr_6929"></span>[6930](#fn_6929) Wonderful
distinction, to be sure! Whatever change Union and Unity may undergo,
Solitariness and Solitude is profoundly supreme. Whatever designation
you give the power, it is one and the same.
