<span id="vi.iii.xvi-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iii.xvi-p1.1"></span>Chapter XVI.—Of the Second Baptism—With
Blood.</span>

<span id="vi.iii.xvi-p2"></span>We *have* indeed, likewise, a *second*
font, <span id="fr_8698"></span>[8699](#fn_8698) (itself withal *one
with the former*,) of *blood*, to wit; concerning which the Lord said,
“I have to be baptized with a baptism,” <span
id="fr_8699"></span>[8700](#fn_8699) when He had been baptized already.
For He had come “by means of water and blood,” <span
id="fr_8700"></span>[8701](#fn_8700) just as John has written; that He
might be baptized by the water, glorified by the blood; to make *us*, in
like manner, *called* by *water, chosen* <span
id="fr_8701"></span>[8702](#fn_8701) by *blood*. These two baptisms He
sent out from the wound in His pierced side, <span
id="fr_8702"></span>[8703](#fn_8702) in order that they who believed in
His blood might be bathed with the water; they who had been bathed in
the water might likewise drink the blood. <span
id="fr_8703"></span>[8704](#fn_8703) This is the baptism which both
stands in lieu of the fontal bathing <span
id="fr_8704"></span>[8705](#fn_8704) when that has not been received,
and restores it when lost.
