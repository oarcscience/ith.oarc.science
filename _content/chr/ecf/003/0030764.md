<span id="vi.iv.xx-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.xx-p1.1"></span>Chapter XX.—Of Women’s Dress.</span>

<span id="vi.iv.xx-p2"></span>So far, however, as regards the dress of
women, the variety of observance compels us—men of no consideration
whatever—to treat, presumptuously indeed, after the most holy
apostle, <span id="fr_8870"></span>[8871](#fn_8870) except in so far as
it will not be presumptuously if we treat the subject in accordance with
the apostle. Touching modesty of dress and ornamentation, indeed, the
prescription of Peter <span id="fr_8871"></span>[8872](#fn_8871)
likewise is plain, checking as he does with the same mouth, because with
the same Spirit, as Paul, the glory of garments, and the pride of gold,
and the meretricious elaboration of the hair.
