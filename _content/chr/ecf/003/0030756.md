<span id="vi.iv.xii-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.xii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XII.—We Must Be Free Likewise from
All Mental Perturbation.</span>

<span id="vi.iv.xii-p2"></span>Nor merely from anger, but altogether
from *all* perturbation of mind, ought the exercise of prayer to be
free, uttered from a spirit such as the Spirit unto whom it is sent. For
a defiled spirit cannot be acknowledged by a holy Spirit, <span
id="fr_8837"></span>[8838](#fn_8837) nor a sad by a joyful, <span
id="fr_8838"></span>[8839](#fn_8838) nor a fettered by a free. <span
id="fr_8839"></span>[8840](#fn_8839) No one grants reception to his
adversary: no one grants admittance except to his compeer.
