<span id="vi.iii.xxi-p1"></span> <span class="c17"><span
id="vi.iii.xxi-p1.1"></span>Elucidation.</span>

<span id="vi.iii.xxi-p2"></span> ————————————

<span id="vi.iii.xxi-p3"></span>The argument (p. 673, note 6,) is
conclusive, but not clear. The disciples of John must have been baptized
by him, ([Luke vii. 29-30](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#029)<span
id="vi.iii.xxi-p3.1"></span>) and “all the people,” must have included
those whom Jesus called.  But, this was not Christ’s baptism:  See [Acts
19:2](../../../bib/kjv/act019.htm#002),
[5](../../../bib/kjv/act019.htm#005)<span id="vi.iii.xxi-p3.2"></span>.
Compare note 8, p. 673. And see the American Editor’s “Apollos.”
