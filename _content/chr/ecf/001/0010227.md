### <span id="v.x.iv-p0.1"></span>Chapter IV.

<span id="v.x.iv-p1"></span>Let not the widows be overlooked; on account
of <span id="fr_1137"></span>[1138](#fn_1137) our Lord be thou their
guardian, and let nothing be done without thy will; also do thou nothing
without the will of God, as indeed thou doest not. Stand rightly. Let
there be frequent <span id="fr_1138"></span>[1139](#fn_1138) assemblies:
ask every man \[to them\] by his name. <span
id="v.x.iv-p3.1"></span>Despise not slaves, either male or female; but
neither let them be contemptuous, but let them labour the more as for
the glory of <span id="page_100">p. 100</span> <span
id="v.x.iv-Page_100"></span> God, that they may be counted worthy of a
more precious freedom, which is of God. Let them not desire to be set
free out of the common \[fund\], lest they be found the slaves of lust.
