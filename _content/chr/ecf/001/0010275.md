### <span id="v.xv.xii-p0.1"></span>Chapter XII.—Salutations.

<span id="v.xv.xii-p1"></span><span id="v.xv.xii-p1.1"></span>I salute
the holy presbytery. I salute the sacred deacons, and that person most
dear to me, <span id="fr_1257"></span>[1258](#fn_1257) whom may I
behold, through the Holy Spirit, occupying my place when I shall attain
to Christ. My soul be in place of his. I salute the sub-deacons, the
readers, the singers, the doorkeepers, the labourers, <span
id="fr_1258"></span>[1259](#fn_1258) the exorcists, the
confessors. <span id="fr_1259"></span>[1260](#fn_1259) I salute the
keepers of the holy gates, the deaconesses in Christ. I salute the
virgins betrothed to Christ, of whom may I have joy in the Lord
Jesus. <span id="fr_1260"></span>[1261](#fn_1260) I salute the people of
the Lord, from the smallest to the greatest, and all my sisters in the
Lord.
