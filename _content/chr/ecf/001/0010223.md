## <span id="v.x-p0.1"></span>The Epistle of Ignatius to Polycarp <span id="fr_1130"></span>[1131](#fn_1130)

<span id="v.x-p2"></span><span id="v.x-p2.1"></span>*Ignatius, who is
\[also called\] Theophorus, to Polycarp, bishop of Smyrna, or rather,
who has as his own bishop God the Father, and our Lord Jesus Christ:
\[wishes\] abundance of happiness.*

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_1130"></span>[99:1131](0010223.htm#fr_1130) <span

<span id="v.x-p1"></span> The inscription varies in each of the three
Syriac <span class="upper"><span id="v.x-p1.1"></span>mss.</span>, being
in the first, “The Epistle of my lord Ignatius, the bishop;” in the
second, “The Epistle of Ignatius;” and in the third, “The Epistle of
Ignatius, bishop of Antioch.”
