### <span id="viii.ix.xviii-p0.1"></span>XVIII.

<span id="viii.ix.xviii-p1"></span>To yield and give way to our passions
is the lowest slavery, even as to rule over them is the only liberty.

<span id="viii.ix.xviii-p2"></span>The greatest of all good is to be
free from sin, the next is to be justified; but he must be reckoned the
most unfortunate of men, who, while living unrighteously, remains for a
long time unpunished.

<span id="viii.ix.xviii-p3"></span>Animals in harness cannot but be
carried over a precipice by the inexperience and badness of their
driver, even as by his skilfulness and excellence they will be saved.

<span id="viii.ix.xviii-p4"></span>The end contemplated by a philosopher
is likeness to God, so far as that is possible.—*From the writings of*
<span class="upper"><span id="viii.ix.xviii-p4.1"></span>Antonius
Melissa</span>.
