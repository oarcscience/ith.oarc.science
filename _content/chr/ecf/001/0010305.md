### <span id="v.xviii.i-p0.1"></span>Mary of Cassobelæ to Ignatius <span id="fr_1353"></span>[1354](#fn_1353)

<span id="v.xviii.i-p2"></span><span id="v.xviii.i-p2.1"></span><span
id="v.xviii.i-p2.2"></span>*Maria, a proselyte of Jesus Christ, to
Ignatius Theophorus, most blessed bishop of the apostolic Church which
is at Antioch, beloved in God the Father, and Jesus: Happiness and
safety. We all* <span id="fr_1354"></span>[1355](#fn_1354) *beg for thee
joy and health in Him.*

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_1353"></span>[120:1354](0010305.htm#fr_1353) <span
id="v.xviii.i-p0.2"></span>

<span id="v.xviii.i-p1"></span> Nothing can be said with certainty as to
the place here referred to. Some have conceived that the ordinary
reading, *Maria Cassobolita*, is incorrect, and that it should be
changed to *Maria Castabalitis*, supposing the reference to be to
Castabala, a well-known city of Cilicia. But this and other proposed
emendations rest upon mere conjecture.
