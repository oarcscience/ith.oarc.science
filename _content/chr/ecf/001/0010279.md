### <span id="v.xvi.i-p0.1"></span>Chapter I.—Exhortations to earnestness and moderation.

<span id="v.xvi.i-p1"></span><span class="upper"><span
id="v.xvi.i-p1.1"></span>I Exhort</span> thee in God, that thou add
\[speed\] to thy course, and that thou vindicate thy dignity. Have a
care to preserve concord with the saints. Bear \[the burdens of\] the
weak, that “thou mayest fulfil the law of Christ.” <span
id="fr_1265"></span>[1266](#fn_1265) Devote <span
id="fr_1266"></span>[1267](#fn_1266) thyself to fasting and prayer, but
not beyond measure, lest thou destroy thyself <span
id="fr_1267"></span>[1268](#fn_1267) thereby. Do not altogether abstain
from wine and flesh, for these things are not to be viewed with
abhorrence, since \[the Scripture\] saith, “Ye shall eat the good things
of the earth.” <span id="fr_1268"></span>[1269](#fn_1268) And again, “Ye
shall eat flesh even as herbs.” <span
id="fr_1269"></span>[1270](#fn_1269) And again, “Wine maketh glad the
heart of man, and oil exhilarates, and bread strengthens him.” <span
id="fr_1270"></span>[1271](#fn_1270) But all are to be used with
moderation, as being the gifts of God. “For who shall eat or who shall
drink without Him? For if anything be beautiful, it is His; and if
anything be good, it is His.” <span id="fr_1271"></span>[1272](#fn_1271)
Give attention to reading, <span id="fr_1272"></span>[1273](#fn_1272)
that thou mayest not only thyself know the laws, but mayest also explain
them to others, as the earnest servant <span
id="fr_1273"></span>[1274](#fn_1273) of God. “No man that warreth
entangleth himself with the affairs of this life, that he may please him
who hath chosen him to be a soldier; and if a man also strive for
masteries, yet is he not crowned except he strive lawfully.” <span
id="fr_1274"></span>[1275](#fn_1274) I that am in bonds pray that my
soul may be in place of yours.
