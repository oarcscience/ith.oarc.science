<span id="iv.1.II-p1"></span> II. <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.II-p1.1"></span> BIOGRAPHIES</span>.

<span id="iv.1.II-p2"></span><span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.II-p2.1"></span>Possidius</span> (Calamensis episcopus, a pupil
and friend of Aug.): *Vita Augustini* (brief, but authentic, written
432, two years after his death, in tom. x. Append. 257-280, ed. Bened.,
and in nearly all other editions).

<span id="iv.1.II-p3"></span><span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.II-p3.1"></span>Benedictini Editores</span>: *Vita Augustini ex
ejus potissimum scriptis concinnata*, in 8 books (very elaborate and
extensive), in tom. xi. 1-492, ed. Bened. (in Migne’s reprint, tom. i.
col. 66-578).

<span id="iv.1.II-p4"></span>The biographies of Aug. by <span
class="c9"><span id="iv.1.II-p4.1"></span> Tillemont</span> (*Mém.* tom.
xiii.); <span class="c9"><span id="iv.1.II-p4.2"></span>Ellies
Dupin</span> (in “Nouvelle bibliothèque des auteurs ecclésiastiques,”
tom. ii. and iii.); <span class="c9"><span id="iv.1.II-p4.3"></span>P.
Bayle</span> (in his “Dictionnaire historique et critique,” art.
Augustin); <span class="c9"><span id="iv.1.II-p4.4"></span>Remi
Ceillier</span> (in “Histoire générale des auteurs sacrés et ecclés.,”
vol. xi. and xii.); <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.II-p4.5"></span>Cave</span> (in “Lives of the Fathers,” vol.
ii.); <span class="c9"><span id="iv.1.II-p4.6"></span>Kloth</span> (*Der
heil Aug*., Aachen, 1840, 2 vols.); <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.II-p4.7"></span> Böhringer</span> (*Kirchengeschichte in
Biographien*, vol. i. P. iii. p. 99 sqq., revised ed. Leipzig, 1877-’78,
2 parts); <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.II-p4.8"></span>Poujoulat</span> (*Histoire de S. Aug*. Par.
1843 and 1852, 2 vols.; the same in German by *Fr. Hurter*, Schaff h.
1847, 2 vols.); <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.II-p4.9"></span>Eisenbarth</span> (Stuttg. 1853); <span
class="c9"><span id="iv.1.II-p4.10"></span>C. Bindemann</span> (*Der
heil. Aug*. Berlin, 1844, ‘55, ‘69, 3 vols., the best work in German);
<span class="c9"><span id="iv.1.II-p4.11"></span>Edw. L. Cutts</span>
(*St. Augustin*, London, 1880); <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.II-p4.12"></span>E. de Pressensé</span> (in Smith and Wace,
“Dictionary of Christ. Biogr.” I. 216-225); <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.II-p4.13"></span>Ph. Schaff</span> (*St. Augustin*, Berlin,
1854; English ed. New York and London, 1854, revised and enlarged in
*St. Augustin, Melanchthon and Neander; three biographies*, New York and
London, 1886, pp. 1-106). On Monnica see <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.II-p4.14"></span>Braune</span>: *Monnica and Augustin*. Grimma,
1846.
