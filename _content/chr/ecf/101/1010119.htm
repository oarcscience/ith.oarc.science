Chapter XI.—That Creatures are Mutable and God Alone Immutable.

17. And I viewed the other things below Thee, and perceived that they neither altogether are, nor altogether are not. They are, indeed, because they are from Thee; but are not, because they are not what Thou art. For that truly is which remains immutably. 534 It is good, then, for me to cleave unto God, 535 for if I remain not in Him, neither shall I in myself; but He, remaining in Himself, reneweth all things. 536 And Thou art the Lord my God, since Thou standest not in need of my goodness. 537

This is existence in its true sense.” See also note 3, p. 158.
