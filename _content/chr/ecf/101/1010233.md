<span id="vi.XII-p1"></span><span class="c6"><span
id="vi.XII-p1.1"></span>Book XII.</span>

<span id="vi.XII-p2"></span>————————————

<span id="vi.XII-p3"></span>He continues his explanation of the first
Chapter of Genesis according to the Septuagint, and by its assistance he
argues, especially, concerning the double heaven, and the formless
matter out of which the whole world may have been created; afterwards of
the interpretations of others not disallowed, and sets forth at great
length the sense of the Holy Scripture.
