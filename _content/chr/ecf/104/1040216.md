<span id="iv.x.xxxiv-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="iv.x.xxxiv-p1.1"></span>Chapter 32.—From God Also is the Very Power
to Be Hurtful.</span>

<span id="iv.x.xxxiv-p2"></span>Likewise because the power even of those
that are hurtful is from God alone, thus it stands written, Wisdom
speaking:  "Through me kings reign and tyrants hold the land through
me." <span id="fr_1115"></span>[1116](#fn_1115)   The apostle also
says:  "For there is no power but of God." <span
id="fr_1116"></span>[1117](#fn_1116)   But that it is worthily done is
written in the book of Job:  "Who maketh to reign a man that is a
hypocrite, on account of the perversity of the people." <span
id="fr_1117"></span>[1118](#fn_1117)   And concerning the people of
Israel God says:  "I gave them a king in my wrath." <span
id="fr_1118"></span>[1119](#fn_1118)   For it is not unrighteous, that
the wicked receiving the power of being hurtful, both the patience of
the good should be proved and the iniquity of the evil punished.  For
through power given to the Devil both Job was proved so that he might
appear righteous, <span id="fr_1119"></span>[1120](#fn_1119) and Peter
was tempted lest he should be presumptuous, <span
id="fr_1120"></span>[1121](#fn_1120) and Paul was buffeted lest he
should be exalted, <span id="fr_1121"></span>[1122](#fn_1121) and Judas
was damned so that he should hang himself. <span
id="fr_1122"></span>[1123](#fn_1122)   When, therefore, through the
power which He has given the Devil, God Himself shall have done all
things righteously, nevertheless punishment shall at last be rendered to
the Devil not for these things justly done, but for the unrighteous
willing to be hurtful, which belonged to himself, when it shall be said
to the impious who persevered in consenting to his wickedness, "Go ye
into everlasting fire which my God has prepared for the Devil and his
angels."  <span id="fr_1123"></span>[1124](#fn_1123)
