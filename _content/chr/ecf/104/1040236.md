<span id="v.ii.i-p5"></span><span class="c2"><span
id="v.ii.i-p5.1"></span>A.  Sources.</span>

<span id="v.ii.i-p6"></span>I.    Of course all the Anti-Donatist
writings of Augustin are found in the general editions from Amerbach,
1506, to Migne, 1861.  A few are also collected in Du Pin’s edd. of
Optatus Mil.  1. In the *Monumenta vetera ad Donatistarum Historiam
pertinentia*.  2. In the *Gesta Collationis Carthagini habitae Honorii
Caesaris iussu inter Catholicos et Donatistas*.  See also the different
*Collections of Councils*, Labbe, Baluze, Harduin, Mansi, etc.  Since
these works are discussed in Chapter II. it is unnecessary to repeat the
titles here.  Cp. titles in *Retractationes*:  and *Indiculus librorum,
tractatuum et epistolarum S. Augustini*, ed. *cura Possidii*, cap. III.

<span id="v.ii.i-p7"></span>II.            Separate editions of
Augustin’s Anti-Donatist writings.  (From Schönemann’s *Bibliotheca*,
and other bibliographies.)

<span id="v.ii.i-p8"></span>1.   *S. Augustini liber seu Epistola de
unitate Ecclesiae contra Petiliani Donat.  Epistolam, Argumentis, Notis
atque Analysi illustrata, studio Justi Caluini.  Moguntiae*.  1602.

<span id="v.ii.i-p9"></span>2.   *SS. Cypriani et Augustini de unitate
Ecclesiae tractatus.  Accedit Georgii Calixti, S. Theo. Doct. et in
Acad. Julia Prof. primarii, in eorundem librorum lectionem
Introductionis fragmentum edente Frid. Ulrico Calixto.  Georgii filio. 
Helmæstadii ex typogr.  Calixt*.  1657.  8.

<span id="v.ii.i-p10"></span>3.      *Aurelii Augustini, Episcopi
Hipponensis, Liber de Unitate Ecclesiae contra Donatistas.  Ext. cum
Commentariis uberrimis et utillisimis in Melchioris Lydeckeri Historia
illustrata Ecclesiae Africanae, cujus totum pæne tomum secundum
constituit inscriptum*:

<span id="v.ii.i-p11"></span>*      Tomus secundus ad Librum Augustini
de Unitate Ecclesiae contra Donatistas, de principiis Ecclesiae
Africanae, illiusque fide in Articulis de Capite Christo et Ecclesia, de
Unitate et Schismate, plurimisque Religionis Christianae capitibus
agit.  Ultrajecti apud viduam Guil. Clerck*, 1690.  4.

<span id="v.ii.i-p12"></span>4.   *D. Augustini liber de moderate
coercendis haereticis ed Bonifacium Comitem. Nic. Bergius Revalensis
Holmiae*, 1696, *in* 8.

<span id="v.ii.i-p13"></span>III.            Translations.

<span id="v.ii.i-p14"></span>1.      *<span
id="v.ii.i-p14.1"></span>Epistre ou le Livre de St. Augustin de l’Unité
de l’Eglise, contre Petilien, Evesque Donatiste, avec certaines
observations pour entendre les lieux plus difficiles par Jac.  Tigeou,
imprimé à Reims par Jean de Foigny*.  1567.  8.

<span id="v.ii.i-p15"></span><span id="page_370">p. 370</span> <span
id="v.ii.i-Page_370"></span>2.      *<span
id="v.ii.i-p15.1"></span>L’Epistre à Vincent, Evesque de l’heresie
Rogatiane, traduict de latin par Clément Vaillant.  A Paris, Mathurin
Prevost*.  1573.  8.

<span id="v.ii.i-p16"></span>3.      *<span
id="v.ii.i-p16.1"></span>Traité du Baptême trad. par l’abbé Dujat,
chapelain d’Étampes. Paris*.  1778.  12.

<span id="v.ii.i-p17"></span>4.      Writings in connection with the
Donatist controversy, translated by the Rev. J. R. King, M.A.  In the
Series of Translations of the Works of Augustin.  Edinburgh.  T. & T.
Clark.  1872.

<span id="v.ii.i-p18"></span>5.      *<span
id="v.ii.i-p18.1"></span>Ausgewählte Schriften des heil.  Aurelius
Augustinus, Kirchenlehrers, nach aem Urtexte ubersetzt.  Mit einer
kurzen Lebensbeschreibung des Heiligen von J. Motzberger*.  1871–1879. 
*In the* *<span id="v.ii.i-p18.2"></span>Bibliothek der Kirchenväter,
Kempten*, 1869 *sqq*.

<span id="v.ii.i-p19"></span><span class="c2"><span
id="v.ii.i-p19.1"></span>B.  Literature.</span>

<span id="v.ii.i-p20"></span>This is a selected literature of the
Donatist controversy so far as Augustin was connected with it.

<span id="v.ii.i-p21"></span>I.    In the Benedictine editions occur:

<span id="v.ii.i-p22"></span>1.      Their *Vita S. Aurelii Augustini*. 
*Tom. XI.  Antw., pp*. 1–344.  *Tom. I.  Migne, pp*. 65–578.

<span id="v.ii.i-p23"></span>2.      *Praefatio of Tom. IX*.  *Antw. s.
p.  Migne, pp*. 9–24.

<span id="v.ii.i-p24"></span>3.      *Index opusculorum S. Augustini
contra Donatistas*.  *Tom. IX.  Antw., pp*. 463, 4.  *Migne, pp*.
757–760.

<span id="v.ii.i-p25"></span>4.      *Excerpta et scripta vetera ad
Donatistarum historiam pertinentia*.  *Tom. IX.  Antw., App. pp*. 7–50. 
*Migne, pp*. 773–842.

<span id="v.ii.i-p26"></span>5.      *Epistolarum ordo
chronologicus*.     *Tom. II.  Antw., s. p.  Migne, pp*. 13–48.

<span id="v.ii.i-p27"></span>II.            *Possidius:  Vita S. Aurelii
Augustini*.  *Reprinted in Migne Aug. Op.  Tom. I., pp*. 33–66.  *Cp.
Migne Pat. Lat. L. p*. 407.

<span id="v.ii.i-p28"></span>III.            *Ecclesiastica Historia. 
By the Magdeburg Centuriators*.  1559–1574.  *Tom. II. and III.,
Centuria, IV. and V*., contain the Donatist history.

<span id="v.ii.i-p29"></span>IV.            *Balduinius, Franc*.

<span id="v.ii.i-p30"></span>1.      *Delibatio Africanae historiae
ecclesticae, s.  Optati libri VII. de Schismate Donatistarum, etc. 
Paris*, 1563.  A second edition with improved readings.  *Ib*., 1569. 
In this the prefaces and annotations are of value.  Reprinted in Du
Pin’s ed. of Optatus Mil.

<span id="v.ii.i-p31"></span>2.      *Historia Carthaginensis
Collationis sive disputationis de ecclesia, olim habitae inter
Catholicos et Donatistas.  Paris*, 1566.  8.  *Reprinted in Du Pin. ib*.

<span id="v.ii.i-p32"></span>V.            *Baronius.  Annales
Ecclesiatici*.  1588–1607.  Tom. III.-V., contain the Donatist history.

<span id="v.ii.i-p33"></span>VI.               *Albaspinæus:  Optati
Mel. opera cum notis et observationibus Gabrielis Albaspinæi.  Paris*,
1631.  Valuable mainly for the observations; reprinted in Du Pin’s ed.
of Optatus.

<span id="v.ii.i-p34"></span>VII.               *Casaubonus:  Optati
Mel. de schismate Donatistarum libri VII.  In eosd. notae et
emendationes Merici Casauboni.  Lond*. 1631.  These notes are of value
and are reproduced with those of other editions in the *Annotationes
Variorum* of Du Pin’s ed.

<span id="v.ii.i-p35"></span> <span id="page_371">p. 371</span> <span
id="v.ii.i-Page_371"></span>VIII.               *Valesius Henricus*: 
*Eusebii Pamph. Historia ecc., libri de Vita Constantini, Panegyricus,
Const. Oratio ad Sanctorum coetum, gr. et lat. cum annotatt.  Paris*,
1659 and often.  In this is his dissertation:  *De schismate
Donatistarum*.

<span id="v.ii.i-p36"></span>IX. Long, Thomas, B.D.  History of the
Donatists.  Lond. 1677.  8.

<span id="v.ii.i-p37"></span>X.  *<span id="v.ii.i-p37.1"></span>Du
Pin:  Nouvelle Bibliothéque des Auteurs Ecclésiastiques*.

<span id="v.ii.i-p38"></span>1.   *St. Augustin.  Tom. III.* *<span
id="v.ii.i-p38.1"></span>première partie, pp*. 522–839, 1690. 
Particularly the review of vol. IX. of Augustin’s collected works, pp.
792–811.

<span id="v.ii.i-p39"></span>2.   In *Tom. II.,* *<span
id="v.ii.i-p39.1"></span>Troisième partie*, 1701, there are also many
allusions to the history and literature.

<span id="v.ii.i-p40"></span>3.   In his ed. of *Optatus Mel., Historia
Donatistarum*.

<span id="v.ii.i-p41"></span>XI.    *Ittig, Thomas:  de Haeresiarchis
œvi apostolici at apostol. prox.  Lips*. 1690–1703.  4.

<span id="v.ii.i-p42"></span>XII.               *Leydecker Melchior;
Historia Ecclesiastica Africana.  2 Tom*. 4, See above.  *Traj*. 1690. 
4.

<span id="v.ii.i-p43"></span>XIII.               *Witsius, Hermann: 
Miscellaneorum Sacrorum libri.  2 vols. Amst*. 1692.  4.  In vol. I.
*Dissertatio de schismate Donatistarum*.

<span id="v.ii.i-p44"></span>XIV.               *Bernino*:  *<span
id="v.ii.i-p44.1"></span>Historia di tutte l’heresie descritta da
Domenico Bernino*<span id="v.ii.i-p44.2"></span>.  Venezia 1711.  Tom.
I., contains hist. of Donatism.

<span id="v.ii.i-p45"></span>XV.               *Storren, J. Ph.: *
*<span id="v.ii.i-p45.1"></span>ansführlicher und gründlicher Bericht
von den Namen, Ursprung, v.s.w. der Donatisten.  Frankf*. 1723.  8.

<span id="v.ii.i-p46"></span>XVI.               *Norisius, Henricus*: 
*Opera omnia nunc prim. collecta et ordinata.  Veronae, Tumermani,*
1729–32*, fol.*  4*vols*.  The fourth volume contains his posthumous
work on *History of Donatism*, as finished by Ballerini.

<span id="v.ii.i-p47"></span>XVII.                  *<span
id="v.ii.i-p47.1"></span>Tillemont:  in his Memoires pour servir a
l’histoire Ecclésiastique*:

<span id="v.ii.i-p48"></span>1.      *<span
id="v.ii.i-p48.1"></span>Tom. VI.  Histoire du schisme des Donatistes,
où l’on marque aussi tout ce qui regardé l’Eglise d’Afrique depuis l’an*
<span id="v.ii.i-p48.2"></span>305*, jusques en l’an* 391*que S.
Augustin fut fait Prestre*.  1732.

<span id="v.ii.i-p49"></span>2.      *<span
id="v.ii.i-p49.1"></span>Tom. XIII.  La Vie de Saint Augustin, dans
laquelle on trouvera l’histoire des Donatistes de son temps, et celle
des Pelagiens*.  1732.

<span id="v.ii.i-p50"></span>XVIII.                  *Orsi*:  *Della
Istoria Ecclesiastica descritta da F. Guiseppe Agostino Orsi*.  Tom. IV.
(1741) and V. (1749) contain the history ot the Donatists.

<span id="v.ii.i-p51"></span>XIX.               *Walch, Ch. Wilh. Fr.:*
  *<span id="v.ii.i-p51.1"></span>Entwurf einer vollständigen Historie
der Ketzereien, Spaltungen und Religionsstreitigkeiten, bis auf die
Zeiten der Reformation.  Leipzig*, 1768.  *Vierter Theil: * *<span
id="v.ii.i-p51.2"></span>Von der Spaltung der Donatisten*; with its
three sections:

<span id="v.ii.i-p52"></span>a.      *<span id="v.ii.i-p52.1"></span>Von
der historie der Donatisten*.

<span id="v.ii.i-p53"></span>b.      *<span id="v.ii.i-p53.1"></span>Von
den zwischen den Donatisten und ihren Gegnern geführten
Religionsstreitigkeiten*.

<span id="v.ii.i-p54"></span>c.      *<span
id="v.ii.i-p54.1"></span>Beurtheilung der Donatistichen
Streitigkeiten*.  This work was the beginning of a new critical estimate
of the documents.

<span id="v.ii.i-p55"></span>XX.               *<span
id="v.ii.i-p55.1"></span> Schröckh, Johann Mattheus:  Christliche
Kirchengeschichte.  Sechster Theil*:  1784, but particularly *<span
id="v.ii.i-p55.2"></span>Elfter Theil*, 1786.  A juster estimate of
Donatism.

<span id="v.ii.i-p56"></span> <span id="page_372">p. 372</span> <span
id="v.ii.i-Page_372"></span>XXI.               *Morcellii, Steph. Ant.: 
Africa christiana in tres partes distributa. * 3 *vols.* 4*Brixiae,*
1816–17.  4. *P. II.* for Donatism.

<span id="v.ii.i-p57"></span>XXII.                  *Bindemann, C.: *
*<span id="v.ii.i-p57.1"></span>Der heilige Augustinus*, 1844–1869. 
Bdd. II. & III. contain excellent analyses of the works on Donatism, as
well as a history during Augustin’s life.

<span id="v.ii.i-p58"></span>XXIII.                  *Roux, Adrianus*: 
*Dissertatio de Aurelio Augustino, adversario Donatistarum.  Lugduni
Batavorum*, 1838.  A brief summary of the works and doctrine.

<span id="v.ii.i-p59"></span>XXIV.                  *Ribbeck:*  *<span
id="v.ii.i-p59.1"></span>Donatus und Augustinus oder der erste
entscheidende Kampf zwischen Separatismus und Kirche.  Ein
Kirchenhistorischer Versuch von Ferdinand Ribbeck.  Elberfeld*.  1857. 
8.  An uncritical history; but a vigorous analysis, apologetic and
polemic.

<span id="v.ii.i-p60"></span>XXV.                  *Deutsch:*  *<span
id="v.ii.i-p60.1"></span>Drei Actenstücke zur Geschichte Donatismus. 
Neu herausgegeben und erklärt von Martin Deutsch.  Berlin*, 1875.  The
first work on the textual and historical criticism of the sources.

<span id="v.ii.i-p61"></span>XXVI.                  *Voelter:*  *<span
id="v.ii.i-p61.1"></span>Der Ursprung des Donatismus, nach den Quellen
untersucht und dargestellt von Lic Dr. Daniel Voelter.  Freiburg i. B.
und Tübingen*, 1883.  This keen writer, at present Prof. Ord. in Univ.
of Amsterdam, has gone still further into textual and historical
criticism, and gives fair promise of a more impartial hearing for
Donatism.  It is to be hoped that he will fulfill his qualified promise
of further research.

<span id="v.ii.i-p62"></span>Among the general church histories
particular mention may be made of Gieseler, Neander, Lindner, Niedner,
Robertson, Ritter, Hergenröther, Schaff.  The articles on Augustin,
Donatism and related persons and topics in Ceillier, Ersch und Gruber,
Herzog, Schaff-Herzog, Smith’s Dictionary of Christian Biography, Wetzer
and Welte, Lichtenberger, are more or less noteworthy.  Mention must
also be made of the Patrologies, the biographies, Hefele’s
Conciliengeschichte, the Analyses Patrum, etc.
