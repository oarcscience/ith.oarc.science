<span id="v.v.iii.xxvi"></span>

<span id="v.v.iii.xxvi-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.v.iii.xxvi-p1.1"></span>Chapter 26.—</span>28.  But it is possible
that you may expect of me that I should go on to refute what he has
introduced about Manichæus.  Now, in respect of this, the only thing
that offends me is that he has censured a most pestilent and pernicious
error—I mean the heresy of the Manichæans—in terms of wholly inadequate
severity, if indeed they amount to censure at all, though the Catholic
Church has broken down his defenses by the strongest evidence of
truth. <span id="fr_1961"></span>[1962](#fn_1961)   For the inheritance
of Christ, established in all nations, is secure against heresies which
have been shut out from the inheritance; but, as the Lord says, "How can
Satan cast out Satan?" <span id="fr_1962"></span>[1963](#fn_1962) so how
can the error of the Donatists have power to overthrow the error of the
Manichæans? <span id="fr_1963"></span>[1964](#fn_1963)
