<span id="v.iv.viii.xvi-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.iv.viii.xvi-p1.1"></span>Chapter 16.—</span>26.  Nicomedes of
Segermi <span id="fr_1640"></span>[1641](#fn_1640) said:  "My judgment
is that heretics coming to the Church should be baptized, because they
can obtain no remission of sins among sinners outside." <span
id="fr_1641"></span>[1642](#fn_1641)

<span id="v.iv.viii.xvi-p4"></span>27.  The answer to which is:  The
judgment of the whole Catholic Church is that heretics, being already
baptized with the baptism of Christ, although in heresy, should not be
rebaptized on coming to the Church.  For if there is no remission of
sins among sinners, neither can sinners within the Church remit sins;
and yet those who have been baptized by them are not rebaptized.
