<span id="v.iv.ix.xxxiii-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.iv.ix.xxxiii-p1.1"></span>Chapter 33.—</span>64.  Donatulus of
Capse <span id="fr_1846"></span>[1847](#fn_1846) said:  "I also have
always entertained this opinion, that heretics, who have gained nothing
outside the Church, should be baptized when they are converted to the
Church." <span id="fr_1847"></span>[1848](#fn_1847)

<span id="v.iv.ix.xxxiii-p4"></span>65.  To this the answer is:  They
have, indeed, gained nothing outside the Church, but that is nothing
towards salvation, not nothing towards the sacrament.  For salvation is
peculiar to the good; but the sacraments are common to the good and bad
alike.
