<span id="v.iv.ix.xxvi-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.iv.ix.xxvi-p1.1"></span>Chapter 26.—</span>50.  Also another
Lucius of Membresa <span id="fr_1829"></span>[1830](#fn_1829) said:  "It
is written, ‘God heareth not sinners.’ <span
id="fr_1830"></span>[1831](#fn_1830)   How can he who is a sinner be
heard in baptism?" <span id="fr_1831"></span>[1832](#fn_1831)

<span id="v.iv.ix.xxvi-p5"></span><span id="page_507">p. 507</span>
<span id="v.iv.ix.xxvi-Page_507"></span>51.  We answer:  How is the
covetous man heard, or the robber, and usurer, and murderer?  Are they
not sinners?  And yet Cyprian, while he finds fault with them in the
Catholic Church, yet tolerates them.
