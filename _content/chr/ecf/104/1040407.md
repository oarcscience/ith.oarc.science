<span id="v.iv.ix.viii-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.iv.ix.viii-p1.1"></span>Chapter 8.—</span>14.  Pelagianus of
Luperciana <span id="fr_1775"></span>[1776](#fn_1775) said:  "It is
written, ‘Either the Lord is God, or Baal is God.’ <span
id="fr_1776"></span>[1777](#fn_1776)   So now either the Church is the
Church, or heresy is the Church.  Further, if heresy be not the Church,
how can the baptism of the Church exist among heretics?" <span
id="fr_1777"></span>[1778](#fn_1777)

<span id="v.iv.ix.viii-p5"></span>15.  To him we may answer as follows: 
Either Paradise is Paradise, or Egypt is Paradise.  Further, if Egypt be
not Paradise, how can the water of Paradise be in Egypt?  But it will be
said to us that it extends even thither by flowing forth from Paradise. 
In like manner, therefore, baptism extends to heretics.  Also we say: 
Either the rock is the Church, or the sand is the Church.  Further,
since the sand is not the Church, how can baptism exist with those who
build upon the sand by hearing the words of Christ and doing them
not? <span id="fr_1778"></span>[1779](#fn_1778)   And yet it does exist
with them; and in like manner also it exists among the heretics.
