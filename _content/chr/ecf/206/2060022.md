<span id="v.XI-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="v.XI-p1.1"></span>Letter XI. To the Virgins of Æmona.</span>

<span id="v.XI-p2"></span>Æmona was a Roman colony not far from Stridon,
Jerome’s birthplace. The virgins to whom the note is addressed had
omitted to answer his letters, and he now writes to upbraid them for
their remissness. The date of the letter is 374 <span class="c17"><span
id="v.XI-p2.1"></span>a.d.</span>

<span id="v.XI-p3"></span>This scanty sheet of paper shows in what a
wilderness I live, and because of it I have to say much in few words.
For, desirous though I am to speak to you more fully, this miserable
scrap compels me to leave much unsaid. Still ingenuity makes up for lack
of means, and by writing small I can say a great deal. Observe, I
beseech you, how I love you, even in the midst of my difficulties, since
even the want of materials does not stop me from writing to you.

<span id="v.XI-p4"></span>Pardon, I beseech you, an aggrieved man: if I
speak in tears and in anger it is because I have been injured. For in
return for my regular letters you have not sent me a single syllable.
Light, I know, has no communion with darkness, <span
id="fr_141"></span>[142](#fn_141) and God’s handmaidens no fellowship
with a sinner, yet a harlot was allowed to wash the Lord’s feet with her
tears, <span id="fr_142"></span>[143](#fn_142) and dogs are permitted to
eat of their masters’ crumbs. <span id="fr_143"></span>[144](#fn_143) It
was the Saviour’s mission to call sinners and not the righteous; for, as
He said Himself, “they that be whole need not a physician.” <span
id="fr_144"></span>[145](#fn_144) He wills the repentance of a sinner
rather than his death, <span id="fr_145"></span>[146](#fn_145) and
carries home the poor stray sheep on His own shoulders. <span
id="fr_146"></span>[147](#fn_146) So, too, when the prodigal son
returns, his father receives him with joy. <span
id="fr_147"></span>[148](#fn_147) Nay more, the apostle says: “Judge
nothing before the time.” <span id="fr_148"></span>[149](#fn_148) For
“who art thou that judgest another man’s servant? To his own master he
standeth or falleth.” <span id="fr_149"></span>[150](#fn_149) And “let
him that standeth take heed lest he fall.” <span
id="fr_150"></span>[151](#fn_150) “Bear ye one another’s burdens.” <span
id="fr_151"></span>[152](#fn_151)

<span id="v.XI-p16"></span>Dear sisters, man’s envy judges in one way,
Christ in another; and the whisper of a corner is not the same as the
sentence of His tribunal. Many ways seem right to men which are
afterwards found to be wrong. <span id="fr_152"></span>[153](#fn_152)
And a treasure is often stowed in earthen vessels. <span
id="fr_153"></span>[154](#fn_153) Peter thrice denied his Lord, yet his
bitter tears restored him to his place. “To whom much is forgiven, the
same loveth much.” <span id="fr_154"></span>[155](#fn_154) No word is
said of the flock as a whole, yet the angels joy in heaven over the
safety of one sick ewe. <span id="fr_155"></span>[156](#fn_155) And if
any one demurs to this reasoning, the Lord Himself has said: “Friend, is
thine eye evil because I am good?” <span
id="fr_156"></span>[157](#fn_156)
