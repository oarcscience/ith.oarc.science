<span id="iv.X-p1"></span><span class="c18"><span
id="iv.X-p1.1"></span>Book X.</span>

<span id="iv.X-p2"></span> ————————————

<span id="iv.X-p3"></span>Argument—In this book Augustin teaches that
the good angels wish God alone, whom they themselves serve, to receive
that divine honor which is rendered by sacrifice, and which is called
“latreia.”  He then goes on to dispute against Porphyry about the
principle and way of the soul’s cleansing and deliverance.
