<span id="v.vi.xiii-p1"></span><span class="c2"><span
id="v.vi.xiii-p1.1"></span>Chapter 13.—Same Subject, Continued.</span>

<span id="v.vi.xiii-p2"></span>21.  Whatever, then, is in accordance
with the habits of those with whom we are either compelled by necessity,
or undertake as a matter of duty, to spend this life, is to be turned by
good and great men to some prudent or benevolent end, either directly,
as is our duty, or figuratively, as is allowable to prophets.
