### <span id="iii.v.liii-p0.1"></span>Elucidation

<span id="iii.v.liii-p1"></span> (On the tenth of the kalends of April,
p. 301.)  

<span id="iii.v.liii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iii.v.liii-p2.1"></span>Serious</span> difficulties are encountered
by the learned in reconciling <span
id="iii.v.liii-p2.2"></span>Lactantius with himself, if, indeed, the
fault be not one of his copyists rather than his own. In the fourth book
of the <span id="iii.v.liii-p2.3"></span>Institutes <span
id="fr_1998"></span>[1999](#fn_1998) his language is thus given by <span
id="iii.v.liii-p3.2"></span>Baluzius: <span
id="fr_1999"></span>[2000](#fn_1999) —  

<span id="iii.v.liii-p5"></span>“<span
id="iii.v.liii-p5.1"></span>Extremis temporibus Tiberii Cæsaris, <span
id="iii.v.liii-p5.2"></span>ut scriptum legimus<span
id="iii.v.liii-p5.3"></span>, Dominus noster <span
id="iii.v.liii-p5.4"></span>Jesus Christus<span
id="iii.v.liii-p5.5"></span>, a Judæis cruciatus est <span
id="iii.v.liii-p5.6"></span>post diem decimum kalendarum Aprilis<span
id="iii.v.liii-p5.7"></span>, duobus Geminis consulibus.”  

<span id="iii.v.liii-p6"></span><span
id="iii.v.liii-p6.1"></span>Lactantius was writing in Nicomedia, and may
have quoted from memory what he had read, perhaps in the report of
Pilate himself. The expression <span class="Foreign"><span
id="iii.v.liii-p6.2"></span>post diem decimum kalendarum Aprilis</span>
is ambiguous: and Jarvis says, “My impression is, that it means ‘after
the tenth day before the kalends of April;’ that is, after the 23d of
March.” <span id="fr_2000"></span>[2001](#fn_2000)  

<span id="iii.v.liii-p8"></span>But here our author says, according to
the accurate edition of <span id="iii.v.liii-p8.1"></span>Walchius <span
id="fr_2001"></span>[2002](#fn_2001) (<span
id="iii.v.liii-p9.3"></span>a.d. 1715),—  

<span id="iii.v.liii-p10"></span>“<span
id="iii.v.liii-p10.1"></span>Exinde tetrarchas habuerunt usque ad
Herodem, qui fuit sub imperio Tiberii Cæsaris: cujus anno quinto decimo,
id est duobus Geminis consulibus, <span
id="iii.v.liii-p10.2"></span>ante diem septimam Calendarum Aprilium<span
id="iii.v.liii-p10.3"></span>, Judæi Christum cruci affixerunt.”  

<span id="iii.v.liii-p11"></span>But here, on the authority of forty
manuscripts, <span id="iii.v.liii-p11.1"></span>Du Fresnoy reads, “<span
id="iii.v.liii-p11.2"></span>ante<span id="iii.v.liii-p11.3"></span>
diem decimam,” which he labours to reconcile with “<span
id="iii.v.liii-p11.4"></span>post<span id="iii.v.liii-p11.5"></span>
diem decimum,” as above. <span id="iii.v.liii-p11.6"></span>Jarvis
adheres to the reading <span id="iii.v.liii-p11.7"></span>septimam,
supported by more than fifty manuscripts, and decides for the 23d of
March.  

<span id="iii.v.liii-p12"></span>He cites <span
id="iii.v.liii-p12.1"></span>Augustine to the same effect in the noted
passage: <span id="fr_2002"></span>[2003](#fn_2002) —  

<span id="iii.v.liii-p14"></span>“<span
id="iii.v.liii-p14.1"></span>Ille autem mense conceptum et passum esse
Christum, et Paschæ observatio et dies ecclesiis notissimus Nativitatis
ejus ostendit. Qui enim mense nono natus est octavo kalendas Janvarias
profecto mense primo conceptus est circa octavum kalendas Aprilis, quod
tempus passionis ejus fuit.”  

<span id="iii.v.liii-p15"></span>This, <span
id="iii.v.liii-p15.1"></span>Augustine considers to be “seething a kid
in mother’s milk,” after a mystical sense; cruelly making the cross to
coincide with the maternity of the Virgin, who beheld her Son an
innocent victim on the anniversary of her salutation by the angel.
