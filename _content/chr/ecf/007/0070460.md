#### <span id="ix.viii.i-p0.1"></span>Sec. I.—On the Two Ways, <span id="fr_3342"></span>[3343](#fn_3342) —The Way of Life and the Way of Death.

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p1.3"></span>That There are Two Ways,—The One Natural, of Life, and the Other Introduced Afterwards, of Death; And that the Former is from God, and the Latter of Error, from the Snares of the Adversary.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p2.1"></span>I.</span> <span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p2.2"></span>The</span> lawgiver <span
id="ix.viii.i-p2.3"></span>Moses said to the Israelites, “Behold, I have
set before your face the way of life and the way of death;” <span
id="fr_3343"></span>[3344](#fn_3343) and added, “Choose life, that thou
mayest live.” <span id="fr_3344"></span>[3345](#fn_3344) Elijah the
prophet also said to the people: “How long will you halt with both your
legs? If the Lord be God, follow Him.” <span
id="fr_3345"></span>[3346](#fn_3345) The Lord <span
id="ix.viii.i-p5.3"></span>Jesus also said justly: “No one can serve two
masters: for either he will hate the one, and love the other; or else he
will hold to the one, and despise the other.” <span
id="fr_3346"></span>[3347](#fn_3346) We also, following our teacher
Christ, “who is the Saviour of all men, especially of those that
believe,” <span id="fr_3347"></span>[3348](#fn_3347) are obliged to say
that there are two ways—the one of life, the other of death; <span
id="fr_3348"></span>[3349](#fn_3348) which have no comparison one with
another, for they are very different, <span
id="fr_3349"></span>[3350](#fn_3349) or rather entirely separate; and
the way of life is that of nature, but that of death was afterwards
introduced,—it not being according to the mind of God, but from the
snares of the adversary. <span id="fr_3350"></span>[3351](#fn_3350)  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p10.1"></span>Moral Exhortations of the Lord’s Constitutions Agreeing with the Ancient Prohibitions of the Divine Laws. The Prohibition of Anger, Spite, Corruption, Adultery, and Every Forbidden Action.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p11"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p11.1"></span>II</span>. The first way, therefore, is that
of life; and is this, <span id="fr_3351"></span>[3352](#fn_3351) which
the law also does appoint: “To love the Lord God with all thy mind, and
with all thy soul, who is the one and only God, besides whom there is no
other;” <span id="fr_3352"></span>[3353](#fn_3352) “and thy neighbour as
thyself.” <span id="fr_3353"></span>[3354](#fn_3353) And whatsoever thou
wouldest not should be done to thee, that do not thou to another.” <span
id="fr_3354"></span>[3355](#fn_3354) “Bless them that curse you; pray
for them that despitefully use you.” <span
id="fr_3355"></span>[3356](#fn_3355) “Love your enemies; for what thanks
is it if ye love those that love you? for even the Gentiles do the
same.” <span id="fr_3356"></span>[3357](#fn_3356) “But do ye love those
that hate you, and ye shall have no enemy.” For says He, “Thou shalt not
hate any man; no, not an Egyptian, nor an Edomite;” <span
id="fr_3357"></span>[3358](#fn_3357) for they are all the workmanship of
God. Avoid not the persons, but the sentiments, of the wicked. “Abstain
from fleshly and worldly lusts.” <span
id="fr_3358"></span>[3359](#fn_3358) “If any one gives thee a stroke on
thy right cheek, turn to him the other also.” <span
id="fr_3359"></span>[3360](#fn_3359) Not that revenge is evil, but that
patience is more honourable. For <span id="ix.viii.i-p20.3"></span>David
says, “If I have made returns to them that repaid me evil.” <span
id="fr_3360"></span>[3361](#fn_3360) “If any one compel thee to go a
mile, go with him twain.” <span id="fr_3361"></span>[3362](#fn_3361)
And, “He that will sue thee at the law, and take away thy coat, let him
have thy cloak also.” <span id="fr_3362"></span>[3363](#fn_3362) “And
from him that taketh thy goods, require them not again.” <span
id="fr_3363"></span>[3364](#fn_3363) “Give to him that asketh thee, and
from him that would borrow of thee do not shut thy hand.” <span
id="fr_3364"></span>[3365](#fn_3364) For “the righteous man is pitiful,
and lendeth.” <span id="fr_3365"></span>[3366](#fn_3365) For your Father
would have you give to all, who Himself “maketh His sun to rise on the
evil and on the good, and sendeth His rain on the just and on the
unjust.” <span id="fr_3366"></span>[3367](#fn_3366) It is therefore
reasonable to give to all out of thine own labours; for says He, “Honour
the Lord out of thy righteous <span id="page_466">p. 466</span> <span
id="ix.viii.i-Page_466"></span>labours,” <span
id="fr_3367"></span>[3368](#fn_3367) but so that the saints be
preferred. <span id="fr_3368"></span>[3369](#fn_3368) “Thou shalt not
kill;” <span id="fr_3369"></span>[3370](#fn_3369) that is, thou shalt
not destroy a man like thyself: for thou dissolvest what was well made.
Not as if all killing were wicked, but only that of the innocent: but
the killing which is just is reserved to the magistrates alone. “Thou
shalt not commit adultery:” for thou dividest one flesh into two. “They
two shall be one flesh:” <span id="fr_3370"></span>[3371](#fn_3370) for
the husband and wife are one in nature, in consent, in union, in
disposition, and the conduct of life; but they are separated in sex and
number. “Thou shall not corrupt boys:” <span
id="fr_3371"></span>[3372](#fn_3371) for this wickedness is contrary to
nature, and arose from Sodom, which was therefore entirely consumed with
fire sent from God. <span id="fr_3372"></span>[3373](#fn_3372) “Let such
a one be accursed: and all the people shall say, So be it.” <span
id="fr_3373"></span>[3374](#fn_3373) “Thou shall not commit
fornication:” for says He, “There shall not be a fornicator among the
children of Israel.” <span id="fr_3374"></span>[3375](#fn_3374) “Thou
shalt not steal:” for <span id="ix.viii.i-p35.2"></span>Achan, when he
had stolen in Israel at Jericho, was stoned to death; <span
id="fr_3375"></span>[3376](#fn_3375) and <span
id="ix.viii.i-p36.2"></span>Gehazi, who stole, and told a lie, inherited
the leprosy of <span id="ix.viii.i-p36.3"></span>Naaman; <span
id="fr_3376"></span>[3377](#fn_3376) and <span
id="ix.viii.i-p37.2"></span>Judas, who stole the poor’s money, betrayed
the Lord of glory to the Jews, <span
id="fr_3377"></span>[3378](#fn_3377) and repented, and hanged himself,
and burst asunder in the midst, and all his bowels gushed out; <span
id="fr_3378"></span>[3379](#fn_3378) and <span
id="ix.viii.i-p39.3"></span>Ananias, and <span
id="ix.viii.i-p39.4"></span>Sapphira his wife, who stole their own
goods, and “tempted the Spirit of the Lord,” were immediately, at the
sentence of <span id="ix.viii.i-p39.5"></span>Peter our fellow-apostle,
struck dead. <span id="fr_3379"></span>[3380](#fn_3379)  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p40.2"></span>The Prohibition of Conjuring, Murder of Infants, Perjury, and False Witness.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p41"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p41.1"></span>III</span>. Thou shalt not use magic. <span
id="fr_3380"></span>[3381](#fn_3380) Thou shalt not use witchcraft; for
He says, “Ye shall not suffer a witch to live.” <span
id="fr_3381"></span>[3382](#fn_3381) Thou shall not slay thy child by
causing abortion, nor kill that which is begotten; for “everything that
is shaped, and has received a soul from God, if it be slain, shall be
avenged, as being unjustly destroyed.” <span
id="fr_3382"></span>[3383](#fn_3382) “Thou shalt not covet the things
that belong to thy neighbour, as his wife, or his servant, or his ox, or
his field.” “Thou shalt not forswear thyself; for it is said, “Thou
shalt not swear at all.” <span id="fr_3383"></span>[3384](#fn_3383) But
if that cannot be avoided, thou shalt swear truly; for “every one that
swears by Him shall be commended.” <span
id="fr_3384"></span>[3385](#fn_3384) “Thou shalt not bear false
witness;” for “he that falsely accuses the needy provokes to anger Him
that made him.” <span id="fr_3385"></span>[3386](#fn_3385)  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p47.2"></span>The Prohibition of Evil-Speaking and Passion, of Deceitful Conduct, or Idle Words, Lies, Covetousness, and Hypocrisy.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p48"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p48.1"></span>IV</span>. Thou shall not speak evil; <span
id="fr_3386"></span>[3387](#fn_3386) for says He, “Love not to speak
evil, lest thou beest taken away.” Nor shalt thou be mindful of
injuries; for “the ways of those that remember injuries are unto
death.” <span id="fr_3387"></span>[3388](#fn_3387) Thou shall not be
double-minded nor double-tongued; for “a man’s own lips are a strong
snare to him,” <span id="fr_3388"></span>[3389](#fn_3388) and “a
talkative person shall not be prospered upon earth.” <span
id="fr_3389"></span>[3390](#fn_3389) Thy words shall not be vain; for
“ye shall give an account of every idle word.” <span
id="fr_3390"></span>[3391](#fn_3390) Thou shalt not tell lies: for says
He, “Thou shalt destroy all those that speak lies.” <span
id="fr_3391"></span>[3392](#fn_3391) Thou shalt not be covetous nor
rapacious: for says He, “Woe to him that is covetous towards his
neighbour with an evil covetousness.” <span
id="fr_3392"></span>[3393](#fn_3392)  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p55.2"></span>The Prohibition of Malignity, Acceptation of Persons, Wrath, Malice, and Envy.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p56"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p56.1"></span>V</span>. Thou shalt not be an hypocrite,
lest thy “portion be with them.” <span
id="fr_3393"></span>[3394](#fn_3393) Thou shalt not be ill-natured nor
proud: for “God resisteth the proud.” <span
id="fr_3394"></span>[3395](#fn_3394) “Thou shalt not accept persons in
judgment; for the judgment is the Lord’s.” “Thou shalt not hate any man;
thou shalt surely reprove thy brother, and not become guilty on his
account;” <span id="fr_3395"></span>[3396](#fn_3395) and, “Reprove a
wise man, and he will love thee.” <span
id="fr_3396"></span>[3397](#fn_3396) Eschew all evil, and all that is
like it: for says He, “Abstain from injustice, and trembling shall not
come nigh thee.” <span id="fr_3397"></span>[3398](#fn_3397) Be not soon
angry, nor spiteful, nor passionate, nor furious, nor daring, lest thou
undergo the fate of <span id="ix.viii.i-p61.2"></span>Cain, and of <span
id="ix.viii.i-p61.3"></span>Saul, and of <span
id="ix.viii.i-p61.4"></span>Joab: for the first of these slew his
brother <span id="ix.viii.i-p61.5"></span>Abel, because <span
id="ix.viii.i-p61.6"></span>Abel was found to be preferred before him
with God, and because <span id="ix.viii.i-p61.7"></span>Abel’s sacrifice
was preferred; <span id="fr_3398"></span>[3399](#fn_3398) the second
persecuted holy <span id="ix.viii.i-p62.2"></span>David, who had slain
<span id="ix.viii.i-p62.3"></span>Goliah the Philistine, being envious
of the praises of the women who danced; <span
id="fr_3399"></span>[3400](#fn_3399) the third slew two generals <span
id="page_467">p. 467</span> <span id="ix.viii.i-Page_467"></span>of
armies—<span id="ix.viii.i-p63.2"></span>Abner of Israel, and <span
id="ix.viii.i-p63.3"></span>Amasa of Judah. <span
id="fr_3400"></span>[3401](#fn_3400)  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p64.2"></span>Concerning Augury and Enchantments.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p65"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p65.1"></span>VI</span>. Be not a diviner, for that leads
to idolatry; <span id="fr_3401"></span>[3402](#fn_3401) for says Samuel,
“Divination is sin;” <span id="fr_3402"></span>[3403](#fn_3402) and,
“There shall be no divination in Jacob, nor soothsaying in
Israel.” <span id="fr_3403"></span>[3404](#fn_3403) Thou shalt not use
enchantments or purgations for thy child. Thou shall not be a soothsayer
nor a diviner by great or little birds. Nor shalt thou learn wicked
arts; for all these things has the law forbidden. <span
id="fr_3404"></span>[3405](#fn_3404) Be not one that wishes for evil,
for thou wilt be led into intolerable sins. Thou shalt not speak
obscenely, nor use wanton glances, nor be a drunkard; for from such
causes arise whoredoms and adulteries. Be not a lover of money, lest
thou “serve mammon instead of God.” <span
id="fr_3405"></span>[3406](#fn_3405) Be not vainglorious, nor haughty,
nor high-minded. For from all these things arrogance does spring.
Remember him who said: “Lord, my heart is not haughty, nor mine eyes
lofty: I have not exercised myself in great matters, nor in things too
high for me; but I was humble.” <span
id="fr_3406"></span>[3407](#fn_3406)  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p71.2"></span>The Prohibition of Murmuring, Insolence, Pride, and Arrogance.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p72"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p72.1"></span>VII</span>. Be not a murmurer, remembering
the punishment which those underwent who murmured against <span
id="ix.viii.i-p72.2"></span>Moses. Be not self-willed, be not malicious,
be not hard-hearted, be not passionate, be not mean-spirited; for all
these things lead to blasphemy. But be meek, as were <span
id="ix.viii.i-p72.3"></span>Moses and <span
id="ix.viii.i-p72.4"></span>David, <span
id="fr_3407"></span>[3408](#fn_3407) since “the meek shall inherit the
earth.” <span id="fr_3408"></span>[3409](#fn_3408)  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p74.2"></span>Concerning Long-Suffering, Simplicity, Meekness, and Patience.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p75"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p75.1"></span>VIII</span>. Be slow to wrath; for such a
one is very prudent, since “he that is hasty of spirit is a very
fool.” <span id="fr_3409"></span>[3410](#fn_3409) Be merciful; for
“blessed are the merciful: for they shall obtain mercy.” <span
id="fr_3410"></span>[3411](#fn_3410) Be sincere, quiet, good, “trembling
at the word of God.” <span id="fr_3411"></span>[3412](#fn_3411) Thou
shalt not exalt thyself, as did the Pharisee; for “every one that
exalteth himself shall be abased,” <span
id="fr_3412"></span>[3413](#fn_3412) and “that which is of high esteem
with man is abomination with God.” <span
id="fr_3413"></span>[3414](#fn_3413) Thou shalt not entertain confidence
in thy soul; for “a confident man shall fall into mischief.” <span
id="fr_3414"></span>[3415](#fn_3414) Thou shalt not go along with the
foolish, but with the wise and righteous; for “he that walketh <span
id="fr_3415"></span>[3416](#fn_3415) with wise men shall be wise, but he
that walketh with the foolish shall be known.” <span
id="fr_3416"></span>[3417](#fn_3416) Receive the afflictions that fall
upon thee with an even mind, and the chances of life without over-much
sorrow, knowing that a reward shall be given to thee by God, as was
given to <span id="ix.viii.i-p83.2"></span>Job and to <span
id="ix.viii.i-p83.3"></span>Lazarus. <span
id="fr_3417"></span>[3418](#fn_3417)  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p84.3"></span>That It is Our Duty to Esteem Our Christian Teachers Above Our Parents—The Former Being the Means of Our Well-Being, the Other Only of Our Being.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p85"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p85.1"></span>IX</span>. Thou shalt honour him that speaks
to thee the word of God, and be mindful of him day and night; and thou
shalt reverence him, <span id="fr_3418"></span>[3419](#fn_3418) not as
the author of thy birth, but as one that is made the occasion of thy
well-being. For where the doctrine concerning God is, there God is
present. Thou shalt every day seek the face of the saints, that thou
mayest acquiesce in their words.  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p86.2"></span>That We Ought Not to Divide Ourselves from the Saints, But to Make Peace Between Those that Quarrel, to Judge Righteously, and Not to Accept Persons.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p87"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p87.1"></span>X</span>. Thou shalt not make schisms among
the saints, but be mindful of the followers of <span
id="ix.viii.i-p87.2"></span>Corah. <span
id="fr_3419"></span>[3420](#fn_3419) Thou shalt make peace between those
that are at variance, as <span id="ix.viii.i-p88.2"></span>Moses did
when he persuaded them to be friends. <span
id="fr_3420"></span>[3421](#fn_3420) Thou shalt judge righteously; for
“the judgment is the Lord’s.” <span id="fr_3421"></span>[3422](#fn_3421)
Thou shalt not accept persons when thou reprovest for sins; but do as
<span id="ix.viii.i-p90.2"></span>Elijah and <span
id="ix.viii.i-p90.3"></span>Micaiah did to <span
id="ix.viii.i-p90.4"></span>Ahab, and <span
id="ix.viii.i-p90.5"></span>Ebedmelech the Ethiopian to <span
id="ix.viii.i-p90.6"></span>Zedekiah, and <span
id="ix.viii.i-p90.7"></span>Nathan to <span
id="ix.viii.i-p90.8"></span>David, and <span
id="ix.viii.i-p90.9"></span>John to <span
id="ix.viii.i-p90.10"></span>Herod. <span
id="fr_3422"></span>[3423](#fn_3422)  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p91.4"></span>Concerning Him that is Double-Minded and Desponding.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p92"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p92.1"></span>XI</span>. Be not of a doubtful mind in thy
prayer, whether it shall be granted or no. For the Lord said to me <span
id="ix.viii.i-p92.2"></span>Peter upon the sea: “O thou of little faith,
wherefore didst thou doubt?” <span id="fr_3423"></span>[3424](#fn_3423)
“Be not thou ready to stretch out thy hand to receive, and to shut it
when thou shouldst give.” <span id="fr_3424"></span>[3425](#fn_3424)
<span id="page_468">p. 468</span> <span id="ix.viii.i-Page_468"></span>
 

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p94.2"></span>Concerning Doing Good.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p95"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p95.1"></span>XII</span>. If thou hast by the work of thy
hands, give, that thou mayest labour for the redemption of thy sins; for
“by alms and acts of faith sins are purged away.” <span
id="fr_3425"></span>[3426](#fn_3425) Thou shalt not grudge to give to
the poor, nor when thou hast given shalt thou murmur; for thou shalt
know who will repay thee thy reward. For says he: “He that hath mercy on
the poor man lendeth to the Lord; according to his gift, so shall it be
repaid him again.” <span id="fr_3426"></span>[3427](#fn_3426) Thou shalt
not turn away from him that is needy; for says he: “He that stoppeth his
ears, that he may not hear the cry of the needy, himself also shall
call, and there shall be none to hear him.” <span
id="fr_3427"></span>[3428](#fn_3427) Thou shall communicate in all
things to thy brother, and shall not say <span
id="ix.viii.i-p98.2"></span>thy goods are thine own; for the common
participation of the necessaries of life is appointed to all men by God.
Thou shalt not take off thine hand from thy son or from thy daughter,
but shalt teach them the fear of God from their youth; for says he:
“Correct thy son, so shall he afford thee good hope.” <span
id="fr_3428"></span>[3429](#fn_3428)  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p99.2"></span>How Masters Ought to Behave Themselves to Their Servants, and How Servants Ought to Be Subject.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p100"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p100.1"></span>XIII</span>. Thou shall not command thy
man-servant, or thy maid-servant, who trust in the same God, with
bitterness of soul, lest they groan against thee, and wrath be upon thee
from God. And, ye servants, “be subject to your masters,” <span
id="fr_3429"></span>[3430](#fn_3429) as to the representatives of God,
with attention and fear, “as to the Lord, and not to men.” <span
id="fr_3430"></span>[3431](#fn_3430)  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p102.2"></span>Concerning Hypocrisy, and Obedience to the Laws, and Confession of Sins.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p103"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p103.1"></span>XIV</span>. Thou shalt hate all hypocrisy;
and whatsoever is pleasing to the Lord, that shalt thou do. By no means
forsake the commands of the Lord. But thou shalt observe what things
thou hast received from Him, neither adding to them nor taking away from
them. “For thou shalt not add unto His words, lest He convict thee, and
thou becomest a liar.” <span id="fr_3431"></span>[3432](#fn_3431) Thou
shalt confess thy sins unto the Lord thy God; and thou shalt not add
unto them, that it may be well with thee from the Lord thy God, who
willeth not the death of a sinner, but his repentance.  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p104.2"></span>Concerning the Observance Due to Parents.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p105"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p105.1"></span>XV</span>. Thou shalt be observant to thy
father and mother as the causes of thy being born, that thou mayest live
long on the earth which the Lord thy God giveth thee. Do not overlook
thy brethren or thy kinsfolk; for “thou shalt not overlook those nearly
related to thee.” <span id="fr_3432"></span>[3433](#fn_3432)  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p106.2"></span>Concerning the Subjection Due to the King and to Rulers.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p107"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p107.1"></span>XVI</span>. Thou shalt fear the king,
knowing that his appointment is of the Lord. His rulers thou shalt
honour as the ministers of God, for they are the revengers of all
unrighteousness; to whom pay taxes, tribute, and every oblation with a
willing mind.  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p107.2"></span>Concerning the Pure Conscience of Those that Pray.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p108"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p108.1"></span>XVII</span>. Thou shalt not proceed to thy
prayer in the day of thy wickedness, before thou hast laid aside thy
bitterness. This is the way of life, in which may ye be found, through
<span id="ix.viii.i-p108.2"></span>Jesus Christ our Lord.  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p108.3"></span>That the Way Which Was Afterward Introduced by the Snares of the Adversary is Full of Impiety and Wickedness.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p109"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p109.1"></span>XVIII</span>. But the way of death <span
id="fr_3433"></span>[3434](#fn_3433) is known by its wicked practices:
for therein is the ignorance of God, and the introduction of many evils,
and disorders, and disturbances; whereby come murders, adulteries,
fornications, perjuries, unlawful lusts, thefts, idolatries, magic arts,
witchcrafts, rapines, false-witnesses, hypocrisies, double-heartedness,
deceit, pride, malice, insolence, covetousness, obscene talk, jealousy,
confidence, haughtiness, arrogance, impudence, persecution of the good,
enmity to truth, love of lies, ignorance of righteousness. For they who
do such things do not adhere to goodness, or to righteous judgment: they
watch not for good, but for evil; from whom meekness and patience are
far off, who love vain things, pursuing after reward, having no pity on
the poor, not labouring for him that is in misery, nor knowing Him that
made them; murderers of infants, destroyers of the workmanship of God,
that turn away from the needy, adding affliction to the afflicted, the
flatterers of the rich, the despisers of the poor, full of sin. May you,
children, be delivered from all these.  

##### <span id="ix.viii.i-p110.3"></span>That We Must Not Turn from the Way of Piety Either to the Right Hand or to the Left. An Exhortation of the Lawgiver.

<span id="ix.viii.i-p111"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="ix.viii.i-p111.1"></span>XIX</span>. See that no one seduce
thee <span id="fr_3434"></span>[3435](#fn_3434) from piety; for says He:
“Thou mayst not turn aside from <span id="page_469">p. 469</span> <span
id="ix.viii.i-Page_469"></span>it to the right hand, or to the left,
that thou mayst have understanding in all that thou doest.” <span
id="fr_3435"></span>[3436](#fn_3435) For if thou dost not turn out of
the right way, thou wilt not be ungodly.
