### <span id="iii.vi.ii-p0.1"></span>A Poem on the Passion of the Lord

##### <span id="iii.vi.ii-p0.2"></span><span id="iii.vi.ii-p0.3"></span>Formerly Ascribed to <span id="iii.vi.ii-p0.4"></span>Lactantius

<span id="iii.vi.ii-p1"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iii.vi.ii-p1.1"></span>Whoever</span> you are who approach, and are
entering the precincts <span id="fr_2068"></span>[2069](#fn_2068) of the
middle of the temple, stop a little and look upon me, who, though
innocent, suffered for your crime; lay me up in your mind, keep me in
your breast. I am He who, pitying the bitter misfortunes of men, came
hither as a messenger <span id="fr_2069"></span>[2070](#fn_2069) of
offered peace, and as a full atonement <span
id="fr_2070"></span>[2071](#fn_2070) for the fault of men. <span
id="fr_2071"></span>[2072](#fn_2071) Here the brightest light from above
is restored to the earth; here is the merciful image of safety; here I
am a rest to you, the right way, the true redemption, the banner <span
id="fr_2072"></span>[2073](#fn_2072) of God, and a memorable sign of
fate. It was on account of you and your life that I entered the Virgin’s
womb, was made man, and suffered a dreadful death; nor did I find rest
anywhere in the regions of the earth, but everywhere threats, everywhere
labours. First of all a wretched dwelling <span
id="fr_2073"></span>[2074](#fn_2073) in the land of Judæa was a shelter
for me at my birth, and for my mother with me: here first, amidst the
outstretched sluggish cattle, dry grass gave me a bed in a narrow stall.
I passed my earliest years in the Pharian <span
id="fr_2074"></span>[2075](#fn_2074) regions, being an exile in the
reign of <span id="iii.vi.ii-p8.1"></span>Herod; and after my return to
Judæa I spent the rest of my years, always engaged <span
id="fr_2075"></span>[2076](#fn_2075) in fastings, and the extremity of
poverty itself, and the lowest circumstances; always by healthful
admonitions applying the minds of men to the pursuit of genial
uprightness, uniting with wholesome teaching many evident miracles: on
which account impious Jerusalem, harassed by the raging cares of envy
and cruel hatred, and blinded by madness, dared to seek for me, though
innocent, by deadly punishment, a cruel death on the dreadful cross. And
if you yourself wish to discriminate these things more fully, <span
id="fr_2076"></span>[2077](#fn_2076) and if it delights you to go
through all my groans, and to experience griefs with me, put
together <span id="fr_2077"></span>[2078](#fn_2077) the designs and
plots, and the impious price of my innocent blood, and the pretended
kisses of a disciple, <span id="fr_2078"></span>[2079](#fn_2078) and the
insults and strivings of the cruel multitude; and, moreover, the blows,
and tongues prepared <span id="fr_2079"></span>[2080](#fn_2079) for
accusations. Picture to your mind both the witnesses, and the
accursed <span id="fr_2080"></span>[2081](#fn_2080) judgment of the
blinded <span id="iii.vi.ii-p14.2"></span>Pilate, and the immense cross
pressing my shoulders and wearied back, and my painful steps to a
dreadful death. Now survey me from head to foot, deserted as I am, and
lifted up afar from my beloved mother. Behold and see my locks clotted
with blood, and my blood-stained neck under my very hair, and my head
drained <span id="fr_2081"></span>[2082](#fn_2081) with cruel thorns,
and pouring down like rain <span id="fr_2082"></span>[2083](#fn_2082)
from all sides a stream <span id="fr_2083"></span>[2084](#fn_2083) of
blood over my divine face. Survey my compressed and sightless eyes, and
my afflicted cheeks; see my parched tongue poisoned with gall, and my
countenance pale with death. Behold my hands pierced with nails, and my
arms drawn out, and the great wound in my side; see the blood streaming
from it, and my perforated <span id="fr_2084"></span>[2085](#fn_2084)
feet, and blood-stained limbs. Bend your knee, and with lamentation
adore the venerable wood of the cross, and with lowly countenance
stooping <span id="fr_2085"></span>[2086](#fn_2085) to the earth, which
is wet with innocent blood, sprinkle it with rising tears, and at
times <span id="fr_2086"></span>[2087](#fn_2086) bear me and my
admonitions in your devoted heart. Follow the footsteps of my life, and
while you look upon my torments and cruel death, remembering my
innumerable pangs of body and soul, learn to endure hardships, <span
id="fr_2087"></span>[2088](#fn_2087) and to watch over your own safety.
These memorials, <span id="fr_2088"></span>[2089](#fn_2088) if at any
time you find pleasure in thinking over them, if in your mind there is
any confidence to bear <span id="iii.vi.ii-p22.2"></span>anything like
my <span id="page_328">p. 328</span> <span
id="iii.vi.ii-Page_328"></span><span
id="iii.vi.ii-p22.3"></span>sufferings), <span
id="fr_2089"></span>[2090](#fn_2089) if the piety due, and gratitude
worthy of my labours shall arise, will be incitements <span
id="fr_2090"></span>[2091](#fn_2090) to true virtue, and they will be
shields against the snares of an enemy, aroused <span
id="fr_2091"></span>[2092](#fn_2091) by which you will be safe, and as a
conqueror bear off the palm in every contest. If these memorials shall
turn away your senses, which are devoted to a perishable <span
id="fr_2092"></span>[2093](#fn_2092) world, from the fleeting shadow of
earthly beauty, the result will be, that you will not venture, <span
id="fr_2093"></span>[2094](#fn_2093) enticed by empty hope, to trust the
frail <span id="fr_2094"></span>[2095](#fn_2094) enjoyments of fickle
fortune, and to place your hope in the fleeting years of life. But,
truly, if you thus regard this perishable world, <span
id="fr_2095"></span>[2096](#fn_2095) and through your love of a better
country deprive yourself <span id="fr_2096"></span>[2097](#fn_2096) of
earthly riches and the enjoyment of present things, <span
id="fr_2097"></span>[2098](#fn_2097) the prayers of the pious will bring
you up <span id="fr_2098"></span>[2099](#fn_2098) in sacred habits, and
in the hope of a happy life, amidst severe punishments, will cherish you
with heavenly dew, and feed you with the sweetness of the promised good.
Until the great favour of God shall recall your happy <span
id="fr_2099"></span>[2100](#fn_2099) soul to the heavenly regions, <span
id="fr_2100"></span>[2101](#fn_2100) your body being left after the
fates of death. Then freed from all labour, then joyfully beholding the
angelic choirs, and the blessed companies of saints in perpetual bliss,
it shall reign with me in the happy abode of perpetual peace.
