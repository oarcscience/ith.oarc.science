<span id="iii.i-p5"></span><span class="c8"><span

<span id="iii.i-p6"></span><span class="c18"><span
id="iii.i-p6.1"></span>Chapter I</span><span class="c1"><span
id="iii.i-p6.2"></span>.—*Literature*.</span>

<span id="iii.i-p7"></span><span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p7.1"></span>i. editions of chrysostom’s works.</span>

<span id="iii.i-p8"></span><span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p8.1"></span>S. Joannis Chrysostomi</span>, *archiepiscopi
Constantinopolitani, Opera omnia quæ exstant vel quæ ejus nomine
circumferuntur, ad <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p8.2"></span>mss.</span> codices Gallicos, Vaticanos,
Anglicos, Germanicosque castigata, etc. Opera et studio D.*<span
class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p8.3"></span>Bernardi de Montfaucon</span>,
*monachi ordinis S. Benedicti e congregatione S. Mauri, opem ferentibus
aliis ex codem sodalitio, monachis*. Greek and Latin, Paris, 1718–’38,
in 13 vols., fol. This is the best edition, and the result of about
twenty years of the patient labor of Montfaucon (d. Dec. 21, 1741, 86
years old), and several assistants of the brotherhood of St. Maur. More
than three hundred <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p8.4"></span>mss.</span> were made use of, but the eight
principal <span class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p8.5"></span>mss.</span>, as
Field has shown, were not very carefully collated. Montfaucon, who at
the date of the completion of his edition was 83 years old, prepared
valuable prefaces to every treatise and set of homilies, arranged the
works in chronological order, and added in vol. XIII. learned
dissertations on the life, doctrine, discipline and heresies of the age
of Chrysostom.

<span id="iii.i-p9"></span>The Benedictine edition was reprinted at
Venice, 1734–’41, in 13 vols. fol.; at Paris, ed. by <span
class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p9.1"></span>F. de Sinner (Gaume)</span>,
1834–’39, in 13 vols. (an elegant edition, with some additions); and,
with various improvements and corrections, by <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p9.2"></span>J. P. Migne</span>, Petit-Montrouge, 1859–’63, in
13 vols. The last is the most complete edition, but inferior in paper
and type to that of Gaume. Migne uses the critical text of Field in
Matthew and the Pauline Epp. He had previously edited a Latin Version,
1842, in 9 vols.

<span id="iii.i-p10"></span>The edition of Sir <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p10.1"></span>Henry Savile</span> (Provost of Eton), Etonæ,
1612, in 8 vols. fol., is less complete than the Benedictine edition,
but gives a more correct Greek text (as was shown by F. Dübner from a
collation of manuscripts) and valuable notes. Savile personally examined
the libraries of Europe and spent £8,000 on his edition. His wife was so
jealous of his devotion to Chrysostom that she threatened to burn his
manuscripts.

<span id="iii.i-p11"></span>The edition of <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p11.1"></span>Fronton le Duc</span>, a French Jesuit, and the
two brothers, <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p11.2"></span>Frederick</span> and <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p11.3"></span>Claude Morel</span>, was published at Paris,
1636, in 12 vols. fol., Greek and Latin.

<span id="iii.i-p12"></span>A selection of Chrysostom’s works (*Opera
præstantissima*) in Greek and Latin, was edited by <span
class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p12.1"></span>T. G. Lomler,</span>
Rudolphopoli (Rudolstadt), 1840 (unfinished).

<span id="iii.i-p13"></span>The best edition of the *Greek* text of the
*Homilies* on *Matthew*, and all the *Pauline Epistles* is by Dr. <span
class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p13.1"></span>Frederick Field</span>, of the
Church of England (d. 1883), in the “*Bibliotheca Patrum Ecclesiæ
Orientalis qui ante Orientis et Occidentis schisma floruerunt*.” The
Homilies on Matthew appeared at Cambridge, 1839, 3 vols.; the Homilies
on the Epistles of Paul and the Hebrews, Oxford, 1839–’62, in 7 vols.

<span id="iii.i-p14"></span>The treatise *De Sacerdotio* (<span
class="Greek"><span id="iii.i-p14.1"></span>περί ἱερωσύνης</span>) was
separately edited by <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p14.2"></span>Erasmus</span> in Greek (Basel, 1525, from the
press of Frobenius), by <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p14.3"></span>J. Hughes</span>, in Greek and Latin (Cambridge,
1710), and by <span class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p14.4"></span>J. A.
Bengel</span>, the com<span id="page_4">p. 4</span> <span
id="iii.i-Page_4"></span>mentator, in Greek (Stuttgart, 1725, and
repeatedly reprinted since at Leipzig, 1825, 1834, 1872, by C.
Tauchnitz). <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p14.5"></span>Lomler</span> (*Chrys. Opera*, pp. viii. and
ix.) enumerates twenty-three separate editions and translations of the
treatise on the Priesthood.

<span id="iii.i-p15"></span><span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p15.1"></span>ii. translations</span>.—(*a*) <span
class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p15.2"></span>german translations.</span>

<span id="iii.i-p16"></span>The treatise on the Priesthood has been
translated by <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p16.1"></span>Hasselbach</span>, 1820; <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p16.2"></span>Ritter</span>, 1821, and others. The *<span
id="iii.i-p16.3"></span>Bibliothek der Kirchenväter* (Rom. Cath.),
published at Kempten in Bavaria, devotes ten small volumes to St.
Chrysostom, including the *Priesthood*, ascetic *Treatises*, and
*Homilies*, translated by <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p16.4"></span>Joh. Chrysostomus Mitterrutzner</span>,
1869–’84. German translations of selected *Homilies* by <span
class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p16.5"></span>J. A. Cramer</span> (Leipzig,
1748–’51, 10 vols.); <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p16.6"></span>Feder</span> (Augsburg, 1786); <span
class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p16.7"></span> Ph. Mayer</span> (Nürnberg,
1830); <span class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p16.8"></span>W. Arnoldi</span>
(Trier, 1835); <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p16.9"></span>Augusti</span> (*Predigten der Kirchenväter*,
vols. I. and II., Leipzig, 1839); <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p16.10"></span>Jos. Lutz</span> (Tübingen, 2d ed. 1859); <span
class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p16.11"></span>Gust. Leonhardi</span>
(Leipzig, 1888, selected sermons and orations, in vol I. of
*Klassikerbibliothek der Christl. Predigtliteratur*).

<span id="iii.i-p17"></span>(*b*) <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p17.1"></span>english translations.</span>

<span id="iii.i-p18"></span>The work on the *Priesthood* was translated
by <span class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p18.1"></span>Hollier</span>
(London, 1728); <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p18.2"></span>Bunce</span> (London, 1759); <span
class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p18.3"></span> Hohler</span> (Cambridge,
1837); <span class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p18.4"></span>Marsh</span>
(London, 1844); <span class="c12"><span id="iii.i-p18.5"></span>Harris
Cowper</span> (London, 1866); and <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.i-p18.6"></span>Stephens</span> (N. York, 1888, prepared for
this “Library”).

<span id="iii.i-p19"></span>The *Homilies on the Statues* and on the
*New Testament* were translated by several scholars for the “Oxford
Library of the Fathers,” 1839–’77, 16 vols. The earlier parts (on the
First Epistle to the Corinthians, and on the Statues) are based on the
text of Montfaucon and Savile, the later parts on the improved text of
Field. The Oxford translation has been revised and annotated by American
scholars for this “Library,” and new translations of other works of St.
Chrysostom have been added, namely, the treatise on the Priesthood, the
Exhortation to the fallen Theodore, Letters, Tracts, and Special
Homilies (in this first volume).
