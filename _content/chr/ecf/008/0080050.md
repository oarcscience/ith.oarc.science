<span id="v.iv.xi-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="v.iv.xi-p1.1"></span>Chapter XI.—Admonitory History of the
Incestuous Children of David.</span>

<span id="v.iv.xi-p2"></span>Hast thou not read concerning Amnon and
Tamar, the children of David?  This Amnon conceived a passion for his
sister, and humbled her, and did not spare her, because he longed for
her with a shameful passion; and he proved wicked and profligate because
of his constant intercourse with her, without the fear of God, and he
“wrought uncleanness in Israel.” <span id="fr_482"></span>[483](#fn_482)
  Therefore, it is not proper for us, nor right for us, to associate
with sisters, *indulging* in laughter and looseness; but *we ought to
behave towards them* with all chasteness and purity, and in the fear of
the Lord.
