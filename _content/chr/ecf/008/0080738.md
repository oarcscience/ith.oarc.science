<span id="vi.iv.vi.li-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.vi.li-p1.1"></span>Chapter LI.—His Teaching Concerning the
Law.</span>

<span id="vi.iv.vi.li-p2"></span>“And His sending to the scribes and
teachers of the existing Scriptures, as to those who knew the true
things of the law that then was, is well known.  And also that He said,
‘I am not come to destroy the law,’ <span
id="fr_995"></span>[996](#fn_995) and yet that He appeared to be
destroying it, is the part of one intimating that the things which He
destroyed did not belong to the law.  And His saying, ‘The heaven and
the earth shall pass away, but one jot or one tittle shall not pass from
the law,’ <span id="fr_996"></span>[997](#fn_996) intimated that the
things which pass away before the heaven and the earth do not belong to
the law in reality.
