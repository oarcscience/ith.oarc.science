<span id="vi.iv.xix.xi-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.xix.xi-p1.1"></span>Chapter XI.—</span>[Gen. I.
26](../../../bib/kjv/gen001.htm#026)<span id="vi.iv.xix.xi-p1.2"></span>
<span class="c1"><span id="vi.iv.xix.xi-p1.3"></span>Appealed to by
Simon.</span>

<span id="vi.iv.xix.xi-p2"></span>And Simon said:  “Since I see that you
frequently speak of the God who created you, learn from me how you are
impious even to him.  For there are evidently two who created, as the
Scripture says: <span id="fr_1288"></span>[1289](#fn_1288)   ‘And God
said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness.’  Now ‘let us
make,’ implies two or more; certainly not one only.”
