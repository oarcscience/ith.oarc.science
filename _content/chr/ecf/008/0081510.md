<span id="x.v.xiii-p2"></span><span class="c1"><span

13](../../../bib/kjv/gen022.htm#013)<span

</span>

<span id="x.v.xiii-p4"></span>The Syriac and the Hebrew use the word
“suspended,” <span id="fr_3628"></span>[3629](#fn_3628) as more clearly
typifying the cross.

<span id="x.v.xiii-p6"></span>The word Sabek <span
id="fr_3629"></span>[3630](#fn_3629) some have rendered
*remission*, <span id="fr_3630"></span>[3631](#fn_3630) others
*upright*, <span id="fr_3631"></span>[3632](#fn_3631) as if the meaning,
agreeing with the popular belief, were—a goat walking erect up to a
bush, and there standing erect caught by his horns, so as to be a plain
type of the cross.  For this reason it is not translated, because the
single Hebrew word signifies in other languages <span
id="fr_3632"></span>[3633](#fn_3632) many things.  To those, however,
who ask it is proper to give an answer, and to say that Sabek denotes
*lifted up*. <span id="fr_3633"></span>[3634](#fn_3633)
