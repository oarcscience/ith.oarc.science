<span id="xiv.iii.xcv-Page_405"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xcv-p1.1"></span>Canon XCIV.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xcv-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xcv-p2.1"></span>The</span> canon subjects to penalties
those who take heathen oaths, and we decree to them excommunication.

<span id="xiv.iii.xcv-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xcv-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xcv-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xcv-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XCIV.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xcv-p5"></span>Whoever uses Gentile oaths, is worthy
of punishment, for he is cut off.

<span id="xiv.iii.xcv-p6"></span>The reference is to canon lxxxj. of St.
Basil’s canons.

<span id="xiv.iii.xcv-p7"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xcv-p7.1"></span>Van Espen.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xcv-p8"></span>Tertullian (*De Idolatria*, cap. xx.)
supposes that to swear by the false gods of the Gentiles, contains in
itself some idolatry, an opinion shared by St. Basil, comparing those
using such oaths with them who betrayed Christ, and who are partakers of
the talk of devils.
