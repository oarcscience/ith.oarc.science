<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-Page_457"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p1.1"></span>Canon XXX.  (Greek xxxiii.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p2"></span>*Concerning the accused or
accuser*. <span id="fr_432"></span>[433](#fn_432)

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p4.1"></span>Likewise</span> it seemed good that the
accused, or the accusor, if (living in the same place as the accused) he
fears some evil may be done him by the tumultuous multitude, may choose
for himself a place near by, where the cause may be determined, and
where there will be no difficulty in producing the witnesses.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXX.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxi-p6"></span>*Accuser or accused may select for
himself a safe place if he fears violence.*
