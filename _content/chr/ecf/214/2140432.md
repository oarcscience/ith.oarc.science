<span id="xiv.iii.xlix-p1"></span> <span id="page_388">p. 388</span>
<span id="xiv.iii.xlix-Page_388"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xlix-p1.1"></span>Canon XLVIII.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xlix-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xlix-p2.1"></span>The</span> wife of him who is advanced to
the Episcopal dignity, shall be separated from her husband by their
mutual consent, and after his ordination and consecration to the
episcopate she shall enter a monastery situated at a distance from the
abode of the bishop, and there let her enjoy the bishop’s provision. 
And if she is deemed worthy she may be advanced to the dignity of a
deaconess.

<span id="xiv.iii.xlix-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xlix-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xlix-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xlix-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XLVIII.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xlix-p5"></span>*She who is separated from one about
to be consecrated bishop, shall enter a monastery after his ordination,
situated at a distance from the See city, and she shall be provided for
by the bishop.*
