<span id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p1.1"></span>Canon CIV.  (Greek cvii.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p2"></span>Of these who ask from the Emperor that
secular judges may take cognizance of their causes.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p3.1"></span>It</span> seemed good that whoever should
seek from the Emperor, that secular judges should take cognizance of his
business, should be deprived of his office; if however, he had asked
from the Emperor an episcopal trial, no objection should be made.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon CIV.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p6"></span>Let not him be a bishop who from the
Emperor seeks a public judgment.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p7"></span>This canon is Canon X. of Carthage,
<span class="sc"><span id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p7.1"></span>a.d.</span> 407.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p8"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p8.1"></span>Johnson</span>.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cv-p9"></span>See Canon Ant., 12.
