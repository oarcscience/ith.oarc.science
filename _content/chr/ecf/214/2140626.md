<span id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-p1.1"></span>Canon CII.  (Greek cv.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-p2"></span>Of those who put away their wives or
husbands, that so they remain.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-p3.1"></span>It</span> seemed good that according to
evangelical and apostolical discipline a man who had been put away from
his wife, and a woman put away from her husband should not be married to
another, but so should remain, or else be reconciled the one to the
other; <span id="page_494">p. 494</span> <span
id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-Page_494"></span>but if they spurn this law, they
shall be forced to do penance, covering which case we must petition that
an imperial law be promulgated.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon CII.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-p6"></span>Married people who are loosed must
remain unmarried or else be reconciled, otherwise they shall be forced
to do penance.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-p7"></span>This canon is Canon viij. of
Carthage, <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.ciii-p7.1"></span>a.d.</span> 407, and is found in the
*Corpus Juris Canonici*, Gratian’s *Decretum*, P. II., Causa xxxij.,
Quæst. vij., can. v.
