<span id="viii.vii.iii.xiii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.xiii-p1.1"></span>Canon XII.</span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.xiii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.xiii-p2.1"></span>Bishops</span> are to be appointed to
the ecclesiastical government by the judgment of the metropolitans and
neighbouring bishops, after having been long proved both in the
foundation of their faith and in the conversation of an honest life.

<span id="viii.vii.iii.xiii-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.xiii-p3.1"></span>Note.</span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.xiii-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.xiii-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XII.</span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.xiii-p5"></span>Whoever is most approved in faith
and life and most learned, he is fit to be chosen bishop.

<span id="viii.vii.iii.xiii-p6"></span>The first part of this canon is
in conformity with the provision in the IV. canon of Nice.
