<span id="xiv.iii.lxxv-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.lxxv-p1.1"></span>Canon LXXIV.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxv-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.lxxv-p2.1"></span>It</span> is not permitted to hold what
are called Agapæ, that is love-feasts, in the Lord’s houses or churches,
nor to eat within the house, nor to spread couches.  If any dare to do
so let him cease therefrom or be cut off.

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxv-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.lxxv-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxv-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.lxxv-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXIV.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxv-p5"></span>Agapæ are not to be held in the
churches, nor shall beds be put up.  Whoso refuse to give up these, let
them be cut off.

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxv-p6"></span>This is a renewal of canon xxviij., of
Laodicea, on which canon see the notes.
