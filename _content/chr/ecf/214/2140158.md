<span id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-p1.1"></span>Canon XXI.</span>

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-p2"></span>A <span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-p2.1"></span>bishop</span> may not be translated from
one parish to another, either intruding himself of his own suggestion,
or under compulsion by the people, or by constraint of the bishops; but
he shall remain in the Church to which he was allotted by God from the
<span id="page_119">p. 119</span> <span
id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-Page_119"></span>beginning, and shall not be
translated from it, according to the decree formerly passed on the
subject.

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXI.</span>

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-p5"></span>A bishop even if compelled by the
people, and compelled by the bishops, must not be translated to another
diocese.

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-p6"></span>See the treatment of the translation
of bishops in the Excursus to canon xv. of Nice.

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-p7"></span>Compare this canon with Apostolical
Canon number xiv.

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xxi-p8"></span>This canon is found in the *Corpus
Juris Canonici*, Gratian’s *Decretum*, Pars II., Causa VII., Quæst. I.,
can. xxv., from Isidore’s version.
