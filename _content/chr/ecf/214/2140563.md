<span id="xv.iv.iv.xl-Page_461"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p1.1"></span>Canon XXXIX.  (Greek xlii.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p2"></span>*That a bishop should not be called the
chief of the priests.* <span id="fr_440"></span>[441](#fn_440)

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p4.1"></span>That</span> the bishop of the first see
shall not be called Prince of the Priests or High Priest (*Summus
Sacerdos*) or any other name of this kind, but only Bishop of the First
See.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p5"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p5.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p6"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p6.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXXIX.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p7"></span>The first bishop shall not be called
Prince of the Priests nor High Priest but Bishop of the first see.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p8"></span>This canon is Canon xxv. of the Synod
of Hippo, <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p8.1"></span>a.d.</span> 393.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p9"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p9.1"></span>Johnson.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p10"></span>“The bishop of the Prime See,” i.e.,
The primate.  So Xantippus is called bishop of the Prime.  So in
Numidia, Nicetius in Mauritania, in the original Latin between Can. 85,
and Can. 86, and see Can. 86.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xl-p11"></span>N.B.  Justellus on this canon shews,
that Tertullian, Optatus, and Augustine, did apply these titles to their
own African bishops; and therefore supposes, that the meaning of the
canon was to suppress the flame of vain glory, which proceeded from
these sparks of lofty titles.
