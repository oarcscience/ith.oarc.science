<span id="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p1.1"></span>Canon LXXXVI.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p2.1"></span>Those</span> who to the destruction of
their own souls procure and bring up harlots, if they be clerics, they
are to be \[cut off and\] deposed, if laymen to be cut off.

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXXVI.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p5"></span>Whoever gathers together harlots to
the ruin of souls, shall be cut off.

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxxvii-p6"></span>The brackets enclose the reading of
Hervetus.  But Zonaras had this same text, and therefore it may be
safely followed instead of that of Balsamon, as edited by Beveridge.
