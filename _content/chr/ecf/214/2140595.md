<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii"></span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p1.1"></span>Canon LXXI.  (Greek lxxiv.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p2"></span>Of those who leave in neglect their
own people.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p3.1"></span>Moreover</span> it seemed good that no
one should be allowed to leave his chief cathedral and go to another
church built in the diocese, or to neglect the care and frequent
attendance upon his own cathedral by reason of too great care for his
own affairs.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXI.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p6"></span>It seemed good that no bishop shall
translate himself to another see, leaving his own, nor that through a
care for his own affairs he should neglect his diocese.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p7"></span>This is Canon vj. of Carthage,
September, <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p7.1"></span>a.d.</span> 401.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p8"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p8.1"></span>Johnson.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p9"></span>See Canons 53 (57), 56 (60).

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxii-p10"></span>“Principalis Cathedra,” his own
Cathedral.
