<span id="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p2.1"></span>Marriages</span> and birthday feasts
are not to be celebrated in Lent.

<span id="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon LII.</span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p5"></span>Marriage shall not be celebrated
in Lent, nor birthdays.

<span id="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p6"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p6.1"></span>Hefele.</span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.lvii-p7"></span>By “birthday feasts” in this
canon the *natalitia martyrum* is not to be understood as in the
preceding canon, but the birthday feasts of princes.  This, as well as
the preceding rule, was renewed in the sixth century by Bishop Martin of
Bracara, now Braga, in Portugal.
