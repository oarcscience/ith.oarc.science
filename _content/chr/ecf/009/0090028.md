<span id="iv.iii.xix-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xix-p1.1"></span>Section XIX.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xix-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_1331"></span>[1332](#fn_1331) And in the fourth watch of the
night Jesus came unto them, walking upon the \[2\]  <span
id="fr_1332"></span>[1333](#fn_1332) water, after they had rowed <span
id="fr_1333"></span>[1334](#fn_1333) with difficulty about twenty-five
or thirty furlongs.  <span id="page_73">p. 73</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xix-Page_73"></span>\[3\]  <span
id="fr_1334"></span>[1335](#fn_1334) And when he drew near unto their
boat, his disciples saw him walking on the water; and they were
troubled, and supposed that it was a false appearance; and they cried
\[4\] out from their fear.   <span id="fr_1335"></span>[1336](#fn_1335)
But Jesus straightway spoke unto them, and said, Take courage, \[5\] for
it is I; fear not.   <span id="fr_1336"></span>[1337](#fn_1336) Then
Cephas answered and said unto him, My Lord, if it be thou, \[6\] bid me
to come unto thee on the water.   <span
id="fr_1337"></span>[1338](#fn_1337) And Jesus said unto him, Come.  And
\[7\] Cephas went down out of the boat, and walked on the water to come
unto Jesus.   <span id="fr_1338"></span>[1339](#fn_1338) But \[Arabic,
p. 74\] when he saw the wind strong, he feared, and was on the point of
sinking; \[8\] and he lifted up his voice, and said, My Lord, save me. 
 <span id="fr_1339"></span>[1340](#fn_1339) And immediately our Lord
stretched out his hand and took *hold of* him, and said unto him, \[9\]
Thou of little faith, why didst thou doubt?   <span
id="fr_1340"></span>[1341](#fn_1340) And when Jesus came near, he went
up \[10\] unto them into the boat, he and Simon, and immediately the
wind ceased.   <span id="fr_1341"></span>[1342](#fn_1341) And those that
were in the ship came and worshipped him, and said, Truly thou art the
\[11\] Son of God.   <span id="fr_1342"></span>[1343](#fn_1342) And
straightway that ship arrived at the land which they made for.  \[12\]
 <span id="fr_1343"></span>[1344](#fn_1343) And when they came out of
the ship to the land, they marvelled greatly and were \[13\] perplexed
in themselves:   <span id="fr_1344"></span>[1345](#fn_1344) and they had
not understood by means of <span id="fr_1345"></span>[1346](#fn_1345)
that bread, because their heart was gross.

<span id="iv.iii.xix-p18"></span>\[14\]  <span
id="fr_1346"></span>[1347](#fn_1346) And when the people of that region
knew of the arrival of Jesus, they made haste in all that land, and
began to bring those that were diseased, <span
id="fr_1347"></span>[1348](#fn_1347) borne in their \[15\] beds to the
place where they heard that he was.   <span
id="fr_1348"></span>[1349](#fn_1348) And wheresoever the place *might
be* which he entered, of the villages or the cities, they laid the sick
in the markets, and sought of him that they might touch <span
id="fr_1349"></span>[1350](#fn_1349) were it only the edge of his
garment:  and all that touched <span
id="fr_1350"></span>[1351](#fn_1350) him were healed and lived. <span
id="fr_1351"></span>[1352](#fn_1351)

<span id="iv.iii.xix-p25"></span>\[16\]  <span
id="fr_1352"></span>[1353](#fn_1352) And on the day after that, the
multitude which was standing on the shore of the sea saw that there was
there no other ship save that into which the disciples had \[17\] gone
up,  <span id="fr_1353"></span>[1354](#fn_1353) and that Jesus went not
up into the ship with his disciples (but there were other ships from
Tiberias near <span id="fr_1354"></span>[1355](#fn_1354) the place where
they ate the bread when Jesus blessed \[18\] *it*):   <span
id="fr_1355"></span>[1356](#fn_1355) and when that multitude saw that
Jesus was not there, nor yet his disciples, they \[19\] \[Arabic, p.
75\] went up into those ships, and came to Capernaum, and sought Jesus. 
 <span id="fr_1356"></span>[1357](#fn_1356) And when they found him on
the other side of the sea, they said unto him, Our \[20\] Master, when
camest thou hither?   <span id="fr_1357"></span>[1358](#fn_1357) Jesus
answered and said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, Ye have not
sought me because of <span id="fr_1358"></span>[1359](#fn_1358) your
seeing the signs, but because of \[21\] your eating the bread and being
satisfied.   <span id="fr_1359"></span>[1360](#fn_1359) Serve not the
food which perisheth, but the food which abideth in eternal life, <span
id="fr_1360"></span>[1361](#fn_1360) which the Son of man will give unto
you:  him <span id="fr_1361"></span>[1362](#fn_1361) \[22\] hath God the
Father sealed.   <span id="fr_1362"></span>[1363](#fn_1362) They said
unto him, What shall we do that we may \[23\] work the work of God? 
 <span id="fr_1363"></span>[1364](#fn_1363) Jesus answered and said unto
them, This is the work of \[24\] God, that ye believe in him whom he
hath sent.   <span id="fr_1364"></span>[1365](#fn_1364) They said unto
him, What sign hast thou done, that we may see, and believe in thee?
what hast thou wrought?  \[25\]  <span
id="fr_1365"></span>[1366](#fn_1365) Our fathers ate the manna in the
wilderness; as it was written, Bread from heaven \[26\] gave he them to
eat.   <span id="fr_1366"></span>[1367](#fn_1366) Jesus said unto them,
Verily, verily, I say unto you, Moses gave you not bread from \[27\]
heaven; but my Father gave <span id="fr_1367"></span>[1368](#fn_1367)
you the bread of truth <span id="fr_1368"></span>[1369](#fn_1368) from
heaven.   <span id="fr_1369"></span>[1370](#fn_1369) The bread of God is
that which came down from heaven and gave the \[28, 29\] world life. 
 <span id="fr_1370"></span>[1371](#fn_1370) They said unto him, Our
Lord, give us at all times this bread.   <span
id="fr_1371"></span>[1372](#fn_1371) Jesus said unto them, I am the
bread of life:  whosoever cometh unto me shall not hunger, \[30\] and
whosoever believeth in me shall not thirst for ever.   <span
id="fr_1372"></span>[1373](#fn_1372) But I said unto you, \[31\] Ye have
seen me, and have not believed.   <span
id="fr_1373"></span>[1374](#fn_1373) And all that my Father hath given
to me cometh unto me; and whosoever cometh unto me I shall not cast him
forth without.  \[32\]  <span id="fr_1374"></span>[1375](#fn_1374) I
came down from heaven, not to do my *own* will, but to do the will of
him \[33\] that sent me;  <span id="fr_1375"></span>[1376](#fn_1375) and
this is the will of him that sent me, that I should lose nothing of
\[34\] \[Arabic, p. 76\] that which he gave me, but raise it up in the
last day.   <span id="fr_1376"></span>[1377](#fn_1376) This is the will
of my Father, that every one that seeth the Son, and believeth in him,
should have eternal life; and I will raise him up in the last day.

<span id="iv.iii.xix-p51"></span>\[35\]  <span
id="fr_1377"></span>[1378](#fn_1377) The Jews therefore murmured against
him because of his saying, I am the bread \[36\] which came down from
heaven.   <span id="fr_1378"></span>[1379](#fn_1378) And they said, Is
not this Jesus, the son of Joseph, whose father and mother we know? then
how saith this *man*, I came down from \[37, 38\] heaven?   <span
id="fr_1379"></span>[1380](#fn_1379) Jesus answered and said unto them,
Murmur not one with another.   <span
id="fr_1380"></span>[1381](#fn_1380) No <span id="page_74">p. 74</span>
<span id="iv.iii.xix-Page_74"></span>man is able to come unto me, except
the Father which sent me draw him; and I will \[39\] raise him up in the
last day.   <span id="fr_1381"></span>[1382](#fn_1381) It is written in
the prophet, They shall all be the taught of God.  Every one who heareth
from the Father now, <span id="fr_1382"></span>[1383](#fn_1382) and
learneth of him, cometh \[40\] unto me.   <span
id="fr_1383"></span>[1384](#fn_1383) No man now seeth the Father; but he
that is from God, he it is that seeth \[41\] the Father.   <span
id="fr_1384"></span>[1385](#fn_1384) Verily, verily, I say unto you,
Whosoever believeth in me hath eternal \[42, 43\] life.   <span
id="fr_1385"></span>[1386](#fn_1385) I am the bread of life.   <span
id="fr_1386"></span>[1387](#fn_1386) Your fathers ate the manna in the
wilderness, and \[44\] they died.   <span
id="fr_1387"></span>[1388](#fn_1387) This is the bread which came down
from heaven, that a man may eat \[45\] of it, and not die.   <span
id="fr_1388"></span>[1389](#fn_1388) I am the bread of life which came
down from heaven:  and if a man eat of this bread he shall live for
ever:   <span id="fr_1389"></span>[1390](#fn_1389) and the bread which I
shall give is my body, which I give for the life of the world.

<span id="iv.iii.xix-p65"></span>\[46\]  <span
id="fr_1390"></span>[1391](#fn_1390) The Jews therefore quarrelled one
with another, and said, How can he give us \[47\] \[Arabic, p. 77\] his
body that we may eat it?   <span id="fr_1391"></span>[1392](#fn_1391)
Jesus said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, If ye do not eat
the body of the Son of man and drink his blood, ye shall \[48\] not have
life in yourselves.   <span id="fr_1392"></span>[1393](#fn_1392)
Whosoever eateth of my body and drinketh of my blood \[49\] hath eternal
life; and I will raise him up in the last day.   <span
id="fr_1393"></span>[1394](#fn_1393) My body truly is meat, <span
id="fr_1394"></span>[1395](#fn_1394) and \[50\] my blood truly is
drink. <span id="fr_1395"></span>[1396](#fn_1395)    <span
id="fr_1396"></span>[1397](#fn_1396) Whosoever eateth my body and
drinketh my blood abideth \[51\] in me, and I in him— <span
id="fr_1397"></span>[1398](#fn_1397) as the living Father sent me, and I
am alive because of the \[52\] Father; and whosoever eateth me, he also
shall live because of me.   <span id="fr_1398"></span>[1399](#fn_1398)
This is the bread which came down from heaven:  and not according as
your fathers ate the \[53\] manna, and died:  whosoever eateth of this
bread shall live for ever.   <span id="fr_1399"></span>[1400](#fn_1399)
This he said in \[54\] the synagogue, when he was teaching in
Capernaum.   <span id="fr_1400"></span>[1401](#fn_1400) And many of his
disciples, when they heard, said, This word is hard; who is he that can
hear it?
