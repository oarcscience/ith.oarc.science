<span id="iv.iii.xxxiii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xxxiii-p1.1"></span>Section XXXIII.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiii-p2"></span>\[1\] \[Arabic, p. 125\]  <span
id="fr_2240"></span>[2241](#fn_2240) And when evening came, Jesus went
forth outside of the city, he and his \[2\] disciples.   <span
id="fr_2241"></span>[2242](#fn_2241) And as they passed in the morning,
the disciples saw that fig tree \[3\] withered away from its root. 
 <span id="fr_2242"></span>[2243](#fn_2242) And they passed by, and
said, How did the fig tree dry \[4\] up immediately?   <span
id="fr_2243"></span>[2244](#fn_2243) And Simon remembered, and said unto
him, My Master, behold, <span id="page_94">p. 94</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xxxiii-Page_94"></span>\[5\] that fig tree which thou didst
curse hath dried up.   <span id="fr_2244"></span>[2245](#fn_2244) And
Jesus answered and said \[6\] unto them, Let there be in you the faith
of God.   <span id="fr_2245"></span>[2246](#fn_2245) Verily I say unto
you, if ye believe, and doubt not in your hearts, and assure yourselves
that that will be which \[7\] ye say, ye shall have what ye say.   <span
id="fr_2246"></span>[2247](#fn_2246) And if ye say to this mountain,
Remove, and \[8\] fall <span id="fr_2247"></span>[2248](#fn_2247) into
the sea, it shall be.   <span id="fr_2248"></span>[2249](#fn_2248) And
all that ye ask God in prayer, and believe, he \[9, 10\] will give you. 
 <span id="fr_2249"></span>[2250](#fn_2249) And the apostles <span
id="fr_2250"></span>[2251](#fn_2250) said unto our Lord, Increase
our <span id="fr_2251"></span>[2252](#fn_2251) faith.   <span
id="fr_2252"></span>[2253](#fn_2252) He said unto them, If there be in
you faith like a grain of mustard, ye shall say to this fig tree, Be
thou torn up, and be thou planted in the sea; and it will obey you. 
\[11\]  <span id="fr_2253"></span>[2254](#fn_2253) Who of you hath a
servant driving a yoke of oxen or tending sheep, and if he \[12\] come
from the field, will say unto him straightway, Go and sit down?   <span
id="fr_2254"></span>[2255](#fn_2254) Nay, <span
id="fr_2255"></span>[2256](#fn_2255) he will say unto him, Make ready
for me wherewith I may sup, and gird thy waist, and serve me, till I eat
and drink; and afterwards thou shalt eat and drink also.  \[13\]  <span
id="fr_2256"></span>[2257](#fn_2256) Doth that servant haply, who did
what he was bid, receive his praise?  I think \[14\] not.   <span
id="fr_2257"></span>[2258](#fn_2257) So ye also, when ye have done all
that ye were bid, say, We are idle servants; what it was our duty to do,
we have done.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiii-p21"></span>\[15\]  <span
id="fr_2258"></span>[2259](#fn_2258) For this reason I say unto you,
Whatever ye pray and ask, believe that ye \[16\] \[Arabic, p. 126\]
receive, and ye shall have.   <span id="fr_2259"></span>[2260](#fn_2259)
And when ye stand to pray, forgive what is in your heart against *any*
man; and your Father which is in heaven will \[17\] forgive you also
your wrong-doings.   <span id="fr_2260"></span>[2261](#fn_2260) But if
ye forgive not men their wrong-doings, neither will your Father forgive
you also your wrong-doings.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiii-p25"></span>\[18\]  <span
id="fr_2261"></span>[2262](#fn_2261) And he spake unto them a parable
also, that they should pray at all times, and \[19\] not be slothful: 
 <span id="fr_2262"></span>[2263](#fn_2262) There was a judge in a city,
who feared not God, nor was ashamed \[20\] for men:   <span
id="fr_2263"></span>[2264](#fn_2263) and there was a widow in that city;
and she came unto him, and said, \[21\] Avenge me of mine adversary. 
 <span id="fr_2264"></span>[2265](#fn_2264) And he would not for a long
time:  but afterwards he said within himself, If of God I have no fear,
and before men I have no shame; \[22\]  <span
id="fr_2265"></span>[2266](#fn_2265) *yet* because this widow vexeth
*me*, I will avenge her, that she come not at all times \[23, 24\] and
annoy me.   <span id="fr_2266"></span>[2267](#fn_2266) And our Lord
said, Hear ye what the judge of injustice said.   <span
id="fr_2267"></span>[2268](#fn_2267) And shall not God *still* more do
vengeance for his elect, who call upon him in the night \[25\] and *in*
the day, and grant them respite?   <span
id="fr_2268"></span>[2269](#fn_2268) I say unto you, He will do
vengeance for them speedily. Thinkest thou the Son of man will come and
find faith on the earth?

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiii-p34"></span>\[26, 27\]  <span
id="fr_2269"></span>[2270](#fn_2269) And they came again to Jerusalem. 
 <span id="fr_2270"></span>[2271](#fn_2270) And it came to pass, on one
of the days, as Jesus was walking in the temple, and teaching the
people, and preaching *the* \[28\] *gospel*,  <span
id="fr_2271"></span>[2272](#fn_2271) that the chief priests and the
scribes with the elders came upon him, and said unto him, Tell us: 
 <span id="fr_2272"></span>[2273](#fn_2272) By what power doest thou
this? and who gave thee this \[29\] power to do that?   <span
id="fr_2273"></span>[2274](#fn_2273) And Jesus said unto them, I also
will ask you one word, and if \[30\] ye tell me, I also shall tell you
by what power I do that.   <span id="fr_2274"></span>[2275](#fn_2274)
The baptism of John, from \[31\] what place *is* it? from heaven or of
men?   <span id="fr_2275"></span>[2276](#fn_2275) Tell me.   <span
id="fr_2276"></span>[2277](#fn_2276) And they reflected within
themselves, \[Arabic, p. 127\] and said, If we shall say unto him, From
heaven; he will say unto \[32\] us, For what reason did ye not believe
him?   <span id="fr_2277"></span>[2278](#fn_2277) But <span
id="fr_2278"></span>[2279](#fn_2278) if we shall say, Of men; \[33\] we
fear <span id="fr_2279"></span>[2280](#fn_2279) that the people will
stone us, all of them.   <span id="fr_2280"></span>[2281](#fn_2280) And
all of them were holding <span id="fr_2281"></span>[2282](#fn_2281)
\[34\] to John, that he was a true prophet.   <span
id="fr_2282"></span>[2283](#fn_2282) They answered and said unto him, We
know \[35\] not.   <span id="fr_2283"></span>[2284](#fn_2283) Jesus said
unto them, Neither tell I you also by what power I work.  What think
ye?  A man had two sons; and he went to the first, and said unto him, My
\[36\] son, go to-day, and till in the vineyard.   <span
id="fr_2284"></span>[2285](#fn_2284) And he answered and said, I do not
wish \[37\] to:  but finally he repented, and went.   <span
id="fr_2285"></span>[2286](#fn_2285) And he went to the other, and said
unto \[38\] him likewise.   <span id="fr_2286"></span>[2287](#fn_2286)
And he answered and said, Yea, my lord:  and went not.  Which of these
two did the will of his father?  They said unto him, The first.  Jesus
said unto them, Verily I say unto you, The publicans and harlots go
before you into \[39\] the kingdom of God.   <span
id="fr_2287"></span>[2288](#fn_2287) John came unto you in the way of
righteousness, and ye believed him not; but the publicans and harlots
believed him; and ye, not even when ye saw, did ye repent at last, that
ye might believe in him.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiii-p54"></span>\[40\]  <span
id="fr_2288"></span>[2289](#fn_2288) Hear another parable:  A man was a
householder, and planted a vineyard, and surrounded it with a hedge, and
digged in it a winepress, and built in it a tower, \[41, 42\]  <span
id="fr_2289"></span>[2290](#fn_2289) and gave it to husbandmen, and went
to a distance for a long time.   <span
id="fr_2290"></span>[2291](#fn_2290) So when the time of the fruits
came, he sent his servants <span id="fr_2291"></span>[2292](#fn_2291)
unto the husbandmen, that they might <span id="page_95">p. 95</span>
<span id="iv.iii.xxxiii-Page_95"></span>\[43\] send him of the
produce <span id="fr_2292"></span>[2293](#fn_2292) of his vineyard. 
 <span id="fr_2293"></span>[2294](#fn_2293) And those husbandmen beat
him, and \[44\] sent him away empty.   <span
id="fr_2294"></span>[2295](#fn_2294) And he sent unto them another
servant also; and they \[45\] stoned him, and wounded <span
id="fr_2295"></span>[2296](#fn_2295) him, and sent him away with
shameful handling.   <span id="fr_2296"></span>[2297](#fn_2296) And he
sent again another; and they slew him.  And he sent many other servants
unto \[46\] them.   <span id="fr_2297"></span>[2298](#fn_2297) And the
husbandmen took his servants, and one they beat, and another they \[47\]
stoned, and another they slew.   <span
id="fr_2298"></span>[2299](#fn_2298) So he sent again other servants
more than the first; and \[48\] \[Arabic, p. 128\] they did likewise
with them.   <span id="fr_2299"></span>[2300](#fn_2299) So the owner of
the vineyard said, What shall I do?  I will send my beloved son:  it may
be they will see him and be \[49, 50\] ashamed.   <span
id="fr_2300"></span>[2301](#fn_2300) So at last he sent unto them his
beloved son that he had.   <span id="fr_2301"></span>[2302](#fn_2301)
But the husbandmen, when they saw the son, said amongst themselves, This
is the heir.  \[51, 52\]  <span id="fr_2302"></span>[2303](#fn_2302) And
they said, We will slay him, and so the inheritance will be ours. 
 <span id="fr_2303"></span>[2304](#fn_2303) So they took \[53\] him, and
put him forth without the vineyard, and slew him.   <span
id="fr_2304"></span>[2305](#fn_2304) When then the lord \[54\] of the
vineyard shall come, what will he do with those husbandmen?   <span
id="fr_2305"></span>[2306](#fn_2305) They said unto him, He will destroy
them in the worst of ways, <span id="fr_2306"></span>[2307](#fn_2306)
and give the vineyard to \[55\] other husbandmen, who will give him
fruit in its season.   <span id="fr_2307"></span>[2308](#fn_2307) Jesus
said unto them, Have ye never read in the scripture,

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiii-p75"></span>The stone which the builders
declared to be base,

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiii-p76"></span> <span
id="fr_2308"></span>[2309](#fn_2308) The same came to be at the head of
the corner:

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiii-p78"></span>\[56\]  <span
id="fr_2309"></span>[2310](#fn_2309) From God was this,

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiii-p80"></span>And it is wonderful in our eyes?

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiii-p81"></span>\[57\]  <span
id="fr_2310"></span>[2311](#fn_2310) Therefore I say unto you, The
kingdom of God shall be taken from you, and \[58\] given to a people
that will produce fruit.   <span id="fr_2311"></span>[2312](#fn_2311)
And whosoever falleth on this stone shall be broken in pieces:  but on
whomsoever it falleth, it will grind him to \[59\] powder.   <span
id="fr_2312"></span>[2313](#fn_2312) And when the chief priests and the
Pharisees heard his parables, they \[60\] perceived that it was
concerning them he spake.   <span id="fr_2313"></span>[2314](#fn_2313)
And they sought to seize him; and they feared the multitude, because
they were holding to him as the prophet.
