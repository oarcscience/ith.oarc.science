<span id="iv.iii.xxvii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xxvii-p1.1"></span>Section XXVII.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xxvii-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_1867"></span>[1868](#fn_1867) Therefore the kingdom of heaven is
like a certain king, who would make a \[2\] reckoning with his
servants.   <span id="fr_1868"></span>[1869](#fn_1868) And when he began
to make *it*, they brought to him <span id="page_85">p. 85</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xxvii-Page_85"></span>one who \[3\] owed him ten
talents. <span id="fr_1869"></span>[1870](#fn_1869)    <span
id="fr_1870"></span>[1871](#fn_1870) And because he had not wherewith to
pay, his lord ordered that he should be sold, he, and his wife, and
children, and all that he \[4\] had, and payment be made.   <span
id="fr_1871"></span>[1872](#fn_1871) So that servant fell down and
worshipped *him*, and said unto him, My lord, have patience with me, and
I shall pay thee everything.  \[5\]  <span
id="fr_1872"></span>[1873](#fn_1872) And the lord of that servant had
compassion, and released him, and forgave him his \[6\] debt.   <span
id="fr_1873"></span>[1874](#fn_1873) And that servant went out, and
found one of his fellow-*servants*, who owed him \[Arabic, p. 104\] a
hundred pence; <span id="fr_1874"></span>[1875](#fn_1874) and he took
him, and dealt severely with him, and said \[7\] unto him, Give me what
thou owest.   <span id="fr_1875"></span>[1876](#fn_1875) So the
fellow-servant fell down at his \[8\] feet, and besought him, and said,
Grant me respite, and I will pay thee.   <span
id="fr_1876"></span>[1877](#fn_1876) And he would not; but took him, and
cast him into prison, till he should give him his debt.  \[9\]  <span
id="fr_1877"></span>[1878](#fn_1877) And when their fellow-*servants*
saw what happened, it distressed them much; and \[10\] they came and
told their lord of all that had taken place.   <span
id="fr_1878"></span>[1879](#fn_1878) Then his lord called him, and said
unto him, *Thou* wicked servant, all that debt I forgave thee, because
\[11\] thou besoughtest me:   <span id="fr_1879"></span>[1880](#fn_1879)
was it not then incumbent on thee also to have mercy on thy \[12\]
fellow-servant, as I had mercy on thee? <span
id="fr_1880"></span>[1881](#fn_1880)    <span
id="fr_1881"></span>[1882](#fn_1881) And his lord became wroth, and
delivered \[13\] him to the scourgers, till he should pay all that he
owed.   <span id="fr_1882"></span>[1883](#fn_1882) So shall my Father
which is in heaven do unto you, if one forgive not his brother his wrong
conduct <span id="fr_1883"></span>[1884](#fn_1883) from \[14\] his
heart.   <span id="fr_1884"></span>[1885](#fn_1884) Take heed
within <span id="fr_1885"></span>[1886](#fn_1885) yourselves:  if thy
brother sin, rebuke him; and if he \[15\] repent, forgive him.   <span
id="fr_1886"></span>[1887](#fn_1886) And if he act wrongly towards thee
seven times in a day, and on that day return seven times unto thee, and
say, I repent towards thee; forgive him.  \[16\]  <span
id="fr_1887"></span>[1888](#fn_1887) And if thy brother act wrongly
towards thee, go and reprove him between thee and \[17\] him alone:  if
he hear thee, thou hast gained thy brother.   <span
id="fr_1888"></span>[1889](#fn_1888) But if he hear thee not, take with
thee one or two, and so <span id="fr_1889"></span>[1890](#fn_1889) at
the mouth of two or three every saying shall \[18\] be established. 
 <span id="fr_1890"></span>[1891](#fn_1890) And if he listen not to
these also, tell the congregation; <span
id="fr_1891"></span>[1892](#fn_1891) and if he listen not even to the
congregation, let him be unto thee as a publican and a Gentile. <span
id="fr_1892"></span>[1893](#fn_1892)   \[19\]  <span
id="fr_1893"></span>[1894](#fn_1893) Verily I say unto you, All that ye
bind on earth shall be bound in heaven:  \[20\] and what ye loose on
earth shall be loosed in heaven.   <span
id="fr_1894"></span>[1895](#fn_1894) I say unto you also, If two of you
agree on earth to ask, everything shall <span
id="fr_1895"></span>[1896](#fn_1895) be granted them from my Father
\[21\] \[Arabic, p. 105\] which is in heaven.   <span
id="fr_1896"></span>[1897](#fn_1896) For where two or three are gathered
in my name, there \[22\] am I amongst them.   <span
id="fr_1897"></span>[1898](#fn_1897) Then Cephas drew near to him, and
said unto him, My Lord, how many times, if my brother act wrongly
towards me, should I forgive him? \[23\] until seven times?   <span
id="fr_1898"></span>[1899](#fn_1898) Jesus said unto him, I say not unto
thee, Until seven; but, Until seventy \[24\] times seven, seven. <span
id="fr_1899"></span>[1900](#fn_1899)    <span
id="fr_1900"></span>[1901](#fn_1900) And the servant that knoweth his
lord’s will, and maketh not \[25\] ready for him according to his will,
shall meet with much punishment;  <span
id="fr_1901"></span>[1902](#fn_1901) but he that knoweth not, and doeth
something for which he meriteth punishment, shall meet with slight
punishment.  Every one to whom much hath been given, much shall be asked
of him; and he that hath had much committed to him, much shall be \[26\]
required at his hand.   <span id="fr_1902"></span>[1903](#fn_1902) I
came to cast fire upon the earth; and I would that it had \[27\] been
kindled already. <span id="fr_1903"></span>[1904](#fn_1903)    <span
id="fr_1904"></span>[1905](#fn_1904) And I have a baptism to be baptized
with, and greatly am \[28\] I straitened till it be accomplished. 
 <span id="fr_1905"></span>[1906](#fn_1905) See *that ye* despise
not <span id="fr_1906"></span>[1907](#fn_1906) one of these little ones
that believe in me.  Verily I say unto you, Their angels at all times
see the \[29\] face of my Father which is in heaven.   <span
id="fr_1907"></span>[1908](#fn_1907) The Son of man came to save the
thing which was lost.

<span id="iv.iii.xxvii-p44"></span>\[30\]  <span
id="fr_1908"></span>[1909](#fn_1908) And after that, Jesus walked in
Galilee; and he did not like to walk in Judæa, \[31\] because the Jews
sought to kill him.   <span id="fr_1909"></span>[1910](#fn_1909) And
there came people who told him of \[32\] the Galilæans, those whose
blood Pilate had mingled with their sacrifices.   <span
id="fr_1910"></span>[1911](#fn_1910) Jesus answered and said unto them,
Do ye imagine that those Galilæans were sinners \[33\] more than all the
Galilæans, so that this thing has come upon them?   <span
id="fr_1911"></span>[1912](#fn_1911) Nay.  Verily I say unto you
now, <span id="fr_1912"></span>[1913](#fn_1912) that ye shall all also,
if ye repent not, likewise perish.  \[34\]  <span
id="fr_1913"></span>[1914](#fn_1913) Or perchance those eighteen on whom
the palace fell in Siloam, and slew them, do ye imagine that they were
to be condemned <span id="fr_1914"></span>[1915](#fn_1914) more than all
the people that dwell \[35\] \[Arabic, p. 106\] in Jerusalem?  Nay. 
 <span id="fr_1915"></span>[1916](#fn_1915) Verily I say unto you, If ye
do not all repent, ye shall perish like them.

<span id="iv.iii.xxvii-p53"></span>\[36\]  <span
id="fr_1916"></span>[1917](#fn_1916) And he spake unto them this
parable:  A man had a fig tree planted in his vine<span id="page_86">p.
86</span> <span id="iv.iii.xxvii-Page_86"></span>yard; \[37\] and he
came and sought fruit thereon, and found none.   <span
id="fr_1917"></span>[1918](#fn_1917) So he said to the husbandman, Lo,
three years do I come and seek fruit on this fig tree, and find \[38\]
none:  cut it down; why doth it render the ground unoccupied?   <span
id="fr_1918"></span>[1919](#fn_1918) The husbandman said unto him, My
lord, leave it this year also, that I may dig about it, and dung \[39\]
it;  <span id="fr_1919"></span>[1920](#fn_1919) then if it bear fruit—!
and if not, then cut it down in the coming year.

<span id="iv.iii.xxvii-p58"></span>\[40\]  <span
id="fr_1920"></span>[1921](#fn_1920) And when Jesus was teaching on the
sabbath day in one of the synagogues, \[41\] there was there a woman
that had a spirit of disease eighteen years;  <span
id="fr_1921"></span>[1922](#fn_1921) and she was \[42\] bowed down, and
could not straighten herself at all.   <span
id="fr_1922"></span>[1923](#fn_1922) And Jesus saw her, and called
\[43\] her, and said unto her, Woman, be loosed from thy disease. 
 <span id="fr_1923"></span>[1924](#fn_1923) And he put his hand \[44\]
upon her; and immediately she was straightened, and praised God.   <span
id="fr_1924"></span>[1925](#fn_1924) And the chief <span
id="fr_1925"></span>[1926](#fn_1925) of the synagogue answered with
anger, because Jesus had healed on a sabbath, and said unto the
multitudes, There are six days in which work ought to be done; \[45\]
come in them and be healed, and not on the sabbath day.   <span
id="fr_1926"></span>[1927](#fn_1926) But Jesus answered and said unto
him, *Ye* hypocrites, doth not each of you on the sabbath day loose
\[46\] his ox or his ass from the manger, and go and water it?   <span
id="fr_1927"></span>[1928](#fn_1927) Ought not this woman, who is a
daughter of Abraham, and whom the devil <span
id="fr_1928"></span>[1929](#fn_1928) hath bound eighteen years, \[47\]
to be loosed from this bond on the sabbath day?   <span
id="fr_1929"></span>[1930](#fn_1929) And when he said this, they were
all put to shame, those standing, who were opposing him: <span
id="fr_1930"></span>[1931](#fn_1930)   and all the people were pleased
with all the wonders that proceeded from his hand.
