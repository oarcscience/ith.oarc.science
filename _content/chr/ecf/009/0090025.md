<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xvi-p1.1"></span>Section XVI.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_1141"></span>[1142](#fn_1141) Then answered certain of the
scribes and Pharisees, that they might tempt him, \[2\] and said,
Teacher, we desire to see a sign from thee.   <span
id="fr_1142"></span>[1143](#fn_1142) He answered and said, This evil and
adulterous generation <span id="fr_1143"></span>[1144](#fn_1143) seeketh
a sign; and it shall not be given a sign, \[3\] except the sign of Jonah
the prophet.   <span id="fr_1144"></span>[1145](#fn_1144) And as Jonah
was a sign to the inhabitants \[4\] of Nineveh, so shall the Son of man
also be to this generation.   <span id="fr_1145"></span>[1146](#fn_1145)
And as Jonah was in the belly of the great fish three days and three
nights, so shall the Son of man \[5\] be in the heart of the earth three
days and three nights.   <span id="fr_1146"></span>[1147](#fn_1146) The
queen of the south shall rise in the judgement with the people of this
generation, and condemn <span id="fr_1147"></span>[1148](#fn_1147)
them:  for she came from the ends of the earth that she might hear the
wisdom of Solomon; \[6\] \[Arabic, p. 62\] and behold, here is a better
than Solomon.   <span id="fr_1148"></span>[1149](#fn_1148) The men of
Nineveh shall stand in the judgement with this generation, and condemn
it:  for they repented at \[7\] the preaching of Jonah; and behold, here
is a greater than Jonah.   <span id="fr_1149"></span>[1150](#fn_1149)
The unclean spirit, when he goeth out of the man, departeth, and goeth
about through places wherein are no waters, that he may find rest for
himself; and when he findeth *it* not, he \[8\] saith, I will return to
my house whence I came out.   <span id="fr_1150"></span>[1151](#fn_1150)
And if he come and find it \[9\] adorned *and* set in order,  <span
id="fr_1151"></span>[1152](#fn_1151) then he goeth, and associateth with
himself seven other spirits worse than himself; and they enter and dwell
in it:  and the end of that man \[10\] shall be worse than his
beginning.   <span id="fr_1152"></span>[1153](#fn_1152) Thus shall it be
unto this evil generation.

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p15"></span>\[11\]  <span
id="fr_1153"></span>[1154](#fn_1153) And while he was saying that, a
woman from the multitude lifted up her voice, and said unto him, Blessed
is the womb that bare thee, and the breasts that nursed \[12\] thee. 
 <span id="fr_1154"></span>[1155](#fn_1154) But he said unto her,
Blessed is he that heareth the word of God, and keepeth it.

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p18"></span>\[13\]  <span
id="fr_1155"></span>[1156](#fn_1155) And while he was speaking unto the
multitude, there came unto him his mother \[14\] and his brethren, and
sought to speak with him;  <span id="fr_1156"></span>[1157](#fn_1156)
and they were not able, because of \[15\] the multitude; and they stood
without and sent, calling him unto them.   <span
id="fr_1157"></span>[1158](#fn_1157) A man said unto him, Behold, thy
mother and thy brethren *are* standing without, and seek to \[16\] speak
with thee.   <span id="fr_1158"></span>[1159](#fn_1158) But he answered
unto him that spake unto him, Who is my \[17\] mother? and who are my
brethren?   <span id="fr_1159"></span>[1160](#fn_1159) And he beckoned
with his hand, stretching it out towards his disciples, and said,
Behold, my mother! and behold, my brethren!  \[18\]  <span
id="fr_1160"></span>[1161](#fn_1160) And every man that shall do the
will of my Father which is in heaven is my brother, and my sister, and
my mother.

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p25"></span>\[19\]  <span
id="fr_1161"></span>[1162](#fn_1161) And after that, Jesus was going
about in the cities and in the villages, and proclaiming \[Arabic, p.
63\] and preaching the kingdom of God, and his <span
id="fr_1162"></span>[1163](#fn_1162) twelve with him, \[20\]  <span
id="fr_1163"></span>[1164](#fn_1163) and the women which had been healed
of diseases and of evil spirits, Mary \[21\] that *was* called
Magdalene, from whom he had cast out seven devils,  <span
id="fr_1164"></span>[1165](#fn_1164) and Joanna the wife of Chuza
Herod’s steward, and Susanna, and many others, who were ministering to
them of their substance.

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p30"></span>\[22\]  <span
id="fr_1165"></span>[1166](#fn_1165) And after that, Jesus went out of
the house, and sat on the sea shore.   <span
id="fr_1166"></span>[1167](#fn_1166) And there \[23\] gathered unto him
great multitudes.  And when the press of the people was great upon him,
he went up and sat in the boat; and all the multitude was standing on
the \[24\] shore of the sea.   <span
id="fr_1167"></span>[1168](#fn_1167) And he spake to them much in
parables, and said, The sower \[25\] went forth to sow:  and when he
sowed,  <span id="fr_1168"></span>[1169](#fn_1168) some fell on the
beaten highway; and it was \[26\] trodden upon, and the birds ate it. 
 <span id="fr_1169"></span>[1170](#fn_1169) And other fell on the
rocks:  and some, where <span id="page_69">p. 69</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xvi-Page_69"></span>there was not much earth; and straightway
it sprang up, because it had no depth in \[27\] the earth:   <span
id="fr_1170"></span>[1171](#fn_1170) and when the sun rose, it withered;
and because it had no root, it dried \[28\] up.   <span
id="fr_1171"></span>[1172](#fn_1171) And some fell among thorns; and the
thorns sprang up with it, and choked it; \[29\] and it yielded no
fruit.   <span id="fr_1172"></span>[1173](#fn_1172) And other fell into
excellent *and* good <span id="fr_1173"></span>[1174](#fn_1173) ground;
and it came up, and grew, and brought forth fruit, some thirty, and some
sixty, and some \[30\] a hundred.   <span
id="fr_1174"></span>[1175](#fn_1174) And when he said that, he cried, He
that hath ears that hear, let him \[31\] hear.   <span
id="fr_1175"></span>[1176](#fn_1175) And when they were alone, his
disciples came, and asked him, and said unto \[32\] him, What is this
parable? and why spakest thou unto them in parables?   <span
id="fr_1176"></span>[1177](#fn_1176) He \[Arabic, p. 64\] answered and
said unto them, Unto you is given the knowledge of the secrets of the
kingdom of God; but it is not given unto them that are \[33\] without. 
 <span id="fr_1177"></span>[1178](#fn_1177) He that hath shall be given
unto, and there shall be added; and he that \[34\] hath not, that which
he hath shall be taken from him also.   <span
id="fr_1178"></span>[1179](#fn_1178) For this *cause* therefore I speak
unto them in parables; because they see, and see not; and hear, and hear
\[35\] not, nor understand.   <span id="fr_1179"></span>[1180](#fn_1179)
And in them is being fulfilled the prophecy of Isaiah, who said,

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p46"></span>Hearing they shall hear, and shall not
understand;

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p47"></span>And seeing they shall see, and shall
not perceive:

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p48"></span>\[36\]  <span
id="fr_1180"></span>[1181](#fn_1180) The heart of this people is waxed
gross,

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p50"></span>And their hearing with their ears is
become heavy,

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p51"></span>And they have closed their eyes;

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p52"></span>Lest they should see with their eyes,

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p53"></span>And hear with their ears,

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p54"></span>And understand with their hearts,

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p55"></span>And should return,

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p56"></span>And I should heal them.

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p57"></span>\[37, 38\]  <span
id="fr_1181"></span>[1182](#fn_1181) But ye, blessed are your eyes,
which see; and your ears, which hear.   <span
id="fr_1182"></span>[1183](#fn_1182) Blessed \[39\] are the eyes which
see what ye see.   <span id="fr_1183"></span>[1184](#fn_1183) Verily I
say unto you, Many of the prophets and the righteous longed to see what
ye see, and saw not; and to hear what ye \[40\] hear, and heard not. 
 <span id="fr_1184"></span>[1185](#fn_1184) When ye know not this
parable, how shall ye know all parables?  \[41, 42\]  <span
id="fr_1185"></span>[1186](#fn_1185) Hear ye the parable of the sower. 
 <span id="fr_1186"></span>[1187](#fn_1186) The sower which sowed, sowed
the word \[43\] of God.   <span id="fr_1187"></span>[1188](#fn_1187)
Every one who heareth the word of the kingdom, and understandeth it not,
the evil one cometh and snatcheth away the word *that hath been* sown in
his \[44\] heart:  and this is that *which was* sown on the middle of
the highway.   <span id="fr_1188"></span>[1189](#fn_1188) But *that*
which was sown on the rocks is he that heareth the word, and straightway
receiveth \[45, 46\] \[Arabic, p. 65\] it with joy;  <span
id="fr_1189"></span>[1190](#fn_1189) only, it hath no root in his soul,
but his belief in it *is* for a time;  <span
id="fr_1190"></span>[1191](#fn_1190) and whenever there is distress or
persecution because of a <span id="fr_1191"></span>[1192](#fn_1191)
word, he \[47\] stumbleth <span id="fr_1192"></span>[1193](#fn_1192)
quickly.   <span id="fr_1193"></span>[1194](#fn_1193) And *that which
was* sown among the thorns is he that heareth the word;  <span
id="fr_1194"></span>[1195](#fn_1194) and the care of this world, and the
error of riches, and the rest of the \[48\] other lusts enter, and choke
the word, and it becometh without fruit.   <span
id="fr_1195"></span>[1196](#fn_1195) And that which was sown in good
ground is he that heareth my word in a pure *and* good heart, and
understandeth it, and holdeth to it, and bringeth forth fruit with
patience,  <span id="fr_1196"></span>[1197](#fn_1196) and produceth
either a hundredfold or sixtyfold or thirty.

<span id="iv.iii.xvi-p74"></span>\[49\]  <span
id="fr_1197"></span>[1198](#fn_1197) And he said, So is the kingdom of
God, like a man who casteth seed into the \[50\] earth, and sleepeth and
riseth by night and day,  <span id="fr_1198"></span>[1199](#fn_1198) and
the seed groweth and cometh \[51\] up, whence <span
id="fr_1199"></span>[1200](#fn_1199) he knoweth not.   <span
id="fr_1200"></span>[1201](#fn_1200) And the earth bringeth it to the
fruit; and first it \[52\] will be blade, and after it ear, and at last
perfect wheat in the ear:   <span id="fr_1201"></span>[1202](#fn_1201)
and whenever the fruit ripeneth, <span
id="fr_1202"></span>[1203](#fn_1202) he bringeth immediately the sickle,
for the harvest hath come.
