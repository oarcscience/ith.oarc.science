<span id="xii.iv.xxviii-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="xii.iv.xxviii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXVIII.—God Sees All Things: 
Therefore Let Us Avoid Transgression.</span>

<span id="xii.iv.xxviii-p2"></span>Since then all things are seen and
heard \[by God\], let us fear Him, and forsake those wicked works which
proceed from evil <span id="fr_4133"></span>[4134](#fn_4133)
desires; <span id="fr_4134"></span>[4135](#fn_4134) so that, through His
mercy, we may be protected from the judgments to come.  For whither can
any of us flee from His mighty hand?  Or what world will receive any of
those who run away from Him?  For the Scripture saith in a certain
place, “Whither shall I go, and where shall I be hid from Thy presence? 
If I ascend into heaven, Thou art there; if I go away even to the
uttermost parts of the earth, there is Thy right hand; <span
id="fr_4135"></span>[4136](#fn_4135) if I make my bed in the abyss,
there is Thy Spirit.” <span id="fr_4136"></span>[4137](#fn_4136)  
Whither, then, shall anyone go, or where shall he escape from Him who
comprehends all things?
