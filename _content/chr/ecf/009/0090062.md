<span id="iv.iii.liii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.liii-p1.1"></span>Section LIII. <span
id="fr_3702"></span>[3703](#fn_3702) </span>

<span id="iv.iii.liii-p3"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_3703"></span>[3704](#fn_3703) And while they marvelled at that,
behold, two men standing above them, their \[2\] raiment shining: 
 <span id="fr_3704"></span>[3705](#fn_3704) and they were seized with
fright, and bowed down their face to \[3\] the earth:  and they said
unto them, Why seek ye the living *one* with the dead?   <span
id="fr_3705"></span>[3706](#fn_3705) He is not here; he is risen: 
remember what he was speaking unto you while he was in \[4\] Galilee,
and saying,  <span id="fr_3706"></span>[3707](#fn_3706) The Son of man
is to be delivered up into the hands of sinners, \[5\] and to be
crucified, and on the third day to rise.   <span
id="fr_3707"></span>[3708](#fn_3707) But go in haste, and say to his
disciples and to Cephas, He is risen from among the dead; and lo, he
goeth before \[6\] you into Galilee;  <span
id="fr_3708"></span>[3709](#fn_3708) and there ye shall see him,
where <span id="fr_3709"></span>[3710](#fn_3709) he said unto you:  lo,
I have \[7\] told you.   <span id="fr_3710"></span>[3711](#fn_3710) And
they remembered his sayings; and they departed in haste from the \[8\]
tomb with joy and great fear, and hastened and went;  <span
id="fr_3711"></span>[3712](#fn_3711) and perplexity and fear \[9\]
encompassed them; and they told no man anything, for they were afraid. 
 <span id="fr_3712"></span>[3713](#fn_3712) And Mary hastened, and came
to Simon Cephas, and to that other disciple whom Jesus loved, and said
unto them, They have taken our Lord from the sepulchre, and I \[10\]
know not where they have laid him.   <span
id="fr_3713"></span>[3714](#fn_3713) And Simon went out, and that other
disciple, \[11\] and came to the sepulchre.   <span
id="fr_3714"></span>[3715](#fn_3714) And they hastened both together: 
and that disciple \[12\] outran <span
id="fr_3715"></span>[3716](#fn_3715) Simon, and came first to the
sepulchre;  <span id="fr_3716"></span>[3717](#fn_3716) and he looked
down, and saw the \[13\] linen laid; but he went not in.   <span
id="fr_3717"></span>[3718](#fn_3717) And Simon came after him, and
entered into the \[14\] \[Arabic, p. 201\] sepulchre, and saw the linen
laid;  <span id="fr_3718"></span>[3719](#fn_3718) and the scarf with
which his head was bound was not with the linen, but wrapped and laid
aside in a certain place.  \[15\]  <span
id="fr_3719"></span>[3720](#fn_3719) Then entered that disciple which
came first to the sepulchre, and saw, and believed.  \[16\]  <span
id="fr_3720"></span>[3721](#fn_3720) And they knew not yet from the
scriptures that the Messiah was to rise from among \[17\] the dead. 
 <span id="fr_3721"></span>[3722](#fn_3721) And those two disciples went
to their place.

<span id="iv.iii.liii-p23"></span>\[18\]  <span
id="fr_3722"></span>[3723](#fn_3722) But Mary remained <span
id="fr_3723"></span>[3724](#fn_3723) at the tomb weeping:  and while she
wept, she looked \[19\] down into the tomb;  <span
id="fr_3724"></span>[3725](#fn_3724) and she saw two angels sitting in
white raiment, one of them toward his pillow, and the other toward his
feet, where the body of Jesus had been \[20\] laid.   <span
id="fr_3725"></span>[3726](#fn_3725) And they said unto her, Woman, why
weepest thou?  She said unto them, \[21\] They have taken my Lord, and I
know not where they have left him.   <span
id="fr_3726"></span>[3727](#fn_3726) She said that, and turned behind
her, and saw Jesus standing, and knew not that it was \[22\] Jesus. 
 <span id="fr_3727"></span>[3728](#fn_3727) Jesus said unto her, Woman,
why weepest thou? whom seekest thou?  And she supposed <span
id="fr_3728"></span>[3729](#fn_3728) him *to be* the gardener, and said,
My lord, if thou hast taken him, \[23\] tell me where thou hast laid
him, that I may go and take him.   <span
id="fr_3729"></span>[3730](#fn_3729) Jesus said unto her, Mary.  She
turned, and said unto him in Hebrew, Rabboni; which is, being \[24\]
interpreted, Teacher.   <span id="fr_3730"></span>[3731](#fn_3730) Jesus
said unto her, Touch me not; <span id="fr_3731"></span>[3732](#fn_3731)
for I have not ascended yet unto my Father:  go to my brethren, and say
unto them, I ascend unto my Father and your Father, and my God and your
God.

<span id="iv.iii.liii-p34"></span>\[25\]  <span
id="fr_3732"></span>[3733](#fn_3732) And on the First-day on which he
rose, he appeared first unto Mary Magdalene, from whom he had cast out
seven demons.

<span id="iv.iii.liii-p36"></span><span id="page_126">p. 126</span>
<span id="iv.iii.liii-Page_126"></span>\[26\]  <span
id="fr_3733"></span>[3734](#fn_3733) And some of those guards <span
id="fr_3734"></span>[3735](#fn_3734) came to the city, and informed the
chief priests of \[27\] \[Arabic, p. 202\] all that had happened. 
 <span id="fr_3735"></span>[3736](#fn_3735) And they assembled with the
elders, and took \[28\] counsel;  <span
id="fr_3736"></span>[3737](#fn_3736) and they gave money, not a little,
to the guards, and said unto them, Say ye, His disciples came and stole
him by night, while we were sleeping.

<span id="iv.iii.liii-p41"></span>\[29\]  <span
id="fr_3737"></span>[3738](#fn_3737) And if the judge hear that, we will
make a plea with him, and free you of blame.  \[30\]  <span
id="fr_3738"></span>[3739](#fn_3738) And they, when they took the money,
did according to what they taught them.  And this word spread among the
Jews unto this day.

<span id="iv.iii.liii-p44"></span>\[31\]  <span
id="fr_3739"></span>[3740](#fn_3739) And then came Mary Magdalene, and
announced to the disciples that she had seen our Lord, and that he had
said that unto her.

<span id="iv.iii.liii-p46"></span>\[32\]  <span
id="fr_3740"></span>[3741](#fn_3740) And while the first <span
id="fr_3741"></span>[3742](#fn_3741) women <span
id="fr_3742"></span>[3743](#fn_3742) were going in the way to
inform <span id="fr_3743"></span>[3744](#fn_3743) his disciples, <span
id="fr_3744"></span>[3745](#fn_3744) \[33\] Jesus met them, and said
unto them, Peace unto you.   <span id="fr_3745"></span>[3746](#fn_3745)
And they came and took \[34\] hold of his feet, and worshipped him. 
 <span id="fr_3746"></span>[3747](#fn_3746) Then said Jesus unto them,
Fear not:  but go and say to my brethren that they depart into Galilee,
and there they shall see \[35\] me.   <span
id="fr_3747"></span>[3748](#fn_3747) And those women returned, and told
all that to the eleven, and to the rest of the disciples;  <span
id="fr_3748"></span>[3749](#fn_3748) and to those that had been with
him, for they were saddened and \[36\] weeping.   <span
id="fr_3749"></span>[3750](#fn_3749) And those were Mary Magdalene, and
Joanna, and Mary the mother of James, and the rest who were with them: 
and they were those that told the apostles.  \[37\]  <span
id="fr_3750"></span>[3751](#fn_3750) And they, when they heard them say
that he was alive and had appeared unto them, \[38\] did not believe
them:   <span id="fr_3751"></span>[3752](#fn_3751) and these sayings
were before their eyes as the sayings of madness.  \[39\] \[Arabic, p.
203\]  <span id="fr_3752"></span>[3753](#fn_3752) And after that, he
appeared to two of them, on that day, and while they were going to the
village which was named Emmaus, and whose distance \[40\] from Jerusalem
was sixty furlongs. <span id="fr_3753"></span>[3754](#fn_3753)    <span
id="fr_3754"></span>[3755](#fn_3754) And they were talking the one of
them with the \[41\] other of all the things which had happened.   <span
id="fr_3755"></span>[3756](#fn_3755) And during the time of their
talking and \[42\] inquiring with one another, Jesus came and reached
them, and walked with them.   <span id="fr_3756"></span>[3757](#fn_3756)
But \[43\] their eyes were veiled that they should not know him.   <span
id="fr_3757"></span>[3758](#fn_3757) And he said unto them, What are
these sayings which ye address the one of you to the other, as ye walk
and are \[44\] sad?   <span id="fr_3758"></span>[3759](#fn_3758) One of
them, whose name was Cleopas, answered and said unto him, Art thou
perchance alone a stranger to Jerusalem, since thou knowest not what was
in \[45\] it in these days?  He said unto them, What was?   <span
id="fr_3759"></span>[3760](#fn_3759) They said unto him, Concerning
Jesus, he who was from Nazareth, a man who was a prophet, and powerful
in \[46\] speech and deeds before God and before all the people:   <span
id="fr_3760"></span>[3761](#fn_3760) and the chief priests and \[47\]
the elders delivered him up to the sentence of <span
id="fr_3761"></span>[3762](#fn_3761) death, and crucified him.   <span
id="fr_3762"></span>[3763](#fn_3762) But we supposed that he was the one
who was to deliver Israel.  And since all <span
id="fr_3763"></span>[3764](#fn_3763) these \[48\] things happened there
have passed three days.   <span id="fr_3764"></span>[3765](#fn_3764) But
*certain* women of us also \[49\] informed us that they had come to the
sepulchre;  <span id="fr_3765"></span>[3766](#fn_3765) and when they
found not his body, they came and told us that they had seen there the
angels, and they <span id="fr_3766"></span>[3767](#fn_3766) said \[50\]
concerning him that he was alive.   <span
id="fr_3767"></span>[3768](#fn_3767) And some of us also went to the
sepulchre, and \[51\] found the matter as the women had said:  only they
saw him not.   <span id="fr_3768"></span>[3769](#fn_3768) Then said
Jesus \[52\] unto them, Ye lacking in discernment, and heavy in heart to
believe!   <span id="fr_3769"></span>[3770](#fn_3769) Was it not in all
the sayings of the prophets that the Messiah was to suffer these things,
and to \[53\] \[Arabic, p. 204\] enter into his Glory?   <span
id="fr_3770"></span>[3771](#fn_3770) And he began from Moses and from
all the prophets, \[54\] and interpreted to them concerning himself from
all the scriptures.   <span id="fr_3771"></span>[3772](#fn_3771) And
they drew near unto the village, whither they were going:  and he was
leading them to \[55\] imagine that he was as if going to a distant
region.   <span id="fr_3772"></span>[3773](#fn_3772) And they
pressed <span id="fr_3773"></span>[3774](#fn_3773) him, and said unto
him, Abide with us:  for the day hath declined now to the darkness.  And
he went \[56\] in to abide with them.   <span
id="fr_3774"></span>[3775](#fn_3774) And when he sat with them, he took
bread, and blessed, \[57\] and brake, and gave to them.   <span
id="fr_3775"></span>[3776](#fn_3775) And straightway their eyes were
opened, and they \[58\] knew him; and he was taken away from them. <span
id="fr_3776"></span>[3777](#fn_3776)    <span
id="fr_3777"></span>[3778](#fn_3777) And they said the one to the other,
Was not our heart heavy within us, while he was speaking to us in the
way, and interpreting to us the scriptures?

<span id="iv.iii.liii-p85"></span>\[59\]  <span
id="fr_3778"></span>[3779](#fn_3778) And they rose in that hour, and
returned to Jerusalem, and found the eleven \[60\] gathered, and those
that were with them, saying,  <span id="fr_3779"></span>[3780](#fn_3779)
Truly our Lord is risen, and hath \[61\] appeared to Simon.   <span
id="fr_3780"></span>[3781](#fn_3780) And they related what happened in
the way, and how they knew him when he brake the bread.   <span
id="fr_3781"></span>[3782](#fn_3781) Neither believed they that also.
