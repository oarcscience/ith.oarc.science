<span id="iv.iii.xviii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xviii-p1.1"></span>Section XVIII.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xviii-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_1265"></span>[1266](#fn_1265) At that time Herod the tetrarch
heard of the fame of Jesus, and all the things which came to pass at his
hand;  <span id="fr_1266"></span>[1267](#fn_1266) and he marvelled, for
he had obtained excellent \[2\] information concerning him. <span
id="fr_1267"></span>[1268](#fn_1267)    <span
id="fr_1268"></span>[1269](#fn_1268) And *some* men said that John the
Baptist was risen \[3\] from among the dead;  <span
id="fr_1269"></span>[1270](#fn_1269) and <span
id="fr_1270"></span>[1271](#fn_1270) others said that Elijah had
appeared; and others, Jeremiah; \[4\] and others, that a prophet of the
old prophets was risen;  <span id="fr_1271"></span>[1272](#fn_1271) and
others said that he \[5\] was a prophet like one of the prophets. 
 <span id="fr_1272"></span>[1273](#fn_1272) Herod said to his servants,
This is John the Baptist, he whom I beheaded; he is risen from among the
dead:  therefore mighty \[6\] \[Arabic, p. 70\] works result from him. 
 <span id="fr_1273"></span>[1274](#fn_1273) For Herod him*self* had sent
and taken John, and cast him into prison, for the sake of Herodias his
brother Philip’s wife, whom he \[7\] had taken.   <span
id="fr_1274"></span>[1275](#fn_1274) And John said to Herod, Thou hast
no authority to take the wife of thy \[8\] brother.   <span
id="fr_1275"></span>[1276](#fn_1275) And Herodias avoided him and wished
to kill him; and she could not.  \[9\]  <span
id="fr_1276"></span>[1277](#fn_1276) But Herod feared John, for he knew
that he was a righteous man *and* a holy; and \[10\] he guarded him, and
heard him much, and did, and obeyed him with gladness.   <span
id="fr_1277"></span>[1278](#fn_1277) And he wished to kill him; but he
feared the people, for they adhered to him as the \[11\] prophet. 
 <span id="fr_1278"></span>[1279](#fn_1278) And there was a celebrated
day, and Herod had made a feast for his great men on the day of his
anniversary, <span id="fr_1279"></span>[1280](#fn_1279) and for the
officers and for the chief men \[12\] of Galilee.   <span
id="fr_1280"></span>[1281](#fn_1280) And the daughter of Herodias came
in and danced in the midst of the company, and pleased Herod and those
that sat with him.  And the king said to the \[13\] damsel, Ask of me
what thou wilt, and I will give it thee.   <span
id="fr_1281"></span>[1282](#fn_1281) And he sware unto her, \[14\]
Whatsoever thou shalt ask, I will give it thee, to the half of my
kingdom.   <span id="fr_1282"></span>[1283](#fn_1282) And she went out,
and said unto her mother, What shall I ask him? <span
id="fr_1283"></span>[1284](#fn_1283)   She said unto her, The \[15\]
head of John the Baptist.   <span id="fr_1284"></span>[1285](#fn_1284)
And immediately she came in hastily to the king, and said unto him, I
desire in this hour that thou give me on a dish the head of John \[16\]
the Baptist.   <span id="fr_1285"></span>[1286](#fn_1285) And the king
was exceeding sorry; but because of the oath and the \[17\] guests he
did not wish to refuse her.   <span id="fr_1286"></span>[1287](#fn_1286)
But immediately the king sent an executioner, and commanded that he
should bring the head of John:  and he went and cut off <span
id="page_72">p. 72</span> <span id="iv.iii.xviii-Page_72"></span>\[18\]
the head of John in the prison,  <span
id="fr_1287"></span>[1288](#fn_1287) and brought it on a dish, and
delivered it to the \[19\] damsel; and the damsel gave it to her
mother.   <span id="fr_1288"></span>[1289](#fn_1288) And his disciples
heard, and came \[Arabic, p. 71\] and took his body, and buried it. 
 <span id="fr_1289"></span>[1290](#fn_1289) And they came and told <span
id="fr_1290"></span>[1291](#fn_1290) Jesus what \[20\] had happened. 
 <span id="fr_1291"></span>[1292](#fn_1291) And for this cause Herod
said, I beheaded John:  who \[21\] is this, of whom I hear these
things.  And he desired to see him.   <span
id="fr_1292"></span>[1293](#fn_1292) And Jesus, when he heard, removed
thence in a boat to a waste place alone, to the other side of the sea of
the Galilee of Tiberias. <span id="fr_1293"></span>[1294](#fn_1293)

<span id="iv.iii.xviii-p32"></span>\[22\]  <span
id="fr_1294"></span>[1295](#fn_1294) And many saw them going, and knew
them, and hastened by land <span id="fr_1295"></span>[1296](#fn_1295)
from all the cities, and came thither beforehand;  <span
id="fr_1296"></span>[1297](#fn_1296) for they saw the signs which he was
doing on the \[23, 24\] sick.   <span
id="fr_1297"></span>[1298](#fn_1297) And Jesus went up into the
mountain, and sat there with his disciples.   <span
id="fr_1298"></span>[1299](#fn_1298) And \[25\] the feast of the
passover of the Jews was near.   <span
id="fr_1299"></span>[1300](#fn_1299) And Jesus lifted up his eyes, and
saw great multitudes coming to him.   <span
id="fr_1300"></span>[1301](#fn_1300) And he was moved with compassion
for them, for \[26\] they were like sheep that *were* without a
shepherd.   <span id="fr_1301"></span>[1302](#fn_1301) And he received
them, and spake to them concerning the kingdom of God, and healed those
who had need of healing.

<span id="iv.iii.xviii-p41"></span>\[27\]  <span
id="fr_1302"></span>[1303](#fn_1302) And when the evening
approached, <span id="fr_1303"></span>[1304](#fn_1303) his disciples
came to him, and said unto \[28\] him,  <span
id="fr_1304"></span>[1305](#fn_1304) The place is desert, and the time
is past; send away the multitudes of the people, <span
id="fr_1305"></span>[1306](#fn_1305) that they may go to the towns and
villages which are around us, and buy for \[29\] themselves bread; for
they have nothing to eat.   <span id="fr_1306"></span>[1307](#fn_1306)
But he said unto them, They have \[30\] no need to go away; give ye them
what may be eaten.   <span id="fr_1307"></span>[1308](#fn_1307) They
said unto him, We have not here *enough*.   <span
id="fr_1308"></span>[1309](#fn_1308) He said unto Philip, Whence shall
we buy bread that these may eat?  \[31, 32\]  <span
id="fr_1309"></span>[1310](#fn_1309) And he said that proving him; and
he knew what he was resolved to do.   <span
id="fr_1310"></span>[1311](#fn_1310) Philip said \[Arabic, p. 72\] unto
him, Two hundred pennyworth of bread would not suffice them after <span
id="fr_1311"></span>[1312](#fn_1311) \[33\] every one of them hath taken
a small amount.   <span id="fr_1312"></span>[1313](#fn_1312) One of his
disciples said unto \[34\] him (namely, Andrew the brother of Simon
Cephas),  <span id="fr_1313"></span>[1314](#fn_1313) Here is a lad
having five loaves \[35\] of barley and two fishes:  but this amount,
what is it for all these?   <span id="fr_1314"></span>[1315](#fn_1314)
But wilt thou that we go and buy for all the people what may be eaten?
for we have no more \[36\] than these five loaves and the two fishes. 
 <span id="fr_1315"></span>[1316](#fn_1315) And the grass was plentiful
in that place.  Jesus said unto them, Arrange all the people that they
may sit down on the grass, \[37\] fifty people in a company.   <span
id="fr_1316"></span>[1317](#fn_1316) And the disciples did so.  And all
the people sat down \[38\] by companies, by hundreds and fifties. 
 <span id="fr_1317"></span>[1318](#fn_1317) Then Jesus said unto them,
Bring hither \[39\] those five loaves and the two fishes.   <span
id="fr_1318"></span>[1319](#fn_1318) And when they brought him that,
Jesus took the bread and the fish, and looked to heaven, and blessed,
and divided, and gave to \[40\] his disciples to set before them;  <span
id="fr_1319"></span>[1320](#fn_1319) and the disciples set for the
multitudes the bread \[41\] and the fish; and they ate, all of them, and
were satisfied.   <span id="fr_1320"></span>[1321](#fn_1320) And when
they were satisfied, he said unto his disciples, Gather the fragments
that remain over, that nothing \[42\] be lost.   <span
id="fr_1321"></span>[1322](#fn_1321) And they gathered, and filled
twelve baskets with fragments, being those that remained over from those
which ate of the five barley loaves and the two \[43\] fishes.   <span
id="fr_1322"></span>[1323](#fn_1322) And those people who ate were five
thousand, besides the women and children.  \[44\] \[Arabic, p. 73\]
 <span id="fr_1323"></span>[1324](#fn_1323) And straightway he pressed
his disciples to go up into the ship, and that they should go before him
unto the other side to Bethsaida, while he \[45\] him*self* should send
away the multitudes.   <span id="fr_1324"></span>[1325](#fn_1324) And
those people who saw the sign which \[46\] Jesus did, said, Of a truth
this is a prophet who hath come into the world.   <span
id="fr_1325"></span>[1326](#fn_1325) And Jesus knew their purpose to
come and take him, and make him a king; and he left them, and went up
into the mountain alone for prayer.

<span id="iv.iii.xviii-p66"></span>\[47, 48\]  <span
id="fr_1326"></span>[1327](#fn_1326) And when the nightfall was near,
his disciples went down unto the sea, and sat <span
id="fr_1327"></span>[1328](#fn_1327) in a boat, and came to the side of
Capernaum.   <span id="fr_1328"></span>[1329](#fn_1328) And the darkness
came on, and Jesus \[49\] had not come to them.   <span
id="fr_1329"></span>[1330](#fn_1329) And the sea was stirred up against
them by reason of a violent \[50\] wind that blew.   <span
id="fr_1330"></span>[1331](#fn_1330) And the boat was distant from the
land many furlongs, and they were much damaged by the waves, and the
wind was against them.
