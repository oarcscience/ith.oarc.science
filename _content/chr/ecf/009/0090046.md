<span id="iv.iii.xxxvii-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_2514"></span>[2515](#fn_2514) And Jesus said, To judge the world
am I come, so that they that see not may \[2\] see, and they that see
may become blind.   <span id="fr_2515"></span>[2516](#fn_2515) And some
of the Pharisees which were \[3\] with him heard that, and they said
unto him, Can it be that we are blind?   <span
id="fr_2516"></span>[2517](#fn_2516) Jesus said unto them, If ye were
blind, ye should not have sin:  but now ye say, We see:  and because of
this your sin remaineth. <span id="fr_2517"></span>[2518](#fn_2517)

<span id="iv.iii.xxxvii-p7"></span>\[4\] \[Arabic, p. 140\]  <span
id="fr_2518"></span>[2519](#fn_2518) Verily, verily, I say unto you,
Whosoever entereth not into the fold of the sheep by the door, but goeth
up from another place, that *man* is a thief and a \[5, 6\] stealer. 
 <span id="fr_2519"></span>[2520](#fn_2519) But he that entereth by the
door is the shepherd of the sheep.   <span
id="fr_2520"></span>[2521](#fn_2520) And therefore <span
id="fr_2521"></span>[2522](#fn_2521) the keeper of the door openeth for
him the door; and the sheep hear his voice:  and \[7\] he calleth his
sheep <span id="fr_2522"></span>[2523](#fn_2522) by their names, and
they go forth unto him.   <span id="fr_2523"></span>[2524](#fn_2523) And
when he putteth forth his sheep, he goeth before them, and his
sheep <span id="fr_2524"></span>[2525](#fn_2524) follow him:  because
\[8\] they know his voice.   <span id="fr_2525"></span>[2526](#fn_2525)
And after a stranger will the sheep not go, but they flee from \[9\]
him:  because they hear not the voice of a stranger.   <span
id="fr_2526"></span>[2527](#fn_2526) This parable spake Jesus unto
them:  but they knew not what he was saying unto them.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxvii-p17"></span>\[10\]  <span
id="fr_2527"></span>[2528](#fn_2527) Jesus said unto them again, Verily,
verily, I say unto you, I am the door of the \[11\] sheep.   <span
id="fr_2528"></span>[2529](#fn_2528) And all that came are thieves and
stealers:  but the sheep heard them not.  \[12\]  <span
id="fr_2529"></span>[2530](#fn_2529) I am the door:  and if a man enter
by me, he shall live, and shall go in and go out, \[13\] and shall find
pasture.   <span id="fr_2530"></span>[2531](#fn_2530) And the stealer
cometh not, save that he may steal, and kill, and destroy:  but I came
that they might have life, and that they might have \[14\] the thing
*that is* better. <span id="fr_2531"></span>[2532](#fn_2531)    <span
id="fr_2532"></span>[2533](#fn_2532) I am the good shepherd; and the
good shepherd giveth \[15\] himself <span
id="fr_2533"></span>[2534](#fn_2533) for his sheep.   <span
id="fr_2534"></span>[2535](#fn_2534) But the hireling, who is not a
shepherd, and whose the sheep <span id="fr_2535"></span>[2536](#fn_2535)
are not, when he seeth the wolf as it cometh, leaveth the sheep, and
fleeth, \[16\] and the wolf cometh, and snatcheth away the sheep, and
scattereth <span id="fr_2536"></span>[2537](#fn_2536) them:   <span
id="fr_2537"></span>[2538](#fn_2537) and the \[17\] hireling fleeth
because he is an hireling, and hath no care for the sheep.   <span
id="fr_2538"></span>[2539](#fn_2538) I am the \[18\] good shepherd;
 <span id="fr_2539"></span>[2540](#fn_2539) and I know what is mine, and
what is mine knoweth me, as my Father knoweth me, and I know my Father;
and I give myself <span id="fr_2540"></span>[2541](#fn_2540) for the
sheep.  \[19\]  <span id="fr_2541"></span>[2542](#fn_2541) And I have
other sheep also, that are not of this flock:  them also I must invite,
and they shall hear my voice; and all the sheep shall be one, and the
shepherd one.  \[20\] \[Arabic, p. 141\]  <span
id="fr_2542"></span>[2543](#fn_2542) And therefore doth my Father love
me, because I give my life, that I may \[21\] take it again.   <span
id="fr_2543"></span>[2544](#fn_2543) No man taketh it from me, but I
leave it of my own choice.  And I have the right to leave it, and have
the right also to take it.  And this commandment did I receive of my
Father.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxvii-p35"></span>\[22\]  <span
id="fr_2544"></span>[2545](#fn_2544) And there occurred a disagreement
among the Jews because of these sayings.  \[23\]  <span
id="fr_2545"></span>[2546](#fn_2545) And many of them said, He hath a
devil, and is afflicted with madness; <span
id="fr_2546"></span>[2547](#fn_2546) why listen \[24\] ye to him? 
 <span id="fr_2547"></span>[2548](#fn_2547) And others said, These
sayings are not those of *men* possessed with demons.  Can a demon haply
open the eyes of a blind *man?*

<span id="iv.iii.xxxvii-p40"></span>\[25, 26\]  <span
id="fr_2548"></span>[2549](#fn_2548) And the feast of the dedication
came on at Jerusalem:  and it was winter.   <span
id="fr_2549"></span>[2550](#fn_2549) And \[27\] Jesus was walking in the
temple in the porch of Solomon.   <span
id="fr_2550"></span>[2551](#fn_2550) The Jews therefore surrounded him,
and said unto him, Until when dost thou make our hearts anxious?  \[28\]
 <span id="fr_2551"></span>[2552](#fn_2551) If thou art the Messiah,
tell us plainly.  He answered and said unto them, I told you, and ye
believe not:  and the deeds that I do in my Father’s name bear witness
\[29, 30\] to me.   <span id="fr_2552"></span>[2553](#fn_2552) But ye
believe not, because ye are not of my sheep, <span
id="fr_2553"></span>[2554](#fn_2553) as I said unto you.  \[31\]  <span
id="fr_2554"></span>[2555](#fn_2554) And my sheep <span
id="fr_2555"></span>[2556](#fn_2555) hear my voice, and I know them, and
they come after me:   <span id="fr_2556"></span>[2557](#fn_2556) and I
give them eternal life; and they shall not perish for ever, nor shall
any man snatch \[32\] them out of my hands. <span
id="fr_2557"></span>[2558](#fn_2557)    <span
id="fr_2558"></span>[2559](#fn_2558) For the Father, who hath given
*them* unto me, is greater \[33\] than all; and no man is able to take
*them* from the hand of my <span id="fr_2559"></span>[2560](#fn_2559)
Father.   <span id="fr_2560"></span>[2561](#fn_2560) I and \[34, 35\] my
Father are one.   <span id="fr_2561"></span>[2562](#fn_2561) And the
Jews took stones to stone him.   <span
id="fr_2562"></span>[2563](#fn_2562) Jesus said unto them, Many good
deeds from my Father have I shewed you; because of which <span
id="fr_2563"></span>[2564](#fn_2563) of them, \[36\] then, do ye stone
me?   <span id="fr_2564"></span>[2565](#fn_2564) The Jews said unto him,
Not for the good deeds do we stone thee, but because thou blasphemest;
and, whilst thou art a man, makest thyself <span id="page_101">p.
101</span> <span id="iv.iii.xxxvii-Page_101"></span> \[37\] God.   <span
id="fr_2565"></span>[2566](#fn_2565) Jesus said unto them, Is it not
thus written in your law, I said, Ye are gods?  \[38\] \[Arabic, p.
142\]  <span id="fr_2566"></span>[2567](#fn_2566) And if he called those
gods—for <span id="fr_2567"></span>[2568](#fn_2567) to them came the
word of God (and it is \[39\] not possible in <span
id="fr_2568"></span>[2569](#fn_2568) the scripture that *anything*
should be undone)— <span id="fr_2569"></span>[2570](#fn_2569) he then,
whom the Father hath sanctified and sent into the world, do ye say that
he blasphemeth; \[40\] because I said unto you, I am the Son of God? 
 <span id="fr_2570"></span>[2571](#fn_2570) If then I do not the deeds
of my \[41\] Father, ye believe me not. <span
id="fr_2571"></span>[2572](#fn_2571)    <span
id="fr_2572"></span>[2573](#fn_2572) But if I do, *even* if ye believe
not me, believe the deeds:  that ye may know and believe that my Father
is in me, and I in my Father.  \[42\]  <span
id="fr_2573"></span>[2574](#fn_2573) And they sought again to take him: 
and he went forth out of their hands.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxvii-p67"></span>\[43\]  <span
id="fr_2574"></span>[2575](#fn_2574) And he went beyond Jordan to the
place where John was baptizing formerly; \[44\] and abode there.   <span
id="fr_2575"></span>[2576](#fn_2575) And many people came unto him; and
they said, John did not \[45\] work even one sign:  but all that John
said of this man is truth.   <span id="fr_2576"></span>[2577](#fn_2576)
And many believed in him.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxvii-p71"></span>\[46\]  <span
id="fr_2577"></span>[2578](#fn_2577) And there was a sick *man*, named
Lazarus, of the village of Bethany, the brother \[47\] of Mary and
Martha.   <span id="fr_2578"></span>[2579](#fn_2578) And Mary was she
that anointed with sweet ointment the feet of Jesus, and wiped *them*
with her hair; and Lazarus, who was sick, was the \[48\] brother of this
*woman*. <span id="fr_2579"></span>[2580](#fn_2579)    <span
id="fr_2580"></span>[2581](#fn_2580) And his sisters sent unto Jesus,
and said unto him, Our \[49\] Lord, behold, he whom thou lovest is
sick.   <span id="fr_2581"></span>[2582](#fn_2581) But Jesus said, This
sickness is not unto death, but for the glorifying of God, that the Son
of God may be glorified \[50, 51\] because of it.   <span
id="fr_2582"></span>[2583](#fn_2582) And Jesus loved Martha, and Mary,
and Lazarus.   <span id="fr_2583"></span>[2584](#fn_2583) And when he
\[52\] heard that he was sick, he abode in the place where he was two
days.   <span id="fr_2584"></span>[2585](#fn_2584) And after that,
\[53\] he said unto his disciples, Come, let us go into Judæa.   <span
id="fr_2585"></span>[2586](#fn_2585) His disciples said unto him, Our
\[Arabic, p. 143\] Master, now the Jews desire to stone thee; and goest
thou again thither?  \[54, 55\]  <span
id="fr_2586"></span>[2587](#fn_2586) Jesus said unto them, Is not the
day of twelve hours?  If then a man walk in the day, he stumbleth not,
because he seeth the light of the world.   <span
id="fr_2587"></span>[2588](#fn_2587) But if \[56\] a man walk in the
night, he stumbleth, because there is no lamp in him.   <span
id="fr_2588"></span>[2589](#fn_2588) This said Jesus:  and after that,
he said unto them, Lazarus our friend hath fallen asleep; but \[57\] I
am going to awaken him.   <span id="fr_2589"></span>[2590](#fn_2589) His
disciples said unto him, Our Lord, if he hath \[58\] fallen asleep, he
will recover.   <span id="fr_2590"></span>[2591](#fn_2590) But Jesus
said that concerning his death:  while they \[59\] supposed that he
spake of lying down to sleep.   <span
id="fr_2591"></span>[2592](#fn_2591) Then Jesus said unto them plainly,
\[60\] Lazarus is dead.   <span id="fr_2592"></span>[2593](#fn_2592) And
I am glad that I was not there for your sakes, that ye may \[61\]
believe; but let us go thither.   <span
id="fr_2593"></span>[2594](#fn_2593) Thomas, who is called Thama, <span
id="fr_2594"></span>[2595](#fn_2594) said to the disciples, his
companions, Let us also go, and die with him.
