<span id="iv.iii.xxii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xxii-p1.1"></span>Section XXII.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xxii-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_1534"></span>[1535](#fn_1534) And when Jesus came to a certain
village, there drew near to him a leper, and fell at his feet, and
besought him, and said unto him, If thou wilt, thou art able to \[2\]
cleanse me.   <span id="fr_1535"></span>[1536](#fn_1535) And Jesus had
mercy upon him, and stretched forth his hand, and \[3\] touched him, and
said, I will cleanse <span id="fr_1536"></span>[1537](#fn_1536) thee. 
 <span id="fr_1537"></span>[1538](#fn_1537) And immediately his leprosy
departed \[4\] from him, and he was cleansed.   <span
id="fr_1538"></span>[1539](#fn_1538) And he sternly charged him, and
sent him out, \[5\] \[Arabic, p. 85\] and said unto him,  <span
id="fr_1539"></span>[1540](#fn_1539) See that thou tell *not* any man: 
but go and shew thyself to the priests, and offer an offering for thy
cleansing as Moses commanded \[6\] for their testimony.   <span
id="fr_1540"></span>[1541](#fn_1540) But he, when he went out, began to
publish much, and spread abroad the news, so that Jesus could not enter
into any of the cities openly, for the extent to which the report of him
spread, but he remained without in a desert \[7\] place.   <span
id="fr_1541"></span>[1542](#fn_1541) And much people came unto him from
one place and another, <span id="fr_1542"></span>[1543](#fn_1542) to
hear \[8\] his word, and that they might be healed of their pains. 
 <span id="fr_1543"></span>[1544](#fn_1543) And he used to withdraw from
them into the desert, and pray.

<span id="iv.iii.xxii-p13"></span>\[9\]  <span
id="fr_1544"></span>[1545](#fn_1544) And after that, was the feast of
the Jews; and Jesus went up to Jerusalem.

<span id="iv.iii.xxii-p15"></span>\[10\]  <span
id="fr_1545"></span>[1546](#fn_1545) And there was in Jerusalem a place
prepared for bathing, <span id="fr_1546"></span>[1547](#fn_1546) which
was called in \[11\] Hebrew the House of Mercy, having five porches. 
 <span id="fr_1547"></span>[1548](#fn_1547) And there were laid in them
much people of the sick, and blind, and lame, and paralysed, waiting for
the moving \[12\] of the water.   <span
id="fr_1548"></span>[1549](#fn_1548) And the angel from time to time
went down into the place of bathing, <span
id="fr_1549"></span>[1550](#fn_1549) and moved the water; and the first
that went down after the moving \[13\] of the water, every pain that he
had was healed.   <span id="fr_1550"></span>[1551](#fn_1550) And a man
was there who had a \[14\] disease for thirty-eight years.   <span
id="fr_1551"></span>[1552](#fn_1551) And Jesus saw this *man* laid, and
knew <span id="fr_1552"></span>[1553](#fn_1552) that he had \[15\] been
thus a long time; and he said unto him, Wouldest thou be made whole? 
 <span id="fr_1553"></span>[1554](#fn_1553) That diseased one answered
and said, Yea, my Lord, I have no man, when the water moveth, to put me
into the bathing-place; but when I come, another goeth down before \[16,
17\] me.   <span id="fr_1554"></span>[1555](#fn_1554) Jesus said unto
him, Rise, take thy bed, and walk.   <span
id="fr_1555"></span>[1556](#fn_1555) And immediately that man was
healed; and he rose, and carried his bed, and walked.

<span id="iv.iii.xxii-p27"></span>\[18\]  <span
id="fr_1556"></span>[1557](#fn_1556) And that day was a sabbath.  And
when the Jews saw that healed one, they <span
id="fr_1557"></span>[1558](#fn_1557) said \[19\] unto him, It is a
sabbath:  thou hast no authority to carry thy bed.   <span
id="fr_1558"></span>[1559](#fn_1558) And he answered and said unto them,
He that made me whole, the same said unto me, Take thy bed, \[20\]
\[Arabic, p. 86\] and walk.   <span id="fr_1559"></span>[1560](#fn_1559)
They asked him therefore, Who is this man that said unto thee, \[21\]
Take thy bed, and walk?   <span id="fr_1560"></span>[1561](#fn_1560) But
he that was healed knew not who it was; for Jesus had removed from that
place to another, because of the press of the great multitude \[22\]
which was in that place.   <span id="fr_1561"></span>[1562](#fn_1561)
And after two days Jesus happened upon him in the temple, and said unto
him, Behold, thou art whole:  sin not again, lest there come upon <span
id="page_78">p. 78</span> <span id="iv.iii.xxii-Page_78"></span>\[23\]
thee what is worse than the first.   <span
id="fr_1562"></span>[1563](#fn_1562) And that man went, and said to the
Jews that it \[24\] was Jesus that had healed him.   <span
id="fr_1563"></span>[1564](#fn_1563) And because of that the Jews
persecuted Jesus and \[25\] sought to kill him, because he was doing
this on the sabbath.   <span id="fr_1564"></span>[1565](#fn_1564) And
Jesus said unto \[26\] them, My Father worketh until now, and I also
work.   <span id="fr_1565"></span>[1566](#fn_1565) And because of this
especially the Jews sought to kill him, not because he profaned the
sabbath only; but for his saying also that God was his Father, and his
making himself equal with God.  \[27\]  <span
id="fr_1566"></span>[1567](#fn_1566) Jesus answered and said unto them,
Verily, verily, I say unto you, The Son cannot do anything of himself,
but what he seeth the Father do; what the Father doeth, \[28\] that the
Son also doeth like him.   <span id="fr_1567"></span>[1568](#fn_1567)
The Father loveth his Son, and everything that he doeth he sheweth him: 
and more than these works will he shew him, that ye \[29\] may marvel. 
 <span id="fr_1568"></span>[1569](#fn_1568) And as the Father raiseth
the dead and giveth them life, so the Son \[30\] also giveth life to
whomsoever he will.   <span id="fr_1569"></span>[1570](#fn_1569) And the
Father judgeth no man, but hath \[31\] given all judgement unto the Son;
 <span id="fr_1570"></span>[1571](#fn_1570) that every man may honour
the Son, as he honoureth the Father.  And he that honoureth not the Son
honoureth not the Father which \[32\] sent him.   <span
id="fr_1571"></span>[1572](#fn_1571) Verily, verily, I say unto you,
Whosoever heareth my word, and believeth in him that sent me, hath
eternal life, and cometh not into judgement, but passeth from \[33\]
\[Arabic, p. 87\] death unto life.   <span
id="fr_1572"></span>[1573](#fn_1572) Verily, verily, I say unto you, An
hour shall come, and now is also, when the dead shall hear the voice of
the Son of God; and those \[34\] which hear shall live.   <span
id="fr_1573"></span>[1574](#fn_1573) And as the Father hath life in
himself, <span id="fr_1574"></span>[1575](#fn_1574) likewise he gave to
\[35\] the Son also that he might have life in himself, <span
id="fr_1575"></span>[1576](#fn_1575)    <span
id="fr_1576"></span>[1577](#fn_1576) and authority to do judgement also,
\[36\] because <span id="fr_1577"></span>[1578](#fn_1577) he is the Son
of man.   <span id="fr_1578"></span>[1579](#fn_1578) Marvel not then at
that:  I mean the coming of the hour when all that are in the tombs
shall hear his voice, and shall come forth:  \[37\]  <span
id="fr_1579"></span>[1580](#fn_1579) those that have done good, to the
resurrection of life; and those that have done evil *deeds*, to the
resurrection of judgement.

<span id="iv.iii.xxii-p52"></span>\[38\]  <span
id="fr_1580"></span>[1581](#fn_1580) I am not able of myself to do
anything; but as I hear, I judge:  and my judgement \[39\] is just; I
seek not my *own* will, but the will of him that sent me.   <span
id="fr_1581"></span>[1582](#fn_1581) I <span
id="fr_1582"></span>[1583](#fn_1582) bear witness \[40\] of myself, and
so <span id="fr_1583"></span>[1584](#fn_1583) my witness is not true. 
 <span id="fr_1584"></span>[1585](#fn_1584) It is another that beareth
witness \[41\] of me; and I know that the witness which he beareth of me
is true.   <span id="fr_1585"></span>[1586](#fn_1585) Ye have sent
\[42\] unto John, and he hath borne witness of the truth.   <span
id="fr_1586"></span>[1587](#fn_1586) But not from man do I seek \[43\]
witness; but I say that ye may live. <span
id="fr_1587"></span>[1588](#fn_1587)    <span
id="fr_1588"></span>[1589](#fn_1588) That <span
id="fr_1589"></span>[1590](#fn_1589) was a lamp which shineth and \[44\]
giveth light:  and ye were pleased to glory now <span
id="fr_1590"></span>[1591](#fn_1590) in his light.   <span
id="fr_1591"></span>[1592](#fn_1591) But I have witness greater than
that of John:  the works which my Father hath given me to accomplish,
\[45\] those works which I do, bear witness of me, that the Father hath
sent me.   <span id="fr_1592"></span>[1593](#fn_1592) And the Father
which sent me, he hath borne witness of me.  Ye have neither heard his
\[46\] voice at any time, nor seen his appearance.   <span
id="fr_1593"></span>[1594](#fn_1593) And his word abideth not in you;
because \[47\] in him whom he hath sent ye do not believe.   <span
id="fr_1594"></span>[1595](#fn_1594) Search the scriptures, in which ye
rejoice <span id="fr_1595"></span>[1596](#fn_1595) \[48\] that ye have
eternal life;  <span id="fr_1596"></span>[1597](#fn_1596) and they bear
witness of me; and ye do not wish to come to \[49, 50\] \[Arabic, p.
88\] me, that ye may have eternal life.   <span
id="fr_1597"></span>[1598](#fn_1597) I seek not praise of men.   <span
id="fr_1598"></span>[1599](#fn_1598) But I know \[51\] you, that the
love of God is not in you.   <span id="fr_1599"></span>[1600](#fn_1599)
I am come in the name of my Father, and ye received me not; but if
another come in his own name, that *one* will \[52\] ye receive.   <span
id="fr_1600"></span>[1601](#fn_1600) And how can ye believe, while ye
receive praise one from another, and \[53\] praise from God, the One, ye
seek not?   <span id="fr_1601"></span>[1602](#fn_1601) Can it be that ye
think that I will accuse you before the Father?  Ye have one that
accuseth you, Moses, in whom ye have \[54\] rejoiced. <span
id="fr_1602"></span>[1603](#fn_1602)    <span
id="fr_1603"></span>[1604](#fn_1603) If ye believed Moses, ye would
believe me also; Moses wrote of me.  \[55\]  <span
id="fr_1604"></span>[1605](#fn_1604) And if ye believed not his
writings, how shall ye believe my words?
