<span id="iv.iii.i-p2"></span> ————————————

<span id="iv.iii.i-p3"></span><span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.i-p3.1"></span>\[Section I\]</span>

<span id="iv.iii.i-p4"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_74"></span>[75](#fn_74) In the beginning was the Word, and the
Word was with God, and God is the \[2,3\] Word.   <span
id="fr_75"></span>[76](#fn_75) This *was* in the beginning with God. 
 <span id="fr_76"></span>[77](#fn_76) Everything was by his hand, and
\[4\] without him not even one existing thing was *made*.   <span
id="fr_77"></span>[78](#fn_77) In him was life, and the life \[5\] is
the light of men.   <span id="fr_78"></span>[79](#fn_78) And the light
shineth in the darkness, and the darkness apprehended it not.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p10"></span>\[6\]  <span
id="fr_79"></span>[80](#fn_79)  <span id="fr_80"></span>[81](#fn_80)
There was in the days of Herod the king a priest whose name was
Zacharias, of the family of Abijah; and his wife was of the daughters of
Aaron, and her name \[7\] was Elizabeth.   <span
id="fr_81"></span>[82](#fn_81) And they were both righteous before God,
walking in all his commands, \[8\] and in the uprightness of God without
reproach.   <span id="fr_82"></span>[83](#fn_82) And they had no son,
for \[9\] Elizabeth was barren, and they had both advanced in age. 
 <span id="fr_83"></span>[84](#fn_83) And while he discharged \[10\]
\[Arabic, p. 2\] the duties of priest in the order of his service before
God,  <span id="fr_84"></span>[85](#fn_84) according to the custom of
the priesthood it was his turn to burn incense; so he entered the \[11\]
temple of the Lord.   <span id="fr_85"></span>[86](#fn_85) And the whole
gathering of the people were praying without at the \[12\] time of the
incense.   <span id="fr_86"></span>[87](#fn_86) And there appeared unto
Zacharias the angel of the Lord, standing \[13\] at the right of the
altar of incense;  <span id="fr_87"></span>[88](#fn_87) and Zacharias
was troubled when he saw him, \[14\] and fear fell upon him.   <span
id="fr_88"></span>[89](#fn_88) But the angel said unto him, Be not
agitated, <span id="fr_89"></span>[90](#fn_89) Zacharias, for thy prayer
is heard, and thy wife Elizabeth shall bear thee a son, and thou shalt
\[15\] call his name John;  <span id="fr_90"></span>[91](#fn_90) and
thou shalt have joy and gladness, and many shall rejoice \[16\] at his
birth.   <span id="fr_91"></span>[92](#fn_91) And he shall be great
before the Lord, and shall not drink wine nor strong drink, and he shall
be filled with the Holy Spirit <span id="fr_92"></span>[93](#fn_92)
while he is in his mother’s \[17\] womb.   <span
id="fr_93"></span>[94](#fn_93) And he shall turn back many of the
children of Israel to the Lord their \[18\] God.   <span
id="fr_94"></span>[95](#fn_94) And he shall go before him in the spirit,
and in the power of Elijah the prophet, to turn back the heart of the
fathers to the sons, and those that obey not to the knowledge <span
id="fr_95"></span>[96](#fn_95) of the righteous; and to prepare for the
Lord a perfect people.  \[19\]  <span id="fr_96"></span>[97](#fn_96) And
Zacharias said unto the angel, How shall I know this, since I am an old
man \[20\] and my wife is advanced in years?   <span
id="fr_97"></span>[98](#fn_97) And the angel answered and said unto him,
I am Gabriel, that standeth before God; and I was sent to speak unto
thee, and give \[21\] thee tidings of this.   <span
id="fr_98"></span>[99](#fn_98) Henceforth thou shalt be speechless, and
shalt not be able to speak until the day in which this shall come to
pass, because thou didst not trust \[22\] this my word, which shall be
accomplished in its time.   <span id="fr_99"></span>[100](#fn_99) And
the people were standing \[Arabic, p. 3\] awaiting Zacharias, and they
were perplexed at his delaying in the temple.  \[23\] <span
id="fr_100"></span>[101](#fn_100) And when Zacharias went out, he was
not able to speak unto them:  so they knew that he had seen in the
temple a vision; and he made signs unto them, and \[24\] continued
dumb.   <span id="fr_101"></span>[102](#fn_101) And when the days of his
service were completed, he departed to his dwelling.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p34"></span>\[25\]  <span
id="fr_102"></span>[103](#fn_102) And after those days Elizabeth his
wife conceived; and she hid herself five \[26\] months, and said,  <span
id="fr_103"></span>[104](#fn_103) This hath the Lord done unto me in the
days when he looked upon me, to remove my reproach from among men.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p37"></span>\[27\]  <span
id="fr_104"></span>[105](#fn_104) And <span
id="fr_105"></span>[106](#fn_105) in the sixth month Gabriel the angel
was sent from God to Galilee <span id="fr_106"></span>[107](#fn_106) to
a \[28\] city called Nazareth,  <span id="fr_107"></span>[108](#fn_107)
to a virgin given in marriage to a man named Joseph, of the \[29\] house
of David; and the virgin’s name was Mary.   <span
id="fr_108"></span>[109](#fn_108) And the angel entered unto her and
said unto her, Peace be unto thee, thou who art filled with grace.  Our
Lord <span id="page_44">p. 44</span> <span
id="iv.iii.i-Page_44"></span>\[30\] is with thee, thou blessed amongst
women.   <span id="fr_109"></span>[110](#fn_109) And she, when she
beheld, was agitated \[31\] at his word, and pondered what this
salutation could be.   <span id="fr_110"></span>[111](#fn_110) And the
angel said unto \[32\] her, Fear not, Mary, for thou hast found favour
with God.   <span id="fr_111"></span>[112](#fn_111) Thou shalt now
conceive, \[33\] and bear a son, and call his name <span
class="sc"><span id="iv.iii.i-p45.2"></span>Jesus</span>.   <span
id="fr_112"></span>[113](#fn_112) This shall be great, and shall be
called the Son of the Most High; and the Lord God will give him the
throne of \[34\] David his father:  and he shall rule over the house of
Jacob for ever;  <span id="fr_113"></span>[114](#fn_113) and to his
\[35\] kingdom there shall be no end.   <span
id="fr_114"></span>[115](#fn_114) Mary said unto the angel, How shall
this be to \[36\] me when no man hath known me?   <span
id="fr_115"></span>[116](#fn_115) The angel answered and said unto her,
The \[Arabic, p. 4\] Holy Spirit will come, and the power of the Most
High shall rest upon thee, and therefore shall *he* that is born of thee
be pure, and shall be called the Son \[37\] of God.   <span
id="fr_116"></span>[117](#fn_116) And lo, Elizabeth thy kinswoman, she
also hath conceived a son in her old \[38\] age; and this is the sixth
month with her, her that is called barren.   <span
id="fr_117"></span>[118](#fn_117) For nothing is \[39\] difficult for
God.   <span id="fr_118"></span>[119](#fn_118) Mary said, Lo, I am the
handmaid of the Lord; let it be unto me according unto thy word.  And
the angel departed from her.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p53"></span>\[40\]  <span
id="fr_119"></span>[120](#fn_119) And then Mary arose in those days and
went in haste into the hill country, <span
id="fr_120"></span>[121](#fn_120) to a <span
id="fr_121"></span>[122](#fn_121) \[41\] city of Judah;  <span
id="fr_122"></span>[123](#fn_122) and entered into the house of
Zacharias, and asked for the health of \[42\] Elizabeth.   <span
id="fr_123"></span>[124](#fn_123) And when Elizabeth heard the
salutation of Mary, the babe leaped in \[43\] her womb.   <span
id="fr_124"></span>[125](#fn_124) And Elizabeth was filled with the Holy
Spirit; and cried with a loud voice and said unto Mary, Blessed art thou
amongst women, and blessed is the \[44\] fruit that is in thy womb. 
 <span id="fr_125"></span>[126](#fn_125) Whence have I this *privilege*,
that the mother of my \[45\] Lord should come unto me?   <span
id="fr_126"></span>[127](#fn_126) When the sound of thy salutation
reached my ears, \[46\] with great joy rejoiced the babe in my womb. 
 <span id="fr_127"></span>[128](#fn_127) And blessed is she who believed
\[47\] that what was spoken *to her* from the Lord would be fulfilled. 
 <span id="fr_128"></span>[129](#fn_128) And Mary said,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p64"></span>My soul doth magnify the Lord,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p65"></span>\[48\]  <span
id="fr_129"></span>[130](#fn_129) And my spirit hath rejoiced in God my
Saviour,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p67"></span>\[49\]  <span
id="fr_130"></span>[131](#fn_130) Who hath looked upon the low estate of
his handmaiden:

<span id="iv.iii.i-p69"></span>Lo, henceforth, all generations <span
id="fr_131"></span>[132](#fn_131) shall pronounce blessing on me.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p71"></span>\[50\]  <span
id="fr_132"></span>[133](#fn_132) For <span
id="fr_133"></span>[134](#fn_133) he hath done great things for me, who
is mighty,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p74"></span>And holy is his name.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p75"></span>\[51\]  <span
id="fr_134"></span>[135](#fn_134) And his mercy embraceth them who fear
him,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p77"></span>Throughout the ages and the times.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p78"></span>\[52\] \[Arabic, p. 5\]  <span
id="fr_135"></span>[136](#fn_135) He wrought the victory with his arm,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p80"></span>And scattered them that prided themselves
in their opinions.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p81"></span>\[53\]  <span
id="fr_136"></span>[137](#fn_136) He overthrew them that acted haughtily
from their thrones,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p83"></span>And raised the lowly.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p84"></span>\[54\]  <span
id="fr_137"></span>[138](#fn_137) He satisfied with good things the
hungry,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p86"></span>And left the rich without anything.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p87"></span>\[55\]  <span
id="fr_138"></span>[139](#fn_138) He helped Israel his servant,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p89"></span>And remembered his mercy

<span id="iv.iii.i-p90"></span>\[56\]  <span
id="fr_139"></span>[140](#fn_139) (According as he spake with our
fathers)

<span id="iv.iii.i-p92"></span>Unto Abraham and unto his seed for ever.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p93"></span>\[57\]  <span
id="fr_140"></span>[141](#fn_140) And Mary abode with Elizabeth about
three months, and returned unto her house.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p95"></span>\[58, 59\]  <span
id="fr_141"></span>[142](#fn_141) And Elizabeth’s time of delivery was
come; and she brought forth a son.   <span
id="fr_142"></span>[143](#fn_142) And her neighbours and kinsfolk heard
that God had multiplied his mercy towards her; \[60\] and they rejoiced
with her.   <span id="fr_143"></span>[144](#fn_143) And when <span
id="fr_144"></span>[145](#fn_144) it was the eighth day, they came to
circumcise the child, and called him Zacharias, *calling him* by the
name of his father.  \[61\]  <span id="fr_145"></span>[146](#fn_145) And
his mother answered and said unto them, Not so; but he shall be called
John.  \[62\]  <span id="fr_146"></span>[147](#fn_146) And they said
unto her, There is no man of thy kindred that is called by this name. 
\[63, 64\]  <span id="fr_147"></span>[148](#fn_147) And they made signs
to his father, *saying*, How dost thou wish to name him?   <span
id="fr_148"></span>[149](#fn_148) And he asked for a tablet, and wrote
and said, His name is John.  And every one wondered.  \[65\]  <span
id="fr_149"></span>[150](#fn_149) And immediately his mouth was opened,
and his tongue, and he spake and \[66\] praised God.   <span
id="fr_150"></span>[151](#fn_150) And fear fell on all their
neighbours:  and this was spoken of <span
id="fr_151"></span>[152](#fn_151) in all <span id="page_45">p. 45</span>
<span id="iv.iii.i-Page_45"></span>\[67\] the mountains of Judah. 
 <span id="fr_152"></span>[153](#fn_152) And all who heard pondered in
their hearts and said, What shall this child be?  And the hand of the
Lord was with him.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p108"></span>\[68\]  <span
id="fr_153"></span>[154](#fn_153) And Zacharias his father was filled
with the Holy Spirit, and prophesied and said,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p110"></span>\[69\]  <span
id="fr_154"></span>[155](#fn_154) Blessed is the Lord, the God of
Israel,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p112"></span>Who hath cared for his people, and
wrought for it salvation;

<span id="iv.iii.i-p113"></span>\[70\]  <span
id="fr_155"></span>[156](#fn_155) And hath raised for us the horn of
salvation

<span id="iv.iii.i-p115"></span>\[Arabic, p. 6\] In the house of David
his servant

<span id="iv.iii.i-p116"></span>\[71\]  <span
id="fr_156"></span>[157](#fn_156) (As he spake by the mouth of his holy
prophets from eternity),

<span id="iv.iii.i-p118"></span>\[72\]  <span
id="fr_157"></span>[158](#fn_157) That he might save us from our
enemies,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p120"></span>And from the hand of all them that hate
us.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p121"></span>\[73\]  <span
id="fr_158"></span>[159](#fn_158) And he hath performed his mercy
towards our fathers,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p123"></span>And remembered his holy covenants,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p124"></span>\[74\]  <span
id="fr_159"></span>[160](#fn_159) And the oath which he sware unto
Abraham our father,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p126"></span>\[75\]  <span
id="fr_160"></span>[161](#fn_160) That he would give us deliverance from
the hand of our enemies,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p128"></span>And without fear we shall <span
id="fr_161"></span>[162](#fn_161) serve before him

<span id="iv.iii.i-p130"></span>\[76\]  <span
id="fr_162"></span>[163](#fn_162) All our days with equity and
righteousness.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p132"></span>\[77\]  <span
id="fr_163"></span>[164](#fn_163) And as for thee, O child, prophet of
the Most High shalt thou be called.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p134"></span>Thou shalt go forth before the face of
the Lord to prepare his way,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p135"></span>\[78\]  <span
id="fr_164"></span>[165](#fn_164) To give the knowledge of
salvation <span id="fr_165"></span>[166](#fn_165) unto his people,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p138"></span>For the forgiveness of their sins,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p139"></span>\[79\]  <span
id="fr_166"></span>[167](#fn_166) Through the mercy of <span
id="fr_167"></span>[168](#fn_167) the compassion of our God,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p142"></span>With which he careth for <span
id="fr_168"></span>[169](#fn_168) us, to appear <span
id="fr_169"></span>[170](#fn_169) from on high

<span id="iv.iii.i-p145"></span>\[80\]  <span
id="fr_170"></span>[171](#fn_170) To give light to them that sit in
darkness and under the shadow of death,

<span id="iv.iii.i-p147"></span>And to set straight our feet in the way
of peace.

<span id="iv.iii.i-p148"></span>\[81\]  <span
id="fr_171"></span>[172](#fn_171) And the child grew and became strong
in the spirit, and abode in the desert until the time of his appearing
unto the children of Israel.
