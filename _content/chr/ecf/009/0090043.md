<span id="iv.iii.xxxiv-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xxxiv-p1.1"></span>Section XXXIV.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiv-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_2314"></span>[2315](#fn_2314) Then went the Pharisees and
considered how they might ensnare him in a word, \[2\] and deliver him
into the power of the judge, <span id="fr_2315"></span>[2316](#fn_2315)
and into the power of the ruler.   <span
id="fr_2316"></span>[2317](#fn_2316) And they sent unto him their
disciples, with the kinsfolk of Herod; and they said unto him, \[Arabic,
p. 129\] Teacher, we know that thou speakest the truth, and teachest the
way of God with equity, <span id="fr_2317"></span>[2318](#fn_2317) and
art not lifted up <span id="fr_2318"></span>[2319](#fn_2318) by any
man:  for thou actest not so as to \[3\] be seen of any man.   <span
id="fr_2319"></span>[2320](#fn_2319) Tell us now, What is thy opinion? 
Is it lawful that we should \[4\] pay the tribute to Cæsar, or not?
shall we give, or shall we not give?   <span
id="fr_2320"></span>[2321](#fn_2320) But Jesus knew \[5\] their deceit,
and said unto them,  <span id="fr_2321"></span>[2322](#fn_2321) Why
tempt ye me, ye hypocrites?  Shew me the \[6\] penny of the tribute. 
 <span id="fr_2322"></span>[2323](#fn_2322) So they brought unto him a
penny.  Jesus said unto them, To whom belongeth this image and
inscription?  They said unto him, To Cæsar.  \[7, 8\]  <span
id="fr_2323"></span>[2324](#fn_2323) He said unto them, Give what is
Cæsar’s to Cæsar, and what is God’s to God.   <span
id="fr_2324"></span>[2325](#fn_2324) And they could not make him slip in
a *single* word before the people; and they marvelled at his word, and
refrained.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiv-p14"></span>\[9\]  <span
id="fr_2325"></span>[2326](#fn_2325) And on that day came the Sadducees,
and said unto him, <span id="fr_2326"></span>[2327](#fn_2326) There is
no life for \[10\] the dead.   <span
id="fr_2327"></span>[2328](#fn_2327) And they asked him, and said unto
him, Teacher, Moses said unto us, If a man die, not having children, let
his brother take his wife, and raise up seed \[11\] for his brother. 
 <span id="fr_2328"></span>[2329](#fn_2328) Now there *were* with us
seven brethren:  and the first took a wife, \[12\] and died without
children;  <span id="fr_2329"></span>[2330](#fn_2329) and the second
took his wife, and died without children; \[13\]  <span
id="fr_2330"></span>[2331](#fn_2330) and the third also took her; and in
like manner the seven of them also, and they \[14, 15\] died without
leaving children.   <span id="fr_2331"></span>[2332](#fn_2331) And last
of them all the woman died also.   <span
id="fr_2332"></span>[2333](#fn_2332) At the resurrection, then, which of
these seven shall have this woman? for all of them took \[16\] her. 
 <span id="fr_2333"></span>[2334](#fn_2333) Jesus answered and said unto
them, Is it not for this that ye have erred, \[17\] because ye know not
the scriptures, nor the power of God?   <span
id="fr_2334"></span>[2335](#fn_2334) And the sons of this \[18\] world
take wives, and the women become the men’s; <span
id="fr_2335"></span>[2336](#fn_2335)  <span
id="fr_2336"></span>[2337](#fn_2336) but those that have become worthy
of that world, and the resurrection from among the dead, do <span
id="fr_2337"></span>[2338](#fn_2337) not take <span id="page_96">p.
96</span> <span id="iv.iii.xxxiv-Page_96"></span>\[19\] \[Arabic, p.
130\] wives, and the women also do <span
id="fr_2338"></span>[2339](#fn_2338) not become the men’s.   <span
id="fr_2339"></span>[2340](#fn_2339) Nor is it possible that they should
die; but they <span id="fr_2340"></span>[2341](#fn_2340) are like the
angels, and are the children of \[20\] God, because they have become the
children of the resurrection.   <span
id="fr_2341"></span>[2342](#fn_2341) For in <span
id="fr_2342"></span>[2343](#fn_2342) the resurrection of the dead, have
ye not read in the book of Moses, how from the bush God said unto him, I
am the God of Abraham, and the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob? 
\[21\]  <span id="fr_2343"></span>[2344](#fn_2343) And God is not *the
God* of the dead, but of the living:  for all of them are alive with
him.  And ye have erred greatly.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiv-p34"></span>\[22, 23\]  <span
id="fr_2344"></span>[2345](#fn_2344) And when the multitudes heard, they
were wondering at his teaching.   <span
id="fr_2345"></span>[2346](#fn_2345) And \[24\] some of the scribes
answered and said unto him, Teacher, thou hast well said.   <span
id="fr_2346"></span>[2347](#fn_2346) But the rest of the Pharisees, when
they saw his silencing the Sadducees on this point, gathered against him
to contend with him.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiv-p38"></span>\[25\]  <span
id="fr_2347"></span>[2348](#fn_2347) And one of the scribes, of those
that knew the law, when he saw the excellence \[26\] of his answer to
them, desired to try him, and said unto him,  <span
id="fr_2348"></span>[2349](#fn_2348) What shall I do to inherit eternal
life? and,  <span id="fr_2349"></span>[2350](#fn_2349) Which of the
commandments is greater, and has precedence \[27\] in the law?   <span
id="fr_2350"></span>[2351](#fn_2350) Jesus said unto him, The first of
all the commandments is, Hear, O \[28\] Israel; The Lord our God, the
Lord is one:   <span id="fr_2351"></span>[2352](#fn_2351) and thou shalt
love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and
with all thy thought, and with all thy \[29, 30\] strength.   <span
id="fr_2352"></span>[2353](#fn_2352) This is the great and
preëminent <span id="fr_2353"></span>[2354](#fn_2353) commandment. 
 <span id="fr_2354"></span>[2355](#fn_2354) And the second, which is
like it, is, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself.  And another
commandment \[31\] greater than these two there is not.   <span
id="fr_2355"></span>[2356](#fn_2355) On these two commandments, then,
are hung the \[32\] \[Arabic, p. 131\] law and the prophets.   <span
id="fr_2356"></span>[2357](#fn_2356) That scribe said unto him,
Excellent! my Master; <span id="fr_2357"></span>[2358](#fn_2357) thou
hast said truly that he is one, and there is no other outside of him: 
\[33\]  <span id="fr_2358"></span>[2359](#fn_2358) and that a man should
love him with all his heart, and with all his thought, and with all his
soul, and with all his strength, and that he should love his neighbour
as \[34\] himself, is better than all savours and sacrifices.   <span
id="fr_2359"></span>[2360](#fn_2359) And Jesus saw him that he had
answered wisely; and he answered and said unto him, Thou art not far
from the \[35, 36\] kingdom of God.   <span
id="fr_2360"></span>[2361](#fn_2360) Thou hast spoken rightly:  do this,
and thou shalt live.   <span id="fr_2361"></span>[2362](#fn_2361) And
he, as his desire was to justify himself, said unto him, And who is my
neighbour?  \[37\]  <span id="fr_2362"></span>[2363](#fn_2362) Jesus
said unto him, A man went down from Jerusalem to Jericho; and the
robbers fell upon him, and stripped <span
id="fr_2363"></span>[2364](#fn_2363) him, and beat him, his life
remaining in him *but* little, <span
id="fr_2364"></span>[2365](#fn_2364) \[38\] and went away.   <span
id="fr_2365"></span>[2366](#fn_2365) And it happened that there came
down a certain priest that way; \[39\] and he saw him, and passed by. 
 <span id="fr_2366"></span>[2367](#fn_2366) And likewise a Levite also
came and reached \[40\] that place, and saw him, and passed by.   <span
id="fr_2367"></span>[2368](#fn_2367) And a certain Samaritan, as he
journeyed, \[41\] came to <span id="fr_2368"></span>[2369](#fn_2368) the
place where he was, and saw him, and had compassion on him,  <span
id="fr_2369"></span>[2370](#fn_2369) and came near, and bound up his
strokes, <span id="fr_2370"></span>[2371](#fn_2370) and poured on them
wine and oil; and he set <span id="fr_2371"></span>[2372](#fn_2371) him
on the ass, and brought him to the inn, and expended his care upon him. 
\[42\]  <span id="fr_2372"></span>[2373](#fn_2372) And on the morrow of
that day he took out two pence, and gave them to the innkeeper, and said
unto him, Care for him; and if thou spendest upon him more, \[43\] when
I return, I shall give thee.   <span
id="fr_2373"></span>[2374](#fn_2373) Who of these three now, thinkest
thou, is nearest \[44\] to him that fell among the robbers?   <span
id="fr_2374"></span>[2375](#fn_2374) And he said unto him, He that had
compassion \[45\] \[Arabic, p. 132\] on him.   <span
id="fr_2375"></span>[2376](#fn_2375) Jesus said unto him, Go, and do
thou also likewise.  And no man dared afterwards to ask him anything.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxiv-p68"></span>\[46\]  <span
id="fr_2376"></span>[2377](#fn_2376) And he was teaching every day in
the temple.  But the chief priests and scribes and the elders of the
people sought to destroy him:   <span
id="fr_2377"></span>[2378](#fn_2377) and they could <span
id="fr_2378"></span>[2379](#fn_2378) not *find* what \[47\] they should
do with him; and all the people were hanging upon him to hear him. 
\[48\]  <span id="fr_2379"></span>[2380](#fn_2379) And many of the
multitude believed on him, and said, The Messiah, when he \[49\] cometh,
can it be that he will do more than these signs that this *man* doeth? 
 <span id="fr_2380"></span>[2381](#fn_2380) And the Pharisees heard the
multitudes say that of him; and the chief priests sent \[50\]
officers <span id="fr_2381"></span>[2382](#fn_2381) to seize him. 
 <span id="fr_2382"></span>[2383](#fn_2382) And Jesus said unto them, I
am with you *but* a short time <span id="page_97">p. 97</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xxxiv-Page_97"></span>\[51\] yet, and I go to him that sent
me.   <span id="fr_2383"></span>[2384](#fn_2383) And ye shall seek me,
and shall not find me:  \[52\] and where I shall be, ye shall not be
able to come.   <span id="fr_2384"></span>[2385](#fn_2384) The Jews said
within themselves, Whither hath this *man* determined to go that we
shall not be able <span id="fr_2385"></span>[2386](#fn_2385) *to find*
him? can it be that he is determined to go to the regions of the
nations, <span id="fr_2386"></span>[2387](#fn_2386) and teach \[53\] the
heathen?   <span id="fr_2387"></span>[2388](#fn_2387) What is this word
that he said, Ye shall seek me, and shall not find me:  and where I am,
ye cannot come?
