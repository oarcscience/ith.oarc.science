# CHAPTER LI.

THE NAMES OF THE EASTERN CATHOLICS, THE SUCCESSORS OF THE APOSTLES ADDAI
AND MÂRÎ<span id="page_116_fr_1"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">1</span></sup>](#page_116_note_1).

   1. Addai was buried in Edessa.

   2. Mârî (was buried) in the convent of Kônî<span
id="page_116_fr_2"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">2</span></sup>](#page_116_note_2).

   3. Abrîs, called in Greek A\[m\]brosius; the place of his grave is
unknown; he was of the laying on of hands of Antioch.

   4. Abraham was of the laying on of hands of Antioch; he was descended
from the family of Jacob the son of Joseph; his grave is in Ctesiphon.

   5. James, of the laying on of hands of Antioch, was also of the
family of Joseph the husband of Mary; his grave is in Ctesiphon.

   6. A<u>h</u>â-d´abû\[hî\] was of the laying on of hands of Antioch;
his grave is in Ctesiphon.

   7. Sha<u>h</u>lûphâ was of the laying on of hands of Ctesiphon, and
he was buried there.

   8. Pâpâ; his grave is at Ctesiphon.

   9. Simon bar <u>S</u>abbâ\`ê was martyred at Shôshân.

   10. Shah-dôst was buried in Ctesiphon.

<span id="page_117">p. 117</span>

   11. Bar-B<u>e</u>\`esh-sh<u>e</u>mîn<span
id="page_117_fr_1"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">1</span></sup>](#page_117_note_1) was martyred and buried
in Elam (Khûzistân).

   12. Tûmar<u>s</u>â was buried in Ctesiphon.

   13. <u>K</u>âyômâ was buried in Ctesiphon; he abdicated the
patriarchate, and another was put in his place, and was before him until
he died.

   14. Isaac was buried in Ctesiphon.

   15. A<u>h</u>â was buried in Ctesiphon.

   16. Yab-alâhâ was of the school of Mâr \`Abdâ<span
id="page_117_fr_2"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">2</span></sup>](#page_117_note_2); he was buried in
Ctesiphon.

   17. Ma\`nâ dwelt in Persia and was buried there.

   18. Dâd-îshô\` was buried in <u>H</u>êrtâ<span
id="page_117_fr_3"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">3</span></sup>](#page_117_note_3). In his days the strife
between Nestorius and Cyril (of Alexandria) took place.

   19. Bâbôi was martyred and buried in <u>H</u>êrtâ.

   20. A<u>k</u>a<u>k</u> (Acacius) was of the family of Bâbôi the
Catholicus; he was buried in al-Madâïn<span
id="page_117_fr_4"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">4</span></sup>](#page_117_note_4).

   21. Bâbai took a wife, and was buried at Ctesiphon,

   22. Shîlâ took a wife, and was buried in his convent beside
Awânâ<span id="page_117_fr_5"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">5</span></sup>](#page_117_note_5).

   23. Paul was buried in Ctesiphon.

   24. Mâr(î)-abâ was buried in <u>H</u>êrtâ, and was a martyr without
bloodshed.

   25. Ezekiel was buried in <u>H</u>êrtâ.

   26. Îshô\`-yab of Arzôn<span id="page_117_fr_6"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">6</span></sup>](#page_117_note_6) was buried in
<u>H</u>êrtâ.

   27. Sabr-îshô\` was buried in <u>H</u>êrtâ.

   28. Gregory was buried in . . . . . .

   29. Îshô\`-yab of G<u>e</u>dâlâ<span
id="page_117_fr_7"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">7</span></sup>](#page_117_note_7) was buried in . . . . .
.

   31. Îshô\`-yab of Adiabene<span id="page_118_fr_1"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">1</span></sup>](#page_118_note_1) was buried in
Bêth-\`Âbê<span id="page_118_fr_2"></span>[<sup><span

   33. John was buried in . . . . . .

   34. <u>He</u>nân-îshô\` was buried in . . . . . .

   35. <u>Se</u>lîbâ-z<u>e</u>khâ was buried in Ctesiphon.

   36. Pethiôn was buried in Ctesiphon.

   37. Mâr\[î\]-abâ was buried in al-Madâïn.

   38. Jacob was buried in . . . . . .

   39. <u>He</u>nân-îshô\` was buried in . . . . . .

   40. Timothy was buried in his own convent.

   41. Îshô\` (Joshua) the son of Nôn (Nun) was buried in the convent of
Timothy.

   42. George was buried in the same convent.

   43. Sabr-îshô\`<span id="page_118_fr_3"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">3</span></sup>](#page_118_note_3) was buried in the same

   44. Abraham was buried in the same convent.

   45. Athanasius<span id="page_118_fr_4"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">4</span></sup>](#page_118_note_4) was buried in the same
convent.

   46. Sergius was buried in the same convent.

   47. Anôsh (Enos) was buried in the same convent.

   48. John the son of Narsai was buried in the Greek Palace (at
Baghdâd)<span id="page_118_fr_5"></span>[<sup><span

   49. Joannes<span id="page_118_fr_6"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">6</span></sup>](#page_118_note_6) was buried in the Greek
Palace.

   50. John was buried in the Greek Palace.

   51. Abraham was buried in the convent of \`Abdôn.

   52. Emmanuel was buried in the Greek Palace.

   54. \`Abd-îshô\` was buried in the Greek Palace.

   55. Mârî was buried in the Greek Palace.

   56. Joannes<span id="page_119_fr_1"></span>[<sup><span

   57. John was buried in the Greek Palace.

   58. Îshô\`-yab was buried in the Grek Palace.

   59. Elijah (Elîyâ) was buried in the Greek Palace.

   60. John was buried in the Greek Palace.

   61. Sabr-îshô\` was buried in the Greek Palace.

   62. \`Abd-îshô\` was buried in the Greek Palace.

   63. Makkîkhâ was buried in the Greek Palace.

   64. Elijah (Elîyâ) was buried in the Greek Palace.

   65. Bar-<u>s</u>aumâ was buried in the Greek Palace.

   66. \`Abd-îshô\` was buried . . . . . .

   67. Îshô\`-yab was buried in the church of Mâr Sabr-îshô\`.

   68. Elijah (Elîyâ) was buried in the church of Mâr Sabr-îshô\`.

   69. Yab-alâhâ was buried in the church of Mârt\[î\] Maryam (my lady
Mary).

   70. Sabr-îshô\` was buried in the church of Mârt\[î\] Maryam<span
