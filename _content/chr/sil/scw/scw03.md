## *III THE STILL WILDERNESS*

<span id="page_118">p. 118</span>

 

54 (I. 7)  
MAN MUST GO BEYOND GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Where is my hiding-place? Where there's nor I nor Thou.<br />
Where is my final goal towards which I needs must press?<br />
Where there is nothing. Whither shall I journey now?<br />
Still farther on than God—into a Wilderness.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

55 (I. 199)  
GOD BEYOND THE CREATURE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Go, where thou canst not go; see, where light never breaks;<br />
Hear, where no sound is heard: then art thou where God speaks.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

56 (IV. 23)  
DIVINE CONTEMPLATION

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Who in this mortal life would see<br />
The Light that is beyond all light,<br />
Beholds it best by faring forth<br />
Into the darkness of the Night.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

57 (II. 6)  
NOTHING IS THE BEST CONSOLATION

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Best Consolation is in Naught.<br />
If God should quench His shine, then dare<br />
In naked Nothingness to find<br />
Thy Consolation in despair.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_119">p. 119</span>

 

58 (I. 126)  
DESIRE EXPECTS FULFILMENT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>If thou hast still for God a yearning and desire,<br />
Then doth He not embrace thee yet, whole and entire.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

59 (I. 76)  
TO WILL NAUGHT IS TO BE LIKE GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Willing and seeking naught, God is eternal peace:<br />
Willest thou likewise naught, thy peace is even as His.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

60 (II. 248)  
STILLNESS IS LIKE UNTO THE ETERNAL NAUGHT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Stillness and Loneliness are liker naught than Naught:<br />
These willeth then my Will, if my Will willeth aught.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

61 (I. 98)  
THE DEAD WILL RULETH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God needs must do my Will, if Will in me is dead:<br />
I write for Him His Paradigm and Copy-head.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_120">p. 120</span>

 

62 (V. 207)  
THE GREATEST DEED

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The greatest Deed that thou canst do<br />
For God, is to be deedless—best,<br />
Suffering, to suffer unto God,<br />
And, resting, unto Him to rest.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

63 (V. 195)  
GOD IS FOUND IN IDLENESS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Who sits in utter Idleness<br />
Shall come much sooner to the goal<br />
Than he who runneth after God<br />
With sweat of body and of soul.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

64 (IV. 31)  
THE BLESSED IDLENESS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Both John upon the breast and Mary at the feet<br />
Do nought but pass the happy hours away in sweet<br />
Love-dalliance with God.—I would not stir at all,<br />
Could I be idle so, even though the sky should fall.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

65 (I. 171)  
GOD IS FOUND BY NOT SEEKING

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>  God is not here nor there.<br />
Thou seekest where He may be found?<br />
Bound be thy hands and bound thy feet,<br />
  Body and soul be bound.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_121">p. 121</span>

 

66 (I. 240)  
THE PRAYER OF SILENCE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>So high above all things that be<br />
Is God uplifted, man can dare<br />
No utterance: he prayeth best<br />
When Silence is his sum of prayer.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

67 (II. 63)  
THE DEAF HEARETH THE WORD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Unto my hearing momently the Eternal Word doth come<br />
—Believe it, friend, or not—when I am deaf and dumb.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

68 (I. 239)  
GOD IS PRAISED IN SILENCE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Thinkest thou, foolish man, that with thy clapping tongue<br />
Praise of the silent Godhead fitly can be sung?<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

69 (V. 366)  
GOD'S LUTE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>A Heart, as God would have it, wholly still and mute.<br />
Loves to be played upon by Him—it is His lute.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_122">p. 122</span>

 

70 (II. 169)  
SAMENESS BEHOLDETH GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Be naught as all and all as naught, then art thou proved<br />
Worthy to see the face of God, the Well-Beloved.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

71 (I. 125)  
SAMENESS HATH NO PAIN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>To whom all things are one, to him all things are well,<br />
No matter though he lie deep in the pit of Hell.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

72 (II. 134)  
SAMENESS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The man who hath no fatherland,<br />
Who walks a stranger everywhere,<br />
Though he abide in Hell he'll find<br />
His darling country even there.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

73 (II. 42)  
NO HARM IN WHAT IS UNDERNEATH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Who sits above the mountain-tops<br />
And high above the clouds doth ride,<br />
Cares little when the lightnings flame<br />
And the loud-crashing thunders chide.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_123">p. 123</span>

 

74 (V. 136)  
ALL IS ALIKE TO THE WISE MAN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>All things are one to the Wise Man;<br />
He sitteth peacefully and still;<br />
Is his will thwarted, none the less<br />
All things befall as God doth will.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

75 (V. 85)  
WHO KNOWETH NAUGHT IS AT PEACE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Had Adam never plucked the Tree<br />
Of Knowledge and grown wise,<br />
He then had dwelt eternally<br />
At peace in Paradise.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

76 (I. 85)  
HOW GOD'S WORD IS HEARD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>If thou wouldst hear the Eternal Word speak unto thee,<br />
First must thou wholly lose the hearing faculty.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

77 (II. 8)  
MAN LEARNETH BY BEING SILENT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Be silent, silent, dearest one,<br />
Only be silent utterly.<br />
Then far beyond thy farthest wish<br />
God will show goodness unto thee.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_124">p. 124</span>

 

78 (II. 19)  
THE HIGHEST IS STILLNESS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Doing is good; far better prayer;<br />
But best of all if thou dost come<br />
Into the presence of the Lord<br />
With quiet footfall, still and dumb.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

79 (V. 221)  
THE DEAD HEAR NOT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The man who's dead unto himself<br />
Rests tranquil in his thought,<br />
Though all the world speak ill of him.<br />
How so?—Dead men hear naught.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

80 (I. 134)  
NOT PERFECTLY DEAD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>If over this and that thou makest such a stir,<br />
Then art thou not yet laid with God in the sepulchre.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

81 (II. 214)  
WORKS HAVE LIKE WORTH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Have no distinctions. Angels would at God's behest<br />
As lief cart dung as play their harps or take their rest.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_125">p. 125</span>

 

82 (II. 152)  
THE DIVINEST OF ALL

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Naught more divine than this—whatever the event,<br />
In this world or the next, to be indifferent.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

83 (VI. 191)  
WORLD FORSAKEN, LITTLE FORSAKEN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The whole great World is naught. Little has thou resigned,<br />
Though thou hast banished all the World out of thy mind.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

84 (VI. 142)  
ON FORSAKING THE WORLD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Need oftentime determines deed;<br />
And thou dost leave the world, maybe,<br />
Thy heart foreboding that the world<br />
Which thou dost leave is leaving thee.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
