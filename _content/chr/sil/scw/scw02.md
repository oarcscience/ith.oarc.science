## *II GOD*

<span id="page_110">p. 110</span>

 

31 (II. 48)  
GOD IS KNOWN IN THE CREATURE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The hidden God becometh known<br />
 And general to mankind<br />
In the created World of Things<br />
 Which He hath fashioned and designed.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

32 (V. 214)  
GOD IS ALL IN ALL

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God in the Christ is God;<br />
In angels, Form Angelical;<br />
In men is Man; and in the rest<br />
Is what thou wilt, the All in all.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

33 (V. 65)  
GOD CANNOT HIDE HIMSELF

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God cannot ever hide Himself—if hid He seem,<br />
'Tis thou that hast devised a hiding-place for Him.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

34 (V. 91)  
IN WHAT YEAR THE WORLD WAS CREATED

<table data-align="center">

</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

35 (II. 40)  
GOD IS THE SMALLEST AND THE GREATEST

<table data-align="center">

</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

36 (V. 75)  
NAUGHT EXISTETH WITHOUT JOY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Naught that is joyless can endure.<br />
Even the Being of God would pass,<br />
Had He no pleasure in Himself,<br />
And wither like the new-mown grass.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

37 (V. 189)  
GOD IS ETERNALLY IN LOVE WITH HIS OWN BEAUTY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God is so super-beautiful<br />
That He beholdeth in a trance<br />
Of rapture from eternity<br />
The Splendour of His Countenance.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<table data-align="center">

</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

39 (V. 50)  
GOD IS NOT VIRTUOUS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God is not virtuous. Out of Him virtue streams,<br />
As water from the Sea and from the Sun sun-beams.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

40 (V. 93)  
GOD CANNOT BE WRATH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God is not ever wrath with us.<br />
His wrath is but our dream.<br />
It were a thing impossible<br />
That wrath should be in Him.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

41 (I. 137)  
GOD DAMNETH NO MAN

<table data-align="center">

</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

</table>

45 (III. 142)

<table data-align="center">

</tbody>
</table>

 

46 (IV. 177)  
ASTONISHMENT AT GOD'S FAMILIARITY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">

Should dare to make myself the friend<br />
And very hail-fellow of God!<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

47 (I. 279)  
I-HOOD SHAPETH NAUGHT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Now this, now that, thou striv'st to shape<br />
With thine own I for instrument.<br />
Ah, wouldst thou but let God shape all<br />
Accordingly to His intent!<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

48 (IV. 143)

<table data-align="center">

<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">

Than when man's heart soundeth with God in Unison.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

49 (IV. 133)  
MAN IS A COAL.

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />

</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

Me, branch-wise, from the Vine.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

51 (II. 4)

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>

His own denial doth deny.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

</table>
