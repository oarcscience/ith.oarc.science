## *V INWARDNESS*

<span id="page_138">p. 138</span>

 

121 (I. 145)  
WHAT THOU WOULDST HAVE IS WITHIN THEE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>All Heaven is within thee, Man,<br />
And all of Hell within thy heart:<br />
What thou dost choose and will to have,<br />
That hast thou wheresoe'er thou art.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

122 (I. 82)  
HEAVEN IS WITHIN THEE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Heaven is within thee. Stay! Why runn'st thou here and there?<br />
Thou seekest God in vain seekest thou Him elsewhere.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

123 (IV. 183)  
ALL THAT THOU WILT IS WITHIN THEE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>All thou wouldst have lies now within thee, every whit<br />
'Tis thine—so long as thou dost never strive for it.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

124 (III. 118)  
THE PHILOSOPHERS' STONE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Travel within thyself! The Stone<br />
Philosophers with wisest arts<br />
Have vainly sought, cannot be found<br />
By travelling in foreign parts.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

125 (I. 50)  
THE THRONE OF GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Christian, dost thou demand to know<br />
 Where God hath set His Throne?<br />
Even there within thyself, where He<br />
 Gives birth to thee, His Son.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

126 (I. 61)  
GOD MUST BE BORN IN THEE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Though Jesus Christ in Bethlehem<br />
A thousand times his Mother bore,<br />
Is he not born again in thee<br />
Then art thou lost for evermore.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

127 (I. 62)  
THE EXTERNAL HELPETH THEE NOT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>In vain the Cross on Golgotha<br />
Was raised—thou hast not any part<br />
In its deliverance unless<br />
It be raised up within thy heart.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

128 (VI. 209)  
WHAT IS OUTWARD ADDETH NO WORTH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Nothing external to thee, Man,<br />
Can give thee Worth or Dignity:<br />
Fine harness maketh not the horse,<br />
Nor clothes the man's virility.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_140">p. 140</span>

 

129 (VI. 24)  
WHAT A MAN HATH IN HIMSELF HE SEEKETH NOT WITHOUT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>He who hath honour in himself<br />
Seeketh no honour among men.<br />
Seekest thou honour in the world,<br />
'Tis not thine own but alien.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

130 (VI. 169)  
WHAT A MAN WOULD NOT LOSE, THAT MUST HE BE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Wise Man <em>is</em> that which he hath.<br />
The precious Pearl of Paradise<br />
Wouldst thou not lose, then must thou be<br />
Thyself that Pearl of greatest price.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

131 (I. 298)  
HEAVEN IS WITHIN US

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>My Christian, whither runnest thou?<br />
All Heaven within thy heart doth wait.<br />
Why dost thou seek to find it then<br />
By knocking at another's gate?<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

132 (II. 149)  
THOU THYSELF ART ALL THINGS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>How is it possible for thee<br />
To feel desire or suffer dearth?<br />
Thou canst be all things in thyself—<br />
A thousand Angels, Heaven and Earth!<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

133 (VI. 166)  
HE WHO HATH THE KINGDOM IN HIMSELF CANNOT BECOME POOR

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God's Kingdom is within ourselvcs!<br />
If, then, so great a Kingdom be<br />
Already thine, how canst thou fear<br />
The threatening of poverty?<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

THY PRISON IS THYSELF

<colgroup>

</tbody>
</table>

<colgroup>

</table>
