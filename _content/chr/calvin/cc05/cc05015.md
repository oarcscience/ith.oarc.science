<span id="ii.iii.ii-p3"></span>**20.** Moreover, thou shalt not lie
carnally with thy neighbor’s wife, to defile thyself with her.

<span id="ii.iii.ii-p4"></span>**20.** Uxori proximi tui non dabis
concubitum tuum in semine, ut polluaris cum ea.

<span id="ii.iii.ii-p5"></span> 

<span id="ii.iii.ii-p6"></span><span id="ii.iii.ii-p6.1"></span>

<span id="ii.iii.ii-p7"></span>The object of this passage is the same as
that of the foregoing ones. For, whilst all fornication pollutes a man,
there is grosser impurity in adultery, because the sanctity of marriage
is violated, and by the commingling of seed a spurious and illegitimate
offspring is derived. Wherefore, God has justly enumerated this crime
amongst the abominations of the Gentiles, as may be more clearly seen
from the exordium of the chapter from whence this passage is taken.
