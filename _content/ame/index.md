### Pennsylvania Folklore

This part of the site has grown organically rather than by plan, and a
small number of texts about Pennsylvania have coalesced here, for no
particular reason.

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Pow-wows; or Long Lost
Friend](pow/index)</span>  
<span class="c_a">by George Hohman</span>. \[<span
class="c_d">1820</span>\]   <span class="c_b">A small book of folk-magic
and healing by a early nineteenth Century Pennsylvania Dutch
practicioner of 'Pow-wow'.</span> </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Folk-Lore of the Pennsylvania
Germans](fpg/index)</span>   by <span class="c_a">W. J. Hoffman</span>
*Journal of American Folk-lore* \[<span class="c_d">1888-9</span>\]  
<span class="c_b">A grabbag of folklore from 19th Century rural
Pennsylvania, including folk-magic, home remedies, superstitions and the
supernatural.</span> </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[The Realness of Witchcraft in
America](row/index)</span>   by <span class="c_a">A. Monroe Aurand,
Jr.</span> \[<span class="c_d">1942</span>\]   <span class="c_b">a
little pamphlet about Pennsylvania witchcraft.</span>  
Donated by Eliza Yetter of
[sacredspiral.com](https://www.sacredspiral.com). </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Early Life of the Pennsylvania

Donated by Eliza Yetter of
[sacredspiral.com](https://www.sacredspiral.com). </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Little Known Facts about the Amish

### Elsewhere at this site

[The Shakers](../chr/shaker/index)  
The Shakers, who fled persecution in England to flourish in America, are
a unique branch of Christianity with a feminist twist. Known for their
handicrafts, the Shakers declined in the twentieth century due to their
vows of celibacy and the impact of modernism.

[The Mormons](../mor/index)  
The Mormons are the largest world religion indigenous to America; their
revealed scripture is set in an ancient New World setting.

[Native America](../nam/index)  
Intangible Textual Heritage has extensive material on the religions of
the Native Americans.
