*Pow-Wows, or Long Lost Friend*, by John George Hoffman, \[1820\], at
Intangible Textual Heritage

------------------------------------------------------------------------

IN CONCLUSION THE FOLLOWING MORNING PRAYER IS GIVEN, WHICH IS TO BE
SPOKEN BEFORE ENTERING UPON A JOURNEY. IT PROTECTS AGAINST ALL MANNER OF
BAD LUCK.

Oh, Jesus of Nazareth, King of the Jews, yea, a King over the whole
world, protect me \[*name*\] during this day and night, protect me at
all times by thy five holy wounds, that I may not be seized and bound.
The Holy Trinity guard me, that no gun, fire-arm, ball or lead, shall
touch my body; and that they shall be weak like the tears and bloody
sweat of Jesus Christ, in the name of God the Father, the Son and the
Holy Ghost. Amen.
