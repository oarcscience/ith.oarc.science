# The Gospel of Barnabas

### trans. Lonsdale and Laura Ragg

##### \[London, 1907\]

------------------------------------------------------------------------

*The Gospel of Barnabas* is an apocryphal gospel. That is, it is a life
of Jesus purportedly written by a first-hand observer that is at
variance with the picture(s) presented in the Bible. However, it is
unique among apocrypha in that it is a *Muslim* gospel; that is, it
presents Jesus as a human prophet, *not* the son of God, and as a
forerunner of Muhammad. According to western scholarship, it is a
fourteenth-century forgery, extant now only in Spanish and Italian
manuscripts, but even among scholars there is disagreement as to whether
or not some some of the material contained in the book is older. The
*Gospel* has been picked up by some modern Muslims, though, as an
authentic and ancient record of events, and there are many different
printed versions available from various Muslim publishing houses, all
based heavily on the version by the Raggs presented here. It must be
stressed, however, that belief in this Gospel is in no way an article of
Islamic faith, and this site is not the place to discuss either the
authenticity of the book or how widespread belief in or even knowledge
of it is in the Islamic world. A search on Google will turn up dozens of
pages and even entire sites devoted to discussion of the *Gospel of
Barnabas* from all manner of perspectives—Christian, Muslim, and
scholarly—to which sites we must defer for discussion of the topic.
Regardless of the provenance of the document, it is an interesting read,
similar to the many religious romances of the Mediterranean world, such
as the apocryphal acts of the apostles (located
[here](https://wesley.nnu.edu/biblical_studies/noncanon/acts) at the
Noncanonical homepage) and the books of sacred history from the east, a
few of which are located [here](../../chr/index.htm#eastern) at
Intangible Textual Heritage.

[Prologue](gbar000)  
[1. In this first chapter is contained the annunciation of the angel
Gabriel to the Virgin Mary concerning the birth of Jesus.](gbar001)  
[2. The warning of the angel Gabriel given to Joseph concerning the
conception of the Virgin Mary.](gbar002)  
[3. Wonderful birth of Jesus and appearance of angels praising
God.](gbar003)  
[4. Angels announce to the shepherds the birth of Jesus, and they, after
having found him, announce him.](gbar004)  
[5. Circumcision of Jesus.](gbar005)  
[6. Three magi are led by a star in the east to Judaea, and, finding
Jesus, make obeisance to him and gifts.](gbar006)  
[7. The visitation of Jesus by magi, and their return to their own
country, with the warning of Jesus given to them in a dream.](gbar007)  
[8. Jesus is carried in flight to Egypt, and Herod massacres the
innocent children.](gbar008)  
[9. Jesus, having returned to Judaea, holds a wondrous disputation with
the doctors, having come to the age of twelve years.](gbar009)  
[10. Jesus, at the age of thirty years, on Mount Olives, miraculously
receiveth the gospel from the angel Gabriel.](gbar010)  
[11. Jesus miraculously healeth a leper, and goeth into
Jerusalem.](gbar011)  
[12. First sermon of Jesus delivered to the people: wonderful in
doctrine concerning the name of God.](gbar012)  
[13. The remarkable fear of Jesus and his prayer, and the wonderful
comfort of the angel Gabriel.](gbar013)  
[14. After the fast of forty days, Jesus chooseth twelve
apostles.](gbar014)  
[15. Miracle wrought by Jesus at the marriage, turning the water into
wine.](gbar015)  
[16. Wonderful teaching given by Jesus to his apostles concerning
conversion from the evil life.](gbar016)  
[17. In this chapter is clearly perceived the unbelief of Christians,
and the true faith of Mumin.](gbar017)  
[18. Here is shown forth the persecution of the servants of God by the
world, and God's protection saving them.](gbar018)  
[19. Jesus foretelleth his betrayal, and, descending from the mountain,
healeth ten lepers.](gbar019)  
[20. Miracle on the sea wrought by Jesus, and Jesus declares where the
prophet is received.](gbar020)  
[21. Jesus healeth a demoniac, and the swine are cast into the sea.
Afterwards he healeth the daughter of the Canaanites.](gbar021)  
[22. Miserable condition of the uncircumcised in that a dog is better
than they.](gbar022)  
[23. Origin of circumcision, and covenant of God with Abraham, and
damnation of the uncircumcised.](gbar023)  
[24. Notable example how one ought to flee from banqueting and
feasting.](gbar024)  
[25. How one ought to despise the flesh, and how one ought to live in
the world.](gbar025)  
[26. How one ought to love God. And in this chapter is contained the
wonderful contention of Abraham with his father.](gbar026)  
[27. In this chapter is clearly seen how improper is laughter in men:
also the prudence of Abraham.](gbar027)
