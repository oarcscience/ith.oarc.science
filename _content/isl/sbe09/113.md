### THE CHAPTER OF THE DAYBREAK.

### (CXIII. Place of origin doubtful.)

IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.

Say, 'I seek refuge in the Lord of the daybreak, from the evil of what
He has created; and from the evil of the night when it cometh on <span
id="fr_509"></span>[3](#fn_509); and from the evil of the blowers upon
knots <span id="fr_510"></span>[4](#fn_510); \[5\] and from the evil of
the envious when he envies.'

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_509"></span>[344:3](113.htm#fr_509) Or, according to a
traditional explanation given by the prophet to ‘Âyeshah, 'the moon when
it is eclipsed.'

<span id="fn_510"></span>[344:4](113.htm#fr_510) Witches who make knots
in string and blow upon them, uttering at the same time some magical
formula and the name of the persons they wish to injure.
