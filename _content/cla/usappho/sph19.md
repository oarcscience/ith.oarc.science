# <span id="BM18">18</span>

Αρτίωσ μ᾽ ἀ χρυσοπέδιλλοσ Ἀύωσ. [\[transcription\]](xlatall.htm#31)

Just now the golden-sandalled Dawn \[has called\].

There could hardly be a more beautiful epithet than "golden-sandalled"
to apply to the Dawn. It is fully equal in this respect to
"rosy-fingered," and in Greek both words are beautiful in sound.

This is quoted by Ammonius of Alexandria about A.D. 400 to show Sappho's
use of Ἀρτίωσ [\[transcription\]](xlatall.htm#32) .
