------------------------------------------------------------------------

This short treatise of Archimedes on the amount of sand grains which
could be contained in the ancient model of the universe is remarkable.
It uses some extremely large numbers, given that this was antiquity and
Europeans did not yet have the benefit of decimal notation. It also
anticipates the method of Calculus: measuring by subdivision into very
small quanta, in this case grains of sand. Also of interest are
Archimedes' (incorrect but informed) estimates of the relative size of
the sun, moon and earth. The argument can be followed by anyone who has
mastered basic algebra.
