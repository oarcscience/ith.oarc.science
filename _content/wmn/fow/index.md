# Folk-Lore of Women

## By Thomas Firminger Thiselton-Dyer

#### 1906

------------------------------------------------------------------------

*Folklore of misogny* would probably be a better title for this book.
Replete with Victorian stereotypes of women, this book draws on many
different traditions from around the world to portray women as
simultaneously the 'weaker sex' and the source of all evil. Missing or
scarce here are women's voices or traditions. However, this work is an
essential starting point if you want to enumerate traditional male
attitudes about women.

The reverend T.F. Thiselton-Dyer (1843-1928) apparently wrote several
other folkore compilations and monographs on botanical subjects.
