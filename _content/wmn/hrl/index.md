Gilamn's feminist novel, Herland was one of a flood of utopian books at
the turn of the 20th century where sex roles are inverted. Gilman
imagined a society where women not only rule, but are the *only* gender.
Reproducing by parthenogenesis, they worship a female deity. This
particular book later became a must-read for the second-wave feminists,
particularly the 'separatist' wing.
