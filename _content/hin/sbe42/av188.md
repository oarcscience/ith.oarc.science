## VI, 26. Charm to avert evil.

1\. Let me go, O evil; being powerful, take thou pity on us! Set me, O
evil, unharmed, into the world of happiness!  
2. If, O evil, thou dost not abandon us, then do we abandon thee at the
fork of the road. May evil follow after another (man)!  
3. Away from us may thousand-eyed, immortal (evil) dwell! Him whom we
hate may it strike, and him whom we hate do thou surely smite!
