34\. Nor also is there non-contradiction from paryâya; on account of
change, and so on.

Nor is the difficulty to be evaded by the assumption of the soul
assuming a different condition through contraction or dilatation. For
this would imply that the soul is subject

<span id="page_520">p. 520</span>

to change, and all the imperfections springing from it, viz.
non-permanence, and so on, and hence would not be superior to
non-sentient things such as jars and the like.
