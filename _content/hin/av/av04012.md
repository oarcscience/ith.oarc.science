### HYMN XII

#### A charm to mend a broken bone

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401201">1</span></span>Thou art the
healer, making whole, the healer of the broken  
   bone:  
  Make thou this whole, Arundhatī!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401202">2</span></span>Whatever
bone of thine within thy body hath been wrenched or  
   cracked,  
  May Dhātar set it properly and join together limb by limb.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401203">3</span></span>With marrow
be the marrow joined, thy limb united with the  
   limb.  
  Let what hath fallen of thy flesh, and the bone also grow again.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401204">4</span></span>Let marrow
close with marrow, let skin grow united with the  
   skin.  
  Let blood and bone grow strong in thee, flesh grow together  
   with the flesh.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401205">5</span></span>Join thou
together hair with hair, join thou together skin with  
   skin.  
  Let blood and bone grow strong in thee. Unite the broken part,.  
  O Plant.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401206">6</span></span>Arise,
advance, speed forth; the car hath goodly fellies, naves,  
   and wheels!!  
  Stand up erect upon thy feet.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401207">7</span></span>If he be
torn and shattered, having fallen into a pit, or a cast  
   stone have struck him,  
  Let the skilled leech join limb with limb, as 'twere the portions  
   of a car.
