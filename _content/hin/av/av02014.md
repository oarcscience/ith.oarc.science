### HYMN XIV

#### A charm to banish vermin and noxious creatures

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0201401">1</span></span>Forth from
the hall the bold, the sharp, the greedy one, the  
   single-voiced,  
  Sadānvās, and all progeny of Chanda we exterminate.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0201402">2</span></span>We drive you
forth from cattle-shed, from axle, from within the  
   wain,  
  Ye daughters of Magundi, we frighten and chase you from out  
   homes.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0201403">3</span></span>Yonder let
the Arāyis dwell, there where that house is down  
   below.  
  Let utter indigence and all the Yātudhānis settle there.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0201404">4</span></span>May
Bhūtapati drive away, and Indra, the Sadānvās hence.  
  Let Indra with his bolt quell those who sit upon our dwelling's  
   floor.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0201405">5</span></span>Whether ye
be of farm and field, or whether ye be sent by men,  
  Or be ye sprung from Dasyu race, vanish, Sadānvās, and begone.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0201406">6</span></span>I have gone
round their homes as runs a fleet-foot racer round  
   the post,  
  And in all races conquered you. Vanish, Sadānvās, and begone.
