### HYMN XXXVIII

#### A maiden's love-charm

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0703801">1</span></span>I dig this
Healing Herb that makes my lover look on me and  
   weep;  
  That bids the parting friend return and kindly greets him as he  
   comes.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0703802">2</span></span>This Herb
wherewith the Asuri drew Indra downward from the  
  Gods,  
  With this same Herb I draw thee close that I may be most dear  
   to thee.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0703803">3</span></span>Thou art the
peer of Soma, yea, thou art the equal of the Sun, <span
id="page_a285">p. a285</span>  
  The peer of all the Gods art thou: therefore we call thee hither—  
   ward.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0703804">4</span></span>I am the
speaker here, not thou: speak thou where the assembly  
   meets.  
  Thou shalt be mine and only mine, and never mention other  
   dames.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0703805">5</span></span>If thou art
far away beyond the rivers, far away from men,  
  This Herb shall seem to bind thee fast and bring thee back my  
   prisoner.
