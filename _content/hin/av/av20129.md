### HYMN CXXIX

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012901">1</span></span>These mares
come springing forward to Pratipa Prātisutvana.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012903">3</span></span>4. One of
them is Hariknikā. Hariknikā, what seekest thou?  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012905">5</span></span>6. The
excellent, the golden son: where now hast thou aban-  
   doned him?  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012907">7</span></span>8. There
where around those distant trees, three Sisus that are  
   standing there,  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012909">9</span></span>10. Three
adders, breathing angrily, are blowing loud the  
   threatening horn.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012911">11</span></span>12. Hither
hath come a stallion: he is known by droppings on  
   his way,  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012913">13</span></span>14. As by
their dung the course of kine. What wouldst thou in  
   the home of men?  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012915">15</span></span>16. Barley
and ripened rice I seek. On rice and barley hast thou  
   fed,  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012917">17</span></span>18. As the
big serpent feeds on sheep. Cow's hoof and horse's  
   tail hast thou,  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012919">19</span></span>20. Winged
with a falcon's pinion is that harmless swelling of  
   thy tongue.
