### HYMN LXXXIV

#### A charm to accompany the symbolical loosing of sacrificial victims

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0608401">1</span></span>Thou in
whose dread mouth I present oblation, that these bound  
   victims may obtain their freedom,  
  The people deem that thou art Earth: I know thee thoroughly,  
   and I say thou art Destruction.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0608402">2</span></span>Be thou
enriched, O Welfare, with oblations, here among us is  
   thine allotted portion.  
  Free—Hail to thee!—from sin those here and yonder.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0608403">3</span></span>Do thou,
Destruction, thus, without a rival, release us from the  
   iron bonds that hind us.  
  To me doth Yama verily restore thee. To him, to Yama, yea, to  
  Death be worship!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0608404">4</span></span>Thou hast
been fastened to an iron pillar, here compassed with  
   a thousand deaths around thee.  
  In full accord with Yama and the Fathers, send this man up-  
   ward to the loftiest heaven.
