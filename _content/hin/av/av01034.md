### HYMN XXXIV

#### A young man's love-charm

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0103400">0</span></span>1. From
honey sprang this Plant to life; with honey now we dig  
   thee up.  
  Make us as sweet as honey, for from honey hast thou been pro-  
   duced.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0103402">2</span></span>My tongue
hath honey at the tip, and sweetest honey at the root: <span
id="page_a31">p. a31</span>  
  Thou yieldest to my wish and will, and shalt be mine and only  
   mine.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0103403">3</span></span>My coming in
is honey-sweet and honey-sweet, my going forth:  
  My voice and words are sweet: I fain would be like honey in  
   my look.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0103404">4</span></span>Sweeter am I
than honey, yet more full of sweets than licorice:  
  So mayst thou love me as a branch full of all sweets, and only me.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0103405">5</span></span>Around thee
have I girt a zone of sugar-cane to banish hate.  
  That thou mayst be in love with me, my darling never to depart.
