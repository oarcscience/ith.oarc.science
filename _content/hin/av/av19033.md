### HYMN XXXIII

#### A protective and benedictive charm

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1903301">1</span></span>Hundred
stemmed, succulent, and worth a thousand, the Royal  
  Rite of plants, the Water's Agni,  
  Let this same Darbha guard us from all quarters. This Godlike  
  Amulet shall with life endow us.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1903302">2</span></span>Drawn forth
from butter, juicy, sweetly-flavoured, firm as the  
   earth, unshaken, overthrowing.  
  Driving off foes and casting them beneath me, mount with the  
   strength of mighty Ones, O Darbha.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1903303">3</span></span>Thou movest
o'er the earth with vigour: lovely in sacrifice thou  
   sittest on the altar.  
  The Rishis bear thee as a purifier: cleanse thou us from all evil  
   deeds' defilement.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1903304">4</span></span>A stern and
all-victorious king, foe-queller, dear to every man—  
  That energy of Gods and mighty power, I bind this on thee for  
   long life and welfare. <span id="page_239">p. 239</span>  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1903305">5</span></span>Achieve
heroic deeds with Darbha, wearing this Darbha never  
   let thy soul be troubled.  
  In splendour and precedence over others illumine like the Sun  
   the heaven's four regions.
