### HYMN CXXIV

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012401">1</span></span>With what
help will he come to us, wonderful, ever-waxing  
  Friend,  
  With what most mighty company?  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012402">2</span></span>What genuine
and most liberal draught will spirit thee with  
   juice to burst.  
  Open e'en strongly-guarded wealth?  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012403">3</span></span>Do thou who
art protector us thy friends who praise thee  
  With hundred aids approach us.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012404">4</span></span>We will,
with Indra and all Gods to help us, bring these existing  
   worlds into subjection.  
  Our sacrifice, our bodies, and our offspring shall Indra form  
   together with the Ādityas.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012405">5</span></span>With the
Ādityas, with the band of Maruts, may Indra be pro-  
   tector of our bodies.  
  As when the Gods came after they had slaughtered the Asuras,  
   keeping safe their Godlike nature,  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2012406">6</span></span>Brought the
Sun hitherward with mighty powers, and looked  
   about them on their vigorous Godhead. <span id="page_359">p.
359</span>  
  With this may we obtain strength God-appointed, and joy with  
   brave sons through a hundred winters.
