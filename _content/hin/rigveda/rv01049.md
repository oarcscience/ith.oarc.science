### HYMN XLIX. Dawn.

1 E'EN from above the sky's bright realm come, Uṣas, by auspicious
ways:  
Let red steeds bear thee to the house of him who pours the Soma,
juice.  
2 The chariot which thou mountest, fair of shape, O Uṣas light to
move,—  
Therewith, O Daughter of the Sky, aid men of noble fame today.  
3 Bright Uṣas, when thy times return, all quadrupeds and bipeds stir,  
And round about flock winged birds from all the boundaries of heaven.  
4 Thou dawning with thy beams of light illumest all the radiant realm.  
Thee, as thou art, the Kaṇvas, fain for wealth, have called with sacred
songs.
