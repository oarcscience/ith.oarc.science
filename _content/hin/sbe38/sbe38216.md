7\. And on account of definite rules.

'Performing works here (i.e. in this life) let a man wish to live a
hundred years; thus work will not cling to thee, man; there is no other
way than that' (Î*s*a. Up. 2); 'The

<span id="page_290">p. 290</span>

<span class="contnote">\[paragraph continues\]</span> Agnihotra is a
sattra lasting up to old age and death; for through old age one is freed
from it or through death' (*S*at. Brâ. XII, 4, 1, 1); from such definite
rules also it follows that knowledge is merely supplementary to works.

Against all these objections the Sûtrakâra upholds his view in the
following Sûtra.
