39\. And on account of the impossibility of meditation (samâdhi).

Moreover the meditation taught in the Vedânta-texts, whose aim is the
realisation of the Self as represented by the Upanishads, is possible
only if the Self is the agent <span id="fr_34"></span>[2](#fn_34).
Compare the following passages, 'Verily, the Self is to be seen, to be
heard, to be perceived, to be marked' (B*ri*. Up. II, 4, 5); 'The Self
we must seek out, we must try to understand' (*Kh*. Up. VIII, 7, 1);
'Meditate on the Self as Om' (Mu. Up. II, 2, 6).--Therefrom also it
follows that the Self is an agent.
