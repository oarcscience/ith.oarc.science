### KHA*ND*A 4.

1 <span id="fr_243"></span>[1](#fn_242). Now (follows) the Âbhyudayika
(i.e. the *S*râddha ceremony referring to good luck).

<span id="page_111">p. 111</span>

2\. In the fortnight of the increasing moon, on an auspicious day,

3 <span id="fr_244"></span>[3](#fn_243). After the sacrifice to the
mothers has been performed,

4\. And an even number of (Brâhma*n*as) versed in the Veda have been
invited to sit down;

5\. In the forenoon;

6 <span id="fr_245"></span>[6](#fn_244). The rite is performed from left
to right.

7 <span id="fr_246"></span>[7](#fn_245). The murmuring with the omission
of the verses belonging to the Manes.

8\. The Darbha blades are straight.

9 <span id="fr_247"></span>[9](#fn_246). Barley is to be used instead of
sesamum.

10\. The lumps are mixed with curds, jujube fruits, fried grain.

11 <span id="fr_248"></span>[11](#fn_247). On inviting (the Manes, he
should say), 'The Nândîmukha (glad-faced?) Manes will I invite.'

12 <span id="fr_249"></span>[12](#fn_248). 'May the Nândîmukha Manes be
rejoiced,' instead of 'imperishable.'

13 <span id="fr_250"></span>[13](#fn_249). 'The Nândîmukha Manes will I
make speak,' when he makes (the Brâhma*n*as) speak.

14 <span id="fr_251"></span>[14](#fn_250). '(Was it) well done?'—thus
are they to be asked whether they are satiated.

<span id="page_112">p. 112</span>

15\. The rest is the same (as in the other kinds of *S*râddha rites), as
far as it is not [prohibited](errata.htm#0) (by contrary rules).
