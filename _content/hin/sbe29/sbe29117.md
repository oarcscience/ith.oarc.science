### KA*ND*IKÂ 18.

1\. Thereby the Godânakarman (i.e. the ceremony of shaving the beard, is
declared).

2\. In the sixteenth year.

3\. Instead of the word 'hair' he should (each time that it occurs in
the Mantras) put the word 'beard.'

4 <span id="fr_450"></span>[4](#fn_449). Here they moisten the beard.

5 <span id="fr_451"></span>[5](#fn_450). (The Mantra is), 'Purify his
head and his face, but do not take away his life.'

6 <span id="fr_452"></span>[6](#fn_451). He gives orders (to the barber
with the words), 'Arrange his hair, his beard, the hair of his body, and
his nails, ending in the north.'

7 <span id="fr_453"></span>[7-8](#fn_452). Having bathed and silently
stood during the rest of the day, let him break his silence in the
presence of his teacher, (saying to him,) 'I give an optional gift (to
thee).'

8\. An ox and a cow is the sacrificial fee.

<span id="page_187">p. 187</span>

9 <span id="fr_454"></span>[9](#fn_453). Let (the teacher) impose (on
the youth the observances declared below) for one year.
