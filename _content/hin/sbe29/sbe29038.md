### KHA*ND*A 8.

1 <span id="fr_162"></span>[1](#fn_161). In the afternoon, having
obtained by begging fried barley grains, he shall sacrifice them with
his hand on the fire according to the rites of the Â*g*ya oblations with
the text, 'The lord of the seat, the

<span id="page_74">p. 74</span>

wonderful' (Rig-veda I, 18, 6 seq.), verse by verse, down to the end of
the hymn,

2 <span id="fr_163"></span>[8\_2](#fn_162). Causing the teacher by (the
gift of) food to pronounce auspicious wishes.

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_161"></span>[73:1](sbe29038.htm#fr_162) 8, 1. This is the
Anuprava*k*anîyahoma treated of by Â*s*valâyana at I, 22, 12 seq. There
it is stated that this sacrifice should be performed as well after the
recitation of the Sâvitrî as after the other <span id="page_74">p.
74</span> portions of the Veda, for instance, as the commentary there
has it, after the Mahânâmnîs, the Mahâvrata, and the Upanishad have been
recited. Nârâya*n*a indicates the time of this sacrifice in the words,
'On that same fast-day (chap. 7, 29) in the afternoon.'

<span id="fn_162"></span>[74:8\_2](sbe29038.htm#fr_163) 'He shall, by
pronouncing such words as svasti bhavanto bruvantu, dispose the teacher
favourably so that he may say svasti!' Nârâya*n*a.
