### KA*ND*IKÂ 18.

1 <span id="fr_752"></span>[1](#fn_751). When he returns from a journey,
he approaches his house in the manner stated above.

2\. When he sees his son, he murmurs, 'From limb by limb thou art
produced; out of the heart thou art born. Thou indeed art the Self
called son; so live a hundred autumns!'

3\. He then kisses his head with (the words), 'With the hi*m*kâra (the
mystical syllable hiṅ) of Pra*g*âpati, which gives thousandfold life, I
kiss thee, N.N.! Live a hundred autumns!'—

4\. And three times with (the words), 'With the hi*m*kâra of the cows.'

5 <span id="fr_753"></span>[5](#fn_752). In his right ear he murmurs,
'Bestow on us, O bountiful, onward-pressing Indra, plentiful, rich
treasures. Give us a hundred autumns to live; give us many heroes,
strong jawed Indra;'

6 <span id="fr_754"></span>[6](#fn_753). In the left ear, 'Indra, bestow
on us the best treasures, insight of mind, happiness, increase of
wealth, health of our bodies, sweetness of speech, and that our days may
be good days.'

7\. For a girl he only kisses the head silently.
