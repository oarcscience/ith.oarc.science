This is a translation of the Hindu law books written about the sixth
century CE by Narada and Brihaspati. These are later works than the
other legal writings in the Sacred Books of the East series, such as
[The Laws of Manu](../manu) (SBE25), [The Institutes of
Vishnu](../sbe07/index) (SBE07), [Sacred Laws of the Aryas, Part
I](../sbe02/index) (SBE02), and [Sacred Laws of the Aryas, Part
II](../sbe14/index) (SBE14), all composed prior to 200 BCE at the
latest. According to Jolly, the courtroom procedure discussed here is
very similar to that described in the earliest known Hindu drama, [The
Little Clay Cart](../lcc/index).
