**THE TWELFTH BOOK**

** **

**Chapter I**

** **

**On the description of Gâyatrî**

 

<span id="page_1139">p. 1139</span>

 

1-7. Nârada said :-- O Deva! The rules of Sadâchâra (right ways of
living) and the all-sin-destroying unequalled Glories of the Devî
Bhagavatî have been described by Thee. And I, too, have heard the nectar
of the Glories of the Devî from Thy lotus mouth. The Chândrâya<u>n</u>a
and other Vratas; described by Thee, are very difficult to practise. So
they are impracticable with the ordinary persons. Therefore, O Lord!
Kindly describe those actions which can easily be carried out by common
persons, at the same time, the Devî's Grace and Siddhis can be obtained
by those practices. Again what thou hast described about the Gâyatrî in
connection with Sadâchâra kindly say which are the chief and foremost as
well as those that are more meritorious. O Best of the Munis! Thou hast
told that there are the twenty-four syllables in the Gâyatrî. Kindly
describe now their Ri<u>s</u>is, Chhandas, Devatâs and other things that
should be known regarding them and thus satisfy my longings.

 

8-27. S’rî Nârâya<u>n</u>a said :-- O Nârada! The twice-born would have
done what they ought to do if they be engaged in repeating their Gâyatrî
only, whether they be able or not able to practise the
Chândrâya<u>n</u>a and the other vratas. Whichever Brâhmi<u>n</u>
repeats the Gâyatrî three thousand times and offers Arghya to the Sun in
the three Sandhyâ times, the Devas worship him; what to speak of other
ordinary persons! Whether he practises Nyâsa or not, if anybody
sincerely repeats the Gâyatrî Devî, Whose Nature is Existence,
Intelligence, and Bliss and meditates on Her, even if he attains siddhi
in one syllable even, then as a result of that, he can vie with the best
of the Brâhma<u>n</u>as, the Moon, and the Sun; nay, with Brahmâ,
Vi<u>sn</u>u, and Mahes'vara even! O Nârada! Now I will tell in due
order the Ri<u>s</u>is, Chhandas, and the Devatâs of the twenty-four
syllables of the Gâyatrî. The Ri<u>s</u>is, in due order, are (1) Vâma
Deva, (2) Attri, (3) Vas'i<u>st</u>ha, (4) S’ukra, (5) Ka<u>n</u>va, (6)
Parâs'ara, (7) the very fiery Vis'vamitra, (8) Kapila, (9)
S’au<u>n</u>aka, (10) Yâjñavalkya, (11) Bharadvâja, (12) the ascetic
Jamadagni, (13) Gautama, (14) Mudgala, (15) Vedavyâsa, (16) Lomas'a,
(17) Agastya, (18) Kaus'ika, (19) Vatsya, (20) Pulastya, (21)
Mâ<u>nd</u>uka, (22) the ascetic in chief Durvâsâ, (23) Nârada and (24)
Kas'yapa.

 

<span id="page_1140">p. 1140</span>

 

Now about the chhandas :-- (1) Gâyatrî, (2) U<u>sn</u>ik, (3)
Anu<u>st</u>up, (4) Brihatî, (5) Pankti, (6) Tri<u>sn</u>up, (7) Jagatî,
(8) Atijagatî, (9) S’akkarî, (10) Ati S’akkarî, (11) Dhriti, (12) Ati
Dhriti, (13) Virâ<u>t</u>, (14) Prastârapankti, (15) K<u>r</u>iti, (16)
Prâkriti, (17) Âkriti, (18) Vik<u>r</u>iti, (19) Samk<u>r</u>iti, (20)
Ak<u>s</u>arapankti, (21) Bhu<u>h</u>, (22) Bhuva<u>h</u>, (23)
Sva<u>h</u> and (24) Jyoti<u>s</u>matî. The Devatâs of the several
letters in due order, are :-- (1) Agni, (2) Prajâpati, (3) Soma, (4)
Îs'âna, (5) Savitâ, (6) Âditya, (7) Brihaspati, (8) Maitrâvaru<u>n</u>a,
(9) Bhagadeva, (10) Aryamâ, (11) Ga<u>n</u>es'a, (12) Tva<u>st</u>râ,
(13) Pû<u>s</u>â, (14) Indrâgnî, (l5) Vâyu, (16) Vâmadeva, (17) Maitrâ
varunî (18) Vis'vadeva, (19) Mâtrikâ, (20) Vi<u>sn</u>u, (21) Vasu, (22)
Rudra Deva, (23) Kuvera, and (24) the twin As'vinî Kumâras. O Nârada!
Thus I have described to you about the the Devatâs of the twenty-four
syllables. The hearing of this destroys all sins and yields the full
results of repeating the mantra Gâyatrî. (Note :-- The Devatâs,
mentioned in the Gâyatrî Brahmâ Kalpa are different from those mentioned
here.)

 

Here ends the first Chapter of the Twelfth Book on the description of
Gâyatrî in the Mahâpurâ<u>n</u>am S’rî Mad Devî Bhâgavatam of 18,000
verses by Mahar<u>s</u>i Veda Vyâsa.
