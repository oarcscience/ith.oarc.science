## \[SECT. CXXXIV.—EMPEROR RI-CHIU (PART IV.—VARIOUS DEEDS).\]

The Heavenly Sovereign thereupon first appointed the Suzerain of Achi to
the office of Treasurer, <span id="fr_2173"></span>[1](#fn_2168) and
likewise bestowed on him domains. <span
id="fr_2174"></span>[2](#fn_2169) Again in this august reign the name of
Waka-sakura Tribe <span id="fr_2175"></span>[3](#fn_2170) was granted to
the Grandees of the Waka-sakura Tribe. <span
id="fr_2176"></span>[4](#fn_2171) Again the gentile name <span
id="fr_2177"></span>[5](#fn_2172) of Dukes of Himeda <span
id="fr_2178"></span>[6](#fn_2173) was granted to the Dukes of Himeda.
Again the Ihare Clan <span id="fr_2179"></span>[7](#fn_2174) was
established.

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_2168"></span>[363:1](kj141.htm#fr_2173) <span
id="page_363">p. 363</span> In Japanese the same word is used for a
"store-house" and for the "treasury." But the appointment here mentioned
would seem really to correspond to what we should call Lord of the
Treasury or Minister of Finance. The characters in the original are
<span id="img_36302"></span> ![](img/36302.jpg).

<span id="fn_2169"></span>[363:2](kj141.htm#fr_2174) Literally, "ration
grounds."

<span id="fn_2170"></span>[363:3](kj141.htm#fr_2175) *Waka-sakura-be*.

<span id="fn_2171"></span>[363:4](kj141.htm#fr_2176) *Waka-sakura-be no
omi*. Conf. Sect. CXXXI, Note 1.

<span id="fn_2172"></span>[363:5](kj141.htm#fr_2177) All the editors
agree in here reading as *kabane* ("gentile name," see Sect. XIV, Note
5) the character <span id="img_36303"></span> ![](img/36303.jpg), which
signifies properly "family name."

<span id="fn_2173"></span>[363:6](kj141.htm#fr_2178) *Himeda no kimi*.
Nothing is known of this family.

<span id="fn_2174"></span>[363:7](kj141.htm#fr_2179) *Ihare-be*. For
Ihare see Sect. XLIII, Note 6. It will be remembered that the Emperor of
whose reign the present Section forms part held his court at Ihare.
