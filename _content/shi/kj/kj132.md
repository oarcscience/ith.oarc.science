## \[SECT. CXXV.—EMPEROR NIN-TOKU (PART VIII.—HE LOVES YATA-NO-WAKI-IRATSUME).\]

The Heavenly Sovereign, loving Yata-no-waki-iratsume, deigned to send
her an august Song. That Song said:

<div style="margin-left: 32px">

"Will the one sedge-stem of Yata, having no children, wither as it
stands? Poor sedge-moor! Sedge-moor indeed is what I may *say*—poor pure
girl!" <span id="fr_2104"></span>[1](#fn_2099)

</div>

Then Yata-no-waki-iratsume replied in a Song, saying:

<div style="margin-left: 32px">

"Even though the one sedge stem of Yata be alone, if the Great Lord say
it is right even though it be alone \[it is right.\]" <span
id="fr_2105"></span>[2](#fn_2100)

</div>

So the Yata Tribe <span id="fr_2106"></span>[3](#fn_2101) was
established as the august proxy of Yata-no-waki-iratsume.

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_2099"></span>[349:1](kj132.htm#fr_2104) In this Song the
Emperor condoles with his mistress on her childlessness: "Will the
single sedge on the moor of Yata die without leaving any offspring?
Sedge, indeed! Yes, sedge is the term I use for my metaphor, but what is
in my thoughts is the girl I love."—There is in the original a
*jeu-de-mots*, not capable of translation into English, between *suge*
or *suga*, "sedge, and *sugashi* pure."

<span id="fn_2100"></span>[349:2](kj132.htm#fr_2105) The girl replies:
"Even though I be childless. I care not if my lord cares not."

<span id="fn_2101"></span>[349:3](kj132.htm#fr_2106) *Yata-be*.
