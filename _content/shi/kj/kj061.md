## \[SECT. LIV.—EMPEROR JIM-MU (PART XI.—HIS AGE AND PLACE OF BURIAL).

His Augustness Kamu-nuna-kaha-mimi ruled the Empire. Altogether the
august years of this Heavenly Sovereign Kamu-yamato-ihare-biko were one
hundred and thirty-seven. <span id="fr_1052"></span>[1](#fn_1048) His
august mausoleum is on the top of the Kashi Spur on the northern side of
Mount Unebi. <span id="fr_1053"></span>[2](#fn_1049)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_1048"></span>[188:1](kj061.htm#fr_1052) <span
id="page_188">p. 188</span> The author's confused style must not here
mislead the student. It is after the decease of the Emperor Jim-mu
(Kamu-yamato-ihare-biko), who attains to the age of a hundred and
thirty-seven, that the Emperor Suwi-zei (Kamu-nuna-kaha-mimi) begins to
rule.

<span id="fn_1049"></span>[188:2](kj061.htm#fr_1053) For *Unebi* see
Sect. LI, Note 9. *Kashi* signifies "oak." The word rendered "spur "is
*wo*.
