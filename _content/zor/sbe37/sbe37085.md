### CHAPTER XXXVI.

*Var*s*tmânsar Nask*.

1\. The thirteenth fargar*d*, U*s*tavaiti <span
id="fr_983"></span>[4](#fn_974), is about the great reward of him who,
through virtuous procedure, may occasion the benefit of a man <span
id="fr_984"></span>[5](#fn_975) *and of* the religion of righteousness
also. 2. This, too, that the maintenance of righteousness <span
id="fr_985"></span>[6](#fn_976) is through the practice *of it*.

3\. About the tokens of a righteous man—that is, the evidence of him—and
*his* reverence for duty *and* good works; also his imperceptible
perversion (kastârîh)—that is, not a single sin is manifest

<span id="page_270">p. 270</span>

in him—and *he is* an accomplisher *of* the stipulations of
Vohûman <span id="fr_986"></span>[1](#fn_977), good thoughts, good
words, and good deeds, and a comprisal of every goodness in the
propitiation of the righteous. 4. About <span
id="fr_987"></span>[2](#fn_978) . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .
