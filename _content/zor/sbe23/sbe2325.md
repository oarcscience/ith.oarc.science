### XX. VANA*NT* YA*S*T.

This Ya*s*t ought to follow immediately after the Tîr Ya*s*t, as it is
derived from the same Sîrôzah formula; the one in which Ti*s*trya is
invoked along with Vana*nt* and Haptôiri*n*ga (Sîrôzah, § 13). It is a
mere supplement to that Ya*s*t. On Vana*nt*, see [p.
97](sbe2313.htm#page_97), note [6](sbe2313.htm#fn_481).

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

0\. May Ahura Mazda be rejoiced! . . . .

Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good . . .

I confess myself a worshipper of Mazda, a follower of Zarathu*s*tra, one
who hates the Daêvas and obeys the laws of Ahura;

For sacrifice, prayer, propitiation, and glorification unto \[Hâvani\],
the holy and master of holiness . . . .

Unto the star Vana*nt*, made by Mazda,

Be propitiation, with sacrifice, prayer, propitiation, and
glorification.

Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . . .

1\. We sacrifice unto the star Vana*nt*, made by Mazda, the holy and
master of holiness.

I will sacrifice unto Vana*nt*, strong, invoked by his own name <span
id="fr_1388"></span>[1](#fn_1322), healing, in order to withstand the
accursed and most foul Khrafstras <span
id="fr_1389"></span>[2](#fn_1323) of the most abominable Angra Mainyu.

2\. Yathâ ahû vairyô: The will of the Lord is the law of holiness . . .
.

I bless the sacrifice and prayer, and the strength and vigour of the
star Vana*nt*, made by Mazda.

Ashem Vohû: Holiness is the best of all good . . . .

\[Give\] unto that man <span id="fr_1390"></span>[3](#fn_1324)
brightness and glory, . . . . give him the bright, all-happy, blissful
abode of the holy Ones.
