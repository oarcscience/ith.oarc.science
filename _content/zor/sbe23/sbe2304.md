### SÎRÔZAH I.

#### 1. Ormazd.

To Ahura Mazda, bright and glorious <span id="fr_5"></span>[2](#fn_5),
and to the Amesha-Spe*ñ*tas <span id="fr_6"></span>[3](#fn_6).

<span id="page_4">p. 4</span>

#### 2. Bahman.

To Vohu-Manô <span id="fr_7"></span>[1](#fn_7); to Peace <span
id="fr_8"></span>[2](#fn_8), whose breath is friendly <span
id="fr_9"></span>[3](#fn_9), and who is more powerful to destroy than
all other creatures <span id="fr_10"></span>[4](#fn_10); to the heavenly
Wisdom <span id="fr_11"></span>[5](#fn_11), made by Mazda; and to the
Wisdom acquired through the ear <span id="fr_12"></span>[5](#fn_11),
made by Mazda.

#### 3. Ardibehe*s*t.

To Asha-Vahi*s*ta, the fairest <span id="fr_13"></span>[6](#fn_12); to
the much-desired Airyaman, made by Mazda <span
id="fr_14"></span>[7](#fn_13); to the instrument made by Mazda <span
id="fr_15"></span>[8](#fn_14); and to the good Saoka <span
id="fr_16"></span>[9](#fn_15), with eyes of love <span
id="fr_17"></span>[10](#fn_16), made by Mazda and holy.

<span id="page_5">p. 5</span>

#### 4. Shahrêvar.

To Khshathra-vairya; to the metals <span id="fr_18"></span>[1](#fn_17);
to Mercy and Charity.

#### 5. Sapendârmad.

To the good Spe*n*ta-Ârmaiti <span id="fr_19"></span>[2](#fn_18), and to
the good Râta <span id="fr_20"></span>[3](#fn_19), with eyes of love,
made by Mazda and holy.

To Haurvatâ*t* <span id="fr_21"></span>[4](#fn_20), the master; to the
prosperity of the seasons and to the years, the masters of holiness.

#### 7. Murdâd.

To Ameretâ*t* <span id="fr_22"></span>[5](#fn_21), the master; to
fatness and flocks; to the plenty of corn; and to the powerful
Gaokerena <span id="fr_23"></span>[6](#fn_22), made by Mazda.

(At the gâh <span id="fr_24"></span>[7](#fn_23) Hâvan): to Mithra <span
id="fr_25"></span>[8](#fn_24), the lord of wide pastures and to Râma
*Hv*âstra <span id="fr_26"></span>[9](#fn_25).

(At the gâh Rapithwin): to Asha-Vahi*s*ta and to Âtar <span
id="fr_27"></span>[10](#fn_26), the son of Ahura Mazda <span
id="fr_28"></span>[11](#fn_27)

<span id="page_6">p. 6</span>

(At the gâh Uzîren): to Apãm Napâ*t* <span
id="fr_29"></span>[1](#fn_28), the tall lord, and to the water made by
Mazda <span id="fr_30"></span>[2](#fn_29).

(At the gâh Aiwisrûthrem): to the Fravashis <span
id="fr_31"></span>[3](#fn_30) of the faithful, and to the females that
bring forth flocks of males <span id="fr_32"></span>[4](#fn_31); to the
prosperity of the seasons; to the well-shapen and tall-formed Strength,
to Verethraghna <span id="fr_33"></span>[5](#fn_32), made by Ahura, and
to the crushing Ascendant <span id="fr_34"></span>[6](#fn_33).

(At the gâh Ushahin): to the holy, devout, fiend-smiting Sraosha <span
id="fr_35"></span>[7](#fn_34), who makes the world grow; to Rashnu
Razi*s*ta <span id="fr_36"></span>[8](#fn_35), and to Ar*s*tâ*t* <span
id="fr_37"></span>[9](#fn_36), who makes the world grow, who makes the
world increase <span id="fr_38"></span>[10](#fn_37).

#### 8. Dai pa Âdar <span id="fr_39"></span>[11](#fn_38).

To the Maker Ahura Mazda, bright and glorious, and to the
Amesha-Spe*n*tas.

<span id="page_7">p. 7</span>

#### 9. Âdar.

To Âtar, the son of Ahura Mazda; to the Glory and to the Weal, made by
Mazda; to the Glory of the Aryas <span id="fr_40"></span>[1](#fn_39),
made by Mazda; to the awful Glory of the Kavis <span
id="fr_41"></span>[2](#fn_40), made by Mazda.

To Âtar, the son of Ahura Mazda; to king Husravah <span
id="fr_42"></span>[3](#fn_41); to the lake of Husravah <span
id="fr_43"></span>[4](#fn_42); to Mount Âsnava*nt* <span
id="fr_44"></span>[5](#fn_43), made by Mazda; to Lake *K*aê*k*asta <span
id="fr_45"></span>[6](#fn_44), made by Mazda; to the Glory of the Kavis,
made by Mazda <span id="fr_46"></span>[7](#fn_45).

<span id="page_8">p. 8</span>

To Âtar, the son of Ahura Mazda; to Mount Raêva*nt* <span
id="fr_47"></span>[1](#fn_46), made by Mazda; to the Glory of the Kavis,
made by Mazda <span id="fr_48"></span>[2](#fn_47).

To Âtar, the beneficent, the warrior; the God who is a full source of
Glory, the God who is a full source of healing.

To Âtar, the son of Ahura Mazda, with all Âtars <span
id="fr_49"></span>[3](#fn_48); to the God Nairyô-Sangha <span
id="fr_50"></span>[4](#fn_49), who dwells in the navel of kings <span
id="fr_51"></span>[5](#fn_50).

#### 10. Âbân.

To the good Waters, made by Mazda; to the holy water-spring Ardvi
Anâhita <span id="fr_52"></span>[6](#fn_51); to all waters made by
Mazda; to all plants made by Mazda.

#### 11. Khorshêd.

To the undying, shining, swift-horsed Sun <span
id="fr_53"></span>[7](#fn_52).

#### 12. Mâh.

To the Moon that keeps in it the seed of the Bull <span
id="fr_54"></span>[8](#fn_53); to the only-created Bull <span
id="fr_55"></span>[9](#fn_54); to the Bull of many species <span
id="fr_56"></span>[10](#fn_55).

<span id="page_9">p. 9</span>

#### 13. Tîr.

To Ti*s*trya <span id="fr_57"></span>[1](#fn_56), the bright and
glorious star; to the powerful Satavaêsa <span
id="fr_58"></span>[2](#fn_57), made by Mazda, who pushes waters forward;
to the stars, made by Mazda, that have in them the seed of the waters,
the seed of the earth, the seed of the plants <span
id="fr_59"></span>[3](#fn_58); to the star Vana*nt* <span
id="fr_60"></span>[4](#fn_59), made by Mazda; to those stars that are
seven in number, the Haptôiri*n*gas <span id="fr_61"></span>[6](#fn_61),
made by Mazda, glorious and healing.

#### 14. Gô*s*.

To the body of the Cow, to the soul of the Cow, to the powerful
Drvâspa <span id="fr_62"></span>[5](#fn_60), made by Mazda and holy.

#### 15. Dai pa Mihir.

To the Maker Ahura Mazda, bright and glorious, and to the
Amesha-Spe*n*tas.

#### 16. Mihir.

To Mithra <span id="fr_63"></span>[6](#fn_61), the lord of wide
pastures, who has a thousand ears and ten thousand eyes, a God invoked
by his own name; to Râma *Hv*âstra <span id="fr_64"></span>[7](#fn_62).

#### 17. Srôsh.

To the holy, strong Sraosha <span id="fr_65"></span>[8](#fn_63), who is
the incarnate Word, a mighty-speared and lordly God.

#### 18. Rashn.

To Rashnu Razi*s*ta <span id="fr_66"></span>[9](#fn_64); to
Ar*s*tâ*t* <span id="fr_67"></span>[10](#fn_65), who makes the

<span id="page_10">p. 10</span>

world grow, who makes the world increase; to the true-spoken speech,
that makes the world grow.

#### 19. Farvardîn.

To the awful, overpowering Fravashis of the holy ones <span
id="fr_68"></span>[1](#fn_66).

#### 20. Bahrâm.

To the well-shapen, tall-formed Strength; to Verethraghna <span
id="fr_69"></span>[2](#fn_67), made by Ahura; to the crushing Ascendant.

#### 21. Râm.

To Râma *Hv*âstra <span id="fr_70"></span>[3](#fn_68); to Vayu <span
id="fr_71"></span>[3](#fn_68), who works highly <span
id="fr_72"></span>[4](#fn_69) and is more powerful to destroy than all
other creatures: to that part of thee, O Vayu, that belongs to
Spe*n*ta-Mainyu <span id="fr_73"></span>[5](#fn_70); to the sovereign
Sky, to the Boundless Time <span id="fr_74"></span>[6](#fn_71), to the
sovereign Time of the long Period s.

#### 22. Bâd.

To the bounteous Wind, that blows below, above, before, and behind; to
the manly Courage.

#### 23. Dai pa Dîn.

To the Maker, Ahura Mazda, bright and glorious; to the Amesha-Spe*n*tas.

#### 24. Dîn.

To the most right *K*ista <span id="fr_75"></span>[7](#fn_72), made by
Mazda and holy; to the good Law <span id="fr_76"></span>[7](#fn_72) of
the worshippers of Mazda.

<span id="page_11">p. 11</span>

#### 25. Ard.

To Ashi Vanguhi <span id="fr_77"></span>[1](#fn_73); to the good
*K*isti <span id="fr_78"></span>[2](#fn_74); to the good Ereth*e* <span
id="fr_79"></span>[3](#fn_75); to the good Rasãstâ*t* <span
id="fr_80"></span>[4](#fn_76); to the Weal and Glory, made by Mazda; to
Pâre*n*di <span id="fr_81"></span>[5](#fn_77), of the light chariot; to
the Glory of the Aryas made by Mazda; to the kingly Glory made. by
Mazda; to that Glory that cannot be forcibly seized <span
id="fr_82"></span>[6](#fn_78), made by Mazda; to the Glory of
Zarathu*s*tra, made by Mazda.

#### 26. Â*s*tâd.

To Ar*s*tâ*t* <span id="fr_83"></span>[7](#fn_79), who makes the world
grow; to Mount Ushi-darena <span id="fr_84"></span>[8](#fn_80), made by
Mazda, the seat of holy happiness.

#### 27. Âsmân.

To the high, powerful Heavens; to the bright, all-happy, blissful abode
of the holy ones.

#### 28. Zemyâd <span id="fr_85"></span>[9](#fn_81).

To the bounteous Earth; to these places, to these fields; to Mount
Ushi-darena <span id="fr_86"></span>[8](#fn_80), made by Mazda, the seat
of holy happiness; to all the mountains made by Mazda, that are seats of
holy happiness, of full happiness; to the kingly Glory made by Mazda;

<span id="page_12">p. 12</span>

to that Glory that cannot be forcibly seized <span
id="fr_87"></span>[1](#fn_82), made by Mazda.

#### 29. Mahraspand.

To the holy, righteousness-performing Mãthra Spe*n*ta <span
id="fr_88"></span>[2](#fn_83); to the Law opposed to the Daêvas, the Law
of Zarathu*s*tra; to the long-traditional teaching <span
id="fr_89"></span>[3](#fn_84); to the good Law of the worshippers of
Mazda; to the Devotion to the Mãthra Spe*n*ta; to the understanding that
keeps <span id="fr_90"></span>[4](#fn_85) the Law of the worshippers of
Mazda; to the knowledge of the Mãthra Spe*n*ta; to the heavenly Wisdom
made by Mazda; to the Wisdom acquired through the ear <span
id="fr_91"></span>[5](#fn_86) and made by Mazda.

#### 30. Anêrân.

To the eternal <span id="fr_92"></span>[6](#fn_87) and sovereign
luminous space <span id="fr_93"></span>[7](#fn_88); to the bright
Garô-nmâna <span id="fr_94"></span>[8](#fn_89); to the sovereign place
of eternal Weal <span id="fr_95"></span>[9](#fn_90); to the
*K*inva*t*-bridge <span id="fr_96"></span>[10](#fn_91), made by Mazda;
to the tall lord Apãm Napâ*t* <span id="fr_97"></span>[11](#fn_92) and
to the water made by Mazda; to Haoma <span
id="fr_98"></span>[12](#fn_93), of holy birth; to the pious and good
Blessing; to the awful cursing thought of the wise <span
id="fr_99"></span>[13](#fn_94); to all the holy Gods of the

<span id="page_13">p. 13</span>

heavenly world and of the material one; to the awful, overpowering
Fravashis of the faithful, to the Fravashis of the first men of the law,
to the Fravashis of the next-of-kin <span
id="fr_100"></span>[1](#fn_95); to every God invoked by his own
name <span id="fr_101"></span>[2](#fn_96).
