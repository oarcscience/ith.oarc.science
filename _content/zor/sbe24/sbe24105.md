### CHAPTER XVII.

1\. The seventeenth subject is this, that when they cut a toothpick
(‘*h*ilâl), or a splinter which they wish to apply to the root of the
teeth, it is necessary that they retain no bark. 2. For if a small
quantity of bark be on it when they apply *it* to the teeth, and they
cast *it* away, if a pregnant woman puts *her* foot upon it, the danger
of that may be that the child comes to harm <span
id="fr_1193"></span>[1](#fn_1184).
