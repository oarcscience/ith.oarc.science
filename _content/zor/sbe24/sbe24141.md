### CHAPTER LIII.

1\. The fifty-third subject is this, that, when, in former <span
id="fr_1359"></span>[3](#fn_1348) times, any one wished to go on a
journey (safar) that might have been at least <span
id="fr_1360"></span>[4](#fn_1349) twelve leagues (parasang), they would
have consecrated a sacred cake, so that no affliction might happen in
that journey, and affairs might be according to *their* wish, and
employments (su*gh*l) <span id="fr_1361"></span>[5](#fn_1350) cheerful.
2. And it is *still* incumbent on every one that, when they wish to go
on a journey, *they are* to consecrate this sacred cake.

3\. *And*, while the person is on the journey, he *should* order the
consecration of this sacred cake, in his house, every Bahirâm day <span
id="fr_1362"></span>[6](#fn_1351), so that that person *may* arrive in
safety (salâmat) at *his* house. 4.

<span id="page_316">p. 316</span>

<span class="contnote">\[paragraph continues\]</span> The dedicatory
formula is this—Amahê hutâ*s*tahê <span
id="fr_1363"></span>[1](sbe24142.htm#fn_1352)—*and* is known to the
priest himself.
