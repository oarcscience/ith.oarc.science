### CHAPTER LXXII.

1\. The seventy-second subject is this, that when any one carries dead
matter to water, *or* to fire, is

<span id="page_337">p. 337</span>

worthy of death <span id="fr_1473"></span>[1](#fn_1462). 2. And <span
id="fr_1474"></span>[2](#fn_1463) it asserts in revelation, that *any*
year when the locust comes profusely <span
id="fr_1475"></span>[3](#fn_1464), it comes for the reason that dead
matter is brought to water and fire. 3. And, *in* like manner, the
winter is colder, and the summer is hotter.

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_1462"></span>[337:1](sbe24160.htm#fr_1473) See Pahl. Vend.
VII, 65-71.

<span id="fn_1463"></span>[337:2](sbe24160.htm#fr_1474) Lp, B29 have
'for.'

<span id="fn_1464"></span>[337:3](sbe24160.htm#fr_1475) La has sâl, and
B29 san for 'year;' Lp has 'that when the bê*s* and locust come
profusely.' The bê*s* may be either a poisonous plant (*Napellus
Moysis*), or 'distress.'
