### SIXTY-EIGHT

He who excels as a soldier is the man who is not warlike. He who fights
the best fight is the man who does not lose his temper. He who truly
conquers an enemy does not lord it over him but treats him with respect.
He who best employs people keeps himself humble.

This is what is meant by the teh of non-rivalry. It is the way to bring
out the good in others. It is the

<span id="page_64">p. 64</span>

oldest principle that has ever been taught. It is in compliance with the
nature of TAO.
