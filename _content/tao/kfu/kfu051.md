No. 23.—Shih Hsing-lin's (\#) Method of warming the Tan-tien.—To cure
the small bowels of air, cold and pain.

<div style="font-size: 93%">

Sit upright, rub the two hands extremely hot, direct them to the navel,
and circulate the air in 49 mouthfuls.

Adding to the taste of the San-ling (\#) Powder.

<div style="font-size: 93%">

*Prescription*.—Take of chu-ling (\#), tuberiform bodies of an unknown
nature, tse-hsieh, pai-shu, fu-ling, cinnamon, hwei-hsiang (\#), Fennel
(Foeniculum vulgaris), betel-nut, mu-t‘ung, chin-ling-tse (\#)
chü-ho-jen (\#), orange seed kernels. Make a decoction, adding a little
salt.

The Figure is the usual one, with the hands embracing the navel as
directed.
