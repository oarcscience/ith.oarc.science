No. 29.—The divine ancestor Hsü (\#) shaking the "Heavenly Pillar."—To
cure all sorts of ulcers on the head, face, shoulders and back.

<div style="font-size: 93%">

Sit upright, let the two hands seize below the heart, agitate the
"heavenly pillar" right and left, with each revolve the air, hem and
blow 24 mouthfuls.

The Figure resembles Numbers 1, 18 (standing) and 23.

The Powder for dispersing the Poison.

<div style="font-size: 93%">

Prescription.—Take of hwang-ch‘in, hwang-lien, rhubarb, pai-chïh,
ch‘iang-hwo, fang-fêng, chin-yin-hwa (\#) Lonicera japonica, lien-chi‘öh
(valves of fruit of Forsythia suspensa), tang-kwei, ching-chieh,
tien-hwa-fen (\#), root of Trichosanthes multiloba, and liquorice,—equal
quantities of each. Make a decoction, and drink.
