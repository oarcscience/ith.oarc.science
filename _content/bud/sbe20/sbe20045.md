### 30.

1\. Now at that time the *Kh*abbaggiya Bhikkhus carried a double pingo
(a yoke over the shoulders with the weight to be carried on both sides).

People murmured, &c., saying, 'Like the king's porters <span
id="fr_404"></span>[2](#fn_396).

They told this matter to the Blessed One.

'You are not, O Bhikkhus, to carry a double pingo. Whosoever does so,
shall be guilty of a dukka*t*a. I allow you, O Bhikkhus, to carry a
single pingo, a pingo for two bearers <span
id="fr_405"></span>[3](#fn_397), and to carry weights on your head, or
your shoulders, or against your hips <span
id="fr_406"></span>[4](#fn_398), and suspended over your backs.'
