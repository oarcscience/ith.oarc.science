### 10 <span id="fr_45"></span>[1](#fn_44).

1\. 'There are ten cases, O Bhikkhus, in which the taking of votes is
invalid; and ten in which the taking of votes is valid.

'Which are the ten in which the taking of votes is invalid? When the
matter in dispute is trivial <span id="fr_46"></span>[2](#fn_45)--when
the case has not run its course (that is, when. the necessary
preliminaries of submission to arbitration have not been carried
out <span id="fr_47"></span>[3](#fn_46))--when regarding the matter in
dispute the Bhikkhus have not formally remembered, or been formally
called upon to remember, the offence <span
id="fr_48"></span>[4](#fn_47)--when the taker of votes <span
id="fr_49"></span>[5](#fn_48) knows that those whose opinions are not in
accordance with the law will be in the majority,

<span id="page_27">p. 27</span>

or probably <span id="fr_50"></span>[1](#fn_49) may be in the
majority--when he knows that the voting will result in a schism in the
Sa*m*gha--when he is in doubt whether the voting will result in a schism
in the Sa*m*gha--when the votes are irregularly given <span
id="fr_51"></span>[2](#fn_50)--when all do not vote equally <span
id="fr_52"></span>[3](#fn_51)--and when they do not vote in accordance
with the view (which they really hold). These are the ten cases in which
the voting is invalid <span id="fr_53"></span>[4](#fn_52).

2\. 'And which are the ten cases in which the voting is valid?'

\[The ten cases are precisely the reverse of the other ten.\]
