### 39.

1\. Now at that time, seedlings belonging to the Sa*m*gha grew upon
private ground, and seedlings belonging to private persons grew upon
ground which was the property of the Sa*m*gha.

They told this thing to the Blessed One.

'Of seedlings belonging to the Sa*m*gha, grown upon private ground, half
the produce, O Bhikkhus, you may have, when you have given a part to the
private owner. Of seedlings belonging to a private person, grown upon
ground the property of the Sa*m*gha, you may have the use, when you have
given a part to the private owner <span
id="fr_265"></span>[1](#fn_261).'

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_261"></span>[143:1](sbe17053.htm#fr_265) Buddhaghosa
explains the 'part' (bhâga*m*) as the twelfth part, which, he says, is
in accordance with the ancient custom of India. Used absolutely, as in
this passage, bhâga usually means 'half.'
