### 7.

1\. Now at that time the Bhikkhus who were sick had need of various
kinds of gum as-medicine. They told this thing to the Blessed One.

'I allow you, O Bhikkhus, the use of gums as medicine--hiṅgu <span
id="fr_104"></span>[1](#fn_102), hiṅgu lac, sipâ*t*ikâ <span
id="fr_105"></span>[2](#fn_103), taka 3, taka-patti 3,
taka-pa*nn*i <span id="fr_106"></span>[3](#fn_104), sa*gg*ulasa <span
id="fr_107"></span>[4](#fn_105), and whatsoever other gums are used for
medicine, and impart (&c., as in chap. 4, down to:) is guilty of a
dukka*t*a offence.'

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_102"></span>[47:1](sbe17021.htm#fr_104) Ferula assa
foetida: Böhtlingk-Roth (sub voce) say it comes from Persia. It is much
used in Hindu medicine. See Wise, 'Hindu System of Medicine,' pp.
152-154.

<span id="fn_103"></span>[47:2](sbe17021.htm#fr_105) The correct
spelling is probably sivâ*t*ikâ. Böhtlingk-Roth under Sivâ*t*ikâ and
Hiṅgu-*s*ivâ*t*ikâ say it is the same as Va*ms*a-pattrî.

<span id="fn_104"></span>[47:3](sbe17021.htm#fr_106) Buddhaghosa merely
says these are kinds of lac. According to Wise, p. 152, lâkshâ is used
as errhine.

<span id="fn_105"></span>[47:4](sbe17021.htm#fr_107) Resin.
