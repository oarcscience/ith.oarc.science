#### No. I.—TRANSLATION OF THE FIRST DYNASTIC TABLET FROM BABYLON

<span class="smallcaps">Obverse</span>

1\. ’Sumu-abi, the king: 15 years.

2\. ’Sumu-la-ilu, the son of the same: 35 years.

3\. Zabû, the son of the same: 14 years.

4\. Abil-Sin, the son of the same: 18 years.

5\. Sin-muballidh, the son of the same: 30 years.

6\. Khammu-ragas, <span id="fr_5"></span>[1](#fn_5) the son of the same:
55 years.

7\. ’Sam’su-iluna, <span id="fr_6"></span>[2](#fn_6) the son of the
same: 35 years.

8\. Ebisum, <span id="fr_7"></span>[3](#fn_7) the son of the same: 25
years.

9\. Ammi-satana, the son of the same: 25 years.

10\. Ammi-sadugga, <span id="fr_8"></span>[4](#fn_8) the son of the
same: 21 years.

11\. ’Sam’su-satana (?), the son of the same: 31 years.

12\. 11 kings of the dynasty of <span class="smallcaps">Babylon</span>.

<span class="smallcaps">Reverse</span>

1\. (The dynasty of) <span class="smallcaps">Uru-azagga</span>. <span
id="fr_9"></span>[5](#fn_9) Anman the king.

2\. Ki-\[<span class="smallcaps">An</span>\] Nigas. <span
id="fr_10"></span>[6](#fn_10)

3\. Damki-ili-su. <span id="fr_11"></span>[7](#fn_11)

<span id="page_14">p. 14</span>

4\. Is-ki-pal. <span id="fr_12"></span>[1](#fn_12)

5\. Sussi. <span id="fr_13"></span>[2](#fn_13)

6\. Gul-ki-sar. <span id="fr_14"></span>[3](#fn_14)

7\. Kirgal-dara-mas, the son of the same.

8\. A-dara-kalama, the son of the same. <span
id="fr_15"></span>[4](#fn_15)

9\. A-kur-du-ana. <span id="fr_16"></span>[5](#fn_16)

10\. Melam-kurkura. <span id="fr_17"></span>[6](#fn_17)

11\. Ea-ga(mil?). <span id="fr_18"></span>[7](#fn_18)

12\. 1\[1\] kings of the dynasty of <span
class="smallcaps">Uru-azagga</span>.

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_5"></span>[13:1](rp20106.htm#fr_5) The first five names of
the dynasty are Semitic. Khammuragas is Kassite or Kossæan, and is
interpreted "of a large family." Sin-muballidh may have married a
foreign wife.

<span id="fn_6"></span>[13:2](rp20106.htm#fr_6) "The Sun-god (is) our
god," another Semitic name.

<span id="fn_7"></span>[13:3](rp20106.htm#fr_7) "The doer," also
Semitic.

<span id="fn_8"></span>[13:4](rp20106.htm#fr_8) Kassite, interpreted
"the family is established."

<span id="fn_9"></span>[13:5](rp20106.htm#fr_9) Uru-azagga is now
represented by a part of the mounds of Telloh (the ancient Sirpurla) or
its immediate vicinity.

<span id="fn_10"></span>[13:6](rp20106.htm#fr_10) Nigas was an Elamite
word.

<span id="fn_11"></span>[13:7](rp20106.htm#fr_11) Semitic, signifying
"gracious is his god."

<span id="fn_12"></span>[14:1](rp20106.htm#fr_12) Perhaps to be read in
Semitic Sapin-mat-nukurti, "the sweeper away of the land of the foe."
The name seems to have been a title.

<span id="fn_13"></span>[14:2](rp20106.htm#fr_13) Perhaps the Semitic
*sussu*, "sixty."

<span id="fn_14"></span>[14:3](rp20106.htm#fr_14) In Semitic
Muabbid-kissati, "the destroyer of hosts."

<span id="fn_15"></span>[14:4](rp20106.htm#fr_15) Apparently, therefore,
the son of the preceding king.

<span id="fn_16"></span>[14:5](rp20106.htm#fr_16) Rendered by the
Semitic Abil-Bel-u’sum-same, "the son of Bel (the lord) of the treasury
of heaven."

<span id="fn_17"></span>[14:6](rp20106.htm#fr_17) "The glory of the
world."

<span id="fn_18"></span>[14:7](rp20106.htm#fr_18) The last character is
partially destroyed. If my restoration is correct, the name would be
Semitic and signify "Ea has rewarded."
