#### <span class="smallcaps">VI. Inscriptions of Ur-Bau and his Reign</span>

No. I.—<span class="smallcaps">On a Statue</span> <span
id="fr_156"></span>[1](#fn_155.md)

<span class="small">COLUMN I</span>

1\. To the god <span class="smallcaps">Nin-girsu</span>  
2. the powerful warrior  
3. of the god <span class="smallcaps">Ellilla</span>,  
4. Ur-Bau  
5. the patesi  
6. of <span class="smallcaps">Shirpurla-ki</span>,  
7. the offspring begotten  
8. by the god <span class="smallcaps">Nin-âgal</span>,  
9. chosen by the immutable will of the goddess <span
class="smallcaps">Niná</span>,  
10. endowed with power by the god <span
class="smallcaps">Nin-girsu</span>,  
11. named with a favourable name by the goddess <span
class="smallcaps">Bau</span>,  
12. endowed with intelligence by the god <span
class="smallcaps">En-ki</span>, <span id="fr_157"></span>[2](#fn_156.md)

<span class="small">COLUMN II</span>

1\. covered with renown by the goddess <span
class="smallcaps">Ninni</span>,  
2. the favourite servant of the god who is king of <span
class="smallcaps">Gishgalla-ki</span>,  
3. the favourite of the goddess <span
class="smallcaps">Duzi-abzu</span>.  
4. I am <span class="smallcaps">Ur-Bau</span>;  
5. the god <span class="smallcaps">Nin-girsu</span> is my king.  
6. The site of … <span id="fr_158"></span>[3](#fn_157.md) he has
excavated.  
7. The earth thence extracted, like precious stones, he has measured
(?);  
8. like a precious metal he has weighed (?) it.

<span id="page_76">p. 76</span>

<span class="small">COLUMN III</span>

1\. According to the plan adopted he has marked out a large space;  
2. into the middle (of it) he has carried this earth,  
3. and he has made its *mundus*. <span id="fr_159"></span>[1](#fn_158.md)  
4. Above, a substructure 6 cubits high, he has built.  
5. Above this substructure  
6. the temple <span class="smallcaps">E-ninnû</span>, which illumines
the darkness (?), 30 cubits in height,  
7. he has built.  
8. For the goddess <span class="smallcaps">Nin-gharsag</span>, <span
id="fr_160"></span>[2](#fn_159.md) the mother of the gods,

<span class="small">COLUMN IV</span>

1\. her temple of <span class="smallcaps">Girsu-ki</span>  
2. he has constructed.  
3. For the goddess <span class="smallcaps">Bau</span>,  
4. the good lady,  
5. the daughter of <span class="smallcaps">Anna</span>,  
6. her temple of <span class="smallcaps">Uru-azagga</span>  
7. he has constructed.  
8. For the goddess <span class="smallcaps">Ninni</span>, the lady
august, the *sovereign* (?),  
9. her temple of <span class="smallcaps">Gishgalla-ki</span>  
10. he has constructed.  
11. For the god <span class="smallcaps">En-ki</span>, the king of <span
class="smallcaps">Eridu</span>,  
12. his temple of <span class="smallcaps">Girsu-ki</span>

<span class="small">COLUMN V</span>

1\. he has constructed.  
2. For the god <span class="smallcaps">Nin-dara</span>, <span
id="fr_161"></span>[3](#fn_160) the lord of *destinies* (?.md),  
3. his temple he has constructed.  
4. For the god <span class="smallcaps">Nin-âgal</span>,

<span id="page_77">p. 77</span>

5\. his god,  
6. his temple  
7. he has constructed.  
8. For the goddess <span class="smallcaps">Nin-mar-ki</span> <span
id="fr_162"></span>[1](#fn_161.md)  
9. the good lady,  
10. the eldest daughter of the goddess <span
class="smallcaps">Niná</span>,  
11. the *Esh-gu-tur* (?), the temple of her constant choice,  
12. he has constructed.

<span class="small">COLUMN VI</span>

1\. For the god …  
2. the shepherd … \[of\] <span class="smallcaps">Gir</span>-\[<span
class="smallcaps">su-ki</span>\],  
3. his temple …  
4. he has constructed.  
5. For the goddess <span class="smallcaps">Kû-Anna</span>, <span
id="fr_163"></span>[2](#fn_162.md)  
6. the lady of the cloudy sky (?),  
7. her temple of <span class="smallcaps">Girsu-ki</span>  
8. he has constructed.  
9. For the goddess <span class="smallcaps">Duzi-abzu</span>,  
10. the lady of <span class="smallcaps">Kinunir-ki</span>,  
11. her temple of <span class="smallcaps">Girsu-ki</span>  
12. he has constructed.

*The remaining inscriptions of Telloh will be translated in the next
volume*.
