#### <span class="smallcaps">III.—Letter of Burna-buryas to Amenophis IV</span> <span id="fr_263"></span>[3](#fn_263.md)

<span class="small">OBVERSE</span>

1\. \[To\] Napkhuhru-ri\[ya\]  
2. the king of <span class="smallcaps">Egypt</span> <span
id="fr_264"></span>[4](#fn_264.md) my brother \[it is spoken\]  
3. thus: Burra-buryas the king of <span
class="smallcaps">Kara-\[Duniyas\]</span>  
4. thy brother-unto myself (is) peace;  
5. unto thee, thy country, thy house, thy wives, thy children,  
6. thy officers, thy horses (and) thy chariots  
7. may there ever be peace!

<span id="page_66">p. 66</span>

8\. I and my brother with one another  
9. have conferred amicably,  
10. and this is what we have said  
11. as follows: as our fathers with one another,  
12. we also have friendly dealings.  
13. Again my ministers  
14. who came with Akhi-dhâbu <span id="fr_265"></span>[1](#fn_265.md)  
15. into the country of <span class="smallcaps">Kinakhkhi</span> <span
id="fr_266"></span>[2](#fn_266.md) trusted to destiny,  
16. from Akhi-dhâbu to visit my brother they passed;  
17. in the city of <span class="smallcaps">Kikhinnatuni</span> of the
country of <span class="smallcaps">Kinakhkhi</span>  
18. Sum-Adda the son of Balumme <span id="fr_267"></span>[3](#fn_267.md)  
19. (and) Sutatna the son of Saratum <span
id="fr_268"></span>[4](#fn_268.md) of the city of <span
class="smallcaps">Akku</span>, <span id="fr_269"></span>[5](#fn_269.md)  
20. when they had sent their men, slew  
21. my ministers and carried off their treasures  
22. which they \[were taking\] for \[a present to the king of <span
class="smallcaps">Egypt</span>\].  
23. I have sent to you \[therefore\] a complainant <span
id="fr_270"></span>[6](#fn_270.md)  
24. \[who\] may speak to thee \[thus\]:

<span class="small">REVERSE</span>

1\. <span class="smallcaps">Kinakhkhi</span> (is) thy country and the
king \[is thy servant?\].  
2. In thy country I have been injured; do thou punish \[the
offender\].  
3. The silver which they carried off \[was\] a present \[for thee\],  
4. and the men who (are) my servants they have slain.  
5. Slay them and requite the blood (of my messengers);  
6. but if thou dost not put these men to death,  
7. (the inhabitants) of the high-road that belongs to me will turn

<span id="page_67">p. 67</span>

8\. and verily will slay thy ambassadors, and  
9. a breach will be made in the agreement (to respect the persons) of
ambassadors,  
10. and this man <span id="fr_271"></span>[1](#fn_271.md) will be estranged
from thee.  
11. One of my men Sum-Adda,  
12. having cut off his feet,  
13. detained him with him;  
14. and as for another man, Sutatna of <span
class="smallcaps">Akku</span>,  
15. having made him stand on (his) head,  
16. he stood upon his face. As for these men  
17. … one has spoken thus: I have seen indeed  
18. … \[what\] thou askest that indeed thou knowest.  
19. \[By way of a pre\]sent, I maneh of alabaster I have despatched to
thee.  
20. \[By\] my ambassadors a costly gift I have \[sent to thee\].  
21. \[On account of the re\]port <span id="fr_272"></span>[2](#fn_272.md)
which my brother has heard  
22. my ambassadors do not detain;  
23. the costly present let them offer \[to thee\].
