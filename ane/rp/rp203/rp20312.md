#### I.—<span class="smallcaps">Letter of Assur-yuballidh, King of Assyria, to Amenophis IV, King Of Egypt</span> <span id="fr_243"></span>[1](#fn_243)

1\. To Napkhurîya (Neferu-kheper-Ra) \[the great king\]  
2. the king of <span class="smallcaps">Egypt</span>, <span
id="fr_244"></span>[2](#fn_244) my brother, \[I write\]  
3. thus, (even I) Assur-yuballidh king of the country of <span
class="smallcaps">Assyria</span>,  
4. the great king, thy brother.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

5\. To thyself, to thy house and thy country may there be peace!

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

6\. That I have seen thy ambassadors  
7. has pleased (me) greatly; thy ambassadors  
8. I have sent for to appear in my presence.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

9\. A chariot \[the choicest?\] in the kingdom with (its) harness  
10. and two white horses, \[together with\]  
11. one chariot without harness and a seal of white alabaster  
12. I have despatched as a present for thee.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

13\. For the great king is produced perpetually  
14. the gold (which) in thy country (is like) the dust

<span id="page_62">p. 62</span>

15\. (that) they collect: why in thy presence  
16. is it brought and kept back, is it withheld and not sent?  
17. All the gold that is my property,  
18. as well as what is lacking to it, send.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

19\. When Asur-nadin-akhi my father  
20. sent (an embassy) to the country of <span
class="smallcaps">Egypt</span> (*Mitsri*),  
21. 20 talents of gold did they despatch to him.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

22\. When the king of the country of <span
class="smallcaps">Khani-rabbatû</span> <span
id="fr_245"></span>[1](#fn_245)  
23. to thy father and the land of <span class="smallcaps">Egypt</span>  
24. sent (an embassy), 20 talents of gold  
25. did they despatch to him.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

26\. \[As\] to the king of <span
class="smallcaps">Khani-\[rab\]bati</span>  
27. \[so\] also to myself  
28. despatch the gold.  
29. \[The road both in\] going and returning  
30. for the hands of my ambassadors  
31. I have made secure. <span id="fr_246"></span>[2](#fn_246)

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

32\. If thou inclinest thy face <span id="fr_247"></span>[3](#fn_247)
favourably  
33. despatch much gold, and thy letter in return  
34. write (to me) and what thou desirest let them take.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

35\. Behold, distant <span id="fr_248"></span>[4](#fn_248) lands  
36. have the ambassadors visited <span id="fr_249"></span>[5](#fn_249)
and they have journeyed to (many) cities.

<span id="page_63">p. 63</span>

37\. As for thy ambassadors  
38. they have delayed on the way because the <span
class="smallcaps">’Suti</span> <span id="fr_250"></span>[1](#fn_250)  
39. threatened <span id="fr_251"></span>[2](#fn_251) them with death,
until I sent and  
40. the <span class="smallcaps">’Suti</span> took *fright* (?) <span
id="fr_252"></span>[3](#fn_252).  
41. My ambassadors … them  
42. \[and\] they did not delay.

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

43\. When the ambassadors (reached) the frontier (of Assyria)  
44. why do they not wait? <span id="fr_253"></span>[4](#fn_253) and  
45. at the frontier they *are in a hurry* (?) <span
id="fr_254"></span>[5](#fn_254)  
46. *It is fitting* (?) at the frontier they should wait  
47. for the king; everything is there  
48. and he has established (it) and at the frontier  
49 he has arranged (it). Against the king who fulfils everything  
50. there is no *charge* (?): why  
51. at the frontier are they *in a hurry* (?),  
52. even the ambassadors who …

*The last three lines are too obliterated for translation*.

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_243"></span>[61:1](rp20312.htm#fr_243) Winckler and Abel:
*Mittheilungen*, i. p. 8.

<span id="fn_244"></span>[61:2](rp20312.htm#fr_244) Written Mitstsari as
in the letters of the king of Mitanni. The Babylonian form of the name
is Mitsri, corresponding to the Hebrew Matsor and Mitsraim. In the later
Assyrian inscriptions the name appears as Mutsur or Mutsri, probably
through confusion with the name of Mutsri, a district to the north of
Assyria.

<span id="fn_245"></span>[62:1](rp20312.htm#fr_245) "Khani the great,"
or Eastern Kappadokia, the capital of which was Malatiyeh.

<span id="fn_246"></span>[62:2](rp20312.htm#fr_246) *Ultammatstsi*.

<span id="fn_247"></span>[62:3](rp20312.htm#fr_247) Literally, "If thou
art good as to thy face."

<span id="fn_248"></span>[62:4](rp20312.htm#fr_248) *Ruqatum*.

<span id="fn_249"></span>[62:5](rp20312.htm#fr_249) *Ilika*.

<span id="fn_250"></span>[63:1](rp20312.htm#fr_250) Nomads, who ranged
through the desert on either side of the Tigris, in Mesopotamia and the
south-east of Assyria.

<span id="fn_251"></span>[63:2](rp20312.htm#fr_251) *Ura‘u*, the Heb.
*yârê*.

<span id="fn_252"></span>[63:3](rp20312.htm#fr_252) Probably to be read
*khardê*, "fear."

<span id="fn_253"></span>[63:4](rp20312.htm#fr_253) *Ultannazazu*.

<span id="fn_254"></span>[63:5](rp20312.htm#fr_254) *Imattu;* the verb
*imati* occurs in K 1282, Rev. 18.
