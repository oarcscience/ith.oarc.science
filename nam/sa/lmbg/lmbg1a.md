 The Arawâks formerly inhabited the whole of the West Indian Islands.
Those in the Lesser Antilies were exterminated by the Caribs, before the
discovery of America by Columbus. The inhabitants of the larger island
perished soon after that event, under the oppression of the Spaniards.

 The few Arawâks who dwell near the coast of Guiana and whose legends
are here given are now the only known representatives of a gentle and
once numerous race.
