104\. T<span class="small">HE</span> G<span class="small">IANT OF</span>
K<span class="small">ANGERSUAK OR</span> C<span class="small">APE</span>
F<span class="small">AREWELL</span>.—The people from the south (or east)
and those from the north (or west) were at war with each other. The
latter had a powerful champion, who was sitting on the top of Kangersuak
to watch the Southlanders passing by. A man who had been killed by him
left a son, who practised angakok science, and <span id="page_450">p.
450</span> revenged his father by inducing the giant to walk with him
over a marshy plain, where he went down, and from beneath pierced the
feet of the giant, and afterwards killed him.
