### 61.

I hîyaha, etc.  
GustA gî'hao dî'nañ kuñg<u>î</u>ñâ'ñgAñ?  
What \| for \| my child \| cries?  
Q!ô'na kun xê'txa ku<u>Lî</u>'ndala-igi î'sîñ <u>g</u>ê'da
ku<u>L</u>indâ'l<u>g</u>asan  
Q!ô'na \| Point \| in front of \| to pass along by canoe \| again \|
cries for that \| she will travel by canoe  
    q!o'l<u>g</u>An djâ'<u>g</u>a?  
    my master's \| wife  
Îhî', etc.

I hîyaha, etc.  
For what is my child crying?  
Does she cry to pass along again by canoe in front of Point Q!ô'na, my
master's wife? <span id="fr_68"></span>[1](#fn_63)  
Îhî', etc.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_63"></span>[42:1](hs063.htm#fr_68) By saying "master's
wife," the singer implies that her child will marry a higher chief than
herself. "Passing along again" refers to the former existence of the
child. Point Q!ô'na is near Nasto.
