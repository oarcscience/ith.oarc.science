## *VII SONSHIP*

<span id="page_152">p. 152</span>

 

167 (V. 250)  
THE SPIRITUAL AND ETERNAL BIRTH ARE ONE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Spiritual Birth within my soul is one<br />
With that whereby the Father doth beget the Son.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

168 (VI. 132)  
GOD'S SOLE FELICITY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>There's joy in giving birth. God's sole Felicity<br />
Is that He bringeth forth His Son eternally.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

169 (III. 175)  
WHAT GOD WROUGHT FROM ETERNITY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>What was God's deed, ere Time began,<br />
Established on His ageless throne?<br />
He loved Himself, and thus He wrought<br />
The generation of His Son.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

170 (I. 201)  
WHY IS GOD BORN?

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>O Mystery! God's lost Himself, and therefore He,<br />
To find Himself again, would be new-born in Me.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_153">p. 153</span>

 

171 (I. 135)  
WITH GOD IS ONLY HIS SON

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Man, be thou born of God! for standeth by His Throne<br />
His own begotten Son—and other standeth none.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

172 (II. 102)  
THE EXTERNAL COMFORTETH ME NOT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>"Hail Mary!" so thou greetedst Her:<br />
Yet, Gabriel, what doth this avail<br />
To me, unless thou likewise come<br />
And greet me with the self-same "Hail!"<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

173 (VI. 134)  
TO BE BORN OF GOD IS TO BE WHOLLY GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God doth engender naught but God.<br />
If He engender thee, His Son,<br />
Then thou becomest God in God,<br />
And Lord upon the Lord God's throne.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

174 (I. 17)  
A CHRISTIAN IS GOD'S SON

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>I also am God's Son. I sit beside His knee.<br />
His Spirit, Flesh and Blood are known to Him in me.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

175 (III. 4)  
A SIGH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>When God became a man, swaddled in straw He lay—<br />
Alas, that I have never been that straw and bay!<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

176 (II. 53)  
THOU ONLY ART WANTING

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Could but my heart become a manger, God would then<br />
Become a Child upon the earth yet once again.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

177 (V. 9)  
EVERYONE MUST BE CHRIST

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The true-born Son of God is Christ and Christ alone,<br />
Yet must each Christian be this Christ, this selfsame Son.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

178 (I. 208)  
BLESSED GLUTTONY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Too much is never good. I hate all Gluttonies—<br />
Yet wish I were as full of God as Jesus is!<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_155">p. 155</span>

 

179 (VI. 236)  
NAUGHT IS HIGHER THAN TO BE GOD'S SON

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God's Son is God with God, rules from the selfsame Throne:<br />
Naught standeth higher than I, if I am this same Son.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

180 (IV. 49)  
THE SPLENDOUR OF CHRIST IN THIS WORLD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Sceptre is a reed; a branch of thorns the Crown;<br />
Nails are Regalia; a deadly Cross the Throne;<br />
The Purple Robe is blood; Bodyguard, murderers;<br />
Hangmen and the dastard crowd, the train of Courtiers;<br />
The Wine is bitter gall; the Music, mocking mirth:<br />
Such the Magnificence of our Lord God on earth.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
