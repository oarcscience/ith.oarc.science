## *XII POVERTY AND RICHES*

<span id="page_192">p. 192</span>

 

293 (II. 56)  
POVERTY AND RICHES

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The man who, what he hath, hath not,<br />
To all things stands indifferent,<br />
He is most poor when he is rich,<br />
Most rich when he is indigent.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

294 (III. 69)  
THE SAINT'S WEALTH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Be poor! On earth the Saint hath naught<br />
Save one thing, which unwillingly<br />
He needs must call his own, to wit,<br />
This Body of Mortality.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

295 (II. 148)  
THE POOR IN SPIRIT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>That man is truly poor who stands<br />
Detached from all things, loose, adrift:<br />
Were God to offer him Himself<br />
I know he would refuse the gift.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

296 (III. 139)  
MAN FINDETH WHAT HE SEEKETH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The poor man seeketh God, the rich man seeketh wealth:<br />
'Tis gold indeed the poor man finds, the rich man filth.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_193">p. 193</span>

 

297 (V. 157)  
THE RICH MAN IS TRULY POOR

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>When of his poverty he maketh much ado,<br />
Doubt not the Rich Man's word—he speaketh what is true.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

298 (VI. 185)  
OUR WEALTH MUST BE WITHIN US

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Be all thy Wealth within thyself.<br />
If lie thy riches otherwhere,<br />
Yea, though thy fortune were the world,<br />
Then art thou rich in naught but Care.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

299 (VI. 189)  
TO DESIRE EVERYTHING IS TO HAVE NOTHING

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Man, if desire for Everything<br />
Possesseth all thy thought,<br />
Then art thou poor as beggars are<br />
And still possessest Naught.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

300 (VI. 86)  
WHO CRAVETH ALL HATH NAUGHT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Who craveth Naught hath All. Who yearneth to possess<br />
The riches of the world, he still is penniless.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_194">p. 194</span>

 

301 (VI. 84)  
THE GREATEST WEALTH AND GAIN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Most Wealth hath he who Wealth doth most disdain,<br />
And gaineth most who most renounceth Gain.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

302 (VI. 168)  
THE WISE MAN HATH NAUGHT IN COFFERS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Naught doth the Wise Man heard in guarded treasuries:<br />
Riches that can be lost he never counteth his.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

303 (VI. 99)  
THE TREASURY OF THE WISE MAN AND THE MISER

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Wise Men are wisely rich—the put<br />
Their gold into a treasure-chest:<br />
The Miser's gold is in his heart—<br />
His heart hath never any rest.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

304 (VI. 167)  
HE WHO IS TRULY RICH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Much having is not being rich.<br />
 The Wealthy Man is he<br />
Who views the loss of all he hath<br />
 With equanimity.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_195">p. 195</span>

 

305 (VI. 100)  
THE WISE MAN FORESTALLS THE THIEF

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Wise Man doth not wait until<br />
 His fortune is bereft:<br />
He steals his foretune from himself<br />
 And so forestalls the theft.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

306 (VI. 103)  
NO PROGRESS WHEN HEAVILY LADEN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Storm-foundered seamen jettison<br />
The weightiest cargo in the hold;<br />
And thinkest thou to win to Heaven<br />
O'erladen with a freight of gold?<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

307 (VI. 179)  
HOW MISERS AND WISE MEN ACT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Miser leaves his gold at last,<br />
Heirs seize on his inheritance:<br />
The Wise Man to the other world<br />
Sends on his riches in advance.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

308 (V. 132)  
THE UNCONCERNED MAN SUFFERETH NO HURT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The man who in this world claims nothing for his own,<br />
Suffereth no great loss when all his house falls down.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_196">p. 196</span>

 

309 (V. 156)  
WHO DESIRETH MUCH, LACKETH MUCH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Who hath enough, hath all. Who craveth more and more,<br />
Betrayeth by his want how scanty is his store.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

310 (VI. 181)  
THE ESTIMATION OF THE FOOL AND THE WISE MAN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Fool thinks he is rich enough<br />
If he possess a sack of gold:<br />
The Wise Man knoweth he is poor<br />
With riches not the world can hold.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
