## *IX ACCIDENT AND ESSENCE*

<span id="page_166">p. 166</span>

 

209 (II. 30)  
ACCIDENT AND ESSENCE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Become essential, Man! When the world fails at last,<br />
Accident falls away, but Essence, that stands fast.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

210 (I. 274)  
THE ACCIDENT MUST GO

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>All Accident must go, all false appearances:<br />
Put off thy specious hues—be pure as Essence is.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

211 (V. 356)  
THE PERFECT DRIVETH OUT THE IMPERFECT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>When full Perfection comes, the imperfect falls aside:<br />
So fades my human part when I am deified.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

212 (I. 108)  
THE ROSE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Rose which here thou seest with thine outward eye<br />
Hath blossomèd in God from all eternity.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_167">p. 167</span>

 

213 (II. 182)  
GOD IS ALL AT ONCE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>There is no After, no Before;<br />
And what to-morrow shall befall<br />
Already from eternity<br />
In Essence God hath seen it all.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

214 (V. 179)  
GOD MAKETH NOTHING NEW

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God maketh no new thing, though new<br />
It seem to us. We think we see<br />
The act of birth, but what is born<br />
Is birthless in eternity.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

215 (I. 71)  
MAN MUST BE ESSENCE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>To practise love is burdensome. 'Tis not enough<br />
Merely to love—we must ourselves, like God, be Love.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

216 (VI. 226)  
A RIGHT JUDGEMENT BRINGETH NOT SADNESS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The true and proper worth of things<br />
Who understandeth to assay,<br />
Will never sorrow overmuch<br />
For aught that Time can bear away.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_168">p. 168</span>

 

217 (VI. 224)  
CHILDREN WEEP FOR DOLLS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>A child that weepeth for its dolls<br />
Maketh thee smile—and are they more<br />
Than dolls and toys, those very things<br />
That thou thyself art weeping for?<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

218 (I. 38)  
EQUAL ESTIMATION MAKETH PEACE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Canst thou divest from diverse things<br />
Their aspect of diversity,<br />
Come Love, come Pain, thou standest fast,<br />
Poised in thy equanimity.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

219 (V. 45)  
VICE IS ONLY APPEARANCE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>All naked Virtue stands, Vice doth apparelled go;<br />
Virtue is truly large, Vice only seemeth so.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

220 (V. 37)  
GOD LOOKETH TO THE ROOT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>'Tis not the Somewhat thou hast done<br />
God values, looks not to the fruit;<br />
Only the How He contemplates,<br />
Only the kernel and the root.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_169">p. 169</span>

 

221 (V. 168)  
THE SINNER DOETH NOTHING WELL

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Though thou dost feed and clothe the needy multitude,<br />
Is goodness not in thee, then is thy deed not good.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

222 (V. 72)  
GOD IS EQUALLY NEAR TO ALL

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God is as near Beelzebub<br />
As He is near the Seraphim,<br />
'Tis only that Beelzebub<br />
Turneth his back on Him.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

223 (V. 30)  
THE DEVIL IS GOOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Devil is as good as thou<br />
 If Being is the test.<br />
What is it that the Devil lacks?<br />
 Peace and a Will at rest.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

224 (V. 15)  
DAMNATION IS IN ESSENCE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Could one that's damned stand in high Heaven, even there<br />
He'd feel within himself all Hell and Hell's despair.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

225 (VI. 208)  
WISE AND FOOLISH BEAUTY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Wiser the maid who knoweth well<br />
Herself is her best loveliness<br />
Than she who dreams she's beautiful<br />
When she puts on a lovely dress.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

226 (VI. 251)  
TO THE FOOL

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Opinions are a shifting sand,<br />
And fools build houses thereupon:<br />
Wise wilt thou never be if thou<br />
Dost build upon Opinion.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

227 (II. 71)  
THE ESSENTIAL MAN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The essential Man is like unto Eternity,<br />
Unchanged by any breath of externality.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

THE SPIRITUAL ALCHEMY
