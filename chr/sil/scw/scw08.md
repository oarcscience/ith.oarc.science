## *VIII TIME AND ETERNITY*

<span id="page_158">p. 158</span>

 

181 (II. 153)  
ETERNITY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>What is Eternity? It is not That, not This,<br />
Not Now, not Aught, not Naught—I know not what it is.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

182 (II. 65)  
ETERNITY IS NOT MEASURED

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Eternity knows naught of hour and day and year.<br />
Alas, I have not found the centre of its sphere!<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

183 (II. 183)  
IN THE CENTRE EVERYTHING IS SEEN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Stand at the centre-point—instantly shall appear<br />
All that befell or now befalls, in Heaven and here.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

184 (I. 47)  
TIME IS ETERNITY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Eternity is one with Time,<br />
Time with Eternity, and hence<br />
Indifference between them lie,<br />
Thyself dost make the difference.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_159">p. 159</span>

 

185 (I. 189)  
MAN MAKETH TIME

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>'Tis thou thyself that makest Time.<br />
And like a clock thy senses run:<br />
Do thou but quiet their unrest—<br />
The clock is stopped and Time is gone.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

186 (V. 23)  
TIME IS NOT FAST

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>"Time flieth fast" we say, but who<br />
Hath seen the fleeting of Time's wings?<br />
Time standeth moveless in a view<br />
That visioneth the Whole of Things.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

187 (V. 148)  
IN ETERNITY ALL HAPPENETH TOGETHER

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>There, in Eternity, events<br />
Together strike a single chime:<br />
There is no After, no Before,<br />
As here, as in the realm of Time.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

188 (IV. 200)  
HOW TO SHORTEN TIME

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Man, if thou findest Time on earth drag on too slow.<br />
Turn unto God—live in the Everlasting Now.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_160">p. 160</span>

 

189 (V. 127)  
THE SOUL IS ABOVE TIME

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Soul, which is eternal Spirit,<br />
Standeth above Time's sovranty:<br />
Already in this present world<br />
She liveth in Eternity.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

190 (V. 111)  
OUT OF GOD, LIVING IS BEING DEAD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Livest thou not in God, O Man,<br />
—And let this be well credited—<br />
Though thou dost live a thousand years,<br />
A thousand years thou art dead.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

191 (II. 168)  
ONE IS AS OLD AS THE OTHER

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>A Child who on this earth but one short hour appears,<br />
Already he hath lived Methuselah's long years.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

192 (III. 180)  
GOD KNOWETH NOT HIS BEGINNING

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>How long hath God been God? Be silent, ask not this!<br />
So long, God knoweth not Himself how long it is.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_161">p. 161</span>

 

193 (IV. 165)  
GOD STILL CREATES THE WORLD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God still creates the World. Strange doth this thing appear?<br />
God knoweth neither Then nor Now, as men know here.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

194 (III. 90)  
THOU MUST BLOSSOM NOW

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>O blossom, blossom, frozen soul!<br />
May is abroad before thy door.<br />
If thou dost blossom not to-day<br />
Then art thou dead for evermore.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

195 (I. 185)  
PLACE IS WITHIN THEE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Nowise dost thou inhabit Place,<br />
 Place doth inhabit thee.<br />
Cast Place away!—then now and here<br />
 Standeth Eternity.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

196 (III. 112)  
THE HEART IS IMMEASURABLE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>A Heart that finds its full contcnt in Time and Place<br />
In truth knows not its own Immeasurableness.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_162">p. 162</span>

 

197 (I. 187)  
THE BREADTH OF THE SOUL

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Heaven is too little for me, Earth a narrow cell:<br />
Where shall I find a space wherein my Soul can dwell?<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

198 (I. 188)  
TIME AND ETERNITY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>From Time into Eternity<br />
Thou tellest me to get me hence!<br />
Between Eternity and Time<br />
Is there then any difference?<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

199 (IV. 90)  
VIRTUE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>"Virtue its own Reward"—so runs the argument:<br />
If the Reward be here, in time, then I dissent.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

200 (IV. 180)  
THE DAY AND DAWN OF SOULS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Here in this present time God dawns in souls, but soon,<br />
When they have put their splendour on, He'll be their noon.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_163">p. 163</span>

 

201 (V. 67)  
HOW FAR THE WAY TO HEAVEN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Think not the journey overlong if thou wouldst fare<br />
To Heaven—take but one short stride and thou art there.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

202 (I. 30)  
THERE IS NO DEATH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>I disbelieve in Death. Hourly I die—what then?<br />
To new and better Life hourly I rise again.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

203 (I. 34)  
DEATH DEIFIETH THEE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>When thou art dead and all thy life<br />
Is taken up by God in His,<br />
Then shalt thou rightly, nor till then,<br />
Rank with the all-high Deities.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

204 (IV. 81)  
DEATH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Death doth not trouble me. 'Tis through that door I come<br />
Unto the place which long hath been my spirit's home.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_164">p. 164</span>

 

205 (IV. 77)  
SPIRITUAL DYING

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Die ere thou diest—dying, then thou diest not:<br />
Die not—perchance then, dying, thou shalt die and rot.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

206 (I. 29)  
ETERNAL DEATH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Death which no new Life within itself doth bear,<br />
Of all deaths 'tis that death which most my soul doth fear.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

207 (IV. 103)  
LIFE AND DEATH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>No richer death than that which Life engendereth,<br />
No noble Life than that which springeth out of death.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

208 (I. 190)  
SAMENESS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>I know not what to do! All things are one to me:<br />
Place, Unplace, Day, Night, Joy, Pain, Time, Eternity.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
