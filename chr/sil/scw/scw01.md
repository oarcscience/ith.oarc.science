## *I GODHEAD*

<span id="page_100">p. 100</span>

 

1 (II. 188)  
BEING IS NOT MEASURED

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Turn wheresoe'er I will, I find no evidence<br />
of End, Beginning, Centre or Circumference.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

2 (I. 263)  
GOD NEVER EXPLORETH HIMSELF

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Thought and Deed of Deity<br />
Are of such richness and extent<br />
That It remaineth to Itself<br />
An Undiscovered Continent.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

3 (VI. 174)  
IN THE SEA MANY ARE ONE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>A Loaf holds many grains of corn<br />
And many myriad drops the Sea:<br />
So is God's Oneness Multitude<br />
And that great Multitude are we.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

4 (V. I)  
ALL INTO ONE AGAIN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The All proceedeth from the One,<br />
And into One must All regress:<br />
If otherwise, the All remains<br />
Asunder-riven manyness.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_101">p. 101</span>

 

5 (I. 25)  
GOD IS NOT GRASPED

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God is an utter Nothingness,<br />
Beyond the touch of Time and Place:<br />
The more thou graspest after Him,<br />
The more he fleeth thy embrace.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

6 (I. 15)

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>What hath been told of God is not enough for me:<br />
My life and light flow from the Super-Deity.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

7 (I. 284)  
MAN MUST GO BEYOND ALL KNOWLEDGE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>What Cherubs know sufficeth not: beyond their zone<br />
I fain would take my flight unto where nothing's known.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

8 (I. 285)  
THE KNOWER MUST BECOME THE KNOWN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Naught ever can be known in God: One and Alone<br />
Is He. To know Him, Knower must be one with Known.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

9 (I. 43)  
MAN LOVETH EVEN WITHOUT KNOWING

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>One only Thing I love and know not what it is:<br />
Because I know it not, therefore I've chosen this.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

MORE KNOWN LESS UNDERSTOOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>

</colgroup>
<tbody>

That what He truly is, thou knowest less and less.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

11 (I. 178)  
THE BLAME IS THINE

<td>If gazing on the Sun endangereth thy sight,<br />
The blame is in thine eyes, and not in that great Light.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

GOD IS WITHOUT WILL

<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />

God in His changelessness eternally is still.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

THE REST AND WORK OF GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>

</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">

</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

14 (III. 170)  
OF ETERNAL MOTION

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">

</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

15 (I. 49)  
REST IS THE HIGHEST GOOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Rest is the highest Good. I'd keep both eyes close pressed,<br />
That He might have repose, were God Himself not Rest.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

16 (I. 44)  
THE SOMETHING MUST BE FORSAKEN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>If thou dost love a Something, Man,<br />
Thou lovest naught that doth abide.<br />
God is not This nor That—do thou<br />
Leave Somethings utterly aside.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

17 (V. 328)  
SIN TROUBLETH NOT GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God feeleth pain for sin in thee<br />
 As in His son,<br />
But in His Self of Deity<br />
 He feeleth none.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

18 (V. 16)  
THROUGH THEE GOD LOSETH NAUGHT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Choose, Man, which of the twain thou wilt,<br />
Thy self-destruction or thy peace.<br />
Through thee God suffereth no loss,<br />
Neither through thee hath He increase.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

19 (V. 34)  
GOD LOVETH NAUGHT BUT HIMSELF

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God is so dear unto Himself,<br />
Folded in self so utterly,<br />
That He can never cherish love<br />
For anything that is not He.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

21 (V. 173)  
GOD HATH NO THOUGHTS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God thinketh naught. Yea verily,<br />
Were thought in Him, then might He sway<br />
—Which were a thing unthinkable—<br />
Now this way, now the other way.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

22 (II. 55)

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>

But not as I or thou or other beings do.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

GOD BECOMES WHAT HE WILLS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>

All that He wills to be—but still<br />

Without a form, an aim, a will.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
