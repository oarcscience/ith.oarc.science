## *XIV THOUGHT AND DEED*

<span id="page_206">p. 206</span>

 

333 (V. 89)  
THOU MUST WIN IT HERE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Here must thy deed be done.<br />
'Twere an undreamed-of thing<br />
That he who wins no Kingdom here<br />
Should there become a King.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

334 (II. 75)  
THOU MUST ALSO BEAR FRUIT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>If thou dost drink the Blood of God<br />
And yet no fruit be found in thee,<br />
On thee shall fall a curse more fell<br />
Than once did blast that barren tree.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

335 (I. 53)  
VIRTUE REMAINETH AT REST

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>If putting Virtue into act<br />
Thou findest drudgery and moil,<br />
Virtue thou hast not yet attained<br />
But after Virtue still dost toil.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

336 (I. 273)  
RISE ABOVE HOLINESS

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>If thou art holy, it is well; but wouldst thou find<br />
Favour with God and Man, leave holiness behind.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_207">p. 207</span>

 

337 (VI. 33)  
MAN MUST CHANGE HIMSELF

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Everything changeth, Man. Canst thou remain alone<br />
Careless of betterment and changeless as a Stone?<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

338 (VI. 72)  
SLUGGARDRY WINNETH NOT HEAVEN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Sluggard, bestir thyself! Wilt thou<br />
For ever lie abed and doze?<br />
Heaven will not fly into thy mouth<br />
While thou art taking thy repose.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

339 (VI. 42)  
WHO STANDS UNMOVED BELONGS NOT TO THE WHOLE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Sun gives movement unto all,<br />
And makes the Stars dance in the sky:<br />
If I still stand immovable<br />
No part in the great Whole have I.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

340 (IV. 203)  
A DARKENED HEART SEETH NOT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Give heed unto the Flame. If lamps are burning dim,<br />
The Bridegroom when he comes, who shall distinguish him.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_208">p. 208</span>

 

341 (VI. 146)  
THE WORK PROVETH THE MASTER

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>A Master of the Craft art thou<br />
When Virtue is but thought and planned:<br />
But when it comes to Workmanship<br />
Thou show'st thyself a Prentice-hand.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

342 (V. 64)  
WE SERVE OURSELVES, NOT GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God is not served by Fast, Vigil or Litany;<br />
Thou rather serv'st thyself, being purified thereby.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

343 (I. 153)  
THOU MUST BECOME A CHILD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Unless thou dost become a Child<br />
Thou canst not enter in the place<br />
Where all God's children are—for thee<br />
The doorway hath too small a space.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

344 (V. 286)  
SIMPLICITY MUST BE INTELLIGENT

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>I honour that Simplicity<br />
To which God joins intelligence,<br />
But scarcely worth the name I count<br />
Simplicity that lacketh sense.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_209">p. 209</span>

 

345 (I. 229)  
ANGER

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Anger is like the fire of Hell.<br />
If it break out within thy breast,<br />
It burneth up the little bed<br />
Whereon the Holy Ghost doth rest.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

346 (VI. 198)  
SAFETY IN SECLUSION

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>If thou wouldst shun those strangers, Bride,<br />
 Who seek to be thy paramours,<br />
Then close the casement shutters fast<br />
 And linger not at open doors.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

347 (VI. 163)  
HATE MAKETH ITSELF HATED

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>If Hate and envy rule thy heart<br />
When thou for gifts dost supplicate,<br />
The gift thy prayers achieve will be<br />
Return of Envy and of Hate.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

348 (IV. 203)  
MAN FINDETH AS HE SEEKETH

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>As thou dost seek, so shalt thou find;<br />
As thou dost knock and dost implore,<br />
So shall the gift be unto thee,<br />
And so the opening of the door.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_210">p. 210</span>

 

349 (IV. 14)  
GOD GIVETH THE GREAT IN THE SMALL

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Take what God giveth thee. He giveth Great in Small,<br />
Gold in base slag, where we surmise it least of all.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

350 (V. 49)  
THE FINEST WISDOM

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Climb not too high; frame no unneedful subtleties;<br />
The finest Wisdom is to be not overwise.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

351 (IV. 122)  
GOD SEETH NOT ABOVE HIMSELF

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God seeth not above Himself.<br />
If thou dost seek to raise thy height<br />
Above thy stature, have a care<br />
Lest thou shouldst vanish from His sight.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

352 (I. 265)  
UNITY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Ah, were men's voices like the wood-birds' melody—<br />
Each happy note distinct, but all in harmony!<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_211">p. 211</span>

 

353 (I. 266)  
NOTHING HATH WORTH FOR THE SCORNER

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The Nightingale mocks not the Cuckoo's note, 'tis true,<br />
And yet you scorn my song if I sing not as you.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

354 (I. 218)  
DIVINE SEEING

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Who in his fellow-man sees God and Christ, none else,<br />
He seeth with the light wherein the Godhead dwells.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

355 (VI. 263)  
CONCLUSION

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Friend, it is now enough. Wouldst thou read more, go hence,<br />
Become thyself the Writing and thyself the Sense.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
