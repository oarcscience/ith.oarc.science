## *IV ONENESS WITH THE DIVINE*

<span id="page_128">p. 128</span>

 

85 (I. 8)  
GOD LIVETH NOT WITHOUT ME

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>I know God cannot live one instant without Me:<br />
If I should come to naught, needs must He cease to be.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

86 (I. 10)  
I AM AS GOD AND GOD AS I

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>I am as great as God, He is as small as I:<br />
No higher than I is He, nor I than He less high.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

87 (I. 14)  
A CHRISTIAN IS AS RICH AS GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>I am as rich as God. He owns no particle<br />
Of dust—believe me, Man!—that is not mine as well.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

88 (II. 178)  
ALL CONSISTS IN I AND THOU  
(C<span class="small">REATOR AND</span> C<span
class="small">REATURE</span>.)

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Naught is but I and Thou. Were there nor Thou nor I,<br />
Then God is no more God, and Heaven falls from the sky.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_129">p. 129</span>

 

89 (I. 68)  
DEEP CALLETH UNTO DEEP

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Deep calls to Deep. My spirit's Deep doth cry amain<br />
To Deep of God: say, which is deeper of the twain?<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

90 (II. 180)  
MAN IS NAUGHT, GOD ALL

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>I am not I nor Thou: Thou art the I in Me:<br />
Therefore I yield the meed of honour unto Thee.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

91 (II. 142)  
THOU MUST BE IT THYSELF

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Ask not what is divine. It were too great a task<br />
To comprehend—unless thou art what thou dost ask.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

92 (I. 81)  
GOD BLOSSOMS OUT OF HIS BRANCHES

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>If thou art born of God, God blossometh in thee:<br />
His Godhead is thy sap and flower-finery.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_130">p. 130</span>

 

93 (II. 120)  
MAN EATETH AND DRINKETH GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>If thou art one with God, truly it may be said<br />
Thou eat'st and drinkest God in every piece of bread.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

94 (III. 20)  
GOD-MAN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>That I may come to wealth, God comes to beggary:<br />
That I may become He, lo! He becometh I.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

95 (I. 96)  
GOD CAN DO NOTHING WITHOUT ME

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God hath no potency to make<br />
A single worm without my aid:<br />
If I sustain it not with Him<br />
Straightway its being is unmade.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

96 (I. 72)  
HOW IS GOD SEEN?

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>No Way there is by which to go<br />
Unto the Light wherein God dwells:<br />
Thou must thyself become the Light<br />
Or God is hidden from thee else.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_131">p. 131</span>

 

97 (IV. 24)  
THE TRANSFORMATION

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Body must into Spirit pass,<br />
And Spirit into Deity,<br />
If thou wouldst have thy dearest wish<br />
And know the perfect ecstasy.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

98 (II. 255)  
FIVE DEGREES IN GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Five ladder-rungs there are in God—<br />
 Slave, Friend, Son, Bride and Spouse.<br />
Who climbeth higher unselfs himself,<br />
 Drops count of I's and Thou's.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

99 (V. 76)  
AS HIS FRIENDSHIP SO THE FRIEND

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Thou drinkest in the soul of him<br />
With whom thou'rt friended—in the end<br />
Becomest God, if friend of God,<br />
And Devil, if the Devil's friend.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

100 (V. 200)  
A MAN IS CHANGED INTO WHAT HE LOVES

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Thou shalt become that thing itself<br />
Which thou dost deem of dearest worth—<br />
God shalt become if thou lov'st God,<br />
And Earth if so thou lovest Earth.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_132">p. 132</span>

 

101 (V. 332)  
WHITHER MAN GOES WHEN HE DIES INTO GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>When I die into God, I once again return<br />
There where I was eternally ere I was born.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

102 (V. 233)  
WHEN MAN IS GOD

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Once I was God in God, or ever I was I,<br />
And can be God again, if this I could but die.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

103 (VI. 175)  
UNION WITH GOD IS EASY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>'Tis easier, Man, to see thyself and God all one<br />
Than open a closed eye—will it and it is done.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

104 (V. 259)  
GOD BECOMETH I BECAUSE I AFORETIME WAS HE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God doth become what now I am,<br />
Assumes my manhood; what He is,<br />
The same aforetime I have been:<br />
Therefore it is He doeth this.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<span id="page_133">p. 133</span>

 

105 (II. 159)  
SPIRIT IS AS ESSENCE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>My Spirit is a partial Being:<br />
It yearns to be recentred in<br />
That Essence whence it broke away,<br />
Its primal Root and Origin.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

106 (IV. 12)  
ALL WEAL IN ONE THING ONLY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>From but one thing my all of Weal,<br />
My all of Peace doth spring;<br />
Though losing much upon the way,<br />
I run with haste to this One Thing.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

109 (IV. 181)  
OF THE BLESSED SOUL

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">

 Hath lost the very sense;<br />
It is a single Light with God<br />
 And one Magnificence.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

110 (V. 126)  
THE DEATH OF I-HOOD STRENGTHENS GOD IN THEE

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>The more the I in me doth fail,<br />
Diminish and sink lower,<br />
So much the more the I of God<br />
Aggrandizeth its power.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

111 (V. 234)  
EVERYTHING RETURNS TO ITS ORIGIN

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>

 God, since God gave it birth?<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

114 (VI. 171)

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>

When God at last absorbeth thee.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

117 (I. 23)  
THE SPIRITUAL MARY

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">

—Nor can I otherwise—God's gift<br />
Of everlasting Happiness.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

118 (I. 276)  
ONE THE OTHER'S BEGINNING AND END

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God is my final end;<br />
If then I am His origin,<br />
From mine His Being floweth out,<br />
To Him my Being floweth in.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

 

119 (I. 100)  
ONE UPHOLDETH THE OTHER

<table data-align="center">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>God's need of me, my need of God,<br />
 Are equal in degree.<br />
He helps to bear my being up<br />
 And I help Him to be.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
