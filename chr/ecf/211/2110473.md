#### <span id="iv.iii.ix.ii-p0.1"></span>Chapter II.

<span id="iv.iii.ix.ii-p1"></span>Of the care with which the malady of
dejection must be healed.

<span id="iv.iii.ix.ii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iii.ix.ii-p2.1"></span>Wherefore</span> if we are anxious to
exert ourselves lawfully in the struggle of our spiritual combat we
ought with no less care to set about healing this malady also. For “as
the moth injures the garment, and the worm the wood, so dejection the
heart of man.” <span id="fr_952"></span>[953](#fn_952.md) With sufficient
clearness and appropriateness has the Divine Spirit expressed the force
of this dangerous and most injurious fault.
