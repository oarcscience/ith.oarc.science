#### <span id="iv.iv.viii.xiv-p0.1"></span>Chapter XIV.

<span id="iv.iv.viii.xiv-p1"></span>An objection, as to how we ought to
believe that devils see into the thoughts of men.

<span id="iv.iv.viii.xiv-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iv.viii.xiv-p2.1"></span>Germanus</span>: In this way, which you
describe, those spirits cannot possibly see into our thoughts. But we
think it utterly absurd to hold such an opinion, when Scripture says:
“If the spirit of him that hath power ascend upon thee;” <span
id="fr_1474"></span>[1475](#fn_1474.md) and again: “When the devil had put
it into the heart of Simon Iscariot to betray the Lord.” <span
id="fr_1475"></span>[1476](#fn_1475.md) How then can we believe that our
thoughts are not open to them, when we feel that for the most part they
spring up and are nursed by their suggestions and instigation?
