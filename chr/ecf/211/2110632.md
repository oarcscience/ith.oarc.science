#### <span id="iv.iv.iv.xiv-p0.1"></span>Chapter XIV.

<span id="iv.iv.iv.xiv-p1"></span>That knowledge of the law is given by
the guidance and illumination of the Lord.

<span id="iv.iv.iv.xiv-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iv.iv.xiv-p2.1"></span>The</span> knowledge also of the law
itself they daily endeavour to gain not by diligence in reading, but by
the guidance and illumination of God as they say to Him: “Show me Thy
ways, O Lord, and teach me Thy paths:” and “open Thou mine eyes: and I
shall see the wondrous things of Thy law:” and “teach me to do Thy will,
for Thou art my God;” and again: “Who teacheth man knowledge.” <span
id="fr_1255"></span>[1256](#fn_1255)
