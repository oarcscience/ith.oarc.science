#### <span id="iv.v.vii.vii-p0.1"></span>Chapter VII.

<span id="iv.v.vii.vii-p1"></span>How nothing should be put before love,
or after anger.

<span id="iv.v.vii.vii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.v.vii.vii-p2.1"></span>As</span> then nothing should be put
before love, so on the other hand nothing should be put <span
id="page_453">p. 453</span> <span
id="iv.v.vii.vii-Page_453"></span>below rage and anger. For all things,
however useful and necessary they seem, should yet be disregarded that
disturbing anger may be avoided, and all things even which we think are
unfortunate should be undertaken and endured that the calm of love and
peace may be preserved unimpaired, because we should reckon nothing more
damaging than anger and vexation, and nothing more advantageous than
love.
