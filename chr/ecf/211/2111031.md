#### <span id="iv.vi.v.xxxi-p0.1"></span>Chapter XXXI.

<span id="iv.vi.v.xxxi-p1"></span>A question, how we ought to understand
the Apostle’s words: “Sin shall not have dominion over you.”

<span id="iv.vi.v.xxxi-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.vi.v.xxxi-p2.1"></span>Germanus</span>: Because this saying of
the Apostle, which promises freedom from care not only to monks but to
all Christians in general, cannot lead us wrong, it seems to us somewhat
obscure. For whereas he maintains that all those who believe the gospel
are at liberty and free from the yoke and dominion of sin, how is it
that the dominion of sin holds vigorous sway over almost all the
baptized, in accordance with the Lord’s words, where He says: “Every one
that doeth sin is the servant of sin”? <span
id="fr_2215"></span>[2216](#fn_2215.md)
