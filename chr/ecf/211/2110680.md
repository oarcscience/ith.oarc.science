#### <span id="iv.iv.vi.xvii-p0.1"></span>Chapter XVII.

<span id="iv.iv.vi.xvii-p1"></span>A question with regard to the
comparison of <span class="font-style:normal"><span
id="iv.iv.vi.xvii-p1.1"></span>seven </span>nations with<span
class="font-style:normal"><span id="iv.iv.vi.xvii-p1.2"></span> eight
</span>faults.

<span id="iv.iv.vi.xvii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iv.vi.xvii-p2.1"></span>Germanus</span>: How then is it that
there are *eight* faults which assault us, when Moses reckons the
nations opposed to the people of Israel as *seven*, and how is it well
for us to take possession of the territory of our faults?
