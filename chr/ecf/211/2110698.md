#### <span id="iv.iv.vii.vii-p0.1"></span>Chapter VII.

<span id="iv.iv.vii.vii-p1"></span>A question whether the man who causes
the death of a good man is guilty, if the good man is the gainer by his
death.

<span id="iv.iv.vii.vii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iv.vii.vii-p2.1"></span>Germanus</span>: Well then, if a good man
does not only suffer no evil by being killed, but actually gains a
reward from his suffering, how can we accuse the man who has done him no
harm but good by killing him?
