#### <span id="iv.iii.vii.ii-p0.1"></span>Chapter II.

<span id="iv.iii.vii.ii-p1"></span>How dangerous is the disease of
covetousness.

<span id="iv.iii.vii.ii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iii.vii.ii-p2.1"></span>But</span> this disease coming upon us at
a later period, and approaching the soul from with<span id="page_249">p.
249</span> <span id="iv.iii.vii.ii-Page_249"></span>out, as it can be
the more easily guarded against and resisted, so, if it is disregarded
and once allowed to gain an entrance into the heart, is the more
dangerous to every one, and with the greater difficulty expelled. For it
becomes “a root of all evils,” <span id="fr_876"></span>[877](#fn_876)
and gives rise to a multiplicity of incitements to sin.
