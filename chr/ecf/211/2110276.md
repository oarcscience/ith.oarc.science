## <span id="iii.xxxv-p0.1"></span>Appendix I.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p1"></span>Note on Section 41, Page 143.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iii.xxxv-p2.1"></span>There</span> is so close an agreement, both in
substance and often in the form of expression, between the preceding
sections (36–42) and the so-called Athanasian Creed, that it led Antelmi
(*Nova de Symb. Athanas. Disquisitio*,) to ascribe that document to
Vincentius as its author, and to suppose that in it we have the
fulfilment of the promise here referred to. If, however, the Creed was
the work of Vincentius, it cannot well be the work promised at the close
of § 41, for Vincentius’s words point to a fuller and more explicit
treatment of the subjects referred to, whereas in the Athanasian Creed,
though the subjects are the same, the treatment of them is very much
briefer and more concise.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p3"></span>Whoever was the author however, if it was
not Vincentius, he must at least, as the subjoined extracts seem to
prove, have been familiar with the Commonitory, as also with St.
Augustine’s writings, of which, as well as of the Commonitory, the Creed
bears evident traces. I subjoin the following instances of agreement
between the Commonitory and the Creed: Antelmi gives several others.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iii.xxxv-p4.1"></span>Commonitory:</span>

<span id="iii.xxxv-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iii.xxxv-p5.1"></span>Athanasian Creed:</span>

<span id="iii.xxxv-p6"></span>Unum Christum Jesum, non duos, eumdemque
Deum pariter atque Hominem confitetur. § 36.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p7"></span>Est ergo Fides recta, ut credamus et
confiteamur, quia Dominus noster Jesus Christus, Dei Filius, Deus
pariter et Homo est. v. 28.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p8"></span>Alia est Persona Patris, alia Filii, alia
Spiritus Sancti. § 37.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p9"></span>Alia est Persona Patris, alia Filii, alia
Spiritus Sancti. v. 5.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p10"></span>Unus idemque Christus, Deus et Homo, Idem
Patri et æqualis et minor, Idem ex Patre ante sæcula genitus, Idem in
sæculo ex Matre generatus, perfectus Deus, perfectus Homo. § 37.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p11"></span>Deus ex substantia Patris, ante sæcula
genitus, Homo ex substantia Matris, in sæculo natus; perfectus Deus
perfectus Homo. vv. 29, 30.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p12"></span>Unus, non corruptibili nescio qua
Divinitatis et Humanitatis confusione, sed integra et singulari quadam
unitate Personæ. § 37.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p13"></span>Unus omnino, non conversione sustantiæ,
sed unitate Personæ. v. 34.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p14"></span>Sicut Verbum in carne caro, ita Homo in
Deo Deus est. § 40.

<span id="iii.xxxv-p15"></span>Unus, non conversione Divinitatis in
carne, sed Adsumptione Humanitatis in Deo. <span
id="fr_525"></span>[526](#fn_525) v. 33.
