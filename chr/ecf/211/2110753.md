#### <span id="iv.iv.ix.ix-p0.1"></span>Chapter IX.

<span id="iv.iv.ix.ix-p1"></span>An objection stating that the fall of
the devil took its origin from the deception of God.

<span id="iv.iv.ix.ix-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iv.ix.ix-p2.1"></span>Germanus</span>: Up till now we used to
believe that the reason and commencement of the ruin and fall of the
devil, in which he was cast out from his heavenly estate, was more
particularly envy, when in his spiteful subtlety he deceived Adam and
Eve.
