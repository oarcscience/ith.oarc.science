#### <span id="iv.iii.vii.vi-p0.1"></span>Chapter VI.

<span id="iv.iii.vii.vi-p1"></span>How difficult the evil of
covetousness is to drive away when once it has been admitted.

<span id="iv.iii.vii.vi-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iii.vii.vi-p2.1"></span>Wherefore</span> let not this evil seem
of no account or unimportant to anybody: for as it can easily be
avoided, so if it has once got hold of any one, it scarcely suffers him
to get at the remedies for curing it. For it is a <span id="page_250">p.
250</span> <span id="iv.iii.vii.vi-Page_250"></span>regular nest of
sins, and a “root of all kinds of evil,” and becomes a hopeless
incitement to wickedness, as the Apostle says, “Covetousness,” i.e. the
love of money, “is a root of all kinds of evil.” <span
id="fr_878"></span>[879](#fn_878.md)
