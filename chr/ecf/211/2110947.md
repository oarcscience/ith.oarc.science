#### <span id="iv.v.viii.xxvii-p0.1"></span>Chapter XXVII.

<span id="iv.v.viii.xxvii-p1"></span>A question whether the saying: “I
have sworn and am purposed” is opposed to the view given above.

<span id="iv.v.viii.xxvii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="iv.v.viii.xxvii-p2.1"></span>Germanus</span>: So far as this
consideration is concerned which has been clearly and fully treated of,
a monk ought never to determine anything for fear lest he turn out a
breaker of his word or else obstinate. And what then can we make of this
saying of the Psalmist: “I have sworn and am purposed to keep Thy
righteous judgments?” <span id="fr_2065"></span>[2066](#fn_2065) What is
“to swear and purpose” except to keep one’s determinations fixedly?
