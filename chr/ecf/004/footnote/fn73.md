Avitus*, No. 94) says that Origen, "after a most lengthened discussion,
in which he asserts that all bodily nature is to be changed into
attenuated and spiritual bodies, and that all substance is to be
converted into one body of perfect purity, and more brilliant than any
splendour (*mundissimum et omni splendore purius*), and such as the
human mind cannot now conceive," adds at the last, "And God will be
\`all in all,0' so that the whole of bodily nature may be reduced into
that substance which is better than all others, into the divine, viz.,
than which none is better." From which, since it seems to follow that
God possesses a body, although of extreme tenuity (*licet tenuissimum*),
Rufinus has either suppressed this view, or altered the meaning of
Origen's words (Ruaeus).
