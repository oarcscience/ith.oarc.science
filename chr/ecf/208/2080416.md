<span id="ix.cccxliv-p1"></span> <span class="c18"><span
id="ix.cccxliv-p1.1"></span>Letter CCCXLIX.</span>

<span id="ix.cccxliv-p2"></span>Libanius to Basil.

<span id="ix.cccxliv-p3"></span><span class="c14"><span
id="ix.cccxliv-p3.1"></span>Will</span> you not give over, Basil,
packing this sacred haunt of the Muses with Cappadocians, and these
redolent of the frost <span id="fr_3284"></span>[3285](#fn_3284) and
snow and all Cappadocia’s good things?  They have almost made me a
Cappadocian too, always chanting their “I salute you.”

<span id="ix.cccxliv-p5"></span>I must endure, since it is Basil who
commands.  Know, however, that I am making a careful study of the
manners and customs of the country, and that I mean to metamorphose the
men into the nobility and the harmony of my Calliope, that they may seem
to you to be turned from pigeons into doves.
