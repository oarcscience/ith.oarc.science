id="ix.ccclii-p1.1"></span>Letter CCCLVII.</span>

<span id="ix.ccclii-p2"></span>Libanius to Basil.

<span id="ix.ccclii-p3"></span><span class="c14"><span
id="ix.ccclii-p3.1"></span>What</span> has made Basil object to the
letter, the proof of philosophy?  I have learned to make fun from you,
but nevertheless your fun is venerable and, so to say, hoary with age. 
But, by our very friendship, by our common pastimes, do away, I charge
you, with the distress caused by your letter…in nothing differing. <span
id="fr_3286"></span>[3287](#fn_3286.md)
