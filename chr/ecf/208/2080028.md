<span id="vi.ii.xi-Page_lxxiv"></span><span class="c1"><span
id="vi.ii.xi-p1.1"></span>X.—Editions and Manuscripts.</span>

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p2"></span>Among the chief editions and <span
class="c14"><span id="vi.ii.xi-p2.1"></span>mss.</span> the following
may be mentioned:

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p3"></span>The Editio Princeps of the complete extant
works of Basil in the original Greek is that which Froben published for
Janus Cornarius at Bale in 1551.  But Froben had already published in
1532, under the editorship of Erasmus, an edition containing the *De
Spiritu Sancto*, the *Hexæmeron*, the *Homilies on the Psalms*,
twenty-nine different *Homilies* and some *Letters*.

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p4"></span>A Venetian edition, published by Fabius in
1535, comprised the *Moralia*, as well as the dubious book on Virginity,
three books against Eunomius, and the tract against the Sabellians,
Arians, and Anomœans.

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p5"></span>The Greek editions had been preceded by a
Latin version at Rome, by Raphael Volaterranus in 1515, of which the
autograph manuscript is in the British Museum, and by another at Paris
in 1525, and by a third Latin edition issued at Cologne in 1531.  These
were followed by other editions printed at Paris, Antwerp, and Cologne. 
In 1618 Fronton du Duc, commonly known as Ducæus, published, in
conjunction with Frederic Morel, an edition in two folio volumes
containing a Latin version as well as the Greek.  The edition of the
French Dominican Father Francis Combefis, was published shortly after
his death in 1679.  The most important step in the direction of accuracy
and completeness was taken by Julian Garnier, a Benedictine Father of
the Congregation of St. Maur.  He revised and corrected the Greek text
of earlier editions on the authority of a number of manuscripts in
Paris, Italy, and England, and issued the first of his three folio
volumes at Paris, at the press of John Baptist Coignard, in 1721.  The
third volume did not appear till 1730, five years after Garnier’s
death.  In the meanwhile the editorial work had been taken up by Prudent
Maran, another Benedictine, to whom are due a careful and voluminous
biographical notice, many notes, and a chronological arrangement of the
Letters.  This was reissued in three 4° volumes in Paris in 1889, and is
the basis of the edition published, with additions, by the Abbé Jacques
Paul Migne, in the *Patrologia Græca*, in 1857.

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p6"></span>An important edition of a separate work is
the revised text, with notes and introduction, of the *De Spiritu
Sancto*, by the Rev. C. F. H. Johnston, published at the Clarendon Press
in 1892.

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p7"></span>German translations were published by
Count Schweikhard at Ingolstadt in 1591 (Ceillier VI. viii. 8), and by
J. von Wendel at Vienna in 1776–78.  There have also been issued *<span
id="vi.ii.xi-p7.1"></span>Basilius des Grossen auserlesenes Homilien,
übersetzt und mit Ammerkungen versehen von J. G. Krabinger*, Landshut,
1839, and *<span id="vi.ii.xi-p7.2"></span>Auserlesene Schriften,
übersetzt von Gröne*, Kempten, 1875.

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p8"></span>Homilies and Orations were published in
Italian in 1711 by Gio. Maria Lucchini.  *Omelie Scelte*, translated by
A. M. Ricci, were published in Florence in 1732.

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p9"></span>Many important extracts are translated
into French in the *<span id="vi.ii.xi-p9.1"></span>Historie Générale
des Auteurs Sacrés* of the Benedictine Remy Ceillier (Paris, 1737).

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p10"></span>E. Fialon, in his *Ét. Hist*. (1869) has
translated the *<span class="Greek"><span
id="vi.ii.xi-p10.1"></span>Ηεξͅμερον</span>*; and in 1889 the *<span
id="vi.ii.xi-p10.2"></span>Panégyrique due Martyr* *Gordius* was
published in French by J. Genouille.

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p11"></span>A complete account of the bibliography of
St. Basil is given in the *Notitia ex Bibliotheca Fabricii* (Ed. Harles,
tom. ix. 1804), in Migne’s ed. vol. i., *Prolegomena* p. ccxli.

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p12"></span>In 1888 a translation of the *De Spiritu
Sancto*, by G. Lewis, was included in the Christian Classic Series.

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p13"></span>Of all the smaller works a great
popularity, as far as popularity can be gauged by the number of editions
and translations, has belonged to the *Advice to the Young* and the
*Homily on the Forty Martyrs*.

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p16"></span>For the *Hexæmeron* the Ben. Edd. used
eight <span class="c14"><span id="vi.ii.xi-p16.1"></span>mss.</span>
styled *Regii*, and numbered respectively 1824, 2286 (originally in the

Quartus* of the *De Spiritu Sancto*), and 2989, two <span

*Colbertinus*, 3069 and 4721, two Coistiniani, 229, <span

and a <span class="c14"><span id="vi.ii.xi-p16.4"></span>ms.</span> in
the Bodleian, “a doctissimo viro Joanne Wolf collatus.”

<span id="vi.ii.xi-p17"></span>The sources of the Ben. Ed. of the
*Letters* were Coislinianus 237, <span class="c14"><span
id="vi.ii.xi-p17.1"></span>XI</span>th c., a Codex Harlæanus of the Xth
or <span class="c14"><span id="vi.ii.xi-p17.2"></span>XI</span>th c.,
and a Codex Medicæus, Codex Regius 2293, Codex Regius 2897, Codex Regius
2896, Codex Regius 2502, Codex Regius 1824, Codex Regius 1906, and Codex
Regius 1908.
