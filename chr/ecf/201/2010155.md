<span id="iii.x.xv-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="iii.x.xv-p1.1"></span>Chapter XIV.—</span>*The False Prophets of the
Phrygians.*

<span id="iii.x.xv-p2"></span><span class="c11"><span
id="iii.x.xv-p2.1"></span>The</span> enemy of God’s Church, who is
emphatically a hater of good and a lover of evil, and leaves untried no
manner of craft against men, was again active in causing strange
heresies to spring up against the Church. <span
id="fr_1563"></span>[1564](#fn_1563.md) For some persons, like venomous
reptiles, crawled over Asia and Phrygia, boasting that Montanus was the
Paraclete, and that the women that followed him, Priscilla and
Maximilla, were prophetesses of Montanus. <span
id="fr_1564"></span>[1565](#fn_1564.md)
