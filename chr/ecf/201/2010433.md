<span id="iv.vi.i.xlviii-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.i.xlviii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XLVIII</span>.—*Celebration of
Constantine’s Decennalia.*

<span id="iv.vi.i.xlviii-p2"></span><span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.i.xlviii-p2.1"></span>While</span> he was thus circumstanced,
he completed the tenth year of his reign. On this occasion he ordered
the celebration of general festivals, and offered prayers of
thanksgiving to God, the King of all, as sacrifices without flame or
smoke. <span id="fr_3147"></span>[3148](#fn_3147) And from this
employment he derived much pleasure: not so from the tidings he received
of the ravages committed in the Eastern provinces.
