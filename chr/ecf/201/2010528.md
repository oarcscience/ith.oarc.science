<span id="iv.vi.iii.ix-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.iii.ix-p1.1"></span>Chapter IX</span>.—*Of the Virtue and Age
of the Two Hundred and Fifty Bishops.*

<span id="iv.vi.iii.ix-p2"></span><span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.iii.ix-p2.1"></span>Of</span> these ministers of God, some
were distinguished by wisdom and eloquence, others by the gravity of
their lives, and by patient fortitude of character, while others again
united in themselves all these graces. <span
id="fr_3244"></span>[3245](#fn_3244) There were among them men whose
years demanded veneration: others were younger, and in the prime of
mental vigor; and some had but recently entered on the course of their
ministry. For the maintenance of all ample provision was daily furnished
by the emperor’s command.
