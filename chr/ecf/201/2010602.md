<span id="iv.vi.iv.xvi-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.iv.xvi-p1.1"></span>Chapter XVI</span>.—*He forbids by Law the
Placing his Likeness in Idol Temples.*

<span id="iv.vi.iv.xvi-p2"></span><span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.iv.xvi-p2.1"></span>In</span> this manner he represented
himself, even through the medium of painting, as habitually engaged in
prayer to God. At the same time he forbade, by an express enactment, the
setting up of any resemblance of himself in any idol temple, that not
even the mere lineaments of his person might receive contamination from
the error of forbidden superstition.
