<span id="iv.vi.ii.xviii-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.ii.xviii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XVIII</span>.—*Death of
Licinius, and Celebration of the Event.*

<span id="iv.vi.ii.xviii-p2"></span><span class="c11"><span
id="iv.vi.ii.xviii-p2.1"></span>He</span> then proceeded to deal with
this adversary of God and his followers according to the laws of war,
and consign them to fitting punishment. Accordingly the tyrant himself,
and they whose counsels had supported him in his impiety, were together
subjected to the just punishment of death. After this, those who had so
lately been deceived by their vain confidence in false deities,
acknowledged with unfeigned sincerity the God of Constantine, and openly
professed their belief in him as the true and only God.
