id="iii.xvii.i-p1.1"></span>On</span> Bk. III. chap. 3, § 5 (note 17,
continued).

<span id="iii.xvii.i-p2"></span>Since this note was in type Dr.
Gardiner’s admirable and exhaustive essay on the authorship of the
Epistle to the Hebrews (in the *Nicene and Post-Nicene Fathers*, First
Series, Vol. XIV. p. 341 sq.) has come to hand, and I have been much
pleased to see that the theory that Barnabas wrote the epistle is
accepted and defended with vigor.
