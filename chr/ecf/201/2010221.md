<span id="iii.xii.iv-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="iii.xii.iv-p1.1"></span>Chapter III.—</span>*Cyprian, and the
Bishops with him, first taught that it was necessary to purify by
Baptism those converted from Heresy.*

<span id="iii.xii.iv-p2"></span><span class="c11"><span
id="iii.xii.iv-p2.1"></span>First</span> of all, Cyprian, pastor of the
parish of Carthage, <span id="fr_2169"></span>[2170](#fn_2169)
maintained that they should not be received except they had been
purified from their error by baptism. But Stephen considering it
unnecessary to add any innovation contrary to the tradition which had
been held from the beginning, was very indignant at this. <span
id="fr_2170"></span>[2171](#fn_2170)
