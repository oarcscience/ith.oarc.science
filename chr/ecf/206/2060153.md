<span id="v.CXLII-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="v.CXLII-p1.1"></span>Letter CXLII. To Augustine.</span>

<span id="v.CXLII-p2"></span>There is good ground for supposing this to
form part of the previous letter. If so, Jerome speaks in a figure of
the success gained by Pelagianism in Palestine. “Jerusalem,” he says,
“is in the hands of Nebuchadnezzar and will not heed the voice of
Jeremiah,” that is, as the context shews, Jerome himself. This letter is
among those of Augustine, number 123.
