<span id="v.CXXI-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="v.CXXI-p1.1"></span>Letter CXXI. To Algasia.</span>

<span id="v.CXXI-p2"></span>Jerome writes to a lady of Gaul named
Algasia to answer eleven questions which she had submitted to him. They
were as follows:—

<span id="v.CXXI-p3"></span>(1) How is [Luke
7:18](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#018.md),
[19](../../../bib/kjv/luk007.htm#019.md)<span id="v.CXXI-p3.1"></span>, to
be reconciled with [John i. 36](../../../bib/kjv/joh001.htm#036.md)<span
id="v.CXXI-p3.2"></span>?

<span id="v.CXXI-p4"></span>(2) What is the meaning of [Matt. xii.
20](../../../bib/kjv/mat012.htm#020.md)<span id="v.CXXI-p4.1"></span>?

<span id="v.CXXI-p5"></span>(3) And of [Matt. xvi.
24](../../../bib/kjv/mat016.htm#024.md)<span id="v.CXXI-p5.1"></span>?

<span id="v.CXXI-p6"></span>(4) And of [Matt.
24:19](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#019.md),
[20](../../../bib/kjv/mat024.htm#020.md)<span id="v.CXXI-p6.1"></span>?

<span id="v.CXXI-p7"></span>(5) And of [Luke ix.
53](../../../bib/kjv/luk009.htm#053.md)<span id="v.CXXI-p7.1"></span>?

<span id="v.CXXI-p8"></span>(6) What is the meaning of the parable of
the unjust steward?

<span id="v.CXXI-p9"></span>(7) What is the meaning of [Rom. v.
7](../../../bib/kjv/rom005.htm#007.md)<span id="v.CXXI-p9.1"></span>?

<span id="v.CXXI-p10"></span>(8) And of [Rom. vii.
8](../../../bib/kjv/rom007.htm#008.md)<span id="v.CXXI-p10.1"></span>?

<span id="v.CXXI-p11"></span>(9) And of [Rom. ix.
3](../../../bib/kjv/rom009.htm#003.md)<span id="v.CXXI-p11.1"></span>?

<span id="v.CXXI-p12"></span>(10) And of [Col. ii.
18](../../../bib/kjv/col002.htm#018.md)<span id="v.CXXI-p12.1"></span>?

<span id="v.CXXI-p13"></span>(11) And of [2 Thes. ii.
3](../../../bib/kjv/th2002.htm#003.md)<span id="v.CXXI-p13.1"></span>?

<span id="v.CXXI-p14"></span>The date of the letter is 406 <span
class="c17"><span id="v.CXXI-p14.1"></span>a.d.</span>
