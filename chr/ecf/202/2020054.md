<span id="ii.v.iv-p1"></span><span class="c11"><span
id="ii.v.iv-p1.1"></span>Chapter IV</span>.—*On the Death of Eusebius
Pamphilus, Acacius succeeds to the Bishopric of Cæsarea.*

<span id="ii.v.iv-p2"></span><span class="c11"><span
id="ii.v.iv-p2.1"></span>At</span> this time Eusebius, who was bishop of
Cæsarea in Palestine, and had the surname of Pamphilus, having died,
Acacius, his disciple, succeeded him in the bishopric. This individual
published several books, and among others a biographical sketch of his
master.
