<span id="v.iii.xxxi"></span>

<span id="v.iii.xxxi-p1"></span> 30. Ye also who have not yet made this
vow, who are able to receive it, receive it. <span
id="fr_2092"></span>[2093](#fn_2092) Run with perseverance, that ye may
obtain. <span id="fr_2093"></span>[2094](#fn_2093) Take ye each his
sacrifices, and enter ye into the courts <span
id="fr_2094"></span>[2095](#fn_2094) of the Lord, not of necessity,
having power over your own will. <span
id="fr_2095"></span>[2096](#fn_2095) For not as, “Thou shall not commit
adultery, Thou shall not kill,” <span
id="fr_2096"></span>[2097](#fn_2096) can it so be said, Thou shalt not
wed. The former are demanded, the latter are offered. If the latter are
done, they are praised: unless the former are done, they are condemned.
In the former the Lord commands us what is due; but in the latter, if ye
shall have spent any thing more, on His return He will repay you. <span
id="fr_2097"></span>[2098](#fn_2097) Think of (whatever that be) within
His wall “a place named, much better than of sons and of
daughters.” <span id="fr_2098"></span>[2099](#fn_2098) Think of “an
eternal name” there. <span id="fr_2099"></span>[2100](#fn_2099) Who
unfolds of what kind that name shall be? Yet, whatever it shall be, it
shall be eternal. By believing and hoping and loving this, ye have been
able, not to shun marriage, as forbidden, but to fly past it, as
allowed.
