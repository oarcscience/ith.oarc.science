<span id="iv.ii.iii-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="iv.ii.iii-p1.1"></span>Chapter 1.—The Author Desires the Gift of
True Wisdom for Laurentius.</span>

<span id="iv.ii.iii-p2"></span>I <span class="c19"><span
id="iv.ii.iii-p2.1"></span>Cannot</span> express, my beloved son
Laurentius, the delight with which I witness your progress in knowledge,
and the earnest desire I have that you should be a wise man: not one of
those of whom it is said, “Where is the wise? where is the scribe? where
is the disputer of this world? hath not God made foolish the wisdom of
this world?” <span id="fr_1088"></span>[1089](#fn_1088) but one of those
of whom it is said, “The multitude of the wise is the welfare of the
world,” <span id="fr_1089"></span>[1090](#fn_1089) and such as the
apostles wishes those to become, whom he tells,” I would have you wise
unto that which is good, and simple concerning evil.” <span
id="fr_1090"></span>[1091](#fn_1090) Now, just as no one can exist of
himself, so no one can be wise of himself, but only by the enlightening
influence of Him of whom it is written,” All wisdom cometh from the
Lord.” <span id="fr_1091"></span>[1092](#fn_1091)
