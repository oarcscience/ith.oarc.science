<span id="v.iii.xli"></span>

<span id="v.iii.xli-p1"></span> 40. And what members of the holy body,
which is the Church, ought more to take care, that upon them the holy
Spirit may rest, than such as profess virginal holiness? But how doth He
rest, where He findeth not His own place? what else than an humbled
heart, to fill, not to leap back from; to raise up, not to weigh down?
whereas it hath been most plainly said, “On whom shall rest My Spirit?
On him that is humble and quiet, and trembles at My words.” <span
id="fr_2156"></span>[2157](#fn_2156.md) Already thou livest righteously,
already thou livest piously, thou livest chastely, holily, with virginal
purity; as yet, however, thou livest here, and art thou not humbled at
hearing, “What, is not human life upon earth a trial?” <span
id="fr_2157"></span>[2158](#fn_2157.md) Doth it not drive thee back from
over-confident arrogance, “Woe unto the world because of
offenses?” <span id="fr_2158"></span>[2159](#fn_2158.md) Dost thou not
tremble, lest thou be accounted among the many, whose “love waxeth cold,
because that iniquity abounds?” <span
id="fr_2159"></span>[2160](#fn_2159.md) Dost thou not smite thy breast,
when thou hearest, “Wherefore, whoso thinketh that he standeth, let him
see to it lest he fall?” <span id="fr_2160"></span>[2161](#fn_2160.md) Amid
these divine warnings and human dangers, do we yet find it so hard to
persuade holy virgins to humility?
