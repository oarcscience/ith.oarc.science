<span id="v.viii.xxiii"></span>

<span id="v.viii.xxiii-p1"></span> 22. But the pleasure of the Creator,
of which is written, “And from the river of Thy pleasure wilt Thou give
them to drink,” <span id="fr_2686"></span>[2687](#fn_2686) is of far
other kind, for it is not, like us, a creature. Unless then its love be
given to us from thence there is no source whence it may be in us. And
consequently, a good will, by which we love God, cannot be in man, save
in whom God also worketh to will. This good will therefore, that is, a
will faithfully subjected to God, <span
id="fr_2687"></span>[2688](#fn_2687) a will set on fire by sanctity of
that ardor which is above, a will which loves God and his neighbor for
God’s sake; whether through love, of which the Apostle Peter makes
answer, “Lord, Thou knowest that I love Thee;” <span
id="fr_2688"></span>[2689](#fn_2688) whether through fear, of which says
the Apostle Paul, “In fear and trembling work out your own
salvation;” <span id="fr_2689"></span>[2690](#fn_2689) whether through
joy, of which he says, “In hope rejoicing, in tribulation
patient;” <span id="fr_2690"></span>[2691](#fn_2690) whether through
sorrow, with which he says he had great grief for his brethren; <span
id="fr_2691"></span>[2692](#fn_2691) in whatever way it endure what
bitterness and hardships soever, it is the love of God which “endureth
all things,” <span id="fr_2692"></span>[2693](#fn_2692) and which is not
shed abroad in our hearts but by the Holy Spirit given unto us. <span
id="fr_2693"></span>[2694](#fn_2693) Whereof piety makes no manner of
doubt, but, as the charity of them which holily love, so the patience of
them which piously endure, is the gift of God. For it cannot be that the
divine Scripture deceiveth or is deceived, which not only in the Old
Books hath testimonies of this thing, when it is said unto God, “My
Patience art Thou,” and, “From Him is my <span id="page_535">p.
535</span> <span id="v.viii.xxiii-Page_535"></span>patience;” <span
id="fr_2694"></span>[2695](#fn_2694) and where another prophet saith,
that we receive the spirit of fortitude; <span
id="fr_2695"></span>[2696](#fn_2695) but also in the Apostolic writings
we read, “Because unto you is given on behalf of Christ, not only to
believe on Him, but to suffer for Him.” <span
id="fr_2696"></span>[2697](#fn_2696) Therefore let not that make the
mind to be as of its own merit uplifted, wherewith he is told that he is
of Another’s mercy gifted.
