<span id="v.i-p1"></span> <span class="c7"><span id="v.i-p1.1"></span>On
Continence.</span>

<span id="v.i-p2"></span><span class="c2"><span
id="v.i-p2.1"></span>\[De Continentia.\]</span>

<span id="v.i-p3"></span>Translated by Rev. C. L. Cornish, M.A., of
Exeter College, Oxford.

<span id="v.i-p4"></span>ST. <span class="c19"><span
id="v.i-p4.1"></span> Augustin</span> speaks of his work *On Continence*
in *Ep.* 231, *Ad Darium Comitem*. \[See vol. 1. of this edition, p.
584.—P.S.\] Possidius, *Ind*. c. 10, mentions it, and it is cited in the
*Collectanea* of Bede or Florus, and by Eugypius. Erasmus is therefore
wrong in ascribing it to Hugo on the ground of the style, which is not
unlike that of the earlier discourses. It is evidently a discourse, and
probably for that reason unnoticed in the *Retractations*. The Manichæan
heresy is impugned after the manner of his early works.—(*Abridged from
Benedictine ed.* vol. vi.)
