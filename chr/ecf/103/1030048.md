<span id="iv.i.v.vii-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="iv.i.v.vii-p1.1"></span>Chapter 6.—Diversity Alone Makes a
Miracle.</span>

<span id="iv.i.v.vii-p2"></span>And who is it that restored to the
corpses their proper souls when the dead rose again, <span
id="fr_378"></span>[379](#fn_378) unless He who gives life to the flesh
in the mother’s womb, in order that they may come into being who yet are
to die? But when such things happen in a continuous kind of river of
ever-flowing succession, passing from the hidden to the visible, and
from the visible to the hidden, by a regular and beaten track, then they
are called natural; when, for the admonition of men, they are thrust in
by an unusual changeableness, then they are called miracles.
