<span id="iv.ii.lxxii-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="iv.ii.lxxii-p1.1"></span>Chapter 70.—Almsgiving Will Not Atone for
Sin Unless the Life Be Changed.</span>

<span id="iv.ii.lxxii-p2"></span>We must beware, however, lest any one
should suppose that gross sins, such as are committed by those who shall
not inherit the kingdom of God, may be daily perpetrated, and daily
atoned for by almsgiving. The life must be changed for the better; and
almsgiving must be used to propitiate God for past sins, not to purchase
impunity for the commission of such sins in the future. For He has given
no man license to sin, <span id="fr_1227"></span>[1228](#fn_1227)
although in His mercy He may blot out sins that are already committed,
if we do not neglect to make proper satisfaction.
