<span id="vi.iv.xi-p1"></span> <span id="page_685">p. 685</span> <span
id="vi.iv.xi-Page_685"></span><span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.xi-p1.1"></span>Chapter XI.—When Praying the Father, You are
Not to Be Angry with a Brother.</span>

<span id="vi.iv.xi-p2"></span>That we may not be as far from the ears of
God as we are from His precepts, <span
id="fr_8825"></span>[8826](#fn_8825.md) the memory of His precepts paves
for our prayers a way unto heaven; of which *precepts* the chief is,
that we go not up unto God’s altar <span
id="fr_8826"></span>[8827](#fn_8826.md) before we compose whatever of
discord or offence we have contracted with our brethren. <span
id="fr_8827"></span>[8828](#fn_8827.md) For what sort of deed is it to
approach the peace of God <span id="fr_8828"></span>[8829](#fn_8828.md)
without peace? the remission of debts <span
id="fr_8829"></span>[8830](#fn_8829.md) while you retain them? How will he
appease his *Father* who is angry with his *brother*, when from the
beginning “all anger” is forbidden us? <span
id="fr_8830"></span>[8831](#fn_8830.md) For even Joseph, when dismissing
his brethren for the purpose of fetching their father, said, “And be not
angry in the way.” <span id="fr_8831"></span>[8832](#fn_8831.md) He warned
*us*, to be sure, at that time (for elsewhere our Discipline is called
“the Way” <span id="fr_8832"></span>[8833](#fn_8832) .md), that when, set
in “the way” of prayer, we go not unto “the Father” with anger. After
that, the Lord, “amplifying the Law,” <span
id="fr_8833"></span>[8834](#fn_8833.md) openly adds *the prohibition of*
anger against a brother to *that of* murder. <span
id="fr_8834"></span>[8835](#fn_8834.md) Not even by an evil word does He
permit it to be vented. <span id="fr_8835"></span>[8836](#fn_8835.md) Ever
if we *must* be angry, our anger must not be maintained beyond sunset,
as the apostle admonishes. <span id="fr_8836"></span>[8837](#fn_8836.md)
But how rash is it either to pass a day without prayer, while you refuse
to make satisfaction to your brother; or else, by perseverance in anger,
to lose your prayer?
