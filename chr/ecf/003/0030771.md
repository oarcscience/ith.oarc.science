<span id="vi.iv.xxvii-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.xxvii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXVII.—Of Subjoining a
Psalm.</span>

<span id="vi.iv.xxvii-p2"></span>The more diligent in prayer are wont to
subjoin in their prayers the “Hallelujah,” <span
id="fr_8933"></span>[8934](#fn_8933) and such kind of psalms, in the
closes of which the company respond. And, of course, every institution
is excellent which, for the extolling and honouring of God, aims
unitedly to bring Him enriched prayer as a choice victim. <span
id="fr_8934"></span>[8935](#fn_8934)
