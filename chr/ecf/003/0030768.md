<span id="vi.iv.xxiv-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.xxiv-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXIV.—Of Place for Prayer.</span>

<span id="vi.iv.xxiv-p2"></span>But how “in every place,” since we are
prohibited <span id="fr_8919"></span>[8920](#fn_8919) (from praying) in
public? In every place, he means, which opportunity or even necessity,
may have rendered suitable: for that which was done by the
apostles <span id="fr_8920"></span>[8921](#fn_8920) (who, in gaol, in
the audience of the prisoners, “began praying and singing to God”) is
not considered to have been done contrary to the precept; nor yet that
which was done by Paul, <span id="fr_8921"></span>[8922](#fn_8921) who
in the ship, in presence of all, “made thanksgiving to God.” <span
id="fr_8922"></span>[8923](#fn_8922)
