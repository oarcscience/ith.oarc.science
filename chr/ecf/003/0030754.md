<span id="vi.iv.x-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.x-p1.1"></span>Chapter X.—We May Superadd Prayers of Our Own
to the Lord’s Prayer.</span>

<span id="vi.iv.x-p2"></span>Since, however, the Lord, the Foreseer of
human necessities, <span id="fr_8823"></span>[8824](#fn_8823) said
separately, after delivering His Rule of Prayer, “Ask, and ye shall
receive;” <span id="fr_8824"></span>[8825](#fn_8824) and *since* there
are petitions which are made according to the circumstances of each
individual; our additional wants have the right—after beginning with the
legitimate and customary prayers as a foundation, as it were—of rearing
an outer superstructure of petitions, yet with remembrance of *the
Master’s* precepts.
