<span id="v.v.xxx-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="v.v.xxx-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXX.—Another Passage in the Sacred
History of the Creation, Released from the Mishandling of
Hermogenes.</span>

<span id="v.v.xxx-p2"></span>The following words will in like manner
apparently corroborate the conjecture of Hermogenes, “And darkness was
upon the face of the deep, and the Spirit of God moved upon the face of
the water;” <span id="fr_6425"></span>[6426](#fn_6425.md) as if these
blended <span id="fr_6426"></span>[6427](#fn_6426.md) substances, presented
us with arguments for his massive pile *of Matter*. <span
id="fr_6427"></span>[6428](#fn_6427.md) Now, so discriminating an
enumeration of certain and distinct elements (as we have in this
passage), which severally designates “darkness,” “the deep,” “the Spirit
of God,” “the waters,” forbids the inference that anything confused or
(from such confusion) uncertain is meant. Still more, when He ascribed
to them their own places, <span id="fr_6428"></span>[6429](#fn_6428.md)
“darkness *on the face of* the deep,” “the Spirit *upon the face of* the
waters,” He repudiated all confusion in the substances; and by
demonstrating their separate position, <span
id="fr_6429"></span>[6430](#fn_6429.md) He demonstrated also their
distinction.  Most absurd, indeed, would it be that Matter, which is
introduced to our view as “without form,” should have its “formless”
condition maintained by so many words indicative of form, <span
id="fr_6430"></span>[6431](#fn_6430.md) without any intimation of what that
confused body <span id="fr_6431"></span>[6432](#fn_6431.md) is, which must
of course be supposed to be unique, <span
id="fr_6432"></span>[6433](#fn_6432.md) since it is without form. <span
id="fr_6433"></span>[6434](#fn_6433.md) For that which is without form is
uniform; but even <span id="fr_6434"></span>[6435](#fn_6434.md) that which
is without form, when it is blended together <span
id="fr_6435"></span>[6436](#fn_6435.md) from various component parts, <span
id="fr_6436"></span>[6437](#fn_6436.md) must necessarily have one outward
appearance; <span id="fr_6437"></span>[6438](#fn_6437.md) and it has not
any appearance, until it has the one appearance (which comes) from many
parts *combined*. <span id="fr_6438"></span>[6439](#fn_6438.md) Now Matter
either had those specific parts <span
id="fr_6439"></span>[6440](#fn_6439.md) within itself, from the words
indicative of which it had to be understood—I mean “darkness,” and “the
deep,” and “the Spirit,” and “the waters”—or it had them not. If it had
them, how is it introduced as being “without form?” <span
id="fr_6440"></span>[6441](#fn_6440.md) If it had them not, how does it
become known? <span id="fr_6441"></span>[6442](#fn_6441.md)
