### <span id="v.xviii.ii-p0.1"></span>Chapter I.—Occasion of the epistle.

<span id="v.xviii.ii-p1"></span><span id="v.xviii.ii-p1.1"></span><span
class="upper"><span id="v.xviii.ii-p1.2"></span>Since</span> Christ has,
to our wonder, <span id="fr_1355"></span>[1356](#fn_1355) been made
known among us to be the Son of the living God, and to have become man
in these last times by means of the Virgin Mary, <span
id="fr_1356"></span>[1357](#fn_1356) of the seed of David and Abraham,
according to the announcements previously made regarding Him and through
Him by the company of the prophets, we therefore beseech and entreat
that, by thy wisdom, Maris our friend, bishop of our native
Neapolis, <span id="fr_1357"></span>[1358](#fn_1357) which is near
Zarbus, <span id="fr_1358"></span>[1359](#fn_1358) and Eulogius, and
Sobelus the presbyter, be sent to us, that we be not destitute of such
as preside over the divine word as Moses also says, “Let the Lord God
look out a man who shall guide this people, and the congregation of the
Lord shall not be as sheep which have no shepherd.” <span
id="fr_1359"></span>[1360](#fn_1359)
