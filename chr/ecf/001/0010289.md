### <span id="v.xvii.i-p0.1"></span>Chapter I.—Reason for writing the epistle.

<span id="v.xvii.i-p1"></span><span class="upper"><span
id="v.xvii.i-p1.1"></span>Being</span> mindful of your love and of your
zeal in Christ, which ye have manifested towards us, we thought it
fitting to write to you, who display such a godly and spiritual love to
the brethren, <span id="fr_1296"></span>[1297](#fn_1296) to put you in
remembrance of your Christian course, <span
id="fr_1297"></span>[1298](#fn_1297) “that ye all speak the same thing,
being of one mind, thinking the same thing, and walking by the same rule
of faith,” <span id="fr_1298"></span>[1299](#fn_1298) as Paul admonished
you. For if there is one God of the universe, the Father of Christ, “of
whom are all things;” <span id="fr_1299"></span>[1300](#fn_1299) and one
Lord Jesus Christ, our \[Lord\], “by whom are all things;” <span
id="fr_1300"></span>[1301](#fn_1300) and also one Holy Spirit, who
wrought <span id="fr_1301"></span>[1302](#fn_1301) in Moses, and in the
prophets and apostles; and also one baptism, which is administered that
we should have fellowship with the death of the Lord; <span
id="fr_1302"></span>[1303](#fn_1302) and also one elect Church; there
ought likewise to be but one faith in respect to Christ. For “there is
one Lord, one faith, one baptism; one God and Father of all, who is
through all, and in all.” <span id="fr_1303"></span>[1304](#fn_1303)
