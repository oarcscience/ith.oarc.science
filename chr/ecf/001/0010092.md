### <span id="iv.ii.x-p0.1"></span>Chapter X.—Exhortation to the practice of virtue. <span id="fr_390"></span>[391](#fn_390.md)

<span id="iv.ii.x-p2"></span><span id="iv.ii.x-p2.1"></span><span
id="iv.ii.x-p2.2"></span><span id="iv.ii.x-p2.3"></span>Stand fast,
therefore, in these things, and follow the example of the Lord, being
firm and unchangeable in the faith, loving the brotherhood, <span
id="fr_391"></span>[392](#fn_391.md) and being attached to one another,
joined together in the truth, exhibiting the meekness of the Lord in
your intercourse with one another, and despising no one. When you can do
good, defer it not, because “alms delivers from death.” <span
id="fr_392"></span>[393](#fn_392.md) Be all of you subject one to
another <span id="fr_393"></span>[394](#fn_393.md) “having your conduct
blameless among the Gentiles,” <span id="fr_394"></span>[395](#fn_394.md)
that ye may both receive praise for your good works, and the Lord may
not be blasphemed through you. But woe to him by whom the name of the
Lord is blasphemed! <span id="fr_395"></span>[396](#fn_395.md) Teach,
therefore, sobriety to all, and manifest it also in your own conduct.
