### <span id="iv.ii.i-p0.1"></span>Chapter I.—Praise of the Philippians.

<span id="iv.ii.i-p1"></span>I have greatly rejoiced with you in our
Lord Jesus Christ, because ye have followed the example <span
id="fr_338"></span>[339](#fn_338) of true love \[as displayed by God\],
and have accompanied, as became you, those who were bound in chains, the
fitting ornaments of saints, and which are indeed the diadems of the
true elect of God and our Lord; and because the strong root of your
faith, spoken of in days <span id="fr_339"></span>[340](#fn_339) long
gone by, endureth even until now, and bringeth forth fruit to our Lord
Jesus Christ, who for our sins suffered even unto death, <span
id="iv.ii.i-p3.2"></span><span id="iv.ii.i-p3.3"></span>\[but\] “whom
God raised from the dead, having loosed the bands of the grave.” <span
id="fr_340"></span>[341](#fn_340) “In whom, though now ye see Him not,
ye believe, and believing, rejoice with joy unspeakable and full of
glory;” <span id="fr_341"></span>[342](#fn_341) into which joy many
desire to enter, knowing that “by grace ye are saved, not of
works,” <span id="fr_342"></span>[343](#fn_342) but by the will of God
through Jesus Christ.
