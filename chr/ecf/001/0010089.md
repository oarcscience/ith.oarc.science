### <span id="iv.ii.vii-p0.1"></span>Chapter VII.—Avoid the Docetæ, and persevere in fasting and prayer.

<span id="iv.ii.vii-p1"></span><span id="iv.ii.vii-p1.1"></span><span
id="iv.ii.vii-p1.2"></span><span id="iv.ii.vii-p1.3"></span>“For
whosoever does not confess that Jesus Christ has come in the flesh, is
antichrist;” <span id="fr_376"></span>[377](#fn_376) and whosoever does
not confess the testimony of the cross, <span
id="fr_377"></span>[378](#fn_377) is of the devil; and whosoever
perverts the oracles of the Lord to his own lusts, and says that there
is neither a resurrection nor a judgment, he is the first-born of
Satan. <span id="fr_378"></span>[379](#fn_378) <span
id="iv.ii.vii-p4.1"></span><span id="iv.ii.vii-p4.2"></span><span
id="iv.ii.vii-p4.3"></span><span id="iv.ii.vii-p4.4"></span><span
id="iv.ii.vii-p4.5"></span>Wherefore, forsaking the vanity of many, and
their false doctrines, let us return to the word which has been handed
down to us from <span id="fr_379"></span>[380](#fn_379) the beginning;
“watching unto prayer,” <span id="fr_380"></span>[381](#fn_380) and
persevering in fasting; beseeching in our supplications the all-seeing
God “not to lead us into temptation,” <span
id="fr_381"></span>[382](#fn_381) <span id="page_35">p. 35</span> <span
id="iv.ii.vii-Page_35"></span> as the Lord has said: “The spirit truly
is willing, but the flesh is weak.” <span
id="fr_382"></span>[383](#fn_382)
