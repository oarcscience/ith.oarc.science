### <span id="v.xv.iv-p0.1"></span>Chapter IV.—Continuation.

<span id="v.xv.iv-p1"></span>The Evangelists, too, when they declared
that the one Father was “the only true God,” <span
id="fr_1228"></span>[1229](#fn_1228.md) did not omit what concerned our
Lord, but wrote: “In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with
God, and the Word was God. The same was in the beginning with God. All
things were made by Him, and without Him was not anything made that was
made.” <span id="fr_1229"></span>[1230](#fn_1229.md) And concerning the
incarnation: “The Word,” says \[the Scripture\], “became flesh, and
dwelt among us.” <span id="fr_1230"></span>[1231](#fn_1230.md) And again:
“The book of the generation of Jesus Christ, the son of David, the son
of Abraham.” <span id="fr_1231"></span>[1232](#fn_1231.md) And those very
apostles, who said “that there is one God,” <span
id="fr_1232"></span>[1233](#fn_1232.md) said also that “there <span
id="page_111">p. 111</span> <span id="v.xv.iv-Page_111"></span> is one
Mediator between God and men.” <span
id="fr_1233"></span>[1234](#fn_1233.md) Nor were they ashamed of the
incarnation and the passion. For what says \[one\]? “The man Christ
Jesus, who gave Himself” <span id="fr_1234"></span>[1235](#fn_1234.md) for
the life and salvation of the world.
