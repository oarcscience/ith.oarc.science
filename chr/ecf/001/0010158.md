### <span id="v.iii.xii-p0.1"></span>Chapter XII.—Ye are superior to me.

<span id="v.iii.xii-p1"></span>May I enjoy you in all respects, if
indeed I be worthy! For though I am bound, I am not worthy to be
compared to any of you that are at liberty. <span
id="v.iii.xii-p1.1"></span><span id="v.iii.xii-p1.2"></span>I know that
ye are not puffed up, for ye have Jesus Christ in yourselves. And all
the more when I commend you, I know that ye cherish modesty <span
id="fr_710"></span>[711](#fn_710) of spirit; as it is written, “The
righteous man is his own accuser.” <span
id="fr_711"></span>[712](#fn_711)

<span id="v.iii.xii-p4"></span>May I enjoy you in all respects, if
indeed I be worthy! For though I am bound, I am not worthy to be
compared to one of you that are at liberty. <span
id="v.iii.xii-p4.1"></span><span id="v.iii.xii-p4.2"></span>I know that
ye are not puffed up, for ye have Jesus in yourselves. And all the more
when I commend you, I know that ye cherish modesty <span
id="fr_712"></span>[713](#fn_712) of spirit; as it is written, “The
righteous man is his own accuser;” <span
id="fr_713"></span>[714](#fn_713) and again, “Declare thou first thine
iniquities, that thou mayest be justified;” <span
id="fr_714"></span>[715](#fn_714) and again, “When ye shall have done
all things that are commanded you, say, We are unprofitable
servants;” <span id="fr_715"></span>[716](#fn_715) “for that which is
highly esteemed among men is abomination in the sight of God.” <span
id="fr_716"></span>[717](#fn_716) For says \[the Scripture\], “God be
merciful to me a sinner.” <span id="fr_717"></span>[718](#fn_717)
Therefore those great ones, Abraham and Job, <span
id="fr_718"></span>[719](#fn_718) styled themselves “dust and
ashes <span id="fr_719"></span>[720](#fn_719) before God. And David
says, “Who am I before Thee, O Lord, that Thou hast glorified me
hitherto?” <span id="fr_720"></span>[721](#fn_720) And Moses, who was
“the meekest of all men,” <span id="fr_721"></span>[722](#fn_721) saith
to God, “I am of a feeble voice, and of a slow tongue.” <span
id="fr_722"></span>[723](#fn_722) Be ye therefore also of a humble
spirit, that ye may be exalted; for “he that abaseth himself shall be
exalted, and he that exalteth himself shall be abased.” <span
id="fr_723"></span>[724](#fn_723)
