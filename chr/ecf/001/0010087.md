### <span id="iv.ii.v-p0.1"></span>Chapter V.—The duties of deacons, youths, and virgins.

<span id="iv.ii.v-p1"></span>Knowing, then, that “God is not
mocked,” <span id="fr_364"></span>[365](#fn_364.md) we ought to walk worthy
of His commandment and glory. <span id="iv.ii.v-p2.2"></span><span
id="iv.ii.v-p2.3"></span>In like manner should the deacons be blameless
before the face of His righteousness, as being the servants of God and
Christ, <span id="fr_365"></span>[366](#fn_365.md) and not of men. They
must not be slanderers, double-tongued, <span
id="fr_366"></span>[367](#fn_366.md) or lovers of money, but temperate in
all things, compassionate, industrious, walking according to the truth
of the Lord, who was the servant <span id="fr_367"></span>[368](#fn_367.md)
of all. <span id="iv.ii.v-p5.2"></span>If we please Him in this present
world, we shall receive also the future world, according as He has
promised to us that He will raise us again from the dead, and that if we
live <span id="fr_368"></span>[369](#fn_368.md) worthily of Him, “we shall
also reign together with Him,” <span id="fr_369"></span>[370](#fn_369.md)
provided only we believe. In like manner, let the young men also be
blameless in all things, being especially careful to preserve purity,
and keeping themselves in, as with a bridle, from every kind of evil.
For it is well that they should be cut off from <span
id="fr_370"></span>[371](#fn_370.md) the lusts that are in the world, since
“every lust warreth against the spirit;” <span
id="fr_371"></span>[372](#fn_371.md) and “neither fornicators, nor
effeminate, nor abusers of themselves with mankind, shall inherit the
kingdom of God,” <span id="fr_372"></span>[373](#fn_372.md) nor those who
do things inconsistent and unbecoming. Wherefore, it is needful to
abstain from all these things, being subject to the presbyters and
deacons, as unto God and Christ. <span id="iv.ii.v-p10.2"></span>The
virgins also must walk in a blameless and pure conscience.
