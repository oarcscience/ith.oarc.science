### <span id="v.ii.xiv-p0.1"></span>Chapter XIV.—Exhortations to faith and love.

<span id="v.ii.xiv-p1"></span><span id="v.ii.xiv-p1.1"></span><span
id="v.ii.xiv-p1.2"></span><span id="v.ii.xiv-p1.3"></span>None of these
things is hid from you, if ye perfectly possess that faith and love
towards Christ Jesus <span id="fr_579"></span>[580](#fn_579) which are
the beginning and the end of life. For the beginning is faith, and the
end is love. <span id="fr_580"></span>[581](#fn_580) Now these two,
being inseparably connected together, <span
id="fr_581"></span>[582](#fn_581) are of God, while all other things
which are requisite for a holy life follow after them. <span
id="v.ii.xiv-p4.1"></span><span id="v.ii.xiv-p4.2"></span>No man
\[truly\] making a profession of faith sinneth; <span
id="fr_582"></span>[583](#fn_582) nor does he that possesses love hate
any one. The tree is made manifest by its fruit; <span
id="fr_583"></span>[584](#fn_583) so those that profess themselves to be
Christians shall be recognised by their conduct. For there is not now a
demand for mere profession, <span id="fr_584"></span>[585](#fn_584) but
that a man be found continuing in the power of faith to the end.

<span id="v.ii.xiv-p8"></span><span id="v.ii.xiv-p8.1"></span><span
id="v.ii.xiv-p8.2"></span><span id="v.ii.xiv-p8.3"></span>Wherefore none
of the devices of the devil shall be hidden from you, if, like Paul, ye
perfectly possess that faith and love towards Christ <span
id="fr_585"></span>[586](#fn_585) which are the beginning and the end of
life. The beginning of life is faith, and the end is love. And these two
being inseparably connected together, do perfect the man of God; while
all other things which are requisite to a holy life follow after them.
<span id="v.ii.xiv-p9.2"></span><span id="v.ii.xiv-p9.3"></span>No man
making a profession of faith ought to sin, nor one possessed of love to
hate his brother. For He that said, “Thou shalt love the Lord thy
God,” <span id="fr_586"></span>[587](#fn_586) said also, “and thy
neighbour as thyself.” <span id="fr_587"></span>[588](#fn_587) Those
that profess themselves to be Christ’s are known not only by what they
say, but by what they practise. “For the tree is known by its
fruit.” <span id="fr_588"></span>[589](#fn_588)
