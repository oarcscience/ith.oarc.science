### <span id="v.xvii.ii-p0.1"></span>Chapter II.—Unity of the three divine persons.

<span id="v.xvii.ii-p1"></span><span id="v.xvii.ii-p1.1"></span>There is
then one God and Father, and not two or three; One who is; and there is
no other besides Him, the only true \[God\]. For “the Lord thy God,”
saith \[the Scripture\], “is one Lord.” <span
id="fr_1304"></span>[1305](#fn_1304.md) And again, “Hath not one God
created us? Have we not all one Father? <span
id="fr_1305"></span>[1306](#fn_1305.md) And there is also one Son, God the
Word. For “the only-begotten Son,” saith \[the Scripture\], “who is in
the bosom of the Father.” <span id="fr_1306"></span>[1307](#fn_1306.md) And
again, “One Lord Jesus Christ.” <span
id="fr_1307"></span>[1308](#fn_1307.md) And in another place, “What is His
name, or what His Son’s name, that we may know?” <span
id="fr_1308"></span>[1309](#fn_1308.md) And there is also one
Paraclete. <span id="fr_1309"></span>[1310](#fn_1309.md) For “there is
also,” saith \[the Scripture\], “one Spirit,” <span
id="fr_1310"></span>[1311](#fn_1310.md) since “we have been called in one
hope of our calling.” <span id="fr_1311"></span>[1312](#fn_1311.md) And
again, “We have drunk of one Spirit,” <span
id="fr_1312"></span>[1313](#fn_1312.md) with what follows. And it is
manifest that all these gifts \[possessed by believers\] “worketh one
and the self-same Spirit.” <span id="fr_1313"></span>[1314](#fn_1313.md)
There are not then either three Fathers, <span
id="fr_1314"></span>[1315](#fn_1314.md) or three Sons, or three Paracletes,
but one Father, and one Son, and one Paraclete. Wherefore also the Lord,
when He sent forth the apostles to make disciples of all nations,
commanded them to “baptize in the name of the Father, and of the Son,
and of the Holy Ghost,” <span id="fr_1315"></span>[1316](#fn_1315.md) not
unto one \[person\] having three names, nor into three \[persons\] who
became incarnate, but into three possessed of equal honour.
