### <span id="v.xiv.v-p0.1"></span>Chapter V.—Refutation of the previously mentioned errors.

<span id="v.xiv.v-p1"></span>And that He Himself is not God over all,
and the Father, but His Son, He \[shows when He\] says, “I ascend unto
my Father and your Father, and to my God and your God.” <span
id="fr_1193"></span>[1194](#fn_1193) And again, “When all things shall
be subdued unto Him, then shall He also Himself be subject unto Him that
put all things under Him, that God may be all in all.” <span
id="fr_1194"></span>[1195](#fn_1194) Wherefore it is one \[Person\] who
put all things under, and who is all in all, and another \[Person\] to
whom they were subdued, who also Himself, along with all other things,
becomes subject \[to the former\].
