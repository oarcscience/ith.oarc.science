### <span id="iv.ii.iv-p0.1"></span>Chapter IV.—Various exhortations.

<span id="iv.ii.iv-p1"></span>“But the love of money is the root of all
evils.” <span id="fr_359"></span>[360](#fn_359.md) Knowing, therefore, that
“as we brought nothing into the world, so we can carry nothing
out,” <span id="fr_360"></span>[361](#fn_360.md) let us arm ourselves with
the armour of righteousness; <span id="fr_361"></span>[362](#fn_361.md) and
let us teach, first of all, ourselves to walk in the commandments of the
Lord. <span id="iv.ii.iv-p4.2"></span><span
id="iv.ii.iv-p4.3"></span><span id="iv.ii.iv-p4.4"></span><span
id="iv.ii.iv-p4.5"></span>Next, \[teach\] your wives \[to walk\] in the
faith given to them, and in love and purity tenderly loving their own
husbands in all truth, and loving all \[others\] equally in all
chastity; and to train up their children in the knowledge and fear of
God. <span id="iv.ii.iv-p4.6"></span>Teach the widows to be discreet as
respects the faith of the Lord, praying continually <span
id="fr_362"></span>[363](#fn_362.md) for all, being far from all
slandering, evil-speaking, false-witnessing, love of money, and every
kind of evil; knowing that they are the altar <span
id="fr_363"></span>[364](#fn_363.md) of God, that He clearly perceives all
things, and that nothing is hid from Him, neither reasonings, nor
reflections, nor any one of the secret things of the heart.
