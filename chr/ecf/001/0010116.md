### <span id="iv.iv.xviii-p0.1"></span>Chapter XVIII.—The body of Polycarp is burned.

<span id="iv.iv.xviii-p1"></span><span id="iv.iv.xviii-p1.1"></span>The
centurion then, seeing the strife excited by the Jews, placed the
body <span id="fr_467"></span>[468](#fn_467) in the midst of the fire,
and consumed it. Accordingly, we afterwards took up his bones, as being
more precious than the most exquisite jewels, and more purified <span
id="fr_468"></span>[469](#fn_468) than gold, and deposited them in a
fitting place, whither, being gathered together, as opportunity is
allowed us, with joy and rejoicing, the Lord shall grant us to celebrate
the anniversary <span id="fr_469"></span>[470](#fn_469) of his
martyrdom, both in memory of those who have already finished their
course, <span id="fr_470"></span>[471](#fn_470) and for the exercising
and preparation of those yet to walk in their steps.
