<span id="iii.iv.iii.xxix-p1"></span> <span class="c12"><span
id="iii.iv.iii.xxix-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXIX</span>.—*Concerning the
Descent to Hades.*

<span id="iii.iv.iii.xxix-p2"></span>The soul <span
id="fr_2244"></span>[2245](#fn_2244.md) when it was deified descended into
Hades, in order that, just as the Sun of Righteousness <span
id="fr_2245"></span>[2246](#fn_2245.md) rose for those upon the earth, so
likewise He might bring light to those who sit under the earth in
darkness <span id="page_73b">p. 73b</span> <span
id="iii.iv.iii.xxix-Page_73b"></span>and shadow of death <span
id="fr_2246"></span>[2247](#fn_2246.md) : in order that just as He brought
the message of peace to those upon the earth, and of release to the
prisoners, and of sight to the blind <span
id="fr_2247"></span>[2248](#fn_2247.md) , and became to those who believed
the Author of everlasting salvation and to those who did not believe a
reproach of their unbelief <span id="fr_2248"></span>[2249](#fn_2248.md) ,
so He might become the same to those in Hades <span
id="fr_2249"></span>[2250](#fn_2249.md) : *That every knee should bow to
Him, of things in heaven, and things in earth and things under the
earth* <span id="fr_2250"></span>[2251](#fn_2250.md) . And thus after He
had freed those who had been bound for ages, straightway He rose again
from the dead, shewing us the way of resurrection.
