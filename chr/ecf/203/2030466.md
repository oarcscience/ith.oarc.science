<span id="v.iii.lxxxv-p1"></span> <span class="c12"><span
id="v.iii.lxxxv-p1.1"></span>Chapter LXXXIII.</span>

<span id="v.iii.lxxxv-p2"></span><span class="c12"><span
id="v.iii.lxxxv-p2.1"></span>Methodius</span>, <span
id="fr_2505"></span>[2506](#fn_2505) bishop of Olympus in Lycia and
afterwards of Tyre, composed books *Against Porphyry* written in
polished and logical style also a *Banquet of the ten virgins,* an
excellent work *On the resurrection,* against Origen and *On the
Pythonissa* and *On free will,* also against Origen. He also wrote
commentaries *On Genesis* and *On the Song of Songs* and many others
which are widely read. At the end of the recent <span id="page_379">p.
379</span> <span id="v.iii.lxxxv-Page_379"></span>persecution or, as
others affirm, in the reign of Decius and Valerianus, he was crowned
with martyrdom at Chalcis in Greece.
