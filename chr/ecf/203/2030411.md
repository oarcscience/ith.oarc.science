<span id="v.iii.xxx-p2"></span><span class="c12"><span
id="v.iii.xxx-p2.1"></span>Pinytus <span
id="fr_2427"></span>[2428](#fn_2427.md) </span> of Crete, bishop of the
city of Gnosus, wrote to Dionysius bishop of the Corinthians, an
exceedingly elegant letter in which he teaches that the people are not
to be forever fed on milk, lest by chance they be overtaken by the last
day while yet infants, but that they ought to be fed also on solid food,
that they may go on to a spiritual old age. He flourished under Marcus
Antoninus Verus and Lucius Aurelius Commodus. <span
id="fr_2428"></span>[2429](#fn_2428.md)
