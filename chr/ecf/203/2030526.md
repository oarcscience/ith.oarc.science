<span id="v.iv.vii-p2"></span><span class="c12"><span
id="v.iv.vii-p2.1"></span>Heliodorus <span
id="fr_2579"></span>[2580](#fn_2579) </span> the Presbyter wrote a book
entitled *An introductory treatise on the nature of things,* in which he
showed that the beginning of things was one, that nothing was coaeval
with God, that God was not the creator of evil, but in such wise the
creator of all good, that matter, which is used for <span
id="fr_2580"></span>[2581](#fn_2580) evil, was created by God after evil
was discovered, and that nothing material whatever can be regarded as
established in any other way than by God, and that there was no other
creator than God, who, when by His foreknowledge He knew that nature was
to be changed, <span id="fr_2581"></span>[2582](#fn_2581) warned of
punishment.
