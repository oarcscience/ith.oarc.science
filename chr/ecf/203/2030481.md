<span id="v.iii.c-p2"></span><span class="c12"><span
id="v.iii.c-p2.1"></span>Acacius</span>, <span
id="fr_2524"></span>[2525](#fn_2524) who, because he was blind in one
eye, they nicknamed “the one-eyed,” bishop of the church of Cæsarea in
Palestine, wrote seventeen volumes *On Ecclesiastes* and six of
*Miscellaneous questions,* and many treatises besides on various
subjects. He was so influential in the reign of the emperor Constantius
that he made Felix bishop of Rome in the place of Liberius.
