<span id="vi.iv.vii.xvii-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.vii.xvii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XVII.—“Their Makers are Like
Unto Them.”</span>

<span id="vi.iv.vii.xvii-p2"></span>“You will hold it reasonable for
ignorant men to be moderately indignant at these fancies.  But what must
we say to the learned, some of whom, professing themselves to be
grammarians and sophists, affirm that these acts are worthy of gods? 
For, being themselves incontinent, they lay hold of this mythical
pretext; and as imitators of the gods, <span
id="fr_1040"></span>[1041](#fn_1040.md) they practise unseemly things with
freedom.
