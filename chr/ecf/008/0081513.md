<span id="x.v.xvi-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="x.v.xvi-p1.1"></span>IX. <span id="fr_3640"></span>[3641](#fn_3640)
</span>

<span id="x.v.xvi-p3"></span>*Head of the Lord*—*His* simple Divinity;
because He is the Beginning and Creator of all things:  in Daniel. <span
id="fr_3641"></span>[3642](#fn_3641)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p5"></span>*The white hair of the Lord*, because He is
“the Ancient of Days:”  as above.

<span id="x.v.xvi-p6"></span>*The eyes of the Lord*—the Divine
inspection:  because He sees all things.  Like that in the apostle:  For
all things are naked and open in His eyes.” <span
id="fr_3642"></span>[3643](#fn_3642)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p8"></span>*The eyelids of the Lord*—hidden spiritual
mysteries in the Divine precepts.  In the Psalm:  “His eyelids question,
that is prove, the children of men.” <span
id="fr_3643"></span>[3644](#fn_3643)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p10"></span>*The smelling of the Lord*—His delight in
the prayers or works of the saints.  In Genesis:  “And the Lord smelled
an odour of sweetness.” <span id="fr_3644"></span>[3645](#fn_3644)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p12"></span>*The mouth of the Lord*—His Son, or word
*addressed* to men.  In the prophet, “The mouth of the Lord hath
spoken;” <span id="fr_3645"></span>[3646](#fn_3645) and elsewhere, “They
provoked His mouth to anger.” <span id="fr_3646"></span>[3647](#fn_3646)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p15"></span>*The tongue of the Lord*—His Holy Spirit. 
In the Psalm:  “My tongue is a pen.” <span
id="fr_3647"></span>[3648](#fn_3647)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p17"></span>*The face of the Lord*—His manifestation. 
In Exodus, “My face shall go before thee;” <span
id="fr_3648"></span>[3649](#fn_3648) and in the prophet, “The face of
the Lord divided them.” <span id="fr_3649"></span>[3650](#fn_3649)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p20"></span>*The word of the Lord*—*His* Son.  In the
Psalm:  “My heart hath uttered a good word.” <span
id="fr_3650"></span>[3651](#fn_3650)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p22"></span>*The arm of the Lord*—*His* Son, by whom
He hath wrought all His works.  In the prophet Isaiah:  “And to whom is
the arm of the Lord revealed?” <span
id="fr_3651"></span>[3652](#fn_3651)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p24"></span>*The right hand of the Lord*—that is,
*His* Son; as also above in the Psalm:  “The right hand of the Lord hath
done valiantly.” <span id="fr_3652"></span>[3653](#fn_3652)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p26"></span>*The right hand of the Lord*—*electio
omnis*.  As in Deuteronomy:  “In His right hand *is* a fiery law.” <span
id="fr_3653"></span>[3654](#fn_3653)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p28"></span>*The wings of the Lord*—Divine
protection.  In the Psalm:  “In the shadow of Thy wings will I
hope.” <span id="fr_3654"></span>[3655](#fn_3654)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p30"></span>*The shoulder of the Lord*—the Divine
power, by which He condescends to carry the feeble.  In Deuteronomy: 
“He took them up, and put them on His shoulders.” <span
id="fr_3655"></span>[3656](#fn_3655)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p32"></span>*The hand of the Lord*—Divine operation. 
<span id="page_761">p. 761</span> <span id="x.v.xvi-Page_761"></span>In
the prophet:  “Have not my hands made all these things?” <span
id="fr_3656"></span>[3657](#fn_3656)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p34"></span>*The finger of the Lord*—the Holy Spirit,
by whose operation the tables of the law in Exodus are said to have been
written; <span id="fr_3657"></span>[3658](#fn_3657) and in the Gospel: 
“If I by the finger of God cast out demons” <span
id="fr_3658"></span>[3659](#fn_3658)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p37"></span>*The fingers of the Lord*—The lawgiver
Moses, or the prophets.  In the Psalm:  “I will regard the heavens,”
that is, the books of the Law and the Prophets, “the works of Thy
fingers.” <span id="fr_3659"></span>[3660](#fn_3659)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p39"></span>*The wisdom of the Lord*—*His Son*.  In
the apostle:  “Christ the power of God, and the wisdom of God;” <span
id="fr_3660"></span>[3661](#fn_3660) and in Solomon:  “The wisdom of the
Lord reacheth from one end to the other mightily.” <span
id="fr_3661"></span>[3662](#fn_3661)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p42"></span>*The womb of the Lord*—the hidden recess
of Deity out of which He brought forth His Son.  In the Psalm:  “Out of
the womb, before Lucifer, have I borne Thee.” <span
id="fr_3662"></span>[3663](#fn_3662)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p44"></span>*The feet of the Lord*—His immoveableness
and eternity.  In the Psalm:  “And thick darkness was under His
feet.” <span id="fr_3663"></span>[3664](#fn_3663)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p46"></span>*The throne of the Lord*—angels, or
saints, or simply sovereign dominion. <span
id="fr_3664"></span>[3665](#fn_3664)   In the Psalm:  “Thy throne, O
God, is for ever and ever.” <span id="fr_3665"></span>[3666](#fn_3665)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p49"></span>*Seat*—the same as above, angels or
saints, because the Lord sits upon these.  In the Psalm:  “The Lord sat
upon His holy seat.” <span id="fr_3666"></span>[3667](#fn_3666)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p51"></span>*The descent of the Lord*—His visitation
of men.  As in Micah:  “Behold, the Lord shall come forth from His
place; He shall come down trampling under foot the ends of the
earth.” <span id="fr_3667"></span>[3668](#fn_3667)   Likewise in a bad
sense.  In Genesis:  “The Lord came down to see the tower.” <span
id="fr_3668"></span>[3669](#fn_3668)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p54"></span>*The ascent of the Lord*—the raising up of
man, who is taken from earth to heaven.  In the Psalm:  “Who ascendeth
above the heaven of heavens to the east.” <span
id="fr_3669"></span>[3670](#fn_3669)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p56"></span>*The standing of the Lord*—the patience of
the Deity, by which He bears with sinners that they may come to
repentance.  As in Habakkuk:  “He stood and measured the earth; <span
id="fr_3670"></span>[3671](#fn_3670) and in the Gospel:  “Jesus stood,
and bade him be called,” <span id="fr_3671"></span>[3672](#fn_3671) that
is, the blind man.

<span id="x.v.xvi-p59"></span>*The transition of the Lord*—His
assumption of our flesh, through which by His birth, His death, His
resurrection, His ascent into heaven, He made transitions, so to say. 
In the Song of Songs:  “Behold, He cometh, leaping upon the mountains,
bounding over the hills.” <span id="fr_3672"></span>[3673](#fn_3672)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p61"></span>*The going* <span
id="fr_3673"></span>[3674](#fn_3673) *of the Lord*—His coming or
visitation.  In the Psalm.

<span id="x.v.xvi-p63"></span>*The way of the Lord*—the operation of the
Deity.  As in Job, in speaking of the devil:  “He is the beginning of
the ways of the Lord.” <span id="fr_3674"></span>[3675](#fn_3674)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p65"></span>Again:  *The ways of the Lord*—His
precepts.  In Hosea:  “For the ways of the Lord are straight, and the
just shall walk in them.” <span id="fr_3675"></span>[3676](#fn_3675)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p67"></span>*The footsteps of the Lord*—the signs of
*His* secret operations.  As in the Psalm:  “And Thy footsteps shall not
be known.” <span id="fr_3676"></span>[3677](#fn_3676)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p69"></span>*The knowledge of the Lord*—that which
makes men to know Him.  To Abraham *He says:  “*Now I know that thou
fearest the Lord;” <span id="fr_3677"></span>[3678](#fn_3677) that is, I
have made thee to know.

<span id="x.v.xvi-p71"></span>*The ignorance of God* <span
id="fr_3678"></span>[3679](#fn_3678) is His disapproval.  In the
Gospel:  “I know you not.” <span id="fr_3679"></span>[3680](#fn_3679)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p74"></span>*The remembrance of God*—His mercy, by
which He rejects and has mercy on whom He will.  So in Genesis:  “The
Lord remembered Noah;” <span id="fr_3680"></span>[3681](#fn_3680) and in
another passage:  “The Lord hath remembered His people.” <span
id="fr_3681"></span>[3682](#fn_3681)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p77"></span>*The repentance of the Lord*—*His* change
of procedure. <span id="fr_3682"></span>[3683](#fn_3682)   As in the
book of Kings:  “It repented me that I have made Saul king.” <span
id="fr_3683"></span>[3684](#fn_3683)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p80"></span>*The anger and wrath of the Lord*—the
vengeance of the Deity upon sinners, when He bears with them with a view
to punishment, does not *at once* judge them according to *strict*
equity.  As in the Psalm:  “In His anger and in His wrath will He
trouble them.” <span id="fr_3684"></span>[3685](#fn_3684)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p82"></span>*The sleeping of the Lord*—when, in the
thoughts of some, His faithfulness is not sufficiently wakeful.  In the
Psalm:  “Awake, why sleepest Thou, O Lord?” <span
id="fr_3685"></span>[3686](#fn_3685)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p84"></span>*The watches of the Lord*—in the
guardianship of His elect He is always at hand by the presence of *His*
Deity.  In the Psalm:  “Lo! He will not slumber nor sleep.” <span
id="fr_3686"></span>[3687](#fn_3686)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p86"></span>*The sitting of the Lord*—His ruling.  In
the Psalm:  “The Lord sitteth upon His holy seat.” <span
id="fr_3687"></span>[3688](#fn_3687)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p88"></span>*The footstool of the Lord*—man assumed by
the Word; or His saints, as some think.  In the Psalm:  “Worship ye His
footstool, for it is holy.”

<span id="x.v.xvi-p89"></span>*The walking of the Lord*—the delight of
<span id="page_762">p. 762</span> <span id="x.v.xvi-Page_762"></span>the
Deity in the walks of His elect.  In the prophet:  “I will walk in them,
and will be their Lord.” <span id="fr_3688"></span>[3689](#fn_3688)

<span id="x.v.xvi-p91"></span>*The trumpet of the Lord*—His mighty
voice.  In the apostle:  “At the command, and at the voice of the
archangel, and at the trumpet of God, shall He descend from
heaven.” <span id="fr_3689"></span>[3690](#fn_3689)
