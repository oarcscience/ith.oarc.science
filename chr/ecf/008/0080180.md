<span id="vi.iii.iv.xliii-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iii.iv.xliii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XLIII.—No God But
Jehovah.</span>

<span id="vi.iii.iv.xliii-p2"></span>“Wherefore the Scripture exclaims
in name of the God of the Jews, saying, ‘Behold, behold, seeing that I
am God, and there is none else besides me, I will kill, and I will make
alive; I will smite, and I will heal; and there is none who can deliver
out of my hands.’ <span id="fr_643"></span>[644](#fn_643.md)   See
therefore how, by some ineffable virtue, the Scripture, opposing the
future errors of those who should affirm that either in heaven or on
earth there is any other god besides Him who is the God of the Jews,
decides thus:  ‘The Lord your God is one God, in heaven above, and in
the earth beneath; and besides Him there is none else.’ <span
id="fr_644"></span>[645](#fn_644.md)   How, then, hast thou dared to say
that there is any other God besides Him who is the God of the Jews?  And
again the Scripture says, ‘Behold, to the Lord thy God belong the
heaven, and the heaven of heavens, the earth, and all things that are in
them:  nevertheless I have chosen your fathers, that I might love them,
and you after them.’ <span id="fr_645"></span>[646](#fn_645.md)   Thus that
judgment is supported by the Scripture on every side, that He who
created the world is the true and only God.
