<span id="viii.ix.ii-p1"></span> <span id="page_641">p. 641</span> <span
id="viii.ix.ii-Page_641"></span><span class="c6"><span
id="viii.ix.ii-p1.1"></span>The Decrees of the Same, from the Codex of
Decrees in Sixteen Books, from the Fifth Book, and the Seventh and Ninth
Chapters.</span>

<span id="viii.ix.ii-p2"></span><span class="c1"><span
id="viii.ix.ii-p2.1"></span>I.</span>

<span id="viii.ix.ii-p3"></span>That the oblation of the altar should be
made each Lord’s day.

<span id="viii.ix.ii-p4"></span><span class="c9"><span
id="viii.ix.ii-p4.1"></span>We</span> decree that on each Lord’s day the
oblation of the altar should be made by men and women in bread and wine,
in order that by means of these sacrifices they may be released from the
burden of their sins.

<span id="viii.ix.ii-p5"></span><span class="c1"><span
id="viii.ix.ii-p5.1"></span>II.</span>

<span id="viii.ix.ii-p6"></span>That an illiterate presbyter may not
venture to celebrate mass.

<span id="viii.ix.ii-p7"></span>The sacrifice is not to be accepted from
the hand of a priest who is not competent to discharge the prayers or
actions (*actiones*) and other observances in the mass according to
religious usage.
