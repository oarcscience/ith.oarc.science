<span id="vi.iv.xi.xvi-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.xi.xvi-p1.1"></span>Chapter XVI.—Cannibalism.</span>

<span id="vi.iv.xi.xvi-p2"></span>“And the men who were with them there
for the first time were eager to do the like.  Thus, although we are
born neither good nor bad, we become *one or the other;* and having
formed habits, we are with difficulty drawn from them.  But when
irrational animals fell short, these bastard men tasted also human
flesh.  For it was not a long step to the consumption of flesh like
their own, having first tasted it in other forms.
