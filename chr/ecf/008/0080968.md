<span id="vi.iv.xiv.xxxiv-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.xiv.xxxiv-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXXIV.—Peter’s Daily
Work.</span>

<span id="vi.iv.xiv.xxxiv-p2"></span>Having said this, he dismissed the
multitudes; and according to his custom, having partaken of food with
those dearest to him, he went to rest.  And thus doing and discoursing
day by day, he strongly buttressed the law of God, challenging the
reputed gods with the reputed <span class="c9"><span
id="vi.iv.xiv.xxxiv-p2.1"></span>Genesis</span>, <span
id="fr_1137"></span>[1138](#fn_1137) and arguing that there is no
automatism, but that the world is governed according to providence.
