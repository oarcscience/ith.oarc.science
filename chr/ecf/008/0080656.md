<span id="vi.iv.v.xxiii-p1"></span><span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.v.xxiii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXIII.—Simon a Disciple of the
Baptist.</span>

<span id="vi.iv.v.xxiii-p2"></span>“But that he came to deal with the
doctrines of religion happened on this wise.  There was one John, a
day-baptist, <span id="fr_933"></span>[934](#fn_933.md) who was also,
according to the method of combination, the forerunner of our Lord
Jesus; and as the Lord had twelve apostles, bearing the number of the
twelve months of the sun, so also he, *John*, had thirty chief men,
fulfilling the monthly reckoning of the moon, in which number was a
certain woman called Helena, <span id="fr_934"></span>[935](#fn_934.md)
that not even this might be without a dispensational significance.  For
a woman, being half a man, made up the imperfect number of the
triacontad; as also in the case of the moon, whose revolution does not
make the complete course of the month. <span
id="fr_935"></span>[936](#fn_935.md)   But of these thirty, the first and
the most esteemed by John was Simon; and the reason of his not being
chief after the death of John was as follows:—
