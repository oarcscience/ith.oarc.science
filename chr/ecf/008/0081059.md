<span id="vi.iv.xix.vii-p1"></span> <span class="c1"><span
id="vi.iv.xix.vii-p1.1"></span>Chapter VII.—Peter Appeals to the Old
Testament to Prove the Unity of God.</span>

<span id="vi.iv.xix.vii-p2"></span>And Peter said:  “I shall reply
briefly to what you have said.  The law, which frequently speaks of
gods, itself says to the Jewish multitude, <span
id="fr_1268"></span>[1269](#fn_1268.md) ‘Be<span id="page_314">p.
314</span> <span id="vi.iv.xix.vii-Page_314"></span>hold, the heaven of
heavens is the Lord’s thy God, with all that therein is;’ implying that,
even if there are gods, they are under Him, that is, under the God of
the Jews.  And again: <span id="fr_1269"></span>[1270](#fn_1269.md)   ‘The
Lord thy God, He is God in heaven above, and upon the earth beneath, and
there is none other except Him.’  And somewhere else the Scripture says
to the Jewish multitude, <span id="fr_1270"></span>[1271](#fn_1270.md) ‘The
Lord your God is God of gods;’ so that, even if there are gods, they are
under the God of the Jews.  And somewhere else the Scripture says in
regard to Him, <span id="fr_1271"></span>[1272](#fn_1271.md) God, the great
and true, who regardeth not persons, nor taketh reward, He doth execute
the judgment of the fatherless and widow.’  The Scripture, in calling
the God of the Jews great and true, and executing judgment, marked out
the others as small, and not true.  But also somewhere else the
Scripture says, <span id="fr_1272"></span>[1273](#fn_1272.md) ‘As I live,
saith the Lord, there is no other God but me.  I am the first, I am
after this; except me there is no God.’  And again: <span
id="fr_1273"></span>[1274](#fn_1273.md)   ‘Thou shalt fear the Lord thy
God, and Him only shalt thou serve.’  And again: <span
id="fr_1274"></span>[1275](#fn_1274.md)   ‘Hear, O Israel, the Lord your
God is one Lord.’  And many passages besides seal with an oath that God
is one, and except Him there is no God.  Whence I wonder how, when so
many passages testify that there is one God, you say that there are
many.”
