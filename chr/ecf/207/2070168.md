<span id="iv.iv.viii.v-p2"></span>On Laconicism.  To be laconic is not
merely, as you suppose, to write few words, but to say a great deal in
few words.  Thus I call Homer very brief and Antimachus lengthy.  Why? 
Because I measure the length by the matter and not by the letters.
