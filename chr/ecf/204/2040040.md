id="v.v.v-p2.1"></span>And of the principal Councils held, during the
lifetime of Athanasius</span>.

<span id="v.v.v-p3"></span>N.B.—The names *of bishops* in italics are
open to doubt regarding their date.

<span id="v.v.v-p4"></span>An asterisk prefixed to a bishop’s name means
that he was elected when the see was not *de facto* vacant (the case of
Ursinus of Rome in 366 is not free from doubt).

<span id="v.v.v-p5"></span>† after the name of a synod indicates that
although not formally Arian it was held under the influence of Eusebius
of Nicomedia.

<span id="v.v.v-p6"></span>\* denotes a synod more or less implicated in
Arianism by its creeds (N.B. no creed at Arles or Milan, 353–355).

<span id="v.v.v-p7"></span>\*\* denote a formally Arian synod.

<span id="v.v.v-p8"></span>‘Semi-Arian’ synods are printed in italics.
