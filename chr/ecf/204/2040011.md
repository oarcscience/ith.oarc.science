<span id="v.i.vi-p1"></span> §6. <span class="c10"><span
id="v.i.vi-p1.1"></span>Patristic Monographs</span>. (a) Among the very
numerous works of this kind, the most useful for our purpose are <span
class="c10"><span id="v.i.vi-p1.2"></span>Zahn</span>, *Marcellus von
Ancyra*, 1867, very important for doctrinal history; <span
class="c10"><span id="v.i.vi-p1.3"></span>Reinkens</span>, *Hilarius von
Poitiers*, 1864; <span class="c10"><span
id="v.i.vi-p1.4"></span>Fialon</span>, *St. Basile*, 1868; <span
class="c10"><span id="v.i.vi-p1.5"></span>Ullmann</span>, *Gregorius von
Nazianz* (2 ed., 1867, part of earlier ed. trans. by Cox, 1855); <span
class="c10"><span id="v.i.vi-p1.6"></span>Krüger</span>, *Lucifer von
Calaris* (excellent, especially for the Council of 362). Under this head
may be mentioned the numerous excellent articles in *Dict. Chr. Biog*.
referred to in their respective connexions.

<span id="v.i.vi-p2"></span>(b) *On the doctrine of Athanasius*. In
addition to the works of Ceillier and Möhler referred to above, <span
class="c10"><span id="v.i.vi-p2.1"></span>Atzberger</span>, *Die
Logoslehre des h. Ath*. (Munich, 1880); <span class="c10"><span
id="v.i.vi-p2.2"></span>Voigt</span>, *Die Lehre des Athan*. (Bremen,
1861); <span class="c10"><span id="v.i.vi-p2.3"></span>Pell</span>,
*Lehre des h. Ath. von der Sünde und Erlösung* (Passau, 1888, a careful
and meritorious analysis, candidly in the interest of Roman Catholicism.
Difficulties not always faced).

<span id="v.i.vi-p3"></span>The above list of authorities, &c., does not
pretend to completeness, nor to enumerate the sources for *general*
secular or Church history. But in what relates specially to Athanasius
it is hoped that an approximation to either requirement has been
attained. Works bearing on more special points are referred to in their
proper places. In particular, a special <span class="c10"><span
id="v.i.vi-p3.1"></span>Brief Bibliography</span> is prefixed to the
<span class="c10"><span id="v.i.vi-p3.2"></span>Vita Antonii</span>.
