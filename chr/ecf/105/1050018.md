<span id="x.iii-p1"></span> <span class="c7"><span

and on the Baptism of Infants</span>

<span id="x.iii-p2"></span><span class="c9"><span
id="x.iii-p2.1"></span>by aurelius augustin, bishop of hippo;</span>

<span id="x.iii-p3"></span><span class="c44"><span
id="x.iii-p3.1"></span>In Three Books,</span>

<span id="x.iii-p4"></span><span class="c9"><span
id="x.iii-p4.1"></span>Addressed to Marcellinus, a.d. 412.</span>

<span id="x.iii-p5"></span>————————————

<span id="x.iii-p6"></span><span class="c25"><span
id="x.iii-p6.1"></span>Book I.</span>

<span id="x.iii-p7"></span>In which he refutes those who maintain, that
Adam must have died even if he had never sinned; and that nothing of his
sin has been transmitted to his posterity by natural descent. He also
shows, that death has not accrued to man by any necessity of his nature,
but as the penalty of sin; He then proceeds to prove that in Adam’s sin
his entire offspring is implicated, showing that infants are baptized
for the express purpose of receiving the remission of original sin.
