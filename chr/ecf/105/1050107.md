<span id="x.iv.xviii-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="x.iv.xviii-p1.1"></span>Chapter 18 \[XIII.\]—Perfect Human
Righteousness is Imperfect.</span>

<span id="x.iv.xviii-p2"></span>There are then on earth righteous men,
there are great men, brave, prudent, chaste, patient, <span
id="page_52">p. 52</span> <span id="x.iv.xviii-Page_52"></span>pious,
merciful, who endure all kinds of temporal evil with an even mind for
righteousness’ sake. If, however, there is truth—nay, because there is
truth—in these words, “If we say we have no sin, we deceive
ourselves,” <span id="fr_532"></span>[533](#fn_532) and in these, “In
Thy sight shall no man living be justified,” they are not without sin;
nor is there one among them so proud and foolish as not to think that
the Lord’s Prayer is needful to him, by reason of his manifold sins.
