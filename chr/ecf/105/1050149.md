<span id="x.v-p1"></span> <span class="c25"><span
id="x.v-p1.1"></span>Book III.</span>

<span id="x.v-p2"></span><span class="c9"><span id="x.v-p2.1"></span>In
the Shape of a Letter Addressed to the Same Marcellinus.</span>

<span id="x.v-p3"></span>In which Augustin refutes some errors of
Pelagius on the question of the merits of sins and the baptism of
infants—being sundry arguments of his which he had interspersed among
his expositions of Saint Paul, in opposition to original sin.

<span id="x.v-p4"></span>To his beloved son Marcellinus, Augustin,
bishop and servant of Christ and of the servants of Christ, sendeth
greeting in the Lord.
