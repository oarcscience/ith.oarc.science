<span id="v.i-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span id="v.i-p1.1"></span>A
Select Bibliography of the Pelagian Controversy.</span>

<span id="v.i-p2"></span>(*Adapted from Dr. Schaff’s Church History,
vol. iii*.)

<span id="v.i-p3"></span>————————————

<span id="v.i-p4"></span>I. <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p4.1"></span>Three</span> works of <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p4.2"></span>Pelagius</span>, printed among the works of Jerome
(Vallarsius’ edition, vol. xi.): viz., the *Expositions on Paul’s
Epistles*, written before 410 (but somewhat, especially in Romans,
interpolated); the *Epistle to Demetrias*, 413; and the *Confession of
Faith*, 417, addressed to Innocent I. Copious fragments of other works
(*On Nature, In Defence of Free Will, Chapters, Letter to Innocent*) are
found quoted in Augustin’s refutations; as also of certain works by
<span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p4.3"></span>Cœlestius</span> (e.g., his
*Definitions, Confession to Zosimus*), and of the writings of <span
class="c16"><span id="v.i-p4.4"></span>Julian.</span> Here also belong
<span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p4.5"></span>Cassian’s</span>
*Collationes Patrum*, and the works of the other semi-Pelagian writers.

<span id="v.i-p5"></span>II. <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p5.1"></span>Augustin’s</span> anti-Pelagian treatises; also his
work *On Heresies*, 88, 428; many of his letters, as e.g., those
numbered by the Benedictines, 140, 157, 178, 179, 190, 191, 193, 194;
and many of his letters, as e.g., 155, 163, 165, 168, 169, 174, 176,
293, 294, etc. <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p5.2"></span>Jerome’s</span> *Letter to Ctesiphon* (133), and
his three books of *Dialogue against the Pelagians* (vol. ii. of
Vallarsius); <span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p5.3"></span>Paulus
Orosius</span>’ *Apology against Pelagius*; <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p5.4"></span>Marius Mercator’s</span> *Commonitoria*; <span
class="c16"><span id="v.i-p5.5"></span>Prosper of Aquitaine’s</span>
writings as also those of such late writers as <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p5.6"></span>Avitus, Cæsarius, Fulgentius</span>, who bore the
brunt of the semi-Pelagian controversy.

<span id="v.i-p6"></span>III. The collections of *Acta* of the councils
and other public documents, in <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p6.1"></span> Mansi</span> and in the appendix to the
Benedictine edition of Augustin’s anti-Pelagian writings (vol.x.).

<span id="v.i-p7"></span>IV. <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p7.1"></span>Literature</span>.—A. Special works on the subject:
<span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.2"></span>Gerh. Joh. Vossius</span>,
*Hist. de Controversiis quas Pelagius ejusque reliquiæ moverunt*, 1655;
<span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.3"></span>Henr. Norisius</span>,
*Historia Pelagiana*, etc., 1673; <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p7.4"></span>Garnier</span>, *Dissert. vii. quibus integra
continuentur Pelagianorum Hist*. (in his edition of Marius Mercator, I.
113); the <span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.5"></span>Præfatio</span>
to vol. x. of the Benedictine edition of Augustin’s works; <span
class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.6"></span>Corn. Jansenius</span>,
*Augustinus sive doctrina S. Augustini, etc., adversus Pelagianos et
Massilienses*, 1640; <span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.7"></span>Jac.
Sirmond</span>, *Historia Prædestinatiana*, 1648; <span
class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.8"></span>Tillemont</span>, *Mémoires*
xiii. 1-1075; <span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.9"></span>Ch. Wilh. Fr.
Walch</span>, *Ketzerhìstorie*, Bd. iv. and v., 1770; <span
class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.10"></span>Johann Geffken</span>, *Historia
semi-pelagianismi antiquissima*, 1826; G. F. <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p7.11"></span>Wiggers</span>, *Versuch einer pragmatischen
Darstellung des Augustinismus und Pelagianismus*, 1821–1833 (Part I.
dealing with Pelagianism proper, in an E. T. by Professor Emerson,
Andover, 1840); J.L. <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p7.12"></span>Jacobi</span>, *Die Lehre des Pelagius*, 1842; P.
<span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.13"></span>Schaff</span>, *The
Pelagian Controversy*, in the *Bibliotheca Sacra*, May, 1884; <span
class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.14"></span>Theod. Gangauf</span>,
*Metaphysische Psychologie des Heiligen Augustinus*, 1852; <span
class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.15"></span>Julius Müller</span>, *Die
Christliche Lehre von der Sünde*, 5th edition 1866 (E. T. by Urwick,
Edinburgh); Do., *Der Pelagianismus*, 1854; F. <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p7.16"></span>Wörter</span>, *Der Pelagianismus* u. s. w. 1866;
<span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.17"></span> Mozley</span>, *On the
Augustinian Doctrine of Predestination*, 1855; <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p7.18"></span>Nourrisson</span>, *La philosophie de S.
Augustin*, 1868; <span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.19"></span>
Bright</span>, *Select anti-Pelagian Treatises of St. Augustine*, 1880;
<span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p7.20"></span>William Cunningham</span>
(not to be confounded with the Scotch professor of that name), *S.
Austin and his Place in the History of Christian Thought*, being the
Hulsean Lectures for 1885; <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p7.21"></span>James Field Spalding</span>, *The Teaching and
Influence of St. Augustine*, 1886; <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p7.22"></span>Hermann Reuter</span>, *Augustinische Studien*,
1887.

<span id="v.i-p8"></span>B. The appropriate section in the Histories of
Doctrine, as for example those of <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p8.1"></span>Münchner, Baumgarten-Crusius, Hagenbach</span>
(also E. T.), <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p8.2"></span>Neander</span> (also E. T.), <span
class="c16"><span id="v.i-p8.3"></span>Baur, Beck, Thomasius,
Harnack</span> (vol. ii. in the press); and in English, W. <span
class="c16"><span id="v.i-p8.4"></span>Cunningham, Shedd</span>, etc.

<span id="v.i-p9"></span>C. The appropriate chapters in the various
larger church histories, e.g., those of <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p9.1"></span> Schröckh, Fleury, Gieseler</span> (also E. T.),
<span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p9.2"></span> Neander</span> (also
E.T.), <span class="c16"><span id="v.i-p9.3"></span>Hefele</span>
(*History of the Councils*, also E. T.), <span class="c16"><span
id="v.i-p9.4"></span> Kurtz</span> (also E. T.); and in English, <span
class="c16"><span id="v.i-p9.5"></span> Schaff, Milman,
Robertson</span>, etc.
