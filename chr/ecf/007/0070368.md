### <span id="iv.i-p0.1"></span>On Easter

### <span id="iv.i-p0.2"></span>Poem of Venantius Honorius <span id="fr_2103"></span>[2104](#fn_2103) Clementianus Fortunatus, On Easter.

<span id="iv.i-p2"></span><span id="iv.i-p2.1"></span>The seasons blush
varied with the flowery, fair weather <span
id="fr_2104"></span>[2105](#fn_2104) , and the gate of the pole lies
open with greater light. His path in the heaven raises the
fire-breathing <span id="fr_2105"></span>[2106](#fn_2105) sun higher,
who goes forth on his course, <span id="fr_2106"></span>[2107](#fn_2106)
and enters the waters of the ocean. Armed with rays traversing the
liquid elements, in this <span id="fr_2107"></span>[2108](#fn_2107)
brief night he stretches out the day in a circle. The brilliant
firmament <span id="fr_2108"></span>[2109](#fn_2108) puts forth its
clear countenance, and the bright stars show their joy. The fruitful
earth pours forth its gifts with varied increase, <span
id="fr_2109"></span>[2110](#fn_2109) when the year has well returned its
vernal riches. <span id="fr_2110"></span>[2111](#fn_2110) Soft beds of
violets paint the purple plain; the meadows are green with plants, <span
id="fr_2111"></span>[2112](#fn_2111) and the plant shines with its
leaves. By degrees gleaming brightness of the flowers <span
id="fr_2112"></span>[2113](#fn_2112) comes forth; all the herbs smile
with their blossoms. <span id="fr_2113"></span>[2114](#fn_2113) The seed
being deposited, the corn springs up far and wide <span
id="fr_2114"></span>[2115](#fn_2114) in the fields, promising to be able
to overcome the hunger of the husbandman. Having deserted its stem, the
vine-shoot bewails its joys; the vine gives water only from the source
from which it is wont to give wine. The swelling bud, rising with tender
down from the back of its mother, prepares its bosom for bringing forth.
Its foliage <span id="fr_2115"></span>[2116](#fn_2115) having been torn
off in the wintry season, the verdant grove now renews its leafy
shelter. Mingled together, the willow, the fir, the hazel, the
osier, <span id="fr_2116"></span>[2117](#fn_2116) the elm, the maple,
the walnut, each tree applauds, delightful with its leaves. Hence the
bee, about to construct its comb, leaving the hive, humming over the
flowers, carries off honey with its leg. The bird which, having closed
its song, was dumb, sluggish with the wintry cold, returns to its
strains. Hence <span id="iv.i-p15.5"></span>Philomela attunes her notes
with her own instruments, <span id="fr_2117"></span>[2118](#fn_2117) and
the air becomes sweeter with the re-echoed melody. Behold, the favour of
the reviving world bears witness that all gifts have returned together
with its Lord. For in honour of Christ rising triumphant after <span
id="iv.i-p16.5"></span>His descent to the gloomy Tartarus, the grove on
every side with its leaves <span id="iv.i-p16.6"></span>expresses
approval, the plants with their flowers express approval. <span
id="fr_2118"></span>[2119](#fn_2118) The light, the heaven, the fields,
and the sea duly praise the God ascending above the stars, having
crushed the laws of hell. Behold, He who was crucified reigns as God
over all things, and all created objects offer prayer to their Creator.
Hail, festive day, to be reverenced throughout the world, <span
id="fr_2119"></span>[2120](#fn_2119) on which God has conquered hell,
and gains the stars! The changes of the year and of the months, the
bounteous light of the days, the splendour of the hours, all things with
voice applaud. <span id="fr_2120"></span>[2121](#fn_2120) Hence, in
honour of you, the wood with its foliage applauds; hence the vine, with
its silent shoot, gives thanks. Hence the thickets now resound with the
whisper of birds; amidst these the sparrow sings with exuberant <span
id="fr_2121"></span>[2122](#fn_2121) love. O Christ, Thou Saviour of the
world, merciful Creator and Redeemer, the only offspring from the
Godhead of the Father, flowing in an indescribable <span
id="fr_2122"></span>[2123](#fn_2122) manner from the heart of Thy
Parent, Thou self-existing Word, and powerful from the mouth of Thy
Father, equal to Him, of one mind with Him, His fellow, coeval with the
Father, from whom at first <span id="fr_2123"></span>[2124](#fn_2123)
the world derived its origin! Thou <span id="page_330">p. 330</span>
<span id="iv.i-Page_330"></span>dost suspend the firmament, <span
id="fr_2124"></span>[2125](#fn_2124) Thou heapest together the soil,
Thou dost pour forth the seas, by whose <span
id="fr_2125"></span>[2126](#fn_2125) government all things which are
fixed in their places flourish. Who seeing that the human race was
plunged in the depth <span id="fr_2126"></span>[2127](#fn_2126) <span
id="iv.i-p25.2"></span>of misery, that Thou mightest rescue man, didst
Thyself also become man: nor wert Thou willing only to be born with a
body, <span id="fr_2127"></span>[2128](#fn_2127) but Thou becamest
flesh, which endured to be born and to die. Thou dost undergo <span
id="fr_2128"></span>[2129](#fn_2128) funeral obsequies, Thyself the
author of life and <span id="iv.i-p27.3"></span>framer of the world,
Thou dost enter <span id="fr_2129"></span>[2130](#fn_2129) the path of
death, in giving the aid of salvation. The gloomy chains of the infernal
law yielded, and chaos feared to be pressed by the presence <span
id="fr_2130"></span>[2131](#fn_2130) of the light. Darkness perishes,
put to flight by the brightness of Christ; the thick pall of
eternal <span id="fr_2131"></span>[2132](#fn_2131) night falls. But
restore the promised <span id="fr_2132"></span>[2133](#fn_2132) pledge,
I pray Thee, O power benign! The third day has returned; arise, my
buried One; it is not becoming that Thy limbs should lie in the lowly
sepulchre, nor that worthless stones should press <span
id="iv.i-p31.3"></span>that which is the ransom <span
id="fr_2133"></span>[2134](#fn_2133) of the world. It is unworthy that a
stone should shut in with a confining <span
id="fr_2134"></span>[2135](#fn_2134) rock, and cover Him in whose
fist <span id="fr_2135"></span>[2136](#fn_2135) all things are enclosed.
Take away the linen clothes, I pray; leave the napkins in the tomb: Thou
art sufficient for us, and without Thee there is nothing. Release the
chained shades of the infernal prison, and recall to the upper
regions <span id="fr_2136"></span>[2137](#fn_2136) whatever sinks to the
lowest depths. Give back Thy face, that the world may see the light;
give back the day which flees from us at Thy death. But returning, O
holy conqueror! Thou didst altogether fill the heaven! <span
id="fr_2137"></span>[2138](#fn_2137) Tartarus lies depressed, nor
retains its rights. The ruler of the lower regions, insatiably opening
his hollow jaws, who has always been a spoiler, becomes <span
id="fr_2138"></span>[2139](#fn_2138) a prey to Thee. Thou rescuest an
innumerable people from the prison of death, and they follow in freedom
to the place whither their leader <span
id="fr_2139"></span>[2140](#fn_2139) approaches. The fierce monster in
alarm vomits forth the multitude whom he had swallowed up, and the
Lamb <span id="fr_2140"></span>[2141](#fn_2140) withdraws the sheep from
the jaw of the wolf. Hence re-seeking the tomb from the lower
regions, <span id="fr_2141"></span>[2142](#fn_2141) having resumed Thy
flesh, as a warrior Thou carriest back ample trophies to the heavens.
Those whom chaos held in punishment <span
id="fr_2142"></span>[2143](#fn_2142) he <span
id="fr_2143"></span>[2144](#fn_2143) has now restored; and those whom
death might seek, a new life holds. Oh, sacred King, behold a great part
of Thy triumph shines forth, when the sacred laver blesses pure souls! A
host, clad in white, <span id="fr_2144"></span>[2145](#fn_2144) come
forth from the bright waves, and cleanse their old <span
id="fr_2145"></span>[2146](#fn_2145) fault in a new stream. The white
garment also designates bright souls, and the shepherd has enjoyments
from the snow-white flock. The priest <span id="iv.i-p44.3"></span>Felix
is added sharing <span id="fr_2146"></span>[2147](#fn_2146) in this
reward, who wishes to give double talents to his Lord. Drawing those who
wander in Gentile error to better things, that a beast of prey may not
carry them away, He guards the fold of God. Those whom guilty Eve had
before infected, He now restores, fed <span
id="fr_2147"></span>[2148](#fn_2147) with abundant milk at the bosom of
the Church. By cultivating rustic hearts with mild conversations, a crop
is produced from a briar by the bounty of <span
id="iv.i-p46.3"></span>Felix. The Saxon, a fierce nation, living as it
were after the manner of wild beasts, when you, O sacred One! apply a
remedy, the beast of prey resembles <span
id="fr_2148"></span>[2149](#fn_2148) the sheep. About to remain with you
through an age with the return <span
id="fr_2149"></span>[2150](#fn_2149) of a hundred-fold, you fill the
barns with the produce of an abundant harvest. May this people, free
from stain, be strengthened <span id="fr_2150"></span>[2151](#fn_2150)
in your arms, and may you bear to the stars a pure pledge to God. May
one crown be bestowed on you from on high <span
id="iv.i-p49.3"></span>gained from yourself <span
id="fr_2151"></span>[2152](#fn_2151) , may another flourish gained from
your people.  

##### <span id="iv.i-p50.3"></span>General Note.

<span id="iv.i-p51"></span>A fine passage illustrating the gush of early
Christian devotion at Easter, “breaking into all the heavenly joy of the
new creation,” will be found in Professor <span
id="iv.i-p51.1"></span>Milligan’s remarkable work on <span
id="iv.i-p51.2"></span>The Resurrection of our Lord (London, Macmillan,
1884). The author is “professor of divinity and biblical criticism in
the University of Aberdeen.”
