#### <span id="viii.iii.i-p0.1"></span>Chapter I.—The Two Ways; The First Commandment.

<span id="viii.iii.i-p1"></span>1. <span class="sc"><span
id="viii.iii.i-p1.1"></span>There</span> are two ways, <span
id="fr_2369"></span>[2370](#fn_2369) one of life and one of death; <span
id="fr_2370"></span>[2371](#fn_2370) but a great difference between the
two ways. 2. The way of life, then, is this: First, thou shalt love
God <span id="fr_2371"></span>[2372](#fn_2371) who made thee; second,
thy neighbour as thyself; <span id="fr_2372"></span>[2373](#fn_2372) and
all things whatsoever thou wouldst should not occur to thee, thou also
to another do not do. <span id="fr_2373"></span>[2374](#fn_2373) 3. And
of these sayings <span id="fr_2374"></span>[2375](#fn_2374) the teaching
is this: Bless them that curse you, and pray for your enemies, and fast
for them that persecute you. <span id="fr_2375"></span>[2376](#fn_2375)
For what thank <span id="viii.iii.i-p8.4"></span>is there, if ye love
them that love you? Do not also the Gentiles do the same? <span
id="fr_2376"></span>[2377](#fn_2376) But do ye love them that hate you;
and ye shall not have an enemy. <span
id="fr_2377"></span>[2378](#fn_2377) 4. Abstain thou from fleshly and
worldly lusts. <span id="fr_2378"></span>[2379](#fn_2378) If one give
thee a blow upon thy right cheek, turn to him the other also; <span
id="fr_2379"></span>[2380](#fn_2379) and thou shalt be perfect. If one
impress thee for one mile, go with him two. <span
id="fr_2380"></span>[2381](#fn_2380) If one take away thy cloak, give
him also thy coat. <span id="fr_2381"></span>[2382](#fn_2381) If one
take from thee thine own, ask it not back, <span
id="fr_2382"></span>[2383](#fn_2382) for indeed thou art not able. 5.
Give to every one that asketh thee, and ask it not back; <span
id="fr_2383"></span>[2384](#fn_2383) for the Father willeth that to all
should be given of our own blessings (free gifts). <span
id="fr_2384"></span>[2385](#fn_2384) Happy <span
id="viii.iii.i-p17.6"></span>is he that giveth according to the
commandment; for he is guiltless. Woe to him that receiveth; for if one
having need receiveth, he is guiltless; but he <span
id="viii.iii.i-p17.7"></span>that receiveth not having need, shall pay
the penalty, why he received and for what, and, coming into straits
(confinement), <span id="fr_2385"></span>[2386](#fn_2385) he shall be
examined concerning the things which he hath done, and he shall not
escape thence until he pay back the last farthing. <span
id="fr_2386"></span>[2387](#fn_2386) 6. But also now concerning this, it
hath been said, Let thine alms sweat <span
id="fr_2387"></span>[2388](#fn_2387) in thy hands, until thou know to
whom thou shouldst give.
