id="vi.X-p1.1"></span>Book X.</span>

<span id="vi.X-p2"></span>————————————

<span id="vi.X-p3"></span>Having manifested what he was and what he is,
he shows the great fruit of his confession; and being about to examine
by what method God and the happy life may be found, he enlarges on the
nature and power of memory. Then he examines his own acts, thoughts and
affections, viewed under the threefold division of temptation; and
commemorates the Lord, the one mediator of God and men.
