<span id="vi.X.I-p1"></span><span class="c2"><span
id="vi.X.I-p1.1"></span>Chapter I.—In God Alone is the Hope and Joy of
Man.</span>

<span id="vi.X.I-p2"></span>1. <span class="c9"><span
id="vi.X.I-p2.1"></span>Let</span> me know Thee, O Thou who knowest me;
let me know Thee, as I am known. <span id="fr_812"></span>[813](#fn_812)
O Thou strength of my soul, enter into it, and prepare it for Thyself,
that Thou mayest have and hold it without “spot or wrinkle.” <span
id="fr_813"></span>[814](#fn_813) This is my hope, “therefore have I
spoken;” <span id="fr_814"></span>[815](#fn_814) and in this hope do I
rejoice, when I rejoice soberly. Other things of this life ought the
less to be sorrowed for, the more they are sorrowed for; and ought the
more to be sorrowed for, the less men do sorrow for them. For behold,
“Thou desirest truth,” <span id="fr_815"></span>[816](#fn_815) seeing
that he who does it “cometh to the light.” <span
id="fr_816"></span>[817](#fn_816) This wish I to do in confession in my
heart before Thee, and in my writing before many witnesses.
