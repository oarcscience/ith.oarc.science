<span id="iv.1.III-p1"></span> III. <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p1.1"></span>special treatises on the system of
augustin</span>.

<span id="iv.1.III-p2"></span>(1) The *Theology* of Augustin. The Church
Histories of <span class="c9"><span id="iv.1.III-p2.1"></span>Neander,
Baur, Hase</span> (his large work, 1885, vol. I. 514 sqq.), and the
Doctrine Histories of <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p2.2"></span>Neander, Gieseler, Baur, Hagenbach, Shedd,
Nitzsch, Schwane, Bach, Harnack</span> (in preparation, first vol.,
1886).

<span id="iv.1.III-p3"></span>The voluminous literature on the Pelagian
controversy embraces works of <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p3.1"></span>G. J. Voss, Garnier, Jansen</span> (died 1638;
*Augustinus*, 1640, 3 vols.; he read Aug. twenty times and revived his
system in the R. Cath. Church, but was condemned by the Pope), Cardinal
<span class="c9"><span id="iv.1.III-p3.2"></span> Noris</span>
(*Historia Pelagiana*, Florence, 1673), <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p3.3"></span>Walch</span> (*Ketzergeschichte*, vols. IV.
and V., 1768 and 1770), <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p3.4"></span>Wiggers</span> (*Augustinismus und
Pelagianismus*, 1821 and 1833), <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p3.5"></span> Bersot</span> (*Doctr. de St. Aug. sur la
liberté et la Providence*, Paris, 1843), <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p3.6"></span>Jacobi</span> (*Lehre des Pelagius*, 1842),
<span class="c9"><span id="iv.1.III-p3.7"></span>Jul. Müller</span>
(*Lehre von der Sünde*, 5th ed. 1866, Engl. transl. by Urwick, 1868),
<span class="c9"><span id="iv.1.III-p3.8"></span>Mozley</span>
(*Augustinian Doctrine of Predestination*, London, 1855, very able),
<span class="c9"><span id="iv.1.III-p3.9"></span>W. Bright</span>
(Introduction to his ed. of the Anti-Pelag. writings of Aug. Oxford
1880), and others. See <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p3.10"></span>Schaff</span>, vol III. 783-785.

<span id="iv.1.III-p4"></span><span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p4.1"></span>Van Goens</span>: *De Aur. August. apologeta,
sec. 1 de Civitate Dei*. Amstel. 1838.

<span id="iv.1.III-p5"></span><span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p5.1"></span>Nirschl</span> (Rom. Cath.). *Ursprung und
Wesen des Bösen nach der Lehre des heil. Augustin*. 1854.

<span id="iv.1.III-p6"></span><span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p6.1"></span>F. Ribbeck</span>: *Donatus und Augustinus,
oder der erste entscheidende Kampf zwischen Separatismus und Kirche*.
Elberfeld, 1858, 2 vols.

<span id="iv.1.III-p7"></span><span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p7.1"></span>Fr. Nitzsch</span>: *Augustin’s Lehre vom
Wunder*. Berlin, 1865.

<span id="iv.1.III-p8"></span><span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p8.1"></span>Gangauf</span>: *Des heil. August. Lehre von
Gott dem dreieinigen*. Augsburg, 1866. <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p8.2"></span>Emil Feuerlein</span>: *Ueber die Stellung
Augustin’s in der Kirchen=und Kulturgeschichte*, in Sybel’s “Histor.
Zeitschrift” for 1869, vol. XI. 270-313. <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p8.3"></span>Naville</span>: *Saint Augustin, Etude sur le
développement de sa pensée*. Genève, 1872. <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p8.4"></span> Ernst</span>: *Die Werke und Tugenden der
Ungläubigen nach Augustin*. Freiburg, 1872. <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p8.5"></span>Aug. Dorner</span> (son of Is. A. D.):
*Augustinus, sein theol. System und seine religionsphilosophische
Anschauung*. Berlin, 1873 (comp. his art. in Herzog’s “Encycl.” 2d ed.
I. 781-795, abridged in Schaff-Herzog I. 174 sqq.). <span
class="c9"><span id="iv.1.III-p8.6"></span>Ch. H. Collett</span>: *St.
Aug., a Sketch of his Life and Writings as affecting the controversy
with Rome*. London, 1883. <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p8.7"></span>H. Reuter</span> (Prof. of Church <span
id="page_3">p. 3</span> <span id="iv.1.III-Page_3"></span>History in
Göttingen): *Augustinische Studien*, in Brieger’s “Zeitschrift für
Kirchengeschichte,” for 1880-’86 (several articles on Aug.’s doctrine of
the church, of predestination, the kingdom of God, etc.,—very valuable).

id="iv.1.III-p9.4"></span>Prantl</span> (*Geschichte der Logik im
Abendlande*, Leipzig, 1853, I. 665-672); <span class="c9"><span
id="iv.1.III-p9.5"></span> Huber</span> (*Philosophie der Kirchenväter*,
München, 1859), and in the following special works:

id="iv.1.III-p10.1"></span>Theod. Gangauf</span>: *Metaphysische

<span class="c9"><span id="iv.1.III-p10.2"></span>T. Théry</span>: *Le

id="iv.1.III-p10.4"></span>Nourrisson</span>: *La philosophie de saint

id="iv.1.III-p10.5"></span>Reinkens</span>: *Geschichtsphilosophie des

id="iv.1.III-p10.8"></span>A. F. Hewitt</span>: *The Problems of the
Age, with Studies in St. Augustin.* New York, 1868. <span
class="c9"><span id="iv.1.III-p10.9"></span>G. Loesche</span>: *De

<span id="iv.1.III-p11"></span>(3) On Aug. as a Latin author see <span

id="iv.1.III-p11.2"></span>Ebert</span>: *Geschichte der latein.
