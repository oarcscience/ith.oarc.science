<span id="vi.XIII.XIV-p1"></span><span class="c2"><span
id="vi.XIII.XIV-p1.1"></span>Chapter XIV.—That Out of the Children of
the Night and of the Darkness, Children of the Light and of the Day are
Made.</span>

<span id="vi.XIII.XIV-p2"></span>15. And so say I too, O my God, where
art Thou? Behold where Thou art! In Thee I breathe a little, when I pour
out my soul by myself in the voice of joy and praise, the sound of him
that keeps holy-day. <span id="fr_1240"></span>[1241](#fn_1240.md) And yet
it is “cast down,” because it relapses and becomes a deep, or rather it
feels that it is still a deep. Unto it doth my faith speak which Thou
hast kindled to enlighten my feet in the night, “Why art thou cast down,
O my soul? and why art thou disquieted in me? hope thou in God;” <span
id="fr_1241"></span>[1242](#fn_1241.md) His “word is a lamp unto my
feet.” <span id="fr_1242"></span>[1243](#fn_1242.md) Hope and endure until
the night,—the mother of the wicked,—until the anger of the Lord be
overpast, <span id="fr_1243"></span>[1244](#fn_1243.md) whereof we also
were once children who were sometimes darkness, <span
id="fr_1244"></span>[1245](#fn_1244.md) the remains whereof we carry about
us in our body, dead on account of sin, <span
id="fr_1245"></span>[1246](#fn_1245.md) “until the day break and the
shadows flee away.” <span id="fr_1246"></span>[1247](#fn_1246.md) “Hope
thou in the Lord.” In the morning I shall stand in Thy presence, and
contemplate Thee; <span id="fr_1247"></span>[1248](#fn_1247.md) I shall for
ever confess unto Thee. <span id="fr_1248"></span>[1249](#fn_1248.md) In
the morning I shall stand in Thy presence, and shall see “the health of
my countenance,” <span id="fr_1249"></span>[1250](#fn_1249.md) my God, who
also shall quicken our mortal bodies by the Spirit that dwelleth in
us, <span id="fr_1250"></span>[1251](#fn_1250.md) because in mercy He was
borne over our inner darksome and floating deep. Whence we have in this
pilgrimage received “an earnest” <span
id="fr_1251"></span>[1252](#fn_1251.md) that we should now be light, whilst
as yet we “are saved by hope,” <span
id="fr_1252"></span>[1253](#fn_1252.md) and are the children of light, and
the children of the day,—not the children of the night nor of the
darkness, <span id="fr_1253"></span>[1254](#fn_1253.md) which yet we have
been. <span id="fr_1254"></span>[1255](#fn_1254.md) Betwixt whom and us, in
this as yet uncertain state of human knowledge, Thou only dividest, who
provest our hearts <span id="fr_1255"></span>[1256](#fn_1255.md) and
callest the light day, and the darkness night. <span
id="fr_1256"></span>[1257](#fn_1256.md) For who discerneth us but Thou? But
what have we that we have not received of Thee? <span
id="fr_1257"></span>[1258](#fn_1257.md) Out of the same lump vessels unto
honour, of which others also are made to dishonour. <span
id="fr_1258"></span>[1259](#fn_1258.md)
