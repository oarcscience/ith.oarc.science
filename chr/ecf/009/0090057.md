<span id="iv.iii.xlviii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xlviii-p1.1"></span>Section XLVIII.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xlviii-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_3308"></span>[3309](#fn_3308) This said Jesus, and went forth
with his disciples to a place which was called Gethsemane, <span
id="fr_3309"></span>[3310](#fn_3309) on <span
id="fr_3310"></span>[3311](#fn_3310) the side that is in the plain <span
id="fr_3311"></span>[3312](#fn_3311) of Kidron, the mountain, <span
id="fr_3312"></span>[3313](#fn_3312) the place \[2\] in which was a
garden; and he entered thither, he and his disciples.   <span
id="fr_3313"></span>[3314](#fn_3313) And Judas the \[3\] betrayer knew
that place:  for Jesus oft-times met with his disciples there.   <span
id="fr_3314"></span>[3315](#fn_3314) And when Jesus came to the place,
he said to his disciples, Sit ye here, so that I may go and pray; \[4,
5\] \[Arabic, p. 181\] and pray ye, that ye enter not into temptations. 
 <span id="fr_3315"></span>[3316](#fn_3315) And he took with him Cephas
and the sons of Zebedee together, James and John; and he began to \[6\]
look sorrowful, and to be anxious.   <span
id="fr_3316"></span>[3317](#fn_3316) And he said unto them, My soul is
distressed unto \[7\] death:  abide ye here, and watch with me.   <span
id="fr_3317"></span>[3318](#fn_3317) And he withdrew from them a little,
\[8\] the space of a stone’s throw;  <span
id="fr_3318"></span>[3319](#fn_3318) and he kneeled, <span
id="fr_3319"></span>[3320](#fn_3319) and fell on his face, and prayed,
so \[9\] that, if it *were* possible, this hour *might* pass <span
id="fr_3320"></span>[3321](#fn_3320) him.   <span
id="fr_3321"></span>[3322](#fn_3321) And he said, Father, thou art able
for all things; if thou wilt, let this cup pass me:   <span
id="fr_3322"></span>[3323](#fn_3322) but let not my will be *done*,
\[10\] but let thy will be *done*.   <span
id="fr_3323"></span>[3324](#fn_3323) And he came to his disciples, and
found them sleeping; \[11\] and he said unto Cephas, Simon, didst thou
sleep?   <span id="fr_3324"></span>[3325](#fn_3324) Could ye thus not
for one hour \[12\] watch with me?   <span
id="fr_3325"></span>[3326](#fn_3325) Watch and pray, that ye enter not
into temptations:  the spirit is \[13\] willing and ready, but the body
is weak. <span id="fr_3326"></span>[3327](#fn_3326)    <span
id="fr_3327"></span>[3328](#fn_3327) And he went again a second time,
and prayed, and said, My Father, if it is not possible with regard
to <span id="fr_3328"></span>[3329](#fn_3328) this cup that it pass,
\[14\] except I drink it, thy will be *done*.   <span
id="fr_3329"></span>[3330](#fn_3329) And he returned again, and found
his disciples sleeping, for their eyes were heavy from their grief and
anxiety; and they knew not \[15\] what to say to him.   <span
id="fr_3330"></span>[3331](#fn_3330) And he left them, and went away
again, and prayed a third \[16\] time, and said the very same word. 
 <span id="fr_3331"></span>[3332](#fn_3331) And there appeared unto him
an angel from \[17\] heaven, encouraging him.   <span
id="fr_3332"></span>[3333](#fn_3332) And being afraid <span
id="fr_3333"></span>[3334](#fn_3333) he prayed continuously: <span
id="fr_3334"></span>[3335](#fn_3334)   and his sweat <span
id="fr_3335"></span>[3336](#fn_3335) \[18\] \[Arabic, p. 182\] became
like a stream of blood, and fell on the ground.   <span
id="fr_3336"></span>[3337](#fn_3336) Then he rose from \[19\] his
prayer, and came to his disciples, and found them sleeping.   <span
id="fr_3337"></span>[3338](#fn_3337) And he \[20\] said unto them, Sleep
now, and rest:   <span id="fr_3338"></span>[3339](#fn_3338) the end hath
arrived, <span id="fr_3339"></span>[3340](#fn_3339) and the hour hath
come; \[21\] and behold, the Son of man is betrayed into the hands of
sinners.   <span id="fr_3340"></span>[3341](#fn_3340) Arise, let us
go: <span id="fr_3341"></span>[3342](#fn_3341)   for he hath come that
betrayeth me.

<span id="iv.iii.xlviii-p37"></span>\[22\]  <span
id="fr_3342"></span>[3343](#fn_3342) And while he was still speaking,
came Judas the betrayer, one of the twelve, and with him a great
multitude carrying lanterns and torches <span
id="fr_3343"></span>[3344](#fn_3343) and swords and staves, from the
chief priests and scribes and elders of the people, and with him the
footsoldiers \[23\] of the Romans. <span
id="fr_3344"></span>[3345](#fn_3344)    <span
id="fr_3345"></span>[3346](#fn_3345) And Judas the betrayer gave them a
sign, and said, He whom I shall kiss, he is he:  take him with
care, <span id="fr_3346"></span>[3347](#fn_3346) and lead him
*away*. <span id="fr_3347"></span>[3348](#fn_3347)

<span id="iv.iii.xlviii-p44"></span><span id="page_118">p. 118</span>
<span id="iv.iii.xlviii-Page_118"></span>\[24\]  <span
id="fr_3348"></span>[3349](#fn_3348) And Jesus, because he knew
everything that should come upon him, went forth \[25\] unto them. 
 <span id="fr_3349"></span>[3350](#fn_3349) And immediately Judas the
betrayer came to Jesus, and said, Peace, \[26\] my Master; and kissed
him.   <span id="fr_3350"></span>[3351](#fn_3350) And Jesus said unto
him, Judas, with a kiss betrayest \[27\] thou the Son of man?   <span
id="fr_3351"></span>[3352](#fn_3351) *Was it* for that thou camest, my
friend?  And Jesus said \[28\] to those that came unto him, Whom seek
ye?   <span id="fr_3352"></span>[3353](#fn_3352) They said unto him,
Jesus the Nazarene.  Jesus said unto them, I am he.  And Judas the
betrayer also was standing \[29\] with them.   <span
id="fr_3353"></span>[3354](#fn_3353) And when Jesus said unto them, I am
he, they retreated backward, and \[30\] fell to the ground.   <span
id="fr_3354"></span>[3355](#fn_3354) And Jesus asked them again, Whom
seek ye?  They answered, \[31\] Jesus the Nazarene.   <span
id="fr_3355"></span>[3356](#fn_3355) Jesus said unto them, I told you
that I am he:  and if ye seek \[32\] me, let these go away:  that the
word might be fulfilled which he spake,  <span
id="fr_3356"></span>[3357](#fn_3356) Of those \[33\] \[Arabic, p. 183\]
whom thou hast given me I lost not even one.   <span
id="fr_3357"></span>[3358](#fn_3357) Then came those that were with
Judas, and seized Jesus, and took him.

<span id="iv.iii.xlviii-p55"></span>\[34\]  <span
id="fr_3358"></span>[3359](#fn_3358) And when his disciples saw what
happened, they said, Our Lord, shall we smite \[35\] them with swords? 
 <span id="fr_3359"></span>[3360](#fn_3359) And Simon Cephas had a
sword, and he drew it, and struck the servant of the chief priest, and
cut off his right ear.  And the name of that servant \[36\] was
Malchus.   <span id="fr_3360"></span>[3361](#fn_3360) Jesus said unto
Cephas, The cup which my Father hath given \[37\] me, shall I not drink
it?   <span id="fr_3361"></span>[3362](#fn_3361) Put the sword into its
sheath:  for all that take with <span
id="fr_3362"></span>[3363](#fn_3362) the \[38\] sword shall die by the
sword.   <span id="fr_3363"></span>[3364](#fn_3363) Thinkest <span
id="fr_3364"></span>[3365](#fn_3364) thou that I am not able to ask of
my \[39\] Father, and he shall now raise up for me more than <span
id="fr_3365"></span>[3366](#fn_3365) twelve tribes of angels?   <span
id="fr_3366"></span>[3367](#fn_3366) Then \[40\] how should the
scriptures which were spoken be fulfilled, that thus it must be?   <span
id="fr_3367"></span>[3368](#fn_3367) Your \[41\] leave in this. <span
id="fr_3368"></span>[3369](#fn_3368)    <span
id="fr_3369"></span>[3370](#fn_3369) And he touched the ear of him that
was struck, and healed it.  And in that hour Jesus said to the
multitudes, As they come out against a thief are ye come out against me
with swords and staves to take me?  Daily was I with you in \[42\] the
temple sitting teaching, and ye took me not:   <span
id="fr_3370"></span>[3371](#fn_3370) but this is your hour, and the
power \[43\] of darkness.   <span id="fr_3371"></span>[3372](#fn_3371)
And that was, that the scriptures of the prophets might be fulfilled.

<span id="iv.iii.xlviii-p70"></span>\[44\]  <span
id="fr_3372"></span>[3373](#fn_3372) Then the disciples all left him,
and fled.  And the footsoldiers and the officers \[45\] and the
soldiers <span id="fr_3373"></span>[3374](#fn_3373) of the Jews seized
Jesus, and came.   <span id="fr_3374"></span>[3375](#fn_3374) And a
certain <span id="fr_3375"></span>[3376](#fn_3375) young man \[46\]
followed him, and he was wrapped in a towel, naked:   <span
id="fr_3376"></span>[3377](#fn_3376) and they seized him; so he \[47\]
\[Arabic, p. 184\] left the towel, and fled naked.   <span
id="fr_3377"></span>[3378](#fn_3377) Then they took Jesus, and bound
him, and brought him to Annas first; because he was the father in law of
Caiaphas, \[48\] who was chief priest that year.   <span
id="fr_3378"></span>[3379](#fn_3378) And Caiaphas was he that counselled
the Jews, that it was necessary that one man should die instead of the
people.

<span id="iv.iii.xlviii-p78"></span>\[49\]  <span
id="fr_3379"></span>[3380](#fn_3379) And Simon Cephas and one of the
other disciples followed Jesus.  And the chief \[50\] priest knew that
disciple, and he entered with Jesus into the court;  <span
id="fr_3380"></span>[3381](#fn_3380) but Simon was standing without at
the door.  And that other disciple, whom the chief priest knew, \[51\]
went out and spake unto her that kept the door, and she brought Simon
in.   <span id="fr_3381"></span>[3382](#fn_3381) And when the maid that
kept the door saw Simon, she looked stedfastly at him, and said unto
him, Art not thou also one of the disciples of this man, I mean Jesus
the \[52\] Nazarene?   <span id="fr_3382"></span>[3383](#fn_3382) But he
denied, and said, Woman, I know him not, neither know I even \[53\] what
thou sayest.   <span id="fr_3383"></span>[3384](#fn_3383) And the
servants and the soldiers rose, and made a fire in the \[54\] middle of
the court, that they might warm themselves; for it was cold.   <span
id="fr_3384"></span>[3385](#fn_3384) And when \[55\] the fire burned up,
they sat down around it.   <span id="fr_3385"></span>[3386](#fn_3385)
And Simon also came, and sat down with them to warm himself, that he
might see the end of what should happen.
