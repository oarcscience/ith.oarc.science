<span id="iv.iii.iii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.iii-p1.1"></span>Section III.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p2"></span>\[1, 2\]  <span
id="fr_233"></span>[234](#fn_233) And after that, <span
id="fr_234"></span>[235](#fn_234) the Magi came from the east to
Jerusalem,  <span id="fr_235"></span>[236](#fn_235) and said, Where is
the King of the Jews which was born?  We have seen his star in the east,
and have \[3\] come to worship him.   <span
id="fr_236"></span>[237](#fn_236) And Herod the king heard, and he was
troubled, and all \[4\] Jerusalem with him.   <span
id="fr_237"></span>[238](#fn_237) And he gathered all the chief priests
and the scribes of the \[5\] people, and asked them in what place <span
id="fr_238"></span>[239](#fn_238) the Messiah should be born.   <span
id="fr_239"></span>[240](#fn_239) They said, In Bethlehem of Judæa: 
thus it is written in the prophet,

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p10"></span>\[6\]  <span
id="fr_240"></span>[241](#fn_240) Thou also, Bethlehem of Judah,

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p12"></span>Art not contemptible among the kings of
Judah:

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p13"></span>From thee shall go forth a king,

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p14"></span>And he shall be a shepherd to my people
Israel.

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p15"></span>\[7\]  <span
id="fr_241"></span>[242](#fn_241) Then Herod called the Magi secretly,
and inquired of them the time at which \[8\] the star appeared to them. 
 <span id="fr_242"></span>[243](#fn_242) And he sent them to Bethlehem,
and said unto them, Go and search about the child diligently; and when
ye have found him, come and \[9\] make known to me, that I also may go
and worship him.   <span id="fr_243"></span>[244](#fn_243) And they,
when they \[Arabic, p. 11\] heard the king, departed; and lo, the star
which they had seen in the east went before them, until it came and
stood above the place where the child \[10, 11\] was.   <span
id="fr_244"></span>[245](#fn_244) And when they beheld the star, they
rejoiced with very great joy.   <span id="fr_245"></span>[246](#fn_245)
And they entered the house and beheld the child with Mary his mother,
and fell down worshipping him, and opened their saddle-bags and offered
to him offerings, gold and \[12\] myrrh and frankincense.   <span
id="fr_246"></span>[247](#fn_246) And they saw in a dream <span
id="fr_247"></span>[248](#fn_247) that they should not return to Herod,
and they travelled by another way in going to their country.

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p23"></span>\[13\]  <span
id="fr_248"></span>[249](#fn_248) And when they had departed, the angel
of the Lord appeared in a dream to Joseph, and said unto him, Rise, take
the child and his mother, and flee into Egypt, and be thou there until I
speak to thee; for Herod is determined to seek the child \[14\] to slay
him.   <span id="fr_249"></span>[250](#fn_249) And Joseph arose and took
the child and his mother in the night, and \[15\] fled into Egypt,
 <span id="fr_250"></span>[251](#fn_250) and remained in it until the
time of the death of Herod:  that that might be fulfilled which was said
by the Lord in the prophet, which said, From \[16\] Egypt did I call my
son.   <span id="fr_251"></span>[252](#fn_251) And Herod then, when he
saw that he was mocked of the Magi, was very angry, and sent and killed
all the male children which were in Bethlehem and all its borders, from
two years old and under, according to the time \[17\] which he had
inquired from the Magi.   <span id="fr_252"></span>[253](#fn_252) Then
was fulfilled the saying in Jeremiah the prophet, which said,

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p29"></span>\[18\]  <span
id="fr_253"></span>[254](#fn_253) A voice was heard in Ramah,

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p31"></span>Weeping and much lamentation;

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p32"></span>Rachel weeping <span
id="fr_254"></span>[255](#fn_254) for her children,

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p34"></span>And not willing to be consoled for
their loss.

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p35"></span>\[19\]  <span
id="fr_255"></span>[256](#fn_255) But when Herod the king died, the
angel of the Lord appeared in a dream to \[20\] Joseph in Egypt,  <span
id="fr_256"></span>[257](#fn_256) and said unto him, Rise and take the
child and his mother, and \[Arabic, p. 12\] go into the land of Israel;
for they have died who sought the child’s life.  \[21\]  <span
id="fr_257"></span>[258](#fn_257) And Joseph rose and took the child and
his mother, and came to the land \[22\] of Israel.   <span
id="fr_258"></span>[259](#fn_258) But when he heard that Archelaus had
become king over Judæa instead of Herod his father, he feared to go
thither; and he saw in a dream that he should <span id="page_48">p.
48</span> <span id="iv.iii.iii-Page_48"></span>\[23\] go into the land
of Galilee,  <span id="fr_259"></span>[260](#fn_259) and that he should
abide in a city called Nazareth:  that the saying in the prophet might
be fulfilled, that he should be called a Nazarene.

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p41"></span>\[24\]  <span
id="fr_260"></span>[261](#fn_260) And the child grew, and became strong
in spirit, becoming filled with wisdom; and the grace of God was upon
him.

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p43"></span>\[25\]  <span
id="fr_261"></span>[262](#fn_261) And his kinsfolk <span
id="fr_262"></span>[263](#fn_262) used to go every year to Jerusalem at
the feast of the passover.  \[26\]  <span
id="fr_263"></span>[264](#fn_263) And when he was twelve years old, they
went up according to their custom, \[27\] to the feast.   <span
id="fr_264"></span>[265](#fn_264) And when the days were accomplished,
they returned; and the child \[28\] Jesus remained in Jerusalem, and
Joseph and his mother knew not:   <span
id="fr_265"></span>[266](#fn_265) and they supposed that he was with the
children of their company.  And when they had gone one day’s journey,
they sought him beside their people and those who knew them, \[29\] and
they found him not;  <span id="fr_266"></span>[267](#fn_266) so they
returned to Jerusalem and sought him again.  \[30\]  <span
id="fr_267"></span>[268](#fn_267) And after three days they found him in
the temple, sitting in the midst of the teachers, \[31\] hearing them
and asking them *questions*;  <span id="fr_268"></span>[269](#fn_268)
and all who heard him wondered at \[32\] his wisdom and his words. 
 <span id="fr_269"></span>[270](#fn_269) And when they saw him they
wondered, and his mother said unto him, My son, why hast thou dealt with
us thus? behold, I and thy father \[33\] have been seeking for thee with
much anxiety.   <span id="fr_270"></span>[271](#fn_270) And he said unto
them, Why were \[34\] ye seeking me? know <span
id="fr_271"></span>[272](#fn_271) ye not that I must be in the house of
my Father?   <span id="fr_272"></span>[273](#fn_272) And they \[35\]
understood not the word which he spake unto them.   <span
id="fr_273"></span>[274](#fn_273) And he went down with them, and came
to Nazareth; and he was obedient to them:  and his mother used to keep
all these sayings in her heart.

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p57"></span>\[36\] \[Arabic, p. 13\]  <span
id="fr_274"></span>[275](#fn_274) And Jesus grew in his stature and
wisdom, and in grace with God and men.

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p59"></span>\[37\]  <span
id="fr_275"></span>[276](#fn_275) And in the fifteenth year of the reign
of Tiberius Cæsar, when Pontius Pilate was governor in Judæa, and one of
the four rulers, Herod, in Galilee; and Philip his brother, one of the
four rulers, in Ituræa and in the district of Trachonitis; and \[38\]
Lysanias, one of the four rulers, in Abilene;  <span
id="fr_276"></span>[277](#fn_276) in the chief-priesthood of Annas and
Caiaphas, the command <span id="fr_277"></span>[278](#fn_277) of God
went forth to John the son of Zacharias in the \[39\] desert.   <span
id="fr_278"></span>[279](#fn_278) And he came into all the region which
is about Jordan, proclaiming the \[40\] baptism of repentance unto <span
id="fr_279"></span>[280](#fn_279) the forgiveness of sins.   <span
id="fr_280"></span>[281](#fn_280) And he was preaching in the \[41\]
wilderness of Judæa, and saying,  <span
id="fr_281"></span>[282](#fn_281) Repent ye; the kingdom of heaven is
come near.  \[42\]  <span id="fr_282"></span>[283](#fn_282) This is he
that was spoken *of* in Isaiah the prophet,

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p68"></span>The voice which crieth in the desert,

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p69"></span>\[43\]  <span
id="fr_283"></span>[284](#fn_283) Prepare ye the way of the Lord,

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p71"></span>And make straight in the plain, paths
for our God.

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p72"></span>\[44\]  <span
id="fr_284"></span>[285](#fn_284) All the valleys shall become filled,

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p74"></span>And all the mountains and hills shall
become low;

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p75"></span>And the rough shall become plain, And
the difficult place, easy;

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p76"></span>\[45\]  <span
id="fr_285"></span>[286](#fn_285) And all flesh shall see the
salvation <span id="fr_286"></span>[287](#fn_286) of God.

<span id="iv.iii.iii-p79"></span>\[46\]  <span
id="fr_287"></span>[288](#fn_287) This *man* came to bear witness, that
he might bear witness to the light, that \[47\] every man might believe
through his mediation.   <span id="fr_288"></span>[289](#fn_288) He was
not the light, but that he \[48\] might bear witness to the light,
 <span id="fr_289"></span>[290](#fn_289) which was the light of truth,
that giveth light to \[49\] every man coming into the world.   <span
id="fr_290"></span>[291](#fn_290) He was in the world, and the world was
made \[50\] by him, and the world knew \[51\] him not.   <span
id="fr_291"></span>[292](#fn_291) He came unto his own, and his own
received him not.   <span id="fr_292"></span>[293](#fn_292) And those
who received him, to them gave he the power <span
id="fr_293"></span>[294](#fn_293) that they might \[52\] be sons of
God,—those which believe in his name:   <span
id="fr_294"></span>[295](#fn_294) which were born, not of blood, \[53\]
nor of the will of the flesh, nor of the will of a man, but of God. 
 <span id="fr_295"></span>[296](#fn_295) And the Word became flesh, and
took up his abode among us; and we saw his glory as the glory \[54\] of
the only *Son* from the Father, which is full of grace and equity. <span
id="fr_296"></span>[297](#fn_296)    <span
id="fr_297"></span>[298](#fn_297) John bare witness \[Arabic, p. 14\] of
him, and cried, and said, This is he that I said cometh after me and
\[55\] was before me, because he was before me. <span
id="fr_298"></span>[299](#fn_298)    <span
id="fr_299"></span>[300](#fn_299) And of his fullness received \[56\] we
all grace for grace.   <span id="fr_300"></span>[301](#fn_300) For the
law was given through the mediation of Moses, but truth and grace
were <span id="fr_301"></span>[302](#fn_301) through Jesus Christ.
