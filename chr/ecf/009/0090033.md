<span id="iv.iii.xxiv-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xxiv-p1.1"></span>Section XXIV.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xxiv-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_1678"></span>[1679](#fn_1678) And he said unto them, Verily I say
unto you, There be here now some standing that shall not taste death,
until they see the kingdom of God come <span
id="fr_1679"></span>[1680](#fn_1679) with strength,  <span
id="fr_1680"></span>[1681](#fn_1680) and the Son of man who cometh in
his kingdom.

<span id="iv.iii.xxiv-p6"></span>\[2\]  <span
id="fr_1681"></span>[1682](#fn_1681) And after six days Jesus took Simon
Cephas, and James, and John his brother, \[3\] and brought them up into
a high mountain, the three of them only.   <span
id="fr_1682"></span>[1683](#fn_1682) And while they \[4\] were praying,
Jesus changed, and became after the fashion of another person;  <span
id="fr_1683"></span>[1684](#fn_1683) and his face shone like the sun,
and his raiment was very white like the snow, and as \[5\] the light of
lightning, so that nothing on earth can whiten <span
id="fr_1684"></span>[1685](#fn_1684) like it.   <span
id="fr_1685"></span>[1686](#fn_1685) And there appeared \[6\] unto him
Moses and Elijah talking to Jesus.   <span
id="fr_1686"></span>[1687](#fn_1686) And they thought that the time
\[7\] of his decease which was to be accomplished at Jerusalem was
come.   <span id="fr_1687"></span>[1688](#fn_1687) And Simon and those
that were with him were heavy in the drowsiness of sleep; and with
effort they roused themselves, and saw his glory, and those two men that
were standing with him.  \[8\] \[Arabic, p. 93\]  <span
id="fr_1688"></span>[1689](#fn_1688) And when they began to depart from
him, Simon said unto Jesus, My \[9\] Master, it is good for us to be
here:   <span id="fr_1689"></span>[1690](#fn_1689) and if thou wilt, we
will make here three tabernacles; one for thee, and one for Moses, and
one for Elijah;  <span id="fr_1690"></span>[1691](#fn_1690) not knowing
\[10\] what he said, because of the fear which took possession of them. 
 <span id="fr_1691"></span>[1692](#fn_1691) And while he \[11\] was yet
saying that, a bright cloud overshadowed them.   <span
id="fr_1692"></span>[1693](#fn_1692) And when they saw Moses \[12\] and
Elijah that they had entered into that cloud, they feared again.   <span
id="fr_1693"></span>[1694](#fn_1693) And a voice was heard out of the
cloud, saying, This is my beloved Son, whom I have chosen; \[13\] hear
ye therefore him.   <span id="fr_1694"></span>[1695](#fn_1694) And when
this voice was heard, Jesus was found alone.  \[14\]  <span
id="fr_1695"></span>[1696](#fn_1695) And the disciples, when they heard
the voice, fell on their faces from the fear which \[15\] took hold of
them.   <span id="fr_1696"></span>[1697](#fn_1696) And Jesus came and
touched them and said, Arise, be not \[16\] afraid.   <span
id="fr_1697"></span>[1698](#fn_1697) And they lifted up their eyes, and
saw Jesus as he was.

<span id="iv.iii.xxiv-p24"></span>\[17\]  <span
id="fr_1698"></span>[1699](#fn_1698) And when they went down from the
mountain, Jesus charged them, and said unto them, Tell not what ye have
seen to any man, until the Son of man rise from \[18\] among the dead. 
 <span id="fr_1699"></span>[1700](#fn_1699) And they kept the word
within themselves, and told no man in <span id="page_81">p. 81</span>
<span id="iv.iii.xxiv-Page_81"></span>\[19\] those days what they had
seen.   <span id="fr_1700"></span>[1701](#fn_1700) And they reflected
among themselves, What is this \[20\] word which he spake unto us, I,
when I am risen from among the dead?   <span
id="fr_1701"></span>[1702](#fn_1701) And his disciples asked him, and
said, What is that which the scribes say, then, that Elijah \[21\] must
first come?   <span id="fr_1702"></span>[1703](#fn_1702) He said unto
them, Elijah cometh first to set in order everything, \[Arabic, p. 94\]
and as it was written of the Son of man, that he should suffer many
things, \[22\] and be rejected.   <span
id="fr_1703"></span>[1704](#fn_1703) But I say unto you, that Elijah is
come, and they knew him not, and have done unto him whatsoever they
desired, as it was written of him.  \[23, 24\]  <span
id="fr_1704"></span>[1705](#fn_1704) In like manner the Son of man is to
suffer of them.   <span id="fr_1705"></span>[1706](#fn_1705) Then
understood the disciples that he spake unto them concerning John the
Baptist.

<span id="iv.iii.xxiv-p33"></span>\[25\]  <span
id="fr_1706"></span>[1707](#fn_1706) And on that day whereon they came
down from the mountain, there met him a multitude of many people
standing with his disciples, and the scribes were discussing \[26\] with
them.   <span id="fr_1707"></span>[1708](#fn_1707) And the people, when
they saw Jesus, were perplexed, <span
id="fr_1708"></span>[1709](#fn_1708) and in the \[27\] midst of their
joy hastened <span id="fr_1709"></span>[1710](#fn_1709) and saluted
him.   <span id="fr_1710"></span>[1711](#fn_1710) And on that day came
certain of the Pharisees, and said unto him, Get thee out, and go hence;
for Herod seeketh \[28\] to kill thee.   <span
id="fr_1711"></span>[1712](#fn_1711) Jesus said unto them, Go ye and say
to this fox, Behold, I am casting out demons, and I heal to-day and
to-morrow, and on the third day I am perfected.  \[29\]  <span
id="fr_1712"></span>[1713](#fn_1712) Nevertheless I must be
watchful <span id="fr_1713"></span>[1714](#fn_1713) to-day and
to-morrow, and on the last day I shall depart; for it cannot be that a
prophet perish outside of Jerusalem.

<span id="iv.iii.xxiv-p42"></span>\[30\]  <span
id="fr_1714"></span>[1715](#fn_1714) And after that, there came to him a
man from that multitude, and fell upon his knees, and said unto him, I
beseech thee, my Lord, look upon my son;  <span
id="fr_1715"></span>[1716](#fn_1715) he is my \[31\] only *child*:  and
the spirit cometh upon him suddenly.   <span
id="fr_1716"></span>[1717](#fn_1716) A lunacy <span
id="fr_1717"></span>[1718](#fn_1717) hath come upon \[32\] him, and he
meeteth with evils.   <span id="fr_1718"></span>[1719](#fn_1718) And
when it cometh upon him, it beateth him about; <span
id="fr_1719"></span>[1720](#fn_1719) \[33\] and he foameth, and gnasheth
his teeth, and wasteth; <span id="fr_1720"></span>[1721](#fn_1720)  
 <span id="fr_1721"></span>[1722](#fn_1721) and many times it hath
thrown him into the water and into the fire to destroy him, and it
hardly leaveth him after \[34\] \[Arabic, p. 95\] bruising him.   <span
id="fr_1722"></span>[1723](#fn_1722) And I brought him near to thy
disciples, and they could \[35\] not heal him.   <span
id="fr_1723"></span>[1724](#fn_1723) Jesus answered and said, O
faithless and perverse generation, till when shall I be with you? and
till when shall I bear with you? bring thy son \[36\] hither.   <span
id="fr_1724"></span>[1725](#fn_1724) And he brought him unto him:  and
when the spirit saw him, immediately \[37\] it beat him about; and he
fell upon the ground, and was raging and foaming.   <span
id="fr_1725"></span>[1726](#fn_1725) And Jesus asked his father, How
long is the time during which he hath been thus?  He \[38\] said unto
him, From his youth until now.   <span
id="fr_1726"></span>[1727](#fn_1726) But, my Lord, help me wherein thou
\[39\] canst, and have mercy upon me.   <span
id="fr_1727"></span>[1728](#fn_1727) Jesus said unto him, If thou canst
believe!  All \[40\] things are possible to him that believeth.   <span
id="fr_1728"></span>[1729](#fn_1728) And immediately the father of the
child \[41\] cried out, weeping, and said, I believe, my Lord; help my
lack of faith.   <span id="fr_1729"></span>[1730](#fn_1729) And when
Jesus saw the hastening of the people, and their coming at the sound, he
rebuked that unclean spirit, and said to it, Thou dumb <span
id="fr_1730"></span>[1731](#fn_1730) spirit that speakest not, I command
\[42\] thee, <span id="fr_1731"></span>[1732](#fn_1731) come out of him,
and enter not again into him.   <span
id="fr_1732"></span>[1733](#fn_1732) And that spirit, devil, <span
id="fr_1733"></span>[1734](#fn_1733) cried out much, and bruised him,
and came out; and that child fell as one dead, and \[43\] many thought
that he had died.   <span id="fr_1734"></span>[1735](#fn_1734) But Jesus
took him by his hand, and raised him \[44\] up, and gave him to his
father; and that child was healed from that hour.   <span
id="fr_1735"></span>[1736](#fn_1735) And the people all marvelled at the
greatness of God.

<span id="iv.iii.xxiv-p65"></span>\[45\]  <span
id="fr_1736"></span>[1737](#fn_1736) And when Jesus entered into the
house, his disciples came, and asked him \[46\] privately, <span
id="fr_1737"></span>[1738](#fn_1737) and said unto him, Why were we not
able to heal him?   <span id="fr_1738"></span>[1739](#fn_1738) Jesus
said unto \[Arabic, p. 96\] them, Because of your unbelief.  Verily I
say unto you, If ye have faith as a grain of mustard seed, ye shall say
to this mountain, Remove hence; \[47\] and it shall remove; and nothing
shall overcome you.   <span id="fr_1739"></span>[1740](#fn_1739) But it
is impossible to cast out this kind by anything except by fasting and
prayer.

<span id="iv.iii.xxiv-p70"></span>\[48\]  <span
id="fr_1740"></span>[1741](#fn_1740) And when he went forth thence, they
passed through Galilee:  and he would not \[49\] that any man should
know it. <span id="fr_1741"></span>[1742](#fn_1741)    <span
id="fr_1742"></span>[1743](#fn_1742) And he taught his disciples, and
said unto them, \[50\]  <span id="fr_1743"></span>[1744](#fn_1743) Keep
ye these sayings in your ears and your hearts:  for the Son of man is to
be <span id="page_82">p. 82</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xxiv-Page_82"></span>delivered into the hands of men, and
they shall kill him; and when he is killed, he \[51\] shall rise on the
third day.   <span id="fr_1744"></span>[1745](#fn_1744) But they knew
not the word which he spake unto them, for it was concealed from them,
that they should not perceive it; and they feared to \[52\] ask him
about this word.   <span id="fr_1745"></span>[1746](#fn_1745) And they
were exceeding sorrowful.
