<span id="iv.iii.xxxix-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_2652"></span>[2653](#fn_2652) And Jesus six days before the
passover <span id="fr_2653"></span>[2654](#fn_2653) came to Bethany,
where was Lazarus, \[2\] whom Jesus raised from among the dead.   <span
id="fr_2654"></span>[2655](#fn_2654) And they made <span
id="fr_2655"></span>[2656](#fn_2655) a feast for him there:  \[3\] and
Martha was serving; while Lazarus was one of them that sat with him. 
 <span id="fr_2656"></span>[2657](#fn_2656) And \[4\] at the time of
Jesus’ being at Bethany in the house of Simon the leper,  <span
id="fr_2657"></span>[2658](#fn_2657) great multitudes of the Jews heard
that Jesus was there:  and they came, not because of Jesus alone, but
\[Arabic, p. 147\] that they might look also on Lazarus, whom he raised
from among the dead.  \[5, 6\]  <span
id="fr_2658"></span>[2659](#fn_2658) And the chief priests considered
*how* they might kill Lazarus also;  <span
id="fr_2659"></span>[2660](#fn_2659) because \[7\] many of the Jews were
going on his account, and believing in Jesus.   <span
id="fr_2660"></span>[2661](#fn_2660) And Mary took a case of the
ointment of fine nard, of great price,  <span
id="fr_2661"></span>[2662](#fn_2661) and opened it, and poured \[8\] it
out on the head of Jesus as he was reclining;  <span
id="fr_2662"></span>[2663](#fn_2662) and she anointed his feet, and
wiped them with her hair:  and the house was filled with the odour of
the ointment.  \[9, 10\]  <span id="fr_2663"></span>[2664](#fn_2663) But
Judas Iscariot, one of the disciples, he that was to betray him, said,
 <span id="fr_2664"></span>[2665](#fn_2664) Why was \[11\] not this
ointment sold for three hundred pence, and given unto the poor?   <span
id="fr_2665"></span>[2666](#fn_2665) This he said, not because of his
care for the poor, but because he was a thief, and the chest \[12\] was
with him, and what was put <span id="fr_2666"></span>[2667](#fn_2666)
into it he used to bear.   <span id="fr_2667"></span>[2668](#fn_2667)
And that displeased the rest of the disciples also within themselves,
and they said, Why went this ointment \[13\] to waste?   <span
id="fr_2668"></span>[2669](#fn_2668) It was possible that it should be
sold for much, and the poor be given \[14\] it.   <span
id="fr_2669"></span>[2670](#fn_2669) And they were angry with <span
id="fr_2670"></span>[2671](#fn_2670) Mary.   <span
id="fr_2671"></span>[2672](#fn_2671) And Jesus perceived *it*, and said
unto them,  <span id="fr_2672"></span>[2673](#fn_2672) Leave her; why
molest ye her? a good work hath she accomplished on me:   <span
id="fr_2673"></span>[2674](#fn_2673) for the \[15\] day of my burial
kept she it.   <span id="fr_2674"></span>[2675](#fn_2674) At all times
the poor are with you, and when ye \[16\] wish ye can do them a
kindness:   <span id="fr_2675"></span>[2676](#fn_2675) but I am not at
all times with you.   <span id="fr_2676"></span>[2677](#fn_2676) And for
this *cause*, when she poured <span id="fr_2677"></span>[2678](#fn_2677)
this ointment on my body, it is as if she did it for my burial, \[17\]
and anointed my body beforehand.   <span
id="fr_2678"></span>[2679](#fn_2678) And verily I say unto you, In every
place where this my gospel shall be proclaimed in all the world, what
she did shall be told for a memorial of her.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxix-p30"></span>\[18, 19\] \[Arabic, p. 148\]  <span
id="fr_2679"></span>[2680](#fn_2679) And when Jesus said that, he went
out leisurely to go to Jerusalem.   <span
id="fr_2680"></span>[2681](#fn_2680) And when he arrived at Bethphage
and at Bethany, beside the mount which is \[20\] called the mount of
Olives,  <span id="fr_2681"></span>[2682](#fn_2681) Jesus sent two of
his disciples, and he said unto them, Go \[21\] into this village that
is opposite you:   <span id="fr_2682"></span>[2683](#fn_2682) and when
ye enter it, ye shall find an ass tied, and \[22\] a colt with
him, <span id="fr_2683"></span>[2684](#fn_2683) which no man ever yet
mounted:  loose him, and bring them <span
id="fr_2684"></span>[2685](#fn_2684) unto me.   <span
id="fr_2685"></span>[2686](#fn_2685) And if any man say unto you, Why
loose ye them? say unto him thus, We \[23\] seek them for our Lord; and
straightway send them hither.   <span
id="fr_2686"></span>[2687](#fn_2686) All this was, that what was said in
the prophet might be fulfilled, which said,

<span id="iv.iii.xxxix-p39"></span>\[24\]  <span
id="fr_2687"></span>[2688](#fn_2687) Say ye unto the daughter of Zion,

<span id="iv.iii.xxxix-p41"></span>Behold, thy King cometh unto thee,

<span id="iv.iii.xxxix-p42"></span>Meek, and riding upon an ass,

<span id="iv.iii.xxxix-p43"></span>And upon a colt the foal of an ass.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxix-p44"></span>\[25\]  <span
id="fr_2688"></span>[2689](#fn_2688) And the disciples did not know this
at that time:  but after that Jesus was glorified, his disciples
remembered that these *things* were written of him, and *that* this
\[26\] they had done unto him.   <span
id="fr_2689"></span>[2690](#fn_2689) And when the two disciples went,
they found as he had \[27\] said unto them, and they did as Jesus
charged them.   <span id="fr_2690"></span>[2691](#fn_2690) And when they
loosed them, \[28\] their owners said unto them, Why loose ye them? 
 <span id="fr_2691"></span>[2692](#fn_2691) They said unto them, We seek
\[29\] them for our Lord.   <span id="fr_2692"></span>[2693](#fn_2692)
And they let them *go*.  And they brought the ass and the colt, \[30\]
and they placed on the colt their garments; and Jesus mounted it. 
 <span id="fr_2693"></span>[2694](#fn_2693) And most of the multitudes
spread their garments on the ground before him:  and others cut branches
\[31\] from the trees, and threw *them* in the way.   <span
id="fr_2694"></span>[2695](#fn_2694) And when he neared his <span
id="fr_2695"></span>[2696](#fn_2695) descent from \[Arabic, p. 149\] the
mount of Olives, all the disciples began to rejoice and to praise God
with \[32\] a loud voice for all the powers which they had seen;  <span
id="fr_2696"></span>[2697](#fn_2696) and they said, Praise in the
highest; Praise to the Son of David: Blessed is he that cometh in the
name \[33\] of the Lord;  <span id="fr_2697"></span>[2698](#fn_2697) and
blessed <span id="fr_2698"></span>[2699](#fn_2698) is the kingdom that
cometh, *that* of <span id="fr_2699"></span>[2700](#fn_2699) our father
David:   <span id="fr_2700"></span>[2701](#fn_2700) Peace in heaven, and
praise in the highest.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxix-p58"></span><span id="page_104">p. 104</span>
<span id="iv.iii.xxxix-Page_104"></span>\[34\]  <span
id="fr_2701"></span>[2702](#fn_2701) And a great multitude, that which
came to the feast, when they heard that Jesus \[35\] was coming to
Jerusalem, took young palm branches, <span
id="fr_2702"></span>[2703](#fn_2702)  <span
id="fr_2703"></span>[2704](#fn_2703) and went forth to meet him, and
cried and said, Praise:  Blessed is he that cometh in the name of the
Lord, the \[36\] King of Israel.   <span
id="fr_2704"></span>[2705](#fn_2704) Certain therefore of the Pharisees
from among the multitudes \[37\] said unto him, Our Master, rebuke thy
disciples.   <span id="fr_2705"></span>[2706](#fn_2705) He said unto
them, Verily I say unto you, If these were silent, the stones would cry
out.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxix-p64"></span>\[38, 39\]  <span
id="fr_2706"></span>[2707](#fn_2706) And when he drew near, and saw the
city, he wept over it, and said,  <span
id="fr_2707"></span>[2708](#fn_2707) Would that thou hadst known the
things that are <span id="fr_2708"></span>[2709](#fn_2708) for thy
peace, in this thy day! now that is \[40\] hidden from thine eyes. 
 <span id="fr_2709"></span>[2710](#fn_2709) There shall come unto thee
days when thine enemies \[41\] shall encompass thee, and straiten thee
from every quarter,  <span id="fr_2710"></span>[2711](#fn_2710) and
shall get possession of <span id="fr_2711"></span>[2712](#fn_2711) thee,
and thy children within thee; and they shall not leave in thee a stone
upon another; because thou knewest not the time of thy visitation.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxix-p71"></span>\[42\]  <span
id="fr_2712"></span>[2713](#fn_2712) And when he entered into Jerusalem,
the whole city was agitated, and they said, \[43\] Who is this?   <span
id="fr_2713"></span>[2714](#fn_2713) And the multitudes said, This is
Jesus, the prophet that is from Nazareth \[44\] of Galilee.   <span
id="fr_2714"></span>[2715](#fn_2714) And the multitude which was with
him bare witness that he called \[45\] Lazarus from the grave, and
raised him from among the dead.   <span
id="fr_2715"></span>[2716](#fn_2715) And for this *cause* great
multitudes went out to meet him, because they heard the sign which he
did.
