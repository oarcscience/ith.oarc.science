<span id="iv.iii.liv-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_3782"></span>[3783](#fn_3782) And while they talked
together, <span id="fr_3783"></span>[3784](#fn_3783) the evening of that
day arrived which was the First-day; and the doors were shut where the
disciples were, because of the fear of the \[2\] Jews;  <span
id="fr_3784"></span>[3785](#fn_3784) and Jesus came and stood among
them, and said unto them, Peace *be* with you:  I am he; fear not. 
 <span id="fr_3785"></span>[3786](#fn_3785) But they were agitated, and
became afraid, and supposed that they \[3\] saw a spirit.   <span
id="fr_3786"></span>[3787](#fn_3786) Jesus said unto them, Why are ye
agitated? and why do thoughts rise \[4\] \[Arabic, p. 205\] in <span
id="fr_3787"></span>[3788](#fn_3787) your hearts?   <span
id="fr_3788"></span>[3789](#fn_3788) See my hands and my feet, that I am
he:  feel me, and know that a spirit hath not flesh and bones, as ye see
me having that.  \[5\]  <span id="fr_3789"></span>[3790](#fn_3789) And
when he had said this, he shewed them his hands and his feet and his
side. <span id="fr_3790"></span>[3791](#fn_3790)   \[6\]  <span
id="fr_3791"></span>[3792](#fn_3791) And they were until this time
unbelieving, from their joy and their wonder.  He \[7\] said unto them,
Have ye anything here to eat?   <span
id="fr_3792"></span>[3793](#fn_3792) And they gave him a portion of
broiled fish and of honey. <span id="fr_3793"></span>[3794](#fn_3793)  
 <span id="fr_3794"></span>[3795](#fn_3794) And he took *it*, and ate
before them.

<span id="iv.iii.liv-p16"></span>\[8\]  <span
id="fr_3795"></span>[3796](#fn_3795) And he said unto them, These are
the sayings which I spake unto you, while I was with you, that <span
id="fr_3796"></span>[3797](#fn_3796) everything must be fulfilled, which
is written in the law of \[9\] Moses, and the prophets, and the psalms,
concerning me.   <span id="fr_3797"></span>[3798](#fn_3797) Then opened
he their \[10\] heart, that they might understand the scriptures; and he
said unto them,  <span id="fr_3798"></span>[3799](#fn_3798) Thus it is
written, and thus it is necessary <span
id="fr_3799"></span>[3800](#fn_3799) that the Messiah suffer, and rise
from among the \[11\] dead on the third day;  <span
id="fr_3800"></span>[3801](#fn_3800) and *that* repentance unto the
forgiveness of sins be preached \[12\] in his name among all the
peoples; and the beginning shall be from Jerusalem.   <span
id="fr_3801"></span>[3802](#fn_3801) And \[13\] ye shall be witnesses of
that.  And I send unto you the promise of my Father.   <span
id="fr_3802"></span>[3803](#fn_3802) And \[14\] when the disciples heard
that, they were glad.   <span id="fr_3803"></span>[3804](#fn_3803) And
Jesus said unto them again, \[15\] Peace *be* with you:  as my Father
hath sent me, I also send you.   <span
id="fr_3804"></span>[3805](#fn_3804) And when he had said this, he
breathed on them, and said unto them, Receive ye the Holy Spirit: 
\[16\]  <span id="fr_3805"></span>[3806](#fn_3805) and if ye forgive
sins to *any* man, they shall be forgiven him; and if ye retain them
against *any* man, they shall be retained.

<span id="iv.iii.liv-p28"></span>\[17\]  <span
id="fr_3806"></span>[3807](#fn_3806) But Thomas, one of the twelve,
called Thama, was not there with the disciples \[18\] when Jesus came. 
 <span id="fr_3807"></span>[3808](#fn_3807) The disciples therefore said
unto him, We have seen our Lord.  But he said unto them, If I do not see
in his hands the places of the nails, and put on them my fingers, and
pass my hand over his side, I will not believe.

<span id="iv.iii.liv-p31"></span>\[19\]  <span
id="fr_3808"></span>[3809](#fn_3808) And after eight days, on the next
First-day, the disciples were assembled again within, and Thomas with
them.  And Jesus came, the doors being shut, and stood \[20\] \[Arabic,
p. 206\] in the midst, and said unto them, Peace *be* with you.   <span
id="fr_3809"></span>[3810](#fn_3809) And he said to Thomas, Bring hither
thy finger, and behold my hands; and bring *hither* \[21\] thy hand, and
spread it on my side:  and be not unbelieving, but believing.   <span
id="fr_3810"></span>[3811](#fn_3810) Thomas \[22\] answered and said
unto him, My Lord and my God.   <span
id="fr_3811"></span>[3812](#fn_3811) Jesus said unto him, Now since thou
hast seen me, thou hast believed:  blessed are they that have not seen
me, and have believed.

<span id="iv.iii.liv-p36"></span>\[23\]  <span
id="fr_3812"></span>[3813](#fn_3812) And many other signs did Jesus
before his disciples, and they are they which \[24\] are not written in
this book:   <span id="fr_3813"></span>[3814](#fn_3813) but these
that <span id="fr_3814"></span>[3815](#fn_3814) are written also *are*
that ye may believe in Jesus the Messiah, the Son of God; and *that*
when ye have believed, ye may have in his name eternal life.

<span id="iv.iii.liv-p40"></span>\[25\]  <span
id="fr_3815"></span>[3816](#fn_3815) And after that, Jesus shewed
*himself* again to his disciples at the sea of Tiberias; \[26\] and he
shewed *himself* unto them thus.   <span
id="fr_3816"></span>[3817](#fn_3816) And there were together Simon
Cephas, and Thomas which was called Twin, <span
id="fr_3817"></span>[3818](#fn_3817) and Nathanael who was of Cana of
Galilee, \[27\] and the sons of Zebedee, and two other of the
disciples.   <span id="fr_3818"></span>[3819](#fn_3818) Simon Cephas
said unto them, I go to catch fish.  They said unto him, And we also
come with thee.  And they went forth, and went up into the boat; and in
that night they caught nothing.  \[28\]  <span
id="fr_3819"></span>[3820](#fn_3819) And when the morning arrived, Jesus
stood on the shore of the sea:  but the disciples \[29\] knew not that
it was Jesus.   <span id="fr_3820"></span>[3821](#fn_3820) And Jesus
said unto them, Children, have ye anything \[30\] to eat?  They said
unto him, No.   <span id="fr_3821"></span>[3822](#fn_3821) He said unto
them, Cast your net from the right side of the boat, and ye shall
find. <span id="fr_3822"></span>[3823](#fn_3822)   And they threw, and
they were not able \[31\] to draw the net for the abundance of the fish
that were come <span id="fr_3823"></span>[3824](#fn_3823) into it. 
 <span id="fr_3824"></span>[3825](#fn_3824) And that <span
id="page_128">p. 128</span> <span
id="iv.iii.liv-Page_128"></span>disciple whom Jesus loved said to
Cephas, This is our Lord.  And Simon, when he heard that it was our
Lord, took his tunic, and girded it on his waist (for he was \[32\]
naked), and cast himself into the sea to come to Jesus.   <span
id="fr_3825"></span>[3826](#fn_3825) But some others of the disciples
came in the boat <span id="fr_3826"></span>[3827](#fn_3826) (and they
were not far from the land, but about two \[33\] \[Arabic, p. 207\]
hundred cubits), and drew that net of fish.   <span
id="fr_3827"></span>[3828](#fn_3827) And when they went up on the \[34\]
land, they saw live coals laid, and fish laid thereon, and bread. 
 <span id="fr_3828"></span>[3829](#fn_3828) And Jesus \[35\] said unto
them, Bring of this fish which ye have now caught.   <span
id="fr_3829"></span>[3830](#fn_3829) Simon Cephas therefore went up, and
dragged the net to the land, full of great fish, a hundred and
fifty-three \[36\] fishes:  and with all this weight that net was not
rent.   <span id="fr_3830"></span>[3831](#fn_3830) And Jesus said unto
them, Come and sit down.  And no man of the disciples dared to ask him
who he was, for they knew that it was our Lord.  But he did not appear
to them in his *own* \[37, 38\] form.   <span
id="fr_3831"></span>[3832](#fn_3831) And Jesus came, and took bread and
fish, and gave unto them.   <span id="fr_3832"></span>[3833](#fn_3832)
This is the third time that Jesus appeared to his disciples, when he had
risen from among the dead.

<span id="iv.iii.liv-p59"></span>\[39\]  <span
id="fr_3833"></span>[3834](#fn_3833) And when they had breakfasted,
Jesus said to Simon Cephas, Simon, son of Jonah, lovest thou me more
than these?  He said unto him, Yea, my Lord; thou \[40\] knowest that I
love thee.   <span id="fr_3834"></span>[3835](#fn_3834) Jesus said unto
him, Feed for me my lambs.  He said unto him again a second time, Simon,
son of Jonah, lovest thou me?  He said unto him, Yea, my Lord; thou
knowest that I love thee.  He said unto him, Feed for \[41\] me my
sheep. <span id="fr_3835"></span>[3836](#fn_3835)    <span
id="fr_3836"></span>[3837](#fn_3836) He said unto him again the third
time, Simon, son of Jonah, lovest thou me?  And it grieved Cephas that
he said unto him three times, Lovest thou me?  He said unto him, My
Lord, thou knowest everything; thou knowest that I \[42\] love thee. 
 <span id="fr_3837"></span>[3838](#fn_3837) Jesus said unto him, Feed
for me my sheep. <span id="fr_3838"></span>[3839](#fn_3838)   Verily,
verily, I say unto thee, When thou wast a child, thou didst gird thy
waist for thyself, and go whither \[Arabic, p. 208\] thou wouldest:  but
when thou shalt be old, thou shalt stretch out thy hands, and another
shall gird thy waist, and take thee whither thou wouldest not.  \[43\]
 <span id="fr_3839"></span>[3840](#fn_3839) He said that to him to
explain by what death he was to glorify God.  And when he \[44\] had
said that, he said unto him, Come after me.   <span
id="fr_3840"></span>[3841](#fn_3840) And Simon Cephas turned, and saw
that disciple whom Jesus loved following him; he which at the supper
leaned <span id="fr_3841"></span>[3842](#fn_3841) on \[45\] Jesus’
breast, and said, My Lord, who is it that betrayeth thee?   <span
id="fr_3842"></span>[3843](#fn_3842) When therefore Cephas saw him, he
said to Jesus, My Lord, and this *man*, what shall be in his \[46\]
case? <span id="fr_3843"></span>[3844](#fn_3843)    <span
id="fr_3844"></span>[3845](#fn_3844) Jesus said unto him, If I will that
this *man* remain until I come, what is \[47\] that to thee? follow thou
me.   <span id="fr_3845"></span>[3846](#fn_3845) And this word spread
among the brethren, that that disciple should not die:  but Jesus said
not that he should not die; but, If I will that this *man* remain until
I come, what is that to thee?

<span id="iv.iii.liv-p73"></span>\[48\]  <span
id="fr_3846"></span>[3847](#fn_3846) This is the disciple which bare
witness of that, and wrote it:  and we know that his witness is true.
