<span id="iv.iii.x-p1"></span> <span id="page_59">p. 59</span> <span
id="iv.iii.x-Page_59"></span><span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.x-p1.1"></span>Section X.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.x-p2"></span>\[1\] \[Arabic, p. 38\]  <span
id="fr_752"></span>[753](#fn_752.md) No man can serve two masters; and that
because it is necessary that he hate one of them and love the other, and
honour one of them and despise the \[2\] other.   <span
id="fr_753"></span>[754](#fn_753.md) Ye cannot serve God and possessions. 
And because of this I say unto you, Be not anxious for yourselves, <span
id="fr_754"></span>[755](#fn_754.md) what ye shall eat and what ye shall
drink; neither for your bodies, what ye shall put on.  Is not the life
better than the food, and the body \[3\] than the raiment?   <span
id="fr_755"></span>[756](#fn_755.md) Consider the birds of the heaven,
which sow not, nor reap, nor store in barns; and *yet* your Father which
is in heaven feedeth them.  Are not ye \[4\] better than they?   <span
id="fr_756"></span>[757](#fn_756.md) Who of you when he trieth is able to
add to his stature one \[5\] cubit?   <span
id="fr_757"></span>[758](#fn_757.md) If then ye are not able for a small
*thing*, why are ye anxious about the \[6, 7\] rest?   <span
id="fr_758"></span>[759](#fn_758.md) Consider the wild lily, how it grows,
although it toils not, nor spins;  <span
id="fr_759"></span>[760](#fn_759.md) and I say unto you that Solomon in the
greatness of his glory was not clothed like one of \[8\] them.   <span
id="fr_760"></span>[761](#fn_760.md) And if God so clothe the grass of the
field, which to-day is, and to-morrow \[9\] is cast <span
id="fr_761"></span>[762](#fn_761.md) into the oven, how much more shall be
unto you, O ye of little faith!   <span
id="fr_762"></span>[763](#fn_762.md) Be not anxious, so as to say, What
shall we eat? or, What shall we drink? or, With \[10\] what shall we be
clothed?   <span id="fr_763"></span>[764](#fn_763.md) Neither let your
minds be perplexed in this:   <span id="fr_764"></span>[765](#fn_764.md)
all these *things* the nations of the world seek; and your Father which
is in heaven knoweth \[11\] your need of all these things.   <span
id="fr_765"></span>[766](#fn_765.md) Seek ye first the kingdom of God, and
his righteousness; \[12\] \[Arabic, p. 39\] and all these shall come to
you as something additional for you.   <span
id="fr_766"></span>[767](#fn_766.md) Be not anxious for the morrow; for the
morrow shall be anxious for what belongs to it.  Sufficient unto the day
is its evil.

<span id="iv.iii.x-p18"></span>\[13\]  <span
id="fr_767"></span>[768](#fn_767.md) Judge not, that ye be not judged: 
 <span id="fr_768"></span>[769](#fn_768.md) condemn <span
id="fr_769"></span>[770](#fn_769.md) not, that ye be not condemned: \[14\]
 <span id="fr_770"></span>[771](#fn_770.md) forgive, *and* it shall be
forgiven you:  release, and ye shall be released:  give, that ye may be
given *unto*; with good measure, abundant, full, they shall thrust <span
id="fr_771"></span>[772](#fn_771.md) into your \[15\] bosoms.   <span
id="fr_772"></span>[773](#fn_772.md) With what measure ye measure it shall
be measured to you.  See *to it* what ye hear:  with what measure ye
measure it shall be measured to you; and ye \[16\] shall be given more. 
 <span id="fr_773"></span>[774](#fn_773.md) I say unto those that hear, He
that hath shall be given *unto*; and he that hath not, that which he
regards <span id="fr_774"></span>[775](#fn_774.md) as his shall be taken
from him.

<span id="iv.iii.x-p27"></span>\[17\]  <span
id="fr_775"></span>[776](#fn_775.md) And he spake unto them a parable, Can
a blind man haply guide a blind man? \[18\] shall <span
id="fr_776"></span>[777](#fn_776.md) they not both fall into a hollow? 
 <span id="fr_777"></span>[778](#fn_777.md) A disciple is not better than
his master; \[19\] every perfect man shall be as his master.   <span
id="fr_778"></span>[779](#fn_778.md) Why lookest thou at the mote which is
in the eye of thy brother, but considerest not the column that is in
thine *own* eye?  \[20\]  <span id="fr_779"></span>[780](#fn_779.md) Or how
canst thou say to thy brother, Brother, I will take out the mote from
thine eye; and the column which is in thine eye thou seest not?  Thou
hypocrite, take out first the column from thine eye; and then shalt thou
see to take out the mote from the eye of thy brother.

<span id="iv.iii.x-p33"></span>\[21\]  <span
id="fr_780"></span>[781](#fn_780.md) Give not that which is holy unto the
dogs, neither cast your pearls before the swine, lest they trample them
with their feet, and return and wound you.

<span id="iv.iii.x-p35"></span>\[22\]  <span
id="fr_781"></span>[782](#fn_781.md) And he said unto them, Who of you,
that hath a friend, goeth to him at midnight, \[23\] and saith unto him,
My friend, lend me three loaves;  <span
id="fr_782"></span>[783](#fn_782.md) for a friend hath come \[24\] to me
from a journey, and I have nothing to offer to him:   <span
id="fr_783"></span>[784](#fn_783.md) and that friend shall \[Arabic, p.
40\] answer him from within, and say unto him, Trouble me not; for the
door is shut, and my children are with me in bed, and I cannot rise and
give thee?  \[25\]  <span id="fr_784"></span>[785](#fn_784.md) And verily I
say unto you, If he will not give him because of friendship, yet because
\[26\] of *his* importunity he will rise and give him what he seeketh. 
 <span id="fr_785"></span>[786](#fn_785.md) And I also say unto you, Ask,
*and* ye shall be given *unto*; seek, *and* ye shall find; knock, *and*
it shall be \[27\] opened unto you.   <span
id="fr_786"></span>[787](#fn_786.md) Every one that asketh receiveth, and
he that seeketh findeth, and \[28\] he that knocketh, it shall be opened
to him.   <span id="fr_787"></span>[788](#fn_787.md) What father of you,
shall his son ask for bread—will he, think you, give him a stone?  <span
id="fr_788"></span>[789](#fn_788.md) and if he ask of him a fish, will he,
\[29\] think you,  <span id="fr_789"></span>[790](#fn_789.md) instead of
the fish give him a serpent? and if he ask him for an egg, will <span
id="page_60">p. 60</span> <span id="iv.iii.x-Page_60"></span>\[30\] he,
think you, extend to him a scorpion?   <span
id="fr_790"></span>[791](#fn_790.md) If ye then, *although* being evil,
know the gifts *which are* good, and give them to your children, how
much more shall your \[31\] Father which is in heaven give the Holy
Spirit to them that ask him?   <span id="fr_791"></span>[792](#fn_791.md)
Whatsoever ye would that men should do to you, do ye even so to them: 
this is the law and the prophets.

<span id="iv.iii.x-p47"></span>\[32\]  <span
id="fr_792"></span>[793](#fn_792.md) Enter <span
id="fr_793"></span>[794](#fn_793.md) ye by the narrow gate; for the wide
gate and the broad way lead to destruction, \[33\] and many they be
which go therein.   <span id="fr_794"></span>[795](#fn_794.md) How narrow
is the gate and straitened the way leading to life! and few be they that
find it.

<span id="iv.iii.x-p51"></span>\[34\]  <span
id="fr_795"></span>[796](#fn_795.md) Beware of false prophets, which come
to you in sheep’s <span id="fr_796"></span>[797](#fn_796.md) clothing,
while within \[35\] they are ravening wolves.   <span
id="fr_797"></span>[798](#fn_797.md) But by their fruits ye shall know
them.   <span id="fr_798"></span>[799](#fn_798.md) For every tree is known
by its fruit.  For figs are not gathered <span
id="fr_799"></span>[800](#fn_799.md) of thorns, neither are grapes plucked
of \[36\] briers.   <span id="fr_800"></span>[801](#fn_800.md) Even so
every good tree bringeth forth good fruit, but the evil tree bringeth
\[37\] \[Arabic, p. 41\] forth evil fruit.   <span
id="fr_801"></span>[802](#fn_801.md) The good tree cannot bring forth evil
fruit, neither *can* the \[38\] evil tree bring forth good fruit. 
 <span id="fr_802"></span>[803](#fn_802.md) The good man from the good
treasures that are in his heart bringeth forth good *things*; and the
evil man from the evil treasures that are in his heart bringeth forth
evil *things*:  and from the overflowings of the \[39\] heart the lips
speak.   <span id="fr_803"></span>[804](#fn_803.md) Every tree that beareth
not good fruit is cut *down* and cast \[40, 41\] into the fire.   <span
id="fr_804"></span>[805](#fn_804.md) Therefore by their fruits ye shall
know them.   <span id="fr_805"></span>[806](#fn_805.md) Not all that say
unto me, My Lord, my Lord, shall enter the kingdom of the heavens; but
he that doeth \[42\] the will of my Father which is in heaven.   <span
id="fr_806"></span>[807](#fn_806.md) Many shall say unto me in that day, My
Lord, my Lord, did we not prophesy in thy name, and in thy name cast out
\[43\] devils, and in thy name do many powers?   <span
id="fr_807"></span>[808](#fn_807.md) Then shall I say unto them, I never
\[44\] knew you:  depart from me, ye servants of iniquity.   <span
id="fr_808"></span>[809](#fn_808.md) Every man that cometh unto \[45\] me,
and heareth my sayings, and doeth them, I will shew you to what he is
like:   <span id="fr_809"></span>[810](#fn_809.md) he is like the wise man
which built a house, and digged and went deep, and laid the \[46\]
foundations on a rock:   <span id="fr_810"></span>[811](#fn_810.md) and the
rain came down, and the rivers overflowed, and the winds blew, and shook
that house, and it fell not:  for its foundation was laid on \[47\]
rocks.   <span id="fr_811"></span>[812](#fn_811.md) And every one that
heareth these my words, and doeth them not, is like \[48\] the foolish
man which built his house on sand, without foundation:   <span
id="fr_812"></span>[813](#fn_812.md) and the rain descended, and the rivers
overflowed, and the winds blew, and smote upon that house, and it fell: 
and the fall of it was great.
