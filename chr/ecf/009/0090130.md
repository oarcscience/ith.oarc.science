<span id="xii.iv.xxxv-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="xii.iv.xxxv-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXXV.—Immense is This Reward.  How
Shall We Obtain It?</span>

<span id="xii.iv.xxxv-p2"></span>How blessed and wonderful, beloved, are
the gifts of God!  Life in immortality, splendour in righteousness,
truth in perfect confidence, <span id="fr_4176"></span>[4177](#fn_4176.md)
faith in assurance, self-control in holiness!  And all these fall under
the cognizance of our understandings \[now\]; what then shall those
things be which are prepared for such as wait for Him?  The Creator and
Father of all worlds, <span id="fr_4177"></span>[4178](#fn_4177.md) the
Most Holy, <span id="fr_4178"></span>[4179](#fn_4178.md) alone knows their
amount and their beauty.  Let us therefore earnestly strive to be found
in the number of those that wait for Him, in order that we may share in
His promised gifts.  But how, beloved, shall this be done?  If our
understanding be fixed by faith towards God; if we earnestly seek the
things <span id="fr_4179"></span>[4180](#fn_4179.md) which are pleasing and
acceptable to Him; if we do the things which are in harmony with His
blameless will; and if we follow the way of truth, casting away from us
all unrighteousness and iniquity, <span
id="fr_4180"></span>[4181](#fn_4180.md) along with all covetousness, <span
id="fr_4181"></span>[4182](#fn_4181.md) strife, evil practices, deceit,
whispering, and evil-speaking, all hatred of God, pride and haughtiness,
vain glory and ambition. <span id="fr_4182"></span>[4183](#fn_4182.md)  
For they that do such things are hateful to God; and not only they that
do them, but also those that take pleasure in them that do them. <span
id="fr_4183"></span>[4184](#fn_4183.md)   For the Scripture saith, “But to
the sinner God said, Wherefore dost thou declare my statutes, and take
my covenant into thy mouth, seeing thou hatest instruction, and castest
my words behind thee?  When thou sawest a thief, thou consentedst
with <span id="fr_4184"></span>[4185](#fn_4184.md) him, and didst make thy
portion with adulterers.  Thy mouth has abounded with wickedness, and
<span id="page_240">p. 240</span> <span
id="xii.iv.xxxv-Page_240"></span>thy tongue contrived <span
id="fr_4185"></span>[4186](#fn_4185.md) deceit.  Thou sittest, and speakest
against thy brother; thou slanderest <span
id="fr_4186"></span>[4187](#fn_4186.md) thine own mother’s son.  These
things thou hast done, and I kept silence; thou thoughtest, wicked one,
that I should be like to thyself.  But I will reprove thee, and set
thyself before thee.  Consider now these things, ye that forget God,
lest He tear you in pieces, like a lion, and there be none to
deliver. <span id="fr_4187"></span>[4188](#fn_4187.md)   The sacrifice of
praise will glorify me, and a way is there by which I will show him the
salvation of God.” <span id="fr_4188"></span>[4189](#fn_4188.md)
