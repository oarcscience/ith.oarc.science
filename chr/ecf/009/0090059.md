<span id="iv.iii.l-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_3460"></span>[3461](#fn_3460.md) And Pilate said unto the chief
priests and the multitude, I have not found \[2\] against this man
anything.   <span id="fr_3461"></span>[3462](#fn_3461.md) But they cried
out and said, He hath disquieted <span
id="fr_3462"></span>[3463](#fn_3462.md) our people with his teaching in all
Judæa, and he began <span id="fr_3463"></span>[3464](#fn_3463.md) from
Galilee and unto this \[3\] place.   <span
id="fr_3464"></span>[3465](#fn_3464.md) And Pilate, when he heard the name
of Galilee, asked, Is this man a Galilæan?  \[4\]  <span
id="fr_3465"></span>[3466](#fn_3465.md) And when he learned that he was
under the jurisdiction of Herod, he sent him to Herod:  for he was in
Jerusalem in those days.

<span id="iv.iii.l-p9"></span>\[5\]  <span
id="fr_3466"></span>[3467](#fn_3466.md) And Herod, when he saw Jesus,
rejoiced exceedingly:  for he had desired to see him for a long time,
because he had heard regarding him many things; and he counted on <span
id="fr_3467"></span>[3468](#fn_3467.md) \[6\] \[Arabic, p. 189\] seeing
some sign from him.   <span id="fr_3468"></span>[3469](#fn_3468.md) And he
questioned him with many words; but \[7\] Jesus answered him not a
word.   <span id="fr_3469"></span>[3470](#fn_3469.md) And the scribes and
chief priests were \[8\] standing *by*, and they accused him
vehemently.   <span id="fr_3470"></span>[3471](#fn_3470.md) And Herod
scoffed at him, he and his servants; and when he had scoffed at him, he
clothed him in robes of scarlet, \[9\] and sent him to Pilate.   <span
id="fr_3471"></span>[3472](#fn_3471.md) And on that day Pilate and Herod
became friends, there having been <span
id="fr_3472"></span>[3473](#fn_3472.md) enmity between them before that.

<span id="iv.iii.l-p17"></span>\[10, 11\]  <span
id="fr_3473"></span>[3474](#fn_3473.md) And Pilate called the chief priests
and the rulers of the people,  <span
id="fr_3474"></span>[3475](#fn_3474.md) and said unto them, Ye brought unto
me this man, as the perverter of your people:  and I have tried him
before you, and have not found in this man any cause <span
id="fr_3475"></span>[3476](#fn_3475.md) of all that ye \[12\] seek <span
id="fr_3476"></span>[3477](#fn_3476.md) against him:   <span
id="fr_3477"></span>[3478](#fn_3477.md) nor yet Herod:  for I sent him unto
him; and he hath done \[13\] nothing for which he should deserve death. 
 <span id="fr_3478"></span>[3479](#fn_3478.md) So now I will chastise him,
and let \[14, 15\] him go.   <span id="fr_3479"></span>[3480](#fn_3479.md)
The multitude all cried out and said, Take him from us, take him. 
 <span id="fr_3480"></span>[3481](#fn_3480.md) And \[16\] the chief priests
and the elders accused him of many things.   <span
id="fr_3481"></span>[3482](#fn_3481.md) And during their \[17\] accusation
he answered not a word.   <span id="fr_3482"></span>[3483](#fn_3482.md)
Then Pilate said unto him, Hearest thou not \[18\] how many *things*
they witness against thee?   <span id="fr_3483"></span>[3484](#fn_3483.md)
And he answered him not, not even one word:  and Pilate marvelled at
that.

<span id="iv.iii.l-p29"></span>\[19\]  <span
id="fr_3484"></span>[3485](#fn_3484.md) And when the judge sat on his
tribune, his wife sent unto him, and said unto him, See that thou have
nothing to do with that righteous *man*:  for I have suffered much in my
dream <span id="fr_3485"></span>[3486](#fn_3485.md) to-day because of him.

<span id="iv.iii.l-p32"></span>\[20\]  <span
id="fr_3486"></span>[3487](#fn_3486.md) And at every feast the custom of
the judge was to release to the people one \[21\] prisoner, him whom
they would.   <span id="fr_3487"></span>[3488](#fn_3487.md) And there was
in their prison a well-known prisoner, \[22, 23\] called Barabbas. 
 <span id="fr_3488"></span>[3489](#fn_3488.md) And when they assembled,
Pilate said unto them,  <span id="fr_3489"></span>[3490](#fn_3489.md) Ye
have a custom, that I should release unto you a prisoner at the
passover:  will ye that I \[24\] release unto you the King of the Jews? 
 <span id="fr_3490"></span>[3491](#fn_3490.md) And they all cried out and
said, Release not \[Arabic, p. 190\] unto us this *man*, but release
unto us Barabbas.  And this Barabbas was a \[25\] robber,  <span
id="fr_3491"></span>[3492](#fn_3491.md) who for sedition <span
id="fr_3492"></span>[3493](#fn_3492.md) and murder, which was in the city,
was cast into the <span id="page_121">p. 121</span> <span
id="iv.iii.l-Page_121"></span>\[26\] prison.   <span
id="fr_3493"></span>[3494](#fn_3493.md) And all the people cried out and
began to ask *him to do* as the custom was \[27\] that he should do with
them.   <span id="fr_3494"></span>[3495](#fn_3494.md) And Pilate answered
and said unto them, Whom will ye that I release unto you?  Barabbas, or
Jesus which is called the Messiah, the \[28\] King of the Jews?   <span
id="fr_3495"></span>[3496](#fn_3495.md) For Pilate knew that envy had moved
them to deliver him up. \[29\]  <span
id="fr_3496"></span>[3497](#fn_3496.md) And the chief priests and the
elders asked the multitudes to deliver Barabbas, and \[30\] to destroy
Jesus.   <span id="fr_3497"></span>[3498](#fn_3497.md) The judge answered
and said unto them, Whom of the two will \[31\] ye that I release unto
you?  They said, Barabbas.   <span id="fr_3498"></span>[3499](#fn_3498.md)
Pilate said unto them, And \[32\] Jesus which is called the Messiah,
what shall I do with him?   <span id="fr_3499"></span>[3500](#fn_3499.md)
They all cried out \[33\] and said, Crucify him.   <span
id="fr_3500"></span>[3501](#fn_3500.md) And Pilate spake to them again, for
he desired to release \[34\] Jesus;  <span
id="fr_3501"></span>[3502](#fn_3501.md) but they cried out and said,
Crucify him, crucify him, and release unto us \[35\] Barabbas.   <span
id="fr_3502"></span>[3503](#fn_3502.md) And Pilate said unto them a third
time, What evil hath this *man* done?  I have not found in him any
cause <span id="fr_3503"></span>[3504](#fn_3503.md) to necessitate death: 
I will chastise him and \[36\] let him go.   <span
id="fr_3504"></span>[3505](#fn_3504.md) But they increased in
importunity <span id="fr_3505"></span>[3506](#fn_3505.md) with a loud
voice, and asked him to crucify him.  And their voice, and the voice of
the chief priests, prevailed.  \[37\]  <span
id="fr_3506"></span>[3507](#fn_3506.md) Then Pilate released unto them that
one who was cast into prison for sedition and murder, Barabbas, whom
they asked for:   <span id="fr_3507"></span>[3508](#fn_3507.md) and he
scourged Jesus with whips. <span id="fr_3508"></span>[3509](#fn_3508.md)

<span id="iv.iii.l-p56"></span>\[38\]  <span
id="fr_3509"></span>[3510](#fn_3509.md) Then the footsoldiers of the judge
took Jesus, and went into the prætorium, and \[39\] \[Arabic, p. 191\]
gathered unto him all of the footsoldiers.   <span
id="fr_3510"></span>[3511](#fn_3510.md) And they stripped him, and put on
\[40\] him a scarlet cloak.   <span id="fr_3511"></span>[3512](#fn_3511.md)
And they clothed him in garments of purple, and plaited \[41\] a crown
of thorns, and placed it on his head, and a reed in his right hand;
 <span id="fr_3512"></span>[3513](#fn_3512.md) and while they mocked at him
and laughed, they fell down on their knees before him, and bowed \[42\]
down to <span id="fr_3513"></span>[3514](#fn_3513.md) him, and said,
Hail, <span id="fr_3514"></span>[3515](#fn_3514.md) King of the Jews! 
 <span id="fr_3515"></span>[3516](#fn_3515.md) And they spat in his face,
and took the reed from his hand, and struck him on his head,  <span
id="fr_3516"></span>[3517](#fn_3516.md) and smote his cheeks.  \[43\]
 <span id="fr_3517"></span>[3518](#fn_3517.md) And Pilate went forth
without again, and said unto the Jews, I bring him forth to \[44\] you,
that ye may know that I do not find, in examining <span
id="fr_3518"></span>[3519](#fn_3518.md) him, even one crime. <span
id="fr_3519"></span>[3520](#fn_3519.md)    <span
id="fr_3520"></span>[3521](#fn_3520.md) And Jesus went forth without,
wearing the crown of thorns and the purple garments.  \[45\]  <span
id="fr_3521"></span>[3522](#fn_3521.md) Pilate said unto them, Behold, the
man!  And when the chief priests and the soldiers <span
id="fr_3522"></span>[3523](#fn_3522.md) saw him, they cried out and said,
Crucify him, crucify him.  Pilate said unto them, Take him yourselves,
and crucify him:  for I find not a cause <span
id="fr_3523"></span>[3524](#fn_3523.md) against \[46\] him.   <span
id="fr_3524"></span>[3525](#fn_3524.md) The Jews said unto him, We have a
law, and according to our law he deserves \[47\] death, because he made
himself the Son of God.   <span id="fr_3525"></span>[3526](#fn_3525.md) And
when Pilate heard this word, \[48\] his fear increased;  <span
id="fr_3526"></span>[3527](#fn_3526.md) and he entered again into the
porch, and said to Jesus, Whence \[49\] art thou?   <span
id="fr_3527"></span>[3528](#fn_3527.md) But Jesus answered him not a word. 
Pilate said unto him, Speakest <span
id="fr_3528"></span>[3529](#fn_3528.md) thou not unto me? knowest thou not
that I have authority to release thee, and have \[50\] authority to
crucify thee?   <span id="fr_3529"></span>[3530](#fn_3529.md) Jesus said
unto him, Thou hast not any <span id="fr_3530"></span>[3531](#fn_3530.md)
authority over me, if thou wert not given *it* from above:  therefore
the sin of him that delivered \[51\] me up unto thee is greater than thy
sin.   <span id="fr_3531"></span>[3532](#fn_3531.md) And for this word
Pilate wished to release him:  but the Jews cried out, If thou let him
go, thou art not a friend of Cæsar:  for every one that maketh himself a
king is against Cæsar.
