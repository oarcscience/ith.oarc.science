<span id="iv.iii.viii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.viii-p1.1"></span>Section VIII.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_601"></span>[602](#fn_601) And the Pharisees went out, and
consulted together concerning him, that they \[2\] might destroy him. 
 <span id="fr_602"></span>[603](#fn_602) And Jesus perceived, and
removed thence:  and great multitudes \[3\] followed him; and he healed
all of them:   <span id="fr_603"></span>[604](#fn_603) and he forbade
them that they should \[4\] not make him known: <span
id="fr_604"></span>[605](#fn_604)    <span
id="fr_605"></span>[606](#fn_605) that the saying in Isaiah the prophet
might be fulfilled, which said,

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p8"></span>\[5\]  <span
id="fr_606"></span>[607](#fn_606) Behold, my servant <span
id="fr_607"></span>[608](#fn_607) with whom I am pleased;

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p11"></span>My beloved in whom my soul hath
delighted: <span id="fr_608"></span>[609](#fn_608)

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p13"></span>My spirit have I put upon him,

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p14"></span>And he shall proclaim to the nations
judgement.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p15"></span>\[6\]  <span
id="fr_609"></span>[610](#fn_609) He shall not dispute, nor cry out;

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p17"></span>And no man shall hear his voice in the
marketplace.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p18"></span>\[7\]  <span
id="fr_610"></span>[611](#fn_610) And a bruised reed shall he not break,

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p20"></span>And a smoking lamp <span
id="fr_611"></span>[612](#fn_611) shall he not extinguish,

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p22"></span><span id="page_56">p. 56</span> <span
id="iv.iii.viii-Page_56"></span>Until he shall bring forth judgement
unto victory.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p23"></span>\[8\]  <span
id="fr_612"></span>[613](#fn_612) And the nations shall rejoice in his
name. <span id="fr_613"></span>[614](#fn_613)

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p26"></span>\[9\]  <span
id="fr_614"></span>[615](#fn_614) And in those days Jesus went out to
the mountain that he might pray, and he \[10\] spent the night <span
id="fr_615"></span>[616](#fn_615) there in prayer to God.   <span
id="fr_616"></span>[617](#fn_616) And when the morning was come, he
called the disciples.   <span id="fr_617"></span>[618](#fn_617) And he
went towards the sea:  and there followed him much people \[11\] from
Galilee that he might pray, <span id="fr_618"></span>[619](#fn_618)
 <span id="fr_619"></span>[620](#fn_619) and from Judæa, and from
Jerusalem, and from Idumæa, and from beyond Jordan, and from Tyre, and
from Sidon, and from Decapolis; \[12\] and great multitudes came unto
him, which had heard what he did.   <span
id="fr_620"></span>[621](#fn_620) And he spake to his disciples to bring
him the boat because of the multitudes, that they \[13\] might not
throng him.   <span id="fr_621"></span>[622](#fn_621) And he healed
many, so that they were almost falling on \[Arabic, p. 31\] him <span
id="fr_622"></span>[623](#fn_622) on account of their seeking to get
near him.  And <span id="fr_623"></span>[624](#fn_623) those that had
\[14\] plagues and unclean spirits,  <span
id="fr_624"></span>[625](#fn_624) as soon as they beheld him, would
fall, and \[15\] cry out, and say, Thou art the Son of God.   <span
id="fr_625"></span>[626](#fn_625) And he rebuked them much, that they
\[16\] should not make him known.   <span
id="fr_626"></span>[627](#fn_626) And those that were under the
constraint of <span id="fr_627"></span>[628](#fn_627) unclean \[17\]
spirits were healed.   <span id="fr_628"></span>[629](#fn_628) And all
of the crowd were seeking to come near <span
id="fr_629"></span>[630](#fn_629) him; because power went out from him,
and he healed them all.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p43"></span>\[18, 19\]  <span
id="fr_630"></span>[631](#fn_630) And when Jesus saw the multitudes, he
went up to the mountain.   <span id="fr_631"></span>[632](#fn_631) And
he called his disciples, and chose from them twelve; and they are those
whom he named \[20\] apostles:   <span id="fr_632"></span>[633](#fn_632)
Simon, whom he named Cephas, and Andrew his brother, and James and
\[21\] John, and Philip and Bartholomew,  <span
id="fr_633"></span>[634](#fn_633) and Matthew and Thomas, and James the
son \[22\] of Alphæus, and Simon which *was* called the Zealot,  <span
id="fr_634"></span>[635](#fn_634) and Judas the son of James, \[23\] and
Judas the Iscariot, being he that had betrayed him. <span
id="fr_635"></span>[636](#fn_635)    <span
id="fr_636"></span>[637](#fn_636) And Jesus went down with them and
stood in the plain, and the company of his disciples, and the great
\[24\] multitude of people.   <span id="fr_637"></span>[638](#fn_637)
And these twelve he chose to be with him, and that he might \[25\] send
them to preach, and to have power to heal the sick and to cast out
devils.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p52"></span>\[26\]  <span
id="fr_638"></span>[639](#fn_638) Then he lifted up his eyes unto them,
and opened his mouth,  <span id="fr_639"></span>[640](#fn_639) and
taught them, and said,

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p55"></span>\[27\]  <span
id="fr_640"></span>[641](#fn_640) Blessed are the poor in spirit:  for
the kingdom of heaven is theirs.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p57"></span>\[28\]  <span
id="fr_641"></span>[642](#fn_641) Blessed are the sorrowful:  for they
shall be comforted.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p59"></span>\[29\]  <span
id="fr_642"></span>[643](#fn_642) Blessed are the humble:  for they
shall inherit the earth.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p61"></span>\[30\]  <span
id="fr_643"></span>[644](#fn_643) Blessed are they that hunger and
thirst after righteousness:  for they shall be satisfied.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p63"></span>\[31\]  <span
id="fr_644"></span>[645](#fn_644) Blessed are the merciful:  for on them
shall be mercy.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p65"></span>\[32\] \[Arabic, p. 32\]  <span
id="fr_645"></span>[646](#fn_645) Blessed are the pure in their hearts: 
for they shall see God.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p67"></span>\[33\]  <span
id="fr_646"></span>[647](#fn_646) Blessed are the peacemakers:  for they
shall be called the sons of God.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p69"></span>\[34\]  <span
id="fr_647"></span>[648](#fn_647) Blessed are they that were
persecuted <span id="fr_648"></span>[649](#fn_648) for righteousness’
sake:  for the kingdom of heaven is theirs.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p72"></span>\[35\]  <span
id="fr_649"></span>[650](#fn_649) Blessed are ye when men shall hate
you, and separate you from them, and persecute you, and reproach you,
 <span id="fr_650"></span>[651](#fn_650) and shall speak against you
with all evil talk, for my \[36\] sake, falsely.   <span
id="fr_651"></span>[652](#fn_651) Then rejoice and be glad, for your
reward is great in heaven:  for so persecuted they the prophets before
you.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p76"></span>\[37\]  <span
id="fr_652"></span>[653](#fn_652) But woe unto you rich! for ye have
received your consolation.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p78"></span>\[38\]  <span
id="fr_653"></span>[654](#fn_653) Woe unto you that are satisfied! ye
shall hunger.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p80"></span>Woe unto you that laugh now! ye shall
weep and be sad.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p81"></span>\[39\]  <span
id="fr_654"></span>[655](#fn_654) Woe unto you when men praise you! for
so did their fathers use to do to the false prophets.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p83"></span>\[40\]  <span
id="fr_655"></span>[656](#fn_655) Unto you do I say, *ye* which hear,
 <span id="fr_656"></span>[657](#fn_656) Ye are the salt of the earth: 
if then the salt become tasteless, wherewith shall it be salted?  For
any purpose it is of no use, but \[41\] is thrown outside, and men tread
upon it.   <span id="fr_657"></span>[658](#fn_657) Ye are the light of
the world.  It is \[42\] impossible that a city built on a mountain
should be hid.   <span id="fr_658"></span>[659](#fn_658) Neither do they
light a <span id="page_57">p. 57</span> <span
id="iv.iii.viii-Page_57"></span>lamp and place it under a bushel, but on
the lamp-stand, and it giveth light to all \[43\] who are in the house. 
 <span id="fr_659"></span>[660](#fn_659) So shall <span
id="fr_660"></span>[661](#fn_660) your light shine before men, that they
may see \[44\] your good works, and glorify your Father which is in
heaven.   <span id="fr_661"></span>[662](#fn_661) There is nothing
\[45\] secret that shall not be revealed, or hidden that shall not be
known.   <span id="fr_662"></span>[663](#fn_662) Whoever hath ears that
hear, let him hear.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p92"></span>\[46\]  <span
id="fr_663"></span>[664](#fn_663) Think not that I came to destroy the
law or the prophets; I came not to destroy, \[47\] but to complete. 
 <span id="fr_664"></span>[665](#fn_664) Verily I say unto you, Until
heaven and earth shall pass, there \[Arabic, p. 33\] shall not pass one
point or one letter of the law, until all of it shall be \[48\]
*accomplished*.   <span id="fr_665"></span>[666](#fn_665) Every one who
shall violate now one of these small commandments, and shall teach men
so, shall be called lacking in the kingdom of heaven:  every one that
shall do and teach shall <span id="fr_666"></span>[667](#fn_666) be
called great in the kingdom \[49\] of heaven.   <span
id="fr_667"></span>[668](#fn_667) I say unto you now, unless your
righteousness abound more than that of the scribes and Pharisees, ye
shall not enter the kingdom of heaven.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p98"></span>\[50\]  <span
id="fr_668"></span>[669](#fn_668) Ye have heard that it was said to the
ancients, Do not kill; and every one that \[51\] killeth is worthy of
the judgement.   <span id="fr_669"></span>[670](#fn_669) But I say unto
you that every one who is angry with his brother without a cause is
worthy of the judgement; and every one that saith to his brother, Thou
foul one, is condemned <span id="fr_670"></span>[671](#fn_670) by the
synagogue; and whosoever \[52\] saith to him, Thou fool, is worthy of
the fire of Gehenna.   <span id="fr_671"></span>[672](#fn_671) If thou
art now offering thy gift at the altar, and rememberest there that thy
brother hath conceived \[53\] against thee any grudge,  <span
id="fr_672"></span>[673](#fn_672) leave thy gift at the altar, and go
first and satisfy thy \[54\] brother, and then return and offer thy
gift.   <span id="fr_673"></span>[674](#fn_673) Join <span
id="fr_674"></span>[675](#fn_674) thine adversary quickly,  <span
id="fr_675"></span>[676](#fn_675) and while thou art still with him in
the way, give a ransom and free thyself from him; \[55\] lest thine
adversary deliver thee to the judge,  <span
id="fr_676"></span>[677](#fn_676) and the judge deliver thee to the
tax-collector, \[56\] and thou fall into prison.   <span
id="fr_677"></span>[678](#fn_677) And verily I say unto thee, Thou shalt
not go out thence until thou payest the last farthing.

<span id="iv.iii.viii-p109"></span>\[57, 58\]  <span
id="fr_678"></span>[679](#fn_678) Ye have heard that it was said, Do not
commit adultery:   <span id="fr_679"></span>[680](#fn_679) but I now say
unto you, that every one that looketh at a woman lusting after her hath
forthwith already \[59\] \[Arabic, p. 34\] committed adultery with her
in his heart.   <span id="fr_680"></span>[681](#fn_680) If thy right eye
injure thee, put it out and cast it from thee; for it is preferable for
thee that one of thy \[60\] members should perish, and not thy whole
body go into the fire *of hell*.   <span
id="fr_681"></span>[682](#fn_681) And if thy right hand injure thee, cut
it off and cast it from thee; and it is better for thee that \[61\] one
of thy members should perish, and not thy whole body fall into Gehenna. 
 <span id="fr_682"></span>[683](#fn_682) It was said that he that
putteth away his wife *should* give her a writing of divorcement: 
\[62\]  <span id="fr_683"></span>[684](#fn_683) but I say unto you, that
every one that putteth away his wife, except for the cause of adultery,
hath made it lawful for <span id="fr_684"></span>[685](#fn_684) her to
commit adultery:  and whosoever taketh one that is put away committeth
adultery.
