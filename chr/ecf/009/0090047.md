<span id="iv.iii.xxxviii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xxxviii-p1.1"></span>Section XXXVIII.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xxxviii-p2"></span>\[1, 2\]  <span
id="fr_2595"></span>[2596](#fn_2595) And Jesus came to Bethany, and
found him *already* four days in the grave.   <span
id="fr_2596"></span>[2597](#fn_2596) And Bethany was beside Jerusalem,
and its distance from it *was* a sum of fifteen furlongs; <span
id="fr_2597"></span>[2598](#fn_2597) \[3\]  <span
id="fr_2598"></span>[2599](#fn_2598) and many of the Jews came unto Mary
and Martha, to comfort their heart \[4\] because of their brother. 
 <span id="fr_2599"></span>[2600](#fn_2599) And Martha, when she heard
that Jesus had come, went \[5\] out to meet him:  but Mary was sitting
in the house.   <span id="fr_2600"></span>[2601](#fn_2600) Martha then
said unto Jesus, \[6\] My Lord, if thou hadst been here, my brother had
not died.   <span id="fr_2601"></span>[2602](#fn_2601) But I know now
that, \[7\] whatever thou shalt ask of God, he will give thee.   <span
id="fr_2602"></span>[2603](#fn_2602) Jesus said unto her, Thy brother
shall \[8\] rise.   <span id="fr_2603"></span>[2604](#fn_2603) Martha
said unto him, I know that he shall rise in the resurrection at the last
day.  \[9\]  <span id="fr_2604"></span>[2605](#fn_2604) Jesus said unto
her, I am the resurrection, and the life:  whosoever believeth in \[10\]
\[Arabic, p. 144\] me, even though he die, he shall live:   <span
id="fr_2605"></span>[2606](#fn_2605) and every living one that believeth
\[11\] in me shall never die.  Believest thou this?   <span
id="fr_2606"></span>[2607](#fn_2606) She said unto him, Yea, my Lord:  I
believe that thou art the Messiah, the Son of God, that cometh into the
\[12\] world.   <span id="fr_2607"></span>[2608](#fn_2607) And when she
had said that, she went and called Mary her sister secretly, \[13\] and
said unto her, Our Master hath come, and summoneth thee.   <span
id="fr_2608"></span>[2609](#fn_2608) And Mary, when \[14\] she heard,
rose in haste, and came unto him.   <span
id="fr_2609"></span>[2610](#fn_2609) (And Jesus then had not come into
\[15\] the village, but was in the place where Martha met him.)   <span
id="fr_2610"></span>[2611](#fn_2610) And the Jews also that were with
her in the house, to comfort her, when they saw that Mary rose up and
went out in haste, went after her, because they supposed that she was
going to the \[16\] tomb to weep.   <span
id="fr_2611"></span>[2612](#fn_2611) And Mary, when she came to where
Jesus was, and saw him, fell at his feet, and said unto him, If thou
hadst been here, my Lord, my brother had <span id="page_102">p.
102</span> <span id="iv.iii.xxxviii-Page_102"></span>\[17\] not died. 
 <span id="fr_2612"></span>[2613](#fn_2612) And Jesus came; and when he
saw her weeping, and the Jews that were \[18\] with her weeping, he was
troubled <span id="fr_2613"></span>[2614](#fn_2613) in himself, and
sighed; and he said,  <span id="fr_2614"></span>[2615](#fn_2614) In what
\[19\] place have ye laid him?  And they said unto him, Our Lord, come
and see.   <span id="fr_2615"></span>[2616](#fn_2615) And \[20\] the
tears of Jesus came. <span id="fr_2616"></span>[2617](#fn_2616)    <span
id="fr_2617"></span>[2618](#fn_2617) The Jews therefore said, See the
greatness of his love for \[21\] him!   <span
id="fr_2618"></span>[2619](#fn_2618) But some of them said, Could not
this *man*, who opened the eyes of that \[22\] blind *man*, have caused
that this *man* also should not die?   <span
id="fr_2619"></span>[2620](#fn_2619) And Jesus came to the place of
burial, being troubled within himself.  And the place of burial was a
cave, \[23\] and a stone was placed at its door.   <span
id="fr_2620"></span>[2621](#fn_2620) Jesus therefore said, Take these
stones *away*.  Martha, the sister of him *that was* dead, said unto
him, My Lord, he hath come to \[24\] stink for some time:  he hath
been <span id="fr_2621"></span>[2622](#fn_2621) four days *dead*. 
 <span id="fr_2622"></span>[2623](#fn_2622) Jesus said unto her, Did not
I say \[25\] \[Arabic, p. 145\] unto thee, If thou believest, thou shalt
see the glory of God?   <span id="fr_2623"></span>[2624](#fn_2623) And
they removed those stones.  And Jesus lifted his eyes on high, and said,
My Father, \[26\] I thank thee since thou didst hear me.   <span
id="fr_2624"></span>[2625](#fn_2624) And I know that thou at all times
hearest me:  but I say this unto thee because of this multitude that is
standing, that they \[27\] may believe that thou didst send me.   <span
id="fr_2625"></span>[2626](#fn_2625) And when he had said that, he cried
with a \[28\] loud voice, Lazarus, come forth.   <span
id="fr_2626"></span>[2627](#fn_2626) And that dead *man* came out,
having his hands and feet bound with bandages, and his face wrapped in a
scarf.  Jesus said unto them, Loose him, and let him go.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxviii-p35"></span>\[29\]  <span
id="fr_2627"></span>[2628](#fn_2627) And many of the Jews which came
unto Mary, when they saw the deed of Jesus, \[30\] believed in him. 
 <span id="fr_2628"></span>[2629](#fn_2628) But some of them went to the
Pharisees, and informed them of all that Jesus did.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxviii-p38"></span>\[31\]  <span
id="fr_2629"></span>[2630](#fn_2629) And the chief priests and the
Pharisees gathered, and said, What shall we do? \[32\] for lo, this man
doeth many signs.   <span id="fr_2630"></span>[2631](#fn_2630) And if we
leave him thus, all men will believe \[33\] in him:  and the Romans will
come and take our country and people.   <span
id="fr_2631"></span>[2632](#fn_2631) And one of them, who was called
Caiaphas, the chief priest he was in that year, said unto them, \[34\]
 <span id="fr_2632"></span>[2633](#fn_2632) Ye know not anything, nor
consider that it is more advantageous for us that one \[35\] man should
die instead of the people, and not that the whole people perish.   <span
id="fr_2633"></span>[2634](#fn_2633) And this he said not of himself: 
but because he was the chief priest of <span
id="fr_2634"></span>[2635](#fn_2634) that year, he \[36\] prophesied
that Jesus was to die instead of the people;  <span
id="fr_2635"></span>[2636](#fn_2635) and not instead of the people
alone, but that he might gather the scattered children of God together. 
\[37\]  <span id="fr_2636"></span>[2637](#fn_2636) And from that day
they considered *how* to kill him.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxviii-p47"></span>\[38\] \[Arabic, p. 146\]  <span
id="fr_2637"></span>[2638](#fn_2637) And Jesus did not walk openly
amongst the Jews, but departed thence to a place near the wilderness, to
a town <span id="fr_2638"></span>[2639](#fn_2638) called Ephraim; and he
was there, going \[39\] about with his disciples.   <span
id="fr_2639"></span>[2640](#fn_2639) And the passover of the Jews was
near:  and many went \[40\] up from the villages unto Jerusalem before
the feast, to purify themselves.   <span
id="fr_2640"></span>[2641](#fn_2640) And they sought for Jesus, and said
one to another in the temple, What think ye of his \[41\] holding back
from the feast?   <span id="fr_2641"></span>[2642](#fn_2641) And the
chief priests and the Pharisees had given commandment, that, if any man
knew in what place he was, he should reveal *it* to them, that they
might take him.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxviii-p53"></span>\[42\]  <span
id="fr_2642"></span>[2643](#fn_2642) And when the days of his going up
were accomplished, he prepared himself that \[43\] he might go <span
id="fr_2643"></span>[2644](#fn_2643) to Jerusalem.   <span
id="fr_2644"></span>[2645](#fn_2644) And he sent messengers before him,
and departed, <span id="fr_2645"></span>[2646](#fn_2645) and \[44\]
entered into a village <span id="fr_2646"></span>[2647](#fn_2646) of
Samaria, that they might make ready for him.   <span
id="fr_2647"></span>[2648](#fn_2647) And they \[45\] received him not,
because he <span id="fr_2648"></span>[2649](#fn_2648) was prepared for
going to Jerusalem.   <span id="fr_2649"></span>[2650](#fn_2649) And
when James and John his disciples saw *it*, they said unto him, Our
Lord, wilt thou that we speak, and fire come down from heaven, to
extirpate them, as did Elijah also?  \[46\]  <span
id="fr_2650"></span>[2651](#fn_2650) And Jesus turned, and rebuked them,
and said, Ye know not of what spirit ye are.  \[47\]  <span
id="fr_2651"></span>[2652](#fn_2651) Verily the Son of man did not come
to destroy lives, but to give life.  And they went to another village.
