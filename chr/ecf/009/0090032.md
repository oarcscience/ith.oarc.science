<span id="iv.iii.xxiii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xxiii-p1.1"></span>Section XXIII.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xxiii-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_1605"></span>[1606](#fn_1605.md) And Jesus departed thence, and came
to the side of the sea of Galilee, and went \[2\] up into the mountain,
and sat there.   <span id="fr_1606"></span>[1607](#fn_1606.md) And there
came unto him great multitudes, having with them lame, and blind, and
dumb, and maimed, and many others, and \[3\] they cast them at the feet
of Jesus:   <span id="fr_1607"></span>[1608](#fn_1607.md) for they had seen
all the signs which he did in \[4\] Jerusalem, when they were gathered
at the feast.   <span id="fr_1608"></span>[1609](#fn_1608.md) And he healed
them all.   <span id="fr_1609"></span>[1610](#fn_1609.md) And <span
id="page_79">p. 79</span> <span id="iv.iii.xxiii-Page_79"></span>those
multitudes marvelled when they saw dumb *men* speak, and maimed *men*
healed, and lame *men* walk, and blind *men* see; and they praised the
God of Israel.

<span id="iv.iii.xxiii-p8"></span>\[5\]  <span
id="fr_1610"></span>[1611](#fn_1610.md) And Jesus called his disciples, and
said unto them, I have compassion on this multitude, because of their
continuing with me three days, having nothing to eat; and to send them
away fasting I am not willing, lest they faint in the way,  <span
id="fr_1611"></span>[1612](#fn_1611.md) some of them having \[6\] \[Arabic,
p. 89\] come from far.   <span id="fr_1612"></span>[1613](#fn_1612.md) His
disciples said unto him, Whence have we in the desert \[7\] bread
wherewith to satisfy all this multitude?   <span
id="fr_1613"></span>[1614](#fn_1613.md) Jesus said unto them, How \[8\]
many loaves have ye?   <span id="fr_1614"></span>[1615](#fn_1614.md) They
said unto him, Seven, and a few small fishes.  And he \[9\] commanded
the multitudes to sit down upon the ground;  <span
id="fr_1615"></span>[1616](#fn_1615.md) and he took those seven loaves and
the fish, and blessed, and brake, and gave to his disciples to set
before \[10\] them; and the disciples set before the multitudes.   <span
id="fr_1616"></span>[1617](#fn_1616.md) And they all ate, and were
satisfied:  and they took that which remained over of the fragments,
seven basketfuls.  \[11\]  <span id="fr_1617"></span>[1618](#fn_1617.md)
And the people that ate were four thousand men, besides the women and
children.  \[12\]  <span id="fr_1618"></span>[1619](#fn_1618.md) And when
the multitudes departed, he went up into the boat, and came to the
borders of Magada. <span id="fr_1619"></span>[1620](#fn_1619.md)

<span id="iv.iii.xxiii-p19"></span>\[13\]  <span
id="fr_1620"></span>[1621](#fn_1620.md) And the Pharisees and Sadducees
came to him, and began to seek a discussion with him.  And they asked
him to shew them a sign from heaven, tempting him.  \[14\]  <span
id="fr_1621"></span>[1622](#fn_1621.md) And Jesus sighed within himself,
and said, What sign seeketh this evil and adulterous generation?  It
seeketh a sign, and it shall not be given a sign, except the sign \[15\]
of Jonah the prophet.   <span id="fr_1622"></span>[1623](#fn_1622.md)
Verily I say unto you, This generation shall not be given a \[16\]
sign.   <span id="fr_1623"></span>[1624](#fn_1623.md) And he left <span
id="fr_1624"></span>[1625](#fn_1624.md) them, and went up into the boat,
and went away to that side.

<span id="iv.iii.xxiii-p25"></span>\[17\]  <span
id="fr_1625"></span>[1626](#fn_1625.md) And his disciples forgot to take
with them bread, and there was not with them \[18\] in the boat, not
even <span id="fr_1626"></span>[1627](#fn_1626.md) one loaf.   <span
id="fr_1627"></span>[1628](#fn_1627.md) And Jesus charged them, and said,
Take heed, and guard yourselves from the leaven of the Pharisees and
Sadducees, and from the \[19\] leaven of Herod.   <span
id="fr_1628"></span>[1629](#fn_1628.md) And they reflected within
themselves that they had taken with them \[20\] no bread.   <span
id="fr_1629"></span>[1630](#fn_1629.md) And Jesus knew, and said unto them,
Why <span id="fr_1630"></span>[1631](#fn_1630.md) think ye within
yourselves, O *ye* of little faith, and are anxious, because ye have no
bread?  <span id="fr_1631"></span>[1632](#fn_1631.md) until now do ye not
perceive, \[21\] neither understand? is your heart yet hard?   <span
id="fr_1632"></span>[1633](#fn_1632.md) And have ye eyes, and *yet* see
not? \[22\] \[Arabic, p. 90\] and have ye ears, and *yet* hear not?
 <span id="fr_1633"></span>[1634](#fn_1633.md) and do ye not remember when
I brake those five loaves for five thousand? and how many baskets full
of broken \[23\] pieces took ye <span
id="fr_1634"></span>[1635](#fn_1634.md) up?  They said, Twelve.   <span
id="fr_1635"></span>[1636](#fn_1635.md) He said unto them, And the seven
also for four thousand:  how many baskets full of broken pieces took
ye <span id="fr_1636"></span>[1637](#fn_1636.md) up?  They \[24\] said,
Seven.   <span id="fr_1637"></span>[1638](#fn_1637.md) He said unto them,
How have ye not understood that I spake not to you because of <span
id="fr_1638"></span>[1639](#fn_1638.md) the bread, but that ye should
beware of the leaven of the Pharisees \[25\] and Sadducees?   <span
id="fr_1639"></span>[1640](#fn_1639.md) Then they understood that he spake,
not that they should beware of the leaven of the bread, but of the
doctrine of the Pharisees and Sadducees, which he called leaven.

<span id="iv.iii.xxiii-p41"></span>\[26\]  <span
id="fr_1640"></span>[1641](#fn_1640.md) And after that, he came to
Bethsaida.  And they brought to him a certain <span
id="fr_1641"></span>[1642](#fn_1641.md) blind \[27\] *man*, and besought
him that he would touch him.   <span
id="fr_1642"></span>[1643](#fn_1642.md) And he took the hand of that blind
man, and led him out without the village, and spat in his eyes, and laid
his \[28\] hand on him, <span id="fr_1643"></span>[1644](#fn_1643.md) and
asked him, What seest thou?   <span id="fr_1644"></span>[1645](#fn_1644.md)
And that blind man looked intently, \[29\] and said unto him, I see men
as trees walking.   <span id="fr_1645"></span>[1646](#fn_1645.md) And he
placed his hand \[30\] again on his eyes; and they were restored, <span
id="fr_1646"></span>[1647](#fn_1646.md) and he saw everything clearly. 
 <span id="fr_1647"></span>[1648](#fn_1647.md) And he sent him to his
house, and said, Do not enter even into the village, nor tell any man in
the village.

<span id="iv.iii.xxiii-p50"></span>\[31\]  <span
id="fr_1648"></span>[1649](#fn_1648.md) And Jesus went forth, and his
disciples, to the villages of Cæsarea Philippi.  \[32\]  <span
id="fr_1649"></span>[1650](#fn_1649.md) And while he was going in the way,
and his disciples alone, <span id="fr_1650"></span>[1651](#fn_1650.md) he
asked his disciples, \[33\] and said, What do men say of me that I am,
the Son of man? <span id="fr_1651"></span>[1652](#fn_1651.md)    <span
id="fr_1652"></span>[1653](#fn_1652.md) They said unto him, Some say, John
the Baptist; and others, Elijah; and others, Jeremiah, or one of the
\[34, 35\] prophets.   <span id="fr_1653"></span>[1654](#fn_1653.md) He
said unto them, And ye, what say ye that I am?   <span
id="fr_1654"></span>[1655](#fn_1654.md) Simon Cephas answered \[36\]
\[Arabic, p. 91\] and said, Thou art the Messiah, the Son of the living
God.   <span id="fr_1655"></span>[1656](#fn_1655.md) Jesus answered and
said unto him, Blessed art thou, Simon son of Jonah:  flesh and \[37\]
blood hath not revealed *it* unto thee, but my Father which is in
heaven.   <span id="fr_1656"></span>[1657](#fn_1656.md) And I say unto
<span id="page_80">p. 80</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xxiii-Page_80"></span>thee also, that thou art Cephas, <span
id="fr_1657"></span>[1658](#fn_1657.md) and on this rock will I build my
church; and the \[38\] gates of Hades shall not prevail against it. 
 <span id="fr_1658"></span>[1659](#fn_1658.md) To thee will I give the keys
of the kingdom of heaven:  and whatsoever thou shalt bind on earth shall
be bound in heaven; and \[39\] whatsoever thou shalt loose on earth
shall be loosed in heaven.   <span id="fr_1659"></span>[1660](#fn_1659.md)
And he sternly charged his disciples, and warned them that they should
not tell any man concerning him, \[40\] that he was the Messiah.   <span
id="fr_1660"></span>[1661](#fn_1660.md) And henceforth began Jesus to shew
to his disciples \[41\] that he was determined <span
id="fr_1661"></span>[1662](#fn_1661.md) to go to Jerusalem,  <span
id="fr_1662"></span>[1663](#fn_1662.md) and suffer much, and be rejected of
the elders, and of the chief priests, and of the scribes, and be killed,
and on the \[42\] third day rise.   <span
id="fr_1663"></span>[1664](#fn_1663.md) And he was speaking <span
id="fr_1664"></span>[1665](#fn_1664.md) plainly.   <span
id="fr_1665"></span>[1666](#fn_1665.md) And Simon Cephas, as one grieved
\[43\] for him, said, Far be thou, my Lord, from that.   <span
id="fr_1666"></span>[1667](#fn_1666.md) And he turned, and looked upon
\[44\] his disciples, and rebuked Simon, and said,  <span
id="fr_1667"></span>[1668](#fn_1667.md) Get thee behind me, Satan:  for
thou art a stumblingblock unto me:  for thou thinkest not of what
pertains to God, but of what pertains to men.

<span id="iv.iii.xxiii-p71"></span>\[45\]  <span
id="fr_1668"></span>[1669](#fn_1668.md) And Jesus called the multitudes
with his disciples, and said unto them, Whosoever would come after me,
let him deny himself, and take his cross every day, and \[46\] come
after me.   <span id="fr_1669"></span>[1670](#fn_1669.md) And whosoever
would save his life shall lose it; and whosoever \[47\] loseth his life
for my sake, and for the sake of my gospel, shall save it.   <span
id="fr_1670"></span>[1671](#fn_1670.md) What shall \[48\] a man profit, if
he gain all the world, and destroy <span
id="fr_1671"></span>[1672](#fn_1671.md) his own life, <span
id="fr_1672"></span>[1673](#fn_1672.md) or lose it?  <span
id="fr_1673"></span>[1674](#fn_1673.md) or what \[49\] \[Arabic, p. 92\]
will a man give *in* ransom for his life? <span
id="fr_1674"></span>[1675](#fn_1674.md)    <span
id="fr_1675"></span>[1676](#fn_1675.md) Whosoever shall deny me and my
sayings in this sinful and adulterous generation, the Son of man also
will \[50\] deny him, when he cometh in the glory of his Father with his
holy angels.   <span id="fr_1676"></span>[1677](#fn_1676.md) For the Son of
man is about to <span id="fr_1677"></span>[1678](#fn_1677.md) come in the
glory of his Father with his holy angels; and then shall he reward each
man according to his works.
