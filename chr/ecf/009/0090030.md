<span id="iv.iii.xxi-p1"></span> <span id="page_76">p. 76</span> <span

id="iv.iii.xxi-p1.1"></span>Section XXI.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xxi-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_1478"></span>[1479](#fn_1478) And Jesus went out again from the
border*s* of Tyre and Sidon, and came to the \[2\] sea of Galilee,
towards the borders of Decapolis.   <span
id="fr_1479"></span>[1480](#fn_1479) And they brought unto him one dumb
and deaf, and entreated him that he would lay his hand upon him and heal
\[3\] him.   <span id="fr_1480"></span>[1481](#fn_1480) And he drew him
away from the multitude, and went away alone, and spat \[4\] upon his
fingers, and thrust *them* into his ears, and touched his tongue;  <span
id="fr_1481"></span>[1482](#fn_1481) and looked \[5\] to heaven, and
sighed, and said unto him, Be opened.   <span
id="fr_1482"></span>[1483](#fn_1482) And in that hour his ears \[6\]
were opened, and the bond of his tongue was loosed, and he spake with
ease.   <span id="fr_1483"></span>[1484](#fn_1483) And Jesus charged
them much that they should not tell this to any man:  but the more \[7\]
he charged them, *the more* they increased in publishing, and marvelled
much,  <span id="fr_1484"></span>[1485](#fn_1484) and \[Arabic, p. 82\]
said, This *man* doeth everything well:  he made the deaf to hear, and
those that lacked speech to speak.

<span id="iv.iii.xxi-p10"></span>\[8, 9\]  <span
id="fr_1485"></span>[1486](#fn_1485) And while he was passing through
the land of Samaria,  <span id="fr_1486"></span>[1487](#fn_1486) he came
to one of the cities of the Samaritans, called Sychar, beside the field
which Jacob gave to Joseph to \[10\] his son.   <span
id="fr_1487"></span>[1488](#fn_1487) And there was there a spring of
water of Jacob’s.  And Jesus was fatigued from the exertion of the way,
and sat at the spring.  And the time was about the \[11\] sixth
hour. <span id="fr_1488"></span>[1489](#fn_1488)    <span
id="fr_1489"></span>[1490](#fn_1489) And a woman of Samaria came to draw
water; and Jesus said unto \[12\] her, Give me water, that I may drink. 
 <span id="fr_1490"></span>[1491](#fn_1490) And his disciples had
entered into the city \[13\] to buy for themselves food.   <span
id="fr_1491"></span>[1492](#fn_1491) And that Samaritan woman said unto
him, How dost thou, being a Jew, ask me to give thee to drink, while I
am a Samaritan woman?  \[14\]  <span
id="fr_1492"></span>[1493](#fn_1492) (And the Jews mingle not with the
Samaritans. <span id="fr_1493"></span>[1494](#fn_1493) )  Jesus answered
and said unto her, If thou knewest the gift of God, and who this is that
said unto thee, Give me \[15\] to drink; thou wouldest ask him, and he
would give thee the water of life.   <span
id="fr_1494"></span>[1495](#fn_1494) That woman said unto him, My Lord,
thou hast no bucket, and the well is deep:  from \[16\] whence hast thou
the water of life?   <span id="fr_1495"></span>[1496](#fn_1495) Can it
be that thou art greater than our father Jacob, who gave us this well,
and drank from it, and his children, and his sheep?  \[17\]  <span
id="fr_1496"></span>[1497](#fn_1496) Jesus answered and said unto her,
Every one that drinketh of this water shall thirst \[18\] again:   <span
id="fr_1497"></span>[1498](#fn_1497) but whosoever drinketh of the water
which I shall give him shall not thirst for ever:  but the water which I
shall give him shall be in him a spring of water springing \[19\] up
unto eternal life.   <span id="fr_1498"></span>[1499](#fn_1498) That
woman said unto him, My Lord, give me of this water, that \[20\] I may
not thirst again, neither come and draw water from here.   <span
id="fr_1499"></span>[1500](#fn_1499) Jesus said unto her, \[21\]
\[Arabic, p. 83\] Go and call thy husband, and come hither.   <span
id="fr_1500"></span>[1501](#fn_1500) She said unto him, I have no \[22\]
husband.   <span id="fr_1501"></span>[1502](#fn_1501) Jesus said unto
her, Thou saidst well, I have no husband:  five husbands hast thou had,
and this *man* whom thou hast now is not thy husband; and \[23\] in this
thou saidst truly.   <span id="fr_1502"></span>[1503](#fn_1502) That
woman said unto him, My Lord, I perceive thee to \[24\] be a prophet. 
 <span id="fr_1503"></span>[1504](#fn_1503) Our fathers worshipped in
this mountain; and ye say that in Jerusalem \[25\] is the place in which
worship must be.   <span id="fr_1504"></span>[1505](#fn_1504) Jesus said
unto her, Woman, believe me, an hour cometh, when neither in this
mountain, nor yet in Jerusalem, shall ye worship \[26\] the Father. 
 <span id="fr_1505"></span>[1506](#fn_1505) Ye worship that which ye
know not:  but we worship that which \[27\] we know:  for salvation is
of the Jews.   <span id="fr_1506"></span>[1507](#fn_1506) But an hour
cometh, and now is, when the true worshippers shall worship the Father
in spirit and truth:  and the Father also \[28\] seeketh such as these
worshippers.   <span id="fr_1507"></span>[1508](#fn_1507) For God is a
Spirit:  and they that worship him \[29\] must worship him in spirit and
in truth.   <span id="fr_1508"></span>[1509](#fn_1508) That woman said
unto him, I know that \[30\] the Messiah cometh:   <span
id="fr_1509"></span>[1510](#fn_1509) and when he is come, he will teach
us everything.  Jesus said unto her, I that speak unto thee am he.

<span id="iv.iii.xxi-p36"></span>\[31\]  <span
id="fr_1510"></span>[1511](#fn_1510) And while he was speaking, his
disciples came; and they wondered how he would speak <span
id="fr_1511"></span>[1512](#fn_1511) with a woman; but not one of them
said unto him, What seekest thou? or, \[32\] What <span
id="fr_1512"></span>[1513](#fn_1512) speakest thou with her?   <span
id="fr_1513"></span>[1514](#fn_1513) And the woman left her waterpot,
and went to the \[33\] city, and said to the people,  <span
id="fr_1514"></span>[1515](#fn_1514) Come, and see a man who told me all
that *ever* I did:  \[34\] perhaps then he is the Messiah.   <span
id="fr_1515"></span>[1516](#fn_1515) And people went out from the city,
and came to \[35\] him.   <span id="fr_1516"></span>[1517](#fn_1516) And
in the mean while his disciples besought him, and said unto him, Our
\[36, 37\] master, eat.   <span id="fr_1517"></span>[1518](#fn_1517) And
he said unto them, I have food to eat that ye know not.   <span
id="fr_1518"></span>[1519](#fn_1518) And the disciples said amongst
themselves, Can any one have brought him aught to eat? <span
id="fr_1519"></span>[1520](#fn_1519)   <span id="page_77">p. 77</span>
<span id="iv.iii.xxi-Page_77"></span>\[38\]  <span
id="fr_1520"></span>[1521](#fn_1520) Jesus said unto them, My food is to
do the will of him that sent me, and to accomplish \[39\] \[Arabic, p.
84\] his work.   <span id="fr_1521"></span>[1522](#fn_1521) Said ye not
that after four months cometh the harvest? behold, I therefore say unto
you, Lift up your eyes, and behold the lands, \[40\] that they have
become white, and the harvest is already come. <span
id="fr_1522"></span>[1523](#fn_1522)    <span
id="fr_1523"></span>[1524](#fn_1523) And he that reapeth receiveth his
wages, and gathereth the fruit of eternal life; <span
id="fr_1524"></span>[1525](#fn_1524) and the sower and \[41\] the reaper
rejoice together.   <span id="fr_1525"></span>[1526](#fn_1525) For in
this is found the word of truth, One soweth, and \[42\] another
reapeth.   <span id="fr_1526"></span>[1527](#fn_1526) And I sent you to
reap that in which ye have not laboured:  others laboured, and ye have
entered on their labour.

<span id="iv.iii.xxi-p54"></span>\[43\]  <span
id="fr_1527"></span>[1528](#fn_1527) And from that city many of the
Samaritans believed in him because of the words \[44\] of that woman,
who testified and said, He told me all that *ever* I did.   <span
id="fr_1528"></span>[1529](#fn_1528) And when those Samaritans came unto
him, they besought him to abide with them; and he \[45, 46\] abode with
them two days.   <span id="fr_1529"></span>[1530](#fn_1529) And many
believed in him because of his word;  <span
id="fr_1530"></span>[1531](#fn_1530) and they said to that woman, Now
not because of thy saying have we believed in him:  we have heard and
known that this truly is the Messiah, the Saviour of the world.

<span id="iv.iii.xxi-p59"></span>\[47, 48\]  <span
id="fr_1531"></span>[1532](#fn_1531) And after two days Jesus went out
thence and departed to Galilee.   <span
id="fr_1532"></span>[1533](#fn_1532) And Jesus \[49\] testified that a
prophet is not honoured in his own city.   <span
id="fr_1533"></span>[1534](#fn_1533) And when he came to Galilee, the
Galilæans received him.
