<span id="iv.iii.ix-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.ix-p1.1"></span>Section IX.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.ix-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_685"></span>[686](#fn_685.md) Ye have heard also that it was said
unto the ancients, Lie not, but perform unto \[2\] God in thy oaths: 
 <span id="fr_686"></span>[687](#fn_686.md) but I say unto you, Swear not
at all; neither by heaven, for it \[3\] is God’s throne;  <span
id="fr_687"></span>[688](#fn_687.md) nor by the earth, for it is a
footstool under his feet; nor yet by \[4\] Jerusalem, for it is the city
of the great <span id="fr_688"></span>[689](#fn_688.md) King.   <span
id="fr_689"></span>[690](#fn_689.md) Neither shalt thou swear by thy \[5\]
head, for thou canst not make in it one lock of hair black or white. 
 <span id="fr_690"></span>[691](#fn_690.md) But your word shall be either
Yea or Nay, and what is in excess of this is of the evil one.

<span id="iv.iii.ix-p9"></span>\[6, 7\]  <span
id="fr_691"></span>[692](#fn_691.md) Ye have heard that it was said, Eye
for eye, and tooth for tooth:   <span id="fr_692"></span>[693](#fn_692.md)
but I say unto you, Stand not in opposition to the evil; <span
id="fr_693"></span>[694](#fn_693.md) but whosoever smiteth thee on thy
right \[8\] cheek, <span id="fr_694"></span>[695](#fn_694.md) turn to him
also the other.   <span id="fr_695"></span>[696](#fn_695.md) And he that
would sue thee, and take thy tunic, \[9\] leave to him also thy
wrapper.   <span id="fr_696"></span>[697](#fn_696.md) And whosoever
compelleth thee one mile, go with \[10\] \[Arabic, p. 35\] him twain. 
 <span id="fr_697"></span>[698](#fn_697.md) And he that asketh thee, give
unto him:   <span id="fr_698"></span>[699](#fn_698.md) and he that would
borrow of thee, prevent him not.  And prosecute <span
id="fr_699"></span>[700](#fn_699.md) not him that taketh thy \[11\]
substance.   <span id="fr_700"></span>[701](#fn_700.md) And as ye desire
that men should do to you, so do ye also to them.

<span id="iv.iii.ix-p20"></span>\[12, 13\]  <span
id="fr_701"></span>[702](#fn_701.md) Ye have heard that it was said, Love
thy neighbour and hate thine enemy:   <span
id="fr_702"></span>[703](#fn_702.md) but I say unto you, Love your enemies,
and pray for those that curse you, and deal well <span id="page_58">p.
58</span> <span id="iv.iii.ix-Page_58"></span>with those that hate you,
and pray for those who take you with violence and persecute you; \[14\]
 <span id="fr_703"></span>[704](#fn_703.md) that ye may be sons of your
heavenly Father, who maketh his sun to rise on the good and the evil,
and sendeth down his rain on the righteous and the \[15\] unrighteous. 
 <span id="fr_704"></span>[705](#fn_704.md) If ye love them that love you,
what reward shall ye have?  <span id="fr_705"></span>[706](#fn_705.md) for
the publicans \[16\] and sinners also love those that love them.   <span
id="fr_706"></span>[707](#fn_706.md) And if ye do a kindness to those
\[17\] who treat you well, where is your superiority? for sinners also
do likewise.   <span id="fr_707"></span>[708](#fn_707.md) And if ye lend to
him of whom ye hope for a reward, <span
id="fr_708"></span>[709](#fn_708.md) where is your superiority? for the
\[18\] sinners also lend to sinners, seeking recompense from <span
id="fr_709"></span>[710](#fn_709.md) them.   <span
id="fr_710"></span>[711](#fn_710.md) But love your enemies, and do good to
them, and lend, and cut not off the hope of any man; that your reward
may be great, and ye may be the children of the Highest:  for he is
lenient \[19\] towards the wicked and the ungrateful.   <span
id="fr_711"></span>[712](#fn_711.md) Be ye merciful, even as your Father
also is \[20\] merciful.   <span id="fr_712"></span>[713](#fn_712.md) And
if ye inquire for the good of your brethren only, what more have \[21\]
ye done *than others?* is not this the conduct of the publicans also? 
 <span id="fr_713"></span>[714](#fn_713.md) Be ye now <span
id="fr_714"></span>[715](#fn_714.md) perfect, even as your Father which is
in heaven is perfect.

<span id="iv.iii.ix-p35"></span>\[22\]  <span
id="fr_715"></span>[716](#fn_715.md) Consider your alms; do them not before
men to let them see you:  and if it be not \[23\] so, <span
id="fr_716"></span>[717](#fn_716.md) ye have no reward before your Father
which is in the heavens.   <span id="fr_717"></span>[718](#fn_717.md) When
then thou givest an alms now, do not sound a trumpet before thee, as do
the people of hypocrisy, \[Arabic, p. 36\] in the synagogues and the
marketplaces, that men may praise them.  And \[24\] verily say I unto
you, They have received their reward.   <span
id="fr_718"></span>[719](#fn_718.md) But thou, when \[25\] thou doest alms,
let thy left hand not know what thy right hand doeth;  <span
id="fr_719"></span>[720](#fn_719.md) that thine alms may be concealed:  and
thy Father which seeth in secret shall reward thee openly.

<span id="iv.iii.ix-p41"></span>\[26\]  <span
id="fr_720"></span>[721](#fn_720.md) And whenever thou prayest, be not as
the hypocrites, who love to stand in the synagogues and in the corners
of the marketplaces for prayers, that men may behold \[27\] them. 
 <span id="fr_721"></span>[722](#fn_721.md) And verily say I unto you, They
have received their reward.  But thou, when thou prayest, enter into thy
closet, and fasten thy door, and pray to thy Father in secret, and thy
Father which seeth in secret shall reward thee openly.  \[28\]  <span
id="fr_722"></span>[723](#fn_722.md) And whenever ye pray, be not babblers,
as the heathen; for they think that by the \[29\] abundance of their
words they shall be heard.   <span id="fr_723"></span>[724](#fn_723.md)
Then be not ye now like unto them:  \[30\] for your Father knoweth your
request before ye ask him.   <span id="fr_724"></span>[725](#fn_724.md) One
of his disciples said \[31\] unto him, Our Lord, teach us to pray, as
John taught his disciples.   <span id="fr_725"></span>[726](#fn_725.md)
Jesus said unto \[32\] them, Thus now pray ye now: <span
id="fr_726"></span>[727](#fn_726.md)    <span
id="fr_727"></span>[728](#fn_727.md) Our Father which art in heaven,
Hallowed be thy \[33, 34\] name.   <span
id="fr_728"></span>[729](#fn_728.md) Thy kingdom come.  Thy will be
*done*, <span id="fr_729"></span>[730](#fn_729.md) as in heaven, so on
earth.  <span id="fr_730"></span>[731](#fn_730.md) Give us the \[35\] food
of to-day.   <span id="fr_731"></span>[732](#fn_731.md) And forgive us our
trespasses, as we forgave those that trespass*ed* \[36\] against us. 
 <span id="fr_732"></span>[733](#fn_732.md) And bring us not into
temptations, but deliver us from the evil one. For \[37\] thine is the
kingdom, and the power, and the glory, for ever and ever. <span
id="fr_733"></span>[734](#fn_733.md)    <span
id="fr_734"></span>[735](#fn_734.md) If ye forgive \[Arabic, p. 37\] men
their wrong-doing, <span id="fr_735"></span>[736](#fn_735.md) your Father
which is in heaven will forgive you.  \[38\]  <span
id="fr_736"></span>[737](#fn_736.md) But if ye forgive not men, neither
will your Father pardon your wrong-doing.

<span id="iv.iii.ix-p59"></span>\[39\]  <span
id="fr_737"></span>[738](#fn_737.md) When ye fast, do not frown, as the
hypocrites; for they make their faces austere, that they may be seen
of <span id="fr_738"></span>[739](#fn_738.md) men that they are fasting. 
Verily I say unto you, They \[40\] have received their reward.   <span
id="fr_739"></span>[740](#fn_739.md) But when thou fastest, wash thy face
and anoint thy \[41\] head;  <span id="fr_740"></span>[741](#fn_740.md)
that thou make not an appearance to men of fasting, but to thy Father
which is in secret:  and thy Father which seeth in secret shall reward
thee.

<span id="iv.iii.ix-p64"></span>\[42\]  <span
id="fr_741"></span>[742](#fn_741.md) Be not agitated, little flock; for
your Father hath delighted to give you the kingdom.  \[43\]  <span
id="fr_742"></span>[743](#fn_742.md) Sell your possessions, and give in
alms; take to yourselves purses that wax \[44\] not old.   <span
id="fr_743"></span>[744](#fn_743.md) Lay not up treasure on earth, where
moth and worm corrupt, and where \[45\] thieves break through and
steal:   <span id="fr_744"></span>[745](#fn_744.md) but lay up for
yourselves treasure in heaven, where \[46\] moth and worm do not
corrupt, nor thieves break through nor steal:   <span
id="fr_745"></span>[746](#fn_745.md) for where your \[47\] treasure is,
there also will your heart be.   <span id="fr_746"></span>[747](#fn_746.md)
The lamp of the body is the eye:  if then <span
id="fr_747"></span>[748](#fn_747.md) \[48\] thine eye now be sound, thy
whole body also shall be light.   <span
id="fr_748"></span>[749](#fn_748.md) But if thine eye be evil, all thy body
shall be dark.  And if the light which is in thee is darkness, how
\[49\] great is <span id="fr_749"></span>[750](#fn_749.md) thy darkness! 
 <span id="fr_750"></span>[751](#fn_750.md) Be watchful that the light
which is in thee be not darkness.  \[50\]  <span
id="fr_751"></span>[752](#fn_751.md) Because that, if thy whole body is
light, and have no part dark, it shall all be light, as the lamp giveth
light to thee with its flame.
