<span id="xii.iv.xxxvii-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="xii.iv.xxxvii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXXVII.—Christ is Our Leader, and
We His Soldiers.</span>

<span id="xii.iv.xxxvii-p2"></span>Let us then, men and brethren, with
all energy act the part of soldiers, in accordance with His holy
commandments.  Let us consider those who serve under our generals, with
what order, obedience, <span id="fr_4198"></span>[4199](#fn_4198) and
submissiveness they perform the things which are commanded them.  All
are not prefects, nor commanders of a thousand, nor of a hundred, nor of
fifty, nor the like, but each one in his own rank performs the things
commanded by the king and the generals.  The great cannot subsist
without the small, nor the small without the great.  There is a kind of
mixture in all things, and thence arises mutual advantage. <span
id="fr_4199"></span>[4200](#fn_4199)   Let us take our body for an
example. <span id="fr_4200"></span>[4201](#fn_4200)   The head is
nothing without the feet, and the feet are nothing without the head;
yea, the very smallest members of our body are necessary and useful to
the whole body.  But all work <span id="fr_4201"></span>[4202](#fn_4201)
harmoniously together, and are under one common rule <span
id="fr_4202"></span>[4203](#fn_4202) for the preservation of the whole
body.
