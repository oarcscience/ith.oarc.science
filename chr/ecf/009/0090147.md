<span id="xii.iv.lii-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="xii.iv.lii-p1.1"></span>Chapter LII.—Such a Confession is Pleasing
to God.</span>

<span id="xii.iv.lii-p2"></span>The Lord, brethren, stands in need of
nothing; and He desires nothing of any one except that confession be
made to Him.  <span id="page_245">p. 245</span> <span
id="xii.iv.lii-Page_245"></span>For, says the elect David, “I will
confess unto the Lord; and that will please Him more than a young
bullock <span id="fr_4277"></span>[4278](#fn_4277.md) that hath horns and
hoofs.  Let the poor see it, and be glad.” <span
id="fr_4278"></span>[4279](#fn_4278.md)   And again he saith, “Offer <span
id="fr_4279"></span>[4280](#fn_4279.md) unto God the sacrifice of praise,
and pay thy vows unto the Most High.  And call upon me in the day of thy
trouble:  I will deliver thee, and thou shalt glorify me.” <span
id="fr_4280"></span>[4281](#fn_4280.md)   For “the sacrifice of God is a
broken spirit.” <span id="fr_4281"></span>[4282](#fn_4281.md)
