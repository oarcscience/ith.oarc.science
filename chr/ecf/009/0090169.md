<span id="xii.vi.vii-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="xii.vi.vii-p1.1"></span>Chapter VII.—We Must Strive in Order to Be
Crowned.</span>

<span id="xii.vi.vii-p2"></span>Wherefore, then, my brethren, let us
struggle with all earnestness, knowing that the contest is \[in our
case\] close at hand, and that many undertake long voyages to strive for
a corruptible reward; <span id="fr_4367"></span>[4368](#fn_4367) yet all
are not crowned, but those only that have laboured hard and striven
gloriously.  Let us therefore so strive, that we may all be crowned. 
Let us run the straight <span id="fr_4368"></span>[4369](#fn_4368)
course, even the race that is incorruptible; and let us in great numbers
set out <span id="fr_4369"></span>[4370](#fn_4369) for it, and strive
that we may be crowned.  And should we not all be able to obtain the
crown, let us at least come near to it.  We must remember <span
id="fr_4370"></span>[4371](#fn_4370) that he who strives in the
corruptible contest, if he be found acting unfairly, <span
id="fr_4371"></span>[4372](#fn_4371) is taken away and scourged, and
cast forth from the lists.  What then think ye?  If one does anything
unseemly in the incorruptible contest, what shall he have to bear?  For
of those who do not preserve the seal <span
id="fr_4372"></span>[4373](#fn_4372) \[unbroken\], \[the Scripture\]
saith, “Their worm shall not die, and their fire shall not be quenched,
and they shall be a spectacle to all flesh.” <span
id="fr_4373"></span>[4374](#fn_4373)
