<span id="xii.iv.xvii-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="xii.iv.xvii-p1.1"></span>Chapter XVII.—The Saints as Examples of
Humility.</span>

<span id="xii.iv.xvii-p2"></span>Let us be imitators also of those who
in goat-skins and sheep-skins <span id="fr_4078"></span>[4079](#fn_4078)
went about proclaiming the coming of Christ; I mean Elijah, Elisha, and
Ezekiel among the prophets, with those others to whom a like testimony
is borne \[in Scripture\].  Abraham was specially honoured, and was
called the friend of God; yet he, earnestly regarding the glory of God,
humbly declared, “I am but dust and ashes.” <span
id="fr_4079"></span>[4080](#fn_4079)   Moreover, it is thus written of
Job, “Job was a righteous man, and blameless, truthful, God-fearing, and
one that kept himself from all evil.” <span
id="fr_4080"></span>[4081](#fn_4080)   But bringing an accusation
against himself, he said, “No man is free from defilement, even if his
life be but of one day.” <span id="fr_4081"></span>[4082](#fn_4081)  
Moses was called faithful in all God’s house; <span
id="fr_4082"></span>[4083](#fn_4082) and through his
instrumentality, <span id="fr_4083"></span>[4084](#fn_4083) God punished
Egypt with plagues and tortures.  Yet he, though thus greatly honoured,
did not adopt lofty language, but said, when the divine oracle came to
him out of the bush, “Who am I, that Thou sendest me?  I am a man of a
feeble voice and a slow tongue.” <span
id="fr_4084"></span>[4085](#fn_4084)   And again he said, “I am but as
the smoke of a pot.” <span id="fr_4085"></span>[4086](#fn_4085)
