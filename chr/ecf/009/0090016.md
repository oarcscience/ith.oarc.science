<span id="iv.iii.vii-p1"></span> <span id="page_54">p. 54</span> <span
id="iv.iii.vii-Page_54"></span><span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.vii-p1.1"></span>Section VII.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.vii-p2"></span>\[1\] \[Arabic, p. 26\]  <span
id="fr_522"></span>[523](#fn_522) And in the morning of that day he went
out very early, and went to a \[2\] desert place, and was there
praying.   <span id="fr_523"></span>[524](#fn_523) And Simon and those
that were with \[3\] him sought him.   <span
id="fr_524"></span>[525](#fn_524) And when they found him, they said
unto him, All the people seek for \[4\] thee.   <span
id="fr_525"></span>[526](#fn_525) He said unto them, Let us go into the
adjacent villages and towns, that I may \[5\] preach there also; for to
this end did I come.   <span id="fr_526"></span>[527](#fn_526) And the
multitudes were seeking him, and came till they reached him; and they
took hold of him, that he should not \[6\] go away from them.   <span
id="fr_527"></span>[528](#fn_527) But Jesus said unto them, I must
preach of the kingdom of \[7\] God in other cities also:  for because of
this gospel was I sent.   <span id="fr_528"></span>[529](#fn_528) And
Jesus was going about all the cities and the villages, and teaching in
their synagogues, and preaching the gospel of the kingdom, and healing
all the diseases and all the sicknesses, \[8\]  <span
id="fr_529"></span>[530](#fn_529) and casting out the devils.   <span
id="fr_530"></span>[531](#fn_530) And his fame became known  <span
id="fr_531"></span>[532](#fn_531) that <span
id="fr_532"></span>[533](#fn_532) he was teaching in \[9\] every place
and being glorified by every man.   <span
id="fr_533"></span>[534](#fn_533) And when he passed by, he saw Levi the
son of Alphæus sitting among the tax-gatherers; <span
id="fr_534"></span>[535](#fn_534) and he said unto him, Follow \[10\]
me:  and he rose and followed him.   <span
id="fr_535"></span>[536](#fn_535) And the news of him was heard of in
all the land of Syria:  and they brought unto him all those whom
grievous ills had befallen through divers diseases, and those that were
enduring torment, and those that were possessed, and lunatics, <span
id="fr_536"></span>[537](#fn_536) and paralytics; and he healed them.

<span id="iv.iii.vii-p18"></span>\[11, 12\]  <span
id="fr_537"></span>[538](#fn_537) And after some days Jesus entered into
Capernaum again.   <span id="fr_538"></span>[539](#fn_538) And when they
heard that he was in the house, <span id="fr_539"></span>[540](#fn_539)
many gathered, so that it could not hold them, even about \[13\]
\[Arabic, p. 27\] the door; and he made known to them the word of God. 
 <span id="fr_540"></span>[541](#fn_540) And there were there some of
the Pharisees and the teachers of the law, sitting, come from all the
villages of Galilee, and Judæa, and Jerusalem; and the power of the Lord
was \[14\] present to heal them.   <span
id="fr_541"></span>[542](#fn_541) And some men brought a bed with a man
on it who was paralytic.  \[15\] And they sought to bring him in and lay
him before him.   <span id="fr_542"></span>[543](#fn_542) And when they
found no way to bring him in because of the multitude of people, they
went up to the roof, and let him down with his bed from the
roofing, <span id="fr_543"></span>[544](#fn_543) into the midst before
Jesus.  \[16\]  <span id="fr_544"></span>[545](#fn_544) And when Jesus
saw their faith, he said unto the paralytic, My son, thy sins are
forgiven \[17\] thee.   <span id="fr_545"></span>[546](#fn_545) And the
scribes and Pharisees began to think within their hearts, Why doth this
man blaspheme? <span id="fr_546"></span>[547](#fn_546)   Who is it that
is able to forgive sins, but God alone?  \[18\]  <span
id="fr_547"></span>[548](#fn_547) And Jesus knew by the spirit that they
were thinking this within themselves, and he \[19\] said unto them, Why
do ye think this within your heart?   <span
id="fr_548"></span>[549](#fn_548) Which is better, <span
id="fr_549"></span>[550](#fn_549) that it should be said to the
paralytic, Thy sins are forgiven thee, or *that* it should be said
\[20\] to him, Arise, and take thy bed, and walk?   <span
id="fr_550"></span>[551](#fn_550) That ye may know that the Son of man
\[21\] is empowered on earth to forgive sins (and he said to the
paralytic),  <span id="fr_551"></span>[552](#fn_551) I say unto thee,
\[22\] Arise, take thy bed, and go to thine house.   <span
id="fr_552"></span>[553](#fn_552) And he rose forthwith, and took his
bed, and went out in the presence of all.   <span
id="fr_553"></span>[554](#fn_553) And he went to his house praising
God.  \[23\]  <span id="fr_554"></span>[555](#fn_554) And when those
multitudes saw, they feared;  <span id="fr_555"></span>[556](#fn_555)
and amazement took possession of \[24\] them,  <span
id="fr_556"></span>[557](#fn_556) and they praised God, who had given
such power to men.   <span id="fr_557"></span>[558](#fn_557) And they
said, We have seen marvellous things to-day,  <span
id="fr_558"></span>[559](#fn_558) of which we have never before seen the
like.

<span id="iv.iii.vii-p41"></span>\[25\] \[Arabic, p. 28\]  <span
id="fr_559"></span>[560](#fn_559) And after that, Jesus went out, and
saw a publican, named Levi, sitting \[26\] among the publicans: <span
id="fr_560"></span>[561](#fn_560)   and he said unto him, Follow me. 
 <span id="fr_561"></span>[562](#fn_561) And he left \[27\] everything,
and rose, and followed him.   <span id="fr_562"></span>[563](#fn_562)
And Levi made him a great feast in his house.  And there was a great
multitude of the publicans and others sitting with him.  \[28\]  <span
id="fr_563"></span>[564](#fn_563) And the scribes and Pharisees
murmured, and said unto his disciples, Why do ye eat \[29\] and drink
with the publicans and sinners?   <span
id="fr_564"></span>[565](#fn_564) Jesus answered and said unto them, The
physician seeketh not those who are well, but those that are afflicted
with grievous \[30, 31\] sickness. <span
id="fr_565"></span>[566](#fn_565)    <span
id="fr_566"></span>[567](#fn_566) I came not to call the righteous, but
the sinners, to repentance.   <span id="fr_567"></span>[568](#fn_567)
And they said unto him, Why do the disciples of John fast always, and
pray, and the \[32\] Pharisees also, but thy disciples eat and drink? 
 <span id="fr_568"></span>[569](#fn_568) He said unto them, Ye cannot
make \[33\] the sons of the marriage feast <span
id="fr_569"></span>[570](#fn_569) fast, while the bridegroom is with
them.   <span id="fr_570"></span>[571](#fn_570) Days will <span
id="page_55">p. 55</span> <span id="iv.iii.vii-Page_55"></span>come,
when the bridegroom is taken away from them; then will they fast in
those \[34\] days.   <span id="fr_571"></span>[572](#fn_571) And he
spake unto them a parable:   <span id="fr_572"></span>[573](#fn_572) No
man inserteth a new patch and seweth it in a worn garment, lest the
newness of the new take from the worn, and \[35\] there occur a great
rent.   <span id="fr_573"></span>[574](#fn_573) And no man putteth fresh
wine into old skins, lest the wine burst the skins, and the skins be
destroyed, and the wine spilled; but they put \[36\] the fresh wine in
the new skins, and both are preserved.   <span
id="fr_574"></span>[575](#fn_574) And no man drinketh old wine and
straightway desireth fresh; for he saith, The old is better.

<span id="iv.iii.vii-p58"></span>\[37\]  <span
id="fr_575"></span>[576](#fn_575) And while Jesus was walking on the
sabbath day among the sown fields, his disciples \[Arabic, p. 29\]
hungered.  And they were rubbing the ears with their hands, and \[38\]
eating.   <span id="fr_576"></span>[577](#fn_576) But some of the
Pharisees, when they saw them,  <span id="fr_577"></span>[578](#fn_577)
said unto him, See, \[39\] why <span id="fr_578"></span>[579](#fn_578)
do thy disciples on the sabbath day that which is not lawful?   <span
id="fr_579"></span>[580](#fn_579) But Jesus said unto them, Have ye not
read in olden time what David did, when he had need and \[40\] hungered,
he and those that were with him?  <span
id="fr_580"></span>[581](#fn_580) how he entered the house of God, when
Abiathar was high priest, and ate the bread of the table of the Lord,
which it was not lawful that any should eat, save the priests, and gave
to them that were with him also?  \[41\]  <span
id="fr_581"></span>[582](#fn_581) And he said unto them, The sabbath was
created because of man, and man was not \[42\] created because of the
sabbath.   <span id="fr_582"></span>[583](#fn_582) Or have ye not read
in the law, that the priests in \[43\] the temple profane the sabbath,
and *yet* they are blameless?   <span id="fr_583"></span>[584](#fn_583)
I say unto you now, \[44\] that here is what <span
id="fr_584"></span>[585](#fn_584) is greater than the temple.   <span
id="fr_585"></span>[586](#fn_585) If ye had known *this*: <span
id="fr_586"></span>[587](#fn_586)   I love mercy, \[45\] not sacrifice,
ye would not have condemned <span id="fr_587"></span>[588](#fn_587)
those on whom is no blame.   <span id="fr_588"></span>[589](#fn_588) The
\[46\] Lord of the sabbath is the Son of man.   <span
id="fr_589"></span>[590](#fn_589) And his relatives heard, and went out
to take him, and said, He hath gone out of his mind.

<span id="iv.iii.vii-p74"></span>\[47\]  <span
id="fr_590"></span>[591](#fn_590) And on the next <span
id="fr_591"></span>[592](#fn_591) sabbath day he entered <span
id="fr_592"></span>[593](#fn_592) into the synagogue and was teaching. 
\[48\]  <span id="fr_593"></span>[594](#fn_593) And there was there a
man whose right hand was withered.  And the scribes and the Pharisees
were watching him, whether he would heal on the sabbath day, \[49\] that
they might find the means of accusing him.   <span
id="fr_594"></span>[595](#fn_594) But he knew their thoughts, and said
unto the man whose hand was withered, Rise and come near into the midst
of \[50\] the synagogue.   <span id="fr_595"></span>[596](#fn_595) And
when he came and stood, Jesus said unto them, I ask you, which is lawful
to be done on the sabbath day, good or evil? shall lives be saved or
\[51\] \[Arabic, p. 30\] destroyed?   <span
id="fr_596"></span>[597](#fn_596) But they were silent.   <span
id="fr_597"></span>[598](#fn_597) Regarding <span
id="fr_598"></span>[599](#fn_598) them with anger, being grieved because
of the hardness of their hearts.  And he said unto the man, Stretch out
thy hand.  And he stretched it out:  and his hand became straight. 
\[52\]  <span id="fr_599"></span>[600](#fn_599) Then he said unto them,
What man of you shall have one sheep, and if it fall into a \[53\] well
on the sabbath day, will not take it and lift it out?   <span
id="fr_600"></span>[601](#fn_600) And how much is man better than a
sheep!  Wherefore it is lawful on the sabbath to do good.
