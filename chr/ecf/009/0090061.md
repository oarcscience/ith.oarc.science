<span id="iv.iii.lii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.lii-p1.1"></span>Section LII.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.lii-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_3617"></span>[3618](#fn_3617.md) And after that, Jesus knew that all
things were finished; and that the scripture \[2\] might be
accomplished, he said, I thirst.   <span
id="fr_3618"></span>[3619](#fn_3618.md) And there was set a vessel full of
vinegar:  and in that hour one of them hasted, and took a sponge, and
filled it with that \[3\] vinegar,  <span
id="fr_3619"></span>[3620](#fn_3619.md) and fastened it on a reed, and
brought it near <span id="fr_3620"></span>[3621](#fn_3620.md) his mouth to
give him a \[4\] drink.   <span id="fr_3621"></span>[3622](#fn_3621.md) And
when Jesus had taken that vinegar, he said, Everything is finished. 
\[5\]  <span id="fr_3622"></span>[3623](#fn_3622.md) But the rest said, Let
be, that we may <span id="fr_3623"></span>[3624](#fn_3623.md) see whether
Elijah cometh to save him.  \[6, 7\]  <span
id="fr_3624"></span>[3625](#fn_3624.md) And Jesus said, My Father, forgive
them; for they know not what they do.  And Jesus cried again with a loud
voice, and said, My Father, into thy hands I commend <span
id="fr_3625"></span>[3626](#fn_3625.md) my spirit.   <span
id="fr_3626"></span>[3627](#fn_3626.md) He said that, and bowed his head,
and gave up his spirit.

<span id="iv.iii.lii-p13"></span>\[8\]  <span
id="fr_3627"></span>[3628](#fn_3627.md) And immediately the face of <span
id="fr_3628"></span>[3629](#fn_3628.md) the door of the temple was rent
into two parts from \[9\] top to bottom;  <span
id="fr_3629"></span>[3630](#fn_3629.md) and the earth was shaken; and the
stones were split to pieces; and the \[Arabic, p. 197\] tombs were
opened; and the bodies of many saints which slept, arose and \[10\] came
forth;  <span id="fr_3630"></span>[3631](#fn_3630.md) and after his
resurrection they entered into the holy city and \[11\] appeared unto
many.   <span id="fr_3631"></span>[3632](#fn_3631.md) And the officer of
the footsoldiers, and they that were with him who were guarding
Jesus, <span id="fr_3632"></span>[3633](#fn_3632.md) when they saw the
earthquake, and the things which came \[12\] to pass, feared greatly,
and praised God, and said,  <span id="fr_3633"></span>[3634](#fn_3633.md)
This man *was* righteous; and, \[13\] Truly he was the Son of God. 
 <span id="fr_3634"></span>[3635](#fn_3634.md) And all the multitudes that
were come together to the sight, when they saw what came to pass,
returned and smote upon their breasts.

<span id="iv.iii.lii-p22"></span><span id="page_124">p. 124</span> <span
id="iv.iii.lii-Page_124"></span>\[14\]  <span
id="fr_3635"></span>[3636](#fn_3635.md) And the Jews, because of the
Friday, said, Let these bodies not remain on their crosses, <span
id="fr_3636"></span>[3637](#fn_3636.md) because it is the morning of the
sabbath (for that sabbath was a great day); and they asked of Pilate
that they might break the legs of those that were \[15\] crucified, and
take them down.   <span id="fr_3637"></span>[3638](#fn_3637.md) And the
soldiers came, and brake the legs of the \[16\] first, and that other
which was crucified with him:   <span
id="fr_3638"></span>[3639](#fn_3638.md) but when they came to Jesus, \[17\]
they saw that he had died before, so they brake not his legs:   <span
id="fr_3639"></span>[3640](#fn_3639.md) but one of the soldiers
pierced <span id="fr_3640"></span>[3641](#fn_3640.md) him in his side with
a spear, and immediately there came forth blood and \[18\] water. 
 <span id="fr_3641"></span>[3642](#fn_3641.md) And he that hath seen hath
borne witness, and his witness is true:  and he \[19\] knoweth that he
hath said the truth, that ye also may believe.   <span
id="fr_3642"></span>[3643](#fn_3642.md) This he did, that \[20\] the
scripture might be fulfilled, which saith, A bone shall not be broken in
him;  <span id="fr_3643"></span>[3644](#fn_3643.md) and the scripture also
which saith, Let them look upon him whom they pierced. <span
id="fr_3644"></span>[3645](#fn_3644.md)

<span id="iv.iii.lii-p33"></span>\[21\]  <span
id="fr_3645"></span>[3646](#fn_3645.md) And there were in the distance all
the acquaintance of Jesus standing, and the women that came with him
from Galilee, those that followed him and ministered.  \[22\]  <span
id="fr_3646"></span>[3647](#fn_3646.md) One of them was Mary Magdalene; and
Mary the mother of James the little and \[23\] \[Arabic, p. 198\] Joses,
 <span id="fr_3647"></span>[3648](#fn_3647.md) and the mother of the sons
of Zebedee, and Salome, and many others which came up with him unto
Jerusalem;  <span id="fr_3648"></span>[3649](#fn_3648.md) and they saw
that.

<span id="iv.iii.lii-p38"></span>\[24\]  <span
id="fr_3649"></span>[3650](#fn_3649.md) And when the evening of the Friday
was come, because of the entering of the \[25\] sabbath,  <span
id="fr_3650"></span>[3651](#fn_3650.md) there came a rich man, <span
id="fr_3651"></span>[3652](#fn_3651.md) a noble <span
id="fr_3652"></span>[3653](#fn_3652.md) of Ramah, <span
id="fr_3653"></span>[3654](#fn_3653.md) a city of Judah, <span
id="fr_3654"></span>[3655](#fn_3654.md) named Joseph, and he was a good man
and upright;  <span id="fr_3655"></span>[3656](#fn_3655.md) and he was
a <span id="fr_3656"></span>[3657](#fn_3656.md) disciple of Jesus, but
\[26\] was concealing himself for fear of the Jews.   <span
id="fr_3657"></span>[3658](#fn_3657.md) And he did not agree with the
accusers \[27\] in their desire and their deeds:   <span
id="fr_3658"></span>[3659](#fn_3658.md) and he was looking for the kingdom
of God.   <span id="fr_3659"></span>[3660](#fn_3659.md) And this man went
boldly, and entered in unto Pilate, and asked of him the body of \[28\]
Jesus.   <span id="fr_3660"></span>[3661](#fn_3660.md) And Pilate wondered
how he had died already:  and he called the officer of \[29\] the
footsoldiers, and asked him concerning his death before the time. 
 <span id="fr_3661"></span>[3662](#fn_3661.md) And when \[30\] he knew, he
commanded him to deliver up his body unto Joseph.   <span
id="fr_3662"></span>[3663](#fn_3662.md) And Joseph bought for him a winding
cloth of pure linen, and took down the body of Jesus, \[31\] and wound
it in it; and they came and took it.   <span
id="fr_3663"></span>[3664](#fn_3663.md) And there came unto him Nicodemus
also, who of old came unto Jesus by night; and he brought with him
perfume <span id="fr_3664"></span>[3665](#fn_3664.md) \[32\] of myrrh and
aloes, about a hundred pounds.   <span
id="fr_3665"></span>[3666](#fn_3665.md) And they took the body of Jesus,
and wound it in the linen and the perfume, as was the custom of the Jews
to bury.

<span id="iv.iii.lii-p56"></span>\[33\]  <span
id="fr_3666"></span>[3667](#fn_3666.md) And there was in the place where
Jesus was crucified a garden; and in that garden \[34\] a new tomb cut
out in a rock, <span id="fr_3667"></span>[3668](#fn_3667.md) wherein was
never man yet laid.   <span id="fr_3668"></span>[3669](#fn_3668.md) And
they left \[35\] Jesus there because the sabbath had come in, and
because the tomb was near.   <span id="fr_3669"></span>[3670](#fn_3669.md)
And they pushed <span id="fr_3670"></span>[3671](#fn_3670.md) a great
stone, and thrust <span id="fr_3671"></span>[3672](#fn_3671.md) it against
the door of the sepulchre, and \[36\] went away.   <span
id="fr_3672"></span>[3673](#fn_3672.md) And Mary Magdalene and Mary that
was related to Joses came to \[37\] \[Arabic, p. 199\] the sepulchre
after them, <span id="fr_3673"></span>[3674](#fn_3673.md)    <span
id="fr_3674"></span>[3675](#fn_3674.md) and sat opposite the
sepulchre, <span id="fr_3675"></span>[3676](#fn_3675.md) and saw the \[38\]
body, how they took it in and laid it there.   <span
id="fr_3676"></span>[3677](#fn_3676.md) And they returned, and bought
ointment <span id="fr_3677"></span>[3678](#fn_3677.md) and perfume, <span
id="fr_3678"></span>[3679](#fn_3678.md) and prepared <span
id="fr_3679"></span>[3680](#fn_3679.md) *it*, that they might come and
anoint him.  \[39\]  <span id="fr_3680"></span>[3681](#fn_3680.md) And on
the day which was the sabbath day they desisted according to the
command.

<span id="iv.iii.lii-p72"></span>\[40, 41\]  <span
id="fr_3681"></span>[3682](#fn_3681.md) And the chief priests and the
Pharisees gathered unto Pilate, and said unto him,  <span
id="fr_3682"></span>[3683](#fn_3682.md) Our lord, we remember that that
misleader said, while he was alive, After three days \[42\] I rise. 
 <span id="fr_3683"></span>[3684](#fn_3683.md) And now send beforehand and
guard the tomb <span id="fr_3684"></span>[3685](#fn_3684.md) until the
third day, <span id="fr_3685"></span>[3686](#fn_3685.md) lest his disciples
come and steal him by night, and they will say unto the people that he
\[43\] is risen from the dead:  and the last error shall be worse than
the first.   <span id="fr_3686"></span>[3687](#fn_3686.md) He said unto
them, And have ye not guards? <span id="fr_3687"></span>[3688](#fn_3687.md)
go, and take precautions as ye know *how*.  \[44\]  <span
id="fr_3688"></span>[3689](#fn_3688.md) And they went, and set *guards* at
the tomb, and sealed that stone, with the guards.

<span id="iv.iii.lii-p81"></span>\[45\]  <span
id="fr_3689"></span>[3690](#fn_3689.md) And in the evening of the sabbath,
which is the morning of the first *day*, and in \[46\] the dawning <span
id="fr_3690"></span>[3691](#fn_3690.md) while the darkness yet remained,
came Mary Magdalene and the other Mary and other women to see the tomb. 
 <span id="fr_3691"></span>[3692](#fn_3691.md) They brought with them the
\[47\] perfume which they had prepared, and said among themselves,
 <span id="fr_3692"></span>[3693](#fn_3692.md) Who is it that will <span
id="page_125">p. 125</span> <span id="iv.iii.lii-Page_125"></span>\[48\]
remove for us the stone from the door of the tomb? for it was very
great.   <span id="fr_3693"></span>[3694](#fn_3693.md) And when they said
thus, there occurred a great earthquake; and an angel came down \[49\]
from heaven, and came and removed the stone from the door.   <span
id="fr_3694"></span>[3695](#fn_3694.md) And they came and found the stone
removed from the sepulchre, and the angel sitting upon the \[50\]
stone.   <span id="fr_3695"></span>[3696](#fn_3695.md) And his appearance
was as the lightning, and his raiment white as the \[51\] snow:   <span
id="fr_3696"></span>[3697](#fn_3696.md) and for fear of him the guards were
troubled, and became as dead *men*.  \[52\]  <span
id="fr_3697"></span>[3698](#fn_3697.md) And when he went away, the women
entered into the sepulchre; and they found \[53\] \[Arabic, p. 200\] not
the body of Jesus.   <span id="fr_3698"></span>[3699](#fn_3698.md) And they
saw there a young man sitting on the \[54\] right, arrayed in a white
garment; and they were amazed. <span
id="fr_3699"></span>[3700](#fn_3699.md)    <span
id="fr_3700"></span>[3701](#fn_3700.md) And the angel answered and said
unto the women, Fear ye not:  for I know that ye seek Jesus the \[55\]
Nazarene, who hath been crucified.  He is not here; but he is risen, as
he said.   <span id="fr_3701"></span>[3702](#fn_3701.md) Come and see the
place where our Lord lay.
