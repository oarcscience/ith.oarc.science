<span id="iv.iii.xliv-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xliv-p1.1"></span>Section XLIV.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xliv-p2"></span>\[1, 2\]  <span
id="fr_3016"></span>[3017](#fn_3016.md) And when Jesus <span
id="fr_3017"></span>[3018](#fn_3017.md) finished all these sayings, he said
unto his disciples,  <span id="fr_3018"></span>[3019](#fn_3018.md) Ye know
that after two days will be the passover, and the Son of man is
delivered up to be \[3\] crucified.   <span
id="fr_3019"></span>[3020](#fn_3019.md) Then gathered together the chief
priests, and the scribes, and the elders \[4\] of the people, unto the
court of the chief priest, who was called Caiaphas;  <span
id="fr_3020"></span>[3021](#fn_3020.md) and they took counsel together
concerning Jesus, that they might seize him by subtilty, and \[5\] kill
him.   <span id="fr_3021"></span>[3022](#fn_3021.md) But they said, Not
during the feast, lest there take place a disturbance among the people;
 <span id="fr_3022"></span>[3023](#fn_3022.md) for they feared the people.

<span id="iv.iii.xliv-p10"></span>\[6\]  <span
id="fr_3023"></span>[3024](#fn_3023.md) And Satan entered into Judas who
was called Iscariot, who was of the number \[7\] of the twelve.   <span
id="fr_3024"></span>[3025](#fn_3024.md) And he went away, and communed with
the chief priests, and the scribes, and those that held command in the
temple, and said unto them, What \[8\] \[Arabic, p. 167\] would ye pay
me, and I will deliver him unto you?   <span
id="fr_3025"></span>[3026](#fn_3025.md) And they, when they heard *it*,
were pleased, and made ready <span id="fr_3026"></span>[3027](#fn_3026.md)
for him thirty *pieces* of money. <span
id="fr_3027"></span>[3028](#fn_3027.md)   \[9\]  <span
id="fr_3028"></span>[3029](#fn_3028.md) And he promised <span
id="fr_3029"></span>[3030](#fn_3029.md) them, and from that time he sought
an opportunity <span id="fr_3030"></span>[3031](#fn_3030.md) that he might
deliver unto them Jesus without the multitude.

<span id="iv.iii.xliv-p19"></span>\[10\]  <span
id="fr_3031"></span>[3032](#fn_3031.md) And on the first day of unleavened
bread the disciples came to Jesus, and said unto him, Where wilt thou
that we go and make ready for thee that thou mayest eat the passover?

<span id="iv.iii.xliv-p21"></span>\[11\]  <span
id="fr_3032"></span>[3033](#fn_3032.md) And before the feast of the
passover, Jesus knew that the hour was arrived for his departure from
this world unto his Father; and he loved his own in this world, \[12\]
and to the last he loved them.   <span
id="fr_3033"></span>[3034](#fn_3033.md) And at the time of the feast, Satan
put into the \[13\] heart of Judas, the son of Simon Iscariot, to
deliver him up.   <span id="fr_3034"></span>[3035](#fn_3034.md) And Jesus,
because he knew that the Father had delivered into his hands everything,
and that he came \[14\] forth from the Father, and goeth unto God,
 <span id="fr_3035"></span>[3036](#fn_3035.md) rose from supper, and laid
*aside* his \[15\] garments;  <span id="fr_3036"></span>[3037](#fn_3036.md)
and took a towel, *and* girded his waist, and poured water into a bason,
and began to wash the feet of his disciples, and to wipe them with the
towel wherewith \[16\] his waist was girded.   <span
id="fr_3037"></span>[3038](#fn_3037.md) And when he came to Simon Cephas,
Simon said unto \[17\] him, Dost thou, my Lord, wash for me my feet? 
 <span id="fr_3038"></span>[3039](#fn_3038.md) Jesus answered and said unto
\[18\] him, What I do, now thou knowest not; but afterwards thou shalt
learn.   <span id="fr_3039"></span>[3040](#fn_3039.md) Simon said unto him,
Thou shalt never wash for me my feet.  Jesus said unto him, If I \[19\]
wash thee not, thou hast no part with me.   <span
id="fr_3040"></span>[3041](#fn_3040.md) Simon Cephas said unto him, Then,
my \[20\] Lord, wash not for me my feet alone, but my hands also and my
head.   <span id="fr_3041"></span>[3042](#fn_3041.md) Jesus said unto him,
He that batheth <span id="fr_3042"></span>[3043](#fn_3042.md) needeth not
to wash save his feet, whereas his whole \[21\] *body* is clean:  and ye
also are clean, but not all of you.   <span
id="fr_3043"></span>[3044](#fn_3043.md) For Jesus knew him that should
betray him; therefore said he, Ye are not all clean.

<span id="iv.iii.xliv-p34"></span>\[22\] \[Arabic, p. 168\]  <span
id="fr_3044"></span>[3045](#fn_3044.md) So when he had washed their feet,
he took his garments, and sat down, and \[23\] said unto them, Know ye
what I have done unto you?   <span id="fr_3045"></span>[3046](#fn_3045.md)
Ye call me, Master, \[24\] and, Lord:  and ye say well; so I am.   <span
id="fr_3046"></span>[3047](#fn_3046.md) If then I, now, who am your Lord
and Master, have washed for you your feet, how needful is it that ye
should wash one another’s feet!  \[25\]  <span
id="fr_3047"></span>[3048](#fn_3047.md) This have I given you as an
example, that as I have done to you so ye should do \[26\] also.   <span
id="fr_3048"></span>[3049](#fn_3048.md) Verily, verily, I say unto you, No
servant is greater than his lord; nor an \[27\] apostle greater than he
that sent him.   <span id="fr_3049"></span>[3050](#fn_3049.md) If ye know
that, ye are happy if ye do it.  \[28\]  <span
id="fr_3050"></span>[3051](#fn_3050.md) My saying this <span
id="fr_3051"></span>[3052](#fn_3051.md) is not for all of you:  for I know
whom I have chosen:  but that the scripture might be fulfilled, He that
eateth with me bread lifted against me his <span id="page_112">p.
112</span> <span id="iv.iii.xliv-Page_112"></span>\[29\] heel.   <span
id="fr_3052"></span>[3053](#fn_3052.md) Henceforth I say unto you before it
come to pass, that, when it cometh to \[30\] pass, ye may believe that I
am *he*.   <span id="fr_3053"></span>[3054](#fn_3053.md) Verily, verily, I
say unto you, Whosoever receiveth whomsoever I send receiveth me; and
whosoever receiveth me receiveth him that sent me.

<span id="iv.iii.xliv-p45"></span>\[31\]  <span
id="fr_3054"></span>[3055](#fn_3054.md) Who is the great *one*, he that
sitteth, or he that serveth? is it not he that sitteth?  \[32\]  <span
id="fr_3055"></span>[3056](#fn_3055.md) I am among you as he that serveth. 
 <span id="fr_3056"></span>[3057](#fn_3056.md) But ye are they that have
continued with me \[33\] in my temptations;  <span
id="fr_3057"></span>[3058](#fn_3057.md) I promise <span
id="fr_3058"></span>[3059](#fn_3058.md) you, as my Father promised <span
id="fr_3059"></span>[3060](#fn_3059.md) me, the kingdom, that ye may eat
and drink at the table of my kingdom.

<span id="iv.iii.xliv-p52"></span>\[34\]  <span
id="fr_3060"></span>[3061](#fn_3060.md) And the first day <span
id="fr_3061"></span>[3062](#fn_3061.md) came, the feast of unleavened
bread, on which the Jews were \[35\] wont <span
id="fr_3062"></span>[3063](#fn_3062.md) to sacrifice <span
id="fr_3063"></span>[3064](#fn_3063.md) the passover.   <span
id="fr_3064"></span>[3065](#fn_3064.md) And Jesus sent two of his
disciples, Cephas and John, and said unto them, Go and make ready for us
the passover, that we may eat.  \[36, 37\]  <span
id="fr_3065"></span>[3066](#fn_3065.md) And they said unto him, Where wilt
thou that we make ready for thee?   <span
id="fr_3066"></span>[3067](#fn_3066.md) He said unto them, Go, enter the
city;  <span id="fr_3067"></span>[3068](#fn_3067.md) and at the time of
your entering, there shall meet you a man bearing a pitcher of water;
 <span id="fr_3068"></span>[3069](#fn_3068.md) follow him, and the place
where he entereth, say \[38\] to such an one, the master of the house,
 <span id="fr_3069"></span>[3070](#fn_3069.md) Our Master saith, My time is
come, and \[Arabic, p. 169\] at thy *house* I keep the passover.   <span
id="fr_3070"></span>[3071](#fn_3070.md) Where then is the lodging-place
where \[39\] I shall eat with my disciples?   <span
id="fr_3071"></span>[3072](#fn_3071.md) And he will shew you a large upper
room \[40\] spread and made ready:   <span
id="fr_3072"></span>[3073](#fn_3072.md) there then make ready for us. 
 <span id="fr_3073"></span>[3074](#fn_3073.md) And his two disciples went
out, and came to the city, and found as he had said unto them:  and they
made ready the passover as he had said unto them.

<span id="iv.iii.xliv-p67"></span>\[41\]  <span
id="fr_3074"></span>[3075](#fn_3074.md) And when the evening was come, and
the time arrived, Jesus came and reclined, \[42\] and the twelve
apostles with him.   <span id="fr_3075"></span>[3076](#fn_3075.md) And he
said unto them, With desire I have \[43\] desired to eat this passover
with you before I suffer:   <span id="fr_3076"></span>[3077](#fn_3076.md) I
say unto you, that henceforth I shall not eat it, until it is fulfilled
in the kingdom of God.

<span id="iv.iii.xliv-p71"></span>\[44\]  <span
id="fr_3077"></span>[3078](#fn_3077.md) Jesus said that, and was
agitated <span id="fr_3078"></span>[3079](#fn_3078.md) in his spirit, and
testified, and said, Verily, \[45\] verily, I say unto you, One of you,
*he* that eateth with me, shall betray me.   <span
id="fr_3079"></span>[3080](#fn_3079.md) And they were very sorrowful; and
they began to say unto him, one after another of \[46\] them, Can it be
I, Lord?   <span id="fr_3080"></span>[3081](#fn_3080.md) He answered and
said unto them, One of the twelve, \[47\] he that dippeth his hand with
me in the dish, will betray me.   <span
id="fr_3081"></span>[3082](#fn_3081.md) And lo, the hand of \[48\] him that
betrayeth me is on the table.   <span
id="fr_3082"></span>[3083](#fn_3082.md) And the Son of man goeth, as it is
written of him:  woe then to that man by whose hand the Son of man is
betrayed! for it \[49\] would have been better for that man had he not
been born.   <span id="fr_3083"></span>[3084](#fn_3083.md) And the
disciples \[50\] looked one on another, for they knew not to whom he
referred;  <span id="fr_3084"></span>[3085](#fn_3084.md) and they began to
search among themselves, who that might be who was to do *this*.
