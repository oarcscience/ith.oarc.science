<span id="iv.iii.xxxvi-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xxxvi-p1.1"></span>Section XXXVI.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xxxvi-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_2455"></span>[2456](#fn_2455) Verily, verily, I say unto you,
Whosoever keepeth my word shall not see death \[2\] for ever.   <span
id="fr_2456"></span>[2457](#fn_2456) The Jews said unto him, Now we know
that thou hast demons.  Abraham is dead, and the prophets; and thou
sayest, Whosoever keepeth my word shall not \[3\] taste death for ever. 
 <span id="fr_2457"></span>[2458](#fn_2457) Art thou haply greater than
our father Abraham, who is \[4\] dead, and than the prophets, which are
dead? whom makest thou thyself?   <span
id="fr_2458"></span>[2459](#fn_2458) Jesus said unto them, If I glorify
myself, my glory is nothing:  my Father is he that \[5\] glorifieth me;
*of* whom ye say, that he is our <span
id="fr_2459"></span>[2460](#fn_2459) God;  <span
id="fr_2460"></span>[2461](#fn_2460) and *yet* ye have not known him: 
but I know him; and if I should say that I know him not, I should become
\[6\] \[Arabic, p. 137\] a liar like you:  but I know him, and keep his
word.   <span id="fr_2461"></span>[2462](#fn_2461) Abraham your father
\[7\] longed to see my day; and he saw, and rejoiced.   <span
id="fr_2462"></span>[2463](#fn_2462) The Jews said unto him, \[8\] Thou
art now not fifty years old, and hast thou seen Abraham?   <span
id="fr_2463"></span>[2464](#fn_2463) Jesus said unto \[9\] them, Verily,
verily, I say unto you, Before Abraham was, I am.   <span
id="fr_2464"></span>[2465](#fn_2464) And they take <span
id="fr_2465"></span>[2466](#fn_2465) stones to stone him:   <span
id="fr_2466"></span>[2467](#fn_2466) but Jesus concealed himself, and
went out of the temple.  And he passed through them, and went *his way*.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxvi-p15"></span><span id="page_99">p. 99</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xxxvi-Page_99"></span>\[10\]  <span
id="fr_2467"></span>[2468](#fn_2467) And as he passed, he saw a man
blind from his mother’s womb.   <span
id="fr_2468"></span>[2469](#fn_2468) And his \[11\] disciples asked him,
and said, Our Master, who sinned, this *man*, or his parents, so \[12\]
that he was born blind? <span id="fr_2469"></span>[2470](#fn_2469)  
 <span id="fr_2470"></span>[2471](#fn_2470) Jesus said unto them,
Neither did he sin, nor his parents:  \[13\] but that the works of God
may be seen in him. <span id="fr_2471"></span>[2472](#fn_2471)    <span
id="fr_2472"></span>[2473](#fn_2472) It is incumbent on me to do the
deeds of him that sent me, while it is day:  a night will come, and no
man will be \[14\] able to busy himself.   <span
id="fr_2473"></span>[2474](#fn_2473) As long as I am in the world, I am
the light of the world.  \[15\]  <span
id="fr_2474"></span>[2475](#fn_2474) And when he said that, he spat upon
the ground, and made clay of his spittle, and \[16\] smeared *it* on the
eyes of the blind man, and said unto him,  <span
id="fr_2475"></span>[2476](#fn_2475) Go and wash thyself in \[17\] the
pool <span id="fr_2476"></span>[2477](#fn_2476) of Siloam. <span
id="fr_2477"></span>[2478](#fn_2477)    <span
id="fr_2478"></span>[2479](#fn_2478) And he went and washed, and came
seeing.  And his neighbours, which saw him of old begging, said, Is not
this he that was sitting begging?  \[18\]  <span
id="fr_2479"></span>[2480](#fn_2479) And some said, It is he; and others
said, Nay, but he resembles him much.  He \[19, 20\] said, I am he. 
 <span id="fr_2480"></span>[2481](#fn_2480) They said unto him, How then
were thine eyes opened?   <span id="fr_2481"></span>[2482](#fn_2481) He
answered and said unto them, A man named Jesus made clay, and smeared
*it* on my eyes, and said unto me, Go and wash in the water of Siloam: 
and I went and \[21\] washed, and received sight. <span
id="fr_2482"></span>[2483](#fn_2482)    <span
id="fr_2483"></span>[2484](#fn_2483) They said unto him, Where is he? 
He said, I know not.  \[22, 23\] \[Arabic, p. 138\]  <span
id="fr_2484"></span>[2485](#fn_2484) And they brought him that was
previously blind to the Pharisees.   <span
id="fr_2485"></span>[2486](#fn_2485) And the day in which Jesus made
clay and opened with it his eyes was a sabbath \[24\] day.   <span
id="fr_2486"></span>[2487](#fn_2486) And again the Pharisees asked him,
How didst thou receive sight?  And he said \[25\] unto them, He put clay
on mine eyes, and I washed, and received sight.   <span
id="fr_2487"></span>[2488](#fn_2487) The people <span
id="fr_2488"></span>[2489](#fn_2488) of the Pharisees said, This man is
not from God, for he keepeth not the sabbath.  And others said, How can
a man *that is* a sinner do these signs?  And there came \[26\] to be a
division amongst them.   <span id="fr_2489"></span>[2490](#fn_2489) And
again they said to that blind man, Thou, then, what sayest thou of him
that opened for thee thine eyes?  He said unto them, \[27\] I say that
he is a prophet.   <span id="fr_2490"></span>[2491](#fn_2490) And the
Jews did not believe concerning him, that he was blind, and received
sight, until they summoned the parents of him who received \[28\] sight,
and asked them,  <span id="fr_2491"></span>[2492](#fn_2491) Is
this <span id="fr_2492"></span>[2493](#fn_2492) your son, *of* whom ye
said that he was born blind? \[29\] how then, behold, doth he now see? 
 <span id="fr_2493"></span>[2494](#fn_2493) His parents answered and
said, We know \[30\] that this is our son, and that he was born blind: 
 <span id="fr_2494"></span>[2495](#fn_2494) but how he has come to see
now, or who it is that opened his eyes, we know not:  and he also has
reached his prime; \[31\] ask him, and he will speak for himself. 
 <span id="fr_2495"></span>[2496](#fn_2495) This said his parents,
because they were fearing the Jews:  and the Jews decided, that if any
man should confess of him that \[32\] he was the Messiah, they would put
him out of the synagogue.   <span id="fr_2496"></span>[2497](#fn_2496)
For this reason \[33\] said his parents, He hath reached his prime; ask
him.   <span id="fr_2497"></span>[2498](#fn_2497) And they called the
man a second time, him that was blind, and said unto him, Praise God: 
we know that this \[34\] man is a sinner.   <span
id="fr_2498"></span>[2499](#fn_2498) He answered and said unto them,
Whether he be a sinner, I know \[35\] not:  I know one thing, that I was
blind, and I now see.   <span id="fr_2499"></span>[2500](#fn_2499) They
said unto him again, \[36\] \[Arabic, p. 139\] What did he unto thee?
how opened he for thee thine eyes?   <span
id="fr_2500"></span>[2501](#fn_2500) He said unto them, I said unto you,
and ye did not hear:  what <span id="fr_2501"></span>[2502](#fn_2501)
wish ye further to hear? \[37\] ye also, do ye wish to become disciples
to him?   <span id="fr_2502"></span>[2503](#fn_2502) And they reviled
him, and said unto him, Thou art the disciple <span
id="fr_2503"></span>[2504](#fn_2503) of that *man*; but as for us, we
are the disciples of \[38\] Moses.   <span
id="fr_2504"></span>[2505](#fn_2504) And we know that God spake unto
Moses:  but this man, we know not \[39\] whence he is.   <span
id="fr_2505"></span>[2506](#fn_2505) The man answered and said unto
them, From this is the wonder, \[40\] because ye know not whence he is,
and mine eyes hath he opened.   <span
id="fr_2506"></span>[2507](#fn_2506) And we know that God heareth not
the voice of sinners:  but whosoever feareth him, and doeth \[41\] his
will, him he heareth.   <span id="fr_2507"></span>[2508](#fn_2507) From
eternity hath it not been heard of, that a man \[42\] opened the eyes of
a blind *man*, who had been born in blindness.   <span
id="fr_2508"></span>[2509](#fn_2508) If then this *man* \[43\] were not
from God, he could not do that.   <span
id="fr_2509"></span>[2510](#fn_2509) They answered and said unto him,
Thou wast all of thee born in sins, and dost thou teach us?  And they
put him forth without.

<span id="iv.iii.xxxvi-p59"></span>\[44\]  <span
id="fr_2510"></span>[2511](#fn_2510) And Jesus heard of his being put
forth without, and found him, and said unto \[45\] him, Dost thou
believe in the Son of God?   <span id="fr_2511"></span>[2512](#fn_2511)
He that was made whole answered \[46\] and said, Who is he, my Lord,
that I may believe in him?   <span id="fr_2512"></span>[2513](#fn_2512)
Jesus said unto him, \[47\] Thou hast seen him, and he that speaketh to
thee is he.   <span id="fr_2513"></span>[2514](#fn_2513) And he said, I
believe, my Lord.  And he fell down worshipping him.
