<span id="iv.iii.xlix-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xlix-p1.1"></span>Section XLIX.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xlix-p2"></span>\[1, 2\]  <span
id="fr_3386"></span>[3387](#fn_3386) And the chief priest asked Jesus
about his disciples, and about his doctrine. <span
id="fr_3387"></span>[3388](#fn_3387)    <span
id="fr_3388"></span>[3389](#fn_3388) And Jesus said unto him, I was
speaking <span id="fr_3389"></span>[3390](#fn_3389) openly to the
people; and I ever taught in the synagogue, and in the temple, where all
the Jews gather; and I have spoken nothing in \[3\] \[Arabic, p. 185\]
secret.   <span id="fr_3390"></span>[3391](#fn_3390) Why askest thou me?
ask those that have heard, what I spake unto \[4\] them:  for they know
all that I said.   <span id="fr_3391"></span>[3392](#fn_3391) And when
he had said that, one of the soldiers which were standing *there* struck
the cheek <span id="fr_3392"></span>[3393](#fn_3392) of Jesus, and said
unto him, <span id="page_119">p. 119</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xlix-Page_119"></span>\[5\] Dost thou thus answer the chief
priest?   <span id="fr_3393"></span>[3394](#fn_3393) Jesus answered and
said unto him, If I \[6\] have spoken evil, bear witness of evil: <span
id="fr_3394"></span>[3395](#fn_3394)   but if well, why didst thou smite
me?   <span id="fr_3395"></span>[3396](#fn_3395) And Annas sent Jesus
bound unto Caiaphas the chief priest.

<span id="iv.iii.xlix-p13"></span>\[7\]  <span
id="fr_3396"></span>[3397](#fn_3396) And when Jesus went out, Simon
Cephas was standing in the outer court warming \[8\] himself.   <span
id="fr_3397"></span>[3398](#fn_3397) And that maid saw him again, and
began to say to those that stood \[9\] *by*, This *man* also was there
with Jesus the Nazarene.   <span id="fr_3398"></span>[3399](#fn_3398)
And those that stood *by* \[10\] came forward and said to Cephas, Truly
thou art one of his disciples.   <span
id="fr_3399"></span>[3400](#fn_3399) And he \[11\] denied again with an
oath, I know not the man.   <span id="fr_3400"></span>[3401](#fn_3400)
And after a little one of the servants of the chief priest, the kinsman
of him whose ear Simon cut off, saw him; and \[12\] he disputed <span
id="fr_3401"></span>[3402](#fn_3401) and said, Truly this *man* was with
him:   <span id="fr_3402"></span>[3403](#fn_3402) and he also is a
Galilæan; \[13\] and his speech resembles. <span
id="fr_3403"></span>[3404](#fn_3403)    <span
id="fr_3404"></span>[3405](#fn_3404) And he said unto Simon, Did not I
see thee with him \[14\] in the garden?   <span
id="fr_3405"></span>[3406](#fn_3405) Then began Simon to curse, <span
id="fr_3406"></span>[3407](#fn_3406) and to swear, I know not this man
\[15\] whom ye have mentioned.   <span
id="fr_3407"></span>[3408](#fn_3407) And immediately, while he was
speaking, the cock crew \[16\] twice.   <span
id="fr_3408"></span>[3409](#fn_3408) And in that hour Jesus turned, he
being without, and looked stedfastly at Cephas.  And Simon remembered
the word of our Lord, which he said unto him, \[17, 18\]  <span
id="fr_3409"></span>[3410](#fn_3409) Before the cock crow twice, thou
shalt deny me thrice.   <span id="fr_3410"></span>[3411](#fn_3410) And
Simon went forth without, and wept bitterly.

<span id="iv.iii.xlix-p29"></span>\[19\] \[Arabic, p. 186\]  <span
id="fr_3411"></span>[3412](#fn_3411) And when the morning approached,
the servants of all the chief priests and the scribes and the elders of
the people and all the multitude assembled, \[20, 21\] and made a plot;
 <span id="fr_3412"></span>[3413](#fn_3412) and they took counsel
against Jesus to put him to death.   <span
id="fr_3413"></span>[3414](#fn_3413) And they sought false witnesses who
should witness against him, that they might put him to \[22, 23\] death,
and they found not;  <span id="fr_3414"></span>[3415](#fn_3414) but many
false witnesses came,  <span id="fr_3415"></span>[3416](#fn_3415) but
their witness did not \[24, 25\] agree.   <span
id="fr_3416"></span>[3417](#fn_3416) But at last there came two lying
witnesses,  <span id="fr_3417"></span>[3418](#fn_3417) and said, We
heard him say, I will destroy this <span
id="fr_3418"></span>[3419](#fn_3418) temple of God that is made with
hands, and will build another not \[26, 27\] made with hands after three
days.   <span id="fr_3419"></span>[3420](#fn_3419) And not even so did
their witness agree.  But Jesus was silent.   <span
id="fr_3420"></span>[3421](#fn_3420) And the chief priest rose in the
midst, and asked Jesus, and said, \[28\]  <span
id="fr_3421"></span>[3422](#fn_3421) Answerest thou not a word
concerning anything? what do these <span
id="fr_3422"></span>[3423](#fn_3422) witness against \[29, 30\] thee? 
 <span id="fr_3423"></span>[3424](#fn_3423) But Jesus was silent, and
answered him nothing.   <span id="fr_3424"></span>[3425](#fn_3424) And
they took him up \[31\] into their assembly, <span
id="fr_3425"></span>[3426](#fn_3425) and said unto him, If thou art the
Messiah, tell us.   <span id="fr_3426"></span>[3427](#fn_3426) He said
\[32\] unto them, If I tell you, ye will not believe me:   <span
id="fr_3427"></span>[3428](#fn_3427) and if I ask you, ye will not
answer \[33\] me a word, nor let me go.   <span
id="fr_3428"></span>[3429](#fn_3428) And the chief priest answered and
said unto him, I adjure thee by the living God, that thou tell us
whether thou art the Messiah, the \[34, 35\] Son of the living God. 
 <span id="fr_3429"></span>[3430](#fn_3429) Jesus said unto him, Thou
hast said that I am he.   <span id="fr_3430"></span>[3431](#fn_3430)
They all said unto him, Then thou art now the Son of God?  Jesus said,
Ye have said \[36\] that I am he.   <span
id="fr_3431"></span>[3432](#fn_3431) I say unto you, that henceforth ye
shall see the Son of man sitting \[37\] \[Arabic, p. 187\] at the right
hand of power, and coming on the clouds of heaven.   <span
id="fr_3432"></span>[3433](#fn_3432) Then the \[38\] chief priest rent
his tunic, <span id="fr_3433"></span>[3434](#fn_3433) and said, He hath
blasphemed.   <span id="fr_3434"></span>[3435](#fn_3434) And they all
said, Why should we seek now witnesses? we have heard now the blasphemy
from his mouth.  \[39, 40\] What then think ye?   <span
id="fr_3435"></span>[3436](#fn_3435) They all answered and said, He is
worthy of death.   <span id="fr_3436"></span>[3437](#fn_3436) Then some
of them drew near, and spat in his face, and struck him, and scoffed at
him.  \[41\]  <span id="fr_3437"></span>[3438](#fn_3437) And the
soldiers struck him on his cheeks, <span
id="fr_3438"></span>[3439](#fn_3438) and said, Prophesy unto us, *thou*
Messiah:  \[42\] who is he that struck thee?   <span
id="fr_3439"></span>[3440](#fn_3439) And many other things spake they
falsely, <span id="fr_3440"></span>[3441](#fn_3440) and said against
him.

<span id="iv.iii.xlix-p60"></span>\[43\]  <span
id="fr_3441"></span>[3442](#fn_3441) And all of their assembly
arose, <span id="fr_3442"></span>[3443](#fn_3442) and took Jesus, and
brought him bound <span id="fr_3443"></span>[3444](#fn_3443) to \[44\]
the prætorium, <span id="fr_3444"></span>[3445](#fn_3444) and delivered
him up to Pilate the judge;  <span id="fr_3445"></span>[3446](#fn_3445)
but they entered not into the prætorium, that they might not be defiled
when they should eat the passover.

<span id="iv.iii.xlix-p66"></span>\[45\]  <span
id="fr_3446"></span>[3447](#fn_3446) And Jesus stood before the judge. 
And Pilate went forth unto them without, and \[46\] said unto them, What
accusation <span id="fr_3447"></span>[3448](#fn_3447) have ye against
this man?   <span id="fr_3448"></span>[3449](#fn_3448) They answered and
said unto him, If he had not been doing evils, neither should we have
delivered \[47\] him up unto thee.   <span
id="fr_3449"></span>[3450](#fn_3449) We found this *man* leading our
people astray, and restraining from giving tribute to Cæsar, and saying
of himself that he is the King, the Messiah.  \[48\]  <span
id="fr_3450"></span>[3451](#fn_3450) Pilate said unto them, Then take ye
him, and judge him according to your law.  <span id="page_120">p.
120</span> <span id="iv.iii.xlix-Page_120"></span>\[Arabic, p. 188\] The
Jews said unto him, We have no authority to put a man to death:  \[49\]
 <span id="fr_3451"></span>[3452](#fn_3451) that the word might be
fulfilled, which Jesus spake, when he made known by what manner of death
he was to die.

<span id="iv.iii.xlix-p73"></span>\[50\]  <span
id="fr_3452"></span>[3453](#fn_3452) And Pilate entered into the
prætorium, and called Jesus, and said unto him, Art \[51\] thou the King
of the Jews?   <span id="fr_3453"></span>[3454](#fn_3453) Jesus said
unto him, Of thyself saidst thou this, or \[52\] did others tell it thee
concerning me?   <span id="fr_3454"></span>[3455](#fn_3454) Pilate said
unto him, Am I, forsooth, <span id="fr_3455"></span>[3456](#fn_3455) a
Jew?  The sons of thy nation <span id="fr_3456"></span>[3457](#fn_3456)
and the chief priests delivered thee unto me:  what \[53\] hast thou
done?   <span id="fr_3457"></span>[3458](#fn_3457) Jesus said unto him,
My kingdom is not of this world:  if my kingdom were of this world, then
would my servants fight, that I should not be \[54\] delivered to the
Jews:  now my kingdom is not from hence.   <span
id="fr_3458"></span>[3459](#fn_3458) Pilate said unto him, Then *thou
art* a king?  Jesus said unto him, Thou hast said that I am a king.  And
for this was I born, and for this came I into the world, that I should
bear witness \[55\] of the truth.   <span
id="fr_3459"></span>[3460](#fn_3459) And every one that is of the truth
heareth my voice.  Pilate said unto him, And what is the truth?  And
when he said that, he went out again unto the Jews.
