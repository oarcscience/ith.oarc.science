<span id="iv.iii.xxx-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_2053"></span>[2054](#fn_2053) And he spake a parable unto those
which were bidden there, because he saw \[2\] them choose the places
that were in the highest part of the sitting room:   <span
id="fr_2054"></span>[2055](#fn_2054) When a man invites thee to a feast,
do not go and sit at the head of the room; lest there \[3\] be there a
man more honourable than thou,  <span
id="fr_2055"></span>[2056](#fn_2055) and he that invited you come and
say unto thee, Give the place to this man:  and thou be ashamed when
thou risest and \[4\] takest <span id="fr_2056"></span>[2057](#fn_2056)
another place.   <span id="fr_2057"></span>[2058](#fn_2057) But when
thou art invited, go and sit last; so that when he that invited thee
cometh, he may say unto thee, My friend, go up higher:  and \[5\] thou
shalt have praise before all that were invited with thee.   <span
id="fr_2058"></span>[2059](#fn_2058) For every one that exalteth himself
shall be abased; and every one that abaseth himself shall be exalted.

<span id="iv.iii.xxx-p9"></span>\[6\]  <span
id="fr_2059"></span>[2060](#fn_2059) And he said also to him that had
invited him, When thou makest a feast <span
id="fr_2060"></span>[2061](#fn_2060) or a banquet, <span
id="fr_2061"></span>[2062](#fn_2061) do not invite thy friends, nor even
thy brethren, nor thy kinsmen, nor thy \[7\] rich neighbours; lest haply
they also invite thee, and thou have this reward.   <span
id="fr_2062"></span>[2063](#fn_2062) But when thou makest a feast,
invite the poor, and those with withered hand, and the \[8\] lame, and
the blind:   <span id="fr_2063"></span>[2064](#fn_2063) and blessed art
thou, since they have not the means to reward \[9\] thee; that thy
reward may be at the rising of the righteous.   <span
id="fr_2064"></span>[2065](#fn_2064) And when one of them that were
invited heard that, he said unto him, Blessed is he that shall eat bread
in the kingdom of God.

<span id="iv.iii.xxx-p16"></span>\[10, 11\]  <span
id="fr_2065"></span>[2066](#fn_2065) Jesus answered again in parables,
and said,  <span id="fr_2066"></span>[2067](#fn_2066) The kingdom of
heaven hath been likened \[Arabic, p. 115\] to <span
id="fr_2067"></span>[2068](#fn_2067) a certain king, which made a
feast <span id="fr_2068"></span>[2069](#fn_2068) for his son, and
prepared a \[12\] great banquet, <span
id="fr_2069"></span>[2070](#fn_2069) and invited many:  and he sent his
servants at the time of the feast to inform them that were invited,
 <span id="fr_2070"></span>[2071](#fn_2070) Everything is made ready for
you; come.  And \[13\] they would not come, but began all of them with
one voice to make excuse.   <span id="fr_2071"></span>[2072](#fn_2071)
And the first said unto them, Say to him, I have bought a field, and I
must needs go out \[14\] to see it:   <span
id="fr_2072"></span>[2073](#fn_2072) I pray thee to release <span
id="fr_2073"></span>[2074](#fn_2073) me, for I ask to be excused.  And
another said, I have bought five yoke of oxen, and I am going to examine
them:  I pray thee \[15\] to release me, for I ask to be excused. 
 <span id="fr_2074"></span>[2075](#fn_2074) And another said, I have
married a wife, \[16\] and therefore I cannot come.   <span
id="fr_2075"></span>[2076](#fn_2075) And the king sent also other
servants, and said, Say to those that were invited, that my feast is
ready, and my oxen and my fatlings are \[17\] slain, and everything is
ready:  come to the feast.   <span id="fr_2076"></span>[2077](#fn_2076)
But they made light of it, and \[18\] went, one to his field, and
another to his merchandise:   <span id="fr_2077"></span>[2078](#fn_2077)
and the rest took his \[19\] servants, and entreated them shamefully,
and killed them.   <span id="fr_2078"></span>[2079](#fn_2078) And one of
the servants \[20\] came, and informed his lord of what had happened. 
 <span id="fr_2079"></span>[2080](#fn_2079) And when the king heard, he
became angry, and sent his armies; and they destroyed those murderers,
and \[21\] burned their cities.   <span
id="fr_2080"></span>[2081](#fn_2080) Then he said to his servants, The
feast is prepared, but those \[22\] that were invited were not worthy. 
 <span id="fr_2081"></span>[2082](#fn_2081) Go out quickly into the
markets and into the <span id="page_90">p. 90</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xxx-Page_90"></span>partings of the ways of the city, and
bring in hither the poor, and those with pains, and the lame, and the
blind.  And the servants did as the king commanded them.  \[23\]  <span
id="fr_2082"></span>[2083](#fn_2082) And they came, and said unto him,
Our lord, we have done all that thou commandedst \[24\] us, and there is
here still room.   <span id="fr_2083"></span>[2084](#fn_2083) So the
lord said unto his servants, Go out into the roads, and the ways, and
the paths, and every one that ye find, invite \[25\] \[Arabic, p. 116\]
to the feast, and constrain them to enter, till my house is <span
id="fr_2084"></span>[2085](#fn_2084) filled.   <span
id="fr_2085"></span>[2086](#fn_2085) I say unto you, that no one of
those people that were invited shall taste of my feast.  \[26\]  <span
id="fr_2086"></span>[2087](#fn_2086) And those servants went out into
the roads, and gathered all that they found, good and \[27\] bad:  and
the banquet-house was filled with guests.   <span
id="fr_2087"></span>[2088](#fn_2087) And the king entered to see those
\[28\] who were seated, and he saw there a man not wearing a festive
garment:   <span id="fr_2088"></span>[2089](#fn_2088) and he said unto
him, My friend, how didst thou come in here not having on festive
garments?  \[29\]  <span id="fr_2089"></span>[2090](#fn_2089) And he was
silent.  Then the king said to the servants, Bind his hands and his
feet, and put him forth into the outer darkness; there shall be weeping
and \[30\] gnashing of teeth.   <span
id="fr_2090"></span>[2091](#fn_2090) The called are many; and the
chosen, few.

<span id="iv.iii.xxx-p43"></span>\[31\]  <span
id="fr_2091"></span>[2092](#fn_2091) And after that, the time of the
feast of unleavened bread of the Jews arrived, \[32\] and Jesus went out
to go to Jerusalem.   <span id="fr_2092"></span>[2093](#fn_2092) And as
he went in the way, there met him \[33\] ten persons who were lepers,
and stood afar off:   <span id="fr_2093"></span>[2094](#fn_2093) and
they lifted up their voice, and \[34\] said, Our Master, Jesus, have
mercy upon us.   <span id="fr_2094"></span>[2095](#fn_2094) And when he
saw them, he said unto them, Go and shew yourselves unto the priests. 
And when they went, they \[35\] were cleansed.   <span
id="fr_2095"></span>[2096](#fn_2095) And one of them, when he saw
himself cleansed, returned, and \[36\] was praising God with a loud
voice;  <span id="fr_2096"></span>[2097](#fn_2096) and he fell on his
face before the feet of \[37\] Jesus, giving him thanks:  and this *man*
was a Samaritan.   <span id="fr_2097"></span>[2098](#fn_2097) Jesus
answered and said, \[38\] Were not those that were cleansed ten? where
then are the nine?   <span id="fr_2098"></span>[2099](#fn_2098) Not one
of them turned aside to come and praise God, but this *man* who is of a
strange \[39\] people.   <span id="fr_2099"></span>[2100](#fn_2099) He
said unto him, Arise, and go thy way; for thy faith hath given thee
life. <span id="fr_2100"></span>[2101](#fn_2100)

<span id="iv.iii.xxx-p54"></span>\[40\]  <span
id="fr_2101"></span>[2102](#fn_2101) And while they were going up in the
way to Jerusalem, Jesus went in front of them; and they wondered, and
followed him fearing.  And he took his twelve disciples apart, \[41\]
and began to tell them privately <span
id="fr_2102"></span>[2103](#fn_2102) what was about to befall him. 
 <span id="fr_2103"></span>[2104](#fn_2103) And he said unto \[Arabic,
p. 117\] them, We are going up to Jerusalem, and all the things shall be
fulfilled \[42\] that are written in the prophets concerning the Son of
man.   <span id="fr_2104"></span>[2105](#fn_2104) He shall be delivered
to the chief priests and the scribes; and they shall condemn him to
death, \[43\] and deliver him to the peoples; <span
id="fr_2105"></span>[2106](#fn_2105)    <span
id="fr_2106"></span>[2107](#fn_2106) and they shall treat him
shamefully, and scourge \[44\] him, and spit in his face, and humble
him, <span id="fr_2107"></span>[2108](#fn_2107) and crucify him, and
slay him:   <span id="fr_2108"></span>[2109](#fn_2108) and on \[45\] the
third day he shall rise.   <span id="fr_2109"></span>[2110](#fn_2109)
But they understood not one thing of this; but this word was hidden from
them, and they did not perceive these things that were addressed to
them.

<span id="iv.iii.xxx-p64"></span>\[46\]  <span
id="fr_2110"></span>[2111](#fn_2110) Then came near to him the mother of
the (two) sons of Zebedee, she and her (two) sons, and worshipped him,
and asked of him a *certain* thing.   <span
id="fr_2111"></span>[2112](#fn_2111) And he said \[47\] unto her, What
wouldest thou?   <span id="fr_2112"></span>[2113](#fn_2112) And James
and John, her two sons, came forward, and said unto him, Teacher, we
would that all that we ask thou wouldest \[48\] do unto us.   <span
id="fr_2113"></span>[2114](#fn_2113) He said unto them, <span
id="fr_2114"></span>[2115](#fn_2114) What would ye that I should do unto
you?  \[49\]  <span id="fr_2115"></span>[2116](#fn_2115) They said unto
him, Grant us that we may sit, the one on thy right, and the other
\[50\] on thy left, in thy kingdom and thy glory.   <span
id="fr_2116"></span>[2117](#fn_2116) And Jesus said unto them, Ye know
not what ye ask.  Are ye able to drink the cup that I am to drink? and
with the \[51\] baptism that I am to be baptized with, will ye be
baptized?   <span id="fr_2117"></span>[2118](#fn_2117) And they said
unto him, We are able.  Jesus said unto them, The cup that I drink ye
shall drink; and \[52\] with the baptism wherewith I am baptized ye
shall be baptized:   <span id="fr_2118"></span>[2119](#fn_2118) but that
ye should sit on my right and on my left is not mine to give; but *it
is* for him for whom my Father hath prepared *it*.
