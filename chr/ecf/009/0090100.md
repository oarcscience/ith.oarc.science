<span id="xii.iv.v-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="xii.iv.v-p1.1"></span>Chapter V.—No Less Evils Have Arisen from the
Same Source in the Most Recent Times.  The Martyrdom of Peter and
Paul.</span>

<span id="xii.iv.v-p2"></span>But not to dwell upon ancient examples,
let us come to the most recent spiritual heroes. <span
id="fr_4022"></span>[4023](#fn_4022)   Let us take the noble examples
furnished in our own generation.  Through envy <span
id="fr_4023"></span>[4024](#fn_4023) and jealousy the greatest and most
righteous pillars \[of the church\] have been persecuted and put to
death. <span id="fr_4024"></span>[4025](#fn_4024)   Let us set before
our eyes the illustrious <span id="fr_4025"></span>[4026](#fn_4025)
apostles.  Peter, through unrighteous envy, endured not one or two, but
numerous labours; and when he had at length suffered martyrdom, departed
to the place of glory due to him.  Owing to envy, Paul also
obtained <span id="fr_4026"></span>[4027](#fn_4026) the reward of
patient endurance, after being seven times thrown into captivity, <span
id="fr_4027"></span>[4028](#fn_4027) compelled <span
id="fr_4028"></span>[4029](#fn_4028) to flee, and <span id="page_231">p.
231</span> <span id="xii.iv.v-Page_231"></span>stoned.  After preaching
both in the east and west, he gained the illustrious reputation due to
his faith, having taught righteousness <span
id="fr_4029"></span>[4030](#fn_4029) to the whole world, and come to the
extreme limit of the west, <span id="fr_4030"></span>[4031](#fn_4030)
and suffered martyrdom under the prefects. <span
id="fr_4031"></span>[4032](#fn_4031)   Thus was he removed from the
world, and went into the holy place, having proved himself a striking
example of patience.
