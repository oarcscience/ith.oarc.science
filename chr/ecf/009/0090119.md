<span id="xii.iv.xxiv-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="xii.iv.xxiv-p1.1"></span>Chapter XXIV.—God Continually Shows Us in
Nature that There Will Be a Resurrection.</span>

<span id="xii.iv.xxiv-p2"></span>Let us consider, beloved, how the Lord
continually proves to us that there shall be a future resurrection, of
which He has rendered the Lord Jesus Christ <span
id="fr_4113"></span>[4114](#fn_4113.md) the first-fruits <span
id="fr_4114"></span>[4115](#fn_4114.md) by raising Him from the dead.  Let
us contemplate, beloved, the resurrection which is at all times <span
id="fr_4115"></span>[4116](#fn_4115.md) taking place.  Day and night
declare to us a resurrection.  The night sinks to sleep, and the day
arises; the day \[again\] departs, and the night comes on.  Let us
behold <span id="fr_4116"></span>[4117](#fn_4116.md) the fruits \[of the
earth\], how the sowing of grain takes place.  The sower <span
id="fr_4117"></span>[4118](#fn_4117.md) goes forth, and casts it into the
ground, <span id="fr_4118"></span>[4119](#fn_4118.md) and the seed being
thus scattered, though dry and naked when it fell upon the earth, is
gradually dissolved.  Then out of its dissolution the mighty power of
the providence of the Lord raises it up again, and from one seed many
arise and bring forth fruit.
