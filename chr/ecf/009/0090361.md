<span id="xvi.ii.iii.x-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="xvi.ii.iii.x-p1.1"></span>10.  The Pearl of the Gospel in Relation
to the Old Testament.</span>

<span id="xvi.ii.iii.x-p2"></span>“To everything then is its season, and
a time for everything under heaven,” <span
id="fr_5203"></span>[5204](#fn_5203) a time to gather the goodly pearls,
and a time after their gathering to find the one precious pearl, when it
is fitting for a man to go away and sell all that he has in order that
he may buy that pearl.  For as every man who is going to be wise in the
words of truth must first be taught the rudiments, and further pass
through the elementary instruction, and <span id="page_419">p.
419</span> <span id="xvi.ii.iii.x-Page_419"></span>appreciate it highly
but not abide in it, as one who, having honoured it at the beginning but
passed over towards perfection, is grateful for the introduction because
it was useful at the first; so the perfect apprehension of the law and
the prophets is an elementary discipline for the perfect apprehension of
the Gospel, and all the meaning in the words and deeds of Christ.
