<span id="iv.iii.li-p2"></span>\[1\] \[Arabic, p. 192\]  <span
id="fr_3532"></span>[3533](#fn_3532) And when Pilate heard this saying,
he took Jesus out, and sat on the tribune in the place which was called
the pavement of stones, but in the Hebrew \[2\] called Gabbatha.   <span
id="fr_3533"></span>[3534](#fn_3533) And that day was the Friday of the
passover:  and it had reached \[3\] about the sixth hour. <span
id="fr_3534"></span>[3535](#fn_3534)    <span
id="fr_3535"></span>[3536](#fn_3535) And he said to the Jews, Behold,
your King!  And they cried out, Take him, take him, crucify him, crucify
him.  Pilate said unto them, Shall I crucify your King?  The chief
priests said unto him, We have no king except \[4\] Cæsar.   <span
id="fr_3536"></span>[3537](#fn_3536) And Pilate, when he saw *it*,
and <span id="fr_3537"></span>[3538](#fn_3537) he was gaining nothing,
but the tumult <span id="page_122">p. 122</span> <span
id="iv.iii.li-Page_122"></span>was increasing, took water, and washed
his hands before the multitude, and said, I \[5\] am innocent of the
blood of this innocent *man*:  ye shall know. <span
id="fr_3538"></span>[3539](#fn_3538)    <span
id="fr_3539"></span>[3540](#fn_3539) And all the people \[6\] answered
and said, His blood be on us, and on our children.   <span
id="fr_3540"></span>[3541](#fn_3540) Then Pilate commanded to grant them
their request; and delivered up Jesus to be crucified, according to
their wish.

<span id="iv.iii.li-p12"></span>\[7\]  <span
id="fr_3541"></span>[3542](#fn_3541) Then Judas the betrayer, when he
saw Jesus wronged, went and returned the \[8\] thirty *pieces* of money
to the chief priests and the elders,  <span
id="fr_3542"></span>[3543](#fn_3542) and said, I have sinned in my
betraying innocent blood.  And they said unto him, And we, what must we
*do*? \[9\] know thou.   <span id="fr_3543"></span>[3544](#fn_3543) And
he threw down the money in the temple, and departed; and *he* \[10\]
went away <span id="fr_3544"></span>[3545](#fn_3544) and hanged <span
id="fr_3545"></span>[3546](#fn_3545) himself.   <span
id="fr_3546"></span>[3547](#fn_3546) And the chief priests took the
money, and said, We have not authority to cast it into the place of the
offering, <span id="fr_3547"></span>[3548](#fn_3547) for it is the price
\[11\] of blood.   <span id="fr_3548"></span>[3549](#fn_3548) And they
took counsel, and bought with it the plain of the potter, for \[12\] the
burial of strangers.   <span id="fr_3549"></span>[3550](#fn_3549)
Therefore that plain was called, The field of blood, unto \[13\]
\[Arabic, p. 193\] this day.   <span
id="fr_3550"></span>[3551](#fn_3550) Therein <span
id="fr_3551"></span>[3552](#fn_3551) was fulfilled the saying in the
prophet which said, I took thirty *pieces* of money, the price of the
precious *one, which was* fixed \[14\] by the children of Israel;  <span
id="fr_3552"></span>[3553](#fn_3552) and I paid them for the plain of
the potter, as the Lord commanded me.

<span id="iv.iii.li-p25"></span>\[15\]  <span
id="fr_3553"></span>[3554](#fn_3553) And the Jews took Jesus, and went
away to crucify him.   <span id="fr_3554"></span>[3555](#fn_3554) And
when he bare his \[16\] cross and went out, they stripped him of those
purple and scarlet garments which he \[17\] had on, and put on him his
*own* garments.   <span id="fr_3555"></span>[3556](#fn_3555) And while
they were going with him, they found a man, a Cyrenian, coming from the
country, named Simon, the father of Alexander and Rufus:   <span
id="fr_3556"></span>[3557](#fn_3556) and they compelled this *man* to
bear the cross of Jesus.  \[18\]  <span
id="fr_3557"></span>[3558](#fn_3557) And they took the cross and laid it
upon him, that he might bear it, and come after Jesus; and Jesus went,
and his cross behind him.

<span id="iv.iii.li-p31"></span>\[19\]  <span
id="fr_3558"></span>[3559](#fn_3558) And there followed him much people,
and women which were lamenting and \[20\] raving. <span
id="fr_3559"></span>[3560](#fn_3559)   <span
id="fr_3560"></span>[3561](#fn_3560) But Jesus turned unto them and
said, Daughters of Jerusalem, weep not \[21\] for me:  weep for
yourselves, and for your children.   <span
id="fr_3561"></span>[3562](#fn_3561) Days are coming, when they shall
say, Blessed are the barren, and the wombs that bare not, and the
breasts \[22\] that gave not suck.   <span
id="fr_3562"></span>[3563](#fn_3562) Then shall they begin to say to the
mountains, Fall on us; and \[23\] to the hills, Cover us.   <span
id="fr_3563"></span>[3564](#fn_3563) For if they do so in the green
tree, <span id="fr_3564"></span>[3565](#fn_3564) what shall be in the
dry?

<span id="iv.iii.li-p39"></span>\[24\]  <span
id="fr_3565"></span>[3566](#fn_3565) And they brought with Jesus two
others of the malefactors, <span id="fr_3566"></span>[3567](#fn_3566) to
be put to death.

<span id="iv.iii.li-p42"></span>\[25\]  <span
id="fr_3567"></span>[3568](#fn_3567) And when they came unto a certain
place called The skull, and called in the Hebrew Golgotha, they
crucified him there:   <span id="fr_3568"></span>[3569](#fn_3568) they
crucified with him these two \[26\] malefactors, one on his right, and
the other on his left.   <span id="fr_3569"></span>[3570](#fn_3569) And
the scripture was \[27\] \[Arabic, p. 194\] fulfilled, which saith, He
was numbered with the transgressors.   <span
id="fr_3570"></span>[3571](#fn_3570) And they gave him to drink wine and
myrrh, and vinegar which had been mixed with the myrrh;  <span
id="fr_3571"></span>[3572](#fn_3571) and he tasted, and would not drink;
and he received it not.

<span id="iv.iii.li-p48"></span>\[28\]  <span
id="fr_3572"></span>[3573](#fn_3572) And the soldiers, when they had
crucified Jesus, took his garments, and cast lots for them in four
parts, to every party of the soldiers a part; and his tunic was \[29\]
without sewing, from the top woven throughout.   <span
id="fr_3573"></span>[3574](#fn_3573) And they said one to another, Let
us not rend it, but cast lots for it, whose it shall be:  and the
scripture was fulfilled, which saith,

<span id="iv.iii.li-p51"></span>They divided my garments among them,

<span id="iv.iii.li-p52"></span>And cast the lot for my vesture.

<span id="iv.iii.li-p53"></span>\[30, 31\]  <span
id="fr_3574"></span>[3575](#fn_3574) This the soldiers did.  And they
sat and guarded him there.   <span id="fr_3575"></span>[3576](#fn_3575)
And Pilate wrote on a tablet the cause of his death, and put it on the
wood of the cross above his head. <span
id="fr_3576"></span>[3577](#fn_3576)   And there was written upon it
thus:  <span class="sc"><span id="iv.iii.li-p56.2"></span>This is Jesus
the Nazarene, the King of the</span> \[32\] <span class="sc"><span
id="iv.iii.li-p56.3"></span>Jews</span>.   <span
id="fr_3577"></span>[3578](#fn_3577) And this tablet <span
id="fr_3578"></span>[3579](#fn_3578) read many of the Jews:  for the
place where Jesus was crucified was near the city:  and it was written
in Hebrew and Greek and Latin.  \[33\]  <span
id="fr_3579"></span>[3580](#fn_3579) And the chief priests said unto
Pilate, Write not, The King of the Jews; but, He it is \[34\] that <span
id="fr_3580"></span>[3581](#fn_3580) said, I am the King of the Jews. 
 <span id="fr_3581"></span>[3582](#fn_3581) Pilate said unto them, What
hath been \[35\] written hath been written. <span
id="fr_3582"></span>[3583](#fn_3582)    <span
id="fr_3583"></span>[3584](#fn_3583) And the people were standing
beholding; and they <span id="page_123">p. 123</span> <span
id="iv.iii.li-Page_123"></span>\[36\] that passed by were reviling <span
id="fr_3584"></span>[3585](#fn_3584) him, and shaking <span
id="fr_3585"></span>[3586](#fn_3585) their heads, and saying,  <span
id="fr_3586"></span>[3587](#fn_3586) Thou that destroyest the temple,
and buildest it in three days,  <span
id="fr_3587"></span>[3588](#fn_3587) save thyself if thou art the Son
\[37\] of God, and come down from the cross.   <span
id="fr_3588"></span>[3589](#fn_3588) And in like manner the chief
priests and the \[Arabic, p. 195\] scribes and the elders and the
Pharisees derided him, and laughed one with \[38, 39\] another, and
said,  <span id="fr_3589"></span>[3590](#fn_3589) The saviour of others
cannot save himself.   <span id="fr_3590"></span>[3591](#fn_3590) If he
is the Messiah, the chosen of God, and the King of Israel, <span
id="fr_3591"></span>[3592](#fn_3591) let him come down now from the
\[40\] cross, that we may see, and believe in him.   <span
id="fr_3592"></span>[3593](#fn_3592) He that relieth on God—let him
deliver him \[41\] now, if he is pleased with him:  for he said, I am
the Son of God.   <span id="fr_3593"></span>[3594](#fn_3593) And the
soldiers \[42\] also scoffed at him in that they came near unto him,
 <span id="fr_3594"></span>[3595](#fn_3594) and brought him vinegar, and
\[43\] said unto him, If thou art the King of the Jews, save thyself. 
 <span id="fr_3595"></span>[3596](#fn_3595) And likewise the two
robbers <span id="fr_3596"></span>[3597](#fn_3596) also that were
crucified with him reproached him.

<span id="iv.iii.li-p77"></span>\[44\]  <span
id="fr_3597"></span>[3598](#fn_3597) And one of those two malefactors
who were crucified with him reviled him, and \[45\] said, If thou art
the Messiah, save thyself, and save us also.   <span
id="fr_3598"></span>[3599](#fn_3598) But his comrade rebuked him, and
said, Dost thou not even fear God, being thyself also in this \[46\]
condemnation?   <span id="fr_3599"></span>[3600](#fn_3599) And we with
justice, and as we deserved, and according to our deed, <span
id="fr_3600"></span>[3601](#fn_3600) have we been rewarded:  but this
*man* hath not done anything unlawful.  \[47\]  <span
id="fr_3601"></span>[3602](#fn_3601) And he said unto Jesus, Remember
me, my Lord, when thou comest in thy kingdom.  \[48\]  <span
id="fr_3602"></span>[3603](#fn_3602) Jesus said unto him, Verily <span
id="fr_3603"></span>[3604](#fn_3603) I say unto thee, To-day shalt thou
be with me in Paradise.

<span id="iv.iii.li-p85"></span>\[49\]  <span
id="fr_3604"></span>[3605](#fn_3604) And there stood by the cross of
Jesus his mother, and his mother’s sister, <span
id="fr_3605"></span>[3606](#fn_3605) \[50\] Mary <span
id="fr_3606"></span>[3607](#fn_3606) that was related to Clopas, and
Mary Magdalene.   <span id="fr_3607"></span>[3608](#fn_3607) And Jesus
saw his mother, and that disciple whom he loved standing *by*; and he
said to his mother, \[51\] Woman, behold, thy son!   <span
id="fr_3608"></span>[3609](#fn_3608) And he said to that disciple,
Behold, thy mother!  And from that hour that disciple took her unto
him*self*.

<span id="iv.iii.li-p91"></span>\[52\] \[Arabic, p. 196\]  <span
id="fr_3609"></span>[3610](#fn_3609) And from the sixth hour <span
id="fr_3610"></span>[3611](#fn_3610) darkness was on all the land unto
the ninth \[53\] hour, <span id="fr_3611"></span>[3612](#fn_3611) and
the sun became dark.   <span id="fr_3612"></span>[3613](#fn_3612) And at
the ninth hour Jesus cried out with a loud voice, and said, Yail,
Yaili, <span id="fr_3613"></span>[3614](#fn_3613) why hast thou forsaken
me? which <span id="fr_3614"></span>[3615](#fn_3614) is, My \[54\] God,
my God, why hast thou forsaken me?   <span
id="fr_3615"></span>[3616](#fn_3615) And some of those that stood there,
when they heard, said, <span id="fr_3616"></span>[3617](#fn_3616) This
*man* called Elijah.
