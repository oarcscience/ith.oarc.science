<span id="iv.iii.xxviii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xxviii-p1.1"></span>Section XXVIII. <span
id="fr_1931"></span>[1932](#fn_1931) </span>

<span id="iv.iii.xxviii-p3"></span>\[1, 2\] \[Arabic, p. 107\]  <span
id="fr_1932"></span>[1933](#fn_1932) And at that time the feast of
tabernacles of the Jews drew near.   <span
id="fr_1933"></span>[1934](#fn_1933) So the brethren of Jesus said unto
him, Remove now hence, and go to Judæa, that \[3\] thy disciples may see
the deeds that thou doest.   <span id="fr_1934"></span>[1935](#fn_1934)
For no man doeth a thing secretly \[4\] and wisheth to be apparent. 
 <span id="fr_1935"></span>[1936](#fn_1935) If thou doest this, shew
thyself to the world.  For \[5\] up to this time not even the brethren
of Jesus believed on him.   <span id="fr_1936"></span>[1937](#fn_1936)
Jesus said unto them, My time till now has not arrived; but as for you,
your time is alway ready.  \[6\]  <span
id="fr_1937"></span>[1938](#fn_1937) It is not possible for the world to
hate you; but me it hateth, for I bear witness \[7\] against it, that
its deeds are evil.   <span id="fr_1938"></span>[1939](#fn_1938) As for
you, go ye up unto this feast:  but I go \[8\] not up now to this feast;
for my time has not yet been completed.   <span
id="fr_1939"></span>[1940](#fn_1939) He said this, and remained behind
in Galilee.

<span id="iv.iii.xxviii-p12"></span>\[9\]  <span
id="fr_1940"></span>[1941](#fn_1940) But when his brethren went up unto
the feast, he journeyed from Galilee, and \[10\] came to the borders of
Judæa, to *the country* beyond Jordan;  <span
id="fr_1941"></span>[1942](#fn_1941) and there came after \[11\] him
great multitudes, and he healed them all there.   <span
id="fr_1942"></span>[1943](#fn_1942) And he went out, and proceeded
\[12\] to the feast, not openly, but as one that conceals himself. 
 <span id="fr_1943"></span>[1944](#fn_1943) And the Jews sought him
\[13\] at the feast, and said, In what place is this *man?*   <span
id="fr_1944"></span>[1945](#fn_1944) And there occurred much murmuring
there in the great multitude that came to the feast, on his account. 
For \[14\] some said, He is good:  and others said, Nay, but he leadeth
the people astray.   <span id="fr_1945"></span>[1946](#fn_1945) But no
man spake of him openly for fear of the Jews.

<span id="iv.iii.xxviii-p19"></span>\[15\] \[Arabic, p. 108\]  <span
id="fr_1946"></span>[1947](#fn_1946) But when the days of the feast of
tabernacles were half over, Jesus went \[16\] up to the temple, and
taught.   <span id="fr_1947"></span>[1948](#fn_1947) And the Jews
wondered, and said, How doth \[17\] this man know writing, <span
id="fr_1948"></span>[1949](#fn_1948) seeing he hath not learned?   <span
id="fr_1949"></span>[1950](#fn_1949) Jesus answered and said, My
doctrine <span id="fr_1950"></span>[1951](#fn_1950) \[18\] is not mine,
but his that sent me.   <span id="fr_1951"></span>[1952](#fn_1951)
Whoever wisheth to do his will understandeth my doctrine, <span
id="fr_1952"></span>[1953](#fn_1952) whether it be from God, or whether
I speak of mine own accord.  \[19\]  <span
id="fr_1953"></span>[1954](#fn_1953) Whosoever speaketh of his own
accord seeketh praise for himself; but whosoever seeketh praise for him
that sent him, he is true, and unrighteousness in his heart \[20\] there
is none.   <span id="fr_1954"></span>[1955](#fn_1954) Did not Moses give
you the law, and no man of you keepeth the \[21\] law?   <span
id="fr_1955"></span>[1956](#fn_1955) Why seek ye to kill me?  The
multitude answered and said unto him, Thou \[22\] hast demons: <span
id="fr_1956"></span>[1957](#fn_1956)   who seeketh to kill thee?   <span
id="fr_1957"></span>[1958](#fn_1957) Jesus answered and said unto them,
I did \[23\] one deed, and ye all marvel because of this.   <span
id="fr_1958"></span>[1959](#fn_1958) Moses hath given you circumcision
(not because it is from Moses, but it is from the fathers); and ye on
the sabbath <span id="page_87">p. 87</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xxviii-Page_87"></span>\[24\] circumcise a man.   <span
id="fr_1959"></span>[1960](#fn_1959) And if a man is circumcised on the
sabbath day, that the law of Moses may not be broken; are ye angry at
me, because I healed on the sabbath \[25\] day the whole man?   <span
id="fr_1960"></span>[1961](#fn_1960) Judge not with hypocrisy, but judge
righteous judgement.

<span id="iv.iii.xxviii-p35"></span>\[26\]  <span
id="fr_1961"></span>[1962](#fn_1961) And some people from Jerusalem
said, Is not this he whom they seek to slay?  \[27\]  <span
id="fr_1962"></span>[1963](#fn_1962) And lo, he discourseth with them
openly, and they say nothing unto him.  Think \[28\] you that our elders
have learned that this is the Messiah indeed?   <span
id="fr_1963"></span>[1964](#fn_1963) But this man is <span
id="fr_1964"></span>[1965](#fn_1964) known whence he is; and the
Messiah, when he cometh, no man knoweth whence \[29\] he is.   <span
id="fr_1965"></span>[1966](#fn_1965) So Jesus lifted up his voice as he
taught in the temple, and said, Ye both know me, and know whence I am;
and of my own accord am I not come, but he \[30\] \[Arabic, p. 109\]
that sent me is true, he whom ye know not:   <span
id="fr_1966"></span>[1967](#fn_1966) but I know him; for I am \[31\]
from him, and he sent me.   <span id="fr_1967"></span>[1968](#fn_1967)
And they sought to seize him:  and no man \[32\] laid a hand on him,
because his hour had not yet come.   <span
id="fr_1968"></span>[1969](#fn_1968) But many of the multitude believed
on him; and they said, The Messiah, when he cometh, can it be that he
will do more than these signs that this *man* doeth?

<span id="iv.iii.xxviii-p44"></span>\[33\]  <span
id="fr_1969"></span>[1970](#fn_1969) And a man of that multitude said
unto our Lord, Teacher, say to my brother \[34\] that he divide with me
the inheritance.   <span id="fr_1970"></span>[1971](#fn_1970) Jesus said
unto him, Man, who is it that \[35\] appointed me over you as a judge
and divider?   <span id="fr_1971"></span>[1972](#fn_1971) And he said
unto his disciples, Take heed within yourselves of all inordinate
desire; for it is not in abundance of \[36\] possessions that life shall
be.   <span id="fr_1972"></span>[1973](#fn_1972) And he gave them this
parable:  The ground of a \[37\] rich man brought forth abundant
produce:   <span id="fr_1973"></span>[1974](#fn_1973) and he pondered
within himself, and \[38\] said, What shall I do, since I have no place
to store my produce?   <span id="fr_1974"></span>[1975](#fn_1974) And he
said, I will do this:  I will pull down the buildings of my barns, and
build them, and make \[39\] them greater; and store there all my wheat
and my goods.   <span id="fr_1975"></span>[1976](#fn_1975) And I will
say to my soul, Soul, thou hast much goods laid by for many years; take
thine ease, eat, \[40\] drink, enjoy thyself.   <span
id="fr_1976"></span>[1977](#fn_1976) God said unto him, O *thou* of
little intelligence, this night shall thy soul be taken from thee; and
this that thou hast prepared, whose shall it \[41\] be?   <span
id="fr_1977"></span>[1978](#fn_1977) So is he that layeth up treasures
for himself, and is not rich in God.

<span id="iv.iii.xxviii-p54"></span>\[42\]  <span
id="fr_1978"></span>[1979](#fn_1978) And while Jesus was going in the
way, there came near to him a young man <span
id="fr_1979"></span>[1980](#fn_1979) of the rulers, <span
id="fr_1980"></span>[1981](#fn_1980) and fell on his knees, and asked
him, and said, Good Teacher, what is \[43\] it that I must do that I may
have eternal life?   <span id="fr_1981"></span>[1982](#fn_1981) Jesus
said unto him, Why callest thou \[44\] me good, while there is none good
but the one, *even* God? <span id="fr_1982"></span>[1983](#fn_1982)  
 <span id="fr_1983"></span>[1984](#fn_1983) Thou knowest the
commandments. <span id="fr_1984"></span>[1985](#fn_1984)   \[45\]  <span
id="fr_1985"></span>[1986](#fn_1985) If thou wouldest enter into life,
keep the commandments. <span id="fr_1986"></span>[1987](#fn_1986)   The
young \[Arabic, p. 110\] man said unto him, Which of the
commandments? <span id="fr_1987"></span>[1988](#fn_1987)   Jesus said
unto him, \[46\]  <span id="fr_1988"></span>[1989](#fn_1988) Thou shalt
not commit adultery, Thou shalt not steal, Thou shalt not kill, Thou
shalt not bear false witness, Thou shalt not do injury, Honour thy
father \[47\] and thy mother:  and, Love thy neighbour as thyself. 
 <span id="fr_1989"></span>[1990](#fn_1989) That young man said unto
\[48\] him, All these have I kept from my youth:  what then is it that I
lack?   <span id="fr_1990"></span>[1991](#fn_1990) And Jesus \[49\]
looked intently at him, and loved him, and said unto him,  <span
id="fr_1991"></span>[1992](#fn_1991) If thou wouldest be perfect, what
thou lackest is one thing: <span id="fr_1992"></span>[1993](#fn_1992)  
go away and sell everything that thou hast, and give to the poor, and
thou shalt have treasure in heaven:  and take thy \[50\] cross, and
follow me.   <span id="fr_1993"></span>[1994](#fn_1993) And that young
man frowned at this word, and went away \[51\] feeling sad; for he was
very rich.   <span id="fr_1994"></span>[1995](#fn_1994) And when Jesus
saw his sadness, he looked towards his disciples, and said unto them,
How hard it is for them that have possessions to enter the kingdom of
God!
