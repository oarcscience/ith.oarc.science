<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xvii-p1.1"></span>Section XVII.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_1203"></span>[1204](#fn_1203) And he set forth to them another
parable, and said, The kingdom of heaven is \[2\] like a man who sowed
good seed in his field;  <span id="fr_1204"></span>[1205](#fn_1204) but
when men slept, his enemy came \[3\] and sowed tares among the wheat,
and went away.   <span id="fr_1205"></span>[1206](#fn_1205) And when the
blade sprang up \[4\] and brought forth fruit, there were noticed the
tares also.   <span id="fr_1206"></span>[1207](#fn_1206) And the
servants of the master of the house came, and said unto him, Our lord,
didst thou not sow good <span id="page_70">p. 70</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xvii-Page_70"></span>\[5\] \[Arabic, p. 66\] seed in thy
field? whence are there tares in it?   <span
id="fr_1207"></span>[1208](#fn_1207) He said unto them, An enemy hath
done this.  His servants said unto him, Wilt thou that we go \[6\] and
separate it?   <span id="fr_1208"></span>[1209](#fn_1208) He said unto
them, Perhaps, <span id="fr_1209"></span>[1210](#fn_1209) when ye
separate the tares, ye would \[7\] root up with them wheat also.   <span
id="fr_1210"></span>[1211](#fn_1210) Leave them to grow both together
until the harvest:  and in the time of the harvest I will say unto the
reapers, Separate the tares first, and bind them in bundles to be burned
with fire; and gather the wheat into my barns.

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p11"></span>\[8, 9\]  <span
id="fr_1211"></span>[1212](#fn_1211) And he set forth to them another
parable, and said,  <span id="fr_1212"></span>[1213](#fn_1212) To what
is the kingdom of \[10\] God like? and to what shall I liken it? and in
what parable shall I set it forth?   <span
id="fr_1213"></span>[1214](#fn_1213) It \[11\] is like a grain of
mustard seed, which a man took, and planted in his field:   <span
id="fr_1214"></span>[1215](#fn_1214) and of the number of the things
that are sown in the earth it is smaller than all of the things \[12\]
which are sown, which are upon the earth;  <span
id="fr_1215"></span>[1216](#fn_1215) but when it is grown, it is greater
than all the herbs, and produceth large branches, so that the birds of
heaven make *their* nests in its branches.

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p17"></span>\[13, 14\]  <span
id="fr_1216"></span>[1217](#fn_1216) And he set forth to them another
parable:   <span id="fr_1217"></span>[1218](#fn_1217) To what shall I
liken the kingdom of \[15\] God?   <span
id="fr_1218"></span>[1219](#fn_1218) It is like the leaven which a woman
took, and kneaded into three measures of flour, until the whole of it
was leavened.

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p21"></span>\[16\]  <span
id="fr_1219"></span>[1220](#fn_1219) And Jesus spake all that to the
multitudes by way of parables, according as they \[17\] were able to
hear.   <span id="fr_1220"></span>[1221](#fn_1220) And without parables
spake he not unto them; that the saying of the Lord through the prophet
might be fulfilled:

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p24"></span>I will open my mouth in parables;

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p25"></span>And I will utter secrets which were
before the foundations <span id="fr_1221"></span>[1222](#fn_1221) of the
world.

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p27"></span>\[18\]  <span
id="fr_1222"></span>[1223](#fn_1222) But he explained to his disciples
privately everything.

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p29"></span>\[19\]  <span
id="fr_1223"></span>[1224](#fn_1223) Then Jesus left <span
id="fr_1224"></span>[1225](#fn_1224) the multitudes, and came to the
house.  And his disciples came unto him, and said unto him, Explain unto
us that parable about the tares \[20\] \[Arabic, p. 67\] and the field. 
 <span id="fr_1225"></span>[1226](#fn_1225) He answered and said unto
them, He that sowed good seed is \[21\] the Son of man;  <span
id="fr_1226"></span>[1227](#fn_1226) and the field is the world; and the
good seed are the children of the \[22\] kingdom;  <span
id="fr_1227"></span>[1228](#fn_1227) and the tares are the children of
the evil one; and the enemy that sowed them <span
id="fr_1228"></span>[1229](#fn_1228) is Satan; and the harvest is the
end of the world; and the reapers are the angels.  \[23\]  <span
id="fr_1229"></span>[1230](#fn_1229) And as the tares are separated and
burned in the fire, so shall it be in the end of \[24\] this world. 
 <span id="fr_1230"></span>[1231](#fn_1230) The Son of man shall send
his angels, and separate from his kingdom \[25\] all things that injure,
 <span id="fr_1231"></span>[1232](#fn_1231) and all the doers of
iniquity, and they shall cast them into the \[26\] furnace of fire:  and
there shall be weeping and gnashing of teeth.   <span
id="fr_1232"></span>[1233](#fn_1232) Then the righteous shall shine as
the sun in the kingdom of their Father.  Whosoever hath ears that hear,
let him hear.

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p40"></span>\[27\]  <span
id="fr_1233"></span>[1234](#fn_1233) And again the kingdom of heaven is
like treasure hid in a field:  that which a man found and hid; and, for
his pleasure in it, went and sold all that he had, and bought that
field.

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p42"></span>\[28\]  <span
id="fr_1234"></span>[1235](#fn_1234) And again the kingdom of heaven is
like a man *that is* a merchant seeking excellent \[29\] pearls;  <span
id="fr_1235"></span>[1236](#fn_1235) and when he found one pearl of
great price, he went and sold everything that he had, and bought it.

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p45"></span>\[30\]  <span
id="fr_1236"></span>[1237](#fn_1236) And again the kingdom of heaven is
like a net that was cast <span id="fr_1237"></span>[1238](#fn_1237) into
the sea, and \[31\] gathered of every kind:   <span
id="fr_1238"></span>[1239](#fn_1238) and when it was filled, they drew
it up on to the shore of the sea, and sat down to select; and the good
of them they threw into the vessels, \[32\] and the bad they threw
outside.   <span id="fr_1239"></span>[1240](#fn_1239) Thus shall it be
in the end of the world:  the angels \[33\] shall go forth,  <span
id="fr_1240"></span>[1241](#fn_1240) and separate the wicked from among
the good, and shall cast them into the furnace of fire:  there shall be
weeping and gnashing of teeth.

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p51"></span>\[34\]  <span
id="fr_1241"></span>[1242](#fn_1241) Jesus said unto them, Have ye
understood all these *things?*  They said unto \[35\] \[Arabic, p. 68\]
him, Yea, our Lord.   <span id="fr_1242"></span>[1243](#fn_1242) He said
unto them, Therefore every scribe that becometh a disciple of the
kingdom of heaven is like a man that is a householder, who bringeth out
of his treasures the new and the old.

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p54"></span>\[36, 37\]  <span
id="fr_1243"></span>[1244](#fn_1243) And when Jesus had finished all
these parables, he removed thence, and came to his city;  <span
id="fr_1244"></span>[1245](#fn_1244) and he taught them in their
synagogues, so that they were perplexed.  \[38\]  <span
id="fr_1245"></span>[1246](#fn_1245) And when the sabbath came, Jesus
began to teach in the synagogue; and many of \[39\] those that heard
marvelled, and said, Whence came these things to this *man?*  And many
envied him and gave no heed to him, but said, What is this wisdom that
is <span id="page_71">p. 71</span> <span
id="iv.iii.xvii-Page_71"></span>given to this *man*, that there should
happen at his hands such as these mighty works? <span
id="fr_1246"></span>[1247](#fn_1246)   \[40\]  <span
id="fr_1247"></span>[1248](#fn_1247) Is not this a carpenter, son of a
carpenter? and is not his mother called Mary? and \[41\] his brethren,
James, and Joses, and Simon, and Judas?   <span
id="fr_1248"></span>[1249](#fn_1248) And his sisters, all of them,
\[42\] lo, are they not all with us?   <span
id="fr_1249"></span>[1250](#fn_1249) Whence hath this *man* all these
things?  And they were in doubt concerning him.   <span
id="fr_1250"></span>[1251](#fn_1250) And Jesus knew their opinion, and
said unto them, Will ye haply <span id="fr_1251"></span>[1252](#fn_1251)
say unto me this proverb, Physician, heal first thyself:  and all that
\[43\] we have heard that thou didst in Capernaum, do here also in thine
*own* city?   <span id="fr_1252"></span>[1253](#fn_1252) And he said,
Verily I say unto you, A prophet is not received in his *own* city, nor
among \[44\] his brethren:   <span id="fr_1253"></span>[1254](#fn_1253)
for a prophet is not despised, save in his *own* city, and among his
*own* \[45\] kin, and in his *own* house.   <span
id="fr_1254"></span>[1255](#fn_1254) Verily I say unto you, In the days
of Elijah the prophet, there were many widows among the children of
Israel, when the heaven held back \[46\] three years and six months,
 <span id="fr_1255"></span>[1256](#fn_1255) and there was a great famine
in all the land; and Elijah \[Arabic, p. 69\] was not sent to one of
them, save to Zarephath of Sidon, to a woman that was \[47\] a widow. 
 <span id="fr_1256"></span>[1257](#fn_1256) And many lepers were among
the children of Israel in the days of Elisha the prophet; but not one of
them was cleansed, save Naaman the Nabathæan. <span
id="fr_1257"></span>[1258](#fn_1257)   \[48\]  <span
id="fr_1258"></span>[1259](#fn_1258) And he was not able to do there
many mighty works, <span id="fr_1259"></span>[1260](#fn_1259) because of
their unbelief; \[49\] except that he laid his hand upon a few of the
sick, and healed *them*.   <span id="fr_1260"></span>[1261](#fn_1260)
And he marvelled \[50\] at their lack of faith.   <span
id="fr_1261"></span>[1262](#fn_1261) And when those who were in the
synagogue heard, \[51\] they were all filled with wrath; and they rose
up,  <span id="fr_1262"></span>[1263](#fn_1262) and brought him forth
outside the city, and brought him to the brow of the hill upon which
their city was built, that \[52\] they might cast him from its summit: 
 <span id="fr_1263"></span>[1264](#fn_1263) but he passed through among
them and went away.

<span id="iv.iii.xvii-p76"></span>\[53\]  <span
id="fr_1264"></span>[1265](#fn_1264) And he went about in the villages
which *were* around Nazareth, and taught in their synagogues.
