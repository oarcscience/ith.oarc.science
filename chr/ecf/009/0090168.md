<span id="xii.vi.vi-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="xii.vi.vi-p1.1"></span>Chapter VI.—The Present and Future Worlds are
Enemies to Each Other.</span>

<span id="xii.vi.vi-p2"></span>Now the Lord declares, “No servant can
serve two masters.” <span id="fr_4359"></span>[4360](#fn_4359.md)   If we
desire, then, to serve both God and mammon, it will be unprofitable for
us.  “For what will it profit if a man gain the whole world, and lose
his own soul?” <span id="fr_4360"></span>[4361](#fn_4360.md)   This world
and the next are two enemies.  The one urges <span
id="fr_4361"></span>[4362](#fn_4361.md) to adultery and corruption, avarice
and deceit; the other bids farewell to these things.  We cannot,
therefore, be the friends of both; and it behoves us, by renouncing the
one, to make sure <span id="fr_4362"></span>[4363](#fn_4362.md) of the
other.  Let us reckon <span id="fr_4363"></span>[4364](#fn_4363.md) that it
is better to hate the things present, since they are trifling, and
transient, and corruptible; and to love those \[which are to come,\] as
being good and incorruptible.  For if we do the will of Christ, we shall
find rest; otherwise, nothing shall deliver us from eternal punishment,
if we disobey His commandments.  For thus also saith the Scripture in
Ezekiel, “If Noah, Job, and Daniel should rise up, they should not
deliver their children in captivity.” <span
id="fr_4364"></span>[4365](#fn_4364.md)   Now, if men so eminently
righteous are not able by their righteousness to deliver their children,
how <span id="fr_4365"></span>[4366](#fn_4365.md) can we hope to enter into
the royal residence <span id="fr_4366"></span>[4367](#fn_4366.md) of God
unless we keep our baptism holy and undefiled?  Or who shall be our
advocate, <span id="page_253">p. 253</span> <span
id="xii.vi.vi-Page_253"></span>unless we be found possessed of works of
holiness and righteousness?
