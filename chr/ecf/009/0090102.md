<span id="xii.iv.vii-p1"></span> <span class="c2"><span
id="xii.iv.vii-p1.1"></span>Chapter VII.—An Exhortation to
Repentance.</span>

<span id="xii.iv.vii-p2"></span>These things, beloved, we write unto
you, not merely to admonish you of your duty, but also to remind
ourselves.  For we are struggling on the same arena, and the same
conflict is assigned to both of us.  Wherefore let us give up vain and
fruitless cares, and approach to the glorious and venerable rule of our
holy calling. <span id="fr_4036"></span>[4037](#fn_4036)   Let us attend
to what is good, pleasing, and acceptable in the sight of Him who formed
us.  Let us look stedfastly to the blood of Christ, and see how precious
that blood is to God <span id="fr_4037"></span>[4038](#fn_4037) which,
having been shed for our salvation, has set the grace of repentance
before the whole world. <span id="fr_4038"></span>[4039](#fn_4038)   Let
us turn to <span id="fr_4039"></span>[4040](#fn_4039) every age that has
passed, and learn that, from generation to generation, the Lord has
granted a place of repentance to all such as would be converted unto
Him.  Noah preached repentance, and as many as listened to him were
saved. <span id="fr_4040"></span>[4041](#fn_4040)   Jonah proclaimed
destruction to the Ninevites; <span id="fr_4041"></span>[4042](#fn_4041)
but they, repenting of their sins, propitiated God by prayer, and
obtained salvation, although they were aliens \[to the covenant\] of
God.
