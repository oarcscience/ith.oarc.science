<span id="iv.iii.xiv-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.xiv-p1.1"></span>Section XIV.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.xiv-p2"></span>\[1\]  <span
id="fr_1011"></span>[1012](#fn_1011.md) Verily I say unto you, There hath
not arisen among those whom women have borne a greater than John the
Baptist; but he that is little now in the kingdom of heaven is greater
than he.

<span id="iv.iii.xiv-p4"></span>\[2\] \[Arabic, p. 54\]  <span
id="fr_1012"></span>[1013](#fn_1012.md) And all the people which heard, and
the publicans, justified <span id="fr_1013"></span>[1014](#fn_1013.md) God,
for \[3\] they had been baptized with the baptism of John.   <span
id="fr_1014"></span>[1015](#fn_1014.md) But the Pharisees and the scribes
wronged <span id="fr_1015"></span>[1016](#fn_1015.md) the purpose of God in
themselves, in that they were not baptized of \[4\] him.   <span
id="fr_1016"></span>[1017](#fn_1016.md) And from the days of John the
Baptist until now the kingdom of heaven is \[5\] snatched away by
violence.   <span id="fr_1017"></span>[1018](#fn_1017.md) The law and the
prophets *were* until John; and after that, the kingdom of God is
preached, and all press to enter it:   <span
id="fr_1018"></span>[1019](#fn_1018.md) and they that exert themselves \[6,
7\] snatch it away.   <span id="fr_1019"></span>[1020](#fn_1019.md) All the
prophets and the law until John prophesied.   <span
id="fr_1020"></span>[1021](#fn_1020.md) And if ye \[8\] will, then receive
*it*, that he is Elijah, which is to come.   <span
id="fr_1021"></span>[1022](#fn_1021.md) Whosoever hath ears that hear \[9\]
let him hear.   <span id="fr_1022"></span>[1023](#fn_1022.md) Easier is the
perishing of heaven and earth, than the passing away of \[10\] one point
of the law.   <span id="fr_1023"></span>[1024](#fn_1023.md) To whom then
shall I liken the people of this generation, <span
id="fr_1024"></span>[1025](#fn_1024.md) and \[11\] to whom are they like? 
 <span id="fr_1025"></span>[1026](#fn_1025.md) They are like the children
sitting in the market, which call to their companions, and say, We sang
to you, and ye danced not; we wailed to you, \[12\] and ye wept not. 
 <span id="fr_1026"></span>[1027](#fn_1026.md) John the Baptist came
neither eating bread nor drinking wine; \[13\] and ye said, He hath
demons:   <span id="fr_1027"></span>[1028](#fn_1027.md) and the Son of man
came eating and drinking; and ye said, Behold, a gluttonous man, and a
drinker of wine, and an associate of publicans \[14, 15\] and sinners! 
 <span id="fr_1028"></span>[1029](#fn_1028.md) And wisdom was justified of
all her children.   <span id="fr_1029"></span>[1030](#fn_1029.md) And when
he said that, they came to the house.  And there gathered unto him again
multitudes, \[16\] so that they found not bread to eat.   <span
id="fr_1030"></span>[1031](#fn_1030.md) And while he was casting out a
devil which was dumb, when he cast out that devil, that dumb *man*
spake.  And the multitudes \[17\] \[Arabic, p. 55\] marvelled.   <span
id="fr_1031"></span>[1032](#fn_1031.md) And the Pharisees, when they heard,
said, This *man* doth not cast out the devils, except by Beelzebul the
chief of the demons, which is in him.  \[18, 19\]  <span
id="fr_1032"></span>[1033](#fn_1032.md) And others requested of him a sign
from heaven, to tempt him.   <span id="fr_1033"></span>[1034](#fn_1033.md)
And Jesus knew their thoughts, and said unto them in parables, Every
kingdom that withstandeth itself shall become desolate; and every house
or city that disagreeth with itself shall not \[20\] stand:   <span
id="fr_1034"></span>[1035](#fn_1034.md) and if a devil cast out a devil, he
withstandeth himself; neither shall he be \[21\] able to stand, but his
end shall be.   <span id="fr_1035"></span>[1036](#fn_1035.md) Then how now
shall his kingdom stand? for ye \[22\] said that I cast out devils by
Beelzebul.   <span id="fr_1036"></span>[1037](#fn_1036.md) And if I by
Beelzebul cast out the devils, then your children, by what do they cast
them out?  And for this cause they shall \[23\] be judges against you. 
 <span id="fr_1037"></span>[1038](#fn_1037.md) But if I by the Spirit of
God cast out devils, then the kingdom \[24\] of God is come near unto
you.   <span id="fr_1038"></span>[1039](#fn_1038.md) Or how can a man enter
into the house of a valiant *man*, and seize his garments, <span
id="fr_1039"></span>[1040](#fn_1039.md) if he do not beforehand secure
himself <span id="fr_1040"></span>[1041](#fn_1040.md) from \[25\] that
valiant *man?* and then will he cut off <span
id="fr_1041"></span>[1042](#fn_1041.md) his house.   <span
id="fr_1042"></span>[1043](#fn_1042.md) But when the valiant man is \[26\]
armed, guarding his house, his possessions are in peace.   <span
id="fr_1043"></span>[1044](#fn_1043.md) But if one come who is more valiant
than he, he overcometh him, and taketh his whole armour, on which \[27\]
he relieth, and divideth his spoil.   <span
id="fr_1044"></span>[1045](#fn_1044.md) Whosoever is not with me is against
me; and \[28\] whosoever gathereth not with me scattereth abroad. 
 <span id="fr_1045"></span>[1046](#fn_1045.md) For this reason I say unto
you, \[Arabic, p. 56\] that all sins and blasphemies with which men
blaspheme shall be forgiven \[29\] them:   <span
id="fr_1046"></span>[1047](#fn_1046.md) but whosoever shall blaspheme
against the Holy Spirit, there is no \[30\] forgiveness for him for
ever, but he is deserving of eternal punishment:   <span
id="fr_1047"></span>[1048](#fn_1047.md) because they <span id="page_66">p.
66</span> <span id="iv.iii.xiv-Page_66"></span>\[31\] said that he had
an unclean spirit.   <span id="fr_1048"></span>[1049](#fn_1048.md) And he
said also, Every one that speaketh a word against the Son of man, it
shall be forgiven him; but whosoever speaketh against the Holy Spirit,
it shall not be forgiven him, neither in this world, nor in the world to
\[32\] come.   <span id="fr_1049"></span>[1050](#fn_1049.md) Either ye must
make a good tree <span id="fr_1050"></span>[1051](#fn_1050.md) and its
fruit good; or ye must make an evil \[33\] tree <span
id="fr_1051"></span>[1052](#fn_1051.md) and its fruit evil:  for the tree
is known by its fruit.   <span id="fr_1052"></span>[1053](#fn_1052.md) Ye
children of vipers, how can ye, being evil, speak good things? from the
overflowings of the heart the mouth \[34\] speaketh.   <span
id="fr_1053"></span>[1054](#fn_1053.md) The good man from the good
treasures which are in his heart bringeth forth good things; and the
wicked man from the evil treasures which are in his \[35\] heart
bringeth forth evils.   <span id="fr_1054"></span>[1055](#fn_1054.md) I say
unto you, that every idle word which men shall \[36\] speak, they shall
give an answer for in the day of judgement:   <span
id="fr_1055"></span>[1056](#fn_1055.md) for by thy sayings thou shalt be
justified, and by thy sayings thou shalt be judged.

<span id="iv.iii.xiv-p49"></span>\[37\]  <span
id="fr_1056"></span>[1057](#fn_1056.md) And he said to the multitudes, When
ye see the clouds appear from the west, \[38\] straightway ye say that
there cometh rain; and so it cometh to pass.   <span
id="fr_1057"></span>[1058](#fn_1057.md) And when \[39\] the south wind
bloweth, ye say that there will be heat; and it cometh to pass.   <span
id="fr_1058"></span>[1059](#fn_1058.md) And when the evening is come, ye
say, It *will be* fair weather, for the heaven has become \[40\] red. 
 <span id="fr_1059"></span>[1060](#fn_1059.md) And in the morning ye say,
To-day there will be severe weather, for the redness \[Arabic, p. 57\]
of the heaven is paling.   <span id="fr_1060"></span>[1061](#fn_1060.md)
*Ye* hypocrites, ye know to examine the face of the heaven and the
earth; but the signs of this time ye know not to discern.

<span id="iv.iii.xiv-p55"></span>\[41\]  <span
id="fr_1061"></span>[1062](#fn_1061.md) Then they brought to him one
possessed of a demon, dumb *and* blind; and he \[42\] healed him, so
that the dumb and blind began to speak and see.   <span
id="fr_1062"></span>[1063](#fn_1062.md) And all the multitudes wondered,
and said, Is this, think you, the son of David?

<span id="iv.iii.xiv-p58"></span>\[43\]  <span
id="fr_1063"></span>[1064](#fn_1063.md) And the apostles returned unto
Jesus, and told him everything that they had \[44\] done and
wrought. <span id="fr_1064"></span>[1065](#fn_1064.md)    <span
id="fr_1065"></span>[1066](#fn_1065.md) And he said unto them, Come, let us
go into the desert alone, and rest ye a little.  And many were going and
returning, and they had not leisure, not even to eat bread.

<span id="iv.iii.xiv-p62"></span>\[45\]  <span
id="fr_1066"></span>[1067](#fn_1066.md) And after that, there came to *him*
one of the Pharisees, and besought him that he would eat bread with
him.  And he entered into the house of that Pharisee, and \[46\]
reclined.   <span id="fr_1067"></span>[1068](#fn_1067.md) And there was in
that city a woman *that was* a sinner; and when she knew that he was
sitting in the house of that Pharisee, she took a box of sweet ointment,
\[47\] and stood behind him,  <span id="fr_1068"></span>[1069](#fn_1068.md)
towards his feet, weeping, and began to wet his feet with her tears, and
to wipe them with the hair of her head, and to kiss his feet, and \[48\]
anoint them with the sweet ointment.   <span
id="fr_1069"></span>[1070](#fn_1069.md) And when that <span
id="fr_1070"></span>[1071](#fn_1070.md) Pharisee saw *it*, who invited him,
he thought within himself, and said, This *man*, if he were a prophet,
would know who she is and what is her history:  for the woman which
touched him was a sinner.
