<span id="iv.iii.ii-p1"></span> <span class="c6"><span
id="iv.iii.ii-p1.1"></span>Section II.</span>

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p2"></span>\[1\] \[Arabic, p. 7\]  <span
id="fr_172"></span>[173](#fn_172) Now <span
id="fr_173"></span>[174](#fn_173) the birth of Jesus the Messiah was on
this wise:  In the time when his mother was given in marriage to Joseph,
before they came together, \[2\] she was found with child of the Holy
Spirit.   <span id="fr_174"></span>[175](#fn_174) And Joseph her husband
was a just *man* and did not wish to expose her, and he purposed to put
her away secretly.  \[3\]  <span id="fr_175"></span>[176](#fn_175) But
when he thought of this, the angel of the Lord appeared unto him in a
dream, and said unto him, Joseph, son of David, fear not to take Mary
thy wife, for that \[4\] which is begotten <span
id="fr_176"></span>[177](#fn_176) in her is of the Holy Spirit.   <span
id="fr_177"></span>[178](#fn_177) She shall bear a son, and thou shalt
\[5\] call his name Jesus, and he shall save <span
id="fr_178"></span>[179](#fn_178) his people from their sins.   <span
id="fr_179"></span>[180](#fn_179) And all this was that the saying from
the Lord by the prophet might be fulfilled:

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p11"></span>\[6\]  <span
id="fr_180"></span>[181](#fn_180) Behold, the virgin shall conceive, and
bear a son,

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p13"></span>And they shall call his name Immanuel,

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p14"></span>\[7\] which is, being interpreted, With
us is our God.   <span id="fr_181"></span>[182](#fn_181) And when Joseph
arose from his \[8\] sleep, he did as the angel of the Lord commanded
him, and took his wife;  <span id="fr_182"></span>[183](#fn_182) and
knew her not until she brought forth her firstborn son.

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p17"></span>\[9\]  <span
id="fr_183"></span>[184](#fn_183) And in those days there went forth a
decree from Augustus Cæsar that all the <span id="page_46">p. 46</span>
<span id="iv.iii.ii-Page_46"></span>\[10\] people of his dominion <span
id="fr_184"></span>[185](#fn_184) should be enrolled.  <span
id="fr_185"></span>[186](#fn_185) This first enrolment was <span
id="fr_186"></span>[187](#fn_186) while Quirinius \[11, 12\] was
governor of Syria.   <span id="fr_187"></span>[188](#fn_187) And every
man went to be enrolled in his city.   <span
id="fr_188"></span>[189](#fn_188) And Joseph went up also from Nazareth,
a city of Galilee, to Judæa, to the city of David \[13\] which is called
Bethlehem (for he was of the house of David and of his tribe),  <span
id="fr_189"></span>[190](#fn_189) with \[14\] \[Arabic, p. 8\] Mary his
betrothed, she being with child, to be enrolled there.   <span
id="fr_190"></span>[191](#fn_190) And while \[15\] she was there the
days for her being delivered were accomplished.   <span
id="fr_191"></span>[192](#fn_191) And she brought forth her firstborn
son; and she wrapped him in swaddling cloths and laid him in a manger,
because there was no place for them where they were staying.

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p27"></span>\[16\]  <span
id="fr_192"></span>[193](#fn_192) And there were in that region
shepherds abiding, keeping their flock in the watch \[17\] of the
night.   <span id="fr_193"></span>[194](#fn_193) And behold, the angel
of God came unto them, and the glory of the \[18\] Lord shone upon them;
and they were greatly terrified.   <span
id="fr_194"></span>[195](#fn_194) And the angel said unto them, Be not
terrified; for I bring you tidings of great joy which shall be to the
\[19\] whole world;  <span id="fr_195"></span>[196](#fn_195) there is
born to you this day a Saviour, which is the Lord the Messiah, \[20\] in
the city of David.   <span id="fr_196"></span>[197](#fn_196) And this is
a sign for you:  ye shall find a babe wrapped \[21\] in swaddling cloths
and laid in a manger.   <span id="fr_197"></span>[198](#fn_197) And
there appeared with the angels suddenly many heavenly forces
praising <span id="fr_198"></span>[199](#fn_198) God and saying,

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p35"></span>\[22\]  <span
id="fr_199"></span>[200](#fn_199) Praise be to God in the highest,

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p37"></span>And on the earth peace, and good hope to
men.

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p38"></span>\[23\]  <span
id="fr_200"></span>[201](#fn_200) And when the angels departed from them
to heaven, the shepherds spake to one another and said, We will go to
Bethlehem and see this word which hath been, as \[24\] the Lord made
known unto us.   <span id="fr_201"></span>[202](#fn_201) And they came
with haste, and found Mary and \[25\] Joseph, and the babe laid in a
manger.   <span id="fr_202"></span>[203](#fn_202) And when they saw,
they reported the word \[26\] which was spoken to them about the child. 
 <span id="fr_203"></span>[204](#fn_203) And all that heard wondered at
the \[27\] description which the shepherds described <span
id="fr_204"></span>[205](#fn_204) to them.   <span
id="fr_205"></span>[206](#fn_205) But Mary kept these <span
id="fr_206"></span>[207](#fn_206) sayings \[28\] and discriminated <span
id="fr_207"></span>[208](#fn_207) them in her heart.   <span
id="fr_208"></span>[209](#fn_208) And those shepherds returned,
magnifying and praising God for all that they had seen and heard,
according as it was described unto them.

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p48"></span>\[29\] \[Arabic, p. 9\]  <span
id="fr_209"></span>[210](#fn_209) And when eight days were fulfilled
that the child should be circumcised, his name was called Jesus, being
that by which he was called by the angel before his conception in the
womb.

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p50"></span>\[30\]  <span
id="fr_210"></span>[211](#fn_210) And when the days of their
purification according to the law of Moses were \[31\] completed, they
took him up to Jerusalem to present him before the Lord  <span
id="fr_211"></span>[212](#fn_211) (as it is written in the law of the
Lord, Every male opening the womb shall be called the \[32\] holy
*thing* of the Lord),  <span id="fr_212"></span>[213](#fn_212) and to
give a sacrificial victim as it is said in the law of \[33\] the Lord, A
pair of doves or two young pigeons.   <span
id="fr_213"></span>[214](#fn_213) And there was in Jerusalem a man whose
name was Simeon; and this man was upright and pious, and expecting
\[34\] the consolation of Israel; and the Holy Spirit was upon him. 
 <span id="fr_214"></span>[215](#fn_214) And it had been said unto him
by the Holy Spirit, that he should not see death till he had seen with
\[35\] his eyes the Messiah <span id="fr_215"></span>[216](#fn_215) of
the Lord.   <span id="fr_216"></span>[217](#fn_216) And this man came by
the Spirit to the temple; and at the time when his parents brought in
the child Jesus, that they might \[36\] present for him a sacrifice, as
it is written in the law,  <span id="fr_217"></span>[218](#fn_217) he
bare him in his arms and praised God and said,

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p59"></span>\[37\]  <span
id="fr_218"></span>[219](#fn_218) Now loosest thou the bonds of thy
servant, O Lord, in peace, <span id="fr_219"></span>[220](#fn_219)

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p62"></span>According to thy saying;

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p63"></span>\[38\]  <span
id="fr_220"></span>[221](#fn_220) For mine eye hath witnessed thy mercy,

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p65"></span>\[39\]  <span
id="fr_221"></span>[222](#fn_221) Which thou hast made ready because of
the whole world;

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p67"></span>\[40\]  <span
id="fr_222"></span>[223](#fn_222) A light for the unveiling <span
id="fr_223"></span>[224](#fn_223) of the nations,

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p70"></span>And a glory to thy people Israel.

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p71"></span>\[41\]  <span
id="fr_224"></span>[225](#fn_224) And Joseph and his mother were
marvelling at the things which were being said \[42\] concerning him. 
 <span id="fr_225"></span>[226](#fn_225) And Simeon blessed them and
said to Mary his mother, Behold, he is set for the overthrow and rising
of many in Israel; and for a sign of contention; \[43\]  <span
id="fr_226"></span>[227](#fn_226) and a spear <span
id="fr_227"></span>[228](#fn_227) shall pierce <span
id="fr_228"></span>[229](#fn_228) through thine own soul; that the
thoughts of the <span id="page_47">p. 47</span> <span
id="iv.iii.ii-Page_47"></span>\[44\] \[Arabic, p. 10\] hearts of many
may be revealed.   <span id="fr_229"></span>[230](#fn_229) And Anna the
prophetess, the daughter of Phanuel, of the tribe of Asher, was also
advanced in years (and she dwelt \[45\] with her husband seven years
from her virginity,  <span id="fr_230"></span>[231](#fn_230) and she
remained a widow about eighty-four years); and she left not the temple,
and served night and day with \[46\] fasting and prayer.   <span
id="fr_231"></span>[232](#fn_231) And she also rose in that hour and
thanked the Lord, and she \[47\] spake of him with every one who was
expecting the deliverance of Jerusalem.   <span
id="fr_232"></span>[233](#fn_232) And when they had accomplished
everything according to what is in the law of the Lord, they returned to
Galilee, to Nazareth their city.

<span id="iv.iii.ii-p4"></span> This is preceded in Vat. <span
class="sc"><span id="iv.iii.ii-p4.1"></span>ms</span>. by the genealogy,
[Matt. i. 1-17](../../../bib/kjv/mat001.htm#001)<span
id="iv.iii.ii-p4.2"></span> (see Introduction, 13), with the marginal
note *The Beginning of the Gospel of Matthew*.  (Lagarde, *op. cit*.,
1886, p. 154.)  The text presents nothing worthy of note in this place
except that verse 16, construed on the same principle as the preceding
verses, to which, except in the words printed in italics, it is strictly
parallel in construction, reads thus:  “Jacob begat Joseph, *the husband
of Mary*, who *of her* begat Jesus, the Messiah” (*cf*. the remarkable
reading of Sin. Syriac).  As it stands, this is the only possible
interpretation of the words, for *who* is masculine.  But a mistake in
the gender of a relative pronoun is very common in Arabic among
illiterate people, while in Syriac there is, to begin with, no
distinction.  If then we correct the relative, *who of her* will become
*of whom* (fem.), and *begat* will of course be construed as passive. 
We thus get the text followed in Ibn-at-Tayyib’s Commentary, the
ordinary reading of the Peshitta, *of whom was born Jesus*.
