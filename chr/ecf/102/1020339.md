<span id="iv.XII-p2"></span> ————————————

<span id="iv.XII-p3"></span>Argument—Augustin first institutes two
inquiries regarding the angels; namely, whence is there in some a good,
and in others an evil will? and, what is the reason of the blessedness
of the good, and the misery of the evil?  Afterwards he treats of the
creation of man, and teaches that he is not from eternity, but was
created, and by none other than God.
