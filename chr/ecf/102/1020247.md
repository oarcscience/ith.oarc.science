id="iv.IX-p1.1"></span>Book IX.</span>

<span id="iv.IX-p2"></span> ————————————

<span id="iv.IX-p3"></span>Argument—Having in the preceding book shown
that the worship of demons must be abjured, since they in a thousand
ways proclaim themselves to be wicked spirits, Augustin in this book
meets those who allege a distinction among demons, some being evil,
while others are good; and, having exploded this distinction, he proves
that to no demon, but to Christ alone, belongs the office of providing
men with eternal blessedness.
