<span id="iv.XX.27-p1"></span><span class="c2"><span
id="iv.XX.27-p1.1"></span>Chapter 27.—Of the Separation of the Good and
the Bad, Which Proclaim the Discriminating Influence of the Last
Judgment.</span>

<span id="iv.XX.27-p2"></span>The passage also which I formerly quoted
for another purpose from this prophet refers to the last judgment, in
which he says, “They shall be mine, saith the Lord Almighty, in the day
in which I make up my gains,” <span id="fr_1466"></span>[1467](#fn_1466)
etc.  When this diversity between the rewards and punishments which
distinguish the righteous from the wicked shall appear under that Sun of
righteousness in the brightness of life eternal,—a diversity which is
not discerned under this sun which shines on the vanity of this
life,—there shall then be such a judgment as has never before been.
