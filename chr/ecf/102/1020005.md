<span id="iv.ii-p3"></span> ————————————

<span id="iv.ii-p4"></span>Argument—Augustin censures the pagans, who
attributed the calamities of the world, and especially the recent sack
of Rome by the Goths, to the Christian religion, and its prohibition of
the worship of the gods.  He speaks of the blessings and ills of life,
which then, as always, happened to good and bad men alike.  Finally, he
rebukes the shamelessness of those who cast up to the Christians that
their women had been violated by the soldiers.
