<span id="v.iv.viii.iii-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.iv.viii.iii-p1.1"></span>Chapter 3.—</span>5.  But I think that we
have sufficiently shown, both from the canon of Scripture, and from the
letters of Cyprian himself, that bad men, while by no means converted to
a better mind, can have, and confer, and receive baptism, of whom it is
most clear that they do not belong to the holy Church of God, though
they seem to be within it, inasmuch as they are covetous, robbers,
usurers, envious, evil thinkers, and the like; while she is one
dove, <span id="fr_1587"></span>[1588](#fn_1587.md) modest and chaste, a
bride without spot or wrinkle, <span
id="fr_1588"></span>[1589](#fn_1588.md) a garden enclosed, a fountain
sealed, an orchard of pomegranates with pleasant fruits, <span
id="fr_1589"></span>[1590](#fn_1589.md) with all similar properties which
are attributed to her; <span id="page_481">p. 481</span> <span
id="v.iv.viii.iii-Page_481"></span>and all this can only be understood
to be in the good, and holy, and just,—following, that is, not only the
operations of the gifts of God, which are common to good and bad alike,
but also the inner bond of charity conspicuous in those who have the
Holy Spirit, to whom the Lord says, "Whosesoever sins ye remit, they are
remitted unto them; and whosesoever sins ye retain, they are retained."
