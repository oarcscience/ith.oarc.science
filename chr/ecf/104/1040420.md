<span id="v.iv.ix.xxi-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.iv.ix.xxi-p1.1"></span>Chapter 21.—</span>40.  Julianus of
Telepte <span id="fr_1816"></span>[1817](#fn_1816.md) said:  "It is
written, ‘A man can receive nothing, except it be given him from
heaven;’ <span id="fr_1817"></span>[1818](#fn_1817.md) if heresy is from
heaven, it can give baptism." <span id="fr_1818"></span>[1819](#fn_1818.md)

<span id="v.iv.ix.xxi-p5"></span>41.  Let him hear another also saying: 
If covetousness is from heaven, it can give baptism.  And yet the
covetous do confer it; so therefore also may the heretics.
