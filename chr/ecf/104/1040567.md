<span id="v.v.iv.lxxx-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.v.iv.lxxx-p1.1"></span>Chapter 80.—</span>175.  <span
class="c11"><span id="v.v.iv.lxxx-p1.2"></span>Petilianus</span> said: 
"Lastly, what is the justification of persecution?  I ask you, you
wretched men, if it so be that you think that your sin rests on any
authority of law."

<span id="v.v.iv.lxxx-p2"></span>176.  <span class="c11"><span
id="v.v.iv.lxxx-p2.1"></span>Augustin</span> answered:  He who sins,
sins not on the authority of the law, but against the authority of the
law.  But since you ask what <span id="page_571">p. 571</span> <span
id="v.v.iv.lxxx-Page_571"></span>is the justification of persecution, I
ask you in turn whose voice it is that says in the psalm, "Whoso privily
slandereth his neighbor, him will I cut off." <span
id="fr_2188"></span>[2189](#fn_2188.md)   Seek therefore the reason or the
measure of the persecution, and do not display your gross ignorance by
finding fault in general terms with those who persecute the unrighteous.
