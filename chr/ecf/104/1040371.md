<span id="v.iv.viii.xvii-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.iv.viii.xvii-p1.1"></span>Chapter 17.—</span>28.  Monnulus of
Girba <span id="fr_1642"></span>[1643](#fn_1642.md) said:  "The truth of
our mother, the Catholic Church, hath continued, and still continues
among us, brethren, especially in the threefold nature <span
id="fr_1643"></span>[1644](#fn_1643.md) of baptism, as our Lord says, ‘Go,
baptize all nations in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of
the Holy Ghost.’ <span id="fr_1644"></span>[1645](#fn_1644.md)   Since,
therefore," he goes on to say, "we know clearly that heretics have
neither Father, Son, nor Holy Ghost, they <span id="page_488">p.
488</span> <span id="v.iv.viii.xvii-Page_488"></span>ought, on coming to
our mother, the Church, to be truly regenerated and baptized, that the
cancer which they had, and the wrath of condemnation, and the
destructive energy of error <span id="fr_1645"></span>[1646](#fn_1645.md)
may be sanctified by the holy and heavenly laver." <span
id="fr_1646"></span>[1647](#fn_1646.md)

<span id="v.iv.viii.xvii-p7"></span>29.  To this we answer, That all who
are baptized with the baptism that is consecrated in the words of the
gospel have the Father, and the Son, and the Holy Ghost in the sacrament
alone; but that in heart and in life neither do those have them who live
an abandoned and accursed life within.
