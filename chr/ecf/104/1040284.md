<span id="v.iv.v.vi-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.iv.v.vi-p1.1"></span>Chapter 6.—</span>9.  Libosus also of Vaga
says:  "The Lord says in the gospel, ‘I am the Truth.’ <span
id="fr_1272"></span>[1273](#fn_1272)   He does not say, ‘I am custom.’ 
Therefore, when the truth is made manifest, custom must give way to
truth." <span id="fr_1273"></span>[1274](#fn_1273)   Clearly, no one
could doubt that custom must give way to truth where it is made
manifest.  But we shall see presently about the manifestation of the
truth.  Meanwhile he also makes it clear that custom was on the other
side.
