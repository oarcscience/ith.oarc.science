<span id="v.v.iv.lxxvi-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.v.iv.lxxvi-p1.1"></span>Chapter 76.—</span>167.  <span
class="c11"><span id="v.v.iv.lxxvi-p1.2"></span>Petilianus</span> said: 
"But to us the Lord Christ, in opposition to your deadly commands,
commanded simple patience and harmlessness.  For what says He?  ‘A new
commandment I give unto you, That ye love one another; as I have loved
you, that ye also love one another.’  And again, ‘By this shall all men
know that ye are my disciples, if ye have love one to another.’"  <span
id="fr_2179"></span>[2180](#fn_2179.md)

<span id="v.v.iv.lxxvi-p3"></span>168.  <span class="c11"><span
id="v.v.iv.lxxvi-p3.1"></span>Augustin</span> answered:  If you did not
transfer these words, so widely differing from your character, to the
surface of your talk, how could you be covering yourselves with sheep’s
clothing?
