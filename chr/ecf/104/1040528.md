<span id="v.v.iv.xli-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.v.iv.xli-p1.1"></span>Chapter 41.—</span>97.  <span
class="c11"><span id="v.v.iv.xli-p1.2"></span>Petilianus</span> said: 
"And, again, he taught us that schisms should not arise, in the
following terms:  ‘Now this I say, that every one of you saith, I am of
Paul, and I of Apollos, and I of Cephas, and I of Christ.  Is Christ
divided? was Paul crucified for you? or were ye baptized in the name of
Paul?’" <span id="fr_2115"></span>[2116](#fn_2115)

<span id="v.v.iv.xli-p3"></span>98.  <span class="c11"><span
id="v.v.iv.xli-p3.1"></span>Augustin</span> answered:  Remember all of
you who read this, it was Petilianus who quoted these words from the
apostle.  For who could have believed that he would have brought forward
words which tell so much for us against himself?
