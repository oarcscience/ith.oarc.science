<span id="v.iv.vi.xxvi-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.iv.vi.xxvi-p1.1"></span>Chapter 26.—</span>34.  A few things still
remain to be noticed in the epistle to Jubaianus; but since these will
raise the question both of the past custom of the Church and of the
baptism of John, which is wont to excite no small doubt in those who pay
slight attention to a matter which is sufficiently obvious, seeing that
those who had received the baptism of John were commanded by the apostle
to be baptized again  <span id="fr_1467"></span>[1468](#fn_1467) they
are not to be treated in a hasty manner, and had better be reserved for
another book, that the dimensions of this may not be inconveniently
large.
