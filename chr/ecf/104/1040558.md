<span id="v.v.iv.lxxi-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.v.iv.lxxi-p1.1"></span>Chapter 71.—</span>157.  <span
class="c11"><span id="v.v.iv.lxxi-p1.2"></span>Petilianus</span> said: 
"To you the prophet says, ‘Peace, peace; and where is there
peace?’" <span id="fr_2168"></span>[2169](#fn_2168.md)

<span id="v.v.iv.lxxi-p3"></span>158.  <span class="c11"><span
id="v.v.iv.lxxi-p3.1"></span>Augustin</span> answered:  It is you that
say this to us, not the prophet.  We therefore answer you:  If you ask
where peace is to be found, open your eyes, and see of whom it is said,
"He maketh wars to cease in all the world." <span
id="fr_2169"></span>[2170](#fn_2169.md)   If you ask where peace is to be
found, open your eyes to see that city which cannot be hidden, because
it is built upon a hill; open your eyes to see the mountain itself, and
let Daniel show it to you, growing out of a small stone, and filling the
whole earth. <span id="fr_2170"></span>[2171](#fn_2170.md)   But when the
prophet says to you, "Peace, peace; and where is there peace?" what will
you show?  Will you show the party of Donatus, unknown to the countless
nations to whom Christ is known?  It is surely not the city which cannot
be hid; and whence is this, except that it is not founded on the
mountain?  "For He is our peace, who hath made both one," <span
id="fr_2171"></span>[2172](#fn_2171.md) —not Donatus, who has made one into
two.
