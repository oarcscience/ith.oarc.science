<span id="v.iv.vi.xiv-p1"></span> <span class="c11"><span
id="v.iv.vi.xiv-p1.1"></span>Chapter 14.—</span>22.  But we must not
despair of the conversion of any man, whether situated within or
without, so long as "the goodness of God leadeth him to
repentance," <span id="fr_1422"></span>[1423](#fn_1422.md) and "visits
their transgressions with the rod, and their inquiry with stripes."  For
in this way "He does not utterly take from them His
loving-kindness," <span id="fr_1423"></span>[1424](#fn_1423.md) if they
will themselves sometimes "love their own soul, pleasing God." <span
id="fr_1424"></span>[1425](#fn_1424.md)   But as the good man "that shall
endure unto the end, the same shall be saved," <span
id="fr_1425"></span>[1426](#fn_1425.md) so the bad man, whether within or
without, who shall persevere in his wickedness to the end, shall not be
saved.  Nor do we say that "all, wheresoever and howsoever baptized,
obtain the grace of baptism," <span id="fr_1426"></span>[1427](#fn_1426.md)
if by the grace of baptism is understood the actual salvation which is
conferred by the celebration of the sacrament; but many fail to obtain
this salvation even within the Church, although it is clear that they
possess the sacrament, which is holy in itself.  Well, therefore, does
the Lord warn us in the gospel that we should not company with
ill-advisers, <span id="fr_1427"></span>[1428](#fn_1427.md) who walk under
the pretence of Christ’s name; but these are found both within and
without, as, in fact, they do not proceed without unless they have first
been ill-disposed within.  And we know that the apostle said of the
vessels placed in the great house, "If a man therefore purge himself
from these, he shall be a vessel unto honor, sanctified, and meet for
the Master’s use, and prepared unto every good work." <span
id="fr_1428"></span>[1429](#fn_1428.md)   But in what manner each man ought
to purge himself from these he shows a little above, saying, "Let every
one that nameth the name of Christ depart from iniquity," <span
id="fr_1429"></span>[1430](#fn_1429.md) that he may not in the last day,
with the chaff, whether with that which has already been driven from the
threshing-floor, or with that which is to be separated at the last, hear
the command, "Depart from me, ye that work iniquity." <span
id="fr_1430"></span>[1431](#fn_1430.md)   Whence it appears, indeed, as
Cyprian says, that "we are not at once to admit and adopt whatsoever is
professed in the name of Christ, but only what is done in the truth of
Christ." <span id="fr_1431"></span>[1432](#fn_1431.md)   But it is not an
action done in the truth of Christ that men should "seize on estates by
fraudulent pretenses, and increase their gains by accumulated
usury," <span id="fr_1432"></span>[1433](#fn_1432.md) or that they should
"renounce the world in word only;" <span
id="fr_1433"></span>[1434](#fn_1433.md) and yet, that all this is done
within the Church, Cyprian himself bears sufficient testimony.
