<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p1.1"></span>Canon LXIII.  (Greek lxvi.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p2"></span>Of players who have become
Christians.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p3.1"></span>And</span> of them also it must be sought
that if anyone wishes to come to the grace of Christianity from any
ludicrous art (*ludicra arte*) and to remain free of that stain, it be
not lawful for anyone to induce him or compel him to return to the
performance of the same things again.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXIII.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p6"></span>Whoever has turned away from the
stage to adopt an honest life, shall not be led back thereto.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p7"></span>This is Canon vij. of the Synod of
Carthage, June 15 (or 16), <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p7.1"></span>a.d.</span> 401.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p8"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p8.1"></span>Johnson.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxiv-p9"></span>This canon is probably to be
understood of slaves bought by their masters for the service of the
Circ, or Theatre.
