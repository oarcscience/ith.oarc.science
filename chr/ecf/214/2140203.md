<span id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii"></span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p1.1"></span>Canon XXXII.</span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p2.1"></span>It</span> is unlawful to receive
the eulogiæ of heretics, for they are rather <span class="Greek"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p2.2"></span>ἀλογίαι</span> \[*i.e*., follies\],
than eulogiæ \[*i.e*., blessings\].

<span id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon
XXXII.</span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p5"></span>The blessings of heretics are
cursings.

<span id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p6"></span>To keep the Latin play upon the
words the translator has used *bene-dictiones* and *male-dictiones*, but
at the expense of the accuracy of translation.

<span id="viii.vii.iii.xxxvii-p7"></span>This canon is found in the
*Corpus Juris Canonici*, Gratian’s *Decretum*, Pars II., Causa II.,
Quæst. I., Can. lxvj.
