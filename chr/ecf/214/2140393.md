<span id="xiv.iii.x"></span>

<span id="xiv.iii.x-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.x-p1.1"></span>Canon IX.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.x-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.x-p2.1"></span>Let</span> no cleric be permitted to keep a
“public house.”  For if it be not permitted to enter a tavern, much more
is it forbidden to serve others in it and to carry on a trade which is
unlawful for him.  But if he shall have done any such thing, either let
him desist or be deposed.

<span id="xiv.iii.x-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.x-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.x-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.x-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon IX.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.x-p5"></span>If clerics are forbidden to enter public
houses, much more are they forbidden to keep them.  Let them either give
them up or be deposed.

<span id="xiv.iii.x-p6"></span>Compare with this canon liv. of the
Apostolic Canons; xxiv. of Laodicea; and xliij. of the Synod of
Carthage. <span id="fr_357"></span>[358](#fn_357)
