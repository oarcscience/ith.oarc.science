<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p1.1"></span>Canon LXXIII.  (Greek lxxvi.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p2"></span>The date of Easter and the date of
the Council should be announced.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p3.1"></span>Item</span>, it seemed good that the day
of the venerable Easter should be intimated to all by the subscription
of formed letters; and that the same should be observed with regard to
the date of the Council, according to the decree of the Council of
Hippo, that <span id="page_479">p. 479</span> <span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-Page_479"></span>is to say the X. Calends of
September, and that it should be written to the primates of each
province so that when they summon their councils they do not impede this
day.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXIII.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p6"></span>It seemed good that the day of the
Holy Easter should be announced on the day of the annual Synod, or on
the tenth day before the calends of September.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p7"></span>This is Canon viij. of Carthage,
September, <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p7.1"></span>a.d.</span> 401.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p8"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p8.1"></span>Johnson.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p9"></span>See Can. 51 (55).

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p10"></span>“The time of council,” i.e., of the
national council at Carthage.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p11"></span>The Greek canon says <span
class="Greek"><span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxiv-p11.1"></span>ἡ πρὸ δέκα καλανδῶν
Σεπτεμβρίων</span>, and Zonaras makes this the 21st of August, but he
mistakes in his calculation.
