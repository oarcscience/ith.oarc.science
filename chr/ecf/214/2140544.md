<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p1.1"></span>Canon XX.  (Greek xxiii.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p2"></span>Of accused presbyters or clerks.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p3.1"></span>But</span> if presbyters or deacons shall
have been accused, there shall be joined together from the neighbouring
places with the bishop of the diocese, the legitimate number of
colleagues, whom the accused shall seek from the same; that is together
with himself six in the case against a presbyter, in that against a
deacon three.  They shall discuss the causes, and the same form shall be
kept with regard to days and postponements and removals from communion,
and in the discussion of persons between the accusers and the accused.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p4"></span>But the causes of the rest of the
clergy, the bishop of the place shall take cognizance of and determine
alone.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p5"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p5.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p6"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p6.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XX.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p7"></span>When a presbyter is accused, six of
the neighbouring bishops together with the bishop of that region shall
judge the matter.  But for a deacon, three.  What things concern the
other clerics even one bishop shall examine.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p8"></span>This is Canon viii. of Hippo, 393.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p9"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxi-p9.1"></span>Johnson.</span>
