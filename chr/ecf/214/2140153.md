<span id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p1.1"></span>Canon XVI.</span>

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p2.1"></span>If</span> any bishop without a see shall
throw himself upon a vacant church and seize its throne, without a full
synod, he shall be cast out, even if all the people over whom he has
usurped jurisdiction should choose him.  And that shall be \[accounted\]
a full synod, in which the metropolitan is present.

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XVI.</span>

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p5"></span>Whoever without the full synod and
without the Metropolitan Council, shall go over to a vacant church, even
if he has no position, he shall be ejected.

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p6"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p6.1"></span>Beveridge.</span>

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p7"></span>This, together with the following
canon, was recited by Bishop Leontius in the Council of Chalcedon, from
the book of the canons, in which this is called the 95th and the
following the 96th, according to the order observed in that book of the
canons.

<span id="viii.vi.iv.xvi-p8"></span>This canon is found in the *Corpus
Juris Canonici*, Gratian’s *Decretum*, Pars I., Dist. XCII., Can. viij.
in Isidore’s version, and the Roman Correctors note its departure from
the original.
