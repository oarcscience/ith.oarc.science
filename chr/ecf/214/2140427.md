<span id="xiv.iii.xliv"></span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xliv-p1"></span> <span id="page_386">p. 386</span>
<span id="xiv.iii.xliv-Page_386"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xliv-p1.1"></span>Canon XLIII.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xliv-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xliv-p2.1"></span>It</span> is lawful for every Christian to
choose the life of religious discipline, and setting aside the troublous
surgings of the affairs of this life to enter a monastery, and to be
shaven in the fashion of a monk, without regard to what faults he may
have previously committed.  For God our Saviour says:  “Whose cometh to
me, I will in no wise cast out.”

<span id="xiv.iii.xliv-p3"></span>As therefore the monastic method of
life engraves upon us as on a tablet the life of penitence, we
receive <span id="fr_370"></span>[371](#fn_370) whoever approaches
it <span id="fr_371"></span>[372](#fn_371) sincerely; nor is any custom
to be allowed to hinder him from fulfilling his intention.

<span id="xiv.iii.xliv-p6"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xliv-p6.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xliv-p7"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xliv-p7.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XLIII.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xliv-p8"></span>Whoever flees from the surging billows
of life and desires to enter a monastery, shall be allowed to do so.

<span id="xiv.iii.xliv-p9"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xliv-p9.1"></span>Zonaras.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xliv-p10"></span>The greatness or the number of a
man’s sins ought not to make him lose hope of propitiating the divinity
by his penitence, if he turns his eyes to the divine mercy.  This is
what the canon asserts, and affirms that everyone, no matter how wicked
and nefarious his life may have been, may embrace monastic discipline,
which inscribes, as on a tablet, <span id="fr_372"></span>[373](#fn_372)
to us a life of penitence.  For as a tablet describes to us what is
inscribed upon it, so the monastic profession writes and inscribes upon
us penitence, so that it remains for ever.
