<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p1.1"></span>Canon XXXIV.  (Greek xxxvii.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p2"></span>That nothing of those things enacted
in the Synod of Hippo is to be corrected.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p3.1"></span>Bishop</span> <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p3.2"></span>Epigonius</span> said:  In this summary
(*Breviarium*) which was adopted at the Synod of Hippo, we think nothing
should be amended, nor anything added thereto except that the day on
which the holy Feast of Easter falls should be announced in Synod.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXXIV.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p6"></span>Nothing is to be corrected in the
synod of Hippo, nor anything added thereto, except that the time of
celebrating Easter should be announced in time of synod.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p7"></span>The first of these introductions is
that of the Synod of Hippo in <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p7.1"></span>a.d.</span> 393; the next that of
Carthage in <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p7.2"></span>a.d.</span> 394, and the third that of
the same place, held August 28th, <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p7.3"></span>a.d.</span> 397.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p8"></span>This canon (number xxxiv. of the
code) is the beginning of Canon v. of the last named Synod.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p9"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p9.1"></span>Johnson.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxv-p10"></span>See Canons 51 and 73.
