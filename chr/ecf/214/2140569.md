<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p1.1"></span>Canon XLV.  (Greek xlviii.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p2"></span>Concerning those who are sick and
cannot answer for themselves.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p3.1"></span>That</span> the sick are to be baptized
who cannot answer for themselves if their \[servants\] shall have spoken
at their own proper peril a testimony of the good will \[of the sick
man\].

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p4"></span>(Greek Canon xlix.)

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p5"></span>*Concerning players who are doing
penance and are converted to the Lord.* <span
id="fr_444"></span>[445](#fn_444.md)

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p7"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p7.1"></span>That</span> to players and actors and
other persons of that kind, as also to apostates when they are
converted <span id="fr_445"></span>[446](#fn_445.md) and return to God,
grace or reconciliation is not to be denied.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p9"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p9.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p10"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p10.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XLV.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p11"></span>That he who cannot answer for
himself on account of illness is to be baptized when he shall have given
evidence of his desire.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p12"></span>A repentant actor is to be received
to penance.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p13"></span>This canon is made up of Canons
xxxij. and xxxiij. of the Synod of Hippo, <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p13.1"></span>a.d.</span> 393.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p14"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p14.1"></span>Johnson.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xlvi-p15"></span>“Apostates,” i.e., those who
elsewhere are called *Lapsi*; those who had done sacrifice through the
violence of torment in time of persecution, professing in the meantime
that their consciences did not consent to what their hands did.
