<span id="viii.vi.iv.vii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vi.iv.vii-p2.1"></span>No</span> stranger shall be received
without letters pacifical.

<span id="viii.vi.iv.vii-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="viii.vi.iv.vii-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="viii.vi.iv.vii-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vi.iv.vii-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon VII.</span>

<span id="viii.vi.iv.vii-p5"></span>A traveller having no letter pacific
with him is not to be received.

<span id="viii.vi.iv.vii-p6"></span>Compare the Apostolic Canon number
XXXIII.

<span id="viii.vi.iv.vii-p7"></span>For a discussion of the Letters
styled *pacifici*, see notes on next canon.

<span id="viii.vi.iv.vii-p8"></span>This canon is found in the *Corpus
Juris Canonici*, Gratian’s *Decretum*, Pars I., Dist. lxxi., c. ix. in
Isidore’s version.  The Roman Correctors note that Dionysius must have
had a different reading from the Greek we know.
