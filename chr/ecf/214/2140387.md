<span id="xiv.iii.iv-p1"></span> <span id="page_364">p. 364</span> <span
id="xiv.iii.iv-Page_364"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.iv-p1.1"></span>Canon IV.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.iv-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.iv-p2.1"></span>If</span> any bishop, presbyter, deacon,
sub-deacon, lector, cantor, or door-keeper has had intercourse with a
woman dedicated to God, let him be deposed, as one who has corrupted a
spouse of Christ, but if a layman let him be cut off.

<span id="xiv.iii.iv-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.iv-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.iv-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.iv-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon IV.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.iv-p5"></span>A cleric coupled to a spouse of God
shall be deposed.  In the case of a layman he shall be cut off.

<span id="xiv.iii.iv-p6"></span>This canon is found in the *Corpus Juris
Canonici*, Gratian’s *Decretum*, Pars II., Causa XXVII., Q. I., c. vj.

<span id="xiv.iii.iv-p7"></span>A layman ravishing a nun, by secular law
was punished by death.  Balsamon gives the reference thus:  V Cap. primi
tit. iiij. lib. Basilic. or cxxiij. Novel.
