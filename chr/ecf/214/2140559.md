<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p1.1"></span>Canon XXXV.  (Greek xxxviii.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p2"></span>That bishops or clergymen should not
easily set free their sons.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p3.1"></span>That</span> bishops or clerics should
not easily let their children pass out of their power; unless they were
secure of their morals and age, that their own sins may pertain to them.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXXV.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p6"></span>Bishops and clergy shall not set
their children free until their morals are established.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p7"></span>This canon is Canon xiij. of the
Synod of Hippo <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxxvi-p7.1"></span>a.d.</span> 393.
