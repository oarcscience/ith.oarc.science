<span id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p1.1"></span>Canon XCVI.  (Greek xcix.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p2"></span>That from judges who have been
chosen, no appeals may be taken.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p3.1"></span>If</span> an appeal be taken, let him
who makes it choose the judges, and with him he also against whom the
appeal is taken; and from their decision no appeal may be made.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p4"></span>Concerning the delegates of the
different provinces.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p5"></span>When all the delegates of the
different provinces came together, they have been most graciously
received, that is those of the Numidians, Byzacenes, Stifensian Moors,
as well as Cæsarians and Tripolitans.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p6"></span>Concerning the executors of
Churches.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p7"></span>It has seemed good moreover that the
appointment of five executors should be asked for in all matters
pertaining to the necessities of the Church, who shall be portioned off
in the different provinces.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p8"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p8.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p9"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p9.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XCVI.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p10"></span>If one party to a suit takes an
appeal, and if both choose together a judge, no further appeal shall be
allowed.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p11"></span>This canon is Canon ij. of
Carthage, <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xcvii-p11.1"></span>a.d.</span> 407.
