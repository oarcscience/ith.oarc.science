<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxvii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxvii-p1.1"></span>Canon CXVI.  (Greek cxvii.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxvii-p2"></span>That the Saints say with accuracy,
“Forgive us our trespasses.”

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxvii-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxvii-p3.1"></span>Likewise</span> also it seemed good,
that whoever wished that these words of the Lord’s prayer, when we say,
“Forgive us our trespasses” are said by the saints out of humility and
not in truth let them be anathema.  For who would make a lying prayer,
not to men but to God?  Who would say with his lips that he wished his
sins forgiven him, but in his heart that he had no sins to be forgiven.
