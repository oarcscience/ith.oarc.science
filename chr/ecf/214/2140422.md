<span id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p1.1"></span>Canon XXXVIII.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p2.1"></span>The</span> canon which was made by the
Fathers we also observe, which thus decreed:  If any city be renewed by
imperial authority, or shall have been renewed, let the order of things
ecclesiastical follow the civil and public models.

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXXVIII.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p5"></span>If any city is or shall be renewed by
the Emperor, the ecclesiastical order shall follow the political and
public example.

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p6"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p6.1"></span>Van Espen.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p7"></span>The canon of the Fathers which the
Synod wishes observed is XVII of Chalcedon, the notes on which see.

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxix-p8"></span>Here it must be noted that by “civil
and public models” is signified the “pragmatic” or imperial letters, by
which the emperors granted to newly raised up or re-edified towns the
privilege of other cities, or else annexed them to some Province.
