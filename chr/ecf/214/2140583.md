<span id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p1.1"></span>Canon LIX.  (Greek lxiii.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p2"></span>That clerics be not compelled to give
testimony in public concerning the cognizance of their own judgment.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p3.1"></span>It</span> should be petitioned also that
they deign to decree, that if perchance any shall have been willing to
plead their cause in any church according to the Apostolic law imposed
upon the Churches, and it happens that the decision of the clergy does
not satisfy one of the parties, it be not lawful to summon that
clergyman who had been cognitor or present, <span
id="fr_458"></span>[459](#fn_458.md) into judgment as a witness, and that
no person attached to any ecclesiastic be compelled to give testimony.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p5"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p5.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p6"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p6.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon LIX.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p7"></span>A cleric who has decided a case shall
not, if it be displeasing, be summoned to a tribunal to give evidence
concerning it; and no ecclesiastical person shall be forced to give
testimony.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p8"></span>This is Canon iij. of the Synod of
Carthage, June 15 (or 16). <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p8.1"></span>a.d.</span> 401.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p9"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p9.1"></span>Johnson.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p10"></span>“According to the Apostolic law,”
viz., that of St. Paul, [1 Cor. 6:1](../../../bib/kjv/co1006.htm#001.md),
[2](../../../bib/kjv/co1006.htm#002.md)<span
id="xv.iv.iv.lx-p10.1"></span>, etc.  I follow the Greek scholia in
rendering this canon.  In Latin *cognitor* is he that is solicitor, or
advocate, rather than the judge who takes cognizance.
