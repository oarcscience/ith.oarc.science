<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-Page_478"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p1.1"></span>Canon LXX.  (Greek lxxiii.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p2"></span>What clerics should abstain from
their wives.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p3.1"></span>Moreover</span> since incontinence has
been charged against some clergymen with regard to their own wives it
has seemed good that bishops, presbyters, and deacons should according
to the statutes already made abstain even from their own wives; and
unless they do so that they should be removed from the clerical office. 
But the rest of the clergy shall not be forced to this but the custom of
each church in this matter shall be followed.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXX.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p6"></span>Bishops, presbyters and deacons shall
abstain for their wives or else be removed from the ecclesiastical
order.  But the rest of the clergy shall not be forced to the same:  but
let the custom be observed.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p7"></span>This is Canon iv. of Carthage,
September, <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p7.1"></span>a.d.</span> 401.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p8"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p8.1"></span>Johnson.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxi-p9"></span>A repetition of Canon 25 (28).
