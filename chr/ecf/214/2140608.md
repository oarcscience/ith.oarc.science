<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p1.1"></span>Canon LXXXIV.  (Greek lxxxvii.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p2"></span>Of extirpating the remains of the
idols.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p3.1"></span>Item</span>, it seemed good to petition
the most glorious Emperors that the remains of idolatry not only in
images, but in any places whatever or groves or trees, should altogether
be taken away.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p4"></span><span id="page_483">p. 483</span>
<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-Page_483"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXXIV.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p6"></span>Let all remains of idolatry be
abolished whether in statues, or in places, or groves or trees.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p7"></span>This is Canon xviij. of Carthage,
September, <span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p7.1"></span>a.d.</span> 401.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p8"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.lxxxv-p8.1"></span>Johnson</span>.
