<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxi-Page_505"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxi-p1.1"></span>Canon CXXX.  (Greek cxxxi.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxi-p2"></span>That he who has failed to prove one
charge shall not be allowed to give evidence to another.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxi-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxi-p3.1"></span>So</span>, too, it seemed good that as
often as many crimes were laid to clerics by their accusers, and one of
the first examined could not be proved, <span
id="fr_483"></span>[484](#fn_483.md) they should not be allowed to go on
giving evidence on the other counts.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxi-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxi-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon CXXX.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxi-p6"></span>*He who makes many accusations and
proves nothing \[is not to give witness\].*
