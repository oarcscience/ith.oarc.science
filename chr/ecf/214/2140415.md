<span id="xiv.iii.xxxii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xxxii-p1.1"></span>Canon XXXI.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xxxii-p2.1"></span>Clerics</span> who in oratories which are
in houses offer the Holy Mysteries or baptize, we decree ought to do
this with the consent of the bishop of the place.  Wherefore if any
cleric shall not have so done, let him be deposed.

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxii-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xxxii-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxii-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xxxii-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXXI.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxii-p5"></span>Thou mayest not offer in an oratory
in a private house without the consent of the bishop.

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxii-p6"></span>On this whole subject the reader is
referred to the curious and most interesting volume published by
Venantius Monaldini of Venice, in 1765.  I cannot better give its scope
than by copying out its title in full.

<span id="xiv.iii.xxxii-p7"></span>Commentarius
Theologico-canonico-criticus *De ecclesiis*, *earum reverentia*, *et
asylo atque concordia sacerdotii*, *et imperii*, auctore Josepho Aloysio
Assemani.  Accesserunt tractatus cl. virorum D. Josephi de Bonis, *De
Oratoriis Publicis*; ac. R.P. Fortunati a Brixia *De Oratoriis
Domesticis*, in supplementum celeberrimi operis Joannis Baptistæ Gattico
*De Oratoriis Domesticis*, *et usu altaris portatilis*.
