<span id="xiv.iii.lxxviii"></span>

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxviii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.lxxviii-p1.1"></span>Canon LXXVII.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxviii-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.lxxviii-p2.1"></span>It</span> is not right that those who
are dedicated to religion, whether clerics or ascetics, <span
id="fr_382"></span>[383](#fn_382) should wash in the bath with women,
nor should any Christian man or layman do so.  For this is severely
condemned by the heathens.  But if any one is caught in this thing, if
he is a cleric let him be deposed; if a layman, let him be cut off.

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxviii-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.lxxviii-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxviii-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.lxxviii-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXVII.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxviii-p6"></span>A Christian man shall not bathe
with women.  Should a cleric do so he is to be deposed, and a layman cut
off.

<span id="xiv.iii.lxxviii-p7"></span>This is a renewal of the <span
class="sc"><span id="xiv.iii.lxxviii-p7.1"></span>XXX</span>th canon of
Laodicea.  It will be noted, as Zonaras remarks, that the monks must be
counted among the laymen who are to be cut off, since they have no
clerical character or tonsure.
