<span id="viii.vii.iii.v-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.v-p1.1"></span>Canon IV.</span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.v-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.v-p2.1"></span>They</span> who are of the sacerdotal
order ought not to lend and receive usury, nor what is called hemioliæ.

<span id="viii.vii.iii.v-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.v-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.v-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.vii.iii.v-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon IV.</span>

<span id="viii.vii.iii.v-p5"></span>A priest is not to receive usury nor
hemioliæ.

<span id="viii.vii.iii.v-p6"></span>The same rule is laid down in the
seventeenth Canon of Nice.  For a treatment of the whole subject of
usury see excursus to that canon.

<span id="viii.vii.iii.v-p7"></span>Dionysius Exiguus and Isidore have
numbered this canon v., and our fifth they have as iv.

<span id="viii.vii.iii.v-p8"></span>This canon is found in the *Corpus
Juris Canonici*, Gratian’s *Decretum*, Pars I., Dist. XLVI., can. ix.
