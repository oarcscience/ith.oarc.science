<span id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p1.1"></span>Canon XVII.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p2"></span>That any province on account of its
distance, may have its own Primate.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p3.1"></span>It</span> seemed good that Mauretania
Sitiphensis, as it asked, should have a Primate of its own, with the
consent of the Primate of Numidia from whose synod it had been
separated. <span id="fr_423"></span>[424](#fn_423)   And with the
consent of all the primates of the African Provinces and of all the
bishops permission was given, by reason of the great distance between
them.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p5"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p5.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p6"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p6.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XVII.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p7"></span>Mauretania Sitiphensis, on account
of the great distance, is permitted to have its own Primate.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p8"></span>This canon is Canon iij. of the
first series of canons enacted at Hippo in 393.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p9"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p9.1"></span>Johnson.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xviii-p10"></span>N.B.  From this place forward the
Latin and Greek numeration varies; but Justellus’s Edition in Greek and
Latin follows the Latin division.
