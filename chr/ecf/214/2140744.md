<span id="xvii.xxiv"></span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p1.1"></span>IX.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p2"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p2.1"></span>The Canonical Answers of Timothy the Most
Holy Bishop of Alexandria, Who was One of the CL Fathers Gathered
Together at Constantinople, to the Questions Proposed to Him concerning
Bishops and Clerics. <span id="fr_603"></span>[604](#fn_603) </span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p4.1"></span>Question I.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p5"></span>If a lad of seven years old, or a man,
being a catechumen, being present at the oblation, does eat of it
through ignorance, what shall be done in this case?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p6"></span>*Answer*.  Let him be illuminated, i.e.
baptized, for he is called by God.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p7"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p7.1"></span>Question II.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p8"></span>If baptism be desired for a catechumen
that is possessed, what shall be done?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p9"></span>*Answer*.  Let him be baptized at the
hour of death, not otherwise.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p10"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p10.1"></span>Question III.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p11"></span>Ought a communicant to communicate, if
he be possessed?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p12"></span>*Answer*.  If he do not expose or
blaspheme the Mysteries, let him communicate not always, but at certain
times.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p13"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p13.1"></span>Question IV.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p14"></span>If a catechumen be sick, and in a
frenzy, so that he cannot make profession of his faith, can he be
baptized, at the entreaty of his friends?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p15"></span>*Answer*.  He may, if he be not
possessed.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p16"></span><span id="page_613">p. 613</span> <span
id="xvii.xxiv-Page_613"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p16.1"></span>Question V.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p17"></span>Can a man or woman communicate after
performing the conjugal act over night?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p18"></span>*Answer*.  No.  [1 Cor. vii.
5](../../../bib/kjv/co1007.htm#005)<span id="xvii.xxiv-p18.1"></span>.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p19"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p19.1"></span>Question VI.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p20"></span>The day appointed for the baptism of a
woman; on that day it happened that the custom of women was upon her;
ought she then to be baptized?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p21"></span>*Answer*.  No, not till she be clean.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p22"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p22.1"></span>Question VII.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p23"></span>Can a menstruous woman communicate?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p24"></span>*Answer*.  Not until she be clean.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p25"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p25.1"></span>Question VIII.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p26"></span>Ought a woman in child-bed to keep the
Paschal fast?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p27"></span>*Answer*.  No.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p28"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p28.1"></span>Question IX.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p29"></span>Ought a clergyman to perform the
oblation, or pray, while an Arian or heretic is present?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p30"></span>*Answer*.  As to the divine oblation,
the deacon, after the kiss, makes a proclamation, “Let all that are not
Communicants walk off;” therefore such persons ought not to be present,
except they promise to repent, and renounce their heresy.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p31"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p31.1"></span>Question X.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p32"></span>Is a sick man obliged to keep the
Paschal fast?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p33"></span>*Answer*.  No.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p34"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p34.1"></span>Question XI.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p35"></span>If a clergyman be called to celebrate a
marriage, and have heard that it is incestuous; ought he to comply, and
perform the oblation?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p36"></span>*Answer*.  No; he must not be partaker
of other men’s sins.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p37"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p37.1"></span>Question XII.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p38"></span>If a layman ask a clergyman whether he
may communicate after a nocturnal pollution?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p39"></span>*Answer*.  If it proceed from the desire
of a woman, he ought not: but if it be a temptation from Satan, he
ought; for the tempter will ply him when he is to communicate.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p40"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p40.1"></span>Question XIII.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p41"></span>When are man and wife to forbear the
conjugal act?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p42"></span>*Answer*.  On Saturday, and the Lord’s
day; for on those days the spiritual sacrifice is offered.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p43"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p43.1"></span>Question XIV.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p44"></span>Shall there be an oblation for him, who
being distracted, murders himself?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p45"></span>*Answer*.  Not except the case be very
clear that he was distracted.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p46"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p46.1"></span>Question XV.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p47"></span>If one’s wife be possessed to such a
degree, as that she be bound with irons, and the man cannot contain, may
he marry another?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p48"></span>*Answer*.  I can only say it would be
adultery so to do.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p49"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p49.1"></span>Question XVI.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p50"></span>If a man in washing or bathing, swallow
a drop of water, may he communicate after it?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p51"></span>*Answer*.  If Satan find an occasion of
hindering us from the communion, he will the oftener do it.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p52"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p52.1"></span>Question XVII.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p53"></span>Are they, who hear the Word, and do it
not, damned?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p54"></span>*Answer*.  If we neither do it, or
repent that we have not done it.

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p55"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xxiv-p55.1"></span>Question XVIII.</span>

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p56"></span>At what age are sins imputed to us by
God?

<span id="xvii.xxiv-p57"></span>*Answer*.  According to every one’s
capacity and understanding; to one at ten, to another when older.
