<span id="xvii.xv-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xv-p1.1"></span>Of the Same to Diodorus Bishop of Tarsus,
concerning a Man who had taken Two Sisters to Wife.</span>

<span id="xvii.xv-p2"></span>(*Found translated in* lib. cit., *p*. 212
*et seqq.* Epistle clx.)

<span id="xvii.xv-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xv-p3.1"></span>Canon LXXXVII.</span>

<span id="xvii.xv-p4"></span>Contains the preface of his letter to
Diodorus Bishop of Tarsus, in which he tells him of a letter shewed him
in justification of a man’s marrying two sisters bearing his name; but
he hopes it was forged.

<span id="xvii.xv-p5"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xvii.xv-p5.1"></span>Canon LXXXVIII.</span>

<span id="xvii.xv-p6"></span>Contains the rest of the letter, in which
he argues and inveighs against this practice.
