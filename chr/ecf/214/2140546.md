<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p1.1"></span>Canon XXII.  (Greek xxv.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p2"></span>That bishops or other clergymen
shall give nothing to those who are not Catholics.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p3.1"></span>And</span> that to those who are not
Catholic Christians, even if they be blood relations, neither bishops
nor clergymen shall give anything at all by way of donation of their
possessions.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon XXII.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p6"></span>Bishops and clergymen shall give
nothing of their goods to heretics, nor confer aught upon them even if
they be their relatives.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.xxiii-p7"></span>This is Canon xiv. of Hippo, 393.
