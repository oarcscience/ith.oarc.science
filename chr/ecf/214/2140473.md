<span id="xiv.iii.xc-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xc-p1.1"></span>Canon LXXXIX.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xc-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xc-p2.1"></span>The</span> faithful spending the days of the
Salutatory Passion in fasting, praying and compunction of heart, ought
to fast until the midnight of the Great Sabbath:  since the divine
Evangelists, Matthew and Luke, have shewn us how late at night it was
\[that the resurrection took place\], the one by using the words <span
class="Greek"><span id="xiv.iii.xc-p2.2"></span>ὀΨὲ σαββάτων</span>, and
the other by the words <span class="Greek"><span
id="xiv.iii.xc-p2.3"></span>ὄρθρου βαθέος</span>.

<span id="xiv.iii.xc-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xc-p3.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon LXXXIX.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xc-p4"></span>*On the Great Sabbath the fast must be
continued until midnight.*
