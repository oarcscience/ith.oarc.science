<span id="viii.iv.iii.x-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="viii.iv.iii.x-p1.1"></span>Canon X.</span>

<span id="viii.iv.iii.x-p2"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.iv.iii.x-p2.1"></span>Likewise</span>, if a deacon have fallen
into the same sin, let him have the rank of a minister.

<span id="viii.iv.iii.x-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="viii.iv.iii.x-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="viii.iv.iii.x-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.iv.iii.x-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon X.</span>

<span id="viii.iv.iii.x-p5"></span>*A deacon found in the same crime
shall remain a minister* (<span class="Greek"><span
id="viii.iv.iii.x-p5.1"></span>ὑπηρέτης</span>).

<span id="viii.iv.iii.x-p6"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="viii.iv.iii.x-p6.1"></span>Hefele.</span>

<span id="viii.iv.iii.x-p7"></span>By ministers (<span
class="Greek"><span id="viii.iv.iii.x-p7.1"></span>ὑπήρεται</span>) are
meant inferior officers of the Church—the so-called minor orders, often
including the subdeacons.

<span id="viii.iv.iii.x-p8"></span><span id="page_84">p. 84</span> <span
id="viii.iv.iii.x-Page_84"></span>This canon is in the *Corpus Juris
Canonici*, Gratian’s *Decretum*, Pars II., Causa xv., Quæst. viii.,
united with canon ix., and in the following curious form:  “Similiter et
diaconus, si in eodem culpæ genere fuerit involutus, sese a ministerio
cohibebit.”
