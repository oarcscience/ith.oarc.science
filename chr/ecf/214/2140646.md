<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p1.1"></span>Canon CXXII.  (Greek cxxiii.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p2"></span>The sentence of the elected judges
ought not to be spurned.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p3.1"></span>From</span> the judges chosen by common
consent of the parties, no appeal can be taken; and whoever shall be
found to have carried such an appeal and contumaciously to be unwilling
to submit to the judges, when this has been proved to the primate, let
him give letters, that no one of the bishops should communicate with him
until he yield.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon CXXII.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p6"></span>A judge chosen by both parties
cannot be repudiated.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p7"></span>This is Canon xiv. of Carthage,
<span class="sc"><span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p7.1"></span>a.d.</span> 418.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p8"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p8.1"></span>Johnson.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxiii-p9"></span>See Canons 76 (79) and 80 (83).
