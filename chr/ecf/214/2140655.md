<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p1.1"></span>Canon CXXXI.  (Greek CXXXII.)</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p2"></span>Who should be allowed to give
evidence.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p3"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p3.1"></span>They</span> who are forbidden to be
admitted as accusers are not to be allowed to appear as witnesses, nor
any that the accuser may bring from his own household.  And none shall
be admitted to give witness under fourteen years of age.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p4"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p4.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p5"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p5.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon CXXXI.</span>

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p6"></span>And whoso is not past fourteen
years of age \[is not to give witness\].  An accuser is not to produce
witnesses from his own house.

<span id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p7"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xv.iv.iv.cxxxii-p7.1"></span>Johnson.</span>
