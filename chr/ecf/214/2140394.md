<span id="xiv.iii.xi-p1"></span> <span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xi-p1.1"></span>Canon X.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xi-p2"></span>A <span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xi-p2.1"></span>bishop</span>, or presbyter, or deacon who
receives usury, or what is called *hecatostæ*, let him desist or be
deposed.

<span id="xiv.iii.xi-p3"></span><span class="c4"><span
id="xiv.iii.xi-p3.1"></span>Notes.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xi-p4"></span><span class="sc"><span
id="xiv.iii.xi-p4.1"></span>Ancient Epitome of Canon X.</span>

<span id="xiv.iii.xi-p5"></span>A bishop, presbyter, or deacon who takes
usury shall be deposed unless he stops doing so.

<span id="xiv.iii.xi-p6"></span>See notes on canon XVI. of Nice, and the
