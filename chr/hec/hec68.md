Canon for Ascension Day

### ODE VII.

by S. Joseph of the Studium

φωτεινή σε, φῶς.

W   afting Him up on high,  
      The glorious cloud receives  
    The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of Immortality,  
      And earth the Victor leaves:  
    The Heavenly People raise the strain,  
    The Apostles pour the hymn again;—  
      <span class="smallcaps">God</span> of our Fathers, Thou art blest!

Y   e faithful, tell your joys!  
      All hearts with gladness bound!  
    <span class="smallcaps">God</span> is gone up with a merry noise,—  
      The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> with the trumpet's
sound!  
    To Him we cry, by woes once tried,  
    Now glorious at the <span class="smallcaps">Father's</span> side,—  
      <span class="smallcaps">God</span> of our Fathers, Thou art blest!

<span id="page_222">p. 222</span>

Z   ealous for <span class="smallcaps">God</span> of yore,  
      With zeal still Moses burns:  
    "Come, Heavenly Spirits, and adore  
      The Victor Who returns;  
    Rise, Angel legions, rise and sing  
    The ancient hymn to greet the King,—  
      <span class="smallcaps">God</span> of our Fathers, Thou art
blest!"

*Catavasía*.

J   oin’d with the trumpet-peal, the din and shout,  
    Cornet, flute, sackbut, dulcimer rang out,  
    And bade adore the golden deity:  
    The <span class="smallcaps">Spirit's</span> gentler voice gives
praise to Thee,  
O   co-eternal One—O consubstantial Three!
