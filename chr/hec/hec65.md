Canon for Ascension Day

### ODE IV.

by S. Joseph of the Studium

Ἰησοῦς ὁ ζωοδότης.

J   <span class="smallcaps">esus</span>, <span
class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of Life Eternal,  
      Taking those He lov’d the best,  
    Stood upon the Mount of Olives,  
      And His Own the last time blest:  
    Then, though He had never left it,  
      Sought again his <span class="smallcaps">Father's</span> breast.

K   nit is now our flesh to Godhead,  
      Knit in everlasting bands:  
    Call the world to highest festal:  
      Floods and oceans, clap your hands:  
    Angels, raise the song of triumph!  
      Make response, ye distant lands

<span id="page_215">p. 215</span>

L   oosing Death with all its terrors  
      Thou ascended’st up on high;  
    And to mortals, now Immortal,  
      Gavest immortality:  
    As Thine own Disciples saw Thee  
      Mounting victor to the sky

*Catavasía*.

M   onarch of monarchs, Sole of Sole, to Thee,  
    <span class="smallcaps">Word</span>, Glorious in Thy <span
class="smallcaps">Father's</span> Majesty,  
    And sending Thy co-equal <span class="smallcaps">Spirit</span>
bright  
    To teach, to comfort, and to guide aright,  
    Thine own Apostles sang: All glory to Thy might!
