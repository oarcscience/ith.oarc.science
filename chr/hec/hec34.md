"S. Thomas's Sunday"

### ODE IV.

by S. John Damascene.

μέγα τὸ μυστήριον.

"'<span class="smallcaps">Christ</span>, we turn our eyes to Thee,  
"'And this mighty mystery!'  
"Habakkuk exclaimed of old,  
"In the <span class="smallcaps">Holy Spirit</span> bold:  
"'Thou shalt come in time appointed,  
"For the help of Thine anointed!"

Taste of myrrh He deign’d to know,  
Who redeemed the source of woe:  
Now He bids all sickness cease  
Through the honey-comb of peace:  
And to this world deigns to give  
That sweet food by which we live.

<span id="page_124">p. 124</span>

Patient <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>! with loving eye  
Thou invitest Thomas nigh,  
Showing Him that Wounded Side:  
While the world is certified,  
Flow the third day, from the grave,  
<span class="smallcaps">Jesus Christ</span> arose to save.

Blest, O Didymus, the tongue  
Where that first confession hung:  
First the <span class="smallcaps">Saviour</span> to proclaim  
First the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of Life to name:  
Such the graces it supplied,  
—That dear touch of <span class="smallcaps">Jesu's</span> side!

<span class="small">*Catavasia*. "Stand on thy watch-tower, Habakkuk the
Seer." (p. 98.)</span>
