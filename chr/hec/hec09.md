### STICHERA FOR A SUNDAY OF THE FIRST TONE.

by S. Anatolius.

ζοφερᾶς τρικυμίας.

Fierce was the wild billow;  
  Dark was the night;  
Oars labour’d heavily;  
  Foam glimmer’d white;  
Trembled the mariners;  
  Peril was nigh;  
Then said the <span class="smallcaps">God</span> of <span
class="smallcaps">God</span>,  
 —"Peace! It is I!"

Ridge of the mountain-way;  
  Lower thy crest!  
Wail of Euroclydon,  
  Be thou at rest!

<span id="page_58">p. 58</span>

Sorrow can never be,—  
  Darkness must fly,—  
Where saith the Light of Light,  
 —"Peace! It is I!

<span class="smallcaps">Jesu</span>, Deliverer!  
  Come Thou to me:  
Soothe Thou my voyaging  
  Over Life's sea!  
Thou, when the storm of Death  
  Roars, sweeping by,  
Whisper, O Truth of Truth!  
 —"Peace! It is I!"

\[The above hymn has been set by my friend Mr. Helmore; also in H. E.
C., of which it forms No. 1: also by Miss Kerr. The last melody is, to
my mind, especially beautiful.\]
