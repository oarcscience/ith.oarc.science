### AND WILT THOU PARDON, LORD.

<span class="small">The following Stanzas are a Cento from the Canon for
the Monday of the First Tone, in the Paracletice.</span>

by S. Joseph of the Studium

τῶν ἀμαρτιῶν μου τὴν πληθύν.

  And wilt Thou pardon, <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>,  
  A sinner such as I?  
Although Thy book his crimes record  
  Of such a crimson dye?

  So deep are they engrav’d,—  
  So terrible their fear,  
The righteous scarcely shall be sav’d,  
  And where shall I appear?

<span id="page_205">p. 205</span>

  My soul, make all things known  
  To Him Who all things sees:  
That so the <span class="smallcaps">Lamb</span> may yet atone  
  For thine iniquities.

  O Thou Physician blest,  
Make clean my guilty soul!  
  And me, by many a sin oppress’d,  
  Restore, and keep me whole!

  I know not how to praise  
Thy mercy and Thy love:  
  But deign Thy servant to upraise,  
  And I shall learn above!

\[<span class="small">In Mr. Young's book. Composed by Dr.
Schroeder.</span>\]
