Canon for Easter Day

### ODE I.

by S. John Damascene.

αναστάσεως ἡμέρα.

’Tis the Day of Resurrection:  
  Earth! tell it out abroad!  
The Passover of gladness!  
  The Passover of <span class="smallcaps">God</span>!  
From Death to Life Eternal,  
  From this world to the sky,  
Our <span class="smallcaps">Christ</span> hath brought us over,  
  With hymns of victory.

<span id="page_96">p. 96</span>

Our hearts be pure from evil,  
  That we may see aright  
The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> in rays eternal  
  Of Resurrection-Light:  
And, listening to His accents,  
  May hear, so calm and plain,  
His own—*All Hail!*—and hearing,  
  May raise the victor strain!

Now let the Heav’ns be joyful!  
  Let earth her song begin!  
Let the round world keep triumph,  
  And all that is therein:  
Invisible and visible  
  Their notes let all things blend,—  
For <span class="smallcaps">Christ</span> the <span
class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath risen,—  
  Our joy that hath no end.

\[<span class="small">Set by Mr. Helmore: a very spirited melody. Also
in Mr. Young's book: composed by Dr. Schroeder.</span>\]
