# CHAPTER IV<span id="page_8_fr_4"></span>[<sup><span class="small">4</span></sup>](#page_8_note_4).

OF HEAVEN.

   H<span class="small">EAVEN</span> is like a roof to the material
world, and will serve as the floor of the new world. It is by nature
shining and glorious, and is the dwelling-place of the invisible hosts.
When God spread out this firmament, He brought up above it a third part
of the waters, and above these is the heaven of light and of the
luminaries. Hence people say 'the heaven, and the heaven of heavens<span
id="page_8_fr_5"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">5</span></sup>](#page_8_note_5)'; for we call both the
firmament and the waters which are above it 'heaven.' Some consider that
the verse 'Let the waters which are above the heavens praise the <span
id="page_9">p. 9</span> name of the Lord<span
id="page_9_fr_1"></span>[<sup><span
class="small">1</span></sup>](#page_9_note_1)' refers to the holy angels
and to our Lord's humanity; but neither the Church nor the orthodox
teachers accept this.
