A VERY GOOD PLASTER.

I doubt very much whether any physician in the United States can make a
plaster equal to this. It heals the white swelling, and has cured the
sore leg of a woman who for eighteen years had used the prescriptions of
doctors in vain.

Take two quarts of cider, one pound of bees-wax, one pound of
sheep-tallow, and one pound of tobacco; boil the tobacco in the cider
till the strength is out, and then strain it, and add the other articles
to the liquid: stir it over a gentle fire till all is dissolved.
