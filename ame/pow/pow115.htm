Pow-Wows, or Long Lost Friend, by John George Hoffman, [1820], at Intangible Textual Heritage

TO PREVENT BAD PEOPLE FROM GETTING ABOUT THE CATTLE.

Take wormwood, gith, five-finger weed, and assafœtida; three cents' worth of each; the straw of horse

p. 41

beans, some dirt swept together behind the door of the stable and a little salt. Tie these all up together with a tape, and put the bundle in a hole about the threshold over which your cattle pass in and out, and cover it well with lignum-vitæ wood. This will certainly be of use.
