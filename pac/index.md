<span id="general"></span>

### General

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Oceanic
Mythology](om/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">Roland B.
Dixon</span> \[<span class="c_d">1916</span>\].   <span class="c_b">A
highly readable and scholarly cross-cultural study of Pacific mythology
and folklore</span>, covering Polynesia, Micronesia, Melanesia,
Indonesia and Australia. It includes summaries of material only
available in obscure 19th Century scholarly journals. </span>

<span id="hawaii"></span>

### Hawaii

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Hawaiian
Mythology](hm/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">Martha Warren
Beckwith</span> \[<span class="c_d">1940</span>\]   <span class="c_b">An
extensive critical study of Hawaiian mythology</span>, with parallels to
other Pacific islander cultures and numerous variations on each text.
</span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[The Kumulipo, A Hawaiian Creation
Chant](ku/index.md)</span>  
translated with commentary by <span class="c_a">Martha Warren
Beckwith</span> \[<span class="c_d">1951</span>\]   <span
class="c_b">The Kumulipo is the Royal Hawaiian Creation chant</span>,
describing the emergence of life from the ocean and listing hundreds of
generations of descendants from the primal gods and goddesses. </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[The Kumulipo](lku/index.md)</span>  
translated by Queen <span class="c_a">Liliuokalani</span> \[<span
class="c_d">1897</span>\]   <span class="c_b">A translation of the
Kumulipo by the last Queen of Hawaii.</span> </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Unwritten Literature of Hawaii:
The Sacred Songs of the Hula](ulh/index.md)</span>   by <span
class="c_a">Nathaniel B. Emerson</span> \[<span
class="c_d">1909</span>\] </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Hawaiian Folk
Tales](hft/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">Thomas G. Thrum</span>
\[<span class="c_d">1907</span>\]   <span class="c_b">An anthology of
classic Hawaiian folklore, including tales of Menehunes and
Kahunas.</span> </span>

------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following is a series of books of Hawaiian mythology, folkore, and
legends by W.D. Westervelt from the turn of the 20th Century. Although
Westervelt often wrote in a romanticized style, the folklore is genuine.

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Legends of
Maui](maui/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">W.D. Westervelt</span>
\[<span class="c_d">1910</span>\]   <span class="c_b">A collection of
Hawaiian and Polynesian legends about the culture hero, Maui.</span>
</span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Hawaiian Legends of Old
Honolulu](hloh/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">W.D.
Westervelt</span> \[<span class="c_d">1915</span>\]   <span
class="c_b">Of all of the sacred landscapes of the Pacific, the area
around Honolulu is rich in lore.</span> </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Hawaiian Legends of
Volcanoes](hlov/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">W.D.
Westervelt</span> \[<span class="c_d">1916</span>\]   <span
class="c_b">The Fire Goddess Pele, her deeds, family and loves.</span>
</span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Hawaiian Legends of Ghosts and
Ghost-Gods](hlog/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">W.D.
Westervelt</span> \[<span class="c_d">1916</span>\]   <span
class="c_b">Tales of the Hawaiian afterlife, and those who returned from
it by magic or cunning.</span> </span>

<span class="c_e"> <span class="c_t">[Hawaiian Historical
Legends](hhl/index.md)</span>   by <span class="c_a">W.D. Westervelt</span>
\[<span class="c_d">1923</span>, no renewal\]   <span
class="c_b">Stories from Hawaii during the period of European
contact.</span> </span>
