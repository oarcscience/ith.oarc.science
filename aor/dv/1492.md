### 1492.

<span id="page_450">p. 450</span>

Lucretius in his third \[book\] 'De Rerum Natura'. The hands, nails and
teeth were (165) the weapons of ancient man.

They also use for a standard a bunch of grass tied to a pole (167).
 <span id="fr_527"></span>[827](#fn_527)

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_527"></span>[450:827](1492.htm#fr_527) : Lucretius, de
rerum natura libri VI were printed first about 1473, at Verona in 1486,
at Brescia in 1495, at Venice in 1500 and in 1515, and at Florence in
1515. The numbers 165 and 167 noted by Leonardo at the end of the two
passages seem to indicate pages, but if so, none of the editions just
mentioned can here be meant, nor do these numbers refer to the verses in
the poems of Lucretius.
