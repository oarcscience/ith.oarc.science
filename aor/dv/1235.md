### 1235.

### THE EAGLE.

The eagle when it is old flies so high that it scorches its feathers,
and Nature allowing that it should renew its youth, it falls into
shallow water  <span id="fr_317"></span>[651](#fn_317.md) . And if its
young ones cannot bear to gaze on the sun  <span
id="fr_318"></span>[652](#fn_318.md) --; it does not feed them with any
bird, that does not wish to die. Animals which much fear

<span id="page_322">p. 322</span>

it do not approach its nest, although it does not hurt them. It always
leaves part of its prey uneaten.

### LUMERPA,--FAME.

This is found in Asia Major, and shines so brightly that it absorbs its
own shadow, and when it dies it does not lose this light, and its
feathers never fall out, but a feather pulled out shines no longer.
