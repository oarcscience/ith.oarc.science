### 1315.

<span id="page_374">p. 374</span>

Fable of the tongue bitten by the teeth.

The cedar puffed up with pride of its beauty, separated itself from the
trees around it and in so doing it turned away towards the wind, which
not being broken in its fury, flung it uprooted on the earth.

The traveller's joy, not content in its hedge, began to fling its
branches out over the high road, and cling to the opposite hedge, and
for this it was broken away by the passers by.
