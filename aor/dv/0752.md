*The Da Vinci Notebooks at Intangible Textual Heritage*

### 752.

<span id="page_35">p. 35</span>

The earth that is dug out from the cellars must be raised on one side so
high as to make a terrace garden as high as the level of the hall; but
between the earth of the terrace and the wall of the house, leave an
interval in order that the damp may not spoil the principal walls.
