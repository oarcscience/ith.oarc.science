*The Da Vinci Notebooks at Intangible Textual Heritage*

### 39.

The eye--which sees all objects reversed--retains the images for some
time. This conclusion is proved by the results; because, the eye having
gazed at light retains some impression of it. After looking (at it)
there remain in the eye images of intense brightness, that make any less
brilliant spot seem dark until the eye has lost the last trace of the
impression of the stronger light.
