### 292.

<span class="margnote"> An exceptional case.</span>Of the edges
\[outlines\] of shadows. Some have misty and ill defined edges, others
distinct ones.

No opaque body can be devoid of light and shade, except it is in a mist,
on ground covered with snow, or when snow is falling on the open country
which has no light on it and is surrounded with darkness.

<span id="page_158">p. 158</span>

And this occurs \[only\] in spherical bodies, because in other bodies
which have limbs and parts, those sides of limbs which face each other
reflect on each other the accidental \[hue and tone\] of their surface.
