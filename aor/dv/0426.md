And its shadows are in the same positions as those were of the opposite
side. Therefore, O Painter! when you do trees close at hand, remember
that if the eye is almost under the tree you will see its leaves
\[some\] on the upper and \[some\] on the under side, and the upper side
will be bluer in proportion as they are seen more foreshortened, and the
same leaf sometimes shows part of the right side and part of the under
side, whence you must make it of two colours.
