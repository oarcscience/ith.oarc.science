#### THIRD LESSON.

'Reflect and observe that whether you go to this world or to that
beyond, in the whole world those who are discerning beings, who abstain
from cruelty <span id="fr_193"></span>[1](#fn_190), relinquish karman.
They are flesh-subduing, called duty-knowing, upright men, aware that
pain results from actions.' Thus say those who have right intuition. (1)

All the professors, conversant with pain, preach renunciation. Thus
thoroughly knowing karman, observing the commandment, wise, unattached
(to the world), recognising thy Self as one <span
id="fr_194"></span>[2](#fn_191), subdue the body, chastise thyself,
weaken thyself: 'just as fire consumes old wood!' Thus with a composed
mind, unattached, 'unhesitatingly avoid wrath!' Considering the
shortness of life 'know pain, or what will come <span
id="fr_195"></span>[3](#fn_192);' one shall feel the several feelings;
and perceive the world suffering under them. (2)

<span id="page_40">p. 40</span>

Those who are free from sinful acts are called anidâna <span
id="fr_196"></span>[1](#fn_193). Hence a very wise man should not be
inflamed (by wrath). Thus I say. (3)
