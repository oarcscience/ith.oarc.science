### KA*ND*IKÂ 5.

1\. Now (follows) the putting up of the water-barrel.

2\. To the north-east he digs a pit like (the pit for) a sacrificial
post, strews into it Ku*s*a grass, fried grains, fruits of the
soap-tree, and other auspicious things, and therein he establishes the
water-barrel with (the words), 'The sea art thou.'

3 <span id="fr_893"></span>[3](#fn_892.md). He pours water into it with
(the verse), 'Ye

<span id="page_350">p. 350</span>

waters, rich in wealth, ye possess goods. Ye bring us good insight and
immortality. Ye are the rulers over wealth and blessed offspring. May
Sarasvatî give strength to him who praises her!'—

4\. And with the three (verses), 'O waters, ye are' (Vâ*g*. Sa*m*hitâ
XI, 50 seqq.).

5\. Then (follows) feeding of the Brâhma*n*as.
