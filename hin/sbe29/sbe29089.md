### KHA*ND*A 8.

1\. If a post puts forth shoots,

2\. Let him prepare a mess of cooked food and offer the boiled rice with
the two (verses), 'In that way bringing forth deeds' (*S*rauta-sûtra
III, 17, 1), 'Of tawny shape, weighty, a giver of vigour' (Rig-veda II,
3, 9). A

3 <span id="fr_309"></span>[8\_3](#fn_308). Should the pot for the
Pra*n*îtâ water, the Â*g*ya- pot, any other earthen (vessel) be damaged
and leak,

4 <span id="fr_310"></span>[4](#fn_309). He sacrifices the two
Sarvaprâya*sk*itta oblations and recites the three verses, 'He who
without' (Rig-veda VIII, I, 12 seq.), over the broken (vessel).

5 <span id="fr_311"></span>[5](#fn_310). Should the two (Ku*s*a blades
which are used as) strainers be spoiled before the completion of the
sacrifice,

6 <span id="fr_312"></span>[6](#fn_311). Let him sacrifice the
Sarvaprâya*sk*itta and make new ones with (the verse), 'In the water,
Agni' (Rig-veda VIII, 43, 9).
