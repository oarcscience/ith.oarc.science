### KA*ND*IKÂ 3.

1 <span id="fr_529"></span>[1](#fn_528.md). He then should recite for
himself (the following texts, viz.) the *Rik*as, the Ya*g*us, the
Sâmans, the Atharvan and Aṅgiras hymns, the Brâhma*n*as, the Kalpa
(Sûtras), the Gâthâs, the (texts in honour of kings and heroes, called)
Nârâ*s*a*m*sîs, the Itihâsas and Purâ*n*as.

2\. In that he recites the *Rik*as, he thereby satiates the gods with
oblations of milk—in that (he recites) the Ya*g*us, with oblations of
ghee—the Sâmans, with oblations of honey—the Atharvan and Aṅgiras hymns,
with oblations of Soma—the Brâhma*n*as, Kalpas, Gâthâs, Nârâ*s*a*m*sîs,
Itihâsas and Purâ*n*as, with oblations of ambrosia.

<span id="page_219">p. 219</span>

3\. In that he recites the *Rik*as, rivers of milk flow, as a funeral
oblation, to his Fathers. In that (he recites) the Ya*g*us, rivers of
ghee—the Sâmans, rivers of honey—the Atharvan and Aṅgiras hymns, rivers
of Soma—the Brâhma*n*as, Kalpas, Gâthâs, Nârâ*s*a*m*sîs, Itihâsas and
Purâ*n*as, rivers of ambrosia.

4\. After he has recited (those texts) as far as he thinks fit, he
should finish with the following (verse),

'Adoration to Brahman! Adoration be to Agni! Adoration to the Earth!
Adoration to the Herbs! Adoration to the Voice! Adoration to the Lord of
the Voice! Adoration I bring to great Vish*n*u!'
