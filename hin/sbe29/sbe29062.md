### KHA*ND*A 14.

1\. On the last (Ash*t*akâ) he sacrifices cakes,

2\. With the words, 'The Ukthya and the Atirâtra. the Sadya*h*krî
together with the metre—Ash*t*akâ!

<span id="page_105">p. 105</span>

<span class="contnote">\[paragraph continues\]</span> Preparer of cakes!
Adoration to thee, O glad-faced one. Svâhâ!

3 <span id="fr_229"></span>[3-6](#fn_228.md). A cow or a goat is the animal
(to be sacrificed), or a mess of cooked food (should be offered).

4\. Or he may optionally offer food to a cow.

5 <span id="fr_230"></span>[5](#fn_229.md). Or he may optionally burn down
brushwood in the forest and say, 'This is my Ash*t*akâ.'

6\. But let him not neglect to do (one of these things). But let him not
neglect to do (one of these things).

Here ends the Third Adhyâya.
