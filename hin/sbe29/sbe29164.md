### KA*ND*IKÂ 10.

1 <span id="fr_723"></span>[1](#fn_722). If (in the chariot.md) of a king
the axle breaks, or something that is bound loosens itself, or the
chariot is overturned, or if another accident happens, or (if one of
these same things occurs) when a bride is carried home, he establishes
the same fire, prepares Â*g*ya, and sacrifices (two Â*g*ya oblations)
separately with the two Mantras, 'Here is joy' (Vâ*g*. Sa*m*h. VIII, 51
a).

2\. Having got ready another chariot, he (i.e. the Purohita or the
bridegroom) should make the king or the woman sit down thereon with (the
formula), 'In royal power' down to the word, 'in sacrifice'

<span id="page_288">p. 288</span>

<span class="contnote">\[paragraph continues\]</span> (Vâ*g*. Sa*m*h.
XX, 10), and with the (verse), 'I have seized thee' (ibid. XII, 11).

3\. The two beasts that draw the chariot, constitute the sacrificial
fee.

4\. (This is) the penance.

5\. Then (follows) feeding of the Brâhma*n*as.
