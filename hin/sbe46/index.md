This is the second volume in the 'Vedic Hymns' section of the Sacred
Books of the East. It was the final SBE text volume to be converted to
etext. While continuing the thread of Vedic linguistics which Max Müller
started in the first Vedic Hymns volume of the SBE, Oldenberg does not
attempt to rise to the same level of analysis of the religious content
of these Rig-veda hymns. In fact, at several points he states that he
really has no idea what some passages in these hymns mean, simply
attempting the best possible translation. For instance, there are
repeated references to the 'Two Mothers' of Agni, which appears to refer
to a pre-Vedic twin mother goddess. However Oldenberg makes no attempt
to explain this beyond identifying the two as Dawn and Night. It would
be of some interest to find out the source of this myth. <span
class="small">--J. B. Hare, September 17th, 2008.</span>
