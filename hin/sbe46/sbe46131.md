### MA*ND*ALA V, HYMN 27.

#### ASH*T*AKA IV, ADHYÂYA 1, VARGA 21.

1\. The good lord has presented me with two oxen together with a car,
the most brilliant Asura among the liberal
givers [1](sbe46131.htm#an_5:27:1:1.md). Tryaru*n*a, the son of
Triv*ri*shan, O Agni Vai*s*vânara [2](sbe46131.htm#an_5:27:1:2.md), has
distinguished himself by (his gift of) ten thousand
(cows?) [3](sbe46131.htm#an_5:27:1:3.md).

2\. To him who gives me one hundred [1](sbe46131.htm#an_5:27:2:1.md) and
twenty cows and two fallow steeds, harnessed and well-yoked, to
Tryaru*n*a grant thy protection, Agni Vai*s*vânara, who art highly
praised and grown strong.

3\. Thus, O Agni, desiring thy favour [1](sbe46131.htm#an_5:27:3:1.md),
Trasadasyu [2](sbe46131.htm#an_5:27:3:2) (sings.md) for the ninth
time [3](sbe46131.htm#an_5:27:3:3) to thee the youngest (god.md)—Tryaru*n*a
who responds to my, the strong-born's, many hymns with (the gift of) a
yoked (chariot) [4](sbe46131.htm#an_5:27:3:4.md)—

4 [1](sbe46131.htm#an_5:27:4:1.md). Who may thus announce
me [2](sbe46131.htm#an_5:27:4:2) to A*s*vamedha the liberal (prince.md):
may he give to him who with his verse strives for gain; may he give to
him who lives in the *Ri*ta for (acquiring)
wisdom [3](sbe46131.htm#an_5:27:4:3.md)—

5\. A*s*vamedha whose gifts, a hundred speckled bulls, delight me like
Soma juices with threefold admixture [1](sbe46131.htm#an_5:27:5:1.md).

6\. Indra-Agni! Bestow on A*s*vamedha, the giver of a hundred (bulls),
abundance of heroes and mighty royal power, like the never-ageing Sun in
heaven.

<span id="page_421">p. 421</span>

#### NOTES.

The *Ri*shis are Tryaru*n*a Traiv*ri*sh*n*a, Trasadasyu Paurukutsya, and
A*s*vamedha Bhârata, or, according to others, Atri alone. The metre is
Trish*t*ubh in verses 1–3, Anush*t*ubh in verses 4–6.

The position of this Sûkta shows that it is a later addition to the
original collection.

##### Verse 1.

Note <span id="an_5:27:1:1">1</span>. With Delbrück, Grassmann, von
Bradke (Dyâus Asura, p. 67) I read maghónâm instead of maghóna*h*. Cf.
III, 3, 4. ásura*h* vipa*h*-*k*ítâm.

Note <span id="an_5:27:1:2">2</span>. On the invocation of Agni in
Dânastutis, comp. H. O., Zeitschrift der Deutschen Morgenländischen
Gesellschaft, XXXIX, 87.

Note <span id="an_5:27:1:3">3</span>. Geldner (Ved. Studien, I, 268) is
right in observing: 'Hier ist unter sahasrâ*n*i eine bestimmte Geldoder
Wertsumme zu verstehen,' and in adding that it is not necessary that
such a sum consisted in cows.

##### Verse 2.

Note <span id="an_5:27:2:1">1</span>. On *s*atâ´, 'one hundred,' compare
Delbrück. Altindische Syntax, p. 82.

##### Verse 3.

Note <span id="an_5:27:3:1">1</span>. Cf. X, 148, 3. sumatím
*k*akâná*h*.

Note <span id="an_5:27:3:2">2</span>. That is, very probably, a
descendant of Trasadasyu.

Note <span id="an_5:27:3:3">3</span>. I do not adopt Sâya*n*a's
explanation navama*m* = navatamâ*m*, though I do not know what the
number 'nine' means here. Ludwig is absolutely right in observing 'dass
man eben hier, wo es sich um specielle concrete, uns aber sonst her
nicht bekannte verhältnisse und ereignisse handelt, eben sich bescheiden
muss, nichts

<span id="page_422">p. 422</span>

zu wissen.'—Prof. Max Müller believes that navish*th*âya makes navamam
for navatamam excusable: 'to the newest god the newest song.'

Note <span id="an_5:27:3:4">4</span>. Sâya*n*a supplies to yuktena, not
rathena, but manasâ.

##### Verse 4.

Note <span id="an_5:27:4:1">1</span>. I do not think that Ludwig is
right in believing that with verse 4 a new, independent section
begins.—Comp. on this verse, vol. xxxii, p. 304 (II, 34, 7, note 3).

Note <span id="an_5:27:4:2">2</span>. Me may be accusative, as it
frequently is. Should it be a dative, we should have to translate: 'Who
may tell A*s*vamedha for my sake.'

Note <span id="an_5:27:4:3">3</span>. Medhâ´m can scarcely depend on
dádat; wisdom is not a gift which liberal princes may bestow on singers.

##### Verse 5.

Note <span id="an_5:27:5:1">1</span>. Of milk, curds, and barley. See
Hillebrandt, Vedische Mythologie, I, p. 209.
