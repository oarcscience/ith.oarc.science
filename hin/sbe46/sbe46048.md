### MA*ND*ALA I, HYMN 150.

#### ASH*T*AKA II, ADHYÂYA 2, VARGA 19.

1\. I thy indigent [1](sbe46048.htm#an_1:150:1:1.md) worshipper say much to
thee, O Agni, dwelling in thy protection as (in the protection) of a
great impeller [2](sbe46048.htm#an_1:150:1:2.md).

2\. Away even from the libation of a rich man who is feeble, who is a
niggard, who never comes forward and does not care for the gods.

3\. The mortal (who worships thee?), O priest, is brilliant, great, most
powerful in heaven. May we, O Agni, addicted to thee, be always
foremost.

#### NOTES.

The same *Ri*shi. Metre, Ush*n*ih.—Verse 1 = SV. I, 97.

##### Verse 1.

Note <span id="an_1:150:1:1">1</span>. On arí, see Bergaigne, Religion
Védique, II, 218.

Note <span id="an_1:150:1:2">2</span>. Or, 'of the great impeller'—the
Sun-god who impels or stimulates his horses? Comp. VI, 6, 6?
