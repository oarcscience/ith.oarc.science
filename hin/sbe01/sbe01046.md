### TWELFTH KHA*ND*A.

1\. The hiṅkâra is, he rubs (the fire-stick); the prastâva, smoke rises;
the udgîtha, it burns; the pratihâra, there are glowing coals; the
nidhana, it goes down; the nidhana, it is gone out. This is the
Rathantara Sâman as interwoven in fire <span
id="fr_143"></span>[3](#fn_142.md).

2\. He who thus knows this Rathantara interwoven in fire, becomes
radiant <span id="fr_144"></span>[4](#fn_143.md) and strong. He reaches the
full life, he lives long, becomes great with children and cattle, great
by fame. The rule is, 'Do not rinse the mouth or spit before the fire.'
