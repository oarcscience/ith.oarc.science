## SEVENTH ADHYÂYA <span id="fr_616"></span>[2](#fn_614).

### FIRST KHA*ND*A.

1\. My speech rests in the mind, my mind rests in speech <span
id="fr_617"></span>[3](#fn_615). Appear to me (thou, the Highest Self)!
You (speech and mind) are the two pins <span
id="fr_618"></span>[4](#fn_616) (that hold the wheels) of the Veda. May
what I have learnt not forsake me <span id="fr_619"></span>[5](#fn_617).
I join day and night with what I have learnt <span
id="fr_620"></span>[6](#fn_618). I shall speak of the real, I shall
speak the true. May this protect me, may this protect the teacher! May
it protect me, may it protect the teacher, yea, the teacher!

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_614"></span>[246:2](sbe01227.htm#fr_616) This seventh
adhyâya contains a propitiatory prayer (*s*ântikaro mantra*h*). It is
frequently left out in the MSS. which contain the Aitareya-upanishad
with *S*aṅkara's commentary, and Dr. Roer has omitted it in his edition.
Sâya*n*a explains it in his commentary on the Aitareya-âranyaka; and in
one MS. of *S*aṅkara's commentary on the Aitareya-upanishad, which is in
my possession, the seventh adhyâya is added with the commentary of
Mâdhavâmâtya, the Â*g*ñâpâlaka of Vîrabukka-mahârâ*g*a.

<span id="fn_615"></span>[246:3](sbe01227.htm#fr_617) The two depend on
each other.

<span id="fn_616"></span>[246:4](sbe01227.htm#fr_618) Ant, explained by
the commentator as ânayanasamartha.

<span id="fn_617"></span>[246:5](sbe01227.htm#fr_619) Cf. *Kh*. Up. IV,
2, 5.

<span id="fn_618"></span>[246:6](sbe01227.htm#fr_620) I repeat it day
and night so that I may not forget it.
