29\. And on account of non-sublation.

The conclusion above arrived at is confirmed by the consideration that
thus only those texts are not stultified

<span id="page_702">p. 702</span>

which enjoin, for those who know Brahman, purity in matters of food with
a view to the origination of knowledge of Brahman. Cp.' when the food is
pure the mind becomes pure' (*Kh*. Up. VII, 26, 2).
