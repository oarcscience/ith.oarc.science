### CHAPTER IX.

1\. A hermit shall wear (his hair in) braids, and dress (in garments
made of) bark and skins; <span id="fr_255"></span>[1](#fn_255)

2\. And he shall not enter a village. <span
id="fr_256"></span>[2](#fn_256)

3\. He shall not step on ploughed (land). <span
id="fr_257"></span>[3](#fn_257)

4\. He shall gather wild growing roots and fruit (only). <span
id="fr_258"></span>[4](#fn_258)

5\. He shall remain chaste. <span id="fr_259"></span>[5](#fn_259)

6\. His heart shall be full of meekness. <span
id="fr_260"></span>[6](#fn_260)

7\. He shall honour guests coming to his hermitage with alms (consisting
of) roots and fruit. <span id="fr_261"></span>[7](#fn_261)

8\. He shall only give, not receive (presents).

9\. He shall bathe at morn, noon, and eve. <span
id="fr_262"></span>[9](#fn_262)

10\. Kindling a fire according to the (rule of the) *S*râma*n*aka
(Sûtra), he shall offer the Agnihotra. <span
id="fr_263"></span>[10](#fn_263)

11\. After (living in this manner during) six months, <span
id="fr_264"></span>[11](#fn_264)

<span id="page_46">p. 46</span>

he shall dwell at the root of a tree, keeping no fire and having no
house.

12\. He (who in this manner) gives (their due) to gods, manes, and men,
will attain endless (bliss in) heaven.
