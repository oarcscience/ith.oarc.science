### HYMN CXXXI

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2013101">1</span></span>He minishes,
he splits in twain: crush it and let it be  
   destroyed. <span id="page_369">p. 369</span>  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2013003">3, 4</span></span>Varuna
with the Vasus goes: the Wind-God hath a hundred  
   reins.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2013005">5, 6</span></span>A hundred
golden steeds hath he, a hundred chariots  
   wrought of gold.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2013007">7, 8</span></span>A hundred
bits of golden bronze, a hundred golden  
   necklaces.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2013009">9, 10</span></span>Lover of
Kusa grass, Unploughed! Fat is not reckoned in  
   the hoof.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2013011">11, 12</span></span>The
ladle doth not hold apart the entrails and the clotted  
   blood.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2013013">13, 14</span></span>This O
Mandūrikā, is mine. Thy trees are standing in a  
   clump.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2013015">15, 16</span></span>The
plain domestic sacrifice, the sacrifice with burning  
   dung.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2013017">17,
18</span></span>Asvattha, Dhava, Khadira, leaf taken from the Aratu.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2013019">19, 20</span></span>The man
pervaded thoroughly lies on the ground as he were  
   slain.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2013021">21, 22</span></span>The
biestings only have they milked: one-and-a half of the  
   wild ass,  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2013023">23</span></span>And two
hides of an elephant.
