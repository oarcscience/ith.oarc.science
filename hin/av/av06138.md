### HYMN CXXXVIII

#### A woman's imprecation on her unfaithful lover

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0613801">1</span></span>O Plant, thy
fame is spread abroad as best of all the herbs that  
   grow.  
  Unman for me to-day this man that he may wear the horn of  
   hair.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0613802">2</span></span>Make him a
eunuch with a horn, set thou the crest upon his-  
   head.  
  Let Indra with two pressing-stones deprive him of his manly  
   strength.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0613803">3</span></span>I have
unmanned thee, eunuch! yea, impotent! made thee-  
   impotent, and robbed thee, weakling! of thy strength.  
  Upon his head we set the horn, we set the branching ornament.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0613804">4</span></span>Duas tuas
venas, a Diis factas, in quibus stat vigor virilis,  
   paxillo ligneo in testiculis ob istam mulierem tibi findo.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0613805">5</span></span>Ut mulieres
mattam (tegetem) facturae arundinem lapide findunt,  
   sic fascinum tuum cum testiculis ob istam mulierem findo.
