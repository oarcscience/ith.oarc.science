### HYMN XXVII

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002701">1</span></span>If I, O
Indra, were, like thee, the single sovran of all wealth.  
  My worshipper should be rich in kine.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002702">2</span></span>I should be
fain, O Lord of Might, to strengthen and enrich the  
   sage,  
  Were I the lord of herds of kine,  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002703">3</span></span>To
worshippers who press the juice thy goodness, Indra, is a  
   cow.  
  Yielding in plenty kine and steeds.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002704">4</span></span>None is
there, Indra, God or man, to hinder thy munificence,  
  The wealth which, lauded, thou wilt give.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002705">5</span></span>The
sacrifice made Indra strong when he unrolled the earth and  
   made  
  Himself a diaden in heaven.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002706">6</span></span>Thine aid we
claim, O Indra, thine who after thou hast waxen  
   great.  
  Hast won all treasures for thine own.
