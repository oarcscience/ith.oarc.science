### HYMN LIV

#### A hymn to Kāla; a continuation of the preceding hymn

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1905401">1</span></span>From Kāla
sprang the Waters, sprang the regions, Brahma,  
  Holy Fire.  
  The Sun ascends by Kāla, and in Kāla sinks again to rest.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1905402">2</span></span>By Kāla
freshly blows the wind, mighty through Kāla is the  
  Earth: on Kāla rests the mighty Sky.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1905403">3</span></span>In Kāla erst
the text produced what is and what is yet to be.  
  From Kāla sprang the Richas, and from Kāla was the Yajus  
   born. <span id="page_256">p. 256</span>  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1905404">4</span></span>They formed
in Kāla sacrifice, eternal portion for the Gods.  
  In Kāla the Gandharvas and Apsarasas and worlds abide.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1905405">5</span></span>Atharvan and
this Angiras in Kāla are supreme o'er heaven.  
  Both this world and the world that is most lofty, the pure  
   worlds and pure intermediate spaces,—  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1905406">6</span></span>Yea, having
conquered all the worlds by Brahma, Kāla as God  
  Supreme is supplicated.
