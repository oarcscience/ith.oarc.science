### HYMN XLVIII

#### A hymn to Night for protection

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904801">1</span></span>Then all
that we accumulate, all that the treasure-chest contains,.  
  All this do we entrust to thee.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904802">2</span></span>Entrust thou
us to Dawn, O Mother Night.  
  May Dawn entrust us to the Day, and Day to thee, O splendid.  
  One.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904803">3</span></span>Whatever
flying thing be here, whatever thing that creeps and.  
   crawls.  
  Whatever fastens on a joint, therefrom do thou protect us,  
  Night.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904804">4</span></span>So guard
thou us from west and east, protect us from the north  
   and south.  
  O splendid One, preserve us: we, the singers of thy praise, are  
   here.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904805">5</span></span>They who are
followers of Night, and they who watch o'er  
   living things.  
  They who protect all cattle, they keep watch and ward over our  
   lives, over our herds keep watch and ward.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904806">6</span></span>Verily,
Night, I know thy name, Dropper of Fatness art thou  
   called.  
  Thee Bharadvāja knew as such: as such be watchful o'er our  
   wealth.
