### HYMN XIV

#### A charm against consumption

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0601401">1</span></span>Remove thou
all Decline that lurks within the members and the  
   joints,  
  The firmly-settled heart-disease that racks the bones and rends  
   the limbs.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0601402">2</span></span>From the
consumptive man I pluck Decline as 'twere a severed  
   part.  
  I cut the bond that fetters him, even as a root of cucumber.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0601403">3</span></span>Begone,
Consumption, hence away, like a young foal that runs.  
   at speed.  
  Then, not pernicious to our men, flee, yearly visitant like grass!
