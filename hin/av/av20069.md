### HYMN LXIX

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006901">1</span></span>May he stand
by us in our need and in abundance for our  
   wealth:  
  With riches may he come to us;  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006902">2</span></span>Whose pair
of tawny horses yoked in battles foemen challenge  
   not:  
  To him, to Indra, sing your song.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006903">3</span></span>Nigh to the
Soma-drinker come, for his enjoyment, these bright  
   drops,  
  The Somas mingled with the curd.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006904">4</span></span>Thou, grown
at once to perfect strength, wast born to drink the  
  Soma juice, strong Indra, for preeminence.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006905">5</span></span>O Indra,
lover of the song, may these quick Somas enter thee:  
  May they bring bliss to thee the Sage.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006900">0</span></span>6. O Lord of
Hundred Powers, our chants of praise and lauds have  
   strengthened thee:  
  So strengthen thee the songs we sing!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006907">7</span></span>Indra, whose
succour never fails, accept this treasure thousand-  
   fold,  
  Wherein all manly powers abide.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006908">8</span></span>O Indra,
thou who lovest song, let no man hurt our bodies,  
   keep.  
  Slaughter far from us, for thou canst.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006912">12</span></span>Thereafter
they, as is their wont, threw off the state of babes  
   urborn,  
  Taking their sacrificial name.
