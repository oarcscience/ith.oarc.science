### HYMN CXV

#### A charm against Misfortune

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0711501">1</span></span>Hence. Evil
Fortune! fly away, vanish from this place and from  
   that.  
  We fix thee with an iron hook unto the man who hateth us.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0711502">2</span></span>Granting us
riches, Savitar! golden-banded, send thou away  
   from us to other regions  
  That Fortune who, flying, abominable, hath, as a creeper climbs•  
   a tree, assailed me.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0711503">3</span></span>One and a
hundred Fortunes all together are at his birth born  
   with a mortal's body.  
  Of these we send away the most unlucky: keep lucky ones for  
   us, O Jātavedas.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0711504">4</span></span>I have
disparted these and those like cows who stray on common  
   land.  
  Here let auspicious Fortunes stay: hence have I banished evil  
   ones.
