### HYMN XLIV

#### A curative and protective charm

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904401">1</span></span>Thou art the
lengthening of life, thy name is Universal Cure:  
  Then, Ointment! send felicity; Waters, send happiness and  
   peace.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904402">2</span></span>The yellow
hue, the feverish heat, the shooting pain that rends II  
   the limbs,  
  All the consumptive malady let the Ointment drive from out  
   thy frame.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904403">3</span></span>Let the
Salve born upon the earth, benignant, giving life to man.  
  Make the swift rider on the car sinless, exempt from sudden  
   death.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904404">4</span></span>Preserve our
breath, O Vital Breath, have mercy on our life, O  
  Life.  
  From snares of Nirriti do thou, O Nirriti, deliver us.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904405">5</span></span>Thou art the
babe of Sindhu, thou art lightnings' flower, wind,  
   breath, and Sun: thou art the eye and milk of heaven.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904406">6</span></span>Gods'
Ointment from the Three Peaked Hill, preserve thou me  
   on every side.  
  No plants of earth surpass thee, none from mountain or from  
   cultured ground.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904407">7</span></span>Now hath it
gently crept within, fiend-slaying, chasing malady.  
  And driving all diseases hence, and evil omens, banished them.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904408">8</span></span>Full many a
falsehood, O thou King Varuna, man hath uttered  
   here:  
  Do thou who hast a thousand powers preserve us from that  
   misery. <span id="page_247">p. 247</span>  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904409">9</span></span>If we have
cried, O Waters! Cows! if we have cried, O  
  Varuna!  
  For this endowed with thousand powers! deliver us from  
   misery.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904410">10</span></span>Mitra and
Varuna, O Salve have closely followed after thee  
  May they, when they have followed thee afar, restore thee for  
   our use.
