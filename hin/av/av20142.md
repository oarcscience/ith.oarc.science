### HYMN CXLII

#### Hymn to Dawn and the Asvins

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2014201">1</span></span>Together
with the Goddess, with the Asvins' Speech have I  
   awoke.  
  Thou, Goddess, hast disclosed the hymn and holy gift from  
   mortal men.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2014202">2</span></span>Awake the
Asvins, Goddess Dawn! Up, mighty Lady of Sweet  
  Strains!  
  Rise straightway, priest of sacrifice! High glory to the glad-  
   dening draught!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2014203">3</span></span>Thou, Dawn,
approaching with thy light, shinest together with  
   the Sun, <span id="page_378">p. 378</span>  
  And to this man-protecting home the chariot of the Asvins  
   comes.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2014204">4</span></span>When yellow
stalks give forth the juice as cows from udders  
   pour their milk,  
  And voices sound the song of praise, the Asvins' worshippers  
   show first.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2014205">5</span></span>Forward for
glory and for strength, protection that shall con-  
   quer men,  
  And power and skill, most sapient Ones!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2014206">6</span></span>When, Asvins
worthy of our lauds, ye seat you in the father's  
   house.  
  With wisdom or the bliss ye bring.
