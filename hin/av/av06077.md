### HYMN LXXVII

#### A charm to bring the cattle home

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0607701">1</span></span>Firm stands
the heaven, firm stands the earth, firm stands this  
   universal world,  
  Firm stand the rooted mountains. I have put the horses in the  
   stall.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0607702">2</span></span>I call the
Herdsman, him who knows the way to drive the  
   cattle forth,  
  Who knows the way to drive them home, to drive them back  
   and drive them in.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0607703">3</span></span>O Jātavedas
turn them back: a hundred homeward ways be  
   thine!  
  Thou hast a thousand avenues: by these restore our kine to us.
