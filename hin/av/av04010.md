### HYMN X

#### A charm accompanying investiture with an amulet of shell

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401001">1</span></span>Child of the
wind firmament, sprung from the lightning and the  
   light,  
  May this the gold-born Shell that bears the pearl preserve us  
   from distress.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401002">2</span></span>Shell that
wast born from out the sea, set at the head of  
   things that shine!  
  With thee we slay the Rākshasas and overcome voracious fiends.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401003">3</span></span>We stay
disease and indigence, and chase Sadānvās with the  
  Shell.  
  May the all-healing Shell that bears the pearl preserve us from  
   distress.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401004">4</span></span>Born in the
heaven, sprung from the sea, brought to us hither  
   from the flood.  
  This gold-born Shell shall be to us an amulet to lengthen life.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401005">5</span></span>From ocean
sprang the Amulet, from Vritra sprang the Lord of  
  Day:  
  May this protect us round about from shaft of God and Asura.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401006">6</span></span>Peerless
'mid golden ornaments art thou: from Soma wast thou  
   born.  
  Thou gleamest on the quiver, thou art beautiful upon the car:  
   may it prolong our days of life!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0401007">7</span></span>Bone of the
Good became the pearl's shell-mother endowed with  
   soul it moveth in the waters.  
  I bind this on thee for life, strength, and vigour, for long life  
   lasting through a hundred autumns.  
  May the pearl's mother keep and guard thee safely!
