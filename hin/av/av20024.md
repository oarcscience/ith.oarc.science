### HYMN XXIV

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002401">1</span></span>Come to the
juice that we have pressed, to Soma, Indra! blent  
   with milk:  
  Come, favouring us, thy bay-drawn car!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002402">2</span></span>Come, Indra,
to this gladdening drink, placed on the grass,  
   pressed out with stones:  
  Wilt thou not drink thy fill thereof?  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002403">3</span></span>To Indra
have my songs of praise gone forth, thus rapidly sent  
   hence,  
  To turn him to the Soma-draught.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002404">4</span></span>Hither with
songs of praise we call Indra to drink the Soma.  
   juice:  
  Will he not come to us by lauds?  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002405">5</span></span>Indra, these
Somas are expressed, Take them within thy belly,  
  Lord Of Hundred Powers, thou Prince of wealth.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002406">6</span></span>We know thee
winner of the spoil and resolute in battles, Sage!  
  Therefore thy blessing we implore.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002407">7</span></span>Borne hither
by thy stallions, drink, Indra, this juice which we  
   have pressed,  
  Mingled with barley and with milk.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002408">8</span></span>Indra, for
thee in thine own place I urge the Soma for thy  
   draught: <span id="page_284">p. 284</span>  
  Deep in thy heart let it remain.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002409">9</span></span>We call on
thee, the Ancient One, Indra, to drink the Soma  
   juice,  
  We Kusikas who seek thine aid.
