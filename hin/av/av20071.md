### HYMN LXXI

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007101">1</span></span>Mighty is
Indra, yea, supreme; greatness becomes the  
  Thunderer!  
  Wide as the heaven extends his power;  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007102">2</span></span>Which aideth
those to win them sons who come as heroes to the  
   fight,  
  Or singers loving holy thoughts.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007103">3</span></span>His belly
drinking deepest draughts of Soma like an ocean  
   swells,  
  Like wide streams from the cope of heaven.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007107">7</span></span>Come, Indra,
and delight thee with the juice at all the Soma  
   feasts,  
  Protector, mighty in thy strength.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007108">8</span></span>To Indra
pour ye forth the juice, the active gladdening juice, to  
   him  
  The gladdening omnific God.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007109">9</span></span>O Lord of
all men, fair of cheek, rejoice thee in the gladdening  
   buds,  
  Present at these drink-offerings.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007110">10</span></span>Songs have
outpoured themselves to thee, Indra, the strong, the  
   guardian Lord.  
  And raised themselves unsatisfied.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007111">11</span></span>Send to us
bounty manifold, O Indra, worthy of our wish,  
  For power supreme is only thine.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007112">12</span></span>O Indra,
stimulate thereto us emulously fain for wealth.  
  And glorious, O most splendid One.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007113">13</span></span>Give,
Indra, wide and lofty fame, wealthy in cattle and in  
   strength,  
  Lasting our life-time, failing not.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007114">14</span></span>Grant us
high fame, O Indra, grant riches bestowing thousands,  
   those  
  Fair fruits of earth borne home in wains.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007115">15</span></span>Praising
with songs the praise-worthy who cometh to our aid,  
   we call  
  Indra, the Treasure-Lord of wealth.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2007116">16</span></span>To lofty
Indra, dweller by each libation, the pious man Sings  
   forth aloud a strengthening hymn.
