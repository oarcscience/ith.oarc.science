### HYMN XXII

#### A prose hymn of homage to various portions of the Atharva-veda, to the Rishis, and to Brahma

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902201">1</span></span>With the
first five chapters of the Angirases, Hail! <span id="page_229">p.
229</span>  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902202">2</span></span>To the
sixth, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902203">3</span></span>To the
seventh and eight, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902204">4</span></span>The
black-clawed ones, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902205">5</span></span>To the
golden-hued ones, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902206">6</span></span>To the small
ones, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902207">7</span></span>To those
composed in strophes, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902208">8</span></span>To the first
shells, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902209">9</span></span>To the
second shells, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902210">10</span></span>To the
third shells, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902211">11</span></span>To the
penultimates, Hail.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902212">12</span></span>To the last
ones, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902213">13</span></span>To the
latter ones, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902214">14</span></span>To the
Rishis, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902215">15</span></span>To those
with hair in tufts, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902216">16</span></span>To the
Ganas, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902217">17</span></span>To the
great Ganas, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902218">18</span></span>To all the
Vidagana Angirases, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902219">19</span></span>To those
two with separate thousands, Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902220">20</span></span>To Brahma,
Hail!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1902221">21</span></span>Collected
manly powers are topped by Brahma. Brahma at first  
   spread out the loftiest heaven.  
  Brahma was born first of all things existing. Who then is meet  
   to be that Brahma's rival?
