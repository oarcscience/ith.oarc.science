### HYMN II

#### A charm against dysentery

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0100201">1</span></span>We know the
father of the shaft, Parjanya, liberal nourisher,  
  Know well his mother: Prithivī, Earth with her manifold  
   designs.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0100202">2</span></span>Do thou, O
Bowstring, bend thyself around us: make my body  
   stone.  
  Firm in thy strength drive far away malignities and hateful  
   things.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0100203">3</span></span>When,
closely clinging round the wood, the bowstring sings  
   triumph to the swift and whizzing arrow,  
  Indra, ward off from us the shaft, the missile.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0100204">4</span></span>As in its
flight the arrow's point hangs between earth and  
   firmament,  
  So stand this Munja grass between ailment and dysenteric ill!
