### HYMN LXI

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006101">1</span></span>We sing this
strong and wild delight of thine which conquers in  
   the fray,  
  Which, Caster of the Stone, gives room and shine like gold.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006102">2</span></span>Wherewith
thou also foundest lights for Āyu and for Manu's  
   sake:  
  Now joying in!!this sacred grass thou beamest forth.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006103">3</span></span>This day to
singers of the hymn praise, as of old, this, might of  
   thine.  
  Win thou the waters, day by day, thralls of the strong.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006104">4</span></span>Sing forth
to him whom many men invoke, to him whom many  
   laud:  
  Invite the potent Indra with your songs of praise;  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006105">5</span></span>Whose lofty
might—for doubly strong is he—supports the  
   heaven and earth.  
  And hills and plains and floods and light with manly power.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006106">6</span></span>Such,
praised by many! thou art King: alone thou smitest foe-  
   men dead,  
  To gain, O Indra, spoils of war and high renown.
