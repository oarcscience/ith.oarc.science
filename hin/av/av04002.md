### HYMN III

#### A Charm against tigers, wolves, thieves and other noxious creatures

 

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0400301">1</span></span>Three have
gone hence and passed away, the man, the tiger,  
   and the wolf.  
  Down, verily, the rivers flow, down-goeth the celestial Tree,.  
   down let our foemen bend and bow.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0400302">2</span></span>On distant
pathway go the wolf, on pathway most remote the  
   thief!  
  On a far road speed forth the rope with teeth, and the malicious  
   man!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0400303">3</span></span>We crush and
rend to pieces both thine eyes, O Tiger, and thy  
   jaws and all the twenty claws we break.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0400304">4</span></span>We break and
rend the tiger first of creatures that are armed.  
   with teeth;  
  The robber then, and then the snake, the sorcerer, and then the  
   wolf.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0400305">5</span></span>The thief
who cometh near to-day departeth bruised and crush-  
   ed to bits.  
  By nearest way let him be gone. Let Indra slay him with his  
   bolt.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0400306">6</span></span>Let the
beast's teeth be broken off, shivered and shattered be  
   his ribs! <span id="page_a108">p. a108</span>  
  Slack be thy bowstring: downward go the wild beast that  
   pursues the hare!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0400307">7</span></span>Open not
what thou hast compressed, close not what thou hast  
   not compressed.  
  Indra's and Soma's child, thou art Atharvan's tiger-crushing  
   charm.
