### HYMN LVI

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2005601">1</span></span>Indra,
foe-slayer, hath been raised to joy and power by the men.  
  Him, verily, we invocate in battles whether great or small: be  
   he our aid in fights for spoil.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2005602">2</span></span>For, Hero,
thou art like a host, art giver of abundant prey.  
  Strengthening even the feeble, thou aidest the sacrificer, thou  
   givest the worshipper ample wealth.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2005603">3</span></span>When war and
battles are on foot, booty is laid before the bold. <span
id="page_310">p. 310</span>  
  Yoke thou thy wildly rushing Bays. Whom wilt thou slay and  
   whom enrich? Do thou, O Indra, make us rich,  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2005604">4</span></span>He,
righteous-hearted, at each time of rapture gives us herds of  
   kine.  
  Gather in both thy hands for us treasures of many hundred  
   sorts. Sharpen thou us, and bring us wealth.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2005605">5</span></span>Refresh
thee, Hero, with the juice outpoured for bounty and  
   for strength.  
  We know thee Lord of ample store, to thee have sent our heart's  
   desires: be therefore our protector thou.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2005606">6</span></span>These
people, Indra, keep for thee all that is worthy of thy  
   choice.  
  Discover thou, as Lord, the wealth of men who offer up no  
   gifts: bring thou to us this wealth of theirs.
