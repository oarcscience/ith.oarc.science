### HYMN XLVI

#### A charm accompanying investiture with an amulet that ensures safety and victory

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904601">1</span></span>For manly
strength Prajāpati bound thee on first, invincible.  
  This for long life on thee I bind for splendour, strength, and  
   energy. Invincible, let it guard thee well.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904602">2</span></span>Erect,
invincible, be this man's watchful keeper: let not the  
  Panis or the sorcerers harm thee.  
  Shake off thy foes as Indra scattered Dasyus: quell all enemies.  
  Invincible, let it guard thee well.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904603">3</span></span>Indra hath
lent the power of sight, and vital breath and strength  
   to this.  
  Whom even a hundred combatants, striking, have failed to over-  
   come. Invincible, let it guard thee well.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904604">4</span></span>Around thy
limbs I place the mail of Indra who hath become  
   the Gods' imperial Sovran.  
  Again let all the Deities bring thee hither. Invincible let it guard  
   thee well.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904605">5</span></span>One and a
hundred manly powers, a thousand lives hath this  
  Amulet, unconquered ever.  
  Go forth a tiger, strike down all thy foemen: let him who would  
   oppose fall low beneath thee. Invincible, let it guard thee  
   well.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904606">6</span></span>Drawn forth
from butter, rich in milk and sweetness, hundred-  
   lived, thousand-homed, bestowing vigour,  
  Kindly, delightsome, full of sap, and mighty, invincible let it  
   guard thee well.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1904607">7</span></span>That thou
mayst be pre-eminent, slayer of rivals, rivalless, <span
id="page_249">p. 249</span>  
  May Savitar cause thee to be chief and controller of thy kin.  
  Invincible, let it guard thee well.
