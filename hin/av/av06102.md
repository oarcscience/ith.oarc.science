### HYMN CII

#### A man's love charm

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0610201">1</span></span>Even as this
ox, O Asvins, steps and turns together with his  
   mate,  
  So let thy fancy turn itself, come nearer, and unite with me.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0610202">2</span></span>I, as the
shaft-horse draws the mare beside him, draw thee to  
   myself.  
  Like grass that storm and wind have rent, so be thy mind at-  
   tached to me!  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0610203">3</span></span>Swiftly from
Bhaga's hands I bear away a love-compelling charm  
  Of ointment and of sugar-cane, of Spikenard and the Kushtha  
   plant.
