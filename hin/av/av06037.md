### HYMN XXXVII

#### A charm to divert Imprecation personified

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0603701">1</span></span>Hitherward,
having yoked his steeds, came Imprecation,  
   thousand-eyed,  
  Seeking my curser, as a wolf the home of one who owneth sheep.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0603702">2</span></span>Avoid us,
Imprecation! as consuming fire avoids the lake.  
  Smite thou the man who curses us, as the sky's lightning strikes  
   the tree.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0603703">3</span></span>Who curses
us, himself uncursed, or, cursed, who curses us  
   again,  
  Him cast I as a sop to Death, as to a dog one throws a bone.
