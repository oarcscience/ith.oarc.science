### HYMN LXXVI

#### A charm to cure scrofulous pustules and scrofula

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0707601">1</span></span>Rapidly
dropping, quick to drop, more evil than the evil ones,  
  More sapless than a dried-up bone, swifter than salt to melt  
   away.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0707602">2</span></span>Pustules
that rise upon the neck, Pustules upon the shoulder-  
   joints,  
  Pustules that, falling of themselves, spring up on every twofold  
   limb:  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0707603">3</span></span>I have
expelled and banished all Scrofula harboured in the head,  
  And that which bores the breast-bone through, and that which  
   settles in the sole.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0707604">4</span></span>Scrofula
flies borne on by wings: it penerates and holds the  
   man.  
  Here is the cure of either kind, the chronic and the transient.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0707605">5</span></span>We know
thine origin, Scrofula! know whence thou, Scrofula,  
   art born.  
  How hast thou then struck this man here, him in whose house  
   we sacrifice?  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_0707606">6</span></span>Boldly drink
Soma from the beaker, Indra! hero in war for  
   treasure! Vritra-slayer.  
  Fill thyself full at the mid-day libation: thyself possessing riches  
   grant us riches.
