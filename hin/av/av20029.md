### HYMN XXIX

  I. For thou, O Indra, art the God whom hymns and praises  
   magnify:  
  Thou blessest those who worship thee.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002902">2</span></span>Bay horses
with their long manes bring Indra to drink the  
  Soma juice,  
  The Bountiful to our sacrifice.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002903">3</span></span>With waters'
foam thou torest off, Indra, the head of Namuchi,  
  Subduing all contending hosts.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002904">4</span></span>The Dasyus,
when they fain would climb by magic arts and  
   mount to heaven,  
  Thou, Indra, castest down to earth.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2002905">5</span></span>As
Soma-drinker conquering all, thou scatteredst to every side  
  Their band who poured no gifts to thee.
