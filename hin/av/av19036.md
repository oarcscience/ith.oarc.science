### HYMN XXXVI

#### A charm against disease and evil spirits

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1903601">1</span></span>The
Hundred-haired hath banished hence fiends and Consump-  
   tions by its might.  
  With splendour hath the charm that scares demons of ill-name  
   mounted up.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1903602">2</span></span>It drives
off demons with its horns and sorceresses with its root,  
  It stays Consumption with its waist: from this no wickedness  
   escapes.  
<span class="margnote"><span
id="an_1903603">3</span></span>Consumptions, light and serious, and
those which sounds  
   accompany,  
  All these the Amulet, Hundred-haired, scarer of fiends, hath  
   banished hence.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1903604">4</span></span>A hundred
men hath it produced, hundred Consumptions chased  
   away,  
  All fiends of evil-name it hath smitten, and shakes the  
  Rākshasas.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1903605">5</span></span>The Bull
that weareth horns of gold, this Amulet with hundred  
   hairs,  
  Hath cleft the demons of ill-name and overcome the Rākshasas.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1903606">6</span></span>Hundred
she-fiends, a hundred of Gandharvas and Apsarasas,  
  A hundred of dog-mated nymphs, I keep away with Hundred-  
  Hair
