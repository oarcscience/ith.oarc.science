### HYMN LXVIII

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006804">4</span></span>Go to the
wise unconquered One, ask thou of Indra, skilled in  
   song,  
  Him who is better than thy friends.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006805">5</span></span>Whether the
men who mock us say, Depart unto another place,  
  Ye who serve Indra and none else;  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006806">6</span></span>Or whether,
God of wondrous deeds, all our true people call us  
   blest,  
  Still may we dwell in Indra's care.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006807">7</span></span>Unto the
swift One bring the swift, man-cheering, grace of  
   sacrifice.  
  That to the Friend gives wings and joy.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006808">8</span></span>Thou,
Satakratu, drankest this and wast the Vritras' slayer;  
   thou.  
  Helpest the warrior in the fray.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006809">9</span></span>We
strengthen, Satakratu, thee, yea, thee the powerfull in fight,  
  That, Indra, we may win us wealth.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006810">10</span></span>To him the
mighty stream of wealth, prompt Friend of him who  
   pours the juice,  
  Yea, to this Indra sing your song.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006841">41</span></span>O come ye
hither, sit ye down: to Indra sing ye forth your  
   song,  
  Companions, bringing hymns of praise.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2006812">12</span></span>To him the
richest of the rich, the Lord of treasures excellent,  
  Indra, with Soma juice outpoured.
