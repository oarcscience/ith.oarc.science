### HYMN XVIII

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2001801">1</span></span>This, even
this, O Indra, we implore: as thy devoted friends,  
  The Kanvas praise thee with their hymns.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2001802">2</span></span>Naught else,
O Thunderer, have I praised in the skilled singer's  
   eulogy:  
  On thy laud only have I thought.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2001803">3</span></span>The Gods
seek him who presses out the Soma; they desire not  
   sleep:  
  Incessantly they punish sloth.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2001804">4</span></span>Faithful to
thee we loudly sing, heroic Indra, songs to thee.  
  Mark, gracious Lord, this act of ours.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2001805">5</span></span>Give us not
up to man's reproach, to foeman's hateful calumny:  
  In thee alone is all my strength.  
<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2001806">6</span></span>Thou art
mine ample coat of mail, my champion, Vritra-slayes,  
   thou.  
  With thee for Friend I brave the foe.
