### SECOND BRÂHMA*N*A.

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:1">10:3:2:1</span></span>1. As
to this they say, 'What metre and what deity are the head of the
fire-altar?' The metre Gâyatrî and the deity Agni are its head.

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:2">10:3:2:2</span></span>2.
'What metre and what deity are its neck?' The metre Ush*n*ih and the
deity Savit*ri* are its neck.

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:3">10:3:2:3</span></span>3.
'What metre and what deity are its spine?' The metre B*ri*hatî and the
deity B*ri*haspati are its spine.

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:4">10:3:2:4</span></span>4.
'What metre and what deity are its wings?' The metres B*ri*hat and
Rathantara and the deities Heaven and Earth are its wings.

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:5">10:3:2:5</span></span>5.
'What metre and what deity are its waist?' The metre Trish*t*ubh and the
deity Indra are its waist.

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:6">10:3:2:6</span></span>6.
'What metre and what deity are its hips?' The metre *G*agatî and the
deity Âditya (the sun) are its hips.

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:7">10:3:2:7</span></span>7.
'What metre and what deity are the vital air whence the seed flows?' The
metre Ati*kh*andas and the deity Pra*g*âpati.

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:8">10:3:2:8</span></span>8.
'What metre and what deity are that downward vital air?' The metre
Ya*gñ*âya*gñ*iya and the deity Vai*s*vânara.

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:9">10:3:2:9</span></span>9.
'What metre and what deity are the thighs?' The metre Anush*t*ubh and
that deity, the Vi*s*vedevâ*h*, are the thighs.

<span id="page_331">p. 331</span>

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:10">10:3:2:10</span></span>10.
'What metre and what deity are the knees?' The metre Paṅkti and that
deity, the Maruts, are the knees.

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:11">10:3:2:11</span></span>11.
'What metre and what deity are the feet?' The metre Dvipadâ and the
deity Vish*n*u are the feet.

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:12">10:3:2:12</span></span>12.
'What metre and what deity are the vital airs?' The metre Vi*kh*andas
and the deity Vâyu (the wind) are the vital airs.

<span class="margnote"><span id="10:3:2:13">10:3:2:13</span></span>13.
'What metre and what deity are the defective and redundant parts?' The
metre (of the verse) wanting a syllable (or syllables) and that deity,
the waters, are the defective and redundant parts. This, then, is the
knowledge of the body (of the altar), and suchlike is the deity that
enters into this body; and, indeed, there is in this (sacrificial
performance) no other prayer for the obtainment of heavenly bliss <span
id="fr_643"></span>[1](#fn_633).
