### PA*T*ALA 1, SECTION 5.

1\. Then (follows) in the sixth month the Annaprâ*s*ana (i.e. the first
feeding with solid food).

2\. In the fortnight of the increasing moon, under an auspicious
constellation, he puts wood on the fire, performs the rites down to the
Vyâh*ri*ti oblations, and sacrifices (with the Mantras), 'This, O
Varu*n*a' (&c.; see I, chap. 27, Sûtra 2, down to): 'Hail! Good luck!'
Then he gives (to the child) threefold food to eat, curds, honey, and
ghee, with (the formula), 'Bhû*h* I lay into thee! Bhuva*h* I lay into
thee! Suva*h* I lay into thee!'

3\. Then he gives him (other) food to eat with (the formula), 'I give
thee to eat the essence of water and of the plants. May water and plants
be kind towards thee. May water and plants do no harm to thee.'
