### ROMULUS.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_RR">RR</span></span>BIRTH.--But, in
my opinion, the origin of so great a city, and the establishment of an
empire next in power to that of the gods, was due to the Fates. The
vestal Rhea, being deflowered by force, when she had

<span id="page_294">p. 294</span>

brought forth twins, declares Mars to be the father. . . . <span
class="small">TITUS LIVY, HISTORY OF ROME. BOOK I, CHAPTER IV.</span>

TRANSLATION.--"On the nones of Qintilis or on the Quirinalia, as the
king was reviewing his people, the Sun withdrew its light; and while the
earth lay in darkness, Mars descended in a hurricane and tempest, and
bore away his perfected son in a fiery chariot to heaven." <span
class="small">B. G. NIEBUHR, THE HISTORY OF ROME, VOL. i, PAGE
197.</span>
