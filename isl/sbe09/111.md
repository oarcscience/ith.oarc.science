### THE CHAPTER OF ABU LAHEB <span id="fr_506"></span>[2](#fn_506).

### (CXI. Mecca.)

IN the name of the merciful and compassionate God.  
Abu Laheb's two hands shall perish, and he shall perish! <span
id="page_344">p. 344</span>  
His wealth shall not avail him, nor what he has earned!  
He shall broil in a fire that flames <span
id="fr_507"></span>[1](#fn_507), and his wife carrying faggots!--\[5\]
on her neck a cord of palm fibres.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Footnotes

<span id="fn_506"></span>[343:2](111.htm#fr_506) See Introduction, p.
xxviii. Abu Laheb, 'the father of the flame,' <span id="page_344">p.
344</span> was the nickname of ‘Abd el ’Huzzâ, uncle of Mohammed, and a
bitter opponent of Islam.

<span id="fn_507"></span>[344:1](111.htm#fr_507) A pun upon his name.
