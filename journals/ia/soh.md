## SONG OF HAFIZ.

   The following translation, in the measure of the original, of the
famous song of Hafiz, is taken from the *Calcutta Review:*—

<table data-align="center" data-cellpadding="10">
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td>Singer, O sing with all thine art,<br />
   Strains ever charming, sweetly new;<br />
Seek for the wine that opes the heart,<br />
   Ever more sparkling, brightly new!<br />
With thine own loved one, like a toy,<br />
   Seated apart in heavenly joy,<br />
Snatch from her lips kiss after kiss,<br />
   Momently still renew the bliss!<br />
Boy with the silver anklets, bring<br />
   Wine to inspire me as I sing;<br />
Hasten to pour in goblet bright<br />
   Nectar of Shiraz, soul's delight,<br />
Life is but life, and pleasures thine,<br />
   Long as thou quaff'st the quick'ning wine;<br />
Pour out the flagon's nectary wealth,<br />
   Drink to thy loved one many a health.<br />
Thou who hast stole my heart away,<br />
   Darling, for me thy charms display,<br />
Deck and adorn thy youth's soft bloom,<br />
   Use each fair dye and sweet perfume.<br />
Zephyr morn, when passing by<br />
   Bow'r of my love, this message sigh,<br />
Strains from her Hafiz fond and true,<br />
   Strains still more sparkling, sweetly new!<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
