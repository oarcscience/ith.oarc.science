# 35

### Priapus

Pedicabere, fur, semel; sed idem  
si deprensus eris bis, irrumabo.  
quod si tertia furta molieris,  
ut poenam patiare et hanc et illam,  
pedicaberis irrumaberisque.

Thief, for first thieving shalt be swived, but an  
Again arrested shalt be irrumate;  
And, shouldst attempt to plunder time the third,  
This and that penalty thou shalt endure,  
Being both pedicate and irrumate.

Thou shalt be bardashed,\[1\] thief, for the first theft; and if twice
caught, I will irrumate thee. But if thou shalt attempt a third theft,
that thou mayst suffer penalties twain, I will both sodomise and
irrumate thee.

\[1. *Bardache*, meaning a catamite. Italian *bardascia*, from the
Arabic *baradaj*, a captive, a slave. The old English was *ingle* or
*yngle* (a bardachio, a catamite, a boy kept for sodomy). In Latin
Bulgarus means a Bulgarian or a heretic, from which our vulgar modern
word 'bugger' is derived, as is the Italian *bugiardo* and the French
*bougre*.\]
