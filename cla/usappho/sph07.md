# <span id="BM6">6</span>

     ... Ἕλθε, Κύπρι,  
Χπρυσίασιν ἐν κυλίκεσσιν ἄβραισ  
συμμεμιγμένον θαλίαισι νέκταρ  
     οἰνοχόεισα. [\[transcription\]](xlatall.htm#10)

Come, goddess of Cyprus, and in golden cups serve nectar delicately
mixed with delights.

Come hither foam-born Cyprian goddess, come,  
And in golden goblets pour richest nectar  
All mixed in most ethereal perfection,  
     Thus to delight us.

Quoted by Athenaeus, who wrote in the first half of the third century
A.D. The fragment is apparently part of an invocation to Aphrodite.
