#### VIII. Scoticè

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Nü Yüeh Chi Ming;* narrative. A pleasant picture of domestic life.
A wife sends her husband from her side to his hunting, expresses her
affection, and encourages him to cultivate virtuous friendships.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>Says oor gudewife,
"The cock is crawin’."  
Quoth oor gudeman, "The day is dawin’."  
"Get up, gudeman, an’ tak a spy;  
See gin the mornin’ star be high,  
Syne tak a saunter roon’ aboot;  
There’s rowth o’ dyukes and geese to shoot.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>"Lat flee, and
bring them hame to me,  
An’ sic a dish as ye sail pree.  
In comin’ times as ower the strings  
Your noddin’ heed in rapture hings,  
Supreme ower care, nor fasht wi’ fears,  
We’ll baith grow auld in worth and years, <span id="page_94">p.
94</span>

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>"An’ when we meet
the friends ye like,  
I’ll gie to each some little fyke;—  
The lasses beads, trocks to their brithers,  
An’ auld-warld fairlies to their mithers.  
Some knickknack lovin’ hands will fin’  
To show the love that dwalls within.

</div>
