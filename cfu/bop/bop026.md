#### XII

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Yeh Yu Ssŭ Chün;* allusive and narrative. A virtuous young lady
resists the attempts of a seducer.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>In the wild lies
an antelope dead,  
  Wrapt up in a mat of white grass.  
With her thoughts of the spring comes a maid,  
  Whom a treacherous fop watches pass.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>Scrubby oaks grow
the forest around;  
  In the wild there lies stretched a dead deer,  
Close and tight with the white matting bound.  
  As a gem see the maiden appear. <span id="page_22">p. 22</span>

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>"Hold thy hand,
and beware, sir," she cries.  
  "Be thou civil, and haste not to wrong.  
Meddle not with my handkerchief's ties.  
  Do not make my dog bark. Pass along."

</div>
