#### X

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Chan Lu;* allusive. A festal ode, appropriate to the convivial
entertainment of the feudal princes at the royal court.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>The dew lies heavy
all around,  
Nor, till the sun shines, leaves the ground. <span id="page_210">p.
210</span>  
Far into night we feasting sit;  
We drink, and none his place may quit.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>The dew lies
heavy, and its gems  
Stud the luxuriant, grassy stems.  
The happy night with wassail rings;  
So feasted here the former kings.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>The jujube and the
willow tree  
All fretted with the dew we see.  
Each guest's a prince of noble line,  
In whom the virtues all combine.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_4">4</span></span>The *t‘ung* and
*i* their fruits display,  
Pendent from every graceful spray.  
My guests are joyous and serene,  
No haggard eye, no ruffled mien.

</div>
