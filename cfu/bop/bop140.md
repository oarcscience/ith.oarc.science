#### II

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Tung Mên Chih Fên;* narrative. Wanton associations of the young
people of Ch‘ên.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>The white elms by
the east gate grow,  
  And clumps of oaks crown Yüan Ch‘iu’s head;  
There Tzŭ-chung’s daughter oft we see,  
  Dancing about beneath their shade.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>On a bright
morning they have fixed,  
  To seek the plain that southward lies.  
Then from her task of twisting hemp,  
  See! dancing through the mart she hies.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>The morning fair,
young men and girls  
  Together go. You hear a youth  
Say to his mate, "O sunflower bright,  
  Pledge me with pepper stalk your truth!"

</div>
