### <span class="smallcaps">Book IV. The Odes of Yung</span>

#### I

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Po Chou;* allusive. Protest of a widow against being urged to marry
again.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>In the mid Ho that
cypress boat floats free,  
While friends a second marriage press on me.  
I see my husband's youthful forehead there,  
And on it the twin tufts of falling hair.  
Rather than wed again I'll die, I swear!  
O mother dear, O Heaven supreme, why should  
You not allow my vow, and aid my purpose good?

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>Near to the bank
that cypress boat floats free,  
While friends a second marriage press on me.  
He was my only one, with forehead fair,  
And on it the twin tufts of falling hair.  
Till death to shun the evil thing I swear!  
O mother dear, O Heaven supreme, why should  
You not allow my vow, and aid my purpose good?

</div>
