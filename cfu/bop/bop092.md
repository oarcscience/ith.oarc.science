#### XV

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Tung Mên Chih Shan;* narrative, A woman thinks of her lover's
residence, and complains that he does not come to her.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>By th’ eastern
gate, flat lies the ground,  
  And madder there grows on the slope.  
Hard by my lover's house is found;  
  He keeps away, and mocks my hope. <span id="page_99">p. 99</span>

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>Where chestnuts
grow, near th’ eastern gate,  
  There stands a row, where is your home.  
My heart turns aye to you, its mate,  
  But ah! to me you never come!

</div>
