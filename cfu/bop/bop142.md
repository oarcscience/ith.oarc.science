#### IV

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Tung Mên Chih Ch‘ih;* allusive. The praise of some virtuous and
intelligent lady.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>To steep your
hemp, you seek the moat,  
  Where lies the pool, th’ east gate beyond.  
*I* seek that lady, good and fair,  
  Who can to me in song respond. <span id="page_154">p. 154</span>

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>To steep your
grass-cloth plants, you seek  
  The pool that near the east gate lies.  
*I* seek that lady, good and fair,  
  Who can with me hold converse wise.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>Out by the east
gate, to the moat,  
  To steep your rope rush, you repair.  
Her pleasant converse to enjoy,  
  *I* seek that lady, good and fair.

</div>
