#### VI

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Chiu Yü;* allusive and narrative. The people of the east express
their admiration of the duke of Chou, and sorrow at his returning to the
west.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>The nine
inclosures of the net  
  The rud and bream keep tight.  
Our prince in dragon robe we see,  
  And skirt with figures bright.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>The geese brief
time fly round the isles;  
  Home bends the duke his way.  
’Twas only for two passing nights  
  He deigned with us to stay.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>Back to the land
now fly the geese;  
  The duke comes not again.  
’Twas only for two passing nights  
  He could with us remain.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_4">4</span></span>Short time the
single dragon robe  
  Among us we have had.  
Our duke, O take not to the west,  
  Nor bid our hearts be sad!

</div>
