#### III

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Chüan Êrh;* narrative. Lamenting the absence of a cherished friend,
probably of a husband.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>Though small my
basket, all my toil  
  Filled it with mouse-ears but in part.  
I set it on the path, and sighed  
  For the dear master of my heart.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>My steeds,
o’ertasked, their progress stayed,  
  When midway up that rocky height.  
Give me a cup from that gilt vase,  
  When shall this longing end in sight?

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>To mount that
lofty ridge I drove,  
  Until my steeds all changed their hue.  
A cup from that rhinoceros’ horn  
  May help my longing to subdue.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_4">4</span></span>Striving to reach
that flat-topped hill,  
  My steeds, worn out, relaxed their strain;  
My driver also sank oppressed:  
  I'll never see my lord again!

</div>
