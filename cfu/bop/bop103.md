#### V

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Tung Fang Wei Ming;* narrative and metaphorical. The irregularity
and disorder of the court of Ch‘i; as seen especially in the time of
giving the morning audience.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>I was putting my
clothes on upside down,  
  Before the eastern sky was clear.  
I was putting my clothes on upside down,  
  When a call to the court came here.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>I was putting my
clothes on upside down,  
  Ere showed the east the rising flame.  
I was putting my clothes on upside down,  
  When from the court an order came. <span id="page_109">p. 109</span>

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>On garden fence,
made but of willow wands,  
  E’en reckless fellows look with fear.  
Our prince can't tell the night from dawn;—  
  Too soon, or late, his calls appear!

</div>
