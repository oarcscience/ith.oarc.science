#### XVII

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Ching Nü;* narrative. A gentleman deplores his disappointment in
not being met by a lady according to engagement, and celebrates her
gifts and beauty.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>O sweet maiden, so
fair and retiring,  
  At the corner I'm waiting for you;  
And I'm scratching my head, and inquiring  
  What on earth it were best I should do.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>Oh! the maiden, so
handsome and coy,  
  For a pledge gave a slim rosy reed.  
Than the reed is she brighter, my joy;  
  On her loveliness how my thoughts feed!

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>In the pastures a
*t‘i* blade she sought,  
  And she gave it, so elegant, rare.  
Oh! the grass does not dwell in my thought,  
  But the donor, more elegant, fair.

</div>
