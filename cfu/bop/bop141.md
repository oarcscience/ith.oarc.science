#### III

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Hêng Mên;* narrative. The contentment and happiness of a poor
recluse.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>My only door some
pieces of crossed wood,  
  Within it I can rest enjoy. <span id="page_153">p. 153</span>  
I drink the water wimpling from the spring;  
  Nor hunger can my peace destroy.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>Purged from.
ambition's aims, I say, "For fish,  
  We need not bream caught in the Ho;  
Nor, to possess the sweets of love, require  
  To Ch‘i, to find a Chiang, to go.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>"The man contented
with his lot, a meal  
  Of fish without Ho carp can make;  
Nor needs, to rest in his domestic joy,  
  A Tzŭ of Sung as wife to take."

</div>
