#### V

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Kan T‘ang;* narrative. The love of the people for the memory of the
duke of Shao makes them love the trees beneath which he had rested.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>O fell not that
sweet pear tree!  
  See how its branches spread.  
    Spoil not its shade,  
    For Shao's chief laid  
  Beneath it his weary head.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>O clip not that
sweet pear tree!  
  Each twig and leaflet spare.  
    ’Tis sacred now,  
    Since the lord of Shao,  
  When weary, rested him there.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>O touch not that
sweet pear tree!  
  Bend not a twig of it now.  
    There long ago,  
    As the stories show,  
  Oft halted the chief of Shao.

</div>
