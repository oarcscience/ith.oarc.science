#### VIII

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Chien Chien Chih Shih;* narrative. Commemorating the hardships of a
long and difficult expedition to the east, aggravated by great rains.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>How high those
frowning rocks arise!  
  With awe they fill the mind. <span id="page_328">p. 328</span>  
Our way through streams, o’er mountains lies;  
  Toilsome the march we find.  
Eastward our expedition goes,  
Nor has our chief one hour's repose.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>Those frowning
rocks the heights surmount,  
  And fill the mind with dread.  
O’er hills, through streams, our steps we count;  
  When shall our march be sped?  
Our warrior hastens on the track,  
Nor thinks he of our drawing back.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>Look at the swine,
with legs all white,  
  Washed by the pools from stain!  
The moon wades through the Hyads bright,  
  Foretelling heavier rain.  
He at whose word we eastward fare  
No leisure has for other care.

</div>
