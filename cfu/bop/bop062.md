#### V

<div style="font-size: 90%">

The *Chu Kan;* narrative. A daughter of the house of Wei, married in
another state, expresses her longing to revisit Wei.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 20%; margin-right: 20%; font-size: 110%;">

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_1">1</span></span>With long and
tapering rods,  
  You angle in the Ch‘i.  
I think of you, dear friends,  
  Here far removed from Wei.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_2">2</span></span>Ch‘üan-yüan upon
the left,  
  Ch‘i on the right I view.  
But married far away,  
  To home I bade adieu.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_3">3</span></span>Those streams,
this on the right,  
  That on the left, appear.  
The laugh that shows your teeth,  
  Your tinkling gems, I hear.

<span class="margnote"><span id="an_4">4</span></span>I watch the cedar
oars  
  On Ch‘i, and boats of pine.  
O might I travel there,  
  And soothe this heart of mine!

</div>
